Senecio viridis var. radiatus

Senecio viridis var. radiatus, commonly known as the radiate greenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a radiate pattern, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
