Senecio ovalis

Senecio ovalis, commonly known as the oval leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, oval-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to store water in its leaves.

Senecio ovalis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
