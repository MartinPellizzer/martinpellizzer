Sedum hobsonii

Sedum hobsonii, commonly known as the Hobson's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a compact rosette, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it an ideal plant for arid and semi-arid landscapes.
