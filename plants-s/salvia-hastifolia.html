Salvia hastifolia

Salvia hastifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in gardens.

Salvia hastifolia is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses among Indigenous communities.
