Silene saxifraga var. pirinica

Silene saxifraga var. pirinica, commonly known as the Pirin catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.
