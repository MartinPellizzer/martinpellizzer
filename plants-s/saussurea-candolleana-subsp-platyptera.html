Saussurea candolleana subsp. platyptera

Saussurea candolleana subsp.

platyptera, commonly known as the flat-leafed saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves that are often flat and broad.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in mountainous regions.
