Stenocline ericoides

Stenocline ericoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats. It produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
