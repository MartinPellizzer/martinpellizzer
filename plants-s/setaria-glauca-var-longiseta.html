Setaria glauca var. longiseta

Setaria glauca var. longiseta, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it an effective pioneer species in disturbed areas.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
