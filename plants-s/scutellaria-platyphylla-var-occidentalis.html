Scutellaria platyphylla var. occidentalis

Scutellaria platyphylla var. occidentalis, commonly known as Western blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
