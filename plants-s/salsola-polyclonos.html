Salsola polyclonos

Salsola polyclonos, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America.

It is an annual or biennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive drought.

Salsola polyclonos is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
