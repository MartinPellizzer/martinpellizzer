Solanum aracc-papa

Solanum aracc-papa, commonly known as the purple potato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots that produce edible purple-colored potatoes. The plant typically grows in cool, high-altitude climates and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while its flowers are small and usually white or pale purple.

The tubers are rich in nutrients and are cultivated for their culinary and nutritional value.
