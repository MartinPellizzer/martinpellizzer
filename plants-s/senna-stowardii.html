Senna stowardii

Senna stowardii, commonly known as the Christmas tree senna, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the winter months. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance.

Senna stowardii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its year-round greenery and seasonal floral display.
