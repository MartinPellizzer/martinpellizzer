Salvia × cadevallii

Salvia × cadevallii, commonly known as the Blue Salvia, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color, providing a nice contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia × cadevallii is also known for its attractiveness to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
