Silene chia

Silene chia, commonly known as Mexican hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Silene chia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its historical significance includes use by indigenous peoples for both medicinal and ceremonial purposes.
