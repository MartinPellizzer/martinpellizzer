Scutellaria brachyspica

Scutellaria brachyspica, commonly known as narrow-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria brachyspica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
