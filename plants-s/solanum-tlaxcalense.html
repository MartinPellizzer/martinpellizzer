Solanum tlaxcalense

Solanum tlaxcalense, commonly known as the Tlaxcalan tomato, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, round fruits that resemble tomatoes in appearance. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in dry or semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ecological studies.
