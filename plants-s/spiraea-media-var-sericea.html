Spiraea media var. sericea

Spiraea media var. sericea, commonly known as silver weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its cascading branches and showy flowers.

It features finely cut, silvery-white leaves that give it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its elegant, flowing form and seasonal interest.
