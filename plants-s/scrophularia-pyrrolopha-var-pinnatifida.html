Scrophularia pyrrolopha var. pinnatifida

Scrophularia pyrrolopha var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are tubular and usually pale purple to yellow, arranged in spikes.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
