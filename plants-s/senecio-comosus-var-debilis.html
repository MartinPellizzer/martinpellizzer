Senecio comosus var. debilis

Senecio comosus var. debilis, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in arid regions.
