Symplocos aggregata

Symplocos aggregata, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos aggregata is valued for its ornamental berries and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
