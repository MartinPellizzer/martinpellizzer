Scolymocephalus melaleucus

Scolymocephalus melaleucus, commonly known as the blackwood or blackwood tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the bark is dark and fissured.

Scolymocephalus melaleucus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses by Indigenous Australians.
