Syzygium petelotii

Syzygium petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of New Guinea, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Syzygium petelotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
