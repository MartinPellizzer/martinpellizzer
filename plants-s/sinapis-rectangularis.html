Sinapis rectangularis

Sinapis rectangularis, commonly known as the broadleaf mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately lobed leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by pods containing several seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Sinapis rectangularis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
