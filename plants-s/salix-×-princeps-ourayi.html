Salix × princeps-ourayi

Salix × princeps-ourayi, commonly known as the Ouray willow, is a hybrid species native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in wetlands. The plant has slender, often multi-stemmed branches and produces catkins in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The Ouray willow is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks and prevent erosion.
