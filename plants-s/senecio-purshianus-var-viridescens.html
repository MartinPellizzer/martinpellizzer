Senecio purshianus var. viridescens

Senecio purshianus var. viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it its varietal name.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in chaparral and woodland areas.
