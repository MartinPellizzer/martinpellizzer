Silene platyphylla f. involucrata

Silene platyphylla f. involucrata, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in arid or semi-arid regions. It is a perennial herb that often forms dense clumps, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
