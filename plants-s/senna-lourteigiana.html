Senna lourteigiana

Senna lourteigiana, commonly known as the golden senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

Senna lourteigiana is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
