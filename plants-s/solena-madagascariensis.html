Solena madagascariensis

Solena madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar solena, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Solena madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
