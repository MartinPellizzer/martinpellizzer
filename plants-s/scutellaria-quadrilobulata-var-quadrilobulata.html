Scutellaria quadrilobulata var. quadrilobulata

Scutellaria quadrilobulata var. quadrilobulata, commonly known as the four-lobed skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodland habitats and features lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, two-lipped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its genus, Scutellaria, is characterized by a distinctive cap-like structure called a scutellum beneath the flower.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
