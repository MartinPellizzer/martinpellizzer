Sauropus gracilis

Sauropus gracilis, commonly known as the slender evergreen or narrow-leaf sauropus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually 5 to 15 centimeters long and are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small and yellowish, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

Sauropus gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
