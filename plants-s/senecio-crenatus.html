Senecio crenatus

Senecio crenatus, commonly known as the serrated groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive seed heads.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
