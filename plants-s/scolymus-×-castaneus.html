Scolymus × castaneus

Scolymus × castaneus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a hybrid plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces distinctive, spiky seed heads that persist into winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Scolymus × castaneus is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
