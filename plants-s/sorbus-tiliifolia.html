Sorbus tiliifolia

Sorbus tiliifolia, commonly known as the whitebeams, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

Sorbus tiliifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive bark and foliage.
