Salvia tepicensis

Salvia tepicensis, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive minty fragrance.

Salvia tepicensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
