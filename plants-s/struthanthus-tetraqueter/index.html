Struthanthus Tetraqueter

Struthanthus evening primrose, scientifically known as Struthanthus tetraqueter, is a striking member of the evening primrose family native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States. This plant is renowned for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the late afternoon, adding a burst of color to the desert landscape. It thrives in harsh conditions, showcasing remarkable adaptability to extreme temperatures and limited water availability. The plant's unique structure and ecological role make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike. Its beauty and resilience highlight the diversity of life in one of the world's most challenging environments.
