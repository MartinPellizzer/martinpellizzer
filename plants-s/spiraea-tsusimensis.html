Spiraea tsusimensis

Spiraea tsusimensis, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

Spiraea tsusimensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
