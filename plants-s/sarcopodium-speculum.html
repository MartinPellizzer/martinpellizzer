Sarcopodium speculum

Sarcopodium speculum, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its translucent, ribbon-like leaves that resemble ghostly forms.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on rhizomes for anchoring and nutrient absorption. Its leaves are typically green on the upper surface and transparent on the underside, creating a striking visual effect.

Sarcopodium speculum plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing excess nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
