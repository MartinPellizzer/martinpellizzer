Senecio lujae

Senecio lujae, commonly known as the Lujan's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, gray-green foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots enable it to thrive in well-drained soils and withstand periods of drought.

Senecio lujae is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
