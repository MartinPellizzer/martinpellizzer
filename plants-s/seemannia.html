Seemannia

Seemannia, commonly known as the white sage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Seemannia has lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
