Solidago caesia var. axillaris

Solidago caesia var. axillaris, commonly known as the axillary goldenrod, is a North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and marshes, often in the eastern United States. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for various invertebrates.
