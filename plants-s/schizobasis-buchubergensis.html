Schizobasis buchubergensis

Schizobasis buchubergensis, commonly known as the Buchberg mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Schizobasis buchubergensis is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
