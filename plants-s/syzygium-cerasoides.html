Syzygium cerasoides

Syzygium cerasoides, commonly known as the Java apple or cempaka, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Syzygium cerasoides is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
