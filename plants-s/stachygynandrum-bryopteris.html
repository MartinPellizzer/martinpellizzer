Stachygynandrum bryopteris

Stachygynandrum bryopteris, commonly known as the golden moss fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, golden hairs, giving it a striking appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or on rocky surfaces. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with sporangia clustered in small, umbel-like formations.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
