Scrophularia laevigata subsp. simplicifolia

Scrophularia laevigata subsp.

simplicifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowers are tubular and clustered in spikes, often displaying shades of pink or purple.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
