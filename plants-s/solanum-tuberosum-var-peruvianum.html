Solanum tuberosum var. peruvianum

Solanum tuberosum var. peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian potato, is a variant of the common potato with distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to the Andean regions of South America. The plant produces tubers that are typically smaller and more irregular in shape compared to the standard potato variety. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

The flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
