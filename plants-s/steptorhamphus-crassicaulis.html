Steptorhamphus crassicaulis

Steptorhamphus crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed grass.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Steptorhamphus crassicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
