Sinapis radicata

Sinapis radicata, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant has a basal rosette of dark green, lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters called racemes. Its seeds are contained in slender, elongated pods that split open when mature.

Sinapis radicata is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural fields.
