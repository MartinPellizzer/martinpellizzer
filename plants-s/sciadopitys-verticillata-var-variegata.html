Sciadopitys verticillata var. variegata

Sciadopitys verticillata var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Japanese white pine, is a unique evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant has a slow growth rate and forms a conical shape, making it a striking ornamental tree. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

Its distinctive foliage and historical significance in Japanese culture make it a valued specimen in botanical gardens and private collections.
