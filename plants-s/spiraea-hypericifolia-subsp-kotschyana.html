Spiraea hypericifolia subsp. kotschyana

Spiraea hypericifolia subsp.

kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's weeping spirea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, cascading clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has slender, arching stems that give it a weeping appearance, and its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
