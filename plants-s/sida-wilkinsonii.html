Sida wilkinsonii

Sida wilkinsonii, commonly known as the African mallow, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thick, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Sida wilkinsonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
