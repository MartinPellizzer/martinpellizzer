Scolopendrium vulgare var. multifidum

Scolopendrium vulgare var. multifidum, commonly known as the common clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern-like plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, spore-bearing strobili that form at the tips of its branching stems. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Scolopendrium vulgare var. multifidum plays a role in providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
