Strophanthus luteolus

Strophanthus luteolus, commonly known as the yellow-stalked African milkvine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its woody vines and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Strophanthus luteolus is also noted for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds used in traditional medicine.
