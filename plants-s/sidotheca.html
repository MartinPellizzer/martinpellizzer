Sidotheca

Sidotheca, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, erect flowering stem that bears a cluster of small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while the flowers are followed by spherical, dry fruits called achenes. Sidotheca species are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

They are often used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental appeal.
