Salvia lavandulifolia var. vellerea

Salvia lavandulifolia var. vellerea, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves that are used both in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for herbal teas and natural remedies.
