Steirodiscus tagetes

Steirodiscus tagetes, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright orange and yellow petals. The plant has a bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spherical flower heads that are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.
