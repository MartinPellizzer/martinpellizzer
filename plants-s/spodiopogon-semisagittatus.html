Spodiopogon semisagittatus

Spodiopogon semisagittatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched, with small, spike-like clusters of flowers at the tips. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Spodiopogon semisagittatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
