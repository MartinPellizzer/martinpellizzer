Sebastiania hispida f. villosula

Sebastiania hispida f. villosula, commonly known as the hairy Sebastiania, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and distinctive yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
