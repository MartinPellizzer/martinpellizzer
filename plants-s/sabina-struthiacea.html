Sabina struthiacea

Sabina struthiacea, commonly known as the desert juniper, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of blue-gray or grayish-green berries. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater.

Sabina struthiacea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
