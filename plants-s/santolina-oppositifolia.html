Santolina oppositifolia

Santolina oppositifolia, commonly known as Spanish santolina, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
