Scheuchzeria palustris var. americana

Scheuchzeria palustris var. americana, commonly known as the American swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and has a robust root system that helps stabilize the soil.

This species plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
