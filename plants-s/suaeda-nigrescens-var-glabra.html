Suaeda nigrescens var. glabra

Suaeda nigrescens var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to arid and saline environments.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, and its small, greenish flowers are typically borne in dense clusters. The plant produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, enhancing its ability to colonize disturbed or saline soils. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and desert margins where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its morphology reflects evolutionary adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.
