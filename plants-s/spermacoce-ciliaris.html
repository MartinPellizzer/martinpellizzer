Spermacoce ciliaris

Spermacoce ciliaris, commonly known as the silky spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Spermacoce ciliaris is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, open habitats.
