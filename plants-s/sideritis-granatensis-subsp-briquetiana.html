Sideritis granatensis subsp. briquetiana

Sideritis granatensis subsp.

briquetiana, commonly known as the Granada sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is found in areas with a temperate climate. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
