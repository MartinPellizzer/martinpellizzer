Solanum andigenum var. carhua

Solanum andigenum var. carhua, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red upon ripening. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
