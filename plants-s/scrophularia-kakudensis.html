Scrophularia kakudensis

Scrophularia kakudensis, commonly known as the Japanese figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pink or purple and arranged in clusters.

Scrophularia kakudensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
