Sedum occidentale

Sedum occidentale, commonly known as Western stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to North America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellowish-orange flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
