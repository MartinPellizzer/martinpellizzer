Schrankia microphylla var. floridana

Schrankia microphylla var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils, often in open woodlands or along coastal areas. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to full sun conditions.

The species is considered a valuable component of native plant communities due to its ecological role and ornamental appeal.
