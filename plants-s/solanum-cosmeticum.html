Solanum cosmeticum

Solanum cosmeticum, commonly known as the beauty berry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The plant produces violet flowers in the spring, which are followed by the ornamental berries. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract wildlife.

Solanum cosmeticum is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
