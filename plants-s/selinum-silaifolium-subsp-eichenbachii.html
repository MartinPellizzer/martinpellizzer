Selinum silaifolium subsp. eichenbachii

Selinum silaifolium subsp.

eichenbachii, commonly known as the Eichenbach's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branched stems.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
