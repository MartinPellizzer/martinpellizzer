Symphonia verrucosa

Symphonia verrucosa, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow-wood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
