Solanum anceps

Solanum anceps, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant typically grows as a vine, with heart-shaped leaves and showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Solanum anceps is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
