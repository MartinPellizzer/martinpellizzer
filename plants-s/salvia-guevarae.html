Salvia guevarae

Salvia guevarae, commonly known as Guevara's sage, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the dry, rocky regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow leaves, and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Salvia guevarae is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
