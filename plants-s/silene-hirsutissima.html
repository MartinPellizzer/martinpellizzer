Silene hirsutissima

Silene hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Silene hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
