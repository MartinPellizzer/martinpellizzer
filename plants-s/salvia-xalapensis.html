Salvia xalapensis

Salvia xalapensis, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Salvia xalapensis is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its purported health benefits.
