Silene campanulata var. greenei

Silene campanulata var. greenei, commonly known as the Greene's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
