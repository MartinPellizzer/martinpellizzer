Scirpus constitutionis

Scirpus constitutionis, commonly known as the constitution sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats, often found in freshwater environments.

Scirpus constitutionis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
