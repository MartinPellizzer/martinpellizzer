Solanum micans

Solanum micans, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a deep blue or black. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum micans is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ornamental value.
