Sabina pacifica

Sabina pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific juniper, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal regions.

Sabina pacifica is valued for its aromatic wood and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
