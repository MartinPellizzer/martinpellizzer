Scirpoides holoschoenus subsp. australis

Scirpoides holoschoenus subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale brown flowers.

The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its morphology and ecological function make it a significant component of wetland ecosystems in Australia.
