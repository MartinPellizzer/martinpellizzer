Silene napuligera

Silene napuligera, commonly known as the Napo rockweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Silene napuligera is known for its resilience and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
