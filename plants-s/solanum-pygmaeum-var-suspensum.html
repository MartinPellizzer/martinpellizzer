Solanum pygmaeum var. suspensum

Solanum pygmaeum var. suspensum, commonly known as the hanging nightshade, is a compact, trailing perennial native to South America.

It features small, velvety leaves and produces clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
