Silene pichleri

Silene pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Silene pichleri is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to high-altitude habitats.
