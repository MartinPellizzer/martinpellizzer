Senecio mauricei

Senecio mauricei, commonly known as the Maurice's groundsel, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and protected in its native habitat.
