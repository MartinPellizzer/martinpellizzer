Sorghum filifolium

Sorghum filifolium, commonly known as the silky sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated for landscaping purposes.
