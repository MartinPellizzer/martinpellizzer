Salix libanotica

Salix libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon willow, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in catkins. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Salix libanotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
