Sarcoglottis ventricosa

Sarcoglottis ventricosa, commonly known as the bladder orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, inflated seed pods that resemble bladders, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Sarcoglottis ventricosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
