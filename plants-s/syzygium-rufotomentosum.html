Syzygium rufotomentosum

Syzygium rufotomentosum, commonly known as the rough-haired clove or rufotomentous clove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and its rough, hairy leaves. It produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain aromatic seeds.

Syzygium rufotomentosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine.
