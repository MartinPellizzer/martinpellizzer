Stipa coreana var. japonica

Stipa coreana var. japonica, commonly known as Korean feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct ligule and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is adapted to temperate climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
