Satureja intricata subvar. dufourii

Satureja intricata subvar. dufourii, commonly known as Dufour's savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong, minty fragrance.

This subspecies is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
