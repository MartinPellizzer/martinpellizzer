Saponaria emineana

Saponaria emineana, commonly known as the Eminean soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains saponins, which are natural detergents. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Saponaria emineana is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
