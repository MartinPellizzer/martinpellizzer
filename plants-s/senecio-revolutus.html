Senecio revolutus

Senecio revolutus, commonly known as the curlydock, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curly, and often hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a loose, open cluster, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Senecio revolutus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
