Symplocos pubescens

Symplocos pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Symplocos pubescens thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
