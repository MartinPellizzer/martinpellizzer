Senecio poeppigii var. discoideus

Senecio poeppigii var. discoideus, commonly known as the "discoideus senecio," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
