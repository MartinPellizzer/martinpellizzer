Solanum unifoliatum

Solanum unifoliatum, commonly known as the single-leaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by yellow berries that contain the seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum unifoliatum is often found in dry, open habitats and can be a weed in agricultural settings.
