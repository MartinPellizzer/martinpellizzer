Spathodea campanulata subsp. congolana

Spathodea campanulata subsp.

congolana, commonly known as the Congolese flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy, flame-colored bracts that surround its small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows as a tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are large, simple, and alternate, often with a slightly waxy texture.

The species is valued for its vibrant floral display and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for ornamental purposes.
