Stipa klemenzii

Stipa klemenzii, commonly known as Klemenz's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often with a rough, hairy texture.

Stipa klemenzii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
