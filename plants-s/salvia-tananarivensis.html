Salvia tananarivensis

Salvia tananarivensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sage, is a perennial shrub native to Madagascar.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces striking violet-blue flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage, typical of the Salvia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
