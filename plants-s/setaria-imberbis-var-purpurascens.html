Setaria imberbis var. purpurascens

Setaria imberbis var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple foxtail, is a variant of the grass species Setaria imberbis.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple tinge on the inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and open areas. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are typically arranged in a dense, upright growth habit.

The inflorescence is a spike-like structure composed of bracts that are often tinged with purple, making it visually striking.
