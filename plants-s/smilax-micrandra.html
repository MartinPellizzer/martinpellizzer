Smilax micrandra

Smilax micrandra, commonly known as the small-leaf greenbriar, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Smilax micrandra thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystem, supporting various wildlife species.
