Silene italica var. wallii

Silene italica var. wallii, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Silene italica var. wallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
