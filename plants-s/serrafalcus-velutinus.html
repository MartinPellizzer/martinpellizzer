Serrafalcus velutinus

Serrafalcus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a delicate, cascading growth habit, making it popular as a hanging plant. Its foliage is usually a deep green, with a slight sheen that enhances its visual appeal.

It prefers bright, indirect light and consistent moisture, making it a low-maintenance addition to indoor gardens.
