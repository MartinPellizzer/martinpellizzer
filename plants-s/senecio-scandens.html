Senecio scandens

Senecio scandens, commonly known as the climbing groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its trailing growth habit and can form dense mats when grown in suitable conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow, with ray florets that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio scandens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
