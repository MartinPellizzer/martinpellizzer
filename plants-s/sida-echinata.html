Sida echinata

Sida echinata, commonly known as prickly sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils. The plant features small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, sometimes hairy surfaces.

Sida echinata is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, and it has adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions.
