Senecio gandin

Senecio gandin, commonly known as the Ethiopian groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia and Eritrea. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family.

Senecio gandin is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
