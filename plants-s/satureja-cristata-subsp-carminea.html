Satureja cristata subsp. carminea

Satureja cristata subsp.

carminea, commonly known as the carmine mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a reddish hue, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
