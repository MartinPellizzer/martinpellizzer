Sethia erythroxyloides

Sethia erythroxyloides, commonly known as the redwood tree, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a tall, evergreen conifer that can grow up to 300 feet in height. The tree has a thick, fibrous bark that provides resistance to fire and pests. Its needles are dark green and shed annually, while its cones are small and woody.

This plant plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
