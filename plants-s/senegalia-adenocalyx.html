Senegalia adenocalyx

Senegalia adenocalyx, commonly known as the yellow burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Acacia genus. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and it is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Senegalia adenocalyx is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its growth habit and drought tolerance make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
