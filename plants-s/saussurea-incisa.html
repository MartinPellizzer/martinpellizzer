Saussurea incisa

Saussurea incisa, commonly known as the Indian saffron or the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color, resembling those of saffron.

Saussurea incisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
