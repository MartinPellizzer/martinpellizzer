Scilla kurdistanica

Scilla kurdistanica, commonly known as the Kurdish squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue or violet star-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, and its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Scilla kurdistanica is often found in alpine and woodland habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
