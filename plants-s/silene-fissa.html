Silene fissa

Silene fissa, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.
