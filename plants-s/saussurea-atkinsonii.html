Saussurea atkinsonii

Saussurea atkinsonii, commonly known as the Atkinson's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces a distinctive, often aromatic scent.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
