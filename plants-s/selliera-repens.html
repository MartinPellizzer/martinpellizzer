Selliera repens

Selliera repens, commonly known as the carpet flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and easy care, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
