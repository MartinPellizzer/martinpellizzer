Symplocos magdalenae

Symplocos magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 4 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy surface.

Symplocos magdalenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
