Sarcochilus siamensis

Sarcochilus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and purple, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Sarcochilus siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
