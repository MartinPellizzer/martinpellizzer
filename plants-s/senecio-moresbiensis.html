Senecio moresbiensis

Senecio moresbiensis, commonly known as the Moresby's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Senecio moresbiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
