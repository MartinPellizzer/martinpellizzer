Syzygium fluvicola

Syzygium fluvicola, commonly known as the river syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, fragrant fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Syzygium fluvicola thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found near riverbanks and in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitat.
