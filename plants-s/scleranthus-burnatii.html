Scleranthus burnatii

Scleranthus burnatii, commonly known as the Burnat's thorned weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and rocky habitats. The plant features slender, branched stems covered with small, sharp spines. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Scleranthus burnatii is adapted to arid environments, with a rapid life cycle that allows it to complete its growth and reproduction before the onset of drought.
