Stevia chamaedrys

Stevia chamaedrys, commonly known as mountain sweet or rock stevia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Stevia chamaedrys is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its compact growth habit and delicate flowers.
