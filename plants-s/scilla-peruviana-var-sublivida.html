Scilla peruviana var. sublivida

Scilla peruviana var. sublivida, commonly known as the Peruvian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moderate climates.
