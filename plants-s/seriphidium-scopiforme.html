Seriphidium scopiforme

Seriphidium scopiforme, commonly known as the desert hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows from 30 to 60 centimeters tall, with slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Seriphidium scopiforme is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in harsh, sun-exposed conditions.
