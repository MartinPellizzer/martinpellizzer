Solanum riobambense

Solanum riobambense, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often found in cloud forests and rocky soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually purple or violet in color. Its fruit, a yellow or orange berry, contains toxic compounds and is not suitable for human consumption.

Solanum riobambense is part of the nightshade family, which includes many economically important plants.
