Stuckenia filiformis subsp. alpina

Stuckenia filiformis subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a flowering plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its slender, filamentous stems and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant typically grows in freshwater lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, often forming dense underwater vegetation. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, adapted for efficient light absorption in aquatic environments.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
