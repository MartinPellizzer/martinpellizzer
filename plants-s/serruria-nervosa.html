Serruria nervosa

Serruria nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined serruria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it its scientific name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
