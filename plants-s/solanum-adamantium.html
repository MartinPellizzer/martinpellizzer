Solanum adamantium

Solanum adamantium, commonly known as the diamondleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, diamond-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruits are typically berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its unique appearance and toxic properties, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
