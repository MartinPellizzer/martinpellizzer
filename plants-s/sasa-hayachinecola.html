Sasa hayachinecola

Sasa hayachinecola, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Hayachine bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often used in traditional landscaping and as a ornamental plant. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.

Sasa hayachinecola is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
