Senecio variegatus

Senecio variegatus, commonly known as the variegated groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features serrated, dark green leaves with white or yellow markings, and it produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value comes from its distinctive foliage and long blooming period.
