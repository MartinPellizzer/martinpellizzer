Scorzonera hissarica

Scorzonera hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar scorzonera, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and can be quite large, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scorzonera hissarica is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
