Suregada comorensis

Suregada comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian sedge, is a perennial herb native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for small organisms.
