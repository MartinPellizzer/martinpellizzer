Stevia podocephala

Stevia podocephala, commonly known as the Hawaiian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained interest in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
