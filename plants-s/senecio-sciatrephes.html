Senecio sciatrephes

Senecio sciatrephes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is occasionally grown in gardens.
