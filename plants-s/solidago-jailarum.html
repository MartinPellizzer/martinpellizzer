Solidago jailarum

Solidago jailarum, commonly known as the Mexican goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small golden spheres. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces numerous small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Solidago jailarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
