Sida nervosa

Sida nervosa, commonly known as the smallleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Sida nervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
