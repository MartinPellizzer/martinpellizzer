Sideritis scordioides var. hirta

Sideritis scordioides var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy sickle vetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for their antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects.
