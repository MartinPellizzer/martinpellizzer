Sideritis plumosa

Sideritis plumosa, commonly known as the featherleaf sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Sideritis plumosa belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
