Satureja paradoxa

Satureja paradoxa, commonly known as paradox mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, greenish-white flowers and fragrant, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and rosemary.

Satureja paradoxa is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
