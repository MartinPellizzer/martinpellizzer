Scrophularia aquatica var. pubescens

Scrophularia aquatica var. pubescens, commonly known as water figwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, often marshy habitats and is often found near streams or in flooded meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
