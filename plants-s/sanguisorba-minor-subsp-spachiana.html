Sanguisorba minor subsp. spachiana

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

spachiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burnet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland edges and meadows.

The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that appear in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
