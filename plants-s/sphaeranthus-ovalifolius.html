Sphaeranthus ovalifolius

Sphaeranthus ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaf globeflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence that resembles a small sphere, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are oval-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and are pollinated by insects.
