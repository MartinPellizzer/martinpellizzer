Syzygium hutchinsonii

Syzygium hutchinsonii, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by spherical, bright red fruits resembling apples. The fruit contains a single seed and is often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in forested areas.
