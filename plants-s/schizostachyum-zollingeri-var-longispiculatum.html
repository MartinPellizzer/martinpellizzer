Schizostachyum zollingeri var. longispiculatum

Schizostachyum zollingeri var. longispiculatum, commonly known as the long-spined bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, feathery foliage. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique appearance.
