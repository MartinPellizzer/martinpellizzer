Serpyllopsis caespitosa var. elongata

Serpyllopsis caespitosa var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated serpyllopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a tufted growth habit with slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous root system, which aids in its survival in nutrient-poor soils.
