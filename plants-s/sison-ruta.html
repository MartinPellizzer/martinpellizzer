Sison ruta

Sison ruta, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains volatile oils, including compounds like rutin and methyl eugenol, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in gardens and wild areas.

Sison ruta has been historically used in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and carminative effects.
