Sideritis libanotica

Sideritis libanotica, commonly known as Lebanese sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Sideritis libanotica is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
