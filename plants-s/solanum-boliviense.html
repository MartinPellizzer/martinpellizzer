Solanum boliviense

Solanum boliviense, commonly known as the Bolivian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or purple and bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that contains seeds and is often used in traditional medicine.
