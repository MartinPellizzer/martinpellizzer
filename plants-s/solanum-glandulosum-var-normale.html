Solanum glandulosum var. normale

Solanum glandulosum var. normale, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features upright stems covered in glandular hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are also covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
