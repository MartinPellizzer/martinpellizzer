Solidago ulmifolia var. ulmifolia

Solidago ulmifolia var. ulmifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are arranged in panicles and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
