Silene petersonii var. typica

Silene petersonii var. typica, commonly known as Peterson's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
