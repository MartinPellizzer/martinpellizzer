Silene chirensis var. macropetala

Silene chirensis var. macropetala, commonly known as the large-petaled catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows.

Its unique floral structure and habitat make it a notable species in botanical studies.
