Senecio euxinus

Senecio euxinus, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers and distinctive inflated calyx that encases the fruit. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often eaten raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Senecio euxinus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
