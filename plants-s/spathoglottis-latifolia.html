Spathoglottis latifolia

Spathoglottis latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike bearing numerous small flowers.

Spathoglottis latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
