Satureja kudoi

Satureja kudoi, commonly known as Siberian savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are aromatic and have a minty, slightly spicy scent, making it a popular herb in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Satureja kudoi thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
