Smilax fluminensis

Smilax fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or yellow smilax, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny tendrils. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a waxy surface.

Smilax fluminensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
