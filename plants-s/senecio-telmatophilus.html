Senecio telmatophilus

Senecio telmatophilus, commonly known as the marsh heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, fleshy leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio telmatophilus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
