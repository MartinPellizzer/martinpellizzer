Saxifraga hugueninii

Saxifraga hugueninii, commonly known as the Huguenin's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga hugueninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
