Sasa chiisanensis

Sasa chiisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial shrub-like grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stiff, upright culms that form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasa chiisanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
