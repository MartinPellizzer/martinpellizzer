Stachys kurdica subsp. asterocalyx

Stachys kurdica subsp.

asterocalyx, commonly known as the Kurdish hedgehog plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant features dense clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it often grows in rocky or gravelly soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
