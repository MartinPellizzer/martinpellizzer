Syzygium kuchingense

Syzygium kuchingense, commonly known as the Kuching apple or langsat, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, red-orange fruits with a thick, leathery rind. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a juicy, white aril, which is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
