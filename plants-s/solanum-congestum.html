Solanum congestum

Solanum congestum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Solanum congestum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and flowering display.
