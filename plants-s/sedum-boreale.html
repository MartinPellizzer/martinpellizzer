Sedum boreale

Sedum boreale, commonly known as Arctic sedum, is a perennial succulent native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Sedum boreale is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is used in landscaping for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
