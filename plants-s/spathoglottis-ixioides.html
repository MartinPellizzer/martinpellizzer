Spathoglottis ixioides

Spathoglottis ixioides, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its inflorescence consists of a single, showy spike.

Spathoglottis ixioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
