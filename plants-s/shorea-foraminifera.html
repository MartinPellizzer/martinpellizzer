Shorea foraminifera

Shorea foraminifera, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 50 meters tall, characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
