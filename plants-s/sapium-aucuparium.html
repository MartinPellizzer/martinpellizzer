Sapium aucuparium

Sapium aucuparium, commonly known as the American persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe.
