Stelis tridactyloides

Stelis tridactyloides, commonly known as the three-fingered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical and subtropical forests. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small and delicate, often arranged in a raceme, with a unique trichome-like structure on the petals.

Stelis tridactyloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
