Salix dolichostyla

Salix dolichostyla, commonly known as the long-styled willow, is a species of willow native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas. Its leaves are elongated and have a smooth, slightly waxy surface.

Salix dolichostyla plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
