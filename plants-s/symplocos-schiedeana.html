Symplocos schiedeana

Symplocos schiedeana, commonly known as the whiteleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces showy, dark purple berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and turn reddish in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

Symplocos schiedeana thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
