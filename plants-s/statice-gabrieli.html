Statice gabrieli

Statice gabrieli, commonly known as the Gabrieli's sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt, adapting it to coastal environments.

Statice gabrieli is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
