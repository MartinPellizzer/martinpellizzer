Sisyrinchium weirii

Sisyrinchium weirii, commonly known as the Sierra daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
