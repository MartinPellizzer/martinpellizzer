Sarracenia × wilksiana

Sarracenia × wilksiana, commonly known as the Wilks' pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, tubular leaves that form deep, pitcher-shaped traps. These traps secrete a sticky liquid that lures and digests insects, providing the plant with essential nutrients. The plant prefers acidic, boggy soils and requires high humidity and bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its striking red and green coloration makes it a popular choice for both botanical gardens and private collections.
