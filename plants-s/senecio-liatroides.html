Senecio liatroides

Senecio liatroides, commonly known as brittlestem senecio, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its stems are often covered in a fine, woolly hair, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
