Sequoiadendron

Sequoiadendron, commonly known as the giant sequoia, is a species of coniferous tree native to California's Sierra Nevada mountains.

It is one of the largest and oldest living trees, with some individuals reaching heights of over 300 feet and ages exceeding 3,000 years. The tree has a distinctive reddish-brown bark that is thick and fire-resistant, helping it survive in its native environment. Its cones contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, and its leaves are small, scale-like structures that are shed annually.

The giant sequoia plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to carbon sequestration.
