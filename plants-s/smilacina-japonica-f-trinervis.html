Smilacina japonica f. trinervis

Smilacina japonica f. trinervis, commonly known as the Japanese climbing smilax, is a variant of the smilacina genus native to East Asia.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
