Syzygium operculatum var. obovatum

Syzygium operculatum var. obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf lilly pilly, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which appear in spring. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
