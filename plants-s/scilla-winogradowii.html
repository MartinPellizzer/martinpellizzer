Scilla winogradowii

Scilla winogradowii, commonly known as the Armenian scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Scilla winogradowii is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable conditions.
