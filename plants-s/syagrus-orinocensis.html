Syagrus orinocensis

Syagrus orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits, which are edible and have a sweet, nutty flavor. Syagrus orinocensis thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and riverine areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
