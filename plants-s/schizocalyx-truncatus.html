Schizocalyx truncatus

Schizocalyx truncatus, commonly known as the rough-leaved hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often lobed at the base. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Schizocalyx truncatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
