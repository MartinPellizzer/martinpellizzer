Setaria parviflora

Setaria parviflora, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and its drought tolerance.

Setaria parviflora is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
