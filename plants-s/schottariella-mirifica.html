Schottariella mirifica

Schottariella mirifica, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and greenhouses.
