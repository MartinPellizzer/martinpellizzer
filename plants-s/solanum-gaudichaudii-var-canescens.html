Solanum gaudichaudii var. canescens

Solanum gaudichaudii var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically orange to red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
