Shorea leucobotrya

Shorea leucobotrya, commonly known as white-fruited mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. This tree is typically found in coastal areas, including mangrove forests, where it thrives in brackish water conditions. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.
