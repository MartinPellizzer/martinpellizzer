Saguaster capitis-yorki

Saguaster capitis-yorki, commonly known as the Yorki skullflower, is a rare and unique plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, skull-like floral structure that resembles a human face, with intricate patterns of petals and sepals. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish. Its flowers bloom in the spring, producing a striking contrast of white and purple hues.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species and is protected in its native range.
