Stanhopea tigrina var. atrata

Stanhopea tigrina var. atrata, commonly known as the Black Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
