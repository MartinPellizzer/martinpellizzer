Sisyrinchium sarmentosum

Sisyrinchium sarmentosum, commonly known as the creeping blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance.

Sisyrinchium sarmentosum is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
