Sedum proponticum

Sedum proponticum, commonly known as the Pontic stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southeastern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is a perennial species that spreads via rhizomes, forming dense mats on the ground.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
