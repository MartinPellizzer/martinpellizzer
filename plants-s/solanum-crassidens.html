Solanum crassidens

Solanum crassidens, commonly known as the rough bladdernut, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into rounded, bladder-like fruits. Its botanical features include glandular hairs and a distinctive, rough texture on its foliage.

Solanum crassidens is often found in moist, disturbed habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
