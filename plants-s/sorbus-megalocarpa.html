Sorbus megalocarpa

Sorbus megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that are dark red to black in color, which mature in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges.

Sorbus megalocarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
