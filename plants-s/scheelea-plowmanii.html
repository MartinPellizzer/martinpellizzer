Scheelea plowmanii

Scheelea plowmanii, commonly known as the Plowman's palm, is a rare and unique species of cycad native to the island of Trinidad.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and faces threats from habitat loss and environmental changes.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this remarkable botanical species.
