Sorghum exsertum

Sorghum exsertum, commonly known as the slender sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, two-lobed flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
