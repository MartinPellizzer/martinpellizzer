Senecio pseudosubsessilis

Senecio pseudosubsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is known for its hairy, narrow leaves. It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
