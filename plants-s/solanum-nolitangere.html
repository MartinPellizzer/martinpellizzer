Solanum nolitangere

Solanum nolitangere, commonly known as the "do not touch" tomato, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. It is typically found in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is not suitable for consumption and is often avoided in agricultural settings.
