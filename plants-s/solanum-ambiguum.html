Solanum ambiguum

Solanum ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it often grows in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for food or ornamental purposes.
