Sudamerlycaste denningiana

Sudamerlycaste denningiana, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
