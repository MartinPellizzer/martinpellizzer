Saponaria stenopetala

Saponaria stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved soaproot, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils. It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is known for its mild soap-like scent.

Saponaria stenopetala is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
