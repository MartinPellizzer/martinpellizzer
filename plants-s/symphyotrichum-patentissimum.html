Symphyotrichum patentissimum

Symphyotrichum patentissimum, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
