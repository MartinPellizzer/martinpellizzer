Silene antirrhina var. vaccariifolia

Silene antirrhina var. vaccariifolia, commonly known as the Vaccariifolia campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
