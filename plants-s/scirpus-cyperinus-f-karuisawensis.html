Scirpus cyperinus f. karuisawensis

Scirpus cyperinus f. karuisawensis, commonly known as the Karuisawa sedge, is a variant of the common sedge found in wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in marshy areas and is adapted to moist or waterlogged soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
