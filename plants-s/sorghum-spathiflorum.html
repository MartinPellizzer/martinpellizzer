Sorghum spathiflorum

Sorghum spathiflorum, commonly known as the white sorghum, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed grains that are typically used for food or animal feed. It thrives in warm climates and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
