Sisymbrium crassifolium var. scaposum

Sisymbrium crassifolium var. scaposum, commonly known as the tall leafed broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often found in association with grasses and other herbaceous species. Its structure consists of a stem that attaches to the host plant, with no true leaves, and it produces small, yellow flowers.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a role in ecological systems by influencing host plant growth and nutrient cycling.
