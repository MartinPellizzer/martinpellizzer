Scolopia rigida

Scolopia rigida, commonly known as rigid scolopia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in terminal umbels, blooming in early spring.

Scolopia rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
