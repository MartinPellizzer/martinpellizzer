Senecio fluminensis

Senecio fluminensis, commonly known as the river senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Senecio fluminensis is also known for its ability to produce a milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
