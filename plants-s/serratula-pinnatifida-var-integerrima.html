Serratula pinnatifida var. integerrima

Serratula pinnatifida var. integerrima, commonly known as the tall golden ragwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear yellow-green composite flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in some areas.
