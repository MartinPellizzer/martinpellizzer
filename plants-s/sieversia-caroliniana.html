Sieversia caroliniana

Sieversia caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina sievegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of swamps, thriving in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Sieversia caroliniana plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
