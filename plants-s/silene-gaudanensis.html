Silene gaudanensis

Silene gaudanensis, commonly known as the Gaudan's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Silene gaudanensis is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
