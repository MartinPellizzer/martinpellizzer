Sesbania benthamiana var. erubescens

Sesbania benthamiana var. erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered sesbania, is a variant of the sesbania genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, making it visually distinctive. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

This variant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
