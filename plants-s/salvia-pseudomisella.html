Salvia pseudomisella

Salvia pseudomisella, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are often hairy and have a strong, minty fragrance.

Salvia pseudomisella is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
