Salvia connivens

Salvia connivens, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the late afternoon and evening. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia connivens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
