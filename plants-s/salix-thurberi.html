Salix thurberi

Salix thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and stream sides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
