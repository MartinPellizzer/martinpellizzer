Saxifraga moschata var. firmata

Saxifraga moschata var. firmata, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils. The plant features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with a slightly woolly texture, and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are often clustered together, creating a delicate and showy display.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
