Sedella carinthiaca

Sedella carinthiaca, commonly known as the Carinthian sedella, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Sedella carinthiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
