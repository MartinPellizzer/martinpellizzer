Scleromelum aurantiacum

Scleromelum aurantiacum, commonly known as the yellow scaly melon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, characterized by its dense, scaly stems and waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red fruits that are edible when ripe.

Scleromelum aurantiacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
