Selaginella moritziana var. elongata

Selaginella moritziana var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often elongated and slightly falcate. It produces cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the reproductive organs.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
