Sedum athoum

Sedum athoum, commonly known as the silver rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring or early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its low maintenance and drought tolerance make it an ideal plant for gardeners looking to add ornamental value with minimal effort.
