Sambucus javanica var. borealis

Sambucus javanica var. borealis, commonly known as the northern elderberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when ripe, though they are typically harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
