Sedum tachingshuianum

Sedum tachingshuianum, commonly known as the Tachingshuian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in dry landscapes.
