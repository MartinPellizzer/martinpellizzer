Stachys dregeana

Stachys dregeana, commonly known as the purpleflower betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features upright stems that bear whorled clusters of violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Stachys dregeana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and drought tolerance.
