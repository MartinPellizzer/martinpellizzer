Sideritis lasiantha

Sideritis lasiantha, commonly known as the Greek oregano or mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are aromatic, hairy, and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Sideritis lasiantha thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.
