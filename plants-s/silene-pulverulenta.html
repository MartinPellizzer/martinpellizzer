Silene pulverulenta

Silene pulverulenta, commonly known as the dusty pink campion, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its common name.

Silene pulverulenta is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native environments.
