Scilla petiolata

Scilla petiolata, commonly known as the long-stemmed scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, and its flowers are often arranged in a loose umbel. It is also known for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
