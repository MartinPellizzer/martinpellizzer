Skeptrostachys latipetala

Skeptrostachys latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petioled stachys, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
