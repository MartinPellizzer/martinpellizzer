Silene affghanica

Silene affghanica, commonly known as the Afghan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene affghanica is often found in mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
