Streptopus streptopoides f. atrocarpus

Streptopus streptopoides f. atrocarpus, commonly known as the dark-stemmed starwort, is a rare and distinctive variant of the starwort genus.

It is characterized by its dark purple or blackish stems, which contrast with its bright yellow flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure, which features five pointed petals and a central cluster of stamens.
