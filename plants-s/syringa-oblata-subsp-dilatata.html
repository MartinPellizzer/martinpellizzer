Syringa oblata subsp. dilatata

Syringa oblata subsp.

dilatata, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and has a rounded, spreading form. Syringa oblata subsp.

dilatata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its showy floral display.
