Salix cardiophylla subsp. urbaniana

Salix cardiophylla subsp.

urbaniana, commonly known as the urban willow, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often grayish-green in color.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near waterways or in wetlands.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
