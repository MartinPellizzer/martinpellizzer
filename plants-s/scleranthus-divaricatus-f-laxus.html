Scleranthus divaricatus f. laxus

Scleranthus divaricatus f. laxus, commonly known as the loose-stemmed scarlet pimpernel, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize open spaces make it an adaptable and resilient species.
