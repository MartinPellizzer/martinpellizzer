Samyda parvifolia

Samyda parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved samyda, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are small, ovate, and often pubescent, with a distinctive pale green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbellate clusters, which are followed by small, woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Samyda parvifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
