Sorbus banatica

Sorbus banatica, commonly known as the Bornean whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, apple-like berries that are edible for some bird species. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often with shallow fissures, and it can grow up to 20 meters in height in suitable conditions.

Sorbus banatica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
