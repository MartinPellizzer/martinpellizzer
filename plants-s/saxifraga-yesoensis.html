Saxifraga yesoensis

Saxifraga yesoensis, commonly known as the Yesso saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers, which are usually white or pink, bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to mountainous landscapes.

Saxifraga yesoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
