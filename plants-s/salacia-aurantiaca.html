Salacia aurantiaca

Salacia aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers and woody stems, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is known for its adaptability to tropical climates. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain seeds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
