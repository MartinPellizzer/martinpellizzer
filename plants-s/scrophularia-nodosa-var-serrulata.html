Scrophularia nodosa var. serrulata

Scrophularia nodosa var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated figwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Scrophularia nodosa var. serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and blooms.
