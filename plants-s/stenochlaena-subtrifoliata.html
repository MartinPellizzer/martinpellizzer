Stenochlaena subtrifoliata

Stenochlaena subtrifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three lance-shaped leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking foliage.
