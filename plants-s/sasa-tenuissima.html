Sasa tenuissima

Sasa tenuissima, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.

Sasa tenuissima is also known for its ability to form dense thickets, which can help prevent soil erosion.
