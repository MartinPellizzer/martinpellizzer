Salvia comosa var. hypoglauca

Salvia comosa var. hypoglauca, commonly known as the silver-haired sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, gray-green in color, and often have a fuzzy texture. It produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet to blue, typically blooming in summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
