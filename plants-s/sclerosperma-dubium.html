Sclerosperma dubium

Sclerosperma dubium, commonly known as the African milkwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing a milky sap, which is the source of its common name. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The tree plays an important role in local ecosystems and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
