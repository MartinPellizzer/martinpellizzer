Smilacina szechuanica

Smilacina szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat.

Smilacina szechuanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
