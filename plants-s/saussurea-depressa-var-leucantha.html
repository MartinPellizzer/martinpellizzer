Saussurea depressa var. leucantha

Saussurea depressa var. leucantha, commonly known as the white saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in its native range.
