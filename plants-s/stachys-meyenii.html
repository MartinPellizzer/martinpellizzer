Stachys meyenii

Stachys meyenii, commonly known as Andean mint, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Stachys meyenii thrives in cool, moist environments and is well-suited for growing in temperate climates.
