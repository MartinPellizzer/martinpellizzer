Symplocos botryantha var. stenophylla

Symplocos botryantha var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
