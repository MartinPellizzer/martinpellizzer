Syzygium insulare

Syzygium insulare, commonly known as the Island Syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest environments.

Syzygium insulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
