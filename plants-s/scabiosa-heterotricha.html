Scabiosa heterotricha

Scabiosa heterotricha, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine environments. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Scabiosa heterotricha is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
