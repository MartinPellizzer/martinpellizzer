Scutellaria playfairii var. playfairii

Scutellaria playfairii var. playfairii, commonly known as Playfair's blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
