Sarissus

Sarissus, commonly known as the "saffron crocus," is a member of the Iridaceae family, characterized by its striking, vibrant flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. The plant produces delicate, cup-shaped blooms in shades of yellow to orange, often resembling saffron in color. Its foliage consists of grass-like leaves that emerge from a short rhizome.

Sarissus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
