Stachys debilis

Stachys debilis, commonly known as white deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Stachys debilis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
