Sesleria calcarea f. elongata

Sesleria calcarea f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated limestone grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and its ability to thrive in calcareous soils. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky outcrops and sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, pale green to yellowish flowers.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
