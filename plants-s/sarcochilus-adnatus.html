Sarcochilus adnatus

Sarcochilus adnatus, commonly known as the Tropicana orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, with flowers clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Sarcochilus adnatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
