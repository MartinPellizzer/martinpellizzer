Scandix georgica

Scandix georgica, commonly known as the Georgian scurvy grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Scandix georgica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
