Spiraea rubiacea

Spiraea rubiacea, commonly known as redleaf weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The foliage is typically dark green, turning vibrant red in the fall, which adds ornamental value to the plant. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its attractive flowers and seasonal color changes.
