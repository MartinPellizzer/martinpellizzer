Symphyandra armena subsp. daralaghezica

Symphyandra armena subsp.

daralaghezica, commonly known as the Armenian symphyandra, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia. It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants such as buckwheat and knotweed.

The plant features slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats, with small, ovate leaves arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh mountainous conditions.
