Sambucus melanocarpa var. furstenbergii

Sambucus melanocarpa var. furstenbergii, commonly known as the dwarf elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
