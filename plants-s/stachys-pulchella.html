Stachys pulchella

Stachys pulchella, commonly known as the pretty hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its hairy foliage and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Stachys pulchella is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to mild frosts.
