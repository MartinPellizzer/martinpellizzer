Silene crassifolia subsp. crassifolia

Silene crassifolia subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy pink to reddish-purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat succulent.
