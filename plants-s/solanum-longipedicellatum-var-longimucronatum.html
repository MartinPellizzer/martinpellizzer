Solanum longipedicellatum var. longimucronatum

Solanum longipedicellatum var. longimucronatum, commonly known as the long-tailed nightshade, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, characterized by its distinctive, elongated calyx lobes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The fruit is a berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature, containing several seeds.
