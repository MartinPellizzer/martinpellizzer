Saxifraga ferruginea var. macounii

Saxifraga ferruginea var. macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's rusty saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, reddish-brown leaves covered in a fine layer of white woolly hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
