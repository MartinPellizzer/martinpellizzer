Sicyos ampelophyllus

Sicyos ampelophyllus, commonly known as the giant golden vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries, which contain toxic seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
