Syzygium acutatum

Syzygium acutatum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny exterior that covers its fruit, which is typically bright red or yellow. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its edible, sweet and tangy fruit. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

Syzygium acutatum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
