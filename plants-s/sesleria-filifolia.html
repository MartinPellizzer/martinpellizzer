Sesleria filifolia

Sesleria filifolia, commonly known as the thread-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Sesleria filifolia produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
