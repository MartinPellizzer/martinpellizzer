Saxifraga cortusifolia f. variegata

Saxifraga cortusifolia f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense, compact foliage with striking variegation, often displaying shades of white, yellow, and green on its leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and hardy nature, suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
