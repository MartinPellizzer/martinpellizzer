Spiraea laeta var. tenuis

Spiraea laeta var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender meadowsweet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
