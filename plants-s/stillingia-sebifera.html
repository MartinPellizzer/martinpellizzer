Stillingia sebifera

Stillingia sebifera, commonly known as the yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange roots, which contain a resinous compound called sebifera. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, clustered in loose inflorescences.

Stillingia sebifera is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
