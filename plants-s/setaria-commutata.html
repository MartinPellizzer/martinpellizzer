Setaria commutata

Setaria commutata, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Setaria commutata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
