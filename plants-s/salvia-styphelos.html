Salvia styphelos

Salvia styphelos, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal plants.
