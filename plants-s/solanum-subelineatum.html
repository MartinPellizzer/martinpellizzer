Solanum subelineatum

Solanum subelineatum, commonly known as the red spurge or red devil, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with smooth, often reddish stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Solanum subelineatum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
