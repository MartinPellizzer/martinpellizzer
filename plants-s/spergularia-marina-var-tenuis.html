Spergularia marina var. tenuis

Spergularia marina var. tenuis, commonly known as sea sandspurry, is a halophytic plant adapted to coastal environments.

It thrives in sandy or saline soils, often found in intertidal zones and along shorelines. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help retain moisture in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its resilience to salt spray and wind.
