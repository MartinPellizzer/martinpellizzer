Sedum telephium var. convexum

Sedum telephium var. convexum, commonly known as the convex sedum, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of bright red, star-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically green, sometimes with a reddish tinge, and are arranged in a rosette pattern. This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
