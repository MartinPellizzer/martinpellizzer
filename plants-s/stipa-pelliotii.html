Stipa pelliotii

Stipa pelliotii, commonly known as the Pelliot's feathergrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with long, feathery bristles. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

Stipa pelliotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
