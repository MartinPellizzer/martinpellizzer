Solanum campetrichum

Solanum campetrichum, commonly known as the Campetrichum nightshade, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it often grows in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
