Scilla verdickii

Scilla verdickii, commonly known as the Mediterranean squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in coastal areas. Its bulbs are toxic and should not be ingested.

Scilla verdickii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in sunny, dry conditions.
