Silene bracteosa

Silene bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

Silene bracteosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
