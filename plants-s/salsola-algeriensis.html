Salsola algeriensis

Salsola algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian saltbush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces small, greenish flowers. It is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in saline environments.

Salsola algeriensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
