Silene taygetea

Silene taygetea, commonly known as the Greek catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Greece and the Balkans.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. Silene taygetea is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
