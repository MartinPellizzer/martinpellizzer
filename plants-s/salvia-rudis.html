Salvia rudis

Salvia rudis, commonly known as the slender sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia rudis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
