Senna uniflora

Senna uniflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often hairy stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Senna uniflora is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and rocky habitats.
