Sorbus bakonyensis

Sorbus bakonyensis, commonly known as the Bakony whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, orange-red berries in the autumn.

It is often found in rocky or forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
