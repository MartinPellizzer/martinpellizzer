Symplocos dolichopoda

Symplocos dolichopoda, commonly known as the long-fruited sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.
