Serapicamptis ducoroniae

Serapicamptis ducoroniae, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this species thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

Its unique morphology and vibrant coloration make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
