Styrax ledermannii

Styrax ledermannii, commonly known as the Appalachian snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Styrax ledermannii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
