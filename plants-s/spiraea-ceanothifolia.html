Spiraea ceanothifolia

Spiraea ceanothifolia, commonly known as the rose willow or mountain spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters along the stems. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often reddish or purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Spiraea ceanothifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
