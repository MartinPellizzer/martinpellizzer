Solanum cerinum

Solanum cerinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that are typically red or purple when mature, containing toxic alkaloids. Solanum cerinum is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and a potential source of medicinal compounds.
