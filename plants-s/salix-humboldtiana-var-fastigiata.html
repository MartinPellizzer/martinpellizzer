Salix humboldtiana var. fastigiata

Salix humboldtiana var. fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of wind pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in riparian zones.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental landscaping.
