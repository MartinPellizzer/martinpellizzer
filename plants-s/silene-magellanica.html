Silene magellanica

Silene magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, linear leaves and produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is often found in moist, sandy soils in coastal areas and is adapted to temperate climates.

Silene magellanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
