Schizonotus arboreus

Schizonotus arboreus, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive, elongated spur at the base of the petals. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant is often found in moist, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
