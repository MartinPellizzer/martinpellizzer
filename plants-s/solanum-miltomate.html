Solanum miltomate

Solanum miltomate, commonly known as the purple potato, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots that produce edible, purple-colored potatoes. The plant typically grows in cool, high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are usually white or purple and attract various pollinators.

The tubers are rich in nutrients and have been cultivated by indigenous communities for centuries.
