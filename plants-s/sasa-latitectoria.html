Sasa latitectoria

Sasa latitectoria, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Sasa latitectoria forms dense clumps and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
