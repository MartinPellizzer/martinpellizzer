Salacia wightiana

Salacia wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is not closely related to them. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge.

Salacia wightiana is typically found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
