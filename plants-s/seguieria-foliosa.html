Seguieria foliosa

Seguieria foliosa, commonly known as the Florida bottle palm, is a tropical plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are usually green and can reach lengths of up to 10 feet, providing shade and habitat for various wildlife.

Seguieria foliosa is a slow-growing species that can live for several decades, making it a valuable component of its native ecosystem.
