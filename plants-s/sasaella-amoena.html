Sasaella amoena

Sasaella amoena, commonly known as the sweet fern, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like foliage that emits a pleasant, sweet scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, velvety texture.

Sasaella amoena is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive appearance and pleasant fragrance.
