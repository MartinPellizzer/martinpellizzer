Syzygium angustifolium

Syzygium angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits that are used in various cuisines. Its botanical features include a woody structure with a smooth, greyish bark and a shallow root system adapted to moist environments.

Syzygium angustifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
