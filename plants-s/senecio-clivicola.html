Senecio clivicola

Senecio clivicola, commonly known as the cliff sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Senecio clivicola is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
