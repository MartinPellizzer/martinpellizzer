Scirpus subterminalis var. cylindricus

Scirpus subterminalis var. cylindricus, commonly known as the cylindrical bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
