Struthanthus tenuifolius

Struthanthus tenuifolius, commonly known as the slenderleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with narrow, linear leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive globular shape. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
