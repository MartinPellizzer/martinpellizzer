Stanhopea × horichiana

Stanhopea × horichiana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Stanhopea and Horichis.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, storing water to help it survive dry periods.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its striking floral display.
