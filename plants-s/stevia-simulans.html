Stevia simulans

Stevia simulans, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from compounds called steviol glycosides, which are found in its leaves. Stevia simulans typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as a natural sweetener and has various traditional medicinal uses.
