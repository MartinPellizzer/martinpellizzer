Senecio crassifolius

Senecio crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water, and its yellow flowers bloom in summer.

Senecio crassifolius is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
