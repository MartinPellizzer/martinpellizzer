Scorzonera songorica

Scorzonera songorica, commonly known as the Songorica scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root that is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Scorzonera songorica is valued for its potential medicinal properties and its role in local ecosystems.
