Scutellaria glaphyrostachys

Scutellaria glaphyrostachys, commonly known as the blue scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Scutellaria glaphyrostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
