Solidago virgaurea var. leiocarpa

Solidago virgaurea var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branching stems that are covered in small, yellowish-green to golden-yellow flowers arranged in panicles. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support pollinators in native landscapes.
