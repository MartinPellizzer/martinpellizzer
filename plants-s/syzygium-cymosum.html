Syzygium cymosum

Syzygium cymosum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The fruit contains a single seed and is typically harvested when it turns a bright red or orange color. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.
