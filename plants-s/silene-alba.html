Silene alba

Silene alba, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white, pink, or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Silene alba is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in full sun.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
