Saxifraga zeleborii

Saxifraga zeleborii, commonly known as the Kashmir saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and thrives in cool, moist conditions. It is adapted to withstand harsh climates and has a short growing season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
