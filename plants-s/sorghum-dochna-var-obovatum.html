Sorghum dochna var. obovatum

Sorghum dochna var. obovatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sorghum, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, obovate leaves and compact inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a stout stem with a hairy surface.

It is often used in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop in semi-arid regions.
