Scirpus macrostachys

Scirpus macrostachys, commonly known as the giant bulrush, is a tall, perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces large, clustered spikelets that are typically reddish-brown in color, which are a key feature for identification. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus macrostachys is also valued for its ability to filter water and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.
