Salvia runcinata var. nana

Salvia runcinata var. nana, commonly known as dwarf woolly mint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are rounded and slightly serrated. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorls along the stems, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and medicinal plant collections.
