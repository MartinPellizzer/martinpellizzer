Stachys pseudonigricans

Stachys pseudonigricans, commonly known as the purple mint or purple hedge nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its aromatic leaves and stems make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Stachys pseudonigricans is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it is generally not cultivated for commercial use.
