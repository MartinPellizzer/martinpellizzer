Salvia verbenaca var. sabulicola

Salvia verbenaca var. sabulicola, commonly known as sand sagebrush, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in xerophytic environments.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
