Satureja hintoniora

Satureja hintoniora, commonly known as Hinton's savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and possibly antimicrobial properties.

Satureja hintoniora is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
