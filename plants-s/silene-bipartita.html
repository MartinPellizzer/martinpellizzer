Silene bipartita

Silene bipartita, commonly known as the two-parted catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene bipartita produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable environments.
