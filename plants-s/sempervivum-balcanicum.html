Sempervivum balcanicum

Sempervivum balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a whitish powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Sempervivum balcanicum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
