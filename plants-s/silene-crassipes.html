Silene crassipes

Silene crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Silene crassipes blooms in late spring to early summer, producing small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and flies.
