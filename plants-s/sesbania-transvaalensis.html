Sesbania transvaalensis

Sesbania transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, narrow pods containing edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and grasslands.

Sesbania transvaalensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
