Salix amygdalina var. hoppeana

Salix amygdalina var. hoppeana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically broad, lance-shaped, and turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
