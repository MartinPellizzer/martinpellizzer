Sorbus algoviensis

Sorbus algoviensis, commonly known as the Algoviensis whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange, which attract various bird species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
