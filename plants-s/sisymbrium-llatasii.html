Sisymbrium llatasii

Sisymbrium llatasii, commonly known as the narrowleaf mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and short life cycle make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
