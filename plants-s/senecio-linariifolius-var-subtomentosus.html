Senecio linariifolius var. subtomentosus

Senecio linariifolius var. subtomentosus, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.

Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a woolly texture.
