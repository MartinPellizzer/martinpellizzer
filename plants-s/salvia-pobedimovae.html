Salvia pobedimovae

Salvia pobedimovae, commonly known as the Pobedimov's sage, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a desirable ornamental plant.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
