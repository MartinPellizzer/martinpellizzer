Suaeda nigra

Suaeda nigra, commonly known as black saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in saline soils and is adapted to harsh, drought-prone environments. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces small, yellowish flowers. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed or barren areas.

Suaeda nigra plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
