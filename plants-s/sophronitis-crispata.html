Sophronitis crispata

Sophronitis crispata, commonly known as the curled leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple, flowers that are clustered in racemes. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and produces fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a whitish, waxy coating. Its flowers are notable for their intricate structure, featuring three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being spurred.

Sophronitis crispata thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
