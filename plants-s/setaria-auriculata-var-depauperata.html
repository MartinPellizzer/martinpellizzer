Setaria auriculata var. depauperata

Setaria auriculata var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender foxtail, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, hairless leaves and distinctive, drooping inflorescences that resemble a fox's tail. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The species is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
