Selaginella involvens var. bellula

Selaginella involvens var. bellula, commonly known as the dwarf spikemoss, is a small, evergreen fern ally native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with short, upright shoots that bear spirally arranged leaves. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a waxy surface and a distinct midrib.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dense, carpet-like growth.
