Sagina rosoni

Sagina rosoni, commonly known as Rosy sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas and disturbed ground. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are tiny, white or pink, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

Sagina rosoni is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
