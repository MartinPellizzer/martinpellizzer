Symplocos laeteviridis var. mjoebergii

Symplocos laeteviridis var. mjoebergii, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardiness and visual interest.
