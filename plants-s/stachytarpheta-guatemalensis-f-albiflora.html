Stachytarpheta guatemalensis f. albiflora

Stachytarpheta guatemalensis f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower shrub, is a variant of the Stachytarpheta genus native to Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
