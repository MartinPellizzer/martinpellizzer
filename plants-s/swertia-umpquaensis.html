Swertia umpquaensis

Swertia umpquaensis, commonly known as the Umpqua gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
