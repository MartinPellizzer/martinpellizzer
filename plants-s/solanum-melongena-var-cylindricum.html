Solanum melongena var. cylindricum

Solanum melongena var. cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruit shape.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant produces dark purple to black, smooth-skinned fruits that are typically harvested when immature for optimal flavor and texture. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires consistent moisture during the growing season.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often used in both culinary and ornamental applications.
