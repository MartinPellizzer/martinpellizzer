Syzygium balfourii

Syzygium balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
