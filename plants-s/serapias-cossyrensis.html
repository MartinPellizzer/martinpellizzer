Serapias cossyrensis

Serapias cossyrensis, commonly known as the Cretan orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Crete.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to the Mediterranean landscape.

Serapias cossyrensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
