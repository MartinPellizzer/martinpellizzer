Senefeldera verticillata

Senefeldera verticillata, commonly known as the whorled milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species known for their milky sap.

Senefeldera verticillata is typically found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
