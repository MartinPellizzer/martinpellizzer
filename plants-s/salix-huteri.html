Salix huteri

Salix huteri, commonly known as the Huter's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and long, narrow leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and small, inconspicuous female flowers.

Salix huteri is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
