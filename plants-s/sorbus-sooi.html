Sorbus sooi

Sorbus sooi, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a serrated edge.

Sorbus sooi is valued for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
