Scutellaria dentata var. tomentosa

Scutellaria dentata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with serrated margins, and the flowers are arranged in dense, terminal spikes.

Scutellaria dentata var. tomentosa is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
