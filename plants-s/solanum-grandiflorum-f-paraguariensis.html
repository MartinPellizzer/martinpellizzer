Solanum grandiflorum f. paraguariensis

Solanum grandiflorum f. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a globose berry that changes color from green to yellow or red as it matures.
