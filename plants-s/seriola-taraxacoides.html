Seriola taraxacoides

Seriola taraxacoides, commonly known as the taraxacoid dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Seriola taraxacoides is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
