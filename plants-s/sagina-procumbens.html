Sagina procumbens

Sagina procumbens, commonly known as common sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that typically thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils.

It is characterized by its prostrate habit, with small, succulent leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It is often found in coastal areas, wetlands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is known for its ability to stabilize soil through its dense root system.
