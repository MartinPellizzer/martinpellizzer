Smelowskia pyriformis

Smelowskia pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pear-shaped seed pods that resemble miniature pumpkins. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of the unique fruiting structures. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in grassy areas and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
