Spiraea × nobleana var. pachysandra

Spiraea × nobleana var. pachysandra, commonly known as the Japanese weeping spirea, is a compact, deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, upright growth habit.

It features showy clusters of white or pale pink flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The foliage is typically dark green, with a slightly glossy texture, and it produces small, red berries in the fall. This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of soil types.

It is often used in garden landscapes for its attractive form and seasonal interest.
