Solanum longifilamentum

Solanum longifilamentum, commonly known as the long-filament nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are berries that mature to a yellowish color and contain several seeds.

Solanum longifilamentum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is part of the nightshade family, which includes many economically important plants.
