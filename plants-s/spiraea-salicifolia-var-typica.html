Spiraea salicifolia var. typica

Spiraea salicifolia var. typica, commonly known as the silver-leaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are elliptical, with a silvery-gray underside that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
