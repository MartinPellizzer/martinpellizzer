Scindapsus cuscuarioides

Scindapsus cuscuarioides, commonly known as the silver vine, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing or trailing plant characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that often have a silvery or variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Scindapsus cuscuarioides is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
