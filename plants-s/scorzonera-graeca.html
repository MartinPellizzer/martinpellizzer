Scorzonera graeca

Scorzonera graeca, commonly known as the Greek scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, fleshy root system that is often harvested for its edible qualities. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and slightly lobed. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple-tipped center, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Scorzonera graeca is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
