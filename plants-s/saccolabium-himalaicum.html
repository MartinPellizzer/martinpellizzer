Saccolabium himalaicum

Saccolabium himalaicum, commonly known as the Himalayan saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in mountainous regions.
