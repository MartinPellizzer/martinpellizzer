Silene canopica

Silene canopica, commonly known as the "sky-blue silene," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Silene canopica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural requirements.
