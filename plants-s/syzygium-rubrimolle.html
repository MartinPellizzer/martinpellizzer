Syzygium rubrimolle

Syzygium rubrimolle, commonly known as the red-fruited clove.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
