Simaba glabra var. inaequilatera

Simaba glabra var. inaequilatera, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraria*, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth surface, while the bark is often fissured and reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
