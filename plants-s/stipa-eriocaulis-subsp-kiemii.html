Stipa eriocaulis subsp. kiemii

Stipa eriocaulis subsp.

kiemii, commonly known as the Kiem's feathergrass, is a grass species native to parts of East Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
