Serratula tomentosa

Serratula tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Serratula tomentosa is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
