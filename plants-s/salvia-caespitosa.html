Salvia caespitosa

Salvia caespitosa, commonly known as woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
