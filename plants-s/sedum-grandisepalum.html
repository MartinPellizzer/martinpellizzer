Sedum grandisepalum

Sedum grandisepalum, commonly known as the giant sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It grows in a rosette form, with stems that can reach several feet in height.

Due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
