Setaria brigalow

Setaria brigalow, commonly known as spear grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Setaria brigalow is known for its resilience to drought and grazing, making it an important component of native ecosystems.
