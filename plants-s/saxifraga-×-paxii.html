Saxifraga × paxii

Saxifraga × paxii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirculus, typically found in alpine and arctic regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Saxifraga × paxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
