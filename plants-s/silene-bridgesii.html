Silene bridgesii

Silene bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridges' catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene bridgesii thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.
