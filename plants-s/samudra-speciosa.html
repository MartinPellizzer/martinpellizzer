Samudra speciosa

Samudra speciosa, commonly known as the sea mirror orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble the waves of the ocean, with deep blue and purple hues. The plant thrives in humid, saline environments, often growing near tidal zones or in coastal forests. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its salty habitat.

Samudra speciosa is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is considered a symbol of resilience in maritime ecosystems.
