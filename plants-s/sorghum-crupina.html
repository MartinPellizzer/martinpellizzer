Sorghum crupina

Sorghum crupina, commonly known as red sorghum, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect growth and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Sorghum crupina is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
