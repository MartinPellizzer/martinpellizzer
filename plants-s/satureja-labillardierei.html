Satureja labillardierei

Satureja labillardierei, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or purple, and it produces small, ovoid fruits.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their strong, aromatic properties.
