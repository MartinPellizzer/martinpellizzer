Salix stylosa var. lancifolia

Salix stylosa var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist environments such as streambanks and riparian zones. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery hair. Its catkin-like flowers appear in early spring, contributing to its ecological role in pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
