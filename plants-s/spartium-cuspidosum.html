Spartium cuspidosum

Spartium cuspidosum, commonly known as needle spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrub habitats.

Spartium cuspidosum is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
