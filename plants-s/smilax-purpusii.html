Smilax purpusii

Smilax purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a climbing shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the genus Smilax, which includes over 200 species of flowering plants. The plant features woody stems with sharp, spiny nodes and large, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Smilax purpusii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
