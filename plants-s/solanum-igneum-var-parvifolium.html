Solanum igneum var. parvifolium

Solanum igneum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the flameleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making them harmful to humans and animals.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry conditions.
