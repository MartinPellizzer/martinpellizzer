Salix × pendula

Salix × pendula, commonly known as weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It is native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant features long, slender twigs that drape downward, creating a soft, flowing appearance. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a smooth texture and a bright green color during the growing season.

Salix × pendula prefers moist, well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for water features and gardens.
