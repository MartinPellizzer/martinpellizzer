Stroemia tetrandra

Stroemia tetrandra, commonly known as the four-threaded orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in a basal rosette.

The flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive four-lobed structure that gives the species its common name.
