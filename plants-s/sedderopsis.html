Sedderopsis

Sedderopsis, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Sedderopsis is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
