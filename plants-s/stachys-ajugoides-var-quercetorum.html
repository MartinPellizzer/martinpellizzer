Stachys ajugoides var. quercetorum

Stachys ajugoides var. quercetorum, commonly known as the oak-leaved hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves contain compounds that can cause skin irritation, making it a plant to handle with care.
