Syzygium ignambiense

Syzygium ignambiense, commonly known as the Malabar apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are small, round, and have a sour-sweet flavor. Syzygium ignambiense is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
