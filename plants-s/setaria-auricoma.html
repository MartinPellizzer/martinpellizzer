Setaria auricoma

Setaria auricoma, commonly known as the auriculate foxtail, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical leaves and distinctive bristled inflorescences that resemble a foxtail. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its wide distribution.

Setaria auricoma is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
