Shorea obscura

Shorea obscura, commonly known as black ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is typically found in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Shorea obscura plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
