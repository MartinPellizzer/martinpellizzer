Spathodea campanulata

Spathodea campanulata, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Spathodea campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
