Senegalia taylorii

Senegalia taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically reduced to spines in mature plants, and its yellow flowers that bloom in dense, globular clusters. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Senegalia taylorii is also valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in landscaping in dry climates.
