Sinocalamus affinis f. viridiflavus

Sinocalamus affinis f. viridiflavus, commonly known as the green-yellow bamboo, is a variant of the Sinocalamus affinis species, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to eastern Asia, particularly China. Its culms are typically slender and exhibit a unique greenish-yellow coloration, which sets it apart from other bamboo species. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.
