Solanum rugulosum

Solanum rugulosum, commonly known as the roughleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves with a rough texture and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.

Despite its toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.
