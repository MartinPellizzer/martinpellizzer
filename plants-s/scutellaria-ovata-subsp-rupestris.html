Scutellaria ovata subsp. rupestris

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock skullcup, is a perennial herb native to rocky habitats in the western United States. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy underneath.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
