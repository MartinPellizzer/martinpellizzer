Scutellaria violacea var. hispidior

Scutellaria violacea var. hispidior, commonly known as the hairy blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and pairs of opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a desirable plant for native gardens.
