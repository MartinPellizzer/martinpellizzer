Senecio oophyllus

Senecio oophyllus, commonly known as the leafy groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and daisy-like.

It is a hardy perennial that is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.
