Samanea polycephala

Samanea polycephala, commonly known as the white acacia, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agroforestry systems.
