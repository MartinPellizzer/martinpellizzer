Symplocos lungtauensis

Symplocos lungtauensis, commonly known as the Lungtau tea plant, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that contain a single seed.
