Sonchus glaucescens

Sonchus glaucescens, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its glaucous, bluish-gray leaves and yellow composite flowers. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has a taproot system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative means.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
