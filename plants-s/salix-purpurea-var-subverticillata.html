Salix purpurea var. subverticillata

Salix purpurea var. subverticillata, commonly known as the purple willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and clusters of small, bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a purple or reddish tinge on the underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic qualities and its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
