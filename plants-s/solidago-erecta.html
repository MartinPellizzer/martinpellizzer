Solidago erecta

Solidago erecta, commonly known as upright goldenrod, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago erecta is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
