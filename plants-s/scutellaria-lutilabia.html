Scutellaria lutilabia

Scutellaria lutilabia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueflag, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria lutilabia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
