Sparaxis anemoniflora

Sparaxis anemoniflora, commonly known as the African daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, resembling daffodils. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces offsets that allow it to spread and form clumps. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Sparaxis anemoniflora is known for its long blooming period, typically from late winter to early spring.
