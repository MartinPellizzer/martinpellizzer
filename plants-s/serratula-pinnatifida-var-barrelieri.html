Serratula pinnatifida var. barrelieri

Serratula pinnatifida var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, upright stem covered in dense, prickly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in a spherical cluster, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, with a rough, serrated edge, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
