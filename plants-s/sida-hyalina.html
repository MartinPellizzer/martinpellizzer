Sida hyalina

Sida hyalina, commonly known as the glassy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has hairy, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Sida hyalina is an annual herb that plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
