Salacia belizensis

Salacia belizensis, commonly known as the Belize salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Salaciacae family and is characterized by its woody structure and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Salacia belizensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
