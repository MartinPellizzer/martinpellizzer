Senecio tripartitus

Senecio tripartitus, commonly known as the three-parted groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Senecio tripartitus is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
