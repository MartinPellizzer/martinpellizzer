Syzygium phryganodes

Syzygium phryganodes, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value. Its leaves are glossy and lance-shaped, while its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing seeds.

Syzygium phryganodes is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
