Strobilanthes cernua var. brevistipitata

Strobilanthes cernua var. brevistipitata, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of its stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
