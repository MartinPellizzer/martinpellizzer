Symplocos paniculata var. glabrifolia

Symplocos paniculata var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract wildlife.
