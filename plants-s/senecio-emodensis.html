Senecio emodensis

Senecio emodensis, commonly known as the Emodi Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Senecio emodensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
