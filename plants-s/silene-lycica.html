Silene lycica

Silene lycica, commonly known as the Cyprus silene, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. It plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
