Sesleria latifolia

Sesleria latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in summer, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Sesleria latifolia is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
