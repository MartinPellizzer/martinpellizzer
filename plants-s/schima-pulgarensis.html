Schima pulgarensis

Schima pulgarensis, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red berries. Schima pulgarensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
