Smilax vaginata

Smilax vaginata, commonly known as the arrowleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Smilax vaginata thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Smilax, with adaptations suited to its humid, temperate environment.
