Staphylea arguta

Staphylea arguta, commonly known as the European hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tasting nuts in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Staphylea arguta is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
