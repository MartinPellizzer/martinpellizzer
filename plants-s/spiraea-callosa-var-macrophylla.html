Spiraea callosa var. macrophylla

Spiraea callosa var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are large, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is noted for its graceful appearance and ability to provide seasonal interest throughout the growing season.
