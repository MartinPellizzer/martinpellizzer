Swartzia pendula

Swartzia pendula, commonly known as the weeping swartzia, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that give it a unique, cascading appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, often arranged in clusters.

Swartzia pendula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
