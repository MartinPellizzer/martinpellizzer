Salvia muratae

Salvia muratae, commonly known as the rock sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Salvia muratae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
