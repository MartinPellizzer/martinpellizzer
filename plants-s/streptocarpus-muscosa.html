Streptocarpus muscosa

Streptocarpus muscosa, commonly known as the velvety streptocarpus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of violet and blue. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts.
