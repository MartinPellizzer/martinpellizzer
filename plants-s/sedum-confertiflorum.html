Sedum confertiflorum

Sedum confertiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum confertiflorum is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
