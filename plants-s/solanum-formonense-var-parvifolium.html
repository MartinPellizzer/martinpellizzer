Solanum formonense var. parvifolium

Solanum formonense var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It features oval, hairy leaves with a pointed tip and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that change from green to red as they mature, containing toxic compounds typical of the Solanaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
