Syzygium soliflorum

Syzygium soliflorum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in dense, rounded corymbs. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves and fragrant blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit, a woody capsule, contains several seeds and is used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
