Sebastiania serrulata var. klotzschiana

Sebastiania serrulata var. klotzschiana, commonly known as the Klotzschian waxplant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
