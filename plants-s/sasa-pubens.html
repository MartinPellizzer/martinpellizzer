Sasa pubens

Sasa pubens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

Sasa pubens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in forest ecosystems.
