Senecio guadalajarensis

Senecio guadalajarensis, commonly known as the Guadalajara senecio, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
