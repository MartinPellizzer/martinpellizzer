Spondianthus preussii var. genuinus

Spondianthus preussii var. genuinus, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
