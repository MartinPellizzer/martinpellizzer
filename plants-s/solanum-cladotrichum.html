Solanum cladotrichum

Solanum cladotrichum, commonly known as the hairy leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and peppers. Solanum cladotrichum typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland areas.

Its fruits are small, berry-like, and contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
