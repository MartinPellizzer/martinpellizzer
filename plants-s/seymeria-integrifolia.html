Seymeria integrifolia

Seymeria integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved Seymeria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and simple, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and without serrations. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Seymeria integrifolia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
