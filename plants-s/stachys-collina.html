Stachys collina

Stachys collina, commonly known as mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Stachys collina is also valued for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat ailments such as coughs and fevers.
