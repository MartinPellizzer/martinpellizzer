Solanum tomentosum var. sublyratum

Solanum tomentosum var. sublyratum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that transition from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
