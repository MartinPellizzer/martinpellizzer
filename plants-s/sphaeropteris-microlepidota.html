Sphaeropteris microlepidota

Sphaeropteris microlepidota, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces small, clustered leaves. The plant has a short, stout trunk and is often mistaken for a tree due to its appearance. Its reproductive structures include male and female cones, typical of cycads.

Sphaeropteris microlepidota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
