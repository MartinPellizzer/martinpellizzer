Solanum adoense

Solanum adoense, commonly known as the West African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum adoense is often found in moist, shaded habitats and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
