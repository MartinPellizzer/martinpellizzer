Scolymocephalus fulvus

Scolymocephalus fulvus, commonly known as the golden crown grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its unique coloration and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
