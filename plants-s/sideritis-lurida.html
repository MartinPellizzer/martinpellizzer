Sideritis lurida

Sideritis lurida, commonly known as Greek oregano or mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous areas. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

Sideritis lurida is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
