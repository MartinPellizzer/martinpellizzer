Stelis pulchella

Stelis pulchella, commonly known as the showy stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid tropical environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, yellow, or green, with a distinctive fragrance.

Stelis pulchella is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
