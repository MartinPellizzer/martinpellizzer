Silene viridiflora f. webbiana

Silene viridiflora f. webbiana, commonly known as the green-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a reddish hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is notable for its unique coloration and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
