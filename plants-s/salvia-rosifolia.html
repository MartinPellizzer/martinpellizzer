Salvia rosifolia

Salvia rosifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
