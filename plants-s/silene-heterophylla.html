Silene heterophylla

Silene heterophylla, commonly known as the variable catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, which can range from basal to alternate, depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Silene heterophylla is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
