Sideritis raeseri subsp. attica

Sideritis raeseri subsp.

attica, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are hairy and have a strong, pungent aroma, often used in traditional Greek cuisine and herbal medicine. Sideritis raeseri subsp.

attica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry environments.
