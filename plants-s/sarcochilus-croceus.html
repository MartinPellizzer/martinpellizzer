Sarcochilus croceus

Sarcochilus croceus, commonly known as the golden sarcochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a distinctive yellow and green coloration, with broad, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is known for its resilience in cooler climates.

The genus Sarcochilus is characterized by its unique pseudobulbs and the presence of a fleshy, underground stem.
