Spiraea virgata var. hookeriana

Spiraea virgata var. hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early summer. The foliage is typically dark green, with finely toothed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
