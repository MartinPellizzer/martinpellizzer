Sarcocephalus fluviatilis

Sarcocephalus fluviatilis, commonly known as the river bushwillow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
