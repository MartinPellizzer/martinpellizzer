Satureja glabra

Satureja glabra, commonly known as winter savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the spring. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a strong, pungent scent.

It is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
