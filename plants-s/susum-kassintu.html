Susum kassintu

Susum kassintu, commonly known as the Ethiopian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and robust stem. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

Susum kassintu is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in local communities.
