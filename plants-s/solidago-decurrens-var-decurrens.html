Solidago decurrens var. decurrens

Solidago decurrens var. decurrens, commonly known as the eastern goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces numerous small, bright yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

This species is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides.
