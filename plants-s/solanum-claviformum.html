Solanum claviformum

Solanum claviformum, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning. The plant typically grows as an annual vine with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solanum claviformum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
