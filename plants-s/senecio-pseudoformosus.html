Senecio pseudoformosus

Senecio pseudoformosus, commonly known as the Hawaiian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands. It produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer.
