Syzygium caryophyllatum

Syzygium caryophyllatum, commonly known as the clove tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, crimson-red flowers and its distinctive, woody, cone-shaped fruit. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its essential oil derived from its flower buds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. Syzygium caryophyllatum thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance.
