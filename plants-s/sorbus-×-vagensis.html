Sorbus × vagensis

Sorbus × vagensis, commonly known as the whitebeams, is a hybrid deciduous tree or shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries in the fall. The leaves are typically oval or elliptical, with serrated edges, and they turn yellow or red in autumn. The tree is native to parts of Europe and Asia, and it is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
