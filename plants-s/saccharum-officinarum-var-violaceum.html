Saccharum officinarum var. violaceum

Saccharum officinarum var. violaceum, commonly known as purple sugarcane, is a variant of the sugarcane plant characterized by its striking violet or purple pigmentation in its stalks and leaves.

This coloration is due to the presence of anthocyanin pigments, which are responsible for the plant's unique aesthetic appeal. It is primarily cultivated for ornamental purposes, though it can also be used in limited commercial applications. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and high humidity.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
