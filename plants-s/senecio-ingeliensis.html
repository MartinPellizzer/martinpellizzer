Senecio ingeliensis

Senecio ingeliensis, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Senecio ingeliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
