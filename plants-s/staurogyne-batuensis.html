Staurogyne batuensis

Staurogyne batuensis, commonly known as the Batu Staurogyne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its submerged, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.

Staurogyne batuensis plays a role in providing habitat and oxygenation in its native aquatic ecosystems.
