Syncarpha ferruginea

Syncarpha ferruginea, commonly known as the red sandras or red sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a reddish or purplish hue.

Syncarpha ferruginea is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal and inland sandy habitats.
