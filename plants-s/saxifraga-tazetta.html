Saxifraga tazetta

Saxifraga tazetta, commonly known as the Persian snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear.

Saxifraga tazetta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
