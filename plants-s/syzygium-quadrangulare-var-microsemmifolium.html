Syzygium quadrangulare var. microsemmifolium

Syzygium quadrangulare var. microsemmifolium, commonly known as the dwarf apple fruit tree, is a compact variant of the apple fruit tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of small, edible fruits that resemble tiny apples. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that precede the fruiting season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary use in local cuisines.
