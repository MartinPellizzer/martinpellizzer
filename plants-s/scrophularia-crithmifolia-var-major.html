Scrophularia crithmifolia var. major

Scrophularia crithmifolia var. major, commonly known as the greater dog's mercury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, producing small, tubular blooms in shades of yellow to orange.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
