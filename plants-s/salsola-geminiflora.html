Salsola geminiflora

Salsola geminiflora, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

Salsola geminiflora is often found in sandy soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
