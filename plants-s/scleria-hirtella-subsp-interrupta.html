Scleria hirtella subsp. interrupta

Scleria hirtella subsp.

interrupta, commonly known as the hairy spearplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Scleria hirtella subsp.

interrupta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
