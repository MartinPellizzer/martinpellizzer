Solidago linearifolia var. poeppigii

Solidago linearifolia var. poeppigii, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
