Silene angustifolia subsp. prostrata

Silene angustifolia subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate narrowleaf catchfly, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It typically features prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for native plant gardens.
