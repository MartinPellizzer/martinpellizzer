Senegalia noblickii

Senegalia noblickii, commonly known as the Mexican honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnately compound leaves that are typically reduced to spines in mature plants. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species in desert environments.

Senegalia noblickii plays an important ecological role by providing forage for wildlife and livestock, as well as stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
