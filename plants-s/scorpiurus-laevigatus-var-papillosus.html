Scorpiurus laevigatus var. papillosus

Scorpiurus laevigatus var. papillosus, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The leaves and stems are covered in tiny, stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact.
