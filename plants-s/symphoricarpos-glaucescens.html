Symphoricarpos glaucescens

Symphoricarpos glaucescens, commonly known as the silverleaf snowberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pale blue berries. The leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving the plant its distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide food for wildlife.
