Senecio vitellinus

Senecio vitellinus, commonly known as the yellow groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its flowers are surrounded by specialized bracts that are often mistaken for petals.

Senecio vitellinus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
