Syzygium quadrialatum

Syzygium quadrialatum, commonly known as the Four-winged Syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Syzygium quadrialatum is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
