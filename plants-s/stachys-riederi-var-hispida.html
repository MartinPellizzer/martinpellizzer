Stachys riederi var. hispida

Stachys riederi var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with short, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stachys riederi var. hispida is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
