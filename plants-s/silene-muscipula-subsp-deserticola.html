Silene muscipula subsp. deserticola

Silene muscipula subsp.

deserticola, commonly known as the desert bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant has a succulent habit, with fleshy, lobed leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and creosote bush communities. Silene muscipula subsp.

deserticola is adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
