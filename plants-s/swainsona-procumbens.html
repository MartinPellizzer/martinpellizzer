Swainsona procumbens

Swainsona procumbens, commonly known as the creeping swainsona, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, often hairy leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with its deep root system and ability to store water.

Swainsona procumbens is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by Indigenous communities for various health purposes.
