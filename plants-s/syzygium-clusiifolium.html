Syzygium clusiifolium

Syzygium clusiifolium, commonly known as the flame tree or flame of the forest, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vivid visual display during the flowering season. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
