Stachys germanica subsp. lusitanica

Stachys germanica subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
