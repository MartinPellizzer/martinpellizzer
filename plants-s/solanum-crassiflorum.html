Solanum crassiflorum

Solanum crassiflorum, commonly known as the thickflower nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant has thick, waxy leaves and produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum crassiflorum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
