Swartzia longicarpa

Swartzia longicarpa, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Swartzia longicarpa plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
