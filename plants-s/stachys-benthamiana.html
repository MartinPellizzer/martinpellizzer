Stachys benthamiana

Stachys benthamiana, commonly known as the bentham's betony, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It is often used in botanical research due to its genetic simplicity and ease of cultivation.

Its ability to serve as a model organism makes it valuable in studying plant biology and genetics.
