Silene kemoniana

Silene kemoniana, commonly known as the "Cute Lady's Mantle," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Silene kemoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
