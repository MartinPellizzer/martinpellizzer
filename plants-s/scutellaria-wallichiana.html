Scutellaria wallichiana

Scutellaria wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes plants such as mint and sage, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scutellaria wallichiana is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
