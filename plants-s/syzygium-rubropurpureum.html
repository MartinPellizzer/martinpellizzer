Syzygium rubropurpureum

Syzygium rubropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-leaf clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-purple in full sun. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Syzygium rubropurpureum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
