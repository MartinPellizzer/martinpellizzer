Solidago dubia

Solidago dubia, commonly known as the pale goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks. The plant features upright stems covered in narrow, grass-like leaves. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, forming dense clusters at the top of the stems.

Solidago dubia is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various wetland environments.
