Stelis crassisepala

Stelis crassisepala, commonly known as the broad-stalked stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features long, fleshy pseudobulbs and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air and rain.

Stelis crassisepala thrives in high humidity and bright, indirect light.
