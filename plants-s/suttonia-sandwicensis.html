Suttonia sandwicensis

Suttonia sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian sandalwood, is a rare and endangered tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that was historically highly valued. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
