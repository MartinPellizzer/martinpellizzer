Senecio pseudotites var. glabra

Senecio pseudotites var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
