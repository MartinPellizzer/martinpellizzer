Spatholobus macropterus

Spatholobus macropterus, commonly known as the large-flowered wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruit and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that split open when mature.

This species is often found in dry, sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
