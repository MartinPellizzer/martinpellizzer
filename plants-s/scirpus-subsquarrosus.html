Scirpus subsquarrosus

Scirpus subsquarrosus, commonly known as the squarrose bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, spadix-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that helps it thrive in moist or aquatic conditions.

Scirpus subsquarrosus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
