Senecio laceratus

Senecio laceratus, commonly known as the ragged Robin, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers. The plant features wiry, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens worldwide for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
