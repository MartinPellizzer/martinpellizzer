Senecio villosus

Senecio villosus, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles.

Senecio villosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
