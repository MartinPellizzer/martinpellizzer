Sporobolus nogalensis

Sporobolus nogalensis, commonly known as desert threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted, spike-like inflorescences and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Sporobolus nogalensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
