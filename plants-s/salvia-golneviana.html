Salvia golneviana

Salvia golneviana, commonly known as the Golneva sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia golneviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
