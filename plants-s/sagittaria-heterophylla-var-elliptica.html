Sagittaria heterophylla var. elliptica

Sagittaria heterophylla var. elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and wetlands. The plant has slender, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive V-shaped base. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters above the water surface.

The plant is valued for its ability to provide habitat for aquatic life and for its ornamental appeal in wetland restoration projects.
