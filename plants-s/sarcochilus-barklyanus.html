Sarcochilus barklyanus

Sarcochilus barklyanus, commonly known as the Barkley's saxifrage orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, urn-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, from June to September, and is often found growing in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
