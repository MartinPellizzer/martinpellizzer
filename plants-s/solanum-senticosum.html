Solanum senticosum

Solanum senticosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange when mature, are toxic and should not be consumed.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, though its use is limited due to its poisonous properties.
