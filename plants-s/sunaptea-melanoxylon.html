Sunaptea melanoxylon

Sunaptea melanoxylon, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dark, smooth bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
