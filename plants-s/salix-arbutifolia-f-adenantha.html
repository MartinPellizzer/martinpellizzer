Salix arbutifolia f. adenantha

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its small, catkin-like flowers appear in early spring, followed by the development of narrow, elongated leaves.

This form is a natural variant of Salix arbutifolia, showcasing genetic diversity within the species.
