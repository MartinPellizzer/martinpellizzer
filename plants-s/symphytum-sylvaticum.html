Symphytum sylvaticum

Symphytum sylvaticum, commonly known as the woodland comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, usually in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, containing allantoin and other compounds with healing benefits.
