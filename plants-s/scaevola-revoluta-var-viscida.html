Scaevola revoluta var. viscida

Scaevola revoluta var. viscida, commonly known as the sticky scaevola, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically ovate and covered in a fine, sticky pubescence, which gives the plant its common name. This species thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
