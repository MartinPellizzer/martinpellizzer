Schoenus minimus

Schoenus minimus, commonly known as the dwarf flat sedge, is a small perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats near water bodies. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Schoenus minimus is generally not ornamental but is of interest in ecological studies due to its habitat specificity.
