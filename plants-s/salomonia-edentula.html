Salomonia edentula

Salomonia edentula, commonly known as the toothless salomonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
