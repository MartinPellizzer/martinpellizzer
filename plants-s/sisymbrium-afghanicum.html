Sisymbrium afghanicum

Sisymbrium afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellow flowers and has a hairy, branched stem.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution in arid environments.
