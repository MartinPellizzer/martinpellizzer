Salvertia thyrsiflora

Salvertia thyrsiflora, commonly known as the thrysothyrse, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a thyrsiform inflorescence, which gives it its scientific name. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Salvertia thyrsiflora is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
