Salsola barrilla

Salsola barrilla, commonly known as "palo de agua" or "saltbush," is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, saline environments, often growing in coastal areas and salt marshes. Its leaves are typically small and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Salsola barrilla is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
