Scutellaria macrochlamys

Scutellaria macrochlamys, commonly known as the broad-chalmed skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Scutellaria macrochlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
