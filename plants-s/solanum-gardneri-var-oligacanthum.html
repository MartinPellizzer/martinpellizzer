Solanum gardneri var. oligacanthum

Solanum gardneri var. oligacanthum, commonly known as the narrow-panicle nightshade, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that are yellow when ripe, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
