Syzygium glanduligerum

Syzygium glanduligerum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a slight pubescence. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Syzygium glanduligerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty.
