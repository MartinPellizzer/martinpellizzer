Sagittaria greggii

Sagittaria greggii, commonly known as Gregg's arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer. This species thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
