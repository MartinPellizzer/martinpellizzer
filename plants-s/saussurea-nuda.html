Saussurea nuda

Saussurea nuda, commonly known as the naked saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Saussurea nuda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
