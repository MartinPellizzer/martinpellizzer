Senna silvestris subsp. bifaria

Senna silvestris subsp.

bifaria, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feather-like leaflets, and the plant often grows in shrubland or woodland habitats. Senna silvestris subsp.

bifaria is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
