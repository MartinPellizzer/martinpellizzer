Symplocos uruguensis

Symplocos uruguensis, commonly known as the Uruguay waxtree, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Symplocos uruguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
