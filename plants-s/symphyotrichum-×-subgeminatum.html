Symphyotrichum × subgeminatum

Symphyotrichum × subgeminatum, commonly known as the New England aster, is a hybrid perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall, typically in shades of purple, pink, or white. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
