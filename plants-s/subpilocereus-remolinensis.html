Subpilocereus remolinensis

Subpilocereus remolinensis, commonly known as the Remolinensis cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil. It produces small, white flowers that bloom during the night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in cactus collections.
