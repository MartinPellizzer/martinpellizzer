Salix patzeana

Salix patzeana, commonly known as the Patzeco willow, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers that are typically pollinated by wind. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Salix patzeana is adapted to harsh, mountainous environments and is often found in cloud forests.
