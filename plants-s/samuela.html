Samuela

Samuela, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Samuela thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
