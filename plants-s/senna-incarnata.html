Senna incarnata

Senna incarnata, commonly known as the scarlet coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange bracts that surround its small yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces long, narrow pods that contain seeds. Senna incarnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
