Sida erecta

Sida erecta, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Velvetleaf is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environments, including agricultural fields.

It is an annual herb that can spread rapidly, making it a common weed in many regions.
