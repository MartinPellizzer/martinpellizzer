Senecio doronicum proles ruthenensis

Senecio doronicum proles ruthenensis, commonly known as the Ruthenian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It produces small yellow flowers surrounded by pale green ray florets.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
