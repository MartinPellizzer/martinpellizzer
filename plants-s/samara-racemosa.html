Samara racemosa

Samara racemosa, commonly known as the clustered elms, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered growth habit and elongated, winged fruits called samaras. The tree produces small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and its bark is smooth and greyish-brown. Samara racemosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in woodland areas.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
