Sida grewiifolia

Sida grewiifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in summer. Sida grewiifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
