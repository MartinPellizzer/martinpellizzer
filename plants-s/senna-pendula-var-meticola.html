Senna pendula var. meticola

Senna pendula var. meticola, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in clusters, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators. Its fruits are long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
