Setaria desertorum

Setaria desertorum, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Setaria desertorum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
