Smyrniopsis cachroides

Smyrniopsis cachroides, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed soils.

Smyrniopsis cachroides is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, contributing to the ecological stability of its habitat.
