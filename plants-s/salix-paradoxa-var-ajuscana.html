Salix paradoxa var. ajuscana

Salix paradoxa var. ajuscana, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to China.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery-white underside that gives the plant a striking visual effect. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly planted in gardens and parks.
