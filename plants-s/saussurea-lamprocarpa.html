Saussurea lamprocarpa

Saussurea lamprocarpa, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, achene-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include hairy leaves and a robust root system adapted to cold, arid environments.

Saussurea lamprocarpa is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
