Silene alba subsp. mariziana

Silene alba subsp.

mariziana, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white, pink, or pale purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer. Silene alba subsp.

mariziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
