Strobilanthes tonkinensis var. tonkinensis

Strobilanthes tonkinensis var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin flame flower, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in certain regions.
