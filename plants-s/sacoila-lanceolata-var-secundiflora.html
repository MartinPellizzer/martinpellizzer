Sacoila lanceolata var. secundiflora

Sacoila lanceolata var. secundiflora, commonly known as the secondary-flowered lanceleaf, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a fibrous root system.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance.
