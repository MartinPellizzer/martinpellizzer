Stevia ranunculoides

Stevia ranunculoides, commonly known as sweetleaf or yellow stevia, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Stevia ranunculoides is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of natural sweeteners.
