Sorbus aria f. pseudocretica

Sorbus aria f. pseudocretica, commonly known as the whitebeams, is a botanical variant of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth or slightly serrated edge. This form is known for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens due to its attractive foliage and fruiting habits.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
