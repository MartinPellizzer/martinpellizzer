Seruneum strigosum

Seruneum strigosum, commonly known as the hairy seruneum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a silvery-green coloration. It produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
