Scilla lachenalioides

Scilla lachenalioides, commonly known as the Lachenal's scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Scilla lachenalioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
