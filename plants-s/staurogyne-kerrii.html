Staurogyne kerrii

Staurogyne kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, floating foliage and small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant has whorled leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length, and it thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Staurogyne kerrii is often used in ornamental aquatic settings due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
