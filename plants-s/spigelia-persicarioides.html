Spigelia persicarioides

Spigelia persicarioides, commonly known as the pinkroot or red root, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. Its roots are characterized by their reddish color and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
