Stachys oblongifolia

Stachys oblongifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its ornamental value in garden settings.
