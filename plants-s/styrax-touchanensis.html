Styrax touchanensis

Styrax touchanensis, commonly known as the Mexican frankincense tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are glossy and alternate, while its flowers are small, white or pale green, and clustered in inflorescences.

The bark and wood of Styrax touchanensis are valued for their aromatic properties and have been utilized in various cultural and commercial applications.
