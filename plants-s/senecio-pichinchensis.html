Senecio pichinchensis

Senecio pichinchensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and are surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves, a feature common in the Asteraceae family.

Senecio pichinchensis is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to the cool, humid climate of its native habitat.
