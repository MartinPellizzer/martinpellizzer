Smilacina stellata var. sylvatica

Smilacina stellata var. sylvatica, commonly known as the Appalachian greenbrier, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and woody stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in the understory of forests.

Smilacina stellata var. sylvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
