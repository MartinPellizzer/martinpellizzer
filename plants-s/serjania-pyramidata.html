Serjania pyramidata

Serjania pyramidata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pyramidal serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries containing edible seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Serjania pyramidata is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
