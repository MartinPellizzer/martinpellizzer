Symphyotrichum pratense

Symphyotrichum pratense, commonly known as the meadow blazingstar, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

Symphyotrichum pratense plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
