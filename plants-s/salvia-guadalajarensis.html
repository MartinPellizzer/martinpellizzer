Salvia guadalajarensis

Salvia guadalajarensis, commonly known as the Guadalajara sage, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability to arid conditions contribute to its significance in both horticulture and ecological landscapes.
