Solidago reticulata

Solidago reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a reticulate pattern of veins.

Solidago reticulata plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
