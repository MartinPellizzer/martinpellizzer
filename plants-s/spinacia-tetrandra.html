Spinacia tetrandra

Spinacia tetrandra, commonly known as spinach, is a leafy green vegetable belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are rich in nutrients such as iron, vitamins A and C, and folate. The plant is typically grown in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight. Spinacia tetrandra is an annual plant that produces small, yellow flowers and red berries containing seeds.

Its botanical structure supports both culinary and medicinal uses due to its high nutritional value.
