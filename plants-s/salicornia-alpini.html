Salicornia alpini

Salicornia alpini, commonly known as alpine glasswort, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline and alkaline environments.

It is characterized by its jointed, succulent stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, green or reddish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. It is typically found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and arid regions with high salinity.

Salicornia alpini plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
