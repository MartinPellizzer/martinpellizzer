Salvia × collina

Salvia × collina, commonly known as the hybrid sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple, blue, or white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, Salvia × collina is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
