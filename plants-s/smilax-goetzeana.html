Smilax goetzeana

Smilax goetzeana, commonly known as the Goetze's smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Smilax goetzeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
