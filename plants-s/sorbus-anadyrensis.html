Sorbus anadyrensis

Sorbus anadyrensis, commonly known as the Caucasian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, persistent berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
