Secale cereale

Secale cereale, commonly known as barley, is a cereal grass native to the temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and spike-like inflorescence composed of small, two-lobed flowers. The plant produces edible grains that are harvested when the seeds are mature and dry. Barley is a hardy crop that thrives in cool climates and is cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.

Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a variety of leaf types depending on the growth stage.
