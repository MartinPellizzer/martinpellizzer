Styphnolobium japonicum f. oligophyllum

Styphnolobium japonicum f. oligophyllum, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a variant of the wisteria plant native to China.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of 10-20 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is often considered a problematic species in certain regions.
