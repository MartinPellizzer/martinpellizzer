Salacia aneityensis

Salacia aneityensis, commonly known as the golden salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by distinctive, waxy leaves. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
