Sterculia duckei

Sterculia duckei, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its fruit is an important source of oil, similar to shea butter, and is used in traditional medicine and cosmetics.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
