Soranthus sibiricus

Soranthus sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a dense, terminal inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its botanical name reflects its geographical origin and its characteristic appearance.
