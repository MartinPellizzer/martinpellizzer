Sabatia paniculata var. latifolia

Sabatia paniculata var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open areas such as dunes, roadsides, and disturbed sites. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained environments.
