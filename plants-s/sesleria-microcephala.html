Sesleria microcephala

Sesleria microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or clay soils.

Sesleria microcephala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
