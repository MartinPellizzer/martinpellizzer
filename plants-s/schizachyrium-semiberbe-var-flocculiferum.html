Schizachyrium semiberbe var. flocculiferum

Schizachyrium semiberbe var. flocculiferum, commonly known as bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy seed heads that resemble bottle brushes, which are prominent in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.
