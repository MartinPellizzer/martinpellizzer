Staurogyne subcapitata

Staurogyne subcapitata, commonly known as the small-headed waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often floating at the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its rapid growth makes it an important species in aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
