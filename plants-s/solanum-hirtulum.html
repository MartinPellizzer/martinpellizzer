Solanum hirtulum

Solanum hirtulum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe, are edible but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.

Solanum hirtulum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
