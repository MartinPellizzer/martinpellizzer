Soulangia orientalis

Soulangia orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive blue or violet hue. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
