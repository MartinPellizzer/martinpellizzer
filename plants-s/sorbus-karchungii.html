Sorbus karchungii

Sorbus karchungii, commonly known as the Karchung rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, with 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
