Senecio almasensis

Senecio almasensis, commonly known as the Almas mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered with a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Senecio almasensis is known for its yellowish to brownish composite flowers, which bloom during the summer months.
