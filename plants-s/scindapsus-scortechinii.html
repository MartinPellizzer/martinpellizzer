Scindapsus scortechinii

Scindapsus scortechinii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking variegated leaves that feature a mix of green, white, and silver tones. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by black berries, though it is primarily cultivated for its ornamental foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Scindapsus scortechinii is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
