Statice virgata var. divaricata

Statice virgata var. divaricata, commonly known as the fringed sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the stems are often branched and stiff.

It is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh maritime conditions.
