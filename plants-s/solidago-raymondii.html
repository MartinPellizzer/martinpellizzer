Solidago raymondii

Solidago raymondii, commonly known as Raymond's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Raymond's goldenrod plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
