Sorbus doshonglaensis

Sorbus doshonglaensis, commonly known as the Himalayan whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, primarily found in Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that matures to a reddish color, providing food for various wildlife species.
