Sauvagesia erecta var. parvifolia

Sauvagesia erecta var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sauvagesia, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for ornamental horticulture.
