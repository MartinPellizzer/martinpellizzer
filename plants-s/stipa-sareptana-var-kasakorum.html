Stipa sareptana var. kasakorum

Stipa sareptana var. kasakorum, commonly known as the Kasakorum needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
