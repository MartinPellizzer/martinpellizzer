Sterculia cubensis

Sterculia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban Sterculia, is a deciduous tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
