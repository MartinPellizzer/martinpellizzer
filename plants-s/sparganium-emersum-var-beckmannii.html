Sparganium emersum var. beckmannii

Sparganium emersum var. beckmannii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bur reed, is a wetland plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and clustered growth habit. The plant produces distinctive, spiky inflorescences that are often covered in small, brownish bracts. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, typically growing in dense stands in shallow water or moist soil.

This variety is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes, where it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic environments.
