Sanguisorba minor var. platylopha

Sanguisorba minor var. platylopha, commonly known as narrow-leaved burnet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of tiny, red-tinged flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems covered with soft, hairy foliage. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
