Stevia leucosticta

Stevia leucosticta, commonly known as white stevia, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are often used as a sugar substitute due to their intense sweetness.

Stevia leucosticta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
