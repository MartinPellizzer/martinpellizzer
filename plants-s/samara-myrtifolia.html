Samara myrtifolia

Samara myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf samara, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, winged fruits called samaras, which are adapted for wind dispersal. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a fragrant scent.

Samara myrtifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
