Satureja punctata

Satureja punctata, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs such as mint and oregano. Satureja punctata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and other compounds, make it valuable in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
