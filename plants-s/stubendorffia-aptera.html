Stubendorffia aptera

Stubendorffia aptera, commonly known as the smooth stubendorffia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, with only a fleshy stem that stores water. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
