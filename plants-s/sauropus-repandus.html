Sauropus repandus

Sauropus repandus, commonly known as the broadleaf sauropus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, spreading foliage. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and a smooth margin. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
