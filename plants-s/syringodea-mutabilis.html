Syringodea mutabilis

Syringodea mutabilis, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the cloud forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that change color as they mature, typically transitioning from white to pink or purple. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and exhibits a complex reproductive strategy involving mycorrhizal associations. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in fine hairs, while its roots are specialized for nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Syringodea mutabilis is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
