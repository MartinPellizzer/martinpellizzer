Senegalia albizioides

Senegalia albizioides, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers followed by long, flat pods that contain edible seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils.

Senegalia albizioides is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
