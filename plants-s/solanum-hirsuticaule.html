Solanum hirsuticaule

Solanum hirsuticaule, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Solanum hirsuticaule is often found in moist, shaded areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
