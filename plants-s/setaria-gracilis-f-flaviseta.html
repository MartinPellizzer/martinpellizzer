Setaria gracilis f. flaviseta

Setaria gracilis f. flaviseta, commonly known as the golden beardgrass, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is native to regions of Asia and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. The inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically pale yellow to golden in color. This variety is distinguished by its vibrant, golden hue, which is more pronounced than in the typical form of the species.

It is a hardy plant that thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its visual appeal.
