Scilla lochiae

Scilla lochiae, commonly known as the Lake District scilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western Europe, particularly the Lake District in England. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Scilla lochiae is often found in moist, shaded woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
