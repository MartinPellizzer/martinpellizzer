Stapelia ambigua

Stapelia ambigua, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy, and often fragrant花朵 that resemble decaying flesh, a feature that attracts pollinators like flies. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically small and may be reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

Stapelia ambigua blooms during the summer months, producing striking, long-lasting flowers that are both visually appealing and ecologically significant.
