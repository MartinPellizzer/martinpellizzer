Salix × superata

Salix × superata, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female on separate plants. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it suitable for various ecological and ornamental applications.

However, it can be invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
