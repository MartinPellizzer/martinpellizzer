Sclerostylis rotundifolia

Sclerostylis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed star sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and possesses narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on short, erect spikes. Sclerostylis rotundifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
