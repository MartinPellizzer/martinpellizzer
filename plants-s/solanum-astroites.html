Solanum astroites

Solanum astroites, commonly known as the starry nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it has a short life cycle, often completing its growth within a single growing season.

Solanum astroites is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
