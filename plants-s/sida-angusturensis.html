Sida angusturensis

Sida angusturensis, commonly known as narrowleaf sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Sida angusturensis is notable for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
