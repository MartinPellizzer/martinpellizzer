Strychnos syringiflora

Strychnos syringiflora, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark red heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds.

The tree is also notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its ornate foliage and floral display.
