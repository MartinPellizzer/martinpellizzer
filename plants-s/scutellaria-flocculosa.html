Scutellaria flocculosa

Scutellaria flocculosa, commonly known as the fluffball skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its fluffy, white seed heads that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are small, tubular, and usually purple or blue in color.

Scutellaria flocculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
