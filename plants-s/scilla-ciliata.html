Scilla ciliata

Scilla ciliata, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant during dry periods.

Scilla ciliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
