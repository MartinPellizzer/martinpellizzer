Stachys × wirtgenii

Stachys × wirtgenii, commonly known as the hybrid mint or woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of pale purple to violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Stachys × wirtgenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
