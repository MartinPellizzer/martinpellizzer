Senecio pattersonii

Senecio pattersonii, commonly known as the Patterson's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Senecio pattersonii is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to extreme conditions.
