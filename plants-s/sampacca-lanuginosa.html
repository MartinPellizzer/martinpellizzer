Sampacca lanuginosa

Sampacca lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Sampacca lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
