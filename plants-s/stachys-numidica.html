Stachys numidica

Stachys numidica, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Stachys numidica is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat ailments such as coughs and skin irritations.
