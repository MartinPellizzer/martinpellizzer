Silene velutina

Silene velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Silene velutina is an herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
