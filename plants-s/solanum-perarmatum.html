Solanum perarmatum

Solanum perarmatum, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thick, woody stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. Solanum perarmatum is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.
