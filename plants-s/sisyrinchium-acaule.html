Sisyrinchium acaule

Sisyrinchium acaule, commonly known as the slender blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features a compact, low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Sisyrinchium acaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
