Salvia opertiflora

Salvia opertiflora, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia opertiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
