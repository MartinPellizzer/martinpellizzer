Selinocarpus purpusianus

Selinocarpus purpusianus, commonly known as the Persian mountain rue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, followed by distinctive, ribbed berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers temperate to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
