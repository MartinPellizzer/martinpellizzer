Solanum pamplonense

Solanum pamplonense, commonly known as the Pamplona tomato, is a species native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, cherry-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Solanum pamplonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
