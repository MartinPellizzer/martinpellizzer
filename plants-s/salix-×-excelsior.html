Salix × excelsior

Salix × excelsior, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that create a drooping appearance, giving it a graceful and ornamental look. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. It thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and the tree is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
