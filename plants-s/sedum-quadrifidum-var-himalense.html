Sedum quadrifidum var. himalense

Sedum quadrifidum var. himalense, commonly known as the Himalayan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
