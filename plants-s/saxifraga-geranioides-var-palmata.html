Saxifraga geranioides var. palmata

Saxifraga geranioides var. palmata, commonly known as the palmate saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often divided into lobes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
