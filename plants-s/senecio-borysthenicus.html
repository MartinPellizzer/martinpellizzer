Senecio borysthenicus

Senecio borysthenicus, commonly known as the Siberian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
