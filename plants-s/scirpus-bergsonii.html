Scirpus bergsonii

Scirpus bergsonii, commonly known as Bergson's bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Scirpus bergsonii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
