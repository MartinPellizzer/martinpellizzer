Silphium connatum

Silphium connatum, commonly known as the eastern silphium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and yellow flowers. The plant has a long taproot and produces small, achene-like seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its historical significance and rarity, it is considered an endangered species in many regions.
