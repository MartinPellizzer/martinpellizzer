Senegalia phillippei

Senegalia phillippei, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in drought conditions. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various animals.

Additionally, its wood and foliage have been used traditionally for fuel, fencing, and medicinal purposes.
