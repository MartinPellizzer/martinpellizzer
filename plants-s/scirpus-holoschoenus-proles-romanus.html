Scirpus holoschoenus proles romanus

Scirpus holoschoenus proles romanus, commonly known as the Roman bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous wetland plant characterized by its sturdy, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, pale brown flowers.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, contributing to its aquatic habitat. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting diverse wildlife and improving water quality.
