Salvia oligantha

Salvia oligantha, commonly known as desert sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring hairy stems and leaves to reduce water loss. It is often found in rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
