Statice equisetina var. depauperata

Statice equisetina var. depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in dried flower arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and delicate appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants.
