Schoenoplectus brachyceras

Schoenoplectus brachyceras, commonly known as narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes, ponds, and wet meadows. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Schoenoplectus brachyceras plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
