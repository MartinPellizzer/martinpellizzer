Sida lawrencia

Sida lawrencia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the warmer months. Sida lawrencia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and abandoned fields.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used in traditional medicine and for making textiles in some cultures.
