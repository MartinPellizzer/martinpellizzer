Sida julianae

Sida julianae, commonly known as the Julian's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas and disturbed soils. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, pink or white flowers. It is adapted to saline environments and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Sida julianae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging coastal conditions.
