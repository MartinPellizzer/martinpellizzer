Solanum chlamydogynum var. lateellipticum

Solanum chlamydogynum var. lateellipticum, commonly known as the late-elliptic nightshade, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive calyx that remains attached to the fruit. The fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several small seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
