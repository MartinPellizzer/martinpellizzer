Stevia rapunculoides

Stevia rapunculoides, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweet taste, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Stevia rapunculoides is often cultivated as a natural sweetener and has various traditional medicinal uses.
