Salvia nipponica f. angutidens

Salvia nipponica f. angutidens, commonly known as Japanese purple sage, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet and blue, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
