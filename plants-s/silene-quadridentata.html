Silene quadridentata

Silene quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene quadridentata is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
