Salicornia brachiata

Salicornia brachiata, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, and it thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and estuaries.

Salicornia brachiata is often used in culinary applications for its unique flavor and nutritional value.
