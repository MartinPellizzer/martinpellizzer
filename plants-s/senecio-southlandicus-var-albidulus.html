Senecio southlandicus var. albidulus

Senecio southlandicus var. albidulus, commonly known as the Whiteflower Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in dense clusters, and it produces achenes as its fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with good drainage.
