Satyrium longibracteatum

Satyrium longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, typically arranged in dense clusters. Its growth habit is terrestrial, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swampy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
