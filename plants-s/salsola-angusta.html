Salsola angusta

Salsola angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf Russian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Salsola angusta is considered an invasive species in many parts of the world due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
