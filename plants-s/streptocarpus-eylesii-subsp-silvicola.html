Streptocarpus eylesii subsp. silvicola

Streptocarpus eylesii subsp.

silvicola, commonly known as the forest streptocarpus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the humid forests of eastern Africa. It features oval, dark green leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and blue.

The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in shaded, moist environments. It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in cultivation.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability to specific ecological conditions make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
