Sisyrinchium tinctorium

Sisyrinchium tinctorium, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, grass-like leaves and small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces seeds in capsules that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
