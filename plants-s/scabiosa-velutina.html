Scabiosa velutina

Scabiosa velutina, commonly known as velvet scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scabiosa velutina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
