Sida reflexa

Sida reflexa, commonly known as the prostrate sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically with five petals. Its seeds are enclosed in small, hairy capsules that release seeds when mature.

Sida reflexa is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
