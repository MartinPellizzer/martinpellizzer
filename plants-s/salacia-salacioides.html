Salacia salacioides

Salacia salacioides, commonly known as the mountain pepper, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its botanical features include alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that becomes more branched with age.

Salacia salacioides is well adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
