Styphnolobium japonicum f. pendulum

Styphnolobium japonicum f. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping pagoda tree, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its pendulous, umbrella-like clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers.

Native to China, this cultivar is a form of the pagoda tree, known for its distinctive, drooping branches that create a cascading effect. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets that provide a lush green canopy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types, making it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its role in erosion control.
