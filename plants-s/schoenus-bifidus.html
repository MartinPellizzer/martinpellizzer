Schoenus bifidus

Schoenus bifidus, commonly known as the bifid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions.

Schoenus bifidus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
