Stipa pubescens var. semiglabra

Stipa pubescens var. semiglabra, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered with fine hairs, giving it a soft, silvery appearance. The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to golden florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and steppe environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in arid conditions.
