Salix × pellicolor

Salix × pellicolor, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that hang like curtains, giving it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant has slender, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

The bark is smooth and light gray, and it produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring.
