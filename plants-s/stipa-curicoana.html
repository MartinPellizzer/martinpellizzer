Stipa curicoana

Stipa curicoana, commonly known as the Curicoana grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces tall, narrow spikes of pale green to golden yellow flowers. The plant has fine, hair-like leaves that are often covered with a layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Stipa curicoana is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
