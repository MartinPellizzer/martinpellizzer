Saussurea denticulata

Saussurea denticulata, commonly known as the Tibetan yellow flower or the Chinese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to cold climates with its thick, hairy leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Saussurea denticulata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits and is also used in ornamental horticulture.
