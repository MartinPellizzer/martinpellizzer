Stevia villaricensis

Stevia villaricensis, commonly known as the Paraguay stevia, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and northern Argentina.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweet taste, which comes from steviol glycosides, natural compounds found in its leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained attention as a natural sweetener alternative.
