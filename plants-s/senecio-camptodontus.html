Senecio camptodontus

Senecio camptodontus, commonly known as the curlydock, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of blue-gray, hairy leaves that curl upward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, terminal cluster. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Senecio camptodontus is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
