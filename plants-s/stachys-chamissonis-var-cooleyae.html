Stachys chamissonis var. cooleyae

Stachys chamissonis var. cooleyae, commonly known as Cooley's hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Lamiaceae family.
