Silene incarnata

Silene incarnata, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows up to 2 feet tall, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, globular clusters, giving it its common name.

Silene incarnata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
