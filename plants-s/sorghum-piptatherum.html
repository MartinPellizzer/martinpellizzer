Sorghum piptatherum

Sorghum piptatherum, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a small perennial grass native to Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter, making it well-suited for ornamental or ecological landscaping. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Sorghum piptatherum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
