Salix matsudana f. pendula

Salix matsudana f. pendula, commonly known as weeping willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its graceful, drooping foliage. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
