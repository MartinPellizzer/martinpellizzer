Solanum salicifolium

Solanum salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its willow-like leaves, which are elongated and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum salicifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
