Salvia nydeggeri

Salvia nydeggeri, commonly known as Nydegger's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant produces narrow, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia nydeggeri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
