Silene mirei

Silene mirei, commonly known as the bog gentian, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in acidic, nutrient-poor soils, often in bogs or heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to the moist, shady conditions of its habitat.

Silene mirei plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its environment.
