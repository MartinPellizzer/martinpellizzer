Saxifraga hispidula var. dentata

Saxifraga hispidula var. dentata, commonly known as the hairy saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically features dense, mat-forming foliage with hairy, glandular leaves that are often toothed or serrated, giving it its specific epithet "dentata.". The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Saxifraga hispidula var. dentata is often found in tundra and alpine meadows, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
