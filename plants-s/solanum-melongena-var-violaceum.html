Solanum melongena var. violaceum

Solanum melongena var. violaceum, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species characterized by its deep violet to purple skin.

This variety belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant produces elongated, egg-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they are firm and fully colored. Botanically, it features showy, white or pale purple flowers and has a structure typical of solanaceous plants, with compound leaves and berries.

Its genetic makeup and morphological traits make it a distinctive and valuable cultivar in both culinary and ornamental contexts.
