Selaginella tobagensis

Selaginella tobagensis, commonly known as the Toba spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features slender, upright stems with whorled leaves that are often green or reddish in color. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella tobagensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
