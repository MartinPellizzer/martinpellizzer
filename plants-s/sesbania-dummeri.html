Sesbania dummeri

Sesbania dummeri, commonly known as the red sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a short lifespan, often completing its life cycle within a single growing season. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Sesbania dummeri is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
