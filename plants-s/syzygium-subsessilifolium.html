Syzygium subsessilifolium

Syzygium subsessilifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour-tasting fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Syzygium subsessilifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide shade in arid environments.
