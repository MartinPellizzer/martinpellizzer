Solanum lanceifolium var. litorale

Solanum lanceifolium var. litorale, commonly known as the coastal nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade family native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines, thriving in saline environments. Its berries are toxic, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

This plant plays a role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat for insects and birds.
