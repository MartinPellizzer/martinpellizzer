Sphacele fragrans

Sphacele fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
