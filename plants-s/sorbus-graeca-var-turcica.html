Sorbus graeca var. turcica

Sorbus graeca var. turcica, commonly known as the Turkish whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
