Sempervivum widderi

Sempervivum widderi, commonly known as the Widder's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features fleshy, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with reddish or purple hues, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Sempervivum widderi is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative ground cover due to its compact growth and ornamental appeal.
