Salmia tetragona

Salmia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled salvia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Salmia tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
