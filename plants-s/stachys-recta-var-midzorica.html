Stachys recta var. midzorica

Stachys recta var. midzorica, commonly known as the Midzorica hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered pink to purple flowers in late summer, which are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing burrs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
