Senecio laetus var. analogus

Senecio laetus var. analogus, commonly known as the yellow owl's clover, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that aid in wind pollination.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
