Scrophularia ramosissima

Scrophularia ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features square stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
