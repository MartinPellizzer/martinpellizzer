Solanum luteum subsp. alatum

Solanum luteum subsp.

alatum, commonly known as the winged nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Solanaceae family.
