Sedum malladrae

Sedum malladrae, commonly known as the Mallard's sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
