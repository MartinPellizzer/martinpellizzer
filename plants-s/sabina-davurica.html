Sabina davurica

Sabina davurica, commonly known as the Chinese juniper, is a species of coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sabina davurica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in landscaping and horticulture.
