Saxifraga bronchialis subsp. anadyrensis

Saxifraga bronchialis subsp.

anadyrensis, commonly known as the Anatolian saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey. It typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, hairy texture. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
