Solanum lianiforme

Solanum lianiforme, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous vine native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Solanum lianiforme is considered a weed in some regions and can be toxic to livestock and humans.
