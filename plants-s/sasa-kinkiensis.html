Sasa kinkiensis

Sasa kinkiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Sasa kinkiensis is often used in ornamental gardening and traditional Japanese landscapes.
