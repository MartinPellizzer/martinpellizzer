Salvia aegyptiaca f. colorata

Salvia aegyptiaca f. colorata, commonly known as the Egyptian sage or blue sage, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Egypt and parts of the Middle East.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vivid color and drought tolerance.
