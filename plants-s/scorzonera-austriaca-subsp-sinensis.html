Scorzonera austriaca subsp. sinensis

Scorzonera austriaca subsp.

sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which are borne on slender stems. Its root system is fibrous and often contains a fleshy, edible root that has been used in traditional medicine. Scorzonera austriaca subsp.

sinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
