Sauropus indicus

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with yellowish-green petals, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.
