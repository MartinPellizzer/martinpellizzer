Senecio francoisii

Senecio francoisii, commonly known as the Francois' groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

Senecio francoisii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
