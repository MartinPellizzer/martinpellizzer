Sisymbrium fruticosum

Sisymbrium fruticosum, commonly known as wild radish, is a perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands. The plant has a branched stem covered with fine hairs and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often have a peppery scent when crushed.

The plant is known for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and can be ground into a mustard-like powder.
