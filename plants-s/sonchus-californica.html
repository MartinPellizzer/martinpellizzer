Sonchus californica

Sonchus californica, commonly known as California sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a white or pale underside, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
