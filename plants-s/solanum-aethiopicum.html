Solanum aethiopicum

Solanum aethiopicum, commonly known as the Ethiopian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with simple, ovate leaves and small, white or purple flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow or red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Solanum aethiopicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
