Sasa kagamiana var. shibutamensis

Sasa kagamiana var. shibutamensis, commonly known as the Shibutamensis Sasa, is a variant of the Sasa genus native to Japan.

It is a rhizomatous evergreen bamboo that forms dense clumps, often found in shaded forest environments. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are typically green or slightly bluish in color. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a glossy surface and a pointed tip, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its unique appearance and hardiness.
