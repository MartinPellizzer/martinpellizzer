Senecio integrifolius subsp. karsianus

Senecio integrifolius subsp.

karsianus, commonly known as the Karsianus groundsels, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, lance-shaped leaves with smooth margins.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained conditions to prevent root rot.
