Solanum aethiopicum var. inerme

Solanum aethiopicum var. inerme, commonly known as Ethiopian nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features hairy, alternate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into yellow or orange berries. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided as a food source.
