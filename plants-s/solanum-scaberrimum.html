Solanum scaberrimum

Solanum scaberrimum, commonly known as the rough nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, often prickly stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle it with care.
