Solanum donianum

Solanum donianum, commonly known as the Donian nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
