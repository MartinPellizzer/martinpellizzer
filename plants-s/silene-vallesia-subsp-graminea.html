Silene vallesia subsp. graminea

Silene vallesia subsp.

graminea, commonly known as the grassy campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this species typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a herbaceous perennial.
