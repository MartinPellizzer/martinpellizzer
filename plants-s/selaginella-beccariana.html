Selaginella beccariana

Selaginella beccariana, commonly known as the Beccarian spikemoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. The plant features closely packed, needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems, which are often covered in a layer of fine, scale-like structures. Selaginella beccariana is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures, including microsporangia and megasporangia.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important subject in the study of early land plant evolution.
