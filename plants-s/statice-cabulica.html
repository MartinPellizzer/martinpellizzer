Statice cabulica

Statice cabulica, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, vividly colored flowers, typically in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or dry landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along the stems.

Statice cabulica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate drought and salt spray.
