Syzygium wagapense

Syzygium wagapense, commonly known as the Sarawak apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
