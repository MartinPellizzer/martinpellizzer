Solidago salicina

Solidago salicina, commonly known as the green goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to dry soils in open woods, meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Solidago salicina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
