Sempervivum dzhavachischvilii

Sempervivum dzhavachischvilii, commonly known as the Georgian stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Georgia.

It features fleshy, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with reddish or purple hues, especially under full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
