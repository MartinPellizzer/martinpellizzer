Stachys palustris var. segetalis

Stachys palustris var. segetalis, commonly known as marsh woundwort, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters along the stem, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for pollinators.
