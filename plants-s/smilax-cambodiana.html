Smilax cambodiana

Smilax cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian arrowroot, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spadices, followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Smilax cambodiana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
