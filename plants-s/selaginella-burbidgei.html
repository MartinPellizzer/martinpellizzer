Selaginella burbidgei

Selaginella burbidgei, commonly known as the Burridge's spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with needle-like leaves that form dense, mat-like structures. It reproduces via spores and is known for its ability to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Selaginella burbidgei is also noted for its horticultural value and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
