Senecio × calvescens

Senecio × calvescens, commonly known as the hybrid African Violet, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Senecio serotinus and Senecio crassipes. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange. It is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The botanical features of Senecio × calvescens make it a popular choice in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability.
