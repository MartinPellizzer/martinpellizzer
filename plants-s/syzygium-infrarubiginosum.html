Syzygium infrarubiginosum

Syzygium infrarubiginosum, commonly known as the red-brown syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its small, red or reddish-brown fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Syzygium infrarubiginosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique fruit color and fragrance.
