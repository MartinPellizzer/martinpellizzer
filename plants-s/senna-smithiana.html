Senna smithiana

Senna smithiana, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
