Sonchus briquetianus

Sonchus briquetianus, commonly known as the Briquet's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has alternate, rough, hairy leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means.
