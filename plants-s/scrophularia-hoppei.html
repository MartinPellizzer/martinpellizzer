Scrophularia hoppei

Scrophularia hoppei, commonly known as the Tibetan foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of Tibet and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure typical of the genus.

Scrophularia hoppei is noted for its resilience to harsh climates and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
