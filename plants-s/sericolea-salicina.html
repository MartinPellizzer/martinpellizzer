Sericolea salicina

Sericolea salicina, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline soils and can tolerate high levels of salt in the environment.

Sericolea salicina plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
