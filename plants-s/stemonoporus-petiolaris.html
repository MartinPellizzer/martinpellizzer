Stemonoporus petiolaris

Stemonoporus petiolaris, commonly known as the petiole-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with long, fleshy petioles that support its lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in a raceme, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow.

The botanical significance of Stemonoporus petiolaris lies in its unique morphological adaptations and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
