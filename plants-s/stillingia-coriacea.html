Stillingia coriacea

Stillingia coriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed stillingia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in a rough, bark-like texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by distinctive, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface.

Stillingia coriacea is often found in rocky or forested habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
