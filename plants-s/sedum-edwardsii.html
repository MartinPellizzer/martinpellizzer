Sedum edwardsii

Sedum edwardsii, commonly known as the Edwards' stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sedum edwardsii is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
