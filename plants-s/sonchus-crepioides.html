Sonchus crepioides

Sonchus crepioides, commonly known as the creeping sowthistle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Sonchus crepioides is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
