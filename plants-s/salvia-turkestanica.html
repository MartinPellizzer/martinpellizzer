Salvia turkestanica

Salvia turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkistan sage, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Salvia turkestanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
