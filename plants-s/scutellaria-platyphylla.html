Scutellaria platyphylla

Scutellaria platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped blue or purple flowers that bloom from June to September. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Scutellaria platyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
