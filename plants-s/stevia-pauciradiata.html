Stevia pauciradiata

Stevia pauciradiata, commonly known as the lesser sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its medicinal and sweetening properties, it has gained interest in herbal and alternative medicine practices.
