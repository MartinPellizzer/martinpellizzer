Scirpus muricinux

Scirpus muricinux, commonly known as the spiny bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Scirpus muricinux plays a vital role in filtering water and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
