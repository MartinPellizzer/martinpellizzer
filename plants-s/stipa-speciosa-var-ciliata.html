Stipa speciosa var. ciliata

Stipa speciosa var. ciliata, commonly known as the fringed feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that often have a fringed appearance along the edges. The plant produces delicate, airy panicles of florets that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
