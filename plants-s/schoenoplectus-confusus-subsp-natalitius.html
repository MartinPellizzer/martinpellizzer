Schoenoplectus confusus subsp. natalitius

Schoenoplectus confusus subsp.

natalitius, commonly known as Natal bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, lakeshores, and riverbanks.

The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense arrangement along the stem.

The inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brown flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination.
