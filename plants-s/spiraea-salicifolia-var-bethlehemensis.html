Spiraea salicifolia var. bethlehemensis

Spiraea salicifolia var. bethlehemensis, commonly known as the Bethlehem spiraea, is a flowering shrub native to the Eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and profuse clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, often with a slight pubescence. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of garden conditions.
