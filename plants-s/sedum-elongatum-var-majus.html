Sedum elongatum var. majus

Sedum elongatum var. majus, commonly known as the Giant Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
