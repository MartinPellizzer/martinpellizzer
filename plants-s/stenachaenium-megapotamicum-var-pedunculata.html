Stenachaenium megapotamicum var. pedunculata

Stenachaenium megapotamicum var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Amazonian panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy or flooded areas.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local wildlife.
