Sarcochilus asperatus

Sarcochilus asperatus, commonly known as the rough leathery orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a hairy, leathery stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a yellowish-green hue and red or orange markings. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
