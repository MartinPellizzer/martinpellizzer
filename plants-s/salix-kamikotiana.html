Salix kamikotiana

Salix kamikotiana, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a graceful, cascading appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along water features.
