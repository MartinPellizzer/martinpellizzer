Scrophularia canina var. frutescens

Scrophularia canina var. frutescens, commonly known as dog's mouth or foxglove, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, often branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

This species is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
