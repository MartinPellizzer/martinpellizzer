Salix kouytchensis

Salix kouytchensis, commonly known as the Central Asian willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of silky hair. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for reforestation projects in degraded landscapes.
