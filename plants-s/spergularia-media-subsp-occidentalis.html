Spergularia media subsp. occidentalis

Spergularia media subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal dunes and inland deserts.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.
