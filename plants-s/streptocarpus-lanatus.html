Streptocarpus lanatus

Streptocarpus lanatus, commonly known as the woolly streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Streptocarpus lanatus is often grown for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper conditions.
