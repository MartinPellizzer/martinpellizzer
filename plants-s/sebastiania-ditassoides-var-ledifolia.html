Sebastiania ditassoides var. ledifolia

Sebastiania ditassoides var. ledifolia, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, yellowish-orange inflorescences. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is native to parts of Central and South America.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
