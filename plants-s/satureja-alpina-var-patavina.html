Satureja alpina var. patavina

Satureja alpina var. patavina, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

This variety is valued in both horticulture and traditional herbal medicine for its culinary and therapeutic uses.
