Sesbania muricata

Sesbania muricata, commonly known as the hairy sesbania, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Sesbania muricata is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.
