Spiraea salicifolia var. grosseserrata

Spiraea salicifolia var. grosseserrata, commonly known as the broad-toothed spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are broadly ovate with coarsely serrated margins, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small wildlife.
