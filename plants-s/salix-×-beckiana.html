Salix × beckiana

Salix × beckiana, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the northern regions of the United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal.

The bark of Salix × beckiana is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
