Stapelia congestiflora

Stapelia congestiflora, commonly known as the "Carrion Flower," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and its distinctive, meaty-looking flowers that resemble decaying flesh. The plant produces large, yellowish-green blooms with a strong, unpleasant odor, which attracts pollinators like flies. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Stapelia congestiflora is a popular choice for succulent collectors due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
