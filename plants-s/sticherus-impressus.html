Sticherus impressus

Sticherus impressus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sticherus, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often overlooked due to its subtle appearance.

Despite its modest size, it plays a role in supporting local insect populations and contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
