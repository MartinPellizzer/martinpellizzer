Solanum selachophyllum

Solanum selachophyllum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy species adapted to coastal conditions.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
