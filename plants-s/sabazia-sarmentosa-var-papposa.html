Sabazia sarmentosa var. papposa

Sabazia sarmentosa var. papposa, commonly known as the hairy vine or creeping sabazia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
