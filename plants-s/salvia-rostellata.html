Salvia rostellata

Salvia rostellata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia rostellata is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
