Serapias × rousii

Serapias × rousii, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Serapias aucubifolia and Serapias orchardii.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of southern Europe. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive hooded structure. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Serapias × rousii thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverine habitats.
