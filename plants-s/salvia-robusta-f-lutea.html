Salvia robusta f. lutea

Salvia robusta f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow wood sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
