Salvia laevigata

Salvia laevigata, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue-purple flowers, which bloom in dense clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are slightly hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Salvia laevigata is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
