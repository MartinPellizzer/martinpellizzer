Solanum nigrum var. pilcomayense

Solanum nigrum var. pilcomayense, commonly known as the Andean black nightshade, is a variant of the black nightshade species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible when ripe but contain toxic compounds in their raw form. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Solanaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental potential.
