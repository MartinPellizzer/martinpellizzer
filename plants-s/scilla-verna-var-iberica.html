Scilla verna var. iberica

Scilla verna var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its early blooming habit.

The species is also noted for its resilience and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
