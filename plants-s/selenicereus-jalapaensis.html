Selenicereus jalapaensis

Selenicereus jalapaensis, commonly known as the Mexican night bloom, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to parts of Central America.

It is a climbing cactus that produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night. The plant has a stem that is often segmented and covered with spines, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are typically short-lived but highly visible, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique blooming behavior.
