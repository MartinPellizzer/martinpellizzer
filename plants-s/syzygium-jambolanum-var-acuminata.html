Syzygium jambolanum var. acuminata

Syzygium jambolanum var. acuminata, commonly known as the Java apple or starfruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces distinctive star-shaped fruits with a waxy, yellowish skin and a sweet, tangy flavor. The fruit's unique shape and taste make it a popular ornamental and edible plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
