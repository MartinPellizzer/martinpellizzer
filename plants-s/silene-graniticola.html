Silene graniticola

Silene graniticola, commonly known as the granitic catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. It has a low-growing habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

Silene graniticola is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
