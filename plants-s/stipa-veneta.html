Stipa veneta

Stipa veneta, commonly known as the Venetian needlegrass, is a species of grass native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Stipa veneta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
