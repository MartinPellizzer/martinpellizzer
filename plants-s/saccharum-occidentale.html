Saccharum occidentale

Saccharum occidentale, commonly known as the western sugarcane.

It is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its robust stalks and long, narrow leaves. It is cultivated for its high sugar content in the stems, which are processed into sugar and biofuel.

Its growth requires warm climates and ample water, making it a significant crop in many developing countries.
