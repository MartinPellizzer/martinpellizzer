Sismondaea dioscoreoides

Sismondaea dioscoreoides, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale green flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in harsh environments.

Sismondaea dioscoreoides is adapted to survive in low-water conditions, with deep root systems and a thick cuticle to reduce water loss.
