Sphaerostephanos canescens

Sphaerostephanos canescens, commonly known as the white-flowered stephanos, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry, ascending stems that are covered in a grayish-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Sphaerostephanos canescens is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
