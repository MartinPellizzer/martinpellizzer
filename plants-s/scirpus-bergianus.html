Scirpus bergianus

Scirpus bergianus, commonly known as Berg's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in wetlands and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color, and it blooms from late summer to early fall. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Scirpus bergianus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
