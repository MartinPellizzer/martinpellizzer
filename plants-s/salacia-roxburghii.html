Salacia roxburghii

Salacia roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red as they mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
