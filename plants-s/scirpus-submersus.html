Scirpus submersus

Scirpus submersus, commonly known as the submerged bulrush, is a wetland plant that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help in stabilizing the soil in aquatic habitats.

Scirpus submersus plays an important role in filtering water and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
