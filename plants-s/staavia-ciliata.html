Staavia ciliata

Staavia ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with fine, silky hairs along the edges, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Staavia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
