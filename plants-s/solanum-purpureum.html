Solanum purpureum

Solanum purpureum, commonly known as the purple potato plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots that produce edible purple-colored potatoes. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple, and it thrives in cooler, high-altitude climates.

The tubers are valued for their unique flavor and nutritional content.
