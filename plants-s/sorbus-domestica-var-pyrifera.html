Sorbus domestica var. pyrifera

Sorbus domestica var. pyrifera, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow berries that ripen in the fall. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the tree can grow up to 15 meters tall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
