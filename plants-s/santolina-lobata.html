Santolina lobata

Santolina lobata, commonly known as narrowleaf santolina, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina lobata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
