Saxifraga moschata var. artipetala

Saxifraga moschata var. artipetala, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are generally basal, forming a rosette and are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its aromatic foliage, which emits a subtle, sweet scent when crushed.
