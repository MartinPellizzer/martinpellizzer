Syzygium luteum

Syzygium luteum, commonly known as the golden apple or yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow-orange fruits resembling small apples. Syzygium luteum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
