Satureja cremnophila subsp. amana

Satureja cremnophila subsp.

amana, commonly known as the Balkan savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. Its essential oils contain compounds such as thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its antimicrobial and antioxidant effects.
