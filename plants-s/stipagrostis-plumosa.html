Stipagrostis plumosa

Stipagrostis plumosa, commonly known as the silky grass or silky wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with long, silky hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh climates make it an important species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
