Saponaria aetnensis

Saponaria aetnensis, commonly known as the Aetna soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Sicily.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains saponins, which are natural detergents. Saponaria aetnensis typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rocky or arid gardens.

Its unique appearance and medicinal properties have made it a subject of botanical interest.
