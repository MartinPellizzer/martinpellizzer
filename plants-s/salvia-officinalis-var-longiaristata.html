Salvia officinalis var. longiaristata

Salvia officinalis var. longiaristata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a variant of the common sage plant.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
