Solanum banksianum

Solanum banksianum, commonly known as the Banksian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum banksianum is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
