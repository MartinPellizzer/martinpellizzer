Syzygium abidjaneose

Syzygium abidjaneose, commonly known as the Abidjan rose apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Cameroon and the Central African Republic. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a round, yellowish berry that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
