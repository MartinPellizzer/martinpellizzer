Solanum andigenum f. ppaqui

Solanum andigenum f. ppaqui, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, lobed leaves, and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. Its fruit, a small berry, contains alkaloids and is used in traditional Andean medicine.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is also cultivated in some regions for both practical and aesthetic purposes.
