Sasa kariwaensis

Sasa kariwaensis, commonly known as the Kariwa bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense growth habit. The plant forms thickets and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and contribute to its ability to spread vegetatively.

Sasa kariwaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
