Solanum saltianum

Solanum saltianum, commonly known as the saltian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, typically violet or blue flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans but may serve as a food source for certain wildlife.

Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a preference for well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.
