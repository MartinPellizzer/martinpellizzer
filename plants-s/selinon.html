Selinon

Selinon, commonly known as scorzonera, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called achenes, which contain the seeds. Selinon is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
