Spiraea grandifolia

Spiraea grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, showy flowers in white or pink during late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea grandifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ease of maintenance.
