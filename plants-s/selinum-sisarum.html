Selinum sisarum

Selinum sisarum, commonly known as the common evening-primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and have a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the late afternoon, hence its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Selinum sisarum is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
