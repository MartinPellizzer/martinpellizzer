Solanum retroflexum var. angustifolium

Solanum retroflexum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale green flowers that develop into small, fleshy berries. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
