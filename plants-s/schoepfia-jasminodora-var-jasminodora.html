Schoepfia jasminodora var. jasminodora

Schoepfia jasminodora var. jasminodora, commonly known as the jasmine daphne, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive addition to arid and semi-arid landscapes.
