Solanum campylacanthum subsp. cordifrons

Solanum campylacanthum subsp.

cordifrons, commonly known as the heart-leaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of South America. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are typically berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
