Senecio sedifolius

Senecio sedifolius, commonly known as the stonecrop sedum.

It is a succulent plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, aiding in survival in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscapes.
