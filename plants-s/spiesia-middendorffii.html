Spiesia middendorffii

Spiesia middendorffii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, temperate environments.

Spiesia middendorffii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate cold temperatures.
