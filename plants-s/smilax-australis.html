Smilax australis

Smilax australis, commonly known as the Australian smilax, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It features a climbing habit with thorned stems and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and shrublands, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Smilax australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
