Symphyotrichum tenuifolium var. tenuifolium

Symphyotrichum tenuifolium var. tenuifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and meadows. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
