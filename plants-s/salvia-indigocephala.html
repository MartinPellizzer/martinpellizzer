Salvia indigocephala

Salvia indigocephala, commonly known as bluehead sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads, which are clustered in whorls along the stems. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia indigocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
