Satyrium alpinum

Satyrium alpinum, commonly known as the alpine marsh-orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe, particularly in mountainous areas with moist, acidic soils. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, bogs, and other damp habitats, often in rocky or gravelly ground. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive orchid-like appearance, featuring a spurred labellum.

Satyrium alpinum is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in high-altitude ecosystems.
