Sarcoglottis gutturosa

Sarcoglottis gutturosa, commonly known as the spotted gourd, is a climbing herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a textured surface. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits with a warty surface. Native to tropical regions of Central and South America, it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the gourd family, often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
