Synaedrys caudatifolia

Synaedrys caudatifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved synaedrys, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the rainy season. Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
