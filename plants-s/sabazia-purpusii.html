Sabazia purpusii

Sabazia purpusii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
