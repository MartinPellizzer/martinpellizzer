Sanguisorba tenuifolia f. pilosa

Sanguisorba tenuifolia f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is native to eastern Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and extended blooming period.
