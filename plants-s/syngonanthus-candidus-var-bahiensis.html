Syngonanthus candidus var. bahiensis

Syngonanthus candidus var. bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia white-flowered grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Brazil, characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
