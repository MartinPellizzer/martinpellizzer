Stevia andina

Stevia andina, commonly known as Peruvian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from compounds called steviol glycosides, which are found in its leaves. Stevia andina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

It is widely used as a natural sweetener and has gained popularity for its health benefits and low caloric content.
