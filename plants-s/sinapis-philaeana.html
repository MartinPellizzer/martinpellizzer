Sinapis philaeana

Sinapis philaeana, commonly known as the African mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant has small, yellow flowers and linear, pinnately lobed leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Sinapis philaeana is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive foliage and flowering habit.
