Sterculia cordata var. montana

Sterculia cordata var. montana, commonly known as the mountain sterculia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
