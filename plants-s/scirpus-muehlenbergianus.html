Scirpus muehlenbergianus

Scirpus muehlenbergianus, commonly known as Muhlenberg's bulrush, is a tall, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has tall, stiff, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of brown flowers in the summer.

Scirpus muehlenbergianus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
