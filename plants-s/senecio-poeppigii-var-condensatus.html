Senecio poeppigii var. condensatus

Senecio poeppigii var. condensatus, commonly known as the denseleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing habit with dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often woolly or hairy. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a succulent.
