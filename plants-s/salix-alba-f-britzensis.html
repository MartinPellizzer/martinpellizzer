Salix alba f. britzensis

Salix alba f. britzensis, commonly known as the Britzen willow, is a rare horticultural form of the white willow.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and a distinctive, upright growth habit. The plant is native to central Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after specimen among plant enthusiasts.
