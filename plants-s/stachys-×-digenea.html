Stachys × digenea

Stachys × digenea, commonly known as the hybrid betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Stachys × digenea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
