Stachys lanigera

Stachys lanigera, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and spikes of violet or blue flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, four-lobed flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stachys lanigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
