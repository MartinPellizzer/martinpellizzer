Senecio capillaris

Senecio capillaris, commonly known as threadleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio capillaris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
