Salix caprea var. ramuliflorus

Salix caprea var. ramuliflorus, commonly known as the hairy willow or ramuli-flowered willow, is a variant of the European goat willow.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender branches, which often bear clusters of small, yellowish catkins. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture and a serrated margin.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along streambanks and in wet woodlands.
