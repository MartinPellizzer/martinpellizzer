Scirpus lucidus

Scirpus lucidus, commonly known as the glossy club-rush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered with a waxy, shiny coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil in marshy environments.

Scirpus lucidus is often found in shallow water or damp soil, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland habitats.
