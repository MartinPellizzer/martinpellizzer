Silene eriophora

Silene eriophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Silene eriophora is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of the prairie ecosystem.
