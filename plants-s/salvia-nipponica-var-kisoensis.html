Salvia nipponica var. kisoensis

Salvia nipponica var. kisoensis, commonly known as Kiso sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
