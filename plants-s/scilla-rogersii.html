Scilla rogersii

Scilla rogersii, commonly known as the San Francisco daffodil, is a bulbous perennial native to California.

It produces clusters of bright blue to purple, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Scilla rogersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural collections.
