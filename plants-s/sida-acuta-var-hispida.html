Sida acuta var. hispida

Sida acuta var. hispida, commonly known as the rough sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Sida acuta var. hispida is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a weed in many agricultural settings.
