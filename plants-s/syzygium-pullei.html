Syzygium pullei

Syzygium pullei, commonly known as the white apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.
