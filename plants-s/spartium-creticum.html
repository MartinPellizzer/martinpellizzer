Spartium creticum

Spartium creticum, commonly known as Cretan clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Spartium creticum has a creeping growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in coastal areas and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
