Salacia arborea

Salacia arborea, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright orange, fleshy fruits resembling apples. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in traditional medicine.

Salacia arborea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
