Salsola oppositifolia f. feminea

Salsola oppositifolia f. feminea, commonly known as the female form of the common saxifrage, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy, opposite leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and disturbed soils where it thrives in high-salinity conditions.

Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well-suited for harsh, dry climates.
