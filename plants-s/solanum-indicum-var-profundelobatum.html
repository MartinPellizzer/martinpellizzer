Solanum indicum var. profundelobatum

Solanum indicum var. profundelobatum, commonly known as the deep-lobed nightshade, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with deeply lobed margins. The flowers are typically solitary, bell-shaped, and predominantly white or pale green, often with a slight purple tint. The fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests or along stream banks.
