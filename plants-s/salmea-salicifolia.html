Salmea salicifolia

Salmea salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved salmea, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, willow-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warm season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

Salmea salicifolia is often found in rocky outcrops and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna.
