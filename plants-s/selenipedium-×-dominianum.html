Selenipedium × dominianum

Selenipedium × dominianum, commonly known as the hybrid giant spider-orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces tall, erect stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns. Selenipedium × dominianum is a hybrid between Selenipedium albidum and Selenipedium suavissimum, making it a unique example of natural hybridization in orchids.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is highly valued in conservation efforts and botanical research.
