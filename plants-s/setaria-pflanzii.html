Setaria pflanzii

Setaria pflanzii, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Setaria pflanzii is a hardy perennial that is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and withstand drought conditions.
