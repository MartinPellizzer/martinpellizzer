Solanum leiophyllum

Solanum leiophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that mature to a dark blue or black color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Solanum leiophyllum is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers.
