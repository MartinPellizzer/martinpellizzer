Syringa pinnatifolia var. alashanensis

Syringa pinnatifolia var. alashanensis, commonly known as the Alashan lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, pinnately compound leaves and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
