Stipagrostis dregeana

Stipagrostis dregeana, commonly known as the dwarf grass or dwarf reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Stipagrostis dregeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
