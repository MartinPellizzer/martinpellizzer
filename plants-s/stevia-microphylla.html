Stevia microphylla

Stevia microphylla, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Stevia microphylla is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides.

It is widely used as a natural sweetener and has various applications in herbal medicine.
