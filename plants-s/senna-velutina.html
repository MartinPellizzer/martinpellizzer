Senna velutina

Senna velutina, commonly known as velvet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Senna velutina is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.

Its seeds are often used as a source of protein and can be processed into a flour.
