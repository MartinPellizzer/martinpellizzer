Sophora inhambanensis

Sophora inhambanensis, commonly known as the Inhambane acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also important for wildlife, providing food and shelter for various animals.
