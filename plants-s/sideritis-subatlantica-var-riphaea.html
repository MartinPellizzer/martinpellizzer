Sideritis subatlantica var. riphaea

Sideritis subatlantica var. riphaea, commonly known as the Atlantic sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
