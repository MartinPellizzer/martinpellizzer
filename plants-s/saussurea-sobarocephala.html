Saussurea sobarocephala

Saussurea sobarocephala, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are often yellow or orange and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
