Solanum paralum

Solanum paralum, commonly known as the paralum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are usually yellow or white with five petals.

The fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
