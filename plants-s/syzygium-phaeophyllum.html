Syzygium phaeophyllum

Syzygium phaeophyllum, commonly known as the black-leaf syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, edible drupe with a sweet and tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Syzygium phaeophyllum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
