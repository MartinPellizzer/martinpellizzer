Schinus longifolia var. paraguariensis

Schinus longifolia var. paraguariensis, commonly known as cul de sac, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. The berries contain seeds surrounded by a sticky, aromatic pulp.

It is often found in dry forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
