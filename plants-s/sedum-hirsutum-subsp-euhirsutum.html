Sedum hirsutum subsp. euhirsutum

Sedum hirsutum subsp.

euhirsutum, commonly known as the wooly stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving the plant its "woolly" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
