Satureja mutica

Satureja mutica, commonly known as mountain savory, is a small, aromatic herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. Its leaves are small, oval, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from pink to purple.

Satureja mutica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
