Sonchus cassinianus

Sonchus cassinianus, commonly known as the Cassin's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are generally hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by small, spiky seed heads.
