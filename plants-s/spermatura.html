Spermatura

Spermatura, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its unique common name. The plant produces small, pale flowers that emit a faint, bioluminescent glow at night, a trait that is uncommon in most flowering plants. Spermatura thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural habitat.
