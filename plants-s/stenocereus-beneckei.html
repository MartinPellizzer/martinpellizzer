Stenocereus beneckei

Stenocereus beneckei, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and spines that provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically pollinated by bats and insects. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains edible seeds.

This species is adapted to withstand extreme drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
