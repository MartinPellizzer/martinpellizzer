Sarocalamus abietinus

Sarocalamus abietinus, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and dense, needle-like foliage. The leaves are typically dark green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its timber and is often found in temperate forests at high elevations.
