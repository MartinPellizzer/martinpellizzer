Sida ciliaris var. mexicana

Sida ciliaris var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican sida, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually ovate with hairy surfaces. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in poor soils with limited water availability.

The plant is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
