Scutellaria pilosa var. tenuiter

Scutellaria pilosa var. tenuiter, commonly known as the slender scutellaria, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The flowers are usually blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem, and feature a distinctive hooded calyx. This variant is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
