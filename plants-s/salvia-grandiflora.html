Salvia grandiflora

Salvia grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features aromatic, mint-scented leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Salvia grandiflora is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
