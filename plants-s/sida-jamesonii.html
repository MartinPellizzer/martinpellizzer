Sida jamesonii

Sida jamesonii, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has hairy, branching stems and produces small, pink or white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Sida jamesonii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
