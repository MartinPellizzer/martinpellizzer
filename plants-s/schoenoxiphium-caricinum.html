Schoenoxiphium caricinum

Schoenoxiphium caricinum, commonly known as the Canadian goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and streambanks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Schoenoxiphium caricinum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological value.
