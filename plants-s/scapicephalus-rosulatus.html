Scapicephalus rosulatus

Scapicephalus rosulatus, commonly known as the crown-of-thorns plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, leafless stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.

Scapicephalus rosulatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
