Sunaptea urbani

Sunaptea urbani, commonly known as the urban sunflower, is a flowering plant native to urban environments.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in sunny, compacted areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, making it visually similar to common sunflowers.
