Salvia leucantha f. iobaphes

Salvia leucantha f. iobaphes, commonly known as the white salvia or white hummingbird sage, is a hybrid form of the species Salvia leucantha.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces showy, tubular flower clusters that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
