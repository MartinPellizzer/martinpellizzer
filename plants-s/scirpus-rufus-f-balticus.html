Scirpus rufus f. balticus

Scirpus rufus f. balticus, commonly known as the Baltic bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush, characterized by its distinctive reddish hues.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color, and it produces small, clustered spikelets at the top. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
