Scirpus falcatus

Scirpus falcatus, commonly known as the swordgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often curved, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus falcatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
