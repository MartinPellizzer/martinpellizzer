Senecio lessonianus

Senecio lessonianus, commonly known as the Lesson's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
