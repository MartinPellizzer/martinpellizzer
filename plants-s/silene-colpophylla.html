Silene colpophylla

Silene colpophylla, commonly known as the red sandspur, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red, spiny bracts that enclose its small, white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Silene colpophylla is an invasive species in some regions, posing challenges to native plant communities.
