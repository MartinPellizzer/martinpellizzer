Scilla leptophylla

Scilla leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

Scilla leptophylla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early blooming habit.
