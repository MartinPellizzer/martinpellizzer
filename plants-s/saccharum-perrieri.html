Saccharum perrieri

Saccharum perrieri, commonly known as the Perrieri grass, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It is a tall, perennial grass that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its narrow, blade-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat and thrives in well-drained soils. Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

However, it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
