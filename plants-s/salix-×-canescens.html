Salix × canescens

Salix × canescens, commonly known as the white willow, is a hybrid species within the willow family (Salicaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and finely serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers, streams, or wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is widely cultivated in gardens and riparian zones.
