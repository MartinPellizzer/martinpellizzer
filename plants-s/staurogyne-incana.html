Staurogyne incana

Staurogyne incana, commonly known as the white-stemmed water plant, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are characterized by their pale green to white coloration.

Staurogyne incana plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
