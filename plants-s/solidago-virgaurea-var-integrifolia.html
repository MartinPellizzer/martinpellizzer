Solidago virgaurea var. integrifolia

Solidago virgaurea var. integrifolia, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have smooth, entire margins, giving the plant its species name *integrifolia*. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
