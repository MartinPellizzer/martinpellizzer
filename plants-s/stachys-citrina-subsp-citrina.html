Stachys citrina subsp. citrina

Stachys citrina subsp.

citrina, commonly known as lemon yarrow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, silver-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its medicinal and ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
