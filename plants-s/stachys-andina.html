Stachys andina

Stachys andina, commonly known as Andean betony, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. Stachys andina is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats at high altitudes.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally in some South American cultures for medicinal purposes.
