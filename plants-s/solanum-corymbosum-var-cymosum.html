Solanum corymbosum var. cymosum

Solanum corymbosum var. cymosum, commonly known as the cymose tomato, is a botanical variant of the tomato plant characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescences.

It typically produces small to medium-sized fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important crops like peppers and potatoes. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

This variety is valued for its early maturity and adaptability to various growing conditions.
