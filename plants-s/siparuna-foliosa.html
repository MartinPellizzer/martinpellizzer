Siparuna foliosa

Siparuna foliosa, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces bright blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals but are toxic to humans. Siparuna foliosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its ornamental value has made it popular in horticulture, though it requires specific care to grow successfully.
