Salvia extersa

Salvia extersa, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia extersa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
