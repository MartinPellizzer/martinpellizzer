Schismus brevifolius

Schismus brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved schismus, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental or for erosion control in suitable climates.
