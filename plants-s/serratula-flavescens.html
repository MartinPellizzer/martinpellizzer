Serratula flavescens

Serratula flavescens, commonly known as the yellow centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Serratula flavescens is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
