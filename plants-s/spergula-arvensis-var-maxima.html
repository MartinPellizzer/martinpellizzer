Spergula arvensis var. maxima

Spergula arvensis var. maxima, commonly known as the greater field vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
