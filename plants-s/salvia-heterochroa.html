Salvia heterochroa

Salvia heterochroa, commonly known as the heterochrome sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among horticulturists.
