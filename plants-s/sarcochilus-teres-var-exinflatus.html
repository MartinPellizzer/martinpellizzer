Sarcochilus teres var. exinflatus

Sarcochilus teres var. exinflatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed ghost orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces striking white to pale yellow flowers with intricate, spurred petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic-rich substrates.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
