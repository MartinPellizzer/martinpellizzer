Serpyllum citriodora

Serpyllum citriodora, commonly known as lemon thyme or citron thyme, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, fragrant leaves that emit a citrus-like scent. Its flowers are typically pale pink to white and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Serpyllum citriodora is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its aromatic properties.
