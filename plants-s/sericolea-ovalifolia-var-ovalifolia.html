Sericolea ovalifolia var. ovalifolia

Sericolea ovalifolia var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf sericolea, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
