Syringa venosa var. lactea

Syringa venosa var. lactea, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white or silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
