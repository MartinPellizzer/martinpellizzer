Stanhopea insignis

Stanhopea insignis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

The botanical features of Stanhopea insignis make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
