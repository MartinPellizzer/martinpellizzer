Semiarundinaria densiflora f. villosa

Semiarundinaria densiflora f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the dense-flowered bamboo species.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy nodes, which distinguish it from other forms of the species. The plant is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia. Its culms are typically slender and often exhibit a bluish-green hue.

This ornamental bamboo is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
