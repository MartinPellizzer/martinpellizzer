Scleria trichopoda

Scleria trichopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scleria trichopoda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
