Saxifraga sibirica var. schindleri

Saxifraga sibirica var. schindleri, commonly known as the Siberian saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
