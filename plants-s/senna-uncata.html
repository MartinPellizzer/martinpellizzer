Senna uncata

Senna uncata, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
