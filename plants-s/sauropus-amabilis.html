Sauropus amabilis

Sauropus amabilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red leaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features palmate leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration, especially on the underside, which gives it its common name. The plant is characterized by its branching structure and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, although its botanical applications remain under research.
