Symplocos depauperata

Symplocos depauperata, commonly known as the poorleaf waxmyrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Symplocos depauperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
