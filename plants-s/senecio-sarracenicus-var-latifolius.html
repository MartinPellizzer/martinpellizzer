Senecio sarracenicus var. latifolius

Senecio sarracenicus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in various regions around the world.
