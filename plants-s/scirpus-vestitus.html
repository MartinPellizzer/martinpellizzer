Scirpus vestitus

Scirpus vestitus, commonly known as the white flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically light green to white in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. Scirpus vestitus thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
