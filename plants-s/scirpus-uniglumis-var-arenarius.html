Scirpus uniglumis var. arenarius

Scirpus uniglumis var. arenarius, commonly known as the sand reed, is a perennial grass-like plant native to coastal and wetland areas.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces tall, slender spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or muddy soils and is often found in tidal zones or brackish wetlands. It plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its morphology is adapted to withstand salt spray and fluctuating water levels.
