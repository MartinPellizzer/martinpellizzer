Salvia muscarioides

Salvia muscarioides, commonly known as the blue salvia or woodland sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
