Stachys pubens

Stachys pubens, commonly known as sticky betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers in whorls along the upper stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Stachys pubens is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
