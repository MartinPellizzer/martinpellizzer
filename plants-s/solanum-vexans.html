Solanum vexans

Solanum vexans, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
