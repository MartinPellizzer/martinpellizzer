Stereospermum arcuatum

Stereospermum arcuatum, commonly known as the arcuate sandpaper tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its rough, sandpaper-like bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
