Senecio ductoris

Senecio ductoris, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio ductoris is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
