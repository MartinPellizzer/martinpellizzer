Selaginella hartwegiana

Selaginella hartwegiana, commonly known as Hartweg's spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a rosette of fleshy, scale-like leaves that form a dense, low-growing structure. It produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Selaginella hartwegiana is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
