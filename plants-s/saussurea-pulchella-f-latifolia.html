Saussurea pulchella f. latifolia

Saussurea pulchella f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved saussurea, is a variant of the saussurea plant characterized by its wide, flat leaves.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a central disc that adds to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
