Saxifraga nelsoniana subsp. pacifica

Saxifraga nelsoniana subsp.

pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along the Pacific coast, thriving in moist, well-drained environments.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually rounded and have a slightly hairy texture, adapted to withstand coastal conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
