Salvia cataonica

Salvia cataonica, commonly known as the Greek sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Salvia cataonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many gardens.
