Solanum fauriei

Solanum fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries.

The berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making the plant potentially harmful to humans and animals.
