Sideritis sericea

Sideritis sericea, commonly known as silky sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Sideritis sericea is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties.
