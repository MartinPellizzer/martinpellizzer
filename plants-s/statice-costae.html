Statice costae

Statice costae, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is native to coastal regions and thrives in saline or well-drained soils. Its narrow, linear leaves are typically blue-green in color and contribute to its ornamental value.

Statice costae is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and resilience to drying.
