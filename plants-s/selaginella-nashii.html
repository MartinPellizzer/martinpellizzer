Selaginella nashii

Selaginella nashii, commonly known as the Nash's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are borne on the upper parts of the plant.

Selaginella nashii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability.
