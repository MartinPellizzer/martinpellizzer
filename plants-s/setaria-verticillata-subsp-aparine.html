Setaria verticillata subsp. aparine

Setaria verticillata subsp.

aparine, commonly known as mouseear grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution in various habitats.
