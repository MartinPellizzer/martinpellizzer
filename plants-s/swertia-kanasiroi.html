Swertia kanasiroi

Swertia kanasiroi, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and has a robust root system adapted to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Swertia kanasiroi is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
