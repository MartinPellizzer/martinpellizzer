Scutellaria laeteviolacea var. laeteviolacea

Scutellaria laeteviolacea var. laeteviolacea, commonly known as the blue scutellaria, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
