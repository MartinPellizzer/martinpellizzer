Spergula arvensis var. laevis

Spergula arvensis var. laevis, commonly known as smooth field sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, smooth leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
