Scutellaria epilobiifolia

Scutellaria epilobiifolia, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its unique morphology includes opposite leaves and a distinctive, tubular corolla that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Scutellaria epilobiifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
