Siraitia africana

Siraitia africana, commonly known as the African giant watermelon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruits that can weigh several kilograms. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers and has a vigorous climbing habit. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is prized for its high water content and sweet, juicy flesh, making it a popular crop in its native region.
