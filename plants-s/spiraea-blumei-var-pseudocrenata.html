Spiraea blumei var. pseudocrenata

Spiraea blumei var. pseudocrenata, commonly known as the narrowleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features arching branches with clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a slightly serrated margin, and they turn yellow in the autumn. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact size and graceful form make it suitable for borders, rock gardens, or as a specimen plant.
