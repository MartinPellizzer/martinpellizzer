Saxifraga nambulana

Saxifraga nambulana, commonly known as the Nambulana saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
