Syringa alba

Syringa alba, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

Syringa alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its sweet scent and showy blooms.
