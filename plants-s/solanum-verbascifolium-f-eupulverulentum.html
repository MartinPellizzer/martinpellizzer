Solanum verbascifolium f. eupulverulentum

Solanum verbascifolium f. eupulverulentum, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a botanical variant characterized by its densely hairy, velvety foliage.

This plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a distinctive silver or grayish-green color due to the thick layer of fine pubescence. The flowers are small, typically yellow or white, and the fruit is a spherical berry that ripens to a deep blue or purple.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique texture, making it a popular choice in specialty gardens.
