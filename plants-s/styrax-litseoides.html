Styrax litseoides

Styrax litseoides, commonly known as the silky ash or litsea styrax, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark purple, egg-shaped berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Styrax litseoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
