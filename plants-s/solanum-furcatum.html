Solanum furcatum

Solanum furcatum, commonly known as the forked nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its berries are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.

Solanum furcatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
