Serratula cupuliformis

Serratula cupuliformis, commonly known as the cup-shaped sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, hairy leaves and a tall, slender stem that bears yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its flowers are arranged in a single, terminal head, giving it a distinctive cup-like appearance.

Serratula cupuliformis is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
