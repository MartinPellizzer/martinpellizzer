Solanum jasminoides subvar. glaberrimum

Solanum jasminoides subvar. glaberrimum, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and smooth, glabrous stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance and are pollinated primarily by insects.
