Sporobolus acinifolius

Sporobolus acinifolius, commonly known as desert threeawn, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces spikelets that are tightly clustered and have awns, which are bristle-like structures that aid in seed dispersal. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Sporobolus acinifolius is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
