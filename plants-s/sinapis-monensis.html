Sinapis monensis

Sinapis monensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian mustard, is an annual herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Sinapis monensis is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and agricultural studies in the region.
