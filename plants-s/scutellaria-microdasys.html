Scutellaria microdasys

Scutellaria microdasys, commonly known as the small-leaved skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria microdasys is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
