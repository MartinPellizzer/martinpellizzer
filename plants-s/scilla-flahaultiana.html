Scilla flahaultiana

Scilla flahaultiana, commonly known as the Flahault's scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch to woodland or rock garden settings. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for shaded garden areas.

Scilla flahaultiana is relatively low maintenance and can naturalize over time, creating a charming display in the right conditions.
