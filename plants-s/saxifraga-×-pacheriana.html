Saxifraga × pacheriana

Saxifraga × pacheriana, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with dark green, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine plantings.
