Stenostephanus sessilifolius

Stenostephanus sessilifolius, commonly known as the sessile-leaved stephanus, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a spherical or disk-like shape, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are sessile, meaning they lack a petiole and are directly attached to the stem.

It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
