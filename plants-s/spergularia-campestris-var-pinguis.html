Spergularia campestris var. pinguis

Spergularia campestris var. pinguis, commonly known as the fat sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its flowers are small, pink or white, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
