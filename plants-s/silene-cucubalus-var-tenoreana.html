Silene cucubalus var. tenoreana

Silene cucubalus var. tenoreana, commonly known as the Sicilian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred structure. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting various insect life.
