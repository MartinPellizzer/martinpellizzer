Sesleria caerulea var. interrupta

Sesleria caerulea var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive interrupted pattern of silver or white markings along the leaf blades. The plant produces small, loose clusters of pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
