Seriphidium kochiiforme

Seriphidium kochiiforme, commonly known as the Kashmir thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellowish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh climatic conditions. Its structure includes a tall, slender stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves.
