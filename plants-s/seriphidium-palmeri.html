Seriphidium palmeri

Seriphidium palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

Seriphidium palmeri is often found in rocky crevices and along dry streambeds, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
