Sedum laxum var. latifolium

Sedum laxum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to North America.

It features fleshy, upright stems and broad, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
