Salvia stenophylla

Salvia stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Salvia stenophylla is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
