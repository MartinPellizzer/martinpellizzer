Senecio laciniatus

Senecio laciniatus, commonly known as the ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its scientific name reflects its resemblance to the genus Senecio and its lacerated leaf structure.

Senecio laciniatus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a hardy perennial.
