Sorbus pekinensis

Sorbus pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 7 leaflets, and its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange in color. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
