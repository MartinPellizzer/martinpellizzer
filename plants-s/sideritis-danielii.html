Sideritis danielii

Sideritis danielii, commonly known as Daniel's ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Sideritis danielii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
