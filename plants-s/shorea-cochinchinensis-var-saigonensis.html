Shorea cochinchinensis var. saigonensis

Shorea cochinchinensis var. saigonensis, commonly known as Vietnamese rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich reddish-brown color. The plant typically grows in lowland evergreen forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The species is highly valued for its durable and aesthetically pleasing timber, though it is also threatened by overharvesting and habitat loss.
