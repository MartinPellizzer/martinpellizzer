Solanum pongoense

Solanum pongoense, commonly known as the Pongoense nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is not typically cultivated for food or ornamental purposes.
