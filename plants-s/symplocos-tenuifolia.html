Symplocos tenuifolia

Symplocos tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Symplocos tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
