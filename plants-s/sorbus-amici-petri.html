Sorbus amici-petri

Sorbus amici-petri, commonly known as the Amici-Petri rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters and its distinctive, oval-shaped fruits that turn red when mature. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
