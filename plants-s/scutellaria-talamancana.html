Scutellaria talamancana

Scutellaria talamancana, commonly known as Talamancan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Scutellaria talamancana typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
