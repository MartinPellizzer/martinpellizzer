Serratula lyratifolia

Serratula lyratifolia, commonly known as the lyreleaf sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive lyre-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Serratula lyratifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
