Stuckenia mongolica

Stuckenia mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian waterplantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of central and eastern Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water bodies, such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with some floating on the water surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in summer.

Stuckenia mongolica plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
