Syzygium acutisepalum

Syzygium acutisepalum, commonly known as the narrow-sepal syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in the dry season.

Syzygium acutisepalum is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
