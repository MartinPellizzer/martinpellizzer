Scaevola humifusa var. pulvinaris

Scaevola humifusa var. pulvinaris, commonly known as the dwarf fairy fanflower, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to Australia.

It features small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
