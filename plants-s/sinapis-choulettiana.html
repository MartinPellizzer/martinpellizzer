Sinapis choulettiana

Sinapis choulettiana, commonly known as the Choulett's mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a cover crop, it is cultivated in some agricultural and horticultural settings.
