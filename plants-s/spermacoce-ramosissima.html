Spermacoce ramosissima

Spermacoce ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a branched stem covered in fine hairs, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Spermacoce ramosissima is adapted to disturbed soils and thrives in open, sunny environments.
