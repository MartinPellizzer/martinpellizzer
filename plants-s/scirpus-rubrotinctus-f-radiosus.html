Scirpus rubrotinctus f. radiosus

Scirpus rubrotinctus f. radiosus, commonly known as the red-stained sedge, is a variant of the sedge genus Scirpus.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish tinge in its foliage, which becomes more pronounced under certain environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has slender, triangular stems and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
