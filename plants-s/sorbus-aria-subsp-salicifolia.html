Sorbus aria subsp. salicifolia

Sorbus aria subsp.

salicifolia, commonly known as the whitebeam or white-ash tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red, persistent berries.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, often resembling those of willow trees. This subspecies is native to Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates.

It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, contributing to biodiversity in its habitat.
