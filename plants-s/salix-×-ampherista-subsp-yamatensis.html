Salix × ampherista subsp. yamatensis

Salix × ampherista subsp.

yamatensis, commonly known as the Yamatenshima willow, is a hybrid species native to Japan. It is characterized by its slender, often weeping stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or streams.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to wetland environments.
