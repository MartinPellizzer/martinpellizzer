Syzygium fluviatile

Syzygium fluviatile, commonly known as the river apple or water apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, round, greenish-yellow berry that ripens to a reddish hue. The plant thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
