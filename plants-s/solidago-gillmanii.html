Solidago gillmanii

Solidago gillmanii, commonly known as Gillman's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its bright yellow inflorescence consists of numerous small florets arranged in a rounded cluster.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
