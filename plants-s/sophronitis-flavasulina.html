Sophronitis flavasulina

Sophronitis flavasulina, commonly known as the yellow-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent yellow veins, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
