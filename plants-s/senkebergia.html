Senkebergia

Senkebergia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their thick, fleshy leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Senkebergia species are typically low-growing, often forming dense mats or compact shrubs. They thrive in humid, well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

The genus is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
