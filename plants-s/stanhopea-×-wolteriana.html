Stanhopea × wolteriana

Stanhopea × wolteriana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Stanhopea and Wolterina orchids.

It is characterized by its intricate, fragrant floral structure with a prominent, often colorful lip that serves to attract pollinators. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both scientific study and botanical gardens.
