Solanum macrantherum

Solanum macrantherum, commonly known as the large-flowered nightshade, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. Its leaves are usually ovate with a rough texture, and it produces small, toxic berries that ripen to a bright yellow or orange color.

Solanum macrantherum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
