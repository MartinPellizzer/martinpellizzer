Sinapis alba var. ditrichocarpa

Sinapis alba var. ditrichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy mustard, is a variant of the white mustard plant.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its seeds are small and oblong, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional herbal practices.
