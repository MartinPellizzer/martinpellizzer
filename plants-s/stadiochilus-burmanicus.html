Stadiochilus burmanicus

Stadiochilus burmanicus, commonly known as the Burmese toothbrush plant, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture.

Stadiochilus burmanicus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
