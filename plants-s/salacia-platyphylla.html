Salacia platyphylla

Salacia platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
