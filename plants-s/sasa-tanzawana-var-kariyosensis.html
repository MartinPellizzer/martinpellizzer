Sasa tanzawana var. kariyosensis

Sasa tanzawana var. kariyosensis, commonly known as the Kariyosense bamboo, is a variant of the Tanzawana bamboo native to Japan.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dense clumps, forming thick mats on forest floors. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This bamboo variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
