Salix barclayi var. veritomonticola

Salix barclayi var. veritomonticola, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streams, meadows, and forest edges. Its flowers are catkin-like, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

This variety is adapted to the cool, temperate climates of its native range and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.
