Senecio pinnatifolius var. pleiocephalus

Senecio pinnatifolius var. pleiocephalus, commonly known as the yellow owl's eye, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble an owl's eye, with a central dark center surrounded by white petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with narrow, linear leaves arranged in a pinnate pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
