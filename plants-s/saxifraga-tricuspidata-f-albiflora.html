Saxifraga tricuspidata f. albiflora

Saxifraga tricuspidata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered rock saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic, with a hairy texture adapted to cold climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
