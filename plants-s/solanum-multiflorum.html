Solanum multiflorum

Solanum multiflorum, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with smooth, often thornless stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by berries that change from green to yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important species like tomatoes and potatoes.
