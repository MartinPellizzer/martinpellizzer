Salvia viscosa

Salvia viscosa, commonly known as sticky sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky resin, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, violet to blue-purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Salvia viscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
