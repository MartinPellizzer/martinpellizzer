Stachytarpheta crassifolia subsp. rotundifolia

Stachytarpheta crassifolia subsp.

rotundifolia, commonly known as the rounded-leaved ironweed, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to round leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct serrated margin.

The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
