Stapelia namaquensis

Stapelia namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua star, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers that resemble the appearance of a star or a flower with multiple petals. The plant produces a sweet, honey-like scent, which attracts pollinators such as flies and beetles. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Stapelia namaquensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
