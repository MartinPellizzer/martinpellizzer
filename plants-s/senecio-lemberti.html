Senecio lemberti

Senecio lemberti, commonly known as the Lemberti's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a grayish-green hue.

Senecio lemberti produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
