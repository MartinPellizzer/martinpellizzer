Senecio transmarinus var. major

Senecio transmarinus var. major, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces small, tubular disc florets that are clustered in composite flower heads.

Senecio transmarinus var. major is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
