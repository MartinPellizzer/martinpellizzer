Stylophorum paniculatum

Stylophorum paniculatum, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features palmately divided leaves with a delicate, trifoliate structure, and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a panicle. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it is often found in the eastern United States.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
