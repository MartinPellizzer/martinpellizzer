Sisymbrium dahuricum

Sisymbrium dahuricum, commonly known as Siberian wallflower, is an annual flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has pinnately divided leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Sisymbrium dahuricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
