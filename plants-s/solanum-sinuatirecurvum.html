Solanum sinuatirecurvum

Solanum sinuatirecurvum, commonly known as the wavy-leaf nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive wavy, recurved leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berry-like fruits, which are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
