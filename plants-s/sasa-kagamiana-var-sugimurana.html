Sasa kagamiana var. sugimurana

Sasa kagamiana var. sugimurana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or sugimurana sasa, is a variant of the sasa plant native to Japan.

It is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae, characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and as a food source. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green coloration.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas across central and southern Japan.
