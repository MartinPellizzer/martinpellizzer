Stipa patagonica

Stipa patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, spiky seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. This plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands, and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, contributing to soil stabilization.

Stipa patagonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
