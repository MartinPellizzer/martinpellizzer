Sporobolus teretifolius

Sporobolus teretifolius, commonly known as bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are often pink to reddish in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
