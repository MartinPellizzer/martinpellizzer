Succisa corniculata

Succisa corniculata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped seed pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its flowers are showy, with bright red petals and a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its resilience and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
