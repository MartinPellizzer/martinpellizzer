Stelis ricii

Stelis ricii, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on rocky substrates and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose raceme, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Stelis ricii is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and requires minimal water.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a distinctive species within the Orchidaceae family.
