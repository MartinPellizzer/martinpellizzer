Sorghum subglabrescens var. rubrocernuum

Sorghum subglabrescens var. rubrocernuum, commonly known as the red-stemmed sorghum, is a variant of sorghum characterized by its distinctive reddish stem coloration.

It is a tall, grass-like plant native to Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny grains, which are typically used for forage or traditional purposes. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a robust root system that aids in water retention.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique pigmentation and ecological resilience.
