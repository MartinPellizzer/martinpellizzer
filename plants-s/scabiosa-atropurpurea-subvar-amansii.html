Scabiosa atropurpurea subvar. amansii

Scabiosa atropurpurea subvar. amansii, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and upright stems that bear clusters of five-petaled blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its dark purple flowers and its affinity for sunny habitats.
