Sinocalamus yunnanicus

Sinocalamus yunnanicus, commonly known as the Yunnan bamboo, is a tall, woody perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Sinocalamus yunnanicus is also valued for its strong, durable culms, which have been historically used in construction and crafts.
