Senecio acutifolius

Senecio acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaf senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
