Solisia solisioides

Solisia solisioides, commonly known as the golden spurge, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which reduces water loss through transpiration.

Solisia solisioides is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
