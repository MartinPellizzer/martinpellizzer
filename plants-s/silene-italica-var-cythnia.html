Silene italica var. cythnia

Silene italica var. cythnia, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Silene italica var. cythnia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
