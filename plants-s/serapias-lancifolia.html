Serapias lancifolia

Serapias lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Serapias lancifolia is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
