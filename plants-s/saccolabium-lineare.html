Saccolabium lineare

Saccolabium lineare, commonly known as the linear saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Saccolabium lineare is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
