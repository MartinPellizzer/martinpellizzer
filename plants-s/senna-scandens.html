Senna scandens

Senna scandens, commonly known as the climbing senna, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Senna scandens is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain environments.
