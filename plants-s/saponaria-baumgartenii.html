Saponaria baumgartenii

Saponaria baumgartenii, commonly known as the Balkan soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its roots contain saponins, which give it its name and make it useful in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
