Sorghum purpureosericeum subsp. dimidiatum

Sorghum purpureosericeum subsp.

dimidiatum, commonly known as the purple silky sorghum, is a species native to Africa. It is characterized by its tall stature and striking purple foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers, typically in shades of red or purple. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, drought-tolerant plants.
