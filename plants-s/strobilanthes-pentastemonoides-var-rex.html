Strobilanthes pentastemonoides var. rex

Strobilanthes pentastemonoides var. rex, commonly known as the Royal Ghost Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble ghostly, translucent petals, often with a unique coloration that ranges from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often as an epiphyte on tree trunks or branches. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a complex inflorescence structure.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists alike.
