Scilla rigidifolia var. nervosa

Scilla rigidifolia var. nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have distinct parallel veins. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and delicate appearance.
