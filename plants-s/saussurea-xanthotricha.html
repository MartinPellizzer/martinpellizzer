Saussurea xanthotricha

Saussurea xanthotricha, commonly known as the yellow-haired saffron, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stem and dense, yellowish to brownish hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing at high elevations with poor soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
