Syngonanthus gracilis var. araxaensis

Syngonanthus gracilis var. araxaensis, commonly known as the Araxa slender flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
