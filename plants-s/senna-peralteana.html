Senna peralteana

Senna peralteana, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly serrated.

Senna peralteana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
