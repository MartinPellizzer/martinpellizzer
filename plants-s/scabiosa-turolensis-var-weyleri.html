Scabiosa turolensis var. weyleri

Scabiosa turolensis var. weyleri, commonly known as the Sierra Madre scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
