Salix × hermaphroditica

Salix × hermaphroditica, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Salix alba and Salix cinerea.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, temperate regions. The plant features slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces catkins, which are the primary means of pollination in willow species.

Salix × hermaphroditica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
