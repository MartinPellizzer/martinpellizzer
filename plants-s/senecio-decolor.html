Senecio decolor

Senecio decolor, commonly known as the yellow ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties in some traditional uses.

However, it can be toxic to livestock if ingested in large quantities.
