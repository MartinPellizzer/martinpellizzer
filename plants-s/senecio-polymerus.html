Senecio polymerus

Senecio polymerus, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as small, hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio polymerus is often found in rocky soils and is considered an important component of desert ecosystems.
