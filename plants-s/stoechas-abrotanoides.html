Stoechas abrotanoides

Stoechas abrotanoides, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.
