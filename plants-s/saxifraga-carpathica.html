Saxifraga carpathica

Saxifraga carpathica, commonly known as the Carpathian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Saxifraga carpathica is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
