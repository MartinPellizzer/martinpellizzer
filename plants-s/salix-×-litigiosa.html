Salix × litigiosa

Salix × litigiosa, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern regions of the United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams, wetlands, and other water bodies.

Salix × litigiosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
