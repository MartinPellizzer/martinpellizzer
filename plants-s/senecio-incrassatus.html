Senecio incrassatus

Senecio incrassatus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a garden plant.
