Stachys nemorivaga

Stachys nemorivaga, commonly known as the woodland betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from pink to purple.

Stachys nemorivaga is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
