Silvia crassiramea

Silvia crassiramea, commonly known as the thickstem silvia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Silvia crassiramea thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests or rocky slopes.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
