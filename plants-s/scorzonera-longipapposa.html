Scorzonera longipapposa

Scorzonera longipapposa, commonly known as the long-pappous scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant features a robust taproot, which is often harvested for its edible tuberous roots. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

Scorzonera longipapposa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
