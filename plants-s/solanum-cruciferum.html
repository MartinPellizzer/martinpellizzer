Solanum cruciferum

Solanum cruciferum, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, toxic berries that contain alkaloids such as solanin and scopolamine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
