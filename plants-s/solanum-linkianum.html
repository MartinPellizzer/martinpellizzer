Solanum linkianum

Solanum linkianum, commonly known as the Linkian nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Solanum linkianum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
