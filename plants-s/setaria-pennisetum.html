Setaria pennisetum

Setaria pennisetum, commonly known as finger grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, slender spikes that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Setaria pennisetum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
