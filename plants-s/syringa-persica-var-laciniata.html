Syringa persica var. laciniata

Syringa persica var. laciniata, commonly known as the Persian lilac with lobed leaves, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are palmately lobed, giving the plant a distinctive and ornamental appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
