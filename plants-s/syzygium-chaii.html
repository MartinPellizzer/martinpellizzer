Syzygium chaii

Syzygium chaii, commonly known as the Thai star fruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed star-shaped fruit. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy, ovate, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The fruit develops from an inflorescence of small white flowers and is known for its tart-sweet flavor.
