Sanguisorba minor var. microcarpa

Sanguisorba minor var. microcarpa, commonly known as dwarf marsh rose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to wet meadows and marshes in North America.

It features dense clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to waterlogged soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
