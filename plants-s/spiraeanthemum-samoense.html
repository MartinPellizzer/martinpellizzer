Spiraeanthemum samoense

Spiraeanthemum samoense, commonly known as the Samoan spirea, is a flowering plant native to the islands of Samoa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant pink or white flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
