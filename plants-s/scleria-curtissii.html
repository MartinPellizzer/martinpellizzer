Scleria curtissii

Scleria curtissii, commonly known as the curly bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scleria curtissii is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
