Sanguisorba minor subsp. balearica

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

balearica, commonly known as the Balearic sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are often fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. Sanguisorba minor subsp.

balearica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
