Serianthes grandiflora

Serianthes grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy and can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a trunk that is often buttressed at the base. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a leathery texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
