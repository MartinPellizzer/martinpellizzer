Setaria geniculata var. pauciseta

Setaria geniculata var. pauciseta, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
