Senecio persicifolius

Senecio persicifolius, commonly known as the peachleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, peacheshaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
