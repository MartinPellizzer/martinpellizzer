Sisyrinchium colubriferum

Sisyrinchium colubriferum, commonly known as the snake-like blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is named for its snake-like appearance, with narrow leaves and elongated flower clusters.
