Sideritis matris-filiae

Sideritis matris-filiae, commonly known as the mother-of-pearl plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Sideritis matris-filiae is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
