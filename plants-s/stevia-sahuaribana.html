Stevia sahuaribana

Stevia sahuaribana, commonly known as the Sahuaro stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Stevia sahuaribana is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, making it a natural sweetener.

It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
