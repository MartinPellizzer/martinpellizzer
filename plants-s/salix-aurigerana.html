Salix aurigerana

Salix aurigerana, commonly known as the golden willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside.

The golden willow is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
