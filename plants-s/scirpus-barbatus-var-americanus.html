Scirpus barbatus var. americanus

Scirpus barbatus var. americanus, commonly known as the American bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ability to filter water and support diverse wildlife.
