Silene jenisea var. vulgaris

Silene jenisea var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Silene jenisea var. vulgaris is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
