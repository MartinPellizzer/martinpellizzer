Siphonanthus costulatus

Siphonanthus costulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed siphonanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
