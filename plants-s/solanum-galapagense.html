Solanum galapagense

Solanum galapagense, commonly known as the Galápagos nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the arid climate of the islands. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum galapagense is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

It plays a role in the local ecosystem, providing food for various birds and insects.
