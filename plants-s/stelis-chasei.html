Stelis chasei

Stelis chasei, commonly known as the Chase's spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and delicate, featuring a combination of white, green, and yellow hues.

Stelis chasei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
