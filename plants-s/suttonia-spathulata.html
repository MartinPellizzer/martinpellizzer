Suttonia spathulata

Suttonia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved suttonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Suttonia spathulata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
