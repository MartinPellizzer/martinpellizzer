Sumacus copallina

Sumacus copallina, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is notable for its vibrant yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests.

Sumacus copallina is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
