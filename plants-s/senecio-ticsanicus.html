Senecio ticsanicus

Senecio ticsanicus, commonly known as the Ticsanicus groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in shades of yellow, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
