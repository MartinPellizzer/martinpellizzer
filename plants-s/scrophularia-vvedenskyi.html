Scrophularia vvedenskyi

Scrophularia vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Siberian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and it produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple.

Scrophularia vvedenskyi is often found in open habitats and is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season.
