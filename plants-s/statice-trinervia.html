Statice trinervia

Statice trinervia, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in salty, well-drained soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Statice trinervia is also valued in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
