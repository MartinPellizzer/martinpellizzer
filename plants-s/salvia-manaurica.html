Salvia manaurica

Salvia manaurica, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Salvia manaurica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and compact growth habit.
