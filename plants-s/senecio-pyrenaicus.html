Senecio pyrenaicus

Senecio pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Senecio pyrenaicus is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
