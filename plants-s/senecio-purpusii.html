Senecio purpusii

Senecio purpusii, commonly known as the Cape mountain snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It has silver-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Senecio purpusii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
