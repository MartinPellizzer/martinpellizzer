Smilax tetraptera

Smilax tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Smilax tetraptera thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to form dense ground cover.
