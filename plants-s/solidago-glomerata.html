Solidago glomerata

Solidago glomerata, commonly known as clustered goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its bright yellow inflorescences bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Solidago glomerata is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
