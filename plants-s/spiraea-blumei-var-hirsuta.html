Spiraea blumei var. hirsuta

Spiraea blumei var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
