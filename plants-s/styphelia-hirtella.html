Styphelia hirtella

Styphelia hirtella, commonly known as the hairy whorltongue, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically thrives in acidic, sandy soils and is often found in pine forests and heathlands. The plant has small, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in whorls along the stems, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Styphelia hirtella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
