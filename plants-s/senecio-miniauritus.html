Senecio miniauritus

Senecio miniauritus, commonly known as the silverleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray leaves with a woolly texture, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

Senecio miniauritus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and drought tolerance.
