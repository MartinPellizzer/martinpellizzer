Senecio johnstonii var. dalei

Senecio johnstonii var. dalei, commonly known as the Johnston's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and linear, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has a low, spreading growth habit and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
