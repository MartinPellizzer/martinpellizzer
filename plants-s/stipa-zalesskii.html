Stipa zalesskii

Stipa zalesskii, commonly known as the Zalesskii feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that are often pink or purplish in color. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered with fine hairs.

Stipa zalesskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
