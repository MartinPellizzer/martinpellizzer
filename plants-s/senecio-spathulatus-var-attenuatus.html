Senecio spathulatus var. attenuatus

Senecio spathulatus var. attenuatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its native range.
