Salvia brachiata

Salvia brachiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia brachiata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
