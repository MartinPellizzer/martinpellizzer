Sanguisorba tenuifolia var. kurilensis

Sanguisorba tenuifolia var. kurilensis, commonly known as Kuril narrowleaf sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Russian Far East and the Kuril Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky shores. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.
