Sacoglottis ovicarpa

Sacoglottis ovicarpa, commonly known as the eggfruit, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, yellow, egg-shaped fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, lowland areas.

The fruit is used in local cuisines and has potential medicinal properties.
