Spinacia oleracea var. hyemalis

Spinacia oleracea var. hyemalis, commonly known as winter spinach, is a cultivated variety of spinach that is adapted to colder climates.

It is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cooler temperatures, making it a valuable crop in temperate regions. The plant produces large, tender leaves that are rich in nutrients such as iron and vitamins. It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has a sprawling growth habit.

This variety is often used in winter gardens and can be harvested throughout the cooler months.
