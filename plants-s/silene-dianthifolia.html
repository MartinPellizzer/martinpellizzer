Silene dianthifolia

Silene dianthifolia, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in racemes. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in open habitats such as meadows and sandy soils.

Silene dianthifolia is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a common wildflower in its native range.
