Sterculia mindorensis

Sterculia mindorensis, commonly known as Mindanao sterculia, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland to montane forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.

The species plays a role in local ecosystems and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
