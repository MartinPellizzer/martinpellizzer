Senecio tipocochensis

Senecio tipocochensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is a small, succulent shrub that has adapted to the extreme arid conditions of its environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning to conserve moisture. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Senecio tipocochensis plays a vital role in the local ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert organisms.
