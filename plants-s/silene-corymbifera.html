Silene corymbifera

Silene corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant in the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Silene corymbifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
