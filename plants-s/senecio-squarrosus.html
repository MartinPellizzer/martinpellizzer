Senecio squarrosus

Senecio squarrosus, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
