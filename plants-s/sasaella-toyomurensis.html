Sasaella toyomurensis

Sasaella toyomurensis, commonly known as the Toyomura bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver-green coloration.

Sasaella toyomurensis is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.
