Solanum cerasiferum subsp. crepinii

Solanum cerasiferum subsp.

crepinii, commonly known as the cherry tomato bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, cherry-like tomatoes. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy fruits, which are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
