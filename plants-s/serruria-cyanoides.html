Serruria cyanoides

Serruria cyanoides, commonly known as the blue serruria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal regions. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, giving it its scientific name.

Serruria cyanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
