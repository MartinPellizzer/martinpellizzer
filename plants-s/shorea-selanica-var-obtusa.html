Shorea selanica var. obtusa

Shorea selanica var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed ironwood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its thick, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. This variant is distinguished by its obtuse leaf tips and robust growth habits, often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
