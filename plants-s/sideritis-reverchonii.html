Sideritis reverchonii

Sideritis reverchonii, commonly known as the Greek violet or mountain violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge.

Sideritis reverchonii is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties.
