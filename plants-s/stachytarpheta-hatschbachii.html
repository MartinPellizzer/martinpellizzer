Stachytarpheta hatschbachii

Stachytarpheta hatschbachii, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, tubular flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Stachytarpheta hatschbachii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
