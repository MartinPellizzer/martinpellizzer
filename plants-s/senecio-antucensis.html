Senecio antucensis

Senecio antucensis, commonly known as the Antuco groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. Its leaves are often hairy and narrow, while its yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

Senecio antucensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
