Senna trichosepala

Senna trichosepala, commonly known as the white senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, three-lobed pods. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine, and it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
