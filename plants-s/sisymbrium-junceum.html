Sisymbrium junceum

Sisymbrium junceum, commonly known as field pennycress, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer. Its fruit is a short, beak-like silique containing several seeds.

It is characterized by its rapid growth cycle and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
