Scilla sylvestris

Scilla sylvestris, commonly known as woodland scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade to full sun. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and evergreen, providing ornamental value throughout the year.

Scilla sylvestris is valued in gardens for its early bloom and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
