Serjania seemannii

Serjania seemannii, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or yellow serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjania, which includes approximately 150 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, simple leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow or orange berries.

The tree is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
