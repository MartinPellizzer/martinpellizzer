Saxifraga clusii var. leucanthemifolia

Saxifraga clusii var. leucanthemifolia, commonly known as the white marguerite saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a low-growing habit with fleshy, woolly leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with yellow centers, resembling daisies, during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
