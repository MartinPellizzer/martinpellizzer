Solanum andigenum f. incrassatum

Solanum andigenum f. incrassatum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that ripens to a deep red or orange hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
