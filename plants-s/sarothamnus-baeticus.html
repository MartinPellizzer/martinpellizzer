Sarothamnus baeticus

Sarothamnus baeticus, commonly known as the Iberian thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a compact growth habit, which help it conserve water.

Sarothamnus baeticus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying Mediterranean flora.
