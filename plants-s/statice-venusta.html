Statice venusta

Statice venusta, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and other coastal habitats. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Statice venusta is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
