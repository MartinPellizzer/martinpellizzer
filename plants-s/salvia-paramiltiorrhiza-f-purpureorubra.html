Salvia paramiltiorrhiza f. purpureorubra

Salvia paramiltiorrhiza f. purpureorubra, commonly known as the purple-red sage, is a variant of the Chinese sage plant characterized by its striking violet to reddish-purple flower bracts.

It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in whorled clusters, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
