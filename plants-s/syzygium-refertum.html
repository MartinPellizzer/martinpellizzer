Syzygium refertum

Syzygium refertum, commonly known as the rough tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
