Solanum furcatum var. acutidentatum

Solanum furcatum var. acutidentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it produces small, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid and semi-arid regions.
