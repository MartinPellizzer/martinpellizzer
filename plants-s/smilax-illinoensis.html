Smilax illinoensis

Smilax illinoensis, commonly known as Illinois arrowhead, is a perennial vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green to reddish berries that are toxic to humans but serve as food for certain wildlife. The plant has a robust, thorny stem that can climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
