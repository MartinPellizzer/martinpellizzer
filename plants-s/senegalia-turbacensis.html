Senegalia turbacensis

Senegalia turbacensis, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its compound leaves, which are typically feathery and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing small, hard seeds. Senegalia turbacensis is adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife.
