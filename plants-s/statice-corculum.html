Statice corculum

Statice corculum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue, purple, or pink flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender stems, and it is known for its ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Statice corculum is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and vibrant color.
