Steris alpina subsp. borealis

Steris alpina subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and North America. It typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, often found in tundra and mountainous environments.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale blue. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season, blooming in late summer.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators in its harsh habitat.
