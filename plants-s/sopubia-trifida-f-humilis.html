Sopubia trifida f. humilis

Sopubia trifida f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf thimbleweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster at the end of each stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
