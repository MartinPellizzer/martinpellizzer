Sauromatum arunachalense

Sauromatum arunachalense, commonly known as the Arunachal lily, is a striking plant native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in Arunachal Pradesh.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, showy flowers with a distinctive red color and yellow throat. The plant features a robust rhizome system, which helps it thrive in the moist, temperate forest understories where it grows. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
