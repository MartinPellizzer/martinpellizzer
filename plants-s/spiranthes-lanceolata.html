Spiranthes lanceolata

Spiranthes lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf spirea, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, spiral-arranged flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

The species is known for its graceful appearance and is often found in natural habitats across the eastern United States.
