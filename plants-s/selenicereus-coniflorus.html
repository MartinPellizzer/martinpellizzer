Selenicereus coniflorus

Selenicereus coniflorus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing cactus that produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance. The plant has a segmented stem covered in spines and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
