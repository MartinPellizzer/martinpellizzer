Styphelia phyllostachys

Styphelia phyllostachys, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, typically containing several seeds. Styphelia phyllostachys thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
