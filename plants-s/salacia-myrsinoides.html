Salacia myrsinoides

Salacia myrsinoides, commonly known as the golden vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

Salacia myrsinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
