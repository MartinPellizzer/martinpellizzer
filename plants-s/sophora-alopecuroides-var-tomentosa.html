Sophora alopecuroides var. tomentosa

Sophora alopecuroides var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles, followed by elongated pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
