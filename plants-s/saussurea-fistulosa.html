Saussurea fistulosa

Saussurea fistulosa, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron or the blood grass, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, Tibet, and other mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of yellowish-orange flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Tibetan medicine for its purported health benefits. Its rhizomes and flowers are often harvested for use in herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions with minimal rainfall.
