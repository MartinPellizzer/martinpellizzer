Sorbus aucuparia lanuginosa

Sorbus aucuparia lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy rowan, is a shrub or small tree native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or boreal forest environments.
