Senecio leonardii

Senecio leonardii, commonly known as Leonard's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in the spring and summer, producing yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets.
