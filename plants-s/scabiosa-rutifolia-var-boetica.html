Scabiosa rutifolia var. boetica

Scabiosa rutifolia var. boetica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
