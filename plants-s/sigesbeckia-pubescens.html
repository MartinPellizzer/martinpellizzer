Sigesbeckia pubescens

Sigesbeckia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers that are similar in appearance to those of the dandelion, with ray florets surrounding a central disk. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
