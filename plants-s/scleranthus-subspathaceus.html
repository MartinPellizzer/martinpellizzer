Scleranthus subspathaceus

Scleranthus subspathaceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of dry, open habitats.
