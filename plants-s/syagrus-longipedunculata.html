Syagrus longipedunculata

Syagrus longipedunculata, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by small, round fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Syagrus longipedunculata is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions.
