Salvia dominica

Salvia dominica, commonly known as sacred basil or holy basil, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia dominica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various climates.
