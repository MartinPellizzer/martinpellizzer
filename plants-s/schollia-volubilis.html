Schollia volubilis

Schollia volubilis, commonly known as the creeping schollia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its stems are trailing and can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground.

Schollia volubilis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions.
