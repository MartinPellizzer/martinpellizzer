Solidago macrophylla f. pseudomensalis

Solidago macrophylla f. pseudomensalis, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purplish tint on the underside.

This form is distinguished by its larger leaves and more robust growth compared to the typical form of Solidago macrophylla.
