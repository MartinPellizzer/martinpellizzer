Senecio narynensis

Senecio narynensis, commonly known as the Naryn Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native habitat.
