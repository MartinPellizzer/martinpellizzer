Spiesia setosa

Spiesia setosa, commonly known as the silky spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

The plant is sometimes considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
