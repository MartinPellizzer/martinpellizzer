Seymouria heritieri

Seymouria heritieri, commonly known as the Seymour Island fern, is a fossil plant from the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its feather-like fronds and is considered an early relative of modern ferns. The plant's structure suggests it was a vascular plant with complex root systems. It thrived in swampy environments, contributing to the formation of coal deposits.

Its study provides insights into the evolution of early land plants and paleoecological conditions of the time.
