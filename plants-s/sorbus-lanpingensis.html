Sorbus lanpingensis

Sorbus lanpingensis, commonly known as the Lanping whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically the Yunnan Province, and is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical, and often have a finely toothed margin.

The fruit is a small, aggregate berry that matures to a dark red or black color, providing food for various wildlife species.
