Sisyrinchium distantiflorum

Sisyrinchium distantiflorum, commonly known as the slender blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies, and it thrives in well-drained soils. The species is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
