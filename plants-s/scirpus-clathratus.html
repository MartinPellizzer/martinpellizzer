Scirpus clathratus

Scirpus clathratus, commonly known as the clustered bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow water or moist soils. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces fluffy, white or brown seed heads that are often used for decorative purposes. Its roots help stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus clathratus is an important species for wildlife, providing habitat and food for various birds and insects.
