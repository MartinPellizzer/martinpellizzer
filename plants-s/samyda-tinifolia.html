Samyda tinifolia

Samyda tinifolia, commonly known as the slender senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Samyda tinifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
