Spiraea × rubella

Spiraea × rubella, commonly known as the rose spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that bloom in early summer. The flowers are typically pink or red, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has fine, dark green foliage that turns yellow in the fall.

It is well-suited for borders, hedges, and woodland gardens due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
