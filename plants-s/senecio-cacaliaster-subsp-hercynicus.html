Senecio cacaliaster subsp. hercynicus

Senecio cacaliaster subsp.

hercynicus, commonly known as the Hercynian ragwort, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and yellow flowers arranged in composite heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
