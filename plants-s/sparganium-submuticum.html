Sparganium submuticum

Sparganium submuticum, commonly known as narrow-leaved burr rush, is a wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spiky, reddish-brown inflorescences that resemble burrs. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along lake shores. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

Sparganium submuticum plays a role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing shelter for aquatic organisms.
