Silene crassifolia var. angustifolia

Silene crassifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf honesty, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. It is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other members such as carnations and pinks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
