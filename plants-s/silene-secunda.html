Silene secunda

Silene secunda, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading habit with narrow, hairy leaves. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Silene secunda plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
