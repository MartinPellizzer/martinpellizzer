Sporobolus panicoides

Sporobolus panicoides, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as dunes, prairies, and disturbed areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil, it is a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
