Symplocos punctulata

Symplocos punctulata, commonly known as the spotted evergreen, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, red berries. Symplocos punctulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
