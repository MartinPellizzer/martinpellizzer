Saxifraga fortunei var. rosea

Saxifraga fortunei var. rosea, commonly known as the pink stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in dense clusters of small, pink to rose-colored flowers. The leaves are typically succulent, with a bluish-green hue and a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
