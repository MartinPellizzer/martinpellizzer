Setaria longicauda

Setaria longicauda, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Setaria longicauda is drought-tolerant and can thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
