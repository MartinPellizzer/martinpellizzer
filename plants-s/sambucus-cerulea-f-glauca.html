Sambucus cerulea f. glauca

Sambucus cerulea f. glauca, commonly known as the blue elderberry, is a compact shrub native to North America.

It features clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible but typically used for making jams and wines. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn red in the fall.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
