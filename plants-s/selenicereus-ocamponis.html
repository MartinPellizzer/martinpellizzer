Selenicereus ocamponis

Selenicereus ocamponis, commonly known as the Ocampon's moon cactus, is a climbing cactus native to Central America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, segmented stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting pollinators such as bats and moths. Its stems are often covered in spines, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil.

Selenicereus ocamponis is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior.
