Streptocarpus hirtinervis

Streptocarpus hirtinervis, commonly known as the hairy-veined streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture on the veins, giving it its common name. This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
