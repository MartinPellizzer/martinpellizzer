Salmea scandens subsp. paraguariensis

Salmea scandens subsp.

paraguariensis, commonly known as Paraguayan climbing salmea, is a vine native to South America, particularly Paraguay and parts of Brazil. It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oblong seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
