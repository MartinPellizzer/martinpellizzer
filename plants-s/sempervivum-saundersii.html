Sempervivum saundersii

Sempervivum saundersii, commonly known as the chicken's egg plant, is a succulent species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, grass-like leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Sempervivum saundersii is also known for its ability to propagate easily through offsets, which can be separated and replanted to create new plants.
