Sorbus torminalis f. longifolia

Sorbus torminalis f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved whitebeam, is a horticultural form of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by clusters of red, berry-like fruits in the autumn. It is a slow-growing shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
