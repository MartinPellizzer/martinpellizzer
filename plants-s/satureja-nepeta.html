Satureja nepeta

Satureja nepeta, commonly known as caraway mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary applications, while the seeds are similar to those of true caraway.

Satureja nepeta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and herbal gardens.
