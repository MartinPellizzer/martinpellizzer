Saccharum ciliare

Saccharum ciliare, commonly known as the silky grass or sweet grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and fine, silky hairs along the leaf surfaces. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways due to its preference for moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Saccharum ciliare is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of natural fiber.
