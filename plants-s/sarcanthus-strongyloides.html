Sarcanthus strongyloides

Sarcanthus strongyloides, commonly known as the swamp bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest aquatic insects. The plant has slender, branching stems and is often found in shallow, nutrient-poor waters. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, with the bladders being the primary photosynthetic organs.

Sarcanthus strongyloides plays a unique role in its ecosystem by contributing to nutrient cycling in aquatic habitats.
