Saxifraga thyrsiflora

Saxifraga thyrsiflora, commonly known as the mountain saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are often basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga thyrsiflora is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
