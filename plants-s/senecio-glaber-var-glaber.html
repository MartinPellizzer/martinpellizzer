Senecio glaber var. glaber

Senecio glaber var. glaber, commonly known as the naked stem senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairless stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
