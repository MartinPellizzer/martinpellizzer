Scleranthus densifolius

Scleranthus densifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved thornweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant has slender, erect stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and densely arranged along the stem, contributing to its common name.

Scleranthus densifolius is often considered a weed but can serve as a host plant for certain insect species.
