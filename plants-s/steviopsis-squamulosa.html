Steviopsis squamulosa

Steviopsis squamulosa, commonly known as the silver leaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, scale-like hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is closely related to the well-known stevia species and shares similar chemical properties.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
