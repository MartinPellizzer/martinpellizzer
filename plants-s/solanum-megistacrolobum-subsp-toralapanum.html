Solanum megistacrolobum subsp. toralapanum

Solanum megistacrolobum subsp.

toralapanum, commonly known as the Toralapanum nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco.

The plant typically features hairy stems and ovate, pubescent leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often toxic.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations.
