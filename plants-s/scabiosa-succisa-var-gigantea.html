Scabiosa succisa var. gigantea

Scabiosa succisa var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it produces numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
