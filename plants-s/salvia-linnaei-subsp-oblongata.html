Salvia linnaei subsp. oblongata

Salvia linnaei subsp.

oblongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

It is valued in both horticulture and traditional medicine for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
