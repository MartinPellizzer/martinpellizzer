Sida pyramidata

Sida pyramidata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Sida pyramidata is an annual herb that thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields.

It is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential weed in certain environments.
