Scleria hispidula

Scleria hispidula, commonly known as the hairy bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with dense, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet with three to five flowers, each surrounded by three bracts.

Scleria hispidula thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.
