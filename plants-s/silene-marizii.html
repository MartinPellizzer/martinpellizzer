Silene marizii

Silene marizii, commonly known as the Marizii catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene marizii is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
