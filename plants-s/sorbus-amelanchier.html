Sorbus amelanchier

Sorbus amelanchier, commonly known as serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, turning from yellow to red. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in autumn.

Sorbus amelanchier is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
