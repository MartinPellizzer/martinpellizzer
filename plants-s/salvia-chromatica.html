Salvia chromatica

Salvia chromatica, commonly known as the chromatic sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long flowering period.

The species is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which emits a subtle mint-like scent.
