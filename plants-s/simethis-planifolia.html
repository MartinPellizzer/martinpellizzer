Simethis planifolia

Simethis planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaf simethis, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming during the wet season. Its roots are known to contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Simethis planifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid environment to flourish.
