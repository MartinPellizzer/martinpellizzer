Scrophularia biserrata

Scrophularia biserrata, commonly known as the serrated scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

Scrophularia biserrata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
