Senecio pomacochanus f. arachnoideus

Senecio pomacochanus f. arachnoideus, commonly known as the spider-hair senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that resembles spider webs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
