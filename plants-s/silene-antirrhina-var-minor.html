Silene antirrhina var. minor

Silene antirrhina var. minor, commonly known as the lesser catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and dry meadows. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
