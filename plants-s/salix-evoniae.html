Salix evoniae

Salix evoniae, commonly known as the Evonia willow, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and along streams.

Salix evoniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing riverbanks.
