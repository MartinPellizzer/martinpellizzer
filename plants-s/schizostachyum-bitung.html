Schizostachyum bitung

Schizostachyum bitung, commonly known as the Bitung rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, flexible stems used for making furniture and handicrafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by feather-like, compound leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to overharvesting, it is considered a threatened species in its natural habitat.
