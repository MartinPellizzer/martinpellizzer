Saccharum spontaneum var. klaga

Saccharum spontaneum var. klaga, commonly known as wild sugarcane, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth and large, blade-like leaves. This variant is known for its high sugar content in its stems, making it a valuable resource for both traditional and industrial uses. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense stands.

The plant is also notable for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
