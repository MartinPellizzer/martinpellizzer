Scrophularia capusii

Scrophularia capusii, commonly known as the greater bedstraw, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Scrophularia capusii is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
