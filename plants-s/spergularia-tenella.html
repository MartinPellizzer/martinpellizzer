Spergularia tenella

Spergularia tenella, commonly known as sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to sandy and coastal areas.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are opposite and linear, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Spergularia tenella is adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient species in arid and coastal regions.
