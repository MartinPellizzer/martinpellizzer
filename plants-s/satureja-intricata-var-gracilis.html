Satureja intricata var. gracilis

Satureja intricata var. gracilis, commonly known as slender mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and cooking for their pungent, savory flavor.

The species is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
