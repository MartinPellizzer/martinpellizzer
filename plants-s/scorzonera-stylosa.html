Scorzonera stylosa

Scorzonera stylosa, commonly known as the Italian salsify, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, fleshy root that is often harvested for its edible qualities. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits.

Scorzonera stylosa is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.
