Sinomenium acutum var. cinereum

Sinomenium acutum var. cinereum, commonly known as the grey sinomenium, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive bluish-green underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its leaves and fruits have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
