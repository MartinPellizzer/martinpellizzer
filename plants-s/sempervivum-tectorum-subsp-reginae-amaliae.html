Sempervivum tectorum subsp. reginae-amaliae

Sempervivum tectorum subsp.

reginae-amaliae, commonly known as the Queen Amalia's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Alps. It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a powdery coating.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a houseplant. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This subspecies is prized for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
