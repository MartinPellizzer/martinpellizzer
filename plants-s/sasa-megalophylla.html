Sasa megalophylla

Sasa megalophylla, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests.

Sasa megalophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and as a food source in some cultures.
