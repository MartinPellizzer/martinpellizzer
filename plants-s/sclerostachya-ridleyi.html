Sclerostachya ridleyi

Sclerostachya ridleyi, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure. Sclerostachya ridleyi typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for taxonomic and ecological studies.
