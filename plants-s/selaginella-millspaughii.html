Selaginella millspaughii

Selaginella millspaughii, commonly known as the Appalachian clubmoss, is a vascular fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a compact and dense appearance. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. It exhibits a unique ability to survive desiccation by entering a state of dormancy, which allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

Selaginella millspaughii plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
