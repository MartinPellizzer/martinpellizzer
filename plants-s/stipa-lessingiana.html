Stipa lessingiana

Stipa lessingiana, commonly known as the Lessing's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
