Salvia ringens

Salvia ringens, commonly known as the hummingbird sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
