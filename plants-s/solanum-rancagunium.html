Solanum rancagunium

Solanum rancagunium, commonly known as the Rancagunium nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its fruit is a yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated or used in agriculture.
