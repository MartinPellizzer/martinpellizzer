Senecillis

Senecillis, commonly known as the common thistle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads, which consist of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Senecillis has a taproot system and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during winter, with flowering occurring in the summer months.
