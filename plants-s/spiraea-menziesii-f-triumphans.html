Spiraea menziesii f. triumphans

Spiraea menziesii f. triumphans, commonly known as the golden spiraea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features showy, flat-topped clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender, arching stems covered in fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its foliage is dark green and turns coppery red in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
