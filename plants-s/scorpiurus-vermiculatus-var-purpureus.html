Scorpiurus vermiculatus var. purpureus

Scorpiurus vermiculatus var. purpureus, commonly known as purple false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and silver-white veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
