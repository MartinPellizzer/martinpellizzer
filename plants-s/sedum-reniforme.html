Sedum reniforme

Sedum reniforme, commonly known as the "Roundleaf Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, rounded leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, depending on light exposure. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum reniforme is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.
