Syzygium jugorum

Syzygium jugorum, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Syzygium jugorum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
