Scutellaria parnassica

Scutellaria parnassica, commonly known as the Parnassus skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically range in color from purple to blue. The plant grows in rocky or alpine habitats and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Scutellaria parnassica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
