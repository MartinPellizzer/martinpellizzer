Solanum paniculatum var. chulumani

Solanum paniculatum var. chulumani, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves. It produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or for traditional medicinal uses.
