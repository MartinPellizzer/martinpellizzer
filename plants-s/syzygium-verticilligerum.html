Syzygium verticilligerum

Syzygium verticilligerum, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Syzygium verticilligerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
