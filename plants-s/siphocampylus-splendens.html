Siphocampylus splendens

Siphocampylus splendens, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting dark center. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Siphocampylus splendens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
