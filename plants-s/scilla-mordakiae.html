Scilla mordakiae

Scilla mordakiae, commonly known as the Mordakian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

Scilla mordakiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
