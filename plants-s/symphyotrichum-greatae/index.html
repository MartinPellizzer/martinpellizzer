Symphyotrichum Greatae

Symphyotrichum greatae is a rare and intriguing species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. This member of the sunflower family, Asteraceae, is known for its distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grassy areas, often found in the Carolinas and Georgia. Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and faces threats from development and habitat loss. Conservation efforts are underway to protect and preserve this unique botanical treasure.
