Sphaerocarya edulis

Sphaerocarya edulis, commonly known as the edible globe fruit, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, deciduous tree that produces round, fleshy fruits with a thick, leathery rind. The fruit's interior contains a single large, starchy seed surrounded by a sweet, edible pulp. The plant is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content, making it a traditional food in local cuisines.

Its botanical characteristics include compound leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters.
