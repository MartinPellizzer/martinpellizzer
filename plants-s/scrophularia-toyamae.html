Scrophularia toyamae

Scrophularia toyamae, commonly known as the Japanese figwort, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Scrophularia toyamae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
