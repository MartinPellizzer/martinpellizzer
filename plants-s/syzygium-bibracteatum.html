Syzygium bibracteatum

Syzygium bibracteatum, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody pods containing edible, sweet-tart seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
