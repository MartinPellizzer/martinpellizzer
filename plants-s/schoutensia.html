Schoutensia

Schoutensia, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and a slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near the forest floor. Schoutensia has small, green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their subtle fragrance, attracting various pollinators.
