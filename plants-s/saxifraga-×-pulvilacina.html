Saxifraga × pulvilacina

Saxifraga × pulvilacina, commonly known as the rockfoam, is a hybrid plant species within the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga × pulvilacina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny soils.
