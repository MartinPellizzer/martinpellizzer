Sporobolus longifolius

Sporobolus longifolius, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading growth habit and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and restoration settings.
