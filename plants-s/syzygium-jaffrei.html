Syzygium jaffrei

Syzygium jaffrei, commonly known as the Jaffrei apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a crisp, juicy pulp. Syzygium jaffrei is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
