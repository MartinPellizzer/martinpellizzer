Syringa wulingensis

Syringa wulingensis, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Syringa wulingensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
