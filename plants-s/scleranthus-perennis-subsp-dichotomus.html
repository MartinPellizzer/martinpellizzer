Scleranthus perennis subsp. dichotomus

Scleranthus perennis subsp.

dichotomus, commonly known as the forked sicklegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

The plant has slender, erect stems that often branch near the base, giving it a distinctive forked appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and the leaves are linear with a rough, hairy texture.

It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
