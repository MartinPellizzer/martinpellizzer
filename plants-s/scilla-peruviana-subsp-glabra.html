Scilla peruviana subsp. glabra

Scilla peruviana subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the Peruvian squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South America. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

The subspecies glabra is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous leaves compared to other varieties.
