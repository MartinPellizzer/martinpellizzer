Schoenus halconensis

Schoenus halconensis, commonly known as the Halconensis sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Schoenus halconensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
