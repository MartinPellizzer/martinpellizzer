Sabina przewalskii f. pendula

Sabina przewalskii f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese juniper, is a unique cultivar of the Chinese juniper.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. It is native to the mountainous regions of western China and is valued for its hardiness and adaptability.

This evergreen shrub or small tree is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in various soil conditions.
