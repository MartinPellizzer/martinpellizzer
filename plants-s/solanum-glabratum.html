Solanum glabratum

Solanum glabratum, commonly known as the smooth nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, glandular leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Solanum glabratum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other toxic and economically important plants.
