Sasa mirabunda

Sasa mirabunda, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense, upright clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Sasa mirabunda is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
