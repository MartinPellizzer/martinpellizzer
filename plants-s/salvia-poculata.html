Salvia poculata

Salvia poculata, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia poculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
