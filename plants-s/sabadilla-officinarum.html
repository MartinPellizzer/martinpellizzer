Sabadilla officinarum

Sabadilla officinarum, commonly known as the Spanish fly or sabadilla, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or greenish flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It is also cultivated for its alkaloids, which have been historically used in medicinal and contraceptive applications.

However, due to its toxic properties, handling and consumption require caution.
