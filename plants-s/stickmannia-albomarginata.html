Stickmannia albomarginata

Stickmannia albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin stickman plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a prominent white margin, which gives it its common name. It produces clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stickmannia albomarginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
