Silene sclerophylloides

Silene sclerophylloides, commonly known as the hard-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid conditions, showcasing a thick cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Silene sclerophylloides is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent-like foliage.
