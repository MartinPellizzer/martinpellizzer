Statice sebkarum f. glomerata

Statice sebkarum f. glomerata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is native to coastal regions and thrives in salty, well-drained soils. Its salt tolerance makes it a valuable species for coastal landscaping and erosion control.

The foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with the flowers appearing in late summer to early autumn.
