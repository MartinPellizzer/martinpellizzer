Salix limosa var. elliptica

Salix limosa var. elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches wide. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, in the spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing stream banks and providing habitat for wildlife.
