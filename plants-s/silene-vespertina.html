Silene vespertina

Silene vespertina, commonly known as evening pink, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene vespertina is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of dry, open habitats.
