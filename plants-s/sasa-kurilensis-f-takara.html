Sasa kurilensis f. takara

Sasa kurilensis f. takara, commonly known as the Takara sasa, is a clump-forming bamboo species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often covered with a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, triangular leaves that are typically a deep green color with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
