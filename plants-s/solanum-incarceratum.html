Solanum incarceratum

Solanum incarceratum, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant typically grows as a vine, featuring heart-shaped leaves and producing small, berry-like fruits that are edible when ripe. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, showy blossoms, and a preference for warm, humid climates.

Solanum incarceratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
