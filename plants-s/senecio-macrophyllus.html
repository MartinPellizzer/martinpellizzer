Senecio macrophyllus

Senecio macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant senecio, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, blue-green leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its size and striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
