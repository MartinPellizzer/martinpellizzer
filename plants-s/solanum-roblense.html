Solanum roblense

Solanum roblense, commonly known as the Robles nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a hairy texture.

Solanum roblense belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other toxic and economically important plants.
