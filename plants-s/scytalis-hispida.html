Scytalis hispida

Scytalis hispida, commonly known as the rough scytalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that are often branched and covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

Scytalis hispida is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
