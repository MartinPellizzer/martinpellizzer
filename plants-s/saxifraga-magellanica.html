Saxifraga magellanica

Saxifraga magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly the southern regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, with five petals. Its leaves are often succulent and adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions.

Saxifraga magellanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
