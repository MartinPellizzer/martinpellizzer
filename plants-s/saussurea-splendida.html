Saussurea splendida

Saussurea splendida, commonly known as the splendid yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a unique, aromatic compound known as saussurea, which is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is a subject of interest in botanical and pharmacological research.
