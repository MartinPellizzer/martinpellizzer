Scirpus molestus

Scirpus molestus, commonly known as the prickly sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and narrow, pointed leaves that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions.

Scirpus molestus is known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soil and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
