Solanum delicatulum

Solanum delicatulum, commonly known as the delicate nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by berries that mature to a deep purple color. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Solanum delicatulum is part of the nightshade family, which includes both edible and toxic species.
