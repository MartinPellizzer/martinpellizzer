Salacia baumii

Salacia baumii, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
