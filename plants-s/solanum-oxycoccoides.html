Solanum oxycoccoides

Solanum oxycoccoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange, are edible but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.

Solanum oxycoccoides is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
