Syzygium debruijnii

Syzygium debruijnii, commonly known as the Java apple or white cempedak, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a unique fruit with a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Syzygium debruijnii is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
