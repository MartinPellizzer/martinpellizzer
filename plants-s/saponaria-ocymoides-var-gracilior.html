Saponaria ocymoides var. gracilior

Saponaria ocymoides var. gracilior, commonly known as slender soaproot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces cylindrical, fleshy roots that contain saponins, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact size and delicate flowers make it an attractive addition to gardens with limited space.
