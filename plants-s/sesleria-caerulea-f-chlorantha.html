Sesleria caerulea f. chlorantha

Sesleria caerulea f. chlorantha, commonly known as the blue carpet grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers in late summer, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice in horticultural settings.
