Salvia trifilis

Salvia trifilis, commonly known as three-flowered sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, often emitting a mild mint-like fragrance.

Salvia trifilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
