Solenostemon ocymoides var. monostachyus

Solenostemon ocymoides var. monostachyus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved feverfew, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
