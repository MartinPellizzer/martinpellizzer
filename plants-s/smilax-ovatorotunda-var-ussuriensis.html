Smilax ovatorotunda var. ussuriensis

Smilax ovatorotunda var. ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri spurgevine, is a perennial climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features a robust, woody stem covered in spines and has heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
