Setaria plicatilis

Setaria plicatilis, commonly known as beakgrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and disturbed soils. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Setaria plicatilis is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
