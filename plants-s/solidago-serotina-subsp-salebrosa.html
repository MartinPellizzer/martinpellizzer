Solidago serotina subsp. salebrosa

Solidago serotina subsp.

salebrosa, commonly known as late goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in panicles.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
