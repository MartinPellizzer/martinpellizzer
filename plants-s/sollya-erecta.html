Sollya erecta

Sollya erecta, commonly known as the upright sollya, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sollya erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
