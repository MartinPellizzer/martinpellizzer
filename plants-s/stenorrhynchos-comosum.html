Stenorrhynchos comosum

Stenorrhynchos comosum, commonly known as the woolly bulbous orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, woolly appearance, with hairy stems and leaves that give it a distinctive look. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
