Sporobolus maderaspatanus

Sporobolus maderaspatanus, commonly known as the Madras grass, is a perennial grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Sporobolus maderaspatanus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
