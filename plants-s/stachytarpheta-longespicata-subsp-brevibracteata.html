Stachytarpheta longespicata subsp. brevibracteata

Stachytarpheta longespicata subsp.

brevibracteata, commonly known as the short-bracted blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America. It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
