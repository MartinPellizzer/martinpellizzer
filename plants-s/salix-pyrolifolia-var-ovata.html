Salix pyrolifolia var. ovata

Salix pyrolifolia var. ovata, commonly known as the ovate willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and narrow, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silvery hair. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
