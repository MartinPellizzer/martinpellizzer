Solanum tuberosum f. isla-imilla

Solanum tuberosum f. isla-imilla, commonly known as the Isla Imilla potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato characterized by its unique tuber morphology.

This variety exhibits a distinctive shape, often with a rounded or irregular form, and displays a range of skin colors, including purple, red, and white. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils. It is valued for its potential culinary uses and is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal as well as its edible tubers.

The f. isla-imilla form is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
