Saussurea mongolica

Saussurea mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian safflower, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Mongolia and northern China.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The roots of Saussurea mongolica are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
