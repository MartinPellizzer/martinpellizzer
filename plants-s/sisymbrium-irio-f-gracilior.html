Sisymbrium irio f. gracilior

Sisymbrium irio f. gracilior, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers and lacks chlorophyll, making it a parasitic plant that relies on host plants for nutrients. The plant has a slender, branching stem with small, scale-like leaves. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often found growing on the roots of other plants.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
