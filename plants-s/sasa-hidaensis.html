Sasa hidaensis

Sasa hidaensis, commonly known as the Hida bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly sweet aroma.

Sasa hidaensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
