Senegalia amazonica

Senegalia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments, and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is adapted to wet tropical climates and plays a role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
