Sasa japonica f. hakonensis

Sasa japonica f. hakonensis, commonly known as the Hakone bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming bamboo species native to Japan.

It features slender, dark green culms that are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its graceful appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
