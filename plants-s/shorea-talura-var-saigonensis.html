Shorea talura var. saigonensis

Shorea talura var. saigonensis, commonly known as the Saigon yellow-wood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its durable, heavy timber with a golden-yellow heartwood. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

The plant is valued for its wood in construction and furniture-making due to its strength and resistance to decay.
