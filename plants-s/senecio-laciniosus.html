Senecio laciniosus

Senecio laciniosus, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Senecio laciniosus is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
