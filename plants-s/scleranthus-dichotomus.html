Scleranthus dichotomus

Scleranthus dichotomus, commonly known as the two-branched sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scleranthus dichotomus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
