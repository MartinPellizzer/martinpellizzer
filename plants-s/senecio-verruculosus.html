Senecio verruculosus

Senecio verruculosus, commonly known as the wooly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
