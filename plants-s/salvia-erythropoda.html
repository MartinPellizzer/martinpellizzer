Salvia erythropoda

Salvia erythropoda, commonly known as the red sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red or violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia erythropoda is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
