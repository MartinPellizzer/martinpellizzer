Sedum talichiense

Sedum talichiense, commonly known as the Talich's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is appreciated by horticulturists and gardeners in dry climates.
