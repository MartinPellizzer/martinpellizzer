Silene graminifolia lusus multiflora

Silene graminifolia lusus multiflora, commonly known as the grass-like catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous structure with slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
