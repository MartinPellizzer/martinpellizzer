Sisymbrium hybridum

Sisymbrium hybridum, commonly known as wild radish, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a taproot system with a basal rosette of leaves that are usually lobed or deeply divided. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release seeds.

Sisymbrium hybridum is often found in disturbed soils and can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
