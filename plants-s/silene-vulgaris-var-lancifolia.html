Silene vulgaris var. lancifolia

Silene vulgaris var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in open habitats with poor to moderate soil.

The species is known for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
