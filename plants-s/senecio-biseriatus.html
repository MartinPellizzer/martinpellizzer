Senecio biseriatus

Senecio biseriatus, commonly known as the two-rowed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged in two rows along the stem. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
