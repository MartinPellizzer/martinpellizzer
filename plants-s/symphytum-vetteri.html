Symphytum vetteri

Symphytum vetteri, commonly known as the Vetter's comfrey, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces violet-blue flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its healing abilities.
