Solanum simile var. capsiciforme

Solanum simile var. capsiciforme, commonly known as the "cape gooseberry," is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It produces small, orange to red berries that are edible and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The berries are rich in vitamins and have been used in traditional medicine.
