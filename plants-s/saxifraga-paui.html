Saxifraga paui

Saxifraga paui, commonly known as the Pau's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a member of the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to retain water in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga paui is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
