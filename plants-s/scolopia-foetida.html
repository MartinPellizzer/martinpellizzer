Scolopia foetida

Scolopia foetida, commonly known as stinking nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a rough texture, and it has a strong, unpleasant odor.

Scolopia foetida is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
