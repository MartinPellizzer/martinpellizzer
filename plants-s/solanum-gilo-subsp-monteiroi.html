Solanum gilo subsp. monteiroi

Solanum gilo subsp.

monteiroi, commonly known as the Andean cherry or yellow eggplant, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America. It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellow or orange berries.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its fruit is edible and has a tangy, slightly acidic flavor, making it a traditional food in some local communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
