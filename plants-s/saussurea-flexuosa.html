Saussurea flexuosa

Saussurea flexuosa, commonly known as the Himalayan swamp safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of yellow to orange flower heads. The plant has fibrous roots and is adapted to cold, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitat, supporting local biodiversity.
