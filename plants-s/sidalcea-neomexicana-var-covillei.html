Sidalcea neomexicana var. covillei

Sidalcea neomexicana var. covillei, commonly known as the Coville's checker-mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
