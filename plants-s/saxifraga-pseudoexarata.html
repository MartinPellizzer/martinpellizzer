Saxifraga pseudoexarata

Saxifraga pseudoexarata, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a flowering plant species native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve moisture in harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and usually pink or white, arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem.

Saxifraga pseudoexarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
