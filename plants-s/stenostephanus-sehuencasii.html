Stenostephanus sehuencasii

Stenostephanus sehuencasii, commonly known as the Huayna Picchu cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andean region of Peru.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny leaves, adapted to the dry and rocky environments of its habitat. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are pollinated by insects. It thrives in high-altitude areas with limited rainfall and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
