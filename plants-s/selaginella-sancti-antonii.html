Selaginella sancti-antonii

Selaginella sancti-antonii, commonly known as the sacred spikerush, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its spire-like arrangement of leaves.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique ability to survive extreme environmental conditions, including desiccation. Native to arid regions of South America, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils with minimal water availability. The plant's small, scale-like leaves are arranged in dense, spiral clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
