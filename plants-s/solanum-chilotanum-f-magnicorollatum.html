Solanum chilotanum f. magnicorollatum

Solanum chilotanum f. magnicorollatum, commonly known as the large corolla nightshade, is a unique botanical variant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers, and is characterized by its striking floral features. The plant is distinguished by its large, showy corolla that gives it its common name, making it a notable ornamental species. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments and has a woody structure with hairy leaves.

Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, yellow berries that are edible but not commonly cultivated.
