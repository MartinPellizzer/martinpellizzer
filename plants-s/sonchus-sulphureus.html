Sonchus sulphureus

Sonchus sulphureus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Sonchus sulphureus is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
