Solanum paaschenianum

Solanum paaschenianum, commonly known as the Paaschen's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, often yellow or white flowers. Its fruits are berries that can be toxic, containing alkaloids similar to those found in other members of the Solanum genus.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.
