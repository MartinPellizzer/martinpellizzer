Strigosella africana var. laxa

Strigosella africana var. laxa, commonly known as the African strigella, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with a warm climate.
