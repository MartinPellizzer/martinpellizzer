Saussurea ninae

Saussurea ninae, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
