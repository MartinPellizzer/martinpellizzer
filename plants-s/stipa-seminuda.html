Stipa seminuda

Stipa seminuda, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.

Stipa seminuda is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resilience to drought conditions.
