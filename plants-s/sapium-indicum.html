Sapium indicum

Sapium indicum, commonly known as Indian horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces woody, spiny capsules that contain toxic seeds, which are not suitable for human consumption. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
