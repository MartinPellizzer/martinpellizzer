Sorghum fasciculare

Sorghum fasciculare, commonly known as the purpletop sorghum, is a tall, warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are typically green or purple in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a landscape plant or used in erosion control.
