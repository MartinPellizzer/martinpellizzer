Samyda spinulosa

Samyda spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny samyda, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive spiny leaves, which provide a protective mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, tropical environments. Samyda spinulosa has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
