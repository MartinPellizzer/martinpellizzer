Sanguisorba alveolosa

Sanguisorba alveolosa, commonly known as the alveolate teasel, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, often with a hairy texture.

Sanguisorba alveolosa is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
