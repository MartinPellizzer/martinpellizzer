Semecarpus nicobarensis

Semecarpus nicobarensis, commonly known as the Nicobar satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Nicobar Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are alternate and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are a source of food for various wildlife.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and furniture making.
