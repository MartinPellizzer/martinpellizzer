Steptorhamphus persicus

Steptorhamphus persicus, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features spiny leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Steptorhamphus persicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
