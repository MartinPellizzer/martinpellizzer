Stelis aggregata

Stelis aggregata, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Stelis aggregata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
