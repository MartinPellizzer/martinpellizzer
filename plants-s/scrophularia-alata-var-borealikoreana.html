Scrophularia alata var. borealikoreana

Scrophularia alata var. borealikoreana, commonly known as the Boreal Korean dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are tubular, often tinged with purple or pink hues. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Scrophulariaceae family, sharing similar botanical traits and ecological preferences.
