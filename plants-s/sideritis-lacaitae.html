Sideritis lacaitae

Sideritis lacaitae, commonly known as the Balkan germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Sideritis lacaitae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
