Sorbus budaiana

Sorbus budaiana, commonly known as the Buda whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rounded crown and smooth, grey bark. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by orange-red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are elliptical, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall.

It is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
