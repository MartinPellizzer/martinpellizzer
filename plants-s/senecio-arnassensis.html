Senecio arnassensis

Senecio arnassensis, commonly known as the Arnold's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture.

Senecio arnassensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
