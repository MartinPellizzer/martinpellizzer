Silene armeria var. angustifolia

Silene armeria var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
