Solanum kriegeri

Solanum kriegeri, commonly known as the Krieger's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant bears small, yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe but contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Solanum kriegeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
