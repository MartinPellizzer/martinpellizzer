Salix × wardiana

Salix × wardiana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season. It thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Salix × wardiana is also known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
