Sicyos hillebrandii var. anunu

Sicyos hillebrandii var. anunu, commonly known as the Anunu giant watermelon, is a unique variant of the giant watermelon species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

This plant is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that can weigh several kilograms, with thick, fibrous rinds and sweet, juicy flesh. Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, sharing traits with other gourd plants. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmate leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into its distinctive fruit.

It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
