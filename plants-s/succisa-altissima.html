Succisa altissima

Succisa altissima, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue.

Succisa altissima thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
