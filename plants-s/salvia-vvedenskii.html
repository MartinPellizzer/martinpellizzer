Salvia vvedenskii

Salvia vvedenskii, commonly known as the Russian sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Salvia vvedenskii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
