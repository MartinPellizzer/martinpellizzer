Silene sarawschanica

Silene sarawschanica, commonly known as the Sarawak silene, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene sarawschanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
