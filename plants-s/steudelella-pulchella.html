Steudelella pulchella

Steudelella pulchella, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. The plant features a slender rhizome with a few leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred labellum, which is a characteristic feature of many orchid species.

Steudelella pulchella is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
