Sorindeia nitida

Sorindeia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf sorindeia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, gray-green leaves that are covered in a silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by elliptical, red-brown fruits. Sorindeia nitida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and habitat preferences make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
