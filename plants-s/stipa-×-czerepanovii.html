Stipa × czerepanovii

Stipa × czerepanovii, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is a hybrid between Stipa capillata and Stipa pennata, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces feathery inflorescences that are often used for ornamental purposes.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
