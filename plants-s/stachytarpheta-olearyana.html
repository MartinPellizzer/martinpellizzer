Stachytarpheta olearyana

Stachytarpheta olearyana, commonly known as the purpletop verbena, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flower clusters that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Stachytarpheta olearyana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
