Sisyrinchium magnicapsulare

Sisyrinchium magnicapsulare, commonly known as the large-capsule blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds, typical of the Iridaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
