Serratula macrophylla

Serratula macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved sow thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in disturbed soils and grasslands. Serratula macrophylla is known for its medicinal properties, though it can be toxic in high doses.
