Senecio spicatus

Senecio spicatus, commonly known as the spikecentric dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces slender, spiky flower stalks.

Senecio spicatus is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
