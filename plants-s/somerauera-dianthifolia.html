Somerauera dianthifolia

Somerauera dianthifolia, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
