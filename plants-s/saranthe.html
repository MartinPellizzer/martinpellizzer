Saranthe

Saranthe, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the genus Nelumbo in the family Nymphaeaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, buoyant stalks. The plant's leaves are broad and circular, often floating on the surface of still waters. Saranthe has been revered in various cultures for its symbolic and medicinal properties.

Its flowers typically open in the morning and close at night, a behavior known as nyctinasty.
