Salvia trichostephana

Salvia trichostephana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Salvia trichostephana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
