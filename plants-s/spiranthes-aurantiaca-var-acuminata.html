Spiranthes aurantiaca var. acuminata

Spiranthes aurantiaca var. acuminata, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers are usually pink to orange in color, and it produces a distinctive, grass-like foliage.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
