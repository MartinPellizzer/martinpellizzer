Solanum nigrum var. chlorocarpum

Solanum nigrum var. chlorocarpum, commonly known as black nightshade variant with green fruit, is a variant of the common black nightshade plant.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow with five petals and are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves.
