Salix × felina

Salix × felina, commonly known as the cat willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
