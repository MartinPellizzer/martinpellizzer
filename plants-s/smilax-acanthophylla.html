Smilax acanthophylla

Smilax acanthophylla, commonly known as the scarlet smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy, glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.

Smilax acanthophylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
