Serapias grandiflora

Serapias grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The flowers are typically arranged in a loose raceme and have a distinctive, spurred structure typical of the orchid family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
