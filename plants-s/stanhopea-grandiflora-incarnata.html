Stanhopea grandiflora incarnata

Stanhopea grandiflora incarnata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow blooms with a distinctive, spicy scent. It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique floral structure and aromatic qualities make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
