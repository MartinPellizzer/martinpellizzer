Sorbus torminalis f. genuina

Sorbus torminalis f. genuina, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn.

The species is known for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
