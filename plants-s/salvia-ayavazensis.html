Salvia ayavazensis

Salvia ayavazensis, commonly known as Ayavaz sage, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

Salvia ayavazensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited to rocky or arid environments.
