Schizostachyum curranii

Schizostachyum curranii, commonly known as the Curran's bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional construction and crafts. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable resource and a potential invasive species in certain regions.

Schizostachyum curranii is also noted for its durability and flexibility, which contribute to its wide range of applications.
