Senecio pseudaureus

Senecio pseudaureus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing a compact growth habit. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in dense, daisy-like heads. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

Senecio pseudaureus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
