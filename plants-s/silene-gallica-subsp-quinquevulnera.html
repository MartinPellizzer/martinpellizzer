Silene gallica subsp. quinquevulnera

Silene gallica subsp.

quinquevulnera, commonly known as the five-wound campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The subspecies is distinguished by its five prominent veins on the leaves, giving it its common name.
