Stomatium pluridens

Stomatium pluridens, commonly known as the multidentate mouthplant, is a unique species with distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the family Stomaticeae and is characterized by its intricate, tooth-like structures along its leaf margins. The plant exhibits a complex floral morphology, with multiple stamens and a specialized gynoecium that aids in pollination. Its leaves are often arranged in a spiral pattern, enhancing light absorption.

Stomatium pluridens thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common inhabitant of tropical rainforests.
