Senecillis kanaitzensis

Senecillis kanaitzensis, commonly known as the Kanaitz Mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow ray flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
