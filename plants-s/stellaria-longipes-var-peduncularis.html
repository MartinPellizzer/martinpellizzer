Stellaria longipes var. peduncularis

Stellaria longipes var. peduncularis, commonly known as the slender starwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its stems are slender and often branching, with small, oval leaves arranged oppositely.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated peduncles, which support the flowering heads.
