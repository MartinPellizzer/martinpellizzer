Syzygium heyneanum var. alternans

Syzygium heyneanum var. alternans, commonly known as the variable syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its alternate leaf arrangement, which gives it its specific variety name. Its leaves are typically elliptical or lanceolate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are borne in clusters, often accompanied by a fragrant scent.
