Scilla rautanenii

Scilla rautanenii, commonly known as the Armenian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in mountainous areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Scilla rautanenii is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
