Symplocos pentagyna

Symplocos pentagyna, commonly known as the five-angled evergreen or Indian hawthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. Symplocos pentagyna thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dense foliage and attractive fruit.
