Scleria mitis var. atrobrunnea

Scleria mitis var. atrobrunnea, commonly known as the black-brown scurfpea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces distinctive, dark brown, waxy-coated seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dune environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
