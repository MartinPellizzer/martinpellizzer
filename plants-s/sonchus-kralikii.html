Sonchus kralikii

Sonchus kralikii, commonly known as Kralik's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It features lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
