Scilla anthericoides

Scilla anthericoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

Scilla anthericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
