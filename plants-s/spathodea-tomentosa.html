Spathodea tomentosa

Spathodea tomentosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving the plant its scientific name "tomentosa.". The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice in tropical and subtropical regions.
