Syzygium cordatum

Syzygium cordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sour fruits resembling small, yellowish pods. Syzygium cordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cuisines and medicinal practices.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.
