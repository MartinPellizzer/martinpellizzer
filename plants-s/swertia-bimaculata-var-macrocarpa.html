Swertia bimaculata var. macrocarpa

Swertia bimaculata var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited swertia, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayas and is characterized by its upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.
