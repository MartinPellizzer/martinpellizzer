Senna mexicana var. latifolia

Senna mexicana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf senna, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that contain several black, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
