Swartzia flaemingii var. arumateuana

Swartzia flaemingii var. arumateuana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and attractive, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The species is valued for its timber, which is highly prized in musical instrument making due to its tonal qualities.
