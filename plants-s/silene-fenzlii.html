Silene fenzlii

Silene fenzlii, commonly known as the Fenzli's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Silene fenzlii is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
