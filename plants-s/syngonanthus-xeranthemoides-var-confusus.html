Syngonanthus xeranthemoides var. confusus

Syngonanthus xeranthemoides var. confusus, commonly known as the fuzzy sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
