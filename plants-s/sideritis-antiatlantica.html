Sideritis antiatlantica

Sideritis antiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Spain and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas.

Sideritis antiatlantica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
