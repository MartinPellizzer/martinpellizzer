Soranthe montana

Soranthe montana, commonly known as the mountain soranthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. It is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

The botanical characteristics of Soranthe montana make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
