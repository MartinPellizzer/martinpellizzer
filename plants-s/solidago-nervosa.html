Solidago nervosa

Solidago nervosa, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
