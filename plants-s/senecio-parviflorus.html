Senecio parviflorus

Senecio parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
