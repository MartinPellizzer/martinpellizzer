Sideroxylon reclinatum subsp. rufotomentosum

Sideroxylon reclinatum subsp.

rufotomentosum, commonly known as the red-tomentose ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree characterized by its dense, woody structure and thick, hairy foliage.

The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture and a distinctive reddish or brownish pubescence. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional crafts and construction.
