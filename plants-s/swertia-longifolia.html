Swertia longifolia

Swertia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Swertia longifolia is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
