Senecio longipes var. dentatus

Senecio longipes var. dentatus, commonly known as the serrated bishop's cap, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. It is often found in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
