Schrenkia vaginata

Schrenkia vaginata, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Schrenkia vaginata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
