Serratula monticola

Serratula monticola, commonly known as the mountain sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a rough, serrated edge. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are often hairy and covered in fine white hairs.

Serratula monticola is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
