Santolina coronopifolia

Santolina coronopifolia, commonly known as Spanish santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with a compact, mounded growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flower clusters in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.
