Salsola smithii

Salsola smithii, commonly known as Smith's saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, salt-tolerant berries. Salsola smithii is often found in sandy soils and is a key component of desert ecosystems.

Its ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it an important plant for soil stabilization and wildlife habitat.
