Sunania filiformis f. trichorachis

Sunania filiformis f. trichorachis, commonly known as the hairy sunania, is a rare and distinctive variant of the sunania genus.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a unique appearance compared to the typical form. This plant is native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its botanical features include slender, filiform stems and lanceolate leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its morphological variation and ecological adaptations.
