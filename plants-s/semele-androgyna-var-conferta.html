Semele androgyna var. conferta

Semele androgyna var. conferta, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.

The varietal name "conferta" refers to the closely clustered inflorescences, a distinguishing feature of this variant.
