Selaginella cuspidata var. emmeliana

Selaginella cuspidata var. emmeliana, commonly known as the Emmelian spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing vascular plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its diminutive, needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around its stems. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobila that contain spores, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
