Sida quinquenervia

Sida quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with five distinct veins, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the warmer months.

Sida quinquenervia is known for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
