Senecio ambraceus var. glaber

Senecio ambraceus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are generally smooth and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
