Serrafalcus arenarius

Serrafalcus arenarius, commonly known as the sand lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate salt spray.
