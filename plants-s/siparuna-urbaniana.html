Siparuna urbaniana

Siparuna urbaniana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant and showy appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist lowland forests.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
