Stevia triflora

Stevia triflora, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for both ornamental and medicinal gardening.

Stevia triflora has been traditionally used for its sweet taste and potential health benefits.
