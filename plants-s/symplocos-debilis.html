Symplocos debilis

Symplocos debilis, commonly known as the weakleaf waxberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are often fragrant. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.
