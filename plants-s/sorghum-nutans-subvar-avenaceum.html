Sorghum nutans subvar. avenaceum

Sorghum nutans subvar. avenaceum, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short stature, typically reaching heights of less than 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish bracts. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a prominent midrib.

This subspecies is often cultivated for forage and has potential uses in biofuel production due to its high biomass yield.
