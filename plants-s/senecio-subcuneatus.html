Senecio subcuneatus

Senecio subcuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Senecio subcuneatus is adapted to arid conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.
