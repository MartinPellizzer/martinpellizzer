Simaba glabra subsp. trijuga

Simaba glabra subsp.

trijuga, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraria trijuga*, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
