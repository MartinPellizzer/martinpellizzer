Scoparia montevidensis

Scoparia montevidensis, commonly known as the Uruguay thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs.

Scoparia montevidensis is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
