Salvia pterocalyx

Salvia pterocalyx, commonly known as the mountain sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America and South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia pterocalyx is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
