Setaria modesta

Setaria modesta, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a species of grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing, perennial plant that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Setaria modesta is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
