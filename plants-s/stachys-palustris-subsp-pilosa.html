Stachys palustris subsp. pilosa

Stachys palustris subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as swamp betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It features hairy, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
