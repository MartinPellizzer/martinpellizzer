Senecio asclepiadeus

Senecio asclepiadeus, commonly known as the milkweed plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which can be toxic to some animals and humans. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Senecio asclepiadeus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
