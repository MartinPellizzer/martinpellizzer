Salsola pinnatifida

Salsola pinnatifida, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a sprawling growth habit, and is often found in dry, arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and alternate, with a rough texture that helps reduce water loss.

Salsola pinnatifida is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
