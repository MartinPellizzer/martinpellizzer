Solanum vernei subsp. ballsii

Solanum vernei subsp.

ballsii, commonly known as the Ball's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often lobed or serrated.

The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its berries are initially green and turn red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.
