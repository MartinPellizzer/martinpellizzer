Senecio eriospermus var. crambefolius

Senecio eriospermus var. crambefolius, commonly known as the woollyleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has a low-growing habit with multiple stems and is known for its ability to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
