Salacia amplifolia

Salacia amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf salacia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and often heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in primary rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
