Stipa czekanovskii

Stipa czekanovskii, commonly known as the Czekanovsky's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers, typically in late summer. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Stipa czekanovskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
