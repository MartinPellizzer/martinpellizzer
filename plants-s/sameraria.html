Sameraria

Sameraria, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Sameraria thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its roots are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
