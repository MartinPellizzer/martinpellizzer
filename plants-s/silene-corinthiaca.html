Silene corinthiaca

Silene corinthiaca, commonly known as the Corinthian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

Silene corinthiaca is an annual or biennial plant, depending on the climate, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
