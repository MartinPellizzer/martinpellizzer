Solenopsis dregeana

Solenopsis dregeana, commonly known as the velvet tamarisk, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety coating, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, appearing in clusters along the stems.

Solenopsis dregeana is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
