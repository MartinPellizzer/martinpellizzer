Senecio tauricus

Senecio tauricus, commonly known as the Tauric groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy inflorescence.

Senecio tauricus is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
