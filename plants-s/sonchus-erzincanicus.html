Sonchus erzincanicus

Sonchus erzincanicus, commonly known as the Erzincan sowthistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Turkey and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a potential weed in agricultural areas.
