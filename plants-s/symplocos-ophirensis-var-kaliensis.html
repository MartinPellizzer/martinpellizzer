Symplocos ophirensis var. kaliensis

Symplocos ophirensis var. kaliensis, commonly known as the Kaliensis wax tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, usually red or orange when mature, and contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
