Siphocampylus cernuus var. nipensis

Siphocampylus cernuus var. nipensis, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features slender, hairy stems that often trail or climb, with lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
