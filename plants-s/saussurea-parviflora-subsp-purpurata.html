Saussurea parviflora subsp. purpurata

Saussurea parviflora subsp.

purpurata, commonly known as the purple flowered saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and features dense, woolly foliage.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, with some varieties displaying a subtle purplish tinge. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
