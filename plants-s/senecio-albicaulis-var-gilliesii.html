Senecio albicaulis var. gilliesii

Senecio albicaulis var. gilliesii, commonly known as the white-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
