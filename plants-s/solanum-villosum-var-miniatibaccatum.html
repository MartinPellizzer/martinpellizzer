Solanum villosum var. miniatibaccatum

Solanum villosum var. miniatibaccatum, commonly known as the miniature black nightshade, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of tiny, black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other toxic and economically important species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
