Scolymus clusii

Scolymus clusii, commonly known as the Egyptian salsify, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, inflated fruits. Its root system is fibrous, and the roots are often harvested for culinary use.

Scolymus clusii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
