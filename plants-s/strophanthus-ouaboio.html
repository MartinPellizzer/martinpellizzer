Strophanthus ouaboio

Strophanthus ouaboio, commonly known as the African blood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that contain toxic cardiac glycosides, which have been used historically in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

However, due to its toxic properties, handling and consumption require caution.
