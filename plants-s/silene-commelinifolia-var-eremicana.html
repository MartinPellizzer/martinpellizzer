Silene commelinifolia var. eremicana

Silene commelinifolia var. eremicana, commonly known as the desert hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its succulent-like leaves and deep root system. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

The species is also notable for its unique leaf morphology, which resembles a tongue or a hound's tongue, giving it its common name.
