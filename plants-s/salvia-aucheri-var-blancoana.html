Salvia aucheri var. blancoana

Salvia aucheri var. blancoana, commonly known as the White Salvia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features upright stems covered in dense, silvery-white velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
