Salix dealbata

Salix dealbata, commonly known as the white willow, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with white on the underside. The tree produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is typically found in coastal and riverine habitats. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it can grow up to 15 meters in height.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
