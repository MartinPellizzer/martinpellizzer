Symplocos bogotensis

Symplocos bogotensis, commonly known as the Colombian wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, dark berries that mature in the fall and are an important food source for local wildlife.

Symplocos bogotensis thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
