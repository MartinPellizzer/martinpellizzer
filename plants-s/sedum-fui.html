Sedum fui

Sedum fui, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is native to various regions across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, often thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or yellow, depending on the species. Sedum fui is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its low maintenance and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and drought-resistant landscaping.
