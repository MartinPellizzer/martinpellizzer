Sideritis incana var. henryi

Sideritis incana var. henryi, commonly known as the pale hairvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy foliage and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, and this variety is no exception.
