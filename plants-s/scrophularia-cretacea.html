Scrophularia cretacea

Scrophularia cretacea, commonly known as the Crete dog's mercury, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in coastal areas and limestone soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in early spring.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
