Sideritis niveotomentosa

Sideritis niveotomentosa, commonly known as the white-hairy ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in rocky and limestone soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Sideritis species are valued for their medicinal properties, though specific uses of Sideritis niveotomentosa are less well-documented.
