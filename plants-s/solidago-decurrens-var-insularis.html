Solidago decurrens var. insularis

Solidago decurrens var. insularis, commonly known as the island goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in yellow-orange composite flower heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
