Scrophularia rugosa

Scrophularia rugosa, commonly known as rough scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The flowers are typically reddish-purple, tubular, and clustered in whorls along the stem, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and grassy areas.

This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it has historical uses in traditional medicine.
