Solidago houghtonii

Solidago houghtonii, commonly known as Houghton's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

Houghton's goldenrod is an important nectar source for pollinators and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
