Stachytarpheta maximiliani

Stachytarpheta maximiliani, commonly known as the purpletop verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces spikes of vibrant purple or violet flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
