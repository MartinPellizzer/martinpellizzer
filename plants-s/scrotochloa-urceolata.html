Scrotochloa urceolata

Scrotochloa urceolata, commonly known as the bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped seed heads that are often adorned with long, feathery bristles. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
