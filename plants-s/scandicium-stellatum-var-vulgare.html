Scandicium stellatum var. vulgare

Scandicium stellatum var. vulgare, commonly known as the star-shaped scandicium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It features delicate, star-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
