Senecio cunninghamii var. flindersensis

Senecio cunninghamii var. flindersensis, commonly known as Flinders Senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
