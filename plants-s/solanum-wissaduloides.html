Solanum wissaduloides

Solanum wissaduloides, commonly known as the Wissaduloides nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and clusters of small, white or purple flowers. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe, contain toxic compounds and are not edible.

Solanum wissaduloides is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
