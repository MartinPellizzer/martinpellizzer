Senecio atropurpureus subsp. tomentosus

Senecio atropurpureus subsp.

tomentosus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and striking purple to pink flower heads that resemble coneflowers.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features include a robust root system and a compact growth habit.
