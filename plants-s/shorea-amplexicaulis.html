Shorea amplexicaulis

Shorea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leafed dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the petiole, giving it its scientific name. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it an important species in local economies.

Shorea amplexicaulis plays a significant role in the ecosystems of tropical rainforests, contributing to biodiversity and carbon sequestration.
