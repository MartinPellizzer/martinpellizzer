Stachys megalodonta

Stachys megalodonta, commonly known as the giant horse mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters along the stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a strong, minty aroma.

Stachys megalodonta is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
