Sageretia decussata

Sageretia decussata, commonly known as the cross-leaved sageretia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cross-shaped leaf arrangement. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Sageretia decussata is typically found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
