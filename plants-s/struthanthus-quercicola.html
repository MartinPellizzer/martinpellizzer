Struthanthus quercicola

Struthanthus quercicola, commonly known as the oakleaf eveningprimrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting pollinators such as moths and beetles. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically hairy as well. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
