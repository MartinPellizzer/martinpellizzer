Scaligeria glaucescens

Scaligeria glaucescens, commonly known as the blue scaligeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale blue, often appearing in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
