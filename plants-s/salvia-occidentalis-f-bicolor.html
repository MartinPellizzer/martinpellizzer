Salvia occidentalis f. bicolor

Salvia occidentalis f. bicolor, commonly known as the western sage with bicolor, is a variant of the western sage plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking bicolor flowers, which typically feature purple and white petals. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This ornamental plant is valued for its drought tolerance and vibrant floral display.
