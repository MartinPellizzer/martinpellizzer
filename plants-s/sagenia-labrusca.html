Sagenia labrusca

Sagenia labrusca, commonly known as the fox grape, is a deciduous vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its small, dark blue to black berries. The plant produces flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, and it is often found growing in moist, wooded areas. Sagenia labrusca is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

Its berries are sometimes used in local traditional practices and can be edible when ripe.
