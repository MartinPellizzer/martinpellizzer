Sedum sieboldii var. kagamontanum

Sedum sieboldii var. kagamontanum, commonly known as the Kagamontan sedum, is a succulent plant native to East Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms.
