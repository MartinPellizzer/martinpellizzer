Solanum atricoeruleum

Solanum atricoeruleum, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species such as tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and can grow up to several meters in length.

Solanum atricoeruleum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique flowering pattern.
