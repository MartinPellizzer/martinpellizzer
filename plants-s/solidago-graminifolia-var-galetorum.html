Solidago graminifolia var. galetorum

Solidago graminifolia var. galetorum, commonly known as the rock goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and bright yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer. This plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and dry slopes. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
