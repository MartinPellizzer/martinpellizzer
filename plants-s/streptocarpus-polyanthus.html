Streptocarpus polyanthus

Streptocarpus polyanthus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of velvety, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often in clusters at the end of leafy stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Streptocarpus polyanthus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
