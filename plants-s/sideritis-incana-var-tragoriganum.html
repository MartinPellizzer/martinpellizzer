Sideritis incana var. tragoriganum

Sideritis incana var. tragoriganum, commonly known as the white-haired ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features silver-gray, hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
