Sorbus Aucuparia Subsp. Gorodkovii

Sorbus aucuparia subsp. gorodkovii is a lesser-known variant of the common rowan tree. It is native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and parts of Western Asia. This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and smaller, more vibrant berries. It thrives in harsh environments, often growing at high altitudes. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
