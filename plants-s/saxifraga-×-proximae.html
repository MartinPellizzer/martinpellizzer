Saxifraga × proximae

Saxifraga × proximae, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga × proximae is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
