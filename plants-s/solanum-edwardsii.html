Solanum edwardsii

Solanum edwardsii, commonly known as the Edwards's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The fruit is a berry that turns from green to yellow when mature, containing several seeds.
