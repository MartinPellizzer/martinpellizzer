Salix taishanensis var. taishanensis

Salix taishanensis var. taishanensis, commonly known as the Taishan willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
