Statice pectinata var. incompta

Statice pectinata var. incompta, commonly known as the sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or saline soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
