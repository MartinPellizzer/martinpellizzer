Solanum mortonii

Solanum mortonii, commonly known as the Morton's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually violet or blue in color. Its fruit is a fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Solanum mortonii is part of the nightshade family, which includes both edible and toxic species.
