Stipa pennata subvar. laevis

Stipa pennata subvar. laevis, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used for erosion control.
