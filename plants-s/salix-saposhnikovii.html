Salix saposhnikovii

Salix saposhnikovii, commonly known as the Chinese willow or Siberian willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of China, Mongolia, and Russia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forest edges.

Salix saposhnikovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
