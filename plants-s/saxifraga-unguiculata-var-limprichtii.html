Saxifraga unguiculata var. limprichtii

Saxifraga unguiculata var. limprichtii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant has dark green, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variant is noted for its resilience and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
