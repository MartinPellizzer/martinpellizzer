Spirotheca rosea

Spirotheca rosea, commonly known as the rose spirotheca, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, rose-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, green bark that becomes more rugged with age. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Spirotheca rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
