Sporobolus bosseri

Sporobolus bosseri, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are often bristly and hairy. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to poor soil conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a feature that aids in its spread across dry landscapes.

Sporobolus bosseri plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife.
