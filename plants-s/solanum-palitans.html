Solanum palitans

Solanum palitans, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Solanum palitans belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other notable species like tomatoes and potatoes.
