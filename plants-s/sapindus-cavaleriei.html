Sapindus cavaleriei

Sapindus cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces smooth, green fruits that turn red when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a starchy, soap-like pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
