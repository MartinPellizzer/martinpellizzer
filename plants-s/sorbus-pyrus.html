Sorbus pyrus

Sorbus pyrus, commonly known as whitebeam, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges.

Sorbus pyrus is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive bark and seasonal interest.
