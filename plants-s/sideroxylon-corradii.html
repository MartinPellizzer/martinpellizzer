Sideroxylon corradii

Sideroxylon corradii, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a member of the Sapotaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a rounded crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by smooth, spherical fruits containing a single seed.
