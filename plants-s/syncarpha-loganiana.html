Syncarpha loganiana

Syncarpha loganiana, commonly known as the Logan's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, seed-filled bracts that persist into winter. Syncarpha loganiana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
