Scilla elisae

Scilla elisae, commonly known as the Elisae scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of striking blue-violet flowers in early spring, typically from March to April. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Scilla elisae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
