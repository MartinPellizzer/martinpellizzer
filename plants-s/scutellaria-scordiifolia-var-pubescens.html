Scutellaria scordiifolia var. pubescens

Scutellaria scordiifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear or hairy scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy underside, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments.
