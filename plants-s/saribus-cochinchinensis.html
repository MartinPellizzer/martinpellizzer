Saribus cochinchinensis

Saribus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the coconut palm, is a tall, slender palm native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, unbranched trunk with a fibrous, spongy core. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves and bears yellow, fleshy fruits that contain coconut water and edible kernels. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize coastal soils.

The coconut palm is widely cultivated for its fruit, fiber, and oil.
