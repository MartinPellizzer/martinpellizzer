Stipa basiplumosa

Stipa basiplumosa, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including countries like Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Stipa basiplumosa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
