Stemmatella

Stemmatella, commonly known as the common stemmatella, is a small annual herb native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, with slender, branching stems that are often hairy. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, producing tiny, bell-shaped blooms that are usually white or pale green.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.
