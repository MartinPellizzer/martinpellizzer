Scaevola nitida

Scaevola nitida, commonly known as the silver fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils and salt spray. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Scaevola nitida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
