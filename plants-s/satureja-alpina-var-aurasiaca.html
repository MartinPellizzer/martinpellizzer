Satureja alpina var. aurasiaca

Satureja alpina var. aurasiaca, commonly known as the Aurasic mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and potential medicinal properties.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
