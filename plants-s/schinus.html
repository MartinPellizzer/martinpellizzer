Schinus

Schinus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the cashew family, Anacardiaceae.

It includes several species, many of which are shrubs or small trees native to the Americas. The plants are characterized by their compound leaves, which are often feather-like in appearance, and their small, usually red or orange berries. Schinus species are known for their aromatic properties and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

They also play a role in ecological systems as a source of food for various wildlife species.
