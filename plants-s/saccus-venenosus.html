Saccus venenosus

Saccus venenosus, commonly known as the venom sac plant, is a unique species characterized by its specialized structures called sacci, which are modified leaves that store and release toxic compounds.

These sacci are typically found at the base of the plant and are often brightly colored to deter herbivores. The plant is native to arid regions and has adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils through efficient water retention mechanisms. Its toxic properties have made it a subject of interest in both botanical research and traditional medicine.

Despite its harmful nature, Saccus venenosus plays a role in its ecosystem by influencing herbivore behavior and soil composition.
