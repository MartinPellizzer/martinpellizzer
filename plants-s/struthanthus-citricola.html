Struthanthus citricola

Struthanthus citricola, commonly known as the orange globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Struthanthus citricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
