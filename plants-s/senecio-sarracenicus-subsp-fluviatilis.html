Senecio sarracenicus subsp. fluviatilis

Senecio sarracenicus subsp.

fluviatilis, commonly known as the river senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and stream sides. It has narrow, succulent leaves that help it retain water in arid environments.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
