Spilanthes beccabunga var. parvula

Spilanthes beccabunga var. parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered bladderleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in wet soils and its historical use in herbal remedies.
