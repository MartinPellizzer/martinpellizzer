Semenovia montana

Semenovia montana, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Semenovia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
