Schlumbergera gaertneri

Schlumbergera gaertneri, commonly known as the holiday cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented stems that resemble leaves, which are actually modified stems adapted for storing water. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Schlumbergera gaertneri is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
