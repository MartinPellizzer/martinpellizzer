Solidago glutinosa var. gillmanii

Solidago glutinosa var. gillmanii, commonly known as Gillman's sticky goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, rayless flowers.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
