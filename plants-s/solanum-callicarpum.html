Solanum callicarpum

Solanum callicarpum, commonly known as the red berry nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Solanum callicarpum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
