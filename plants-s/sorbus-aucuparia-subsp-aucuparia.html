Sorbus aucuparia subsp. aucuparia

Sorbus aucuparia subsp.

aucuparia, commonly known as the rowan or mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, Asia, and North America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges, rocky areas, and open woodlands.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical uses in traditional medicine.
