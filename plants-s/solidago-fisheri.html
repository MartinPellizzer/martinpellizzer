Solidago fisheri

Solidago fisheri, commonly known as the Fishers' goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small golden clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting various pollinators.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
