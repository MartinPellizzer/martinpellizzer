Salvia hapalophylla

Salvia hapalophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Salvia hapalophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
