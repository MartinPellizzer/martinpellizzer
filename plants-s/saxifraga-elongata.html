Saxifraga elongata

Saxifraga elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, often branched stems that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

Saxifraga elongata thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
