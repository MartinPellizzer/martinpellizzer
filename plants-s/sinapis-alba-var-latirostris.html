Sinapis alba var. latirostris

Sinapis alba var. latirostris, commonly known as the broad-nosed mustard, is a variant of the white mustard plant.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The seeds are small and elongated, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
