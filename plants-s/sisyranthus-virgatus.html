Sisyranthus virgatus

Sisyranthus virgatus, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are usually white or pale green with a subtle fragrance.

Sisyranthus virgatus is often found in association with other orchids and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
