Sorbus bachmarensis

Sorbus bachmarensis, commonly known as the Bachmar rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically orange-red in color, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Sorbus bachmarensis is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
