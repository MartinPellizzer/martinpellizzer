Silene striata

Silene striata, commonly known as the striped catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene striata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
