Syzygium tierneyanum

Syzygium tierneyanum, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Syzygium tierneyanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
