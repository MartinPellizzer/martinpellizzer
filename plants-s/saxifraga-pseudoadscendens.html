Saxifraga pseudoadscendens

Saxifraga pseudoadscendens, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats, with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, upright stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Saxifraga pseudoadscendens is valued for its hardiness and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
