Statice gracilis

Statice gracilis, commonly known as slender sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other salt-tolerant plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and spikes of small, pink or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes.
