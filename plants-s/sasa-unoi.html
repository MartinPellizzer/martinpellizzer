Sasa unoi

Sasa unoi, commonly known as the Hawaiian bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It grows in dense clusters, forming thickets that provide habitat for various native species. The plant has long, slender culms that are often used in traditional Hawaiian crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Sasa unoi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
