Senecio larranagae

Senecio larranagae, commonly known as the Larranaga's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

Senecio larranagae is often found in open grasslands and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
