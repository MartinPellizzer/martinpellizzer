Syzygium calophyllifolium

Syzygium calophyllifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
