Scirpus lusulinus

Scirpus lusulinus, commonly known as the western bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered with a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers.

Scirpus lusulinus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
