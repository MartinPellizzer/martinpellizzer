Sida tuberculata var. tuberculata

Sida tuberculata var. tuberculata, commonly known as the wooly sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
