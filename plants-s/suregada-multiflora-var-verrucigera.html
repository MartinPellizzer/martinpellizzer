Suregada multiflora var. verrucigera

Suregada multiflora var. verrucigera, commonly known as the hairy multiflora.

It is a shrub native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
