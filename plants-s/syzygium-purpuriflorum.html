Syzygium purpuriflorum

Syzygium purpuriflorum, commonly known as the purple-flowered tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a woody pod containing several flat, bean-like seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
