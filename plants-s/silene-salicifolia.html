Silene salicifolia

Silene salicifolia, commonly known as willowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates. It is an annual or biennial species, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Silene salicifolia is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
