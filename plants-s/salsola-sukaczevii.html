Salsola sukaczevii

Salsola sukaczevii, commonly known as the Sukaczev's saltwort, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding survival in dry conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources in nutrient-poor soils.

Salsola sukaczevii is often found in desert regions and coastal areas where salt tolerance is essential for survival.
