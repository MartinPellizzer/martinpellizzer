Stevia philippiana var. panulensis

Stevia philippiana var. panulensis, commonly known as Philippine stevia, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, giving it its specific epithet "panulensis.". It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and potential medicinal purposes.
