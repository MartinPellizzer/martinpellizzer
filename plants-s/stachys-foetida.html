Stachys foetida

Stachys foetida, commonly known as bear's garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, garlic-like aroma. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and can be harvested for its pungent, edible roots.

Stachys foetida is often found in rocky or woodland soils and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
