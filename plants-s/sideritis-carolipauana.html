Sideritis carolipauana

Sideritis carolipauana, commonly known as Carolipauan heath, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
