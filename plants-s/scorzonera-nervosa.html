Scorzonera nervosa

Scorzonera nervosa, commonly known as the nervous scorzonerina, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots are often tuberous and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera nervosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
