Stanhopea napoensis

Stanhopea napoensis, commonly known as the Napo orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Stanhopea napoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
