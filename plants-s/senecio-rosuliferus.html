Senecio rosuliferus

Senecio rosuliferus, commonly known as the Rosulate Senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to propagate through both seeds and offsets.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
