Sherwoodia galacifolia

Sherwoodia galacifolia, commonly known as the silver-leafed shrub, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
