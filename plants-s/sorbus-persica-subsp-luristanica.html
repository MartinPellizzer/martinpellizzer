Sorbus persica subsp. luristanica

Sorbus persica subsp.

luristanica, commonly known as the Luristan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the highlands of Iran. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
