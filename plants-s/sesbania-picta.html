Sesbania picta

Sesbania picta, commonly known as the painted sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful pods that range from yellow to red, often with contrasting patterns. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Sesbania picta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant and unique pod structures.
