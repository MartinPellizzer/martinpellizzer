Silene longisepala

Silene longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepal silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features elongated sepals that are often mistaken for petals, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Silene longisepala is primarily found in alpine meadows and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
