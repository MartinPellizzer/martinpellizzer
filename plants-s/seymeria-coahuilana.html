Seymeria coahuilana

It is a low-growing shrub with small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the spring season. Seymeria coahuilana is adapted to extreme drought conditions and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat fragility, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human disturbance.
