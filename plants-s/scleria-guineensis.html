Scleria guineensis

Scleria guineensis, commonly known as the African bulbous grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and clustered inflorescence. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often grows in moist, sandy soils near water bodies.

Scleria guineensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
