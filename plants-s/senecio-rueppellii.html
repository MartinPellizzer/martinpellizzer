Senecio rueppellii

Senecio rueppellii, commonly known as the Cape Vulture's Beak, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features linear, blue-green leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Senecio rueppellii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
