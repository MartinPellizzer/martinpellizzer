Salvia persepolitana

Salvia persepolitana, commonly known as Persian sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma.

Salvia persepolitana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
