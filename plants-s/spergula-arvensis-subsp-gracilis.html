Spergula arvensis subsp. gracilis

Spergula arvensis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender field sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, ditches, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
