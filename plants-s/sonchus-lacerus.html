Sonchus lacerus

Sonchus lacerus, commonly known as the hairy sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features alternate, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. Its seeds are small and dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
