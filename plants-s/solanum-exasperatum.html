Solanum exasperatum

Solanum exasperatum, commonly known as the prickly nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Solanum exasperatum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
