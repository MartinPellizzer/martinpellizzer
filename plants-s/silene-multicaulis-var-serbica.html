Silene multicaulis var. serbica

Silene multicaulis var. serbica, commonly known as the Serbica bluemink, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Silene multicaulis var. serbica is also of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological adaptations.
