Swertia polyantha

Swertia polyantha, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. The plant features striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy and branched.

Swertia polyantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
