Scleria indica

Scleria indica, commonly known as the African flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, three-lobed flowers.

Scleria indica is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
