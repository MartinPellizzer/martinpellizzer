Solidago rugosa var. laevicaulis

Solidago rugosa var. laevicaulis, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a slightly grayish or whitish underside.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
