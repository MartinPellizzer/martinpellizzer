Stenostephanus borarum

Stenostephanus borarum, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Stenostephanus borarum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
