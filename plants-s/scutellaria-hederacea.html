Scutellaria hederacea

Scutellaria hederacea, commonly known as creeping skullcap, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

Scutellaria hederacea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
