Smilax undulata

Smilax undulata, commonly known as the waxy beggar's lice, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its waxy, spiny stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Smilax undulata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
