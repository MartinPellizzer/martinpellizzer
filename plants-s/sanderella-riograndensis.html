Sanderella riograndensis

Sanderella riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Sanderella riograndensis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
