Salvia pseudeuphratica

Salvia pseudeuphratica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Salvia pseudeuphratica is also valued for its medicinal properties and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
