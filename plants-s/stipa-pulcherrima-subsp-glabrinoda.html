Stipa pulcherrima subsp. glabrinoda

Stipa pulcherrima subsp.

glabrinoda, commonly known as the silky needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, awned lemmas. Its foliage is often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in garden settings.
