Serratula nitens

Serratula nitens, commonly known as the slender sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. It features alternate, oblanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Serratula nitens is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
