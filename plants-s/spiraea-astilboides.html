Spiraea astilboides

Spiraea astilboides, commonly known as the Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves that are green on top and white underneath, creating a striking visual contrast. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Spiraea astilboides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland settings.
