Sebastiania hypoleuca var. angustifolia

Sebastiania hypoleuca var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sebastiania, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in moist, acidic habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in suitable climates.
