Strophanthus bullenianus

Strophanthus bullenianus, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed African milkvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems, bright yellow flowers, and milky sap, which contains toxic cardiac glycosides. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences and bloom during the rainy season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
