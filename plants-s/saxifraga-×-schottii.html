Saxifraga × schottii

Saxifraga × schottii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Saxifraga × schottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
