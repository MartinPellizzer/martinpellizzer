Sorocea ilicifolia var. hilarii

Sorocea ilicifolia var. hilarii, commonly known as the Hilarii Buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark, smooth berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
