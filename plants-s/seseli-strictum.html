Seseli strictum

Seseli strictum, commonly known as rigid seseli, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and compound umbel flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and open habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically valued for its potential health benefits.
