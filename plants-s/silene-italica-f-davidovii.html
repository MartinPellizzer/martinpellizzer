Silene italica f. davidovii

Silene italica f. davidovii, commonly known as the Davidovii form of the Italian campion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
