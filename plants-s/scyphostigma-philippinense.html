Scyphostigma philippinense

Scyphostigma philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine star cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, branched stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warm season, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Scyphostigma philippinense is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in arid gardens.
