Stanhopea insignis var. speciosa

Stanhopea insignis var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy stanhopea, is a tropical orchid native to the Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
