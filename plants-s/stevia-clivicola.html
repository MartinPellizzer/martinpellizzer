Stevia clivicola

Stevia clivicola, commonly known as cliff stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Stevia clivicola is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural sweeteners.
