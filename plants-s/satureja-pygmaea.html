Satureja pygmaea

Satureja pygmaea, commonly known as dwarf savory, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with tiny, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in whorls along the stems.

Satureja pygmaea is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its aromatic properties and use in traditional herbal medicine.
