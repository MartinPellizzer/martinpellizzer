Senecio canalipes

Senecio canalipes, commonly known as the canyon senecio, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio canalipes is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh, sun-exposed conditions.
