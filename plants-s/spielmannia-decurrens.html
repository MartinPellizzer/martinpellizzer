Spielmannia decurrens

Spielmannia decurrens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, decurrent leaves that often trail along the ground. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptability to various soil types.
