Salvia × karamanensis

Salvia × karamanensis, commonly known as the Karaman sage, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in whorled clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a popular choice in horticulture.
