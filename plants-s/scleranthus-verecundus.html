Scleranthus verecundus

Scleranthus verecundus, commonly known as the creeping thornweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on the ground, with small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Scleranthus verecundus is characterized by its tough, woody stems and its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
