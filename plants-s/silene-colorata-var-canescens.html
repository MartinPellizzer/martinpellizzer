Silene colorata var. canescens

Silene colorata var. canescens, commonly known as the silver-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are also covered in fine pubescence.

Silene colorata var. canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
