Sorghum nutans subvar. agrostoides

Sorghum nutans subvar. agrostoides, commonly known as the slender grass sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides due to its adaptability. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically inconspicuous.

The plant is valued for its potential in forage and as a source of biofuel due to its high biomass yield.
