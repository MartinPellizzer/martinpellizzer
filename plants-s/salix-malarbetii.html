Salix malarbetii

Salix malarbetii, commonly known as the Malarbetii willow, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Salix.

It is native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Pamir and Tien Shan mountain ranges. This shrub or small tree typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color, and it produces catkins during the spring.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with limited soil and harsh weather conditions.
