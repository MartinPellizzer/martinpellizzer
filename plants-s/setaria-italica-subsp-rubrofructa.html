Setaria italica subsp. rubrofructa

Setaria italica subsp.

rubrofructa, commonly known as red foxtail, is a grass species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its reddish-brown inflorescence, which distinguishes it from other Setaria species.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Red foxtail is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
