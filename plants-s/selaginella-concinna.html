Selaginella concinna

Selaginella concinna, commonly known as the lesser spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a stem that is usually less than 10 centimeters tall, with whorled leaves that are often bright green and lance-shaped. Selaginella concinna is characterized by its spore-producing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its branches.

This species is often used in horticulture due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
