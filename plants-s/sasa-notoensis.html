Sasa notoensis

Sasa notoensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain slopes. Sasa notoensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can form dense clumps that help prevent soil erosion.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish itself in various woodland habitats.
