Sorbus rutilans

Sorbus rutilans, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. The leaves are typically oval or elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The tree is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks, contributing to its ecological importance.
