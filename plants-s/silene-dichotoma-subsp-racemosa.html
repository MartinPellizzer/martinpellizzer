Silene dichotoma subsp. racemosa

Silene dichotoma subsp.

racemosa, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and flies.
