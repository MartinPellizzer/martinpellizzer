Stauropsis nagarensis

Stauropsis nagarensis, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spiky seed heads after flowering.

Stauropsis nagarensis is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
