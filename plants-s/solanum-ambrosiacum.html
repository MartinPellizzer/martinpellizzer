Solanum ambrosiacum

Solanum ambrosiacum, commonly known as the ambrosia tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they can vary in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
