Silene dumanii

Silene dumanii, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems that grow close to the ground. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Silene dumanii is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, desert conditions.
