Statice capillifolia

Statice capillifolia, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.

Statice capillifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy shorelines.
