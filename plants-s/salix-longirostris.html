Salix longirostris

Salix longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that appear in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Salix longirostris is typically found in moist, well-drained soils along streams and in alpine meadows.
