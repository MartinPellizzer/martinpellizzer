Schizonotus argenteus var. fissus

Schizonotus argenteus var. fissus, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It possesses a distinctive appearance with its silvery-white foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's leaves are leathery and have a dense covering of fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic silver sheen. It relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also providing shelter for various insects and birds.

Its ecological role is significant in maintaining biodiversity within forest ecosystems.
