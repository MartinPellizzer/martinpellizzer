Syzygium komatiense

Syzygium komatiense, commonly known as the komatiense tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round, yellowish fruits that turn red when mature, containing edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruit in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
