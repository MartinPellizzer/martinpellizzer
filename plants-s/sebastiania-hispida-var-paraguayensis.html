Sebastiania hispida var. paraguayensis

Sebastiania hispida var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy blue berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental berries and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
