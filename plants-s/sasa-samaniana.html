Sasa samaniana

Sasa samaniana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that form dense clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa samaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts.
