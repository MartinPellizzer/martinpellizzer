Scutellaria ovalifolia subsp. hirsuta

Scutellaria ovalifolia subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as hairy oval-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
