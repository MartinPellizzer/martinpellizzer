Sorbus bissetii

Sorbus bissetii, commonly known as the Bisset's rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
