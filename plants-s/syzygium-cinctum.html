Syzygium cinctum

Syzygium cinctum, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a reddish tint along the veins. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

The species is also notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
