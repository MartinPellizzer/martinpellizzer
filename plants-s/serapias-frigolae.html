Serapias frigolae

Serapias frigolae, commonly known as the alpine orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Serapias frigolae is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high altitudes.
