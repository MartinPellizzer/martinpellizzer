Salvia officinalis var. alba

Salvia officinalis var. alba, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces showy, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is known for its strong, herbal scent and is often used in traditional medicine and spiritual practices. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
