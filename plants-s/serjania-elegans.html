Serjania elegans

Serjania elegans, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple to blue berries. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Serjania elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking fruit and floral display.
