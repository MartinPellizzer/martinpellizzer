Stipa zalesskyi subsp. ucrainica

Stipa zalesskyi subsp.

ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, forest edges, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces dense, fluffy panicles of flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
