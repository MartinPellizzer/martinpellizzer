Satureja × benthamineolens

Satureja × benthamineolens, commonly known as hybrid savory, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Satureja hortensis and Satureja vermiculata.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This perennial herb is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in herbal medicine and cooking.
