Syzygium minimum

Syzygium minimum, commonly known as the dwarf apple or small apple, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, often resembling miniature apples. Syzygium minimum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
