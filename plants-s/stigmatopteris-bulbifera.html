Stigmatopteris bulbifera

Stigmatopteris bulbifera, commonly known as the bulbiferous shield fern, is a unique species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered with small, bulb-like structures called bulbils, which play a role in vegetative reproduction. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, supporting the growth of the fronds and bulbils.

The botanical features of S. bulbifera make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
