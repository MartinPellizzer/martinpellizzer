Saccolabium crucicallus

Saccolabium crucicallus, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironwood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system for water retention. Its wood is hard and dense, making it valuable for traditional uses.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
