Salsola denudata

Salsola denudata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spiny heads, which are adapted for wind pollination. Salsola denudata is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments due to its deep root system and water-storing tissues.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural areas but plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed habitats.
