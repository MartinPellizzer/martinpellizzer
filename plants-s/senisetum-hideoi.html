Senisetum hideoi

Senisetum hideoi, commonly known as the Hideo's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often adorned with a fuzzy texture. It produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Senisetum hideoi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
