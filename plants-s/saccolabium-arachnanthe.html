Saccolabium arachnanthe

Saccolabium arachnanthe, commonly known as the spider-thorn acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often reduced to scales, a feature typical of many acacia species. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Saccolabium arachnanthe is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient component of arid ecosystems.
