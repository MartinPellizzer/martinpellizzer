Sesleria mutica

Sesleria mutica, commonly known as the common sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Sesleria mutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine-textured foliage.
