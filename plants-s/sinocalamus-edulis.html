Sinocalamus edulis

Sinocalamus edulis, commonly known as the edible bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has hollow, segmented stems with a smooth, greenish-brown surface. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This bamboo is valued not only for its timber but also for its edible shoots, which are commonly consumed in traditional Chinese cuisine.
