Sisyrinchium nanum

Sisyrinchium nanum, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate blue or violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is often found in the eastern and central United States.

Sisyrinchium nanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
