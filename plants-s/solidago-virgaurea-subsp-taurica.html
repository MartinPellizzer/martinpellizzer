Solidago virgaurea subsp. taurica

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

taurica, commonly known as the Crimean goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

The plant features erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer to early autumn, forming dense, fluffy inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
