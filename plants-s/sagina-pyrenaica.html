Sagina pyrenaica

Sagina pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant has small, fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters near the base of the plant.

Sagina pyrenaica is a hardy species that demonstrates adaptation to cold and arid conditions.
