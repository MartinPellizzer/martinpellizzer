Suaeda corniculata var. drepanophylla

Suaeda corniculata var. drepanophylla, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It exhibits succulent leaves and stems that store water, aiding in survival in harsh conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It is often found in coastal dunes and saline soils along the Atlantic and Mediterranean coasts.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
