Salmasia lendigera

Salmasia lendigera, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features slender stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs.

Salmasia lendigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
