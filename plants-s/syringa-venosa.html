Syringa venosa

Syringa venosa, commonly known as the weeping lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading clusters of fragrant, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Syringa venosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
