Senna talpana

Senna talpana, commonly known as the purple senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod fruits. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred structure. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, fibrous pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Senna talpana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
