Scleranthus annuus var. annotinus

Scleranthus annuus var. annotinus, commonly known as the common scarlet burnet, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and waste areas. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive hairy texture.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
