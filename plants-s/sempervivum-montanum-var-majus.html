Sempervivum montanum var. majus

Sempervivum montanum var. majus, commonly known as the Mountain Houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features fleshy, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge, depending on light exposure. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in cold climates, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care.
