Solanum pliophyllidium

Solanum pliophyllidium, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, and its flowers are usually yellow or purple, clustered in small umbels. The fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers.
