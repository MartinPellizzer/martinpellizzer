Senecio hansweberi

Senecio hansweberi, commonly known as the Hansweber's groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
