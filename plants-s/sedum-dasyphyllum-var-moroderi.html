Sedum dasyphyllum var. moroderi

Sedum dasyphyllum var. moroderi, commonly known as the Moroder's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
