Spergula pentandra subsp. morisonii

Spergula pentandra subsp.

morisonii, commonly known as the Morison's sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and triangular leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
