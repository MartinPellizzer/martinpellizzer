Solanum balbisii var. purpureum

Solanum balbisii var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple devil's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces small, round, purple berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is broad and dark green, with a slightly hairy texture.
