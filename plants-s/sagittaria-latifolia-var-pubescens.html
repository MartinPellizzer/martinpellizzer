Sagittaria latifolia var. pubescens

Sagittaria latifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as broadleaf arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow water and wetlands, where it forms dense, floating or submerged mats. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing both male and female flowers on separate plants.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing water bodies.
