Scleranthus reichenbachii

Scleranthus reichenbachii, commonly known as the common thornweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Scleranthus reichenbachii is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
