Stachys fruticetorum

Stachys fruticetorum, commonly known as woodland betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and purple or pink flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, typical of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

Stachys fruticetorum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
