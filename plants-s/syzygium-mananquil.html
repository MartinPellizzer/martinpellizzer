Syzygium mananquil

Syzygium mananquil, commonly known as the Mananquil apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. Syzygium mananquil thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and edible fruit make it a notable species in both horticulture and local cuisine.
