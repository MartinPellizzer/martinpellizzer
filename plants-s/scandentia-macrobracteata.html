Scandentia macrobracteata

Scandentia macrobracteata, commonly known as the giant bract scandentia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy bracts that resemble leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, textured surface.

Scandentia macrobracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
