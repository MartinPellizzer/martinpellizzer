Solanum asymmetriphyllum var. longiflorum

Solanum asymmetriphyllum var. longiflorum, commonly known as the asymmetric-leaved nightshade, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly asymmetrical leaves, which are a distinguishing feature of the species. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, blooming in the evening. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

This variant is particularly noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unusual foliage and vibrant floral display.
