Stylidium amphora

Stylidium amphora, commonly known as the bottle-shaped sun-orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped flower spike that resembles a narrow, elongated vessel. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged along the elongated stem. Stylidium amphora is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its morphology is adapted to conserve water, with a thickened stem that stores moisture and reduces transpiration.
