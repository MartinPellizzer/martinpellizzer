Sasa samaniana var. yoshinoi

Sasa samaniana var. yoshinoi, commonly known as the Yoshino bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa samaniana species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that grows in dense, grass-like clusters, typically in moist, shaded environments. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a pale green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
