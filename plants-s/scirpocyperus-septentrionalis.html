Scirpocyperus septentrionalis

Scirpocyperus septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually inconspicuous. It is native to North America and is often found in the northern United States and southern Canada.

Scirpocyperus septentrionalis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
