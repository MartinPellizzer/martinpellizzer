Saussurea caespitosa var. caespitosa

Saussurea caespitosa var. caespitosa, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that forms dense, tufted clumps with fibrous roots and short, stiff stems. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flower heads, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

This species is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
