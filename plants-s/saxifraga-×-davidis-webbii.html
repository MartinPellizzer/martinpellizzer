Saxifraga × davidis-webbii

Saxifraga × davidis-webbii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically rosette-shaped, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.
