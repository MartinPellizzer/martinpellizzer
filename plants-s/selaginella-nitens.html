Selaginella nitens

Selaginella nitens, commonly known as the slender spikemoss, is a vascular fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its structure consists of a short, upright stem with whorled leaves that are often spirally arranged.

Selaginella nitens is characterized by its ability to reproduce via spores and exhibits a unique life cycle with distinct sporophyte and gametophyte stages.
