Salicornia hispanica

Salicornia hispanica, commonly known as Spanish glasswort, is a halophyte plant that thrives in saline environments such as coastal marshes and salt marshes.

It is characterized by its jointed, succulent stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, green or reddish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, and the stems are often used in culinary applications due to their salty, crunchy texture.

Salicornia hispanica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
