Selaginella kerstingii var. kerstingii

Selaginella kerstingii var. kerstingii, commonly known as the Kersting's spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant features whorled leaves that are often dark green and have a leathery texture, with a central stem that bears clusters of spore-producing structures called strobila. It is characterized by its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive system involving both spores and seeds.

This plant is also noted for its ability to regenerate from its rhizomes, making it resilient in various ecological settings.
