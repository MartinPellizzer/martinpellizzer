Senecio crassiflorus var. andryaloides

Senecio crassiflorus var. andryaloides, commonly known as the Andryaloides groundsels, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
