Solanum eminens

Solanum eminens, commonly known as the highland nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a strong scent.

Solanum eminens belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
