Solanum confine

Solanum confine, commonly known as the narrowleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption.
