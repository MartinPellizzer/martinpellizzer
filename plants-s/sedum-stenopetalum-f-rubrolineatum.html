Sedum stenopetalum f. rubrolineatum

Sedum stenopetalum f. rubrolineatum, commonly known as the red-line stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and leaves that are often covered in a powdery white coating. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
