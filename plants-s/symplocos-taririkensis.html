Symplocos taririkensis

Symplocos taririkensis, commonly known as the Taririkensis waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, red drupes that are edible. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
