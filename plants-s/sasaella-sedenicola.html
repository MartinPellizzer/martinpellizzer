Sasaella sedenicola

Sasaella sedenicola, commonly known as the slender sasa, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

Sasaella sedenicola is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
