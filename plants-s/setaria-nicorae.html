Setaria nicorae

Setaria nicorae, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Setaria nicorae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various invertebrates.
