Stipa × talassica

Stipa × talassica, commonly known as the hybrid meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Stipa capillata and Stipa pennata, characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry culms. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

Stipa × talassica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
