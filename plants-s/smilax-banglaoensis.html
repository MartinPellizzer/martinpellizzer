Smilax banglaoensis

Smilax banglaoensis, commonly known as the Banglao arrowroot, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus Smilax, which includes over 300 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, which often have sharp spines, and its compound leaves with a distinctive, serrated edge. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for some animals.

Smilax banglaoensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
