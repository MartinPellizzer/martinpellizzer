Serjania parvifolia

Serjania parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Serjania parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
