Scleria elata var. latior

Scleria elata var. latior, commonly known as the broadleaf speargrass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant features grass-like leaves that are broad and flat, with a fibrous root system. Its flowering stems produce small, three-parted flowers that are often greenish or white in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
