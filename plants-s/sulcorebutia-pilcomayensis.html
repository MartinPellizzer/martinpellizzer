Sulcorebutia pilcomayensis

Sulcorebutia pilcomayensis, commonly known as the Pilcomayo crassula, is a flowering plant native to Bolivia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
