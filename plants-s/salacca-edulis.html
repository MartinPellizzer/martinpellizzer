Salacca edulis

Salacca edulis, commonly known as the sugar plum or snake fruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange fruits that have a sweet, aromatic flavor and a texture similar to a mix between a kiwi and a pear. The fruit is rich in nutrients, including vitamin C, and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, Salacca edulis is cultivated in tropical regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
