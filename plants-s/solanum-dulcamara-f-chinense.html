Solanum dulcamara f. chinense

Solanum dulcamara f. chinense, commonly known as Chinese bindweed, is a variant of the common morning glory.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale purple to white, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are green when immature and turn red when ripe.

This plant is native to eastern Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
