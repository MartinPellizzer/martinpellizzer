Stevia villaregalis

Stevia villaregalis, commonly known as the yellow stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and serrated leaves. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its medicinal and sweetening properties, it has gained interest in both traditional and modern herbal applications.
