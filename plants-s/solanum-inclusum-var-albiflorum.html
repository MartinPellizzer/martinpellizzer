Solanum inclusum var. albiflorum

Solanum inclusum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, wooded areas. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, smooth, green berries that ripen to a dark purple.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
