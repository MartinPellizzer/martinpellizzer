Saussurea pumila

Saussurea pumila, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at elevations above 3,000 meters. The plant features a short, thick stem with narrow, grayish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Saussurea pumila is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Tibetan medicine for various ailments.
