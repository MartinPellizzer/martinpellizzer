Syzygium guineense subsp. parvifolium

Syzygium guineense subsp.

parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy surface.

The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The wood of this species is valued for its fine grain and is used in traditional craftsmanship and furniture making.
