Salvia whitehousei

Salvia whitehousei, commonly known as Whitehouse sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. This species is characterized by its gray-green foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
