Solanum plousianthemum var. gracilifilum

Solanum plousianthemum var. gracilifilum, commonly known as the slender-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in the evening. The plant has thin, elongated leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to its native habitat.
