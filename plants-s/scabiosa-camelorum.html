Scabiosa camelorum

Scabiosa camelorum, commonly known as the Persian scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features wiry stems and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa camelorum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
