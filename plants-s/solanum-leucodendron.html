Solanum leucodendron

Solanum leucodendron, commonly known as the white-fruited nightshade, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round, white berries that mature to a yellowish color.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
