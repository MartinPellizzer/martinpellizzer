Setaria abyssinica var. annua

Setaria abyssinica var. annua, commonly known as annual fingergrass, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It is notable for its ability to thrive in dry conditions and has been studied for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
