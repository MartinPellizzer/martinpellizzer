Sarcopyramis gracilis

Sarcopyramis gracilis, commonly known as the slender pyramidal lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a unique botanical structure.

It consists of a symbiotic association between a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga, and a fungal component, usually from the Ascomycota division. The thallus of Sarcopyramis gracilis is usually flat and leaf-like, with a distinct, often bright green or yellowish coloration. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, commonly found on tree bark or soil in temperate regions.

This lichen is notable for its slow growth and ecological role in nutrient cycling within forest ecosystems.
