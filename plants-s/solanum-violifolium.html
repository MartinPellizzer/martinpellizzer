Solanum violifolium

Solanum violifolium, commonly known as violetleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, violet-colored flowers and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or edible purposes.
