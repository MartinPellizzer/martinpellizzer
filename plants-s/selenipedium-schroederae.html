Selenipedium schroederae

Selenipedium schroederae, commonly known as the giant panicle orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family with unique botanical features.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy panicles of white or pale green flowers. Its leaves are broad, elongated, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Selenipedium schroederae is known for its slow growth and long flowering cycle, making it a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
