Steenhammera virginica

Steenhammera virginica, commonly known as the Virginia steenhammer, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually greenish-white in color.

Steenhammera virginica is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
