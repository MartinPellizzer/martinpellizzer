Sida serratifolia

Sida serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom throughout the growing season.

Sida serratifolia is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
