Salix × iwahisana

Salix × iwahisana, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a weeping appearance, often adorned with small, narrow leaves. The plant is native to Japan and is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Salix × iwahisana is also valued for its ability to thrive in wetland environments, making it useful for erosion control and habitat restoration.
