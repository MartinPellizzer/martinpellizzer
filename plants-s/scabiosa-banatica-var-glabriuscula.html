Scabiosa banatica var. glabriuscula

Scabiosa banatica var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the silky scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that produce clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with a distinctive central disc. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
