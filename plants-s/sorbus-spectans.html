Sorbus spectans

Sorbus spectans, commonly known as the showy whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and turn yellow in the autumn.

Sorbus spectans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
