Saccolabium sterrophyllum

Saccolabium sterrophyllum, commonly known as the white-flowered sicklepod, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by distinctive, curved pods. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with deep roots and a capacity for drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in ecological restoration projects.
