Swertia albomarginata subsp. purpusii

Swertia albomarginata subsp.

purpusii, commonly known as the white-margin gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a white margin, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
