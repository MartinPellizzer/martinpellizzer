Sporobolus brevipilis

Sporobolus brevipilis, commonly known as the short-haired dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
