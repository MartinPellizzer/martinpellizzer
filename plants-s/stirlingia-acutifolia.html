Stirlingia acutifolia

Stirlingia acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved starlingia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and forested areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in the lower North Island and parts of the South Island of New Zealand.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
