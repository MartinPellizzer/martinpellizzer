Siderodendrum multiflorum var. angustifolium

Siderodendrum multiflorum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironwood, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
