Senecio brachyglossus

Senecio brachyglossus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features wiry, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dry gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to arid landscapes.
