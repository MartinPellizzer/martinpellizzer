Sesbania coccinea var. marchionica

Sesbania coccinea var. marchionica, commonly known as the red sesbania, is a tropical legume native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant is a fast-growing shrub or small tree that thrives in warm, humid climates. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

Its ornamental value makes it popular in horticulture, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
