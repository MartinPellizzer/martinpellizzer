Salvia rubra

Salvia rubra, commonly known as red sage, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red or violet flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

Salvia rubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 10.
