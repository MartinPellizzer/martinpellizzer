Setaria bathiei

Setaria bathiei, commonly known as the African dogstail grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that resemble a dog's tail, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a fibrous root system.

Setaria bathiei is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential in phytoremediation.
