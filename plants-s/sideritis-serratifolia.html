Sideritis serratifolia

Sideritis serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Sideritis serratifolia is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
