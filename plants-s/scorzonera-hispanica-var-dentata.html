Scorzonera hispanica var. dentata

Scorzonera hispanica var. dentata, commonly known as the Spanish scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, followed by cylindrical, spiny seed heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Scorzonera hispanica var. dentata is also valued for its edible roots, which have a slightly sweet and nutty flavor.
