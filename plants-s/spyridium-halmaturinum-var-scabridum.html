Spyridium halmaturinum var. scabridum

Spyridium halmaturinum var. scabridum, commonly known as the rough spyridium, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of South Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the cooler months, typically from July to September. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The variety scabridum is characterized by its more pronounced leaf scarring and a more compact growth form compared to other varieties.
