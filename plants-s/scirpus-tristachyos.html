Scirpus tristachyos

Scirpus tristachyos, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense stands in marshes and along the edges of lakes and ponds. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of greenish-white. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Scirpus tristachyos plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
