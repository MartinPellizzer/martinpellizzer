Spergularia marginata subsp. tunetana

Spergularia marginata subsp.

tunetana, commonly known as the Mediterranean sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean Basin. It is a small, herbaceous annual with succulent leaves and pink or red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a rosette growth form, with leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to hot, dry climates.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of coastal and inland dune ecosystems.
