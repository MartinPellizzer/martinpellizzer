Scirpus atrovirens f. georgianus

Scirpus atrovirens f. georgianus, commonly known as the Georgian sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its botanical features include dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to moist soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
