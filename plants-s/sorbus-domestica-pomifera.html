Sorbus domestica pomifera

Sorbus domestica pomifera, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
