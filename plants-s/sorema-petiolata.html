Sorema petiolata

Sorema petiolata, commonly known as the slender sorrowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous growth habit, which aid in its survival in wetland habitats.

Sorema petiolata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
