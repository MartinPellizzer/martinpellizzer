Sporobolus osceolensis

Sporobolus osceolensis, commonly known as the prairie dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a feature that contributes to its adaptability and survival in arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
