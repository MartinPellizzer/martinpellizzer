Solanum gilo var. sparseaculeatum

Solanum gilo var. sparseaculeatum, commonly known as the Andean cherry or yellow cherry tomato, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Andes region of South America and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy, branching stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and the fruits are edible, often used in local cuisines.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular crop in high-altitude regions.
