Silene epilosa

Silene epilosa, commonly known as the upright catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene epilosa is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.
