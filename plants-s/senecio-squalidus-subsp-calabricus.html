Senecio squalidus subsp. calabricus

Senecio squalidus subsp.

calabricus, commonly known as the coastal senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in dune formations and along the edges of coastal forests.
