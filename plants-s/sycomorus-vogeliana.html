Sycomorus vogeliana

Sycomorus vogeliana, commonly known as the Vogel's fig tree, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grey bark. The tree produces small, clustered figs that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
