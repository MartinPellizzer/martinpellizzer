Symplocos ifsiana

Symplocos ifsiana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Symplocos ifsiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
