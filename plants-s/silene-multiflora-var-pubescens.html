Silene multiflora var. pubescens

Silene multiflora var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mullein, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in fine, soft hairs.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its distinctive floral structures and ecological importance to pollinators.
