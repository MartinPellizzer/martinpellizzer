Siphoneranthemum cooperi

Siphoneranthemum cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's siphoneranthemum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
