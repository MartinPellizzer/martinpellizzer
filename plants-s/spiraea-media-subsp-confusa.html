Spiraea media subsp. confusa

Spiraea media subsp.

confusa, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features slender, arching stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white or pale pink flowers.

The leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and have a matte green surface, providing a pleasing contrast to the showy blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife.
