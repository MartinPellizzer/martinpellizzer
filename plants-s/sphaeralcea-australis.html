Sphaeralcea australis

Sphaeralcea australis, commonly known as the coastal globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular, yellow flower heads that resemble small globes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal and inland desert environments.

Sphaeralcea australis is an important plant in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
