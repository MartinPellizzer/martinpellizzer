Stadtmannia oppositifolia subsp. rhodesica

Stadtmannia oppositifolia subsp.

rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodesian starflower, is a small shrub native to southern Africa. It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
