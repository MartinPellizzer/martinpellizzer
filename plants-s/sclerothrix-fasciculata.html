Sclerothrix fasciculata

Sclerothrix fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered crust lichen, is a foliose lichen that forms dense, crust-like growths on rock surfaces.

It is characterized by its tightly packed, pale green to yellowish thallus with a distinctive, fibrous texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in temperate and tropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as soredia, are dispersed through wind and water.

Sclerothrix fasciculata plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its ecological niche.
