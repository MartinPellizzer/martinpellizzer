Saxifraga aconitifolia

Saxifraga aconitifolia, commonly known as the blue saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit with fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wool. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Saxifraga aconitifolia is often found in mountain meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
