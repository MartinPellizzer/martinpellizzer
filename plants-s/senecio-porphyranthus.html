Senecio porphyranthus

Senecio porphyranthus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a tall, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a conical shape at the top of the stem.

Senecio porphyranthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
