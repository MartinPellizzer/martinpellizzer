Symplocos racemosa

Symplocos racemosa, commonly known as the Indian snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers and red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
