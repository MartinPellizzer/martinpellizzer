Statice delicatula subsp. raddiana

Statice delicatula subsp.

raddiana, commonly known as the slender sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal scrub habitats. Its narrow leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for coastal gardens and floral arrangements.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.
