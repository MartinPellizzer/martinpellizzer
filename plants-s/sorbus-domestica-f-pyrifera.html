Sorbus domestica f. pyrifera

Sorbus domestica f. pyrifera, commonly known as the common whitebeam or European whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are usually oval or elliptical with a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
