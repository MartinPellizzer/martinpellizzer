Silene mellifera subsp. nevadensis

Silene mellifera subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Silene mellifera subsp.

nevadensis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions of Nevada and adjacent states.
