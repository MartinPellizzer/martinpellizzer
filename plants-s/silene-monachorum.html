Silene monachorum

Silene monachorum, commonly known as the monk's sorrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branching.

Silene monachorum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
