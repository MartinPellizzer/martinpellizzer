Satureja ericifolia subsp. rupestris

Satureja ericifolia subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as rock sage or mountain thyme, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant features aromatic, small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale pink to purple and appear in terminal clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer. Satureja ericifolia subsp.

rupestris is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
