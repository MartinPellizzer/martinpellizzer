Simaba glandulifera

Simaba glandulifera, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerocarpus glanduliferus*, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
