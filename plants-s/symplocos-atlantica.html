Symplocos atlantica

Symplocos atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for various bird species.

Symplocos atlantica is valued in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
