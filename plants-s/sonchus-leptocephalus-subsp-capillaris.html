Sonchus leptocephalus subsp. capillaris

Sonchus leptocephalus subsp.

capillaris, commonly known as slenderleaf sowthistle, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is characterized by its upright stem and alternate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and may have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain insects and livestock.
