Scutellaria laeteviolacea var. maekawae

Scutellaria laeteviolacea var. maekawae, commonly known as the Maekawa blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
