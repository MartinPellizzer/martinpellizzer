Sasa nipponica

Sasa nipponica, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a clump-forming perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, bamboo-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Sasa nipponica is often found in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover or as a decorative element in gardens.
