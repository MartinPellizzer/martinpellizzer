Spirillus lucens

Spirillus lucens, commonly known as the glowing spiral plant, is a unique species characterized by its spiral-shaped leaves that emit a faint bioluminescent glow at night.

This plant is native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its botanical structure features a slender, woody stem with alternating spiral patterns on its surface. The leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Spirillus lucens is noted for its slow growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental and mystical appearance.
