Sycomorus thonningiana

Sycomorus thonningiana, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, clustered flowers and fruits that are important for wildlife, providing food for birds and mammals. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often planted in urban landscapes and conservation areas.
