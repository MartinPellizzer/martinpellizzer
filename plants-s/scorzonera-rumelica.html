Scorzonera rumelica

Scorzonera rumelica, commonly known as the Rumelian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is robust, and the roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera rumelica thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
