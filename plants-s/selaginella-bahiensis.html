Selaginella bahiensis

Selaginella bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with slender, spiky leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella bahiensis is valued for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability and its potential use in horticultural applications.
