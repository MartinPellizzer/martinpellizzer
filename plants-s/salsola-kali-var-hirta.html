Salsola kali var. hirta

Salsola kali var. hirta, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a perennial plant native to the steppes of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.

Its common name reflects its historical use as forage for livestock, though it is more frequently recognized for its invasive tendencies.
