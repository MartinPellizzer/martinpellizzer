Sindora wallichii var. siamensis

Sindora wallichii var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam sindora, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces legumes as its fruit, which contain seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
