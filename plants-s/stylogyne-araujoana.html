Stylogyne araujoana

Stylogyne araujoana, commonly known as the Brazilian starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small shrub with delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest for botanical research and conservation efforts.
