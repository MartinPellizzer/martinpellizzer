Salix matsudana f. tortuosa

Salix matsudana f. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted willow, is a unique cultivar of the weeping willow species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling, and twisting branches that create an ornamental, flowing appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
