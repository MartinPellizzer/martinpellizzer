Setaria boninensis

Setaria boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Bonin Islands and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It is typically found in moist, sandy soils and thrives in coastal environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
