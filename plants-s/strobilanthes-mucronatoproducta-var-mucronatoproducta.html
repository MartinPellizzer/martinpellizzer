Strobilanthes mucronatoproducta var. mucronatoproducta

Strobilanthes mucronatoproducta var. mucronatoproducta, commonly known as the Malabar flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar to various pollinators.
