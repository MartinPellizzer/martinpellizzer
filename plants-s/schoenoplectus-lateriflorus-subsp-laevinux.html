Schoenoplectus lateriflorus subsp. laevinux

Schoenoplectus lateriflorus subsp.

laevinux, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its role in pollination and ecological diversity. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important species in wetland restoration projects.

Its botanical structure supports a variety of aquatic and terrestrial wildlife, enhancing the ecological stability of its habitat.
