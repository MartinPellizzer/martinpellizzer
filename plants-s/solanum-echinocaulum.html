Solanum echinocaulum

Solanum echinocaulum, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum echinocaulum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain regions.
