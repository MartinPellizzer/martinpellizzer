Schnella spicata

Schnella spicata, commonly known as the spiked bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, showy blooms.
