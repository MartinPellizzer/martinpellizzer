Staurogyne zeylanica

Staurogyne zeylanica, commonly known as the Java water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, branching stems that often bear small, opposite leaves. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in slow-moving or still freshwater environments. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, with four to five petals.

Staurogyne zeylanica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
