Solanum igneum

Solanum igneum, commonly known as the fiery nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers. The plant produces small, toxic berries that are typically orange or yellow when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
