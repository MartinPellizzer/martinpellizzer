Solidago amplexicaulis

Solidago amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow blooms. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are clasping, wrapping around the stem, which gives it its common name.

The flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
