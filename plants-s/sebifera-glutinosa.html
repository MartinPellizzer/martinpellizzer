Sebifera glutinosa

Sebifera glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky-seed tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, sticky seeds that adhere to the fur of animals, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
