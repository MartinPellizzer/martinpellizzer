Scirpus velatus

Scirpus velatus, commonly known as the silky bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams. The plant produces feathery, silky flower spikes that appear in late summer, giving it its common name. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and filter water.

Scirpus velatus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
