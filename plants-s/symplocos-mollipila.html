Symplocos mollipila

Symplocos mollipila, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Symplocos mollipila thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
