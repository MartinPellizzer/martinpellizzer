Sida betulifolia

Sida betulifolia, commonly known as the buckthorn sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Sida betulifolia is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
