Serapias latifolia atrorubens

Serapias latifolia atrorubens, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and striking red-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
