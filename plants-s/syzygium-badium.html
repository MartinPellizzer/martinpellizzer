Syzygium badium

Syzygium badium, commonly known as the white apple or white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a fragrant appearance. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant aroma when crushed.

Syzygium badium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
