Silene vulgaris subsp. megalosperma

Silene vulgaris subsp.

megalosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more robust seeds compared to other subspecies of Silene vulgaris.
