Silene foetida subsp. gayana

Silene foetida subsp.

gayana, commonly known as the stinking nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, strongly scented flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in shrubland or forest margins. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branched.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting nocturnal pollinators and its adaptation to arid environments.
