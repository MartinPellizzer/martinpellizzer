Satureja alpina var. suaveolens

Satureja alpina var. suaveolens, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong, minty fragrance.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb for its distinctive flavor.
