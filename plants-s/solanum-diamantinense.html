Solanum diamantinense

Solanum diamantinense, commonly known as the Diamond Hill nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and often have a hairy texture, while its fruits are small, spherical berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Solanum diamantinense is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
