Symplocos latouchei

Symplocos latouchei, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red berries, which are rich in vitamin C. Symplocos latouchei thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in traditional medicine and is valued for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
