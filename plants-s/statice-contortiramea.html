Statice contortiramea

Statice contortiramea, commonly known as the twisted glass gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its striking, glass-like appearance due to its stiff, angular leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments.

Statice contortiramea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
