Smilax mazatlanensis

Smilax mazatlanensis, commonly known as the Mazatlan arrowhead, is a species of climbing shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded forest understories.

Smilax mazatlanensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
