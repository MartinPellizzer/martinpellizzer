Saxifraga pyrenaica

Saxifraga pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga pyrenaica is often found in open, sunny habitats where it can thrive with minimal soil coverage.
