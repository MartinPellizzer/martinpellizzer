Schoenoplectus Novae-Angliae

Schoenoplectus novae-angliae is a unique plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. This species is characterized by its grass-like appearance and its ability to thrive in water-saturated environments. It plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife. Due to its ecological importance, it is often studied for its potential in wetland restoration projects.
