Scirpus orgyalis

Scirpus orgyalis, commonly known as the tall bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and cylindrical, spike-like flower heads. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help stabilize soil, making it an important species for wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus orgyalis plays a role in filtering water and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
