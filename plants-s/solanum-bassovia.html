Solanum bassovia

Solanum bassovia, commonly known as the Bassovia nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. Its fruit is a fleshy berry that changes color from green to yellow as it matures.

Solanum bassovia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.
