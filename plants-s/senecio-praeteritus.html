Senecio praeteritus

Senecio praeteritus, commonly known as the slender senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, featuring narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
