Simaba cuspidata var. nigrescens

Simaba cuspidata var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-leafed simaba, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that turn almost black in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
