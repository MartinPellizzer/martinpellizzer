Struthanthus avicularius

Struthanthus avicularius, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and dry slopes. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

The species is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, showcasing resilience and efficient water conservation mechanisms.
