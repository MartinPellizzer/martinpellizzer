Saxifraga × lecomtei

Saxifraga × lecomtei, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga × lecomtei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in garden settings with similar environmental conditions.
