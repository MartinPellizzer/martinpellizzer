Stephania japonica var. australis

Stephania japonica var. australis, commonly known as the Australian Japanese stephania, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
