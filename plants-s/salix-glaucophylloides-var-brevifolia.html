Salix glaucophylloides var. brevifolia

Salix glaucophylloides var. brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf willow, is a variant of the glaucophylloides willow species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to specific regions of Asia, particularly in China. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving them a distinctive bluish-gray appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The shortleaf willow is known for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
