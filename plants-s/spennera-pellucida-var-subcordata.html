Spennera pellucida var. subcordata

Spennera pellucida var. subcordata, commonly known as the waxy leaf spennera, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the canopy of rainforests. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by yellowish fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
