Sporobolus pectinatus

Sporobolus pectinatus, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with fluffy, pale blue to white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Sporobolus pectinatus is also valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage plant for livestock.
