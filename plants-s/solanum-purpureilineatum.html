Solanum purpureilineatum

Solanum purpureilineatum, commonly known as the purple-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its striking purple foliage and small, clustered white flowers. It produces berries that range in color from green to red, depending on their maturity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
