Scabiosa saxatilis subsp. grosii

Scabiosa saxatilis subsp.

grosii, commonly known as the rock scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
