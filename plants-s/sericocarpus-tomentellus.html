Sericocarpus tomentellus

Sericocarpus tomentellus, commonly known as the hairy silverweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by distinctive, bur-like fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Sericocarpus tomentellus is adapted to cold climates and is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
