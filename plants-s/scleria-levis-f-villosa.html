Scleria levis f. villosa

Scleria levis f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy marsh sedge, is a variant of the marsh sedge species native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy texture.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
