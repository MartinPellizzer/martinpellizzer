Solanum canescens

Solanum canescens, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, fleshy berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Solanum canescens belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
