Salvia fuscomanicata

Salvia fuscomanicata, commonly known as the black man's mint, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly minty scent when crushed.

Salvia fuscomanicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens.
