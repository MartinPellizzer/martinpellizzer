Scleria catophylla var. chondrocarpa

Scleria catophylla var. chondrocarpa, commonly known as the purple-rooted sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and distinctive, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, often with a purple hue, which is a key identifying feature.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
