Syzygium compongense

Syzygium compongense, commonly known as the white clydesdale, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, white, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a pleasant fragrance.

Syzygium compongense is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and aromatic properties.
