Serjania meridionalis var. foveata

Serjania meridionalis var. foveata, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
