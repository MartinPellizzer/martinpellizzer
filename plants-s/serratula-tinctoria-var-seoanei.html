Serratula tinctoria var. seoanei

Serratula tinctoria var. seoanei, commonly known as the Spanish tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. It has been historically used for its medicinal properties and as a dye plant due to its pigments.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other varieties of Serratula.
