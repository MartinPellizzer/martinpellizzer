Senecio citrinus

Senecio citrinus, commonly known as the yellow pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, spherical flower heads that resemble small pincushions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green leaves beneath the flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio citrinus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
