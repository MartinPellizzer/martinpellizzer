Senecio pseudoorientalis

Senecio pseudoorientalis, commonly known as the Eastern False Knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
