Scleria depauperata

Scleria depauperata, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Scleria depauperata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
