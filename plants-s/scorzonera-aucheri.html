Scorzonera aucheri

Scorzonera aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian scurvygrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features a robust taproot that is often harvested for its edible, tuberous roots. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Scorzonera aucheri is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
