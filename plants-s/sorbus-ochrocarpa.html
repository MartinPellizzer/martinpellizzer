Sorbus ochrocarpa

Sorbus ochrocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-leafed whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
