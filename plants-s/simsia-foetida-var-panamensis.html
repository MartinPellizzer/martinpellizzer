Simsia foetida var. panamensis

Simsia foetida var. panamensis, commonly known as the Panama stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, unpleasant odor, which is a distinguishing feature. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and scent, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
