Satureja menthifolia

Satureja menthifolia, commonly known as wild thyme or creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications for their pungent, thyme-like flavor.

Satureja menthifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
