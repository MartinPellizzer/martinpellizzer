Senecillis songarica

Senecillis songarica, commonly known as the Songaric Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

Senecillis songarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
