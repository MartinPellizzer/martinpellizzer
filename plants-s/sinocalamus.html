Sinocalamus

Sinocalamus, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo, is a genus of grass-like plants native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The genus includes several species, such as Sinocalamus fuscus and Sinocalamus tomentosus, which are often found in moist, mountainous regions. These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Their growth habit and ecological role make them important components of their native habitats.
