Scirpus lacustris var. foliosus

Scirpus lacustris var. foliosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a wetland plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and the edges of lakes. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while its flowering stems are often reddish-brown and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
