Sedum moorcroftianum

Sedum moorcroftianum, commonly known as the Moorcroft's stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
