Saxifraga cespitosa subsp. rosacea

Saxifraga cespitosa subsp.

rosacea, commonly known as the Rosy saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense, low-growing mats.

The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to cold climates and has a compact growth form to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species within its native habitat.
