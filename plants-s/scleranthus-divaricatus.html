Scleranthus divaricatus

Scleranthus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable sicklegrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

It is often found in temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
