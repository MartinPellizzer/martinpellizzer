Stachys orientalls

Stachys orientalls, commonly known as oriental betony, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is noted for its purported calming and healing properties.
