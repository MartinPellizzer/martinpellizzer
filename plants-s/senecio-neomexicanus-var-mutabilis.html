Senecio neomexicanus var. mutabilis

Senecio neomexicanus var. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
