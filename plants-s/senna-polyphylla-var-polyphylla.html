Senna polyphylla var. polyphylla

Senna polyphylla var. polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved senna, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The seeds of Senna polyphylla var. polyphylla are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
