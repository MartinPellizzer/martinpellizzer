Solidago curtisii

Solidago curtisii, commonly known as Curtis's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces bright yellow flowers in late summer and early fall, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Curtis's goldenrod is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
