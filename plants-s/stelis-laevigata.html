Stelis laevigata

Stelis laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its stems are short and stout, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red veins.
