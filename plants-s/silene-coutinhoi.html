Silene coutinhoi

Silene coutinhoi, commonly known as the Coutinho's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas. It has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a raceme.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Silene coutinhoi is considered a rare and endangered species.
