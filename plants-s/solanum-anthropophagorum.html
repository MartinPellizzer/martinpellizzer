Solanum anthropophagorum

Solanum anthropophagorum, commonly known as the "man-eating nightshade," is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not cultivated for food or medicinal use.
