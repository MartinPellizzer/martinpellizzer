Satureja cuneifolia var. canescens

Satureja cuneifolia var. canescens, commonly known as silver leaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in Mediterranean cuisine for its aromatic, slightly peppery flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other herbs like rosemary and oregano.
