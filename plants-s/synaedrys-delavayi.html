Synaedrys delavayi

Synaedrys delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high elevations.

Synaedrys delavayi is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
