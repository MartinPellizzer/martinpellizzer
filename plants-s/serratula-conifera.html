Serratula conifera

Serratula conifera, commonly known as the needle-leaved serratula, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
