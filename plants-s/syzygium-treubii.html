Syzygium treubii

Syzygium treubii, commonly known as the Java apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red or yellow fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. Syzygium treubii is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
