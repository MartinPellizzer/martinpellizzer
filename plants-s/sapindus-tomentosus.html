Sapindus tomentosus

Sapindus tomentosus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, spiny fruits that contain a single seed. Its fruit is notable for containing a soap-like substance, which has been traditionally used for cleaning purposes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
