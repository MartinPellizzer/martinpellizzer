Salix hastata var. tenuifolia

Salix hastata var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshes. Its catkins are a key feature, with male and female flowers appearing on separate branches.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
