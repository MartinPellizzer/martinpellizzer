Saxifraga × kellereri

Saxifraga × kellereri, commonly known as the rockfoil, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or well-drained soil conditions, often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are usually succulent, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments.

Saxifraga × kellereri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
