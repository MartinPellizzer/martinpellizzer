Scirpus lanceiglumis

Scirpus lanceiglumis, commonly known as the narrow leaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, spiky flower heads that emerge from water or moist soil. The plant thrives in shallow water and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its rhizomes help stabilize the soil, making it an important species for wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus lanceiglumis is also used in phytoremediation to help filter pollutants from water.
