Silene vulgaris f. polypetala

Silene vulgaris f. polypetala, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant characterized by its pink to purple petals and showy inflorescence.

It is a perennial species native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces a tall, branching stem that bears numerous flowers. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
