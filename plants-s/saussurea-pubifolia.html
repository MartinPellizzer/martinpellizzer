Saussurea pubifolia

Saussurea pubifolia, commonly known as the hairy saffron.

It is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, characterized by its tall, branched stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated by insects, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
