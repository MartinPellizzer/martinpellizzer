Scleria pterota, commonly known as the broad-leaved bulbous grass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and underground rhizomes. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
