Sclerolaena cristata

Sclerolaena cristata, commonly known as the crested screwtree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Sclerolaena cristata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
