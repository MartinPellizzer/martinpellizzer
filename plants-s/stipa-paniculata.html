Stipa paniculata

Stipa paniculata, commonly known as the panicled feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that adds to its ornamental value.

Stipa paniculata is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
