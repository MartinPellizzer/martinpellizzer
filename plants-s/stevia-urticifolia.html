Stevia urticifolia

Stevia urticifolia, commonly known as Brazilian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates, making it adaptable to different environments. Its leaves contain high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages.

Stevia urticifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
