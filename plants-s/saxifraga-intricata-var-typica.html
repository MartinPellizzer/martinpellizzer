Saxifraga intricata var. typica

Saxifraga intricata var. typica, commonly known as the intricate saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically succulent, with a textured surface that aids in water retention in its harsh environment.
