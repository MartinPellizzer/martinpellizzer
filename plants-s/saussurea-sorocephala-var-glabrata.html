Saussurea sorocephala var. glabrata

Saussurea sorocephala var. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and produces tall, branched stems with narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

This variant is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, setting it apart from other varieties of Saussurea.
