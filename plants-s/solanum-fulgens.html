Solanum fulgens

Solanum fulgens, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a reddish throat. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Solanum fulgens belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
