Sedum banlanense

Sedum banlanense, commonly known as the Banlanese stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are often thick and waxy, aiding in water retention.

Sedum banlanense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
