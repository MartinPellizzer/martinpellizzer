Scabiosa taygetea subsp. taygetea

Scabiosa taygetea subsp.

taygetea, commonly known as the Greek ladies' mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountains of Greece. It features rosettes of gray-green, woolly leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers with five petals, resembling small balls, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
