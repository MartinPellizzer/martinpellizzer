Shorea asahi

Shorea asahi, commonly known as the Asahi ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forests, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a prominent midrib.

The flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in spike-like inflorescences, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.
