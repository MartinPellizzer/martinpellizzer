Scirpus lineatus f. elongatus

Scirpus lineatus f. elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush characterized by its elongated, slender stems.

It is a perennial wetland plant that thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clumps.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
