Stachys recta subsp. leucoglossa

Stachys recta subsp.

leucoglossa, commonly known as white-lipped betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly toothed margin.

The plant produces spikes of showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
