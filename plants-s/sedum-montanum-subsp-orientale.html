Sedum montanum subsp. orientale

Sedum montanum subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the eastern stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to parts of Asia and the Middle East. It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
