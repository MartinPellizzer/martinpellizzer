Stevia subpubescens var. subpubescens

Stevia subpubescens var. subpubescens, commonly known as the lesser stevia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is valued for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides, making it a natural sweetener.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
