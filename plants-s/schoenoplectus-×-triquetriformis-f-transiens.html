Schoenoplectus × triquetriformis f. transiens

Schoenoplectus × triquetriformis f. transiens, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a hybrid wetland plant that thrives in shallow water or moist soils.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along the margins of slow-moving water bodies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that provide habitat for aquatic life.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and filtering water.
