Scirpus leptus

Scirpus leptus, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus leptus plays an important role in providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
