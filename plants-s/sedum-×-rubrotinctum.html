Sedum × rubrotinctum

Sedum × rubrotinctum, commonly known as the Crimson Blush Stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the eastern United States.

It features fleshy, upright stems and produces clusters of vibrant pink to red flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Sedum × rubrotinctum prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance once established.
