Salacia staudtiana

Salacia staudtiana, commonly known as the Staudt's salacia, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color.

Salacia staudtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
