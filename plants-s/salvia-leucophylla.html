Salvia leucophylla

Salvia leucophylla, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of pale blue to white flowers. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its fragrant leaves and flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and traditional medicinal uses.
