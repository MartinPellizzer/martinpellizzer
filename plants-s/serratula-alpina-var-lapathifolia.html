Serratula alpina var. lapathifolia

Serratula alpina var. lapathifolia, commonly known as alpine sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Serratula alpina var. lapathifolia is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
