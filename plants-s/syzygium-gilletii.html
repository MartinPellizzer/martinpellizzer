Syzygium gilletii

Syzygium gilletii, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Syzygium gilletii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
