Sabina morrisonicola

Sabina morrisonicola, commonly known as Morrison's juniper, is a species of juniper native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree, typically growing to a height of 1 to 5 meters. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed, forming a dense, compact structure. Its cones are small and berry-like, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
