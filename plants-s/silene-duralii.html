Silene duralii

Silene duralii, commonly known as the Durali's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene duralii is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline environments.
