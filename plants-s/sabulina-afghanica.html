Sabulina afghanica

Sabulina afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan sandwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to survive in arid and rocky environments.

Sabulina afghanica is often found in dry, gravelly soils and is well-suited to harsh alpine conditions.
