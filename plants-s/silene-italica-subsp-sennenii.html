Silene italica subsp. sennenii

Silene italica subsp.

sennenii, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Silene italica subsp.

sennenii is often found in southern Europe, including parts of Italy and Greece.
