Souroubea dasystachya

Souroubea dasystachya, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, similar to those of the common dandelion. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
