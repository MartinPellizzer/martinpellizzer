Saussurea longifolia

Saussurea longifolia, commonly known as the Indian saffron or the Himalayan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of yellow to orange-colored flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin known for its medicinal properties. Its botanical features include fibrous roots, narrow lance-shaped leaves, and a complex inflorescence structure.

It is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic uses in traditional medicine systems.
