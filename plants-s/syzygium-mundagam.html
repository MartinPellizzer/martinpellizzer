Syzygium mundagam

Syzygium mundagam, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

Syzygium mundagam is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in woodworking and perfumery.
