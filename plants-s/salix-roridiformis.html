Salix roridiformis

Salix roridiformis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, giving it a distinctive and elegant look. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery-green upper surface and a white underside. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

This species is often found near water sources and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
