Setaria liebmannii f. trichorhachis

Setaria liebmannii f. trichorhachis, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and dense, compact growth habit.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides. The plant has a slender stem with a basal rosette of leaves, and its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
