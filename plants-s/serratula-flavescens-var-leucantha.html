Serratula flavescens var. leucantha

Serratula flavescens var. leucantha, commonly known as the white serreta, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are yellow to white in color, with slender, serrated leaves that give the species its common name.

It is often found in Mediterranean regions and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.
