Solidago flexuosa

Solidago flexuosa, commonly known as the creeping goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread across the ground. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are bright yellow, clustered in panicles, and bloom in late summer to early autumn.
