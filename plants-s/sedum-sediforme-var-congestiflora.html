Sedum sediforme var. congestiflora

Sedum sediforme var. congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the eastern United States.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, upright stems that produce small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and drought-tolerant.
