Secamone whytei

Secamone whytei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has elliptic to ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped and emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Secamone whytei is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
