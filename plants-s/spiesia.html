Spiesia

Spiesia, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and broad, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant is native to wetlands and marshy areas in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Spiesia species are often found growing in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.

Its flowers are small and clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.
