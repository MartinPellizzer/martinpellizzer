Symplocos mathewsii

Symplocos mathewsii, commonly known as Mathews' waxleaf.

It is a shrub native to the Western Ghats of India, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos mathewsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
