Sanvitalia acinifolia

Sanvitalia acinifolia, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to autumn.
