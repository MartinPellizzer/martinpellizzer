Suaeda fruticosa var. purpurascens

Suaeda fruticosa var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to saline and drought conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments. Its leaves are often coated with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
