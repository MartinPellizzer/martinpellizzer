Syzygium tenuirhachis

Syzygium tenuirhachis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round, sour fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and horticultural studies.
