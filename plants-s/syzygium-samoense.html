Syzygium samoense

Syzygium samoense, commonly known as the Samoan apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the islands of Samoa and nearby regions in the Pacific.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red or yellow fruits resembling small apples.

Syzygium samoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in traditional dishes.
