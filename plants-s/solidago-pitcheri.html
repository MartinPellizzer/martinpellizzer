Solidago pitcheri

Solidago pitcheri, commonly known as the Pitcher's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solidago pitcheri is also noted for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
