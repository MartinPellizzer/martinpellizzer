Stachys labiosa var. zepcensis

Stachys labiosa var. zepcensis, commonly known as the Zepcensian betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
