Scaevola saligna f. longifolia

Scaevola saligna f. longifolia, commonly known as the silver bush or coastal scaevola, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a compact shrub with silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and pink, which bloom throughout the year. It is well adapted to sandy soils and salt spray, making it a hardy choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a popular plant for landscaping in arid and coastal environments.
