Solidago corymbosa

Solidago corymbosa, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows, prairies, and along roadsides.

Solidago corymbosa plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
