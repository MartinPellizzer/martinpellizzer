Sesleria calcaria var. elongata

Sesleria calcaria var. elongata, commonly known as the long-leaved rock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive basal rosette growth habit. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or limestone-rich habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
