Salvia cuspidata subsp. gilliesii

Salvia cuspidata subsp.

gilliesii, commonly known as Gillies' sage, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a gray-green hue and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
