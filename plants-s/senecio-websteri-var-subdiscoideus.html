Senecio websteri var. subdiscoideus

Senecio websteri var. subdiscoideus, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary strategies for survival in extreme climates.
