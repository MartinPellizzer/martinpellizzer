Stachys herrerana

Stachys herrerana, commonly known as the Herreran hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Stachys herrerana is noted for its hairy foliage and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
