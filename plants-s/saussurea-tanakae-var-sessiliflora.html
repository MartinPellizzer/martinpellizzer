Saussurea tanakae var. sessiliflora

Saussurea tanakae var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and sessile, giving it the varietal name *sessiliflora*.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
