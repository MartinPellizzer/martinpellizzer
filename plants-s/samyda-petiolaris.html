Samyda petiolaris

Samyda petiolaris, commonly known as the slender petiole sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Samyda petiolaris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
