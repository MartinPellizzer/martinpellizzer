Saccolabium pseudodistichum

Saccolabium pseudodistichum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its woody stems are strong and durable, making it a valuable resource in traditional medicine and for construction.

Saccolabium pseudodistichum is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
