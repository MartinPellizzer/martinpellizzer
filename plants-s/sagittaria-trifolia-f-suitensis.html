Sagittaria trifolia f. suitensis

Sagittaria trifolia f. suitensis, commonly known as the suitensis arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, adapting to aquatic conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge above the water surface, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in shallow, still waters with nutrient-rich soil.

This form is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
