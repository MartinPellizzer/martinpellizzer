Sagina apetala var. peduncularis

Sagina apetala var. peduncularis, commonly known as the pedunculate sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, closely spaced, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is closely related to other Sagina species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
