Scirpus subprolifer

Scirpus subprolifer, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, usually brownish or reddish flowers, which are clustered at the top of the stem. Scirpus subprolifer is often found in shallow water or moist soils, playing a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable conditions.
