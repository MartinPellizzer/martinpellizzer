Sabicea homblei

Sabicea homblei, commonly known as the Hombli's sabicea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
