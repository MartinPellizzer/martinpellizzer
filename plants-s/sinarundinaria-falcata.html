Sinarundinaria falcata

Sinarundinaria falcata, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound fronds that are typically bright green and have a distinctive, narrow, sickle-shaped appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock faces or in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to rocky substrates, while its spores are released from sporangia located on the undersides of the fronds.

Sinarundinaria falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
