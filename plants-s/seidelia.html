Seidelia

Seidelia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that resemble sea urchins, often with bright blue or purple hues. The plants are typically found in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing their adaptability to harsh environments. They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
