Salix cordata f. subintegra

Salix cordata f. subintegra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a variant of the narrow-leaved willow characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often serrated leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and woodland edges. Its catkins are an important food source for pollinators, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The foliage provides habitat for various insect species, contributing to its ecological significance.
