Swartzia fistuloides

Swartzia fistuloides, commonly known as the African ebony, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and is often found in lowland rainforests. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in some regions.
