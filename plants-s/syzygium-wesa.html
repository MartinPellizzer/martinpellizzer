Syzygium wesa

Syzygium wesa, commonly known as the Wesa apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits resembling apples in appearance. The fruits are typically yellow or red when ripe and are valued for their sweet, aromatic flavor.

Syzygium wesa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
