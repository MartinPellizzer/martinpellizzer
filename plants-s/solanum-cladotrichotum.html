Solanum cladotrichotum

Solanum cladotrichotum, commonly known as the hairy leaf nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its hairy foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with compound leaves that are often glandular and covered in fine hairs. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing several seeds.

Solanum cladotrichotum is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, including dry and disturbed soils.
