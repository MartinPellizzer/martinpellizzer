Serratula tinctoria var. monticola

Serratula tinctoria var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain sowthistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils. It has a taproot system and can grow up to 60 cm in height.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in dyeing, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
