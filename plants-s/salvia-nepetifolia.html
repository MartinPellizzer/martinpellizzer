Salvia nepetifolia

Salvia nepetifolia, commonly known as catmint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a minty, slightly bitter aroma.

Salvia nepetifolia is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
