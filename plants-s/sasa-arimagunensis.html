Sasa arimagunensis

Sasa arimagunensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Sasa arimagunensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
