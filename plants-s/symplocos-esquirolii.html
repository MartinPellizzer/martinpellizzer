Symplocos esquirolii

Symplocos esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's waxleaf, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with thick, waxy leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red drupes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
