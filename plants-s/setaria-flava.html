Setaria flava

Setaria flava, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that turn golden yellow when mature. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Setaria flava is also an important forage plant for livestock in some regions.
