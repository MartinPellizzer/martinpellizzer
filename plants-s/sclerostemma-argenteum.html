Sclerostemma argenteum

Sclerostemma argenteum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in botany and horticulture.
