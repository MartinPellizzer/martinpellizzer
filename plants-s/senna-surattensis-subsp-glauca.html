Senna surattensis subsp. glauca

Senna surattensis subsp.

glauca, commonly known as Indian senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.
