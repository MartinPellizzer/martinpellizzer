Solanum gussonei

Solanum gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in scrublands or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
