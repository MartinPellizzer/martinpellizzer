Sericographis macrophylla

Sericographis macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf sericographis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach several inches in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
