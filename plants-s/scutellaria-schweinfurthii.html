Scutellaria schweinfurthii

Scutellaria schweinfurthii, commonly known as Schweinfurth's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
