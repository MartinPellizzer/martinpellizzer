Salix commutata var. rubicunda

Salix commutata var. rubicunda, commonly known as the red willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish-brown branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and is typically found in riparian habitats along streams and rivers. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
