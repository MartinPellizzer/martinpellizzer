Sorghum fulvum

Sorghum fulvum, commonly known as the red sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and vibrant red or purple hues in its leaves and stems. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in warm climates, making it a valuable crop in arid regions. Its seeds are used for food, animal feed, and even biofuel production.

Additionally, Sorghum fulvum plays an important role in traditional agriculture and cultural practices in many parts of Africa.
