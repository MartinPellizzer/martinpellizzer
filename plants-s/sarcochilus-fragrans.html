Sarcochilus fragrans

Sarcochilus fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant sarcocarp, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong, sweet scent. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, and the plant thrives in warm, humid environments.

Sarcochilus fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive aroma.
