Scilla clusii var. comata

Scilla clusii var. comata, commonly known as the woolly scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is popular among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
