Strombocactus pulcherrimus

Strombocactus pulcherrimus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to pale blue spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often borne in clusters at the stem tips. Strombocactus pulcherrimus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a favored plant among cactus enthusiasts.
