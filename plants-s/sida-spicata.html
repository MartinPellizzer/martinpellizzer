Sida spicata

Sida spicata, commonly known as coastal spurrey, is an annual herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in saline or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high wind and salt spray. The plant has small, green, ovate leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white flowers. It is often found in dunes, beaches, and coastal prairies.

Sida spicata is known for its ability to stabilize sand dunes and prevent erosion.
