Solanum bipinnatifidum

Solanum bipinnatifidum, commonly known as the purple passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with finely divided lobes, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, purple-tinged fruits. The fruit's skin is thick and leathery, while the interior contains a sweet, aromatic pulp with numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
