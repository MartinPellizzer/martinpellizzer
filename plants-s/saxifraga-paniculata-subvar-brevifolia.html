Saxifraga paniculata subvar. brevifolia

Saxifraga paniculata subvar. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and arranged in a panicle, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
