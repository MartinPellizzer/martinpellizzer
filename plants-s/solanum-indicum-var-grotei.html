Solanum indicum var. grotei

Solanum indicum var. grotei, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
