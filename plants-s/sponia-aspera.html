Sponia aspera

Sponia aspera, commonly known as the rough sponia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Sponia aspera is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
