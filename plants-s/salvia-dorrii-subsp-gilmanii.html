Salvia dorrii subsp. gilmanii

Salvia dorrii subsp.

gilmanii, commonly known as Gilman's sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
