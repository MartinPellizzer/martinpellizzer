Senecio sichotensis

Senecio sichotensis, commonly known as the Sichotens Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Senecio sichotensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh mountain climates.
