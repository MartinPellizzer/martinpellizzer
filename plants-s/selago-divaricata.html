Selago divaricata

Selago divaricata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically arranged in clusters.

Selago divaricata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
