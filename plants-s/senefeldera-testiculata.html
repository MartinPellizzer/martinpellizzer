Senefeldera testiculata

Senefeldera testiculata, commonly known as the testicle grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Senefeldera testiculata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
