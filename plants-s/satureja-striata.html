Satureja striata

Satureja striata, commonly known as striped savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often marked with distinct white or silver stripes. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its strong herbal scent and medicinal properties.

Satureja striata is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies due to its pungent flavor and potential health benefits.
