Stylandra pumila

Stylandra pumila, commonly known as the dwarf stylandra, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish or pale yellow in color.

Stylandra pumila is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in shaded gardens.
