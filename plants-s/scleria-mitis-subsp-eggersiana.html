Scleria mitis subsp. eggersiana

Scleria mitis subsp.

eggersiana, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its adaptability and ecological importance, it is often found in riparian zones and wet meadows.
