Salix jayetiana

Salix jayetiana, commonly known as Jayet's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, particularly in the Alps and the Carpathian Mountains. This shrub or small tree typically grows to a height of 1 to 5 meters, featuring slender branches and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
