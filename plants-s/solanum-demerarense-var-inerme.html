Solanum demerarense var. inerme

Solanum demerarense var. inerme, commonly known as the inermes nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It produces small, white or pale green flowers followed by berries that can be toxic if ingested.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and alternate leaves with a serrated margin.
