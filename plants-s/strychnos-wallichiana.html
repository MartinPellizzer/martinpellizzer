Strychnos wallichiana

Strychnos wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and fruit.
