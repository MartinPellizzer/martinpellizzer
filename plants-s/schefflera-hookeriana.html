Schefflera hookeriana

Schefflera hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped inflorescence that consists of numerous white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster. The plant has compound leaves that are typically divided into several leaflets, giving it a lush, feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Schefflera hookeriana is also valued for its ability to adapt to various light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
