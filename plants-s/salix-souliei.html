Salix souliei

Salix souliei, commonly known as the Sichuan willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its graceful appearance.
