Syringa pubescens var. kamibayashii

Syringa pubescens var. kamibayashii, commonly known as the Japanese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a soft, hairy texture. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its showy blooms and pleasant fragrance.
