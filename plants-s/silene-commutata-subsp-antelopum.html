Silene commutata subsp. antelopum

Silene commutata subsp.

antelopum, commonly known as the antelope's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptability to arid environments.
