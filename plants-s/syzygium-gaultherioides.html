Syzygium gaultherioides

Syzygium gaultherioides, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits resembling small apples. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Syzygium gaultherioides thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland and hill forests.
