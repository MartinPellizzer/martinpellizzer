Sinapis tripinnata

Sinapis tripinnata, commonly known as field mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are usually finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
