Serissa myrtifolia

Serissa myrtifolia, commonly known as the silver serissa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while its stems are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Serissa myrtifolia is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in bonsai and as a decorative plant.
