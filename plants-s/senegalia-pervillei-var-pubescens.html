Senegalia pervillei var. pubescens

Senegalia pervillei var. pubescens, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, fluffy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor, sandy soils.

The silky acacia is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
