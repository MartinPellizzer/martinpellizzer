Salvia bornmuelleri

Salvia bornmuelleri, commonly known as the desert sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring deep roots and drought-resistant foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
