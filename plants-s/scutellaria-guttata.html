Scutellaria guttata

Scutellaria guttata, commonly known as the spotted beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue or purple coloration. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Scutellaria guttata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
