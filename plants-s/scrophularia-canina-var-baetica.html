Scrophularia canina var. baetica

Scrophularia canina var. baetica, commonly known as the Iberian dog's mouth, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with two lips, and are usually pink or purple in color.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
