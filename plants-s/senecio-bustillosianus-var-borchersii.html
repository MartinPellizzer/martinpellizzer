Senecio bustillosianus var. borchersii

Senecio bustillosianus var. borchersii, commonly known as the Borchers' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dryland gardens.
