Sesbania disperma

Sesbania disperma, commonly known as the African sesbania, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically an annual or perennial shrub that can grow up to several meters in height. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution in arid and semi-arid regions.
