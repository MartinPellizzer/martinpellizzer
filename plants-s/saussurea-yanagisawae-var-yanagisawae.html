Saussurea yanagisawae var. yanagisawae

Saussurea yanagisawae var. yanagisawae, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
