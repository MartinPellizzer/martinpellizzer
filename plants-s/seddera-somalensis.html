Seddera somalensis

Seddera somalensis, commonly known as the Somali seddera, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa, particularly Somalia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage is often narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Seddera somalensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
