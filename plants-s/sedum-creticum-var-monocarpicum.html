Sedum creticum var. monocarpicum

Sedum creticum var. monocarpicum, commonly known as the Cretan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in dry landscapes.
