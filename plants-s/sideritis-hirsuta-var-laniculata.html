Sideritis hirsuta var. laniculata

Sideritis hirsuta var. laniculata, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
