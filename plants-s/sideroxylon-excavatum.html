Sideroxylon excavatum

Sideroxylon excavatum, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has a straight trunk and can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually elliptical in shape.

The flowers are small and yellowish, often clustered in panicles, and the plant produces woody capsules as fruit.
