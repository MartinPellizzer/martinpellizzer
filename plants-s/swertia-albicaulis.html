Swertia albicaulis

Swertia albicaulis, commonly known as the white-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Swertia albicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
