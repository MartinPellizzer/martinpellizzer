Seriphidium terrae-albae var. kurdaicum

Seriphidium terrae-albae var. kurdaicum, commonly known as the Kurdish white soil wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
