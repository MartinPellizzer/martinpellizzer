Silene ordossica

Silene ordossica, commonly known as the Ordos sandcrane, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of northern China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to harsh, sandy environments, with deep roots that help it survive in low-water conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss.

Silene ordossica plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
