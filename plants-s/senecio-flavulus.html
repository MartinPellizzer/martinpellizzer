Senecio flavulus

Senecio flavulus, commonly known as the yellow senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio flavulus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and vibrant color.
