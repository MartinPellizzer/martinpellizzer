Serratula semiserrata

Serratula semiserrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, serrated leaves and produces yellow composite flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

Serratula semiserrata is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
