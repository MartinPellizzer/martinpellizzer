Stipa calamagrostis subsp. hirsuta

Stipa calamagrostis subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as hairy false beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
