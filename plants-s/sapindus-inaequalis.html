Sapindus inaequalis

Sapindus inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal soapberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a soap-like substance, which has been used traditionally for cleaning purposes. Its bark and leaves are also utilized in local medicinal practices for their potential therapeutic properties.

The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
