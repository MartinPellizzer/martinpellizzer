Sabia obovatifolia

Sabia obovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf sabia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its robust, clustered growth habit. The plant features large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. Its inflorescences are clustered at the base of the plant, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Sabia obovatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in warm, humid environments.
