Satureja myriantha

Satureja myriantha, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and emit a strong, peppery scent when crushed.

Satureja myriantha is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
