Solanum virgatum

Solanum virgatum, commonly known as the Virginia nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, often containing toxic compounds.
