Scleria chasmema

Scleria chasmema, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows, where it thrives in full to partial sunlight. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Scleria chasmema is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability.
