Saxifraga pentadactylis var. caballeroi

Saxifraga pentadactylis var. caballeroi, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Sierra Nevada mountains in California.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are generally basal, with a broad, rounded shape and a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species adapted to alpine ecosystems.
