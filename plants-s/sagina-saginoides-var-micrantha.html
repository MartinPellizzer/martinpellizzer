Sagina saginoides var. micrantha

Sagina saginoides var. micrantha, commonly known as moss campion, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, closely packed leaves and tiny, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine and tundra ecosystems.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in horticultural settings for ground cover in cool climates.
