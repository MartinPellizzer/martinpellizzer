Sasa persimilis

Sasa persimilis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or silver bamboo, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often covered in a silvery or grayish sheen. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Sasa persimilis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
