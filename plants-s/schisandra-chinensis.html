Schisandra chinensis

Schisandra chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese five-fruit or five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red berries that contain five distinct flavors—sweet, sour, bitter, spicy, and umami—hence its common name. The plant belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine. Its fruits are harvested when fully ripe and used in various health supplements and culinary applications.

Schisandra chinensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate forest environments.
