Searsia incisa var. effusa

Searsia incisa var. effusa, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant has small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
