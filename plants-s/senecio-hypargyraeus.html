Senecio hypargyraeus

Senecio hypargyraeus, commonly known as the silverleaf senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
