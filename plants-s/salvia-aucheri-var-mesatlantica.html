Salvia aucheri var. mesatlantica

Salvia aucheri var. mesatlantica, commonly known as the Armenian sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces spikes of blue to violet flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its adaptation to cold, dry climates.
