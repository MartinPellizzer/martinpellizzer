Saxifraga sinomontana var. amabilis

Saxifraga sinomontana var. amabilis, commonly known as the Chinese saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its resilience to cold climates and its ability to bloom in early spring.
