Silene saxifraga

Silene saxifraga, commonly known as the rockpink, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Silene saxifraga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
