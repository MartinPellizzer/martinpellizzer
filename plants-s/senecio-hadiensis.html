Senecio hadiensis

Senecio hadiensis, commonly known as the Natal ragged gentian, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Senecio hadiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
