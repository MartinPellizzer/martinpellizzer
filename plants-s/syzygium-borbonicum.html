Syzygium borbonicum

Syzygium borbonicum, commonly known as the Madagascar apple or borbonica, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its small, round, red or yellow fruits that resemble apples. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy appearance.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and they are often clustered in small groups, contributing to its ornamental value.
