Sasa koiyeana

Sasa koiyeana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in landscaping. Sasa koiyeana is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable environments.
