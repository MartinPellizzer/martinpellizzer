Saussurea delavayi

Saussurea delavayi, commonly known as the Yunnan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of western China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

Saussurea delavayi is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
