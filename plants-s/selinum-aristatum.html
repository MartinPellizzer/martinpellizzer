Selinum aristatum

Selinum aristatum, commonly known as the featherleaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Selinum aristatum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
