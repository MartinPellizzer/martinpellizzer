Scorzonera hispanica var. undulata

Scorzonera hispanica var. undulata, commonly known as the undulating scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green stems and basal rosettes of elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces pale yellow to orange flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Scorzonera hispanica var. undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
