Solanum laeve

Solanum laeve, commonly known as the smooth nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and ovate leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are usually yellow with purple veins, and it produces small, oval berries that turn from green to yellow when ripe.

The plant is notable for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
