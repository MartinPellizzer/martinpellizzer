Syzygium griseum

Syzygium griseum, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Syzygium griseum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and economic importance in the fragrance and pharmaceutical industries.
