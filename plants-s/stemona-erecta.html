Stemona erecta

Stemona erecta, commonly known as the upright stemona, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Stemona erecta is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits and is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
