Serapias × rainei

Serapias × rainei, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Serapias orchid species.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, thriving in moist, calcareous soils. Its floral structure is similar to its parent species, featuring three sepals and three petals, with the lower petal forming a specialized landing platform.

Serapias × rainei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
