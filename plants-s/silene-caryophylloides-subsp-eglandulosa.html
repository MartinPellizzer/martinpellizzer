Silene caryophylloides subsp. eglandulosa

Silene caryophylloides subsp.

eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandless campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
