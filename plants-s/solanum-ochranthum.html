Solanum ochranthum

Solanum ochranthum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Solanum ochranthum is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.
