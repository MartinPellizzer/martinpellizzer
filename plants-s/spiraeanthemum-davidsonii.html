Spiraeanthemum davidsonii

Spiraeanthemum davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's spirea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
