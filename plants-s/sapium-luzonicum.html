Sapium luzonicum

Sapium luzonicum, commonly known as the Philippine sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits that contain seeds. It typically grows in lowland forests and is valued for its timber and ecological role.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
