Scilla amoenula

Scilla amoenula, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Scilla amoenula is also valued for its ability to naturalize and return year after year.
