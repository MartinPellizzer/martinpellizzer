Salvia vergeduzica

Salvia vergeduzica, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia vergeduzica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
