Salix integra f. pendula

Salix integra f. pendula, commonly known as weeping willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It is native to Asia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant features long, slender, drooping branches that create a graceful, flowing appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often planted near water features or in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
