Synaedrys scyphigera

Synaedrys scyphigera, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silvery, fuzzy foliage that helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Synaedrys scyphigera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
