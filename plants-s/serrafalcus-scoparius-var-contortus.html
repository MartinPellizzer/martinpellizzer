Serrafalcus scoparius var. contortus

Serrafalcus scoparius var. contortus, commonly known as the twisted spurge, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted stems that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and alternate along the stem, with a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
