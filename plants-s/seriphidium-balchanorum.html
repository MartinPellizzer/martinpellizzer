Seriphidium balchanorum

Seriphidium balchanorum, commonly known as the Balkan tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has feathery, aromatic leaves and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
