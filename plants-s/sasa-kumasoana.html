Sasa kumasoana

Sasa kumasoana, commonly known as the bamboo grass or mountain bamboo, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant forms dense clumps and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Sasa kumasoana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
