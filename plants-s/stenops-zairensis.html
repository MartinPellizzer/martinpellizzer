Stenops zairensis

Stenops zairensis, commonly known as the Zaire stenops, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or white, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to habitat loss and limited study, very little is known about its ecological role and propagation methods.
