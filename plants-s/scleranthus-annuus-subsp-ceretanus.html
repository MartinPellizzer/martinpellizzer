Scleranthus annuus subsp. ceretanus

Scleranthus annuus subsp.

ceretanus, commonly known as the European scarlet burnet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. Its flowers are usually arranged in dense, spiky inflorescences, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
