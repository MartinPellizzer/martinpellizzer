Solanum dubium var. denseaculeatum

Solanum dubium var. denseaculeatum, commonly known as the denseaculeate nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruit, a small, fleshy berry, is typically yellow or orange when ripe.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is noted for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.
