Salvia xanthophylla

Salvia xanthophylla, commonly known as the yellowflower sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia xanthophylla is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
