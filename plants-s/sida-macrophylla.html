Sida macrophylla

Sida macrophylla, commonly known as broadleaf sida, is a flowering plant species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Sida macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
