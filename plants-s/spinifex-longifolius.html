Spinifex longifolius

Spinifex longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaf spinifex, is a hardy, low-growing plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as grasses, and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dunes.

Spinifex longifolius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
