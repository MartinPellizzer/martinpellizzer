Salix × triandroides

Salix × triandroides, commonly known as the hybrid weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Salix triandra and Salix matsudana, resulting in a plant with distinctive cascading branches that create a weeping appearance. The leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a pale green underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist soils and is often planted near water features for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in wetland environments.
