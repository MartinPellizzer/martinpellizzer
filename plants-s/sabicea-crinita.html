Sabicea crinita

Sabicea crinita, commonly known as the hairy sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its unique feature is the hairy texture of its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name.

Sabicea crinita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
