Scrophularia pyrenaica

Scrophularia pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, with tubular corollas that are pink to purplish in color.

Scrophularia pyrenaica is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in certain regions.
