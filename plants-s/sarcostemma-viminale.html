Sarcostemma viminale

Sarcostemma viminale, commonly known as the creeping milkvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Its milky sap, which is toxic, is a notable feature of its botanical description.
