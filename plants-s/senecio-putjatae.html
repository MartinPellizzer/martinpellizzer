Senecio putjatae

Senecio putjatae, commonly known as the Cape Town groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Senecio putjatae is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
