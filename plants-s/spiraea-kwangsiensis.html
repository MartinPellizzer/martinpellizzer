Spiraea kwangsiensis

Spiraea kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Kwangsi weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, cascading clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a delicate, weeping appearance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and turn yellow in the fall.

Spiraea kwangsiensis is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
