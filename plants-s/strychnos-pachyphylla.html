Strychnos pachyphylla

Strychnos pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved strychnos, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing its seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to survive in harsh, semi-desert conditions.
