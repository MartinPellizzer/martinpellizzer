Sabina zanonii

Sabina zanonii, commonly known as the Zanon's juniper, is a rare and endangered species native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and clustered blue berries. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountainous conditions. Its botanical features include aromatic foliage and a compact growth habit, making it distinct within the Juniperus genus.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
