Solanum plukenetii

Solanum plukenetii, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries that change color as they mature. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in agricultural settings.
