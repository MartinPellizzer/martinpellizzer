Sasa pseudonebulosa

Sasa pseudonebulosa, commonly known as the cloud-like bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Sasa pseudonebulosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in woodland areas.
