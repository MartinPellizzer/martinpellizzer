Senecio condimentarius

Senecio condimentarius, commonly known as the edible groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, and its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant is known for its peppery taste, which gives it its common name.
