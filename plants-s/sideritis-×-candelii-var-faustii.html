Sideritis × candelii var. faustii

Sideritis × candelii var. faustii, commonly known as the Cretan ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in summer. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinct mint-like aroma.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and fragrance.
