Sonchus phoeniciformis

Sonchus phoeniciformis, commonly known as the golden crownflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, roadsides, and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive reddish-brown center.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
