Salvia pendula var. betonicifolia

Salvia pendula var. betonicifolia, commonly known as the hanging sage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, drooping stems that bear small, violet-blue flowers arranged in whorled clusters. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
