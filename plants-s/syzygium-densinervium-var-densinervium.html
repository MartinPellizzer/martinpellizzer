Syzygium densinervium var. densinervium

Syzygium densinervium var. densinervium, commonly known as the dense-veined syzygium, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are densely veined, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
