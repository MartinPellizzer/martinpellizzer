Silene glaucophylla

Silene glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucous leaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, bluish-green leaves and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Silene glaucophylla is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.
