Saxifraga Exarata Subsp. Basaltica

Saxifraga exarata subsp. basaltica is a striking alpine plant known for its vibrant pink and white flowers. It thrives in rocky, high-altitude environments where few other species can survive. This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in areas with basaltic rock formations. Its ability to grow in harsh conditions makes it a symbol of resilience in the plant world. The unique appearance and ecological significance of Saxifraga exarata subsp. basaltica make it a fascinating subject for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
