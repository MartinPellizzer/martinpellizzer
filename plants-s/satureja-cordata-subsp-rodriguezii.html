Satureja cordata subsp. rodriguezii

Satureja cordata subsp.

rodriguezii, commonly known as Rodriguez's savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing small, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, peppery scent. Its flowers are usually pale purple to pink, arranged in whorls along the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other members of the Satureja genus.
