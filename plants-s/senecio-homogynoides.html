Senecio homogynoides

Senecio homogynoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and a sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
