Stipa drobovii var. iskanderkulica

Stipa drobovii var. iskanderkulica, commonly known as the Iskanderkul grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native environment.
