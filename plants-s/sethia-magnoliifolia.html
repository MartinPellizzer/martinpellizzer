Sethia magnoliifolia

Sethia magnoliifolia, commonly known as the golden magnolia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, yellowish-green flowers that resemble those of the magnolia genus. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Sethia magnoliifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
