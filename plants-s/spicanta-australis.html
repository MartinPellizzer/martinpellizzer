Spicanta australis

Spicanta australis, commonly known as the Southern Spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with a slight pubescence.

Spicanta australis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
