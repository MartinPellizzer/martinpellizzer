Sophora kronei

Sophora kronei, commonly known as the Korean silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fluffy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, spongy material. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

Sophora kronei is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
