Serratula indica

Serratula indica, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are rough and lobed, often with a distinctive serrated edge.

Serratula indica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
