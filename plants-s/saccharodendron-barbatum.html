Saccharodendron barbatum

Saccharodendron barbatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Saccharodendron barbatum is often found in disturbed habitats and can become an invasive species in some regions.
