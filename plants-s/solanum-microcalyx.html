Solanum microcalyx

Solanum microcalyx, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a small shrub native to the southern United States.

It produces small, violet-colored berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy flowers.
