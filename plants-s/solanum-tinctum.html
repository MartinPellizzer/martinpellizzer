Solanum tinctum

Solanum tinctum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, with a distinctive scent when crushed.

The flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals and a prominent central stigma.
