Smilax colubrina

Smilax colubrina, commonly known as red-twigged smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive, spiny stems that are often reddish in color, especially during the winter months. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries, which are a food source for various birds and wildlife. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with a waxy coating.

Smilax colubrina thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
