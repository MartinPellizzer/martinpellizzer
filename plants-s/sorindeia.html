Sorindeia

Sorindeia, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a small shrub that thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by water.

Sorindeia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
