Saussurea diamantiaca f. longifolia

Saussurea diamantiaca f. longifolia, commonly known as the longifoliate snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to minimize water loss in its harsh habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
