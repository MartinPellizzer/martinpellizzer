Solidago canescens subsp. gilvocanescens

Solidago canescens subsp.

gilvocanescens, commonly known as the yellowflower goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils.

The plant features erect stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer to early autumn, forming dense, spherical clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
