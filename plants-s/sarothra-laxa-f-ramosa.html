Sarothra laxa f. ramosa

Sarothra laxa f. ramosa, commonly known as the branched or spreading form of the common thyme, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant features narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and prolonged blooming period.
