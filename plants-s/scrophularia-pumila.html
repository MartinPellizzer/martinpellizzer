Scrophularia pumila

Scrophularia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf figwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas. It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal uses.

Scrophularia pumila is also valued in horticulture for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
