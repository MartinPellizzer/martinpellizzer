Scutellaria debeerstii

Scutellaria debeerstii, commonly known as the De Beer's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Scutellaria debeerstii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blossoms.
