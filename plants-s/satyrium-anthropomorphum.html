Satyrium anthropomorphum

Satyrium anthropomorphum, commonly known as the human-like satyr, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a prominent, showy lip that resembles a human face. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, primarily bees and wasps, due to their specialized shape and coloration.

The orchid's botanical significance lies in its intricate morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
