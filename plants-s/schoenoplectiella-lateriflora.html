Schoenoplectiella lateriflora

Schoenoplectiella lateriflora, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stems, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Schoenoplectiella lateriflora thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
