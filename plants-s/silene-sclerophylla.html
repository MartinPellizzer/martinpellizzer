Silene sclerophylla

Silene sclerophylla, commonly known as the hard-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, gray-green leaves that are adapted to store water and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in shrublands or grasslands.

Silene sclerophylla is well-suited to harsh environments due to its drought-resistant traits and efficient water use.
