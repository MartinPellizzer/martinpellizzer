Salix × flichei

Salix × flichei, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix integra and Salix schwerinii, exhibiting a combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces catkins in early spring, which are a key feature of its reproductive biology.

Salix × flichei is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive form and adaptability to various soil conditions.
