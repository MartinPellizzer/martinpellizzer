Scleranthus tenellus

Scleranthus tenellus, commonly known as the slender sickleweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to thrive in saline or sandy environments.

Scleranthus tenellus is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
