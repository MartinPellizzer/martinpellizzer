Stipa sibirica f. pubicalyx

Stipa sibirica f. pubicalyx, commonly known as the Siberian feathergrass, is a grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine or steppe environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall rugged, grass-like appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid regions.
