Salix × semihastata

Salix × semihastata, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern regions of China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly heart-shaped at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
