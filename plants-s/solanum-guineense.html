Solanum guineense

Solanum guineense, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
