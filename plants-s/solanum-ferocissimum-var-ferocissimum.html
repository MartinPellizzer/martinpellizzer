Solanum ferocissimum var. ferocissimum

Solanum ferocissimum var. ferocissimum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces purple-black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Native to South America, it thrives in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
