Sida aprica var. solanacea

Sida aprica var. solanacea, commonly known as the yellow mallow, is a variant of the Sida genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
