Senna pentagonia

Senna pentagonia, commonly known as the five-angled senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five leaflets that are oval-shaped and slightly serrated.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
