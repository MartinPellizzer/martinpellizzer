Sebaea hymenosepala

Sebaea hymenosepala, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Sebaea hymenosepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
