Sophora fulvida

Sophora fulvida, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn bright yellow in the fall, and its clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The tree is relatively fast-growing and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
