Spiranthes llaveana var. violacea

Spiranthes llaveana var. violacea, commonly known as the violet lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, spiraling inflorescence of violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The species is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates.
