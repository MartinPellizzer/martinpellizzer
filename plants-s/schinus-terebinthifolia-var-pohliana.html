Schinus terebinthifolia var. pohliana

Schinus terebinthifolia var. pohliana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and seasonal fruit display.
