Sertifera gracilis

Sertifera gracilis, commonly known as the slender sertifera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Sertifera gracilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
