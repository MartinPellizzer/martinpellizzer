Swartzia alata

Swartzia alata, commonly known as the winged swartzia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its distinctive winged seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. Swartzia alata typically grows in humid, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
