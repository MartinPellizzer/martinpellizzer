Sasa segawana

Sasa segawana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, forming thickets in forest understories. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value. It is often used in landscaping for its ability to create natural barriers and its aesthetic appeal.

Sasa segawana is also valued in traditional Japanese culture for its use in crafts and as a food source.
