Syrenia praevisa

Syrenia praevisa, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a fragrant scent when cut.

Syrenia praevisa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
