Shorea negrosensis

Shorea negrosensis, commonly known as the Negros ebony, is a tropical tree species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface.

The species plays a significant role in the ecological and economic systems of its native habitat.
