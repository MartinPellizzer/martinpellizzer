Scorzoneroides carpetana subsp. duboisii

Scorzoneroides carpetana subsp.

duboisii, commonly known as the Dubois's carpetweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn.

The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restorations.
