Satureja cristata subsp. kosaninii

Satureja cristata subsp.

kosaninii, commonly known as the mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous areas. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
