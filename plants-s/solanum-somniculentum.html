Solanum somniculentum

Solanum somniculentum, commonly known as the sleepy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its berries are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.

The species is characterized by its unique nocturnal flowering habit and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
