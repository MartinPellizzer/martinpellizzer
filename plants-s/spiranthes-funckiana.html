Spiranthes funckiana

Spiranthes funckiana, commonly known as the Funck's spiral orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, elongated seed pods that mature in late summer.

Spiranthes funckiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a pollinator attractant.
