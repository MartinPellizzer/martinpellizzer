Sehima nervosa

Sehima nervosa, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its stems are covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Sehima nervosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
