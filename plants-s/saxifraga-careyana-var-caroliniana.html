Saxifraga careyana var. caroliniana

Saxifraga careyana var. caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly toothed margin and a waxy surface.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
