Simaba guianensis var. schomburgkiana

Simaba guianensis var. schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's simaba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in regions of Venezuela and Guyana.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by ornamental red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
