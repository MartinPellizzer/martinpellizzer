Senna purpurea

Senna purpurea, commonly known as the purple senna, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces striking yellow-orange flowers with prominent black markings. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Senna purpurea is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
