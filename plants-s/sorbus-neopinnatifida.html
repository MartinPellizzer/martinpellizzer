Sorbus neopinnatifida

Sorbus neopinnatifida, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive flaky texture that becomes more pronounced with age.
