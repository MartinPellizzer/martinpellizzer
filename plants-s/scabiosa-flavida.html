Scabiosa flavida

Scabiosa flavida, commonly known as the yellow scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa flavida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
