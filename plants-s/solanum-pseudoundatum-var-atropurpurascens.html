Solanum pseudoundatum var. atropurpurascens

Solanum pseudoundatum var. atropurpurascens, commonly known as the purple-hued nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) characterized by its striking deep purple foliage and flowers.

This plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and oval, pointed leaves. Its flowers are usually small, clustered in umbels, and feature a distinctive purplish hue, complementing the foliage. The fruit is a small, round berry that transitions from green to a vibrant red as it matures.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
