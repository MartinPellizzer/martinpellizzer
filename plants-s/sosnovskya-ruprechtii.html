Sosnovskya ruprechtii

Sosnovskya ruprechtii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy blue to violet flowers in late summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in permafrost conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Sosnovskya ruprechtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in colder regions.
