Sesleria cylindrica subsp. hispanica

Sesleria cylindrica subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish flatleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a tight rosette. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
