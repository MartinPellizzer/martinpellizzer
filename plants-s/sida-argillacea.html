Sida argillacea

Sida argillacea, commonly known as clayplant, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, clay soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

Sida argillacea is known for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
