Selaginella euclimax

Selaginella euclimax, commonly known as the climbing spikemoss, is a vascular fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, branched stems that often form dense mats or climb over other vegetation. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Selaginella euclimax is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in rocky or forested areas.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
