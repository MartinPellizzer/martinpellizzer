Symplocos ophirensis var. lingaensis

Symplocos ophirensis var. lingaensis, commonly known as the Lingaensis Boxwood, is a shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and glossy, providing a dense canopy. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
