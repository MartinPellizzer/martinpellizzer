Satureja cuneifolia subvar. cazorlana

Satureja cuneifolia subvar. cazorlana, commonly known as the Cazorla thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other members of the thyme genus.
