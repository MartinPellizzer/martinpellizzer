Sternbergia clusiana

Sternbergia clusiana, commonly known as the autumn crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet or purple flowers in the fall, typically from September to November. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. Its flowers are composed of three narrow petals and three broader sepals, forming a showy, cup-like structure.

Sternbergia clusiana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
