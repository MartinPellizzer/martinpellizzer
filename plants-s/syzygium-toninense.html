Syzygium toninense

Syzygium toninense, commonly known as the Tonin's apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family, native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

Syzygium toninense is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
