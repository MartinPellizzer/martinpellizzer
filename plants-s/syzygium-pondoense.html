Syzygium pondoense

Syzygium pondoense, commonly known as the Pondo rose apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-red fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy appearance.

Syzygium pondoense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and the delicious, tart flavor of its fruits.
