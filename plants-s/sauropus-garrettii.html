Sauropus garrettii

Sauropus garrettii, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
