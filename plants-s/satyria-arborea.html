Satyria arborea

Satyria arborea, commonly known as the tree spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and opposite, simple leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The latex it produces is milky and can be toxic, which is a common feature among plants in this family.
