Senites capillaris

Senites capillaris, commonly known as the slender senita cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stems and sparse, reddish-brown spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Senites capillaris has adapted to arid environments through its ability to store water in its thick stems and minimize water loss via reduced leaf surface area.

It plays an important ecological role in desert ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife.
