Solanum aurantiacobaccatum

Solanum aurantiacobaccatum, commonly known as the orange and black nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, colorful fruits. The plant produces small, elongated berries that range in color from orange to black, often with a bumpy texture. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy surface, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its unique fruit appearance.
