Saxifraga × mariae-theresae

Saxifraga × mariae-theresae, commonly known as the Marie-Thérèse saxifrage, is a hybrid plant that originates from crosses between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirculus.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in rocky, alpine, or arctic conditions, showcasing its resilience to cold and nutrient-poor soils.
