Syzygium densinervium

Syzygium densinervium, commonly known as the dense-veined tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent veins. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits. Its bark is thick and rough, often exuding a resinous sap.

Syzygium densinervium is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
