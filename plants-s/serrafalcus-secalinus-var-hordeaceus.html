Serrafalcus secalinus var. hordeaceus

Serrafalcus secalinus var. hordeaceus, commonly known as the barley grass, is a variant of the genus Serrafalcus, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage.

It is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
