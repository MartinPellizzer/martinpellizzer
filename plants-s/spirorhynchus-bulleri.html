Spirorhynchus bulleri

Spirorhynchus bulleri, commonly known as the spiral liverwort, is a small, aquatic liverwort found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in dense mats on the surface of ponds or slow-moving streams. Its structure consists of a thallus with leaf-like structures arranged in a helical pattern.

Spirorhynchus bulleri plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and contributing to water filtration.
