Sambucus canadensis f. aurea

Sambucus canadensis f. aurea, commonly known as the golden elderberry, is a cultivar of the elderberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which turns red in the fall, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and can attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
