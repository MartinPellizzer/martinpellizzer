Selaginella hemicardia

Selaginella hemicardia, commonly known as the heart-shaped spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tips of the stems and produce spores.

Selaginella hemicardia is native to parts of North America and is often found in dry, shaded environments.
