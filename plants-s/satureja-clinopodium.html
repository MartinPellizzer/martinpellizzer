Satureja clinopodium

Satureja clinopodium, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

The leaves are aromatic and have been used traditionally in cooking and herbal medicine.
