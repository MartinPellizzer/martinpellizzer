Sebastiania hispida var. scandens

Sebastiania hispida var. scandens, commonly known as the velvetleaf sebastiania, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are three-lobed capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
