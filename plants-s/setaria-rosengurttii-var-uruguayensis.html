Setaria rosengurttii var. uruguayensis

Setaria rosengurttii var. uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay false millet, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
