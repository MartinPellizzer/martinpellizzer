Sorbus woronowii

Sorbus woronowii, commonly known as the Korean whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
