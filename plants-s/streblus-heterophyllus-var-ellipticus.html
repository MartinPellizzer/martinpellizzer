Streblus heterophyllus var. ellipticus

Streblus heterophyllus var. ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic fanleaf.

It is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its distinctive elliptical leaves that are often arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by elongated, woody fruits that contain seeds.
