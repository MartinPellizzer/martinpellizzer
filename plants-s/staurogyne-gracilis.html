Staurogyne gracilis

Staurogyne gracilis, commonly known as the slender water clover, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stem. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its small, green flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Staurogyne gracilis is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental appearance and ability to oxygenate water.
