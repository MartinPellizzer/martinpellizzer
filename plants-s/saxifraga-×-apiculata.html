Saxifraga × apiculata

Saxifraga × apiculata, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
