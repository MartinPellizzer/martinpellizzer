Spathoglottis stenophylla

Spathoglottis stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow and green coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
