Sarracenia × popei

Sarracenia × popei, commonly known as the Pope's pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular pitchers that secrete digestive enzymes to trap and digest insects. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic bog environments, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are modified into specialized traps that can vary in color from green to reddish hues.

Sarracenia × popei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique ecological adaptations.
