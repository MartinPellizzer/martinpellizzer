Stegitris

Stegitris, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. They produce small, yellow flowers that are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Stegitris species are often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
