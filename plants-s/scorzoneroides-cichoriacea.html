Scorzoneroides cichoriacea

Scorzoneroides cichoriacea, commonly known as the Italian chicory, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in coarse, hairy texture. The plant produces blue to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a bitter taste, similar to other members of the Cichoriaceae family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible leaves and flowers.
