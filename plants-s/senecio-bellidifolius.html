Senecio bellidifolius

Senecio bellidifolius, commonly known as the bellidifolius groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
