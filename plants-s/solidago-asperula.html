Solidago asperula

Solidago asperula, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly serrated edge.
