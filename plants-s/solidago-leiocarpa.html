Solidago leiocarpa

Solidago leiocarpa, commonly known as the smoothstem goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a smooth texture.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
