Sabina virginiana var. pendula

Sabina virginiana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping juniper, is a variant of the common juniper characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and its small, berry-like cones are often blue or purple. It is native to the eastern United States and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appearance.

The weeping juniper is relatively low maintenance and resistant to most pests and diseases.
