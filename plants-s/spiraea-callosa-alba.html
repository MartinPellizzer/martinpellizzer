Spiraea callosa alba

Spiraea callosa alba, commonly known as white meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered with a whitish, waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Spiraea callosa alba is valued in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
