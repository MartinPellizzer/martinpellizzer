Schoenocaulon coulteri

Schoenocaulon coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's mountain-mint, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as canyons and slopes.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples for its purported medicinal properties.
