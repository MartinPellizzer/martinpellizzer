Strophanthus hispidus

Strophanthus hispidus, commonly known as the rough-stalked strophanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers that are usually orange or yellow in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine for various ailments.
