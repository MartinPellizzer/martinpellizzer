Solidago multiradiata var. neomexicana

Solidago multiradiata var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, ray-like flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
