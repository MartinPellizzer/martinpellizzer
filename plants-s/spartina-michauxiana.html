Spartina michauxiana

Spartina michauxiana, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Atlantic coast of North America.

It thrives in brackish and saline wetlands, playing a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and preventing erosion. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. It is highly adaptable to tidal fluctuations and can tolerate periodic submersion in water.

Spartina michauxiana is an important component of salt marsh ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to biodiversity.
