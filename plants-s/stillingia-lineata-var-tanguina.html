Stillingia lineata var. tanguina

Stillingia lineata var. tanguina, commonly known as the Chinese stellaria, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the family Stellariaceae.
