Solanum etuberosum var. chillanense

Solanum etuberosum var. chillanense, commonly known as the Chilean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy, glandular stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

This species is adapted to dry and temperate climates, making it a resilient plant in various environmental conditions.
