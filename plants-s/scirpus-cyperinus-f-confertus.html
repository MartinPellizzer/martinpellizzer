Scirpus cyperinus f. confertus

Scirpus cyperinus f. confertus, commonly known as the dense bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are sturdy and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic wildlife.
