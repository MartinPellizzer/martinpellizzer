Scindapsus glaucescens

Scindapsus glaucescens, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical evergreen climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that often display a striking silver or variegated pattern. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ability to climb using aerial roots. Its foliage is highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Scindapsus glaucescens prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.
