Solanum suberosum var. calvum

Solanum suberosum var. calvum, commonly known as the rough nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
