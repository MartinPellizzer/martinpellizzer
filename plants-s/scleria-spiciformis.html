Scleria spiciformis

Scleria spiciformis, commonly known as the spikeflower sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Scleria spiciformis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils.
