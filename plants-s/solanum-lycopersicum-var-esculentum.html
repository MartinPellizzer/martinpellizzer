Solanum lycopersicum var. esculentum

Solanum lycopersicum var. esculentum, commonly known as the tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems, alternate leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, green fruits that ripen to a bright red color, which are the edible parts commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sunlight for optimal growth and fruit development.

The tomato is an important crop globally, valued for its nutritional content and versatility in cooking.
