Shorea plagata

Shorea plagata, commonly known as the Malay ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, often with a glossy upper surface.

The flowers are small and yellowish, and the tree produces woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.
