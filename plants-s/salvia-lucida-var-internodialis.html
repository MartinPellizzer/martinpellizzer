Salvia lucida var. internodialis

Salvia lucida var. internodialis, commonly known as narrowleaf blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for native gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinators.
