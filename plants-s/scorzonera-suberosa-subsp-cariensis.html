Scorzonera suberosa subsp. cariensis

Scorzonera suberosa subsp.

cariensis, commonly known as the Carpathian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features a robust, hairy stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Scorzonera suberosa subsp.

cariensis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
