Salix lasiogyne subsp. yuhkii

Salix lasiogyne subsp.

yuhkii, commonly known as the Yuhkii willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
