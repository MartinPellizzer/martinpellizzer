Serruria elevata var. roxburghii

Serruria elevata var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Rooibos or Red Bush tea plant, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems, leathery leaves, and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in dry conditions. It is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used to produce a popular herbal tea.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
