Stipagrostis zeyheri subsp. sericans

Stipagrostis zeyheri subsp.

sericans, commonly known as the sericant grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in dryland landscaping.
