Sisyrinchium grande

Sisyrinchium grande, commonly known as the large blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, blue to violet flowers with prominent yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium grande is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
