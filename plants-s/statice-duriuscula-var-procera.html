Statice duriuscula var. procera

Statice duriuscula var. procera, commonly known as tall sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, salt-tolerant growth habit and its ability to thrive in coastal environments. The plant produces spikes of small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness.

Statice duriuscula var. procera is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought-resistant qualities.
