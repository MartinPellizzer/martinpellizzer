Silene telavivensis

Silene telavivensis, commonly known as the Tel Aviv catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Israel.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions.

Silene telavivensis is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
