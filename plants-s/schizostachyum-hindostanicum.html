Schizostachyum hindostanicum

Schizostachyum hindostanicum, commonly known as the Indian bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers. It is often used for construction, as a source of fiber, and in traditional crafts due to its durable and flexible nature.

Schizostachyum hindostanicum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
