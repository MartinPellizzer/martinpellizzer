Stachys atherocalyx

Stachys atherocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in whorled clusters, producing small, pale blue or violet blooms.

Stachys atherocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
