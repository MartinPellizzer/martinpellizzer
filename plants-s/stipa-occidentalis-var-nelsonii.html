Stipa occidentalis var. nelsonii

Stipa occidentalis var. nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and compact, cylindrical seed heads. This variety thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage is typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
