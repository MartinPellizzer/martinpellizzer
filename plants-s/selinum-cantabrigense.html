Selinum cantabrigense

Selinum cantabrigense, commonly known as the Spanish flatvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are small and usually pale yellow, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Selinum cantabrigense is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
