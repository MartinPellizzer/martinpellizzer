Scutellaria malvifolia

Scutellaria malvifolia, commonly known as the violet scutellaria, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Scutellaria malvifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a desirable plant for native gardens and conservation efforts.
