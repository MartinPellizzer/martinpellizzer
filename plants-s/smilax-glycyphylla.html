Smilax glycyphylla

Smilax glycyphylla, commonly known as sweet sarsapilla, is a perennial climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with spines and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Smilax glycyphylla thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
