Schinus huigan

Schinus huigan, commonly known as the huigan tree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family, native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a broad, rounded crown and smooth, grayish bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a slightly serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries containing several seeds.

Schinus huigan is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
