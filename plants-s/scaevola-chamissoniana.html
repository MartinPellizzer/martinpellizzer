Scaevola chamissoniana

Scaevola chamissoniana, commonly known as the coastal scabiosa, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrublands.

Scaevola chamissoniana is well adapted to salt spray and is an important species in coastal ecosystems.
