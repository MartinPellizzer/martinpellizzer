Senecio yegua var. pilotus

Senecio yegua var. pilotus, commonly known as the Yegua milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branched stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils.

Senecio yegua var. pilotus is often found in desert and semi-desert environments, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
