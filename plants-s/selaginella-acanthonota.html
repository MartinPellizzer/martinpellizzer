Selaginella acanthonota

Selaginella acanthonota, commonly known as the thread moss or spike moss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a rosette-like structure with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Selaginella acanthonota is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures, including microsporangia and megasporangia.

This species is also recognized for its role in ecological succession and its use in horticulture as an ornamental plant.
