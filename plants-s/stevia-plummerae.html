Stevia plummerae

Stevia plummerae, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves contain high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

Stevia plummerae is often cultivated for its sweet taste and use in herbal remedies.
