Syringa rhodopea

Syringa rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fragrant, panicle-shaped inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features dark green, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Syringa rhodopea is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its showy floral display.
