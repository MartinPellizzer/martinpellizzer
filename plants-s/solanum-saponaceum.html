Solanum saponaceum

Solanum saponaceum, commonly known as the soapberry nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.

The plant contains alkaloids and saponins, which can be toxic to animals and humans if ingested.
