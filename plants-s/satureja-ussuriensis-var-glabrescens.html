Satureja ussuriensis var. glabrescens

Satureja ussuriensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as Siberian savory, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent aroma.

The variety glabrescens is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other varieties of Satureja.
