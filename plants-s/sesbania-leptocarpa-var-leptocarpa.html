Sesbania leptocarpa var. leptocarpa

Sesbania leptocarpa var. leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a tropical leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used as a protein-rich food source. It is adapted to thrive in warm, tropical climates and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
