Scleranthus annuus

Scleranthus annuus, commonly known as the common sicklethorn, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and has a distinctive appearance with spiny, sickle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, round fruits. Its stems are often covered in stiff hairs, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Scleranthus annuus is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
