Solanum mendax

Solanum mendax, commonly known as the false nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Solanum mendax is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and foliage.
