Syzygium bankense

Syzygium bankense, commonly known as the white crape myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a crape-like appearance. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Syzygium bankense is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
