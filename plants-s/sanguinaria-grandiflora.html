Sanguinaria grandiflora

Sanguinaria grandiflora, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a single, fleshy rhizome and lance-shaped leaves that emerge after the flowers. Its sap is reddish and has been used historically for medicinal purposes.

Sanguinaria grandiflora thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
