Saussurea stenocephala

Saussurea stenocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

Saussurea stenocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
