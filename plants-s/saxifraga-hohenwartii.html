Saxifraga hohenwartii

Saxifraga hohenwartii, commonly known as the Hohenwart's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette formation, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Saxifraga hohenwartii thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
