Salix Mackenzieana

Salix mackenzieana, also known as Mackenzie willow, is a rare and delicate plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. This species thrives in harsh, cold environments where few other plants can survive. Its slender stems and small, inconspicuous leaves are adapted to withstand strong winds and permafrost conditions. The willow plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife. Due to climate change and human activity, Salix mackenzieana faces increasing threats to its survival.
