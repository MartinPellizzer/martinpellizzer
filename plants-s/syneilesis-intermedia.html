Syneilesis intermedia

Syneilesis intermedia, commonly known as the Indian nettle or wild nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other members known for their stinging hairs. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

Syneilesis intermedia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
