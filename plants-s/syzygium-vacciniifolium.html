Syzygium vacciniifolium

Syzygium vacciniifolium, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to edible, red or yellow berries. These berries are valued for their tart flavor and are used in traditional cuisine and beverages.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
