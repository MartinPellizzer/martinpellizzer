Sclerolaena decurrens

Sclerolaena decurrens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a basal rosette, and the stems are hairy and branching.

Sclerolaena decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
