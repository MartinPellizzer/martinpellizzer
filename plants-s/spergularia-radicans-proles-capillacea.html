Spergularia radicans proles capillacea

Spergularia radicans proles capillacea, commonly known as sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
