Salvia antennifera

Salvia antennifera, commonly known as the featherleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, gray-green leaves that have a feathery appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia antennifera is also valued for its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance.
