Solanum melongena subsp. cumingii

Solanum melongena subsp.

cumingii, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a subspecies of the eggplant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, purple fruits that are typically harvested when still immature.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value as well as its culinary use. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while the flowers are white with yellow stamens.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its vibrant fruit color and unique flavor profile.
