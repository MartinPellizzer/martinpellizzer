Strobilanthes perrottetiana

Strobilanthes perrottetiana, commonly known as the "Firecracker" or "Brahmastra" flower, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble fireworks, which bloom in vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and short flowering period, often lasting only a few weeks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in opposite pairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique and eye-catching floral display.
