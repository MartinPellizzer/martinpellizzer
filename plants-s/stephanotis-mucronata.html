Stephanotis mucronata

Stephanotis mucronata, commonly known as the white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of true jasmine. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
