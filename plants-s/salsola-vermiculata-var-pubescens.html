Salsola vermiculata var. pubescens

Salsola vermiculata var. pubescens, commonly known as sandmat, is a halophytic plant adapted to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in coastal dunes, salt marshes, and other saline soils.

Its extensive root system aids in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in harsh environments.
