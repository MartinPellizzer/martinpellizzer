Scaevola ivifolia

Scaevola ivifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf scaevola, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Scaevola ivifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
