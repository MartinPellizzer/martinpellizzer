Salix × friesiana var. antverpiensis

Salix × friesiana var. antverpiensis, commonly known as the Antwerp weeping willow, is a hybrid willow species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a graceful, cascading appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and has small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly planted in urban landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
