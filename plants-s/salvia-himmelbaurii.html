Salvia himmelbaurii

Salvia himmelbaurii, commonly known as Himmelsbaur's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

Salvia himmelbaurii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes and native gardens.
