Senecio × rolandi-bonaparte

Senecio × rolandi-bonaparte, commonly known as the hybrid groundsels, is a composite plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
