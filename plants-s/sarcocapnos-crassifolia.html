Sarcocapnos crassifolia

Sarcocapnos crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sarcocapnos, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
