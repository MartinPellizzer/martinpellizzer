Selaginella medogensis

Selaginella medogensis, commonly known as the Medogensis spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a creeping stem with small, scale-like leaves that are clustered around the stem. It produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the branches.

Selaginella medogensis is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it an important species in ecological studies of desert ecosystems.
