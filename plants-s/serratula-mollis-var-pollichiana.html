Serratula mollis var. pollichiana

Serratula mollis var. pollichiana, commonly known as the Pollichian goldenweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a rough, serrated edge. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, composite inflorescences, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
