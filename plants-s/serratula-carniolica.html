Serratula carniolica

Serratula carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is an herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Serratula carniolica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
