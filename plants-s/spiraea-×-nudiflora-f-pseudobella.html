Spiraea × nudiflora f. pseudobella

Spiraea × nudiflora f. pseudobella, commonly known as the paleform spiraea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in late spring. The foliage is typically dark green, turning reddish in the fall, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.

This cultivar is noted for its compact size and prolonged flowering period, enhancing its appeal in both residential and public gardens.
