Scirpus verecundus

Scirpus verecundus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help stabilize soil in marshy environments.

Scirpus verecundus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
