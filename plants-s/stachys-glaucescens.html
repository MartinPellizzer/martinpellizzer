Stachys glaucescens

Stachys glaucescens, commonly known as blueweed or woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and gravelly areas. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Stachys glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
