Sophora tomentosa subsp. havanensis

Sophora tomentosa subsp.

havanensis, commonly known as the Cuban spongegum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas. It is characterized by its rough, grayish bark and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
