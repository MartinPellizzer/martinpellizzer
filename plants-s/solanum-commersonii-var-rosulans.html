Solanum commersonii var. rosulans

Solanum commersonii var. rosulans, commonly known as the Rosulate Bladderpod, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features small, green, bladder-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a unique appearance. It produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in rocky or dry environments.
