Sisyrinchium pumilum

Sisyrinchium pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its compact size and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Sisyrinchium pumilum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
