Scutellaria ventenatii var. trianae

Scutellaria ventenatii var. trianae, commonly known as the Trianas' blue skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
