Specklinia rubidantha

Specklinia rubidantha, commonly known as the red-veined staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, tree-like fronds that resemble antlers, giving it its common name. The plant has a dense, fibrous root system that helps it anchor to trees and absorb moisture from the air. Its leaves are typically dark green with prominent red veins, adding to its ornamental value.

Specklinia rubidantha thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
