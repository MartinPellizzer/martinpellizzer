Senna bosseri

Senna bosseri, commonly known as the golden senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and bloom during the warmer months. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Senna bosseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
