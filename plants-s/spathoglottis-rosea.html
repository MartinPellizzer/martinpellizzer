Spathoglottis rosea

Spathoglottis rosea, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its striking pink or purple flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its flowers are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, giving it a unique and elegant appearance.

Spathoglottis rosea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
