Senecio tristis var. comberi

Senecio tristis var. comberi, commonly known as the narrowleaf Senecio, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
