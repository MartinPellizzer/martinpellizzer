Selaginella cumingiana

Selaginella cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its creeping, branched stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophylls that bear spores, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
