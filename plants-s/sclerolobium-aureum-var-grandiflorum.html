Sclerolobium aureum var. grandiflorum

Sclerolobium aureum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This species is notable for its ecological and ornamental value, particularly in horticulture.
