Salix babylonica salamonii

Salix babylonica salamonii, commonly known as the weeping willow variant, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that hang like delicate curtains, giving it a graceful and ornamental appearance. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal beauty.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
