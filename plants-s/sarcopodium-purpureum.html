Sarcopodium purpureum

Sarcopodium purpureum, commonly known as the purple groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves with a purplish hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
