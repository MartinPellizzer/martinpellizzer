Sedum sanguineum

Sedum sanguineum, commonly known as crimson sedum, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are typically green, turning reddish in full sun, and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
