Salsola tamariscifolia

Salsola tamariscifolia, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its spiny, branching stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive nature in some regions.

Salsola tamariscifolia is also known for its ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it a potential candidate for phytoremediation.
