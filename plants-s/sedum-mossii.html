Sedum mossii

Sedum mossii, commonly known as the mossy stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum mossii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or arid environments.
