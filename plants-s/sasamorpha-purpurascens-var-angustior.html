Sasamorpha purpurascens var. angustior

Sasamorpha purpurascens var. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leafed purplestem, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems that turn a striking purple hue as the plant matures. The leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, with a bluish-green coloration. It produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
