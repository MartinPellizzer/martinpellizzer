Streptopus geniculatus

Streptopus geniculatus, commonly known as the zigzag mallow, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
