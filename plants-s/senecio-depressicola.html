Senecio depressicola

Senecio depressicola, commonly known as the dwarf senecio, is a low-growing succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens and succulent collections.
