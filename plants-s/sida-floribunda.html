Sida floribunda

Sida floribunda, commonly known as the showy sida, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming from summer to fall. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a distinctive pubescent surface.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in warm, sunny environments.
