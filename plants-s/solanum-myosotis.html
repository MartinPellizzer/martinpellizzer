Solanum myosotis

Solanum myosotis, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the afternoon, hence its common name.

Solanum myosotis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and vigorous growth habit.
