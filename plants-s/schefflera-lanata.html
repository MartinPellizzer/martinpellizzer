Schefflera lanata

Schefflera lanata, commonly known as the woolly schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and large, palmate leaves that are covered in soft, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue, spherical berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
