Stachys rifana

Stachys rifana, commonly known as the rock betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in dry, sunny environments. The plant features upright, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, forming dense clusters of purple to pinkish-purple blooms.

Stachys rifana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
