Salvia decipiens

Salvia decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and gray-green, hairy foliage. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping. Its unique flowering period extends from late spring to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia decipiens is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
