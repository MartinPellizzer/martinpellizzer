Salvia taiwaniana

Salvia taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan sage, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
