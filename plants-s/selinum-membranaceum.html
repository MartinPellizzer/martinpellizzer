Selinum membranaceum

Selinum membranaceum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed garlic chives, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic and can be used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Selinum membranaceum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its subtle, garlic-like fragrance.
