Stachys hyssopoides

Stachys hyssopoides, commonly known as narrowleaf hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in whorled clusters. Its stems are often square and covered in fine hairs, a characteristic common in the Lamiaceae family.

Stachys hyssopoides is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
