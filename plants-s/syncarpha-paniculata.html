Syncarpha paniculata

Syncarpha paniculata, commonly known as the panicled synbranch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Syncarpha paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
