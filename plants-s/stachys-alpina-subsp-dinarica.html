Stachys alpina subsp. dinarica

Stachys alpina subsp.

dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric betony, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, forming dense spikes of purple to pinkish-purple blooms.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
