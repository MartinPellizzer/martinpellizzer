Salvia platyfrons

Salvia platyfrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters at the tips of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
