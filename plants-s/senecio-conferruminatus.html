Senecio conferruminatus

Senecio conferruminatus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
