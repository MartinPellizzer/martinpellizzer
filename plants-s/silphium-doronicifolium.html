Silphium doronicifolium

Silphium doronicifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved silphium, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 20 inches in length. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and wetlands.

Due to its historical significance and rarity, it is considered an important species for ecological and botanical study.
