Sesleria × tatrorum

Sesleria × tatrorum, commonly known as the hybrid tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically forms compact clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often gray-green in color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or purple flowers.

Sesleria × tatrorum is well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
