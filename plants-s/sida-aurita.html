Sida aurita

Sida aurita, commonly known as velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in moist, disturbed soils, often found in agricultural fields. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

Sida aurita is also known for its ability to produce a significant amount of seed, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.
