Spyridium eriocephalum var. vexilliferum

Spyridium eriocephalum var. vexilliferum, commonly known as the flag-leaf spyridium, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers with prominent, showy sepals that resemble flags. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
