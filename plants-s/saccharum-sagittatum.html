Saccharum sagittatum

Saccharum sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowhead grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaf bases. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It can reach heights of up to 2 meters and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Saccharum sagittatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
