Selatium incurvum

Selatium incurvum, commonly known as the curly selatium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that curl inward, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Selatium incurvum is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
