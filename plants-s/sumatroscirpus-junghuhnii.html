Sumatroscirpus junghuhnii

Sumatroscirpus junghuhnii, commonly known as the Java water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are often used in ornamental water gardens. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring warm and humid conditions. Its rhizomes spread across the water surface, anchoring itself to submerged objects.

Sumatroscirpus junghuhnii plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
