Sporobolus junceus

Sporobolus junceus, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
