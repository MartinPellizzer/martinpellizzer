Sasa megalogluma

Sasa megalogluma, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, creating thickets that provide habitat for various wildlife species. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss in its high-altitude environment.

Sasa megalogluma is also valued for its strong, durable stalks, which have been used traditionally in construction and crafts.
