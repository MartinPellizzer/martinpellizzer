Sisyrinchium idahoense var. segetum

Sisyrinchium idahoense var. segetum, commonly known as the Idaho basketflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers with reflexed petals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pollinator attractant and a component of native plant communities.
