Sikira aromatica

Sikira aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic sikira, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight to flourish. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are used in traditional medicine for their calming and antiseptic properties.

Due to its unique aroma and medicinal value, it is a prized plant in local botanical gardens and herbal practices.
