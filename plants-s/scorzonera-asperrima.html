Scorzonera asperrima

Scorzonera asperrima, commonly known as the rough scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in coarse hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine.

Scorzonera asperrima thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
