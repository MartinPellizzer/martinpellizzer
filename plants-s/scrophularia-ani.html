Scrophularia ani

Scrophularia ani, commonly known as the African figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes various species known for their medicinal properties. The plant typically features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy in texture. Its flowers are usually tubular and come in shades of pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia ani is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
