Sorbus umbellata subsp. meridionalis

Sorbus umbellata subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe. It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

This subspecies is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
