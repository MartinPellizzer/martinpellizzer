Senecio ovatus subsp. ovatus

Senecio ovatus subsp.

ovatus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue-eyed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens despite its potential invasiveness in some regions.
