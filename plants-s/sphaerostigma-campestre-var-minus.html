Sphaerostigma campestre var. minus

Sphaerostigma campestre var. minus, commonly known as the small-flowered sphaerostigma, is a variant of the sphaerostigma genus native to certain regions of Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, dry fruits known as capsules.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
