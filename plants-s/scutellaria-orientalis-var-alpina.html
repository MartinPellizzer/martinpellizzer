Scutellaria orientalis var. alpina

Scutellaria orientalis var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows and tundra environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to violet in color, and it produces small, nut-like seeds.

Scutellaria orientalis var. alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
