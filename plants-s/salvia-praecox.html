Salvia praecox

Salvia praecox, commonly known as early purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive conical shape, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia praecox thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
