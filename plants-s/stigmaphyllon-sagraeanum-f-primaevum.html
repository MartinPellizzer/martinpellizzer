Stigmaphyllon sagraeanum f. primaevum

Stigmaphyllon sagraeanum f. primaevum, commonly known as the early-flowering stigmaphyllon, is a rare bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with red flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in high humidity and partial shade, making it a unique addition to specialized botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
