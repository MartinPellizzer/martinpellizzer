Solidago maxonii

Solidago maxonii, commonly known as Maxon's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Solidago maxonii plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
