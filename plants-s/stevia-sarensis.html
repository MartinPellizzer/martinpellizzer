Stevia sarensis

Stevia sarensis, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are harvested and used in various food products as a low-calorie sweetener alternative.

Stevia sarensis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
