Schinus pilifera var. pilifera

Schinus pilifera var. pilifera, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
