Solms-laubachia retropilosa

Solms-laubachia retropilosa, commonly known as the backward-hair grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial plant native to southern Africa, particularly found in regions with sandy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Solms-laubachia retropilosa is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes a distinctive backward-facing arrangement of the lemma and palea.
