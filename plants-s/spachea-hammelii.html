Spachea hammelii

Spachea hammelii, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

Spachea hammelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
