Scirpus validus f. megastachyus

Scirpus validus f. megastachyus, commonly known as the giant bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush genus Scirpus.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, fibrous roots. This plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it forms dense colonies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are large and often more branched than in other Scirpus species.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
