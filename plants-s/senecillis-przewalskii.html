Senecillis przewalskii

Senecillis przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. It is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
