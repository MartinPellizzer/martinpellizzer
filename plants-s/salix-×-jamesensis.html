Salix × jamesensis

Salix × jamesensis, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix discolor and Salix interior, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as swamps and stream banks, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a finely toothed margin, while its catkins are a distinguishing feature.

Salix × jamesensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
