Sideritis hirsuta subsp. hirtula

Sideritis hirsuta subsp.

hirtula, commonly known as the hairy sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Sideritis hirtula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
