Sesleria subacaulis

Sesleria subacaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood sorrel, is a small, evergreen perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Sesleria subacaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
