Sesbania brenningii

Sesbania brenningii, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, pod-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in warm climates and is often used as a forage crop or in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are edible and can be consumed either raw or cooked.
