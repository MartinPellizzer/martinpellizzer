Stenophyllus junciformis

Stenophyllus junciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Juncaginaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are usually borne at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Stenophyllus junciformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
