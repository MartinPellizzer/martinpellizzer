Silene pyriformis

Silene pyriformis, commonly known as the bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive bladders-like structures on its stems, which are specialized seed pods that aid in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Silene pyriformis produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
