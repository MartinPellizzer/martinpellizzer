Sida albinervia

Sida albinervia, commonly known as the white-veined sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, pale green to white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Sida albinervia is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
