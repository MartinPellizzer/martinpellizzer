Senna birostris var. huancavensis

Senna birostris var. huancavensis, commonly known as the Huancavelica senna, is a shrub native to the Andean region of Peru.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated pods containing yellowish seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for medicinal purposes.
