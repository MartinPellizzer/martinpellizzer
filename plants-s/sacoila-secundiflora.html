Sacoila secundiflora

Sacoila secundiflora, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a slender, woody stem with smooth, greyish bark that peels off in thin layers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

Sacoila secundiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
