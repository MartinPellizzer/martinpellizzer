Sorbus pohuashanensis var. manshuriensis

Sorbus pohuashanensis var. manshuriensis, commonly known as the Manchurian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, serrated leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and resilience to cold weather.
