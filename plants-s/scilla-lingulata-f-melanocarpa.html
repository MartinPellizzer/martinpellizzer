Scilla lingulata f. melanocarpa

Scilla lingulata f. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited scilla, is a botanical variant of the scilla genus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, dark purple to black berries that contrast with its blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique fruit color.
