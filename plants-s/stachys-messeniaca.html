Stachys messeniaca

Stachys messeniaca, commonly known as the Greek hedgehog, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which are covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

Stachys messeniaca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
