Scorpiurus subvillosus subvar. eriocarpus

Scorpiurus subvillosus subvar. eriocarpus, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes nettles and related plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are typically arranged in pairs.
