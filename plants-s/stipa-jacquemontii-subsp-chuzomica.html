Stipa jacquemontii subsp. chuzomica

Stipa jacquemontii subsp.

chuzomica, commonly known as the Chuzomica needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
