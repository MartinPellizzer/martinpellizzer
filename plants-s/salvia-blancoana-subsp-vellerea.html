Salvia blancoana subsp. vellerea

Salvia blancoana subsp.

vellerea, commonly known as the velvet sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and hairy, velvety foliage, which give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and showy flowers.

The subspecies vellerea is distinguished by its dense, soft pubescence and compact growth habit.
