Scrophularia canina var. pyrenaica

Scrophularia canina var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean dog's mercury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers with two lips, typical of the genus Scrophularia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and flies.
