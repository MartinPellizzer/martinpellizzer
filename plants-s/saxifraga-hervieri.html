Saxifraga hervieri

Saxifraga hervieri, commonly known as the Hervier's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy, fleshy leaves that help it conserve moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in mountainous areas.

Saxifraga hervieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
