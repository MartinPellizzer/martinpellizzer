Spiraea serratifolia

Spiraea serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features upright, multi-branched stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in ornamental landscaping for its showy blooms and hardy nature.
