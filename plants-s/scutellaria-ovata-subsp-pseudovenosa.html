Scutellaria ovata subsp. pseudovenosa

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

pseudovenosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

The plant features oval, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive basal rosette. Its flowers are blue to purple, tubular, and clustered in whorls along the stem.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
