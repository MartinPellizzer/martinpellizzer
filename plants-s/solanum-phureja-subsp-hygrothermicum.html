Solanum phureja subsp. hygrothermicum

Solanum phureja subsp.

hygrothermicum, commonly known as the mountain potato, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with a fine pubescence, while its flowers are usually white or pale green.

This subspecies is valued not only for its culinary use but also for its potential medicinal properties.
