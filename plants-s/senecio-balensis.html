Senecio balensis

Senecio balensis, commonly known as the Balensian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks. Senecio balensis produces small yellow flowers arranged in a daisy-like pattern, and it has hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
