Stenocalyx myrtifolius

Stenocalyx myrtifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rainforests.

Stenocalyx myrtifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
