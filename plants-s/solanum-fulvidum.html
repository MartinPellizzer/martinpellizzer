Solanum fulvidum

Solanum fulvidum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by berries that change color from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum fulvidum is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
