Solidago pulcherrima

Solidago pulcherrima, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 meters and has lance-shaped, serrated leaves. Its flowering period attracts a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Solidago pulcherrima is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
