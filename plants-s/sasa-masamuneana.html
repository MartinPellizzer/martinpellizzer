Sasa masamuneana

Sasa masamuneana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a smooth texture. Sasa masamuneana is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form thick, lush stands.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is well adapted to shaded forest conditions.
