Stachytarpheta longespicata var. parvifolia

Stachytarpheta longespicata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
