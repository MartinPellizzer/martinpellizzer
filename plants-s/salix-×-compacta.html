Salix × compacta

Salix × compacta, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its narrow, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and along waterways.
