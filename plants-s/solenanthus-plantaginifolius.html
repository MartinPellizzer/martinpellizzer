Solenanthus plantaginifolius

Solenanthus plantaginifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved solenanthus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually in shades of pink or white.

Solenanthus plantaginifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
