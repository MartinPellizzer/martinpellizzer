Stenoloma capillaceum

Stenoloma capillaceum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized reproductive structures.

Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, clustered flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Stenoloma capillaceum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
