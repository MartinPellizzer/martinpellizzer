Soldanella alpina var. pyrolifolia

Soldanella alpina var. pyrolifolia, commonly known as the Alpine soldanella, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often appearing in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette or alternate along the stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
