Scleranthus collinus

Scleranthus collinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sand-plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the genus Scleranthus, which includes several species of annual or biennial herbs. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Scleranthus collinus is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.
