Stachys bicolor

Stachys bicolor, commonly known as heartleaf lamb's ear, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features soft, velvety leaves that are typically heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of pale violet to pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Stachys bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its visual texture and color.
