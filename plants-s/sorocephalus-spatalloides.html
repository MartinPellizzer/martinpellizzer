Sorocephalus spatalloides

Sorocephalus spatalloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sorocephalus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Sorocephalus spatalloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
