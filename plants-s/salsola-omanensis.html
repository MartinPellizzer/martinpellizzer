Salsola omanensis

Salsola omanensis, commonly known as the Oman gooseweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low water availability, showcasing features such as reduced leaf surface area and deep root systems.

Salsola omanensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
