Silene caryophylla

Silene caryophylla, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and rocky areas. The plant produces striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. Silene caryophylla is known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is often found in disturbed environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
