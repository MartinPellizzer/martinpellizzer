Sirium myrtifolium

Sirium myrtifolium, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a glossy appearance.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, clustered in terminal panicles, and bloom during the wet season.
