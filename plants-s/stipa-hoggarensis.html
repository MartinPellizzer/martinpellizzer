Stipa hoggarensis

Stipa hoggarensis, commonly known as Hogg's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in open plains and rocky outcrops, exhibiting a robust root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive with minimal water.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
