Spiraea media subsp. oblongifolia

Spiraea media subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of fragrant, white flowers in early summer.

The leaves are elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface, turning red or orange in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ease of maintenance.
