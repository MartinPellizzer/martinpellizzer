Syringa × rothomagensis var. saugeana

Syringa × rothomagensis var. saugeana, commonly known as the saugean lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are medium green and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
