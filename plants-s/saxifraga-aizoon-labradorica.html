Saxifraga aizoon labradorica

Saxifraga aizoon labradorica, commonly known as Labrador saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh climates.

Saxifraga aizoon labradorica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
