Secale cereale var. anatolicum

Secale cereale var. anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian rye, is a botanical variant of rye that originates from Anatolia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to temperate climates. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed grains. It is often cultivated for its grains, which are used in bread and animal feed.

This variety is valued for its resilience to adverse environmental conditions.
