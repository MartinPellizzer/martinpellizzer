Stapelia × albicans

Stapelia × albicans, commonly known as the hybrid African milkweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive, foul odor to attract pollinators. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Stapelia × albicans is a popular choice for succulent gardens and indoor plant collections due to its resilience and unique appearance.
