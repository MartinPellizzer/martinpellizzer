Syzygium scytophyllum

Syzygium scytophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.

The plant is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
