Synaedrys robinsonii

Synaedrys robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Synaedrys robinsonii is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
