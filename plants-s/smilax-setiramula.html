Smilax setiramula

Smilax setiramula, commonly known as the Florida arrowwood, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.

Smilax setiramula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
