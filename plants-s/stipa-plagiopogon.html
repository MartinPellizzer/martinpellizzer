Stipa plagiopogon

Stipa plagiopogon, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and dense, spike-like inflorescences that often appear in summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Stipa plagiopogon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
