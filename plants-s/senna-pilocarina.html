Senna pilocarina

Senna pilocarina, commonly known as the golden senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and hairy, and it produces elongated, slender pods containing several seeds.

Senna pilocarina is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.
