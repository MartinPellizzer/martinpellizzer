Selenicereus glaber subsp. mirandae

Selenicereus glaber subsp.

mirandae, commonly known as the Miranda's night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico. It is a cactus species that produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night.

The plant has a tall, unbranched stem with spiny areoles, typical of the Cactaceae family. Its flowers are ephemeral, lasting only a few hours, and are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
