Selenicereus triangularis

Selenicereus triangularis, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that often grow on trees or shrubs. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open for only a few hours. Its flowers are typically followed by a red, fleshy fruit that contains numerous seeds.

Selenicereus triangularis is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
