Sasa kohyafoemina

Sasa kohyafoemina, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense clusters.

Sasa kohyafoemina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and basket-making.
