Schoenus adnatus

Schoenus adnatus, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. This plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds or in swampy areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Schoenus adnatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
