Scirpus androgyna

Scirpus androgyna, commonly known as the male and female sedge, is a unique species of flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is notable for its dioecious nature, meaning that male and female flowers are found on separate plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its grass-like appearance is characterized by narrow, ribbon-shaped leaves and tall, clustered inflorescences.

Scirpus androgyna plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
