Stipa dielsii

Stipa dielsii, commonly known as the Diels' needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Stipa dielsii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
