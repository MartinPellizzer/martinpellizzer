Swertia dissimilis

Swertia dissimilis, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its erect stem and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Swertia dissimilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
