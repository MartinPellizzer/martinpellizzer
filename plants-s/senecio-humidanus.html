Senecio humidanus

Senecio humidanus, commonly known as the swamp senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Senecio humidanus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
