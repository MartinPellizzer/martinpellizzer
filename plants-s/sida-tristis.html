Sida tristis

Sida tristis, commonly known as the weeping sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sida tristis is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
