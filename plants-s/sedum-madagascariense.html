Sedum madagascariense

Sedum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, ranging in color from white to pink, and bloom during the dry season.

Sedum madagascariense is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
