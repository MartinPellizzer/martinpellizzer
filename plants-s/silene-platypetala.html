Silene platypetala

Silene platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many showy, often pink or white-flowered species. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Silene platypetala is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
