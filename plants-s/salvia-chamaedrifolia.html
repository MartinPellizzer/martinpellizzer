Salvia chamaedrifolia

Salvia chamaedrifolia, commonly known as creeping sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a serrated edge, often with a fragrant aroma.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
