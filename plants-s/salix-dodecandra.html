Salix dodecandra

Salix dodecandra, commonly known as the white willow, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is often found in moist, swampy areas. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
