Silene filipetala subsp. parviflora

Silene filipetala subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in grassy areas and open woodlands.

The plant features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pink to purple flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value.
