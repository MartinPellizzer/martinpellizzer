Saurauia villosa var. straussiana

Saurauia villosa var. straussiana, commonly known as the hairy saurauia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
