Salicornia gracillima

Salicornia gracillima, commonly known as sea beans or glassweed, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and tidal flats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Salicornia gracillima is a valuable source of nutrition for both wildlife and humans, particularly in regions with high salinity.
