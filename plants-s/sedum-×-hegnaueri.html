Sedum × hegnaueri

Sedum × hegnaueri, commonly known as the hybrid stonecrop, is a succulent plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species.

It typically features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is native to regions in Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hybrid nature results in a variety of cultivars with different growth habits and flower colors.
