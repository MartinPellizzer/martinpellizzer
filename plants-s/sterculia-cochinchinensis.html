Sterculia cochinchinensis

Sterculia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese sterculia or red sterculia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
