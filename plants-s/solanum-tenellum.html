Solanum tenellum

Solanum tenellum, commonly known as the small nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features small, hairy leaves with a slightly lobed shape. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, yellow berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds.

Solanum tenellum is often found in moist, shady environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
