Sesamum angustifolium

Sesamum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain tiny, elliptical seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its oil-rich seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and agricultural practices.
