Salvia atomara

Salvia atomara, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Salvia atomara is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
