Sagraea lima

Sagraea lima, commonly known as the Lima bean plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is an annual legume that produces pods containing edible seeds, which are widely used in global cuisine. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. Its root system fixes nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable crop for soil enrichment.

The seeds are rich in protein and are cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.
