Solanum bombycinum

Solanum bombycinum, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble those of the passionfruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures.

Solanum bombycinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral displays.
