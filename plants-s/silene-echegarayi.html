Silene echegarayi

Silene echegarayi, commonly known as the Echegaray's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene echegarayi is also notable for its small, capsule-like fruit that disperses seeds by wind.
