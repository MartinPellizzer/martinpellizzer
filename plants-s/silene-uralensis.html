Silene uralensis

Silene uralensis, commonly known as the Ural catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene uralensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
