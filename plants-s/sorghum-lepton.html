Sorghum lepton

Sorghum lepton, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial plant native to Africa and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in challenging climates.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both forage and grain production.
