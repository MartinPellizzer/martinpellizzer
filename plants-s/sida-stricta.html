Sida stricta

Sida stricta, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to various regions of the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the stems are often erect and branched.

Sida stricta is known for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and as a source of natural fibers.
