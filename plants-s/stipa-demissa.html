Stipa demissa

Stipa demissa, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Stipa demissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
