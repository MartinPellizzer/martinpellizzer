Selaginella solomonii

Selaginella solomonii, commonly known as the Solomon's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that form a tight rosette, giving it a unique appearance. It is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found in forest understories.

Selaginella solomonii is also notable for its ability to survive in dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.
