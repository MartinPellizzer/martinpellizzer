Sideritis ciliata var. mokpoensis

Sideritis ciliata var. mokpoensis, commonly known as the Mokpo mountain sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying shades of purple or pink, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
