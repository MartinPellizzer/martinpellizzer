Scrophularia oblongifolia

Scrophularia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf figwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scrophularia oblongifolia is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
