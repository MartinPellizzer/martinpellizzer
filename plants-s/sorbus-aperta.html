Sorbus aperta

Sorbus aperta, commonly known as the open whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, open growth habit and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by white, aggregate fruits known as pomes. Sorbus aperta is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and natural ecosystems.
