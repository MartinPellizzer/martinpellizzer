Salvia bahorucona

Salvia bahorucona, commonly known as Bahorucona sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
