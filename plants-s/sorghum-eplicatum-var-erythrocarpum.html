Sorghum eplicatum var. erythrocarpum

Sorghum eplicatum var. erythrocarpum, commonly known as red-fruited sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum genus native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-colored fruits, which are typically small and clustered in panicles. The plant is an annual grass that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a robust root system that aids in nutrient absorption.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has limited agricultural use compared to other sorghum species.
