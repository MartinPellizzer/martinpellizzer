Selaginella penniformis

Selaginella penniformis, commonly known as the common spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its linear, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the spores for reproduction.

Selaginella penniformis is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
