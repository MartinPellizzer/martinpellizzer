Senecio farinifer

Senecio farinifer, commonly known as the powdery senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woolly, silver-gray leaves covered in fine, powdery hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
