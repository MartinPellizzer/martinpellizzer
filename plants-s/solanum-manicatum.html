Solanum manicatum

Solanum manicatum, commonly known as the "Manicat" or "Cape gooseberry," is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and oval, yellowish-green berries. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Solanum manicatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant flowers and unique appearance.
