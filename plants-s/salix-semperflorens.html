Salix semperflorens

Salix semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside that adds to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its hardy nature and continuous flowering make it a popular choice in horticulture.
