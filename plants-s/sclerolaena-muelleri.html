Sclerolaena muelleri

Sclerolaena muelleri, commonly known as the mountain thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers followed by red or orange fruits that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Sclerolaena muelleri is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
