Senecio peltophorus

Senecio peltophorus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio peltophorus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
