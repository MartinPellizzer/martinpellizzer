Symplocos purpurascens

Symplocos purpurascens, commonly known as purpleleaf sandpaper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, sandpaper-like leaves that turn a striking reddish-purple color in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Symplocos purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
