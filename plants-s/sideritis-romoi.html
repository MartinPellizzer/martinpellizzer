Sideritis romoi

Sideritis romoi, commonly known as the Greek violet or mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. Sideritis romoi is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny habitats.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
