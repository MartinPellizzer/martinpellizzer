Saxifraga cuneata subsp. corbariensis

Saxifraga cuneata subsp.

corbariensis, commonly known as the Corbarien saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the British Isles. It thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal and maritime environments.

The plant features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are pinkish-white with five petals, appearing in clusters during the late spring to early summer.

It is a hardy species that is well adapted to harsh, wind-exposed habitats.
