Sambucus adnata var. puberula

Sambucus adnata var. puberula, commonly known as the woolly elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered white flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces dark purple berries in the fall, which are edible but should be consumed in moderation. Its hairy foliage and stems give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often found in moist woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to wildlife.
