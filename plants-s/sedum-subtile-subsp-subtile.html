Sedum subtile subsp. subtile

Sedum subtile subsp.

subtile, commonly known as the narrowleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and linear, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
