Stelis steganopus

Stelis steganopus, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Stelis steganopus is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
