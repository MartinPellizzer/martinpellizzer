Scutellaria integrifolia var. hispida

Scutellaria integrifolia var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with a square stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces violet to blue flowers in whorls along the stem, typical of the mint family (Lamiaceae). It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its hairy foliage and compact growth habit make it distinctive within its ecological community.
