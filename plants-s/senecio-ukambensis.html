Senecio ukambensis

Senecio ukambensis, commonly known as the East African groundsel, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It features upright stems with narrow, hairy leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Senecio ukambensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
