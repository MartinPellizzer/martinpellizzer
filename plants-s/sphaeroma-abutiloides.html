Sphaeroma abutiloides

Sphaeroma abutiloides, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
