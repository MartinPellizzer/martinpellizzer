Scandix glochidiata

Scandix glochidiata, commonly known as the burro bush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Scandix glochidiata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
