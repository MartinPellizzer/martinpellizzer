Stipa × fallacina

Stipa × fallacina, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a hybrid between Stipa capillata and Stipa spicata, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and alpine meadows. Its slender, tufted culms and fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences are notable botanical features.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
