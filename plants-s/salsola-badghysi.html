Salsola badghysi

Salsola badghysi, commonly known as the Badghis saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in saline and drought-prone environments, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization. Its growth habit includes low, sprawling stems that help it conserve water and reduce heat stress.

Salsola badghysi is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
