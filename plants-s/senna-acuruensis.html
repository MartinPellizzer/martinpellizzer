Senna acuruensis

Senna acuruensis, commonly known as the Acuru tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is known for its yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.

The seeds of Senna acuruensis are used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.
