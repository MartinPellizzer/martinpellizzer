Senecio madagascariensis

Senecio madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, featuring serrated leaves and yellow-orange flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
