Scirpus eriophorum var. nipponicum

Scirpus eriophorum var. nipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in wetland habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an important pioneer species in disturbed wetlands.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
