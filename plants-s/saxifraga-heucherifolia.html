Saxifraga heucherifolia

Saxifraga heucherifolia, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rosette of velvety, waxy leaves that are typically green with purplish veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pink, blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Saxifraga heucherifolia is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
