Sida calopoda

Sida calopoda, commonly known as velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Sida calopoda is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
