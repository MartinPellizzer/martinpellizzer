Sclerobasis

Sclerobasis, commonly known as the "sclerobasis" or a type of plant structure, refers to a hard, woody base found in certain plants.

It is typically a thickened, fibrous layer that supports the plant's stem or root system. This structure is often composed of sclerenchyma cells, which are specialized for strength and rigidity. The sclerobasis plays a crucial role in providing mechanical support and protecting the plant from environmental stress.

Its presence is a key botanical feature in various plant families, particularly in those with robust growth habits.
