Senecio cynthioides

Senecio cynthioides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, blue-green leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to colonize new areas effectively.

Due to its resilience, it is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
