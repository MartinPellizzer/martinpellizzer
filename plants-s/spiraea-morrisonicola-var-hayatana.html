Spiraea morrisonicola var. hayatana

Spiraea morrisonicola var. hayatana, commonly known as the Japanese spirea, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting floral display.
