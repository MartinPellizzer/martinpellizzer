Silene cucubalus subsp. alpina

Silene cucubalus subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments.

The plant features showy pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
