Seriphidium szowitzianum

Seriphidium szowitzianum, commonly known as the Armenian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a low-growing, herbaceous structure with narrow, linear leaves. Its yellowish flower heads appear in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
