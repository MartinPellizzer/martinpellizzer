Swartzia latifolia var. sylvestris

Swartzia latifolia var. sylvestris, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued in the furniture and musical instrument industries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds.
