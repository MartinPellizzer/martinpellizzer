Schizoglossum stenoglossum subsp. latifolium

Schizoglossum stenoglossum subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf schizoglossum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
