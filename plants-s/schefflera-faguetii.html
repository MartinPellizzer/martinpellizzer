Schefflera faguetii

Schefflera faguetii, commonly known as the Faguet's umbrella plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Araliaceae family.

Native to Madagascar, it is characterized by its large, compound leaves that radiate from a central stem, giving it a distinctive umbrella-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red berries, which are a feature of its reproductive cycle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
