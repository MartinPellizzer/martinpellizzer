Solanum meyeri-johannis

Solanum meyeri-johannis, commonly known as the Meyer's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
