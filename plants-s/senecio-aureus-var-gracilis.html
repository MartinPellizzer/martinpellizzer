Senecio aureus var. gracilis

Senecio aureus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender yellow beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun.
