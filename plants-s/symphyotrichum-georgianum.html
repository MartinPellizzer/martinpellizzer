Symphyotrichum georgianum

Symphyotrichum georgianum, commonly known as the Georgia aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and typically grows in open woodlands and along roadsides. The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads in the fall, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has narrow, linear leaves and a bushy growth habit, often reaching heights of up to 2 feet.

Symphyotrichum georgianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
