Scrophularia sintenisii

Scrophularia sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis' figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Scrophularia sintenisii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
