Spiraea media var. rotundifolia

Spiraea media var. rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaved weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cascading clusters of white flowers that bloom in early to mid-spring. The leaves are oval-shaped and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. This plant thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
