Salvia corazonica

Salvia corazonica, commonly known as the heartleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and aromatic properties. Salvia corazonica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
