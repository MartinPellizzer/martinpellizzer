Saccolabium hariotianum

Saccolabium hariotianum, commonly known as the Harriot's saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Saccolabium hariotianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
