Stipa tianschanica var. gobica

Stipa tianschanica var. gobica, commonly known as the Gobi sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. This plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use. Its root system helps stabilize sandy soils, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.

The plant plays a role in preventing soil erosion and provides habitat for various small organisms.
