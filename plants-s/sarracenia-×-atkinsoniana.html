Sarracenia × atkinsoniana

Sarracenia × atkinsoniana, commonly known as the Atkinson's pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, tubular, red-tinged leaves that form specialized pitcher structures to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its pitchers are often adorned with fine hairs and nectar-producing glands that attract prey.

This hybrid is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and unique ecological adaptations.
