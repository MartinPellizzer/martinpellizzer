Syzygium hookeri

Syzygium hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's clove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia, particularly Queensland. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Syzygium hookeri is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
