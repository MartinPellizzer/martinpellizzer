Salix planifolia var. nelsonii

Salix planifolia var. nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's willow, is a variant of the broadleaf willow.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces catkins in early spring, which are characteristic of willow species.

This variety is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
