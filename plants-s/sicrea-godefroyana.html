Sicrea godefroyana

Sicrea godefroyana, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage, with flowers that resemble small stars. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it thrives in nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
