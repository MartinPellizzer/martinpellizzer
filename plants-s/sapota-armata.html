Sapota armata

Sapota armata, commonly known as the Brazilian sapote, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The fruit is a smooth, green or yellowish oval with a sweet, creamy pulp that is rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
