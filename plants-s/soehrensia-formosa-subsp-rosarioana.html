Soehrensia formosa subsp. rosarioana

Soehrensia formosa subsp.

rosarioana, commonly known as the Rosarioana beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba. It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are a notable feature, often attracting birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.
