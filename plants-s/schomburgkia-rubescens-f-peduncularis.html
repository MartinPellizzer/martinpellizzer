Schomburgkia rubescens f. peduncularis

Schomburgkia rubescens f. peduncularis, commonly known as the pink-flowered cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its vibrant, showy blooms.

It originates from a cross between Schomburgkia rubescens and Cattleya trianae, resulting in a plant with distinctive pink and white petals. The flowers are typically borne on long, upright peduncles, giving the plant its "peduncularis" designation. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a horizontal rhizome with new growth emerging from the base.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
