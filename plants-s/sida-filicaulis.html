Sida filicaulis

Sida filicaulis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or agricultural fields.

Sida filicaulis is known for its rapid growth and ability to compete aggressively with other plants.
