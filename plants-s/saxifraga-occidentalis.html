Saxifraga occidentalis

Saxifraga occidentalis, commonly known as the western saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky, alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Saxifraga occidentalis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.
