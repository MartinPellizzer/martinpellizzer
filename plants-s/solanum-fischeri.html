Solanum fischeri

Solanum fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and yellow flowers. Its berries are small, fleshy, and contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.
