Semecarpus travancoricus

Semecarpus travancoricus, commonly known as the Travancore satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Hippocastanaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
