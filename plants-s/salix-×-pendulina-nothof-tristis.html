Salix × pendulina nothof. tristis

Salix × pendulina nothof.

tristis, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect. It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is a natural hybrid between Salix tristis and other willow species.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color on the underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adaptable to a variety of soil conditions.
