Syzygium propinquum

Syzygium propinquum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a resinous sap.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
