Scleria dioica

Scleria dioica, commonly known as the slender false flax, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale blue flowers. Scleria dioica is often found in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and in grasslands.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
