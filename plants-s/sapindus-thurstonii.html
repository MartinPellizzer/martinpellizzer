Sapindus thurstonii

Sapindus thurstonii, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruiting habits.
