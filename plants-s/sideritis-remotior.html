Sideritis remotior

Sideritis remotior, commonly known as the Greek mountain tea, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and thyme. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Sideritis remotior thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.
