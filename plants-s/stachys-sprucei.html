Stachys sprucei

Stachys sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce's hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

Stachys sprucei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
