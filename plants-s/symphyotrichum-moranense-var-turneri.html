Symphyotrichum moranense var. turneri

Symphyotrichum moranense var. turneri, commonly known as Turner's New England aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features showy purple to pink ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
