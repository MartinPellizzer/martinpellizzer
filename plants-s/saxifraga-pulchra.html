Saxifraga pulchra

Saxifraga pulchra, commonly known as the "Beauty of the Sierra," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Saxifraga pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
