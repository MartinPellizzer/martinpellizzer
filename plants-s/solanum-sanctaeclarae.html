Solanum sanctaeclarae

Solanum sanctaeclarae, commonly known as the Holy Clara's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for cultivation and consumption.
