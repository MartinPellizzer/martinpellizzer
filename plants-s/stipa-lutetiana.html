Stipa lutetiana

Stipa lutetiana, commonly known as the French needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.

Stipa lutetiana is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
