Saussurea triangulata var. hoasii

Saussurea triangulata var. hoasii, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features a slender, branched stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers, which are arranged in a panicle-like inflorescence. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered with dense white hairs.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
