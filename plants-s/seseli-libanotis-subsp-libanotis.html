Seseli libanotis subsp. libanotis

Seseli libanotis subsp.

libanotis, commonly known as Lebanon thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and appear in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators.

It is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
