Stenoglossum hamatum

Stenoglossum hamatum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. Native to the tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it often grows in association with other epiphytic plants.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
