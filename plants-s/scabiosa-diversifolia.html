Scabiosa diversifolia

Scabiosa diversifolia, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Scabiosa diversifolia is a perennial species that often forms low, compact rosettes, and it is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
