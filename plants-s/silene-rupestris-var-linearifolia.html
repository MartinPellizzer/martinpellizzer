Silene rupestris var. linearifolia

Silene rupestris var. linearifolia, commonly known as the rock pink or rock campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, giving the plant a distinctive, grass-like appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
