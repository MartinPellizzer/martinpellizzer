Sasa tsukubensis var. melinacra

Sasa tsukubensis var. melinacra, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or melinacra sasa, is a variant of the sasa plant native to Japan.

It is a perennial grass-like plant that belongs to the grass family (Poaceae). The plant typically grows in dense clumps, forming a mat-like structure with long, slender leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize areas effectively.
