Sideritis ochroleuca var. mairei

Sideritis ochroleuca var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's white heath, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
