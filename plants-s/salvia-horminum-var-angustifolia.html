Salvia horminum var. angustifolia

Salvia horminum var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal practices.
