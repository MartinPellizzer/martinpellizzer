Solanum salzmannianum

Solanum salzmannianum, commonly known as the Salzmann's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
