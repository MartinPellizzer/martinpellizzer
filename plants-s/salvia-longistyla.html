Salvia longistyla

Salvia longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia longistyla is often grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
