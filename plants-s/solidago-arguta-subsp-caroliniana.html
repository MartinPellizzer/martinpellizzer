Solidago arguta subsp. caroliniana

Solidago arguta subsp.

caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks.

The plant features upright, branching stems that are hairy and often reddish in color. Its showy golden-yellow inflorescences consist of numerous small disk flowers surrounded by sterile ray florets.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
