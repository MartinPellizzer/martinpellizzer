Spennera brachybotrya

Spennera brachybotrya, commonly known as the compact-leaved spennera, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, stiff leaves that are arranged in a compact rosette, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often concealed within the foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
