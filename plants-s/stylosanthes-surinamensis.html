Stylosanthes surinamensis

Stylosanthes surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam stylo, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is a perennial forage plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing several seeds. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in pasture systems.

Stylosanthes surinamensis is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
