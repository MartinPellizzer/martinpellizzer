Solanum neoanglicum

Solanum neoanglicum, commonly known as the New Zealand nightshade, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant of caution.

Solanum neoanglicum is often found in coastal and forested areas, thriving in a variety of soil types.
