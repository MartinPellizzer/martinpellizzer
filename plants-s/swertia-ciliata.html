Swertia ciliata

Swertia ciliata, commonly known as the Indian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have ciliate margins, giving it its scientific name.

Swertia ciliata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
