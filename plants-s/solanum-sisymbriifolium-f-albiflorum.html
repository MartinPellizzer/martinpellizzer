Solanum sisymbriifolium f. albiflorum

Solanum sisymbriifolium f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its white or pale yellow flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia. It produces small, berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
