Serruria triplicatoternata

Serruria triplicatoternata, commonly known as the three-lobed serruria, is a South African plant belonging to the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
