Sarcostemma lysimachioides

Sarcostemma lysimachioides, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance through its succulent stems and leaves.

Sarcostemma lysimachioides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
