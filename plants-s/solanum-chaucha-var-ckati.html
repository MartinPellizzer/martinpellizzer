Solanum chaucha var. ckati

Solanum chaucha var. ckati, commonly known as the Cakati nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
