Sideritis atlantica

Sideritis atlantica, commonly known as Atlantic sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean, particularly in Greece and the western Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and thyme. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, cylindrical seed pods.

Sideritis atlantica is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
