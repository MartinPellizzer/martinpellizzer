Solanum acropterum

Solanum acropterum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually narrow and lance-shaped. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a spherical berry that ripens to a dark purple color.

Solanum acropterum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
