Senna speciosa

Senna speciosa, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Senna speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
