Stenia falcata

Stenia falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf stenia, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowering period occurs during the dry season, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Stenia falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
