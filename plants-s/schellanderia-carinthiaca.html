Schellanderia carinthiaca

Schellanderia carinthiaca, commonly known as the Carinthian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Schellanderia carinthiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
