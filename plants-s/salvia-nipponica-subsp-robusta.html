Salvia nipponica subsp. robusta

Salvia nipponica subsp.

robusta, commonly known as Japanese wood sage, is a perennial shrub native to Japan. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces showy purple to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its upright growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
