Salix × antverpiensis

Salix × antverpiensis, commonly known as the Antwerp willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright green, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters and prefers moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance.

The flowers are small and catkin-like, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.
