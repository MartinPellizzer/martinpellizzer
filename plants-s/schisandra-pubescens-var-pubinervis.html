Schisandra pubescens var. pubinervis

Schisandra pubescens var. pubinervis, commonly known as the pubescent five-flavor vine, is a member of the Schisandraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive pubescent texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
