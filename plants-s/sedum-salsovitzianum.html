Sedum salsovitzianum

Sedum salsovitzianum, commonly known as the Salsovitz's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
