Stellaria glauca lusus ciliolata

Stellaria glauca lusus ciliolata, commonly known as the silverweed or lesser starwort, is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its prostrate or ascending habit.

It features small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. The leaves are usually alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine, silky hairs that give the plant a silvery appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This plant is known for its ability to spread via seeds and its tolerance to various environmental conditions.
