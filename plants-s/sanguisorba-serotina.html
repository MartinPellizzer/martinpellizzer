Sanguisorba serotina

Sanguisorba serotina, commonly known as late sanguisorba, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, reddish-orange flowers. The plant produces fibrous roots and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Sanguisorba serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinators.
