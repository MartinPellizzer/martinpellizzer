Shorea wangtianshuea subsp. vietnamensis

Shorea wangtianshuea subsp.

vietnamensis, commonly known as Vietnamese yellow ebony, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Cambodia, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a high oil content.

The plant typically grows in lowland and hill evergreen forests, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody fruits containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its durable timber and is also studied for its ecological role in tropical forest ecosystems.
