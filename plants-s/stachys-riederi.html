Stachys riederi

Stachys riederi, commonly known as the Armenian betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage and produces spikes of small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which have a mint-like scent, and its preference for well-drained, rocky soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stachys riederi is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
