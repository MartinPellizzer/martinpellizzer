Silene vulgaris f. latifolia

Silene vulgaris f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to red flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene vulgaris f. latifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
