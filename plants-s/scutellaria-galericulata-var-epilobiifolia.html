Scutellaria galericulata var. epilobiifolia

Scutellaria galericulata var. epilobiifolia, commonly known as the purple flowered skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its botanical characteristics include a square stem, hairy foliage, and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
