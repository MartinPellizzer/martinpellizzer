Statice ebracteata

Statice ebracteata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt-tolerant hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to saline environments.

Statice ebracteata is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms.
