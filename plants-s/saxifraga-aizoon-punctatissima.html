Saxifraga aizoon punctatissima

Saxifraga aizoon punctatissima, commonly known as the Sierra Valley saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, alpine environments. The plant has basal leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture. It produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Its adaptations to harsh, high-altitude conditions make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
