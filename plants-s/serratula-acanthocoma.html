Serratula acanthocoma

Serratula acanthocoma, commonly known as the thorned sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features hairy, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Serratula acanthocoma is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, rocky environments.

It is considered a weed in some areas due to its aggressive growth habit.
