Symplocos koidzumiana

Symplocos koidzumiana, commonly known as the Koidzumi laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn, and it produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. Symplocos koidzumiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color changes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
