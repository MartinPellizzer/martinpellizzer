Saxifraga embergeri

Saxifraga embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Saxifraga embergeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
