Stachytarpheta strigosa var. alainii

Stachytarpheta strigosa var. alainii, commonly known as Alain's stachytarpheta, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
