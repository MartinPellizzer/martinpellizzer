Schaueria populifolia

Schaueria populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf schaueria, is a perennial herb native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its tropical environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the wet season. Schaueria populifolia is often found growing in shaded, moist areas, such as under the canopy of taller trees.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on tropical plant adaptations and ecological interactions.
