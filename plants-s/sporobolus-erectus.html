Sporobolus erectus

Sporobolus erectus, commonly known as the upright dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and steppe environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
