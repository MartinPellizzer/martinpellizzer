Selaginella porphyrospora

Selaginella porphyrospora, commonly known as the red-stemmed spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with upright, spirally arranged leaves that are often a deep green or reddish hue. Its reproductive structures include microsporangia and megasporangia, which produce spores for sexual reproduction.

Selaginella porphyrospora is notable for its ability to survive in nutrient-poor soils and its resilience to environmental stress.
