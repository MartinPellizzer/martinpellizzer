Sasaella decipiens

Sasaella decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive sasa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ability to mimic other plants, it is sometimes referred to as a deceptive species in ecological studies.
