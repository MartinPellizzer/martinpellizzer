Sterculia tannaensis

Sterculia tannaensis, commonly known as the Tanna tallow tree, is a tropical tree native to the island of Tanna in Vanuatu.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has cultural significance to the local communities and is used for various traditional purposes.
