Styrax pachyphyllus

Styrax pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaved styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Styrax pachyphyllus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
