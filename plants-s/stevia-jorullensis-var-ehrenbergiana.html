Stevia jorullensis var. ehrenbergiana

Stevia jorullensis var. ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the Jorullensis stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for containing high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

Due to its medicinal and commercial potential, it has gained interest in herbal and natural product research.
