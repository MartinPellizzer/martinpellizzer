Shorea montigena

Shorea montigena, commonly known as the yellow meranti, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its tall stature and buttressed roots. The tree produces heavy, durable timber that is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth surface and are arranged alternately along the branches.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in panicles, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.
