Silene dionysii var. pumila

Silene dionysii var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf winecup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is a rare and delicate species, often found in specific microhabitats.
