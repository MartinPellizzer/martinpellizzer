Scirpus subquadriflorus

Scirpus subquadriflorus, commonly known as the four-flowered bulrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that bears four rows of spikelets, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist to submerged soils. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Scirpus subquadriflorus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and filtering water.
