Scirpus nudus

Scirpus nudus, commonly known as the naked sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in summer, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. Scirpus nudus thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its presence often indicates good water quality and contributes to habitat diversity for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
