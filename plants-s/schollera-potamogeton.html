Schollera potamogeton

Schollera potamogeton, commonly known as water shield, is a semi-aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or wetlands, with its leaves floating on the surface while its roots anchor in the substrate. The plant has broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer, aiding in buoyancy. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or yellowish, and appear in clusters.

Schollera potamogeton plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
