Scutellaria celtidifolia

Scutellaria celtidifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Scutellaria celtidifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
