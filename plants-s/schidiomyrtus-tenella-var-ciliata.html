Schidiomyrtus tenella var. ciliata

Schidiomyrtus tenella var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and its distinctive, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
