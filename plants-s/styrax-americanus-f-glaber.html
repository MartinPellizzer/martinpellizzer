Styrax americanus f. glaber

Styrax americanus f. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed American styrax, is a rare and distinctive variant of the American styrax.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lanceolate, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Due to its rarity and unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturalists alike.
