Satureja herpyllomorpha

Satureja herpyllomorpha, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional medicine for its antiseptic properties.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
