Senecio variostipellatus

Senecio variostipellatus, commonly known as the variable-stippled groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive variegated pattern, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
