Senecio subcrassifolius

Senecio subcrassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in dry gardens.
