Scirpus ablepharus

Scirpus ablepharus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often submerged or floating, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems.

Scirpus ablepharus is valued for its ability to stabilize shorelines and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
