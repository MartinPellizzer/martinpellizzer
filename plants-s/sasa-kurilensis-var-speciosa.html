Sasa kurilensis var. speciosa

Sasa kurilensis var. speciosa, commonly known as the ornamental bamboo, is a deciduous shrub native to the Japanese islands and parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by triangular, edible fruits. Its foliage is often used in traditional Japanese landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a versatile ornamental plant.
