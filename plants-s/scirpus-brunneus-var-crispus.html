Scirpus brunneus var. crispus

Scirpus brunneus var. crispus, commonly known as the curly bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright stems that are often covered with a fine, hairy coating. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive crinkled or curled appearance along the edges. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to reddish flowers in the summer months.

This plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
