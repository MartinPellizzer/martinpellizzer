Scirpus uniflorus

Scirpus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, spike-like inflorescence at the top of its stem, bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers. Scirpus uniflorus typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Its presence often indicates good water quality and plays a role in stabilizing shoreline vegetation.
