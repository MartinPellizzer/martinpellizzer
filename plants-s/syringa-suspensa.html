Syringa suspensa

Syringa suspensa, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and downy underneath. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Syringa suspensa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
