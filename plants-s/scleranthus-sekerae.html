Scleranthus sekerae

Scleranthus sekerae, commonly known as the Sekera's thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Scleranthus sekerae is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
