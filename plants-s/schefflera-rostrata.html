Schefflera rostrata

Schefflera rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown composed of multiple leaflets arranged in a radiating pattern. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits.

Schefflera rostrata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
