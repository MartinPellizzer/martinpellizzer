Sorbus decora var. groenlandica

Sorbus decora var. groenlandica, commonly known as the Greenland whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to Greenland.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in cold, harsh environments. The plant features white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall. Its leaves are elliptical with a serrated edge, and the bark is often grayish-brown with shallow fissures.

This species is adapted to Arctic and subarctic climates, showcasing resilience to extreme conditions.
