Spiraea japonica var. incisa

Spiraea japonica var. incisa, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features finely cut, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

This ornamental plant is valued for its compact growth habit and showy floral displays.
