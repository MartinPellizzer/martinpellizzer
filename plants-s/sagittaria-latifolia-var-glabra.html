Sagittaria latifolia var. glabra

Sagittaria latifolia var. glabra, commonly known as broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that are broad and smooth, with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences that rise above the water.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.
