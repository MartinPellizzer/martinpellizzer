Spiranthes richardiana

Spiranthes richardiana, commonly known as Richard's spiral orchid, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescences. The flowers are typically white or pale green, with three sepals and three petals, and are arranged in a tight, coiled pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands.

The plant is noted for its unique floral structure and is often found growing in colonies.
