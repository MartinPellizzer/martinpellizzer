Saccolabium bivittatum

Saccolabium bivittatum, commonly known as the two-striped legume, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive two-colored pattern, and its flowers are typically pale or yellowish in color.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
