Silene italica

Silene italica, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Silene italica is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
