Solanum sepiaceum

Solanum sepiaceum, commonly known as the ghost pepper or bhut jolokia, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that develop into red, elongated fruits. The plant is notable for producing one of the hottest chili peppers in the world, with a high capsaicin content. Its botanical features include hairy stems, alternate leaves, and a distinctive, pungent aroma.

Solanum sepiaceum is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
