Scorzonera laciniata var. decumbens

Scorzonera laciniata var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing habit, with stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are typically lobed and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
