Selaginella sartorii var. venezuelensis

Selaginella sartorii var. venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan spikemoss, is a vascular fern that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features creeping stems with whorled leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are clustered at the tips of the stems and contain spores.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in maintaining soil stability and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
