Stevia lundiana

Stevia lundiana, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are typically harvested and processed to extract the sweet compounds.

Stevia lundiana is often cultivated as a herbal plant for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
