Scilla pneumonanthe

Scilla pneumonanthe, commonly known as the lungwort squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine. Scilla pneumonanthe thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
