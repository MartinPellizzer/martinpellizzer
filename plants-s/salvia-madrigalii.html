Salvia madrigalii

Salvia madrigalii, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its medicinal properties have been utilized in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.

Salvia madrigalii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
