Stachys elongata

Stachys elongata, commonly known as the slender betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys elongata thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
