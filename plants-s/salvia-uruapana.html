Salvia uruapana

Salvia uruapana, commonly known as the Uruapan sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia uruapana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
