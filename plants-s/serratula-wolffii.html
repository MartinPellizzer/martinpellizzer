Serratula wolffii

Serratula wolffii, commonly known as the Wolff's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disc florets. The plant features a robust taproot system and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its yellow to orange flower heads appear in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Serratula wolffii is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine soils.
