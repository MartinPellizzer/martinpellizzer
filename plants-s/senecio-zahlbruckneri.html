Senecio zahlbruckneri

Senecio zahlbruckneri, commonly known as the Zahner's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens.
