Satureja sericifolia

Satureja sericifolia, commonly known as silky mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, silvery-gray, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Satureja sericifolia is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties.
