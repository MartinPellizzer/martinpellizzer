Saxifraga pedemontana cymosa

Saxifraga pedemontana cymosa, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in cymes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Saxifraga pedemontana cymosa is often found in mountain meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
