Silene aprica var. ryukyuensis

Silene aprica var. ryukyuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of its native habitat. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces capsules as its fruiting structure.

Silene aprica var. ryukyuensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
