Senna alcaparra

Senna alcaparra, commonly known as Portuguese senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and serrated.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are used in herbal remedies for their laxative effects.
