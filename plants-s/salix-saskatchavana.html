Salix saskatchavana

Salix saskatchavana, commonly known as the Saskatchewan willow, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it helps prevent soil erosion.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
