Sauropus elachophyllus var. latior

Sauropus elachophyllus var. latior, commonly known as the broadleaf sauropus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its foliage is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate partial shade.
