Silene pacifica

Silene pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific catchfly, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Silene pacifica is notable for its ability to thrive in coastal and alpine environments.
