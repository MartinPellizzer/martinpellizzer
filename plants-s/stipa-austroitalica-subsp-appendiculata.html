Stipa austroitalica subsp. appendiculata

Stipa austroitalica subsp.

appendiculata, commonly known as the Italian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Italy and parts of the Balkans. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a fibrous texture and is adapted to temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
