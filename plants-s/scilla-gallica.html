Scilla gallica

Scilla gallica, commonly known as the Persian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scilla gallica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize over time.
