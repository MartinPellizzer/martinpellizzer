Scirpus × celakovskyanus

Scirpus × celakovskyanus, commonly known as the giant bulrush, is a hybrid wetland plant that thrives in marshy and aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, triangular spikelets at the top of its stems, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to spread rapidly and form dense stands in waterlogged soils.

Scirpus × celakovskyanus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
