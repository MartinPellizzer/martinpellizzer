Septemeranthus nicobaricus

Septemeranthus nicobaricus, commonly known as the Nicobar morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
