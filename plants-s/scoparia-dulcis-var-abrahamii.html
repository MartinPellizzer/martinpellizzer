Scoparia dulcis var. abrahamii

Scoparia dulcis var. abrahamii, commonly known as Abraham's sweet scolopendra, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
