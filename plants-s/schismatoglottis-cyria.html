Schismatoglottis cyria

Schismatoglottis cyria, commonly known as the Cyria's slit-lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with strikingly colored petals and sepals, typically in shades of pink, purple, or white. Schismatoglottis cyria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts of tropical orchids.
