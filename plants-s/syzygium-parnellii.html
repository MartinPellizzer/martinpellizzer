Syzygium parnellii

Syzygium parnellii, commonly known as the Parnell's apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are yellow when ripe. Syzygium parnellii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, tropical environment to flourish.
