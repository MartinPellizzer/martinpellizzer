Solanum landbeckii

Solanum landbeckii, commonly known as the Landbeck's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berry-like fruits.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
