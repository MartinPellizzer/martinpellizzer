Setariopsis italica

Setariopsis italica, commonly known as the Italian grass or Italian millet, is a small annual grass species native to southern Europe.

It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is used as a forage crop in some regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it can thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed.
