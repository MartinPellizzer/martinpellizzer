Syringa amurensis var. mandshurica

Syringa amurensis var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Amur honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is characterized by its strong, aromatic scent and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
