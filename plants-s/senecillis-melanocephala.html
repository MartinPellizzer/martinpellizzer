Senecillis melanocephala

Senecillis melanocephala, commonly known as the black-headed senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires full sun and minimal water.
