Senacia glauca

Senacia glauca, commonly known as the blue senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery blue bloom. Its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk, and it produces small, elongated fruits.

Senacia glauca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
