Saxifraga punctata subsp. astilboides

Saxifraga punctata subsp.

astilboides, commonly known as the spotted saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves with distinctive red or purple markings.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticulture.
