Sethia lanceolata var. obtusifolia

Sethia lanceolata var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved Sethia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lanceolate, slightly fleshy leaves that are broad at the base and taper to a point, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
