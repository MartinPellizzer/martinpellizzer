Scirpus victoriensis

Scirpus victoriensis, commonly known as Victoria reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flowering spikes that rise above narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and lake margins, where it forms dense stands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its ecological dominance in these habitats.

The flowers are typically green or brown, and the plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
