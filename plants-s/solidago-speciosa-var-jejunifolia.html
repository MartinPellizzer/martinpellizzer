Solidago speciosa var. jejunifolia

Solidago speciosa var. jejunifolia, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a variant of the showy goldenrod native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant produces tall, branching stems with clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
