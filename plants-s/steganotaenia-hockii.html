Steganotaenia hockii

Steganotaenia hockii, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in a raceme, during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in its native habitat.
