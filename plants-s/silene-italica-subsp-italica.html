Silene italica subsp. italica

Silene italica subsp.

italica, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system. Silene italica subsp.

italica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
