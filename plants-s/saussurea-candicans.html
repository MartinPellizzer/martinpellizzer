Saussurea candicans

Saussurea candicans, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow to orange composite flower heads. The plant produces numerous small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
