Senecio mayurii

Senecio mayurii, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its striking appearance, it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant in various regions.
