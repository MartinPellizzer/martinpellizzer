Sida michoacana

Sida michoacana, commonly known as Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Sida michoacana is known for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and as a source of natural fiber.
