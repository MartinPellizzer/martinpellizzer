Scirpus glaucophyllus

Scirpus glaucophyllus, commonly known as the blue-leaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a distinctive bluish-green coloration.

Scirpus glaucophyllus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
