Senecio glastifolius

Senecio glastifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features wiry, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
