Secale chaldicum

Secale chaldicum, commonly known as the Persian rye or Chaldean rye, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its seeds are often used as a grain, and the plant is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal in gardens.
