Sticherus lanuginosus

Sticherus lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly sticherus, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lanugo-like foliage, which gives it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
