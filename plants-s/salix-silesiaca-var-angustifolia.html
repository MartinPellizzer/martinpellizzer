Salix silesiaca var. angustifolia

Salix silesiaca var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green and appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
