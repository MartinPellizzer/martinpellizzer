Sedum oreganum var. tenue

Sedum oreganum var. tenue, commonly known as dwarf stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the western United States.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, low-maintenance plants.
