Scilla kestilana

Scilla kestilana, commonly known as the Kestilana squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant is typically found in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant for extended periods.

Scilla kestilana is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and hardy nature.
