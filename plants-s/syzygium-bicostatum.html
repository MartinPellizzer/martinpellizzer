Syzygium bicostatum

Syzygium bicostatum, commonly known as the two-costed sandoric.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.
