Salvia cinnabarina var. typica

Salvia cinnabarina var. typica, commonly known as the scarlet salvia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its vibrant color and compact growth habit make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
