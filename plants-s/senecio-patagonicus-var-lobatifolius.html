Senecio patagonicus var. lobatifolius

Senecio patagonicus var. lobatifolius, commonly known as the Patagonian ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, adapting to its arid environment. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is noted for its resilience in harsh, dry conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
