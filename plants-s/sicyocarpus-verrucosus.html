Sicyocarpus verrucosus

Sicyocarpus verrucosus, commonly known as the rough-skin nutmeg, is a tropical shrub native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its woody stem, simple leaves with a rough texture, and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that mature to a dark brown color. Its most notable feature is the aromatic seed kernel, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The species is valued for its unique botanical and aromatic properties.
