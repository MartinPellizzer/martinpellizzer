Scirpus rothii

Scirpus rothii, commonly known as the Roth's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces clusters of small, cylindrical flowers at the top of the stems, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize wetland areas effectively.

Scirpus rothii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
