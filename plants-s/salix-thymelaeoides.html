Salix thymelaeoides

Salix thymelaeoides, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in riparian zones.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
