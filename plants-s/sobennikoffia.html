Sobennikoffia

Sobennikoffia, commonly known as the dwarf pincushion cactus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plants are characterized by their compact, globular shape and spiny stems, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. They produce small, colorful flowers that bloom in spring, adding visual interest to their otherwise spiky appearance.

Sobennikoffia species are popular among cactus enthusiasts due to their unique form and relatively low maintenance requirements.
