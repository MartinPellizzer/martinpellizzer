Syringa persica var. coriacea

Syringa persica var. coriacea, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its bark is thick and corky, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
