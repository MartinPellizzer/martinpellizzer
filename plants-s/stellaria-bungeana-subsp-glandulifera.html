Stellaria bungeana subsp. glandulifera

Stellaria bungeana subsp.

glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to central Asia. It features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive glandular texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
