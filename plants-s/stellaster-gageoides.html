Stellaster gageoides

Stellaster gageoides, commonly known as the Gage's star-moth, is a species of sea star found in the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the family Asteriidae and is characterized by its distinctive, radiating arms that resemble a star. The plant, more accurately a marine invertebrate, has a unique anatomy with a central disc and numerous short, tapering rays. It thrives in shallow coastal waters and is often found on rocky substrates.

Due to its ecological role in benthic communities, it is an important species for marine biodiversity.
