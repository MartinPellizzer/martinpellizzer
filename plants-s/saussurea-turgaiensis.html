Saussurea turgaiensis

Saussurea turgaiensis, commonly known as the Turgai saffron, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in Kazakhstan and parts of Russia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellowish to orange-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, grassy steppes and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.

Its scientific name reflects its geographical origin, with "turgaiensis" referring to the Turgai region in Kazakhstan.
