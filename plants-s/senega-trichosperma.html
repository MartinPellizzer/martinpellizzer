Senega trichosperma

Senega trichosperma, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flower heads that resemble mist. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support pollinators like bees and butterflies.
