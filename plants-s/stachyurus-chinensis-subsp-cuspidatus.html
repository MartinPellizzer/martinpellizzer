Stachyurus chinensis subsp. cuspidatus

Stachyurus chinensis subsp.

cuspidatus, commonly known as the Chinese weeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
