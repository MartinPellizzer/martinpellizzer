Scirpus pulchellus

Scirpus pulchellus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color, with inconspicuous flowers. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Scirpus pulchellus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife species.
