Siphocampylus oblongifolius var. crenatus

Siphocampylus oblongifolius var. crenatus, commonly known as the crenate siphocampylus, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with wavy, crenate margins that give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
