Styloma rockiana

Styloma rockiana, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the rainy season, usually between late summer and early autumn.

Styloma rockiana is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
