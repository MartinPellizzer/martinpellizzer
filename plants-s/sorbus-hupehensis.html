Sorbus hupehensis

Sorbus hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, spherical berries that persist through the winter. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also notable for its ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
