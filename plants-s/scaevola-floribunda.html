Scaevola floribunda

Scaevola floribunda, commonly known as the common fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
