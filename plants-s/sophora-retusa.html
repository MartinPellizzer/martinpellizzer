Sophora retusa

Sophora retusa, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in gardens and natural habitats.
