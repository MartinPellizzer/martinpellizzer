Sorbus thaiszii

Sorbus thaiszii, commonly known as the Thai whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries that attract various wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

Sorbus thaiszii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
