Synaedrys teysmannii

Synaedrys teysmannii, commonly known as the Teyssmann's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated greenish-brown flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without being parasitic, and relies on specific environmental conditions for survival. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, highlighting its ecological specialization.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, Synaedrys teysmannii is considered critically endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
