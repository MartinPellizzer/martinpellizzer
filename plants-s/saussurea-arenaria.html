Saussurea arenaria

Saussurea arenaria, commonly known as the desert gentian, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its deep root system and reduced leaf surface. Its fibrous roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Saussurea arenaria plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
