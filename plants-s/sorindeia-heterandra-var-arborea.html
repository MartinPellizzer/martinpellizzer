Sorindeia heterandra var. arborea

Sorindeia heterandra var. arborea, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded crown, smooth bark, and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, round, orange-brown fruits that contain two seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types.
