Scutellaria pubescens

Scutellaria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its genus, Scutellaria, is characterized by a distinctive hooded flower structure known as a scutellum.

Scutellaria pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
