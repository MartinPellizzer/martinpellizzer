Sasa kassizanensis

Sasa kassizanensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Sasa kassizanensis is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.
