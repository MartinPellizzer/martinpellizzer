Sasa kogasensis var. nasuensis

Sasa kogasensis var. nasuensis, commonly known as the Nasu bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa kogasensis species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant that belongs to the Poaceae family. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are often a vibrant green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
