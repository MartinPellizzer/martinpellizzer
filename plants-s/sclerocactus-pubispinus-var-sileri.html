Sclerocactus pubispinus var. sileri

Sclerocactus pubispinus var. sileri, commonly known as the Silverspot Sclerocactus, is a unique cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray spines that provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its stems are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments.

This variety is noted for its slow growth and requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
