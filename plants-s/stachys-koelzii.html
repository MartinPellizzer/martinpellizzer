Stachys koelzii

Stachys koelzii, commonly known as the Koelz's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, purple to pink flowers in whorled clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Stachys koelzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering structure.
