Sethia obtusifolia var. straminea

Sethia obtusifolia var. straminea, commonly known as the yellow Sethia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a blunt tip and a smooth upper surface. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
