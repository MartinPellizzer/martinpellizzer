Solanum pterocaulum var. dichotomiflorum

Solanum pterocaulum var. dichotomiflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
