Smilax hirsutior

Smilax hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with spiny edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax hirsutior is known for its aggressive growth and is sometimes considered a noxious weed.
