Silene longitubulosa

Silene longitubulosa, commonly known as the long-tubular catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Silene longitubulosa is a perennial species that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
