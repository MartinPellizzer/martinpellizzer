Saccolabium borneense

Saccolabium borneense, commonly known as the Bornean saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Saccolabium borneense is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
