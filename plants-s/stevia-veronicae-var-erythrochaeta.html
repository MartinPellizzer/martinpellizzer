Stevia veronicae var. erythrochaeta

Stevia veronicae var. erythrochaeta, commonly known as red-hair stevia, is a variant of the stevia plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-hued leaves, which set it apart from other stevia species. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, making it a popular natural sweetener.

Due to its unique appearance and potential health benefits, it is cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
