Sanguisorba garganica

Sanguisorba garganica, commonly known as the Gargano sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like buckwheat and rhubarb. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid.

Sanguisorba garganica typically blooms with clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer.
