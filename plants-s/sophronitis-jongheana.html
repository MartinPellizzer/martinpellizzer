Sophronitis jongheana

Sophronitis jongheana, commonly known as the Jonghe's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that aids in pollination.

Sophronitis jongheana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
