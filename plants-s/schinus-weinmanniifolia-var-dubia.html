Schinus weinmanniifolia var. dubia

Schinus weinmanniifolia var. dubia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or Brazilian peppertree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to northeastern Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a distinctive, feathery appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale yellow, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
