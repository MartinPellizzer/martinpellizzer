Semecarpus ferrugineus

Semecarpus ferrugineus, commonly known as the ironwood or red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elliptical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a reddish or ferrugineous (iron-like) appearance, giving the species its scientific name.

The tree is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
