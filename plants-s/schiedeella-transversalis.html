Schiedeella transversalis

Schiedeella transversalis, commonly known as the transverse schiedeella, is a rare and unique member of the Schiedeellaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by the development of its distinctive, three-winged fruits. Native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments with high humidity.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
