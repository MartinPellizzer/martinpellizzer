Sedum crenulatissimum

Sedum crenulatissimum, commonly known as the scalycup stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or purple, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
