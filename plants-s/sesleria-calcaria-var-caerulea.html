Sesleria calcaria var. caerulea

Sesleria calcaria var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-eyed sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique coloration and compact growth habit.
