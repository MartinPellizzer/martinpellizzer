Saccolabium humblotii

Saccolabium humblotii, commonly known as the Humblot's saccolabium, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in savannas and grasslands.

Its seeds are enclosed in a pod-like fruit, which splits open to release the seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
