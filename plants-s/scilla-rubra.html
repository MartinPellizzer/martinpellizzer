Scilla rubra

Scilla rubra, commonly known as red scilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to red flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous base.

Scilla rubra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
