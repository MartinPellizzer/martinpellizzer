Singana

Singana, commonly known as the African violet, is a popular flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with upright stems and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers, which come in a variety of colors, bloom throughout the year under proper conditions.
