Scabiosa succisa var. pratensis

Scabiosa succisa var. pratensis, commonly known as the common scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
