Saussurea cirsium

Saussurea cirsium, commonly known as the common snow lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy composite flower heads. The plant typically produces yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by pale blue to white ray florets. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to survive in harsh mountainous regions.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
