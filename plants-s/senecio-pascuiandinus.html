Senecio pascuiandinus

Senecio pascuiandinus, commonly known as the Andean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

Senecio pascuiandinus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
