Senecio rahmeri

Senecio rahmeri, commonly known as the Cape York senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

Senecio rahmeri produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
