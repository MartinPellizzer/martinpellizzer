Stipa pulcherrima subsp. bavarica

Stipa pulcherrima subsp.

bavarica, commonly known as the Bavarian feathergrass, is a grass species native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its slender, wiry culms and fine, silky florets that give it a delicate, feathery appearance.

This subspecies typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its low maintenance requirements.
