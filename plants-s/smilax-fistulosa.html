Smilax fistulosa

Smilax fistulosa, commonly known as the yellow smilax, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features thick, woody stems with sharp, spiny projections, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swamps, and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.
