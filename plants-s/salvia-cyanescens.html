Salvia cyanescens

Salvia cyanescens, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Salvia cyanescens is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
