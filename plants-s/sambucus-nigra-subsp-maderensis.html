Sambucus nigra subsp. maderensis

Sambucus nigra subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's elderberry, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution due to their potential toxicity. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
