Stryphnodendron excelsum

Stryphnodendron excelsum, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tall, evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It can grow up to 50 meters in height and has a broad, buttressed trunk. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain multiple seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts. Its leaves are compound, with long, narrow leaflets, and it flowers with small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant is notable for its symbiotic relationship with specific insects for pollination and its role in supporting diverse wildlife.
