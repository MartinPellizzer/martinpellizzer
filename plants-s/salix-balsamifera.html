Salix balsamifera

Salix balsamifera, commonly known as the balsam poplar, is a deciduous tree native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

The tree has a rapid growth rate and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
