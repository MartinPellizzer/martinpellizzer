Spiraea × tristis

Spiraea × tristis, commonly known as weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. Its leaves are ovate and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the flowering stems.

Spiraea × tristis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
