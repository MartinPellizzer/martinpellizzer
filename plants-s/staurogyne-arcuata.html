Staurogyne arcuata

Staurogyne arcuata, commonly known as the curly waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has long, flexible stems that often coil or curl, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, arranged in whorls around the stem.

Staurogyne arcuata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
