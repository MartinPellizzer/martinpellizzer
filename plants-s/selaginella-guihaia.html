Selaginella guihaia

Selaginella guihaia, commonly known as the Chinese clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a rosette of scale-like leaves and produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila. It is characterized by its ability to survive in low-light conditions and has a unique reproductive system involving both spores and seeds.

Selaginella guihaia is also notable for its role in ecological succession and its use in traditional medicine.
