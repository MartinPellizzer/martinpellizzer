Stenocactus albatus

Stenocactus albatus, commonly known as the white-stemmed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers during the spring and summer months. Its stem is covered in a thick, waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Stenocactus albatus is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
