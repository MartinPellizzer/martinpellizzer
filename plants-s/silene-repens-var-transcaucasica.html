Silene repens var. transcaucasica

Silene repens var. transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian dogwood, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, often hairy stems and basal leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
