Saussurea frondosa

Saussurea frondosa, commonly known as the Alpine yellowhead, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the European Alps and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cold, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

Saussurea frondosa is often found in mountainous regions at elevations above 2,000 meters.
