Senecio furusei

Senecio furusei, commonly known as the Japanese groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream sides. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Senecio furusei produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
