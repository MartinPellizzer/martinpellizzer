Syzygium venosum

Syzygium venosum, commonly known as the velvet apple or red velvet apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. Syzygium venosum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
