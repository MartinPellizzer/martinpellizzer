Shorea philippinensis

Shorea philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine ebony, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a high oil content. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The tree plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat and is valued for its durable, high-quality timber.
