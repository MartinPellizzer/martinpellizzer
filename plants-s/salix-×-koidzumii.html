Salix × koidzumii

Salix × koidzumii, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix integra and Salix triandra, characterized by its drooping branches that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces catkins in early spring, and its leaves are broad, oval, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.
