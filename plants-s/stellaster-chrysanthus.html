Stellaster chrysanthus

Stellaster chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden starflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals that radiate from a central point, resembling a star. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stellaster chrysanthus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
