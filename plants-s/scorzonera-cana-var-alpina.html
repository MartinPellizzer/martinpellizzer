Scorzonera cana var. alpina

Scorzonera cana var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, preferring cool, moist environments. The plant features a robust taproot, which is often edible and has a slightly bitter taste. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with five petals.

Scorzonera cana var. alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
