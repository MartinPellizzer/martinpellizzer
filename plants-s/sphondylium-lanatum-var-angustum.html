Sphondylium lanatum var. angustum

Sphondylium lanatum var. angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed woodspurry, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as pomes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland edges and grasslands.
