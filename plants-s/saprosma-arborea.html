Saprosma arborea

Saprosma arborea, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, rounded canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic qualities.
