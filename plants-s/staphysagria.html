Staphysagria

Staphysagria, commonly known as dropwort, is a perennial herb belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered flowers that resemble small drops of water, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Staphysagria is also known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
