Salvia jamzadii

Salvia jamzadii, commonly known as the Afghan sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Salvia jamzadii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
