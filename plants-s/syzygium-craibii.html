Syzygium craibii

Syzygium craibii, commonly known as the red fruit tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its ornamental red fruits that appear in clusters. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Syzygium craibii is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit display.
