Saussurea pseudoeriostemon

Saussurea pseudoeriostemon, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, thriving at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
