Salix × heerii

Salix × heerii, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

Salix × heerii is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
