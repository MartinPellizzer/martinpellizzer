Satureja remota

Satureja remota, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Satureja remota is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
