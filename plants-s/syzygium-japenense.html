Syzygium japenense

Syzygium japenense, commonly known as the Japanese clove.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Japan, characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers and red, fleshy fruits. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its aromatic properties. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its bark is smooth and greyish.

Syzygium japenense is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in traditional medicine.
