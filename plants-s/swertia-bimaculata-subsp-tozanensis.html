Swertia bimaculata subsp. tozanensis

Swertia bimaculata subsp.

tozanensis, commonly known as the Tozan mountain gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
