Solanum bipatens var. acuminatum

Solanum bipatens var. acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed nightshade, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive purple calyx, and the plant produces small, globose berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments in the understory of tropical forests.
