Stevia pubescens

Stevia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense heads. Its leaves contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are used as natural sweeteners.

Stevia pubescens is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
