Stylosanthes mexicana

Stylosanthes mexicana, commonly known as Mexican stylo, is a perennial legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions. It is often used as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
