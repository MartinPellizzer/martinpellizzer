Salvia cristalensis

Salvia cristalensis, commonly known as the crystalline sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia cristalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
