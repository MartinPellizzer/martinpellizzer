Salvia verticillata subsp. amasiaca

Salvia verticillata subsp.

amasiaca, commonly known as Armenian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Salvia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
