Selaginella belangeri f. olivacea

Selaginella belangeri f. olivacea, commonly known as the olive spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, cone-like structures that bear spores. Native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, while the stems are slender and branching.

The plant is often found in rocky or forested areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and moisture retention.
