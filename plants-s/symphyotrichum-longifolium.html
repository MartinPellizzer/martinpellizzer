Symphyotrichum longifolium

Symphyotrichum longifolium, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy purple or pink composite flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to dry soils in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

Symphyotrichum longifolium is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
