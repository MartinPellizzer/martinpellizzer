Sonchus pinnatus f. candolleanus

Sonchus pinnatus f. candolleanus, commonly known as the candollean thistle, is a variant of the common sowthistle, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and floral structure.

It is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, typically found in temperate regions. The leaves are pinnately lobed, with a rough texture and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers form a composite head of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and in waste areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
