Spiraea fortunei var. subpaniculata

Spiraea fortunei var. subpaniculata, commonly known as the panicled spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its delicate floral display and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
