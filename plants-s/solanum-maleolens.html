Solanum maleolens

Solanum maleolens, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that display a complex pattern of purple and white petals. The plant produces berries that are typically orange or yellow when mature, containing toxic seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum maleolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
