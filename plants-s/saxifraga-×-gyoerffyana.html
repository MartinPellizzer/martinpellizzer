Saxifraga × gyoerffyana

Saxifraga × gyoerffyana, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically gray-green in color, often covered with a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Saxifraga × gyoerffyana is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
