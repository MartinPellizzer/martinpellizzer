Sorbus gilgitana

Sorbus gilgitana, commonly known as the Gilgit rowan, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellowish to orange-red berries. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

The fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species in its native habitat.
