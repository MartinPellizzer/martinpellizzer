Syzygium leucoxylon var. phaeophyllum

Syzygium leucoxylon var. phaeophyllum, commonly known as the blackwood ironwood, is a rare and hardy species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while its flowers are small and fragrant, often blooming in clusters.

This variety is notable for its slow growth and resilience in poor soil conditions, making it a unique subject of botanical study.
