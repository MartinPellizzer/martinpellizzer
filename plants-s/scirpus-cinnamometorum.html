Scirpus cinnamometorum

Scirpus cinnamometorum, commonly known as the cinnamon rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus cinnamometorum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
