Stachys sylvatica

Stachys sylvatica, commonly known as wood betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its opposite leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces spikes of purple or pink flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Wood betony is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
