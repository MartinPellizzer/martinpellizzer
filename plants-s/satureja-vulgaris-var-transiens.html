Satureja vulgaris var. transiens

Satureja vulgaris var. transiens, commonly known as Transalpine thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, often square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their high content of essential oils.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.
