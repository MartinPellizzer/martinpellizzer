Sisymbrium gariepinum var. nemorosum

Sisymbrium gariepinum var. nemorosum, commonly known as the woodland mustard, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and moist soils, preferring partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
