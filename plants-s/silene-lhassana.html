Silene lhassana

Silene lhassana, commonly known as the Himalayan campion, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and showy pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene lhassana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
