Schoenia filifolia

Schoenia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or brown and appear in clusters.

Schoenia filifolia is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its ornamental value.
