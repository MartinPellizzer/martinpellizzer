Salsola sieberi var. vesceritensis

Salsola sieberi var. vesceritensis, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a halophytic plant adapted to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, green or yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert regions of Central Asia.

Its resilience makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
