Solidago macrophylla f. mensalis

Solidago macrophylla f. mensalis, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a striking perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features bright yellow, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its showy flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
