Syncephalum arbutifolium

Syncephalum arbutifolium, commonly known as the redcurrant plant, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, red, edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves. The plant has alternate, simple leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees.
