Spiraea ovalis var. teretiuscula

Spiraea ovalis var. teretiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface, often turning yellow in the fall. This plant thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
