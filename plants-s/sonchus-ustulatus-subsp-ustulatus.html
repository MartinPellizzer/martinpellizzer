Sonchus ustulatus subsp. ustulatus

Sonchus ustulatus subsp.

ustulatus, commonly known as the scarlet erigeron, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough, hairy texture. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.
