Saxifraga fauriei

Saxifraga fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga fauriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
