Saxifraga caucasica

Saxifraga caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.

Saxifraga caucasica is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
