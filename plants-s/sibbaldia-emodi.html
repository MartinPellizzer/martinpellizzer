Sibbaldia emodi

Sibbaldia emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a small shrub that typically grows at elevations between 2,500 and 5,000 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Sibbaldia emodi is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
