Spinacia oleracea var. kochii

Spinacia oleracea var. kochii, commonly known as the Siberian spinach, is a hardy, cold-tolerant variety of spinach that thrives in cooler climates.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly curled leaves and robust growth habit. This variety is particularly valued for its ability to withstand frost and heavy snow, making it suitable for extended harvest periods. The leaves are rich in nutrients, including iron and vitamins A and C, contributing to its nutritional profile.

It is often cultivated in regions with short growing seasons, such as Siberia and northern Europe.
