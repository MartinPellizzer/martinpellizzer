Saxifraga coarctata var. elliptica

Saxifraga coarctata var. elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It features rosettes of elliptic, dark green leaves with a rough texture and a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in late summer, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
