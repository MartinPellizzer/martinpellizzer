Sphaeralcea caespitosa var. williamsiae

Sphaeralcea caespitosa var. williamsiae, commonly known as Williams' globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, spiny growth habit and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive globular shape. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
