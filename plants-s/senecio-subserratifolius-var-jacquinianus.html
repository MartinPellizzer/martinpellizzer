Senecio subserratifolius var. jacquinianus

Senecio subserratifolius var. jacquinianus, commonly known as the Jacob's beard or Jacob's plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear small, yellow flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, and the species is known for its drought tolerance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
