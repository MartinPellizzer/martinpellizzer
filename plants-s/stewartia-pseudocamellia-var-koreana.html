Stewartia pseudocamellia var. koreana

Stewartia pseudocamellia var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean Stewartia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Korea.

It is characterized by its elegant, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by persistent, cup-shaped fruits. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its slow growth rate and unique foliage make it a desirable addition to botanical collections.
