Solanum juripeba var. laccatum

Solanum juripeba var. laccatum, commonly known as the purple passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, purple-tinged fruits. The fruits are edible, with a sweet and aromatic pulp containing numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique, flavorful fruit.
