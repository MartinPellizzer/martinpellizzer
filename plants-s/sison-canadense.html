Sison canadense

Sison canadense, commonly known as Canada sison, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. It is often found in the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
