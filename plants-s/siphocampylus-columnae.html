Siphocampylus columnae

Siphocampylus columnae, commonly known as the columnar siphocampylus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant has fibrous roots and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
