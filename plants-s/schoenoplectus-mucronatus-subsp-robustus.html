Schoenoplectus mucronatus subsp. robustus

Schoenoplectus mucronatus subsp.

robustus, commonly known as the robust tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, often forming dense tussocks.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is well adapted to waterlogged soils and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
