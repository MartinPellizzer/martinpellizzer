Sinapis bracteolata

Sinapis bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate mustard, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, green, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemose inflorescences, which are followed by slender, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Sinapis bracteolata is a hardy species that can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
