Scaevola trinervia

Scaevola trinervia, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple, with five petals arranged in a distinctive spiral pattern. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Scaevola trinervia thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.
