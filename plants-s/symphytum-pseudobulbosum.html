Symphytum pseudobulbosum

Symphytum pseudobulbosum, commonly known as the purple bulbous bugloss, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulbous root system that stores nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
