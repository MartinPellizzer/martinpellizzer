Symphytum tuberosum subsp. tuberosum

Symphytum tuberosum subsp.

tuberosum, commonly known as the common comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces showy, spiky flower clusters in shades of blue, purple, or white during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
