Solanum dulcamara var. hastiifolia

Solanum dulcamara var. hastiifolia, commonly known as the climbing nightshade, is a variant of the common woody nightshade.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that transition from white to purple as they mature. Its berries are typically yellow or red, containing toxic alkaloids.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
