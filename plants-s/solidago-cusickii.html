Solidago cusickii

Solidago cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the central and northern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often covered with fine, soft hairs.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer to early fall, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.
