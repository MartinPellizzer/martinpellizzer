Syzygium bujangii

Syzygium bujangii, commonly known as the Bujang apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits resembling small apples. Syzygium bujangii thrives in tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
