Saxifraga cochlearis

Saxifraga cochlearis, commonly known as the spiral saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and its distinctive spiral arrangement of flowers. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Saxifraga cochlearis is adapted to cold climates and is considered a hardy species capable of surviving in harsh environmental conditions.
