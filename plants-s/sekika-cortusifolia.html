Sekika cortusifolia

Sekika cortusifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
