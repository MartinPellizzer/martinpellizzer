Senna apiculata

Senna apiculata, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Senna apiculata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its bright, showy blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
