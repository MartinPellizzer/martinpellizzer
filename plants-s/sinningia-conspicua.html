Sinningia conspicua

Sinningia conspicua, commonly known as the showy gloxinia, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Sinningia conspicua is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation indoors.
