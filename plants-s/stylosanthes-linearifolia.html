Stylosanthes linearifolia

Stylosanthes linearifolia, commonly known as the slender stylo, is a perennial leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage crop in pasture systems.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability enhances soil fertility, contributing to its ecological and agricultural importance.
