Sterculia bicolor

Sterculia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored sterculia, is a tropical tree native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-colored flowers, which typically feature a white base with a red or pink upper portion. The plant produces large, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets that provide shade beneath the tree.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
