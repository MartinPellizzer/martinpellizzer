Sedum glaucum var. minus

Sedum glaucum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to regions of Asia and Europe.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage and reducing transpiration. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
