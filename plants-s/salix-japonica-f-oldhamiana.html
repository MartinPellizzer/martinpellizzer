Salix japonica f. oldhamiana

Salix japonica f. oldhamiana, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, often adorned with small, bright yellow catkins in the spring. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This cultivar is valued for its graceful form and is commonly used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
