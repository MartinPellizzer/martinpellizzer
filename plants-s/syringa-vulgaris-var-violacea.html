Syringa vulgaris var. violacea

Syringa vulgaris var. violacea, commonly known as the purple lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of fragrant, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has a rounded, compact form. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, turning yellow in the fall.

The species is well-suited for USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8 and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
