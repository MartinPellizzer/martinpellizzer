Scleroxylum venulosum

Scleroxylum venulosum, commonly known as the mangrove apple, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark, which is rough and often covered in a network of fine, vein-like markings. The plant thrives in brackish or saline environments, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually elliptical in shape.

Scleroxylum venulosum produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
