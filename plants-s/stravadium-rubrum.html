Stravadium rubrum

Stravadium rubrum, commonly known as the Crimson Flameflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals, which are adorned with intricate veining that enhances its visual appeal. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet, featuring upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes.
