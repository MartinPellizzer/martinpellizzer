Senecio greggii

Senecio greggii, commonly known as Gregg's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces yellow flowers in the spring.

Gregg's groundsel is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
