Saccolabium amboinense

Saccolabium amboinense, commonly known as the Amboina thornapple, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine, though its botanical characteristics are less well-documented compared to more widely studied species.
