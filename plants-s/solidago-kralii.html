Solidago kralii

Solidago kralii, commonly known as Kral's goldenrod, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swales. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Kral's goldenrod is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
