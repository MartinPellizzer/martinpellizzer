Searsia dura

Searsia dura, commonly known as the hard thornbush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. Searsia dura is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
