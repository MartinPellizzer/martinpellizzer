Silene littorea f. albiflora

Silene littorea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered seaside catchfly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shorelines, thriving in saline and windy conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

This plant is notable for its ability to withstand harsh coastal environments and is often found in maritime habitats.
