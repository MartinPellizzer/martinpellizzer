Senecio microchaeta

Senecio microchaeta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Senecio microchaeta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
