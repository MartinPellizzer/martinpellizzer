Saxifraga nervosa f. major

Saxifraga nervosa f. major, commonly known as the large-veined saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with prominent veins that give the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
