Symplocos macrocarpa subsp. kanarana

Symplocos macrocarpa subsp.

kanarana, commonly known as the Kanara gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Western Ghats of India. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in evergreen forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
