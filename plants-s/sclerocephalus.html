Sclerocephalus

Sclerocephalus, commonly known as the rock cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, erect growth habit and is typically found in rocky or arid environments. The plant features alternate, linear leaves and produces small, white or yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, a typical feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Sclerocephalus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soils.
