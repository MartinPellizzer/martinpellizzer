Struthiopteris christii

Struthiopteris christii, commonly known as the Christ's barren fern, is a species of fern native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forests or along stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
