Syzygium valdecoriaceum

Syzygium valdecoriaceum, commonly known as the golden apple or golden guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, yellowish-orange drupe with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
