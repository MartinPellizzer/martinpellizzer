Senecio ledifolius var. schlimii

Senecio ledifolius var. schlimii, commonly known as Schlim's ice plant, is a low-growing succulent native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent structure allows it to thrive in rocky soils and full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
