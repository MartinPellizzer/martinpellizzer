Styrax trichostemon

Styrax trichostemon, commonly known as the fragrant styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark red, sticky berries. Styrax trichostemon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to temperate climates.
