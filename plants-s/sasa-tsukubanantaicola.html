Sasa tsukubanantaicola

Sasa tsukubanantaicola, commonly known as the Tsukuba bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that grow in dense clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually a deep green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
