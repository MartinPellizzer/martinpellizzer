Sloanea javanica

Sloanea javanica, commonly known as the Javan sloanea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Sloanea javanica is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental potential and traditional uses.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
