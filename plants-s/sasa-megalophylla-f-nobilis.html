Sasa megalophylla f. nobilis

Sasa megalophylla f. nobilis, commonly known as the noble bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface that gives the plant a refined appearance. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
