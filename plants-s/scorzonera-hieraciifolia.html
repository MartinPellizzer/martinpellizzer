Scorzonera hieraciifolia

Scorzonera hieraciifolia, commonly known as the alpine scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are narrow and often divided. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Scorzonera hieraciifolia is often found in meadows and open slopes at high altitudes.
