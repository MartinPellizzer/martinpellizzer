Sasaella ramosa var. suwekoana

Sasaella ramosa var. suwekoana, commonly known as the Suwekoan bamboo, is a variant of the sasaella genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a clump-forming grass that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in local crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, providing a dense foliage that contributes to the understory vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and sustainable use in traditional practices.
