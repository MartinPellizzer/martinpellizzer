Salpichroa glandulosa subsp. glandulosa

Salpichroa glandulosa subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular salpichroa, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in rocky soils and arid environments, often found in desert and chaparral habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its glandular structures, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological adaptations.
