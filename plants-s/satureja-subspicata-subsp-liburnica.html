Satureja subspicata subsp. liburnica

Satureja subspicata subsp.

liburnica, commonly known as Balkan savory, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a pungent, savory aroma.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
