Sporobolus commutatus var. cordofanus

Sporobolus commutatus var. cordofanus, commonly known as the slender sandgrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

This variety is often found in coastal dunes and other disturbed habitats.
