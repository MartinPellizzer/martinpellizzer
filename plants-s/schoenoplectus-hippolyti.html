Schoenoplectus hippolyti

Schoenoplectus hippolyti, commonly known as the narrowleaf tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and dense, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in environments with consistent hydration. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Schoenoplectus hippolyti plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and maintaining water quality.
