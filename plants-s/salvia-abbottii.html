Salvia abbottii

Salvia abbottii, commonly known as Abbott's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in native plant gardens.
