Stachys officinalis var. tangerina

Stachys officinalis var. tangerina, commonly known as lemon betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Lemon betony is noted for its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves, which are used in herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardens.
