Syzygium rhomboideum

Syzygium rhomboideum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
