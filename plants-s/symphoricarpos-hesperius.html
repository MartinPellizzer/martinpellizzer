Symphoricarpos hesperius

Symphoricarpos hesperius, commonly known as the western snowberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
