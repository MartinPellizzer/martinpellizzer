Selinum pimpinelloides

Selinum pimpinelloides, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Selinum pimpinelloides is often used in herbal medicine for its mild diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
