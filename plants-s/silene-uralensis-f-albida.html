Silene uralensis f. albida

Silene uralensis f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered stonecrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
