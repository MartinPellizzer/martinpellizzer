Scutellaria trianae

Scutellaria trianae, commonly known as the Trianas' skullcup, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Scutellaria trianae is often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
