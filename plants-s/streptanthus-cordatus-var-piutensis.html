Streptanthus cordatus var. piutensis

Streptanthus cordatus var. piutensis, commonly known as the Piute's narrowleaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds.

This variety is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
