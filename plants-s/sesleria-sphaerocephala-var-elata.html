Sesleria sphaerocephala var. elata

Sesleria sphaerocephala var. elata, commonly known as the tall rosette grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, upright cluster. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems with spikelets that bear small, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
