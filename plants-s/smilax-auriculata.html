Smilax auriculata

Smilax auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of sharp, spiny tendrils used for climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped with a distinctive auriculate base, giving it its common name.

Smilax auriculata is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
