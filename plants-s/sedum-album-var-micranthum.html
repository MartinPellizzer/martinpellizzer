Sedum album var. micranthum

Sedum album var. micranthum, commonly known as white stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to Europe and western Asia.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage. The plant produces clusters of small, white star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including coastal and alpine environments.
