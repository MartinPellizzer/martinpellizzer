Setachna solstitialis

Setachna solstitialis, commonly known as the summer solstice orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue petals and a distinctive, elongated spur. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its blooming period coincides with the summer solstice, giving it its common name.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
