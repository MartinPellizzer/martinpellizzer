Scilla cilicica

Scilla cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, round, white berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Scilla cilicica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
