Statice pauciflora

Statice pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered glasswort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its salt-tolerant nature, making it a halophyte. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters. Its succulent leaves are fleshy and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Statice pauciflora is often cultivated in coastal gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in saline conditions.
