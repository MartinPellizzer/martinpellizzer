Syzygium fratris

Syzygium fratris, commonly known as the red-fruited syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Syzygium fratris thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
