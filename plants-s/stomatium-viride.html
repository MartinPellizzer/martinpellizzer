Stomatium viride

Stomatium viride, commonly known as the green mouth plant, is a unique species characterized by its specialized leaf structures.

Its leaves are modified into small, green, cup-like structures called "stomata," which serve as sites for gas exchange. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Stomatium viride has a fibrous root system that helps anchor it in soil and absorb nutrients.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studying plant adaptations to humid climates.
