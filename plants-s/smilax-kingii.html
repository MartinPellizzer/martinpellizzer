Smilax kingii

Smilax kingii, commonly known as King's smilax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and spiny growth habit. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a bright red, berry-like structure that ripens in the fall.

Smilax kingii is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
