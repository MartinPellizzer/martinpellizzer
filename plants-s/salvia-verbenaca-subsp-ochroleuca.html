Salvia verbenaca subsp. ochroleuca

Salvia verbenaca subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as the white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems covered in gray-green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of white to pale purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it both a horticultural and ecological asset.
