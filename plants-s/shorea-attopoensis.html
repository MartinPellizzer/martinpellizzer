Shorea attopoensis

Shorea attopoensis, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
