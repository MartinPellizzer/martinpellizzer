Stellaria tetrasticha

Stellaria tetrasticha, commonly known as the four-ribbed starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its delicate, often hairy foliage and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a distinctive ribbed structure.

The flowers, which are pink to white, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and flies.
