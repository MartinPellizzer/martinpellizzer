Salvia urticifolia var. longifolia

Salvia urticifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as longleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers in the summer, which are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage has a distinctive minty aroma, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This variety is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
