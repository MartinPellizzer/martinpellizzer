Scrophularia herminii var. gallaecica

Scrophularia herminii var. gallaecica, commonly known as the Galician scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with similar floral structures.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in its native range.
