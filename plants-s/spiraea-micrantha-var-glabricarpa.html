Spiraea micrantha var. glabricarpa

Spiraea micrantha var. glabricarpa, commonly known as the smooth-fruited spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features upright, slender stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color during the growing season. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that matures in late summer, containing several seeds.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage and floral display.
