Solidago virgaurea var. fallit-tirones

Solidago virgaurea var. fallit-tirones, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has lance-shaped leaves that are green on top and grayish-white underneath. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important component of ecological habitats.
