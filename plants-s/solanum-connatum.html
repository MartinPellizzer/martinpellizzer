Solanum connatum

Solanum connatum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly rough texture and a distinct aroma.

Solanum connatum belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
