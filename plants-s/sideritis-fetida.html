Sideritis fetida

Sideritis fetida, commonly known as the bitterweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes plants like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Sideritis fetida has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it often grows in dry, rocky soils.

It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
