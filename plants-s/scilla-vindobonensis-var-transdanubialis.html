Scilla vindobonensis var. transdanubialis

Scilla vindobonensis var. transdanubialis, commonly known as the Transdanubian scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitat.
