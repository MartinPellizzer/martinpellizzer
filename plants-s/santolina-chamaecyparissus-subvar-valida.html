Santolina chamaecyparissus subvar. valida

Santolina chamaecyparissus subvar. valida, commonly known as the silver santolina, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage that is both drought-tolerant and heat-resistant, making it ideal for Mediterranean climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring, adding a subtle ornamental touch to gardens. Its dense growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens, borders, or as a ground cover.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic essential oils and narrow, needle-like leaves.
