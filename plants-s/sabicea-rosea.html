Sabicea rosea

Sabicea rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered sabicea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small, stipitate stipules. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
