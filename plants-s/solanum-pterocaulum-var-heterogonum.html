Solanum pterocaulum var. heterogonum

Solanum pterocaulum var. heterogonum, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often opening in the evening. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic compounds, making them harmful to humans and animals.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils in woodland and grassland habitats.
