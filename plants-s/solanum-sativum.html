Solanum sativum

Solanum sativum, commonly known as the tomato, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its bushy growth habit, simple alternate leaves, and showy yellow flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is a berry that contains numerous seeds and is rich in nutrients such as vitamins and antioxidants. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is widely cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary applications worldwide.
