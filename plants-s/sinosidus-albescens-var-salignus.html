Sinosidus albescens var. salignus

Sinosidus albescens var. salignus, commonly known as the silverleaf sinosidus, is a variant of the Sinosidus albescens species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its pale, almost silvery foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
