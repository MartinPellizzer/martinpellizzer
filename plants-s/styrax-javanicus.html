Styrax javanicus

Styrax javanicus, commonly known as the Java styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a perfume. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various climates, though it thrives best in temperate regions.
