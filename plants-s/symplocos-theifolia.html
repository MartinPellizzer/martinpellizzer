Symplocos theifolia

Symplocos theifolia, commonly known as the thieving wood or thief's wood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture, while its bark is thick and fissured, providing a unique appearance.

Symplocos theifolia is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and stream valleys.
