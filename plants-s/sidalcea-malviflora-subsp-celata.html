Sidalcea malviflora subsp. celata

Sidalcea malviflora subsp.

celata, commonly known as the Sierra foothill soapwort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and features showy pink or purple flowers. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
