Senecio taquetii

Senecio taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Senecio taquetii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
