Sterigma contortuplicatum

Sterigma contortuplicatum, commonly known as the twisted spiral fern, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate, spiraling fronds.

Its botanical structure features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to anchor and spread across moist, shaded environments. The fronds are composed of overlapping, curling segments that create a distinctive, contorted appearance. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in temperate rainforests.

This plant is notable for its complex morphology, which has made it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
