Senecio tacuaremboensis

Senecio tacuaremboensis, commonly known as the Tacuarembo groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
