Solanum delagoense subsp. pliomorphum

Solanum delagoense subsp.

pliomorphum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are followed by yellow, egg-shaped berries. It is often found in rocky or wooded areas and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
