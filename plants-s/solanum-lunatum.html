Solanum lunatum

Solanum lunatum, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its fragrant blooms. Its leaves are typically ovate and hairy, while the fruit is a small, yellow berry.

Solanum lunatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal beauty.
