Syzygium anamalaianum

Syzygium anamalaianum, commonly known as the Anamalai apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. Syzygium anamalaianum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
