Salvia carnosa subsp. argentea

Salvia carnosa subsp.

argentea, commonly known as silver sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems covered in dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it its common name.

The plant produces spikes of violet to blue-lilac flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
