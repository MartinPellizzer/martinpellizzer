Sorbus chamaemespilus var. ovalifolia

Sorbus chamaemespilus var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by yellowish-orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
