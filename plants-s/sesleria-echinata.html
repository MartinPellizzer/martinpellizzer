Sesleria echinata

Sesleria echinata, commonly known as the spiked sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Sesleria echinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact form and drought tolerance.
