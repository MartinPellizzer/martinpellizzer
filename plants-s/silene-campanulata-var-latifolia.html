Silene campanulata var. latifolia

Silene campanulata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a hairy texture, and the stems are erect and branching.

Silene campanulata var. latifolia is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
