Stelis ocreosa

Stelis ocreosa, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a distinctive hood-like structure on the petals.

Stelis ocreosa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or botanical gardens.
