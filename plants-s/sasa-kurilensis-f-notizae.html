Sasa kurilensis f. notizae

Sasa kurilensis f. notizae, commonly known as the Kuril bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to the northern regions of Japan and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage structure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
