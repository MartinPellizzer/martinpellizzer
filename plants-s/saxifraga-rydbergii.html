Saxifraga rydbergii

Saxifraga rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga rydbergii is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions where it contributes to the alpine flora.
