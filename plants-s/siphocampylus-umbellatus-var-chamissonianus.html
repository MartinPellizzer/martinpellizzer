Siphocampylus umbellatus var. chamissonianus

Siphocampylus umbellatus var. chamissonianus, commonly known as the Chamisson's rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
