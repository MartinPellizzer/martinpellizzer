Smilax sagittifolia

Smilax sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax sagittifolia is characterized by its woody stems and is known for its ability to climb or trail along the ground.
