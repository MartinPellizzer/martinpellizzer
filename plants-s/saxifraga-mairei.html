Saxifraga mairei

Saxifraga mairei, commonly known as the Maire's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink or white petals that attract pollinators. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Saxifraga mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
