Smilax coriacea var. ilicifolia

Smilax coriacea var. ilicifolia, commonly known as the prickly pear or coralberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny stems and clusters of small, bright red berries that are edible when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The berries are a valuable food source for birds and other wildlife.
