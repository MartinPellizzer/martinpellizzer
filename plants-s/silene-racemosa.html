Silene racemosa

Silene racemosa, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Silene racemosa is often found in the eastern and central regions of the United States.
