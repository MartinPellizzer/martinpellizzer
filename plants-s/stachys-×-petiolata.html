Stachys × petiolata

Stachys × petiolata, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Stachys × petiolata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
