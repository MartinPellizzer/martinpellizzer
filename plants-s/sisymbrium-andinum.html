Sisymbrium andinum

Sisymbrium andinum, commonly known as Andean mustards, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species of mustard plants. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in temperate to cold climates. It produces small, yellow flowers and has slender, branching stems.

Its leaves are usually pinnately divided, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
