Schizachyrium monostachyon

Schizachyrium monostachyon, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces tall, slender seed heads that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species for prairie and meadow habitats.

Schizachyrium monostachyon plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
