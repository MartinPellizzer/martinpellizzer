Salix fusca subsp. myrtoides

Salix fusca subsp.

myrtoides, commonly known as the red willow, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are a key feature in its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in riparian areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological stability and wildlife habitat.
