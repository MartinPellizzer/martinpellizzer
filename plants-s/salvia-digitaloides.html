Salvia digitaloides

Salvia digitaloides, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Salvia digitaloides is also valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
