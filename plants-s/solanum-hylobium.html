Solanum hylobium

Solanum hylobium, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. Solanum hylobium is often found in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits, though it is less common than other solanum species.
