Sedum telephium var. viridulum

Sedum telephium var. viridulum, commonly known as the green stonecrop, is a low-growing, mat-forming succulent native to Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow-orange flowers in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
