Saxifraga ajugifolia var. aitonis

Saxifraga ajugifolia var. aitonis, commonly known as the Aiton's saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
