Salix wallichiana f. longistyla

Salix wallichiana f. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled willow, is a rare and distinctive form of the weeping willow.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and a unique floral structure that includes long, prominent staminodes. This plant is native to the Himalayas and thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticulture and botanical research.

The long-styled form is a natural variant that showcases the genetic diversity within the Salix genus.
