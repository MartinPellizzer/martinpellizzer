Stilpnophyllum grandifolium

Stilpnophyllum grandifolium, commonly known as the grandifoliate starleaf, is a unique plant species characterized by its striking, large, waxy leaves that are arranged in a star-like pattern.

Native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, it thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale blue, which bloom during the spring season. Its leaves are not only aesthetically pleasing but also serve as a habitat for various small invertebrates.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
