Senecio grisebachii var. subincanus

Senecio grisebachii var. subincanus, commonly known as the gray-haired groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its grayish, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
