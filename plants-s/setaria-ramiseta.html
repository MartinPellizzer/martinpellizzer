Setaria ramiseta

Setaria ramiseta, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
