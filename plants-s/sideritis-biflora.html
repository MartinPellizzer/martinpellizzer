Sideritis biflora

Sideritis biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and thyme. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Sideritis biflora is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
