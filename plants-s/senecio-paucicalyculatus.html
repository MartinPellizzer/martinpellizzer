Senecio paucicalyculatus

Senecio paucicalyculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
