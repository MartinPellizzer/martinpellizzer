Sorbus aucuparia var. duhmbergii

Sorbus aucuparia var. duhmbergii, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
