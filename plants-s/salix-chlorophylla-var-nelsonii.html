Salix chlorophylla var. nelsonii

Salix chlorophylla var. nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's willow, is a variant of the willow genus characterized by its unique chlorophyll content and leaf morphology.

It exhibits a dense, bushy growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, reflecting its name. The plant is native to specific regions in the western United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering catkins appear in early spring, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic variation and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
