Salvia pygmaea var. simplicior

Salvia pygmaea var. simplicior, commonly known as the dwarf meadow sage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows and open woodlands, preferring full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
