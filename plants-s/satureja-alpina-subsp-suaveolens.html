Satureja alpina subsp. suaveolens

Satureja alpina subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils, which contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong, spicy fragrance.

This subspecies is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its distinctive flavor.
