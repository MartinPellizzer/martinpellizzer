Senecio viridiflavus

Senecio viridiflavus, commonly known as the green-yellow senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Senecio viridiflavus is not widely cultivated but is sometimes grown as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.
