Sasa kagamiana f. sugimurana

Sasa kagamiana f. sugimurana, commonly known as the Sugimura's bamboo, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a compact, clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered with a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by narrow, cylindrical seed pods.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
