Struthiopteris contigua

Struthiopteris contigua, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a large, robust fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its massive, shield-shaped fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, supporting the growth of its large fronds.

The species plays an important role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
