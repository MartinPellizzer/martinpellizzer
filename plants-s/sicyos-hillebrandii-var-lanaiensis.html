Sicyos hillebrandii var. lanaiensis

Sicyos hillebrandii var. lanaiensis, commonly known as the Lanai bottle gourd, is a variant of the bottle gourd native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a climbing vine with large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, elongated fruits that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its fruit is typically harvested when still green and used in various culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in Hawaiian culture.
