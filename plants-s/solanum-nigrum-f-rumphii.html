Solanum nigrum f. rumphii

Solanum nigrum f. rumphii, commonly known as the black nightshade variant, is a botanical variant of the common nightshade.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family, characterized by its hairy stems and small, dark berries. The leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually purple or white. This variety is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils.

It contains toxic compounds such as solanine, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
