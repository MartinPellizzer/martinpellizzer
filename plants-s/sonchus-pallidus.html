Sonchus pallidus

Sonchus pallidus, commonly known as the pale sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are edible when young but become bitter and less palatable as they mature.

The pale sowthistle is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind.
