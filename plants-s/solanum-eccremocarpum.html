Solanum eccremocarpum

Solanum eccremocarpum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a strong, unpleasant odor.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
