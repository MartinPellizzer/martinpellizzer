Setaria gracilipes

Setaria gracilipes, commonly known as slender beardgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in bracts, and the plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness.

Setaria gracilipes is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
