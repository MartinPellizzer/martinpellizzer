Secamone cristata subsp. densiflora

Secamone cristata subsp.

densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered secamone, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
