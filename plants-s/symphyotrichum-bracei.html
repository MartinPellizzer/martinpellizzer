Symphyotrichum bracei

Symphyotrichum bracei, commonly known as the Bracken's New England Aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, swamps, and riparian areas. It produces showy purple to pinkish-red flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Symphyotrichum bracei is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
