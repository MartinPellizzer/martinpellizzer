Saxifraga ferruginea var. foliacea

Saxifraga ferruginea var. foliacea, commonly known as the rusty saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, reddish-brown stems. The leaves are typically oval to elliptic, with a waxy texture and a distinctive rust-colored underside. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
