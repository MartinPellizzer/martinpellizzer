Symplocos laeteviridis var. pauciflora

Symplocos laeteviridis var. pauciflora, commonly known as the rare greenleaf symplocos, is a shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in late spring. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
