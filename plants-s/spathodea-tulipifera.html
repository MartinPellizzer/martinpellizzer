Spathodea tulipifera

Spathodea tulipifera, commonly known as the African tulip, is a striking tropical shrub native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stalks, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Spathodea tulipifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
