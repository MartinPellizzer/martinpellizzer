Saxifraga crassifolia var. elliptica

Saxifraga crassifolia var. elliptica, commonly known as the broad-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are elliptical in shape, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and usually have five petals, ranging in color from white to pink.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a hardy perennial in colder climates.
