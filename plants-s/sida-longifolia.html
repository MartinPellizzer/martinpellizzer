Sida longifolia

Sida longifolia, commonly known as longleaf sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching near the base. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with hairy surfaces and prominent veins. Small white or pink flowers are arranged in clusters, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
