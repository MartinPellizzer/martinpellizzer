Saxifraga lyallii

Saxifraga lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas with high elevation.

Saxifraga lyallii is an important component of New Zealand's native flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
