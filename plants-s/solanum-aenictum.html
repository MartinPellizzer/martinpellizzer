Solanum aenictum

Solanum aenictum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, dark berries. The plant produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy surface, and it typically grows in disturbed soils.

Despite its toxicity, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
