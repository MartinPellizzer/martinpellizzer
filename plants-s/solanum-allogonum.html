Solanum allogonum

Solanum allogonum, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by smooth, spherical berries that turn from green to red as they mature. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Solanum allogonum is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
