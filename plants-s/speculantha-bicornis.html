Speculantha bicornis

Speculantha bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive two-horned structure on its flower petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
