Sempervivum velenovskyi

Sempervivum velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, grass-like leaves that are typically green with reddish or purple hues, especially in cooler temperatures. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Sempervivum velenovskyi is a low-maintenance plant that is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant.
