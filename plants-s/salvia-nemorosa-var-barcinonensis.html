Salvia nemorosa var. barcinonensis

Salvia nemorosa var. barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcino violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy violet to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in early to mid-summer, producing clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
