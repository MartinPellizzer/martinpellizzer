Solanum crotalobasis

Solanum crotalobasis, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas, characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are usually black when mature and contain toxic compounds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
