Stewartia japonica var. grandiflora

Stewartia japonica var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Japanese Stewartia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its ornamental value comes from its elegant form, glossy foliage, and attractive bark.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
