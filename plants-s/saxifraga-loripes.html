Saxifraga loripes

Saxifraga loripes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or gray hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Saxifraga loripes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
