Sida racemosa

Sida racemosa, commonly known as coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sida racemosa is adapted to salt spray and is often found in dune ecosystems.
