Sanguisorba nudicaulis

Sanguisorba nudicaulis, commonly known as narrowleaf sorrel, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Sanguisorba nudicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinators.
