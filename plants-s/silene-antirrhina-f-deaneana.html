Silene antirrhina f. deaneana

Silene antirrhina f. deaneana, commonly known as the Deane's campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with five petals, typical of the genus Silene. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, often in temperate regions.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
