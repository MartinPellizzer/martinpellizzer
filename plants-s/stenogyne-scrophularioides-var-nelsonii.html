Stenogyne scrophularioides var. nelsonii

Stenogyne scrophularioides var. nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's stenogyne, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

This variety is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare species within its native range.
