Sedum grisebachii

Sedum grisebachii, commonly known as the silver sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, blue-gray leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.

Sedum grisebachii is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in rocky or sandy environments.
