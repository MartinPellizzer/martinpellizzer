Salvia verbenaca var. oblongifolia

Salvia verbenaca var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
