Symplocos arbutifolia

Symplocos arbutifolia, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Symplocos arbutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
