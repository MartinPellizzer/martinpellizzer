Spiraea lancifolia var. decumbens

Spiraea lancifolia var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping spiraea, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features slender, arching stems that often lie close to the ground, forming a dense mat. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, providing a fine-textured appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
