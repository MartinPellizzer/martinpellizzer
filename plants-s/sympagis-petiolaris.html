Sympagis petiolaris

Sympagis petiolaris, commonly known as the leafy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are typical of the orchid family. Sympagis petiolaris thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing along the forest floor.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to tropical gardens.
