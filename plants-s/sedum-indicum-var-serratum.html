Sedum indicum var. serratum

Sedum indicum var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to regions of Asia, including China and India.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, often blue-green leaves that are serrated at the edges. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Sedum indicum var. serratum is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
