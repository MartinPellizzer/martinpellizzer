Solanum heterotricum

Solanum heterotricum, commonly known as the heterotricum nightshade, is a rare and unusual species within the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring both green and purple stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by berries that mature to a deep blue or black color. It is native to specific regions in Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
