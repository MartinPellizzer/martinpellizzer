Silene pontica

Silene pontica, commonly known as the Pontic campion, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. Silene pontica has simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
