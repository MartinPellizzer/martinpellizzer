Sisymbrium amplexicaule var. tenuicaule

Sisymbrium amplexicaule var. tenuicaule, commonly known as narrow-leaved broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats and is often found parasitizing species within the legume family. Its life cycle involves germinating near the roots of a host plant and establishing a connection through its haustoria.

Due to its specialized parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in ecological interactions within its habitat.
