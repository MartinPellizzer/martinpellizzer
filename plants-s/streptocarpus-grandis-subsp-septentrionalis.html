Streptocarpus grandis subsp. septentrionalis

Streptocarpus grandis subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern streptocarpus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the highlands of South America. It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
