Senecio goetzei

Senecio goetzei, commonly known as the Goetze's groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of its stems. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Senecio goetzei is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
