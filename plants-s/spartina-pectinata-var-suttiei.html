Spartina pectinata var. suttiei

Spartina pectinata var. suttiei, commonly known as the Sutter's cordgrass, is a variant of the cordgrass species native to the San Francisco Bay estuary.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments, and is adapted to tidal wetlands. The plant has long, grass-like blades that are typically flat and have a fibrous texture. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ecological importance is significant in maintaining the health of coastal ecosystems.
