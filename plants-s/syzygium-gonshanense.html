Syzygium gonshanense

Syzygium gonshanense, commonly known as the Guangshan tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical leaves with a smooth surface. Its fruit is a small, hard pod containing several seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value.

Syzygium gonshanense is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
