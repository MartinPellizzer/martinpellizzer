Samanea saman

Samanea saman, commonly known as the rain tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its spreading canopy and distinctive umbrella-shaped crown, which provides shade and habitat for various organisms. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets that fold together at night.

Samanea saman is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in urban areas for its aesthetic and environmental benefits.
