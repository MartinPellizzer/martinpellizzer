Solanum nelsonii var. vadosum

Solanum nelsonii var. vadosum, commonly known as the Nelson's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a potential weed in some regions.
