Silene rubella var. turbinata

Silene rubella var. turbinata, commonly known as the Turbinated Bladder Campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, turbinated (turbinate) flower shape. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Silene rubella var. turbinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
