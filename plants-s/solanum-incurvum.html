Solanum incurvum

Solanum incurvum, commonly known as the curly nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Solanum incurvum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
