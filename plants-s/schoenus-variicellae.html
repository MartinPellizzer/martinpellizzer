Schoenus variicellae

Schoenus variicellae, commonly known as the variable bulbous sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
