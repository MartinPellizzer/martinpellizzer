Scutellaria galericulata var. pauciflora

Scutellaria galericulata var. pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, a characteristic feature of the Scutellaria genus.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
