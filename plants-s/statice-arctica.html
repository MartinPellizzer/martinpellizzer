Statice arctica

Statice arctica, commonly known as Arctic glassweed, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pale blue to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Statice arctica is often found in tundra and rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
