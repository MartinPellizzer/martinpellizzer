Senecio griffithii

Senecio griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
