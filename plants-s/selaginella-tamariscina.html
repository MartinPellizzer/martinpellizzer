Selaginella tamariscina

Selaginella tamariscina, commonly known as the 'spikemoss' or 'common spikemoss,' is a vascular fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinct appearance with upright, needle-like leaves and a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense mats. It is characterized by its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures called microsporangia and megasporangia.

Selaginella tamariscina is also noted for its high tolerance to drought and its ability to regenerate from spores, making it a resilient species in various ecosystems.
