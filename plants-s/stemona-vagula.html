Stemona vagula

Stemona vagula, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a thin, smooth layer of bark. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Stemona vagula is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
