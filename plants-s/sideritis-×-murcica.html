Sideritis × murcica

Sideritis × murcica, commonly known as the Murcian ironweed, is a hybrid species within the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula, particularly in southern Spain. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
