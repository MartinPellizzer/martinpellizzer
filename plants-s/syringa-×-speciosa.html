Syringa × speciosa

Syringa × speciosa, commonly known as the weeping bush honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a spreading, weeping habit due to its cascading branches. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, providing a lush backdrop to the showy flowers.

Syringa × speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and fragrance.
