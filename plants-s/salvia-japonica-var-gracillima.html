Salvia japonica var. gracillima

Salvia japonica var. gracillima, commonly known as Japanese sage or slender sage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both traditional and modern horticultural applications.
