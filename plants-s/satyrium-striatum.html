Satyrium striatum

Satyrium striatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf twayblade, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, delicate flower that typically blooms in early spring. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive stripe, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
