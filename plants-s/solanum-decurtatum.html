Solanum decurtatum

Solanum decurtatum, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, and they contain several seeds.

Solanum decurtatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
