Sorbus hazslinszkyana

Sorbus hazslinszkyana, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
