Solanum cormanthum

Solanum cormanthum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
