Senecio armeriifolius

Senecio armeriifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with silvery-gray, woolly leaves that are covered in dense trichomes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
