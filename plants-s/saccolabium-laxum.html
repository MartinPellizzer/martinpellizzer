Saccolabium laxum

Saccolabium laxum, commonly known as the slender sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Saccolabium laxum is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
