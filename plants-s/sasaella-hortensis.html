Sasaella hortensis

Sasaella hortensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Sasaella hortensis is valued for its ability to form thick, bushy mats, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded areas.
