Sudamerlycaste dyeriana

Sudamerlycaste dyeriana, commonly known as the Brazilian moonflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the dense rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its humid, tropical habitat. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments with rich, well-draining soil.
