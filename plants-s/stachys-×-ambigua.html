Stachys × ambigua

Stachys × ambigua, commonly known as hedge nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Stachys × ambigua is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
