Scleranthus striatipes

Scleranthus striatipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thornweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, producing pale green to yellowish blooms.

Scleranthus striatipes is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
