Salvia azurea devilleana

Salvia azurea devilleana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
