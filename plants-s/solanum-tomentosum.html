Solanum tomentosum

Solanum tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a deep red or purple, often containing toxic compounds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
