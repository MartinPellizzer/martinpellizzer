Salix eriocephala var. famelica

Salix eriocephala var. famelica, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Its growth habit and ornamental features make it a valuable plant for ecological and landscape purposes.
