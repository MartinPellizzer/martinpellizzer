Shorea kunstleri

Shorea kunstleri, commonly known as the Malacca ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib.

Shorea kunstleri plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
