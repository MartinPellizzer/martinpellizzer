Scabiosa procera

Scabiosa procera, commonly known as the tall scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. Scabiosa procera is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
