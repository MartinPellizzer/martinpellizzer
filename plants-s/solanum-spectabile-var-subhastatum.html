Solanum spectabile var. subhastatum

Solanum spectabile var. subhastatum, commonly known as the showy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a purple tinge. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
