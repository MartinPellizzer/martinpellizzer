Syringa meyeri

Syringa meyeri, commonly known as Meier's lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia, particularly China.

It is valued for its fragrant, cone-shaped clusters of flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Syringa meyeri is also noted for its adaptability to various climates and its resistance to many common pests and diseases.
