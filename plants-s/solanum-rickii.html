Solanum rickii

Solanum rickii, commonly known as Rick's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. Its berries are edible when ripe but contain toxic compounds that can be harmful if consumed in large quantities.

Solanum rickii is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in warm, sunny environments.
