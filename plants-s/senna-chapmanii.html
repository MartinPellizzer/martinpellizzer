Senna chapmanii

Senna chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's senna, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

Senna chapmanii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
