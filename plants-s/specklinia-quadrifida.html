Specklinia quadrifida

Specklinia quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it prefers bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Specklinia quadrifida is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
