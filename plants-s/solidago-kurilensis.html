Solidago kurilensis

Solidago kurilensis, commonly known as Kuril goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the Kuril Islands and parts of eastern Russia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in coastal environments. Its bright yellow inflorescences bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Kuril goldenrod is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
