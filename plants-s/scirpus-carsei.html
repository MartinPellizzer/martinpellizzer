Scirpus carsei

Scirpus carsei, commonly known as the marsh club-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in aquatic environments.

Scirpus carsei plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
