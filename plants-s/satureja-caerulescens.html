Satureja caerulescens

Satureja caerulescens, commonly known as blue savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Satureja caerulescens is valued for its fragrance and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
