Skimmia japonica subsp. japonica

Skimmia japonica subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese skimmia, is a shrub native to southern Japan. It features evergreen foliage with glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in autumn. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
