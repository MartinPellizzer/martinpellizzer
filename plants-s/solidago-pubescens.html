Solidago pubescens

Solidago pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent goldenrod, is a species of flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
