Senecio breviramulus

Senecio breviramulus, commonly known as the short-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
