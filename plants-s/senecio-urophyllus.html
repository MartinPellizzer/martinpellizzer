Senecio urophyllus

Senecio urophyllus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio urophyllus is also noted for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its spread in suitable environments.
