Sinapis rugosa

Sinapis rugosa, commonly known as rough mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to Europe and western Asia. It produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

Sinapis rugosa is often used in herbal medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
