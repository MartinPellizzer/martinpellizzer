Sapium cornutum var. poggei

Sapium cornutum var. poggei, commonly known as the horned cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features distinctive, horn-like projections on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
