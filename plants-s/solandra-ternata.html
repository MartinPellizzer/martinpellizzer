Solandra ternata

Solandra ternata, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Solandra ternata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
