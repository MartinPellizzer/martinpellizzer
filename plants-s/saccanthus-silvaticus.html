Saccanthus silvaticus

Saccanthus silvaticus, commonly known as the forest saccanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to greenish flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.
