Scaevola restiacea

Scaevola restiacea, commonly known as the coastal scaevola, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of eastern and southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found along shores and dunes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to withstand salt spray and arid conditions.

Scaevola restiacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
