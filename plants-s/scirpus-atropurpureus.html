Scirpus atropurpureus

Scirpus atropurpureus, commonly known as purple jointed rush, is a tall, grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features long, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with purple, especially at the nodes, giving it its common name. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus atropurpureus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
