Sanguisorba minor var. glauca

Sanguisorba minor var. glauca, commonly known as blue stemmed sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features distinctive blue-gray foliage and produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in moist, wooded areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are covered in a dense, waxy coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
