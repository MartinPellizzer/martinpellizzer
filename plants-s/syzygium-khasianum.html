Syzygium khasianum

Syzygium khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi laurel, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Syzygium khasianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
