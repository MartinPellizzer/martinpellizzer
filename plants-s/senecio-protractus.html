Senecio protractus

Senecio protractus, commonly known as the protractus groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

Senecio protractus is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
