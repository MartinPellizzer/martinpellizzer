Solanum tuberosum var. sesquimensale

Solanum tuberosum var. sesquimensale, commonly known as the sesquimensal potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which often includes elongated and irregularly shaped roots. This variety is native to the Andean region of South America, where it has been cultivated for centuries. The plant exhibits typical solanaceous features, including hairy stems and showy flowers.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and potential for agricultural improvement.
