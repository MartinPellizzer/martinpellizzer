Sasa shiwotana

Sasa shiwotana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or black bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Sasa shiwotana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese gardens.
