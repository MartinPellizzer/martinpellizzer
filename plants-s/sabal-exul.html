Sabal exul

Sabal exul, commonly known as the Florida cabbage tree, is a tall, palm-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant has large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 6 feet in length. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles.

Sabal exul is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to tolerate standing water and poor soil conditions.
