Solena talangiana

Solena talangiana, commonly known as the talangian solena, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solena talangiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
