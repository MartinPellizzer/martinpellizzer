Schizostachyum silicatum

Schizostachyum silicatum, commonly known as the siliceous bamboo, is a tall, woody grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional construction and crafts. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Schizostachyum silicatum is valued for its durability and is also used in the production of paper and textiles.
