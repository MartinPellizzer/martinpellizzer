Sporobolus praecox

Sporobolus praecox, commonly known as early spearmint grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, grass-like blades with a distinctive blue-green coloration, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its reproductive strategy includes the ability to disperse seeds via wind, a characteristic common in many grass species.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to drought conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in dry landscapes.
