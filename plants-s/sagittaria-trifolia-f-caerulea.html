Sagittaria trifolia f. caerulea

Sagittaria trifolia f. caerulea, commonly known as the blue arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are often a striking blue-green color. The plant typically grows in shallow water, such as ponds, marshes, and wetlands. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by dark blue berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
