Spartium purgans

Spartium purgans, commonly known as redroot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown seeds that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, particularly as a laxative.

Spartium purgans thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
