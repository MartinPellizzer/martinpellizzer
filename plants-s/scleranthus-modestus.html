Scleranthus modestus

Scleranthus modestus, commonly known as the modest thornweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, often hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant's leaves are narrow and pointed, typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Scleranthus modestus is known for its ability to tolerate dry conditions and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
