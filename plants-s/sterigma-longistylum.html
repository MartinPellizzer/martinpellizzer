Sterigma longistylum

Sterigma longistylum, commonly known as the long-stemmed starflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features showy, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its stems are slender and often branching, supporting the delicate floral display.

The botanical interest of Sterigma longistylum lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to high-altitude environments.
