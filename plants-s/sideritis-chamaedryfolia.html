Sideritis chamaedryfolia

Sideritis chamaedryfolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, forming spikes that bloom in summer.

Sideritis chamaedryfolia is valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
