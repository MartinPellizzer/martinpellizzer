Salacia cuspidicoma

Salacia cuspidicoma, commonly known as the spiky salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark contains compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Salacia cuspidicoma is often found in moist, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in the understory vegetation.
