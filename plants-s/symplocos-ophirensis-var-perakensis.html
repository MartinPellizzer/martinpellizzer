Symplocos ophirensis var. perakensis

Symplocos ophirensis var. perakensis, commonly known as the Perak sycamore, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical regions.
