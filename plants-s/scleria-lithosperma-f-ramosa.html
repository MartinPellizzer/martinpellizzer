Scleria lithosperma f. ramosa

Scleria lithosperma f. ramosa, commonly known as the branched rock sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of two distinct spikelets, one terminal and one lateral, which are often subtended by bracts.

This subspecies is known for its compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
