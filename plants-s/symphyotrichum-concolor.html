Symphyotrichum concolor

Symphyotrichum concolor, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the stems are often hairy.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
