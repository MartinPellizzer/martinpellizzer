Semarillaria alata

Semarillaria alata, commonly known as the winged semarillaria, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wing-like structures on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
