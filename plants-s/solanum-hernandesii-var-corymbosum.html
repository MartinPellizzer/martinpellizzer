Solanum hernandesii var. corymbosum

Solanum hernandesii var. corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, usually green or yellowish flowers that are clustered in cymes, giving it its varietal name. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, often containing seeds.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important species like tomatoes and peppers.
