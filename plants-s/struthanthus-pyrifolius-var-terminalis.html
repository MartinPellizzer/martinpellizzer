Struthanthus pyrifolius var. terminalis

Struthanthus pyrifolius var. terminalis, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular, succulent leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, with the stems often rooting at the nodes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
