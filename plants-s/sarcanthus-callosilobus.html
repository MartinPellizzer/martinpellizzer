Sarcanthus callosilobus

Sarcanthus callosilobus, commonly known as the rough-leaved sarcanthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Sarcanthus callosilobus thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
