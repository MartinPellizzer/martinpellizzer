Salvia manantlanensis

Salvia manantlanensis, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Salvia manantlanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
