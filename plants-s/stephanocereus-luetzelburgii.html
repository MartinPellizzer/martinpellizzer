Stephanocereus luetzelburgii

Stephanocereus luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's cactus, is a species of cactus native to northern South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces white or yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

This cactus is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
