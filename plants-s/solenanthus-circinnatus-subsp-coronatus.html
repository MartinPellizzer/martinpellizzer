Solenanthus circinnatus subsp. coronatus

Solenanthus circinnatus subsp.

coronatus, commonly known as the crowned solenanthus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dune environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive crown-like arrangement of flowers at the tips of the branches.
