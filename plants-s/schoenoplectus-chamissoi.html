Schoenoplectus chamissoi

Schoenoplectus chamissoi, commonly known as the Chamisso's bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are wind-pollinated. It is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
