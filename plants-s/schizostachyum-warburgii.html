Schizostachyum warburgii

Schizostachyum warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's bamboo, is a tall, woody grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, straight culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture and is often used for construction and handicrafts due to its durable nature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Schizostachyum warburgii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
