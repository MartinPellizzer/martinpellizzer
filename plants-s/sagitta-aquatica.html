Sagitta aquatica

Sagitta aquatica, commonly known as the water arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a V-shaped leaf blade that resembles an arrowhead. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or green flowers that are often submerged, followed by distinctive triangular fruits. Sagitta aquatica thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
