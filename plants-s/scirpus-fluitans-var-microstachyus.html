Scirpus fluitans var. microstachyus

Scirpus fluitans var. microstachyus, commonly known as the floating sedge, is a aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It has slender, grass-like stems that float on the water surface, with small, clustered spikes of brownish or reddish flowers. The plant's leaves are narrow and typically submerged or floating, aiding in its ability to thrive in waterlogged environments. It plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland restoration and ecological balance.
