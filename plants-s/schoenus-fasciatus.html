Schoenus fasciatus

Schoenus fasciatus, commonly known as the striped grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that often display distinct striping. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
