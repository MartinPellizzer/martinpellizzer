Sasa hukudaeana

Sasa hukudaeana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stalks that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth, grassy texture. Sasa hukudaeana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form thickets, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
