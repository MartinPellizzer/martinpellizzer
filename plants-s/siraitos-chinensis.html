Siraitos chinensis

Siraitos chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves and conical shape, which provide year-round visual interest. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
