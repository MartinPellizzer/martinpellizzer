Schizachyrium semiberbe

Schizachyrium semiberbe, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces feathery, silver-tan seed heads that are prominent in late summer and autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Schizachyrium semiberbe is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
