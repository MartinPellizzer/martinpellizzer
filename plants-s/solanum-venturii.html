Solanum venturii

Solanum venturii, commonly known as the Venturi nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its berries are small, yellowish, and contain toxic compounds, making it potentially hazardous to animals and humans.

Solanum venturii is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and chemical composition.
