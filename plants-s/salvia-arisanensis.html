Salvia arisanensis

Salvia arisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Salvia arisanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
