Syringa × chinensis

Syringa × chinensis, commonly known as Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet and has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Syringa × chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping for its showy floral display.
