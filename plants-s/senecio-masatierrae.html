Senecio masatierrae

Senecio masatierrae, commonly known as the Masatierra Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
