Salix cinerea var. pedicellata

Salix cinerea var. pedicellata, commonly known as the pedicellate gray willow, is a variant of the gray willow native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its grayish bark and slender, pendulous branches. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery-gray underside. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

This species is often found in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands.
