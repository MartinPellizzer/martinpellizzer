Sarcanthus machadonis

Sarcanthus machadonis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Sarcanthus machadonis make it a distinctive and visually striking species in its native habitat.
