Senecio arabidifolius

Senecio arabidifolius, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by papery, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its resilience and low water requirements, it is often used in xeriscaping and habitat restoration projects.
