Salix × fastidiosa

Salix × fastidiosa, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant has long, slender leaves that are typically bright green on top and pale underneath. It thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The bark of Salix × fastidiosa is smooth and light gray, and it can produce catkins in the spring.
