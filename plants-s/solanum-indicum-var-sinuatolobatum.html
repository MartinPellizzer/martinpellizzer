Solanum indicum var. sinuatolobatum

Solanum indicum var. sinuatolobatum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are often lobed and have a sinuate margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is typically avoided for consumption but studied for its potential medicinal properties.
