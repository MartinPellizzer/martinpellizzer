Syzygium coarctatum

Syzygium coarctatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, woody fruits containing edible seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
