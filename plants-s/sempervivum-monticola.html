Sempervivum monticola

Sempervivum monticola, commonly known as the Alpine Houseplant or the Mountain Houseplant, is a succulent species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions. The plant produces tall flowering stalks with small, star-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its ability to form offsets allows for easy propagation and propagation through leaf cuttings.
