Sideritis tafraoutiana

Sideritis tafraoutiana, commonly known as Moroccan sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It is adapted to grow in rocky and sandy soils, demonstrating resilience to drought conditions.

Sideritis tafraoutiana is also valued for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.
