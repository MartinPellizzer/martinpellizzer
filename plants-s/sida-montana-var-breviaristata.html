Sida montana var. breviaristata

Sida montana var. breviaristata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sida, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of desert and rocky habitats.
