Sophora microphylla var. fulvida

Sophora microphylla var. fulvida, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

The golden wattle is an important symbol of Australian biodiversity and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
