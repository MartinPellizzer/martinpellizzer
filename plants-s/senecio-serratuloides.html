Senecio serratuloides

Senecio serratuloides, commonly known as the serrated groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Senecio serratuloides is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
