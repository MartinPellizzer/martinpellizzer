Solanum incomptum var. lugens

Solanum incomptum var. lugens, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically features slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a yellowish, berry-like structure that ripens to a reddish color.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
