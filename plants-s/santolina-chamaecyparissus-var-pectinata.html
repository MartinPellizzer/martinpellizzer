Santolina chamaecyparissus var. pectinata

Santolina chamaecyparissus var. pectinata, commonly known as the silver pectinata santolina, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with a soft, velvety texture, and it produces small, yellow flowers in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a border plant. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, requiring well-drained soil.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
