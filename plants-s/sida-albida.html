Sida albida

Sida albida, commonly known as the white sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Sida albida is also known for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
