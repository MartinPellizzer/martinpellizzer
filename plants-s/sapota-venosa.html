Sapota venosa

Sapota venosa, commonly known as the veined sapota, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its broad, veined leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is similar to a persimmon, with a smooth, yellow skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. Its growth habit includes a short trunk and sprawling branches, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its nutritional content.
