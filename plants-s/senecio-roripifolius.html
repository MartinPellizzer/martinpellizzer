Senecio roripifolius

Senecio roripifolius, commonly known as the blueweed or bluecup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. Its flowers are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, and the plant is known for its striking color contrast between the blue flowers and green foliage.

Senecio roripifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
