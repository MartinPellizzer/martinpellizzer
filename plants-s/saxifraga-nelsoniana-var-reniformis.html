Saxifraga nelsoniana var. reniformis

Saxifraga nelsoniana var. reniformis, commonly known as the kidney-shaped saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are clustered in tight inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental appeal and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
