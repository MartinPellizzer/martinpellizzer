Salvia canariensis

Salvia canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
