Stalagmitis javanensis

Stalagmitis javanensis, commonly known as the Javanese Stalagmite Plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, columnar growth form that resembles stalagmites, hence its common name. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and waxy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Stalagmitis javanensis is also noted for its ability to produce small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season.
