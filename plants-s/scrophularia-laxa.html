Scrophularia laxa

Scrophularia laxa, commonly known as the loose scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Scrophularia laxa is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
