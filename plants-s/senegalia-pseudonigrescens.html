Senegalia pseudonigrescens

Senegalia pseudonigrescens, commonly known as the blackbrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green foliage, which helps it thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. It has deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers, making it drought-tolerant.

Senegalia pseudonigrescens plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
