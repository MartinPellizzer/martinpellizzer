Syzygium lorentzianum

Syzygium lorentzianum, commonly known as the Lorentzian tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, elongated pods containing seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include a smooth, grayish bark and a preference for well-drained soils in humid climates.

Syzygium lorentzianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
