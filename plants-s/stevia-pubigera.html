Stevia pubigera

Stevia pubigera, commonly known as the woolly stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is closely related to other stevia species, such as Stevia rebaudiana, which is well-known for its sweet leaves.

Botanically, Stevia pubigera is valued for its potential medicinal properties and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
