Solanum fundatum

Solanum fundatum, commonly known as the wild nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, white or violet flowers and produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
