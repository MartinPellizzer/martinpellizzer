Sakoanala villosa subsp. menabeensis

Sakoanala villosa subsp.

menabeensis, commonly known as the Menabe velvet tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its velvety, hairy foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a soft texture, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
