Symphytum hirsutum

Symphytum hirsutum, commonly known as woolly groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a rosette of thick, hairy, gray-green leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a single head at the end of each stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional remedies.
