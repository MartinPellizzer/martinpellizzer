Syzygium zeylanicum var. lineare

Syzygium zeylanicum var. lineare, commonly known as the linear-leaf clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to dark red, clove-like buds. These buds are harvested for their aromatic oils and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
