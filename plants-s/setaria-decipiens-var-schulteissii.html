Setaria decipiens var. schulteissii

Setaria decipiens var. schulteissii, commonly known as the Schulteissii foxtail, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular spikelets that resemble the tail of a fox, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

The inflorescence is a dense, spike-like structure that matures into a fluffy, wind-dispersed seed head.
