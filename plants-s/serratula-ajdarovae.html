Serratula ajdarovae

Serratula ajdarovae, commonly known as the Ajdarov's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its leaves are generally serrated, giving it the genus name "Serratula.".

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche.
