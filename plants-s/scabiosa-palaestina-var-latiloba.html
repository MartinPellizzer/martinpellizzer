Scabiosa palaestina var. latiloba

Scabiosa palaestina var. latiloba, commonly known as the Palestine cloverflower, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It features delicate, pink to purple flower heads that resemble small clover blossoms, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in rocky or arid habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
