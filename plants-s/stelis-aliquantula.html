Stelis aliquantula

Stelis aliquantula, commonly known as the lace orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, airy inflorescence and small, intricate flowers that resemble lace. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Stelis aliquantula is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
