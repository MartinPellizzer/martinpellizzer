Solanum quadripartitum

Solanum quadripartitum, commonly known as the four-parted nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers and produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum quadripartitum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
