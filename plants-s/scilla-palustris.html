Scilla palustris

Scilla palustris, commonly known as marsh squill, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of blue to violet, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a bulbous base, typical of the Amaryllidaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

Scilla palustris plays an important ecological role by providing early nectar sources for pollinators.
