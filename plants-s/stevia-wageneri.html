Stevia wageneri

Stevia wageneri, commonly known as the dwarf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it is often grown as a decorative and functional plant.
