Spiradiclis coccinea

Spiradiclis coccinea, commonly known as the red spiradiclis, is a strikingly colorful plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, Spiradiclis coccinea is often cultivated for its visual appeal in both gardens and botanical collections.
