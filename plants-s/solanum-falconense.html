Solanum falconense

Solanum falconense, commonly known as the Falcon's egg plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually oval with a rough texture, and the flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit, which is the most notable feature, is fleshy and contains several seeds, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
