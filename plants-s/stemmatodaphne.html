Stemmatodaphne

Stemmatodaphne, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, glossy, evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its berries are usually red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.
