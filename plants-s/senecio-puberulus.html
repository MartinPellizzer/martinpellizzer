Senecio puberulus

Senecio puberulus, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose, branched inflorescences.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
