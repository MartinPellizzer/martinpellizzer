Salpinctium natalense

Salpinctium natalense, commonly known as Natal's thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for local wildlife.
