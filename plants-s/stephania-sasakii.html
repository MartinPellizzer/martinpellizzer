Stephania sasakii

Stephania sasakii, commonly known as the Sasakii stephania, is a flowering plant belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its cultural significance in local communities.
