Symphytum caucasicum

Symphytum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of violet or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
