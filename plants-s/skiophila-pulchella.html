Skiophila pulchella

Skiophila pulchella, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Skiophila pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
