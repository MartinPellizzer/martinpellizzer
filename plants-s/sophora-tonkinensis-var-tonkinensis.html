Sophora tonkinensis var. tonkinensis

Sophora tonkinensis var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish to golden flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
