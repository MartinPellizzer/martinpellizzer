Saxifraga carinthiaca

Saxifraga carinthiaca, commonly known as the Carpathian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, oval leaves that are covered in a fine, white waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in a compact inflorescence.

Saxifraga carinthiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging, high-altitude habitats.
