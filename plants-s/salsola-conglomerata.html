Salsola conglomerata

Salsola conglomerata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in dry, saline soils and often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its rapid spread and invasive potential in new environments.

Salsola conglomerata is also known for its ability to survive extreme temperatures and low water availability.
