Sideritis virgata var. lavandifolia

Sideritis virgata var. lavandifolia, commonly known as lavender-scented ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, fragrant aroma reminiscent of lavender.

This variety is often grown for its ornamental value and mild aromatic properties.
