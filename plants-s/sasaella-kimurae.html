Sasaella kimurae

Sasaella kimurae, commonly known as the Korean bamboo or Kimura's bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.

Sasaella kimurae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
