Schivereckia monticola subsp. stenocarpa

Schivereckia monticola subsp.

stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit schivereckia, is a member of the Ericaceae family. It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and attractive foliage.
