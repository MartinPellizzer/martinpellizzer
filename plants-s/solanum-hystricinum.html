Solanum hystricinum

Solanum hystricinum, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that start green and ripen to a deep red, often containing toxic compounds such as solanine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
