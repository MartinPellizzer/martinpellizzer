Scutellaria versicolor var. mollis

Scutellaria versicolor var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-flowered bluebeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear clusters of small, tubular flowers.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
