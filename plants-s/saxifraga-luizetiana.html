Saxifraga luizetiana

Saxifraga luizetiana, commonly known as the Luizet's saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga luizetiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to harsh, mountainous conditions.
