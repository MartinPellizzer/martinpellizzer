Scilla non-scripta

Scilla non-scripta, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Scilla non-scripta is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
