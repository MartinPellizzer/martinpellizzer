Serapias lingua f. pallidiflora

Serapias lingua f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, tongue-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
