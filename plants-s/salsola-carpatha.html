Salsola carpatha

Salsola carpatha, commonly known as Armenian spinach or saltbush, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and saline conditions. The plant produces small, green, edible leaves that are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional cuisines. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salsola carpatha is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh climates.
