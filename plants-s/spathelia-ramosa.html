Spathelia ramosa

Spathelia ramosa, commonly known as the clustered spathelia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive spatulate shape.

Spathelia ramosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific tropical conditions.
