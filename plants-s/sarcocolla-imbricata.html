Sarcocolla imbricata

Sarcocolla imbricata, commonly known as the overlapping bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized bladder-like structures.

These bladders, which are small, sac-like organs, are used to trap small aquatic organisms for nutrient absorption. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are often submerged, while the bladders are found at the water's surface or just below.

Sarcocolla imbricata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to the food web through its predatory behavior.
