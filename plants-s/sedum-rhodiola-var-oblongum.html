Sedum rhodiola var. oblongum

Sedum rhodiola var. oblongum, commonly known as the oblongleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments in North America.

It features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, adapted to store water and withstand drought. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy and low-maintenance ornamental plant.
