Sempervivum montanum var. heterophyllum

Sempervivum montanum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge, depending on light exposure. The plant produces upright flowering stems topped with clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries.
