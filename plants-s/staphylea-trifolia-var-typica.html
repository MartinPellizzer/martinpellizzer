Staphylea trifolia var. typica

Staphylea trifolia var. typica, commonly known as the European hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible hazelnuts that mature in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
