Schefflera delavayi var. ochrasceus

Schefflera delavayi var. ochrasceus, commonly known as the golden schefflera, is a variant of the Schefflera genus native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a golden or yellowish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries, which are attractive to birds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its compact size and ornamental foliage.
