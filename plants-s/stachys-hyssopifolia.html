Stachys hyssopifolia

Stachys hyssopifolia, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Stachys hyssopifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
