Scorpiurus muricatus var. eriocarpus

Scorpiurus muricatus var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the hairy nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettle species. The plant is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
