Staphylea bumalda

Staphylea bumalda, commonly known as sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with 5 to 9 ovate leaflets, and its flowers are small, fragrant, and white or pink in color. The plant produces woody, thorny stems and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
