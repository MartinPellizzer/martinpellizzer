Sesbania macowaniana

Sesbania macowaniana, commonly known as the Macowan's sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and is known for its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some cultures.
