Semenovia suffruticosa

Semenovia suffruticosa, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Semenovia suffruticosa is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
