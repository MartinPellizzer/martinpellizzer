Stevia occidentalis

Stevia occidentalis, commonly known as western stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used as sugar substitutes. Stevia occidentalis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both traditional medicine and modern natural health products.
