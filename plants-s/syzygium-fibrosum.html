Syzygium fibrosum

Syzygium fibrosum, commonly known as the fibrous tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fibrous pods containing seeds. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.

Syzygium fibrosum thrives in humid climates and is valued for both its ecological and economic significance.
