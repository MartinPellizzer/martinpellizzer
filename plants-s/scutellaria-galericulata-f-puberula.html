Scutellaria galericulata f. puberula

Scutellaria galericulata f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it the "pubescent" descriptor.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil.
