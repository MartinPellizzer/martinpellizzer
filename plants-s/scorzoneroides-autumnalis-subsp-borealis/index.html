Scorzoneroides Autumnalis Subsp. Borealis

Scorzoneroides autumnalis subsp. borealis is a rare and lesser-known plant species found in the northern regions of North America. It thrives in cool, moist environments such as tundra and alpine meadows where few plants can survive. This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf structure and pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes. Understanding and protecting this plant is crucial for preserving biodiversity in cold climates.
