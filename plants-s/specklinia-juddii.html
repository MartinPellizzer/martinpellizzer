Specklinia juddii

Specklinia juddii, commonly known as the Judd's sympodial orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, where new shoots arise from the base of the plant. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance.

Specklinia juddii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
