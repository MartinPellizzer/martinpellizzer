Schefflera confinis

Schefflera confinis, commonly known as the broadleaf schefflera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like clusters of leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Schefflera confinis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
