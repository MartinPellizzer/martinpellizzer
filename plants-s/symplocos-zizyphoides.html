Symplocos zizyphoides

Symplocos zizyphoides, commonly known as the Indian beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
