Solanum ferocissimum var. typicum

Solanum ferocissimum var. typicum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a species native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its berries contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
