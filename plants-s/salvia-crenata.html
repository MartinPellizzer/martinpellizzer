Salvia crenata

Salvia crenata, commonly known as purple sage, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. Salvia crenata typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
