Saxifraga dahurica

Saxifraga dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the cold climates of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late summer. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga dahurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
