Sideritis hirsuta var. luxurians

Sideritis hirsuta var. luxurians, commonly known as the hairy sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is noted for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
