Sphaeromorphaea littoralis

Sphaeromorphaea littoralis, commonly known as the coastal bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in brackish and freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its simple, branching structure and the presence of specialized bladder-like structures called pneumatocysts. These bladders help the plant float and facilitate gas exchange. The plant is often found in shallow, still waters near the coast.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates.
