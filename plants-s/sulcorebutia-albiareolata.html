Sulcorebutia albiareolata

Sulcorebutia albiareolata, commonly known as the White-areolated Cereus, is a succulent native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems and white, waxy tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers that typically bloom at night, adding to its ornamental value. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique features, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
