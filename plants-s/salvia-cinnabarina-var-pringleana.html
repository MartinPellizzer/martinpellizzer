Salvia cinnabarina var. pringleana

Salvia cinnabarina var. pringleana, commonly known as the Pringle's scarlet sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-red bracts that surround the small, tubular flowers, which are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.

The species is closely related to other members of the Salvia genus, sharing similar growth habits and floral structures.
