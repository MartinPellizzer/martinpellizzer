Salix dolichostyla subsp. serissifolia

Salix dolichostyla subsp.

serissifolia, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its long, drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
