Solanum tarijense var. pojoense

Solanum tarijense var. pojoense, commonly known as the Pojoense nightshade, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
