Stapelia dummeri

Stapelia dummeri, commonly known as the carrion flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and its distinctive, waxy, meaty-looking flowers that resemble decaying flesh. The plant's flowers emit a strong, unpleasant odor to attract pollinators such as flies and beetles. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Stapelia dummeri is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
