Syzygium graeffei

Syzygium graeffei, commonly known as the Java apple or *Syzygium malabaricum*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits resembling small apples.

Syzygium graeffei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
