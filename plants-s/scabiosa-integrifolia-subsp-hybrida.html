Scabiosa integrifolia subsp. hybrida

Scabiosa integrifolia subsp.

hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid carpet bugloss, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
