Solidago sempervirens

Solidago sempervirens, commonly known as evergreen goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types, including moist and well-drained conditions.

Solidago sempervirens is often found in woodlands, open fields, and along streambanks, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
