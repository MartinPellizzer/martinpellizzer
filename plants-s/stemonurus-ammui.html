Stemonurus ammui

Stemonurus ammui, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Stemonurus ammui is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
