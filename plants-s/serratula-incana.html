Serratula incana

Serratula incana, commonly known as hoary serratula, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, woolly foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

However, it is considered toxic and should be handled with care.
