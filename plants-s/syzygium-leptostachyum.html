Syzygium leptostachyum

Syzygium leptostachyum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and long, pendulous clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a bluish-green hue.

Syzygium leptostachyum is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
