Salix maccalliana

Salix maccalliana, commonly known as the MacCall's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. This shrub or small tree typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
