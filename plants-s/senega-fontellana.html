Senega fontellana

Senega fontellana, commonly known as the Florida gentian, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swamps, wetlands, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
