Syzygium dolichostylum

Syzygium dolichostylum, commonly known as the long-stalked clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Syzygium dolichostylum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
