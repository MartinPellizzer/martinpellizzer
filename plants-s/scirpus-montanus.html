Scirpus montanus

Scirpus montanus, commonly known as the mountain bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers. Scirpus montanus thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond margins, and along streams.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
