Shawia elaeophila

Shawia elaeophila, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and ethereal orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, spider-like flowers and lack of leaves, relying entirely on its roots for photosynthesis. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its unique morphology and habitat requirements make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Shawia elaeophila is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
