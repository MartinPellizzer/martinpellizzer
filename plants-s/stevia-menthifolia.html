Stevia menthifolia

Stevia menthifolia, commonly known as sweetleaf mint, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is cultivated for its intensely sweet leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Stevia menthifolia is often used as a natural sweetener and in herbal teas.
