Sanguisorba betoloniana

Sanguisorba betoloniana, commonly known as the Betula-leaved sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems that bear alternate, lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by reddish-brown, spiky fruits. Sanguisorba betoloniana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
