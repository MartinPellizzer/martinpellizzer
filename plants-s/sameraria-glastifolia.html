Sameraria glastifolia

Sameraria glastifolia, commonly known as the silver-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in forest understories.

Sameraria glastifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
