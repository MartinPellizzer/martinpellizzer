Solanum paniculatum f. repandum

Solanum paniculatum f. repandum, commonly known as the redcurrant bush, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, red berries that are edible. The plant produces flowers that are typically white or pale green, which develop into the berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine.
