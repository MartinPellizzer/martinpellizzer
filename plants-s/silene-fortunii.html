Silene fortunii

Silene fortunii, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Silene fortunii is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, although it remains relatively rare in the wild.
