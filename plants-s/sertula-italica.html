Sertula italica

Sertula italica, commonly known as the Italian sertula, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like parsley and carrots. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Sertula italica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
