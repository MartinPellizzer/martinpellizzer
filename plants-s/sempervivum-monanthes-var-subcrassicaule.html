Sempervivum monanthes var. subcrassicaule

Sempervivum monanthes var. subcrassicaule, commonly known as the thick-leaved houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense, fleshy rosettes of dark green leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces upright, branched stems that bear small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
