Staurogynopsis paludosa

Staurogynopsis paludosa, commonly known as the marsh aster, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and purple. Its flowering period occurs during the late summer and early fall, attracting various pollinators.

Staurogynopsis paludosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
