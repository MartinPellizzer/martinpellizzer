Schoenus tendo subsp. achaetus

Schoenus tendo subsp.

achaetus, commonly known as the narrowleaf marsh grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often less than 1 cm wide, giving it a distinct appearance. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves and bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
