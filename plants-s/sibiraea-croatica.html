Sibiraea croatica

Sibiraea croatica, commonly known as the Croatian sibiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses and cherries, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and is adapted to cooler climates. Its berries, which are edible, have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
