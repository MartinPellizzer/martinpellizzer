Statice monopetala

Statice monopetala, commonly known as the single-petaled glass gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically small and clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It blooms from late summer into early autumn, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
