Stevia micropappa

Stevia micropappa, commonly known as the small-leaved stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Stevia micropappa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
