Scrophularia cilicica

Scrophularia cilicica, commonly known as Cilician figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are clustered in whorls, with tubular corollas that are usually pink or purple in color.

Scrophularia cilicica is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
