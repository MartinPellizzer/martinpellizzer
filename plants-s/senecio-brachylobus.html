Senecio brachylobus

Senecio brachylobus, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
