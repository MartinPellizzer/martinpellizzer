Secale strictum subsp. africanum

Secale strictum subsp.

africanum, commonly known as African ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in temperate climates and is often used for forage.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
