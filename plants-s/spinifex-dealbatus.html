Spinifex dealbatus

Spinifex dealbatus, commonly known as the silver spinifex, is a hardy grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which results from the presence of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion makes it ecologically significant in its native habitat.

Spinifex dealbatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
