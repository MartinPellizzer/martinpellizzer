Solanum nemophilum var. brachycarpum

Solanum nemophilum var. brachycarpum, commonly known as the short-fruited nightshade, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or undergrowth. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually purple or white.

The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that ripens to a dark blue or black color, containing several seeds.
