Siphocampylus planchonis var. polyphyllus

Siphocampylus planchonis var. polyphyllus, commonly known as the mountain holly or silverleaf holly, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that have a silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid regions.
