Stachys micrantha

Stachys micrantha, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Stachys micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
