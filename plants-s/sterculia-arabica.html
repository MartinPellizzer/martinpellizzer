Sterculia arabica

Sterculia arabica, commonly known as the African oil bean, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant is notable for its hard, woody pods that contain a valuable oilseed used in the production of margarine and soap. The seeds are also used as a source of protein in some regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
