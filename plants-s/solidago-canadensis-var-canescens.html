Solidago canadensis var. canescens

Solidago canadensis var. canescens, commonly known as the gray goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and bright yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
