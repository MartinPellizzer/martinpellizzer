Salvia coelestina

Salvia coelestina, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, sky-blue flower clusters that resemble mist, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia coelestina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
