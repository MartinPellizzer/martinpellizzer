Sphaerocephalus aucheri

Sphaerocephalus aucheri, commonly known as the spherical bellflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its round, globe-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Sphaerocephalus aucheri is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
