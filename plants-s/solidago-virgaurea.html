Solidago virgaurea

Solidago virgaurea, commonly known as goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branching.

Solidago virgaurea plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
