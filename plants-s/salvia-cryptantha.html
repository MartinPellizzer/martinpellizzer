Salvia cryptantha

Salvia cryptantha, commonly known as blue curl sage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia cryptantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
