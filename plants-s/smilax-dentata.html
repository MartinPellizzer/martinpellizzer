Smilax dentata

Smilax dentata, commonly known as the toothed smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Smilax dentata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and thickets.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
