Shorea maranti

Shorea maranti, commonly known as white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, often reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and is known for its ecological importance in supporting diverse wildlife.
