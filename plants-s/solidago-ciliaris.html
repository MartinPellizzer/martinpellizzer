Solidago ciliaris

Solidago ciliaris, commonly known as the silverleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist soils along streambanks and wetlands. Its leaves are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Solidago ciliaris is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
