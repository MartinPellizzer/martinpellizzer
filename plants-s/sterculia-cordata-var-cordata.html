Sterculia cordata var. cordata

Sterculia cordata var. cordata, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
