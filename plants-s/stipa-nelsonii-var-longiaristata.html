Stipa nelsonii var. longiaristata

Stipa nelsonii var. longiaristata, commonly known as the long-awned needlegrass, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves with fine, hairy tips. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
