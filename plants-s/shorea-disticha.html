Shorea disticha

Shorea disticha, commonly known as the two-needle shorea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its straight, cylindrical trunk and smooth, pale bark. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are small and yellowish, typically clustered in spikes, and it produces woody fruits containing two seeds.

Shorea disticha is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
