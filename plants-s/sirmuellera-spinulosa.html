Sirmuellera spinulosa

Sirmuellera spinulosa, commonly known as the spinulose sirmuellera, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine, spin-like protrusions, giving it its common name.

Sirmuellera spinulosa is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
