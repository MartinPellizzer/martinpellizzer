Senecio heteroschizus

Senecio heteroschizus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers range from white to yellow.

Senecio heteroschizus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
