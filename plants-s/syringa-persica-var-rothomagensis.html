Syringa persica var. rothomagensis

Syringa persica var. rothomagensis, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white to pale purple, typically in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blossoms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
