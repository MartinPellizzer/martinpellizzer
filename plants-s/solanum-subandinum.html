Solanum subandinum

Solanum subandinum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, yellow or orange berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally by indigenous communities for various therapeutic purposes.
