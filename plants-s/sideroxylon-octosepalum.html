Sideroxylon octosepalum

Sideroxylon octosepalum, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
