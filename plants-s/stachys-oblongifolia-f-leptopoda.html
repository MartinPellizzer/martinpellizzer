Stachys oblongifolia f. leptopoda

Stachys oblongifolia f. leptopoda, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.
