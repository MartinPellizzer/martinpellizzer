Senecillis sibirica

Senecillis sibirica, commonly known as Siberian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Senecillis sibirica is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, alpine conditions.
