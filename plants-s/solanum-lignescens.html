Solanum lignescens

Solanum lignescens, commonly known as the woody nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale purple, followed by yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.

Solanum lignescens is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
