Schinus aurantiodora

Schinus aurantiodora, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree or copaiba.

It is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. The bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
