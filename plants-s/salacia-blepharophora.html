Salacia blepharophora

Salacia blepharophora, commonly known as the eyeleaf plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its unique, eye-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are thick and leathery, with a waxy surface that helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive foliage.
