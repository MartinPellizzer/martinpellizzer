Scrophularia amana

Scrophularia amana, commonly known as the common scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of pink or purple tubular flowers. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia amana is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
