Satureja imbricata

Satureja imbricata, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils contain compounds such as thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong, spicy fragrance.

Satureja imbricata is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
