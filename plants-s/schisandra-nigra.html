Schisandra nigra

Schisandra nigra, commonly known as black schisandra, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, warty berries that mature in late summer. The plant belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is known for its aromatic, resinous fruits. Its leaves are oval and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are woody and branched.

Schisandra nigra is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
