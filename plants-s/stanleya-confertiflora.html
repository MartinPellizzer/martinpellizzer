Stanleya confertiflora

Stanleya confertiflora, commonly known as the thick-flowered larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their spurred petals and are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies.

Stanleya confertiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
