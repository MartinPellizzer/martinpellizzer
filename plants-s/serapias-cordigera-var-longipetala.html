Serapias cordigera var. longipetala

Serapias cordigera var. longipetala, commonly known as the long-petaled orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers with long, slender petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and cliffs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety is distinguished by its elongated petals, which set it apart from other varieties within the Serapias genus.
