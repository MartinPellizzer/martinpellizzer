Senna alexandrina

Senna alexandrina, commonly known as Alexandrian senna, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by long, slender pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its laxative properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in dry conditions.
