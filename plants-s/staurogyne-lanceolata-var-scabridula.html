Staurogyne lanceolata var. scabridula

Staurogyne lanceolata var. scabridula, commonly known as the roughleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branched stems that often form dense underwater stands, contributing to aquatic habitat structure. The leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

This plant plays an important role in water ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
