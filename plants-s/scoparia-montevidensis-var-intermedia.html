Scoparia montevidensis var. intermedia

Scoparia montevidensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Montevideo scoparia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellow flowers. Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
