Spathulopetalum moniliforme

Spathulopetalum moniliforme, commonly known as the chainflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a dense cluster of small, white to cream-colored flowers arranged in a chain-like pattern. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

Spathulopetalum moniliforme is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
