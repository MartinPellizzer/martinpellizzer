Scutellaria cypria var. elatior

Scutellaria cypria var. elatior, commonly known as the tall skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching towards the upper part. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
