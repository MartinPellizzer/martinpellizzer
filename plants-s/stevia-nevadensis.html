Stevia nevadensis

Stevia nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-arid environments.

Due to its medicinal and commercial value, it has been the subject of various botanical and agricultural studies.
