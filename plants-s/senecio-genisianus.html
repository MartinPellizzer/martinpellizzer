Senecio genisianus

Senecio genisianus, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy, silver-green leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
