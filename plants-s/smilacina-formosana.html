Smilacina formosana

Smilacina formosana, commonly known as the Formosa greenbriar, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, dark green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor. Smilacina formosana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of temperate flora and ecological interactions.
