Setaria glauca var. pulchella

Setaria glauca var. pulchella, commonly known as blue false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or disturbed soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to moist environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
