Sorbus aria var. sudetica

Sorbus aria var. sudetica, commonly known as the Bohemian whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow berries that appear in clusters, and its leaves are typically elliptical with a slightly serrated margin. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks. It has a slow growth rate and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive bark and foliage.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
