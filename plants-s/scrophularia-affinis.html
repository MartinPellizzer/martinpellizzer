Scrophularia affinis

Scrophularia affinis, commonly known as narrow-leaved figwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

Scrophularia affinis is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
