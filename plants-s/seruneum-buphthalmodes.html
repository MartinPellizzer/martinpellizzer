Seruneum buphthalmodes

Seruneum buphthalmodes, commonly known as the eye-of-the-crocodile, is a rare and striking plant native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, greenish-brown leaves that resemble the shape of a crocodile's eye. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss, Seruneum buphthalmodes is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
