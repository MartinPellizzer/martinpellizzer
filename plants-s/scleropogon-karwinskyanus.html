Scleropogon karwinskyanus

Scleropogon karwinskyanus, commonly known as the Karwinsky's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rigid texture typical of sedges.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.
