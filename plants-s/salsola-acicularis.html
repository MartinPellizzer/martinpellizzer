Salsola acicularis

Salsola acicularis, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with slender, spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, often giving it a prickly appearance. Salsola acicularis is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and saline environments, and is often found in disturbed soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
