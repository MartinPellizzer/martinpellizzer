Saccolabium coplandii

Saccolabium coplandii, commonly known as the golden wattle of the Northern Territory, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native region.
