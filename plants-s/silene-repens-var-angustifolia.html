Silene repens var. angustifolia

Silene repens var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bluetail, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems that often lie flat on the ground. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, typically with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
