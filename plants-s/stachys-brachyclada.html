Stachys brachyclada

Stachys brachyclada, commonly known as woolly betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowering stems are usually branched and bear small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stachys brachyclada is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
