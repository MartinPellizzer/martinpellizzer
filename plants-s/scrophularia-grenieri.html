Scrophularia grenieri

Scrophularia grenieri, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of two-lipped, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Scrophularia grenieri prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with similar floral structures and ecological preferences.
