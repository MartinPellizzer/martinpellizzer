Syzygium fulvotomentosum

Syzygium fulvotomentosum, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody pods containing edible, sour-tasting seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its seeds in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid climates and is well adapted to coastal environments.
