Setaria plicata

Setaria plicata, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Switchgrass is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it a valuable species for ecological restoration and biofuel production.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in marginal lands contribute to its importance in sustainable agriculture and environmental conservation efforts.
