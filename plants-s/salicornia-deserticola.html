Salicornia deserticola

Salicornia deserticola, commonly known as desert glasswort, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, jointed stems that often appear glassy due to their high salt content. This species is typically found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or reddish, and it reproduces through seeds and vegetative propagation.

Salicornia deserticola is an important plant in ecological studies due to its resilience and role in salt-tolerant ecosystems.
