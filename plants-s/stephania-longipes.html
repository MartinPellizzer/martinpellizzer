Stephania longipes

Stephania longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed stephania, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically followed by berries containing toxic seeds. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, though they contain harmful alkaloids.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
