Sasa naucinodosa

Sasa naucinodosa, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grassy aroma when crushed.

Sasa naucinodosa thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
