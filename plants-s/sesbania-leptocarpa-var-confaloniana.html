Sesbania leptocarpa var. confaloniana

Sesbania leptocarpa var. confaloniana, commonly known as the slender sesbania, is a variant of the sesbania genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines in some parts of Asia.
