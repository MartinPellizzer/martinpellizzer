Spiraea hemicryptophyta

Spiraea hemicryptophyta, commonly known as the meadow spirea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Spiraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy clusters of white or pink flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Spiraea hemicryptophyta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural and cultivated landscapes.
