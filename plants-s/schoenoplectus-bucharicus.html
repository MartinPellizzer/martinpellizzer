Schoenoplectus bucharicus

Schoenoplectus bucharicus, commonly known as the Bukharan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and shallow water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its robust rhizome system, which helps it thrive in moist soils.

Schoenoplectus bucharicus plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
