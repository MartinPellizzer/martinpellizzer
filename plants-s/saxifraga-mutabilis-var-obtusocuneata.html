Saxifraga mutabilis var. obtusocuneata

Saxifraga mutabilis var. obtusocuneata, commonly known as the variable saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, and it produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is characterized by its ability to change leaf shape and color depending on environmental conditions, hence its common name.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh climates.
