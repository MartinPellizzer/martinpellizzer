Salvia repens var. keiensis

Salvia repens var. keiensis, commonly known as the Keiensis mint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has aromatic leaves with a mint-like fragrance, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal collections.
