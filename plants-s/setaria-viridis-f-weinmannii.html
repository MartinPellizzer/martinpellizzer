Setaria viridis f. weinmannii

Setaria viridis f. weinmannii, commonly known as green millet or slender green millet, is a variant of the green millet species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for forage or as a cover crop.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its ecological and agricultural significance.
