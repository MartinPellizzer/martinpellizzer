Syzygium subsimile

Syzygium subsimile, commonly known as the small wax apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a waxy, smooth skin that turns red when ripe. The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy green surface with a slightly serrated margin.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native regions.
