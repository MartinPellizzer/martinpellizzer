Saxifraga sublinearifolia

Saxifraga sublinearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Saxifraga sublinearifolia is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
