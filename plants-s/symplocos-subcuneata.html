Symplocos subcuneata

Symplocos subcuneata, commonly known as the narrow-angled evergreen huckleberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forest understories and along stream banks. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are white and clustered in umbels, followed by small, red berries that are edible.

Symplocos subcuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
