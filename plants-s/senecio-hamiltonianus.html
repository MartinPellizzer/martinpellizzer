Senecio hamiltonianus

Senecio hamiltonianus, commonly known as Hamilton's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and succulent foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio hamiltonianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
