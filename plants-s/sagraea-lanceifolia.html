Sagraea lanceifolia

Sagraea lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf sagraea, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh habitat. Its growth pattern is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and a compact form.

Sagraea lanceifolia is noted for its unique botanical features, making it a distinctive species in the flora of Socotra.
