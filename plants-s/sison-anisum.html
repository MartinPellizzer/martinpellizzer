Sison anisum

Sison anisum, commonly known as cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and has feathery, aromatic leaves. Its seeds, which are the primary part used, are rich in essential oils and have a warm, earthy flavor.

Cumin is widely used in global cuisines and also has medicinal properties.
