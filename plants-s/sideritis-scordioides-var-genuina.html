Sideritis scordioides var. genuina

Sideritis scordioides var. genuina, commonly known as Greek oregano or wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and cooking for their pungent, aromatic properties.
