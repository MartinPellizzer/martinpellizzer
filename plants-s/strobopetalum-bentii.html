Strobopetalum bentii

Strobopetalum bentii, commonly known as the Sierra lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their aromatic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
