Stenomesson viridiflorum var. elwesii

Stenomesson viridiflorum var. elwesii, commonly known as the Elwes' greenflower sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikelets. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and a distinct, often erect flowering stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
