Scabiosa taygetea subsp. portae

Scabiosa taygetea subsp.

portae, commonly known as the Greek scabious, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in clusters, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and wildflower meadows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and ecological landscapes.
