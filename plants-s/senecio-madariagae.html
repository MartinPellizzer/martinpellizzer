Senecio madariagae

Senecio madariagae, commonly known as the Madariaga's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and nutrient-poor soils. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
