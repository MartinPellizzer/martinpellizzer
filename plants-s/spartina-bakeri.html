Spartina bakeri

Spartina bakeri, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western coast of North America.

It thrives in brackish and saline wetlands, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Spartina bakeri plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ability to tolerate saltwater makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
