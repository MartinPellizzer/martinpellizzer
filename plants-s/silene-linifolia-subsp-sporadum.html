Silene linifolia subsp. sporadum

Silene linifolia subsp.

sporadum, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers and linear leaves, which give it its specific epithet "linifolia.".

This subspecies is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
