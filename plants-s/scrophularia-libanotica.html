Scrophularia libanotica

Scrophularia libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Scrophularia libanotica is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in horticulture.
