Stenoloma fumarioides

Stenoloma fumarioides, commonly known as the fumarioid false hellebore, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, glaucous leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear small, pale green to white flowers arranged in loose clusters. Its flowers are typically bell-shaped and have a distinctive, fleshy appearance.

Stenoloma fumarioides is adapted to moist environments and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.
