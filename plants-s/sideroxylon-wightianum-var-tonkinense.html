Sideroxylon wightianum var. tonkinense

Sideroxylon wightianum var. tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has a straight, cylindrical trunk with smooth, grayish bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a waxy coating.

The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing several seeds.
