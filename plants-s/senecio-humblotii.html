Senecio humblotii

Senecio humblotii, commonly known as the Humblot's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads.

Its foliage is usually gray-green, with narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.
