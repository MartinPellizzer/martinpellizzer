Senecio Discolor

Blueweed, scientifically known as Senecio discolor, is a striking plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its vibrant blue flowers and fuzzy, silver-green foliage that catch the light beautifully. Often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, it thrives in arid environments where few other plants can survive. Despite its ornamental appeal, it is also a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a fascinating subject for both botanists and gardeners alike.
