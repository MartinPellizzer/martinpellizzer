Staurogyne longifolia

Staurogyne longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and elongated, narrow leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Staurogyne longifolia thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its dense growth helps stabilize sediments and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
