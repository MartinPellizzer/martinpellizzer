Stachys palustris f. cleoniquei

Stachys palustris f. cleoniquei, commonly known as the cleonique's waterplantain, is a rare and distinctive variant of the waterplantain species.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes, producing purple to pink blooms that attract pollinators.

This form is notable for its unique morphological characteristics and ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
