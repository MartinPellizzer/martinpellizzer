Symphytum longipetiolatum

Symphytum longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with long petioles, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Symphytum longipetiolatum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its healing qualities.
