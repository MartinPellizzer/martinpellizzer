Sterculia stipulata var. stipulata

Sterculia stipulata var. stipulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed sterculia, is a tropical tree native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
