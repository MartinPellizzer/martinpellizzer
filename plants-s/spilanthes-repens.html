Spilanthes repens

Spilanthes repens, commonly known as the wandering onion, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its strong, pungent odor.

Spilanthes repens is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
