Solanum retrorsum

Solanum retrorsum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with toothed edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Solanum retrorsum is also known for its edible berries, though they are typically not consumed by humans.
