Sutera fissifolia

Sutera fissifolia, commonly known as the fissured leaf sutera, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Sutera fissifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
