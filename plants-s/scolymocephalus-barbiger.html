Scolymocephalus barbiger

Scolymocephalus barbiger, commonly known as the hairy-headed scolymus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features upright stems with hairy foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive hairy appearance and the shape of its head-like inflorescence.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
