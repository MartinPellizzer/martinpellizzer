Saxifraga aizoon var. montana

Saxifraga aizoon var. montana, commonly known as the Sierra saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Saxifraga aizoon var. montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
