Symplocos macrophylla var. kurgensis

Symplocos macrophylla var. kurgensis, commonly known as Kurgensian wax myrtle, is a variant of the wax myrtle genus native to specific regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
