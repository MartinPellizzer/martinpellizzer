Sarocalamus racemosus

Sarocalamus racemosus, commonly known as the racemose bamboo, is a species of grass found primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces inflorescences that are racemose, meaning they are arranged in a branching, spike-like structure. Its leaves are narrow and often have a smooth texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Sarocalamus racemosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
