Syzygium lababiense

Syzygium lababiense, commonly known as the Lababai apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a sweet, aromatic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Syzygium lababiense is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
