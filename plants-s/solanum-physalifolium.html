Solanum physalifolium

Solanum physalifolium, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, balloon-like fruit that develops from its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are typically large and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum physalifolium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
