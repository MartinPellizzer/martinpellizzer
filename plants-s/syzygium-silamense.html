Syzygium silamense

Syzygium silamense, commonly known as the Silamense tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are used in local cuisines. Syzygium silamense thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes in its native region.
