Steptorhamphus linczevskii

Steptorhamphus linczevskii, commonly known as the Linczevsky's lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its crustose growth form, forming a thin, firm layer on rock surfaces, typically in shaded, humid environments. The thallus is usually pale yellow to orange in color, with a distinct, granular texture. This lichen is often found in temperate regions and is used as an indicator species for environmental health.
