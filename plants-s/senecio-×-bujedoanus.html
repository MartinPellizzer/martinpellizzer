Senecio × bujedoanus

Senecio × bujedoanus, commonly known as the Bujedoan groundsel, is a hybrid plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It exhibits a compact growth habit with a low, spreading form, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and unique genetic makeup, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
