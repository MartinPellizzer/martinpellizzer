Salvadora persica var. indica

Salvadora persica var. indica, commonly known as the Indian toothbrush plant, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its roots and leaves contain compounds with antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
