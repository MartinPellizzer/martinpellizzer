Sedum hierapetrae

Sedum hierapetrae, commonly known as the rock sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum hierapetrae is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
