Silene involuta

Silene involuta, commonly known as the twisted catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
