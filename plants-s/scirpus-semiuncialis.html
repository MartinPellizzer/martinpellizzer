Scirpus semiuncialis

Scirpus semiuncialis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, narrow leaves that are usually less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered spikelets that mature into dark brown seeds.

Scirpus semiuncialis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
