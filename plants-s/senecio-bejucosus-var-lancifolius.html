Senecio bejucosus var. lancifolius

Senecio bejucosus var. lancifolius, commonly known as the lanceleaf senecio, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by distinctive, woolly seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
