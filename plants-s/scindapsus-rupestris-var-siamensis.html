Scindapsus rupestris var. siamensis

Scindapsus rupestris var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam nettle or Siam arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive netted pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant is a member of the Araceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
