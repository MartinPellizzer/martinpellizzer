Symplocos megabotrys

Symplocos megabotrys, commonly known as the large-leafed wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Symplocos is known for its aromatic foliage and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
