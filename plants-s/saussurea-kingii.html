Saussurea kingii

Saussurea kingii, commonly known as King's saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic compound known as safflower, which is used in traditional medicine and as a dye. Its growth is adapted to cold, arid environments, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Saussurea kingii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
