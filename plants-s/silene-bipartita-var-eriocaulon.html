Silene bipartita var. eriocaulon

Silene bipartita var. eriocaulon, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, whitish hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
