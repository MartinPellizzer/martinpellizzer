Salix safsaf

Salix safsaf, commonly known as the Kashmir willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure and grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, typical of the Salix genus. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a serrated margin, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Salix safsaf is adapted to high-altitude environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
