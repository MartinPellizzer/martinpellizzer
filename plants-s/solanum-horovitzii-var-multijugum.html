Solanum horovitzii var. multijugum

Solanum horovitzii var. multijugum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features elliptical, glossy leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The flowers are typically white or pale green, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated calyx. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties, though its use is limited due to its toxicity.
