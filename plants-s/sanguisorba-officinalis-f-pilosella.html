Sanguisorba officinalis f. pilosella

Sanguisorba officinalis f. pilosella, commonly known as the hairy sanguisorba, is a variant of the common sanguisorba.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
