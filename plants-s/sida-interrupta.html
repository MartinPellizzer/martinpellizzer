Sida interrupta

Sida interrupta, commonly known as Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Sida interrupta is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.
