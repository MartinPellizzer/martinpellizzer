Stachys musili

Stachys musili, commonly known as musk thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Musk thyme is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
