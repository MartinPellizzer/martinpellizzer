Salvia macrosiphon var. brachycalycina

Salvia macrosiphon var. brachycalycina, commonly known as the narrow calyx sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its inflorescence consists of whorled clusters of tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
