Simsia annectens var. grayi

Simsia annectens var. grayi, commonly known as the Gray's Simsia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a conical shape, and it produces small, elongated fruits known as achene.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
