Stellaria vestita var. amplexicaulis

Stellaria vestita var. amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with prostrate stems that often root at the nodes. The plant has small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite and often clasping the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
