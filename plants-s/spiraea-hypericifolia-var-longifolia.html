Spiraea hypericifolia var. longifolia

Spiraea hypericifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, arching stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they turn yellow in the fall. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
