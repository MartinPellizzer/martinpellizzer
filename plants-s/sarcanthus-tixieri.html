Sarcanthus tixieri

Sarcanthus tixieri, commonly known as the Tixieri's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and striking, pale pink or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or in dense forest undergrowth. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Sarcanthus tixieri is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
