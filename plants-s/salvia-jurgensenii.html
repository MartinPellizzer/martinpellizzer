Salvia jurgensenii

Salvia jurgensenii, commonly known as Jurgensen's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native gardens.
