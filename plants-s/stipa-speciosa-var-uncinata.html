Stipa speciosa var. uncinata

Stipa speciosa var. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds visual interest to the landscape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
