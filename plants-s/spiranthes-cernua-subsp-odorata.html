Spiranthes cernua subsp.

odorata, commonly known as the fragrant twocurved spikerel. This perennial orchid is native to North America and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators like moths and beetles.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
