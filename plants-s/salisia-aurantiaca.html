Salisia aurantiaca

Salisia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange salisia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, while its fruits are small, dry follicles.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
