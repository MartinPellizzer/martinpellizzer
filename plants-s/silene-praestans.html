Silene praestans

Silene praestans, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pink to magenta flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Silene praestans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
