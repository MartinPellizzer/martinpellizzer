Salvia mekongensis

Salvia mekongensis, commonly known as Mekong sage, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Salvia mekongensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
