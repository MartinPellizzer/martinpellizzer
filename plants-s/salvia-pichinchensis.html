Salvia pichinchensis

Salvia pichinchensis, commonly known as the Pichincha sage, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
