Spennera paludosa

Spennera paludosa, commonly known as the marsh spennera, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Spennera paludosa thrives in moist, poorly drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
