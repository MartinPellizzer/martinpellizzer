Styrax pearcei var. bolivianus

Styrax pearcei var. bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian fringetree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters along the branches. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy texture.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
