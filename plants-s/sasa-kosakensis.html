Sasa kosakensis

Sasa kosakensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food source. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

Sasa kosakensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
