Scrophularia smithii subsp. hierrensis

Scrophularia smithii subsp.

hierrensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy blooms.

This subspecies is often found in open woodlands and alpine meadows, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
