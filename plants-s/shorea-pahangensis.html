Shorea pahangensis

Shorea pahangensis, commonly known as Pahang ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, often with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

Shorea pahangensis plays an important ecological role in its native forests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
