Sasa tikusialpina

Sasa tikusialpina, commonly known as the alpine bamboo, is a perennial grass native to high-altitude regions of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are usually covered with a fine layer of hair, adapting to cold and harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with cool climates.

Sasa tikusialpina plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various alpine organisms.
