Salvia umbraticola

Salvia umbraticola, commonly known as shade sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies, and it attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia umbraticola is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in shaded environments.
