Saccharum officinale

Saccharum officinale, commonly known as sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its sturdy, fibrous stalks that contain high concentrations of sucrose. The plant thrives in warm climates with ample sunlight and water, making it a staple in sugar production. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a rough texture, while the stem is segmented and often used for both commercial and industrial purposes.

Saccharum officinale plays a significant role in agriculture and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
