Syndesmanthus articulatus var. hirtus

Syndesmanthus articulatus var. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy leaf syndesmanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
