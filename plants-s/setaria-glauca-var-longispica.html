Setaria glauca var. longispica

Setaria glauca var. longispica, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural settings.
