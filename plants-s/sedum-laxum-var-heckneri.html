Sedum laxum var. heckneri

Sedum laxum var. heckneri, commonly known as the Heckner's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically blue-green, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
