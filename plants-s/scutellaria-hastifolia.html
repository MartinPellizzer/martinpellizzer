Scutellaria hastifolia

Scutellaria hastifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its small, tubular flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
