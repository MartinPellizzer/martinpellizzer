Sesbania sudanica subsp. occidentalis

Sesbania sudanica subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the Fabaceae family.

The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used for forage and soil improvement.

Its rapid growth makes it a valuable species for agroforestry and ecological restoration projects.
