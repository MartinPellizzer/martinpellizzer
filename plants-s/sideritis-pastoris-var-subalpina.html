Sideritis pastoris var. subalpina

Sideritis pastoris var. subalpina, commonly known as alpine ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces small, hairy seed capsules.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
