Sabicea sanguinosa subsp. sanguinosa

Sabicea sanguinosa subsp.

sanguinosa, commonly known as the red-flowered sabicea, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the legume family, Fabaceae.
