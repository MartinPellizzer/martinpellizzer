Schisandra pubescens

Schisandra pubescens, commonly known as the hairy five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, reddish berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is notable for its aromatic, five-flavored berries, which are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often covered in fine, white pubescence.

Schisandra pubescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.
