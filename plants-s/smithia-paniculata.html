Smithia paniculata

Smithia paniculata, commonly known as the panicled smilacina, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and slender, vine-like stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are often borne on the upper parts of the stems. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Smithia paniculata is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
