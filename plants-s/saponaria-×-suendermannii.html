Saponaria × suendermannii

Saponaria × suendermannii, commonly known as the foxtail soapwort, is a hybrid plant in the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of small, pink or white flowers resembling a fox's tail. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Its foliage is lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a distinctive soapy scent when crushed.

Saponaria × suendermannii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
