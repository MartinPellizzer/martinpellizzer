Saussurea kirigaminensis

Saussurea kirigaminensis, commonly known as the Kirigamine saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root systems. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellowish and surrounded by pale green bracts, contributing to its unique ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
