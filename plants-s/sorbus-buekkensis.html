Sorbus buekkensis

Sorbus buekkensis, commonly known as the Buekkensis whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often flattened or clustered fruiting bodies called samaras. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges, while its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
