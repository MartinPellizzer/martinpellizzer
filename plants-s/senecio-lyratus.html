Senecio lyratus

Senecio lyratus, commonly known as the Lyreleaf Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lyre-shaped, with a distinctive lobed structure, and its flowers are small yellow daisies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
