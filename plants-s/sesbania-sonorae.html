Sesbania sonorae

Sesbania sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran sesbania, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert region of North America.

It is a leguminous species, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

Sesbania sonorae is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant in semi-arid regions.
