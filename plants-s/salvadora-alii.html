Salvadora alii

Salvadora alii, commonly known as the toothbrush tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Salvadora alii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical use in dental care.
