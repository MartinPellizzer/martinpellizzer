Salvia durandiana

Salvia durandiana, commonly known as the Indian sage or durian sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowering stems often bear clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
