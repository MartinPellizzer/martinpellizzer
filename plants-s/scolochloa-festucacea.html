Scolochloa festucacea

Scolochloa festucacea, commonly known as slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense tussocks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Scolochloa festucacea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
