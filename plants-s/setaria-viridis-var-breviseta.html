Setaria viridis var. breviseta

Setaria viridis var. breviseta, commonly known as green foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and spike-like inflorescences composed of bracts. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and agricultural areas.

This plant is also notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
