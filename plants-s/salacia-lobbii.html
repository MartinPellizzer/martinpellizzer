Salacia lobbii

Salacia lobbii, commonly known as the African ghost tree, is a member of the Salaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, while its fruit is a small, fleshy berry.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
