Syntherisma malacophylla

Syntherisma malacophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a rare and fascinating plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are thick and succulent, adapted to store water in its arid habitat. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid environment to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
