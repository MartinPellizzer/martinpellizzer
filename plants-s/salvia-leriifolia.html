Salvia leriifolia

Salvia leriifolia, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its tall, spires of violet to purple flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a fragrant aroma. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
