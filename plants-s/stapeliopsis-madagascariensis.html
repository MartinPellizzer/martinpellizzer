Stapeliopsis madagascariensis

Stapeliopsis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a slow growth rate, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

The species is also noted for its ability to form offsets, which can be used for propagation.
