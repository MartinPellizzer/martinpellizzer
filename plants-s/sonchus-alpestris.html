Sonchus alpestris

Sonchus alpestris, commonly known as Alpine sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Sonchus alpestris is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks at high elevations.
