Sida jussiaeana

Sida jussiaeana, commonly known as seaside mallow, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in sandy or saline soils, adapting well to harsh coastal environments. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are pink or purple, arranged in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Sida jussiaeana is often found in dunes and salt marshes, playing a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
