Senecio lancidentatus

Senecio lancidentatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in arid regions.
