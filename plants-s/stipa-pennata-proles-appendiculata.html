Stipa pennata proles appendiculata

Stipa pennata proles appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically green to pale brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
