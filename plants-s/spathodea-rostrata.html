Spathodea rostrata

Spathodea rostrata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
