Stylidium claytonioides

Stylidium claytonioides, commonly known as the clayton's spurred orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique spurred labellum, which is a specialized structure that aids in pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its flowers are usually small and colorful, with a distinctive shape that facilitates interaction with specific pollinators.
