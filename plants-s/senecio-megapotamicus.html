Senecio megapotamicus

Senecio megapotamicus, commonly known as the Andean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
