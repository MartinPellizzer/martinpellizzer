Soehrensia huascha subsp. huascha

Soehrensia huascha subsp.

huascha, commonly known as the Huascha holly, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy dark green surface and produces small, white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
