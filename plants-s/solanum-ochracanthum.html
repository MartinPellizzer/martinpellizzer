Solanum ochracanthum

Solanum ochracanthum, commonly known as the red-stemmed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum ochracanthum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
