Spiraea ribifolia

Spiraea ribifolia, commonly known as the redleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of small, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are elliptical and turn a striking red or orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to the plant. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Spiraea ribifolia is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
