Senecio jacquinianus var. expansus

Senecio jacquinianus var. expansus, commonly known as the broadleaf groundsell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
