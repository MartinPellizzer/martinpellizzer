Sarcomphalus platyphyllus

Sarcomphalus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear purple to violet flowers in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
