Sorghum cernuum var. yemense

Sorghum cernuum var. yemense, commonly known as the Ethiopian lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikelets, which are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
