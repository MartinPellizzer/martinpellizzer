Saxifraga exarata var. kusnezowii

Saxifraga exarata var. kusnezowii, commonly known as the Kusnezow's saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features upright stems with clustered, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in dense inflorescences, adding ornamental value to its habitat.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species in its native range.
