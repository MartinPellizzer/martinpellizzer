Syzygium jaherii

Syzygium jaherii, commonly known as the Java apple or *Pomelo*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces large, round fruits with a thick, leathery rind and juicy, sweet flesh. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Syzygium jaherii is cultivated for its edible fruit and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
