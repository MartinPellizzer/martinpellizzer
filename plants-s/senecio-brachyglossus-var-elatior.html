Senecio brachyglossus var. elatior

Senecio brachyglossus var. elatior, commonly known as the Blueweed or Blueweed variety, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
