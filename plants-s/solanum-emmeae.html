Solanum emmeae

Solanum emmeae, commonly known as the Emmea nightshade, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically features hairy stems and produces small, violet-colored flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats.

Solanum emmeae is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance.
