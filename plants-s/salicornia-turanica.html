Salicornia turanica

Salicornia turanica, commonly known as Turk's saltwort, is a halophyte plant that thrives in saline environments.

It is native to Central Asia and is often found in coastal and arid regions with high salt content in the soil. The plant has prostrate or erect stems covered in small, succulent leaves that help it retain water. Its flowers are small and greenish, typically arranged in clusters.

Salicornia turanica is valued for its nutritional properties and is used in traditional cuisines and as a source of bioactive compounds.
