Senecio fistulosus

Senecio fistulosus, commonly known as the slender senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio fistulosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and drought tolerance.
