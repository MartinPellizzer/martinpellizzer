Staphylodendron trifoliatum

Staphylodendron trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved staphylodendron, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
