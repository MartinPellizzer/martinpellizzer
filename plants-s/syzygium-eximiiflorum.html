Syzygium eximiiflorum

Syzygium eximiiflorum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

Syzygium eximiiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a shade tree in tropical landscapes.
