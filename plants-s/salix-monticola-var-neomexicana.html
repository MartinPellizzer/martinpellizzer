Salix monticola var. neomexicana

Salix monticola var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and catkin flowers, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. This species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky slopes and canyon walls. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in early spring.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of desert riparian ecosystems.
