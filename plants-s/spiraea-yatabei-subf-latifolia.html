Spiraea yatabei subf. latifolia

Spiraea yatabei subf.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and they turn yellow in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
