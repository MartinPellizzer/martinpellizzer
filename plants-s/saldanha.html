Saldanha

Saldanha, commonly known as the "sea holly," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower heads. The plant belongs to the genus Eryngium and is often found in coastal areas and dry soils. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Saldanha is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resistance to drought.
