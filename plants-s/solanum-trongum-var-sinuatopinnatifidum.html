Solanum trongum var. sinuatopinnatifidum

Solanum trongum var. sinuatopinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically small, yellowish, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, toxic berry that turns red when mature, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
