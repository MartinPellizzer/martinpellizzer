Sambucus sieboldiana var. buergeriana

Sambucus sieboldiana var. buergeriana, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
