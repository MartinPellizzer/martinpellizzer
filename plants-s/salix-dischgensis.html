Salix dischgensis

Salix dischgensis, commonly known as the Dischgen's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Salix.

It is native to central and eastern Europe, particularly found in regions of Germany, Austria, and the Czech Republic. This shrub or small tree typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows. It has slender, often drooping branches and produces catkins in early spring.

The leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
