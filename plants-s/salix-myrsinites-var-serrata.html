Salix myrsinites var. serrata

Salix myrsinites var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it typically blooms in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
