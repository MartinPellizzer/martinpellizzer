Syzygium trunciflorum

Syzygium trunciflorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The tree produces edible, sour fruits that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Syzygium trunciflorum thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.
