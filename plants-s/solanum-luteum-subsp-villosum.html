Solanum luteum subsp. villosum

Solanum luteum subsp.

villosum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, orange berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Solanaceae family.
