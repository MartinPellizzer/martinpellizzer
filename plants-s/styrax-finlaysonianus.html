Styrax finlaysonianus

Styrax finlaysonianus, commonly known as the Florida styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple, waxy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
