Smilacina tubifera

Smilacina tubifera, commonly known as the swamp yellowroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that thrives in wet, wooded areas and is characterized by its yellowish roots and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread through underground stems, forming dense colonies.

Smilacina tubifera is often found in moist habitats such as swamps, stream banks, and shaded forests.
