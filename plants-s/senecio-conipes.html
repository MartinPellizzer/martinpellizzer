Senecio conipes

Senecio conipes, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive addition to xeriscapes.
