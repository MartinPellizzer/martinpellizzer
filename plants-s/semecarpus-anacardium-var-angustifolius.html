Semecarpus anacardium var. angustifolius

Semecarpus anacardium var. angustifolius, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or narrow-leaved chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and alternate along the stem. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spiny, woody fruits containing a single, hard-shelled seed. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shady forest environments.
