Sebastiania corniculata f. salicifolia

Sebastiania corniculata f. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leafed sebastiania, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, willow-like leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
