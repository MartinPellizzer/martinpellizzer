Silene carnea

Silene carnea, commonly known as the pink campion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Silene carnea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
