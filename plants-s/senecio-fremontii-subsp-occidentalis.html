Senecio fremontii subsp. occidentalis

Senecio fremontii subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Senecio, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers in the summer months.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
