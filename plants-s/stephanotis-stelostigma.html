Stephanotis stelostigma

Stephanotis stelostigma, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling the starry sky. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are woody and twining. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also have traditional uses in some cultures.
