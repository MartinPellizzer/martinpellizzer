Scirpus pseudoarticulatus

Scirpus pseudoarticulatus, commonly known as the false arrowhead sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas, pond margins, and along the edges of streams, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a triangular cross-section, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-sided spikelets that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Scirpus pseudoarticulatus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
