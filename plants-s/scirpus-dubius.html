Scirpus dubius

Scirpus dubius, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus dubius plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
