Sabia croizatiana

Sabia croizatiana, commonly known as the Zulu sage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Sabia croizatiana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in local habitats.
