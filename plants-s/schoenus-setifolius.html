Schoenus setifolius

Schoenus setifolius, commonly known as the tufted swamp grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Schoenus setifolius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
