Stipa austroitalica subsp. theresiae

Stipa austroitalica subsp.

theresiae, commonly known as the Tuscan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Italy. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, typically in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
