Solanum opuliflorum

Solanum opuliflorum, commonly known as the showy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale purple, with a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species such as tomatoes and peppers. Solanum opuliflorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It produces small, oval berries that are toxic to humans and animals.
