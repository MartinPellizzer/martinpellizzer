Spiranthes romanzoffiana

Spiranthes romanzoffiana, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence that resembles a twisted ribbon, with pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and bogs. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
