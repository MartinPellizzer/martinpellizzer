Sobolewskia sibirica

Sobolewskia sibirica, commonly known as Siberian sobolewskia, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets.

Sobolewskia sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
