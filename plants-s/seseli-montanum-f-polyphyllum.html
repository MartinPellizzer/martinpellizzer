Seseli montanum f. polyphyllum

Seseli montanum f. polyphyllum, commonly known as the mountain seseli, is a botanical variant of the seseli genus.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and distinctive, often hairy foliage. The plant typically features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
