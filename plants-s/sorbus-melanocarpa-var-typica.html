Sorbus melanocarpa var. typica

Sorbus melanocarpa var. typica, commonly known as the black mountain ash, is a species of rowan tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that mature in the fall. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin. The plant produces showy clusters of white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive berries.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.
