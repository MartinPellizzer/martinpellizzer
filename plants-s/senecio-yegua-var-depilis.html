Senecio yegua var. depilis

Senecio yegua var. depilis, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

This variety is a hardy perennial that contributes to the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
