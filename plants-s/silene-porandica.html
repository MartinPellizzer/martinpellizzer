Silene porandica

Silene porandica, commonly known as the Armenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene porandica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
