Salix bonplandiana var. toumeyi

Salix bonplandiana var. toumeyi, commonly known as the Tschekanovskii willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typically yellow-green in color and contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
