Senecio doratophyllus

Senecio doratophyllus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and sometimes covered in fine hairs, while the flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Senecio doratophyllus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
