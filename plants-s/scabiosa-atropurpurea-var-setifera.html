Scabiosa atropurpurea var. setifera

Scabiosa atropurpurea var. setifera, commonly known as the wooly scabious, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in dense, white to grayish hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive look.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
