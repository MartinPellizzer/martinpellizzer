Silphium integrifolium var. wrightii

Silphium integrifolium var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's compassplant, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a single, terminal head. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Silphium integrifolium var. wrightii is known for its medicinal properties and historical use by indigenous peoples.
