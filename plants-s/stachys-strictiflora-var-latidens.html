Stachys strictiflora var. latidens

Stachys strictiflora var. latidens, commonly known as broad-leaved hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, glandular hairs. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a broad base and serrated margins, often tinged with purple on the underside. Small, pink to purple flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, typical of the mint family.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
