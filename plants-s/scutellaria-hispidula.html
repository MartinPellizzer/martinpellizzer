Scutellaria hispidula

Scutellaria hispidula, commonly known as hairy skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Scutellaria hispidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
