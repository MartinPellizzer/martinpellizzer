Symphytum × uplandicum f. discolor

Symphytum × uplandicum f. discolor, commonly known as the pink spotted comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with distinctive pink or reddish spots. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its medicinal properties and has been historically used for its healing qualities.
