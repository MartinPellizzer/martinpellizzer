Stephania subpeltata

Stephania subpeltata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stephania, is a climbing shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
