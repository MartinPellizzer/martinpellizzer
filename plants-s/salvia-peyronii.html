Salvia peyronii

Salvia peyronii, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like scent when crushed.

Salvia peyronii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
