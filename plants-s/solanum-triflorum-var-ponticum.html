Solanum triflorum var. ponticum

Solanum triflorum var. ponticum, commonly known as the Pontic nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids.

It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
