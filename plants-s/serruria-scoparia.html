Serruria scoparia

Serruria scoparia, commonly known as the Cape rose or yellow bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are serrated, giving it the genus name "Serruria," and it is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
