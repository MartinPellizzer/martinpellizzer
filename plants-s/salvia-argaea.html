Salvia argaea

Salvia argaea, commonly known as the Mexican beebalm, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

Salvia argaea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
