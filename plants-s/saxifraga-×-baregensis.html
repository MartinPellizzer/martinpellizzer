Saxifraga × baregensis

Saxifraga × baregensis, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats or cushions, adapted to harsh alpine environments. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in cold, dry conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals, and bloom in late summer.

Saxifraga × baregensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky soils.
