Sclerocalyx mexicanum

Sclerocalyx mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican rock fig, is a woody shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round, and edible, often used in local cuisines.

Sclerocalyx mexicanum is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
