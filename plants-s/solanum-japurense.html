Solanum japurense

Solanum japurense, commonly known as the Japanese nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum japurense is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
