Sideritis incana var. serrata

Sideritis incana var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant features silver-gray, hairy foliage and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
