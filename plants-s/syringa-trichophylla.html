Syringa trichophylla

Syringa trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy leaf lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Syringa trichophylla is valued for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
