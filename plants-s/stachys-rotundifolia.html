Stachys rotundifolia

Stachys rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and opposite, round or oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Stachys rotundifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
