Syringa sempervirens

Syringa sempervirens, commonly known as the lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Syringa sempervirens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
