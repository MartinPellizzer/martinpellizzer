Salvia sphacelioides

Salvia sphacelioides, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet or blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
