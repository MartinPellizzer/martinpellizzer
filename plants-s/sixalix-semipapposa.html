Sixalix semipapposa

Sixalix semipapposa, commonly known as the lesser snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species, including birds and small mammals.
