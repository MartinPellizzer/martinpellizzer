Spiraea nervosa var. kiusiana

Spiraea nervosa var. kiusiana, commonly known as the Japanese spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, rounded clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are elliptical, serrated, and have a bluish-green hue with a fuzzy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
