Striga fulgens

Striga fulgens, commonly known as the flameflower, is a striking plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Striga fulgens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
