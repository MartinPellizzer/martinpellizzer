Schisandra hanceana

Schisandra hanceana, commonly known as Chinese five-fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed berries that mature to a bright red color. The plant is valued for its aromatic, resinous fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Schisandra hanceana typically grows in moist, shaded environments within forests.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in early spring.
