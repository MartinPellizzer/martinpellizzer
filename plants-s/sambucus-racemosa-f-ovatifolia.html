Sambucus racemosa f. ovatifolia

Sambucus racemosa f. ovatifolia, commonly known as the American elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves with ovate-shaped leaflets, giving it its specific epithet "ovatifolia.". The plant produces clusters of small, white to greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. These berries are edible when ripe but contain toxic seeds.

It is often found in wetlands, forest edges, and disturbed soils, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
