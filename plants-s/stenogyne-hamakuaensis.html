Stenogyne hamakuaensis

Stenogyne hamakuaensis, commonly known as the Hamakua stenogyne, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It belongs to the genus Stenogyne, which is part of the mint family, Lamiaceae. This species is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats, often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, it is considered endangered and is protected under conservation efforts in Hawaii.
