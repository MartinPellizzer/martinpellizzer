Sorbus thibetica

Sorbus thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
