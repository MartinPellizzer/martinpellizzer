Scutellaria amoena var. cinerea

Scutellaria amoena var. cinerea, commonly known as gray gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in a distinctive grayish-blue powdery coating. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or violet and bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
