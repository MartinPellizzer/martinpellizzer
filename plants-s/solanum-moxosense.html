Solanum moxosense

Solanum moxosense, commonly known as the Moxos nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Paraguay.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and smooth, oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Its fruits are berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
