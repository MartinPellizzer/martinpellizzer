Sanvitalia tenuis

Sanvitalia tenuis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sanvitalia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, perennial shrub that produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Sanvitalia tenuis is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
