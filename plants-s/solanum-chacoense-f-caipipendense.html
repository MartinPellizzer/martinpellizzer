Solanum chacoense f. caipipendense

Solanum chacoense f. caipipendense, commonly known as the Caipipende tomato, is a wild tomato species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The plant is a herbaceous shrub with hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and potential agricultural use in breeding programs.
