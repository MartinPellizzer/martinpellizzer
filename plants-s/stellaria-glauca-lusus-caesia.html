Stellaria glauca lusus caesia

Stellaria glauca lusus caesia, commonly known as the blue starflower, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, star-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, typically with a hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
