Sterculia apetala var. elata

Sterculia apetala var. elata, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are soft and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, pale pink or white flowers with a distinctive, elongated calyx. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
