Sideritis curvidens f. mutica

Sideritis curvidens f. mutica, commonly known as the rough-haired ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
