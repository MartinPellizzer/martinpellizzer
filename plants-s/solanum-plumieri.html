Solanum plumieri

Solanum plumieri, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet flowers with five petals and a distinctive, intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other animals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Solanum plumieri belongs to the nightshade family and contains alkaloids, making it both ecologically significant and potentially toxic.
