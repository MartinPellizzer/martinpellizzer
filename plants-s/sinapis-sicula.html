Sinapis sicula

Sinapis sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and have a distinct mustard-like aroma.

Sinapis sicula is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
