Salsola oxyanthera

Salsola oxyanthera, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial shrub that thrives in saline and sandy soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by succulent, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Salsola oxyanthera is adapted to extreme environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh desert environments.
