Sabicea villosa var. sellowii

Sabicea villosa var. sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's sabicea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, which give it its species name "villosa.". Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces leguminous pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.
