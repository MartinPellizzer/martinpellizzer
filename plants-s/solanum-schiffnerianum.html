Solanum schiffnerianum

Solanum schiffnerianum, commonly known as the Schiffer's nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that mature to a dark blue or black color. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly rough texture, and the plant can grow as an upright shrub or a trailing vine.

Solanum schiffnerianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
