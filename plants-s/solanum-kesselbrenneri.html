Solanum kesselbrenneri

Solanum kesselbrenneri, commonly known as the Kesselbrenner's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Andes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or purple in color. Its fruit is a berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum kesselbrenneri belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
