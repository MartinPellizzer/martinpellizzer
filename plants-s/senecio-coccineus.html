Senecio coccineus

Senecio coccineus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that range in color from orange to red. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Senecio coccineus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to add vibrant color to garden landscapes.
