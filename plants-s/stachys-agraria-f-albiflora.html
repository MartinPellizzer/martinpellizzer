Stachys agraria f. albiflora

Stachys agraria f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
