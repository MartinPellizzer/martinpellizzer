Saccharum hildebrandtii

Saccharum hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, pale yellow flowers. It is often found in wetland or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
