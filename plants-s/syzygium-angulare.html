Syzygium angulare

Syzygium angulare, commonly known as the angular satinash, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. It produces small white flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish fruits resembling small cherries.

Syzygium angulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in home gardens.
