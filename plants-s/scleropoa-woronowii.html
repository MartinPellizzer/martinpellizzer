Scleropoa woronowii

Scleropoa woronowii, commonly known as the Woronow's feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are arranged in loose, open panicles. Scleropoa woronowii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low precipitation and high temperatures.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
