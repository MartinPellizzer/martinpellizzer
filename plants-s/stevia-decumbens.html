Stevia decumbens

Stevia decumbens, commonly known as sweetleaf or sweet herb, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used as alternatives to sugar. Stevia decumbens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal cultivation.

Its use in traditional medicine and as a natural sweetener has gained increasing recognition in modern health practices.
