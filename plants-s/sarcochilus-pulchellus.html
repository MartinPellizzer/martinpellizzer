Sarcochilus pulchellus

Sarcochilus pulchellus, commonly known as the golden helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a robust, fleshy rhizome and produces striking, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive helmet-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forests or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it blooms during the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
