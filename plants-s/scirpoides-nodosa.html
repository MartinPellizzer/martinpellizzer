Scirpoides nodosa

Scirpoides nodosa, commonly known as the nodular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Scirpoides nodosa thrives in saturated soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to the structure and biodiversity of its habitat.
