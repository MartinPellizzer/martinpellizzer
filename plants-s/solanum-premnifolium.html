Solanum premnifolium

Solanum premnifolium, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
