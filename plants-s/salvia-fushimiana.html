Salvia fushimiana

Salvia fushimiana, commonly known as the Japanese sage, is a perennial shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Salvia fushimiana is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
