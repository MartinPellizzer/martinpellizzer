Sciodaphyllum zunacense

Sciodaphyllum zunacense, commonly known as the Zunac cloud forest tree, is a rare and endemic species native to the cloud forests of Peru.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and a distinctive, slender trunk that often grows in dense, humid environments. The plant exhibits a unique adaptation to high-altitude conditions, with thick, waxy leaves that help retain moisture. It plays a crucial role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various endemic species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat fragmentation, Sciodaphyllum zunacense is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and deforestation.
