Syzygium spissifolium

Syzygium spissifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense foliage, with leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits similar to those of the tamarind. Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and ecological purposes.

Syzygium spissifolium thrives in humid climates and is often found in forested areas.
