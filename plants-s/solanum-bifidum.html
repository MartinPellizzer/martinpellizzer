Solanum bifidum

Solanum bifidum, commonly known as the two-lobed nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Solanum bifidum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
