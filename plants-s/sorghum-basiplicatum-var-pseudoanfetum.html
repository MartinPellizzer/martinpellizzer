Sorghum basiplicatum var. pseudoanfetum

Sorghum basiplicatum var. pseudoanfetum, commonly known as the African millet, is a drought-tolerant grass native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, hard grains that are rich in carbohydrates and protein. It thrives in poor soil conditions and is often used as a staple food crop in arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both agricultural and ecological studies.
