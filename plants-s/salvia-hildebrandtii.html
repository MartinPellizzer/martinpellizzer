Salvia hildebrandtii

Salvia hildebrandtii, commonly known as Hildebrandt's sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, narrow leaves, and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and often hairy foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is also valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
