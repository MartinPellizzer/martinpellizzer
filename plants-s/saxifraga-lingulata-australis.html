Saxifraga lingulata australis

Saxifraga lingulata australis, commonly known as the Australian spoon-leaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Australia.

It features succulent, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland heathlands.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
