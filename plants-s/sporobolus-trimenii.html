Sporobolus trimenii

Sporobolus trimenii, commonly known as desert threeawn, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ability to thrive in harsh environments. It is adapted to low water conditions and can tolerate high temperatures and poor soil quality.

Sporobolus trimenii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
