Stephanocoma berckheyoides

Stephanocoma berckheyoides, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Stephanocoma berckheyoides is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.
