Suaeda tschujensis

Suaeda tschujensis, commonly known as the Tschujan saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in saline and dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources in nutrient-poor soils.

Suaeda tschujensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and is often found in coastal and inland saline habitats.
