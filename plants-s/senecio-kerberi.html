Senecio kerberi

Senecio kerberi, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Senecio kerberi thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
