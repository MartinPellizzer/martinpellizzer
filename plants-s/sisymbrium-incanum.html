Sisymbrium incanum

Sisymbrium incanum, commonly known as hoary salsify, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a rough texture and a distinctive gray-green color.

Sisymbrium incanum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
