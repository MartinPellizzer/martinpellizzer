Silene andryalifolia

Silene andryalifolia, commonly known as the Andes bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy coating.

Silene andryalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
