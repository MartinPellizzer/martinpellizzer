Simira cordifolia

Simira cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved simira, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland areas. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces oval, woody fruits containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
