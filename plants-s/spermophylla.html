Spermophylla

Spermophylla, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators. Spermophylla is adapted to survive in extreme conditions with minimal water, showcasing remarkable drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and botanical studies.
