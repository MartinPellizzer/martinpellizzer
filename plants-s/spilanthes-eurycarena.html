Spilanthes eurycarena

Spilanthes eurycarena, commonly known as the broadleaf Indian mustard, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

Spilanthes eurycarena is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
