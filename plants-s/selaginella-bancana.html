Selaginella bancana

Selaginella bancana, commonly known as the banana spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features delicate, spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy cuticle. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are borne on specialized shoots and contain spores.

Selaginella bancana is characterized by its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique morphological adaptations for water retention.
