Succisa tardans

Succisa tardans, commonly known as the late-flowering bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowering stems are upright and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Succisa tardans is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.
