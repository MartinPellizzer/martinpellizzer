Styrax psilophyllus

Styrax psilophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types contributes to its presence in diverse habitats.
