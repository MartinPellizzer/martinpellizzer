Stereospermum crenulatum

Stereospermum crenulatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red maple, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its timber and has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
