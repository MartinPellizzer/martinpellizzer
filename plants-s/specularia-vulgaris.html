Specularia vulgaris

Specularia vulgaris, commonly known as common catchfly, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with four petals that are adorned with distinctive, silky, hair-like structures. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Specularia vulgaris thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
