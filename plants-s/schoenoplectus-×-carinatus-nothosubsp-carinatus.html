Schoenoplectus × carinatus nothosubsp. carinatus

Schoenoplectus × carinatus nothosubsp.

carinatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a hybrid plant native to wetland environments in North America. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and ecological importance in stabilizing wetland habitats.
