Satureja albanica

Satureja albanica, commonly known as Albanian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cooking for their pungent, thyme-like scent.
