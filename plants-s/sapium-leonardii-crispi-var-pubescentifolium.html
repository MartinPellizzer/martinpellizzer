Sapium leonardii-crispi var. pubescentifolium

Sapium leonardii-crispi var. pubescentifolium, commonly known as the woolly-leafed cupuaçu, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its pubescent, or hairy, leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are similar to those of the cupuaçu tree. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
