Saxifraga × thomasiana

Saxifraga × thomasiana, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.

Saxifraga × thomasiana is also known for its ability to tolerate cold climates and is sometimes used in rock gardens.
