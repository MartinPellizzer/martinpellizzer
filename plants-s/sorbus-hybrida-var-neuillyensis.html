Sorbus hybrida var. neuillyensis

Sorbus hybrida var. neuillyensis, commonly known as the Neuilly whitebeam, is a hybrid species of rowan or whitebeam native to France.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters and its persistent, red or orange berries that provide food for birds. The leaves are typically oval with a serrated edge and turn yellow in autumn. This plant is often found in woodland edges and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

It has moderate growth and is relatively hardy, making it suitable for garden cultivation.
