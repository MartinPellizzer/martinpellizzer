Salix formosa

Salix formosa, commonly known as the willow-herb, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, riverbanks, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant features slender, often zigzagging stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, which are typical of the Salicaceae family.

Salix formosa plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
