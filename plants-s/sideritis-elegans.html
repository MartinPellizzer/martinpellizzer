Sideritis elegans

Sideritis elegans, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and thyme. Sideritis elegans is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
