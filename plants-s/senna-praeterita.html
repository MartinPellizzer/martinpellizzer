Senna praeterita

Senna praeterita, commonly known as the golden senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Senna praeterita is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.
