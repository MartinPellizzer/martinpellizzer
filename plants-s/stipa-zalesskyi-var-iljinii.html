Stipa zalesskyi var. iljinii

Stipa zalesskyi var. iljinii, commonly known as the Iljin's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and tufted inflorescences that resemble delicate plumes. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest in gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
