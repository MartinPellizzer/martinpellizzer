Salpichroa colubrina

Salpichroa colubrina, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salpichroa colubrina is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
