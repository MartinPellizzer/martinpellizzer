Stachys jaimehintonii

Stachys jaimehintonii, commonly known as Jaime Hinton's hedgehog.

It is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China, characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, spiky foliage. The species produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and often hairy foliage.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
