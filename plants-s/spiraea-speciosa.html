Spiraea speciosa

Spiraea speciosa, commonly known as the showy spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Spiraea speciosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
