Sagina brachysepala

Sagina brachysepala, commonly known as the narrow-fruit snowflake, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

Sagina brachysepala thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
