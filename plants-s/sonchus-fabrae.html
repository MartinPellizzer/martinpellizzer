Sonchus fabrae

Sonchus fabrae, commonly known as the Italian dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
