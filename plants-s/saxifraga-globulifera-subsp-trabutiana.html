Saxifraga globulifera subsp. trabutiana

Saxifraga globulifera subsp.

trabutiana, commonly known as the Trabut's globular saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, rounded, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

The plant blooms with clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that are typically arranged in a globular or spherical shape, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts.
