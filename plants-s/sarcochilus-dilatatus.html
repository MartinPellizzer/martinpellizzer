Sarcochilus dilatatus

Sarcochilus dilatatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed helleborine, is an epiphytic orchid native to Australia.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense, white to pale green velvety coating. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with yellow petals and a deep purple lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
