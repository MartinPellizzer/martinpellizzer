Saxifraga hochstetteriana

Saxifraga hochstetteriana, commonly known as Hochstetter's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Saxifraga hochstetteriana is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in the unique ecosystems of New Zealand's native flora.
