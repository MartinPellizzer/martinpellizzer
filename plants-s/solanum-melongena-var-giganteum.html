Solanum melongena var. giganteum

Solanum melongena var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species characterized by its large, elongated fruits.

This plant is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to several meters in height, making it a striking addition to gardens. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are typically white or pale purple. The fruit, which can weigh several kilograms, matures to a deep purple color with a smooth, glossy surface.

It is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible, flavorful fruit.
