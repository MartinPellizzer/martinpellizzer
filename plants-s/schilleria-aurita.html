Schilleria aurita

Schilleria aurita, commonly known as the golden starbursts, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that resemble small stars. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its inflorescences consist of clusters of tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

Schilleria aurita is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
