Salix pentandra f. bracteata

Salix pentandra f. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate willow, is a variant of the common willow species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and catkin flowers, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
