Symplocos banaensis

Symplocos banaensis, commonly known as the Bana hill tea, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
