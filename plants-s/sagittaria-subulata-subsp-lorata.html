Sagittaria subulata subsp. lorata

Sagittaria subulata subsp.

lorata, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small, white flowers that bloom in spikes.

The plant thrives in shallow water environments such as ponds, marshes, and wetlands. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize new areas effectively.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
