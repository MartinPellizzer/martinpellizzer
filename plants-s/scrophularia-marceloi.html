Scrophularia marceloi

Scrophularia marceloi, commonly known as the Marcel's figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are tubular and often pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in horticulture.
