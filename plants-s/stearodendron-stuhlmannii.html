Stearodendron stuhlmannii

Stearodendron stuhlmannii, commonly known as the yellow sterculia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.

Stearodendron stuhlmannii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
