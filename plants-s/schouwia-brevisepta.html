Schouwia brevisepta

Schouwia brevisepta, commonly known as the narrow-septate schouwia, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming structure and vibrant, colorful inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention.

Schouwia brevisepta is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
