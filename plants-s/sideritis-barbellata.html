Sideritis barbellata

Sideritis barbellata, commonly known as the hairy sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pink or violet, clustered in whorls along the stems.

Sideritis barbellata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties.
