Sebastiania hispida var. tomentosa

Sebastiania hispida var. tomentosa, commonly known as the velvety sebastiania, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
