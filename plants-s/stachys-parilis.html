Stachys parilis

Stachys parilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys parilis is often found in grassy areas and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.
