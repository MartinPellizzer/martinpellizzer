Satureja graeca subsp. macrotricha

Satureja graeca subsp.

macrotricha, commonly known as Greek mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are fragrant, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.
