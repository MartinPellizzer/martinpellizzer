Saxifraga hederacea

Saxifraga hederacea, commonly known as ivy saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers in early summer, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Saxifraga hederacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
