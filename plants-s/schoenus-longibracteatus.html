Schoenus longibracteatus

Schoenus longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and linear, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets with elongated bracts, giving it its common name.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.
