Scabiosa fussiana

Scabiosa fussiana, commonly known as the Italian sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It is a perennial species that prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Scabiosa fussiana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
