Scutellaria angulosa

Scutellaria angulosa, commonly known as narrow-leaf skullcap, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria angulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
