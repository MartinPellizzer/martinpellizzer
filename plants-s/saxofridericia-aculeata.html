Saxofridericia aculeata

Saxofridericia aculeata, commonly known as the roughleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Saxofridericia aculeata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
