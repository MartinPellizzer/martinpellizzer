Spatholobus gengmaensis

Spatholobus gengmaensis, commonly known as the Gengma tamarind tree, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding visual interest to its natural habitat.
