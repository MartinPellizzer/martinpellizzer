Solanum sulphureum

Solanum sulphureum, commonly known as the golden nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and toxic alkaloids present in its leaves and berries. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky habitats, thriving in arid to semi-arid conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
