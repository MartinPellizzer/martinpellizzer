Styrax paralleloneurus

Styrax paralleloneurus, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The botanical features of Styrax paralleloneurus make it a distinctive member of the Styracaceae family.
