Syzygium folidorhachis

Syzygium folidorhachis, commonly known as the white sand apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in tropical regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
