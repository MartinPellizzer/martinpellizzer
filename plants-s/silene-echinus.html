Silene echinus

Silene echinus, commonly known as the prickly evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped seed pods and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and upright.

Silene echinus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
