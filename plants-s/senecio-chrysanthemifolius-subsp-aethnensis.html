Senecio chrysanthemifolius subsp. aethnensis

Senecio chrysanthemifolius subsp.

aethnensis, commonly known as the Aetna chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Sicily. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, high-altitude environment of Mount Etna.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
