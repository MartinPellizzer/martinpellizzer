Sarcopteryx martyana

Sarcopteryx martyana, commonly known as the Martyan's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure and striking, translucent petals. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them, relying instead on air, rain, and debris for sustenance. Sarcopteryx martyana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is highly sensitive to environmental changes, making it a species of conservation concern.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator of ecosystem health and its potential for scientific research into plant adaptation strategies.
