Scrophularia takesimensis

Scrophularia takesimensis, commonly known as the Siberian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia takesimensis is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
