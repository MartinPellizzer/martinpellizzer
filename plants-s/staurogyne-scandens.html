Staurogyne scandens

Staurogyne scandens, commonly known as the creeping water hyssop, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and typically have a violet or purple color.

Staurogyne scandens is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
