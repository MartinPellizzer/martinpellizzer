Sorbus latifolia var. ambigens

Sorbus latifolia var. ambigens, commonly known as the broadleaf whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
