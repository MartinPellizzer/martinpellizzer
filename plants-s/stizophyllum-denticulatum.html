Stizophyllum denticulatum

Stizophyllum denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed stizophyllum, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its intricate, branching structure.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant features finely serrated edges on its leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures are often concealed within specialized thalloid formations.

Stizophyllum denticulatum plays a minor but ecologically significant role in its habitat by providing microhabitats for aquatic invertebrates.
