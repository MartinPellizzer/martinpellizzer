Satureja brauneana

Satureja brauneana, commonly known as Brazilian thyme, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and produces small, pink or violet flowers. Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Satureja brauneana is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol.
