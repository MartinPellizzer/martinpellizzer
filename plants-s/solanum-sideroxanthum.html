Solanum sideroxanthum

Solanum sideroxanthum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. It has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape.

The flowers are typically white or pale green, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen.
