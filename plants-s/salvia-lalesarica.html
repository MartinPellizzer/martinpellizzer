Salvia lalesarica

Salvia lalesarica, commonly known as the Balkan sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like scent.

Salvia lalesarica is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
