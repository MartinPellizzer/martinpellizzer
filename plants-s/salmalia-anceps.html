Salmalia anceps

Salmalia anceps, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its fruits are woody pods containing flat, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Salmalia anceps is also valued for its fast growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
