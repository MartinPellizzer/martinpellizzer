Spermacoce squarrosa

Spermacoce squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy false mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or herbaceous plant characterized by its erect stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and are followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The plant has a cosmopolitan distribution and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
