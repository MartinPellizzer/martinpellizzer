Senecio sericeus var. candolleanus

Senecio sericeus var. candolleanus, commonly known as the silverleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
