Saxifraga ferruginea f. vreelandii

Saxifraga ferruginea f. vreelandii, commonly known as the rusty saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves that are often tinged with rust-colored markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
