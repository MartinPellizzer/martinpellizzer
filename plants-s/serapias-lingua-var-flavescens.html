Serapias lingua var. flavescens

Serapias lingua var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-tongued orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that resemble a tongue. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive, elongated lip that gives it its common name.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
