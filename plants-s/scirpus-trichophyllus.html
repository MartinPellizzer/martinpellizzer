Scirpus trichophyllus

Scirpus trichophyllus, commonly known as the hairy bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers at the top of its stems, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams.

Scirpus trichophyllus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
