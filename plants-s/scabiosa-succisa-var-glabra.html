Scabiosa succisa var. glabra

Scabiosa succisa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower meadows.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
