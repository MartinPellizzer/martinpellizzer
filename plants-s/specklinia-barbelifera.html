Specklinia barbelifera

Specklinia barbelifera, commonly known as the feather-rooted staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety fronds that resemble a staghorn, giving it its common name. The plant has a unique root system that forms a thick, cushion-like structure to anchor it to trees or other surfaces. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, with a leathery texture and a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Specklinia barbelifera thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
