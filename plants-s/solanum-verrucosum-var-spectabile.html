Solanum verrucosum var. spectabile

Solanum verrucosum var. spectabile, commonly known as the showy nightshade, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and lobed, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange, are toxic and should not be consumed.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant floral display.
