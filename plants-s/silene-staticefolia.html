Silene staticefolia

Silene staticefolia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that help it withstand salty sea air and strong winds. Silene staticefolia is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in coastal landscapes.
