Syringa tomentella subsp. yunnanensis

Syringa tomentella subsp.

yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan lilac, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China. It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and clusters of fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves with a velvety texture and a compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in colder regions.
