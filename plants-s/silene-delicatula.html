Silene delicatula

Silene delicatula, commonly known as the slender campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene delicatula produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread in suitable habitats.
