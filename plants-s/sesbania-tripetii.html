Sesbania tripetii

Sesbania tripetii, commonly known as the Indian velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. It thrives in warm climates and is often used in agricultural systems for soil enrichment.

Sesbania tripetii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
