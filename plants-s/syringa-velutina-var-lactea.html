Syringa velutina var. lactea

Syringa velutina var. lactea, commonly known as the white lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety hair. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
