Syagrus × serroana

Syagrus × serroana, commonly known as the golden palm, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Syagrus romanzoffiana and Syagrus oleracea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant is native to Brazil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Syagrus × serroana is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal areas.
