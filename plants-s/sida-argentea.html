Sida argentea

Sida argentea, commonly known as silverleaf sida, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features alternate, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Sida argentea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
