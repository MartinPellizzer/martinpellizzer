Scirpus supinus var. saximontanus

Scirpus supinus var. saximontanus, commonly known as the mountain sedge, is a perennial plant native to alpine regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils at high elevations. It is adapted to cold climates and has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
