Syringa pubescens subsp. microphylla

Syringa pubescens subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It features small, dark green leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.

This subspecies is often used in gardens for its compact size and elegant floral display.
