Stipa nicorae

Stipa nicorae, commonly known as the Nicora needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Stipa nicorae thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
