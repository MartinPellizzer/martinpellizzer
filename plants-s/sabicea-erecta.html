Sabicea erecta

Sabicea erecta, commonly known as the upright sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often bean-like pods. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with compound leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the family.

Sabicea erecta is often found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in partial shade.
