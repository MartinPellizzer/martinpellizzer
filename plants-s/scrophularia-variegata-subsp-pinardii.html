Scrophularia variegata subsp. pinardii

Scrophularia variegata subsp.

pinardii, commonly known as the Pinard's figwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive silver or grayish-green coloration. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
