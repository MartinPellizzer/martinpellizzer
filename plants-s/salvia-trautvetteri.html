Salvia trautvetteri

Salvia trautvetteri, commonly known as Trautvetter's sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salvia trautvetteri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
