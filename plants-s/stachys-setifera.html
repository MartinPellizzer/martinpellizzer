Stachys setifera

Stachys setifera, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Stachys setifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
