Saxifraga voroschilovii

Saxifraga voroschilovii, commonly known as the Voroschilov's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is a perennial species that produces seeds through self-fertilization, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
