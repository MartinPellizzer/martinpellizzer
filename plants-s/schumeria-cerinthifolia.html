Schumeria cerinthifolia

Schumeria cerinthifolia, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has strap-like, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Schumeria cerinthifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
