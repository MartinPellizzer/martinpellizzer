Sasa mukogunensis

Sasa mukogunensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streamside areas. It forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various wildlife.

Sasa mukogunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in basket weaving and thatching.
