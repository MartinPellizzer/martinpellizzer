Sorbus cashmiriana f. typica

Sorbus cashmiriana f. typica, commonly known as the Kashmir whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, orange-red berries in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
