Solanum cyaneopurpureum

Solanum cyaneopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and bilaterally symmetrical, featuring five petals that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature.
