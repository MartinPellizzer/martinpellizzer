Salacia laevigata subsp. obtusa

Salacia laevigata subsp.

obtusa, commonly known as the smooth salacia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salicaceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lanceolate leaves with a prominent midrib. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in catkin-like inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is often valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
