Scolymocephalus coccineus

Scolymocephalus coccineus, commonly known as the red-headed tansy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scolymocephalus coccineus is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its vibrant blooms and historical medicinal uses.
