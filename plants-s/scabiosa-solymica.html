Scabiosa solymica

Scabiosa solymica, commonly known as the Carpathian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers arranged in a flat-topped infloreshcence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scabiosa solymica is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun and well-drained conditions.
