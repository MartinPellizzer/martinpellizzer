Schizococcus

Schizococcus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant features a globular stem covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Schizococcus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
