Senecio petrocallis

Senecio petrocallis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio petrocallis is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
