Silene batangensis

Silene batangensis, commonly known as the Batang catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene batangensis is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
