Senecio flammeus var. glabrifolius

Senecio flammeus var. glabrifolius, commonly known as the Flameweed or Red-hot Poker, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking red-orange bracts that surround its yellow flowers, creating a fiery appearance. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are typically hairless, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
