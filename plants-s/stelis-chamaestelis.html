Stelis chamaestelis

Stelis chamaestelis, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall low-growing habit.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its specialized mycorrhizal relationships for nutrient uptake.
