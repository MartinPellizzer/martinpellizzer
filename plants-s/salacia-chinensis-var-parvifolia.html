Salacia chinensis var. parvifolia

Salacia chinensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
