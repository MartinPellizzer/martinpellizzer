Solidago hirtipes

Solidago hirtipes, commonly known as the hairy goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

Solidago hirtipes plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native environment.
