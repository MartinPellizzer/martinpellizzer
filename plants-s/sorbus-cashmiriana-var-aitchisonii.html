Sorbus cashmiriana var. aitchisonii

Sorbus cashmiriana var. aitchisonii, commonly known as the Kashmir snowberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are a striking feature of the plant. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and they turn a vibrant yellow or orange in autumn. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of garden conditions.
