Symphyotrichum lanceolatum subsp. hesperium

Symphyotrichum lanceolatum subsp.

hesperium, commonly known as the western coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, yellow to orange floral heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to other Symphyotrichum species.
