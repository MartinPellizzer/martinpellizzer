Syzygium tenuilimbum

Syzygium tenuilimbum, commonly known as the slender-leafed tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain edible seeds.

Syzygium tenuilimbum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
