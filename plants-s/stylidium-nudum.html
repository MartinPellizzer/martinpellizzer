Stylidium nudum

Stylidium nudum, commonly known as the naked spurred orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred petals and a distinctive, elongated flower structure. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a short, narrow spur at the base of the petal.

Stylidium nudum is a short-lived perennial, often flowering in spring and early summer.
