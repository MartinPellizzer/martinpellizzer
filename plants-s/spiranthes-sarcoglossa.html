Spiranthes sarcoglossa

Spiranthes sarcoglossa, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiral arrangement of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are highly fragrant.

This orchid is an important part of the ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and moths.
