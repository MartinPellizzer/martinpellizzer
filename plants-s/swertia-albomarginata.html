Swertia albomarginata

Swertia albomarginata, commonly known as white-margin gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a white or yellow marginal stripe.

Swertia albomarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
