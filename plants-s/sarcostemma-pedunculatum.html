Sarcostemma pedunculatum

Sarcostemma pedunculatum, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to parts of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Sarcostemma pedunculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
