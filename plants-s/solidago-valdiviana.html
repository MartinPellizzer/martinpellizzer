Solidago valdiviana

Solidago valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate forests and is adapted to the cool, humid climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Solidago valdiviana plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
