Salix vulpina var. nikkoensis

Salix vulpina var. nikkoensis, commonly known as the Nikko willow, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in shaded woodland areas.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
