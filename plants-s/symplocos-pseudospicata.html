Symplocos pseudospicata

Symplocos pseudospicata, commonly known as the false spiky woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species in understory habitats of moist, acidic soils.
