Soldanella alpina var. typica

Soldanella alpina var. typica, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a small perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cold, moist conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

It is often found in mountain meadows and is a characteristic species of alpine flora.
