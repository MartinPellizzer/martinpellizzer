Schizostachyum arunachalense

Schizostachyum arunachalense, commonly known as the Arunachal bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, fibrous stalks that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

This species is valued for its durable bamboo-like material, which is used in local construction and handicrafts.
