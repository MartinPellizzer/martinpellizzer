Staurogyne kingiana

Staurogyne kingiana, commonly known as the redstem water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in South America.

It has slender, branching stems that are typically reddish in color, and its leaves are small, opposite, and whorled, often with a dark green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, and it thrives in still or slow-moving waters.
