Salix wuana

Salix wuana, commonly known as Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

Its rapid growth and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping.
