Sphaerorrhiza burchellii

Sphaerorrhiza burchellii, commonly known as the African bubble bladdernut, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring woody stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that resemble bladders. These fruits contain seeds enclosed in a tough, leathery coat, which is adapted for dispersal by animals.
