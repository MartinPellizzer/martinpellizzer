Stipa narynica

Stipa narynica, commonly known as the Narynica grass, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Stipa narynica typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
