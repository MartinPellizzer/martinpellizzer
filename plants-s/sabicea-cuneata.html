Sabicea cuneata

Sabicea cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its bark is often smooth and may have a reddish or brownish hue, while its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound.

Sabicea cuneata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
