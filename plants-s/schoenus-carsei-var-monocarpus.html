Schoenus carsei var. monocarpus

Schoenus carsei var. monocarpus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical structure and habitat preferences make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
