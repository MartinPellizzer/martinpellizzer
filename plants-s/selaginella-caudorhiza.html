Selaginella caudorhiza

Selaginella caudorhiza, commonly known as the forked root spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small, evergreen fern ally that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its creeping stems and clustered, needle-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and contain spores, which are essential for its propagation.

Selaginella caudorhiza exhibits remarkable adaptability, allowing it to survive in a variety of ecological conditions.
