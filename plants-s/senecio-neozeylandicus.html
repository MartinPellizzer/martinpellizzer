Senecio neozeylandicus

Senecio neozeylandicus, commonly known as the New Zealand groundsel, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
