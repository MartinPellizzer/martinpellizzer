Scopularia secunda

Scopularia secunda, commonly known as the lesser bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Scopularia secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
