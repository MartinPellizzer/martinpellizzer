Swartzia tomentosa var. cardonae

Swartzia tomentosa var. cardonae, commonly known as the woolly swartzia, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, white pubescent foliage and elongated, woody pods that contain numerous seeds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its bark is thick and corky, providing some resistance to pests and diseases.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
