Sabicea pumila

Sabicea pumila, commonly known as the dwarf sabicea, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, which includes many plants with nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, pinnate leaves and produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, pod-like structure containing several seeds.

Sabicea pumila is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
