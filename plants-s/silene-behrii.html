Silene behrii

Silene behrii, commonly known as the Behri's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene behrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
