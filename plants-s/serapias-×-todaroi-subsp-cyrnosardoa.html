Serapias × todaroi subsp. cyrnosardoa

Serapias × todaroi subsp.

cyrnosardoa, commonly known as the Balkan marsh orchid, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy habitats, thriving in moist, calcareous soils. Its flowers are composed of three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being more prominent and spurred.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
