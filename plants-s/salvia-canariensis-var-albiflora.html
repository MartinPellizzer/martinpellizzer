Salvia canariensis var. albiflora

Salvia canariensis var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflowered sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces clusters of white to pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
