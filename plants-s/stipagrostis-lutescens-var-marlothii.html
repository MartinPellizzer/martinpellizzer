Stipagrostis lutescens var. marlothii

Stipagrostis lutescens var. marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's golden grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically golden in color. This plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats. Its growth habit is low and spreading, with a dense root system that helps stabilize soil.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
