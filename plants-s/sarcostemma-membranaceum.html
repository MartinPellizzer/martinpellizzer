Sarcostemma membranaceum

Sarcostemma membranaceum, commonly known as the membranous sarcostem, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thin, membranous leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
