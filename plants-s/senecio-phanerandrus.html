Senecio phanerandrus

Senecio phanerandrus, commonly known as the Cape golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

Senecio phanerandrus is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun and requires minimal water once established.
