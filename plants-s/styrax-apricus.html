Styrax apricus

Styrax apricus, commonly known as the Japanese styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a fragrant, resinous sap that has been historically used in perfumery and traditional medicine. Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
