Solanum versicolor

Solanum versicolor, commonly known as the variable nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its strikingly variable flower colors, which can range from white to blue, purple, and red. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solanum versicolor is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
