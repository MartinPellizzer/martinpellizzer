Solanum bahianum

Solanum bahianum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. Its fruits are typically yellow or orange berries that mature in late summer.

Solanum bahianum is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
