Scrophularia sambucifolia subvar. viridiflora

Scrophularia sambucifolia subvar. viridiflora, commonly known as the greenflower scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and a distinctive, glandular surface. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with tubular corollas that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting a range of insect life.
