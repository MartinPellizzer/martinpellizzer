Senecio semiamplexicaulis

Senecio semiamplexicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branched stem covered in dense, woolly hairs, and its leaves are narrow, fleshy, and often curled. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and compact form, it is relatively easy to care for in a controlled environment.
