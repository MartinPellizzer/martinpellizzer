Setaria gracilis

Setaria gracilis, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in prairies and open woodlands.

Setaria gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
