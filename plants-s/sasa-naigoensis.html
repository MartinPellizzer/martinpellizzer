Sasa naigoensis

Sasa naigoensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Sasa naigoensis thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
