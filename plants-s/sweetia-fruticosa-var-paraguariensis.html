Sweetia fruticosa var. paraguariensis

Sweetia fruticosa var. paraguariensis, commonly known as Paraguayan sweetia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are also used in local herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.
