Sibbaldia procumbens var. valdehirta, commonly known as the creeping sibbaldia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to cold and nutrient-poor environments. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, terminal inflorescences.
