Stachys venulosa

Stachys venulosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Stachys venulosa is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
