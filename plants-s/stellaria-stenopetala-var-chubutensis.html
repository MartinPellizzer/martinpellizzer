Stellaria stenopetala var. chubutensis

Stellaria stenopetala var. chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut starwort, is a perennial herb native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped white flowers and thin, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often covered with fine pubescence.

This variety is notable for its adaptations to harsh climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
