Spergularia purpurea var. longipes

Spergularia purpurea var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed sandspurry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland dunes. Its leaves are linear and alternate, and the fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds.

It is a short-lived perennial, often found in disturbed or open habitats.
