Sphaerostigma deserti

Sphaerostigma deserti, commonly known as the desert globular cactus, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss.

Sphaerostigma deserti is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
