Synaphlebium davallioides

Synaphlebium davallioides, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily infests coniferous trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and fleshy, berry-like fruits. The plant produces a sticky substance called viscin, which helps it adhere to its host and facilitates seed dispersal. It is native to parts of North America and is often found in forested areas with suitable host trees.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the growth and health of its host plants.
