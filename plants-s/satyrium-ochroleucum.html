Satyrium ochroleucum

Satyrium ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-tipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white petals with a subtle yellow tip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or among leaf litter. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive labellum that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Satyrium ochroleucum is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
