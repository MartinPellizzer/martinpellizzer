Sclerocarpus africanus

Sclerocarpus africanus, commonly known as the African sandbur, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, ball-like fruits that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
