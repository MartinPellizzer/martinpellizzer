Serratula arvensis

Serratula arvensis, commonly known as field sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a tall, branching stem that produces yellow flowers. Native to Europe, it is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. The plant contains sesquiterpene lactones, which can be toxic to livestock.

It is considered a weed in many regions due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
