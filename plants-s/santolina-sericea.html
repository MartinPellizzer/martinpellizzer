Santolina sericea

Santolina sericea, commonly known as silver santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, aromatic foliage that is covered in fine, silver-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina sericea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
