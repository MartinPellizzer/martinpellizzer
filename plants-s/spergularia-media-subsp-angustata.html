Spergularia media subsp. angustata

Spergularia media subsp.

angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to sandy and coastal environments. It is a perennial herb with small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant has thin, succulent stems and narrow, grass-like leaves adapted to arid conditions. Its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds via wind, aiding in its colonization of open, disturbed habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in maritime regions.
