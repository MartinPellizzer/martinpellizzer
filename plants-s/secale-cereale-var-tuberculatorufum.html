Secale cereale var. tuberculatorufum

Secale cereale var. tuberculatorufum, commonly known as the red-spored rye, is a variant of rye characterized by its distinctive reddish spores.

It is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its inflorescence bears spikelets that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name.

The variety is notable for its unique spore coloration, which distinguishes it from other rye varieties.
