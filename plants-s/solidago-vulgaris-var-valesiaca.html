Solidago vulgaris var. valesiaca

Solidago vulgaris var. valesiaca, commonly known as the Valesiaca goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
