Sabicea elliptica

Sabicea elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, round berries that are edible for some bird species.

Sabicea elliptica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to semi-shaded conditions.
