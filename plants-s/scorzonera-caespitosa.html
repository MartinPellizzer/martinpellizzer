Scorzonera caespitosa

Scorzonera caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent black markings, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Scorzonera caespitosa is often found in rocky soils and alpine environments, where it thrives in full sun.

Its root system is fibrous and may have medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
