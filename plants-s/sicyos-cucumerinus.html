Sicyos cucumerinus

Sicyos cucumerinus, commonly known as the bottle gourd or calabash, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant produces large, hollow, cylindrical fruits that can grow up to 3 meters in length. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The plant is cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary and traditional applications.
