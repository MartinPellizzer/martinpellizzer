Scilla violacea

Scilla violacea, commonly known as violet scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of striking violet-blue flowers in early spring, which are typically bell-shaped and arranged in a umbel-like structure. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Scilla violacea is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable conditions.
