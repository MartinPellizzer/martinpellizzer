Swida meyeri

Swida meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's swida, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with small, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Swida meyeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and adaptability to harsh climates.
