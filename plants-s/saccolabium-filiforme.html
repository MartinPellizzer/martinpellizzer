Saccolabium filiforme

Saccolabium filiforme, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae), which is known for its milky sap and specialized floral structures. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and ecosystems.
