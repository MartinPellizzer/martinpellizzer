Salvia verbenaca var. vernalis

Salvia verbenaca var. vernalis, commonly known as spring sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
