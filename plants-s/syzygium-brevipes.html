Syzygium brevipes

Syzygium brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf apple or small-fruited rambutan, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy, red or yellow berries that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Syzygium brevipes is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its edible fruit, which is sometimes used in local cuisines.
