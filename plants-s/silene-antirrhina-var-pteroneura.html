Silene antirrhina var. pteroneura

Silene antirrhina var. pteroneura, commonly known as the winged catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

The variety pteroneura is distinguished by its uniquely winged seed pods, which are a notable botanical feature.
