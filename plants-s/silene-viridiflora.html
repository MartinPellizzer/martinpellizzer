Silene viridiflora

Silene viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

Silene viridiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
