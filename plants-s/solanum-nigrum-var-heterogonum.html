Solanum nigrum var. heterogonum

Solanum nigrum var. heterogonum, commonly known as the black nightshade variant, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that turn from green to black as they mature. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions across Europe and Asia. It has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually yellow with five petals.

This variant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
