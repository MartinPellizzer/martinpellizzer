Spiraea expansa nivea

Spiraea expansa nivea, commonly known as the white meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early to mid-spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
