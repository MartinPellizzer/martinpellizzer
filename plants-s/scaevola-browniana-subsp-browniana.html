Scaevola browniana subsp. browniana

Scaevola browniana subsp.

browniana, commonly known as the coastal scabious, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, showy blue to violet flowers.

The plant has silver-gray foliage with a woolly texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops.

Its adaptability to saline conditions makes it an important component of coastal vegetation.
