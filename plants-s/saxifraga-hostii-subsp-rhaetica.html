Saxifraga hostii subsp. rhaetica

Saxifraga hostii subsp.

rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

The plant features basal rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and clustered in terminal inflorescences.

It is a hardy species that thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountain meadows and scree slopes.
