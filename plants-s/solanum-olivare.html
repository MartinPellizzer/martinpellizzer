Solanum olivare

Solanum olivare, commonly known as the oliveleaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a spherical berry that ripens to a yellow or orange color, containing several seeds.

Solanum olivare is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas, and it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
