Sideritis ajpetriana

Sideritis ajpetriana, commonly known as the Ajpetriana rock rose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and they bloom in summer.

Sideritis ajpetriana is valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in herbal remedies.
