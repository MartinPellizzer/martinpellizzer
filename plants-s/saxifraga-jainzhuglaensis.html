Saxifraga jainzhuglaensis

Saxifraga jainzhuglaensis, commonly known as the Tibetan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, often white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its ecological niche.
