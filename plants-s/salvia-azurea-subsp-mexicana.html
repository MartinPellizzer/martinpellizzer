Salvia azurea subsp. mexicana

Salvia azurea subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to central Mexico. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in whorled clusters along the stems.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractiveness to pollinators like bees and butterflies.
