Sorghum saccharatum

Sorghum saccharatum, commonly known as sweet sorghum, is a tall, warm-season grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust stalks and large, feathery inflorescences that produce both grain and syrup. The plant is cultivated for its high biomass yield, making it a valuable resource for biofuel and livestock feed. Its leaves are broad and serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal in some regions.

Additionally, sweet sorghum is drought-tolerant and thrives in a variety of soil types, enhancing its adaptability to different climates.
