Spadactis radiciflora

Spadactis radiciflora, commonly known as the root-flower orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate floral structure, with a spadix-like inflorescence that gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often arranged in a dense cluster, creating a visually appealing display. Its roots are not only essential for anchoring the plant but also play a role in nutrient absorption from the surrounding soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Spadactis radiciflora is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
