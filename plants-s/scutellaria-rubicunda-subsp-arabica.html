Scutellaria rubicunda subsp. arabica

Scutellaria rubicunda subsp.

arabica, commonly known as the Arabian skullcap, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching. Scutellaria rubicunda subsp.

arabica is often found in open habitats such as steppes and coastal areas.
