Saxifraga granatensis

Saxifraga granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. It is a perennial species that is well adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh mountain conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
