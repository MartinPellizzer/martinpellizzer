Sapium bingyricum

Sapium bingyricum, commonly known as Chinese soap tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Sapium bingyricum thrives in well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
