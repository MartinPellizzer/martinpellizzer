Syzygium slootenii

Syzygium slootenii, commonly known as the yellow-berried tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, yellow berries that turn red when ripe, containing edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
