Senecio obovatus var. elliottii

Senecio obovatus var. elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's obovate ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and canyon walls. The plant features lanceolate to obovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters.

It is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to arid environments.
