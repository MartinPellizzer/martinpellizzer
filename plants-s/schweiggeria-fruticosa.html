Schweiggeria fruticosa

Schweiggeria fruticosa, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with silver-gray foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and full sun exposure. Its botanical features include small, hairy leaves and showy composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
