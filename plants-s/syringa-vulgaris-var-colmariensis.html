Syringa vulgaris var. colmariensis

Syringa vulgaris var. colmariensis, commonly known as the Colmar lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
