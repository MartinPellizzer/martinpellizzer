Solanum maingayi

Solanum maingayi, commonly known as the Maingay's nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically used in traditional medicine or agriculture.
