Smilax nigrescens

Smilax nigrescens, commonly known as blackberry vine, is a perennial climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, recurved spines and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its edible, dark purple to black berries. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Smilax nigrescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
