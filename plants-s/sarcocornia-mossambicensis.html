Sarcocornia mossambicensis

Sarcocornia mossambicensis, commonly known as the mangrove spinifex, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant thrives in coastal areas, including salt marshes and estuaries, where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its roots form dense mats that help filter out excess salt from the environment.

Sarcocornia mossambicensis is also an important component of the food web in tidal ecosystems.
