Syntherisma sanguinalis

Syntherisma sanguinalis, commonly known as the bloodroot plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Syntherisma sanguinalis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
