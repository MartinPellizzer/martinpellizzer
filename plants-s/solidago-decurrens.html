Solidago decurrens

Solidago decurrens, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy clusters of bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago decurrens thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
