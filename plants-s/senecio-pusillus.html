Senecio pusillus

Senecio pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf groundsel, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, featuring delicate, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Senecio pusillus thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
