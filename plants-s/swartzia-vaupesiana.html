Swartzia vaupesiana

Swartzia vaupesiana, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Swartzia vaupesiana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
