Silene compacta

Silene compacta, commonly known as the dwarf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, clustered leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Silene compacta is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
