Schizostachyum parvifolium

Schizostachyum parvifolium, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Schizostachyum parvifolium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
