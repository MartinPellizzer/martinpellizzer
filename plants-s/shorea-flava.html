Shorea flava

Shorea flava, commonly known as yellow meranti, is a species of tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued in the timber industry. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a buttressed base. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and fruiting bodies that contribute to its ecological role in tropical rainforests.
