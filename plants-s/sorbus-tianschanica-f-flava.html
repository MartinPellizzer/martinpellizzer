Sorbus tianschanica f. flava

Sorbus tianschanica f. flava, commonly known as the yellowleaf whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish leaves, which distinguish it from the typical green foliage of the species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as pomes, which are typically orange-red in color. It thrives in rocky or mountainous environments, preferring well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to harsh climates.
