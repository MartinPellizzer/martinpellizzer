Swertia affinis

Swertia affinis, commonly known as Indian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its attractive, small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Swertia affinis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
