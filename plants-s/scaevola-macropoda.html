Scaevola macropoda

Scaevola macropoda, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and stems adapted to withstand salty sea air and sandy soils. It thrives in coastal dunes and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Scaevola macropoda plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
