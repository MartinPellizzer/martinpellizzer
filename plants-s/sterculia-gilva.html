Sterculia gilva

Sterculia gilva, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its hard wood and has traditional uses in local communities.
