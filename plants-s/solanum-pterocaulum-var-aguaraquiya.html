Solanum pterocaulum var. aguaraquiya

Solanum pterocaulum var. aguaraquiya, commonly known as the Aguaraquiya nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. Its berries, which are toxic, are often mistaken for edible fruits due to their appearance.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, temperate climates.
