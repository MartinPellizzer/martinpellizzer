Salvia aurita

Salvia aurita, commonly known as the silverleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of showy violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Salvia aurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
