Scoparia macrantha

Scoparia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered scoparia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Scoparia macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
