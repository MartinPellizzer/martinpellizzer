Senecio pyrenaicus subsp. cespitosus

Senecio pyrenaicus subsp.

cespitosus, commonly known as the Pyrenean groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features wiry, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowering heads are yellow, composed of numerous small tubular flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tuft-like growth habit, giving it a cespitose appearance.
