Symplocos neriifolia

Symplocos neriifolia, commonly known as blackleaf waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Symplocos neriifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
