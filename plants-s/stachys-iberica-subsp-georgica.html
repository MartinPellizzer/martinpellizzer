Stachys iberica subsp. georgica

Stachys iberica subsp.

georgica, commonly known as Georgian betony, is a perennial herb native to the Balkans and parts of Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing spikes of violet to purple flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
