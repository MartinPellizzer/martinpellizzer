Saxifraga × prudhommei

Saxifraga × prudhommei, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

Saxifraga × prudhommei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
