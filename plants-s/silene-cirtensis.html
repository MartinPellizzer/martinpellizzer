Silene cirtensis

Silene cirtensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Silene cirtensis is an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
