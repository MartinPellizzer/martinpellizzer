Scolymocephalus nitidus

Scolymocephalus nitidus, commonly known as the silverleaf scolymus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in arid and rocky soils.

Scolymocephalus nitidus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
