Solenospermum oblongifolium

Solenospermum oblongifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Australia.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, star-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits resembling lychees. Its botanical classification places it in the family Malvaceae, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities and fruit.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.
