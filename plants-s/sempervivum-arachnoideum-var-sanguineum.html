Sempervivum arachnoideum var. sanguineum

Sempervivum arachnoideum var. sanguineum, commonly known as the Crimson Hens and Chicks, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its compact rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are often adorned with a fine, spider-like coating of white trichomes. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers on tall, slender stems during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for xeriscaping and alpine gardens.
