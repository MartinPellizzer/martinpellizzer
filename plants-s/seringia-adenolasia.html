Seringia adenolasia

Seringia adenolasia, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces.

Seringia adenolasia is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
