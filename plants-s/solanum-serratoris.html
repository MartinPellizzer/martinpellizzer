Solanum serratoris

Solanum serratoris, commonly known as the serrated nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its serrated leaves and small, violet flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically berries that mature to a yellow or orange color.

Solanum serratoris is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain areas.
