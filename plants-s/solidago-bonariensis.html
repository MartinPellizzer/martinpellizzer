Solidago bonariensis

Solidago bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine golden rod, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago bonariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
