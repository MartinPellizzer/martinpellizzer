Saxifraga wardii var. glabripedicellata

Saxifraga wardii var. glabripedicellata, commonly known as the woolly saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white woolly hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is often found in mountainous regions of Kazakhstan and Tajikistan.
