Stipa sosnowskyi

Stipa sosnowskyi, commonly known as Siberian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with long, silky hairs. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including low temperatures and poor nutrient availability. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often found in alpine or steppe habitats.

Stipa sosnowskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
