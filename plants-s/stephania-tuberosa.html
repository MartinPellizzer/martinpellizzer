Stephania tuberosa

Stephania tuberosa, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces yellowish-green flowers in racemes. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Stephania tuberosa is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade.
