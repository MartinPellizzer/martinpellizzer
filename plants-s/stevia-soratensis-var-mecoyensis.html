Stevia soratensis var. mecoyensis

Stevia soratensis var. mecoyensis, commonly known as the Mechoyensis stevia, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its medicinal and commercial value, it is increasingly being studied for its potential health benefits.
