Sabicea laurentii var. velutina

Sabicea laurentii var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are pinnate, with small, rounded leaflets that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
