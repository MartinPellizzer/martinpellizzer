Senecio oreophyton

Senecio oreophyton, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and often hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio oreophyton produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
