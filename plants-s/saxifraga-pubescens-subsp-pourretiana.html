Saxifraga pubescens subsp. pourretiana

Saxifraga pubescens subsp.

pourretiana, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It thrives in rocky and alpine habitats, often found in moist, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

The plant features dense, hairy foliage that helps protect it from harsh weather conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
