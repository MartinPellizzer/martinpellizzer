Syzygium busuense

Syzygium busuense, commonly known as the Bornean apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy green leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. The fruit contains a single large seed and is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.

Syzygium busuense is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
