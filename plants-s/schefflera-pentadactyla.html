Schefflera pentadactyla

Schefflera pentadactyla, commonly known as the five-finger plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, umbrella-like crown composed of multiple leaflets radiating from a central stem. The leaves are typically dark green, glossy, and have a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
