Sesamum oleiferum

Sesamum oleiferum, commonly known as the sesame plant, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Pedaliaceae and is characterized by its upright stems and pairs of opposite leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, oil-rich seeds. These seeds are widely used for culinary purposes and as a source of oil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in both agriculture and traditional medicine.
