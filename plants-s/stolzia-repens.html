Stolzia repens

Stolzia repens, commonly known as the creeping stolzia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that spread along the ground and produce clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Stolzia repens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
