Solanum cymosum

Solanum cymosum, commonly known as the desert tomatillo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, green, husk-like fruits that encase its yellowish, edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and adapted to sandy soils.

Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
