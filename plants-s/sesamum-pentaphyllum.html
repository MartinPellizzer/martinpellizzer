Sesamum pentaphyllum

Sesamum pentaphyllum, commonly known as the five-lobed sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Sesamum pentaphyllum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil health.
