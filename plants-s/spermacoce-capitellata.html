Spermacoce capitellata

Spermacoce capitellata, commonly known as the tropical milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Spermacoce capitellata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
