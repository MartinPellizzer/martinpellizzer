Stipa laeviculmis

Stipa laeviculmis, commonly known as the smooth false wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to central Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a soft appearance. It is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Stipa laeviculmis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
