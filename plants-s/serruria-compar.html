Serruria compar

Serruria compar, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in spring and summer. Its bark is smooth and often reddish, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Serruria compar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
