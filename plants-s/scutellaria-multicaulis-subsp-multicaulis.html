Scutellaria multicaulis subsp. multicaulis

Scutellaria multicaulis subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the multibracted skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has slender, creeping stems that produce numerous small, violet-blue flowers.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive calyx that is often more prominent than the petals. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

It is a valuable species for ecological gardens and supports a variety of pollinators.
