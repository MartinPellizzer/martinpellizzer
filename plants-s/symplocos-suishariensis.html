Symplocos suishariensis

Symplocos suishariensis, commonly known as the Japanese waxleaf.

It is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, particularly Japan and Korea. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that remain green throughout the growing season. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall.

Symplocos suishariensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
