Sicyos microcarpus subsp. lanaiensis

Sicyos microcarpus subsp.

lanaiensis, commonly known as the Lanai bottle gourd, is a climbing vine native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical fruits that mature to a pale green color with a smooth, waxy surface.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has palmate leaves with three to five lobes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is notable for its historical and cultural significance in Hawaiian agriculture and traditional practices.
