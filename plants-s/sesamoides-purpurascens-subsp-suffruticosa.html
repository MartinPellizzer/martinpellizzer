Sesamoides purpurascens subsp. suffruticosa

Sesamoides purpurascens subsp.

suffruticosa, commonly known as the purple-flowered sesamoid, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
