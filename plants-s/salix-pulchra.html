Salix pulchra

Salix pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are important for pollination by wind. Salix pulchra thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
