Solanum mirabile

Solanum mirabile, commonly known as the starry nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant produces small, toxic berries that are not suitable for human consumption. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum mirabile is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and dramatic floral display.
