Securidaca revoluta

Securidaca revoluta, commonly known as the African velvetbean, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are pinnate, with 8 to 12 pairs of leaflets, and the fruit is a legume that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
