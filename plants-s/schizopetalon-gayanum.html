Schizopetalon gayanum

Schizopetalon gayanum, commonly known as the Sri Lankan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate and delicate floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in shades of white and yellow, making it a unique addition to the region's flora.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
