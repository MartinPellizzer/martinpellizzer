Scilla peruviana var. venusta

Scilla peruviana var. venusta, commonly known as the Peruvian glory of the snow, is a striking bulbous perennial native to the Andes.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms.
