Saxifraga pseudopallida

Saxifraga pseudopallida, commonly known as the pale saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga pseudopallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
