Stenostephanus gracilis

Stenostephanus gracilis, commonly known as the slender stephanus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its delicate appearance.

Stenostephanus gracilis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
