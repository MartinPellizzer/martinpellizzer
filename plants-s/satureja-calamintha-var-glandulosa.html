Satureja calamintha var. glandulosa

Satureja calamintha var. glandulosa, commonly known as glandular savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and spikes of pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called schizocarps, which contain two one-seeded fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its pungent, peppery aroma.
