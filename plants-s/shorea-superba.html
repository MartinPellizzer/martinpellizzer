Shorea superba

Shorea superba, commonly known as the yellow meranti or white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its heavy, durable wood with a golden-yellow color. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, reaching heights of up to 50 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

Shorea superba plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of its native region.
