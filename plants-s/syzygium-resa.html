Syzygium resa

Syzygium resa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Syzygium resa is valued for its durable wood, which has been historically used in furniture and musical instruments.
