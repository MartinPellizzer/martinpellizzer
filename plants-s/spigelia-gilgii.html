Spigelia gilgii

Spigelia gilgii, commonly known as Gilg's pipsissewa, is a member of the Styracaceae family.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and Florida. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy dark green surface. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences, typically blooming in spring with pinkish-white blooms.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
