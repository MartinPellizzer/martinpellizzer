Setaria pumila subsp. pallide-fusca

Setaria pumila subsp.

pallide-fusca, commonly known as pale-fuscent foxtail, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny spikelets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various temperate regions.
