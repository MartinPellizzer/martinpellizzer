Spathodea filiformis

Spathodea filiformis, commonly known as the slender flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are often spirally arranged along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in panicles. Its growth habit is often bushy, with a tendency to form dense foliage.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
