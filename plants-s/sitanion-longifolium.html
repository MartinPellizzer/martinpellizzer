Sitanion longifolium

Sitanion longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved sitanion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Sitanion longifolium is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is a perennial species that is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
