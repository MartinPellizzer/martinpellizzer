Scapisenecio pectinatus var. major

Scapisenecio pectinatus var. major, commonly known as the giant pectinatus, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a soft, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticulture for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
