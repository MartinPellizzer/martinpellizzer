Symphyotrichum laeve subsp. concinnum

Symphyotrichum laeve subsp.

concinnum, commonly known as the smooth coneflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white to pale pink petals and a prominent yellow disk in the center. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
