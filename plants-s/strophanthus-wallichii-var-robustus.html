Strophanthus wallichii var. robustus

Strophanthus wallichii var. robustus, commonly known as the African milkvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries. Its latex contains cardiac glycosides, making it both toxic and historically significant in traditional medicine.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
