Syngonanthus simplex var. appendiculifera

Syngonanthus simplex var. appendiculifera, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces white or pale green berries that are typically consumed by birds, which aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of parasitic plant interactions.
