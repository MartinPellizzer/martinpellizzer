Symplocos balfourii

Symplocos balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's waxberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are a source of food for various wildlife species. Symplocos balfourii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
