Shorea assamica f. koordersii

Shorea assamica f. koordersii, commonly known as the Assam teak, is a variant of the Shorea assamica species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 40 meters tall, characterized by its rough, dark bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oblong fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland and hill forests.

Its wood is highly valued for its durability and workability, making it an important timber species in the region.
