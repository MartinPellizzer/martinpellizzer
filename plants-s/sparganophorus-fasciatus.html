Sparganophorus fasciatus

Sparganophorus fasciatus, commonly known as the striped burrgrass, is a perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted, hairy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces distinctive, spiny, burr-like fruits that contain seeds, aiding in its dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along fence rows.

Botanically, it is classified within the family Amaranthaceae, which includes many species with similar growth habits and ecological roles.
