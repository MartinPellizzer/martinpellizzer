Saussurea triptera var. savatieri

Saussurea triptera var. savatieri, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
