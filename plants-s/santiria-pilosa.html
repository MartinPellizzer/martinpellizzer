Santiria pilosa

Santiria pilosa, commonly known as the hairy santiria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Santiria pilosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
