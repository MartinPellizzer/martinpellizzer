Scolosanthus crucifer

Scolosanthus crucifer, commonly known as the cruciferous scolosanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scolosantaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of small, clustered flowers arranged in a cross-like pattern, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are often white or pale yellow, and they attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.
