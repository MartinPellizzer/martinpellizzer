Syringodea flammea

Syringodea flammea, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, fiery red and orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant features long, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Syringodea flammea thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and blooming habits make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and botanical collections.
