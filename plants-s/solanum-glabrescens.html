Solanum glabrescens

Solanum glabrescens, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries.

The berries are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.
