Syringa villosa var. limprichtii

Syringa villosa var. limprichtii, commonly known as the Limpricht's weeping lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has greyish-green, softly hairy leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is prized for its unique weeping form and ornamental value in landscaping.
