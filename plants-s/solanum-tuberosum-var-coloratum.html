Solanum tuberosum var. coloratum

Solanum tuberosum var. coloratum, commonly known as the colored potato, is a variant of the common potato characterized by its vibrant skin colors.

This variety exhibits a range of hues including purple, blue, and green, which are due to the presence of anthocyanins. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial with stolons and tubers that develop beneath the soil. Its leaves are alternate and palmately lobed, with a hairy texture.

The flowers are usually white or pale blue, and the plant is native to the Andes region of South America.
