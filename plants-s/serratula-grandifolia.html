Serratula grandifolia

Serratula grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered sickleweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in clustered inflorescences and bloom in late summer.

Serratula grandifolia is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped leaves and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
