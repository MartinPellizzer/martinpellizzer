Scilla monophyllos var. pumila

Scilla monophyllos var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
