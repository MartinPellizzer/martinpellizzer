Sasa pseudonipponica

Sasa pseudonipponica, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a clump-forming evergreen plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa pseudonipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Japanese culture.
