Sauropus oblongifolius

Sauropus oblongifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf sauropus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are usually dark green and oval in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability.
