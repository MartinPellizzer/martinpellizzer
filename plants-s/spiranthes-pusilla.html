Spiranthes pusilla

Spiranthes pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf ladies' tresses, is a small, perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Spiranthes pusilla is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
