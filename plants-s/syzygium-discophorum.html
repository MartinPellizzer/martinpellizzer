Syzygium discophorum

Syzygium discophorum, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include smooth bark, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Syzygium discophorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in tropical ecosystems.
