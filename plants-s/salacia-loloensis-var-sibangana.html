Salacia loloensis var. sibangana

Salacia loloensis var. sibangana, commonly known as the Sibangana salacia, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and a pointed apex.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, making it an important component of the local flora and potential source of medicinal compounds.
