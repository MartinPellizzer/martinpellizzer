Salix arctica var. antiplasta

Salix arctica var. antiplasta, commonly known as the Arctic willow, is a low-growing shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and catkin flowers. The plant thrives in cold, harsh environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its stems are often prostrate or creeping, forming dense mats that help insulate the ground.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
