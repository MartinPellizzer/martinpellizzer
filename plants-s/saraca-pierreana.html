Saraca pierreana

Saraca pierreana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Saraca pierreana is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
