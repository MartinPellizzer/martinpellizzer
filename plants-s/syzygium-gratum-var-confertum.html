Syzygium gratum var. confertum

Syzygium gratum var. confertum, commonly known as the fragrant satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.

The wood is also prized for its fine grain and aromatic properties, making it useful in carpentry and furniture making.
