Sedum susannae var. susannae

Sedum susannae var. susannae, commonly known as the Susan's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in small, rounded leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it an ideal plant for arid or semi-arid environments.
