Sesbania pterocarpa

Sesbania pterocarpa, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain small, edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen in the soil.

Sesbania pterocarpa is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
