Scabiosa japonica f. littoralis

Scabiosa japonica f. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal scabious, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging maritime environments.
