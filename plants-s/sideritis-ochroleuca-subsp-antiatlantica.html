Sideritis ochroleuca subsp. antiatlantica

Sideritis ochroleuca subsp.

antiatlantica, commonly known as the Canary Island germander, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants.

The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
