Syzygium seemannianum

Syzygium seemannianum, commonly known as the Java apple or *Pomelo*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces large, round fruits with thick, leathery rinds and a sweet, acidic pulp. Syzygium seemannianum is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
