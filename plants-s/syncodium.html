Syncodium

Syncodium, commonly known as the "spider plant," is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, evergreen perennial that produces small, clustered white flowers followed by red berries. The plant has strap-like, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Syncodium is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and minimal water requirements.

Its ability to propagate through offsets makes it a popular choice for gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
