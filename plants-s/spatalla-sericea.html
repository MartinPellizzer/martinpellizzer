Spatalla sericea

Spatalla sericea, commonly known as the silky spurge, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Spatalla sericea is adapted to arid environments and is considered a drought-tolerant species.
