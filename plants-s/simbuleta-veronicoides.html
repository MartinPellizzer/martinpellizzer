Simbuleta veronicoides

Simbuleta veronicoides, commonly known as the golden veronica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, toothed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant, decorative plants.
