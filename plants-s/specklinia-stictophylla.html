Specklinia stictophylla

Specklinia stictophylla, commonly known as the speckled fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive speckled appearance on the underside. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are short and thick, allowing it to anchor itself to its host while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Specklinia stictophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
