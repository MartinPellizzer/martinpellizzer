Sarothamnus commutatus

Sarothamnus commutatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy species often found in disturbed soils. Its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
