Scutellaria galericulata f. rosea

Scutellaria galericulata f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered blue skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a distinctive aromatic scent.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant color and medicinal potential.
