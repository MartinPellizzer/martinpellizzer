Salvia horminoides

Salvia horminoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia horminoides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
