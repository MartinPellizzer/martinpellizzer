Sciophila torresiana

Sciophila torresiana, commonly known as the Torresian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Sciophila torresiana is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species in tropical climates.
