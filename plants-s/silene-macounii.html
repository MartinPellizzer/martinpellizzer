Silene macounii

Silene macounii, commonly known as Macoun's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
