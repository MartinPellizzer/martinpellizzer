Smallanthus siegesbeckius

Smallanthus siegesbeckius, commonly known as the Andean quinoa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and robust, branched stems. The plant produces small, edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its nutritional value and adaptability, it has gained attention as a potential alternative crop for sustainable agriculture.
