Sophora tomentosa subsp. longiflora

Sophora tomentosa subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to a variety of soil types. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Fabaceae family.
