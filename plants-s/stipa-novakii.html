Stipa novakii

Stipa novakii, commonly known as the Novak's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical seed heads that resemble needles, which are typically golden to brown in color. This plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Stipa novakii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
