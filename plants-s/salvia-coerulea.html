Salvia coerulea

Salvia coerulea, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower spikes that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes.

Salvia coerulea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
