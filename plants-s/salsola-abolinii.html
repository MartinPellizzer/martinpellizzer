Salsola abolinii

Salsola abolinii, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and dry steppes. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Salsola abolinii plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
