Silene vallesia var. caucasica

Silene vallesia var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or ascending.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
