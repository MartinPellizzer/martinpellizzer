Silene membrancea

Silene membrancea, commonly known as the membranous catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Silene membrancea is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to arid conditions.
