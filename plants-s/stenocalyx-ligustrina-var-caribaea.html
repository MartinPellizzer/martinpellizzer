Stenocalyx ligustrina var. caribaea

Stenocalyx ligustrina var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean privet, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean region.

It features opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
