Spondias tuberosa

Spondias tuberosa, commonly known as the Brazilian wild peach or pitahaya, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, red, fleshy fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
