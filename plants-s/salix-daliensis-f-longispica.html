Salix daliensis f. longispica

Salix daliensis f. longispica, commonly known as the longispiculate weeping willow, is a unique variant of the weeping willow species native to China.

This cultivar is characterized by its long, drooping catkins and slender, cascading branches that create a distinctive, flowing appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
