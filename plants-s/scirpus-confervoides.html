Scirpus confervoides

Scirpus confervoides, commonly known as the common rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Scirpus confervoides produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the top of the stems.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
