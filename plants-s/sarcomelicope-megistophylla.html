Sarcomelicope megistophylla

Sarcomelicope megistophylla, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
