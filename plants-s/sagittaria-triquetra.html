Sagittaria triquetra

Sagittaria triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow waters, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in spikes, which bloom in late summer.

Sagittaria triquetra plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.
