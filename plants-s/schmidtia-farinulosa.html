Schmidtia farinulosa

Schmidtia farinulosa, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive silvery-white to grayish hairs covering its leaves and stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Schmidtia farinulosa thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in New Zealand's native flora.
