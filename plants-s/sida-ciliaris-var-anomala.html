Sida ciliaris var. anomala

Sida ciliaris var. anomala, commonly known as the hairy sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green leaves with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
