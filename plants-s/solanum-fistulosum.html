Solanum fistulosum

Solanum fistulosum, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
