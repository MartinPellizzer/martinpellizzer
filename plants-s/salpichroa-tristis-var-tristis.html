Salpichroa tristis var. tristis

Salpichroa tristis var. tristis, commonly known as the slender false flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodland edges. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in terminal spikes and are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
