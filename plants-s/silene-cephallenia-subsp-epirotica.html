Silene cephallenia subsp. epirotica

Silene cephallenia subsp.

epirotica, commonly known as the Epirotic campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils.

The plant features simple, linear leaves and produces pink to purple flowers in racemes. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
