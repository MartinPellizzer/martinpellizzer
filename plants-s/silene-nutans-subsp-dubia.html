Silene nutans subsp. dubia

Silene nutans subsp.

dubia, commonly known as the nodding campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy pink or red flowers.

The subspecies dubia is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and slightly different flower morphology compared to the typical form of Silene nutans. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is often found in the wild and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
