Senecio colombianus

Senecio colombianus, commonly known as the Colombian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Senecio colombianus is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
