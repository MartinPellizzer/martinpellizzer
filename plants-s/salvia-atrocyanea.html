Salvia atrocyanea

Salvia atrocyanea, commonly known as black sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black foliage and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface and a strong aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants.
