Sperlingia

Sperlingia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that resemble small, colorful sea urchins. These plants are typically found in coastal areas, where they thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Sperlingia species are known for their drought tolerance and ability to withstand salt spray.

Their unique appearance makes them popular in garden landscapes and as ornamental plants.
