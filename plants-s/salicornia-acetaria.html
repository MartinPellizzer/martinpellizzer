Salicornia acetaria

Salicornia acetaria, commonly known as sea beans or glassweed, is a halophyte plant that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are reduced to scale-like structures, and it is often found in coastal areas or salt marshes.

Salicornia acetaria plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
