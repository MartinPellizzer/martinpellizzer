Sphaerocephalus chorassanicus

Sphaerocephalus chorassanicus, commonly known as the Turkmenian sand lily, is a unique flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, bulb-like structure and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater. Its flowers bloom during brief periods of rainfall, making it a rare and striking sight in its natural habitat.

The Turkmenian sand lily plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
