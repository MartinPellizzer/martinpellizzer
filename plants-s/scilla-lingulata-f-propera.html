Scilla lingulata f. propera

Scilla lingulata f. propera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved squill.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are used in traditional medicine and horticulture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
