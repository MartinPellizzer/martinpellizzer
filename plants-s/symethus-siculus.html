Symethus siculus

Symethus siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian symethus, is a rare and ancient plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, sprawling growth habit and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. Its botanical significance lies in its historical use in traditional medicine and its role in ecological studies.

Due to habitat loss, it is now considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.
