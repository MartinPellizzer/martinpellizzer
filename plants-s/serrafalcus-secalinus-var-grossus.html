Serrafalcus secalinus var. grossus

Serrafalcus secalinus var. grossus, commonly known as the broad-leaved wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a serrated margin, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a useful plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
