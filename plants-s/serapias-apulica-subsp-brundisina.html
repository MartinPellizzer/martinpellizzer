Serapias apulica subsp. brundisina

Serapias apulica subsp.

brundisina, commonly known as the Brindisi orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Italy. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces a single flowering stem with several blooms. It thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species.
