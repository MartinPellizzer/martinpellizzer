Salvia staminea subsp. armeniaca

Salvia staminea subsp.

armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional medicine for its calming and antispasmodic effects.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
