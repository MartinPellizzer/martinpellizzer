Spyridium pumilum

Spyridium pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf spyridium, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Spyridium pumilum is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyllous forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
