Sclarea argentea

Sclarea argentea, commonly known as silver angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in complex umbels, and it produces a tall, hollow stem.

The roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their essential oils.
