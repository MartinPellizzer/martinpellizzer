Sphenoclea zeylanica

Sphenoclea zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a tropical shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The fruit is widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient.
