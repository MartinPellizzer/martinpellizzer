Styphelia nesophila

Styphelia nesophila, commonly known as the narrowleaf wintergreen, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in coastal forests and is often found in areas with high rainfall. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. It is adapted to cool, humid climates and thrives in shaded environments.

Styphelia nesophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
