Solanum cornutum

Solanum cornutum, commonly known as the horned nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Solanum cornutum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance.
