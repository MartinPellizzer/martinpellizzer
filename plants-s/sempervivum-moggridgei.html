Sempervivum moggridgei

Sempervivum moggridgei, commonly known as the Moggridge's houseleek, is a species of succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit, with fleshy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wool. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice among gardeners and succulent enthusiasts.
