Silene eldana

Silene eldana, commonly known as the Elanda catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Silene eldana is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
