Strobilanthes lawangensis

Strobilanthes lawangensis, commonly known as the Lawangensis flame flower, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble flowers, which are often brightly colored, typically red or orange. The plant is herbaceous, with opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique floral structures make it a popular subject for botanical study and horticultural interest.
