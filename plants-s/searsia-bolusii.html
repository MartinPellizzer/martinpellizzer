Searsia bolusii

Searsia bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus wild pear, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and leathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that appear in spring, followed by yellowish-orange fruits resembling pears. Searsia bolusii is well adapted to arid conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
