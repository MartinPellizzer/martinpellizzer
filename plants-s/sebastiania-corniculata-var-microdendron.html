Sebastiania corniculata var. microdendron

Sebastiania corniculata var. microdendron, commonly known as the dwarf flame tree, is a compact, evergreen shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
