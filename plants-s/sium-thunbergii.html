Sium thunbergii

Sium thunbergii, commonly known as water parsnip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments, thriving in shallow water or saturated soil. The flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters, and it produces small, dark brown fruits.
