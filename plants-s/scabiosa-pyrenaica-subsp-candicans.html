Scabiosa pyrenaica subsp. candicans

Scabiosa pyrenaica subsp.

candicans, commonly known as the Pyrenean scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value in natural habitats.
