Syzygium hemsleyanum subsp. paucinervium

Syzygium hemsleyanum subsp.

paucinervium, commonly known as the Hemsley's syzygium, is a member of the Myrtaceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often in tropical rainforests.

The subspecies paucinervium is distinguished by its reduced venation in the leaves compared to other subspecies of Syzygium hemsleyanum.
