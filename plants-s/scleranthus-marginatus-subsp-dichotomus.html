Scleranthus marginatus subsp. dichotomus

Scleranthus marginatus subsp.

dichotomus, commonly known as the forked evening-primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture. It prefers disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
