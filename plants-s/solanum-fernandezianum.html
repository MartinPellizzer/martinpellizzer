Solanum fernandezianum

Solanum fernandezianum, commonly known as the Fernandezian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. Its leaves are usually ovate and slightly hairy, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum fernandezianum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
