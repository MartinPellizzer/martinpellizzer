Satanocrater coccineus

Satanocrater coccineus, commonly known as the red devil's flower, is a striking member of the Crassulaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
