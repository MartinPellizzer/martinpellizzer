Solanum usambarense

Solanum usambarense, commonly known as the Usambare nightshade, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and the plant is often found in the understory of forests.

Solanum usambarense belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
