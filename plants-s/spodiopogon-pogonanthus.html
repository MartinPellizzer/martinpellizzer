Spodiopogon pogonanthus

Spodiopogon pogonanthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf tussockgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or marshy environments.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
