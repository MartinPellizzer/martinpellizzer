Schizanthus integrifolius

Schizanthus integrifolius, commonly known as the common gladecress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, bright flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is an annual herb that produces small, edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Schizanthus integrifolius make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
