Salix berberifolia subsp. kimurana

Salix berberifolia subsp.

kimurana, commonly known as the Korean weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its slender, pendulous branches that create a weeping appearance, and its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth margin.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
