Solanum kerrii

Solanum kerrii, commonly known as Kerri's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum kerrii belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
