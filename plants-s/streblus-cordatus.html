Streblus cordatus

Streblus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf strangler fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a climbing shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. Its fruit is a small, green fig that turns yellow when mature.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for various animals and birds.
