Salvia tippenhaueri

Salvia tippenhaueri, commonly known as the Tippenhauer sage, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. This plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

The plant is valued in xeriscaping for its ornamental appeal and low water requirements.
