Silene cariensis subsp. muglae

Silene cariensis subsp.

muglae, commonly known as the Mugla catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Turkey. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. It has a low-growing habit with narrow, linear leaves. Silene cariensis subsp.

muglae is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its habitat.
