Scilla spicata

Scilla spicata, commonly known as the spike squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers on tall, leafless stems. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a striking touch to gardens and natural landscapes.

Scilla spicata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
