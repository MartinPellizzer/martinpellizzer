Statice aretiifolia

Statice aretiifolia, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are typically blue-green in color, and it is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.

Statice aretiifolia is widely cultivated as a ornamental plant for its long-lasting blooms and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
