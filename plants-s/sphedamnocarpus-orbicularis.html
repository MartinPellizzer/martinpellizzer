Sphedamnocarpus orbicularis

Sphedamnocarpus orbicularis, commonly known as the globe-shaped burr plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive, spherical fruits.

The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical climates. Its fruit, which resembles a small globe, develops from a single seed enclosed in a fibrous husk. The botanical structure of the fruit is adapted for dispersal by animals, as the husk can be easily detached and carried.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
