Scorzonera incana

Scorzonera incana, commonly known as the hairy scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with a taproot that is often used in traditional medicine. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive central disk.

Scorzonera incana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
