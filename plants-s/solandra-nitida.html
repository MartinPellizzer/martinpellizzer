Solandra nitida

Solandra nitida, commonly known as the silver moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants. The plant is characterized by its silvery, velvety foliage and large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. Solandra nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.

It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
