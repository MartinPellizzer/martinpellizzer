Senecio jacobaea var. flosculosus

Senecio jacobaea var. flosculosus, commonly known as the lesser ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, while its leaves are covered in fine hairs.

It is considered a weed in some regions and can be toxic to livestock.
