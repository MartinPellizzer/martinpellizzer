Solanum dolichocarpum

Solanum dolichocarpum, commonly known as the long-fruited nightshade, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, egg-shaped berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically white or pale blue.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
