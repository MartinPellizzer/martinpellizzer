Spiraea rubra

Spiraea rubra, commonly known as red bridal wreath, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cascading clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The foliage is typically dark green, turning reddish in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
