Salvia officinalis var. frankei

Salvia officinalis var. frankei, commonly known as German sage, is a cultivar of the common sage plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and tall spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value in gardens. The leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine, similar to other sage varieties.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.
