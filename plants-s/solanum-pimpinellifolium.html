Solanum pimpinellifolium

Solanum pimpinellifolium, commonly known as the cherry tomato, is a species of nightshade plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, herbaceous annual plant with hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into bright red, cherry-sized fruits. It is closely related to other tomato species and is often used in breeding programs to improve tomato varieties.

Its botanical features make it an important model for studying fruit development and plant genetics.
