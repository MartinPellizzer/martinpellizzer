Setaria costata

Setaria costata, commonly known as the rough fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, pointed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Setaria costata is known for its adaptability and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
