Sphaeropteris glauca

Sphaeropteris glauca, commonly known as the blue cycad, is a unique member of the cycad family, characterized by its striking blue-green foliage.

It is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk that develops a thick, fibrous bark over time. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

Sphaeropteris glauca is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
