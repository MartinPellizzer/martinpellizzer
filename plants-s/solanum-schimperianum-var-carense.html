Solanum schimperianum var. carense

Solanum schimperianum var. carense, commonly known as the Cerrado nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, smooth, orange-red berries.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in the Cerrado biome, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
