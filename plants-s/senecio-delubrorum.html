Senecio delubrorum

Senecio delubrorum, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as a low, sprawling shrub with gray-green, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio delubrorum is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
