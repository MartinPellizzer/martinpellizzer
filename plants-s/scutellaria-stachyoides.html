Scutellaria stachyoides

Scutellaria stachyoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands, and it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and often hairy.

Scutellaria stachyoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
