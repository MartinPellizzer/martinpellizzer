Smilax corbularia

Smilax corbularia, commonly known as the arrowleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and spiny stems, which are typical features of the Smilax genus. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Smilax corbularia is a hardy species that can tolerate partial shade and is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping.
