Stachys heraclea subsp. malessana

Stachys heraclea subsp.

malessana, commonly known as the Malessa betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
