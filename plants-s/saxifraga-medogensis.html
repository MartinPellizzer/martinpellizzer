Saxifraga medogensis

Saxifraga medogensis, commonly known as the Tibetan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga medogensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme altitudes and its potential ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
