Solanum geniculatistrigosum

Solanum geniculatistrigosum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
