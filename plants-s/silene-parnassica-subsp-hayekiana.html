Silene parnassica subsp. hayekiana

Silene parnassica subsp.

hayekiana, commonly known as the Parnassus silene, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps and surrounding areas. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and striking pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
