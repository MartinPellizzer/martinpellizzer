Spiranthes biflora

Spiranthes biflora, commonly known as the twin-flower spirea, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence composed of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms from late summer into early autumn.

Spiranthes biflora is a perennial plant that plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.
