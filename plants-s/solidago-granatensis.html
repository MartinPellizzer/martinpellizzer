Solidago granatensis

Solidago granatensis, commonly known as the granate goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Solidago granatensis plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
