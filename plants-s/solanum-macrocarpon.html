Solanum macrocarpon

Solanum macrocarpon, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, egg-shaped fruits that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow with purple streaks, and the fruit is usually harvested when it turns from green to yellow or red.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in various cultures.
