Sauropus compressus var. puberulus

Sauropus compressus var. puberulus, commonly known as the velvetleaf sauropus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, velvety texture. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter, pubescent underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a range of soil types.
