Streptanthus arcuatus

Streptanthus arcuatus, commonly known as the wavy pod wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are linear to oblanceolate, and it blooms in late winter to early spring.
