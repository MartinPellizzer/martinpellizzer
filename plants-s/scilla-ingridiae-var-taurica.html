Scilla ingridiae var. taurica

Scilla ingridiae var. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric scilla, is a rare and striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Russia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is closely related to other members of the Hyacinthaceae family, sharing similar growth habits and floral structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a desirable addition to rock gardens.
