Salvia daghestanica

Salvia daghestanica, commonly known as the Caucasian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia daghestanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
