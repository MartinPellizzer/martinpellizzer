Stanfordia californica

Stanfordia californica, commonly known as the California starflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its star-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal prairies and open woodlands.

Its short life cycle and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
