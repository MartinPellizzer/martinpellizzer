Saccharum brasilianum

Saccharum brasilianum, commonly known as Brazilian sugarcane, is a tall, perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks that contain a high concentration of sucrose. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring ample sunlight and water. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a prominent midrib, and it produces panicles of small, greenish flowers.

Saccharum brasilianum is widely cultivated for its sugar content and has significant economic importance in the global sugar industry.
