Saponaria pamphylica

Saponaria pamphylica, commonly known as the Pamphylian soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Saponaria pamphylica is also noted for its mild soap-like scent, which gives it its common name.
