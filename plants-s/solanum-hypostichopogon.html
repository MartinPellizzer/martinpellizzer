Solanum hypostichopogon

Solanum hypostichopogon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
