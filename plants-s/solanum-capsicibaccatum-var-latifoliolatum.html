Solanum capsicibaccatum var. latifoliolatum

Solanum capsicibaccatum var. latifoliolatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved bitter apple, is a species native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is known for its bitter taste and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
