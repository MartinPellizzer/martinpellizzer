Selaginella macroura

Selaginella macroura, commonly known as the long-stemmed spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance.

It is a vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features erect, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves that are often green or reddish in color. Selaginella macroura is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and has a unique reproductive system involving microspores and megaspores.

It is also notable for its capacity to regenerate from spores, which allows it to persist in various ecological niches.
