Scrophularia rimarum

Scrophularia rimarum, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Scrophularia rimarum is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance to pollinators.
