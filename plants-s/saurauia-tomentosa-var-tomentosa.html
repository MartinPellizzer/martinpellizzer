Saurauia tomentosa var. tomentosa

Saurauia tomentosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly saurauia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white pubescence covering its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture and are often clustered at the base of the stems.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in racemes, followed by small, orange-red berries.
