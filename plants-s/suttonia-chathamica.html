Suttonia chathamica

Suttonia chathamica, commonly known as the Chatham Islands hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Chatham Islands of New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming during the spring and summer months.

Suttonia chathamica is characterized by its fibrous, woody stems and is adapted to the coastal and maritime climate of its native habitat.
