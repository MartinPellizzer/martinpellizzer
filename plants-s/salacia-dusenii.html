Salacia dusenii

Salacia dusenii, commonly known as the Dusen's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Salacia dusenii is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
