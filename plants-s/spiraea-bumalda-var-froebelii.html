Spiraea bumalda var. froebelii

Spiraea bumalda var. froebelii, commonly known as Froebel's weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely cut, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its graceful, weeping habit.
