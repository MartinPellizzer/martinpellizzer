Syzygium mirabile

Syzygium mirabile, commonly known as the golden apple or *Santol*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, round, yellow or orange berry that is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its cultivation is popular in regions with warm, humid climates due to its adaptability and aesthetic qualities.
