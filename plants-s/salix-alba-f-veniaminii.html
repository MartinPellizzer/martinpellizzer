Salix alba f. veniaminii

Salix alba f. veniaminii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a decorative cultivar of the white willow.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist soils and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

This cultivar is particularly noted for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
