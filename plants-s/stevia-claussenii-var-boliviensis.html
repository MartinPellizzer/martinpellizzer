Stevia claussenii var. boliviensis

Stevia claussenii var. boliviensis, commonly known as Bolivian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid climates.

Its use in traditional medicine and as a natural sweetener has gained increasing interest in recent years.
