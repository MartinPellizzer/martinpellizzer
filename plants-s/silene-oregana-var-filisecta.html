Silene oregana var. filisecta

Silene oregana var. filisecta, commonly known as the Oregon silene or the filisecta silene, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are often clustered in racemes.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated, thread-like leaves, which give it its specific epithet "filisecta.".
