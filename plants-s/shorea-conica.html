Shorea conica

Shorea conica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The tree typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.
