Solanum lythrocarpum

Solanum lythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited nightshade, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bright red berries, which are edible but contain toxic compounds. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
