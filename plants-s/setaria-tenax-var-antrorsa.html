Setaria tenax var. antrorsa

Setaria tenax var. antrorsa, commonly known as black dropsettle, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black bracts that are tightly wrapped around the inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
