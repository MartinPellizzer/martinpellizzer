Scolymus hispanicus

Scolymus hispanicus, commonly known as Spanish scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow to orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Scolymus hispanicus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
