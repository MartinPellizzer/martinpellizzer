Spondias purpurea f. lutea

Spondias purpurea f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow mombin, is a variant of the mombin tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a yellowish-green coloration. Its fruit is a yellow or orange drupe, often with a distinctive red or purple calyx that remains attached.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
