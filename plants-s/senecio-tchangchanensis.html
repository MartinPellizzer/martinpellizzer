Senecio tchangchanensis

Senecio tchangchanensis, commonly known as the Chiangchan Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are often narrow and hairy, and it produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
