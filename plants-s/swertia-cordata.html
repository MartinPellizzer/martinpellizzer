Swertia cordata

Swertia cordata, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. Its flowers typically bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Swertia cordata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
