Symplocos globosa

Symplocos globosa, commonly known as the globe wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. Symplocos globosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
