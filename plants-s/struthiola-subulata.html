Struthiola subulata

Struthiola subulata, commonly known as the slender sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Struthiola subulata is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is considered a characteristic species of desert ecosystems.
