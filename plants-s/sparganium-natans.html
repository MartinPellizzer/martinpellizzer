Sparganium natans

Sparganium natans, commonly known as floating burr rush, is a water plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its floating stems that bear clusters of spadices, which are yellowish-green, spike-like flowers. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats on the water surface.

The fruiting structures, called burrs, are distinctive and can adhere to animals or clothing, aiding in seed dispersal.
