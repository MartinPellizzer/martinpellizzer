Scutellaria aurea

Scutellaria aurea, commonly known as golden skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Scutellaria aurea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
