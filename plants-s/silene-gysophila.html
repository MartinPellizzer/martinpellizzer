Silene gysophila

Silene gysophila, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in dunes or along the coastline. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit.

Silene gysophila is listed as a threatened species due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
