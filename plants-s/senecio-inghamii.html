Senecio inghamii

Senecio inghamii, commonly known as Ingham's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy white or gray seeds.
