Spodiopogon ciliaris

Spodiopogon ciliaris, commonly known as the silverhair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Spodiopogon ciliaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
