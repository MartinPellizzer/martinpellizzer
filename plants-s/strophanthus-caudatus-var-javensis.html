Strophanthus caudatus var. javensis

Strophanthus caudatus var. javensis, commonly known as the Java strophanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also have historical use in traditional medicine.

The plant contains cardiac glycosides, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
