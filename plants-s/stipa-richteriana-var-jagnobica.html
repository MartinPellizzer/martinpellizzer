Stipa richteriana var. jagnobica

Stipa richteriana var. jagnobica, commonly known as the Jagnobica needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and compact, spikelike inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold and arid conditions. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.

This variety is distinguished by its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
