Salsola aethiopica

Salsola aethiopica, commonly known as African gooseweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Salsola aethiopica is adapted to thrive in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Its extensive root system allows it to survive in extreme conditions, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
