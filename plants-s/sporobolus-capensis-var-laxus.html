Sporobolus capensis var. laxus

Sporobolus capensis var. laxus, commonly known as the slender speargrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Sporobolus capensis var. laxus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
