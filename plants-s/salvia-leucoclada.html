Salvia leucoclada

Salvia leucoclada, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of pale blue to white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Salvia leucoclada is valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional medicine and spiritual practices.
