Sagittaria sagittifolia var. arifolia

Sagittaria sagittifolia var. arifolia, commonly known as arrowhead plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a distinctive leaf sheath that encloses the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that emerge above the water surface. It is often used in water gardens and pond landscapes due to its ornamental value and ability to improve water quality.

This variety is also valued for its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for insects and small fish.
