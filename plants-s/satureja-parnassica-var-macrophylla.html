Satureja parnassica var. macrophylla

Satureja parnassica var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is distinguished by its large, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and possibly anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
