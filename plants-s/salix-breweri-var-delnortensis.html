Salix breweri var. delnortensis

Salix breweri var. delnortensis, commonly known as the northern willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of wind pollination. Its leaves are elliptical with a rough texture and often have a bluish-green hue.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
