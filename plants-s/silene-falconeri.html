Silene falconeri

Silene falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Silene falconeri is also noted for its short stature, often reaching only a few centimeters in height.
