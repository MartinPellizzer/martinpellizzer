Sclerothamnus pentaphyllus

Sclerothamnus pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leafed shrub, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, gray-green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Sclerothamnus pentaphyllus is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
