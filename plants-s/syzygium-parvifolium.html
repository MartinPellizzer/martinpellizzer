Syzygium parvifolium

Syzygium parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, yellowish fruits resembling small tamarinds.

Syzygium parvifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
