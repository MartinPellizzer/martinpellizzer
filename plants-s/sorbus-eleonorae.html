Sorbus eleonorae

Sorbus eleonorae, commonly known as the Eleanor's whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in woodland edges and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Sorbus eleonorae is often used in hedgerows and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
