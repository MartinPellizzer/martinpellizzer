Sladenia integrifolia

Sladenia integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf sladenia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and unbroken along the edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
