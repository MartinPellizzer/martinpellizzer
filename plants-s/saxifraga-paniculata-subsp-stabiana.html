Saxifraga paniculata subsp. stabiana

Saxifraga paniculata subsp.

stabiana, commonly known as the mountain saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky and gravelly soils, often in high-altitude environments where conditions are harsh.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves and produces erect, branched stems topped with clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological niches.
