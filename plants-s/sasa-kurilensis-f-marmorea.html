Sasa kurilensis f. marmorea

Sasa kurilensis f. marmorea, commonly known as the white-flowered sasa, is a variant of the sasa plant native to the northern regions of Japan and the Russian Far East.

It is a rhizomatous evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a distinctive white or silver underside. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often white or pale pink, appearing in early spring.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its ability to form thick, attractive ground covers.
