Saxifraga subverticillata subsp. colchica

Saxifraga subverticillata subsp.

colchica, commonly known as the Caucasian saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of oblanceolate leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
