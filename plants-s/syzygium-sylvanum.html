Syzygium sylvanum

Syzygium sylvanum, commonly known as the satinwood or white sand apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its wood is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in furniture and cabinet-making.

Syzygium sylvanum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
