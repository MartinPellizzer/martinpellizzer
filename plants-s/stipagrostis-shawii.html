Stipagrostis shawii

Stipagrostis shawii, commonly known as the desert grass or Shaw's grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Stipagrostis shawii is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in harsh environments.
