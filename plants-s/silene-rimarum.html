Silene rimarum

Silene rimarum, commonly known as the Rimarum catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene rimarum is noted for its small, inconspicuous fruits and its role in supporting local insect populations.
