Senecio rhodanthus

Senecio rhodanthus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky stems and vibrant orange to red flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters at the tips of its stems. Senecio rhodanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
