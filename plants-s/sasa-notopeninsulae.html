Sasa notopeninsulae

Sasa notopeninsulae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks.

Sasa notopeninsulae is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
