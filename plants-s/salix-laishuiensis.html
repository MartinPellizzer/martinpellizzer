Salix laishuiensis

Salix laishuiensis, commonly known as the Lashui willow, is a rare species of willow native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often near streams or in wetlands. Its leaves are elongated and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered an important species for botanical studies and conservation efforts.
