Senna pilosior

Senna pilosior, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Senna pilosior is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
