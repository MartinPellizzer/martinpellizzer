Senecio millelobatus

Senecio millelobatus, commonly known as the "Thousand-leaf Senecio," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, spoon-shaped leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
