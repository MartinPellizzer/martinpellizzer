Sarcostemma marsupiflorum

Sarcostemma marsupiflorum, commonly known as the cup-shaped milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-like structures called "marsupia" that enclose the flowers, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a rough, hairy texture.

Sarcostemma marsupiflorum is often found in rocky or arid environments, showing a degree of drought tolerance.
