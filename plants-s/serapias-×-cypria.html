Serapias × cypria

Serapias × cypria, commonly known as the hybrid cypripeta, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Serapias grandiflora and Serapias cypria.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typically blooming in late spring. It is a perennial herb that prefers full sun to partial shade and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
