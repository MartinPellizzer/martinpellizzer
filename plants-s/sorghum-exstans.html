Sorghum exstans

Sorghum exstans, commonly known as the slender sorghum, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves, typically growing up to 2-3 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
