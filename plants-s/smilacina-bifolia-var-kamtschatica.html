Smilacina bifolia var. kamtschatica

Smilacina bifolia var. kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatake smilacina, is a variant of the greenbriar plant native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic study.
