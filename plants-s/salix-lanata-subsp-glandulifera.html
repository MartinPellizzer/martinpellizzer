Salix lanata subsp. glandulifera

Salix lanata subsp.

glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular willow, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its silky, hairy leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. Its growth habit is typically upright, with multiple stems forming a dense, bushy structure.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.
