Salix cantoniensis

Salix cantoniensis, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a tendency to peel in thin strips.

Salix cantoniensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
