Sloanea heteroneura

Sloanea heteroneura, commonly known as the Brazilian mahogany, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a medium to large evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically clustered in axillary catkins.

The wood of Sloanea heteroneura is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, though its harvesting is regulated due to conservation concerns.
