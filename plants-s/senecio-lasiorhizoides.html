Senecio lasiorhizoides

Senecio lasiorhizoides, commonly known as the woolly root senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, fibrous roots and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
