Scrophularia hoppei var. floribunda

Scrophularia hoppei var. floribunda, commonly known as the Himalayan foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple in color and bloom in late summer to early autumn. This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
