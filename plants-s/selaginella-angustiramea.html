Selaginella angustiramea

Selaginella angustiramea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobila.

It is a vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, branching stems and clustered leaves that give it a moss-like appearance.

Selaginella angustiramea is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
