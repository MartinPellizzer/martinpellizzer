Schizophragma integrifolium f. denticulatum

Schizophragma integrifolium f. denticulatum, commonly known as the denticulate honeysuckle, is a compact, woody shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with smooth or slightly toothed margins, and the plant produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
