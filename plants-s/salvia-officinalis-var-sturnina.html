Salvia officinalis var. sturnina

Salvia officinalis var. sturnina, commonly known as lemon sage, is a variant of the common sage plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive lemon-scented leaves and small, violet-blue flowers. This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in herbal medicine and culinary applications due to its aromatic properties.

The plant is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
