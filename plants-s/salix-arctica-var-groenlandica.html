Salix arctica var. groenlandica

Salix arctica var. groenlandica, commonly known as Arctic willow, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tundra ecosystems.
