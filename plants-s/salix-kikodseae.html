Salix kikodseae

Salix kikodseae, commonly known as the Kikodse willow, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Salix kikodseae plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides habitat for local wildlife.
