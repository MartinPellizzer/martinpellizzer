Sedum stapfii

Sedum stapfii, commonly known as the Stapf's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements make it an attractive addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
