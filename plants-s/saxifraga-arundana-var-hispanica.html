Saxifraga arundana var. hispanica

Saxifraga arundana var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers in the spring. It is adapted to harsh environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
