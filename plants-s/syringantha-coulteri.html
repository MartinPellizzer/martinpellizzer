Syringantha coulteri

Syringantha coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's syringa, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Syringantha coulteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
