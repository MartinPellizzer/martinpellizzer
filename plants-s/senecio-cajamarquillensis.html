Senecio cajamarquillensis

Senecio cajamarquillensis, commonly known as the Peruvian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, aiding in water retention.

Senecio cajamarquillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
