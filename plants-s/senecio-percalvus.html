Senecio percalvus

Senecio percalvus, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio percalvus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
