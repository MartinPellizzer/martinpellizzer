Solanum salviifolium var. longiflorum

Solanum salviifolium var. longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered blueberry bush, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its attractive, dark green, serrated leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species such as tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit, which is a small, edible berry, is typically harvested in late summer and is prized for its sweet, tangy flavor.
