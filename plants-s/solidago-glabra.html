Solidago glabra

Solidago glabra, commonly known as white beardtongue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Solidago glabra is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
