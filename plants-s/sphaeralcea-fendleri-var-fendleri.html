Sphaeralcea fendleri var. fendleri

Sphaeralcea fendleri var. fendleri, commonly known as desert four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in the late afternoon, hence its common name. The plant has hairy, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

The species is known for its resilience to drought and high temperatures.
