Scorzonera araneosa

Scorzonera araneosa, commonly known as the spider flower or woolly scorzonera, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a tuberous root system that is sometimes used in traditional medicine. Scorzonera araneosa thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitats.
