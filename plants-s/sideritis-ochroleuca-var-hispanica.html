Sideritis ochroleuca var. hispanica

Sideritis ochroleuca var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale purple or white, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
