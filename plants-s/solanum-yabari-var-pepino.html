Solanum yabari var. pepino

Solanum yabari var. pepino, commonly known as the pepino melon, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The pepino melon is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content, making it a popular choice in specialty markets.
