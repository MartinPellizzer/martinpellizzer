Solanum pascoense

Solanum pascoense, commonly known as the Pasco nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky areas and produces small, purple flowers that bloom in the evening. Its berries are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.

Solanum pascoense is often found in desert environments and is adapted to survive in arid conditions.
