Silene dilatata

Silene dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Silene dilatata is an important species for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
