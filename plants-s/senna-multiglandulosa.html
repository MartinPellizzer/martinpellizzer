Senna multiglandulosa

Senna multiglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with multiple small glands on the leaflets, giving it its scientific name.

Senna multiglandulosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
