Salix przewalskii var. glabra

Salix przewalskii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth willow, is a shrub native to central Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Mongolia, and Russia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and slender, often drooping branches. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a silvery-green underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wet meadows.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
