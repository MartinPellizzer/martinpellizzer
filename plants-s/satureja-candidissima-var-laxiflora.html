Satureja candidissima var. laxiflora

Satureja candidissima var. laxiflora, commonly known as white wood sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

This variety is noted for its medicinal uses and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
