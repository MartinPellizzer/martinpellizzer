Scutellaria pycnoclada

Scutellaria pycnoclada, commonly known as the dense-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Scutellaria pycnoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
