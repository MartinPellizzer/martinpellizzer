Statice dubyeana

Statice dubyeana, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, vividly colored flowers, typically in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Statice dubyeana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh, maritime conditions.
