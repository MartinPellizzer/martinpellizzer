Syagrus deflexa

Syagrus deflexa, commonly known as the flexed palm or Brazilian palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits, which are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. Syagrus deflexa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and hardy nature.
