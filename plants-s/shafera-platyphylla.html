Shafera platyphylla

Shafera platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf shafera, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive flat, broad leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often blooming in the spring. Shafera platyphylla is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access underground water.
