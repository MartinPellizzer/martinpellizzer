Salvia × kanitziana

Salvia × kanitziana, commonly known as the Kanitz's sage, is a hybrid perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
