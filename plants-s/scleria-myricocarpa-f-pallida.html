Scleria myricocarpa f. pallida

Scleria myricocarpa f. pallida, commonly known as the pale-flowered bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous growth habit, which helps it thrive in moist soils.

The pale coloration of its flowers distinguishes it from other forms of the species.
