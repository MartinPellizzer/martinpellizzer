Symphyotrichum dumosum var. strictior

Symphyotrichum dumosum var. strictior, commonly known as the narrowleaf heath aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to shaded environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to lavender, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its compact growth habit and dense foliage make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
