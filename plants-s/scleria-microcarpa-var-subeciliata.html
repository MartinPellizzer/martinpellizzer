Scleria microcarpa var. subeciliata

Scleria microcarpa var. subeciliata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-green, with a basal rosette of foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, three-lobed flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
