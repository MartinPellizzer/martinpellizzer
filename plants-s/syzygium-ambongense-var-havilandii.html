Syzygium ambongense var. havilandii

Syzygium ambongense var. havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's satinash, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, characterized by aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible, spherical berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
