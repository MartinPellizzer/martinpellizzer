Scleranthus hyssopifolius

Scleranthus hyssopifolius, commonly known as the purple sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand dry conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscaping.
