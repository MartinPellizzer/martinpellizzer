Salvia pavonii

Salvia pavonii, commonly known as the Mexican scarlet sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Salvia pavonii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
