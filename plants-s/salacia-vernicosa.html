Salacia vernicosa

Salacia vernicosa, commonly known as the golden vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a glossy surface that reflects light.

Salacia vernicosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in herbal remedies for its purported medicinal properties.
