Symplocos cernua var. mexicana

Symplocos cernua var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican dewberry, is a shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs and evergreen plants.
