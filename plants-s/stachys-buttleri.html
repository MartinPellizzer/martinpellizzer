Stachys buttleri

Stachys buttleri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which bloom in late summer. Stachys buttleri is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
