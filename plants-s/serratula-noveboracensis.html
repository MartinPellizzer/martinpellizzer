Serratula noveboracensis

Serratula noveboracensis, commonly known as New York thistle, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a hairy stem.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a native species in its range.
