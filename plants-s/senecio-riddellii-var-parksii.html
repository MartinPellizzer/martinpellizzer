Senecio riddellii var. parksii

Senecio riddellii var. parksii, commonly known as the Park's ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright yellow with a central disk.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
