Syzygium chrysanthum

Syzygium chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden apple or the yellow apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Syzygium chrysanthum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and unique fruit.
