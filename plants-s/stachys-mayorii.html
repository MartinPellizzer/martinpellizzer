Stachys mayorii

Stachys mayorii, commonly known as the Armenian betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching toward the top. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stachys mayorii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
