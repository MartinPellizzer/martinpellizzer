Sisymbrium humile var. hygrophilum

Sisymbrium humile var. hygrophilum, commonly known as the water honesty plant, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and pinnately divided leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
