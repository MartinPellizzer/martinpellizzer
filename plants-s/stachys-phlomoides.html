Stachys phlomoides

Stachys phlomoides, commonly known as hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale purple to greenish flowers in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.

Stachys phlomoides is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
