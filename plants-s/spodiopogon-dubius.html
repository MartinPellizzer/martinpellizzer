Spodiopogon dubius

Spodiopogon dubius, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes help it spread and establish dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Spodiopogon dubius plays an important role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
