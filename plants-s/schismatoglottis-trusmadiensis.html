Schismatoglottis trusmadiensis

Schismatoglottis trusmadiensis, commonly known as the Togo mountain orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Cameroon and Nigeria.

It is a herbaceous plant with erect, hairy stems and features showy, spurred flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Schismatoglottis trusmadiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
