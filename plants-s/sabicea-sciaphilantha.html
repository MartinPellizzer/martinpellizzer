Sabicea sciaphilantha

Sabicea sciaphilantha, commonly known as the shade-loving acanthus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately compound leaves that are often adorned with silky, white hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
