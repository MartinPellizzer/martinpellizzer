Solanum indicum subsp. newtonii

Solanum indicum subsp.

newtonii, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its potential toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
