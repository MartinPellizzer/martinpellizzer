Solanum paniculatum

Solanum paniculatum, commonly known as the "cape gooseberry," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible and rich in nutrients. Its botanical characteristics include a pentagonal calyx and a fleshy, aromatic fruit.

Solanum paniculatum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its medicinal and culinary uses.
