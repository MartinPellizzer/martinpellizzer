Syzygium guineense var. urophyllum

Syzygium guineense var. urophyllum, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces large, thick-skinned fruits that are typically deep red when ripe. The fruit's pericarp contains a rich, aromatic pulp that is highly valued for its flavor and medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
