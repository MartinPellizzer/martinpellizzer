Sedum anopetalum subvar. sulfureum

Sedum anopetalum subvar. sulfureum, commonly known as the golden sedum, is a succulent plant native to the western United States.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically yellow to orange in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
