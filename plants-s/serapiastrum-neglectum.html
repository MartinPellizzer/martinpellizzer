Serapiastrum neglectum

Serapiastrum neglectum, commonly known as the Southernwood, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported digestive and antispasmodic properties.

Serapiastrum neglectum is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
