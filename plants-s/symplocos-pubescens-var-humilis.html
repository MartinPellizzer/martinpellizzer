Symplocos pubescens var. humilis

Symplocos pubescens var. humilis, commonly known as the lowland wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
