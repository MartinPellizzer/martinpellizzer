Sambucus cerulea var. arizonica

Sambucus cerulea var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona elderberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clustered inflorescences, followed by dark purple berries. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
