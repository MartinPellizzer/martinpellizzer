Solidago canadensis var. hargeri

Solidago canadensis var. hargeri, commonly known as the Hargery's goldenrod, is a variant of the common goldenrod native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems covered in bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
