Syringa amurensis var. rotundifolia

Syringa amurensis var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the Amur roseleaf lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.

This variety is also noted for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a popular choice in landscaping.
