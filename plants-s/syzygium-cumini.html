Syzygium cumini

Syzygium cumini, commonly known as Java plum or Indian blackberry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature, with a tart and slightly acidic flavor. Syzygium cumini is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.

Its fruit is also used in culinary applications and as a source of natural dye.
