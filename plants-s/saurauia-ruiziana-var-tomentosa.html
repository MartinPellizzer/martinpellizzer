Saurauia ruiziana var. tomentosa

Saurauia ruiziana var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy saurauia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves. The flowers are small, clustered, and often white or pale pink, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
