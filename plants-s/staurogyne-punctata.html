Staurogyne punctata

Staurogyne punctata, commonly known as the punctate waterstar, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often dotted with tiny, raised spots, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes form dense mats on the substrate, which can help stabilize the sediment.

Staurogyne punctata is often used in aquatic gardening for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
