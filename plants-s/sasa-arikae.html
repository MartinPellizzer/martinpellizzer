Sasa arikae

Sasa arikae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that form dense clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a grass-like texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Sasa arikae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and traditional landscaping.
