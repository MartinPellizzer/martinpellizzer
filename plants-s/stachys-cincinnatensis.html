Stachys cincinnatensis

Stachys cincinnatensis, commonly known as the Cincinnati hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Stachys cincinnatensis is often found in the Ohio River valley and surrounding regions.
