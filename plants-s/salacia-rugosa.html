Salacia rugosa

Salacia rugosa, commonly known as the rough salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, leathery leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Salacia rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
