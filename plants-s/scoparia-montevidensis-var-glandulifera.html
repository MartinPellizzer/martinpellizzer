Scoparia montevidensis var. glandulifera

Scoparia montevidensis var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular scoparia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinct glandular structure, contributing to its unique appearance.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its vibrant yellow flowers.
