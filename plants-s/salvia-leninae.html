Salvia leninae

Salvia leninae, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and fragrant leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

Salvia leninae is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in indigenous communities.
