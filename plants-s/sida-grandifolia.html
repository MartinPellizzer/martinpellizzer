Sida grandifolia

Sida grandifolia, commonly known as Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Sida grandifolia is an herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local wildlife and stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
