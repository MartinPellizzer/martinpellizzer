Sasa albomarginata f. minor

Sasa albomarginata f. minor, commonly known as the white-edged bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa albomarginata species.

It is a clump-forming shrub that belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to eastern Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white margin. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is due to its elegant appearance and ability to form dense, compact foliage.
