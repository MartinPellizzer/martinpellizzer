Scrophularia integrifolia

Scrophularia integrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved figwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes various species known for their ornamental and medicinal value. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a hairy texture, and its flowers are tubular, often in shades of pink or purple.

Scrophularia integrifolia is valued in gardens for its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
