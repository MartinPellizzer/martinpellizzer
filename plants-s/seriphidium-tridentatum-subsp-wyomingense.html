Seriphidium tridentatum subsp. wyomingense

Seriphidium tridentatum subsp.

wyomingense, commonly known as the Wyoming tansy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties.

This subspecies is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
