Senecio polyphyllus var. antucensis

Senecio polyphyllus var. antucensis, commonly known as the Antucoensis groundsels, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its small, yellow flowers bloom in summer, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
