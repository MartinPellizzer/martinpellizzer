Schizophragma crassum var. ellipticum

Schizophragma crassum var. ellipticum, commonly known as the broadleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow. Its berries are small, dark berries that mature in late summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
