Sophora heptaphylla

Sophora heptaphylla, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its long, drooping clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces flat, brown pods that contain several seeds.

Sophora heptaphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
