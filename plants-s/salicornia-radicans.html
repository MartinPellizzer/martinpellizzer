Salicornia radicans

Salicornia radicans, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or pink in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, and it relies on wind pollination. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, which help minimize water loss in arid conditions.

Salicornia radicans is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats around the world.
