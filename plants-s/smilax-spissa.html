Smilax spissa

Smilax spissa, commonly known as red root, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax spissa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported health benefits.
