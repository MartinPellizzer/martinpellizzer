Stegania discolor

Stegania discolor, commonly known as the green bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters along the stems. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Stegania discolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
