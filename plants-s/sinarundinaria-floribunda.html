Sinarundinaria floribunda

Sinarundinaria floribunda, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, arborescent form, with multiple erect stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The fronds are pinnately compound, with lanceolate pinnae that are often adorned with small, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky outcrops.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its striking appearance.
