Syngonanthus poggeanus

Syngonanthus poggeanus, commonly known as the Bogotá grass or Bogotá sedge, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude grasslands of the Andes in Colombia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is often found in open, sunny areas and is adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its native habitat.

Syngonanthus poggeanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in maintaining biodiversity in its native region.
