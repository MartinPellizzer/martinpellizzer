Spergula imbricata

Spergula imbricata, commonly known as the common bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growth and is characterized by its fine, narrow leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along riverbanks, and in disturbed areas. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a capacity for vegetative reproduction through rhizomes.

Spergula imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
