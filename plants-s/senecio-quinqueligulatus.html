Senecio quinqueligulatus

Senecio quinqueligulatus, commonly known as the five-lobed groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves with distinct lobes. The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a daisy-like head. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
