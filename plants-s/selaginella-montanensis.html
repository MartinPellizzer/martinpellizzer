Selaginella montanensis

Selaginella montanensis, commonly known as the mountain spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a stem with spirally arranged leaves that are often needle-like in shape. Selaginella montanensis reproduces via spores, which are produced in structures called sporophylls.

It is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
