Sebaea rehmannii

Sebaea rehmannii, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bracts that surround its small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Sebaea rehmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
