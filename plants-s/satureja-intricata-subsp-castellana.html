Satureja intricata subsp. castellana

Satureja intricata subsp.

castellana, commonly known as Castellana savory, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and southern France. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or violet, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

The leaves are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine for their pungent, aromatic properties.
