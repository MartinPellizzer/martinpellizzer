Sabicea

Sabicea, commonly known as the "sabicea genus," is a group of flowering plants belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. They are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. Sabicea species often have stipules and produce pods that contain seeds.
