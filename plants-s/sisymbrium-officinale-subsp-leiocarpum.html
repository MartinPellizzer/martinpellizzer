Sisymbrium officinale subsp. leiocarpum

Sisymbrium officinale subsp.

leiocarpum, commonly known as the smoothpod wild radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pinnately divided leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its fruits are slender, pod-like structures that remain attached to the plant after maturity.

This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it often grows in disturbed soils and open habitats.
