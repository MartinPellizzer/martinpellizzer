Solidago veracruziensis

Solidago veracruziensis, commonly known as Mexican goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or riparian habitats. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native gardens.
