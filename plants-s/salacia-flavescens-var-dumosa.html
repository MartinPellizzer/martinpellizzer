Salacia flavescens var. dumosa

Salacia flavescens var. dumosa, commonly known as the thick-stemmed salacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and dense foliage, which provide habitat for various small animals and insects. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, yellowish fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
