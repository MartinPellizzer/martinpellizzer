Sabulina decandra

Sabulina decandra, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arctic or alpine regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate low nutrient conditions.

Sabulina decandra plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitats.
