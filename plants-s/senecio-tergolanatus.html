Senecio tergolanatus

Senecio tergolanatus, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Senecio tergolanatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
