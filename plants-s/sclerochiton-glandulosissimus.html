Sclerochiton glandulosissimus

Sclerochiton glandulosissimus, commonly known as the hairy sclerochiton, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
