Salmia palmata

Salmia palmata, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that resemble those of a palm. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, similar to dates. It thrives in warm, arid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Salmia palmata plays an important role in both ecological and agricultural systems in its native regions.
