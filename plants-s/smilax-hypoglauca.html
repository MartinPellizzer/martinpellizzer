Smilax hypoglauca

Smilax hypoglauca, commonly known as the pale-green smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in sharp, spiny projections. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a waxy, pale green appearance.

Smilax hypoglauca is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and thickets.
