Stipa comata subsp. intonsa

Stipa comata subsp.

intonsa, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive, soft appearance.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Stipa comata subsp.

intonsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky environments.
