Sasa tonamimontana

Sasa tonamimontana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that grow in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a deep green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Sasa tonamimontana is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

It thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
