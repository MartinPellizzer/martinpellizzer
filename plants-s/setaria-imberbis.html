Setaria imberbis

Setaria imberbis, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa and widely cultivated in Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bristly, spike-like inflorescences that resemble a fox's tail. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it a valuable crop in arid regions. Its seeds are rich in carbohydrates and protein, contributing to both human and animal nutrition.

Setaria imberbis also plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.
