Solanum angustum

Solanum angustum, commonly known as the narrowleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp.

Solanum angustum is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its preference for warm, sunny environments.
