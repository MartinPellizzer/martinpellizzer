Salicornia biennis

Salicornia biennis, commonly known as sea beet, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae, characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by hard, triangular seeds. It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and estuaries, often forming dense vegetation.

Salicornia biennis is also cultivated for its edible shoots, which are used in various culinary applications.
