Smilax barbillana

Smilax barbillana, commonly known as the long-stemmed cat's claw, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of sharp, curved spines along its stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinct veining pattern.

Smilax barbillana is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and thickets.
