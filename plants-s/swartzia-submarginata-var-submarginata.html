Swartzia submarginata var. submarginata

Swartzia submarginata var. submarginata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown wood, which is highly valued for its tonal qualities in musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are often fragrant. Its fruit is a pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and timber use, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for both aesthetic and economic purposes.
