Solanum carolinense var. hirsutum

Solanum carolinense var. hirsutum, commonly known as the Carolina nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a species of ecological interest.
