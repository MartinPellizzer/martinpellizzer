Senecio ehrenbergianus

Senecio ehrenbergianus, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh desert conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio ehrenbergianus is often found in crevices and along rocky outcrops, showcasing its resilience in extreme environments.
