Stachys plumosa subsp. freynii

Stachys plumosa subsp.

freynii, commonly known as the Freyn's hairy betony, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, soft, grayish-green foliage.

The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and xeriscaping.
