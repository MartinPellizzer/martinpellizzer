Solanum selachophyllum var. seorsum

Solanum selachophyllum var. seorsum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the remote regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its unique, translucent, waxy leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts.
