Solanum discolor var. procumbens

Solanum discolor var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy surface and a bluish-green coloration. Its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive purple calyx, and it produces small, oval berries that are yellow when mature.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
