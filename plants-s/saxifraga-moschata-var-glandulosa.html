Saxifraga moschata var. glandulosa

Saxifraga moschata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, opposite leaves with glandular dots, and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
