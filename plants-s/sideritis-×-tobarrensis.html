Sideritis × tobarrensis

Sideritis × tobarrensis, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in rocky or dry soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
