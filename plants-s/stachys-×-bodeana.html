Stachys × bodeana

Stachys × bodeana, commonly known as the hybrid deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Stachys × bodeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.
