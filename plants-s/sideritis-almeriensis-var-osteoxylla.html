Sideritis almeriensis var. osteoxylla

Sideritis almeriensis var. osteoxylla, commonly known as the Almeria sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to southern Spain.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Mediterranean regions.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
