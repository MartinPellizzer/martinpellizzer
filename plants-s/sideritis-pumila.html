Sideritis pumila

Sideritis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf ironwort, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or pale purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Sideritis pumila is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
