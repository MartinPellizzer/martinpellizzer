Senecio maldonadensis

Senecio maldonadensis, commonly known as the Maldonadensis groundsels, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
