Setaria eylesii

Setaria eylesii, commonly known as Eyles' foxtail, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that resemble a foxtail. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Setaria eylesii is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
