Sasaella tanzawana

Sasaella tanzawana, commonly known as Tanzanian sasa, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is native to the highland regions of Tanzania and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of thatching material.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
