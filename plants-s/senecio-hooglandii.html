Senecio hooglandii

Senecio hooglandii, commonly known as the Hoogland's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio hooglandii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
