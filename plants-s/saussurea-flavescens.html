Saussurea flavescens

Saussurea flavescens, commonly known as the yellow saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, thriving in cold, high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Saussurea flavescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
