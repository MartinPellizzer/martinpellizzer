Syzygium quadratum

Syzygium quadratum, commonly known as the Australian apple or red apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Syzygium quadratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
