Solanum bolivianum

Solanum bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple, and it produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not suitable for consumption but is studied for its potential medicinal compounds.
