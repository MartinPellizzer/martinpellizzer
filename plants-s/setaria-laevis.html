Setaria laevis

Setaria laevis, commonly known as silky millet, is an annual grass species native to Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silky texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it a hardy and adaptable crop.

Setaria laevis is often used as a forage crop and in traditional medicine due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
