Semecarpus anacardium var. cuneifolius

Semecarpus anacardium var. cuneifolius, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, with elliptical to lanceolate leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spiny, woody fruits containing a single edible seed. Its bark is thick and rough, often shedding in strips, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and autumn color.
