Seriphidium

Seriphidium, commonly known as desert sandthrift, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. They are characterized by their spiny stems and small, often yellow or white flowers. Many species are drought-tolerant and have adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or as ornamental plants.
