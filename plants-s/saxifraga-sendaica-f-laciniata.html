Saxifraga sendaica f. laciniata

Saxifraga sendaica f. laciniata, commonly known as the lace-leaf saxifrage, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, deeply lobed leaves that give it a feathery appearance, and its small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in alpine meadows. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful protection.
