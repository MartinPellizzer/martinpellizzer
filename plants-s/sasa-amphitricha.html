Sasa amphitricha

Sasa amphitricha, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Sasa amphitricha is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a persistent and resilient plant in its native habitat.
