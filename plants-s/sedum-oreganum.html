Sedum oreganum

Sedum oreganum, commonly known as orange stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the western United States.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in the summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its scientific name reflects its historical use by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
