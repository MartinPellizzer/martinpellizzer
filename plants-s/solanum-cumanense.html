Solanum cumanense

Solanum cumanense, commonly known as the Cumanese nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale green.

The fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.
