Satureja consentina var. hirsuta

Satureja consentina var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in terminal clusters, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

The essential oils of this plant contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
