Synoplectris

Synoplectris, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

These plants are native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where they thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Synoplectris species are typically shrubs or subshrubs with narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow to golden flowers. They often grow in rocky soils and are adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The genus includes several species, such as Synoplectris arizonica and Synoplectris torreyi, which are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
