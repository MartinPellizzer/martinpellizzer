Stelis leprina

Stelis leprina, commonly known as the Leprous Stelis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical forests. Its leaves are usually ovate and covered in a fine, pubescent layer, contributing to its unique appearance.

Stelis leprina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
