Saccolabium triflorum

Saccolabium triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in groups of three, giving it its common name. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Saccolabium triflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
