Syzygium ovatifolium

Syzygium ovatifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour fruits that are used in traditional cuisines. Syzygium ovatifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Its growth habit includes a small to medium-sized tree or shrub with a dense canopy.
