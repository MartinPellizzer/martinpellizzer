Swertia Perennis Subsp. Cuspidata

Swertia perennis subsp. cuspidata is a lesser-known member of the gentian family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Its small, lance-shaped leaves and clustered blooms make it a unique addition to alpine gardens. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in horticultural circles. This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and ecological significance in its native habitats.
