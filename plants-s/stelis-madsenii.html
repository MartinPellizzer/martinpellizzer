Stelis madsenii

Stelis madsenii, commonly known as the Madsen's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to aid in nutrient absorption.

Stelis madsenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
