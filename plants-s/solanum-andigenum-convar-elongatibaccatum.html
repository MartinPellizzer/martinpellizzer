Solanum andigenum convar. elongatibaccatum

Solanum andigenum convar. elongatibaccatum, commonly known as the Andean tomato, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, fleshy berries that range in color from yellow to red, often resembling small tomatoes. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, white to purple flowers. It thrives in cool, high-altitude climates and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses in traditional Andean cuisine.
