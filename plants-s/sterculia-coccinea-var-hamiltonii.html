Sterculia coccinea var. hamiltonii

Sterculia coccinea var. hamiltonii, commonly known as the red-stemmed sterculia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has smooth, often reddish stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
