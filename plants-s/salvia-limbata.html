Salvia limbata

Salvia limbata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia limbata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
