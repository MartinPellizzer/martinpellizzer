Syzygium inophylloides

Syzygium inophylloides, commonly known as the mountain satinash, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the wetter regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round, edible fruits that are often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value is notable due to its attractive foliage and floral display, making it a popular choice for horticultural purposes.

However, it is relatively rare in the wild and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.
