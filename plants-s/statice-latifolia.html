Statice latifolia

Statice latifolia, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Statice latifolia is also popular in horticulture for its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms.
