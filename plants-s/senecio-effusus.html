Senecio effusus

Senecio effusus, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
