Smithia trochainii

Smithia trochainii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Smithia trochainii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
