Sempervivum heuffelii var. kopaonikense

Sempervivum heuffelii var. kopaonikense, commonly known as the Kopaonik houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit with fleshy, often blue-green leaves. The plant produces tall, flowering stems with small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
