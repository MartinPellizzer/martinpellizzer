Solanum clarkiae

Solanum clarkiae, commonly known as Clark's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, which bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a smooth, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

Like other members of the Solanaceae family, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
