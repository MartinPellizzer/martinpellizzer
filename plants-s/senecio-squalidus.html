Senecio squalidus

Senecio squalidus, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It has alternate, rough, and hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, producing seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
