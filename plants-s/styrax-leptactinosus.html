Styrax leptactinosus

Styrax leptactinosus, commonly known as the fragrant styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a slightly serrated margin.

Styrax leptactinosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
