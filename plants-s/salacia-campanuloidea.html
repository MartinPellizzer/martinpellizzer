Salacia campanuloidea

Salacia campanuloidea, commonly known as the bellflower salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Salacia campanuloidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
