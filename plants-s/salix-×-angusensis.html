Salix × angusensis

Salix × angusensis, commonly known as the hybrid weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to the United Kingdom.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix alba and Salix cinerea, resulting in a plant with distinctive cascading branches. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery-green underside that adds to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This species is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is commonly planted in gardens and along riverbanks.
