Senecio koelzii

Senecio koelzii, commonly known as the Koelz's groundsels, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is known for its bright yellow flowers and hairy, silver-green foliage. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, dry climates.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
