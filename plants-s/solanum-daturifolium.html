Solanum daturifolium

Solanum daturifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that contain toxic compounds, making it harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated, though caution is advised due to its poisonous properties.
