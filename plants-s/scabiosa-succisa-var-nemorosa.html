Scabiosa succisa var. nemorosa

Scabiosa succisa var. nemorosa, commonly known as wood poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers resembling poppies. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy and often emit a mild scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Wood poppy is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas, making it a popular choice for naturalistic garden designs.
