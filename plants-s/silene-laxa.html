Silene laxa

Silene laxa, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Silene laxa thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
