Salacca blumeana

Salacca blumeana, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces edible inflorescences that are harvested for their sweet, sticky sap. Its leaves are used in traditional thatching and as roofing material.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits in tropical environments.
