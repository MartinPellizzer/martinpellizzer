Senecio jagensis

Senecio jagensis, commonly known as the jaguar's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing a succulent-like structure to store water. Its bright yellow flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Senecio jagensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally by indigenous communities for various health purposes.
