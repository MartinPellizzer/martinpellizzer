Syzygium banksii

Syzygium banksii, commonly known as the Banks' apple, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are yellow when ripe.

Syzygium banksii is often found in wet sclerophyll forests and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
