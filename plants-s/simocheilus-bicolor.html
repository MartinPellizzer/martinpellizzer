Simocheilus bicolor

Simocheilus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly contrasting leaf colors, with a dark green upper surface and a pale, almost white underside. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its flowers are small and white, typically appearing in the early morning.

The leaves are broad and floating, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
