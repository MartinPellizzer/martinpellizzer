Solanecio tuberosus var. tuberosus

Solanecio tuberosus var. tuberosus, commonly known as the purple potato, is a tuberous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It produces edible underground tubers that are typically purple or violet in color, though they can also be white or yellow. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to the common potato. Its tubers are rich in starch and have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.

The plant is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
