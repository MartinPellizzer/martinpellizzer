Sedum hexangulare

Sedum hexangulare, commonly known as the six-angled stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, six-sided stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Sedum hexangulare is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
