Sonchus bourgeaui

Sonchus bourgeaui, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has a taproot system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its rapid growth, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
