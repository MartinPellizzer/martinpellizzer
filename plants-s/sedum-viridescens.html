Sedum viridescens

Sedum viridescens, commonly known as the silver stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the eastern United States.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including USDA hardiness zones 4 to 9.
