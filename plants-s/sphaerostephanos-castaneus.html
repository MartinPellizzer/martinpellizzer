Sphaerostephanos castaneus

Sphaerostephanos castaneus, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a distinctive, spherical seed head that persists through winter, adding ornamental value to the landscape. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance.

Sphaerostephanos castaneus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
