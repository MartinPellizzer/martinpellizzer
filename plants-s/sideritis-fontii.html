Sideritis fontii

Sideritis fontii, commonly known as the Font's sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Sideritis fontii is valued for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
