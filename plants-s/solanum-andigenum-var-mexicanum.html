Solanum andigenum var. mexicanum

Solanum andigenum var. mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically features hairy stems and alternate leaves with a broad ovate shape. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, spherical berries that are toxic to humans.

This species is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes.
