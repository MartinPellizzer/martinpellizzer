Samyda grandiflora

Samyda grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered samyda, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, attracting various pollinators. Samyda grandiflora thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm, tropical climate.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in suitable regions.
