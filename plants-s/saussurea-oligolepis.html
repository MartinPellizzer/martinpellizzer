Saussurea oligolepis

Saussurea oligolepis, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It features a robust, woody stem with dense, grayish-blue foliage that is adapted to cold and arid conditions. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange composite flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, valued in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

Saussurea oligolepis is an important species in alpine ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and biodiversity in its native habitat.
