Sesleria coerulans

Sesleria coerulans, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, spike-like flower clusters. The flowers are typically blue or purple, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Sesleria coerulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
