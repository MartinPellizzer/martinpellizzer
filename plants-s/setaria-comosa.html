Setaria comosa

Setaria comosa, commonly known as hairy dropseed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a grass species that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Setaria comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
