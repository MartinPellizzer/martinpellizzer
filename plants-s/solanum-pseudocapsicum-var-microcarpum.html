Solanum pseudocapsicum var. microcarpum

Solanum pseudocapsicum var. microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, egg-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. Its fruit is a dry, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for food or ornamental purposes.
