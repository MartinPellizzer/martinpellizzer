Salvia yosgadensis

Salvia yosgadensis, commonly known as the Yosgadensis sage, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of Peru.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Its unique habitat and appearance make it a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
