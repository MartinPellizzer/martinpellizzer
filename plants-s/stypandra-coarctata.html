Stypandra coarctata

Stypandra coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stypandra, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy soils, often in coastal or inland heathlands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding insects.

Stypandra coarctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and seasonal blooms.
