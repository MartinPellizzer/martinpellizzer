Syngonanthus imbricatus

Syngonanthus imbricatus, commonly known as the coastal beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets and are usually green or pale in color.

Syngonanthus imbricatus plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
