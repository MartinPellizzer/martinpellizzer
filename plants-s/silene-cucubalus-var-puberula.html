Silene cucubalus var. puberula

Silene cucubalus var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and features pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
