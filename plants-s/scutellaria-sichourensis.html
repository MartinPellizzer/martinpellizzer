Scutellaria sichourensis

Scutellaria sichourensis, commonly known as the Sichuan skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within forested areas.

Scutellaria sichourensis is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
