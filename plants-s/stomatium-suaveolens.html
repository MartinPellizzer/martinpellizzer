Stomatium suaveolens

Stomatium suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that emit a sweet, spicy aroma. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The stomatium, a specialized structure in its flowers, plays a role in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.
