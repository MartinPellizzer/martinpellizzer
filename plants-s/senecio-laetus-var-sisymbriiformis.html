Senecio laetus var. sisymbriiformis

Senecio laetus var. sisymbriiformis, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features silver-white, woolly foliage that reflects sunlight, helping to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
