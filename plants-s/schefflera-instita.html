Schefflera instita

Schefflera instita, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. Schefflera instita thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
