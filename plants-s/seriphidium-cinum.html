Seriphidium cinum

Seriphidium cinum, commonly known as the gray thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and bract-covered stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually grayish-green with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are small and yellow.
