Sapium marginatum var. stenophyllum

Sapium marginatum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape, with a narrow margin and a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that contain toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
