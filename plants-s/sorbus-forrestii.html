Sorbus forrestii

Sorbus forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Sorbus forrestii is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
