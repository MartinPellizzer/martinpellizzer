Stevia dissoluta

Stevia dissoluta, commonly known as the bitter stevia, is a species native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It is a perennial shrub belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are typically smaller and more intensely flavored compared to other stevia species.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for cultivation in subtropical climates.
