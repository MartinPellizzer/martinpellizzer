Schoenoplectiella laevis

Schoenoplectiella laevis, commonly known as the smooth marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in temperate climates. It plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its smooth, hairless appearance distinguishes it from other species in the Cyperaceae family.
