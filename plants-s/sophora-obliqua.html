Sophora obliqua

Sophora obliqua, commonly known as the Andean lupine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spiny pods. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sophora obliqua is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil in its native habitat.
