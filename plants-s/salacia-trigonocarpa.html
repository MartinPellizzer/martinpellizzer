Salacia trigonocarpa

Salacia trigonocarpa, commonly known as the three-angled salacia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Salaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive three-angled stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough, leathery texture.

Salacia trigonocarpa is often found in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests.
