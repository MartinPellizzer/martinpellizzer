Soldanella alpina var. minima

Soldanella alpina var. minima, commonly known as the alpine soldanella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features delicate, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinctive spurred petal. Its leaves are small, rounded, and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh alpine environment.

This variety is notable for its compact size and is often found in mountainous areas across the Alps and the Pyrenees.
