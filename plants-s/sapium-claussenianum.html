Sapium claussenianum

Sapium claussenianum, commonly known as the Brazilian horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, fleshy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been introduced to various parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
