Solidago vespertina

Solidago vespertina, commonly known as evening goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Solidago vespertina plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late season.
