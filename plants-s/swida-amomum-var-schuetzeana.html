Swida amomum var. schuetzeana

Swida amomum var. schuetzeana, commonly known as the Schuetze's star anise, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped flowers and aromatic, woody fruits that resemble those of the star anise. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in specialized botanical gardens.
