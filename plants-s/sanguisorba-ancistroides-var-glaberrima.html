Sanguisorba ancistroides var. glaberrima

Sanguisorba ancistroides var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth sanguisorba, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, reddish to pinkish flowers. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture, though the variety glaberrima is noted for its smooth, glabrous surface. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and other wetland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
