Scirpus savii var. filiformis

Scirpus savii var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical inflorescences that produce small, clustered flowers. This variety thrives in marshy or aquatic environments, often forming dense stands that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife. Its roots are adapted to waterlogged conditions, and it plays a role in nutrient cycling within wetland ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland restoration projects.
