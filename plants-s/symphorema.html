Symphorema

Symphorema, commonly known as the Australian wattle, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their distinctive yellow or golden flowers. Symphorema species typically have compound leaves and produce small, pod-like fruits. They are often found in a variety of habitats, including forests, woodlands, and coastal areas.

The genus is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
