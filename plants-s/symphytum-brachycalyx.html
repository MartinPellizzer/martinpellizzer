Symphytum brachycalyx

Symphytum brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for its healing potential.
