Scirpus validus f. dutillyanus

Scirpus validus f. dutillyanus, commonly known as the dwarf bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush genus Scirpus.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it helps stabilize soil and support aquatic life.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
