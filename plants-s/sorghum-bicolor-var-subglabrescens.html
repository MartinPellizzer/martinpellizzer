Sorghum bicolor var. subglabrescens

Sorghum bicolor var. subglabrescens, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a variant of sorghum characterized by its shorter stature and reduced growth habit.

It is native to Africa and is cultivated for its high biomass yield and adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, pale green to brown flowers. It is often used in forage production and as a source of biofuel due to its rapid growth and drought tolerance.

Its botanical structure makes it a valuable species in agricultural and ecological studies.
