Saxifraga geranioides var. typica

Saxifraga geranioides var. typica, commonly known as the typical saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
