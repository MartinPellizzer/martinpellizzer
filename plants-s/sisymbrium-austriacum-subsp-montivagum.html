Sisymbrium austriacum subsp. montivagum

Sisymbrium austriacum subsp.

montivagum, commonly known as the alpine mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas and is adapted to cold climates.
