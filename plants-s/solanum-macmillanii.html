Solanum macmillanii

Solanum macmillanii, commonly known as the Macmillan's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum macmillanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
