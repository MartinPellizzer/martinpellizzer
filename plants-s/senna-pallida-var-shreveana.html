Senna pallida var. shreveana

Senna pallida var. shreveana, commonly known as the Shreve senna, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Senna pallida var. shreveana is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
