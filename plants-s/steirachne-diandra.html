Steirachne diandra

Steirachne diandra, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated. Steirachne diandra thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
