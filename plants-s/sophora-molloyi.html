Sophora molloyi

Sophora molloyi, commonly known as the Molloy's Sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
