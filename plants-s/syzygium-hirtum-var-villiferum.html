Syzygium hirtum var. villiferum

Syzygium hirtum var. villiferum, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, ovate leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
