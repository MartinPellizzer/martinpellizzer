Smilax morsaniana

Smilax morsaniana, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a perennial vine native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its sharp, thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Smilax morsaniana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It is a hardy plant that can tolerate a variety of soil types and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
