Solanum indigoferum

Solanum indigoferum, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, purple berries that resemble small eggs. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small white or purple flowers.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their ornamental appeal.
