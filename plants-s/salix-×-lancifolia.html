Salix × lancifolia

Salix × lancifolia, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a distinctive cascading appearance. The plant typically grows in moist soils near rivers, lakes, and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, dark green upper surface.

Salix × lancifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize riverbanks through its extensive root system.
