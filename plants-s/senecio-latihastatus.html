Senecio latihastatus

Senecio latihastatus, commonly known as the African Sunset or Blue Stemmed Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually blue-gray in color, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senecio latihastatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
