Scrophularia umbrosa subsp. robusta

Scrophularia umbrosa subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the robust scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.

The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
