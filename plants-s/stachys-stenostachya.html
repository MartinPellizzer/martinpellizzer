Stachys stenostachya

Stachys stenostachya, commonly known as narrow-stemmed betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces spikes of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to violet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Stachys stenostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
