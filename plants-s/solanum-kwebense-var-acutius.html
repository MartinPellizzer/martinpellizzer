Solanum kwebense var. acutius

Solanum kwebense var. acutius, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that start green and mature to a deep red. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
