Scirpoides holoschoenus subsp. thunbergii

Scirpoides holoschoenus subsp.

thunbergii, commonly known as the Thunberg's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
