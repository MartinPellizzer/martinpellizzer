Silene meyeri var. delectabilis

Silene meyeri var. delectabilis, commonly known as the sweet-scented catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

This variety is noted for its pleasant, sweet fragrance, which is more pronounced in the evening.
