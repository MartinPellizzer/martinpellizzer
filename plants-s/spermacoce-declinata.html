Spermacoce declinata

Spermacoce declinata, commonly known as the spreading false mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated, bladder-like seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Spermacoce declinata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
