Schoenoplectus etuberculatus subsp. nipponicus

Schoenoplectus etuberculatus subsp.

nipponicus, commonly known as the Japanese bulrush, is a wetland plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters in marshy or aquatic environments.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in wetland ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in water filtration and habitat provision for various wildlife species.
