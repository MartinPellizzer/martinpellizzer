Solanum mexiae

Solanum mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces small, round berries that change color from green to red as they mature. Solanum mexiae is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.
