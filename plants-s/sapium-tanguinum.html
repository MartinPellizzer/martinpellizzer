Sapium tanguinum

Sapium tanguinum, commonly known as the Tanguy tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red, spiny fruits that contain seeds.

It is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
