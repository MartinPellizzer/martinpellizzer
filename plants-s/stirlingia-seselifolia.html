Stirlingia seselifolia

Stirlingia seselifolia, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain seeds with feathery appendages, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and extended blooming period.
