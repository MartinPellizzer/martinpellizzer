Sagus spinosa

Sagus spinosa, commonly known as the spiny saguaro, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red fruits that contain edible seeds. Sagus spinosa is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
