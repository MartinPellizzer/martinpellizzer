Seriphidium leucodes

Seriphidium leucodes, commonly known as whiteleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months.

Seriphidium leucodes is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
