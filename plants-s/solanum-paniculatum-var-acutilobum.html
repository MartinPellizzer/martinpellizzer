Solanum paniculatum var. acutilobum

Solanum paniculatum var. acutilobum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features branched stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries. Its leaves and berries contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
