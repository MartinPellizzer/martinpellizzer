Schisandra coccinea

Schisandra coccinea, commonly known as red sanders, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries, which are clustered in small, spherical inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven ovate leaflets, and its stems are woody with a reddish-brown color. Schisandra coccinea is known for its medicinal properties, with the berries containing bioactive compounds such as lignans.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
