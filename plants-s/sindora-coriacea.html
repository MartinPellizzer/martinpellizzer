Sindora coriacea

Sindora coriacea, commonly known as the rough-skin tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rough bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Sindora coriacea is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
