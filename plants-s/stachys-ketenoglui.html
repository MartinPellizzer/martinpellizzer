Stachys ketenoglui

Stachys ketenoglui, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features dense, woolly hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Stachys ketenoglui is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
