Salix × flueggeana

Salix × flueggeana, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind.

The weeping willow is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
