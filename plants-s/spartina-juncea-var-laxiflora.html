Spartina juncea var. laxiflora

Spartina juncea var. laxiflora, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a halophytic grass species native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in brackish and saline wetlands, playing a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and preventing erosion. The plant has long, slender blades and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand tidal flooding and has a fibrous root system that helps bind sediments.

Spartina juncea var. laxiflora is an important component of estuarine ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
