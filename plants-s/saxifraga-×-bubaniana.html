Saxifraga × bubaniana

Saxifraga × bubaniana, commonly known as the pink saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often in moist, shaded areas. The plant features rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, pink or reddish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in its natural habitat.

Saxifraga × bubaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
