Seriphidium mogoltavicum

Seriphidium mogoltavicum, commonly known as the Mogoltav wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as small, hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually yellow to pale green, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Seriphidium mogoltavicum is often found in rocky soils and is part of the local flora in its native habitat.
