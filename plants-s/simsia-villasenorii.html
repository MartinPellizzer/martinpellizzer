Simsia villasenorii

Simsia villasenorii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance blooms.

Simsia villasenorii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
