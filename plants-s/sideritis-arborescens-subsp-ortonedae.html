Sideritis arborescens subsp. ortonedae

Sideritis arborescens subsp.

ortonedae, commonly known as the Ortone's rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and small, aromatic leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
