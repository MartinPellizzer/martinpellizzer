Salvia divaricata

Salvia divaricata, commonly known as woodland sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered with pairs of opposite, gray-green, ovate leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both landscape and container gardening.
