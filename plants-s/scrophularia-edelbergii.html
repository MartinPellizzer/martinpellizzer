Scrophularia edelbergii

Scrophularia edelbergii, commonly known as Edelberg's figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous areas. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with similar floral structures.

Edelberg's figwort is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
