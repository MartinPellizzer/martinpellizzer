Spathoglottis gracilis

Spathoglottis gracilis, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded forest floors. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers in a loose spiral. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure.

Spathoglottis gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegance.
