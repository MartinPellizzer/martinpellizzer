Styrosinia coccinea

Styrosinia coccinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed beauty, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in dense undergrowth. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Styrosinia coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
