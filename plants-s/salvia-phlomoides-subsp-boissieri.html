Salvia phlomoides subsp. boissieri

Salvia phlomoides subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, woody stems covered in dense, grayish-green foliage.

The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
