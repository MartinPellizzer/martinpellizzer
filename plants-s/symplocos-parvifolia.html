Symplocos parvifolia

Symplocos parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered with a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
