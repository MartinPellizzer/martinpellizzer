Symphyandra pendula

Symphyandra pendula, commonly known as the hanging symphyandra, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which consist of clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
