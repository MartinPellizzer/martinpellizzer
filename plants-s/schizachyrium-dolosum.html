Schizachyrium dolosum

Schizachyrium dolosum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Schizachyrium dolosum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
