Saussurea sieboldiana

Saussurea sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
