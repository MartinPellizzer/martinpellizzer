Salix × hiraoana var. brachystachys

Salix × hiraoana var. brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked willow, is a hybrid willow species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green catkins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface, often turning reddish in autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
