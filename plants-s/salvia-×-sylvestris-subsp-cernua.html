Salvia × sylvestris subsp. cernua

Salvia × sylvestris subsp.

cernua, commonly known as woodland sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable nectar source for pollinators.
