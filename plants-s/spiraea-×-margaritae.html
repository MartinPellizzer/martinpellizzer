Spiraea × margaritae

Spiraea × margaritae, commonly known as the snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and its showy, spherical clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea × margaritae is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
