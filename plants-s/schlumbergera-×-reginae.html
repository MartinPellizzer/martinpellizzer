Schlumbergera × reginae

Schlumbergera × reginae, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, flattened stems that form a branching structure, and its vibrant flowers that bloom in winter. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is popular as a houseplant due to its long blooming period and ability to thrive indoors.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a favored ornamental plant.
