Sophia sagittata f. ciliata

Sophia sagittata f. ciliata, commonly known as the ciliated arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive ciliated margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
