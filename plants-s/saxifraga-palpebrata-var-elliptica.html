Saxifraga palpebrata var. elliptica

Saxifraga palpebrata var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are elliptical in shape, often covered in a fine layer of white or pale-colored hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and visually striking addition to rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
