Silene chirensis var. schweinfurthii

Silene chirensis var. schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
