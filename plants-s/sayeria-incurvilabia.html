Sayeria incurvilabia

Sayeria incurvilabia, commonly known as the zigzag acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces flat, papery pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Sayeria incurvilabia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
