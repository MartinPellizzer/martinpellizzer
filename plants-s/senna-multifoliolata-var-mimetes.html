Senna multifoliolata var. mimetes

Senna multifoliolata var. mimetes, commonly known as the mimetic senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into numerous small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
