Sebastiania corniculata var. genuina

Sebastiania corniculata var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue berries that appear in clusters, attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
