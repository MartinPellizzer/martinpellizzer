Scrophularia nervosa var. pruinosopuberula

Scrophularia nervosa var. pruinosopuberula, commonly known as the silky pubescent figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and floral structure, making it a notable species within the Scrophulariaceae family.
