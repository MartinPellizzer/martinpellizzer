Solanum terminale subsp. sanaganum

Solanum terminale subsp.

sanaganum, commonly known as the Sanaganum nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers.

The plant features small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, and it produces berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are usually ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
