Silene cylindriflora

Silene cylindriflora, commonly known as the cylindrical-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene cylindriflora thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often pink or red flowers.
