Solanum englerianum

Solanum englerianum, commonly known as the Engler's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, yellow berries that are edible but can be toxic in large quantities.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
