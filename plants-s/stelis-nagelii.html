Stelis nagelii

Stelis nagelii, commonly known as the Nagel's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a faint fragrance, and it produces numerous small, oval-shaped pseudobulbs.

Stelis nagelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
