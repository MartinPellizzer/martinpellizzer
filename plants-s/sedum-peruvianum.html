Sedum peruvianum

Sedum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens worldwide.
