Sida castanocarpa

Sida castanocarpa, commonly known as the redroot sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sida castanocarpa is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
