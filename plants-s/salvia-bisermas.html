Salvia bisermas

Salvia bisermas, commonly known as purple beard tongue, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and its striking, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia bisermas is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
