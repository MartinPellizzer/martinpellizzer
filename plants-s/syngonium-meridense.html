Syngonium meridense

Syngonium meridense, commonly known as the "Mexican arrowhead," is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf known as a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Syngonium meridense is often used in hanging baskets or as a vine in greenhouses due to its rapid growth and aesthetic appeal.
