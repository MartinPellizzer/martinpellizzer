Serjania sinuata f. ilicifolia

Serjania sinuata f. ilicifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus or Brazilian hibiscus var. ilicifolia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its simple, lanceolate leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Serjania sinuata f. ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
