Salix rorida var. roridiformis

Salix rorida var. roridiformis, commonly known as the weeping willow variant, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist soils near streams or in wetlands, making it a common sight in riparian habitats.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to stabilize riverbanks.
