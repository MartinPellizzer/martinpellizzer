Suaeda sedifolia var. taurica

Suaeda sedifolia var. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric sea bladder, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing a succulent, fleshy structure to store water. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss. The plant produces small, pale green to yellow flowers, often in clusters, during the summer months.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
