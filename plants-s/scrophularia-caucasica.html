Scrophularia caucasica

Scrophularia caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in mountainous areas. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
