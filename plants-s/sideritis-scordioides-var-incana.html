Sideritis scordioides var. incana

Sideritis scordioides var. incana, commonly known as the woolly sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sideritis species are valued for their potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
