Soda florida

Soda florida, commonly known as the Florida soda tree, is a unique species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 feet. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The Florida soda tree is adapted to wetland environments and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
