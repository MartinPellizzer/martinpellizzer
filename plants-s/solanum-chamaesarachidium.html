Solanum chamaesarachidium

Solanum chamaesarachidium, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
