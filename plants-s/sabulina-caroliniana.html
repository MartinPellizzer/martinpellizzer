Sabulina caroliniana

Sabulina caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands. The plant has slender, spreading stems with small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom in early spring.

It is adapted to coastal environments and is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
