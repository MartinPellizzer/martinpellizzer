Salix campestris

Salix campestris, commonly known as the field willow, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks, wet meadows, and other damp habitats. The plant has slender, often zigzagging stems and produces catkins in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated edge.

Salix campestris is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
