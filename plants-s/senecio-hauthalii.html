Senecio hauthalii

Senecio hauthalii, commonly known as the Hauthal's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
