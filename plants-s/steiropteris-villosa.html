Steiropteris villosa

Steiropteris villosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients.

Steiropteris villosa plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various microorganisms and small animals.
