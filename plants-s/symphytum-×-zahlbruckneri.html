Symphytum × zahlbruckneri

Symphytum × zahlbruckneri, commonly known as the Zahler's comfrey, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or blue flowers. The plant is perennial and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland or wetland areas. It has a robust root system that contributes to soil stabilization.

However, due to its potential invasiveness, it is often considered a weed in some regions.
