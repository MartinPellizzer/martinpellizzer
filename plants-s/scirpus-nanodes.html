Scirpus nanodes

Scirpus nanodes, commonly known as the dwarf bulrush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered spikelets that produce tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Scirpus nanodes plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
