Salvia palmae

Salvia palmae, commonly known as the palmleaf sage, is a unique species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, palm-like leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble the fronds of a palm tree. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorls along the stems, which bloom during the summer months. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional herbal practices.

Salvia palmae is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
