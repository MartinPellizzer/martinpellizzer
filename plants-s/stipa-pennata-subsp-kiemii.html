Stipa pennata subsp. kiemii

Stipa pennata subsp.

kiemii, commonly known as the Kiem's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that are often pink to purple in color.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green sheen, and it is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
