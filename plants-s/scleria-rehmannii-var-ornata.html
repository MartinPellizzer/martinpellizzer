Scleria rehmannii var. ornata

Scleria rehmannii var. ornata, commonly known as the ornate umbrella sedge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.
