Solanum dulcamara var. marinum

Solanum dulcamara var. marinum, commonly known as sea holly, is a variant of the bindweed species native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and grows as a climbing or trailing vine. It thrives in saline soils and is often found along shorelines and dunes.

Botanically, it is notable for its glandular hairs and its ability to tolerate high salt concentrations.
