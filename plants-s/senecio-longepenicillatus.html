Senecio longepenicillatus

Senecio longepenicillatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed ragwort, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
