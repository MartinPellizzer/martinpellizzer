Saccolabium aberrans

Saccolabium aberrans, commonly known as the variable saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for extended periods.

Saccolabium aberrans is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
