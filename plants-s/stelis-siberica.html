Stelis siberica

Stelis siberica, commonly known as the Siberian stelis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique adaptations to harsh environments, it is often found in rocky or alpine regions.
