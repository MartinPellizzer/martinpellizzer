Senecio gallicus subsp. coronopifolius

Senecio gallicus subsp.

coronopifolius, commonly known as the crownleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology, which resembles a crown, giving it its common name.
