Solanum cajanumense

Solanum cajanumense, commonly known as the wild tomato or Mexican nightshade, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and ovate, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its potential toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
