Sporobolus fruticulosus

Sporobolus fruticulosus, commonly known as the fruiting spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of spikelets that bear small, fleshy fruits. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in dry conditions, and it often forms dense vegetation in disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough, hairy texture.

Sporobolus fruticulosus is adapted to thrive in environments with low water availability, making it an important component of desert and grassland ecosystems.
