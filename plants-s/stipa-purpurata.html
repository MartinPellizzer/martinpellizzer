Stipa purpurata

Stipa purpurata, commonly known as purple grass or purple feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, feathery panicles that are often tinged with purple or pink hues. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a soft, silky texture that adds a delicate visual appeal.

Stipa purpurata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
