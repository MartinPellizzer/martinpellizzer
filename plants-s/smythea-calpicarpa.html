Smythea calpicarpa

Smythea calpicarpa, commonly known as the mountain grape, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and woody stems, which support its sprawling growth pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Smythea calpicarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
