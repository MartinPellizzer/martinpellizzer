Schleidenia glomerata

Schleidenia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Schleidenia glomerata is often found in moist, sandy soils in woodland areas and is adapted to thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
