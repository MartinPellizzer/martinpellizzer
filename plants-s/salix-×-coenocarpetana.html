Salix × coenocarpetana

Salix × coenocarpetana, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, often adorned with small, oval leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained, fertile soils. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, while its catkins are prominent in early spring.

Salix × coenocarpetana is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
