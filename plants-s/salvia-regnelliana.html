Salvia regnelliana

Salvia regnelliana, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a strong, minty scent.

Salvia regnelliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
