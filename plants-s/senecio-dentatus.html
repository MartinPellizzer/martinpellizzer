Senecio dentatus

Senecio dentatus, commonly known as the toothed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves with distinct tooth-like edges, giving it its scientific name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio dentatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
