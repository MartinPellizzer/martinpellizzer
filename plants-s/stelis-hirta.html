Stelis hirta

Stelis hirta, commonly known as the hairy stelis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. Stelis hirta is often found growing in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth.

Its unique morphology and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
