Strychnos vanprukii var. acuminata

Strychnos vanprukii var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-pointed strychnos, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, often clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is characterized by its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
