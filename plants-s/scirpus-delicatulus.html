Scirpus delicatulus

Scirpus delicatulus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Scirpus delicatulus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
