Sedum cepaea var. gracilescens

Sedum cepaea var. gracilescens, commonly known as the slender stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
