Salix arguta var. lasiandra

Salix arguta var. lasiandra, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and silvery-gray bark that becomes flaky with age. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silky down that gives the plant its common name. It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits that disperse via wind.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
