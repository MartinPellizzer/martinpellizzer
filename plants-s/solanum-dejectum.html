Solanum dejectum

Solanum dejectum, commonly known as the weedy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Solanum dejectum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
