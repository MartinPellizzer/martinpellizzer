Satyrium saxicola

Satyrium saxicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer, and it is pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.

Satyrium saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a specialty plant in horticulture.
