Saussurea dainellii

Saussurea dainellii, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense, yellowish to reddish-orange ray florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cold, arid environments. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Saussurea dainellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking floral display.
