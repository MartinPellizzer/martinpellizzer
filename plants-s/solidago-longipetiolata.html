Solidago longipetiolata

Solidago longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled goldenrod, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped with long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Solidago longipetiolata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
