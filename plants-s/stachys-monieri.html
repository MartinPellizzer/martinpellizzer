Stachys monieri

Stachys monieri, commonly known as redstem deadnettle, is a flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and feature purple or pink petals with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

Stachys monieri is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a food source for certain pollinators.
