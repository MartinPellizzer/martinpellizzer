Sapium obovatum

Sapium obovatum, commonly known as the broadleaf Chinese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features alternate, obovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark purple, egg-shaped berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Sapium obovatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
