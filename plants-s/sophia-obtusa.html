Sophia obtusa

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
