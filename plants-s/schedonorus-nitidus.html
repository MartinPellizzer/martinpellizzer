Schedonorus nitidus

Schedonorus nitidus, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Schedonorus nitidus produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its visual interest and low maintenance requirements.
