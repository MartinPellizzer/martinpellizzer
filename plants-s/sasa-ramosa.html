Sasa ramosa

Sasa ramosa, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface and a grassy aroma. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and mountainous regions.

Sasa ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and as a food source in some cultures.
