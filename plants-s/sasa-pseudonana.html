Sasa pseudonana

Sasa pseudonana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or common sasa, is a clump-forming evergreen grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming extensive colonies in shaded forest environments.

Sasa pseudonana thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often found in mountainous regions.
