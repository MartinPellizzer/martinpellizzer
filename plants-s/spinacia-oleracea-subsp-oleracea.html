Spinacia oleracea subsp. oleracea

Spinacia oleracea subsp.

oleracea, commonly known as spinach, is a leafy green vegetable native to Central and Southwest Asia. It is characterized by its broad, oval leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers and edible seeds, though the leaves are the primary part consumed. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Spinach is rich in nutrients such as iron, vitamins A and C, and antioxidants.
