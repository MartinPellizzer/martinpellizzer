Salix babylonica f. umbraculifera

Salix babylonica f. umbraculifera, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect.

It is native to China and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant features long, slender leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It thrives in moist soils and is often planted near water features.

The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.
