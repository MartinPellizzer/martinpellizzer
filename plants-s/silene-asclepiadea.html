Silene asclepiadea

Silene asclepiadea, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. Silene asclepiadea is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
