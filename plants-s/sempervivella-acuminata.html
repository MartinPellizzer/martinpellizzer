Sempervivella acuminata

Sempervivella acuminata, commonly known as the pointed houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, rosette-shaped foliage composed of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive drought conditions. It produces small, white or pink flowers on slender stems during the summer months.

Sempervivella acuminata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
