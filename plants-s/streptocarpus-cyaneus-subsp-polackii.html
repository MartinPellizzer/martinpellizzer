Streptocarpus cyaneus subsp. polackii

Streptocarpus cyaneus subsp.

polackii, commonly known as the Blue Streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat.

The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique floral display and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
