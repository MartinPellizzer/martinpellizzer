Satureja glabrata

Satureja glabrata, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves with a strong, spicy fragrance. Its flowers are usually purple or pink and are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Satureja glabrata is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
