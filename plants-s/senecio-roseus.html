Senecio roseus

Senecio roseus, commonly known as the rose-gold groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio roseus is also noted for its ability to store water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.
