Sipanea ovalifolia var. ovalifolia

Sipanea ovalifolia var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf sipanea, is a species native to specific regions in southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical structure includes woody stems and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its native environment.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance.
