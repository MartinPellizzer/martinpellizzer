Sorghum serratum

Sorghum serratum, commonly known as the serrated sorghum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stalks and narrow, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its roots are fibrous and can develop into a substantial root system, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Sorghum serratum is often used as forage for livestock and has some traditional medicinal uses in certain cultures.
