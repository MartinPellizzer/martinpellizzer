Serrafalcus secalinus var. glaber

Serrafalcus secalinus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth wheatgrass, is a variant of the wheatgrass species native to temperate regions.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, smooth stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in various herbal and agricultural applications.
