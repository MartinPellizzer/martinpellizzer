Sida angulata

Sida angulata, commonly known as angular sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its angular, ridged stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture.

Sida angulata is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated fields.
