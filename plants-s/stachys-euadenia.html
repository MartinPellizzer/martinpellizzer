Stachys euadenia

Stachys euadenia, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Stachys euadenia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appearance and historical use in herbal medicine.
