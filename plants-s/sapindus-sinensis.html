Sapindus sinensis

Sapindus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The fruit is a spiny capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.
