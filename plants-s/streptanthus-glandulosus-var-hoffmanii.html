Streptanthus glandulosus var. hoffmanii

Streptanthus glandulosus var. hoffmanii, commonly known as the Hoffman's bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, glandular leaves that aid in water retention. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is often found in coastal and inland deserts.
