Sida hongkongensis

Sida hongkongensis, commonly known as Hong Kong sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the summer months. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open fields.

Sida hongkongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
