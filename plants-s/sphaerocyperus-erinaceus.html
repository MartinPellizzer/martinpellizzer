Sphaerocyperus erinaceus

Sphaerocyperus erinaceus, commonly known as the spiky sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular, often spiky stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swamps.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
