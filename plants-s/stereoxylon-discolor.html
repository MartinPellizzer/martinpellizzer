Stereoxylon discolor

Stereoxylon discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered ghostwood, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, often with a slight waxy coating.

The wood of Stereoxylon discolor is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and construction.
