Sideritis argyrea

Sideritis argyrea, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, and its small, pale blue or purple flowers. Sideritis argyrea is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
