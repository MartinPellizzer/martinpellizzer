Statice ocymifolia

Statice ocymifolia, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of salt-resistant hairs.

Statice ocymifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate harsh maritime conditions.
