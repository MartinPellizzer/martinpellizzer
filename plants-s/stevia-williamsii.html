Stevia williamsii

Stevia williamsii, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for containing high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are typically harvested and used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Stevia williamsii thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for organic gardening.
