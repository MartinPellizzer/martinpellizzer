Solanum auctosepalum

Solanum auctosepalum, commonly known as the sweetleaf nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a shrub native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive elliptical shape. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe.

The plant contains alkaloids and is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though caution is advised due to its potential toxicity.
