Sigesbeckia orientalis f. glabrescens

Sigesbeckia orientalis f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth sigesbeckia, is a variant of the common sigesbeckia, characterized by its reduced pubescence.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to East Asia, typically found in moist habitats such as wetlands and marshes. The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in clusters, producing copious amounts of seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration.
