Salvia sieberi

Salvia sieberi, commonly known as Sieber's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia sieberi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, rocky soils.
