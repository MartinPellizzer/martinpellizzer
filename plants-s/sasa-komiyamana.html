Sasa komiyamana

Sasa komiyamana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or komiyamana sasa, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the禾本科 (Poaceae) family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and as a food source. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly fibrous texture.

Sasa komiyamana thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in mountainous regions.
