Salvia russellii

Salvia russellii, commonly known as Russell's sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
