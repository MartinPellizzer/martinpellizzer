Saxifraga × urbium

Saxifraga × urbium, commonly known as the town saxifrage, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of small, often white or pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or disturbed habitats.
