Silene tunica

Silene tunica, commonly known as the tunica catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene tunica is known for its short blooming period, usually in late spring to early summer.
