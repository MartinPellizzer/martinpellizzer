Salix sumiyosensis

Salix sumiyosensis, commonly known as the Sumiyoshi willow, is a species of willow native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along streams and in mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its slender branches, lance-shaped leaves, and catkins that appear in early spring. Its botanical features make it well adapted to humid climates and poor soil conditions.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and ecological value in supporting local wildlife.
