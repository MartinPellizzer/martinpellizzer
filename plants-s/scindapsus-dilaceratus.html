Scindapsus dilaceratus

Scindapsus dilaceratus, commonly known as the Shingle Plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by spadices, which are the characteristic leaf-like structures of the genus. It thrives in humid, bright indirect light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Scindapsus dilaceratus is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
