Smilax cordifolia var. schiedeana

Smilax cordifolia var. schiedeana, commonly known as the Schiede's arrowwood, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
