Schefflera cunninghamii

Schefflera cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain schefflera, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like clusters of leaflets, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually thick and leathery, with a dark green color and a smooth texture.

Schefflera cunninghamii produces small white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native wildlife.
