Spergula maxima var. praevisa

Spergula maxima var. praevisa, commonly known as the large hairy vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable in ecological restoration and as a cover crop.
