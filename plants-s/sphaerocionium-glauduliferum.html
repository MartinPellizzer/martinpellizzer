Sphaerocionium glauduliferum

Sphaerocionium glauduliferum, commonly known as the globular bladder fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive, spherical spore cases.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant produces small, bladder-like structures called sporangia, which contain the spores for reproduction. Its leaves are typically short and clustered, forming a dense, compact growth pattern.

This fern is often found in moist, shaded environments, thriving in humid tropical climates.
