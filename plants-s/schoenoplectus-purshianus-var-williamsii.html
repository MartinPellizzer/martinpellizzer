Schoenoplectus purshianus var. williamsii

Schoenoplectus purshianus var. williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense stands in marshy areas. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies in wet soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
