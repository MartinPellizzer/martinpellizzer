Scleria lelyi

Scleria lelyi, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats across sub-Saharan Africa. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Scleria lelyi is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
