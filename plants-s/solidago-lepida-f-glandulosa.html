Solidago lepida f. glandulosa

Solidago lepida f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often covered with fine, glandular hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support pollinators in native gardens.
