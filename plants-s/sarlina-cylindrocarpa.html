Sarlina cylindrocarpa

Sarlina cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with erect, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens.
