Scabiosa rotata var. porphyrostephana

Scabiosa rotata var. porphyrostephana, commonly known as the purplestephan scabious, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, pink to purple flower heads that are composed of numerous small, fluffy florets, resembling a daisy. The plant has slender, upright stems covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
