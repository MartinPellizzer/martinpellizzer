Serapias oxyglottis

Serapias oxyglottis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Serapias oxyglottis is a perennial species, flowering in spring and often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.
