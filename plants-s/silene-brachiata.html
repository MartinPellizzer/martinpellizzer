Silene brachiata

Silene brachiata, commonly known as the branched catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds.
