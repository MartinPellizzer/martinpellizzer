Salacia pynaertii

Salacia pynaertii, commonly known as the African mountain bignonia, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Salacia pynaertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
