Statice oliveriana

Statice oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with the flowers emerging in dense, cone-shaped clusters.

Statice oliveriana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
