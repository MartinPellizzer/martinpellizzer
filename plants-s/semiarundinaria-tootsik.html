Semiarundinaria tootsik

Semiarundinaria tootsik, commonly known as the Tootsik bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form dense clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
