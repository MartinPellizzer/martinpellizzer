Scabiosa comosa var. lachnophylla

Scabiosa comosa var. lachnophylla, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
