Stachys cataonica

Stachys cataonica, commonly known as the Caucasus betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces spikes of purple or violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Stachys cataonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
