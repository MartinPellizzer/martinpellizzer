Scrophularia puberula

Scrophularia puberula, commonly known as the downy figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with similar floral structures.

Scrophularia puberula is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
