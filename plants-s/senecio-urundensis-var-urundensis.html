Senecio urundensis var. urundensis

Senecio urundensis var. urundensis, commonly known as the Urundensis Groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
