Sabal ghiesbrechtii

Sabal ghiesbrechtii, commonly known as the Florida cabbage palm, is a species of palm native to the southeastern United States.

It is a medium-sized tree that typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 feet, featuring a slender trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
