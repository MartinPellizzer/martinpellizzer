Senegalia cheilanthifolia var. hirtella

Senegalia cheilanthifolia var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy jointed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, spherical clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a low water requirement.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
