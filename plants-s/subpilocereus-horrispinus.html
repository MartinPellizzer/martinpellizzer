Subpilocereus horrispinus

Subpilocereus horrispinus, commonly known as the spiny night bloomer, is a cacti species native to northern South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering habit, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
