Saussurea porphyroleuca

Saussurea porphyroleuca, commonly known as the purple-leaved saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy composite flowers. The plant features dark purple to violet foliage, which gives it its common name, and its flowers typically bloom in shades of yellow to orange. It thrives in alpine and arctic environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
