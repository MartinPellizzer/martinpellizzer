Solanum santosii

Solanum santosii, commonly known as Santos's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a berry that changes color from green to red as it matures, often containing several seeds.
