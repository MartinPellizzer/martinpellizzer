Stelis andreettae

Stelis andreettae, commonly known as the Andreetta's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomes and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
