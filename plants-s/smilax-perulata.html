Smilax perulata

Smilax perulata, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or yellow smilax, is a perennial vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, compound leaves that are often covered in small, hooked spines. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Smilax perulata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different light conditions.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
