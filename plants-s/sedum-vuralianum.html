Sedum vuralianum

Sedum vuralianum, commonly known as the Vural's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months. It grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
