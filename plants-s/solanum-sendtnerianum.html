Solanum sendtnerianum

Solanum sendtnerianum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and edible berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and the fruits are small, yellow, and contain toxic compounds.

Despite its toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
