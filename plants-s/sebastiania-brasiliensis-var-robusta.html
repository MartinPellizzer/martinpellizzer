Sebastiania brasiliensis var. robusta

Sebastiania brasiliensis var. robusta, commonly known as the Brazil starfruit or Brazilian starfruit, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Its ornamental value and unique fruit make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
