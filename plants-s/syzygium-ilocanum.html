Syzygium ilocanum

Syzygium ilocanum, commonly known as the Ilocano apple or wild apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, subacid flavor.

Syzygium ilocanum is often found in lowland and montane forests, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
