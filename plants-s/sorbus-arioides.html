Sorbus arioides

Sorbus arioides, commonly known as the whitebeard serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a serrated margin.

Sorbus arioides is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
