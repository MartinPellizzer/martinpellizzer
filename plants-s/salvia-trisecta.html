Salvia trisecta

Salvia trisecta, commonly known as the three-part sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it its common name. Its showy, violet-blue flowers appear in whorled clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Salvia trisecta is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
