Serapias × capitata

Serapias × capitata, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Serapias auricoma and Serapias microglossa.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant produces tall, slender stems with clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that resemble orchids. Its leaves are grass-like and often form dense rosettes near the base.

Serapias × capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, well-drained soil.
