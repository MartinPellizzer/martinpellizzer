Salvia blancoana var. aurasiaca

Salvia blancoana var. aurasiaca, commonly known as the Aurasiaca Sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped, grey-green leaves with a rough, hairy texture that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
