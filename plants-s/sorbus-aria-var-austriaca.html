Sorbus aria var. austriaca

Sorbus aria var. austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian rowan, is a variant of the rowan tree native to central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–8 meters. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into 5–9 oval-shaped leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and it is often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

The plant has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and colorful berries, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
