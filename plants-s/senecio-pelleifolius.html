Senecio pelleifolius

Senecio pelleifolius, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which are typically bright yellow with white petals. The plant has a rosette-shaped growth pattern, with hairy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio pelleifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
