Salvia eplingiana

Salvia eplingiana, commonly known as Epling's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
