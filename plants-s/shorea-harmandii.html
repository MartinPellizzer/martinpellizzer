Shorea harmandii

Shorea harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces woody fruits containing seeds.

The plant is part of the Dipterocarpaceae family and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
