Saxifraga minlingensis

Saxifraga minlingensis, commonly known as the Minling saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, typically in clusters at the stem tips. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
