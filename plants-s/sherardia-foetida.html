Sherardia foetida

Sherardia foetida, commonly known as the stinking nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are initially green and turn red when ripe, often attracting birds and other wildlife.

It contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and livestock if ingested.
