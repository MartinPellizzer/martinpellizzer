Salvia remissa

Salvia remissa, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and often have a bluish tinge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia remissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
