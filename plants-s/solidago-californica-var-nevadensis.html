Solidago californica var. nevadensis

Solidago californica var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra goldenseed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its showy inflorescences and ecological importance to pollinators.
