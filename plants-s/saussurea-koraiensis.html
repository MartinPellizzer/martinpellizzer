Saussurea koraiensis

Saussurea koraiensis, commonly known as Korean safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Saussurea koraiensis plays a significant role in the local ecosystem and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
