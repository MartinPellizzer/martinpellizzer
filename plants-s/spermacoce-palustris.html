Spermacoce palustris

Spermacoce palustris, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, umbel-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Spermacoce palustris is an important plant for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.
