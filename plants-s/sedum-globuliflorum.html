Sedum globuliflorum

Sedum globuliflorum, commonly known as the globe-flowered stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soil. It is a perennial species that produces a compact rosette of foliage.

Its unique appearance and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
