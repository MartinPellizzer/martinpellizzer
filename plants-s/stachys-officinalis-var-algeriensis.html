Stachys officinalis var. algeriensis

Stachys officinalis var. algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian betony, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Algerian betony is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
