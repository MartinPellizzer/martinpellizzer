Serapias × ambigua

Serapias × ambigua, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Serapias acaulis and Serapias orchidifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, spurred orchid-like flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in wet meadows and marshy habitats, often found in the Mediterranean region. Its floral structure includes a long, slender spur that houses the nectar, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and flies.

The species is notable for its adaptability to moist environments and its role in local biodiversity.
