Saxifraga × schmidtii

Saxifraga × schmidtii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.

Saxifraga × schmidtii is also noted for its frost resistance and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
