Saxifraga dunniana

Saxifraga dunniana, commonly known as the dwarf saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hair. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Saxifraga dunniana thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
