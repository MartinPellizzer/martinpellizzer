Sonchus canariae

Sonchus canariae, commonly known as the Canary Islands sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the island's unique climate. Its leaves are generally hairy and have a distinctive, prickly texture.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
