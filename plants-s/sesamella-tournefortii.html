Sesamella tournefortii

Sesamella tournefortii, commonly known as the Tournefort's sesamoid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Sesamella tournefortii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
