Sabal serrulata

Sabal serrulata, commonly known as the Florida cabbage tree, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a tall, palm-like plant that can grow up to 20 meters in height, featuring a slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swamps and along the edges of freshwater bodies. Its fruit is a three-lobed, spiky capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Sabal serrulata is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
