Salsola deschaseauxiana

Salsola deschaseauxiana, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with fleshy, spiny stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed and barren landscapes.

Salsola deschaseauxiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
