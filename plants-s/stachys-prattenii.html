Stachys prattenii

Stachys prattenii, commonly known as Pratten's hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in whorled clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in moist, calcareous soils.

Stachys prattenii is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded, damp conditions.
