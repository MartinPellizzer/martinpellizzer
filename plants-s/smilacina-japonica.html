Smilacina japonica

Smilacina japonica, commonly known as Japanese climbing fern, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and slender, wiry stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, followed by bright red berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, with a lanceolate shape and a serrated margin.

Smilacina japonica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
