Sphaeralcea fasciculata var. nesiotica

Sphaeralcea fasciculata var. nesiotica, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces globe-shaped seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
