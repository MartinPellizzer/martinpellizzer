Sagina linnaei var. nevadensis

Sagina linnaei var. nevadensis, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant has tiny, rounded leaves that are closely packed, forming a dense mat. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and typically appear in spring.

It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
