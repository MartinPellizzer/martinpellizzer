Salix × buseri

Salix × buseri, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Salicaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix viminalis and Salix triandra, characterized by its slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is typically found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments where it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its catkin-like flowers appear in early spring, followed by small, elongated fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Salix × buseri is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
