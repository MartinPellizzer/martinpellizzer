Sabazia liebmannii var. heterocarpa

Sabazia liebmannii var. heterocarpa, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant features a bulbous stem and linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
