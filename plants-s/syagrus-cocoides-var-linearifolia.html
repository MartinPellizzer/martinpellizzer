Syagrus cocoides var. linearifolia

Syagrus cocoides var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved palm, is a species native to the coastal regions of northeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are long and narrow, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, yellowish-white inflorescences that bloom during the dry season. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
