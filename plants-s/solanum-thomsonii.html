Solanum thomsonii

Solanum thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its fruit is a green berry that turns red when mature, and it contains toxic compounds similar to those found in other members of its family.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated or used in food or medicine.
