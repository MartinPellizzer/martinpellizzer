Symplocos adenophylla var. adenophylla

Symplocos adenophylla var. adenophylla, commonly known as the hairy leafed evergreen, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a distinctive hairy underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitats.
