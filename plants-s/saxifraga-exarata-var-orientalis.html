Saxifraga exarata var. orientalis

Saxifraga exarata var. orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern rock saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small, star-shaped, and usually pink or white, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
