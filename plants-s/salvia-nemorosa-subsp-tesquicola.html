Salvia nemorosa subsp. tesquicola

Salvia nemorosa subsp.

tesquicola, commonly known as woodland sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture.

The plant produces spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
