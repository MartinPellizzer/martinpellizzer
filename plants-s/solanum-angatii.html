Solanum angatii

Solanum angatii, commonly known as the Angatii nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and oval in shape. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

The plant contains various alkaloids and is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
