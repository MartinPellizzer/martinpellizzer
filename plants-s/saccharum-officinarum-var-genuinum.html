Saccharum officinarum var. genuinum

Saccharum officinarum var. genuinum, commonly known as the native sugarcane variety, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks that are rich in sucrose, making it a vital crop for sugar production. The plant has large, elongated leaves with a prominent midrib and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in warm climates with high humidity and requires well-drained, fertile soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both agricultural and ecological systems.
