Salvia verbascifolia

Salvia verbascifolia, commonly known as sunset sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes due to its drought tolerance.
