Smilax morongii

Smilax morongii, commonly known as the Morong's smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of sharp, spiny tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its berries, which are edible for some animals, are dark blue to black in color and contain several seeds.

Smilax morongii is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade.
