Schisandra chinensis var. typica

Schisandra chinensis var. typica, commonly known as the Chinese five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of red berries that turn dark purple as they mature. These berries are renowned for their complex flavor profile, which includes sweet, sour, bitter, spicy, and umami notes.

Schisandra chinensis var. typica is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and use in traditional Chinese medicine.
