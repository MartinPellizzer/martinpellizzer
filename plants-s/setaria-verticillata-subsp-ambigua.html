Setaria verticillata subsp. ambigua

Setaria verticillata subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive zigzag arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist to aquatic habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that is often reddish in color.

The species is known for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
