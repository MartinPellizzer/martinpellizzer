Saccharum roseum

Saccharum roseum, commonly known as the pink sugarcane, is a striking plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks that display a unique pinkish hue, distinguishing it from other sugarcane varieties. The plant belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is known for its ornamental value as well as its potential for biofuel production. Its leaves are broad and blade-like, often with a slight waxy coating.

Saccharum roseum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate to grow optimally.
