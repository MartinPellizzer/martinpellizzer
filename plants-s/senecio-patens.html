Senecio patens

Senecio patens, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with silvery-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, helping it thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Senecio patens is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
