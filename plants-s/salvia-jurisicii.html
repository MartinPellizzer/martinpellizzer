Salvia jurisicii

Salvia jurisicii, commonly known as the Jurisic's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Salvia jurisicii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
