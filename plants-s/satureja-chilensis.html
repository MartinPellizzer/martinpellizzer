Satureja chilensis

Satureja chilensis, commonly known as Chilean thyme, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its small, pink or white flowers appear in summer, and the leaves are often used in traditional medicine and cooking.

Satureja chilensis is valued for its fragrant properties and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
