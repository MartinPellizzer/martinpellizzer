Schisandra axillaris

Schisandra axillaris, commonly known as the axillary schisandra, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and red, fleshy berries. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its berries contain aromatic compounds and have been used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphology and chemical composition.
