Sisymbrium suecicum

Sisymbrium suecicum, commonly known as the Swedish mustard, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Sisymbrium suecicum is notable for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions and its role in ecological succession.
