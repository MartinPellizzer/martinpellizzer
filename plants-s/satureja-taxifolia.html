Satureja taxifolia

Satureja taxifolia, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong, pungent aroma when crushed.

Satureja taxifolia is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its distinctive flavor.
