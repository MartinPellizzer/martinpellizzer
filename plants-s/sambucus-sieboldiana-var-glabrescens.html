Sambucus sieboldiana var. glabrescens

Sambucus sieboldiana var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
