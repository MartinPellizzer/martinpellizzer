Sapota vitiensis

Sapota vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis sapote, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, sweet-tasting fruits with a soft, creamy pulp. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications due to its unique flavor and nutritional value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
