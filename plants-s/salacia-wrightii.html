Salacia wrightii

Salacia wrightii, commonly known as Wright's salacia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, lowland environments. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, flexible stems that support its broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
