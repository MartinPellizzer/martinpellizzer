Scirpus hotarui var. triangulatus

Scirpus hotarui var. triangulatus, commonly known as the triangular bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its triangular-shaped stems and dense, grass-like foliage. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes are usually tall and cylindrical, producing small, clustered flowers.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic life.
