Sideritis arborescens subsp. antiatlantica

Sideritis arborescens subsp.

antiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic rock sedge, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in sunny, well-drained habitats.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowering period occurs in summer, producing small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in spike-like clusters.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
