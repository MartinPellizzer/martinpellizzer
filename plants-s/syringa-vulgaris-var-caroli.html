Syringa vulgaris var. caroli

Syringa vulgaris var. caroli, commonly known as the Carlow shrub or Carlow lilac, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It features fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a rounded, bushy form.

Syringa vulgaris var. caroli is well-suited for small gardens and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
