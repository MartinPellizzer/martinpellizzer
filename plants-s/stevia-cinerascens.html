Stevia cinerascens

Stevia cinerascens, commonly known as the silver leaf stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is cultivated for its leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used as sugar substitutes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Stevia cinerascens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
