Senecio cochabambensis

Senecio cochabambensis, commonly known as the Cochabamba groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cooler climates. Its foliage is generally gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Senecio cochabambensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
