Sphedamnocarpus pruriens subsp. pruriens

Sphedamnocarpus pruriens subsp.

pruriens, commonly known as the itching bush, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, often prickly leaves.

The plant is known for its irritant properties, with its leaves and sap causing skin irritation in some individuals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed or dry soils.

The species is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic effects.
