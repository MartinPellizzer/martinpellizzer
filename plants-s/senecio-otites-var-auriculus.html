Senecio otites var. auriculus

Senecio otites var. auriculus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small bells. The plant has hairy, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
