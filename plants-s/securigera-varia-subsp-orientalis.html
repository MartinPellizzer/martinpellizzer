Securigera varia subsp. orientalis

Securigera varia subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

The plant features slender, branching stems with small, trifoliate leaves that have a distinctive hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, arranged in racemes, and it produces slender, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
