Spiraea nipponica var. nipponica

Spiraea nipponica var. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading branches that bear clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
