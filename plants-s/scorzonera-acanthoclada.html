Scorzonera acanthoclada

Scorzonera acanthoclada, commonly known as the prickly scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in summer. Its roots are fibrous and somewhat tuberous, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera acanthoclada thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
