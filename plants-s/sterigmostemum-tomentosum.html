Sterigmostemum tomentosum

Sterigmostemum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly stemless morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, stemless stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant has hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive, soft texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
