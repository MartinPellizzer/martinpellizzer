Sibbaldia parviflora var. micrantha

Sibbaldia parviflora var. micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf sibbaldia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, branched stems and small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
