Sorghum centroplicatum

Sorghum centroplicatum, commonly known as the white sorghum, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is adapted to thrive in warm climates with well-drained soils.

Sorghum centroplicatum is often used for forage and has potential applications in horticulture and ornamental gardening.
