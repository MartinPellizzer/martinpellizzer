Senecio cedrosensis

Senecio cedrosensis, commonly known as Cedros Island groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cedros in the Gulf of California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio cedrosensis is also noted for its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring and early summer.
