Setaria pseudaristata

Setaria pseudaristata, commonly known as false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, bristly flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Setaria pseudaristata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
