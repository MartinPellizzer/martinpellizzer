Senecio lubumbashianus

Senecio lubumbashianus, commonly known as the Lubumbashi groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
