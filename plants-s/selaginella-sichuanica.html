Selaginella sichuanica

Selaginella sichuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to China.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a low-growing stem with whorled leaves that are often spirally arranged. Its sporophylls are closely clustered and bear microsporangia and megasporangia, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella sichuanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological studies due to its unique adaptations to its habitat.
