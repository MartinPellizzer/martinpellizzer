Sophora brachygyna

Sophora brachygyna, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy, fragrant blossoms.

Sophora brachygyna is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
