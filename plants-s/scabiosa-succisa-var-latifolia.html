Scabiosa succisa var. latifolia

Scabiosa succisa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This perennial plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
