Senecio aureus var. discoideus

Senecio aureus var. discoideus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Its botanical classification places it within the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.
