Satureja acinos var. lancifolia

Satureja acinos var. lancifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty scent, and small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its digestive and antiseptic properties.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
