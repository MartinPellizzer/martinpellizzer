Sponia pendula

Sponia pendula, commonly known as the weeping sponia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of small, fragrant flowers that hang from long, slender stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sponia pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cascading blooms.
