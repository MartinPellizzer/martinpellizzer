Sorbaria rhoifolia

Sorbaria rhoifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the central and northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its fruit is a small, dry drupe that matures to a reddish-brown color, typically appearing in late summer.

This plant is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
