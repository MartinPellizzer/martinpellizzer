Sium ammi

Sium ammi, commonly known as hogweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in an umbel, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Sium ammi is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other plants like carrots and parsley.

Its root is used in traditional medicine, though the plant can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
