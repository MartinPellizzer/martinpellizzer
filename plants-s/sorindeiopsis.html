Sorindeiopsis

Sorindeiopsis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the remote highlands of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant produces delicate, pale purple flowers that bloom only during the night, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Sorindeiopsis is critically endangered, highlighting the need for urgent conservation efforts.
