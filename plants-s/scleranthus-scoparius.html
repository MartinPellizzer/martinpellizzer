Scleranthus scoparius

Scleranthus scoparius, commonly known as the common sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant has slender, erect stems with alternate leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late summer, producing small, dry fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Scleranthus scoparius is characterized by its tough, woody stems and is often found in dry, sunny environments.
