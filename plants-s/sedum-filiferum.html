Sedum filiferum

Sedum filiferum, commonly known as the silver rose, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and landscape purposes.
