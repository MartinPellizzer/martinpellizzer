Salvia calostachya

Salvia calostachya, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Salvia calostachya is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
