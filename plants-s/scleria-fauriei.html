Scleria fauriei

Scleria fauriei, commonly known as the African flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. Scleria fauriei is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
