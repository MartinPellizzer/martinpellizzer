Solanum ipecachuana var. calvescens

Solanum ipecachuana var. calvescens, commonly known as the Amazonian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It features woody stems with smooth, dark green leaves that have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that change color from green to red as they mature. It is characterized by its adaptability to humid, shaded environments and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
