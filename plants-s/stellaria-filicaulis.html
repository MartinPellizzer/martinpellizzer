Stellaria filicaulis

Stellaria filicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved starwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Stellaria filicaulis is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar morphological traits.
