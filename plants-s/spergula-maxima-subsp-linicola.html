Spergula maxima subsp. linicola

Spergula maxima subsp.

linicola, commonly known as the greater wall-rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found along coastal areas and cliffs. Its leaves are linear and somewhat succulent, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

The subspecies linicola is distinguished by its larger size and more robust growth compared to other subspecies of Spergula maxima.
