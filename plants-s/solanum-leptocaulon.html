Solanum leptocaulon

Solanum leptocaulon, commonly known as the slender-stemmed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its thin, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided for consumption.
