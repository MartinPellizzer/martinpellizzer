Salvia holwayi var. holwayi

Salvia holwayi var. holwayi, commonly known as the Holway's sage, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
