Spiraea bumalda f. pruhoniciana

Spiraea bumalda f. pruhoniciana, commonly known as the white spiraea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and a rounded growth habit, making it an attractive ornamental plant. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
