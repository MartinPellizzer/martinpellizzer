Senecio haplogynus

Senecio haplogynus, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white to pale blue flowers that resemble snowflakes. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Senecio haplogynus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
