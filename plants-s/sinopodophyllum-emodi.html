Sinopodophyllum emodi

Sinopodophyllum emodi, commonly known as Himalayan mayapple, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments at high altitudes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued in traditional medicine.
