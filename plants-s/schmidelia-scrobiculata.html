Schmidelia scrobiculata

Schmidelia scrobiculata, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments in southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, with a deep root system for water retention. Schmidelia scrobiculata is often found in rocky crevices and is an important component of its local ecosystem.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions and ecological studies in arid regions.
