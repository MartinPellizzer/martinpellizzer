Stemona asperula

Stemona asperula, commonly known as the rough stemona, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are covered with rough, longitudinal ridges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.
