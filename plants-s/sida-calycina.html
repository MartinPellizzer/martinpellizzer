Sida calycina

Sida calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or smallleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a velvety texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sida calycina is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in agricultural fields.
