Sclerolaena blackiana

Sclerolaena blackiana, commonly known as the blackwood plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
