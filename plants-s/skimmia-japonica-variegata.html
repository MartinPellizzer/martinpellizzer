Skimmia japonica variegata

Skimmia japonica variegata, commonly known as Japanese skimmia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are variegated with white and green patterns, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during late winter or early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Skimmia japonica variegata is valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
