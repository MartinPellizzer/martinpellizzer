Serjania acapulcensis

Serjania acapulcensis, commonly known as the Mexican serjania, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae, which includes shrubs and small trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils.

Serjania acapulcensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
