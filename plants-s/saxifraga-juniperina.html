Saxifraga juniperina

Saxifraga juniperina, commonly known as the Juniper saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, with a slightly hairy texture, and it is known for its hardy nature.

Saxifraga juniperina is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environments.
