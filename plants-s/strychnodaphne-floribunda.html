Strychnodaphne floribunda

Strychnodaphne floribunda, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
