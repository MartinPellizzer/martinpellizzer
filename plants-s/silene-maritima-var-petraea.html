Silene maritima var. petraea

Silene maritima var. petraea, commonly known as the rock campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to coastal and rocky habitats in the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, showy clusters. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds. Its root system is shallow, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, gravelly soils.

Silene maritima var. petraea is often found growing on cliffs, sea walls, and coastal dunes.
