Saxifraga × barbezati

Saxifraga × barbezati, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Saxifraga × barbezati is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
