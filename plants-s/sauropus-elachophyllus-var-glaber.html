Sauropus elachophyllus var. glaber

Sauropus elachophyllus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-leaved sauropus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, smooth, and often fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist undergrowth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant collections.
