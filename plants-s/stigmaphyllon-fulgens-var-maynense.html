Stigmaphyllon fulgens var. maynense

Stigmaphyllon fulgens var. maynense, commonly known as the golden stigmaphyllon, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers, which appear in clusters and bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
