Solanum cocosoides

Solanum cocosoides, commonly known as the "Cocos-like nightshade," is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and dark purple berries. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
