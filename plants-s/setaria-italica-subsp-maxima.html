Setaria italica subsp. maxima

Setaria italica subsp.

maxima, commonly known as Italian millet, is a grass species native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy crop for temperate climates.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its nutritional grains and is used in traditional cuisines and animal feed.
