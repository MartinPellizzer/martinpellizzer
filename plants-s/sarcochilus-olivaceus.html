Sarcochilus olivaceus

Sarcochilus olivaceus, commonly known as the olive helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a greenish-yellow hue and a distinctive, pleated lip. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and often covered in a waxy coating to retain moisture.

Sarcochilus olivaceus thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
