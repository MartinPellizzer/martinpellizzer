Shawia erubescens var. ilicifolia

Shawia erubescens var. ilicifolia, commonly known as the redleaf holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, spiny foliage with leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. Its evergreen nature makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique coloration throughout the seasons.
