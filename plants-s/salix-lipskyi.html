Salix lipskyi

Salix lipskyi, commonly known as the Lisko willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flexible branches and drought tolerance make it suitable for reclamation of degraded soils.

Salix lipskyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
