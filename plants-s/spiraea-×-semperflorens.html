Spiraea × semperflorens

Spiraea × semperflorens, commonly known as the compact spiraea, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscape designs.

It is also valued for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
