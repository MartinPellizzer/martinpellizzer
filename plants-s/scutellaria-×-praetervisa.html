Scutellaria × praetervisa

Scutellaria × praetervisa, commonly known as the hybrid blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria × praetervisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
