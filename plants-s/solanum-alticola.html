Solanum alticola

Solanum alticola, commonly known as the highland nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
