Scirpus savii f. controversus

Scirpus savii f. controversus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments in Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and flowering stems that produce small, clustered spikes of flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a robust growth habit that allows it to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its potential use in phytoremediation.
