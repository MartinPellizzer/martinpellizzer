Scindapsus longistipitatus

Scindapsus longistipitatus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green variegated leaves and long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its aerial roots allow it to attach to surfaces, making it ideal for hanging baskets or trellises.

Scindapsus longistipitatus is also popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
