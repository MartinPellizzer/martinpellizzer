Scrophularia boissieriana

Scrophularia boissieriana, commonly known as the Boissier's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is native to regions of the Mediterranean, including parts of southern Europe and northern Africa. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes.

Scrophularia boissieriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
