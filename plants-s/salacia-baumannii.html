Salacia baumannii

Salacia baumannii, commonly known as the Indian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its woody structure and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, reddish berries that contain seeds, and its leaves are alternate and ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
