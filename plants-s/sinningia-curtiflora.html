Sinningia curtiflora

Sinningia curtiflora, commonly known as the compact gloxinia, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of vibrant pink or red blooms during the summer months. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings.

Sinningia curtiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
