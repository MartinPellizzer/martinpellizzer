Scordium spinosum

Scordium spinosum, commonly known as the spiny scordium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, leaf-like structures and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its botanical features include a short, woody stem and alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Scordium spinosum is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
