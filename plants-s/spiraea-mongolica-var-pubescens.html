Spiraea mongolica var. pubescens

Spiraea mongolica var. pubescens, commonly known as the Mongolian weeping spirea, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, arching clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its graceful form and showy floral display.
