Silene chinensis

Silene chinensis, commonly known as Chinese catchfly, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
