Serianthes calycina

Serianthes calycina, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Borneo, is a rare and endangered tree species native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its unique ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to habitat destruction, Serianthes calycina faces significant conservation challenges.
