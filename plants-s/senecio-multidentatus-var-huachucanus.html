Senecio multidentatus var. huachucanus

Senecio multidentatus var. huachucanus, commonly known as the Huachucan Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.

This variety is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
