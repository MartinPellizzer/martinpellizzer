Sida glabra var. glabra

Sida glabra var. glabra, commonly known as velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Sida glabra var. glabra is known for its adaptability and can become a weed in cultivated areas.
