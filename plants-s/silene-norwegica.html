Silene norwegica

Silene norwegica, commonly known as the Norwegian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene norwegica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
