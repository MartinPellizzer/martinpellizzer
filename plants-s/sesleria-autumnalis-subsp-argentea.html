Sesleria autumnalis subsp. argentea

Sesleria autumnalis subsp.

argentea, commonly known as silver tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers during the autumn season. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry garden settings.
