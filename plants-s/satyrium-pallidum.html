Satyrium pallidum

Satyrium pallidum, commonly known as the pale satyrium, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects, a trait common in many orchid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
