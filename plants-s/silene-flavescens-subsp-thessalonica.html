Silene flavescens subsp. thessalonica

Silene flavescens subsp.

thessalonica, commonly known as the Thessalonica campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils.

The plant produces pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered inflorescences.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
