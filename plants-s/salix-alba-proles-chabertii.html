Salix alba proles chabertii

Salix alba proles chabertii, commonly known as the Chabert's white willow, is a variety of the white willow species native to Europe.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its slender, weeping branches and elongated, narrow leaves. The leaves are typically bright green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath, with a smooth margin. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
