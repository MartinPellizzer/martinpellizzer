Serinia gracilis

Serinia gracilis, commonly known as the slender serinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Serinia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
