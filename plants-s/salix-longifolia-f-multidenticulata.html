Salix longifolia f. multidenticulata

Salix longifolia f. multidenticulata, commonly known as the multi-toothed willow, is a variant of the longleaf willow.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and elongated, narrow leaves with multiple fine teeth along the margins. Its flowers are catkins, which are typically yellowish-green and appear in early spring.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
