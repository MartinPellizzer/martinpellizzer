Stachys caroliniana

Stachys caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive blue-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Stachys caroliniana is valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
