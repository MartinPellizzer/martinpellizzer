Saccolabium odoratissimum

Saccolabium odoratissimum, commonly known as the fragrant saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

The species is noted for its pleasant fragrance, which is particularly noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
