Sideritis flaviflora

Sideritis flaviflora, commonly known as the yellowflower ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands.

Sideritis flaviflora is valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to fully understand its botanical and therapeutic significance.
