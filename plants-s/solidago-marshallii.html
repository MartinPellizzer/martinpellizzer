Solidago marshallii

Solidago marshallii, commonly known as Marshall's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small golden clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
