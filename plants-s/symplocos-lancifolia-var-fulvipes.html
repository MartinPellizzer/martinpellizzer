Symplocos lancifolia var. fulvipes

Symplocos lancifolia var. fulvipes, commonly known as the yellow-veined wax tree, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves with prominent yellow veins. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance.
