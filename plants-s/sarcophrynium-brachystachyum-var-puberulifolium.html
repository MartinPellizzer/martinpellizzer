Sarcophrynium brachystachyum var. puberulifolium

Sarcophrynium brachystachyum var. puberulifolium, commonly known as the velvet-leafed sarcophrynium, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, softly hairy leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
