Syringa inodora

Syringa inodora, commonly known as the fragrant mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, highly fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Syringa inodora is also known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
