Silene flos-cuculi subsp. subintegra

Silene flos-cuculi subsp.

subintegra, commonly known as the "Cuckoo's Flower," is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
