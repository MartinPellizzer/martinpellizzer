Syzygium wallichianum

Syzygium wallichianum, commonly known as the white sand apple, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Syzygium wallichianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant blossoms.
