Saccharum praegrande

Saccharum praegrande, commonly known as the giant bamboo grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Saccharum praegrande is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in bioenergy production.
