Solanum balanidium

Solanum balanidium, commonly known as the Balanidium nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Solanum balanidium is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
