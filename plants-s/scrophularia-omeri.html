Scrophularia omeri

Scrophularia omeri, commonly known as Omer's figwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in limestone areas. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are arranged in whorled clusters and are characterized by their two-lipped structure, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
