Stachys cordatifolia

Stachys cordatifolia, commonly known as heartleaf stachys, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lanceolate to heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces spikes of purple or pink flowers in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
