Scabiosa canescens

Scabiosa canescens, commonly known as the hoary scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Scabiosa canescens is a perennial herb that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
