Solanum fendleri var. physaloides

Solanum fendleri var. physaloides, commonly known as the desert tomatillo, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its fruit, which resembles a small tomato, is edible and used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
