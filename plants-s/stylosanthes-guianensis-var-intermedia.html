Stylosanthes guianensis var. intermedia

Stylosanthes guianensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Guinea stylo, is a perennial leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branched stems that produce small, yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often used as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

Its ability to thrive in poor soils makes it an important species in agricultural and ecological restoration efforts.
