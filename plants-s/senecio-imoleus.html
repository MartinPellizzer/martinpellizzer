Senecio imoleus

Senecio imoleus, commonly known as the Imolese groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow.

Senecio imoleus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive appearance.
