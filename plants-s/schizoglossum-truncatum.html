Schizoglossum truncatum

Schizoglossum truncatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, greenish or white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and often have a truncate or notched tip, which is a distinctive botanical feature.

Schizoglossum truncatum is characterized by its delicate floral structure and is often found in association with other epiphytic and terrestrial orchids.
