Stisseria pulchra

Stisseria pulchra, commonly known as the velvetleaf stisseria, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Stisseria pulchra thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural interest.
