Spilanthes longifolia

Spilanthes longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf pungentroot, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Due to its unique appearance and historical use in traditional medicine, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
