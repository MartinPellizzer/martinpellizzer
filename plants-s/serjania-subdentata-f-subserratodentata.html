Serjania subdentata f. subserratodentata

Serjania subdentata f. subserratodentata, commonly known as the serrated-leaf serjania, is a variant of the serjania genus native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves with serrated margins. The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly rough texture. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
