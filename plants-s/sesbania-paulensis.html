Sesbania paulensis

Sesbania paulensis, commonly known as the Paul's sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a leguminous species belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It produces elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional practices.

Sesbania paulensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in degraded lands.
