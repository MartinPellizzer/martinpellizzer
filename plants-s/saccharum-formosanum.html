Saccharum formosanum

Saccharum formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwanese bamboo cane grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its potential in bioenergy production and as a source of natural fibers.
