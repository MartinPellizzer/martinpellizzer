Selaginella benguetensis

Selaginella benguetensis, commonly known as the Benguet spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous appearance with dense, needle-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including strobila, are distinctive and contribute to its classification as a vascular plant.

Selaginella benguetensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
