Salacia erecta

Salacia erecta, commonly known as the erect salacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in catkins, typical of the family.

Salacia erecta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
