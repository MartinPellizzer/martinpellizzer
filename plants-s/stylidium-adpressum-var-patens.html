Stylidium adpressum var. patens

Stylidium adpressum var. patens, commonly known as the spreading sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, sun-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive, spurred lip.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid environments.
