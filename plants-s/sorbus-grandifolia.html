Sorbus grandifolia

Sorbus grandifolia, commonly known as the largeleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall.

It is often found in moist woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
