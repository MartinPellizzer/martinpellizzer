Serianthes dilmyi

Serianthes dilmyi, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for over a century, making it a long-lived species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
