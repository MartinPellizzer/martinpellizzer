Syringa hirsuta

Syringa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features grayish-green, hairy leaves that are oval in shape and have a textured surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Syringa hirsuta is relatively low maintenance and resistant to many common pests and diseases.
