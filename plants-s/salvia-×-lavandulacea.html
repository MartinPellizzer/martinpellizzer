Salvia × lavandulacea

Salvia × lavandulacea, commonly known as lavender sage, is a hybrid perennial plant that combines traits of both Salvia and Lavandula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and striking spikes of violet or blue flowers. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.

Botanically, it is notable for its showy inflorescences and its role in horticultural hybridization.
