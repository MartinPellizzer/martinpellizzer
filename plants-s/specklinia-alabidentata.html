Specklinia alabidentata

Specklinia alabidentata, commonly known as the silver-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a compact inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses and conservatories.
