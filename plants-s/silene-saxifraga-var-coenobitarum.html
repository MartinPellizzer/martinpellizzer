Silene saxifraga var. coenobitarum

Silene saxifraga var. coenobitarum, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Silene saxifraga var. coenobitarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
