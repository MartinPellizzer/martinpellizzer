Solanum andigenum f. chimaco

Solanum andigenum f. chimaco, commonly known as the Chimaco nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential adaptability to different environmental conditions.
