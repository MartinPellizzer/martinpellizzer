Schoberia salsa

Schoberia salsa, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features bright orange to yellow ray florets and dark brown disk flowers, creating a striking visual display. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Schoberia salsa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
