Scutellaria integrifolia var. floridana

Scutellaria integrifolia var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.
