Selinum elegans

Selinum elegans, commonly known as the slender chervil, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Selinum elegans is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and subtle fragrance.
