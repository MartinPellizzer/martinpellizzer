Salvia absconditiflora

Salvia absconditiflora, commonly known as the hidden-flower sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy texture.

Salvia absconditiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
