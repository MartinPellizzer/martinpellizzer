Sclerocactus intermedius

Sclerocactus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate scabrous cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed, spiny stems and a grayish-green coloration. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Sclerocactus intermedius is a slow-growing species that thrives in full sun and is often used in rock gardens or xeriscaping.
