Smilacina japonica var. robusta

Smilacina japonica var. robusta, commonly known as Japanese climbing fern, is a vigorous vine native to East Asia.

It features long, slender stems that can climb and twine around supports, often reaching several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in forest understories.
