Sida rufescens

Sida rufescens, commonly known as redstem sida, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowering period usually occurs in the summer months, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Sida rufescens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
