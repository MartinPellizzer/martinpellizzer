Scirpus conostachys

Scirpus conostachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brown or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Scirpus conostachys thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.
