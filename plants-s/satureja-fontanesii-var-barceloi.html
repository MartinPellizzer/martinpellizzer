Satureja fontanesii var. barceloi

Satureja fontanesii var. barceloi, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, often mint-scented foliage. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional Spanish cuisine.
