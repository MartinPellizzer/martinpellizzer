Sitobolium davallioides

Sitobolium davallioides, commonly known as the African orchidgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Sitobolium davallioides is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects.
