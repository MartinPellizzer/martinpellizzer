Sedum atratum var. guetardii

Sedum atratum var. guetardii, commonly known as the Guetard's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged blooming period.
