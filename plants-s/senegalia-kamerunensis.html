Senegalia kamerunensis

Senegalia kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often used as a source of fodder for livestock.
