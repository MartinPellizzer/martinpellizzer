Stachys gaziantepensis

Stachys gaziantepensis, commonly known as Gaziantep betony, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky and dry environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
