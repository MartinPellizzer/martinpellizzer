Staurogyne stolonifera

Staurogyne stolonifera, commonly known as the water clover, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies. The plant features small, delicate leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces tiny white or pink flowers during the growing season. Its stoloniferous growth habit allows it to spread horizontally, forming dense mats that can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Staurogyne stolonifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in aquatic gardening.
