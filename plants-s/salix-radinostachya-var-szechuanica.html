Salix radinostachya var. szechuanica

Salix radinostachya var. szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed structure and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral characteristics and adaptability to specific environmental conditions.
