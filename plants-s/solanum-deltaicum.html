Solanum deltaicum

Solanum deltaicum, commonly known as the delta nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, fleshy berries. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum deltaicum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
