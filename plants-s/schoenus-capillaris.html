Schoenus capillaris

Schoenus capillaris, commonly known as the silky grass or slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Schoenus capillaris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
