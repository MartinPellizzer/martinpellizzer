Salix ballii

Salix ballii, commonly known as the Ball's willow, is a species of willow native to the western United States.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine down. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollination.

Salix ballii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
