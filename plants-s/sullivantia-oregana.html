Sullivantia oregana

Sullivantia oregana, commonly known as Oregon mountain-mint, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of coniferous forests, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple and arranged in dense spikes.

The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.
