Selaginella caracasensis

Selaginella caracasensis, commonly known as the Caracas spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its creeping stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spores in clusters called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Selaginella caracasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
