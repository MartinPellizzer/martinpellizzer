Sophora exigua var. elatior

Sophora exigua var. elatior, commonly known as the tall Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing legume native to China.

It features compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers in cascading clusters. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is often cultivated with caution.
