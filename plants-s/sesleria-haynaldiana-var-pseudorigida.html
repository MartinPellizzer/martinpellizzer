Sesleria haynaldiana var. pseudorigida

Sesleria haynaldiana var. pseudorigida, commonly known as the rigid false oat grass, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
