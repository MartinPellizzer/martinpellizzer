Stipa sareptana subsp. praecapillata

Stipa sareptana subsp.

praecapillata, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense tussocks.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils at high elevations. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often slightly drooping.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
