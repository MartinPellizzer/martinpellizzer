Silene simplicicarona

Silene simplicicarona, commonly known as the simple-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Silene simplicicarona is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
