Streblus perakensis

Streblus perakensis, commonly known as the Bornean strangler fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and unique strangler habit, where it envelops its host tree. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig-like fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often covered in a layer of sticky resin.

Streblus perakensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food for various wildlife species.
