Scaphochlamys pennipicta

Scaphochlamys pennipicta, commonly known as the painted shield orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often brightly colored pseudobulbs that are covered in a dense layer of hair. The plant produces showy, spurred orchid flowers that are typically pink or purple, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade in forested or rocky habitats.

Scaphochlamys pennipicta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
