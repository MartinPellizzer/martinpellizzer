Sauropus salignus

Sauropus salignus, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its attractive foliage.
