Silene lineariloba

Silene lineariloba, commonly known as the slender-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its common name.

Silene lineariloba is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.
