Scutellaria orientalis subsp. macrostegia

Scutellaria orientalis subsp.

macrostegia, commonly known as the large-stemmed skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, often branching towards the upper part. Its lance-shaped leaves are arranged oppositely, and it produces small, two-lipped blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
