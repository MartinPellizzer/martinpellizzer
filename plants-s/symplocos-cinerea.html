Symplocos cinerea

Symplocos cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces bright blue berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Symplocos cinerea is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
