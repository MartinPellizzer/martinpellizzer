Saxifraga paniculata

Saxifraga paniculata, commonly known as the clustered saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant features a low-growing habit with dark green, succulent leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are arranged in panicles, giving it a delicate, airy appearance.

Saxifraga paniculata is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
