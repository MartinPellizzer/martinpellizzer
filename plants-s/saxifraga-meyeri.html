Saxifraga meyeri

Saxifraga meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in a terminal inflorescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Saxifraga meyeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
