Saxifraga platypetala

Saxifraga platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Saxifraga platypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
