Siparuna budlejifolia

Siparuna budlejifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its environment. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Siparuna budlejifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
