Salvia bullulata

Salvia bullulata, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy foliage and showy blue to purple flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens. Its aromatic leaves and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Salvia bullulata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
