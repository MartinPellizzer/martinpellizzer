Scolopendrium septentrionale

Scolopendrium septentrionale, commonly known as the northern clubmoss, is a species of fern ally native to the northern United States and Canada.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, acidic soils in shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with narrow, strap-like leaves that form dense, low-growing clusters. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

Scolopendrium septentrionale plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
