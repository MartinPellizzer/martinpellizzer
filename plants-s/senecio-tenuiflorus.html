Senecio tenuiflorus

Senecio tenuiflorus, commonly known as the narrowflower Senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, producing small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
