Sanguisorba minor f. rhodopaea

Sanguisorba minor f. rhodopaea, commonly known as the pink sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has upright, hairy stems and linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and vibrant flower color.
