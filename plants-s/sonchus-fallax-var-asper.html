Sonchus fallax var. asper

Sonchus fallax var. asper, commonly known as the rough sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaves. The plant features serrated, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a rough texture. It produces yellow flowers that develop into prickly, spiny seed heads.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
