Salvia maymanica

Salvia maymanica, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking violet to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia maymanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
