Selaginella microclada

Selaginella microclada, commonly known as the little clubmoss, is a member of the family Selaginellaceae and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central America and northern South America.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a compact and dense look. Selaginella microclada is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
