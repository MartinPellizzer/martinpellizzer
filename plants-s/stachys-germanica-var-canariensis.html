Stachys germanica var. canariensis

Stachys germanica var. canariensis, commonly known as Canary betony, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.
