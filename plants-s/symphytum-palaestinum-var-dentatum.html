Symphytum palaestinum var. dentatum

Symphytum palaestinum var. dentatum, commonly known as the Palestinian comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its varietal name "dentatum.". The plant produces tall spikes of violet or blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
