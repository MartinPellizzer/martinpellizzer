Senecio incisifolius

Senecio incisifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
