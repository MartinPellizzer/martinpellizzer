Sorbus valbrayi

Sorbus valbrayi, commonly known as the Valbray rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters and its distinctive, rounded fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The Valbray rowan is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
