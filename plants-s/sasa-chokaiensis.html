Sasa chokaiensis

Sasa chokaiensis, commonly known as the Chokai bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine and herbal remedies.

Sasa chokaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
