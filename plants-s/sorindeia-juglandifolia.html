Sorindeia juglandifolia

Sorindeia juglandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut or Brazilian chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, with elongated leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are inconspicuous and followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in local ecosystems.
