Shorea pallida

Shorea pallida, commonly known as the pale shorea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its buttressed roots and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its lightweight, pale wood that is often used in construction.

It thrives in humid, lowland rainforests and plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
