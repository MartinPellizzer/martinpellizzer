Symplocos cyanocarpa var. pilosa

Symplocos cyanocarpa var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy blueberry.

It is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, characterized by its small, blue-black berries that are edible. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its varietal name "pilosa.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of this species include its compact growth habit and distinctive fruit morphology.
