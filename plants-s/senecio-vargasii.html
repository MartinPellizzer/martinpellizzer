Senecio vargasii

Senecio vargasii, commonly known as the Vargas's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Senecio vargasii is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along rocky outcrops.
