Salix rotundifolia f. pilosiuscula

Salix rotundifolia f. pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy willow, is a shrub characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It features rounded, often hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a unique appearance. This plant is native to northern regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found near water bodies. Its catkins are an important feature, contributing to its role in pollination and seed dispersal.

The hairy willow is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
