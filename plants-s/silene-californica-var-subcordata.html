Silene californica var. subcordata

Silene californica var. subcordata, commonly known as the Sierra starflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are slender and hairy.

This variety is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
