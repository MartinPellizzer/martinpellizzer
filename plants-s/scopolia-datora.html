Scopolia datora

Scopolia datora, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down.

Scopolia datora is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potentially toxic properties.
