Salvia scapiformis var. pinnata

Salvia scapiformis var. pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive pinnate venation. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental gardens and ecological landscapes.
