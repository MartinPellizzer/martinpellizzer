Sesleria caerulea var. mutica

Sesleria caerulea var. mutica, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flower spikes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique texture and color, which add visual interest to garden landscapes.
