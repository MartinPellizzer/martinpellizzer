Setaria phyllomacra

Setaria phyllomacra, commonly known as the broadleaf foxtail, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, bristly seed heads that are often seen in the wild during the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Setaria phyllomacra is a hardy perennial that plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small insects and birds.
