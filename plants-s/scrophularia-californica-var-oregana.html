Scrophularia californica var. oregana

Scrophularia californica var. oregana, commonly known as Oregon plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually pink to purple in color.

It has medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Native American tribes for various health purposes.
