Solanum mucronatum

Solanum mucronatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

Solanum mucronatum is often found in moist, shaded environments and can be invasive in certain regions.
