Samba scleroxylon

Samba scleroxylon, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to central and western Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has a straight trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and often glossy. Its flowers are small and white, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
