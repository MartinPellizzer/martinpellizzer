Sporobolus hubbardii

Sporobolus hubbardii, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the southwestern and central regions.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers.
