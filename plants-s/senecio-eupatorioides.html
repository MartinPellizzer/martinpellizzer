Senecio eupatorioides

Senecio eupatorioides, commonly known as the blueweed or blue germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Senecio eupatorioides is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
