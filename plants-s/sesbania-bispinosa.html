Sesbania bispinosa

Sesbania bispinosa, commonly known as the two-spined sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Sesbania bispinosa is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
