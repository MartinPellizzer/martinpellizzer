Satureja alpestris

Satureja alpestris, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong, spicy fragrance.

Satureja alpestris is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
