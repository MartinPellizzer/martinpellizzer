Sida periptera

Sida periptera, commonly known as the velvetleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a velvety texture, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sida periptera is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, and it is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
