Satureja teneriffae var. cordifolia

Satureja teneriffae var. cordifolia, commonly known as Canary Island thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces tiny, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal.
