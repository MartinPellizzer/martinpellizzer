Senegalia tonkinensis

Senegalia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate and arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.

The bark and leaves of Senegalia tonkinensis have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
