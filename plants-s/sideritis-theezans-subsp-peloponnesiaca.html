Sideritis theezans subsp. peloponnesiaca

Sideritis theezans subsp.

peloponnesiaca, commonly known as the Peloponnesian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
