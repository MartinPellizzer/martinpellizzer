Senecio acarinus

Senecio acarinus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like appearance with white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Senecio acarinus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
