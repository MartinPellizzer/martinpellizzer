Scabiosa japonica

Scabiosa japonica, commonly known as Japanese scabious, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers with a distinctive, hemispherical shape. Its blooms appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scabiosa japonica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
