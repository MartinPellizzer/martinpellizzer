Silene grisea

Silene grisea, commonly known as the gray catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Silene grisea produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.
