Schisandra elongata var. marmorata

Schisandra elongata var. marmorata, commonly known as the marble-stemmed five-flower.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive mottled stems and leaves, which display a marbled pattern of green and white. It is a member of the Schisandraceae family and is native to specific regions in East Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red berries that are used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
