Solidago vinzentii

Solidago vinzentii, commonly known as Vincent's goldenrod, is a North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Vincent's goldenrod is an important nectar source for pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
