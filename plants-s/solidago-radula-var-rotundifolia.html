Solidago radula var. rotundifolia

Solidago radula var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its showy blooms attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
