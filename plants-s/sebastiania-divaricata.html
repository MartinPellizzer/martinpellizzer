Sebastiania divaricata

Sebastiania divaricata, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting birds and insects. The plant has opposite, simple, elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its fruits are small, three-lobed capsules that split open to release seeds.

Sebastiania divaricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
