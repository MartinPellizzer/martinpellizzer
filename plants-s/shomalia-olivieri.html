Shomalia olivieri

Shomalia olivieri, commonly known as the olive tree, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into drupes, which are typically olive-shaped and contain a single seed. Shomalia olivieri is valued for its fruit, which has been used for food, oil, and medicine for thousands of years.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and economic contexts.
