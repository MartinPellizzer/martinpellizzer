Securinega tinctoria

Securinega tinctoria, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has garnered attention for its potential pharmacological properties.
