Symphytum aintabicum

Symphytum aintabicum, commonly known as the Armenian comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features thick, fleshy rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces tall spikes of violet or blue flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
