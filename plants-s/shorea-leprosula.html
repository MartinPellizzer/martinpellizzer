Shorea leprosula

Shorea leprosula, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, often with a waxy surface.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
