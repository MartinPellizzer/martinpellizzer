Solanum abutilifolium

Solanum abutilifolium, commonly known as the desert tomatillo, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, spiny stems and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into spherical, yellowish fruits resembling tiny tomatoes. Its botanical features include a woody base and a dense, fibrous root system adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Solanum abutilifolium is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh environments.
