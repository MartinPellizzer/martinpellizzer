Silene eckloniana

Silene eckloniana, commonly known as the Cape ironweed, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight waxy coating.

Silene eckloniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
