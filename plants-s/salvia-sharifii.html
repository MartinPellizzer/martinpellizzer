Salvia sharifii

Salvia sharifii, commonly known as Sharif's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia sharifii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and resilience.
