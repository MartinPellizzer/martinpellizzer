Syncarpha vestita

Syncarpha vestita, commonly known as the silverleaf synbranch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Syncarpha vestita typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in its native habitat.
