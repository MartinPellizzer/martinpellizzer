Sonchus gibbosus

Sonchus gibbosus, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a globe, giving it its common name. The plant has basal leaves that are typically lobed or toothed, and its flowering stems produce yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.
