Senecio saxicola

Senecio saxicola, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that thrives in arid and rocky environments, often found in coastal regions. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, and its yellow flowers bloom in spring. Senecio saxicola is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
