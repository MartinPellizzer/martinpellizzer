Sideritis incana subsp. atlantica

Sideritis incana subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic blue mint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
