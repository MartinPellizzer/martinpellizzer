Salix purpurea subf. aragonensis

Salix purpurea subf.

aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and its distinctive reddish-brown bark.

The leaves are typically lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they turn a striking red or purple in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streams and riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
