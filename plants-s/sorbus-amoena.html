Sorbus amoena

Sorbus amoena, commonly known as the sweet cherry tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, and it is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sorbus amoena is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
