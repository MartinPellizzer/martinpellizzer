Schismatoglottis brooksii

Schismatoglottis brooksii, commonly known as the Brooks' schismatoglottis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, rhizomatous stem and produces small, pale green to white flowers arranged in a dense inflorescence. The leaves are typically broad, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a waxy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its unique floral structure and foliage.
