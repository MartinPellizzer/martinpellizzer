Sedum album var. setulosum

Sedum album var. setulosum, commonly known as the silver stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
