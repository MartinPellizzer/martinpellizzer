Solanum vile

Solanum vile, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are often toxic when ripe. Solanum vile is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
