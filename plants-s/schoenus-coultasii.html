Schoenus coultasii

Schoenus coultasii, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Schoenus coultasii is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
