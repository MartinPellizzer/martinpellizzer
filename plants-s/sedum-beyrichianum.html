Sedum beyrichianum

Sedum beyrichianum, commonly known as the silver rose, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a dense, low-growing rosette. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and water-wise gardening.
