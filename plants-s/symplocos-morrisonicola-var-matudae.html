Symplocos morrisonicola var. matudae

Symplocos morrisonicola var. matudae, commonly known as the Matudae waxleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences, which bloom in the spring. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens and as a decorative plant.
