Symphoricarpos racemosus var. trilobus

Symphoricarpos racemosus var. trilobus, commonly known as the three-lobed sandcherry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with three distinct lobes, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
