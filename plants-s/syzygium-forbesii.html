Syzygium forbesii

Syzygium forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow when ripe, containing edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
