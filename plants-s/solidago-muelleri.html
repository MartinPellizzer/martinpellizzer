Solidago muelleri

Solidago muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to dry soils in open woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Solidago muelleri is a valuable component of native plant communities and supports a variety of pollinators.
