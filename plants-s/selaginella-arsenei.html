Selaginella arsenei

Selaginella arsenei, commonly known as the Armenian mossy fern, is a species of spikemoss found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its creeping, prostrate growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces strobila that bear spores, typical of the lycophyte group. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Selaginella arsenei is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
