Salix capusii

Salix capusii, commonly known as the Capusii willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to parts of Central Asia, including regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, greenish-yellow catkins that appear in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Salix capusii is often found in wet or moist habitats such as riverbanks and marshy areas.
