Sophora ambigua

Sophora ambigua, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features striking clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
