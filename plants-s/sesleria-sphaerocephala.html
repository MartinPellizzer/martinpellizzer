Sesleria sphaerocephala

Sesleria sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and produces small, rounded flower heads that are often pink or purple in color. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Sesleria sphaerocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
