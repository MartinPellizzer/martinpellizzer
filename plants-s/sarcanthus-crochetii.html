The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees in the understory of dense tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and pale, often appearing in clusters, and they emit a faint, sweet fragrance during the early morning hours.

Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, Sarcanthus crochetii is considered a critically endangered species, with conservation efforts underway to protect its dwindling population.
