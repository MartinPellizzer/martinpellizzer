Syzygium paucipunctatum

Syzygium paucipunctatum, commonly known as the small-fruited tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody pods containing pale yellow seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
