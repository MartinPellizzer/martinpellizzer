Sciodaphyllum marahuacense

Sciodaphyllum marahuacense, commonly known as the Mexican cloud forest tree, is a species native to the cloud forests of southern Mexico.

It is a tall, evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has broad, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
