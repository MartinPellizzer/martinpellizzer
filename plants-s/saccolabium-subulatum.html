Saccolabium subulatum

Saccolabium subulatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf burrseed, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spiny fruits. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Its unique morphology makes it an important component of its native ecosystem.
