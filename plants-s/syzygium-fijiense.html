Syzygium fijiense

Syzygium fijiense, commonly known as the Fiji apple or wild apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red or yellow berries.

Syzygium fijiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
