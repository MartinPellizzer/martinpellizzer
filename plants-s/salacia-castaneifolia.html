Salacia castaneifolia

Salacia castaneifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered salacia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Salaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, leathery leaflets that provide a dense canopy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
