Sapindus siamensis

Sapindus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam soapnut, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that encase a hard seed, and these fruits are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain saponins, which have cleansing properties and are used in natural detergents.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
