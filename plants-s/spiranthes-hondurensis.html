Spiranthes hondurensis

Spiranthes hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran spiral orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flower arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in late summer to early fall.

Spiranthes hondurensis is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological specificity.
