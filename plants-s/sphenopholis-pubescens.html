Sphenopholis pubescens

Sphenopholis pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sphenophyll, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the family Sphenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including spores, are borne on specialized leaves called sporophylls.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, Sphenopholis pubescens is of significant interest to botanists studying evolutionary plant adaptations.
