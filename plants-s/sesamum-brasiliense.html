Sesamum brasiliense

Sesamum brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian sesame, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its erect stem and alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in capsules and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is closely related to other sesame species.
