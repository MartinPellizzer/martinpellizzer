Scirpus pseudotriqueter

Scirpus pseudotriqueter, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of marsh and pond ecosystems.

Scirpus pseudotriqueter plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
