Sorbus domestica var. albida

Sorbus domestica var. albida, commonly known as the white-fruited whitebeam, is a variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its white or pale yellow fruits that contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant is native to southern Europe and is typically found in woodland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by the distinctive white berries in the autumn.

The tree has a slow growth rate and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
