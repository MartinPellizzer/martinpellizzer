Sarcochilus radicosus

Sarcochilus radicosus, commonly known as the red-stemmed rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or disturbed habitats, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with a reddish hue, and its leaves are broad and grass-like. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spike, and it blooms during the warmer months.

This species is valued for its unique appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
