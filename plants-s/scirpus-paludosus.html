Scirpus paludosus

Scirpus paludosus, commonly known as the marsh club-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowering spikes consist of numerous tiny, brownish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Scirpus paludosus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
