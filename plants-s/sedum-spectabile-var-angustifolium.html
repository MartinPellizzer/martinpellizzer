Sedum spectabile var. angustifolium

Sedum spectabile var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense clusters of star-shaped flowers that range from white to pink. The leaves are narrow, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This plant is notable for its long bloom period and drought tolerance.
