Seymeria anita

Seymeria anita, commonly known as the silverleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves covered in a dense layer of white, powdery wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Seymeria anita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
