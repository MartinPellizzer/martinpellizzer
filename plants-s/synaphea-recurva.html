Synaphea recurva

Synaphea recurva, commonly known as the curly synaphea, is a rare and delicate fern-like plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, recurving fronds that give it a distinctive, flowing appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many ferns.

Due to habitat loss, Synaphea recurva is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and conservation.
