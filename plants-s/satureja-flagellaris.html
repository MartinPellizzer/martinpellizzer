Satureja flagellaris

Satureja flagellaris, commonly known as Turkish oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, oval leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil.

Satureja flagellaris is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional remedies and as a flavoring agent.
