Statice isernii

Statice isernii, commonly known as the Isern's sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and well-drained soils. The plant produces dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its fibrous roots help stabilize coastal dunes, making it an important species for erosion control.

Statice isernii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
