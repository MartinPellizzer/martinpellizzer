Shorea longestipulata

Shorea longestipulata, commonly known as the long-stipule shorea, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and broad, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its durable wood, it is also valued for commercial timber use.
