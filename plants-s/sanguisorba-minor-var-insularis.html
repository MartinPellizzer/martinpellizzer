Sanguisorba minor var. insularis

Sanguisorba minor var. insularis, commonly known as the island sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and a distinctive reddish stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Sanguisorba minor var. insularis is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
