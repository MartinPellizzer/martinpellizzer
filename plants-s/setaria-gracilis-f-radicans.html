Setaria gracilis f. radicans

Setaria gracilis f. radicans, commonly known as the creeping foxtail, is a variant of the foxtail millet species.

It is a grassy plant characterized by its slender, wiry stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous short, bristly spikelets. It is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats make it both a weed and a potential forage plant.
