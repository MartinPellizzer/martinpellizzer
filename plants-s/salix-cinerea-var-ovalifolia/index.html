Salix Cinerea Var. Ovalifolia

Salix cinerea var. ovalifolia is a unique variant of the willow family known for its distinctive oval-shaped leaves. This plant thrives in moist, often shaded environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and riverbanks. Its delicate appearance and ecological importance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in popular horticulture. Understanding its biology and habitat can enhance our appreciation of plant diversity in natural ecosystems.
