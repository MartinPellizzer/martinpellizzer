Sarothamnus vulgaris var. sericeus

Sarothamnus vulgaris var. sericeus, commonly known as silver thyme or silky thyme, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
