Scilla siberica subsp. armena

Scilla siberica subsp.

armena, commonly known as Armenian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is popular in gardens and botanical collections.
