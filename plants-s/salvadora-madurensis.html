Salvadora madurensis

Salvadora madurensis, commonly known as the Madurese sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique fragrance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
