Scorzonera inaequiscapa

Scorzonera inaequiscapa, commonly known as the unequal-flowered scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera inaequiscapa is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
