Senecio napellifolius

Senecio napellifolius, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and chrysanthemums, and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its compact growth habit and long blooming period.

Senecio napellifolius prefers bright, indirect light and moderate watering to maintain its health and vibrancy.
