Scirpus bracteatus

Scirpus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteated bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.
