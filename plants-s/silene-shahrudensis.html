Silene shahrudensis

Silene shahrudensis, commonly known as the Shahrud catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment.

Silene shahrudensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
