Sorbus admonitor

Sorbus admonitor, commonly known as the warning rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries that mature in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
