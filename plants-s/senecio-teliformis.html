Senecio teliformis

Senecio teliformis, commonly known as the silver leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features wiry, branched stems covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually yellow with a white or pale yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Senecio teliformis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.
