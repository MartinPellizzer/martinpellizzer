Sprekelia glauca

Sprekelia glauca, commonly known as the blue-eyed daffodil, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple with a white center, resembling daffodils. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces a cluster of flowers at the top of a sturdy stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Sprekelia glauca is known for its long blooming period, often lasting several weeks in the spring.
