Stryphnodendron obovatum

Stryphnodendron obovatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pataua, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant is also notable for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
