Salix glaucophylla var. lutea

Salix glaucophylla var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow-leaf willow, is a variant of the glaucophylla willow species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green leaves, which are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It produces catkins in the spring, typical of willow species, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
