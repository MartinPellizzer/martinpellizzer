Scabiosa farinosa

Scabiosa farinosa, commonly known as the cloud flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white to pinkish flower heads that resemble clouds, which give it its common name. The plant has basal leaves that are woolly and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Scabiosa farinosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
