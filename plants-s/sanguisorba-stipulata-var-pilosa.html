Sanguisorba stipulata var. pilosa

Sanguisorba stipulata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy sanguisorba, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have prominent stipules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
