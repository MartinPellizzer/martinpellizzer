Stigmaphyllon goudotii

Stigmaphyllon goudotii, commonly known as the yellow stigmaphyllon, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
