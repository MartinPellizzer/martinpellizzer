Silene marschallii subsp. marschallii

Silene marschallii subsp.

marschallii, commonly known as the Marschall's campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and it produces striking pink to purple flowers in racemose inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season. Silene marschallii subsp.

marschallii is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
