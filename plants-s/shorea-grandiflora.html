Shorea grandiflora

Shorea grandiflora, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has large, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It produces yellowish-white flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits.

Shorea grandiflora plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
