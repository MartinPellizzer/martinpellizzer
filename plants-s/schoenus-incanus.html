Schoenus incanus

Schoenus incanus, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-gray inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Schoenus incanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
