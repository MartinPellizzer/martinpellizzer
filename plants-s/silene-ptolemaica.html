Silene ptolemaica

Silene ptolemaica, commonly known as the Ptolemaic catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems. Silene ptolemaica is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
