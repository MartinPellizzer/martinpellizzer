Stachys ballota

Stachys ballota, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Stachys ballota is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
