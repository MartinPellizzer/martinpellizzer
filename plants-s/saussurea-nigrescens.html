Saussurea nigrescens

Saussurea nigrescens, commonly known as the black saffron or Indian saffron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, including parts of Nepal, India, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional Ayurvedic and Tibetan medicine for centuries.

The plant's rhizomes and flowers are used to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory conditions.
