Saxifraga ferruginea var. diffusa

Saxifraga ferruginea var. diffusa, commonly known as the rusty saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, rusty-colored coating, which gives it its common name.

This species is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
