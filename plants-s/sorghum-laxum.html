Sorghum laxum

Sorghum laxum, commonly known as the slender sorghum, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while its seeds are small and enclosed in a hard casing.

Sorghum laxum is often used for forage and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
