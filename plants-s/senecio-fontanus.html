Senecio fontanus

Senecio fontanus, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and woolly, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.

Senecio fontanus is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
