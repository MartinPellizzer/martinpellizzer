Stylogyne ramiflora

Stylogyne ramiflora, commonly known as the branching leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Stylogyne ramiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
