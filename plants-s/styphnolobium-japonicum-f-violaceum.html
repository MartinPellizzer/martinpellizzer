Styphnolobium japonicum f. violaceum

Styphnolobium japonicum f. violaceum, commonly known as the purple-leaf Chinese wisteria, is a cultivar of the wisteria plant characterized by its striking violet foliage.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, pale purple to white flowers in the spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like leaflets, giving it a delicate appearance.

This cultivar is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks due to its aesthetic appeal and vigorous growth.
