Stuckenia filiformis var. austrosibirica

Stuckenia filiformis var. austrosibirica, commonly known as the Siberian filiform waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and wetlands. The plant features slender, thread-like leaves that are often submerged, with some floating leaves at the surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which appear in early summer.

This variety is adapted to tolerate cold climates and is often found in nutrient-rich aquatic habitats.
