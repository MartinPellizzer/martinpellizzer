Senna georgica var. georgica

Senna georgica var. georgica, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, brown pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environments.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional medicine.
