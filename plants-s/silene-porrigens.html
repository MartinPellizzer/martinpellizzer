Silene porrigens

Silene porrigens, commonly known as the purple poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat hairy.

Silene porrigens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
