Senecio phalacrolaenus

Senecio phalacrolaenus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Senecio phalacrolaenus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and rockeries.
