Sapium muelleri

Sapium muelleri, commonly known as the broad-leafed sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a tendency to flake off as the tree matures.

Sapium muelleri is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
