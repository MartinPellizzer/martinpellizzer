Senecio albanensis var. doroniciflorus

Senecio albanensis var. doroniciflorus, commonly known as the Albanian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a bluish-green hue. This variety is closely related to other Senecio species and shares similar ecological preferences, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or limestone areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
