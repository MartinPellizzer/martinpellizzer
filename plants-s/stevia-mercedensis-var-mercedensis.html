Stevia mercedensis var. mercedensis

Stevia mercedensis var. mercedensis, commonly known as the Merced stevia, is a species native to the Central Valley of California.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique genetic makeup, it is considered a distinct variety within the Stevia genus.
