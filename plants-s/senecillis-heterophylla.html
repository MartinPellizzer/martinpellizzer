Senecillis heterophylla

Senecillis heterophylla, commonly known as the variable willow-herb, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape depending on environmental conditions. Its small, pinkish-white flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
