Sesleria uliginosa f. cylindrica

Sesleria uliginosa f. cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical moor grass, is a compact, low-growing perennial grass native to the wetlands of western Europe.

It features slender, cylindrical inflorescences that are often tinged with purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in damp, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and bog environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a silvery-blue hue that adds visual interest.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting invertebrate life.
