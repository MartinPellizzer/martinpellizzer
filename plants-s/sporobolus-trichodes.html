Sporobolus trichodes

Sporobolus trichodes, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to sandy and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a feature that contributes to its ability to colonize open areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its drought tolerance and ecological role in stabilizing soils.
