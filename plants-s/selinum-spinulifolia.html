Selinum spinulifolia

Selinum spinulifolia, commonly known as the prickly goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves that are typically covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Selinum spinulifolia is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
