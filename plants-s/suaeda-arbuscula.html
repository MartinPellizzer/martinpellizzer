Suaeda arbuscula

Suaeda arbuscula, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a small, low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of less than 30 centimeters. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Suaeda arbuscula is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in harsh, saline conditions.
