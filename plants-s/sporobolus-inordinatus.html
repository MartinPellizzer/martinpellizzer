Sporobolus inordinatus

Sporobolus inordinatus, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a tuft of bristly hairs at the tip. The plant has small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny spikelets, each containing a single floret. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray sheen, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Sporobolus inordinatus is adapted to survive in poor soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.
