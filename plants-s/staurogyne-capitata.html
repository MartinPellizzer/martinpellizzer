Staurogyne capitata

Staurogyne capitata, commonly known as the water pennywort, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, opposite leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, with a rounded or slightly pointed tip. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are often submerged, making it challenging to observe in its natural habitat. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Staurogyne capitata is often found in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams.
