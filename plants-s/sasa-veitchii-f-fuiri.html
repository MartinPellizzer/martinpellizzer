Sasa veitchii f. fuiri

Sasa veitchii f. fuiri, commonly known as the Fuiri bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming bamboo species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and its ability to form dense, upright canopies. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to shaded or partially shaded environments. It has a slow growth rate and is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The bamboo's rhizomes spread underground, allowing it to form colonies over time.
