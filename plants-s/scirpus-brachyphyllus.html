Scirpus brachyphyllus

Scirpus brachyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. Scirpus brachyphyllus thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
