Sasa gracillima f. linearifolia

Sasa gracillima f. linearifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa gracillima species native to East Asia.

It is a clump-forming evergreen bamboo characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically shorter and more slender than those of the typical form. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories, and is valued for its ornamental appearance. Its foliage provides a delicate, grass-like texture, making it suitable for ornamental gardening and landscape design.

The plant is also used in traditional Asian medicine for its purported health benefits.
