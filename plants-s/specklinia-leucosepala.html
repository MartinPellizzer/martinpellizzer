Specklinia leucosepala

Specklinia leucosepala, commonly known as the white-veined staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, tree-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its pale, almost white veins contrasting against the darker green foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
