Scutellaria pseudoserrata

Scutellaria pseudoserrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Scutellaria pseudoserrata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
