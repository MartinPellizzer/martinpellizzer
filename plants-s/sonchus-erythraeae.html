Sonchus erythraeae

Sonchus erythraeae, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while the flowers are yellow with a red-tinged base.

It is a short-lived perennial that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
