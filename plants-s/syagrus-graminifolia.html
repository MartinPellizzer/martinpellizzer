Syagrus graminifolia

Syagrus graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaf palm, is a species native to the coastal regions of northeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed structure and long, narrow leaves that resemble grass, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stems. Syagrus graminifolia thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

It is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that is well adapted to the tropical and subtropical climates of its native habitat.
