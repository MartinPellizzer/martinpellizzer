Sexilia missurica

Sexilia missurica, commonly known as the Mississippi sand-plain grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
