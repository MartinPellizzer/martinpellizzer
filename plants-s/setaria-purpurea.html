Setaria purpurea

Setaria purpurea, commonly known as purple foxtail, is a species of grass native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and the presence of long, bristly inflorescences that give it a distinctive, foxtail-like appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or purple in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Setaria purpurea is an annual plant that plays a role in both ecological systems and can sometimes be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
