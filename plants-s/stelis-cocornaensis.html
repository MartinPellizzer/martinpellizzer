Stelis cocornaensis

Stelis cocornaensis, commonly known as the Cocora orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

Stelis cocornaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
