Schisandra crassifolia

Schisandra crassifolia, commonly known as the rough-fruited five-flavor vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its berries are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Schisandra crassifolia thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
