Salacia mortehanii

Salacia mortehanii, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
