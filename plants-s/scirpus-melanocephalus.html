Scirpus melanocephalus

Scirpus melanocephalus, commonly known as black-headed bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish or blackish base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, which develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus melanocephalus plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
