Solidago virgaurea proles nudiflora

Solidago virgaurea proles nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
