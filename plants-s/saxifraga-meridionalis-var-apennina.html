Saxifraga meridionalis var. apennina

Saxifraga meridionalis var. apennina, commonly known as the Southern saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters during the spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
