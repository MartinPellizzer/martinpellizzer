Stelis dimidiata

Stelis dimidiata, commonly known as the half-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive foliage, with leaves that are often divided into two parts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Stelis dimidiata is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
