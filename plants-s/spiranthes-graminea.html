Spiranthes graminea

Spiranthes graminea, commonly known as the grass-pink, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its striking, spiral-shaped inflorescence of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is a perennial herb that produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and moths.

The botanical features of Spiranthes graminea make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native ecosystems.
