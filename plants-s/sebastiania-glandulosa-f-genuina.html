Sebastiania glandulosa f. genuina

Sebastiania glandulosa f. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that persist through winter, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The berries are not only visually striking but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
