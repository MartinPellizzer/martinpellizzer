Salvia dorrii

Salvia dorrii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill sage, is a perennial herb native to California and parts of Nevada.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its flowers are typically blue to purple and bloom in the summer months.

Salvia dorrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
