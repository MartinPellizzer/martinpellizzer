Stylogyne minutiflora

Stylogyne minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered stylogyne, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual plant characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of botanical circles.
