Sclarea viscosissima

Sclarea viscosissima, commonly known as the sticky angelica, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, umbrella-like compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in a dense, spherical cluster. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Sclarea viscosissima thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
