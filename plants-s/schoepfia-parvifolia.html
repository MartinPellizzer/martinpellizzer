Schoepfia parvifolia

Schoepfia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered schoepfia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky habitats and can tolerate full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to xeriscapic gardens and native plant collections.
