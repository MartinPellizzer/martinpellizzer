Sideropogon

Sideropogon, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. Sideropogon typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing a preference for well-drained soils.

It is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species that can thrive in a variety of climates.
