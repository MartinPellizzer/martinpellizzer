Saxifraga paniculata var. navarrensis

Saxifraga paniculata var. navarrensis, commonly known as the Navarre saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils, often in open, sunny habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are characterized by their serrated edges and hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
