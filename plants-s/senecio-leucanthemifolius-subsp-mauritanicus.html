Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp. mauritanicus

Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp.

mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritania ragwort, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It features upright stems with hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
