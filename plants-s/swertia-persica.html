Swertia persica

Swertia persica, commonly known as Persian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Swertia persica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
