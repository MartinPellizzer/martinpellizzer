Spergularia rubra var. stipularis

Spergularia rubra var. stipularis, commonly known as the red sandplain grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and sandy environments.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and inland sandy plains.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for various insect species.
