Sobralia aurantiaca

Sobralia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Sobralia aurantiaca thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
