Senecio kilimanjari

Senecio kilimanjari, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa, particularly Tanzania.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its unique habitat and appearance, it is a notable species in the flora of the East African highlands.
