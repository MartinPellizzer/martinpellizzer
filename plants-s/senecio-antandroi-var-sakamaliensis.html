Senecio antandroi var. sakamaliensis

Senecio antandroi var. sakamaliensis, commonly known as the Sakamaliensis Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.
