Struthiola virgata var. linnaeana

Struthiola virgata var. linnaeana, commonly known as the Linnaean struthiola, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
