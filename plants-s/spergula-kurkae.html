Spergula kurkae

Spergula kurkae, commonly known as Kurka's wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Spergula kurkae is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water from deeper soil layers.
