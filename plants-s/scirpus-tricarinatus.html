Scirpus tricarinatus

Scirpus tricarinatus, commonly known as the three-ribbed bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters in marshy or aquatic environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, appearing in late summer to early autumn. Its roots help stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus tricarinatus is an important species for wildlife, providing habitat and food for various birds and invertebrates.
