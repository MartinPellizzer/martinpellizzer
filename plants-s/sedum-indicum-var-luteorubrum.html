Sedum indicum var. luteorubrum

Sedum indicum var. luteorubrum, commonly known as the orange stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with red or orange, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it an ideal plant for arid environments.
