Sclerolaena longicuspis

Sclerolaena longicuspis, commonly known as the long-fruited rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fleshy, fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
