Sanguisorba japonensis

Sanguisorba japonensis, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pinkish-red flower clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Sanguisorba japonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
