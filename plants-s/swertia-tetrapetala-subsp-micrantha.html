Swertia tetrapetala subsp. micrantha

Swertia tetrapetala subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It features a compact, upright growth habit with lanceolate, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in July and August. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, characteristic of the Gentianaceae family.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
