Salvia leiocalycina

Salvia leiocalycina, commonly known as the blue salvia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
