Salix sclerophylla var. sclerophylloides

Salix sclerophylla var. sclerophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a variant of the broader Salix sclerophylla species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often densely hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in high-altitude ecosystems.
