Senecio leucanthemifolius var. vernalis

Senecio leucanthemifolius var. vernalis, commonly known as the spring everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in alpine and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, making it a notable wildflower during that season.
