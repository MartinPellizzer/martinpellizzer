Sarcochilus platyphyllus

Sarcochilus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed sarcochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a dense, wiry root system and produces striking, bright green to yellowish-green leaves that are broad and strap-like. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near the bases of trees or on rocky outcrops. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
