Schefflera phanerophlebia

Schefflera phanerophlebia, commonly known as the giant umbrella plant, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that resemble an umbrella, with multiple leaflets radiating from a central stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of dense forests.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
