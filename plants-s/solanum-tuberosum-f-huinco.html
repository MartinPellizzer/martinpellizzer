Solanum tuberosum f. huinco

Solanum tuberosum f. huinco, commonly known as the Huinco potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which includes a distinct shape and skin texture compared to standard potato varieties. This variety is native to South America, specifically the Andean region, where it has been cultivated for generations. The plant exhibits typical solanaceous features, such as hairy stems and alternate leaves.

It is valued for its flavor, nutritional content, and adaptability to specific growing conditions.
