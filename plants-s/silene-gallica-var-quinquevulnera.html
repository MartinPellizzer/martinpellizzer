Silene gallica var. quinquevulnera

Silene gallica var. quinquevulnera, commonly known as the five-wound campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
