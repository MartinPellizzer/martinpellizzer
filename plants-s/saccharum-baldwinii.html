Saccharum baldwinii

Saccharum baldwinii, commonly known as Baldwin sugarcane.

It is a tall, perennial grass native to the Caribbean and parts of South America. This species is characterized by its robust stalks and high sugar content, making it valuable for commercial sugarcane production. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates with well-drained soils.

Its growth cycle includes a long vegetative period followed by flowering and seed production.
