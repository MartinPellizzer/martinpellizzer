Serjania punctata

Serjania punctata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red serjania, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Serjania punctata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking flowers and berries.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.
