Saxifraga mertensiana f. eastwoodiae

Saxifraga mertensiana f. eastwoodiae, commonly known as the Eastwood's saxifrage, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or tundra environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

This variety is notable for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its rarity and adaptability to extreme climates.
