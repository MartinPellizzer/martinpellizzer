Stephania glabra

Stephania glabra, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a perennial climbing plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains various alkaloids, some of which have medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its root is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
