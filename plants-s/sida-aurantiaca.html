Sida aurantiaca

Sida aurantiaca, commonly known as yellow mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Sida aurantiaca is typically found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in warm, sunny environments.

It is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
