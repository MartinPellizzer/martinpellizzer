Scirpus cernuus var. subtilis

Scirpus cernuus var. subtilis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, slender, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly smaller and more delicate appearance compared to other Scirpus species.
