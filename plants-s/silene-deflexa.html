Silene deflexa

Silene deflexa, commonly known as the bent-stem catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often curve downward, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pinkish to white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Silene deflexa is often found in moist, sandy soils in open areas such as fields and woodland edges.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.
