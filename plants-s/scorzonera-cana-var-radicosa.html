Scorzonera cana var. radicosa

Scorzonera cana var. radicosa, commonly known as the common scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust root system with thick, fleshy roots that are often harvested for their edible qualities. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple or brown center, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

Scorzonera cana var. radicosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
