Spiraea hingshanensis

Spiraea hingshanensis, commonly known as the Hingshan weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and ecological restoration projects.
