Streptanthus tortuosus var. suffrutescens

Streptanthus tortuosus var. suffrutescens, commonly known as the twisted-stemed jew's ear, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
