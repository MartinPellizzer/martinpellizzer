Solanum plousianthemum subsp. kasima

Solanum plousianthemum subsp.

kasima, commonly known as the Kasima nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
