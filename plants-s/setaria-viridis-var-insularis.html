Setaria viridis var. insularis

Setaria viridis var. insularis, commonly known as the island green millet, is a variant of the green millet species native to certain island regions.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny grains, which are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or used in ecological restoration projects.
