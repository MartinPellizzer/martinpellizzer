Syzygium microcymum

Syzygium microcymum, commonly known as the small-cyme clove.

It is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences called cymes, from which it derives its scientific name. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, aromatic fruits that are edible.

Syzygium microcymum is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
