Stevia dissitifolia

Stevia dissitifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

Stevia dissitifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
