Schoenus capitatus

Schoenus capitatus, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and forms compact, mat-like growths. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Schoenus capitatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
