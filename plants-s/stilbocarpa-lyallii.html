Stilbocarpa lyallii

Stilbocarpa lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's thornapple, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important part of New Zealand's native flora and a subject of ecological study.
