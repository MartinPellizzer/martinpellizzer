Senegalia caraniana

Senegalia caraniana, commonly known as the velvet mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a hardy species in desert environments.

Senegalia caraniana plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
