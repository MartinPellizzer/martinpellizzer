Scleria terrestris var. decolorans

Scleria terrestris var. decolorans, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green with a distinctive yellow stripe along the midrib. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
