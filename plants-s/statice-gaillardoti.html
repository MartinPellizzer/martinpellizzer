Statice gaillardoti

Statice gaillardoti, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often growing in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Statice gaillardoti is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping and floral arrangements.
