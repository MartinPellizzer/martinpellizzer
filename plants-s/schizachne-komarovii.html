Schizachne komarovii

Schizachne komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Schizachne komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
