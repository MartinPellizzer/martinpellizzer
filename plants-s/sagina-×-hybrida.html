Sagina × hybrida

Sagina × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its tiny, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces pink or white flowers that attract pollinators. Sagina × hybrida is typically found in temperate regions and is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardy nature and delicate appearance.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for rock gardens and as a ground cover in shady or partially shaded areas.
