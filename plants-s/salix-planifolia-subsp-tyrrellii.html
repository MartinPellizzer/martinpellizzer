Salix planifolia subsp. tyrrellii

Salix planifolia subsp.

tyrrellii, commonly known as the Tyrrell's willow, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically smooth on both surfaces.

The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
