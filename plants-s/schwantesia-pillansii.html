Schwantesia pillansii

Schwantesia pillansii, commonly known as the Pillans' schwantesia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and ecological significance.
