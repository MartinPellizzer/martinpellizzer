Santolina ageratifolia

Santolina ageratifolia, commonly known as silver santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, aromatic foliage with silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental leaves make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and medicinal gardens.
