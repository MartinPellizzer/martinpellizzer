Solanum youngii

Solanum youngii, commonly known as the Young's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
