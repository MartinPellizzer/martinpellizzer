Sidalcea montana

Sidalcea montana, commonly known as the mountain checkerbloom, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, cylindrical seed pods.

Sidalcea montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also important for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
