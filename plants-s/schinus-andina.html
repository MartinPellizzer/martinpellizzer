Schinus andina

Schinus andina, commonly known as the Andean pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate. It produces small, red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed, and the bark is often used in traditional medicine.
