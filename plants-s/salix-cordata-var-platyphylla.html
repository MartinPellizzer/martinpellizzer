Salix cordata var. platyphylla

Salix cordata var. platyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams, wetlands, or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
