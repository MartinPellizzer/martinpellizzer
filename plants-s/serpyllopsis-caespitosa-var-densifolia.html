Serpyllopsis caespitosa var. densifolia

Serpyllopsis caespitosa var. densifolia, commonly known as the dense foliage serpyllopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and compact, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
