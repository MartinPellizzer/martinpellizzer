Siparuna lozaniana

Siparuna lozaniana, commonly known as the pink siphonostemon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. It prefers humid, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Siparuna lozaniana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
