Sicyos trigeminus

Sicyos trigeminus, commonly known as the three-lobed gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that give way to orange, spiky fruits. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a fibrous, rough-textured fruit exterior.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in various cultures.
