Salicornia perrieri

Salicornia perrieri, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often red or pink in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces edible, seed-like structures called "sea beans.".

Salicornia perrieri is often found in coastal regions and salt marshes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
