Salicornia donaldsonii

Salicornia donaldsonii, commonly known as sea beans, is a flowering plant species native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments such as salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant produces small, green, bean-like fruits that are often used as a vegetable in culinary applications. Its leaves are succulent and can be eaten raw or cooked.

Salicornia donaldsonii plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
