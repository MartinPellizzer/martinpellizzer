Solanum truncatum

Solanum truncatum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and oval-shaped leaves with slightly notched edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that start green and turn red when mature.

Solanum truncatum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes, and it contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
