Stevia lucida var. pueblensis

Stevia lucida var. pueblensis, commonly known as Pueblo stevia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its low water requirements.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a natural sweetener.
