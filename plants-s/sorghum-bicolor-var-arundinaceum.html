Sorghum bicolor var. arundinaceum

Sorghum bicolor var. arundinaceum, commonly known as giant broomcorn millet, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, feathery inflorescences. The plant is cultivated for its starchy grains and fibrous stalks, which have various uses in food and industry. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soils for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability to drought conditions.
