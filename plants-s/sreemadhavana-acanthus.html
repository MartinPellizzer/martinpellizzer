Sreemadhavana acanthus

Sreemadhavana acanthus, commonly known as the Indian Acanthus, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that resemble a ship's prow, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces tall spikes of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and can grow quite deep, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Sreemadhavana acanthus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in ancient architecture.
