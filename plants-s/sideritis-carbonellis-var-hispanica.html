Sideritis carbonellis var. hispanica

Sideritis carbonellis var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Sideritis carbonellis var. hispanica is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
