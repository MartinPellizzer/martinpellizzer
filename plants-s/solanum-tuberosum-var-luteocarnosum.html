Solanum tuberosum var. luteocarnosum

Solanum tuberosum var. luteocarnosum, commonly known as the yellow-fleshed potato, is a variant of the common potato characterized by its yellow or orange flesh.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. The plant produces tubers that are typically smaller than standard potatoes but have a distinct, vibrant color. It is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional profile, making it a sought-after variety in specialty agriculture.

The botanical features include hairy stems, alternate leaves, and flowers that are typically white or purple.
