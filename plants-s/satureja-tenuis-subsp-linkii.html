Satureja tenuis subsp. linkii

Satureja tenuis subsp.

linkii, commonly known as narrowleaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cuisine for their pungent, slightly minty flavor.
