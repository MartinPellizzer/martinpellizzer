Silene vulgaris f. rubriflora

Silene vulgaris f. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking red or pink flowers.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.
