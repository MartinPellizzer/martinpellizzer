Serapias cordigera var. curvifolia

Serapias cordigera var. curvifolia, commonly known as the curved-leaved sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are often yellow or orange in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and often curved, giving it its common name.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
