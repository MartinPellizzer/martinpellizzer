Spondioides villosa

Spondioides villosa, commonly known as the hairy spondias, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red fruits.

Spondioides villosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
