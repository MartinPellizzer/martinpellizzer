Struthiopteris difformis

Struthiopteris difformis, commonly known as the variable shield fern, is a perennial fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant has a distinctive, shield-shaped sorus that is covered with a false indusium, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Struthiopteris difformis is known for its robust growth and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
