Sacciolepis nana

Sacciolepis nana, commonly known as the dwarf sacciolepis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, with compact, rosette-forming foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pale green or white, which appear in clusters at the base of the plant.

Sacciolepis nana is well adapted to moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
