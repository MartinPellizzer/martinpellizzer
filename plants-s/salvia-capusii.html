Salvia capusii

Salvia capusii, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia capusii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
