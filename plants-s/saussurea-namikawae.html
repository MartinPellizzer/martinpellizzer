Saussurea namikawae

Saussurea namikawae, commonly known as the Namikawa saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and are prized for their aromatic properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
