Stipa × brevicallosa

Stipa × brevicallosa, commonly known as the dwarf wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, dense tufts of foliage that often form a low, mat-like structure. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that are typically arranged in a loose panicle, and its leaves are narrow, wiry, and covered in fine hairs. Stipa × brevicallosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential use in restoration ecology.
