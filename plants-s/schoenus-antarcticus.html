Schoenus antarcticus

Schoenus antarcticus, commonly known as Antarctic bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, including Antarctica and sub-Antarctic islands, where it thrives in moist, rocky soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Schoenus antarcticus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its extreme habitat.
