Silene colorata subsp. morisiana

Silene colorata subsp.

morisiana, commonly known as the Morisian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
