Scirpus nipponicus

Scirpus nipponicus, commonly known as the Japanese bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stems, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies in wetland environments.

Scirpus nipponicus is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
