Statice tchefouensis

Statice tchefouensis, commonly known as the Algerian sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vivid blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Statice tchefouensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms.
