Scrophularia kakudensis var. latisepala

Scrophularia kakudensis var. latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer months.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
