Stenotus lanuginosus var. andersonii

Stenotus lanuginosus var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It has a compact growth habit with sturdy stems that often root at the base.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
