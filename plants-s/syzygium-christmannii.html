Syzygium christmannii

Syzygium christmannii, commonly known as the Christmann's apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters.

The fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe that is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor.
