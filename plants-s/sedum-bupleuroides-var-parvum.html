Sedum bupleuroides var. parvum

Sedum bupleuroides var. parvum, commonly known as dwarf blue sedum, is a low-growing succulent native to arid regions of North America.

It features dense clusters of small, blue-gray leaves that are fleshy and adapted to store water. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact form and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
