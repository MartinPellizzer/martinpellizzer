Staphylea simplicifolia

Staphylea simplicifolia, commonly known as the common staphylea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, simple leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
