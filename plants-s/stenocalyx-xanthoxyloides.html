Stenocalyx xanthoxyloides

Stenocalyx xanthoxyloides, commonly known as the yellow-wood tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant has smooth, pale bark and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and construction.
