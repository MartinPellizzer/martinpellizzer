Saxifraga algisii

Saxifraga algisii, commonly known as the Alascan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the rocky regions of Alaska and western Canada.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and rosette-forming leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Saxifraga algisii is adapted to withstand harsh weather conditions and has a limited geographic distribution.
