Smilax puberula

Smilax puberula, commonly known as the hairy smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically oval with a hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

Smilax puberula is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
