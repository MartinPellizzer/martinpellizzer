Solanum decachondrum

Solanum decachondrum, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, and its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum decachondrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
