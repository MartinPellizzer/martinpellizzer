Sicyos bryoniifolius var. ambrosianus

Sicyos bryoniifolius var. ambrosianus, commonly known as the ambrosian cucumber, is a variant of the genus Sicyos, which belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of Bryonia. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, cucumber-like fruits that are typically used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
