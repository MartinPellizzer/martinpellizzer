Scleria woodii var. ornata

Scleria woodii var. ornata, commonly known as the ornate bulbous rush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often bear clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and produces underground bulbs, which are important for its survival in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in wet, marshy environments.

Scleria woodii var. ornata is often found in swampy areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
