Scleria baldwinii

Scleria baldwinii, commonly known as Baldwin's spike-rush, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with the flowering stems rising above the foliage.

Scleria baldwinii plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
