Senna pleurocarpa var. longifolia

Senna pleurocarpa var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf senna, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, slender leaflets that are typically arranged in pairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spikes, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in sandy or coastal soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
