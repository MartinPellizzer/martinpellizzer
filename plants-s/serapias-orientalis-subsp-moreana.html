Serapias orientalis subsp. moreana

Serapias orientalis subsp.

moreana, commonly known as the Balkan sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, with a dense inflorescence of yellow-orange flowers. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive hooded structure, with petals that are often reflexed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
