Sulla aculeolata subsp. aculeolata

Sulla aculeolata subsp.

aculeolata, commonly known as the prickly sulla, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features erect, sparsely branched stems that are covered with stiff, backward-pointing hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by oval, woody pods containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
