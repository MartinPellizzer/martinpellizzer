Stachys xanthantha var. gracilis

Stachys xanthantha var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
