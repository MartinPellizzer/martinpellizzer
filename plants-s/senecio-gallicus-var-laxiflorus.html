Senecio gallicus var. laxiflorus

Senecio gallicus var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the looseflower French senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
