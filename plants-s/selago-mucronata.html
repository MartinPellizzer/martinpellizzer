Selago mucronata

Selago mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved selago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Selago mucronata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
