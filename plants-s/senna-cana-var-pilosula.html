Senna cana var. pilosula

Senna cana var. pilosula, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are yellow and arranged in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
