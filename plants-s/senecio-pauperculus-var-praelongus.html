Senecio pauperculus var. praelongus

Senecio pauperculus var. praelongus, commonly known as the long-stemmed ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a distinctive addition to succulent collections.
