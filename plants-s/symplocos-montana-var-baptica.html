Symplocos montana var. baptica

Symplocos montana var. baptica, commonly known as the mountain waxflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and a dense covering of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos montana var. baptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
