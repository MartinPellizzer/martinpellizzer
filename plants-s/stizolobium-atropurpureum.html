Stizolobium atropurpureum

Stizolobium atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often tinged with purple, and its yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Stizolobium atropurpureum is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
