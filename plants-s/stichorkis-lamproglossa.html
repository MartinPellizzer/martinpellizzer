Stichorkis lamproglossa

Stichorkis lamproglossa, commonly known as the long-tongued orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous inflorescences. The flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a prominent, elongated lip that aids in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
