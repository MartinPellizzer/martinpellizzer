Satureja dolichodonta

Satureja dolichodonta, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to alpine climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oblong fruits.

Satureja dolichodonta is valued for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
