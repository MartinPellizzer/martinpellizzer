Salix × semiviminalis

Salix × semiviminalis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow that exhibits striking ornamental features.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that droop gracefully, creating a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and silvery white underneath. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

Botanically, it is valued for its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
