Senecio lasiorhizus

Senecio lasiorhizus, commonly known as the softleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a fibrous root system and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a head, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Senecio lasiorhizus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
