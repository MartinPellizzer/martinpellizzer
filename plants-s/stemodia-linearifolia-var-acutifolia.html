Stemodia linearifolia var. acutifolia

Stemodia linearifolia var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf stemodia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, linear leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically erect and branching, often growing up to 3 feet tall.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.
