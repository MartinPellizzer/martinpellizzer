Sophora alba

Sophora alba, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, pendulous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Botanically, it is notable for its use in traditional medicine and its role in ecological systems.
