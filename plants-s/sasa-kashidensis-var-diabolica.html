Sasa kashidensis var. diabolica

Sasa kashidensis var. diabolica, commonly known as the Devil's Sasa, is a variant of the Sasa kashidensis plant native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant characterized by its stiff, sword-shaped leaves and dense growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. It is notable for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value.

However, due to its invasive potential in certain regions, it requires careful management.
