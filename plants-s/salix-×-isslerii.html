Salix × isslerii

Salix × isslerii, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading appearance, giving it a distinctive, ornamental look. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of wind pollination. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a serrated edge.

Salix × isslerii is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and along waterways.
