Salvia parviflora

Salvia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Salvia parviflora is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
