Salicornia simonkaiana

Salicornia simonkaiana, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its leaves are often reduced to small scales, while the stems are fleshy and can store water.

Salicornia simonkaiana is also valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
