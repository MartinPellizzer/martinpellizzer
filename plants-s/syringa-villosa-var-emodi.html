Syringa villosa var. emodi

Syringa villosa var. emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan weeping lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its profuse, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has softly hairy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
