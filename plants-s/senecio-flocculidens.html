Senecio flocculidens

Senecio flocculidens, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white to grayish-blue foliage that resembles wool or cotton. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
