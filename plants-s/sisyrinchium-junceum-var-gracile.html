Sisyrinchium junceum var. gracile

Sisyrinchium junceum var. gracile, commonly known as slender blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
