Stenogrammitis brevipubens

Stenogrammitis brevipubens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stenogrammitis, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, closely packed leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant exhibits a thalloid structure, with a flattened, ribbon-like body that grows close to the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are borne on short, stalked leaves called sporophylls.
