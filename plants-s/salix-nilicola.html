Salix nilicola

Salix nilicola, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are elongated, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverine environments.
