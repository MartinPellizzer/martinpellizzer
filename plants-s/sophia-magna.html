Sophia magna

Sophia magna, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a large evergreen shrub or small tree native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant has broad, glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically white or pink, and they bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
