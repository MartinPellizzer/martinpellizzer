Stelis sororcula

Stelis sororcula, commonly known as the lady's tresses orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its striking, fragrant, violet or purple flowers that form a dense, basket-like structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, intricate flower structure.

Stelis sororcula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
