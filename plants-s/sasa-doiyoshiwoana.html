Sasa doiyoshiwoana

Sasa doiyoshiwoana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a clump-forming grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth, grass-like texture.

Sasa doiyoshiwoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Japanese gardens.
