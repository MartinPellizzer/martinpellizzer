Saxifraga hypnoides densa

Saxifraga hypnoides densa, commonly known as the dense saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of northern Europe and Siberia.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage, with small, often white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its leaves are usually succulent, which helps it retain moisture in nutrient-poor conditions.

Saxifraga hypnoides densa is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
