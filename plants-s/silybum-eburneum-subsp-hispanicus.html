Silybum eburneum subsp. hispanicus

Silybum eburneum subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish milk thistle, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of silymarin in its seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its growth cycle includes a period of dormancy during winter, with flowering occurring in the summer months.
