Scrophularia laxiflora

Scrophularia laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. The plant features slender stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular, typically pink or purple, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scrophularia laxiflora is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
