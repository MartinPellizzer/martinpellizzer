Shorea subpeltata

Shorea subpeltata, commonly known as the yellow meranti, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The tree is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow bark and large, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Shorea subpeltata is valued for its durable, heavy timber used in construction and furniture-making.
