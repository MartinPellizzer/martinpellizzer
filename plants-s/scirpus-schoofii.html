Scirpus schoofii

Scirpus schoofii, commonly known as the Schoof's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering heads consist of small, clustered spikes.

Scirpus schoofii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
