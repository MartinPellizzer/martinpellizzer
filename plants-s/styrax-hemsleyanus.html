Styrax hemsleyanus

Styrax hemsleyanus, commonly known as the Chinese styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a sticky resin, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a fragrance. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Styrax hemsleyanus thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.
