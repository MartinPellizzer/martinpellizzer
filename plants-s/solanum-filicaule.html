Solanum filicaule

Solanum filicaule, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
