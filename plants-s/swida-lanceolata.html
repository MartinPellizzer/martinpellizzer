Swida lanceolata

Swida lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf swida, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses and other shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Swida lanceolata is typically found in temperate forests and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
