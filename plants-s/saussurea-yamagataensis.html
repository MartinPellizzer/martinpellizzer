Saussurea yamagataensis

Saussurea yamagataensis, commonly known as Yamagata safflower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Saussurea yamagataensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
