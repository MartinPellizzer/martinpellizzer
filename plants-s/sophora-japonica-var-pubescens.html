Sophora japonica var. pubescens

Sophora japonica var. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese pagoda tree or the weeping pagoda tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping panicles of white or pale yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant has compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with small, ovate leaflets covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
