Salix chienii var. chienii

Salix chienii var. chienii, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are the primary means of pollination and seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its graceful appearance.
