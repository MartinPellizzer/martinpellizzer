Solanum bassoviicarpum

Solanum bassoviicarpum, commonly known as the Bassov's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, specifically Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
