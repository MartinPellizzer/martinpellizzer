Stenocalyx sulcatus var. longifolius

Stenocalyx sulcatus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved roughshrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Rubiaceae family.
