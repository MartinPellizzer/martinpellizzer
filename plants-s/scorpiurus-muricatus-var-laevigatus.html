Scorpiurus muricatus var. laevigatus

Scorpiurus muricatus var. laevigatus, commonly known as the smoothleaf scorpiurus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
