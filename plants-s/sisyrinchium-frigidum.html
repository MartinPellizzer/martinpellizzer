Sisyrinchium frigidum

Sisyrinchium frigidum, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is part of the Iridaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Sisyrinchium frigidum is known for its delicate appearance and early spring blooming period.
