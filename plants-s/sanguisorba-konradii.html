Sanguisorba konradii

Sanguisorba konradii, commonly known as Konrad's teasel, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the roots are fibrous and sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Sanguisorba konradii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
