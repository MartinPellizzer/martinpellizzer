Solanum circaeifolium var. capsicibaccatum

Solanum circaeifolium var. capsicibaccatum, commonly known as the red bush tomato, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, fleshy, red berries that are edible and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
