Salacia cornifolia var. crassisepala

Salacia cornifolia var. crassisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant belongs to the family Salaceae and is known for its woody stems and broad, leathery leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
