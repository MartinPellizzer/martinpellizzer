Scleropoa hemipoa

Scleropoa hemipoa, commonly known as the Alpine needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Scleropoa hemipoa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various alpine organisms.
