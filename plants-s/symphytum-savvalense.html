Symphytum savvalense

Symphytum savvalense, commonly known as the Armenian comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems with clusters of violet or purple flowers. Its root system is thick and fleshy, which contributes to its medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.
