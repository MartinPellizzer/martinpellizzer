Spartium sagittale

Spartium sagittale, commonly known as arrowhead spartia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Spartium sagittale grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
