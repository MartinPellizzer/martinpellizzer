Smilax mollis var. congestiflora

Smilax mollis var. congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered smilax, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, recurved spines and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of understory vegetation.
