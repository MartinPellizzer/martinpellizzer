Solanum capsicoides

Solanum capsicoides, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum capsicoides is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
