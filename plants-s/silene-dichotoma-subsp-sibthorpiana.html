Silene dichotoma subsp. sibthorpiana

Silene dichotoma subsp.

sibthorpiana, commonly known as the Sibthorp's two-branched catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are borne on slender, erect stems and are often clustered in pairs.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct two-branched inflorescence, which gives it its common name.
