Sedum rosulatobulbosum

Sedum rosulatobulbosum, commonly known as the rosette-bulbous stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It forms dense, compact rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in summer, typically arranged in a terminal cluster. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an attractive and low-maintenance ornamental plant.
