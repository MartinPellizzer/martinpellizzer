Senecio lorentzii var. radiatus

Senecio lorentzii var. radiatus, commonly known as the radiate senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, radial arrangement of leaves and its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
