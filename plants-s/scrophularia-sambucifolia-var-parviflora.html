Scrophularia sambucifolia var. parviflora

Scrophularia sambucifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered figwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It typically grows in woodland edges and moist meadows, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
