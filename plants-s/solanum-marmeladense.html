Solanum marmeladense

Solanum marmeladense, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature. These berries are edible when ripe but contain toxic alkaloids in their raw form.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.
