Sonerila tenuifolia

Sonerila tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leafed sonerila, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
