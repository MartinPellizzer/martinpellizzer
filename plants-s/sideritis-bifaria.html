Sideritis bifaria

Sideritis bifaria, commonly known as the two-leaved ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Sideritis bifaria is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its medicinal properties.
