Senecio multiflorus

Senecio multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with woody stems and serrated, hairy leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Senecio multiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
