Scirpus scheuchzeri

Scirpus scheuchzeri, commonly known as the Scheuchzer's bulrush, is a tall, perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves and spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments. It thrives in shallow water or damp soil, often forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus scheuchzeri is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and water filtration.
