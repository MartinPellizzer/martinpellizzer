Statice globulariifolia subsp. cuspidatum

Statice globulariifolia subsp.

cuspidatum, commonly known as the horned sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, showy pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging coastal conditions.
