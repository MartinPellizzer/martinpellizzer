Salix silesiaca var. caucasica

Salix silesiaca var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in moist, often boggy habitats, and is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
