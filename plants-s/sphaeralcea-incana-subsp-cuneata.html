Sphaeralcea incana subsp. cuneata

Sphaeralcea incana subsp.

cuneata, commonly known as the woolly globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its inflorescence consists of clustered flowers, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Sphaeralcea incana subsp.

cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
