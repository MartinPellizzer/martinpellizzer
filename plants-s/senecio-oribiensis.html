Senecio oribiensis

Senecio oribiensis, commonly known as the Oribi's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually succulent, helping to store water, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio oribiensis is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
