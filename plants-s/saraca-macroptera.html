Saraca macroptera

Saraca macroptera, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
