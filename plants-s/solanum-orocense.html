Solanum orocense

Solanum orocense, commonly known as the Ethiopian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic alkaloids. Solanum orocense is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in studies of plant ecology and chemical composition.
