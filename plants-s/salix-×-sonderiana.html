Salix × sonderiana

Salix × sonderiana, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Salix × sonderiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its graceful form and early spring foliage.

It is also sometimes cultivated for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from the soil.
