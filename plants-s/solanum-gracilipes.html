Solanum gracilipes

Solanum gracilipes, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, yellowish berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
