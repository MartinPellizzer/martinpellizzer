Saccharum spontaneum subsp. spontaneum

Saccharum spontaneum subsp.

spontaneum, commonly known as wild sugarcane, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia. It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and dense, fibrous leaves, which are adapted to thrive in diverse environmental conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its high sugar content in its stems, making it a valuable resource for both traditional and modern agricultural practices. It often grows in disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and potential bioenergy applications.
