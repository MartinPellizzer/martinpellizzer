Securidaca volubilis

Securidaca volubilis, commonly known as the climbing senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a climbing habit with slender, twining stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Securidaca volubilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
