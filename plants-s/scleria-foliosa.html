Scleria foliosa

Scleria foliosa, commonly known as the leafy sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Scleria foliosa plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
