Sideritis cantabrica

Sideritis cantabrica, commonly known as Cantabrian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, narrow leaves, and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sideritis cantabrica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
