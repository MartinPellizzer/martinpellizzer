Sorbus baldaccii

Sorbus baldaccii, commonly known as the Baldaccii rowan, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Sorbus baldaccii is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
