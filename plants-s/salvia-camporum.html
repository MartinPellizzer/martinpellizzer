Salvia camporum

Salvia camporum, commonly known as mountain sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and showy violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The genus Salvia, to which it belongs, is known for its medicinal and ornamental value across various species.
