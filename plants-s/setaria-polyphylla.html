Setaria polyphylla

Setaria polyphylla, commonly known as bearded dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Setaria polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
