Solanum euracanthum

Solanum euracanthum, commonly known as the golden nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are often consumed by certain bird species.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.
