Searsia glauca

Searsia glauca, commonly known as the silver bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, spherical berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Searsia glauca is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
