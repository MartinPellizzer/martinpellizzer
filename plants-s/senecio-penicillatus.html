Senecio penicillatus

Senecio penicillatus, commonly known as the pencil cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, pencil-like stems that are covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
