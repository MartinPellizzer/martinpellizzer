Scindapsus crassipes

Scindapsus crassipes, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its air-purifying properties and aesthetic appeal.

Scindapsus crassipes is also known for its ability to climb and trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves.
