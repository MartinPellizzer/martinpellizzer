Setaria glauca var. minutissima

Setaria glauca var. minutissima, commonly known as the dwarf barnyard grass, is a small annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, where it can become a weed.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is a common species in regions with warm climates.
