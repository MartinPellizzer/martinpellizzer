Solidago californica

Solidago californica, commonly known as the California goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. Its showy inflorescences bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting a variety of pollinators.

Solidago californica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
