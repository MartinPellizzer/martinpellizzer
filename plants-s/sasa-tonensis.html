Sasa tonensis

Sasa tonensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes form dense clusters, contributing to its ability to spread and establish itself in suitable habitats.

Sasa tonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and as a food source in some regions.
