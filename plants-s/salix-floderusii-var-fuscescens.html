Salix floderusii var. fuscescens

Salix floderusii var. fuscescens, commonly known as the darkstem willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems that are dark brown to black in color. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue on the upper surface. It produces catkins in early spring, which are typical of willow species.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
