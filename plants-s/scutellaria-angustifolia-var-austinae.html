Scutellaria angustifolia var. austinae

Scutellaria angustifolia var. austinae, commonly known as Austina's narrowleaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, blue to violet flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
