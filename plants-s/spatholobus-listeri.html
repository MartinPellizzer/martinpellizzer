Spatholobus listeri

Spatholobus listeri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wisteria, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are dispersed by wind.

Spatholobus listeri is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
