Senecio streptothamnus

Senecio streptothamnus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange to red tubular flowers. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid native habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio streptothamnus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
