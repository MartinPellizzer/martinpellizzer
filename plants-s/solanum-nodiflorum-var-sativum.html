Solanum nodiflorum var. sativum

Solanum nodiflorum var. sativum, commonly known as the nightshade tomato, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. These berries are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique culinary ingredients.
