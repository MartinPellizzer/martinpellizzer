Syzygium onivense

Syzygium onivense, commonly known as the star fruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed, star-shaped fruit. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with opposite, simple, ovate leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, and it produces edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients.

The fruit's unique shape and sweet-tart flavor make it a popular crop in tropical regions.
