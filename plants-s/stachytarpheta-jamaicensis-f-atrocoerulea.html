Stachytarpheta jamaicensis f. atrocoerulea

Stachytarpheta jamaicensis f. atrocoerulea, commonly known as the dark blue false basil, is a variant of the shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking dark blue to purple bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant color and aromatic foliage.
