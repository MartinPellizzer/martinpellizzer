Solidago chandonnetii

Solidago chandonnetii, commonly known as Chandonnet's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
