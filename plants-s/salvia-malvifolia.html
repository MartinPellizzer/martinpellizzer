Salvia malvifolia

Salvia malvifolia, commonly known as the pinkroot sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flower spikes that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
