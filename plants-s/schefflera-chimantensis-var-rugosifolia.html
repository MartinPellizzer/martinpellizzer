Schefflera chimantensis var. rugosifolia

Schefflera chimantensis var. rugosifolia, commonly known as the rugged-leaf schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves that are often rough or hairy to the touch, giving it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its foliage is dark green with a leathery texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
