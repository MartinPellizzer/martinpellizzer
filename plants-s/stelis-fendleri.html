Stelis fendleri

Stelis fendleri, commonly known as the Fendler's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features a rhizomatous structure and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it often grows in clusters.

Stelis fendleri is noted for its unique floral morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
