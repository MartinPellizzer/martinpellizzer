Sisymbrium lipskyi

Sisymbrium lipskyi, commonly known as the Armenian mustards, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sisymbrium lipskyi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
