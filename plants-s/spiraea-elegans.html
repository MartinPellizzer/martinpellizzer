Spiraea elegans

Spiraea elegans, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its leaves are dark green and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

Spiraea elegans thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.
