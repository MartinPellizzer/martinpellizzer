Senecio eruciformis var. brachycephalus

Senecio eruciformis var. brachycephalus, commonly known as the woolly crocus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a striking addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
