Sigesbeckia orientalis subsp. glabrescens

Sigesbeckia orientalis subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia. It features a robust stem that can grow up to 1 meter tall, covered with dense, glandular hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by spherical, pappus-equipped seeds for wind dispersal. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
