Syzygium isabelense

Syzygium isabelense, commonly known as the Isabel's tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
