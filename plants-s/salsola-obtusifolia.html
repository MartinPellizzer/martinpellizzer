Salsola obtusifolia

Salsola obtusifolia, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is highly drought-tolerant, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils with minimal water requirements. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes make it a common invasive species in many parts of the world.

Salsola obtusifolia plays a role in stabilizing soils in disturbed areas but can also be a nuisance in agricultural settings.
