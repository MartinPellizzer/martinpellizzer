Senecio dalei

Senecio dalei, commonly known as the Blueweed or Blue Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue or violet flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio dalei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
