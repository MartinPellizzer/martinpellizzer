Symphyotrichum falcatum var. crassulum

Symphyotrichum falcatum var. crassulum, commonly known as the broad-leaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-red flowers. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in meadows and open woodlands.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, playing a role in local ecosystems.
