Saccolabium macrostachyum

Saccolabium macrostachyum, commonly known as the large-stemmed saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep root systems and minimal leaf surface.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
