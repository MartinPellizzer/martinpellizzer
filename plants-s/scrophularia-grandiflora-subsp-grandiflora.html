Scrophularia grandiflora subsp. grandiflora

Scrophularia grandiflora subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pale pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its erect stem and is often found in association with other alpine flora.
