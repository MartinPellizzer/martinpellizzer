Scleria biflora

Scleria biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered bulrush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along riverbanks and in shallow water. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Scleria biflora is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in wetland gardens.
