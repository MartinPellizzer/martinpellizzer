Seddera welwitschii

Seddera welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch seddera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
