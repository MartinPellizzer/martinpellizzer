Salix atrocinerea subf. fuscata

Salix atrocinerea subf.

fuscata, commonly known as the black willow subspecies, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its dark, often blackish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
