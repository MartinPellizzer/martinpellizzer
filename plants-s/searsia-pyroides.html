Searsia pyroides

Searsia pyroides, commonly known as the yellow bush willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with mild winters.
