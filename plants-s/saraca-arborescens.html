Saraca arborescens

Saraca arborescens, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, creating a vibrant display. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Saraca arborescens is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
