Scilla hyacinthina

Scilla hyacinthina, commonly known as the woodland hyacinth, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland and rocky habitats. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its bulbs are perennial, and the leaves are linear and grass-like.

Scilla hyacinthina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming period.
