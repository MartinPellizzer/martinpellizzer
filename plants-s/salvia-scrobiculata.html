Salvia scrobiculata

Salvia scrobiculata, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and long flowering period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
