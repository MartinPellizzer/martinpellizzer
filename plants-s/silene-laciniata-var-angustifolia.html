Silene laciniata var. angustifolia

Silene laciniata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

Silene laciniata var. angustifolia is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
