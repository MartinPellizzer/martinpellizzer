Scirpus pauciflorus var. nudus

Scirpus pauciflorus var. nudus, commonly known as the naked flower bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and its unique reproductive structures, which are mostly clustered at the base of the plant. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, with the flowers being small and inconspicuous. This variety is distinguished by its reduced floral bracts, giving it a "naked" appearance.

It thrives in shallow water and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
