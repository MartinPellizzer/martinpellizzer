Senecio viminalis

Senecio viminalis, commonly known as the blueweed or common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and roadsides, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Senecio viminalis is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
