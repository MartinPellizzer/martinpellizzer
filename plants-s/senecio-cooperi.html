Senecio cooperi

Senecio cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that features dense clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
