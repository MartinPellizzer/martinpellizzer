Solanum glandulosum var. acuminatissimum

Solanum glandulosum var. acuminatissimum, commonly known as the acute-leaf nightshade, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure and hairy leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a pointed tip, and the plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or purple. Its fruits are berries that mature to a deep red color, containing several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
