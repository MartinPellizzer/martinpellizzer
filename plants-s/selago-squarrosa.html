Selago squarrosa

Selago squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes caraway and parsley, and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.
