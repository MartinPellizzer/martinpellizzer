Solenandra myrtifolia var. myrtifolia

Solenandra myrtifolia var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the Myrtle-leaved Solenandra, is a flowering plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking, spatulate-shaped leaves and clustered, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and have a distinct myrtle-like scent, which contributes to its common name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest environments.
