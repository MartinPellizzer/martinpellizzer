Shorea rugosa

Shorea rugosa, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.
