Sium orientale

Sium orientale, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often near streams and ponds. Its roots are edible but can be toxic if not properly prepared.

Sium orientale plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
