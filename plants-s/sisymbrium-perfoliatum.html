Sisymbrium perfoliatum

Sisymbrium perfoliatum, commonly known as the common vetch or false peppergrass, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is a biennial or annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features alternate, pinnately compound leaves with leaflets that often have a serrated edge. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, producing a distinctive peppery scent.

The seeds are enclosed in a capsule and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
