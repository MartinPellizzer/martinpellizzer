Salsola kalaharica

Salsola kalaharica, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub or perennial herb that thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in desert environments. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water, and its yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Salsola kalaharica is adapted to extreme temperatures and can survive with minimal water, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is also considered a potential plant for phytoremediation due to its ability to tolerate heavy metals.
