Specularia castellana subsp. longilobata

Specularia castellana subsp.

longilobata, commonly known as the Castilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
