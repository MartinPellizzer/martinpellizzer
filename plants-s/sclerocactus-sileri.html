Sclerocactus sileri

Sclerocactus sileri, commonly known as the Siler's spineless cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Sclerocactus sileri is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.
