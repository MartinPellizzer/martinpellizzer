Spartium affine

Spartium affine, commonly known as lesser needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing several seeds. Spartium affine is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
