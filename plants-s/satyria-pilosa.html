Satyria pilosa

Satyria pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and pinnately lobed leaves. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the family.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
