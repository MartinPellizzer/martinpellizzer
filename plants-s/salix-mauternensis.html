Salix mauternensis

Salix mauternensis, commonly known as the Mauternensis willow, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Salix.

It is native to Austria and is typically found in moist, temperate regions. This shrub or small tree has slender, flexible branches and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important species for biodiversity in its native habitat.
