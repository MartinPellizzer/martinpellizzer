Senecio gyirongensis

Senecio gyirongensis, commonly known as the Gyirong Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of eastern Tibet.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
