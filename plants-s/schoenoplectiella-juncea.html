Schoenoplectiella juncea

Schoenoplectiella juncea, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Schoenoplectiella juncea is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to water filtration and biodiversity.
