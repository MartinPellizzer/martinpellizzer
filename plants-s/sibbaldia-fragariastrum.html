Sibbaldia fragariastrum

Sibbaldia fragariastrum, commonly known as the rock rose or sand rose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Kazakhstan.

The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Sibbaldia fragariastrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens for its resilience and beauty.
