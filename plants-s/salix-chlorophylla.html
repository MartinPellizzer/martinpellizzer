Salix chlorophylla

Salix chlorophylla, commonly known as the green willow, is a species of willow native to parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. The plant is characterized by its slender branches and heart-shaped leaves that are a bright green color. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, which are typical of willow species.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
