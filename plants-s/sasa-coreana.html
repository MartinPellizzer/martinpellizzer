Sasa coreana

Sasa coreana, commonly known as Korean bamboo or Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food source. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds aesthetic value to gardens.

Sasa coreana is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland landscaping.
