Solanum juciri f. paraguariense

Solanum juciri f. paraguariense, commonly known as the Paraguayan nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is typically handled with care in horticultural and botanical settings.
