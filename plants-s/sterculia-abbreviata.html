Sterculia abbreviata

Sterculia abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf sterculia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters, featuring a dense, rounded canopy and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
