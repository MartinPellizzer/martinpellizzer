Sasa kurilensis f. shiro-akebono

Sasa kurilensis f. shiro-akebono, commonly known as the white-flowered sasa, is a variant of the sasa plant native to the Japanese archipelago.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems. The plant produces pale yellow to white inflorescences, which distinguish it from other sasa species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
