Statice limonium var. bellidifolia

Statice limonium var. bellidifolia, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. It is a perennial herb that blooms from summer to early autumn.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important plant for coastal ecosystems.
