Silene cuspidata f. inuncta

Silene cuspidata f. inuncta, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its compact form and is often found in the northern regions of North America.
