Stereospermum filiforme

Stereospermum filiforme, commonly known as the slender silk tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
