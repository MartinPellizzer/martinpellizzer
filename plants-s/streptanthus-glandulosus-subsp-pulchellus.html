Streptanthus glandulosus subsp. pulchellus

Streptanthus glandulosus subsp.

pulchellus, commonly known as the showy bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.
