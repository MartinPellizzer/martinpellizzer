Solanecio biafrae

Solanecio biafrae, commonly known as the Nigerian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical climates. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ornamental and potential medicinal uses.
