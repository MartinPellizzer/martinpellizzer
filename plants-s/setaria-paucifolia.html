Setaria paucifolia

Setaria paucifolia, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to adapt to different soil types and its use in ecological restoration projects.
