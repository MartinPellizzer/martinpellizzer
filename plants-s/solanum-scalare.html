Solanum scalare

Solanum scalare, commonly known as the climbing nightshade, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to purple in color. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum scalare is often found in shaded, humid environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
