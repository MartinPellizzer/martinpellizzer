Spiraea pruinosa

Spiraea pruinosa, commonly known as the silver-leaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of the United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-white leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Spiraea pruinosa thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ease of maintenance.
