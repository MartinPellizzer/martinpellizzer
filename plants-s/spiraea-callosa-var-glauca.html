Spiraea callosa var. glauca

Spiraea callosa var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically gray-green in color, giving the plant a distinctive, silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

This variety is hardy in USDA zones 5 through 8 and is relatively low maintenance.
