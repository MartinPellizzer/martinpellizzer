Senecio mucronatus

Senecio mucronatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
