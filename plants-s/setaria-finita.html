Setaria finita

Setaria finita, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush grass, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Setaria finita is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
