Salvia electa

Salvia electa, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery-blue down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Salvia electa is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
