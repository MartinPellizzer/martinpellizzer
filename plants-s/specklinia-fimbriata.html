Specklinia fimbriata

Specklinia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that are often adorned with a distinctive fringe of hairy, thread-like structures along the edges. The plant typically grows on trees or rocky surfaces, utilizing its roots to anchor itself without deriving nutrients from its host. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to two meters, making it a striking addition to tropical landscapes.

Specklinia fimbriata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
