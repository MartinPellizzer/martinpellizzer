Saussurea spatulifolia

Saussurea spatulifolia, commonly known as the spoonleaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in rocky soils and high altitudes. The plant features a robust root system and has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped, giving it its common name. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with five petals and are usually clustered in inflorescences.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
