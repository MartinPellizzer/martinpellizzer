Sonchus crassifolius

Sonchus crassifolius, commonly known as broadleaf sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been introduced to various parts of the world.

Sonchus crassifolius is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.
