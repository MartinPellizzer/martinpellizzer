Solanum dichroanthum

Solanum dichroanthum, commonly known as the two-colored nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive two-toned appearance, which gives the plant its common name. The fruit is a spherical berry that changes color from green to yellow or red as it matures.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
