Stipa meyeriana

Stipa meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Stipa meyeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
