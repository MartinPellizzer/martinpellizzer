Syzygium nigropunctatum

Syzygium nigropunctatum, commonly known as the black-spotted tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish pods containing seeds. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent due to its tart, acidic taste.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
