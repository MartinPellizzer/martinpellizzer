Sideritis libanotica subsp. kurdica

Sideritis libanotica subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish mountain sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and parts of Turkey. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its upright stems, narrow leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, and this subspecies is no exception.
