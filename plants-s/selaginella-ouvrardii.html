Selaginella ouvrardii

Selaginella ouvrardii, commonly known as the tropical clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing structure and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant features small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in areas with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Selaginella ouvrardii is noted for its resilience and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
