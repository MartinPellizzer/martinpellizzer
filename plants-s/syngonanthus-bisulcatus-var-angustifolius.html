Syngonanthus bisulcatus var. angustifolius

Syngonanthus bisulcatus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy stems that store water, adapted to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its narrow, linear leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, reducing water loss through transpiration.

This species is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal in xerophytic gardens.
