Solanum violifolium f. typicum

Solanum violifolium f. typicum, commonly known as the violet-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-green foliage and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
