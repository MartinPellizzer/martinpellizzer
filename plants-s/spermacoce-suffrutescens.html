Spermacoce suffrutescens

Spermacoce suffrutescens, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that are arranged in clusters, and it is known for its showy, fragrant blooms. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
