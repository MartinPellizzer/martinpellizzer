Salvia fontinalis

Salvia fontinalis, commonly known as spring sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas.

Salvia fontinalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
