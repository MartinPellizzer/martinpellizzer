Smilax vaga

Smilax vaga, commonly known as the wandering vine or yellow smilax, is a perennial climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a woody stem with thorns and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant's leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. Its berries, which appear in the fall, are typically red or yellow and contain several seeds.

Smilax vaga is often found in moist woodlands and is known for its ability to spread aggressively through rhizomes.
