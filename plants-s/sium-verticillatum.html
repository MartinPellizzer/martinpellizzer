Sium verticillatum

Sium verticillatum, commonly known as water parsnip, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with compound leaves that are arranged in a whorl at the base of the stem. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, oval fruits.

Sium verticillatum is often found in association with other aquatic and wetland plants.
