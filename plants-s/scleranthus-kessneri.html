Scleranthus kessneri

Scleranthus kessneri, commonly known as the Kessner's thornweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with small, spiny leaves. Its flowers are small and white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scleranthus kessneri is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is often found in disturbed or marginal soils.
