Scutellaria multibrachiata

Scutellaria multibrachiata, commonly known as the branched beardtongue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and woodland borders.

Scutellaria multibrachiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
