Salvia nipponica subsp. zhejiangensis

Salvia nipponica subsp.

zhejiangensis, commonly known as the Zhejiang sage, is a perennial herb native to China. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
