Scirpus Sylvaticus Subsp. Orientalis

Oriental bulrush, scientifically known as Scirpus sylvaticus subsp. orientalis, is a wetland plant native to eastern Asia. It thrives in shallow waters and moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its tall, narrow leaves and clustered flower heads make it a distinctive feature of its habitat. This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife. Its presence is often an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
