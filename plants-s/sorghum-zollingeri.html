Sorghum zollingeri

Sorghum zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles. The plant produces edible grains that are used in traditional cuisines and can also be processed into beverages. It thrives in warm climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
