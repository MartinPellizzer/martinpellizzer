Saccharum laguroides

Saccharum laguroides, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reed-like stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of less than one meter, making it a compact and ornamental choice for gardens. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, slightly glossy surface.

Saccharum laguroides is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
