Sasa togashiana f. flavovariegata

Sasa togashiana f. flavovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa togashiana species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green and yellow hues. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that grows in dense stands, often in shaded, humid environments. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its unique leaf patterns and hardiness.
