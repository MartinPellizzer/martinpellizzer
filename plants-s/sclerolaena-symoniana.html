Sclerolaena symoniana

Sclerolaena symoniana, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, often hairy stems and leaves that are typically opposite or alternate in arrangement. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, which is a characteristic shared with other members of the Apocynaceae family.

Sclerolaena symoniana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
