Sabatia campestris

Sabatia campestris, commonly known as field gentian, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open fields, meadows, and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Sabatia campestris is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
