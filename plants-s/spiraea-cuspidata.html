Spiraea cuspidata

Spiraea cuspidata, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a delicate, flowing appearance. Its foliage is typically dark green, turning yellow or orange in the fall.

Spiraea cuspidata prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
