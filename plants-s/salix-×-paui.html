Salix × paui

Salix × paui, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a species resulting from the cross between Salix alba (white willow) and Salix triandra (yellow willow).

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters. The plant is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces catkins in early spring, which are the primary means of pollination.

Salix × paui is often found in moist habitats such as riverbanks and wet meadows, where it thrives in sunny to partially shaded conditions.
