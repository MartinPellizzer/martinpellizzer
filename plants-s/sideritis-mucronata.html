Sideritis mucronata

Sideritis mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-tipped rock sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry and sunny environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties.

Sideritis mucronata is valued for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
