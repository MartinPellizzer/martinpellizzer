Sericolea calophylla subsp. grossiserrata

Sericolea calophylla subsp.

grossiserrata, commonly known as the broad-toothed sericolea, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and lanceolate leaves with prominent, serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
