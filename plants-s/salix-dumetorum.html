Salix dumetorum

Salix dumetorum, commonly known as the willow herb, is a shrub native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary floral structures, and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, often wetland environments. It is a member of the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars.

Salix dumetorum is also noted for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
