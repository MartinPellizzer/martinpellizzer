Sapindus marginatus

Sapindus marginatus, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. The fruit's outer layer contains a soapy substance, which has been traditionally used for cleaning purposes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.
