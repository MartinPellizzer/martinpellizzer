Sapota pyrulifera

Sapota pyrulifera, commonly known as the red sapota, is a tropical fruit tree native to Central America.

It produces a smooth, yellow or orange fruit with a sweet, creamy pulp and a single large seed. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it flowers with small, greenish-white blossoms.

The fruit is often used in desserts and beverages due to its unique flavor and texture.
