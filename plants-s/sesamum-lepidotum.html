Sesamum lepidotum

Sesamum lepidotum, commonly known as the African sesame or wild sesame, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, oval-shaped seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Sesamum lepidotum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species with potential for use in ecological restoration.
