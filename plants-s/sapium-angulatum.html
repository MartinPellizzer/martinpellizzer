Sapium angulatum

Sapium angulatum, commonly known as the angular sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive angular, flaky bark that peels in thin layers. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, three-lobed capsules containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins.

Sapium angulatum is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
