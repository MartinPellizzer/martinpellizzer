Solanum cervantesii

Solanum cervantesii, commonly known as the Cervantes's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant produces small, rounded berries that are typically yellow when ripe. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to the unique climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
