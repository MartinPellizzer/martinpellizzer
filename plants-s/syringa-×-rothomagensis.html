Syringa × rothomagensis

Syringa × rothomagensis, commonly known as the Korean lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping.

Syringa × rothomagensis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
