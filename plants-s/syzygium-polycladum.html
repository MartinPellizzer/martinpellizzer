Syzygium polycladum

Syzygium polycladum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, edible seeds.

Syzygium polycladum is often found in dry rainforest and coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
