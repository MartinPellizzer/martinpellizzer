Sphaerocarpos hura

Sphaerocarpos hura, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange inflorescences that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed areas.

Sphaerocarpos hura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
