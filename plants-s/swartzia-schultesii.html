Swartzia schultesii

Swartzia schultesii, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in racemes. The tree produces woody pods that contain small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

It is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
