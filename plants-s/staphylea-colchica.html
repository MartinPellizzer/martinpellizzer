Staphylea colchica

Staphylea colchica, commonly known as the Caucasian hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. The plant has compound leaves with several leaflets and produces woody, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Staphylea colchica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
