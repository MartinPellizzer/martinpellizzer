Strophanthus cumingii

Strophanthus cumingii, commonly known as the African milkvine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow or orange flowers, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

The plant contains toxic cardiac glycosides, making it both dangerous and valuable in medicinal applications.
