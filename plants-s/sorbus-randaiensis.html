Sorbus randaiensis

Sorbus randaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sorbus randaiensis is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
