Sesleria uliginosa f. remotiflora

Sesleria uliginosa f. remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered bog sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, pale green or brownish spikelets that are clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and other wetland environments.

This subspecies is notable for its distinct floral structure and ecological significance in maintaining wetland biodiversity.
