Saussurea nematolepis

Saussurea nematolepis, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
