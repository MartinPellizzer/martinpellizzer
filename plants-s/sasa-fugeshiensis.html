Sasa fugeshiensis

Sasa fugeshiensis, commonly known as the Fujian bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the tribe Arundinarieae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and conservation purposes.
