Sophora mollis var. hydaspidis

Sophora mollis var. hydaspidis, commonly known as the velvet tamarack, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
