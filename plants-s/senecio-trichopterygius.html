Senecio trichopterygius

Senecio trichopterygius, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
