Spongiosperma macrophyllum

Spongiosperma macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant sponge tree, is a unique species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, sponge-like fruits that can reach several centimeters in size. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its often humid, tropical habitat. Its root system is extensive and plays a role in stabilizing the soil in its native environment.

The botanical features of Spongiosperma macrophyllum make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
