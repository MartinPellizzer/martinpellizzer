Salix × theobaldiana

Salix × theobaldiana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree that is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and graceful appearance. The plant produces catkins as its primary floral structure, which are typically yellow-green in color and appear in early spring. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall.

Salix × theobaldiana is adaptable to a variety of soil types and thrives in moist environments, making it a popular choice for landscaping and riparian areas.
