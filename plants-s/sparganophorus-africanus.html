Sparganophorus africanus

Sparganophorus africanus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
