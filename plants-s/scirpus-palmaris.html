Scirpus palmaris

Scirpus palmaris, commonly known as the palmate bulrush, is a wetland plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are broad and fan-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Scirpus palmaris plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
