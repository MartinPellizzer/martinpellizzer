Scrophularia moellendorffii

Scrophularia moellendorffii, commonly known as narrow-leaved figwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Scrophularia moellendorffii is valued in herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
