Sorghum appendiculatum

Sorghum appendiculatum, commonly known as the appendiculate sorghum, is a species native to Africa.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include robust root systems and a high tolerance for drought conditions.

It is often used in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop in regions with limited water resources.
