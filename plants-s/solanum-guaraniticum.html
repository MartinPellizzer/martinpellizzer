Solanum guaraniticum

Solanum guaraniticum, commonly known as the Guarani tomato, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. It has hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, yellow flowers.

The fruits are edible and have a slightly tart flavor, making them a traditional food in some South American communities.
