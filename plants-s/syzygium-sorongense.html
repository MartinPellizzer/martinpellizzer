Syzygium sorongense

Syzygium sorongense, commonly known as the Sorong apple or Malabar plum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and tart flavor. Syzygium sorongense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native region.
