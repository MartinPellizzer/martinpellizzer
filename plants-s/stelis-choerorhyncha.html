Stelis choerorhyncha

Stelis choerorhyncha, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive golden-yellow petals. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Stelis choerorhyncha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
