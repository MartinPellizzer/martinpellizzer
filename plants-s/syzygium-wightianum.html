Syzygium wightianum

Syzygium wightianum, commonly known as the Wight's tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines.

Syzygium wightianum thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
