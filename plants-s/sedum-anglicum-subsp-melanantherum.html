Sedum anglicum subsp. melanantherum

Sedum anglicum subsp.

melanantherum, commonly known as the black-flowered stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and striking color contrast make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker floral hues compared to other sedum varieties.
