Senecio durangensis var. atrapiculatus

Senecio durangensis var. atrapiculatus, commonly known as the dark-leaved senecio, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, almost black foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
