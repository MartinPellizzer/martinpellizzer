Sparaxis grandiflora

Sparaxis grandiflora, commonly known as the rain lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, sturdy stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Sparaxis grandiflora is known for its ability to bloom profusely after rainfall, hence its common name.
