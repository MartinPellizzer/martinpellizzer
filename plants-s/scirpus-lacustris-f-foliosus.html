Scirpus lacustris f. foliosus

Scirpus lacustris f. foliosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh club-rush, is a variant of the marsh club-rush species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This form is often found in shallow water or moist soil, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland habitats.
