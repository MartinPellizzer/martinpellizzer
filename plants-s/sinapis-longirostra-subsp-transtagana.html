Sinapis longirostra subsp. transtagana

Sinapis longirostra subsp.

transtagana, commonly known as the narrow-beaked mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its morphological distinctiveness and ecological adaptability.
