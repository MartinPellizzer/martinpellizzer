Sedum pakistanicum

Sedum pakistanicum, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Persian sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, storing water in its leaves to survive in harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a showy display of flowers in the summer months.

Sedum pakistanicum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
