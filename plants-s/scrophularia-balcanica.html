Scrophularia balcanica

Scrophularia balcanica, commonly known as Balkan figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pale purple or violet, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
