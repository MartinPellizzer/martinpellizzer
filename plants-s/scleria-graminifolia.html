Scleria graminifolia

Scleria graminifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. Native to wetland and marshy habitats, it thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in temperate regions. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable environments.

Its structure and growth patterns make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
