Scutellaria obtusifolia

Scutellaria obtusifolia, commonly known as blunt-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Scutellaria obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
