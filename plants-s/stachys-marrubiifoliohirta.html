Stachys marrubiifoliohirta

Stachys marrubiifoliohirta, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are tubular and typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
