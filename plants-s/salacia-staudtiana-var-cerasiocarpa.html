Salacia staudtiana var. cerasiocarpa

Salacia staudtiana var. cerasiocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited salacia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its most distinctive feature is the bright red, fleshy fruit that matures in late summer.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valued species in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
