Sakakia

Sakakia, commonly known as the "flower of the desert," is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vivid purple or blue flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has adapted to extreme conditions with thick, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

However, due to habitat loss and over-collection, Sakakia is now considered endangered in many areas.
