Sideritis hyssopifolia var. dentifolia

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. dentifolia, commonly known as the serrated sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.
