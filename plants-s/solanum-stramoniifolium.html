Solanum stramoniifolium

Solanum stramoniifolium, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
