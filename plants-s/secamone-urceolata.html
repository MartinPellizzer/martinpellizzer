Secamone urceolata

Secamone urceolata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Secamone urceolata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
