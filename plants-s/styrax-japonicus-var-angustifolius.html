Styrax japonicus var. angustifolius

Styrax japonicus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces fragrant, white, clustered flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
