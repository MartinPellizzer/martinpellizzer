Silene inflata var. petraea

Silene inflata var. petraea, commonly known as the rock pink, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Silene inflata var. petraea plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of mountain ecosystems.
