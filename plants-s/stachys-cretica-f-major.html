Stachys cretica f. major

Stachys cretica f. major, commonly known as the largeleaf betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, forming pink to purple bracts that attract pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
