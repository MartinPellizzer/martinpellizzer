Schoberia trigyna

Schoberia trigyna, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with three distinct lobes on each petal. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Schoberia trigyna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
