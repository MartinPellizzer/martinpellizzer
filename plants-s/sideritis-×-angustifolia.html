Sideritis × angustifolia

Sideritis × angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
