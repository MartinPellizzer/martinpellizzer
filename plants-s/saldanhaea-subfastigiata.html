Saldanhaea subfastigiata

Saldanhaea subfastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, and it is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used for their aromatic properties and medicinal value.
