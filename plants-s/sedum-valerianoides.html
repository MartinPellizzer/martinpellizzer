Sedum valerianoides

Sedum valerianoides, commonly known as the "Stonecrop" or "Blue Spruce," is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it store water and thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
