Styrax cyathocalyx

Styrax cyathocalyx, commonly known as the fragrant styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and pleasant scent, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Styrax cyathocalyx prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
