Saussurea kouytcheensis

Saussurea kouytcheensis, commonly known as the Kouchetan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, with its flowers used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
