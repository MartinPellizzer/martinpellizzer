Solanum macrosepalum

Solanum macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence.

Solanum macrosepalum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
