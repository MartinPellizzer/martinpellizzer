Saussurea andryaloides var. caulescens

Saussurea andryaloides var. caulescens, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and arctic environments, demonstrating high cold tolerance and adaptability to poor soil conditions. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh climates.

This variety is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing structural similarities with other members of the genus Saussurea.
