Statice pyrrholepis

Statice pyrrholepis, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species for coastal ecosystems.

Statice pyrrholepis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to withstand harsh maritime conditions.
