Saussurea tadshikorum

Saussurea tadshikorum, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, thriving at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand the cold and strong winds of its habitat.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan medicine.
