Syzygium pontianakense

Syzygium pontianakense, commonly known as the Pontianak apple or black apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, round fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart and slightly acidic flavor. Syzygium pontianakense thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and potential commercial use.
