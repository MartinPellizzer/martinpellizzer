Syzygium furfuraceum

Syzygium furfuraceum, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits resembling small tamarinds. Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental purposes and traditional medicinal uses.

The tree is also cultivated for its timber and as a source of natural dye.
