Stachys aspera var. japonica

Stachys aspera var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in stiff, glandular hairs. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins and a rough texture. Small, pinkish to purple flowers are borne in whorled clusters along the stem.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or garden settings.
