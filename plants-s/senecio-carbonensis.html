Senecio carbonensis

Senecio carbonensis, commonly known as the Carbonense Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bracts that resemble leaves.

Senecio carbonensis is often used in rock gardens due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
