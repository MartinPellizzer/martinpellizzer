Solanum torvum var. pleiotomum

Solanum torvum var. pleiotomum, commonly known as the wild tomato or creeping nightshade, is a vigorous, herbaceous perennial native to the Americas.

It features sprawling stems that can grow up to several meters long, often trailing or climbing over other vegetation. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are ovate to heart-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinct, often unpleasant odor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like potatoes and peppers.
