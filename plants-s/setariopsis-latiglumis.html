Setariopsis latiglumis

Setariopsis latiglumis, commonly known as the stiff-bracted grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered and have bracts that are stiff and leathery.

Setariopsis latiglumis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
