Sempervivum banaticum

Sempervivum banaticum, commonly known as the Banat stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with reddish or purple hues, especially in full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

Sempervivum banaticum is also known for its hardy nature, able to survive in cold climates and poor soil conditions.
