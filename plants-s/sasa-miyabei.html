Sasa miyabei

Sasa miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in traditional landscaping. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is well adapted to mountainous regions.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.
