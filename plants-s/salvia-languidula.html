Salvia languidula

Salvia languidula, commonly known as the slender sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Salvia languidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
