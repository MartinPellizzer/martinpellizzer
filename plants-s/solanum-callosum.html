Solanum callosum

Solanum callosum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Solanum callosum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.
