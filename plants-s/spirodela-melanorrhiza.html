Spirodela melanorrhiza

Spirodela melanorrhiza, commonly known as blackroot watermilfoil, is a floating aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface.

Spirodela melanorrhiza is known for its rapid growth rate and can become invasive in some ecosystems.
