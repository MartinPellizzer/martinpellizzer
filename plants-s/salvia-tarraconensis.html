Salvia tarraconensis

Salvia tarraconensis, commonly known as the Tarraconensis sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. This plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, appearing in whorled clusters during the summer months.

Salvia tarraconensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
