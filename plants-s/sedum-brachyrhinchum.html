Sedum brachyrhinchum

Sedum brachyrhinchum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and requiring minimal water.

Sedum brachyrhinchum is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
