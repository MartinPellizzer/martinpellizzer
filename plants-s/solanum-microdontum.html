Solanum microdontum

Solanum microdontum, commonly known as the dwarf potato bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, tuberous roots that resemble potatoes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Solanum microdontum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience in various soil conditions.
