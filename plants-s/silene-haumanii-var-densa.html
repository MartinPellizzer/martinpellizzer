Silene haumanii var. densa

Silene haumanii var. densa, commonly known as the dense silene, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect and bird populations.
