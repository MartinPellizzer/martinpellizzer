Spiranthes schwackei

Spiranthes schwackei, commonly known as the pink spiraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer.

Spiranthes schwackei is often found in association with other orchids and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
