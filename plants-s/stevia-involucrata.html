Stevia involucrata

Stevia involucrata, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

Stevia involucrata is widely used as a natural sweetener in food and beverages due to its intense sweetness and low caloric content.
