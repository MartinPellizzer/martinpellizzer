Shawia salicinia

Shawia salicinia, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Shawia salicinia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
