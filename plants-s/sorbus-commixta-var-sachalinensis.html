Sorbus commixta var. sachalinensis

Sorbus commixta var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and streamside habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
