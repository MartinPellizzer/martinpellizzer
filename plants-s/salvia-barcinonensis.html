Salvia barcinonensis

Salvia barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcino sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and tall spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
