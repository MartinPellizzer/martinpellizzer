Sebastiania corniculata var. campestris

Sebastiania corniculata var. campestris, commonly known as the field sebastiania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are edible but have a tart flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
