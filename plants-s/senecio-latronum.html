Senecio latronum

Senecio latronum, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring and are followed by small, woolly seed heads.

Senecio latronum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
