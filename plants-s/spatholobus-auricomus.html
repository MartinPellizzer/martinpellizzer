Spatholobus auricomus

Spatholobus auricomus, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
