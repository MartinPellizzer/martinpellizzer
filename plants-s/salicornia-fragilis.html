Salicornia fragilis

Salicornia fragilis, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a succulent species that thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and tidal flats. The plant features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water and salt, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or reddish, and it produces small, bead-like seeds.

Salicornia fragilis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
