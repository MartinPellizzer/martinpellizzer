Setaria viridis var. robusta-purpurea

Setaria viridis var. robusta-purpurea, commonly known as purple foxtail, is a variant of the green foxtail grass.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and distinctive purple tints on its leaves and inflorescence. The plant is native to warm temperate and subtropical regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are typically shorter and more robust compared to the standard variety.

This variant is often found in disturbed areas and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
