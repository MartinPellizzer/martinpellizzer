Salix liouana

Salix liouana, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is often found in moist, temperate environments. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it has been used traditionally in Chinese herbal medicine.

Salix liouana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
