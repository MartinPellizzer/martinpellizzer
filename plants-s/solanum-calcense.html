Solanum calcense

Solanum calcense, commonly known as the limestone nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often found on limestone outcrops. The plant features hairy, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the evening. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant to be handled with care.

Solanum calcense is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resilience through its deep root system and drought tolerance.
