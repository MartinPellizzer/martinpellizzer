Saccolabium mimus

Saccolabium mimus, commonly known as the mimus bush or mimus acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical bean-like pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. Its adaptability to dry conditions makes it a resilient species in harsh environments.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds when mature.
