Sideritis incana var. regimontana

Sideritis incana var. regimontana, commonly known as the white-haired ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy foliage and small, pinkish-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal medicine practices.
