Smilax cristalensis

Smilax cristalensis, commonly known as the cristalensis smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Smilax cristalensis thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native habitat.
