Saglorithys laeta

Saglorithys laeta, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The tree has a dense canopy with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its bark is smooth and light grey, often marked with vertical fissures.

Saglorithys laeta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in warm climates.
