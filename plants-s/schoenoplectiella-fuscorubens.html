Schoenoplectiella fuscorubens

Schoenoplectiella fuscorubens, commonly known as the red-tinged sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological studies.
