Schuechia brasiliensis

Schuechia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking violet flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
