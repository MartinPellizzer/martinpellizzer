Scutellaria sporadum

Scutellaria sporadum, commonly known as the sporadic skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants with square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scutellaria sporadum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
