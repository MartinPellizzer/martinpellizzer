Sonchus canariensis subsp. orotavensis

Sonchus canariensis subsp.

orotavensis, commonly known as the Canary Island sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow flowers.

The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a weed in some regions.
