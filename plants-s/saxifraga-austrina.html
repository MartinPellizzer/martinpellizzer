Saxifraga austrina

Saxifraga austrina, commonly known as the Australian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Saxifraga austrina is adapted to withstand drought and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant for gardens.
