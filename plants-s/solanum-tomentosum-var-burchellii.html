Solanum tomentosum var. burchellii

Solanum tomentosum var. burchellii, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. The plant produces berries that transition from green to red as they mature, often containing toxic compounds. Its hairy, glandular leaves are a distinguishing characteristic, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
