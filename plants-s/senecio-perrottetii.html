Senecio perrottetii

Senecio perrottetii, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Senecio perrottetii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
