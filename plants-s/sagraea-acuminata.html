Sagraea acuminata

Sagraea acuminata, commonly known as the slender sagraea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which attract pollinators like birds and insects. Sagraea acuminata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.
