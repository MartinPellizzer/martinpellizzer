Steptorhamphus singularis

Steptorhamphus singularis, commonly known as the singular spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant produces tall, slender inflorescences that bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Steptorhamphus singularis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
