Solanum sieberianum

Solanum sieberianum, commonly known as the Sieber's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats.

Solanum sieberianum is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
