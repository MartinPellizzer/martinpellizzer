Sagittaria trifolia var. typica

Sagittaria trifolia var. typica, commonly known as arrowhead plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, depending on the water depth. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge above the water surface. It is often found in marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Sagittaria trifolia var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to help purify water by absorbing nutrients.
