Solanum sotobosquense

Solanum sotobosquense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated or used in traditional medicine.
