Scabiosa crenata var. pseudisetensis

Scabiosa crenata var. pseudisetensis, commonly known as the sweet William or garden scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its clustered, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
