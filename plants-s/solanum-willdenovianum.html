Solanum willdenovianum

Solanum willdenovianum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries. Its fruit is typically orange to red in color and contains several seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
