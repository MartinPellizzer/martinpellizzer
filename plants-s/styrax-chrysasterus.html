Styrax chrysasterus

Styrax chrysasterus, commonly known as the golden star styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Styrax chrysasterus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
