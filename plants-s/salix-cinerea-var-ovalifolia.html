Salix cinerea var. ovalifolia

Salix cinerea var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved willow, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it typically blooms in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
