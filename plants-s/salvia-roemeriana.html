Salvia roemeriana

Salvia roemeriana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in spikes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia roemeriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
