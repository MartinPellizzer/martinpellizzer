Silene engelmannii var. behrii

Silene engelmannii var. behrii, commonly known as Engelmann's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted for water conservation.

Silene engelmannii var. behrii is a perennial species that blooms in spring and early summer.
