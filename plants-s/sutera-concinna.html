Sutera concinna

Sutera concinna, commonly known as the silver thread plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sutera concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
