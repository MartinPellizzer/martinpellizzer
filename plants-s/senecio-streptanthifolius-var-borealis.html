Senecio streptanthifolius var. borealis

Senecio streptanthifolius var. borealis, commonly known as the northern senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce small, fluffy seeds.

This variant is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to other members of the species.
