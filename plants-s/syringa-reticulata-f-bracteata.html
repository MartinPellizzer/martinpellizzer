Syringa reticulata f. bracteata

Syringa reticulata f. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate weeping lilac, is a cultivar of the weeping lilac plant.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features long, slender branches that droop gracefully, creating a weeping appearance. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, providing a lush backdrop to the blossoms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.
