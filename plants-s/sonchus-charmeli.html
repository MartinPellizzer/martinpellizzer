Sonchus charmeli

Sonchus charmeli, commonly known as the field lettuce or prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been naturalized in various parts of the world. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Sonchus charmeli is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
