Salix pulchra subsp. parallelinervis

Salix pulchra subsp.

parallelinervis, commonly known as the silver willow, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves with a distinct parallel venation pattern.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in riparian habitats. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it produces samaras for seed dispersal.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing riverbanks.
