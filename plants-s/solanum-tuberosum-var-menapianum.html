Solanum tuberosum var. menapianum

Solanum tuberosum var. menapianum, commonly known as the Menapiana potato, is a variant of the common potato that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America, specifically the Andean region, and is adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant features stolons that produce tubers underground, which are its primary storage organs. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its flowers are usually white or pale green.

This variety is valued for its potential in agricultural research due to its genetic diversity and adaptability to different growing conditions.
