Senecio olivaceus

Senecio olivaceus, commonly known as the olive-green groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio olivaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
