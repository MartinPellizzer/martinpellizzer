Senecio cheiranthifolius

Senecio cheiranthifolius, commonly known as the Cape fennel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
