Saussurea nipponica var. sikokiana

Saussurea nipponica var. sikokiana, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
