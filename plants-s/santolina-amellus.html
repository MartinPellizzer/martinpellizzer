Santolina amellus

Santolina amellus, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, silver-gray foliage that is aromatic and often used in potpourri and herbal remedies. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in summer, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Santolina amellus is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
