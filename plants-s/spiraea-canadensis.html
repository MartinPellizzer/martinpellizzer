Spiraea canadensis

Spiraea canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian meadow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features arching stems that produce clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea canadensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
