Stigmaphyllon tiliifolium f. grandifolium

Stigmaphyllon tiliifolium f. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved stigmaphyllon, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
