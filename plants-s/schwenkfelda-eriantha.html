Schwenkfelda eriantha

Schwenkfelda eriantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing its seeds. Schwenkfelda eriantha is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils, thriving in humid, warm climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and a subject of interest in horticulture.
