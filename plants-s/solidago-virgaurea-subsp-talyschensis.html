Solidago virgaurea subsp. talyschensis

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

talyschensis, commonly known as the Talyschense goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
