Scabiosa krasanii

Scabiosa krasanii, commonly known as Krasan's scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that resemble small daisies, with numerous tiny florets arranged in a spherical shape. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scabiosa krasanii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
