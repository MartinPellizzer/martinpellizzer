Silene grossheimii

Silene grossheimii, commonly known as the Grossheim's campion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Silene grossheimii is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in studies of alpine flora and plant adaptation.
