Sasa hosidaikitiana

Sasa hosidaikitiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Hosidaikitiana bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, grassy texture.

Sasa hosidaikitiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
