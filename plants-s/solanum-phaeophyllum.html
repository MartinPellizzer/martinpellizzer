Solanum phaeophyllum

Solanum phaeophyllum, commonly known as the purple nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its dark purple to black leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that range in color from green to deep purple, often ripening in late summer. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
