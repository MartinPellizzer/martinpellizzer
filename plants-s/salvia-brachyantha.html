Salvia brachyantha

Salvia brachyantha, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Salvia brachyantha is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
