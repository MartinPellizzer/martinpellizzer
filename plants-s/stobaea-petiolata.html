Stobaea petiolata

Stobaea petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandbur, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spiny bracts that enclose its yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments.

Stobaea petiolata is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.
