Scirpus bufonius

Scirpus bufonius, commonly known as the toad rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in marshy or waterlogged soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, pale brown or greenish flowers.

Scirpus bufonius plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
