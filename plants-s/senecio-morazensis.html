Senecio morazensis

Senecio morazensis, commonly known as the Morazensian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio morazensis is also noted for its ornamental value, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
