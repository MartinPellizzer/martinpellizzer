It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a soft, hairy texture.

Synostemon salignus is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shady environments.
