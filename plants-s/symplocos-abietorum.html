Symplocos abietorum

Symplocos abietorum, commonly known as the white-flowered snowberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds and mammals, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Symplocos abietorum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
