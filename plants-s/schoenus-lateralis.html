Schoenus lateralis

Schoenus lateralis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it forms dense mats. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It produces small, clustered spikelets that contain tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Schoenus lateralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
