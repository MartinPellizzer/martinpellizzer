Saussurea schachimardanica

Saussurea schachimardanica, commonly known as the Armenian safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and surrounding areas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Saussurea schachimardanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential pharmacological properties.
