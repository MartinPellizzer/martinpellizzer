Serjania subtriplinervis

Serjania subtriplinervis, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive three-veined structure, contributing to its scientific name.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals, and are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.
