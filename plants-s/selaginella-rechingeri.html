Selaginella rechingeri

Selaginella rechingeri, commonly known as the Spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats or small clumps. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are often clustered around the stems, giving it a moss-like appearance. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in parts of Africa and the Middle East.

Selaginella rechingeri is well-adapted to dry conditions, with features such as reduced leaf size and a deep root system that help it survive in harsh environments.
