Silene georgievskyi

Silene georgievskyi, commonly known as the Georgievsky's campion, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, erect stem and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene georgievskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
