Sesamoides pygmaea f. egapolitana

Sesamoides pygmaea f. egapolitana, commonly known as the Egapoli stonecrop, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to rocky and arid environments.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
