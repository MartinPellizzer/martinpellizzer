Salacca wagneri

Salacca wagneri, commonly known as the betel palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its fruit, which is edible, contains a single seed surrounded by a fibrous aril.

Salacca wagneri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional practices in its native regions.
