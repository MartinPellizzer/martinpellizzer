Semiarundinaria bicorniculata

Semiarundinaria bicorniculata, commonly known as the two-horned bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and structural strength make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
