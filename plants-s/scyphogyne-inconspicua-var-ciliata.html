Scyphogyne inconspicua var. ciliata

Scyphogyne inconspicua var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy scyphogyne, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, with distinctive, ciliate (fringed) margins on the petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and may have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its overall appearance.

This variety is notable for its subtle beauty and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
