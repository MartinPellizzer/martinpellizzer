Sporobolus tenuispica

Sporobolus tenuispica, commonly known as slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Sporobolus tenuispica is often found in grasslands, shrublands, and along riverbanks.
