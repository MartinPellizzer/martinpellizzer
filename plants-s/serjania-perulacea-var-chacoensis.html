Serjania perulacea var. chacoensis

Serjania perulacea var. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco serjania, is a species native to the Chaco region of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, seasonally arid environments. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement, and its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters. It produces woody, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
