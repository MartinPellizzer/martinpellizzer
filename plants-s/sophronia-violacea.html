Sophronia violacea

Sophronia violacea, commonly known as the violet-eyed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-colored petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are small but ornate, often featuring intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of eyes.

Sophronia violacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
