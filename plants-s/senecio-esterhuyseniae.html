Senecio esterhuyseniae

Senecio esterhuyseniae, commonly known as the Esterhuysenia Senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
