Solanum

Solanum, commonly known as the nightshade family, encompasses a diverse group of flowering plants that are widely distributed across the globe.

These plants typically have simple, alternate leaves and produce berries or capsules as their fruits. Many species within Solanum are economically important, including tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers, which are cultivated for their edible parts. The genus also includes several toxic species, highlighting the importance of proper identification.

Botanically, Solanum is characterized by its unique floral structure and the presence of alkaloids in many of its species.
