Salsola spenceri

Salsola spenceri, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a low-growing, perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a common species in desert regions.

Salsola spenceri plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in areas with sparse vegetation.
