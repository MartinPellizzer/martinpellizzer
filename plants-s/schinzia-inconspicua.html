Schinzia inconspicua

Schinzia inconspicua, commonly known as the slender water clover, is a small aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has delicate, floating leaves that are usually arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often going unnoticed despite its ecological importance.

Schinzia inconspicua plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
