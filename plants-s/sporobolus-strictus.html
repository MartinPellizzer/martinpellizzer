Sporobolus strictus

Sporobolus strictus, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to arid and sandy environments, making it a common sight in prairies and coastal dunes.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
