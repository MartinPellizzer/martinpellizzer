Solanum graveolens var. pentazygum

Solanum graveolens var. pentazygum, commonly known as the purple potato, is a variant of the common nightshade plant.

It is characterized by its deep purple to violet skin and white to pale yellow flesh. The plant is native to the Andean regions of South America and is cultivated for its edible tubers. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

The tubers are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional Andean cuisine for centuries.
