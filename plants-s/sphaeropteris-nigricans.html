Sphaeropteris nigricans

Sphaeropteris nigricans, commonly known as the black-stemmed cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large, evergreen tree-like plant with a thick, trunk-like stem and a crown of pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant produces cones, with the female cones being larger and more showy than the male ones.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
