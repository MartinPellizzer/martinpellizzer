Scrophularia sckellii

Scrophularia sckellii, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in horticulture.
