Stevia pubigera f. typica

Stevia pubigera f. typica, commonly known as the typical stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is valued for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides.

This variety is often cultivated as a natural sweetener and in traditional medicine.
