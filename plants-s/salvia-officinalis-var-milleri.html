Salvia officinalis var. milleri

Salvia officinalis var. milleri, commonly known as Greek sage, is a compact, aromatic perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture and a strong, minty scent. The plant produces spikes of violet or purple flowers in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is a popular choice for both gardens and herbal collections.
