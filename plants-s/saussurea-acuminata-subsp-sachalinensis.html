Saussurea acuminata subsp. sachalinensis

Saussurea acuminata subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of eastern Asia. It typically grows in mountainous areas and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

This subspecies is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
