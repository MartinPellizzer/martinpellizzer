Satureja acinos

Satureja acinos, commonly known as alpine thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Satureja acinos is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
