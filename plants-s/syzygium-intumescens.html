Syzygium intumescens

Syzygium intumescens, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Syzygium intumescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
