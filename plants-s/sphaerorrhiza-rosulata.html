Sphaerorrhiza rosulata

Sphaerorrhiza rosulata, commonly known as the rose-like root plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive rosette-like arrangement of fleshy, succulent leaves that form a compact, basal cluster. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its root system is particularly notable for its swollen, bulbous structure, which stores water and nutrients.

Sphaerorrhiza rosulata is well adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient and intriguing subject for botanical study.
