Solanum chacoense subsp. subtilius

Solanum chacoense subsp.

subtilius, commonly known as the Chaco nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers.

The plant is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange, are edible but contain toxic compounds.

This subspecies is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
