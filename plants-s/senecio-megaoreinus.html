Senecio megaoreinus

Senecio megaoreinus, commonly known as the giant orange daisy, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by pale green ray florets. The plant features a robust, upright stem with rough, hairy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
