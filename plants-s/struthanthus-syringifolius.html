Struthanthus syringifolius

Struthanthus syringifolius, commonly known as the California flaxflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive, fluted appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
