Solanum chiloense

Solanum chiloense, commonly known as Chilean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

The plant is often found in coastal regions and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
