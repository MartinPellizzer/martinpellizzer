Sclerochloa multiculmis var. distans

Sclerochloa multiculmis var. distans, commonly known as the distant panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant has tough, fibrous leaves adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems in its native range.
