Synchaeta norvegica

Synchaeta norvegica, commonly known as the Norwegian sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or alpine environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, including strong winds and low nutrient availability.

Synchaeta norvegica plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
