Saxifraga fortunei var. partita

Saxifraga fortunei var. partita, commonly known as the variegated stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in cold climates, and it is often used in horticultural displays for its ornamental value.
