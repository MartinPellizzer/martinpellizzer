Seriphidium freitagii

Seriphidium freitagii, commonly known as the Freitag's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
