Sipanea dichotoma

Sipanea dichotoma, commonly known as the forked stickleaf, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems that often split into two at the base, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, growing in dense tufts along the stem.

Sipanea dichotoma is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
