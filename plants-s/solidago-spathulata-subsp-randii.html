Solidago spathulata subsp. randii

Solidago spathulata subsp.

randii, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and roadside ditches.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow composite flowers in late summer to early autumn. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
