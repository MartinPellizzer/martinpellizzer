Spinacia spinosa

Spinacia spinosa, commonly known as the prickly spinach, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems covered in small, stiff spines. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a slightly bitter taste. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
