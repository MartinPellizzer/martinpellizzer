Smilacina forrestii

Smilacina forrestii, commonly known as the Appalachian greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. Smilacina forrestii thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
