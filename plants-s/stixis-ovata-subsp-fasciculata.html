Stixis ovata subsp. fasciculata

Stixis ovata subsp.

fasciculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stixis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in rocky outcrops and open grasslands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
