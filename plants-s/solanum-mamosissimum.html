Solanum mamosissimum

Solanum mamosissimum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant has a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Solanum mamosissimum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
