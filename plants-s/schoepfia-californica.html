Schoepfia californica

Schoepfia californica, commonly known as California Schoepfia, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.
