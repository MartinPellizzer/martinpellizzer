Scirpus praetextatus

Scirpus praetextatus, commonly known as the red-stemmed bulrush, is a tall, perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ecological importance in wetland habitats. Scirpus praetextatus thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Its dense growth helps stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
