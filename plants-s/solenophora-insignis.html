Solenophora insignis

Solenophora insignis, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in a star-like pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant features a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a typical adaptation for minimizing water loss.

Solenophora insignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in drought conditions.
