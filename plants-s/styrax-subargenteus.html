Styrax subargenteus

Styrax subargenteus, commonly known as silver styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white, clustered flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and floral display make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and landscapes.
