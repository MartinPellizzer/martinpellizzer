Saccolabium rubrum

Saccolabium rubrum, commonly known as the scarlet cholla, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to red in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its succulent leaves and stems store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

Saccolabium rubrum plays a vital role in desert ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
