Sinapis nigra

Sinapis nigra, commonly known as black mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species and cruciferous vegetables. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a slightly hairy surface, and the seeds are small, black, and triangular in shape.

Black mustard is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
