Shorea gentilis

Shorea gentilis, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

Shorea gentilis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
