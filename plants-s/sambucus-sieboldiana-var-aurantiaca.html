Sambucus sieboldiana var. aurantiaca

Sambucus sieboldiana var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the Japanese elderberry or golden elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of yellowish-orange berries that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are small and white, appearing in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
