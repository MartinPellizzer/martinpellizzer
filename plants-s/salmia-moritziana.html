Salmia moritziana

Salmia moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's salvia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in the summer months.

Salmia moritziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
