Suregada borneensis

Suregada borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the islands of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. The plant has glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers with a fragrant aroma. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

The fruit is edible, though its flavor is often described as tart and slightly acidic.
