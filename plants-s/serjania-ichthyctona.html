Serjania ichthyctona

Serjania ichthyctona, commonly known as the fishbone tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often reddish or yellowish leaves that resemble fish bones. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
