Scaevola decurrens

Scaevola decurrens, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, bright blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has silver-gray foliage and is adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden environments. It is often found in dunes and coastal heathlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.

Scaevola decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, coastal conditions.
