Solanum hexandrum

Solanum hexandrum, commonly known as the Himalayan nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Solanum hexandrum is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
