Salacia coronata

Salacia coronata, commonly known as the Crowned Salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to other flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
