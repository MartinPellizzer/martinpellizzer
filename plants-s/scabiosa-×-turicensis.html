Scabiosa × turicensis

Scabiosa × turicensis, commonly known as the Turin scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fluffy, pink or white flower heads that resemble small clouds, which are composed of numerous tiny, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
