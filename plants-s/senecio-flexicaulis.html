Senecio flexicaulis

Senecio flexicaulis, commonly known as the curly-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serpentine stems that curl and twist as the plant grows. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
