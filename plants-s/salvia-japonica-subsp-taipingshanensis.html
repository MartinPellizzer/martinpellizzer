Salvia japonica subsp. taipingshanensis

Salvia japonica subsp.

taipingshanensis, commonly known as the Taipingshan sage, is a flowering plant native to China. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a popular choice in horticulture.
