Sauvetrea laevilabris

Sauvetrea laevilabris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sauvetrea, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Sauvetrea laevilabris is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
