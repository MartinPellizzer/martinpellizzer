Salix brownei

Salix brownei, commonly known as the Brown's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as stream banks and meadows. The plant has slender, often multi-stemmed branches and produces catkins in early spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture and turn yellow in the fall.

Salix brownei plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
