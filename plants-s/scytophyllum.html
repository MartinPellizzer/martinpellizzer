Scytophyllum

Scytophyllum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine. Scytophyllum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
