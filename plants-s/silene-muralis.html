Silene muralis

Silene muralis, commonly known as the wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or wall environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene muralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and along walls.
