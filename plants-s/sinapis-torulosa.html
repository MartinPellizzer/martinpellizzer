Sinapis torulosa

Sinapis torulosa, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a hairy, branched stem that can grow up to 30 cm in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine, white hairs.

Sinapis torulosa is often found in disturbed soils and is part of the Brassicaceae family, known for its characteristic four-petaled flowers and seed pods.
