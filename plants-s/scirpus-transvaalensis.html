Scirpus transvaalensis

Scirpus transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers at the top of the stems, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus transvaalensis plays an important role in filtering water and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
