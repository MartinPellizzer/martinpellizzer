Sida speciosa

Sida speciosa, commonly known as the showy sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic with serrated edges, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

Sida speciosa is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along roadsides, where it thrives in humid environments.
