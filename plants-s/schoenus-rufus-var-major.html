Schoenus rufus var. major

Schoenus rufus var. major, commonly known as the red tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and reddish-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland grassland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, while the culms are sturdy and upright.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in natural environments.
