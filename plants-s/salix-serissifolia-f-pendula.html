Salix serissifolia f. pendula

Salix serissifolia f. pendula, commonly known as weeping willow, is a decorative cultivar of the willow tree characterized by its cascading branches.

It features long, slender leaves that are typically bright green and have a lanceolate shape. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often planted near water features.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden design.
