Salvia coccinea var. pseudococcinea

Salvia coccinea var. pseudococcinea, commonly known as red sage or scarlet sage, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange-red flower spikes that bloom in summer, adding a bold splash of color to gardens. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.

This variety is particularly valued for its showy blooms and adaptability to warm, sunny climates.
