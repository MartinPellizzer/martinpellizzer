Satureja graeca var. pauciflora

Satureja graeca var. pauciflora, commonly known as Greek thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
