Salvia fulgens

Salvia fulgens, commonly known as the flame beardtongue, is a striking perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Salvia fulgens thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract hummingbirds and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
