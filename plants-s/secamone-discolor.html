Secamone discolor

Secamone discolor, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with thick, often hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

The plant contains various alkaloids and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
