Silene veselskyi subsp. glutinosa

Silene veselskyi subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky Silene, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically in dry, rocky habitats. It is native to Central Asia and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
