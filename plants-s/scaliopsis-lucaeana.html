Scaliopsis lucaeana

Scaliopsis lucaeana, commonly known as the Lucaeana scaleleaf, is a rare and lesser-known botanical species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, featuring small, scale-like leaves that are tightly clustered along the stems. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or pale yellow, and it produces capsule-like fruits that contain minute seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Scaliopsis lucaeana is considered a species of conservation concern.
