Solanum arabicum

Solanum arabicum, commonly known as the desert tomato or wild tomato, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellow or orange fruits that resemble tomatoes. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, while the stems are often woody and branched.

Solanum arabicum is also known for its potential medicinal uses, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
