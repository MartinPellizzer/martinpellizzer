Salvia transsylvanica

Salvia transsylvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features spikes of violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia transsylvanica is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
