Sonchus oleraceus subsp. angustissimus

Sonchus oleraceus subsp.

angustissimus, commonly known as narrowleaf sowthistle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a weed and a potential forage plant.
