Saccolabium buddleiflorum

Saccolabium buddleiflorum, commonly known as the silverleaf bush.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
