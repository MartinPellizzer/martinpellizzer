Sideroxylon retinerve

Sideroxylon retinerve, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds.

The species is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a valuable but rare resource in its native habitat.
