Seruneum puberulum

Seruneum puberulum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and can spread extensively, making it a persistent weed in agricultural fields.

Seruneum puberulum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
