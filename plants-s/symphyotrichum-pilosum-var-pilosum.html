Symphyotrichum pilosum var. pilosum

Symphyotrichum pilosum var. pilosum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a conical shape at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, white hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.
