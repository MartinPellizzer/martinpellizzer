Salvia serboana

Salvia serboana, commonly known as Serbian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Salvia serboana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
