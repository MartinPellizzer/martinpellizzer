Shuria

Shuria, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries. Shuria thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
