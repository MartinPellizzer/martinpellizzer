Schizopsis panurensis

Schizopsis panurensis, commonly known as the Andean cholla, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a spiny, shrub-like cactus that typically grows in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils. The plant features multiple cylindrical stems that are often segmented and covered in sharp spines. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

Schizopsis panurensis is adapted to harsh, arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
