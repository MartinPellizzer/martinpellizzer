Syzygium glomerulatum

Syzygium glomerulatum, commonly known as the red corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, red flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Syzygium glomerulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral displays.
