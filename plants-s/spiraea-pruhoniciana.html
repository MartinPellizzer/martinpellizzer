Spiraea pruhoniciana

Spiraea pruhoniciana, commonly known as the Pruhonice spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea pruhoniciana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
