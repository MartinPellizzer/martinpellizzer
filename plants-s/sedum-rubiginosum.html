Sedum rubiginosum

Sedum rubiginosum, commonly known as the red-skin stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
