Sinapidendron decumbens

Sinapidendron decumbens, commonly known as the creeping sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a sprawling, decumbent stem that lies close to the ground, with small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in conditions with minimal rainfall.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in drought-tolerant gardens.
