Sedum gerardianum

Sedum gerardianum, commonly known as the Gerard's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically green, sometimes with a bluish tinge, and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
