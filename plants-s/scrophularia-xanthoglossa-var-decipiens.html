Scrophularia xanthoglossa var. decipiens

Scrophularia xanthoglossa var. decipiens, commonly known as the yellow-lipped scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of tubular, yellowish-orange flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
