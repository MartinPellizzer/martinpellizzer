Saussurea tenuicaulis

Saussurea tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Saussurea tenuicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural alpine setting.
