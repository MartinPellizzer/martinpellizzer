Senna birostris var. helveola

Senna birostris var. helveola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, elongated leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
