Solenostemma oleifolium

Solenostemma oleifolium, commonly known as the oil plant or wild yam, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into cylindrical, berry-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its milky sap and possesses a complex root system that often forms tubers. Its botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal medicine.

Solenostemma oleifolium is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
