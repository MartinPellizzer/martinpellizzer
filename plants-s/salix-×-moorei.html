Salix × moorei

Salix × moorei, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bright green upper surface and a lighter green underside. It produces catkin-like inflorescences in early spring, which are important for pollination.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
