Senecio platanifolius

Senecio platanifolius, commonly known as the giant ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble the leaves of a plane tree, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
