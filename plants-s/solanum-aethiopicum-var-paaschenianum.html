Solanum aethiopicum var. paaschenianum

Solanum aethiopicum var. paaschenianum, commonly known as the Ethiopian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has ovate, hairy leaves. Its fruit is a small, yellow berry that ripens to red, containing several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
