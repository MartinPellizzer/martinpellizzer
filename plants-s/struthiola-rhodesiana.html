Struthiola rhodesiana

Struthiola rhodesiana, commonly known as the Rhodes thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Struthiola rhodesiana is often found in open vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
