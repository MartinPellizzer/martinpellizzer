Suaeda crassifolia

Suaeda crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments in Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to harsh, drought-prone conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of branches. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Suaeda crassifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils in desert and coastal areas.
