Senecio pampae

Senecio pampae, commonly known as the Pampas groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and arid environments, showcasing a compact, low-growing habit. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
