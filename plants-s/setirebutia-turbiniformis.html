Setirebutia turbiniformis

Setirebutia turbiniformis, commonly known as the Turbiniform Turban Shell, is a species of sea snail found in tropical marine environments.

It belongs to the family Turbinidae and is characterized by its large, spiral-shaped shell with a distinctive turban-like appearance. The shell is typically thick and robust, with a smooth surface and a wide, flaring aperture. The species is often found in shallow coastal waters and is known for its ability to thrive in sandy substrates.

Its reproductive biology and ecological role in marine ecosystems are areas of ongoing study.
