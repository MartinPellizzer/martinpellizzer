Senna georgica

Senna georgica, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Senna georgica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
