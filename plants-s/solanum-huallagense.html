Solanum huallagense

Solanum huallagense, commonly known as the Huallaga nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
