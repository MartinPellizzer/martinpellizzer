Sphaerocionium cristatum

Sphaerocionium cristatum, commonly known as the crown-leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular stem that is often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its textured foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and protect it from herbivores.

Sphaerocionium cristatum is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.
