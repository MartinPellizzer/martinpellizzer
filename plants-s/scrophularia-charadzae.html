Scrophularia charadzae

Scrophularia charadzae, commonly known as the Georgian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous areas. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are tubular and occur in clusters, typically in shades of purple or pink.

Scrophularia charadzae is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.
