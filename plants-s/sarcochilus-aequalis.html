Sarcochilus aequalis

Sarcochilus aequalis, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a distinctive orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its large, tree-like structure with a thick, fibrous trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in New Zealand's native flora.
