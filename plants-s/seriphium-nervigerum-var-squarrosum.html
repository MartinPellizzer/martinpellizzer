Seriphium nervigerum var. squarrosum

Seriphium nervigerum var. squarrosum, commonly known as the roughleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and it is often found in regions with low rainfall.
