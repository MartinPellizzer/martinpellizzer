Selago inconstans

Selago inconstans, commonly known as the wandering grass or variable grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Selago inconstans is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed or marginal habitats.
