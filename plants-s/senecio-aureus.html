Senecio aureus

Senecio aureus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark green, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, inconspicuous disk flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads.

Senecio aureus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and ease of care.
