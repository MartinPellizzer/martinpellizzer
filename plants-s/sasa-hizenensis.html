Sasa hizenensis

Sasa hizenensis, commonly known as the Hizen bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a smooth, slightly waxy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Japanese crafts.
