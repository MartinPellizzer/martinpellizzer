Saprosma indica var. gardneri

Saprosma indica var. gardneri, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
