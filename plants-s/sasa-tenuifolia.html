Sasa tenuifolia

Sasa tenuifolia, commonly known as slender bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clumping growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Sasa tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
