Setaria viridis var. weinmannii

Setaria viridis var. weinmannii, commonly known as slender green millet, is a variant of the green millet species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a grassy plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
