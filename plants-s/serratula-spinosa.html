Serratula spinosa

Serratula spinosa, commonly known as the thistleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Serratula spinosa typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows and along roadsides.

It is often used in traditional medicine, though its toxicity requires caution.
