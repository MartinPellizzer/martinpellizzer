Santalum freycinetianum var. longifolium

Santalum freycinetianum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved sandalwood, is a member of the Santalaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic wood and compound leaves. The leaves are typically long and narrow, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

It is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats, and its wood is highly valued for its fragrance and durability.
