Salvia apiana var. typica

Salvia apiana var. typica, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional ceremonial practices and for their aromatic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
