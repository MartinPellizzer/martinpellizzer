Solanum juvenale

Solanum juvenale, commonly known as the small nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval-shaped berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. Solanum juvenale belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
