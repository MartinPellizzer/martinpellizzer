Setaria gibbosa

Setaria gibbosa, commonly known as bearded dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear bristly, hair-like structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Setaria gibbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for native wildlife.
