Sida linifolia var. brevis

Sida linifolia var. brevis, commonly known as the short-hair mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is known for its drought tolerance and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
