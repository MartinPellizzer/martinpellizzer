Saracha procumbens

Saracha procumbens, commonly known as the creeping sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows along rocky soils or in sandy habitats, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on desert flora and drought resistance.
