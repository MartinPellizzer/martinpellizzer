Staberoha imbricata var. stenoptera

Staberoha imbricata var. stenoptera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed star-leaf, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a star-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
