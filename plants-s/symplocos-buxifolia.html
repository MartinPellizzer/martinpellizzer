Symplocos buxifolia

Symplocos buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf evergreen.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that resemble those of boxwood. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded gardens.
