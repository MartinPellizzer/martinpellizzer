Saxifraga viridipetala

Saxifraga viridipetala, commonly known as the green-petaled saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are often greenish in color. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga viridipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
