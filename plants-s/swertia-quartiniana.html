Swertia quartiniana

Swertia quartiniana, commonly known as the Alpine swertia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Swertia quartiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
