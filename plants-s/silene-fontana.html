Silene fontana

Silene fontana, commonly known as the Fontana bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine environments. It has a unique root system that allows it to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil efficiently.

Silene fontana is also notable for its ability to thrive in cold climates, making it a resilient species in its native range.
