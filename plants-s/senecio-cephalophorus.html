Senecio cephalophorus

Senecio cephalophorus, commonly known as the helmet flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that resemble a helmet, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio cephalophorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
