Seaforthia alexandriae

Seaforthia alexandriae, commonly known as the Alexandria seaforthia, is a rare flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale blue. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand saline conditions and coastal winds. It plays a vital role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
