Sidalcea reptans var. ranunculacea

Sidalcea reptans var. ranunculacea, commonly known as the creeping soapwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
