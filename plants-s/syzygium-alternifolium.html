Syzygium alternifolium

Syzygium alternifolium, commonly known as the clove tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, crimson-colored flowers and dark purple, woody fruits that contain aromatic buds used as spices. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

Syzygium alternifolium is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its medicinal properties.
