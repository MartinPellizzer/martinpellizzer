Sinapis arvensis var. divaricata

Sinapis arvensis var. divaricata, commonly known as field mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is part of the Brassicaceae family, known for its cruciform flowers and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
