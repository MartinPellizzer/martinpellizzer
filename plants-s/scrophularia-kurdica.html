Scrophularia kurdica

Scrophularia kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish figwort, is a perennial herb native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features upright stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, covered with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
