Solidago pringlei

Solidago pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Solidago pringlei plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
