Senegalia guarensis

Senegalia guarensis, commonly known as the Guarensis acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and bipinnate leaves, which are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is often used for erosion control and as a source of fodder for livestock.

Senegalia guarensis plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional practices.
