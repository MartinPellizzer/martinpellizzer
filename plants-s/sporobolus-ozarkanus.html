Sporobolus ozarkanus

Sporobolus ozarkanus, commonly known as the Ozark sandgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, tufted growth and fine, silvery-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and dunes.

Sporobolus ozarkanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
