Scopulophila parryi

Scopulophila parryi, commonly known as the Parry's rock-mahogany, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly found in rocky and arid environments.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Scopulophila parryi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes.
