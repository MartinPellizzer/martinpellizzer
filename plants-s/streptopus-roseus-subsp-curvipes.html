Streptopus roseus subsp. curvipes

Streptopus roseus subsp.

curvipes, commonly known as the pink starwort, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats in its natural habitat.
