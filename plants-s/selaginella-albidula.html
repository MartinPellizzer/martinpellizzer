Selaginella albidula

Selaginella albidula, commonly known as the white spore fern, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, and its sporophytes are typically found in clusters. It is often found in rocky soils or on shaded forest floors in temperate regions.

Selaginella albidula is also notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its use in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
