Solanum cacetanum

Solanum cacetanum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a deep red, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
