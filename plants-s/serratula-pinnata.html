Serratula pinnata

Serratula pinnata, commonly known as the slender sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Serratula pinnata is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
