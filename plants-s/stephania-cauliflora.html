Stephania cauliflora

Stephania cauliflora, commonly known as the climbing beggar-tick, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of milky sap. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Stephania cauliflora is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
