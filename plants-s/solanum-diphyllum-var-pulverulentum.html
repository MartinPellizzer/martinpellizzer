Solanum diphyllum var. pulverulentum

Solanum diphyllum var. pulverulentum, commonly known as the powdered leaf nightshade, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique two-faced leaves, with the upper surface typically being green and the lower surface a lighter, often powdery white or gray. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by berries that change color as they mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicately balanced species within its ecosystem.
