Satyrium papillosum

Satyrium papillosum, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in a loose inflorescence.

The showy orchid plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
