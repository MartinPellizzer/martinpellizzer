Silene colorata proles distachya

Silene colorata proles distachya, commonly known as the purple-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and color variations.
