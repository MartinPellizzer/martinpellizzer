Salix dodgeana

Salix dodgeana, commonly known as the Baja dwarf willow, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
