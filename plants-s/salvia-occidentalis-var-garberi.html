Salvia occidentalis var. garberi

Salvia occidentalis var. garberi, commonly known as Garber's sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, such as chaparral and woodland areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.

The species is also notable for its role in supporting local pollinators.
