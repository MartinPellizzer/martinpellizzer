Sapium melanostictum

Sapium melanostictum, commonly known as the black-stained sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
