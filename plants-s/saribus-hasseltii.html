Saribus hasseltii

Saribus hasseltii, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor spaces.

Saribus hasseltii is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
