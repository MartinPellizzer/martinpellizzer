Salvia bucharica

Salvia bucharica, commonly known as the Afghan sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a popular choice among horticulturists and herbalists.
