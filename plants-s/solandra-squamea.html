Solandra squamea

Solandra squamea, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, round berries that contain toxic seeds, making it potentially harmful if ingested. Solandra squamea thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.
