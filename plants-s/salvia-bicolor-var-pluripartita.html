Salvia bicolor var. pluripartita

Salvia bicolor var. pluripartita, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture, and its stems are sturdy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive color variation and complex floral structure.
