Salix tenella var. trichadenia

Salix tenella var. trichadenia, commonly known as the silver willow or woolly willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a silvery or grayish underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
