Schoenoplectiella erecta

Schoenoplectiella erecta, commonly known as the erect bulrush, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer. Its rhizomes contribute to its ability to thrive in saturated soils, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Schoenoplectiella erecta plays a role in filtering water and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
