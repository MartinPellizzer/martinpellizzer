Scopolia straminiifolia

Scopolia straminiifolia, commonly known as the creeping jenny, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.

Scopolia straminiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
