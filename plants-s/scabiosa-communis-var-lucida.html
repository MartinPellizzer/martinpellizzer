Scabiosa communis var. lucida

Scabiosa communis var. lucida, commonly known as the shining scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
