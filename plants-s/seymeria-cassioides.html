Seymeria cassioides

Seymeria cassioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved seymeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Seymeria cassioides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for its resilience and ornamental value in horticulture.
