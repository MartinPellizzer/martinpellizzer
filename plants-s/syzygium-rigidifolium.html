Syzygium rigidifolium

Syzygium rigidifolium, commonly known as the rigid-leaf clove.

It is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are thick and leathery, which helps it retain moisture in its humid, tropical habitat.

The plant is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
