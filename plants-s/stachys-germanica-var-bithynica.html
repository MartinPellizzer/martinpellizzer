Stachys germanica var. bithynica

Stachys germanica var. bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian betony, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland margins and stream sides.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is noted for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
