Senecio baccharidiflorus

Senecio baccharidiflorus, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble tiny tomatoes, which are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
