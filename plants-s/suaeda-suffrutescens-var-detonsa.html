Suaeda suffrutescens var. detonsa

Suaeda suffrutescens var. detonsa, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It possesses succulent leaves that store water, aiding in survival in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots have a high salt tolerance, allowing it to thrive in coastal soils.

It is often found in dune ecosystems and plays a role in stabilizing sandy landscapes.
