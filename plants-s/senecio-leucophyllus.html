Senecio leucophyllus

Senecio leucophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers. The plant is distinguished by its large, fleshy, white or pale green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in arid regions around the world.
