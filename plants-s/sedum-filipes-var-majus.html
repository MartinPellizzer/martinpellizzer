Sedum filipes var. majus

Sedum filipes var. majus, commonly known as the giant stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and can form dense mats when grown in full sun.

Sedum filipes var. majus is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in rocky or arid environments.
