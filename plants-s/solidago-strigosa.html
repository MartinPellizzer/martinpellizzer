Solidago strigosa

Solidago strigosa, commonly known as hairy goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

Hairy goldenrod is often found in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.
