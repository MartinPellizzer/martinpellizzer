Sterculia grandifolia

Sterculia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
