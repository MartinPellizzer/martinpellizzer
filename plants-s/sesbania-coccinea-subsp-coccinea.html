Sesbania coccinea subsp. coccinea

Sesbania coccinea subsp.

coccinea, commonly known as the red sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is a leguminous species, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, which are hairy and often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
