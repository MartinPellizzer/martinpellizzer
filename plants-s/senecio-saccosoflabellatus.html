Senecio saccosoflabellatus

Senecio saccosoflabellatus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring narrow, linear leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
