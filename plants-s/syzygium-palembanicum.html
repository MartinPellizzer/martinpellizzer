Syzygium palembanicum

Syzygium palembanicum, commonly known as the Palembang tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Syzygium palembanicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
