Stachys noeana

Stachys noeana, commonly known as the Greek violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Stachys noeana is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
