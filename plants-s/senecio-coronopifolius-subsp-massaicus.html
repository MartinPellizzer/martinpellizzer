Senecio coronopifolius subsp. massaicus

Senecio coronopifolius subsp.

massaicus, commonly known as the Massaicus groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is a perennial herb with a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
