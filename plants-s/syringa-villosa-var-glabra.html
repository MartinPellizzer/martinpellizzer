Syringa villosa var. glabra

Syringa villosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features fragrant, panicle-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, gray-green foliage that turns yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and compact growth habit.
