Silphium solidaginoides

Silphium solidaginoides, commonly known as swamp blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It features a tall, unbranched stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads in late summer, which are composed of numerous tiny ray florets. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, acidic soils.

Silphium solidaginoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
