Spyridium stenophyllum subsp. stenophyllum

Spyridium stenophyllum subsp.

stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spyridium, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, aromatic leaves.

The species produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
