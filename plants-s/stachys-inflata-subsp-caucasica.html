Stachys inflata subsp. caucasica

Stachys inflata subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian inflated plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in grassy areas or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the mint family.
