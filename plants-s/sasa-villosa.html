Sasa villosa

Sasa villosa, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasa villosa is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
