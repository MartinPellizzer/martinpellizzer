Syzygium olivifolium

Syzygium olivifolium, commonly known as the oliveleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
