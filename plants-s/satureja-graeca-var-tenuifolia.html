Satureja graeca var. tenuifolia

Satureja graeca var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved Greek thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in terminal clusters, typical of the mint family. Its leaves are narrow, fragrant, and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
