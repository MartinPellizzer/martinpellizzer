Sida guadalupensis

Sida guadalupensis, commonly known as Guadalupine or Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with a rough texture. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish, with five petals and a central disk, and bloom in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its fibrous stems, which have been used historically for textile purposes.
