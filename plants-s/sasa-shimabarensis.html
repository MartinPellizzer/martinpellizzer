Sasa shimabarensis

Sasa shimabarensis, commonly known as the Shimabare bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa shimabarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Japanese culture.
