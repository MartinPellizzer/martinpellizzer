Smilax trachyclada

Smilax trachyclada, commonly known as the rough-stemmed greenbriar, is a woody perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticulture.
