Salvia calocalicina

Salvia calocalicina, commonly known as the golden spike salvia, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and square stems.
