Sporobolus indicus var. diandrus

Sporobolus indicus var. diandrus, commonly known as the sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
