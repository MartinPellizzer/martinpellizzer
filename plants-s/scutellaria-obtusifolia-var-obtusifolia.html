Scutellaria obtusifolia var. obtusifolia

Scutellaria obtusifolia var. obtusifolia, commonly known as blunt-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodlands or shaded areas, preferring rich, loamy soils. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with rounded or blunt tips, and produces spikes of blue to violet flowers. Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.
