Symplocos polyphylla

Symplocos polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries in the fall. Symplocos polyphylla thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
