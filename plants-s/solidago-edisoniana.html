Solidago edisoniana

Solidago edisoniana, commonly known as the Edison goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Solidago edisoniana is valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its role in ecological restoration projects.
