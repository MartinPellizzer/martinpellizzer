Senecio triangularis var. angustifolius

Senecio triangularis var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved African violet, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
