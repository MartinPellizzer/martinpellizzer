Stevia soratensis var. soratensis

Stevia soratensis var. soratensis, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intensely sweet taste, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal cultivation.

Due to its natural sweetness, it is increasingly used as a natural sweetener in food and beverages.
