Sasa kuriyamensis

Sasa kuriyamensis, commonly known as the Kuriyamensis bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The culms are used in traditional crafts and are valued for their flexibility and strength.
