Senecio tricuspidatus

Senecio tricuspidatus, commonly known as the three-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. Its small yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and the leaves are usually trilobed, giving it its common name.

Senecio tricuspidatus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
