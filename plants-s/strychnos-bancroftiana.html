Strychnos bancroftiana

Strychnos bancroftiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing seeds.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
