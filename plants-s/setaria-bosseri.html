Setaria bosseri

Setaria bosseri, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a compact growth habit.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
