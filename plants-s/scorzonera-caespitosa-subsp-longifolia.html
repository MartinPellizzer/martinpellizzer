Scorzonera caespitosa subsp. longifolia

Scorzonera caespitosa subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces yellow flowers with long, slender petals.

The plant has a robust root system, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
