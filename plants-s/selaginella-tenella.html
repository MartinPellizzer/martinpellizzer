Selaginella tenella

Selaginella tenella, commonly known as the clubmoss or lesser clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that form tight clusters, giving it a moss-like appearance. Selaginella tenella is often found in rocky or forested areas and is known for its resilience to drought conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
