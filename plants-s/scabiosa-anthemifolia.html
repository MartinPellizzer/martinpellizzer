Scabiosa anthemifolia

Scabiosa anthemifolia, commonly known as the "Cuckoo's Buttercup," is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of pink or white, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are lance-shaped and more elongated. Scabiosa anthemifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
