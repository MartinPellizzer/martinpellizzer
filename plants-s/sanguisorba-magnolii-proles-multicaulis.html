Sanguisorba magnolii proles multicaulis

Sanguisorba magnolii proles multicaulis, commonly known as the multicaulis sweetroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woolly-haired leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Its root system is fibrous and contains a sweet, edible sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Sanguisorba magnolii proles multicaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.
