Saccharum spontaneum var. edule

Saccharum spontaneum var. edule, commonly known as wild sugarcane, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and high sugar content in its stems. This plant is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its leaves are broad and blade-like, contributing to its ornamental appearance.

It is a valuable resource for both traditional and modern agricultural practices.
