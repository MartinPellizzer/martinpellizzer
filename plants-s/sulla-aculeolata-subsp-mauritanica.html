Sulla aculeolata subsp. mauritanica

Sulla aculeolata subsp.

mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian thornvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in small, sharp spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a hairy texture.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.
