Sorbus thuringiaca var. subaucuparia

Sorbus thuringiaca var. subaucuparia, commonly known as the white-leafed rowan, is a variant of the rowan tree native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale green leaves, which set it apart from other Sorbus species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
