Sesleria caerulea var. insularis

Sesleria caerulea var. insularis, commonly known as the Island blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate blue to violet flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland settings and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native, hardy ground cover.
