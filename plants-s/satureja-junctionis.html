Satureja junctionis

Satureja junctionis, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its small, pink or white flowers are clustered in whorls, and the leaves are fragrant, often used in traditional medicine.

Satureja junctionis is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, known for their antimicrobial properties.
