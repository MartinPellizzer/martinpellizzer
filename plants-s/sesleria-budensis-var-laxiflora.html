Sesleria budensis var. laxiflora

Sesleria budensis var. laxiflora, commonly known as the loose-flowered woodland grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its delicate appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
