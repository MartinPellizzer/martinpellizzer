Solanum demissum f. adpresso-acuminatum

Solanum demissum f. adpresso-acuminatum, commonly known as the compact-acute tomato, is a compact, herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, acute leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright red, cherry-like fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species within the Solanum genus.
