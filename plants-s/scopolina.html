Scopolina

Scopolina, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant contains various alkaloids, including scopolamine, which has been historically used in medicine. Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and of interest in pharmacological research.
