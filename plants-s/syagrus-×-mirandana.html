Syagrus × mirandana

Syagrus × mirandana, commonly known as the hybrid palm, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Syagrus romanzoffiana and Syagrus acanthophylla.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its reproductive system involves the production of both male and female flowers, allowing for self-pollination and cross-pollination.

The botanical features of Syagrus × mirandana make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
