Spiranthes igniorchis

Spiranthes igniorchis, commonly known as the white fringed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spirally arranged flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like and often form a rosette at the base.

The flowers are white with distinctive fringed petals and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees and flies.
