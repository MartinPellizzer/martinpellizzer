Shallonium

Shallonium, commonly known as the golden shallot.

It is a member of the Allium genus, characterized by its bulbous structure and aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often used in culinary applications for their mild, sweet flavor.

Shallonium is typically grown in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
