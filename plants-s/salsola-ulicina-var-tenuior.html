Salsola ulicina var. tenuior

Salsola ulicina var. tenuior, commonly known as narrowleaf Russian thistle, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and disturbed soils due to its tolerance of high salinity and poor nutrients.

This species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open spaces, making it an invasive species in some regions.
