Senecio arizonicus

Senecio arizonicus, commonly known as Arizona groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs.

Arizona groundsel is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
