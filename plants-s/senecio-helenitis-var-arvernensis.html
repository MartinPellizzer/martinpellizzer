Senecio helenitis var. arvernensis

Senecio helenitis var. arvernensis, commonly known as the Arvernian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern France.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
