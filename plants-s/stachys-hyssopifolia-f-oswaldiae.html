Stachys hyssopifolia f. oswaldiae

Stachys hyssopifolia f. oswaldiae, commonly known as the Oswalda's hedge nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pale purple to violet in color. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
