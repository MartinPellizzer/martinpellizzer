Styrax veitchiorus

Styrax veitchiorus, commonly known as the Veitch's styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Styrax veitchiorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
