Silene heterodonta subsp. rosella

Silene heterodonta subsp.

rosella, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
