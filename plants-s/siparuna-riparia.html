Siparuna riparia

Siparuna riparia, commonly known as the river siparuna, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Siparuna riparia thrives in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and forest understories.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
