Spiraea confusa var. sericea

Spiraea confusa var. sericea, commonly known as silver weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that create a weeping appearance, with small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate and have a silvery-white underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
