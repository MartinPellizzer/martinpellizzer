Samanea corymbosa

Samanea corymbosa, commonly known as the silky sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Samanea corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
