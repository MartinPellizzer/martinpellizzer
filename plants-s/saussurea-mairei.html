Saussurea mairei

Saussurea mairei, commonly known as the Maire's saffron, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fluffy inflorescences. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
