Sarracenia × illustrata

Sarracenia × illustrata, commonly known as the illustrated pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, and often brightly colored pitchers that secrete a digestive enzyme-laden liquid to trap and digest insects. The plant's leaves form tubular structures that serve as both a trap and a means of nutrient absorption. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires high humidity and bright light for optimal growth.

Sarracenia × illustrata is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and unique ecological function.
