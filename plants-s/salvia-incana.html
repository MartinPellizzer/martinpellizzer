Salvia incana

Salvia incana, commonly known as rough sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Salvia incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
