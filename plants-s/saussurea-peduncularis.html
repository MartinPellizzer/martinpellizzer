Saussurea peduncularis

Saussurea peduncularis, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic compounds. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flower heads that resemble saffron threads, giving it its common name. Its rhizomes and roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Saussurea peduncularis make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
