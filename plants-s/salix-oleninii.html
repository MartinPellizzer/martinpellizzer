Salix oleninii

Salix oleninii, commonly known as the Siberian willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and tundra environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in arctic and subarctic regions.
