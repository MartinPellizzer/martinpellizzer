Swertia piloglandulosa subsp. biovulata

Swertia piloglandulosa subsp.

biovulata, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
