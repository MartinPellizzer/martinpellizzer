Salix × stuartii

Salix × stuartii, commonly known as the Stuart's willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its graceful appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.

However, it is important to note that it can be invasive in certain areas if not properly managed.
