Saussurea graciliformis

Saussurea graciliformis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its fibrous roots help it anchor in loose, nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its habitat and appearance, it is often considered an indicator species of alpine ecosystems.
