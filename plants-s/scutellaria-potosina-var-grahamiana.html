Scutellaria potosina var. grahamiana

Scutellaria potosina var. grahamiana, commonly known as Graham's bluehead, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and attractive floral display.
