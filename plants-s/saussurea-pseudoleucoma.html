Saussurea pseudoleucoma

Saussurea pseudoleucoma, commonly known as the Himalayan saffron.

It is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
