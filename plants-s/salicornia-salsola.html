Salicornia salsola

Salicornia salsola, commonly known as glasswort, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other regions with high salt content in the soil. The plant has succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its small, scale-like leaves are typically green or red, depending on the species and environmental conditions.

Salicornia salsola is also known for its edible shoots, which are used in various culinary traditions around the world.
