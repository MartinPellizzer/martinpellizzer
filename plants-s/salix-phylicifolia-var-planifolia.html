Salix phylicifolia var. planifolia

Salix phylicifolia var. planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaf willow, is a variant of the common willow species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its flat, broad leaves that are often arranged in a distinctive manner. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its flowering catkins appear in early spring, contributing to its ecological role in pollination.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
