Spiraea kirilowii

Spiraea kirilowii, commonly known as Kirilow's weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Far East, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its foliage turns a striking yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Spiraea kirilowii is well-suited for gardens and naturalized areas due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
