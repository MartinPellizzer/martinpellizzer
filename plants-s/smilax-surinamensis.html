Smilax surinamensis

Smilax surinamensis, commonly known as the yellow smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which often have sharp spines, and its compound leaves that are typically alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Smilax surinamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
