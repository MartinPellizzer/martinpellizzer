Sideritis × viciosoi var. affinis

Sideritis × viciosoi var. affinis, commonly known as the Greek ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically pink or violet, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late summer.

It is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
