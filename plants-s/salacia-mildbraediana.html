Salacia mildbraediana

Salacia mildbraediana, commonly known as the yellow salacia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Salaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and less commonly cultivated species.
