Stachys oblongifolia var. oblongifolia

Stachys oblongifolia var. oblongifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
