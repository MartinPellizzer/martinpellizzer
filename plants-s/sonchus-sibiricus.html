Sonchus sibiricus

Sonchus sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian sowthistle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the dandelion family. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
