Sideritis scardica

Sideritis scardica, commonly known as Greek mountain tea, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Sideritis scardica is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal remedies.
