Solanum kitivuense subsp. englerianum

Solanum kitivuense subsp.

englerianum, commonly known as the Engler's nightshade, is a shrub native to eastern Africa. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces berries that are typically red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided by both livestock and humans.
