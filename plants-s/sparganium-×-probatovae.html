Sparganium × probatovae

Sparganium × probatovae, commonly known as the hybrid bur reed, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Sparganium eurycarpum and Sparganium angustifolium, characterized by its distinctive, spiky, yellowish-brown seed heads. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond margins, and along slow-moving streams. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes.

Sparganium × probatovae plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
