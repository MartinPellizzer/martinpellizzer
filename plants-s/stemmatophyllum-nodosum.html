Stemmatophyllum nodosum

Stemmatophyllum nodosum, commonly known as the nodular stemmatophyllum, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its branching, ribbon-like stems.

It possesses small, clustered leaves that are often mistaken for algae due to its appearance. The plant is notable for its ability to form dense mats on the bottom of freshwater bodies. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with moderate light conditions.

Its reproductive structures are concealed within its vegetative growth, making it challenging to identify without closer examination.
