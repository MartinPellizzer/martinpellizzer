Solanum stenopetalum

Solanum stenopetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in spring. It grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
