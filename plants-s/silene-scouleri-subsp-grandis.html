Silene scouleri subsp. grandis

Silene scouleri subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the Pacific mountainflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
