Selaginella hartii

Selaginella hartii, commonly known as the Hart's spikemoss, is a vascular fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and dense clusters of needle-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Selaginella hartii is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
