Symplocos subsecunda

Symplocos subsecunda, commonly known as the small-fruited wax tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that turn red or orange when mature.

Symplocos subsecunda is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
