Sineoperculum

Sineoperculum, commonly known as the ghost shrimp plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble the appearance of ghostly shrimps, hence its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and prefers moderate to low light conditions. Its roots are often submerged, and it can be cultivated in aquariums or ponds.

Sineoperculum plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nitrates and providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
