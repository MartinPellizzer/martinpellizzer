Saussurea chingiana

Saussurea chingiana, commonly known as the Chinese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clustered, small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
