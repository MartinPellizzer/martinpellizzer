Senecio muelleri

Senecio muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Senecio muelleri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
