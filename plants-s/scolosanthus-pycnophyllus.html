Scolosanthus pycnophyllus

Scolosanthus pycnophyllus, commonly known as the dense-leaved scolosanthus, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Scolosanthus pycnophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
