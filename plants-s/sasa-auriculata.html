Sasa auriculata

Sasa auriculata, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or auriculate bamboo, is a clump-forming evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its narrow, arching leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Sasa auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its dense, upright growth habit.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is relatively low maintenance once established.
