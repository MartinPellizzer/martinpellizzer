Solanum nigrum var. aspergilliflorum

Solanum nigrum var. aspergilliflorum, commonly known as black nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade family with distinctive botanical features.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to black as it matures. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, gardens, and along roadsides.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
