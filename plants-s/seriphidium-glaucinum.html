Seriphidium glaucinum

Seriphidium glaucinum, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Seriphidium glaucinum is also known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
