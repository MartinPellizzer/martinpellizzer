Sonchus pinnatus subsp. canariensis

Sonchus pinnatus subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
