Smilax elegans subsp. microphylla

Smilax elegans subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved arrowwood, is a member of the Smilacaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This subspecies is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
