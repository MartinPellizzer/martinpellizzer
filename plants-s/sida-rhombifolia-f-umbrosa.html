Sida rhombifolia f. umbrosa

Sida rhombifolia f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy mallow, is a variant of the common mallow species.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves and produces small, pale purple flowers. This plant typically grows in shaded or moist environments, such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture.

The plant is known for its adaptability to low-light conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
