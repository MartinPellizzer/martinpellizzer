Senecio macedonicus var. pinnatilobatus

Senecio macedonicus var. pinnatilobatus, commonly known as the Macedonian senecio, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
