Swertia racemosa

Swertia racemosa, commonly known as Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or mountainous terrain, and thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Swertia racemosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
