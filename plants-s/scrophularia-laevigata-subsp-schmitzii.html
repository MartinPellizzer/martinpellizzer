Scrophularia laevigata subsp. schmitzii

Scrophularia laevigata subsp.

schmitzii, commonly known as Schmitz's smooth betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth or slightly hairy. Its flowers are arranged in spikes and are usually pink or purple, with two lips.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and sometimes medicinal uses.
