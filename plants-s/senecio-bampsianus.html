Senecio bampsianus

Senecio bampsianus, commonly known as the Bampsian ragwort, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
