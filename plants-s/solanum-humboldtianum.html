Solanum humboldtianum

Solanum humboldtianum, commonly known as the Humboldt's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum humboldtianum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to thrive in mountainous regions.
