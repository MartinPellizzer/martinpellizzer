Sonchus freynianus

Sonchus freynianus, commonly known as the Freyn's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are usually hairy and lobed, with a distinctive pale green color.

It produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, aiding in wind dispersal.
