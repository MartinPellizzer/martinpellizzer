Silene echinata

Silene echinata, commonly known as the prickly evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Silene echinata thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.
