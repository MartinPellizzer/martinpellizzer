Sapium biglandulosum f. angustifolium

Sapium biglandulosum f. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved Chinese broom.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, followed by inflated, three-lobed seed pods. Native to eastern Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
