Spiraea × racemosa

Spiraea × racemosa, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading clusters of white to pink flowers that appear in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with slender, arching branches that give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall.

Spiraea × racemosa thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
