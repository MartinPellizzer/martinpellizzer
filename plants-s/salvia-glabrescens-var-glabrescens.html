Salvia glabrescens var. glabrescens

Salvia glabrescens var. glabrescens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native gardens and pollinator habitats.
