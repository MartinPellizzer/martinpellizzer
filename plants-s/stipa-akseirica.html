Stipa akseirica

Stipa akseirica, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Stipa akseirica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
