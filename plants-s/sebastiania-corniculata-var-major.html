Sebastiania corniculata var. major

Sebastiania corniculata var. major, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that persist through winter, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in warm, humid climates.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract birds and its use in traditional medicine.
