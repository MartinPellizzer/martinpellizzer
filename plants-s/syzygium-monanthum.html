Syzygium monanthum

Syzygium monanthum, commonly known as the single-flowered apple tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Syzygium monanthum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
