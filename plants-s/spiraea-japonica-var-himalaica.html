Spiraea japonica var. himalaica

Spiraea japonica var. himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is characterized by its finely cut, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy floral display and graceful form.
