Sida rhizomatosa

Sida rhizomatosa, commonly known as slenderleaf cotton or kudzu, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate or climbing growth habit and features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fibrous roots are extensive and contribute to its ability to spread vegetatively.

Sida rhizomatosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
