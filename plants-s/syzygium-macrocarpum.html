Syzygium macrocarpum

Syzygium macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pinkish flowers. The tree produces large, woody pods containing several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in diverse tropical environments.
