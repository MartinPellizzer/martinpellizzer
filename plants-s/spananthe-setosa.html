Spananthe setosa

Spananthe setosa, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the surrounding soil.

Spananthe setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
