Sesamum indicum var. subdentatum

Sesamum indicum var. subdentatum, commonly known as the serrated sesame, is a variant of the sesame plant characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is cultivated for its oil-rich seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces elongated pods containing numerous seeds.

The botanical features of this variety make it well-suited for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
