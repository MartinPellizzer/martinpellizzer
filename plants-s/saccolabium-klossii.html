Saccolabium klossii

Saccolabium klossii, commonly known as the Kloss's saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and yellow. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in humid, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
