Sorindeia biringo

Sorindeia biringo, commonly known as the red-flowered sorindeia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
