Schraderia acetabulosa

Schraderia acetabulosa, commonly known as the bladderleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access groundwater in dry soils.

Schraderia acetabulosa is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it thrives with minimal water and full sun exposure.
