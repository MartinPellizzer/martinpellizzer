Senecio atlanticus

Senecio atlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Senecio atlanticus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
