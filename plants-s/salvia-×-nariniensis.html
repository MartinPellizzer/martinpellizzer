Salvia × nariniensis

Salvia × nariniensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
