Sesamum microcarpum

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Ghana. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are the primary economic product of the species. The seeds are rich in oil and are used in both culinary and industrial applications.
