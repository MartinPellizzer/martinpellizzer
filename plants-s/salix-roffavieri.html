Salix roffavieri

Salix roffavieri, commonly known as the Roffavier's willow, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, often with a bluish-green hue on the upper surface.

Salix roffavieri is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.
