Silene hifacensis

Silene hifacensis, commonly known as the Hifacensis catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.
