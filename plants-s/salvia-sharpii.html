Salvia sharpii

Salvia sharpii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia sharpii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
