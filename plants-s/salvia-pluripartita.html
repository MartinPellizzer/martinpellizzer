Salvia pluripartita

Salvia pluripartita, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in moist soil.

Salvia pluripartita thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
