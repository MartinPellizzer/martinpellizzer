Sedum album var. pallens

Sedum album var. pallens, commonly known as white stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is a perennial species that is drought-tolerant and easy to care for.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers.
