Setaria glauca var. elongata

Setaria glauca var. elongata, commonly known as slender green millet, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet conditions make it an important species in certain ecological contexts.

However, it can also be considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its invasive potential.
