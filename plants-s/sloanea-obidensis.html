Sloanea obidensis

Sloanea obidensis, commonly known as the Obidense Sloanea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Sloanea obidensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
