Syzygium umbilicatum

Syzygium umbilicatum, commonly known as the star fruit or carambola, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed, yellow-orange fruit. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface.

The fruit develops from a five-lobed flower and is known for its tangy, acidic flavor when ripe.
