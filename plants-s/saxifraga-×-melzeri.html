Saxifraga × melzeri

Saxifraga × melzeri, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Saxifraga × melzeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Its hybrid origin results from crosses between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga myrtilloides.
