Senecio pseudoarnica

Senecio pseudoarnica, commonly known as the blueweed or false arnica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.
