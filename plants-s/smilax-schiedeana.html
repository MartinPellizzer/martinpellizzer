Smilax schiedeana

Smilax schiedeana, commonly known as the Mexican arrowroot or yellow smilax, is a climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Smilax schiedeana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
