Sapium biglandulosum var. aubletianum

Sapium biglandulosum var. aubletianum, commonly known as the Aublet's stinkwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by three-winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
