Sinapis heterophylla

Sinapis heterophylla, commonly known as the variable mustard, is an annual herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, which can range from oval to heart-shaped, and its small, yellow flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Sinapis heterophylla is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
