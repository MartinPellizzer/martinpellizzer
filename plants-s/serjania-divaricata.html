Serjania divaricata

Serjania divaricata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or seringueira, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
