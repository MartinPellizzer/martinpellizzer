Sorbus ochracea

Sorbus ochracea, commonly known as the yellow rowan, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange berries, which are a key feature of the plant. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and can be found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
