Setaria pallide-fusca f. penicillata

Setaria pallide-fusca f. penicillata, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to various regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Botanically, it is noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in ecological succession.
