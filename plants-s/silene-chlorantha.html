Silene chlorantha

Silene chlorantha, commonly known as the yellow catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Silene chlorantha is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
