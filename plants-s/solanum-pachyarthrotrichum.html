Solanum pachyarthrotrichum

Solanum pachyarthrotrichum, commonly known as the thick-armed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. It is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats due to its adaptability.

The species is of interest to botanists for its unique morphology and potential ecological role.
