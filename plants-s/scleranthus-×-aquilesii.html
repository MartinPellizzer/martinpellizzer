Scleranthus × aquilesii

Scleranthus × aquilesii, commonly known as the hybrid meadow thorn, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its erect, sparsely hairy stems and small, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

S. × aquilesii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
