Synaedrys lanceifolia

Synaedrys lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf synaedrys, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
