Schima remotiserrata

Schima remotiserrata, commonly known as the Chinese teak or mountain teak, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by woody, egg-shaped capsules containing seeds. Schima remotiserrata is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional construction and furniture-making.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
