Satureja caroliniana

Satureja caroliniana, commonly known as carolina mountain tea, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a fragrant, mint-like aroma.

Satureja caroliniana is often found in woodland and rocky habitats, and it is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
