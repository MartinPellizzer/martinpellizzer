Scrophularia birmanica

Scrophularia birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple in color.

Scrophularia birmanica is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
