Sesbania laevigata

Sesbania laevigata, commonly known as the velvetleaf sesbania, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces long, slender pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.

However, it can also become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
