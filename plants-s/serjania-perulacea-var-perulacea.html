Serjania perulacea var. perulacea

Serjania perulacea var. perulacea, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
