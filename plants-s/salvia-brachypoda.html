Salvia brachypoda

Salvia brachypoda, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
