Solanum caulorhizum

Solanum caulorhizum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments.

Solanum caulorhizum belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
