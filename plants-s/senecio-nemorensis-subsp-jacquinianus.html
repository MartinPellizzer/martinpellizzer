Senecio nemorensis subsp. jacquinianus

Senecio nemorensis subsp.

jacquinianus, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cascading clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species in its native range.
