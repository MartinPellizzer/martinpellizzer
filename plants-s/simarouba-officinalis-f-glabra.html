Simarouba officinalis f. glabra

Simarouba officinalis f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-skin soursop, is a member of the family Meliaceae and is native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers followed by large, spiny fruits that contain a white, pulpy aril surrounding the seeds. Its leaves and bark are used in traditional medicine for their purported antipyretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
