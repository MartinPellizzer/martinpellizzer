Salvia verbenaca var. clandestina

Salvia verbenaca var. clandestina, commonly known as the secret sage, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the sage plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically a pale purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often sought after by horticulturists and botanists.
