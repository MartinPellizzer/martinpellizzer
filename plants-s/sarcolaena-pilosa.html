Sarcolaena pilosa

Sarcolaena pilosa, commonly known as the silky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree with opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
