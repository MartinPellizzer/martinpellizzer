Stellaria × glauciformis

Stellaria × glauciformis, commonly known as the blue star, is a hybrid flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of star-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Stellaria × glauciformis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
