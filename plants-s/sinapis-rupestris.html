Sinapis rupestris

Sinapis rupestris, commonly known as rock mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnate leaves. It is often found in dry, coastal areas and can tolerate saline conditions.

Sinapis rupestris is also used in some traditional herbal remedies.
