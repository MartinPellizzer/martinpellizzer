Sasa kurilensis f. elliptica

Sasa kurilensis f. elliptica, commonly known as the Kuril bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa genus native to the northern regions of Japan and the Russian Far East.

It is a clump-forming, rhizomatous perennial that produces slender, bamboo-like stems with narrow, lanceolate leaves. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaf shape, which distinguishes it from other Sasa species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous or forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in cool, humid climates.
