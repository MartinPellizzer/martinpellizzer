Sarcorhachis sydowii var. hirsuta

Sarcorhachis sydowii var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy leaf fern, is a variant of the genus Sarcorhachis, characterized by its distinctive hairy or pubescent leaves.

This fern is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically leathery and have a rough texture, with a unique arrangement of spore-producing structures called sori. The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for taxonomical study due to its morphological variations.

It is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest undergrowth, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
