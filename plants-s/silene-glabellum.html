Silene glabellum

Silene glabellum, commonly known as the glabrous campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its simple, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Silene glabellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
