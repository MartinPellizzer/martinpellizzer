Salvia orthostachys subsp. soatensis

Salvia orthostachys subsp.

soatensis, commonly known as the Soatan sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
