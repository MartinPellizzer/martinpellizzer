Styrax ramirezii var. micranthus

Styrax ramirezii var. micranthus, commonly known as the Mexican frangipani, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It features showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its floral display.
