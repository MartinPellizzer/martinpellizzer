Salix × laurentiana

Salix × laurentiana, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces catkins as its flowers, which are typically male and female, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

This species is native to the northern hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
