Spilanthes acmella var. oleracea

Spilanthes acmella var. oleracea, commonly known as the pepper flower or Indian mustard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual with hairy, oval-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that resemble small peppers. The plant produces small, seed-containing pods that mature to a dark brown color. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible seeds.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
