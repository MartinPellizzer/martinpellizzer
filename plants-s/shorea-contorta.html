Shorea contorta

Shorea contorta, commonly known as white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and thick, flaky bark. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy upper surface.

The flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically arranged in spikes, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.
