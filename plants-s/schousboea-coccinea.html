Schousboea coccinea

Schousboea coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
