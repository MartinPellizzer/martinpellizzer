Stanhopea oculata var. meleagris

Stanhopea oculata var. meleagris, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often pale yellow with contrasting purple and red markings, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
