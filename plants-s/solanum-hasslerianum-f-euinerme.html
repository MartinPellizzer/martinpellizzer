Solanum hasslerianum f. euinerme

Solanum hasslerianum f. euinerme, commonly known as the smooth-fruited nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves, which are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

The plant has a moderate growth habit and is often found in moist, shady environments.
