Silene cucubalus var. latifolia

Silene cucubalus var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf bladdernut, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small balloons. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late summer.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
