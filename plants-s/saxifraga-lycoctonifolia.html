Saxifraga lycoctonifolia

Saxifraga lycoctonifolia, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in cold, harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and strong winds.

Saxifraga lycoctonifolia plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for pollinators and stabilizing soil in fragile environments.
