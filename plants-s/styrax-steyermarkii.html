Styrax steyermarkii

Styrax steyermarkii, commonly known as the Mexican styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain smooth, glossy seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic resins adds to its botanical significance.
