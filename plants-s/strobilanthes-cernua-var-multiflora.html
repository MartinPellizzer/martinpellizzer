Strobilanthes cernua var. multiflora

Strobilanthes cernua var. multiflora, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, producing numerous flowers along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
