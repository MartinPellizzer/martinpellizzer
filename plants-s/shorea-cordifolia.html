Shorea cordifolia

Shorea cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces heavy, aromatic seeds enclosed in woody capsules, which are dispersed by wind. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.

Shorea cordifolia plays a crucial role in the ecological systems of tropical forests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
