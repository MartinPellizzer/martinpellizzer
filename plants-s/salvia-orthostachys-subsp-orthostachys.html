Salvia orthostachys subsp. orthostachys

Salvia orthostachys subsp.

orthostachys, commonly known as the mountain sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
