Swartzia pilulifera

Swartzia pilulifera, commonly known as the bead tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its unique, bead-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is often used in furniture and construction.

The plant also plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
