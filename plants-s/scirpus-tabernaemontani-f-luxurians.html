Scirpus tabernaemontani f. luxurians

Scirpus tabernaemontani f. luxurians, commonly known as the lush bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, cylindrical flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense stands.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
