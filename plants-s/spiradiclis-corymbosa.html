Spiradiclis corymbosa

Spiradiclis corymbosa, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

Spiradiclis corymbosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
