Seriphium vermiculatum

Seriphium vermiculatum, commonly known as the silkyleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in a dense layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Seriphium vermiculatum produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
