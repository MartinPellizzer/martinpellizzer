Sericotheca microphylla

Sericotheca microphylla, commonly known as the silky-leaved sericotheca, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, silky layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, spherical fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
