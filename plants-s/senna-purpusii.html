Senna purpusii

Senna purpusii, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

Senna purpusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
