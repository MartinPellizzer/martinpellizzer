Scilla rosea

Scilla rosea, commonly known as the rose windflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of delicate, rose-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Scilla rosea is often used in rock gardens and as a spring-flowering ornamental.
