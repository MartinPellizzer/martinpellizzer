Solanum chaucha var. surimana

Solanum chaucha var. surimana, commonly known as the Surimana nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, and it produces small, yellowish berries that contain the toxic alkaloid solasodine.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
