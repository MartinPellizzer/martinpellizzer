Scleranthus candolleanus

Scleranthus candolleanus, commonly known as the Himalayan thorned morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is an annual herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
