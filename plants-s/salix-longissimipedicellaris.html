Salix longissimipedicellaris

Salix longissimipedicellaris, commonly known as the long-stemmed willow, is a rare and specialized species of willow native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typical of willow species, containing both male and female flowers. Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its arid and semi-arid habitat, with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphological features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
