Suregada multiflora var. lamellata

Suregada multiflora var. lamellata, commonly known as the broad-leafed evergreen shrub, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. The plant typically grows in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it a popular choice for coastal landscaping.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
