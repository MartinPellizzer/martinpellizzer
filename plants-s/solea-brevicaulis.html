Solea brevicaulis

Solea brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-snouted sea snake, is a marine plant that thrives in coastal and estuarine environments.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are adapted to filter nutrients from the water, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.

Solea brevicaulis plays a vital role in stabilizing sediment and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
