Senecio uliginosus

Senecio uliginosus, commonly known as bog gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Senecio uliginosus plays an important ecological role by supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its wetland environment.
