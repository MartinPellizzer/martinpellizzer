Senecio chrysanthemoides var. sisymbriiformis

Senecio chrysanthemoides var. sisymbriiformis, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a bushy growth habit, featuring narrow, linear leaves and bright yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
