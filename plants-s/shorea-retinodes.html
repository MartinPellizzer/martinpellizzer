Shorea retinodes

Shorea retinodes, commonly known as the yellow meranti, is a tropical tree belonging to the Dipterocarpaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and Brunei. The tree is characterized by its heavy, durable wood that is highly valued in the timber industry. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Shorea retinodes typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting diverse wildlife.
