Strobilanthes baracatanensis

Strobilanthes baracatanensis, commonly known as the Bornean flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are usually orange to red in color, with tubular corollas and prominent stamens.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
