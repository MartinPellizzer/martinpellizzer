Serratula alatavica

Serratula alatavica, commonly known as the Persian sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Serratula alatavica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
