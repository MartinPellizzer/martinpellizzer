Sorbus hybrida var. thuringiaca

Sorbus hybrida var. thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
