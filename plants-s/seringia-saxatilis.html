Seringia saxatilis

Seringia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock seringia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Seringia saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
