Sesleria argentea var. elongata

Sesleria argentea var. elongata, commonly known as the silver tussock grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

The variety 'elongata' is distinguished by its elongated, grass-like leaves compared to the more compact form of the species.
