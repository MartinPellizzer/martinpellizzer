Syringa × rothomagensis var. alba

Syringa × rothomagensis var. alba, commonly known as the white mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and has dark green, ovate leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

The botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping due to its pleasant fragrance and attractive appearance.
