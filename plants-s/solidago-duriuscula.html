Solidago duriuscula

Solidago duriuscula, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, rough hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer and are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters at the ends of the stems.
