Selaginella mayerhoffii

Selaginella mayerhoffii, commonly known as the African spikemoss, is a member of the spikemoss family (Selaginellaceae) and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with whorled leaves that form a dense, cushion-like structure. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are borne on separate stems and contain spores.

Selaginella mayerhoffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
