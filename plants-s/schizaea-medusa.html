Schizaea medusa

Schizaea medusa, commonly known as the fairy moss, is a rare and unique fern-like plant belonging to the family Schizaeaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching structure and its ability to grow in shaded, moist environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called sporangium-bearing shoots, which are often found at the tips of its branching stems. Schizaea medusa is notable for its lack of true roots, instead relying on rhizomes for anchorage and nutrient absorption.

Its intricate morphology and ecological significance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
