Serjania viridissima

Serjania viridissima, commonly known as the green serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
