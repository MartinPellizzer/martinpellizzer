Syzygium brunneorameum

Syzygium brunneorameum, commonly known as the brown-branch syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red or purple berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy surface.

Syzygium brunneorameum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
