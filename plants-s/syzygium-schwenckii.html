Syzygium schwenckii

Syzygium schwenckii, commonly known as the silky oak, is a tropical rainforest tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, spherical berries. Syzygium schwenckii is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid, warm climates.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitat.
