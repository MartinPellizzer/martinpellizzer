Sebastiania corniculata var. sellowiana

Sebastiania corniculata var. sellowiana, commonly known as the Serra do Cipó or Serra do Cipó tree, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that are a source of food for various wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
