Scorzonera austriaca var. dianthoides

Scorzonera austriaca var. dianthoides, commonly known as the Austrian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
