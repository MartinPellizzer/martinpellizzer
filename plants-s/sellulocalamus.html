Sellulocalamus

Sellulocalamus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has hollow, jointed stems covered in a layer of thick, fibrous material. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Sellulocalamus is valued for its strong, lightweight fibers, which have been used in traditional construction and crafts.
