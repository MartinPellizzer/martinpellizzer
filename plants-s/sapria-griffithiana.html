Sapria griffithiana

Sapria griffithiana, commonly known as the Indian starfruit or the yellow starfruit, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive yellow, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

Sapria griffithiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
