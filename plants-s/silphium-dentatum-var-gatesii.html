Silphium dentatum var. gatesii

Silphium dentatum var. gatesii, commonly known as the Gates' toothache plant, is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, toothed leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in treating toothaches and inflammation. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
