Serapias sylvestris

Serapias sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces a flowering stem that bears several flowers arranged in a loose raceme. Serapias sylvestris thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and ecological preferences make it an important species in its native habitats.
