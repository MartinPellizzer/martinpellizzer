Salvia interrupta subsp. paui

Salvia interrupta subsp.

paui, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in whorled clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its foliage is aromatic and often has a minty scent, typical of many members of the sage family.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
