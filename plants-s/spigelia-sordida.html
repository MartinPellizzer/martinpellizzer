Spigelia sordida

Spigelia sordida, commonly known as the sordid spigelia, is a member of the Loganiaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Carolinas. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in terminal umbels and are typically red or reddish in color.

Spigelia sordida is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
