Solidago eminens

Solidago eminens, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woods. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solidago eminens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
