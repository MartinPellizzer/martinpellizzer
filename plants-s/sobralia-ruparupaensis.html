Sobralia ruparupaensis

Sobralia ruparupaensis, commonly known as the Ruparupa orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that bear numerous small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts, though it remains a relatively rare and specialized species.
