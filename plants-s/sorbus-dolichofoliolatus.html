Sorbus dolichofoliolatus

Sorbus dolichofoliolatus, commonly known as the long-leaf whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries. It is typically found in forested areas and thrives in temperate climates.

The bark of Sorbus dolichofoliolatus is often smooth and grayish, with a distinctive appearance that aids in its identification.
