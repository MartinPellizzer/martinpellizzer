Saxifraga umbellulata var. pectinata

Saxifraga umbellulata var. pectinata, commonly known as the spoon-leaved saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of its stems. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Saxifraga umbellulata var. pectinata is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it adapts well to cold and harsh conditions.
