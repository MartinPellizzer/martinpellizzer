Sterculia discolor

Sterculia discolor, commonly known as the red-flowered sterculia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are large, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
