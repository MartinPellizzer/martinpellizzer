Senecio mamancanacanus

Senecio mamancanacanus, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow ray florets and a central cluster of disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It has a compact growth habit with upright stems and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

Senecio mamancanacanus is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient choice for drought-tolerant landscapes.
