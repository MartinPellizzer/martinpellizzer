Stizolobium mutisianum

Stizolobium mutisianum, commonly known as the Andean acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets, and it produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
