Silene dioica

Silene dioica, commonly known as the common catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that grows up to 30 cm tall, featuring simple, alternate leaves and pink or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Silene dioica is noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
