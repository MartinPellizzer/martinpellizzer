Sarracenia × porphyroneura

Sarracenia × porphyroneura, commonly known as the red-tipped pitcher plant, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves that form deep, tubular pitchers used to trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green with red or orange veins, and they secrete a digestive enzyme-laden liquid to break down prey. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Sarracenia × porphyroneura is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its vibrant appearance and unique ecological adaptations.
