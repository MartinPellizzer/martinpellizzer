Sebastiania brasiliensis f. rufescens

Sebastiania brasiliensis f. rufescens, commonly known as the red-sepaled sebastiania, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.
