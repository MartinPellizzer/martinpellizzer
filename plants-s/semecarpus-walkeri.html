Semecarpus walkeri

Semecarpus walkeri, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or bael tree, is a member of the Sapindaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed. Its fruit is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils.
