Schizostachyum pallidum

Schizostachyum pallidum, commonly known as the pale bamboo or white bamboo, is a tall, clumping grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in panicles, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a grass-like texture.

Schizostachyum pallidum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional crafts and construction.
