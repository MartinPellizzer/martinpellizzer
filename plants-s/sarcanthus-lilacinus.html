Sarcanthus lilacinus

Sarcanthus lilacinus, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale purple to lilac-colored flowers. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Sarcanthus lilacinus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
