Senecio alatopetiolatus

Senecio alatopetiolatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves that are often broad and have prominent veins. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
