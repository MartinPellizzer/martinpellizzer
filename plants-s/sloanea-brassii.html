Sloanea brassii

Sloanea brassii, commonly known as the Brazilian sloanea, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves with a smooth texture and is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Sloanea brassii produces small, round fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange-red aril.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
