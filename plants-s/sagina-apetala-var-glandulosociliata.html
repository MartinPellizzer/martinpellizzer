Sagina apetala var. glandulosociliata

Sagina apetala var. glandulosociliata, commonly known as the glandular-ciliated sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The leaves are typically small, ovate, and densely arranged along the stems, contributing to its compact appearance. The flowers are usually small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters, adding a delicate ornamental quality.

This plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
