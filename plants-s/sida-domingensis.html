Sida domingensis

Sida domingensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pale yellow to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Sida domingensis is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
