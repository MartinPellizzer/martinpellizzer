Solanum castaneum

Solanum castaneum, commonly known as the purple potato or wild potato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots that are often purple in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Solanum castaneum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
