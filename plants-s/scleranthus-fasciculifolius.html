Scleranthus fasciculifolius

Scleranthus fasciculifolius, commonly known as the clustered sand-rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Scleranthus fasciculifolius is a herbaceous annual plant with a short life cycle, completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.
