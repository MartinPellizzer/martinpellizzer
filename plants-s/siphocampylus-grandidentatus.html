Siphocampylus grandidentatus

Siphocampylus grandidentatus, commonly known as the large-toothed sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust stems and large, showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with prominent, toothed margins that give it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Siphocampylus grandidentatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
