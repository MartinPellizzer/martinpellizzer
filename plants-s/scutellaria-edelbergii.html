Scutellaria edelbergii

Scutellaria edelbergii, commonly known as Edelberg's skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Scutellaria edelbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
