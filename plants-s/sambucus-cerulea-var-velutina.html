Sambucus cerulea var. velutina

Sambucus cerulea var. velutina, commonly known as red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that mature in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

The berries are toxic when raw but can be used in cooking after proper preparation.
