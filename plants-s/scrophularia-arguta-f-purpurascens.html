Scrophularia arguta f. purpurascens

Scrophularia arguta f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered dog's mercury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. Its most distinguishing characteristic is its clusters of tubular, purple to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variant is noted for its vibrant coloration and is often found in woodland habitats.
