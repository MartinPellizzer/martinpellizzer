Stipagrostis seelyae

Stipagrostis seelyae, commonly known as the Seely's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in width. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils. Stipagrostis seelyae is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology and conservation.
