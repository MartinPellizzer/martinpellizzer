Solanum tetrachondrum

Solanum tetrachondrum, commonly known as the four-ribbed nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, containing several seeds.

Solanum tetrachondrum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
