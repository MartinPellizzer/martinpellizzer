Sisymbrium mollipilum

Sisymbrium mollipilum, commonly known as the silky broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a taproot system that attaches to the roots of its host. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can be a concern for agricultural crops.
