Spilanthes americana var. ramosa

Spilanthes americana var. ramosa, commonly known as the Indian turnip or stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, pungent fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is commonly found in prairies and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
