Securidaca floribunda

Securidaca floribunda, commonly known as the African sweet pepper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
