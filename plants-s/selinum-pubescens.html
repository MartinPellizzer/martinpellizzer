Selinum pubescens

Selinum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-shaped clusters called umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Selinum pubescens is valued for its aromatic qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
