Solidago rugosa var. celtidifolia

Solidago rugosa var. celtidifolia, commonly known as the rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and clustered, yellowish to golden-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
