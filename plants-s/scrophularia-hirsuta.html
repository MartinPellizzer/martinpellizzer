Scrophularia hirsuta

Scrophularia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia hirsuta is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
