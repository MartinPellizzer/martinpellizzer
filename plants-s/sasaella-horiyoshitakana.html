Sasaella horiyoshitakana

Sasaella horiyoshitakana, commonly known as the Horiyoshi-takana sasa, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
