Solanum adelense

Solanum adelense, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green fruit that turns orange-red when ripe. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is edible, often used in jams, desserts, and as a vegetable in various cuisines.
