Setaria torta

Setaria torta, commonly known as curly bristlegrass, is a species of grass native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a flowering plant in the Poaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, coiled bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Setaria torta is also known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
