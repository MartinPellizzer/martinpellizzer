Scleria oligochondra

Scleria oligochondra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Scleria oligochondra thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for aquatic organisms and stabilizing wetland soils.
