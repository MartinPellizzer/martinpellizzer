Sida carreiroi

Sida carreiroi, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with alternate, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from summer to fall. Its seeds are encased in a smooth, capsule-like fruit, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Sida carreiroi is often found in agricultural fields and is considered a weed in many regions.
