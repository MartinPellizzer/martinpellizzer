Stevia sarensis var. dissiticeps

Stevia sarensis var. dissiticeps, commonly known as the "Hairy Stevia," is a species native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are typically harvested for use in herbal teas and as a sweetener alternative.

The variety is distinguished by its dense pubescence, giving it a unique appearance compared to other stevia species.
