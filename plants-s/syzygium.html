Syzygium

Syzygium, commonly known as the clove tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The most well-known species, Syzygium aromaticum, produces the spice cloves used in cooking and traditional medicine. Syzygium plants are evergreen shrubs or small trees, often featuring fragrant flowers and edible fruits.

They thrive in warm, humid climates and require well-drained soil for optimal growth.
