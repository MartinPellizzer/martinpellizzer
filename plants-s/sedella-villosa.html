Sedella villosa

Sedella villosa, commonly known as the hairy sedella, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sedella villosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
