Sclerochaetium rottboellii

Sclerochaetium rottboellii, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with two lips, typical of the Acanthaceae family. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
