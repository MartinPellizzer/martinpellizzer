Senecio saxosus

Senecio saxosus, commonly known as the rock senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its small yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio saxosus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
