Scirpus bicapitatus

Scirpus bicapitatus, commonly known as the two-headed bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces two distinct flowering heads, which give it its common name, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like. Scirpus bicapitatus thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
