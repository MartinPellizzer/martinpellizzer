Solanum dinteri

Solanum dinteri, commonly known as Dinter's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, spiny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a yellowish or orange berry that contains several seeds.

Solanum dinteri is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, showcasing features such as succulent leaves and deep root systems.
