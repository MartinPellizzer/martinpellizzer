Solanum atropurpureum var. ochrochlorum

Solanum atropurpureum var. ochrochlorum, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple or black color. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.
