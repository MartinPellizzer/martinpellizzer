Stelis neudeckeri

Stelis neudeckeri, commonly known as the Neudecker's orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks, anchoring itself with its roots while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with intricate, pleated petals. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique morphology and elegant blooms make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
