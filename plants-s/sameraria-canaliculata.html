Sameraria canaliculata

Sameraria canaliculata, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy blue to purple flowers in the summer months. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Sameraria canaliculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
