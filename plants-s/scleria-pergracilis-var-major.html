Scleria pergracilis var. major

Scleria pergracilis var. major, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a flowering spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a compact, upright growth habit.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
