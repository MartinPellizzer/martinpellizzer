Syringa pinetorum

Syringa pinetorum, commonly known as the pine bluet, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a dense, upright growth habit. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and turn yellow in the fall.

Syringa pinetorum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
