Streptocarpus rhodesianus subsp. grandiflorus

Streptocarpus rhodesianus subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the Rhodesian streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant typically produces clusters of flowers at the base of the stems, and its blooming period occurs during the warmer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements have made it a favored ornamental plant among gardeners.
