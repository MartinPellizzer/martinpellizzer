Steudnera colocasioides

Steudnera colocasioides, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright yellow spadices surrounded by waxy, green bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Steudnera colocasioides is also valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisines.
