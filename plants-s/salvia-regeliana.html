Salvia regeliana

Salvia regeliana, commonly known as "Regel's sage," is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in whorled clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically velvety and dark green, with a slightly serrated edge.

Salvia regeliana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
