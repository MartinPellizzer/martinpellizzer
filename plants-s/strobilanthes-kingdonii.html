Strobilanthes kingdonii

Strobilanthes kingdonii, commonly known as the Christmas flower, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear vibrant purple to red bracts. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, often around December, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are short-lived but attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Strobilanthes kingdonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
