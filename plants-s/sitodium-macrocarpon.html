Sitodium macrocarpon

Sitodium macrocarpon, commonly known as the large-fruited sitodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features erect, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the production of large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The fruit is a dry capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
