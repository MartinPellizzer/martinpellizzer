Sesleria vaginalis

Sesleria vaginalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It has fibrous roots and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Sesleria vaginalis is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
