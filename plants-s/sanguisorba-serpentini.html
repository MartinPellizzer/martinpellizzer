Sanguisorba serpentini

Sanguisorba serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's blood plant, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, red-tinged flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its rugged aesthetic.

Sanguisorba serpentini is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
