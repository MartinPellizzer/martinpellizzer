Salvia alexandri

Salvia alexandri, commonly known as Alexandrian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia alexandri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
