Schoenus erythrosiphon

Schoenus erythrosiphon, commonly known as the red-siphon grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
