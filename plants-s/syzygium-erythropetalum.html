Syzygium erythropetalum

Syzygium erythropetalum, commonly known as the red-petaled syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, with a moderate to large size.

Syzygium erythropetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
