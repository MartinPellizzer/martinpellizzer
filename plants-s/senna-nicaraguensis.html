Senna nicaraguensis

Senna nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaragua senna, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senna nicaraguensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
