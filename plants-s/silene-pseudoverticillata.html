Silene pseudoverticillata

Silene pseudoverticillata, commonly known as the false whorled catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, whorled arrangement of leaves. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Silene pseudoverticillata is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
