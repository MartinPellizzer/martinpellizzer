Spatalla thunbergii

Spatalla thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks.

Spatalla thunbergii is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
