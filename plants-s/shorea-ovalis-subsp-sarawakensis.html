Shorea ovalis subsp. sarawakensis

Shorea ovalis subsp.

sarawakensis, commonly known as Sarawak ironwood, is a valuable tropical tree native to Borneo. It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly resistant to decay.

The plant typically grows in lowland dipterocarp forests and can reach heights of up to 50 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
