Scleria sumatrensis var. pubescens

Scleria sumatrensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and hairy foliage, which give it its varietal name. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
