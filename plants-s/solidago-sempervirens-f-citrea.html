Solidago sempervirens f. citrea

Solidago sempervirens f. citrea, commonly known as the golden thread, is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces showy, yellow, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
