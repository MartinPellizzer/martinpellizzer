Syzygium rotundifolium

Syzygium rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf apple or *Syzygium cumini*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round, dark purple fruits that are edible and used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in forested areas, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
