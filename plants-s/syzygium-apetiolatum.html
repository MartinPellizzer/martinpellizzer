Syzygium apetiolatum

Syzygium apetiolatum, commonly known as the small-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits resembling small tamarinds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruit in culinary applications.

The tree prefers well-drained soil and a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
