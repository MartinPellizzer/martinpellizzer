Syzygium mauritianum

Syzygium mauritianum, commonly known as the Mauritius apple or the mountain apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. The fruit has a sweet, tart flavor and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and montane regions.
