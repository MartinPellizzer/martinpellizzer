Sparganium × longifolium

Sparganium × longifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr rush, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that bear spikelets adorned with bristly, spiky structures. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a reddish hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into the distinctive burr-like fruits.

Sparganium × longifolium plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
