Scutellaria pusilla

Scutellaria pusilla, commonly known as small skullcap, is a small annual herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with two lips, and it produces small, nut-like seeds.

Scutellaria pusilla is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
