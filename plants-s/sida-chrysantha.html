Sida chrysantha

Sida chrysantha, commonly known as yellow mallow, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Sida chrysantha is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to thrive in sunny, dry environments.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
