Senecio barrosianus

Senecio barrosianus, commonly known as the Barrosian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio barrosianus is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
