Sorbus atrimontis

Sorbus atrimontis, commonly known as the Montane Whitebeam, is a species of rowan tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
