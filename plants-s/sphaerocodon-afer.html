Sphaerocodon afer

Sphaerocodon afer, commonly known as the African globe thistle, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded, fleshy leaves that form a dense, globe-like structure, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in arid environments.
