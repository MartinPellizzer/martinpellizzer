Sorbus aria subsp. carpinifolia

Sorbus aria subsp.

carpinifolia, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, persistent berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance, supporting a variety of wildlife.
