Solanum nigrum proles ochroleucum

Solanum nigrum proles ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-fruited nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade plant characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow fruits.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in disturbed soils, often found in fields and along roadsides. Its flowers are small and usually purple or white, and the leaves are ovate with a rough texture.

While it is not as widely cultivated as other nightshades, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
