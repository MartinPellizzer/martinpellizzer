Sida althaeifolia

Sida althaeifolia, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Sida althaeifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
