Sarcocornia natalensis

Sarcocornia natalensis, commonly known as Natal glassworts, is a halophytic plant native to coastal regions of South Africa.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in estuaries and tidal flats. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. Its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it reproduces through wind pollination.

Sarcocornia natalensis plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
