Scaevola taccada

Scaevola taccada, commonly known as the coastal heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, with showy, overlapping petals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Scaevola taccada is well adapted to salty air and can thrive in coastal environments where other plants may struggle.
