Syzygium chloranthum

Syzygium chloranthum, commonly known as the green-flowered apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Syzygium chloranthum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
