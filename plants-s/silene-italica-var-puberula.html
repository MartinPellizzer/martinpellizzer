Silene italica var. puberula

Silene italica var. puberula, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with hairy stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Silene italica var. puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
