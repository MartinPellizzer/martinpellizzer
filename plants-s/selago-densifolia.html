Selago densifolia

Selago densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf selago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in dense, overlapping leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in clusters during the cooler months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Selago densifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
