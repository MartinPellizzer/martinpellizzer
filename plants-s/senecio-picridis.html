Senecio picridis

Senecio picridis, commonly known as the violet groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a distinctive violet hue.

Senecio picridis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
