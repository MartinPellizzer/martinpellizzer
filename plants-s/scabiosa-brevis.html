Scabiosa brevis

Scabiosa brevis, commonly known as the lesser scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive central boss. Its foliage consists of narrow, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette.

Scabiosa brevis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
