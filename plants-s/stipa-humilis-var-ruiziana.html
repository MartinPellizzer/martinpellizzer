Stipa humilis var. ruiziana

Stipa humilis var. ruiziana, commonly known as the Ruiz's bluegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to South America.

It features slender, blue-green blades that are typically less than 30 cm in height, with a compact growth habit. The plant produces feathery, silver-gray inflorescences that are characteristic of the Stipa genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
