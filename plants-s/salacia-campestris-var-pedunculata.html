Salacia campestris var. pedunculata

Salacia campestris var. pedunculata, commonly known as the creeping salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
