Saussurea depressa

Saussurea depressa, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including the Himalayas and Tibet.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often at elevations above 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cold, arid conditions. Its leaves are usually grayish-green and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The small yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
