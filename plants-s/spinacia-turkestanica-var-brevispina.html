Spinacia turkestanica var. brevispina

Spinacia turkestanica var. brevispina, commonly known as the short-spined spinach, is a lesser-known relative of the common spinach.

It is a leafy annual plant native to Central Asia, characterized by its compact growth habit and small, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves, though it is less commonly consumed than its more popular relatives.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant taxonomy and agriculture.
