Sarothamnus scoparius subsp. cantabricus

Sarothamnus scoparius subsp.

cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian thyme, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Its botanical characteristics include compact growth, hairy stems, and a distinctive, spicy fragrance.
