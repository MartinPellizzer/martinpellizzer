Saracha potosina

Saracha potosina, commonly known as the Potosi bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Saracha potosina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
