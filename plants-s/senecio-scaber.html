Senecio scaber

Senecio scaber, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio scaber is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
