Sobralia pumila

Sobralia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf sobralia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features dark green, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
