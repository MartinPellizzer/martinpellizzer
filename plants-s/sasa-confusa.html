Sasa confusa

Sasa confusa, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Sasa confusa is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to form dense, attractive foliage.
