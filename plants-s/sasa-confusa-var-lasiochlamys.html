Sasa confusa var. lasiochlamys

Sasa confusa var. lasiochlamys, commonly known as the silky sasa, is a perennial bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often covered with a fine, silky layer of hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it forms dense clumps. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its aesthetic qualities in garden settings.
