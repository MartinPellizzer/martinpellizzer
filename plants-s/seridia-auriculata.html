Seridia auriculata

Seridia auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy yellow flowers with white ray florets bloom in late winter to early spring.

Seridia auriculata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
