Silene inflata var. laurifolia

Silene inflata var. laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel-leaved bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly waxy texture, and the plant produces inflated, bladder-like seed pods that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
