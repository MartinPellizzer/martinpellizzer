Stevia philippiana

Stevia philippiana, commonly known as Philippine stevia, is a perennial herb native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used as alternatives to sugar. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it is also valued for its potential health benefits.
