Solanum sanctae-catharinae

Solanum sanctae-catharinae, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the rainforest canopy. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, while its flowers are predominantly white with a hint of purple.

Solanum sanctae-catharinae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
