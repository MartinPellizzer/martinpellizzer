Stipa joannis var. puberula

Stipa joannis var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces airy, feathery seed heads that are often adorned with silky bristles, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rocky or arid landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
