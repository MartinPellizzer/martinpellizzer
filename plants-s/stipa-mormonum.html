Stipa mormonum

Stipa mormonum, commonly known as the Mormon grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is known for its resilience to drought and cold temperatures.

Stipa mormonum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
