Spiraea camtschatica var. occidentalis

Spiraea camtschatica var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is dark green and turns yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
