Stylidium macranthum

Stylidium macranthum, commonly known as the showy sun-orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its unique structure includes a short, erect stem with a single, large, spurred flower.

Stylidium macranthum is also notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, which involves a trigger mechanism that releases pollen when touched.
