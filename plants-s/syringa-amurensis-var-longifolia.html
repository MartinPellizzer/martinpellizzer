Syringa amurensis var. longifolia

Syringa amurensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the Amur honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, elongated leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The variety longifolia is distinguished by its longer, more slender leaves compared to other varieties of Syringa amurensis.
