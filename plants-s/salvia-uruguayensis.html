Salvia uruguayensis

Salvia uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay sage, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Uruguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salvia uruguayensis is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
