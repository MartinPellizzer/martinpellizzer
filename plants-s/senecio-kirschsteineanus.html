Senecio kirschsteineanus

Senecio kirschsteineanus, commonly known as the Kirschstein's groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its slow growth and attractive appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
