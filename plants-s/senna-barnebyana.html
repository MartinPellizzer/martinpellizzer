Senna barnebyana

Senna barnebyana, commonly known as the Mexican senna, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
