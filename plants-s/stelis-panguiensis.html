Stelis panguiensis

Stelis panguiensis, commonly known as the Guianan stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with alternate, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in inflorescences, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow.

Stelis panguiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
