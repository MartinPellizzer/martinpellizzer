Spathoglottis pubescens

Spathoglottis pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spathoglottis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Spathoglottis pubescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
