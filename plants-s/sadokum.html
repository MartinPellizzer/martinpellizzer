Sadokum

Sadokum, commonly known as the "false indigo," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Sadokum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
