Stachys menthoides

Stachys menthoides, commonly known as woodland betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a mint-like fragrance, while its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple.

Stachys menthoides is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.
