Senecio rodriguezii

Senecio rodriguezii, commonly known as Rodriguez's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
