Stanhopea ecornuta

Stanhopea ecornuta, commonly known as the hornless stanhopea, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that often display a mix of yellow, orange, and red hues. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its pseudobulbs are relatively short and cylindrical, supporting its foliage and flowers.

Stanhopea ecornuta is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration.
