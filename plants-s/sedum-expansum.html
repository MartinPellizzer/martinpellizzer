Sedum expansum

Sedum expansum, commonly known as the broadleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, broad leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum expansum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
