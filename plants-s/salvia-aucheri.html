Salvia aucheri

Salvia aucheri, commonly known as Armenian germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features upright, branching stems covered in grayish-white, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Salvia aucheri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
