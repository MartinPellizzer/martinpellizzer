Scleromitrion gracilipes

Scleromitrion gracilipes, commonly known as the slender fringed orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a unique morphology, with hairy stems and intricate, fringed petals that contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a threatened species requiring conservation efforts.
