Stenogyne cinerea

Stenogyne cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf heliotrope, is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands, characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as mesic forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Stenogyne cinerea is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional Hawaiian practices.
