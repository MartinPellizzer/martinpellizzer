Sarcopodium fargesii

Sarcopodium fargesii, commonly known as the Himalayan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine pubescence.

Sarcopodium fargesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate flowers and resilience in cooler climates.
