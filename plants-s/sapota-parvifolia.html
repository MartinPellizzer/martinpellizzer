Sapota parvifolia

Sapota parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are typically small in size. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by fruits that are generally round and have a smooth, leathery skin. The fruit contains a single seed and is known for its sweet, creamy pulp, which is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in humid climates and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
