Senecio jarae

Senecio jarae, commonly known as the Jara's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Senecio jarae features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is of interest for its potential ecological and pharmacological properties.
