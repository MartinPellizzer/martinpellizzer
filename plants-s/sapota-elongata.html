Sapota elongata

Sapota elongata, commonly known as the long sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or reddish when ripe. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a sweet, aromatic scent that attracts pollinators.

The fruit's pulp is sweet and starchy, often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
