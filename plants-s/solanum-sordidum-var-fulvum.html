Solanum sordidum var. fulvum

Solanum sordidum var. fulvum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in dry, disturbed habitats and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally avoided by livestock and humans.
