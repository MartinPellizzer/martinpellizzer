Saxifraga pubescens subvar. secundiflora

Saxifraga pubescens subvar. secundiflora, commonly known as the creeping saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense, fleshy leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Saxifraga pubescens subvar. secundiflora is often found in tundra and mountain meadow habitats.
