Sempervivum chassagnei

Sempervivum chassagnei, commonly known as the Chassagne's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces striking clusters of pink or red flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sempervivum chassagnei is a hardy perennial that can survive in USDA hardiness zones 4 to 8.
