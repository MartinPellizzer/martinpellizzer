Salvia smyrnaea

Salvia smyrnaea, commonly known as the Smyrna sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which are typically tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Salvia smyrnaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
