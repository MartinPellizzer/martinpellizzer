Serratula squarrosa

Serratula squarrosa, commonly known as the rough serratula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Serratula squarrosa is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
