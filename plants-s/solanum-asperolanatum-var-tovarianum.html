Solanum asperolanatum var. tovarianum

Solanum asperolanatum var. tovarianum, commonly known as the Tovarian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in clusters. The plant produces berries that are initially green and turn red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
