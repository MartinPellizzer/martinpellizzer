Serratula coronaria

Serratula coronaria, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a compact, upright growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Serratula coronaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
