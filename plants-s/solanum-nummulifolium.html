Solanum nummulifolium

Solanum nummulifolium, commonly known as the coinleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped, waxy leaves that resemble coins, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing black, glossy seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum nummulifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
