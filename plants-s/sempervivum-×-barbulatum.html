Sempervivum × barbulatum

Sempervivum × barbulatum, commonly known as the stonecrop hybrid, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, or orange during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
