Stachys emersonii

Stachys emersonii, commonly known as Emerson's hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
