Salix hegetschweileri

Salix hegetschweileri, commonly known as the Hegetschweiler willow, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Salix.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically pollinated by wind.

This willow species is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
