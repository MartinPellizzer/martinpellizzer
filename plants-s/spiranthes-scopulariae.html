Spiranthes scopulariae

Spiranthes scopulariae, commonly known as the prairie ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence of pale to dark purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wet meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species in native plant communities.
