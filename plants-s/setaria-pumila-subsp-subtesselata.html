Setaria pumila subsp. subtesselata

Setaria pumila subsp.

subtesselata, commonly known as the dwarf foxtail, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and floodplains. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when young.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
