Sida hislopii

Sida hislopii, commonly known as desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. The plant features woolly, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its showy, yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sida hislopii is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
