Sclerostylis atalantioides

Sclerostylis atalantioides, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with tubular corollas. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
