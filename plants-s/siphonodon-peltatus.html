Siphonodon peltatus

Siphonodon peltatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is often found growing in moist, shady environments such as forest understories.

Siphonodon peltatus is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
