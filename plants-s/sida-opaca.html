Sida opaca

Sida opaca, commonly known as seaside sida, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or saline soils, often found in dunes and tidal marshes. The plant has small, pale green leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white to pink flowers. Its fibrous stems and leaves are adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Sida opaca plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
