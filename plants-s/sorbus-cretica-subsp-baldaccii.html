Sorbus cretica subsp. baldaccii

Sorbus cretica subsp.

baldaccii, commonly known as the Balkan whitebeams, is a shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The leaves are typically oval to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The fruit is a small, aggregate berry that turns red when mature.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
