Salix phanerodictya

Salix phanerodictya, commonly known as the Kashmir willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically covered with a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is well adapted to cold and high-altitude environments. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often found growing in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and mountain slopes.

Salix phanerodictya plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
