Sorbus rossica

Sorbus rossica, commonly known as the Russian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the genus Sorbus, which includes many species of whitebeams and rowans. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, aggregate fruits that ripen in the fall. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
