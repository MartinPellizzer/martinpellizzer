Scilla raewskiana

Scilla raewskiana, commonly known as the Armenian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is often found in rocky or woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in gardens.

Scilla raewskiana is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable conditions.
