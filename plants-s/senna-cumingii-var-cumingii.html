Senna cumingii var. cumingii

Senna cumingii var. cumingii, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feather-like leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine due to their medicinal properties.
