Senecio myriocephalus

Senecio myriocephalus, commonly known as the giant senecio, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features a tall, branched stem with broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in arid climates.
