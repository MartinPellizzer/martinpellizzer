Sagittaria plantaginifolia

Sagittaria plantaginifolia, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with long, slender stems that often extend into the water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands.

Sagittaria plantaginifolia is often used in water gardens and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to help purify water.
