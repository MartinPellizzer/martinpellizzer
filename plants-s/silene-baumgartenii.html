Silene baumgartenii

Silene baumgartenii, commonly known as the Baumann's campion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant produces striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. Silene baumgartenii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It is also of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems.
