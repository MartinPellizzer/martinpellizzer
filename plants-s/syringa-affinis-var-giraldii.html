Syringa affinis var. giraldii

Syringa affinis var. giraldii, commonly known as the meadow sweet, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Syringa affinis var. giraldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
