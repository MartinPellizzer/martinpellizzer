Spatholobus maingayi

Spatholobus maingayi, commonly known as the Maingay's climbing legume, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Spatholobus maingayi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, though its botanical study remains limited compared to more widely researched species.
