Senecio octoglossus

Senecio octoglossus, commonly known as the eight-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, terminal heads, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
