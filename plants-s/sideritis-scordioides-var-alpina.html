Sideritis scordioides var. alpina

Sideritis scordioides var. alpina, commonly known as alpine sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Sideritis species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
