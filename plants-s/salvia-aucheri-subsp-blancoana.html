Salvia aucheri subsp. blancoana

Salvia aucheri subsp.

blancoana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers.

The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long blooming period.
