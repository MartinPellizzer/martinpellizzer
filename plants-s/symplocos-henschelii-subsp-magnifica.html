Symplocos henschelii subsp. magnifica

Symplocos henschelii subsp.

magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent evergreen huckleberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
