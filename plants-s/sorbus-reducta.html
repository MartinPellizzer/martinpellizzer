Sorbus reducta

Sorbus reducta, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rounded, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries in autumn. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, turning red in the fall.

Sorbus reducta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
