Saurauia pedunculata var. leucocarpa

Saurauia pedunculata var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited saurauia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are initially green and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
