Symplocos macrophylla var. glabrifolia

Symplocos macrophylla var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually smooth on both sides. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
