Sacciolepis indica var. oryzetorum

Sacciolepis indica var. oryzetorum, commonly known as the ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Its adaptability makes it a common ornamental and forage plant in various climates.
