Sophora houghiana

Sophora houghiana, commonly known as the Hough's Sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration in its native range.
