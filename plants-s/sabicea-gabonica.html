Sabicea gabonica

Sabicea gabonica, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers. Its fruit is a slender, twisted pod that contains several seeds.

Sabicea gabonica is often found in moist woodland and forest margins, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
