Solanum pensile

Solanum pensile, commonly known as the hanging nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems that often hang from supports, producing small, bell-shaped flowers. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic compounds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens with caution.
