Saxifraga rigoi subsp. maroccana

Saxifraga rigoi subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky and well-drained soils.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
