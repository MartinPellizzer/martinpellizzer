Solanum violifolium var. asarifolium

Solanum violifolium var. asarifolium, commonly known as the asarifolium nightshade, is a perennial herb native to parts of Central and South America.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, shiny berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
