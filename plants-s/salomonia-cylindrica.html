Salomonia cylindrica

Salomonia cylindrica, commonly known as the cylinder bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower buds that resemble small bells, which open to reveal tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces vibrant blue or purple flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
