Symphyotrichum lateriflorum

Symphyotrichum lateriflorum, commonly known as the lateflower heath aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along stream banks.
