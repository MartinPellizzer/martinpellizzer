Sterculia khasiana

Sterculia khasiana, commonly known as Khasi Sterculia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. The bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing effects.
