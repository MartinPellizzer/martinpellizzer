Stelis retroversa

Stelis retroversa, commonly known as the upside-down orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique growth habit, with its flowers facing downward, which is a rare trait among orchids. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on forest floors or in rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Stelis retroversa is valued for its unusual appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
