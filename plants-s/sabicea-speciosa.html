Sabicea speciosa

Sabicea speciosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety pink flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a glossy appearance, while the plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
