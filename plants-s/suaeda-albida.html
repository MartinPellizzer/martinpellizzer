Suaeda albida

Suaeda albida, commonly known as the white seepweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to arid and saline environments, particularly in the Mediterranean region and parts of the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. Suaeda albida is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
