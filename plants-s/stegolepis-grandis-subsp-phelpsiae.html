Stegolepis grandis subsp. phelpsiae

Stegolepis grandis subsp.

phelpsiae, commonly known as Phelps' giant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments.

The plant features tall, triangular stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it is often found in dense stands.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
