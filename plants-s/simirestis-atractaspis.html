Simirestis atractaspis

Simirestis atractaspis, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa, characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink, red, or orange in color. Its milky sap, which is toxic, serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The plant is also known for its role in supporting certain species of butterflies and moths.
