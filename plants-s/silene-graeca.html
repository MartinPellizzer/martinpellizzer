Silene graeca

Silene graeca, commonly known as the Greek catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Silene graeca is an annual or biennial plant, depending on the climate, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
