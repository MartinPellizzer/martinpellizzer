Stachys drummondii

Stachys drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Stachys drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
