Salicornia macrostachya

Salicornia macrostachya, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, green flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne on branched inflorescences. Its leaves are scale-like and reduced, which helps minimize water loss in its arid habitat.

Salicornia macrostachya is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils, making it an important component of these ecosystems.
