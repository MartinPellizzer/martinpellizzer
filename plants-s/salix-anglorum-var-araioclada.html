Salix anglorum var. araioclada

Salix anglorum var. araioclada, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a variant of the willow species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
