Sidalcea triloba

Sidalcea triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often trifoliate leaves. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into three lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Sidalcea triloba thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
