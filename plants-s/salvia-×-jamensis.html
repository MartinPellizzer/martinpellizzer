Salvia × jamensis

Salvia × jamensis, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance and mild mint-like fragrance.

Salvia × jamensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
