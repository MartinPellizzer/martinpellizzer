Sideritis leucantha var. tomentosa

Sideritis leucantha var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly white sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine for their calming and antispasmodic properties.

The varietal name "tomentosa" refers to the dense, woolly texture of its leaves.
