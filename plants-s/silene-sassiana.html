Silene sassiana

Silene sassiana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Silene sassiana is an annual or biennial species with narrow, linear leaves.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to plant biodiversity.
