Sempervivum minus

Sempervivum minus, commonly known as the miniature houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features compact, rosette-shaped foliage composed of fleshy, dark green leaves with a slightly woolly texture. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or container planting.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including cold and arid environments.
