Stapelia gemmiflora var. hircosa

Stapelia gemmiflora var. hircosa, commonly known as the horned stapelia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, greenish-yellow stems that bear a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, meaty flowers that are typically orange to red in color, often with a strong scent resembling rotting meat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is known for its ability to form offsets, making it relatively easy to propagate.
