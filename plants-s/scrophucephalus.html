Scrophucephalus

Scrophucephalus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet or purple flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scrophucephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
