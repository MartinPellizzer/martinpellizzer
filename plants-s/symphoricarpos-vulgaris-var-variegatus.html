Symphoricarpos vulgaris var. variegatus

Symphoricarpos vulgaris var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, waxy berries that appear in the fall, providing food for birds and other wildlife. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy texture and often exhibit variegation, featuring white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
