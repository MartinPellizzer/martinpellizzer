Sarothamnus arboreus var. intermedius

Sarothamnus arboreus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate tree saxifrage, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

This variety is closely related to other members of the genus Sarothamnus, sharing similar ecological and botanical traits.
