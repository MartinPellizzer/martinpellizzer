Symplocos adenophylla var. montana

Symplocos adenophylla var. montana, commonly known as mountain waxflower, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 3,000 meters. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that matures to a dark red or black color.
