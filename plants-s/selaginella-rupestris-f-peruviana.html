Selaginella rupestris f. peruviana

Selaginella rupestris f. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian rock spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its creeping stems and delicate, needle-like leaves. This plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It possesses a unique ability to survive in harsh conditions due to its desiccation-tolerant spores.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
