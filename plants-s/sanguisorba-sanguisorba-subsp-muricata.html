Sanguisorba sanguisorba subsp. muricata

Sanguisorba sanguisorba subsp.

muricata, commonly known as the spiked sorrel, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spiky red bracts that resemble small thorns.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a reddish tinge.

Sanguisorba muricata is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
