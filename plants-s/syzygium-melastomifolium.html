Syzygium melastomifolium

Syzygium melastomifolium, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rainforests.

Syzygium melastomifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
