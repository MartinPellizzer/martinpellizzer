Sorbus torminalis f. obtusa

Sorbus torminalis f. obtusa, commonly known as the blunt-leaf whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a compact, slow-growing species with a rounded form and dense foliage. The leaves are typically broader and more rounded than those of the typical species, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are favored by birds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil conditions.
