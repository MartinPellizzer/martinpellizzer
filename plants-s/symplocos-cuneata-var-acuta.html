Symplocos cuneata var. acuta

Symplocos cuneata var. acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark purple berries that are both ornamental and edible. The leaves are elliptic to ovate, with a pointed tip and a slightly serrated margin. This plant thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and wildlife habitats.
