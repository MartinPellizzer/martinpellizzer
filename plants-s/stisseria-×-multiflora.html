Stisseria × multiflora

Stisseria × multiflora, commonly known as the hybrid stisseria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red upon ripening. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
