Specularia lindheimeri

Specularia lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple calyx. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
