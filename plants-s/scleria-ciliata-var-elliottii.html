Scleria ciliata var. elliottii

Scleria ciliata var. elliottii, commonly known as the Elliott's bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with fine, silky hairs along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually tall and cylindrical, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
