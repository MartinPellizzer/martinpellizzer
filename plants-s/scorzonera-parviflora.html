Scorzonera parviflora

Scorzonera parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust taproot system, which is often edible and has been historically used in traditional medicine. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scorzonera parviflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
