Syringa × nanceiana

Syringa × nanceiana, commonly known as the Korean lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Korea.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 4–8 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Syringa × nanceiana is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.
