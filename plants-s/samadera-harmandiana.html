Samadera harmandiana

Samadera harmandiana, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or scrubland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic wood.
