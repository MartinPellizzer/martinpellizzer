Senecio linariifolius var. angustissima

Senecio linariifolius var. angustissima, commonly known as narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
