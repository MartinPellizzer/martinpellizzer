Senecio latipes

Senecio latipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

Senecio latipes is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
