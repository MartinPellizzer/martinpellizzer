Stenotus armerioides var. gramineus

Stenotus armerioides var. gramineus, commonly known as the grassy stenotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
