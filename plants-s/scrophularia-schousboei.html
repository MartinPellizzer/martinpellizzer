Scrophularia schousboei

Scrophularia schousboei, commonly known as the Schousboe's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which are usually purple or pink, are arranged in whorls along the stem and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia schousboei is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in some species.
