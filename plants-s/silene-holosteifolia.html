Silene holosteifolia

Silene holosteifolia, commonly known as the redstem catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Silene holosteifolia plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
