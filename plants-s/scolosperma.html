Scolosperma

Scolosperma, commonly known as the African water spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, thriving in humid climates. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their mild, slightly sweet flavor.

Scolosperma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
