Salicaria thymifolia

Salicaria thymifolia, commonly known as meadow sweet, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge along the midrib. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
