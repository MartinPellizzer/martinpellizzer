Solanum paucidens

Solanum paucidens, commonly known as the small-fruited nightshade, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a yellow or orange color.

Like other members of the Solanaceae family, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
