Salix alba var. subintegra

Salix alba var. subintegra, commonly known as the white willow with integrated bark, is a variant of the white willow characterized by its unique bark structure.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia, typically reaching heights of 10 to 20 meters. The bark is smooth and often has a fibrous, integrated texture, distinguishing it from other willow species. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a bright green color during the growing season.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ecological benefits.
