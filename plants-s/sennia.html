Sennia

Sennia, commonly known as the blue senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Sennia species are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and cultural contexts.
