Stachys glechomoides

Stachys glechomoides, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stachys glechomoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
