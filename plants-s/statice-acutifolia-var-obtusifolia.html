Statice acutifolia var. obtusifolia

Statice acutifolia var. obtusifolia, commonly known as Sea Lavender, is a perennial flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features narrow, silver-blue foliage that is often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its salt tolerance allows it to flourish in harsh, maritime environments.
