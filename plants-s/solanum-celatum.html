Solanum celatum

Solanum celatum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of star-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Solanum celatum is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
