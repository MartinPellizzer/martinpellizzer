Salix × pygmaea

Salix × pygmaea, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and ponds.

Salix × pygmaea is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and riparian restoration projects.
