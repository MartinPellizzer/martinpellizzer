Salacia luebbertii

Salacia luebbertii, commonly known as the Luebbert's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy surface.

Salacia luebbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
