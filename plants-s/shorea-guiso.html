Shorea guiso

Shorea guiso, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has large, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Shorea guiso plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
