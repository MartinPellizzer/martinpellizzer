Silene subnutans

Silene subnutans, commonly known as the nodding campion, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is known for its delicate, nodding appearance.

Silene subnutans is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
