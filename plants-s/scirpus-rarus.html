Scirpus rarus

Scirpus rarus, commonly known as the rare bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and cylindrical, spike-like flower heads. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Scirpus rarus has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
