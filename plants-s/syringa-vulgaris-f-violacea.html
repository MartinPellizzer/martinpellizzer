Syringa vulgaris f. violacea

Syringa vulgaris f. violacea, commonly known as the purple lilac, is a cultivar of the common lilac that exhibits distinctive violet-blue flower colors.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its fragrant, clustered blossoms that appear in early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to landscape designs.
