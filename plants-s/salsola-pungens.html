Salsola pungens

Salsola pungens, commonly known as prickly gooseweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. It is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a common species in desert regions.

Salsola pungens is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
