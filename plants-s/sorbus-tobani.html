Sorbus tobani

Sorbus tobani, commonly known as the Turkish whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkans and Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
