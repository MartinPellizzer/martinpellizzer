Stelis ospinae

Stelis ospinae, commonly known as the Ospina's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stelis ospinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
