Senecio saliens

Senecio saliens, commonly known as the bouncing bishop's hat, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that resemble small hats. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds in a unique, helmet-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio saliens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
