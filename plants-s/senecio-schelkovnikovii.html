Senecio schelkovnikovii

Senecio schelkovnikovii, commonly known as Schelkovnikov's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
