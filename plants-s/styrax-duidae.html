Styrax duidae

Styrax duidae, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white or yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. Its sap, known as frankincense, has been historically used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens.
