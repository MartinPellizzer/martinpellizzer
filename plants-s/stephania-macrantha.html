Stephania macrantha

Stephania macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered stephania, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are typically followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
