Selwynia laurina

Selwynia laurina, commonly known as the golden bell heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Selwynia laurina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
