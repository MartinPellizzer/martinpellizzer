Sisyrinchium myrioflorum

Sisyrinchium myrioflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

Sisyrinchium myrioflorum is characterized by its showy, star-shaped blooms and is often used in native plant gardens.
