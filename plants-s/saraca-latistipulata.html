Saraca latistipulata

Saraca latistipulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has pinnate leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
