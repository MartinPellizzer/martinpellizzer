Sabina virginiana var. australis

Sabina virginiana var. australis, commonly known as the Australian juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a range of environmental conditions, including coastal and inland regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various climates.
