Schisandra neglecta

Schisandra neglecta, commonly known as the Chinese five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, red berries that contain a mix of aromatic compounds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces yellow flowers in the spring.

Schisandra neglecta is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
