Saccharum punctatum

Saccharum punctatum, commonly known as the punctate bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often marked with distinct, punctate patterns. The plant typically grows in wetland environments and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

Saccharum punctatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
