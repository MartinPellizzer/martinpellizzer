Scutellaria bushii

Scutellaria bushii, commonly known as Bush's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Scutellaria bushii is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in some regions.
