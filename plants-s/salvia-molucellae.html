Salvia molucellae

Salvia molucellae, commonly known as the Moluccan sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and showy, two-lipped purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice among horticulturists.

Salvia molucellae is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
