Struthiola myrsinites

Struthiola myrsinites, commonly known as the mountain tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Struthiola myrsinites is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
