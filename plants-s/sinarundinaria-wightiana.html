Sinarundinaria wightiana

Sinarundinaria wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a dense, tufted cluster at the base. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are wind-pollinated.

Sinarundinaria wightiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
