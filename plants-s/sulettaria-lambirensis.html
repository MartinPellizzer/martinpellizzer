Sulettaria lambirensis

Sulettaria lambirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star grass, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
