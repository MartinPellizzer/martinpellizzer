Sowerbaea subtilis

Sowerbaea subtilis, commonly known as the giant forestiera, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the summer months, which are borne in clusters at the leaf axils. It is well adapted to shady, moist environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
