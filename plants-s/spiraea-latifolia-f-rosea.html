Spiraea latifolia f. rosea

Spiraea latifolia f. rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf spiraea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The foliage is typically dark green, with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
