Sanmartina

Sanmartina, commonly known as the Sanmartina plant, is a species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, which features a combination of lobed and serrated edges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical or subtropical forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, Sanmartina is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
