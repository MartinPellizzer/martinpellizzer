Solanum brevipilum

Solanum brevipilum, commonly known as the short-hair nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not suitable for consumption by humans or livestock.
