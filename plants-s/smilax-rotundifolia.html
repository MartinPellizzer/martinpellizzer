Smilax rotundifolia

Smilax rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the eastern United States.

It features woody stems with thorny branches and is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax rotundifolia is a hardy species that can spread aggressively through its rhizomes.
