Stelis trullifera

Stelis trullifera, commonly known as the coral-like orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of small, coral-like flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Stelis trullifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
