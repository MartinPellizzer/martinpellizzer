Syzygium viburnoides

Syzygium viburnoides, commonly known as the satinwood or satin-leaf tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. Syzygium viburnoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit includes a spreading canopy and a moderate to fast growth rate, making it suitable for landscaping in warm climates.
