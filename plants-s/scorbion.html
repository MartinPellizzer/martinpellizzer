Scorbion

Scorbion, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a toxic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white or greenish flowers. The plant contains various toxic compounds, including cardiac glycosides, which can be harmful if ingested. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
