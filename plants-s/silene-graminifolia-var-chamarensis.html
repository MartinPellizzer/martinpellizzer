Silene graminifolia var. chamarensis

Silene graminifolia var. chamarensis, commonly known as the Chamarensis Bladder Campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
