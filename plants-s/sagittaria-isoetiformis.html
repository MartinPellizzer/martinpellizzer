Sagittaria isoetiformis

Sagittaria isoetiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and shallow waters in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the mud. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Sagittaria isoetiformis thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
