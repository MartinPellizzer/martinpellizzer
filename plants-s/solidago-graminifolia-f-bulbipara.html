Solidago graminifolia f. bulbipara

Solidago graminifolia f. bulbipara, commonly known as the bulbous goldenrod, is a unique variant of the goldenrod genus characterized by its bulbous base.

This plant is native to the northeastern United States and thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, giving it a distinct appearance compared to other goldenrod species.

Its bulbous root system allows it to store water and nutrients, enhancing its resilience in varying environmental conditions.
