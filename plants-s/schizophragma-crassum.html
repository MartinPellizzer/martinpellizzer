Schizophragma crassum

Schizophragma crassum, commonly known as the broadleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a coarsely toothed margin and a hairy underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden use as a decorative vine.

Schizophragma crassum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or walls.
