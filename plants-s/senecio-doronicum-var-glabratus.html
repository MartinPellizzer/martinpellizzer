Senecio doronicum var. glabratus

Senecio doronicum var. glabratus, commonly known as the smoothleaf groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are typically smooth and waxy in texture. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its drought tolerance makes it a suitable plant for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
