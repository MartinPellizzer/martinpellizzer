Senecio spiculosus

Senecio spiculosus, commonly known as the spiked groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Senecio spiculosus is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
