Smeathmannia media

Smeathmannia media, commonly known as the Sydney golden wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has compound leaves with multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Smeathmannia media is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
