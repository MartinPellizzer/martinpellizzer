Satureja juliana f. barbulata

Satureja juliana f. barbulata, commonly known as the woolly mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are densely covered with short, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
