Salix thunbergiana subsp. melanostachys

Salix thunbergiana subsp.

melanostachys, commonly known as black-stalked willow, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its slender, often blackish stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
