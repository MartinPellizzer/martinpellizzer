Stevia soratensis

Stevia soratensis, commonly known as sweetleaf or stevia, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. Stevia soratensis is often cultivated as a natural sweetener and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are typically harvested and processed to extract the sweet compounds for commercial use.
