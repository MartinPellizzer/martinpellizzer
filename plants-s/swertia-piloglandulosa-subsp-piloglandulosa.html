Swertia piloglandulosa subsp. piloglandulosa

Swertia piloglandulosa subsp.

piloglandulosa, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan region. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often covered with glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Tibetan medicine for its purported health benefits.
