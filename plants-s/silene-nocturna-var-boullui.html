Silene nocturna var. boullui

Silene nocturna var. boullui, commonly known as the evening silene, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are tubular and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
