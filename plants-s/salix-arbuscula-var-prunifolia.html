Salix arbuscula var. prunifolia

Salix arbuscula var. prunifolia, commonly known as the pink willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and distinctive pinkish-white flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in riparian habitats.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing stream banks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
