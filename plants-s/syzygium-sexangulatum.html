Syzygium sexangulatum

Syzygium sexangulatum, commonly known as the six-angled star fruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the distinctive star-shaped yellow fruit. The fruit has a sour-sweet flavor and is commonly used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
