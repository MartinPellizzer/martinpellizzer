Stenophyllus curassavicus

Stenophyllus curassavicus, commonly known as the Caribbean starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
