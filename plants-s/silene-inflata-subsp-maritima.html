Silene inflata subsp. maritima

Silene inflata subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small, balloon-like structures.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, where it thrives in salty, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the top of the stems. Silene inflata subsp.

maritima is an important species for coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
