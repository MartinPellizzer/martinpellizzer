Syzygium congestum

Syzygium congestum, commonly known as the Australian blueberry or the myrtle-leaf syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its small, edible berries that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, aromatic surface.

The flowers are white or pink, with five petals, and the plant is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.
