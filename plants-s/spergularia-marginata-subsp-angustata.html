Spergularia marginata subsp. angustata

Spergularia marginata subsp.

angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
