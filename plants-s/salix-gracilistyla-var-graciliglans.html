Salix gracilistyla var. graciliglans

Salix gracilistyla var. graciliglans, commonly known as the slender willow, is a variant of the slender willow species native to parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and flexible branches. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wetlands. Its flowers are catkins, which are typically wind-pollinated, and it produces small, round fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
