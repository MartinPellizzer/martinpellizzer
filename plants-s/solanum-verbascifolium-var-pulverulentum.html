Solanum verbascifolium var. pulverulentum

Solanum verbascifolium var. pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty blight nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It produces berries that range in color from green to red, containing toxic alkaloids.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
