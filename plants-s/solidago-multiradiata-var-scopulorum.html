Solidago multiradiata var. scopulorum

Solidago multiradiata var. scopulorum, commonly known as the rocky mountain goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in native ecosystems.
