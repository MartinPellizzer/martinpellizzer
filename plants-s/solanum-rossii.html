Solanum rossii

Solanum rossii, commonly known as the Ross's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Solanum rossii belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
