Syzygium guineense subsp. macrocarpum

Syzygium guineense subsp.

macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited sand apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height.

The plant produces fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits with a sweet and tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, often with a glossy surface.

The subspecies is valued for its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
