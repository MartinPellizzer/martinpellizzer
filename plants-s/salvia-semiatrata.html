Salvia semiatrata

Salvia semiatrata, commonly known as the pale blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces striking spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
