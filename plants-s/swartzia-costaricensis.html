Swartzia costaricensis

Swartzia costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican Swartzia, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in shades of white or pink, and it produces woody pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
