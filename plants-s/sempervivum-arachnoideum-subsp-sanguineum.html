Sempervivum arachnoideum subsp. sanguineum

Sempervivum arachnoideum subsp.

sanguineum, commonly known as the Crimson Hare's Foot, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with a distinctive red or pink tinge, especially under sunlight.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

This subspecies is popular among rock gardeners for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
