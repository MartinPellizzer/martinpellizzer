Sideritis hirsuta var. hirta

Sideritis hirsuta var. hirta, commonly known as hairy beard vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of short, stiff hairs.

Sideritis hirsuta var. hirta is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
