Schefflera obovata

Schefflera obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like clusters of large, glossy, ovate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Schefflera obovata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
