Salsola tragus subsp. pontica

Salsola tragus subsp.

pontica, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils.

The plant has succulent, spiny stems and small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Salsola tragus subsp.

pontica is adapted to harsh conditions and is often considered an invasive species in many regions.
