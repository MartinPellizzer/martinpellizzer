Scutellaria albituba

Scutellaria albituba, commonly known as the white tube scullion, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scutellaria albituba is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
