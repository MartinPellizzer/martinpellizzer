Selaginella stenophylla

Selaginella stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-bearing structures called strobila. The plant thrives in rocky soils and can often be found in crevices or on slopes. It is a perennial plant with a short, creeping stem that produces erect, cone-like structures for spore dispersal.

Selaginella stenophylla is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
