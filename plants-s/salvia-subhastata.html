Salvia subhastata

Salvia subhastata, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and gray-green, fuzzy leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
