Sceptridium daucifolium var. japonicum

Sceptridium daucifolium var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the tip of a flowering stem. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley.
