Statice caesia var. major

Statice caesia var. major, commonly known as the sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

Statice caesia var. major is often used in floral arrangements and is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
