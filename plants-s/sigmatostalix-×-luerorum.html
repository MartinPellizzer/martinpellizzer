Sigmatostalix × luerorum

Sigmatostalix × luerorum, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated edges.

Sigmatostalix × luerorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows and wildlife habitats.
