Satureja calamintha var. villosissima

Satureja calamintha var. villosissima, commonly known as woolly woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, white hairs, which give it a woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its pleasant aroma and medicinal properties.
