Sinocalamus macroculmis

Sinocalamus macroculmis, commonly known as the broad-awned bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. Its culms are used in traditional crafts and construction, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience to environmental stress.
