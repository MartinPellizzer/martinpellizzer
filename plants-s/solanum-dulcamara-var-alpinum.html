Solanum dulcamara var. alpinum

Solanum dulcamara var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine bindweed, is a variant of the common woody nightshade.

It is a perennial climbing plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale purple. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its berries, which are toxic, are typically red and appear in clusters.

The species is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
