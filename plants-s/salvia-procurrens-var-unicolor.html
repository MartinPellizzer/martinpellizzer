Salvia procurrens var. unicolor

Salvia procurrens var. unicolor, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
