Salix hastata var. angustifolia

Salix hastata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant has slender, angular stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and catkin-like, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
