Sideritis candicans var. multiflora

Sideritis candicans var. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
