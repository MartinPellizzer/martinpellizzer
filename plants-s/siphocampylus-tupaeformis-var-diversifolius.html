Siphocampylus tupaeformis var. diversifolius

Siphocampylus tupaeformis var. diversifolius, commonly known as the Desert Willow, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
