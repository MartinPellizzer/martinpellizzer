Silene dysmica

Silene dysmica, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Silene dysmica is adapted to survive in sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low precipitation.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
