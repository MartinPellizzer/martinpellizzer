Sorghum timorense var. villosissimum

Sorghum timorense var. villosissimum, commonly known as the Timor sorghum, is a perennial grass native to the island of Timor.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its potential in forage and as a source of traditional food in local communities.
