Solanum goniocaulon

Solanum goniocaulon, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to avoid ingestion of any part of the plant.
