Senaea coerulea

Senaea coerulea, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, blue flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the tips of its stems. Senaea coerulea typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
