Solanum zollingeri var. multiflorum

Solanum zollingeri var. multiflorum, commonly known as the Zollinger's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange, are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.

This species is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
