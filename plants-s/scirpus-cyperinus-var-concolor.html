Scirpus cyperinus var. concolor

Scirpus cyperinus var. concolor, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomes help in soil erosion control and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
