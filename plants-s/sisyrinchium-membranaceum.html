Sisyrinchium membranaceum

Sisyrinchium membranaceum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy blue to violet flowers with a white center. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
