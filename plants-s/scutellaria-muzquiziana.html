Scutellaria muzquiziana

Scutellaria muzquiziana, commonly known as the Muzquiz skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Scutellaria muzquiziana is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystem.
