Syringidium

Syringidium, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like appearance and is native to wetland environments in Asia and Africa. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Syringidium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rhizomes contain compounds that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.
