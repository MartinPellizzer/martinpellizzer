Syzygium phillyreoides

Syzygium phillyreoides, commonly known as the satinwood or red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and pale in color.

Syzygium phillyreoides is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
