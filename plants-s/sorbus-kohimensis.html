Sorbus kohimensis

Sorbus kohimensis, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by clusters of small, red berries that are edible for birds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
