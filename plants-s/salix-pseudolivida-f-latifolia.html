Salix pseudolivida f. latifolia

Salix pseudolivida f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved weeping willow, is a rare and ornamental variant of the willow family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it blooms in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its unique foliage and graceful appearance, it is valued in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
