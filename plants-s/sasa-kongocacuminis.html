Sasa kongocacuminis

Sasa kongocacuminis, commonly known as the African bamboo, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense growth habit. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Sasa kongocacuminis is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of thatching material.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
