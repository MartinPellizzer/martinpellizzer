Spiraea daurica

Spiraea daurica, commonly known as the Daurian spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches and dark green, serrated leaves. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Spiraea daurica is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
