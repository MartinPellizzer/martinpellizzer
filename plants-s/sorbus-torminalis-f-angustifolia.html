Sorbus torminalis f. angustifolia

Sorbus torminalis f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically darker in color compared to the standard species. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, persistent berries. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
