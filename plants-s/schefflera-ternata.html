Schefflera ternata

Schefflera ternata, commonly known as the three-lobed umbrella plant, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like arrangement of leaflets. The leaves are typically glossy and have a trifoliate structure, with three distinct lobes. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, red berries.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
