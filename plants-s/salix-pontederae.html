Salix pontederae

Salix pontederae, commonly known as the Pontederia willow, is a species of willow native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, flexible stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in catkins, and it is primarily pollinated by wind.

Salix pontederae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
