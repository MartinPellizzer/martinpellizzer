Sclerocarya caffra var. dentata

Sclerocarya caffra var. dentata, commonly known as the African hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, nut-like fruits enclosed in a woody husk. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and potential economic uses, including as a source of food and traditional medicine.
