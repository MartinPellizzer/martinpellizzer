Solanum puberulum

Solanum puberulum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Solanum puberulum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It contains alkaloids and has been used in traditional medicine, though it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
