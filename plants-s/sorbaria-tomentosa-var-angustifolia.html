Sorbaria tomentosa var. angustifolia

Sorbaria tomentosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by oval, red-brown fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, with a grayish-white underside. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in colder climates.
