Salacia uregaensis var. aurantiaca

Salacia uregaensis var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
