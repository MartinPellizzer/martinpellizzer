Spondias indica

Spondias indica, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red fruits. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Spondias indica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
