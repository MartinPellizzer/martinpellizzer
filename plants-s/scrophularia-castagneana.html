Scrophularia castagneana

Scrophularia castagneana, commonly known as the Castagnean figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and have serrated edges. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, producing showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

Scrophularia castagneana is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
