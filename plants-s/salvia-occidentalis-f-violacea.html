Salvia occidentalis f. violacea

Salvia occidentalis f. violacea, commonly known as the Western sage violet.

It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, characterized by its violet-blue flower spikes and gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
