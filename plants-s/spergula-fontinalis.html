Spergula fontinalis

Spergula fontinalis, commonly known as spring sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to moist, calcareous habitats.

It typically grows in wet meadows, stream banks, and other waterlogged areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. It is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
