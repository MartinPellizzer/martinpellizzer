Stemodia

Stemodia, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lance-shaped. The flowers are typically yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Stemodia species are native to various parts of the world, including Europe, Asia, and Africa.

They are often found in grassy areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
