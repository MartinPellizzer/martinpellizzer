Sasa matsudae

Sasa matsudae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or matsudae bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant forms tall, slender culms that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

Sasa matsudae is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
