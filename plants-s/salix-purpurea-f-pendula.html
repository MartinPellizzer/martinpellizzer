Salix purpurea f. pendula

Salix purpurea f. pendula, commonly known as weeping willow, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its cascading branches that create a unique, flowing appearance.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green with a silvery underside, enhancing its visual appeal. It thrives in moist soils and is often planted near water features or in areas with high groundwater.

The weeping willow is also noted for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its role in erosion control.
