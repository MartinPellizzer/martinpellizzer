Senecio lapidosa

Senecio lapidosa, commonly known as the stonecress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the stems are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Senecio lapidosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
