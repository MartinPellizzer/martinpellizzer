Saxifraga × inclinata

Saxifraga × inclinata, commonly known as the leaning saxifrage, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water.

Saxifraga × inclinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic features.
