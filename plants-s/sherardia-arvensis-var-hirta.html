Sherardia arvensis var. hirta

Sherardia arvensis var. hirta, commonly known as hairy field gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
