Scaphochlamys hasta

Scaphochlamys hasta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scaphochlamys, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruit.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically alternate and trifoliate, with each leaflet being elongated and lance-shaped. The flowers are small and usually arranged in clusters, featuring a unique structure with a narrow, elongated calyx.

The plant is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
