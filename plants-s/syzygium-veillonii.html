Syzygium veillonii

Syzygium veillonii, commonly known as the Veillon's tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, textured surface.

Syzygium veillonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
