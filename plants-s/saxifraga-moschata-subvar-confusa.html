Saxifraga moschata subvar. confusa

Saxifraga moschata subvar. confusa, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer, often accompanied by a subtle, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are usually basal, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered with a fine layer of white or grayish pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
