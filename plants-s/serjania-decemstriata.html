Serjania decemstriata

Serjania decemstriata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or Brazilian cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjania and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain bird species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its hard, dark wood in traditional crafts.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
