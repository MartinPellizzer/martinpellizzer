Stelis mucronata

Stelis mucronata, commonly known as the acorn orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Stelis mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
