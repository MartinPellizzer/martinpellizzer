Salix × krasanii

Salix × krasanii, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a hybrid between Salix alba and Salix schwerinii, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters, with slender, flexible branches that often trail or arch downward. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and they turn yellow in the autumn.

The flowers are catkin-like, appearing in early spring, and the plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its graceful appearance.
