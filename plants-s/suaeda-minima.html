Suaeda minima

Suaeda minima, commonly known as the dwarf seepweed, is a small annual plant native to arid and saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in salty soils and can tolerate extreme drought conditions. It typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and inland saline soils.

Suaeda minima plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
