Sasa buddhistica

Sasa buddhistica, commonly known as the Buddhist bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Sasa buddhistica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional landscaping.

Its growth habits and appearance make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and natural habitats.
