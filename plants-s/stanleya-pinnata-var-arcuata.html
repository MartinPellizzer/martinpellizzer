Stanleya pinnata var. arcuata

Stanleya pinnata var. arcuata, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

This variety is closely related to other penstemon species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
