Soda grandis

Soda grandis, commonly known as the giant soda tree, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. This plant is characterized by its large, waxy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

The plant is also notable for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
