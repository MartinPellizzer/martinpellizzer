Solanum aureum

Solanum aureum, commonly known as the golden nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruits are berries that mature to a striking golden-yellow color, often containing several seeds.

Solanum aureum is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
