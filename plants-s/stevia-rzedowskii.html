Stevia rzedowskii

Stevia rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its unique properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and potential medicinal purposes.
