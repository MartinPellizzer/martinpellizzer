Sideroxylon lanuginosum subsp. albicans

Sideroxylon lanuginosum subsp.

albicans, commonly known as the white silky tamarind, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is characterized by its grayish-white, silky-haired stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is native to parts of Africa and is often found in dry, sandy soils.
