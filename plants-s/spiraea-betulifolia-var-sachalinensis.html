Spiraea betulifolia var. sachalinensis

Spiraea betulifolia var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sachalin spirea, is a shrub native to the eastern Asian region, including parts of Japan and Russia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a serrated margin and a bluish-green hue on the upper surface. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
