Scutellaria brevibracteata subsp. subvelutina

Scutellaria brevibracteata subsp.

subvelutina, commonly known as the velvet bracted skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and pine flatwoods.

Its unique floral structure and habitat preferences make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
