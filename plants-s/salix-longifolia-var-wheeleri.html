Salix longifolia var. wheeleri

Salix longifolia var. wheeleri, commonly known as Wheeler's willow, is a rare variant of the longleaf willow.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to specific regions of the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and elongated, narrow leaves. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
