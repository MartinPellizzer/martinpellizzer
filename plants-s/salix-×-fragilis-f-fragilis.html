Salix × fragilis f. fragilis

Salix × fragilis f. fragilis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate wet conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping and riparian restoration.
