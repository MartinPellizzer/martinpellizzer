Salviacanthus preussii

Salviacanthus preussii, commonly known as the African sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
