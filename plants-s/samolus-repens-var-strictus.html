Samolus repens var. strictus

Samolus repens var. strictus, commonly known as the upright samolus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, spreading stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive blue-green color.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
