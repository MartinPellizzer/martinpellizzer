Syzygium parvulum

Syzygium parvulum, commonly known as the small-fruited clove.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, dark red berries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Syzygium parvulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
