Specklinia syringodes

Specklinia syringodes, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
