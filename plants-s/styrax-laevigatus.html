Styrax laevigatus

Styrax laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces a fragrant, resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and perfumery. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
