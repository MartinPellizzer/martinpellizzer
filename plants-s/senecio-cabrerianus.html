Senecio cabrerianus

Senecio cabrerianus, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is usually gray-green, and it produces showy white to pale blue flowers in early spring.

Senecio cabrerianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
