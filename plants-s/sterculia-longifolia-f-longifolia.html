Sterculia longifolia f. longifolia

Sterculia longifolia f. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf sterculia, is a variant of the sterculia tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
