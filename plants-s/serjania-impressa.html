Serjania impressa

Serjania impressa, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue berries that appear in clusters, attracting various birds and insects. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often described as having a leathery texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Serjania impressa is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
