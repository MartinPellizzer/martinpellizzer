Senecio caucasicus

Senecio caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio caucasicus is often found in alpine or subalpine zones due to its preference for cooler climates.
