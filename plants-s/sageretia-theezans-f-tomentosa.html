Sageretia theezans f. tomentosa

Sageretia theezans f. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sageretia, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, often arranged in clusters, and are followed by small, oval berries.
