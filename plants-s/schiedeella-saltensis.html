Schiedeella saltensis

Schiedeella saltensis, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to saline and drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources.

Schiedeella saltensis plays a crucial role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife in its ecosystem.
