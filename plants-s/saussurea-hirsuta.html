Saussurea hirsuta

Saussurea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has feathery, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Saussurea hirsuta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
