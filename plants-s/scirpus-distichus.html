Scirpus distichus

Scirpus distichus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spikelets along the upper part of the stem. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help in stabilizing the soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus distichus is also valued for its ability to filter water and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
