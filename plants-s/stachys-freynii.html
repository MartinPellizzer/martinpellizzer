Stachys freynii

Stachys freynii, commonly known as Frey's hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Stachys freynii is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
