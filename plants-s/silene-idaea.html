Silene idaea

Silene idaea, commonly known as the sea pink, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its pink or purple flowers and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, linear leaves that help it thrive in salty, windy environments. Silene idaea is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
