Serapias pumila

Serapias pumila, commonly known as the dwarf sand orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces few, small, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

Serapias pumila is a rare and protected species, valued for its unique botanical features and ecological significance.
