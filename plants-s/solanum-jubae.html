Solanum jubae

Solanum jubae, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America but is now cultivated in various regions around the world. The plant produces small, orange, lantern-shaped fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a hairy texture.

Solanum jubae is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
