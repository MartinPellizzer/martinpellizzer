Symplocos silverstonei

Symplocos silverstonei, commonly known as the silverleaf inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are silver-gray in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Symplocos silverstonei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
