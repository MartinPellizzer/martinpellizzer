Silene linifolia var. gracilis

Silene linifolia var. gracilis, commonly known as slender leaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variant is distinguished by its more delicate foliage compared to the typical form of Silene linifolia.
