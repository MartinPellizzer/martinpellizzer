Senecio cappa

Senecio cappa, commonly known as the Cape sundew, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and sticky, glandular hairs on its leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Senecio cappa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
