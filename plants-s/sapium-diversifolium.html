Sapium diversifolium

Sapium diversifolium, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features alternate, simple leaves that vary in shape, often being ovate or elliptic with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are toxic to humans and livestock. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

The botanical characteristics of Sapium diversifolium make it both ecologically significant and a challenge in certain ecosystems.
