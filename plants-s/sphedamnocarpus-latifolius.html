Sphedamnocarpus latifolius

Sphedamnocarpus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf sphaerocarp, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
