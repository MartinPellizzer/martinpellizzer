Silene pravitziana

Silene pravitziana, commonly known as the Pravitz's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene pravitziana is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and ecological importance in its native habitats.
