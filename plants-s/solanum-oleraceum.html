Solanum oleraceum

Solanum oleraceum, commonly known as the common groundcherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that enclose a single seed, which are often referred to as "groundcherries" or "physalis.". Solanum oleraceum is typically found in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates.

It is sometimes considered a weed but can be cultivated for its edible fruits.
