Styphelia laeta var. angustifolia

Styphelia laeta var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved starflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as pine forests and rocky slopes.
