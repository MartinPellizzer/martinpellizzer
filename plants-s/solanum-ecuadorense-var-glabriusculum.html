Solanum ecuadorense var. glabriusculum

Solanum ecuadorense var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian nightshade, is a species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.
