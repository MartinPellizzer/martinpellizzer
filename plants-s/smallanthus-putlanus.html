Smallanthus putlanus

Smallanthus putlanus, commonly known as the Andean mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet and white. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and twining.

Smallanthus putlanus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
