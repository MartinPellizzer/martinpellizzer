Smallanthus jelskii

Smallanthus jelskii, commonly known as the Andean quinoa, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is closely related to quinoa, sharing similar nutritional properties. The plant is characterized by its small, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, edible seeds that are rich in protein and minerals. It thrives in cool, arid climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions, making it a resilient crop.

Due to its nutritional value and adaptability, it has gained attention as a potential alternative to traditional grains.
