Scutellaria colpodea

Scutellaria colpodea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Scutellaria colpodea is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
