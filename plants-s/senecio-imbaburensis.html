Senecio imbaburensis

Senecio imbaburensis, commonly known as the Zimbabwean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a hairy texture.

Senecio imbaburensis is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
