Serratula flexicaulis

Serratula flexicaulis, commonly known as the flexible serrated bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Serratula flexicaulis is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
