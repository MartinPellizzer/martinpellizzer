Salvia pseudorosmarinus

Salvia pseudorosmarinus, commonly known as lemon sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a lemony scent, and its showy, tubular flowers range in color from blue to purple. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its culinary and ornamental value has led to its cultivation in many gardens worldwide.
