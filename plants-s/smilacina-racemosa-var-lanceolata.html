Smilacina racemosa var. lanceolata

Smilacina racemosa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf greenbriar, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its berries, which are typically red or yellow, are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

This species thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
