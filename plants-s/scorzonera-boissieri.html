Scorzonera boissieri

Scorzonera boissieri, commonly known as the Persian scurvygrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

Scorzonera boissieri is also noted for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
