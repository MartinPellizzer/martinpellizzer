Selaginella savatieri

Selaginella savatieri, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its clustered, needle-like leaves that form dense, compact rosettes. Selaginella savatieri reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its leaves.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
