Smilax herbacea var. inodora

Smilax herbacea var. inodora, commonly known as the fragrant smilax, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with thorns and heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
