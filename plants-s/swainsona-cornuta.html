Swainsona cornuta

Swainsona cornuta, commonly known as the horned cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pods and showy, yellow flowers with a striking orange center. The plant typically grows as an erect annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape.

Swainsona cornuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
