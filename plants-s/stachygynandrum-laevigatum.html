Stachygynandrum laevigatum

Stachygynandrum laevigatum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities. Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for cultivation in a variety of tropical and subtropical climates.
