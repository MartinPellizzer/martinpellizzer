Schefflera grayi

Schefflera grayi, commonly known as the Gray's umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of large, leathery leaves that provide shade and structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Schefflera grayi is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
