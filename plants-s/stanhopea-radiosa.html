Stanhopea radiosa

Stanhopea radiosa, commonly known as the Radiosa Stanhopea, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that feature a distinctive yellow and purple coloration with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in high humidity and moderate temperatures.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Stanhopea radiosa is often cultivated in specialized greenhouses.
