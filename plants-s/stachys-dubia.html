Stachys dubia

Stachys dubia, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of purple or pink flowers in the summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Stachys dubia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
