Sonchus jacquinii

Sonchus jacquinii, commonly known as the Mexican sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of the southern United States. The plant has hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
