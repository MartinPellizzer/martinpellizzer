Sinningia bulbosa

Sinningia bulbosa, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and rosette-forming leaves that are typically velvety and dark green. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to blue and white, often with a distinctive throat coloration. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Sinningia bulbosa is also valued for its ability to bloom multiple times during the growing season.
