Serjania adenophylla

Serjania adenophylla, commonly known as the pink serjania, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by various bird species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Serjania adenophylla plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
