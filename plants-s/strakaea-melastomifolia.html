Strakaea melastomifolia

Strakaea melastomifolia, commonly known as the Cape melastome, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
