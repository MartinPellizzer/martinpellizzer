Sinapis inflexa

Sinapis inflexa, commonly known as hoary mustard, is an annual herb native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.

Sinapis inflexa is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants.
