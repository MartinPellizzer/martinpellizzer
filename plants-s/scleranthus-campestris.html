Scleranthus campestris

Scleranthus campestris, commonly known as field thistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has upright stems with spiny leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Amaranthaceae, which includes other plants with similar structural and ecological traits.
