Scutellaria maekawae var. pubescens

Scutellaria maekawae var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
