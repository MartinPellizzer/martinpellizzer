Sideritis montana var. simplicissima

Sideritis montana var. simplicissima, commonly known as mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.
