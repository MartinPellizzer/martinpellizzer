Salvia rumicifolia

Salvia rumicifolia, commonly known as Mexican bush sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.

Salvia rumicifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
