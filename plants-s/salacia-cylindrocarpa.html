Salacia cylindrocarpa

Salacia cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the slender salacia, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, woody stems and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.
