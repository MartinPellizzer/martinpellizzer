Silene rubella var. bergiana

Silene rubella var. bergiana, commonly known as the Bergian campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene rubella var. bergiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
