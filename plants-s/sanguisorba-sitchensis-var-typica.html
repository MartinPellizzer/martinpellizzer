Sanguisorba sitchensis var. typica

Sanguisorba sitchensis var. typica, commonly known as the Sitka sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Alaska and northern British Columbia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, and its stems are often tinged with red or purple hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and rocky shores.

Sanguisorba sitchensis var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
