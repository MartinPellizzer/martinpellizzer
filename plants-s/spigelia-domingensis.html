Spigelia domingensis

Spigelia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican pipevine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful to animals and humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
