Sasa nunobikiensis

Sasa nunobikiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly bluish-green color.

Sasa nunobikiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
