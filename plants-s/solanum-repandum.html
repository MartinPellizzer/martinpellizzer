Solanum repandum

Solanum repandum, commonly known as the trailing nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in a trailing or climbing manner, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by berries that mature to a bright red color. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum repandum is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
