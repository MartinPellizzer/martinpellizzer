Senecio variifolius var. subcanescens

Senecio variifolius var. subcanescens, commonly known as the white-haired groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-white hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its foliage is typically narrow and linear, with variations in leaf shape depending on environmental conditions.
