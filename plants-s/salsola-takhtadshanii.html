Salsola takhtadshanii

Salsola takhtadshanii, commonly known as the Armenian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and its flowers are typically greenish-white in color. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in unstable environments.

Salsola takhtadshanii plays a role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
