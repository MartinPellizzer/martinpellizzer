Solanum nodiflorum var. besseri

Solanum nodiflorum var. besseri, commonly known as the Bessera nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often in coastal areas or disturbed soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale blue, followed by berries that mature to a deep red color. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
