Siphocampylus sceptrum

Siphocampylus sceptrum, commonly known as the sacred candleplant, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble candles, hence its common name. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic. Native to the mountainous regions of Central America, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its unique appearance and historical significance make it a notable ornamental and culturally important species.
