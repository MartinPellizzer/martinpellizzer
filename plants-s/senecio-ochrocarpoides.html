Senecio ochrocarpoides

Senecio ochrocarpoides, commonly known as the yellow-eyed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
