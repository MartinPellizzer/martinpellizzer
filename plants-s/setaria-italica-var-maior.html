Setaria italica var. maior

Setaria italica var. maior, commonly known as giant millet, is a variant of the millet species native to Asia.

It is an annual grass that thrives in temperate climates and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, hard seeds that are rich in carbohydrates and protein, making it a valuable food source. It is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought resistance contribute to its agricultural significance.
