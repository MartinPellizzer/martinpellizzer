Sanguisorba verrucosa proles multicaulis

Sanguisorba verrucosa proles multicaulis, commonly known as the multicaul sanguisorba, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright, branched stems that often produce multiple flowering shoots. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pinkish-red to reddish flowers in late summer. It is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
