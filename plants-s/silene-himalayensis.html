Silene himalayensis

Silene himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan campion, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Silene himalayensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
