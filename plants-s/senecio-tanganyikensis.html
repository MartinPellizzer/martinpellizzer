Senecio tanganyikensis

Senecio tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Tanganyika groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
