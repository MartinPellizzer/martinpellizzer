Scirpus latifolius

Scirpus latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its wide, sword-shaped leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that appear in the summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its extensive root system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality by filtering sediments.

Scirpus latifolius plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
