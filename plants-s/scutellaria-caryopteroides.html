Scutellaria caryopteroides

Scutellaria caryopteroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, rich soils.

Scutellaria caryopteroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
