Samyda subintegra

Samyda subintegra, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf senna, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom throughout the warm seasons. Its fruits are capsule-like structures containing several seeds.

Samyda subintegra is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
