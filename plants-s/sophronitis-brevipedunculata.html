Sophronitis brevipedunculata

Sophronitis brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, often fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth pattern, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.

Sophronitis brevipedunculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
