Stipa pulcherrima subsp. gallica

Stipa pulcherrima subsp.

gallica, commonly known as the Galician feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a graceful appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest in gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its light, airy texture.
