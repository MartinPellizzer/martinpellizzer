Serratula linearifolia

Serratula linearifolia, commonly known as the slender sow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, with a distinctive pointed edge.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
