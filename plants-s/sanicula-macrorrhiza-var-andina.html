Sanicula macrorrhiza var. andina

Sanicula macrorrhiza var. andina, commonly known as the Andean sanicle, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is distinguished by its unique floral structure and chemical composition.
