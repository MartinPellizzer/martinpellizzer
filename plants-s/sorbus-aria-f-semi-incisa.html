Sorbus aria f. semi-incisa

Sorbus aria f. semi-incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a botanical variant of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It is native to parts of Europe and thrives in temperate climates.

This variety is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
