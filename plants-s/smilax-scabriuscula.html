Smilax scabriuscula

Smilax scabriuscula, commonly known as the roughleaf catbriar, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Smilax scabriuscula is known for its aggressive growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
