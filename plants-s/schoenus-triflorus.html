Schoenus triflorus

Schoenus triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tussock-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Schoenus triflorus plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
