Syzygium klossii

Syzygium klossii, commonly known as the Kloss's apple or the Java apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that turn red when ripe. Syzygium klossii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth is typically found in humid, lowland areas with rich, well-drained soil.
