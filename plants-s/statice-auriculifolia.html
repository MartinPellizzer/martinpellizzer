Statice auriculifolia

Statice auriculifolia, commonly known as the earleaf statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements.

Statice auriculifolia is well adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.
