Saussurea conyzoides

Saussurea conyzoides, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Asia, and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and blue or purple in color.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
