Sclerocroton oblongifolius

Sclerocroton oblongifolius, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features lance-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system for water storage.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
