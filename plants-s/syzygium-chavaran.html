Syzygium chavaran

Syzygium chavaran, commonly known as the Chavaran apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Syzygium chavaran is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible fruit.
