Sison nodiflorum

Sison nodiflorum, commonly known as the hanging bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
