Satyrium triphyllum

Satyrium triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaved twayblade, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-parted leaves and a slender, branching stem. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its inconspicuous, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Satyrium triphyllum is often found growing in association with other orchids and is a notable species in ecological studies due to its specific habitat requirements.
