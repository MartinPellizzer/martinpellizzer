Solanum gayanum

Solanum gayanum, commonly known as the Sri Lankan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy stems and elliptical leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple and bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, though the plant is toxic and should not be consumed.
