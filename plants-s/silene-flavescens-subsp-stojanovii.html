Silene flavescens subsp. stojanovii

Silene flavescens subsp.

stojanovii, commonly known as the Balkan campion, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native region.
