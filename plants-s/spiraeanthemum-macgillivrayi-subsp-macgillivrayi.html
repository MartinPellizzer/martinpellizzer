Spiraeanthemum macgillivrayi subsp. macgillivrayi

Spiraeanthemum macgillivrayi subsp.

macgillivrayi, commonly known as the MacGillivray's spirea, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has dark green, elliptical leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific floral structure and geographic distribution within its native range.
