Sesbania macrantha

Sesbania macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Sesbania macrantha also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for ecological restoration projects.
