Sphaeritis paucifolia

Sphaeritis paucifolia, commonly known as the few-leaved globular plant, is a succulent species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, globular shape and small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
