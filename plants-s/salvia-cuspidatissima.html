Salvia cuspidatissima

Salvia cuspidatissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
