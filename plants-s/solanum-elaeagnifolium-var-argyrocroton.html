Solanum elaeagnifolium var. argyrocroton

Solanum elaeagnifolium var. argyrocroton, commonly known as silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elliptical, smooth, and often spiny fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The species is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
