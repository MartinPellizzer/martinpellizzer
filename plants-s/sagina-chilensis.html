Sagina chilensis

Sagina chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean snowgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features prostrate stems that are densely covered with small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or coastal regions.

Sagina chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
