Syngonanthus manikaensis

Syngonanthus manikaensis, commonly known as the Manikaensis thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Syngonanthus manikaensis plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
