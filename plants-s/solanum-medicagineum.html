Solanum medicagineum

Solanum medicagineum, commonly known as the medicinal nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically has hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic if ingested.

Despite its toxicity, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
