Scopolia aculeata

Scopolia aculeata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is known for its strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. Scopolia aculeata contains various alkaloids, making it a plant of interest in both botanical and pharmacological studies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
