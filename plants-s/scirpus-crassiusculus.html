Scirpus crassiusculus

Scirpus crassiusculus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a rosette at the base. Its flowering spikes consist of small, clustered spikes of greenish-brown flowers that appear in late summer.

Scirpus crassiusculus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and wetland organisms.
