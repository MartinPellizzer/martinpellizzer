Senna singueana

Senna singueana, commonly known as African senna, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with pinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are compound and consist of many small leaflets. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Senna singueana is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
