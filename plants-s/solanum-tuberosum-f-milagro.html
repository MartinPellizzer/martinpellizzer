Solanum tuberosum f. milagro

Solanum tuberosum f. milagro, commonly known as the Milagro potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, featuring a distinctive pinkish skin and a sweet, nutty flavor. This variety is native to the Andean highlands of South America, where it thrives in cooler, high-altitude climates. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and exhibits typical solanum growth habits, including hairy stems and compound leaves.

Due to its ornamental and culinary appeal, it is increasingly cultivated in both home gardens and specialty agricultural settings.
