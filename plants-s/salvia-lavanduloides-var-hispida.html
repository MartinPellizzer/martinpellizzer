Salvia lavanduloides var. hispida

Salvia lavanduloides var. hispida, commonly known as Mexican bush sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for rock gardens and borders.
