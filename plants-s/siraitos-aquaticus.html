Siraitos aquaticus

Siraitos aquaticus, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Siraitos aquaticus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
