Serapias ovata

Serapias ovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces showy, butterfly-shaped blooms during the spring season. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Serapias ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
