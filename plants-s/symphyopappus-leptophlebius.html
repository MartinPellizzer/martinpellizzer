Symphyopappus leptophlebius

Symphyopappus leptophlebius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed symphyopappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in dense clusters, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use mechanisms.
