Scrophularia tadshikorum

Scrophularia tadshikorum, commonly known as the Tibetan figwort, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
