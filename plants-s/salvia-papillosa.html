Salvia papillosa

Salvia papillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and tall spikes of blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners.

Salvia papillosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
