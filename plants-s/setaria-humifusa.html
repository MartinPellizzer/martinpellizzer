Setaria humifusa

Setaria humifusa, commonly known as purple millet or false rice, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a low-growing annual grass that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
