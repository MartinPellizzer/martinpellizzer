Saccus tephrophyllus

Saccus tephrophyllus, commonly known as the flame leaf saccus, is a unique plant characterized by its striking, brightly colored foliage.

It belongs to the family Saccaceae and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that change color with the seasons, ranging from deep red to vibrant orange. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in spring.

Saccus tephrophyllus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
