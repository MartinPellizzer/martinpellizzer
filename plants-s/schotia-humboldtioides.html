Schotia humboldtioides

Schotia humboldtioides, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and long, slender leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Schotia humboldtioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
