Syzygium thumra subsp. punctifolium

Syzygium thumra subsp.

punctifolium, commonly known as the spotted-leaf tamarind, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense canopy and smooth, grayish bark.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a distinctive pattern of small, scattered dots on the upper surface. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, followed by yellow, fleshy fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
