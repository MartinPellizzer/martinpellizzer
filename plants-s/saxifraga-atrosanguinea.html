Saxifraga atrosanguinea

Saxifraga atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black-sanguine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are usually dark green with a slightly hairy texture.

Saxifraga atrosanguinea is often found in mountainous and tundra environments where it adapts to cold and harsh conditions.
