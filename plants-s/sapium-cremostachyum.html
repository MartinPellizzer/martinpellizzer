Sapium cremostachyum

Sapium cremostachyum, commonly known as the red swamp gumbo, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in some cultures.
