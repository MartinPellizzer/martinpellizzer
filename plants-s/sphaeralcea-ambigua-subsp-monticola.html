Sphaeralcea ambigua subsp. monticola

Sphaeralcea ambigua subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the mountain globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for xeriscaping.
