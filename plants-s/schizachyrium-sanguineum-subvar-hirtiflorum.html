Schizachyrium sanguineum subvar. hirtiflorum

Schizachyrium sanguineum subvar. hirtiflorum, commonly known as redside grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It features tall, slender stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces plume-like inflorescences that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest in the fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for native plant gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental use.
