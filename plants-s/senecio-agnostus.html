Senecio agnostus

Senecio agnostus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, blooming in summer, and it is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance.

Senecio agnostus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
