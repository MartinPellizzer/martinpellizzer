Siparuna polyantha

Siparuna polyantha, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Siparuna polyantha is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
