Selago polycephala

Selago polycephala, commonly known as the clustered grassvetch, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, creeping stems that produce numerous small, yellowish flowers in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Selago polycephala is often found in association with other flowering plants in grassland ecosystems.
