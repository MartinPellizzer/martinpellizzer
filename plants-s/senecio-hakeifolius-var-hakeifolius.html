Senecio hakeifolius var. hakeifolius

Senecio hakeifolius var. hakeifolius, commonly known as the Hakea-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and features small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The leaves are typically gray-green, with a woolly texture, and are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
