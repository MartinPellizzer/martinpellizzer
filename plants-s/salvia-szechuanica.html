Salvia szechuanica

Salvia szechuanica, commonly known as Szechuan wild sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet-blue flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture, and it is often found in rocky or woodland habitats. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia szechuanica is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
