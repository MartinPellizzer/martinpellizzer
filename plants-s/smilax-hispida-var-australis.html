Smilax hispida var. australis

Smilax hispida var. australis, commonly known as the Australian rough smilax, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and spiny growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and shrublands, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
