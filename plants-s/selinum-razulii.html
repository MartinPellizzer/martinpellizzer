Selinum razulii

Selinum razulii, commonly known as the Mediterranean garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the family. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Selinum razulii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
