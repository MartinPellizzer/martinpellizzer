Schoenoxiphium caricoides

Schoenoxiphium caricoides, commonly known as the water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, with a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in marshy areas and is often found along the edges of ponds, streams, and wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
