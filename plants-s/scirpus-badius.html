Scirpus badius

Scirpus badius, commonly known as the black bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brown in color. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize wetland soils, making it an important species for erosion control.

Scirpus badius thrives in shallow water or moist soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
