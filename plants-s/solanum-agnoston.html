Solanum agnoston

Solanum agnoston, commonly known as the purple nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing solanine and other alkaloids. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it has a short, woody stem.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.
