Saxifraga dongwanensis

Saxifraga dongwanensis, commonly known as the Dongwan saxifrage, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, often clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a variety of shapes, and the flowers can be white, pink, or red, depending on the variety.

Saxifraga dongwanensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential horticultural value.
