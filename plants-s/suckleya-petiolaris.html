Suckleya petiolaris

Suckleya petiolaris, commonly known as the rock mullein, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Suckleya petiolaris is adapted to withstand drought and high elevations, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
