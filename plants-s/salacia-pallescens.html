Salacia pallescens

Salacia pallescens, commonly known as the pale salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Salacia pallescens is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
