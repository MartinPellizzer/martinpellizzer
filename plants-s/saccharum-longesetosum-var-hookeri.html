Saccharum longesetosum var. hookeri

Saccharum longesetosum var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's sugarcane, is a variant of the sugarcane genus characterized by its robust growth and high sugar content.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is adapted to warm, humid climates with well-drained soils. The plant features tall, erect stems covered in dense, fibrous leaf sheaths, and its flowering spikes are often visible during the dry season. It is widely cultivated for its economic value in sugar production and has also been studied for its potential in biofuel applications.

Its botanical structure supports both traditional agricultural practices and modern sustainable energy initiatives.
