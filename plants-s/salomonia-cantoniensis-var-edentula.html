Salomonia cantoniensis var. edentula

Salomonia cantoniensis var. edentula, commonly known as the toothless Chinese bellflower, is a variant of the Chinese bellflower species native to southern China.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth margin, distinguishing it from other varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and seasonal blooming habit.
