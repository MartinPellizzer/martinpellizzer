Salvia virgata f. campestris

Salvia virgata f. campestris, commonly known as the field sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces spikes of showy violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
