Salvia sacculus

Salvia sacculus, commonly known as the dwarf Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia sacculus is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
