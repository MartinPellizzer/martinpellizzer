Stanleya pinnata var. bipinnata

Stanleya pinnata var. bipinnata, commonly known as the twinleaf false Solomon's seal, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are twice-pinnate, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
