Stylophorum ohiense

Stylophorum ohiense, commonly known as the Ohio golden saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, often with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. Its flowers have five petals and are arranged in a loose, racemose inflorescence.

Stylophorum ohiense thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
