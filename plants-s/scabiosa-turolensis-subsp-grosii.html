Scabiosa turolensis subsp. grosii

Scabiosa turolensis subsp.

grosii, commonly known as the Tuscan scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Italy. It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a cluster of small, rounded blossoms.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ecological benefits.
