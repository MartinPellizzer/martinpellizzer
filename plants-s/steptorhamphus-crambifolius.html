Steptorhamphus crambifolius

Steptorhamphus crambifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other daisies and sunflowers.
