Selaginella cabrerensis

Selaginella cabrerensis, commonly known as the Cabreran spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rocky substrates. The plant has a distinctive appearance with needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems. Its life cycle includes both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage, typical of bryophytes.

Selaginella cabrerensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
