Suaeda roborowskii

Suaeda roborowskii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub that thrives in saline and alkaline soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically small and fleshy to reduce water loss. Suaeda roborowskii is adapted to extreme drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
