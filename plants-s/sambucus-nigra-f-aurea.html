Sambucus nigra f. aurea

Sambucus nigra f. aurea, commonly known as the golden elderberry, is a cultivar of the European elderberry.

It is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow flowers that appear in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and a bright green color. This variety is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and can attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, thriving in temperate climates.
