Smilax mossambicensis

Smilax mossambicensis, commonly known as the African arrowroot or black smilax, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Smilax mossambicensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
