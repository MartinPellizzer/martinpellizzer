Salix × sendtneri

Salix × sendtneri, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its weeping habit, with long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect. The plant has slender, light green leaves that are oval in shape and turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are wind-pollinated.

Salix × sendtneri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
