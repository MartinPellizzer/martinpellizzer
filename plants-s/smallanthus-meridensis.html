Smallanthus meridensis

Smallanthus meridensis, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in high-altitude regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are trailing or ascending.

Smallanthus meridensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
