Salix koreensis var. pedunculata

Salix koreensis var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Korean weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Korea.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, often giving it a weeping effect. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
