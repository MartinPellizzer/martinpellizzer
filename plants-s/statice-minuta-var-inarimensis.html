Statice minuta var. inarimensis

Statice minuta var. inarimensis, commonly known as the Inarim Island statice, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the island of Inarim in the South Pacific.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
