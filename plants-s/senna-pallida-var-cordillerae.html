Senna pallida var. cordillerae

Senna pallida var. cordillerae, commonly known as the Cordillera senna, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
