Scirpus sylvaticus f. jungei

Scirpus sylvaticus f. jungei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a botanical variant of the common bulrush.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, cylindrical spikes of small, brownish flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for aquatic life and stabilizing wetland soils.
