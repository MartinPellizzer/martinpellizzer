Searsia obtusata

Searsia obtusata, commonly known as the sour fig, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers and red, fleshy fruits that are edible. The plant is often found in rocky areas and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with a slightly serrated edge.

Searsia obtusata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
