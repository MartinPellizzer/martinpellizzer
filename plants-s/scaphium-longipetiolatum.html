Scaphium longipetiolatum

Scaphium longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled spathiphyllum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that resemble a spathe, which is a modified leaf structure. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Scaphium longipetiolatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
