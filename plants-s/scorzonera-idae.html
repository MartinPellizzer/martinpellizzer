Scorzonera idae

Scorzonera idae, commonly known as the Ida's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a robust, hairy stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Scorzonera idae is known for its edible roots, which are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional cuisines.
