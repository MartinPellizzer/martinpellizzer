Syngonanthus philodicoides var. philodicoides

Syngonanthus philodicoides var. philodicoides, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandmat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive and adapted for deep water absorption, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens for its unique appearance.
