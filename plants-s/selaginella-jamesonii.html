Selaginella jamesonii

Selaginella jamesonii, commonly known as the golden spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen, mat-forming plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features wiry, segmented stems covered with small, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures that produce spores.

Selaginella jamesonii is also recognized for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
