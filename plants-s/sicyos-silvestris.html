Sicyos silvestris

Sicyos silvestris, commonly known as the wild bottle gourd, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It produces large, hollow fruits that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often cultivated for its edible gourds and seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash.
