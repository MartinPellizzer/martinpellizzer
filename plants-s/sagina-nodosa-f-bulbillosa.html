Sagina nodosa f. bulbillosa

Sagina nodosa f. bulbillosa, commonly known as the bulbous sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, rounded leaves that are tightly packed, giving it a dense, cushion-like appearance. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions make it a hardy and resilient species.
