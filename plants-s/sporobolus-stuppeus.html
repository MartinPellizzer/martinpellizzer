Sporobolus stuppeus

Sporobolus stuppeus, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like clusters of pale green to blue-gray flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Sporobolus stuppeus is often used in native plant landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
