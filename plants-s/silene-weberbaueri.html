Silene weberbaueri

Silene weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Silene weberbaueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
