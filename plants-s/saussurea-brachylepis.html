Saussurea brachylepis

Saussurea brachylepis, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to minimize water loss in harsh conditions.

Saussurea brachylepis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
