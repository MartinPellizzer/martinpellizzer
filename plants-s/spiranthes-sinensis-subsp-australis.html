Spiranthes sinensis subsp. australis

Spiranthes sinensis subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, spiraling inflorescence of pale green to white flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as wetlands and forest floors. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, elongated seed pods.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
