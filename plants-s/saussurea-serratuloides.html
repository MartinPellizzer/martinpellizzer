Saussurea serratuloides

Saussurea serratuloides, commonly known as the Alpine thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the alpine regions of Asia, including parts of China, India, and the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, woolly leaves that are adapted to harsh mountain climates. Its flowers are typically yellow or brownish, arranged in a composite head, and bloom during the summer months.

Saussurea serratuloides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
