Smilax longifolia var. variegata

Smilax longifolia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated catbriar, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that transition to bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique, low-maintenance plant.
