Salomonia elongata

Salomonia elongata, commonly known as the elongated salomonia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the flowers are usually yellow or orange, often with a prominent central spur.

Salomonia elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
