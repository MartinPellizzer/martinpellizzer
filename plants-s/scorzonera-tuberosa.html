Scorzonera tuberosa

Scorzonera tuberosa, commonly known as the Italian salsify, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its deep taproot, which is the primary edible part, and its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flower heads with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Scorzonera tuberosa is valued both for its culinary use and its ornamental appeal.
