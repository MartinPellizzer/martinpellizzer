Salvia scapiformis f. gracilis

Salvia scapiformis f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender leaf sage, is a compact perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and pollinator habitats.
