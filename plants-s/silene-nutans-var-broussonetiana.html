Silene nutans var. broussonetiana

Silene nutans var. broussonetiana, commonly known as the drooping campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open, sunny habitats. The plant produces showy pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and the leaves are narrow and slightly hairy.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
