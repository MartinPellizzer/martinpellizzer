Smilax pachysandroides

Smilax pachysandroides, commonly known as the thick-shanked smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny thorns, which help it to climb and protect itself from herbivores. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, waxy texture.

Smilax pachysandroides is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
