Schinopsis lorentzii

Schinopsis lorentzii, commonly known as the yellow heartwood tree, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Brazil, and Paraguay.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, fissured bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

Schinopsis lorentzii is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in construction and as a source of tannins.
