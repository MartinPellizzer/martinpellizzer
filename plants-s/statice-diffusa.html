Statice diffusa

Statice diffusa, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with silver-gray or blue-green hues.

Statice diffusa is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in floral arrangements and as a garden ornamental.
