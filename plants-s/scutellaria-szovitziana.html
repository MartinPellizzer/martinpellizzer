Scutellaria szovitziana

Scutellaria szovitziana, commonly known as the Szovitz's skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
