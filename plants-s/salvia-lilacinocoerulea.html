Salvia lilacinocoerulea

Salvia lilacinocoerulea, commonly known as the blue-lilac sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique coloration and adaptability make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
