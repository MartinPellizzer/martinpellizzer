Stachys pannonica

Stachys pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically have purple or pink hues.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
