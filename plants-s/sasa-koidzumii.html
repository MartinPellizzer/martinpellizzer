Sasa koidzumii

Sasa koidzumii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stalks that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Sasa koidzumii thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in mountainous regions.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form thickets, making it an important component of forest understory ecosystems.
