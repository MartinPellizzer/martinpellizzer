Senecio jobii

Senecio jobii, commonly known as the mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring and summer months.

Senecio jobii is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
