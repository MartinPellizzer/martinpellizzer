Sideritis scordioides var. hyssopifolia

Sideritis scordioides var. hyssopifolia, commonly known as Greek oregano or hyssop-leaved ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, pungent scent. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and culinary uses, similar to other members of the Lamiaceae family.
