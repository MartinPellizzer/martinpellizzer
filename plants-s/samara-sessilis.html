Samara sessilis

Samara sessilis, commonly known as the sessile samara, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its distinctive samaras, which are dry, winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
