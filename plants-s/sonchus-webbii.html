Sonchus webbii

Sonchus webbii, commonly known as the Webb's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and arid environments. The plant features alternate, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a cluster at the ends of the stems, and it produces small, ribbed seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed.
