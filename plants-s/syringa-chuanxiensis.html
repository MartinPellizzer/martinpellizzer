Syringa chuanxiensis

Syringa chuanxiensis, commonly known as the Chuanxi lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pale purple to white, with a strong, sweet scent.

Syringa chuanxiensis is well adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
