Saintmorysia dentata

Saintmorysia dentata, commonly known as the toothed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to absorb water and nutrients from its host. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically white or pale green. It produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals.

Saintmorysia dentata is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its forest habitats.
