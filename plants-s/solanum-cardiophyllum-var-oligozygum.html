Solanum cardiophyllum var. oligozygum

Solanum cardiophyllum var. oligozygum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, yellow berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
