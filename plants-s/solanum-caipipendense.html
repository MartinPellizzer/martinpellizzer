Solanum caipipendense

Solanum caipipendense, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It produces small, yellow, egg-shaped fruits that are edible and have a tangy, sweet flavor. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species like tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically yellow or white.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruits.
