Stemmadenia simulans

Stemmadenia simulans, commonly known as the false pomegranate, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It has simple, opposite, elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries resembling pomegranates. Its stems are woody and often branched, with a rough, bark-like texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
