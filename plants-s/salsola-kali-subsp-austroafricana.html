Salsola kali subsp. austroafricana

Salsola kali subsp.

austroafricana, commonly known as desert spinach, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils. Salsola kali subsp.

austroafricana is adapted to high salinity and temperature extremes, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
