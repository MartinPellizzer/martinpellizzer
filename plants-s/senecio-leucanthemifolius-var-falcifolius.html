Senecio leucanthemifolius var. falcifolius

Senecio leucanthemifolius var. falcifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
