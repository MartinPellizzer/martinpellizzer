Sedum pringlei

Sedum pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
