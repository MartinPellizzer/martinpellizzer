Sideritis argosphacelus var. spicata

Sideritis argosphacelus var. spicata, commonly known as the spiculate ironwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features slender stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
