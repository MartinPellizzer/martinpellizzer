Solena calycina

Solena calycina, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solena calycina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
