Senna acuta

Senna acuta, commonly known as the narrowleaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Senna acuta is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
