Saxifraga sherriffii

Saxifraga sherriffii, commonly known as the Sherriff's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Saxifraga sherriffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
