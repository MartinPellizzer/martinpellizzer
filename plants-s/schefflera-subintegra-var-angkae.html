Schefflera subintegra var. angkae

Schefflera subintegra var. angkae, commonly known as the Angkara schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and large, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, umbrella-like pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by yellowish fruits containing three seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
