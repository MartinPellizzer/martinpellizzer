Stachys swainsonii subsp. argolica

Stachys swainsonii subsp.

argolica, commonly known as the Greek hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs and produces clusters of small, pale purple to pink flowers.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on both surfaces. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
