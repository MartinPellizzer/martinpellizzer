Serapias × halicarnassia

Serapias × halicarnassia, commonly known as the hybrid rock orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Serapias grandiflora and Serapias himalayana.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean and Central Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with distinctive red or purple markings, resembling those of its parent species.

The botanical interest of Serapias × halicarnassia lies in its hybrid characteristics and ornamental value in horticulture.
