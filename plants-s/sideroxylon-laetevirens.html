Sideroxylon laetevirens

Sideroxylon laetevirens, commonly known as the green ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and is adapted to warm, wet climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Sideroxylon laetevirens plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
