Sonerila pilosula

Sonerila pilosula, commonly known as the hairy sonerila, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and numerous stamens, making it an attractive species for ornamental purposes.
