Sondera rosulata

Sondera rosulata, commonly known as the Rosulate Sondera, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern, with thick, waxy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which provide additional protection against intense sunlight and dehydration.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Sondera rosulata is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
