Spiraea rhamniphylla

Spiraea rhamniphylla, commonly known as the silver spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and have a silvery-blue hue on top, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
