Sarcopodium triadenium

Sarcopodium triadenium, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a dense rosette of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically pale green with a bluish or purplish hue. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves, allowing it to survive in arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers during the growing season, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
