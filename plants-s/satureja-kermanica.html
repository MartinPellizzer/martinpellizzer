Satureja kermanica

Satureja kermanica, commonly known as Persian savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountains of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

The leaves are often used in cooking for their pungent, slightly minty flavor.
