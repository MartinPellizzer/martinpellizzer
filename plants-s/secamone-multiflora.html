Secamone multiflora

Secamone multiflora, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers open at night and close by dawn, making it a unique and attractive species for gardens and botanical collections.
