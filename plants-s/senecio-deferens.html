Senecio deferens

Senecio deferens, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters that resemble a snow-like appearance.

Senecio deferens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
