Sedum hernandezii

Sedum hernandezii, commonly known as the Hernandez's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a tight rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Sedum hernandezii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and rocky environments.

It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
