Senecio fremontii var. blitoides

Senecio fremontii var. blitoides, commonly known as the Fremont's groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, blue-green foliage and produces yellow flowers in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and has a rosette growth form.

This variety is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
