Scleranthus leptodus

Scleranthus leptodus, commonly known as slender weed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, dry fruits called achenes. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Scleranthus leptodus is an annual herb that is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
