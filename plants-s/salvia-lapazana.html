Salvia lapazana

Salvia lapazana, commonly known as the Lapazan sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for both gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
