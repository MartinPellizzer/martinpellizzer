Saussurea tomentosa f. albiflora

Saussurea tomentosa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and striking white to pale yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a cluster of small, tubular florets.

Due to its habitat and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
