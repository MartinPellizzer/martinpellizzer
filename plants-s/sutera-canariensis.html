Sutera canariensis

Sutera canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
