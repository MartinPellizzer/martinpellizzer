Saxifraga signata

Saxifraga signata, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, usually pink or purple, and are arranged in clusters at the base of the plant.

Saxifraga signata is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
