Spiraea menziesii

Spiraea menziesii, commonly known as the mountain spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to its landscape. Its leaves are elliptical and turn yellow in the fall, contributing to its seasonal beauty.

Spiraea menziesii is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.
