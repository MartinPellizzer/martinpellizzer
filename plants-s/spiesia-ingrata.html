Spiesia ingrata

Spiesia ingrata, commonly known as the bitter prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, paddle-shaped stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its fruit, often called a "tuna," is edible but has a sharp, acidic taste.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
