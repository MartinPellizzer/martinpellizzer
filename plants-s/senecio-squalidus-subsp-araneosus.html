Senecio squalidus subsp. araneosus

Senecio squalidus subsp.

araneosus, commonly known as the spider's web senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, hairy, yellow-green stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
