Senecio aurellus

Senecio aurellus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
