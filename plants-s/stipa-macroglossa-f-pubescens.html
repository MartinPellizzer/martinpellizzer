Stipa macroglossa f. pubescens

Stipa macroglossa f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy beardgrass, is a variant of the tall beardgrass species native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

This grass is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, elongated spikelets that often have a hairy appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
