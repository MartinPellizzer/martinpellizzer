Salix koriyanagi f. rubra

Salix koriyanagi f. rubra, commonly known as the red willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and leaves that often turn vibrant red or orange in the autumn. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
