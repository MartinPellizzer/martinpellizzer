Stapelia neliana

Stapelia neliana, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are pollinated by flies, which are attracted by their strong odor resembling decaying meat.

Stapelia neliana is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in dry conditions.
