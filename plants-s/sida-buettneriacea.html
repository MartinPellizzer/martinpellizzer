Sida buettneriacea

Sida buettneriacea, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Sida buettneriacea is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
