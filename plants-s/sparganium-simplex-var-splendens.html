Sparganium simplex var. splendens

Sparganium simplex var. splendens, commonly known as the golden bur reed, is a striking wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, spiky flowers, which are actually modified leaves called spikelets. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its vibrant yellow color makes it a popular choice for ornamental wetland gardens.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, woody fruits that disperse seeds by water or wind.
