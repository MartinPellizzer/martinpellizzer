Septas × putativa

Septas × putativa, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between two species of orchids, likely Septa and another related genus.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, featuring three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being more showy. The plant typically produces a tall, erect stem with clusters of fragrant, colorful blossoms. Its flowers are often pollinated by specific insects, showcasing a mutualistic relationship.

This hybrid is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and genetic diversity.
