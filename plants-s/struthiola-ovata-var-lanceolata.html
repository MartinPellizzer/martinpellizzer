Struthiola ovata var. lanceolata

Struthiola ovata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf struthiola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for efficient water absorption, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
