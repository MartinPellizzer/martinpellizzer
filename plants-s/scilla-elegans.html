Scilla elegans

Scilla elegans, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that appear on tall, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scilla elegans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
