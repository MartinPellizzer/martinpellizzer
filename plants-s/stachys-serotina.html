Stachys serotina

Stachys serotina, commonly known as late flowering betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Stachys serotina is often found in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
