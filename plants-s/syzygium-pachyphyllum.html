Syzygium pachyphyllum

Syzygium pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved apple or tamarind tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Syzygium pachyphyllum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warmer regions.
