Saxifraga lancangensis

Saxifraga lancangensis, commonly known as the Lancang saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its botanical features include hairy stems and serrated leaf margins, which help it survive in high-altitude climates.

Saxifraga lancangensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
