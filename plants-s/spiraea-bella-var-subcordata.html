Spiraea bella var. subcordata

Spiraea bella var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that produce clusters of pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The leaves are narrow and ovate, with a slightly heart-shaped base, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is prized for its delicate appearance and seasonal interest, particularly its vibrant spring foliage and summer blooms.
