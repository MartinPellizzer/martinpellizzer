Salacia adolphi-friderici

Salacia adolphi-friderici, commonly known as the golden bower or yellow bower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salcalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Salacia adolphi-friderici is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
