Senecio abrotanoides

Senecio abrotanoides, commonly known as the blueweed or silverweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Senecio abrotanoides is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
