Salaxis abietina

Salaxis abietina, commonly known as the mountain saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clustered foliage. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically succulent and adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Salaxis abietina thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous or high-altitude environments.
