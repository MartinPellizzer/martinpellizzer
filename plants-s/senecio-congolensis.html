Senecio congolensis

Senecio congolensis, commonly known as the Congo groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Central and East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and riverbanks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senecio congolensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
