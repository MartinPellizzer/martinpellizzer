Schotia africana

Schotia africana, commonly known as the African silver cluster-vein, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Schotia africana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.
