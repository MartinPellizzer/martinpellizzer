Shonia tristigma

Shonia tristigma, commonly known as the mountain cedar, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
