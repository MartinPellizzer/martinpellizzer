Sideritis incana subsp. incana

Sideritis incana subsp.

incana, commonly known as white-haired rock sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny environments.

The plant features slender, wiry stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, pale blue or violet flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in late summer.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
