Scirpus quadrangulatus

Scirpus quadrangulatus, commonly known as the square bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its square, ridged stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus quadrangulatus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
