Stachytarpheta glauca var. serratifolia

Stachytarpheta glauca var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower clusters that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and ornate foliage.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
