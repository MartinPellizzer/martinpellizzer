Schizocarphus setifer

Schizocarphus setifer, commonly known as the hairy schizocarph, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescence.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels. Its fruit is a schizocarp, splitting into two mericarps upon maturity, a key botanical feature of the genus.

Schizocarphus setifer is noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
