Salix lasiandra var. abramsii

Salix lasiandra var. abramsii, commonly known as the Arizona willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and catkin-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found along stream banks and in sandy soils. Its bark is typically grayish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and has a relatively slow growth rate.
