Spigelia tetraptera

Spigelia tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged spigelia, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

Spigelia tetraptera is native to parts of Central and South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
