Solanum dewevrei

Solanum dewevrei, commonly known as the Dewevre's nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Cameroon and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals.

The fruit is a fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
