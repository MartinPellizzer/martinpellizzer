Swertia calicina var. major

Swertia calicina var. major, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
