Sophora angulata

Sophora angulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
