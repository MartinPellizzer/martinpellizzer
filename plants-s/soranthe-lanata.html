Soranthe lanata

Soranthe lanata, commonly known as the woolly soranthe, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
