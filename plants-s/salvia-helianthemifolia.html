Salvia helianthemifolia

Salvia helianthemifolia, commonly known as creeping sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
