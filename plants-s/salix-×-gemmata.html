Salix × gemmata

Salix × gemmata, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by oval, greenish-brown seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a serrated margin.

Salix × gemmata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
