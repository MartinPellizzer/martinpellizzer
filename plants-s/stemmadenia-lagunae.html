Stemmadenia lagunae

Stemmadenia lagunae, commonly known as the marsh stemmadenia, is a flowering plant species native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical in shape, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Stemmadenia lagunae plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
