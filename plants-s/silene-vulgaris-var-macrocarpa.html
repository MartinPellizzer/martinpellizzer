Silene vulgaris var. macrocarpa

Silene vulgaris var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, possessing both male and female reproductive structures.

Silene vulgaris var. macrocarpa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
