Securidaca paniculata var. paniculata

Securidaca paniculata var. paniculata, commonly known as the African spurred tortoise plant, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems with distinctive, spurred leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often harvested for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
