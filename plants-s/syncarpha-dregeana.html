Syncarpha dregeana

Syncarpha dregeana, commonly known as the red sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Syncarpha dregeana plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various animals and birds.
