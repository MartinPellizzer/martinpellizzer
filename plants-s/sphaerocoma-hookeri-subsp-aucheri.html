Sphaerocoma hookeri subsp. aucheri

Sphaerocoma hookeri subsp.

aucheri, commonly known as the coastal sphaerocoma, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, round, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with frequent salt exposure.

This subspecies is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
