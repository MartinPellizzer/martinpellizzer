Solanum longistylum

Solanum longistylum, commonly known as the long-styled nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often pubescent, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is a berry that turns from green to red when mature, containing several seeds.
