Scirpus atrofactus

Scirpus atrofactus, commonly known as black rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, dark purple stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies in moist soils.

Scirpus atrofactus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
