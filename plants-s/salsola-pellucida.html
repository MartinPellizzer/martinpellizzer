Salsola pellucida

Salsola pellucida, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Salsola pellucida is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in desert ecosystems.
