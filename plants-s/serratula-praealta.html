Serratula praealta

Serratula praealta, commonly known as the tall golden ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Serratula praealta is known for its medicinal properties, though it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
