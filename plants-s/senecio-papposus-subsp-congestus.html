Senecio papposus subsp. congestus

Senecio papposus subsp.

congestus, commonly known as the clustered groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features narrow, hairy leaves arranged in a basal rosette. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, clustered inflorescence and is often found in desert scrub and grassland environments.
