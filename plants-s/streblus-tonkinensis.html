Streblus tonkinensis

Streblus tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It is often found in lowland forests and thrives in humid, warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
