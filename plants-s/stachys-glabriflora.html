Stachys glabriflora

Stachys glabriflora, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue-purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Stachys glabriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
