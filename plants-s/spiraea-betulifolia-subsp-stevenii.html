Spiraea betulifolia subsp. stevenii

Spiraea betulifolia subsp.

stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's willow-leaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing stream banks.
