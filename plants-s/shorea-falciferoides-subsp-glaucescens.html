Shorea falciferoides subsp. glaucescens

Shorea falciferoides subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous shorea, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its durable timber.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
