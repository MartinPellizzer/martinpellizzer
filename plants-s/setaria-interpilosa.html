Setaria interpilosa

Setaria interpilosa, commonly known as the silky fingergrass, is a flowering plant native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in dry, membranous husks, a typical feature of the Poaceae family.

Setaria interpilosa is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
