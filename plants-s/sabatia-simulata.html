Sabatia simulata

Sabatia simulata, commonly known as the mimic vervain, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Sabatia simulata is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
