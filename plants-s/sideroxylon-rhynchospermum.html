Sideroxylon rhynchospermum

Sideroxylon rhynchospermum, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing seeds.

Sideroxylon rhynchospermum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
