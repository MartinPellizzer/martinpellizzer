Stevia plummerae var. alba

Stevia plummerae var. alba, commonly known as white stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal use.

Its low maintenance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal applications.
