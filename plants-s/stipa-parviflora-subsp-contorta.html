Stipa parviflora subsp. contorta

Stipa parviflora subsp.

contorta, commonly known as slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and slender, tufted inflorescences that give it a delicate appearance.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowering occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, spike-like clusters of pale green to golden flowers. Stipa parviflora subsp.

contorta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
