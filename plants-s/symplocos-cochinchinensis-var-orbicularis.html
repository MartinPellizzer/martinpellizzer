Symplocos cochinchinensis var. orbicularis

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. orbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptic to orbicular leaves that are typically glabrous and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
