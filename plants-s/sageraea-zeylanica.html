Sageraea zeylanica

Sageraea zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood that is highly valued for its fragrance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries containing seeds.

Due to overharvesting, it is now classified as an endangered species.
