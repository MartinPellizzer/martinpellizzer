Somphoxylon ulei

Somphoxylon ulei, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions, making it resilient in semi-desert environments.
