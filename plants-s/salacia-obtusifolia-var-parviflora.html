Salacia obtusifolia var. parviflora

Salacia obtusifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered salacia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

This plant is often found in humid, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
