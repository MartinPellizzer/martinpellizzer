Sauropus grandifolius

Sauropus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf sauropus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically dark green and have a smooth texture, often with a waxy coating. It produces small, clustered yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round fruits.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
