Salvia pratensis var. alba

Salvia pratensis var. alba, commonly known as white meadow sage, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, slender stems that bear whorled leaves which are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
