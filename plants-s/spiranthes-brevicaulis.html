Spiranthes brevicaulis

Spiranthes brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiral arrangement of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a showy inflorescence that blooms in late spring to early summer.

Spiranthes brevicaulis is a perennial plant that plays an important role in its ecosystem by supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
