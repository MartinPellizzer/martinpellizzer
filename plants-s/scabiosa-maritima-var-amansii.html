Scabiosa maritima var. amansii

Scabiosa maritima var. amansii, commonly known as the sea scabious, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and basal leaves that are often hairy, adapted to withstand salty air and coastal winds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
