Solidago montana

Solidago montana, commonly known as mountain goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Solidago montana plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
