Scrophularia alata proles cinerea

Scrophularia alata proles cinerea, commonly known as the gray-haired figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, often grayish stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, downy layer. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are usually pale purple or pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Scrophulariaceae family.
