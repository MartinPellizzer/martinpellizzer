Shorea resina-nigra

Shorea resina-nigra, commonly known as black resin wood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dark, heavy wood that exudes a strong resinous odor. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and has been historically used in traditional construction and craftsmanship.
