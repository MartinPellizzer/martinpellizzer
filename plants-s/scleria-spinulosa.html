Scleria spinulosa

Scleria spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive spiny texture along the edges. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Scleria spinulosa is known for its adaptability to moist soils and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
