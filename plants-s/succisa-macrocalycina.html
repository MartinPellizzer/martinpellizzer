Succisa macrocalycina

Succisa macrocalycina, commonly known as the large calyx succulent, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and stems, which are typical adaptations to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent nature allows it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils with minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-draining soil and full sun.
