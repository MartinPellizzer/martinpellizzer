Salvia leucantha

Salvia leucantha, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia leucantha is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
