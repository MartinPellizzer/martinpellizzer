Stigmaphyllon malpighioides

Stigmaphyllon malpighioides, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, often aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
