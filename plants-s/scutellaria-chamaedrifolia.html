Scutellaria chamaedrifolia

Scutellaria chamaedrifolia, commonly known as creeping skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in whorls along the stem.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential calming and antispasmodic properties.
