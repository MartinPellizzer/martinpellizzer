Scabiosa pulsatilloides subsp. macropoda

Scabiosa pulsatilloides subsp.

macropoda, commonly known as the large-flowered scabious, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers with prominent central stamens, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological restoration projects.
