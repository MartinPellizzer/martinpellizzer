Senecio thyrsoideus

Senecio thyrsoideus, commonly known as the tree-like groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a tall, branched stem with a woody base, resembling a small tree, and produces dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to tolerate drought.

Senecio thyrsoideus prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
