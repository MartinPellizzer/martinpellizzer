Solanum maglia var. witasekianum

Solanum maglia var. witasekianum, commonly known as the Witasek's nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically features hairy stems and produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and potential for hybridization with other Solanum species.
