Solanum fulvivillosum

Solanum fulvivillosum, commonly known as the yellow-hairleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves with fine, hairy veins, and it produces small, violet flowers that give way to orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
