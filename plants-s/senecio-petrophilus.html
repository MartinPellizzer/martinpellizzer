Senecio petrophilus

Senecio petrophilus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its unique habitat and appearance, it is often found in mountainous regions and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
