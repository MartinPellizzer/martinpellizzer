Symphyopappus decussatus var. pohlianus

Symphyopappus decussatus var. pohlianus, commonly known as the Pohli's symphyopappus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves arranged in opposite pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow and are clustered in dense, spherical heads.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
