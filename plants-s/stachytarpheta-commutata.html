Stachytarpheta commutata

Stachytarpheta commutata, commonly known as the purpletop verbena, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet-blue flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking color.

Stachytarpheta commutata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
