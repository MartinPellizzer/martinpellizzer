Serapias hellenica

Serapias hellenica, commonly known as the Greek orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow and orange flowers with a strikingly shaped lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Serapias hellenica is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
