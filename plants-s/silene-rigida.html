Silene rigida

Silene rigida, commonly known as rigid catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. Silene rigida is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
