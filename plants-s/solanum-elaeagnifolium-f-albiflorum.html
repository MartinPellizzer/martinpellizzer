Solanum elaeagnifolium f. albiflorum

Solanum elaeagnifolium f. albiflorum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a variant of the silverleaf nightshade species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are typically clustered in terminal panicles and are known for their pale, almost translucent petals.
