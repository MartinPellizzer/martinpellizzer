Sesleria barcensis

Sesleria barcensis, commonly known as the Barcens mountain grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to white flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
