Scrophularia libanotica var. pontica

Scrophularia libanotica var. pontica, commonly known as the Lebanon scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or arid soils, often found in mountainous areas. The plant features slender, erect stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
