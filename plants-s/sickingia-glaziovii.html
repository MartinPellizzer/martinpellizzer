Sickingia glaziovii

Sickingia glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's ice plant, is a succulent native to South America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance, it is well-suited for dry climates and containers.
