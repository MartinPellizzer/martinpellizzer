Sorbus tapashana

Sorbus tapashana, commonly known as the American whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The bark of Sorbus tapashana is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.
