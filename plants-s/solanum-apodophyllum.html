Solanum apodophyllum

Solanum apodophyllum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum apodophyllum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
