Sapium sebiferum var. multiracemosum

Sapium sebiferum var. multiracemosum, commonly known as the multibranch Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fluffy, white fruit clusters. The plant is characterized by its stout, often multi-branched stems and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native flora.
