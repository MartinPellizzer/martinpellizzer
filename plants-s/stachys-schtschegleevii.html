Stachys schtschegleevii

Stachys schtschegleevii, commonly known as the Schtschegleev's hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense whorls along the stems.

Stachys schtschegleevii is noted for its hairy foliage and is often found in mountainous regions.
