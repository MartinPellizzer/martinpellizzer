Sophora sempervirens

Sophora sempervirens, commonly known as the Persian silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its fruit is a legume pod containing small, hard seeds.

Sophora sempervirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
