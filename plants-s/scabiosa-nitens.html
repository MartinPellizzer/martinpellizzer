Scabiosa nitens

Scabiosa nitens, commonly known as the white scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small, rounded umbels. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scabiosa nitens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
