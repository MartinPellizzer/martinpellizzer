Salacia gracilis

Salacia gracilis, commonly known as the slender salacia, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by fleshy, red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Salacia gracilis is often found in moist, shaded forest environments, where it climbs trees or other vegetation for support.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora and ecological interactions.
