Setaria verticillata var. arenosa

Setaria verticillata var. arenosa, commonly known as sand bluestem, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is drought-tolerant and plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and preventing erosion in vulnerable landscapes.
