Salvia rubrifaux

Salvia rubrifaux, commonly known as the scarlet sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both gardens and natural habitats.
