Scirpus sieberi

Scirpus sieberi, commonly known as the Siberian bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. Scirpus sieberi thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
