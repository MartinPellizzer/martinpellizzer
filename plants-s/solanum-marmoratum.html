Solanum marmoratum

Solanum marmoratum, commonly known as the marble nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a mix of green, white, and purple hues. It produces small, yellow flowers that give way to dark purple berries.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
