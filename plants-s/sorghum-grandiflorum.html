Sorghum grandiflorum

Sorghum grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant broomcorn millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and large, showy inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant produces small, spike-like flowers that are typically green or purple in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used as food or forage.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
