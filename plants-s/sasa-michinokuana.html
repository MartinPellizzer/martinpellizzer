Sasa michinokuana

Sasa michinokuana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has long, slender culms that are typically hollow and jointed, typical of many grasses. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Sasa michinokuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Japanese culture.
