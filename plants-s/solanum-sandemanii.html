Solanum sandemanii

Solanum sandemanii, commonly known as the sandemanii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that mature to a dark blue color. It is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum sandemanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
