Selaginella swartzii

Selaginella swartzii, commonly known as the Christmas moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant in the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, mat-forming plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, spirally arranged leaves that are often green or reddish in color. Selaginella swartzii is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
