Swainsona affinis

Swainsona affinis, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. Swainsona affinis thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
