Synaphea grandis

Synaphea grandis, commonly known as the grand giant fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a thick layer of sori, which are clusters of spore-producing structures. The fronds are large, feather-like, and can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various forest organisms.
