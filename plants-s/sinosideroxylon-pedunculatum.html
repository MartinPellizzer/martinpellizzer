Sinosideroxylon pedunculatum

Sinosideroxylon pedunculatum, commonly known as the Chinese ironwood tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a large, evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and fragrant, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and the tree produces woody capsules as fruit.
