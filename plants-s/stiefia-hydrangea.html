Stiefia hydrangea

Stiefia hydrangea, commonly known as the common hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in cone-like structures called corymbs. Its leaves are typically large, ovate, and have a serrated edge.

Stiefia hydrangea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
