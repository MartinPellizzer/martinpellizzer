Senecio amygdalifolius

Senecio amygdalifolius, commonly known as the almond-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
