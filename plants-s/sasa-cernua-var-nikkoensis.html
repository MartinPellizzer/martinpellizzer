Sasa cernua var. nikkoensis

Sasa cernua var. nikkoensis, commonly known as the Nikko bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It features slender, arching culms that are often covered with a fine, silky pubescence. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a soft, bluish-green hue. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
