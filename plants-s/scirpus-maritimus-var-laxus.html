Scirpus maritimus var. laxus

Scirpus maritimus var. laxus, commonly known as the loose bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often found in estuaries and tidal marshes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color and produce small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its ecological importance.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
