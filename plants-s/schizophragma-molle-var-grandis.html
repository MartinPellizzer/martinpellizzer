Schizophragma molle var. grandis

Schizophragma molle var. grandis, commonly known as the large-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its twining stems. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its showy blooms.
