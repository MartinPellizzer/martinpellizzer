Sarcocornia perennis

Sarcocornia perennis, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, marshes, and salt flats, where it tolerates high concentrations of salt in the soil. Its flowers are small and clustered, often pink or red, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Sarcocornia perennis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife in its native range.
