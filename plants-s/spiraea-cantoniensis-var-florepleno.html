Spiraea cantoniensis var. florepleno

Spiraea cantoniensis var. florepleno, commonly known as the double-flowered weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, cascading clusters of double white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its ornate floral display and compact growth habit.
