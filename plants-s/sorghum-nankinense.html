Sorghum nankinense

Sorghum nankinense, commonly known as Chinese sorghum, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Sorghum nankinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both traditional and modern agricultural purposes.
