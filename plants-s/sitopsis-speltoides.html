Sitopsis speltoides

Sitopsis speltoides, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental or for its potential medicinal properties.
