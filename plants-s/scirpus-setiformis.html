Scirpus setiformis

Scirpus setiformis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, grass-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help in stabilizing wetland soils.

Scirpus setiformis is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along slow-moving waterways.
