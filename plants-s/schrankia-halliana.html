Schrankia halliana

Schrankia halliana, commonly known as the Hall's schrankia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

Schrankia halliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
