Sedum rosea subsp. arcticum

Sedum rosea subsp.

arcticum, commonly known as the Arctic sedum, is a low-growing succulent native to Arctic and alpine regions. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the short summer season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive extreme cold and limited nutrients.
