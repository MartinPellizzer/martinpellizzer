Scutellaria indica f. alba

Scutellaria indica f. alba, commonly known as white skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
