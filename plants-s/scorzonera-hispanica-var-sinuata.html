Scorzonera hispanica var. sinuata

Scorzonera hispanica var. sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features a robust taproot that is often edible and has a nutty, earthy flavor. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive sinuous or wavy margin, giving it its varietal name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
