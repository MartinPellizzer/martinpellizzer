Selaginella whitfordii

Selaginella whitfordii, commonly known as the Whitford's spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with slender, needle-like leaves arranged in spirals around its stems. It produces small, cone-like structures called strobila that contain spores, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella whitfordii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its resilience and unique botanical features.
