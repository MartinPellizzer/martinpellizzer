Salvia exserta

Salvia exserta, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and often have a grayish-blue hue. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia exserta is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
