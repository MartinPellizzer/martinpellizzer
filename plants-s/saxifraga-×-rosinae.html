Saxifraga × rosinae

Saxifraga × rosinae, commonly known as the Rosina saxifrage, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirculus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring. The plant typically thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga × rosinae is often found in the Arctic and subarctic regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
