Sapranthus stenopetalus

Sapranthus stenopetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petaled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
