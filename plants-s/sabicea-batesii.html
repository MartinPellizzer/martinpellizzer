Sabicea batesii

Sabicea batesii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant is notable for its durable, heavy timber, which is highly valued in traditional crafts and furniture making. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Sabicea batesii is considered threatened in some regions.
