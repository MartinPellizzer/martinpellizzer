Saxifraga banmaensis

Saxifraga banmaensis, commonly known as the Banma saxifrage, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, often succulent leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
