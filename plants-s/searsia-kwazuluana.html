Searsia kwazuluana

Searsia kwazuluana, commonly known as the Natal glory bush, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and showy yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant has pinnate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
