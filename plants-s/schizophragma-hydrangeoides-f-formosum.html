Schizophragma hydrangeoides f. formosum

Schizophragma hydrangeoides f. formosum, commonly known as the Formosan honeysuckle, is a unique variant of the Schizophragma genus.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan, characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its adaptability and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
