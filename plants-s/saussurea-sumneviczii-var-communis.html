Saussurea sumneviczii var. communis

Saussurea sumneviczii var. communis, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

It features a robust, fibrous root system and a tall, branching stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy composite flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
