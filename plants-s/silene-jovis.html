Silene jovis

Silene jovis, commonly known as the Jupiter's flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant has a robust root system adapted to the cold and harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Silene jovis plays a significant role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of alpine regions.
