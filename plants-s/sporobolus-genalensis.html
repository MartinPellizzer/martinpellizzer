Sporobolus genalensis

Sporobolus genalensis, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are often covered in stiff, bristly hairs. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its deep root system allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, making it drought-tolerant.

Sporobolus genalensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
