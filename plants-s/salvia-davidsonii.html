Salvia davidsonii

Salvia davidsonii, commonly known as Davidson's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Salvia davidsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
