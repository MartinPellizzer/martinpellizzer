Sisymbrium andinum var. morenoanum

Sisymbrium andinum var. morenoanum, commonly known as Moreno's winter cress, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Its foliage has a peppery flavor, making it a potential candidate for culinary or medicinal use.
