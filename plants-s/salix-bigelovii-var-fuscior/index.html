Salix Bigelovii Var. Fuscior

Fuscous willow, scientifically known as Salix bigelovii var. fuscior, is a unique and resilient shrub native to the western United States. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks. This plant is characterized by its grayish-brown bark and narrow, willow-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its adaptability makes it an important species for stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife. Despite its hardy nature, it remains a relatively obscure plant in the broader landscape of North American flora.
