Spondias novoguineensis

Spondias novoguineensis, commonly known as the African wild mango, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, yellow-orange fruits with a tangy flavor.

Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
