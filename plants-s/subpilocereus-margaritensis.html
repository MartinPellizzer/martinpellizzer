Subpilocereus margaritensis

Subpilocereus margaritensis, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, rounded stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
