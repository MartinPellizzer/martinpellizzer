Solanum celastroides

Solanum celastroides, commonly known as the purple potato vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to South America and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often have a reddish tinge.

Solanum celastroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its beauty and potential medicinal properties.
