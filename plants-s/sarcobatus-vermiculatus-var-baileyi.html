Sarcobatus vermiculatus var. baileyi

Sarcobatus vermiculatus var. baileyi, commonly known as the Bailey's jointweed, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its deep root system and ability to store water. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is part of the Polygonaceae family.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
