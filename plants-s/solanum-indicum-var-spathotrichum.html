Solanum indicum var. spathotrichum

Solanum indicum var. spathotrichum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade or wild tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and ovate, pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, red berries resembling tomatoes. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
