Selaginella brachyblepharis

Selaginella brachyblepharis, commonly known as the clubmoss or spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a short, thick stem and clustered, scale-like leaves. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella brachyblepharis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
