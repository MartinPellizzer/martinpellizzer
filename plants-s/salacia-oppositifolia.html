Salacia oppositifolia

Salacia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved salacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaceae and is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves, which are typically elliptical and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
