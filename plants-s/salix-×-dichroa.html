Salix × dichroa

Salix × dichroa, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, giving it a distinctive and ornamental look. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a bluish-green hue on the upper surface and a pale white underside.

Salix × dichroa is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and along waterways.
