Sayeria terrestris

Sayeria terrestris, commonly known as the groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Sayeria terrestris thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.
