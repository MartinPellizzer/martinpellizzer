Stipa subplumosa

Stipa subplumosa, commonly known as the plumose feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that helps reduce water loss.

Stipa subplumosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
