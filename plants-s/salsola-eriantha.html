Salsola eriantha

Salsola eriantha, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Salsola eriantha plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
