Spiraea californica

Spiraea californica, commonly known as California spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features arching stems covered in small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces tiny, red berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its spring blooms and compact growth make it an attractive and low-maintenance ornamental plant.
