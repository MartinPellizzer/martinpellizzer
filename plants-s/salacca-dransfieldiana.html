Salacca dransfieldiana

Salacca dransfieldiana, commonly known as the yellow ginger or golden banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, elongated leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental features. Its fruit, resembling a small banana, is edible and has a sweet, nutty flavor.

Salacca dransfieldiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and culinary value.
