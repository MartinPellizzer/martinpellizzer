Schrankia trijuga

Schrankia trijuga, commonly known as the three-lobed rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers often appear in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Schrankia trijuga is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
