Statice dubia

Statice dubia, commonly known as the sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, violet or pink flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and rocky shores. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species for coastal ecosystems.

Statice dubia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
