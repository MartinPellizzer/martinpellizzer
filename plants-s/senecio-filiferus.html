Senecio filiferus

Senecio filiferus, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Senecio filiferus is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
