Salacia opacifolia

Salacia opacifolia, commonly known as the glossy salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Salacia opacifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
