Saxifraga hypnoides cantabrica

Saxifraga hypnoides cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features small, dark green, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals, and bloom in early spring.

It is well adapted to harsh environments and is considered a characteristic species of the Mediterranean flora.
