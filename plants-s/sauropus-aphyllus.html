Sauropus aphyllus

Sauropus aphyllus, commonly known as the silver senna, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Sauropus aphyllus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
