Sporobolus mopane

Sporobolus mopane, commonly known as mopane, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and cylindrical, often reddish-brown seed heads. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while its roots form dense networks that help stabilize soil.

Mopane is also valued for its edible shoots and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
