Syzygium boonjee

Syzygium boonjee, commonly known as the Malayan apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow or red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, acidic flavor. Syzygium boonjee thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate to flourish.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of culinary and medicinal uses.
