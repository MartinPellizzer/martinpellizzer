Setaria lindiensis

Setaria lindiensis, commonly known as the Lindiensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color.

Setaria lindiensis is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
