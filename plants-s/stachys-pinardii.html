Stachys pinardii

Stachys pinardii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

Stachys pinardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
