Scrophularia libanotica var. australis

Scrophularia libanotica var. australis, commonly known as the Southern Lipstickflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
