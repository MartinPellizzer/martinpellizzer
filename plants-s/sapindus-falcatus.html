Sapindus falcatus

Sapindus falcatus, commonly known as the African soapberry, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, orange berries that contain a soapy substance, which has been traditionally used for cleaning purposes. Its bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is often found in dry forests and woodlands, adapting well to arid conditions.
