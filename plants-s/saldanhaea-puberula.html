Saldanhaea puberula

Saldanhaea puberula, commonly known as the silky sandalwood, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the western coast of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in coastal fynbos vegetation, often growing in well-drained sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
