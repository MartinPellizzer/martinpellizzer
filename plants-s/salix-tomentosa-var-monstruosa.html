Salix tomentosa var. monstruosa

Salix tomentosa var. monstruosa, commonly known as the woolly willow or giant willow, is a variant of the common willow that exhibits striking botanical features.

It is characterized by its thick, woolly silver-gray foliage, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces catkins, typical of willow species, which are elongated, cylindrical flower clusters. Its leaves are broad and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its distinctive look.

This variety is well-suited for wet or moist environments, making it a popular choice for water gardens and pond edges.
