Solanum tomentosum subsp. megalocarpum

Solanum tomentosum subsp.

megalocarpum, commonly known as the giant hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which are covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by large, fleshy berries that are yellow when mature. It is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking foliage and fruit.
