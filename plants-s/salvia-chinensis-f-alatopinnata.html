Salvia chinensis f. alatopinnata

Salvia chinensis f. alatopinnata, commonly known as the Chinese sage or narrow-leaved sage, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver-white underside, giving it a striking visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period and resistance to common pests and diseases.
