Stegitris atriplicifolius

Stegitris atriplicifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf tansy.

This perennial herb is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their purported antiseptic properties.
