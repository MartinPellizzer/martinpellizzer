Strophanthus balansae

Strophanthus balansae, commonly known as the African blood vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruits are follicles that split open to release seeds with silky, hairy appendages.

The plant is notable for containing cardiotonic steroids, which have been studied for their medicinal properties.
