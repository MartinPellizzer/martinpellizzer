Scorzonera muriculata

Scorzonera muriculata, commonly known as the rough scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and rough, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, glandular surface, and its roots are often thick and fleshy. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Scorzonera muriculata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
