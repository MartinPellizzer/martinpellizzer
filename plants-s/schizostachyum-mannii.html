Schizostachyum mannii

Schizostachyum mannii, commonly known as the African bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, forming a bushy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow flowers, which are followed by feathery, wind-dispersed seeds. Schizostachyum mannii is valued for its rapid growth and is often used in reforestation and as a source of timber and thatching material.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
