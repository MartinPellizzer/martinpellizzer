Stachys aspera var. hispidula

Stachys aspera var. hispidula, commonly known as hispid stachys, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems covered in stiff, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, bristly surface.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
