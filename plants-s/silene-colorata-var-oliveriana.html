Silene colorata var. oliveriana

Silene colorata var. oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's red campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking pink to crimson flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene colorata var. oliveriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
