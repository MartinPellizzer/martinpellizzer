Stachys nigricans

Stachys nigricans, commonly known as blackstem betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Stachys nigricans is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
