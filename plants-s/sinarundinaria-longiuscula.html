Sinarundinaria longiuscula

Sinarundinaria longiuscula, commonly known as the Chinese cliff brake, is a fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant features a creeping rhizome with dense, clustered roots that help anchor it to rocky or shaded environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with lanceolate pinnae that are often slightly curled at the edges.

This species is native to China and is often found growing on cliffs or in rocky crevices in humid, shaded areas.
