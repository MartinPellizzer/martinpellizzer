Suaeda balansae

Suaeda balansae, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments, and possesses succulent leaves that store water to help it survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Suaeda balansae is often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Its edible leaves have been used in traditional cuisines for their nutritional value and unique flavor.
