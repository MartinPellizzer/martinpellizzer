Sapota nigra

Sapota nigra, commonly known as black sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces a fruit with a smooth, black skin and a creamy, sweet pulp. The fruit is rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and is often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and nutrition.
