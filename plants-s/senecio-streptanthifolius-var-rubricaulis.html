Senecio streptanthifolius var. rubricaulis

Senecio streptanthifolius var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features slender, hairy stems that often display a reddish hue, particularly on the lower parts. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly rough texture.

It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, making it a notable addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
