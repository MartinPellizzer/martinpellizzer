Saccolabium fuerstenbergianum

Saccolabium fuerstenbergianum, commonly known as the Mexican bluebonnet, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
