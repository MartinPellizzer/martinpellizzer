Sesamum rosaceum

Sesamum rosaceum, commonly known as the rose sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain tiny, aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Sesamum rosaceum thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
