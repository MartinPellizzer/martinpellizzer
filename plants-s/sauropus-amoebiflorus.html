Sauropus amoebiflorus

Sauropus amoebiflorus, commonly known as the blobflower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, rounded leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the year in humid conditions. Its roots are fibrous and can form a dense network, making it suitable for erosion control in wetland areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
