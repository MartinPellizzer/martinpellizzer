Silene jenisea

Silene jenisea, commonly known as the Mongolian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

Silene jenisea is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
