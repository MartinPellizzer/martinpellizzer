Senecio linearis

Senecio linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender, linear leaves are a distinguishing feature, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

Senecio linearis is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
