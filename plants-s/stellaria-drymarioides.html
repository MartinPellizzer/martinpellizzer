Stellaria drymarioides

Stellaria drymarioides, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or alpine environments. The plant has slender stems that bear opposite, linear leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent leaves and showy blooms.

Stellaria drymarioides is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
