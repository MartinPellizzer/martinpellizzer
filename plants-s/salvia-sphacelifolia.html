Salvia sphacelifolia

Salvia sphacelifolia, commonly known as woodland sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Salvia sphacelifolia thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in woodland habitats.
