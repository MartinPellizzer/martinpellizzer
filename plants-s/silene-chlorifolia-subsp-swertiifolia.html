Silene chlorifolia subsp. swertiifolia

Silene chlorifolia subsp.

swertiifolia, commonly known as the green-leafed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a striking green color.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
