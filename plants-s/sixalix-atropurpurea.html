Sixalix atropurpurea

Sixalix atropurpurea, commonly known as the purpleflowered sixalix, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
