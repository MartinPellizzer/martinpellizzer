Sarcophyllus carnosus

Sarcophyllus carnosus, commonly known as the meat plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, meat-like leaves that are adapted to trap and digest insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its specialized leaves secrete digestive enzymes to break down prey, providing essential nutrients in nutrient-poor soils.

Despite its carnivorous nature, it requires careful maintenance to thrive in cultivation.
