Spergularia marina var. densiflora

Spergularia marina var. densiflora, commonly known as sea sandspurry, is a flowering plant that thrives in coastal environments.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant is adapted to saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and tidal zones. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh maritime conditions and plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
