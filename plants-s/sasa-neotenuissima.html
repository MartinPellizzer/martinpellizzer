Sasa neotenuissima

Sasa neotenuissima, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. It forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
