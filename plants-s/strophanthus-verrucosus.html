Strophanthus verrucosus

Strophanthus verrucosus, commonly known as the African blood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark red or black berries that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough, waxy texture.

Strophanthus verrucosus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in some regions for its medicinal properties.
