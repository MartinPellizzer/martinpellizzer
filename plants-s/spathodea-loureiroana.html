Spathodea loureiroana

Spathodea loureiroana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are broad, dark green, and opposite, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree is often cultivated for its striking appearance and is adaptable to various soil types.
