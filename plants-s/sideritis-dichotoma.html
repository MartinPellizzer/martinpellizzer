Sideritis dichotoma

Sideritis dichotoma, commonly known as the forked rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its branched stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Sideritis dichotoma is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
