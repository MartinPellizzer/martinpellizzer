Sagittaria obtusissima

Sagittaria obtusissima, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with roots submerged in the mud. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters above the water, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Sagittaria obtusissima plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
