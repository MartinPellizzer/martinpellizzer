Sisymbrium strigosum

Sisymbrium strigosum, commonly known as hoary crowsfoot, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a frosty appearance.

Sisymbrium strigosum is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
