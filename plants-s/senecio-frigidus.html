Senecio frigidus

Senecio frigidus, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
