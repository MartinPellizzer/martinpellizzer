Stalagmitis paniculata

Stalagmitis paniculata, commonly known as the candlestick plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble candlesticks, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elongated and slightly serrated, providing a striking contrast to the delicate flowers.

Stalagmitis paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a tropical or subtropical climate.
