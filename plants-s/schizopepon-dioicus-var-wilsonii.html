Schizopepon dioicus var. wilsonii

Schizopepon dioicus var. wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's bitter gourd, is a variant of the bitter gourd native to China.

It is a climbing vine that produces elongated, ribbed fruits with a bitter taste. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in traditional Chinese cuisine despite its bitterness.
