Solanum acanthodes

Solanum acanthodes, commonly known as the rough blight nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are berries that start green and turn red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
