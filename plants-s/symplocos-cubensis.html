Symplocos cubensis

Symplocos cubensis, commonly known as Cuban beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.
