Silene wallichiana

Silene wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, thriving at high altitudes. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Silene wallichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
