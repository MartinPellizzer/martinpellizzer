Setaria multiseta

Setaria multiseta, commonly known as the hairy dropseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with fine, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Setaria multiseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
