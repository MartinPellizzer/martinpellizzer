Sida ciliaris var. fasciculata

Sida ciliaris var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Sida ciliaris var. fasciculata is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
