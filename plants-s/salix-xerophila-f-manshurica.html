Salix xerophila f. manshurica

Salix xerophila f. manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian willow form, is a shrub-like plant native to the colder regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. This form is a natural variant of Salix xerophila, distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as riverbanks and swampy areas, and is adapted to temperate to subarctic climates.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
