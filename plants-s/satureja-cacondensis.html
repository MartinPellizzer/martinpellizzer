Satureja cacondensis

Satureja cacondensis, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Satureja cacondensis is often used in traditional Andean medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
