Shorea costata

Shorea costata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy timber that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic, with a leathery texture and dark green color.

The flowers are small and yellowish, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.
