Schefflera botumirimensis

Schefflera botumirimensis, commonly known as the Botumirim Schefflera, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries, which are often red or orange in color. Schefflera botumirimensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
