Saussurea peduncularis var. arachnoidea

Saussurea peduncularis var. arachnoidea, commonly known as the spiderhead saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant is notable for its distinctive, hairy, thread-like structures that give it a spider-like appearance, hence its common name. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
