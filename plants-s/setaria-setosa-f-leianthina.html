Setaria setosa f. leianthina

Setaria setosa f. leianthina, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, greenish flowers. It is typically found in sandy or disturbed soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

This variant is distinguished by its more delicate and silky appearance compared to the typical form of Setaria setosa.
