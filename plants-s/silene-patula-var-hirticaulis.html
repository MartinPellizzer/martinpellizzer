Silene patula var. hirticaulis

Silene patula var. hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene patula var. hirticaulis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
