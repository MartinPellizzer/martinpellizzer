Saccharum alopecuroides subvar. divaricatum

Saccharum alopecuroides subvar. divaricatum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green leaves and tall, branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
