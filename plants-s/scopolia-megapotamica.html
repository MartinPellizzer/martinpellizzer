Scopolia megapotamica

Scopolia megapotamica, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. Scopolia megapotamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
