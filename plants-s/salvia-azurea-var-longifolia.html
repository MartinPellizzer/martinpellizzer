Salvia azurea var. longifolia

Salvia azurea var. longifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving them a soft texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
