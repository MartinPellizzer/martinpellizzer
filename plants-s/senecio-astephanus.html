Senecio astephanus

Senecio astephanus, commonly known as the silver cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the warmer months, adding visual interest. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
