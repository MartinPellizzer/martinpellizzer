Syzygium apodophyllum

Syzygium apodophyllum, commonly known as the white-leaf tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually pale green to white in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sour fruits.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruit in traditional cuisines.
