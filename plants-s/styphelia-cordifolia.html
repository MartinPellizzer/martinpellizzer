Styphelia cordifolia

Styphelia cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the cool, moist forests of eastern North America.

It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. Styphelia cordifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
