Setaria chrysochaeta

Setaria chrysochaeta, commonly known as golden finger grass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it an important pioneer species in wetland ecosystems.
