Scleria ciliata var. pauciflora

Scleria ciliata var. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered umbrella sedge, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, with a distinctive white or pale edge. Its flowering spikes are usually short and compact, bearing small, inconspicuous white flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
