Saxifraga nanelloides

Saxifraga nanelloides, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, forming a showy inflorescence.

Saxifraga nanelloides is adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
