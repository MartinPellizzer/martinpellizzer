Senecio pseudogerardi

Senecio pseudogerardi, commonly known as the African Sunset or False Gerard's groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble a sunset. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive, cup-shaped structure.

Senecio pseudogerardi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
