Sorbus × tangoensis

Sorbus × tangoensis, commonly known as the Tango serviceberry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of white flowers in spring followed by edible red berries in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
