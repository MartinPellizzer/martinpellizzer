Staphylea colchica var. laxiflora

Staphylea colchica var. laxiflora, commonly known as the Caucasian hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, arching branches and small, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by edible, hazelnut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
