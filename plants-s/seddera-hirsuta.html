Seddera hirsuta

Seddera hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy seddera, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, star-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates, with its thick leaves storing water and reducing transpiration. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to red, bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Seddera hirsuta is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
