Solanum esculentum var. subinerme

Solanum esculentum var. subinerme, commonly known as the potato, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its stoloniferous growth habit, producing underground tubers that serve as its primary storage organ. The plant typically features alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, white or green flowers that develop into berries. The tubers are rich in starch and are a staple food crop worldwide, while the plant contains toxic alkaloids in its green parts.

Due to its genetic diversity, it has been extensively cultivated for various culinary and industrial uses.
