Sasaella iwatekensis

Sasaella iwatekensis, commonly known as the Iwate sasa, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
