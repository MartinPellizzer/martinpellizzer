Syzygium dempoense

Syzygium dempoense, commonly known as the Dempo sand apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling small apples.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
