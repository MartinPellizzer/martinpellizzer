Solanum tenuilobatum

Solanum tenuilobatum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, sparsely lobed leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Solanum tenuilobatum is not widely cultivated but is occasionally studied for its potential medicinal properties.
