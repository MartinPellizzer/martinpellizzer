Senecio pilgerianus

Senecio pilgerianus, commonly known as the ice plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and drought tolerance.

Its scientific name reflects its historical association with the Senecio genus, which includes many other flowering plants.
