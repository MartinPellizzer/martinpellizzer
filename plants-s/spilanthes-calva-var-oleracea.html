Spilanthes calva var. oleracea

Spilanthes calva var. oleracea, commonly known as the "Mexican toothache plant," is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 30 cm tall, featuring hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is characterized by its pungent, peppery odor and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant contains alkaloids and other compounds that have been traditionally used for their analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects.
