Scleropoa memphitica

Scleropoa memphitica, commonly known as the Memphis grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a slow growth rate.

Scleropoa memphitica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry conditions.
