Solena echinocarpa

Solena echinocarpa, commonly known as the spiny globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Solena echinocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
