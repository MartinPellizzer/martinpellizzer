Syzygium karimatense

Syzygium karimatense, commonly known as the Karimatense tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a rough, leathery exocarp. The fruit's interior contains a single seed encased in a fibrous aril, which is edible and has a mildly tart flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruit in traditional cuisines.
