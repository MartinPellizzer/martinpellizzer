Salacca dubia

Salacca dubia, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, syrupy pulp. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in various tropical regions for both its fruit and aesthetic qualities.
