Stipa canadensis

Stipa canadensis, commonly known as Canada wild oat, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense clusters of spikelets along its stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of two rows of bristle-like awns, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Stipa canadensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
