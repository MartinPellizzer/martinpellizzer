Salix lanata subsp. calcicola

Salix lanata subsp.

calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola willow, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and flexible, often pendulous branches.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are small and catkin-like, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
