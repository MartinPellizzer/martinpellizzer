Sorbus motleyi

Sorbus motleyi, commonly known as the Motley whitebeams, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California and Nevada, where it grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, orange to red berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the tree or shrub can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Sorbus motleyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
