Salvia aucheri subsp. aucheri

Salvia aucheri subsp.

aucheri, commonly known as Armenian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color.

The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and borders.
