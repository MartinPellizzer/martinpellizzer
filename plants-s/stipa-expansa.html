Stipa expansa

Stipa expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Stipa expansa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
