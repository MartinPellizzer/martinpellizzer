Scutellaria ovata subsp. venosa

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

venosa, commonly known as the veined skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often veined with a distinctive white or silver coloration.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value and medicinal potential make it a notable species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
