Syzygium jambolanum var. elliptica

Syzygium jambolanum var. elliptica, commonly known as the Java apple or jambul, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with elliptical-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to its unique flavor and nutritional content.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
