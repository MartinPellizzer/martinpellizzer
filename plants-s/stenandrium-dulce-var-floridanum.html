Stenandrium dulce var. floridanum

Stenandrium dulce var. floridanum, commonly known as the Florida sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along waterways, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
