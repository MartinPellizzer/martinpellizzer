Salvia bithynica

Salvia bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian sage, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and parts of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces spikes of violet or blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Salvia bithynica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
