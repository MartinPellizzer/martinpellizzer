Stachys leucoglossa

Stachys leucoglossa, commonly known as white-lipped betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, two-lipped flowers are typically white or pale purple, with a distinctive white lip that gives it its common name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
