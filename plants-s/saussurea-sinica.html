Saussurea sinica

Saussurea sinica, commonly known as the Chinese yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, and it produces a fibrous root system.

Saussurea sinica is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
