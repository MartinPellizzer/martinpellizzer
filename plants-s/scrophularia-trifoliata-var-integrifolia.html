Scrophularia trifoliata var. integrifolia

Scrophularia trifoliata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many hemiparasitic species. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, trifoliate leaves with smooth margins. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive two-lipped structure typical of the family.

It is often found in the understory of forests and can be an indicator of rich, moist soil conditions.
