Smilax rotundifolia var. crenulata

Smilax rotundifolia var. crenulata, commonly known as the smooth smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves with rounded edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to red, spherical berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its distinctive appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
