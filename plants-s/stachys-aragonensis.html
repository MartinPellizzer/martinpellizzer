Stachys aragonensis

Stachys aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features showy violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and used in traditional herbal medicine.

Stachys aragonensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
