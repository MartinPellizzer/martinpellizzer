Stizolobium velutinum

Stizolobium velutinum, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves with numerous leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

The velvet acacia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
