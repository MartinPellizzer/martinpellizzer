Salvia tetrodonta

Salvia tetrodonta, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Salvia tetrodonta is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
