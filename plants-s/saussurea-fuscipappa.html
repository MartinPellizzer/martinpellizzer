Saussurea fuscipappa

Saussurea fuscipappa, commonly known as the Tibetan yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has a deep root system to access limited water resources. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and strong winds.

Saussurea fuscipappa is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in Tibetan medicine.
