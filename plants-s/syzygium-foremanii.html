Syzygium foremanii

Syzygium foremanii, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. Its leaves are lanceolate, glossy, and emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

Syzygium foremanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique olfactory properties.
