Syagrus campestris

Syagrus campestris, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and contain a single seed. Syagrus campestris thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its potential in reforestation efforts.
