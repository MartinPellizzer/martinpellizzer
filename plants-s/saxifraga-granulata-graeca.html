Saxifraga granulata graeca

Saxifraga granulata graeca, commonly known as the Greek sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is a perennial herb with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, granular texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga granulata graeca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
