Sideritis hirsuta var. angustifolia

Sideritis hirsuta var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hirsute sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
