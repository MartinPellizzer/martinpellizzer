Senna papillosa

Senna papillosa, commonly known as the silky senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Senna papillosa is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
