Symplocos chengapae

Symplocos chengapae, commonly known as the Chinese teaberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Symplocos chengapae thrives in acidic soils and is often found in forested areas with partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
