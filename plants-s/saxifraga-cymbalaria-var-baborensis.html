Saxifraga cymbalaria var. baborensis

Saxifraga cymbalaria var. baborensis, commonly known as the Baborensis saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, rocky or woodland habitats, often in shaded environments. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
