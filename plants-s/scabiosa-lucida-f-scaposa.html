Scabiosa lucida f. scaposa

Scabiosa lucida f. scaposa, commonly known as the white scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
