Sporobolus airoides

Sporobolus airoides, commonly known as the arrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Sporobolus airoides is adapted to arid environments and has mechanisms to conserve water and withstand high temperatures.
