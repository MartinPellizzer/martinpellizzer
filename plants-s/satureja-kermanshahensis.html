Satureja kermanshahensis

Satureja kermanshahensis, commonly known as Kermanshah savory, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
