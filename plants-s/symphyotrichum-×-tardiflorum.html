Symphyotrichum × tardiflorum

Symphyotrichum × tardiflorum, commonly known as the late-flowering heath aster, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom late in the growing season. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Symphyotrichum × tardiflorum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in naturalized areas and conservation lands.
