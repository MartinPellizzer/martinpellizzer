Salix × hiraoana

Salix × hiraoana, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a distinctive cascading appearance. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male, while female flowers develop later in the same season. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a bright green color.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful form and adaptability to various soil types.
