Senna pendula var. paludicola

Senna pendula var. paludicola, commonly known as the swamp senna, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil in marshy environments, contributing to ecological balance.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
