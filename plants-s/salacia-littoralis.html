Salacia littoralis

Salacia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Salacia littoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
