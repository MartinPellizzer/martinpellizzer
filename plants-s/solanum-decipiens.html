Solanum decipiens

Solanum decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. Its fruit, a small, bright red berry, is toxic and should not be consumed.

Solanum decipiens is often found in moist, shaded areas and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
