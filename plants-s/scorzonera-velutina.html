Scorzonera velutina

Scorzonera velutina, commonly known as the velvet scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, dark green foliage and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces a tuberous root that is often harvested for its edible qualities. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scorzonera velutina is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
