Satureja serpyllifolia subsp. barbata

Satureja serpyllifolia subsp.

barbata, commonly known as creeping savory, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, aromatic leaves with a distinct mint-like fragrance, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its subspecific name, *barbata*, refers to the bristly or hairy appearance of its leaves.
