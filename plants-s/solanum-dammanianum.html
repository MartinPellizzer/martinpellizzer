Solanum dammanianum

Solanum dammanianum, commonly known as the Dammanian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

The species is often found in humid, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
