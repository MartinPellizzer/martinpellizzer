Salsola sericea

Salsola sericea, commonly known as silver leaf Russian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Eurasia and North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Salsola sericea is highly adaptive to dry conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly.
