Stuckenia striata

Stuckenia striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive striated pattern on the leaf surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom in early summer.

Stuckenia striata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and waterfowl.
