Sida cordifolia var. variegata

Sida cordifolia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated heartleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which often display variegation in green, white, and sometimes pink. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
