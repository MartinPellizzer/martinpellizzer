Salix × intercedens

Salix × intercedens, commonly known as the hybrid weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal.

Salix × intercedens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful form and ability to thrive in moist soils.
