Solanum salviifolium

Solanum salviifolium, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solanum salviifolium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
