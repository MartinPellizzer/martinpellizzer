Styrax nicaraguensis

Styrax nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
