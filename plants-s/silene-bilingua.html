Silene bilingua

Silene bilingua, commonly known as the two-tongued catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Silene bilingua has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.
