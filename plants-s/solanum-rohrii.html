Solanum rohrii

Solanum rohrii, commonly known as the Rohri nightshade, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Cuba and Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers, and is characterized by its toxic alkaloids. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers that are followed by yellow or orange berries. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and the plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
