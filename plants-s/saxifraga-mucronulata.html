Saxifraga mucronulata

Saxifraga mucronulata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga mucronulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
