Scrophularia hispida var. subcrispa

Scrophularia hispida var. subcrispa, commonly known as hairy scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems covered in dense, glandular hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are usually pale purple to pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed sites, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
