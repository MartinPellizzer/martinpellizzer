Silene racemosa var. iberica

Silene racemosa var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant features pink to purple flowers arranged in racemes, blooming from spring to summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
