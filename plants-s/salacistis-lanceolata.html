Salacistis lanceolata

Salacistis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf salacid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Salacistis lanceolata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland areas.

Its unique leaf structure and flowering habit make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticultural collections.
