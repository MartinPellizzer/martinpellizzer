Salvia peloponnesiaca

Salvia peloponnesiaca, commonly known as the Peloponnesian sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in gray-green, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy spikes of violet-blue flowers in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
