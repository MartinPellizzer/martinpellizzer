Strophanthus demeusei

Strophanthus demeusei, commonly known as the African milkvine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, orange-red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its latex contains cardioactive compounds, making it significant in both pharmacology and toxicology.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
