Sesleria phleoides

Sesleria phleoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tussock grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale blue to purple flowers from late summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky outcrops.

Sesleria phleoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
