Selaginella mairei

Selaginella mairei, commonly known as the Maire's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant has a creeping stem that produces dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

Selaginella mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
