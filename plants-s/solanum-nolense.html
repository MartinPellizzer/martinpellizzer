Solanum nolense

Solanum nolense, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has small, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces berries that contain toxic compounds.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
