Solanum quinquangulare

Solanum quinquangulare, commonly known as the five-angled nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum quinquangulare is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
