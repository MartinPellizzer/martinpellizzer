Syzygium wilsonii subsp. cryptophlebium

Syzygium wilsonii subsp.

cryptophlebium, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves and more compact growth habit compared to other Syzygium species.
