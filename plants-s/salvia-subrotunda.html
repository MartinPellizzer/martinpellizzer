Salvia subrotunda

Salvia subrotunda, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
