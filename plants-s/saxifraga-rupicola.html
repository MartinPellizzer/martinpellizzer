Saxifraga rupicola

Saxifraga rupicola, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are succulent, fleshy, and often covered in a fine layer of white or woolly hair.

Saxifraga rupicola is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
