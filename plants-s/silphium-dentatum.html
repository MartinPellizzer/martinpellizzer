Silphium dentatum

Silphium dentatum, commonly known as the toothed silphium, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters. Silphium dentatum is known for its medicinal properties, historically used by Native American tribes for various health purposes.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
