Sida glandulosa

Sida glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Sida glandulosa is an annual herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
