Silene amphorina

Silene amphorina, commonly known as the bottlebrush campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical, red or pink flowers that resemble a bottle brush. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Silene amphorina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
