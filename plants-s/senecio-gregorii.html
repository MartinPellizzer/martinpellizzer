Senecio gregorii

Senecio gregorii, commonly known as the Gregorii's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio gregorii is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various traditional uses among indigenous communities.
