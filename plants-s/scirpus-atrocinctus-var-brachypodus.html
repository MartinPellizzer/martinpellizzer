Scirpus atrocinctus var. brachypodus

Scirpus atrocinctus var. brachypodus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This variant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges.

The plant plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
