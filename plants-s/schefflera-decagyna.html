Schefflera decagyna

Schefflera decagyna, commonly known as the 10-leaf schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Schefflera decagyna is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
