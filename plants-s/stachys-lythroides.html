Stachys lythroides

Stachys lythroides, commonly known as purple betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces spikes of violet or purple flowers. Stachys lythroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
