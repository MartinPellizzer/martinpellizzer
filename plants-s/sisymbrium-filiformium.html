Sisymbrium filiformium

Sisymbrium filiformium, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and thread-like stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas where it can parasitize crops like wheat and barley. Its life cycle involves germinating from seeds and attaching to the roots of a host plant, where it extracts water and nutrients through specialized structures called haustoria.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant yield losses in crops if left unchecked.
