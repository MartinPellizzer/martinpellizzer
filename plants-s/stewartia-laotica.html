Stewartia laotica

Stewartia laotica, commonly known as the Laotian Stewartia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Laos and Vietnam.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside, while the flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters. This species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscape design.
