Salvia mellifera subsp. jonesii

Salvia mellifera subsp.

jonesii, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, grey-green foliage and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to drought conditions. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
