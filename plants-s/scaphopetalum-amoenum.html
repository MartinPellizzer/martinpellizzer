Scaphopetalum amoenum

Scaphopetalum amoenum, commonly known as the star orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scaphopetalum amoenum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
