Symphoria vulgaris

Symphoria vulgaris, commonly known as the common symphory, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its bark is smooth and greyish in color, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
