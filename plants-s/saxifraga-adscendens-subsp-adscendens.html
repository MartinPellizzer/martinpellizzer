Saxifraga adscendens subsp. adscendens

Saxifraga adscendens subsp.

adscendens, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
