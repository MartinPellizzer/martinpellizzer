Scirpus australiensis

Scirpus australiensis, commonly known as the Australian bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, lakes, and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Scirpus australiensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense spikes, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
