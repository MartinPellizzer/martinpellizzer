Stevia salicifolia var. salicifolia

Stevia salicifolia var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, willow-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and herbal use.
