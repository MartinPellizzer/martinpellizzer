Sideritis hirsuta subsp. endressii

Sideritis hirsuta subsp.

endressii, commonly known as the hairy sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance and contributing to its common name.
