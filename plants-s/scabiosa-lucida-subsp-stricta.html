Scabiosa lucida subsp. stricta

Scabiosa lucida subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the upright scabious, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy beneath, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
