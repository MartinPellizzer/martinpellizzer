Sebastiania corniculata var. rufescens

Sebastiania corniculata var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-sepaled sebastiania, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.
