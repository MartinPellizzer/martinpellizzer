Saxifraga diversifolia var. soulieana

Saxifraga diversifolia var. soulieana, commonly known as the Soulie's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in a loose cluster at the end of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
