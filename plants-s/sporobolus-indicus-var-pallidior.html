Sporobolus indicus var. pallidior

Sporobolus indicus var. pallidior, commonly known as the pale sandreed, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit with narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dunes.

Its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil make it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
