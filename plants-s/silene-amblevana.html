Silene amblevana

Silene amblevana, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Silene amblevana is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
