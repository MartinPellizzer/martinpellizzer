Scilla sicula

Scilla sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is part of the Amaryllidaceae family and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Scilla sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.
