Saccolabium crassilabre

Saccolabium crassilabre, commonly known as the broad-lipped saccolabium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Angola. The plant is characterized by its woody shrub form, with compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Saccolabium crassilabre is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
