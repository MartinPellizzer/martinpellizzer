Spergula rubra var. decipiens

Spergula rubra var. decipiens, commonly known as the red false oat, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster that resembles a small oat, hence its common name.

The leaves are narrow and hairy, with the lower leaves often tinged with red, adding to its distinctive appearance.
