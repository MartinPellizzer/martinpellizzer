Scirpus reticularis

Scirpus reticularis, commonly known as the netleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive net-like vein pattern on the underside, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Scirpus reticularis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
