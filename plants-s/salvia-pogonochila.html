Salvia pogonochila

Salvia pogonochila, commonly known as the Mexican beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
