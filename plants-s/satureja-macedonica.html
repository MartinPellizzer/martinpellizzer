Satureja macedonica

Satureja macedonica, commonly known as Macedonian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in traditional cuisine and have medicinal properties due to their high concentration of essential oils.
