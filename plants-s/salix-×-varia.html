Salix × varia

Salix × varia, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree or shrub belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix alba (white willow) and Salix matsudana (Chinese willow), resulting in its distinctive cascading foliage. The plant is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
