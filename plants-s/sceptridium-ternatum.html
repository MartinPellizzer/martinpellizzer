Sceptridium ternatum

Sceptridium ternatum, commonly known as the three-leaf fern, is a small, delicate fern native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with trifoliate leaves that have a finely divided, feathery appearance. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Sceptridium ternatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
