Stilpnopappus pratensis var. crotonifolius

Stilpnopappus pratensis var. crotonifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf filaree, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, open habitats like meadows and woodland edges.
