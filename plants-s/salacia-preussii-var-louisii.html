Salacia preussii var. louisii

Salacia preussii var. louisii, commonly known as the Preuss's salacia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a climbing plant belonging to the Salaceae family, characterized by its vigorous growth and long, trailing stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, which helps in reducing water loss in its humid habitat.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
