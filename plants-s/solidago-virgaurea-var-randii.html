Solidago virgaurea var. randii

Solidago virgaurea var. randii, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
