Stemodia lanceolata f. laxiflora

Stemodia lanceolata f. laxiflora, commonly known as the laxiflora form of the lanceolate stemodia, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in inflorescences along the stem. The plant is native to certain regions of Australia and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This form is notable for its loose flowering arrangement, which distinguishes it from other forms of the species.
