Selaginella subcaulescens

Selaginella subcaulescens, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces strobila, which are cone-like structures that bear sporangia for spore production. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Selaginella subcaulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems as a pioneer species in certain habitats.
