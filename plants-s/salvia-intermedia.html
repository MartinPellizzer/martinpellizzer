Salvia intermedia

Salvia intermedia, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces striking spikes of violet to blue flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

Salvia intermedia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
