Solanum maracayuense

Solanum maracayuense, commonly known as the Maracayu or the Purple Passionflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble those of other passionflowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by local communities. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum maracayuense plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various insects and birds.
