Silene adelphiae

Silene adelphiae, commonly known as the Appalachian silene, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Silene adelphiae plays an important role in its native ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various small organisms.
