Stipa karakabinica

Stipa karakabinica, commonly known as the Karakabinsk grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, cylindrical spikelets that are typically golden-brown in color. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Stipa karakabinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
