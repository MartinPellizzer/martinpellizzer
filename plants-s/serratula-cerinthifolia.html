Serratula cerinthifolia

Serratula cerinthifolia, commonly known as the woolly centipede grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, sandy conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
