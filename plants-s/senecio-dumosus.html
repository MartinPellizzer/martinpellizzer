Senecio dumosus

Senecio dumosus, commonly known as the bushy groundsel, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, fleshy foliage that is typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
