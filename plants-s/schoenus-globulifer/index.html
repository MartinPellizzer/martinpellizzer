Schoenus Globulifer

Globular sedge, scientifically known as Schoenus globulifer, is a unique aquatic plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spherical, seed-like structures that give it its common name. This plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in wetlands and marshy areas. Its distinctive appearance makes it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike. Despite its ecological importance, it remains relatively understudied and overlooked in many regions.
