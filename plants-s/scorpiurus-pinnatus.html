Scorpiurus pinnatus

Scorpiurus pinnatus, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, bright yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish tinge on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Scorpiurus pinnatus is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
