Scolochloa

Scolochloa, commonly known as the "buttercup grass," is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce yellow or greenish flowers. Scolochloa species are often found in moist, temperate environments, such as meadows and wetlands.
