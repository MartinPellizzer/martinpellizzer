Synostemon glaucus

Synostemon glaucus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Synostemon glaucus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
