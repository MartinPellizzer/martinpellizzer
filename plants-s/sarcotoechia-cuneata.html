Sarcotoechia cuneata

Sarcotoechia cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sarcotoechia, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
