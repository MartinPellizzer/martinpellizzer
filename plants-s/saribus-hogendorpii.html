Saribus hogendorpii

Saribus hogendorpii, commonly known as the Hogue's palm, is a rare and endangered species native to the Philippines.

It is a monocot belonging to the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers, which are pollinated by insects.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
