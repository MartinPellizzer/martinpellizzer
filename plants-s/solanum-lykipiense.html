Solanum lykipiense

Solanum lykipiense, commonly known as the Kariakoo tomato, is a species of nightshade plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
