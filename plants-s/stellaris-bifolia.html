Stellaris bifolia

Stellaris bifolia, commonly known as the twin-leaf star, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its striking, star-shaped blooms.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. The plant features two distinct leaflets on each leaf, giving it its scientific name, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a profusion of white to pale yellow petals.

Stellaris bifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
