Stevia berlandieri var. podadenia

Stevia berlandieri var. podadenia, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with tiny, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. Stevia berlandieri var. podadenia is noted for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

It is often cultivated for its sweetening properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
