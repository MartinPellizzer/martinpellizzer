Solanum sinuatirecurvum subsp. crispatellum

Solanum sinuatirecurvum subsp.

crispatellum, commonly known as the crinkled leaf nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive crinkled, wavy leaves that give it its subspecific name.

The plant typically produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, yellow berries. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
