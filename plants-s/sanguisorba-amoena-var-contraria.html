Sanguisorba amoena var. contraria

Sanguisorba amoena var. contraria, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, red or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking color and compact growth habit.
