Senecio rauhii

Senecio rauhii, commonly known as the Blue Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features striking blue-gray foliage with a waxy texture, and its leaves are often adorned with fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
