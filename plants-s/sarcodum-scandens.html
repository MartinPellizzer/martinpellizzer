Sarcodum scandens

Sarcodum scandens, commonly known as the climbing sarcodum, is a vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its stems are flexible and capable of climbing using tendrils.

Sarcodum scandens is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
