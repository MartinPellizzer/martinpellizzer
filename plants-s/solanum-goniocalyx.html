Solanum goniocalyx

Solanum goniocalyx, commonly known as the waxy begonia, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, with a distinctive calyx that is often more prominent than the petals.

Solanum goniocalyx is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
