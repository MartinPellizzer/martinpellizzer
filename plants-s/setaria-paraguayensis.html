Setaria paraguayensis

Setaria paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted shoots and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Setaria paraguayensis is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
