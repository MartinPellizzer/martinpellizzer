Selinum galbanum

Selinum galbanum, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, branched stems and small, aromatic, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Selinum galbanum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
