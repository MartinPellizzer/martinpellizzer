Sparaxis pendula

Sparaxis pendula, commonly known as the hanging sparaxis, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Sparaxis pendula is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom reliably in mild climates.
