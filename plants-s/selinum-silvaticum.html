Selinum silvaticum

Selinum silvaticum, commonly known as woodland garlic, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Selinum silvaticum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
