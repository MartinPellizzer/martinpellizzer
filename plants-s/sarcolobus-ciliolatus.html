Sarcolobus ciliolatus

Sarcolobus ciliolatus, commonly known as the silky lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive, soft, and velvety appearance due to its finely branched thallus.

It is characterized by its pale to dark green or brownish coloration, with a delicate, leaf-like structure that allows it to absorb moisture efficiently. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree bark or soil in temperate regions. Its growth is slow, and it plays a role in nutrient cycling within forest ecosystems.

Sarcolobus ciliolatus is also notable for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with algae, which contribute to its photosynthetic capabilities.
