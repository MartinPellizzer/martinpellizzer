Siphocampylus dentatus var. clarus

Siphocampylus dentatus var. clarus, commonly known as the brightleaf siphocampylus, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale purple, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
