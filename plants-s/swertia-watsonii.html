Swertia watsonii

Swertia watsonii, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Swertia watsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
