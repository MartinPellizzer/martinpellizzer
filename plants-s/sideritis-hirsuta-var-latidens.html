Sideritis hirsuta var. latidens

Sideritis hirsuta var. latidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, with this variety being used traditionally for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
