Solanum valerianum

Solanum valerianum, commonly known as the valerian apple or wild tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in the understory of forests. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the berries turn from green to red as they mature.

Solanum valerianum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
