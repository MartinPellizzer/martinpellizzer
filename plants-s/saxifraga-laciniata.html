Saxifraga laciniata

Saxifraga laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has palmate, finely divided leaves that give it a distinctive, lace-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Saxifraga laciniata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
