Scutellaria laeteviolacea var. abbreviata

Scutellaria laeteviolacea var. abbreviata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, violet-blue flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Its botanical structure includes square stems and opposite leaves, typical of the genus Scutellaria.
