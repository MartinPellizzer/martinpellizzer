Spiradiclis umbelliformis

Spiradiclis umbelliformis, commonly known as the spiral bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale blue, bloom during the spring and summer months.

Spiradiclis umbelliformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage structure.
