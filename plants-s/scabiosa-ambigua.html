Scabiosa ambigua

Scabiosa ambigua, commonly known as the nodding scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.

Scabiosa ambigua is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
