Sagittaria acutifolia

Sagittaria acutifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and wetlands. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with long, slender stems. Its small white flowers appear in clusters above the water surface during the summer months.

Sagittaria acutifolia plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
