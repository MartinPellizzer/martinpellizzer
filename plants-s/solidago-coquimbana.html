Solidago coquimbana

Solidago coquimbana, commonly known as the Coquimbo goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to arid and saline environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with lance-shaped leaves.

Solidago coquimbana plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing coastal soils.
