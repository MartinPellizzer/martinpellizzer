Salvia bauhini

Salvia bauhini, commonly known as the Brazilian sage, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia bauhini is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
