Simblocline

Simblocline, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Simblocline is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
