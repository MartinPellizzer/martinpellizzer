Sasa uyemurana

Sasa uyemurana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems that form dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to create natural screens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Sasa uyemurana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
