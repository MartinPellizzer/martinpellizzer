Stapelia unguipetala

Stapelia unguipetala, commonly known as the "Cape Town meat plant," is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of arid environment adaptations. The plant produces striking, waxy, meat-colored flowers with a strong odor, resembling decaying flesh to attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually small and rounded, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Stapelia unguipetala is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
