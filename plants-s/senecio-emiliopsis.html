Senecio emiliopsis

Senecio emiliopsis, commonly known as the Emiliopsis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Senecio emiliopsis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its role in supporting local wildlife.
