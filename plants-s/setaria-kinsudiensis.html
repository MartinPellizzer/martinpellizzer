Setaria kinsudiensis

Setaria kinsudiensis, commonly known as the Kinsud grass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it useful in ecological studies and as a potential forage plant in certain regions.
