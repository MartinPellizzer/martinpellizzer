Salvia assurgens

Salvia assurgens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in cone-shaped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia assurgens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
