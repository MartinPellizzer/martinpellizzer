Stobaea pinnata

Stobaea pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed stobaea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and arranged in a pinnate pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
