Sedum fabaria var. mongolica

Sedum fabaria var. mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to regions of East Asia, including Mongolia and northern China.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
