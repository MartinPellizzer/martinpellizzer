Senecio transilvanicus

Senecio transilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.
