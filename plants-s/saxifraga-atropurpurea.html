Saxifraga atropurpurea

Saxifraga atropurpurea, commonly known as purple saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically purple or reddish in color, which help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

Saxifraga atropurpurea is well adapted to cold climates and is an important component of polar and alpine ecosystems.
