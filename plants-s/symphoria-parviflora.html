Symphoria parviflora

Symphoria parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered symphoria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Symphoria parviflora is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
