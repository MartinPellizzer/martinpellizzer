Salvia alba

Salvia alba, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to biodiversity in the ecosystem.
