Sanicula pengshuiensis

Sanicula pengshuiensis, commonly known as the Pengshui sanicle, is a flowering plant native to China, specifically found in the Pengshui region of Sichuan Province.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
