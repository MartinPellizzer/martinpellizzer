Saxifraga cortusifolia f. incisa

Saxifraga cortusifolia f. incisa, commonly known as the serrated saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with dark green, ovate leaves that have serrated margins. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in challenging garden conditions.
