Silene commutata var. angustifolia

Silene commutata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
