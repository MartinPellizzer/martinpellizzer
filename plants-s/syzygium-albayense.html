Syzygium albayense

Syzygium albayense, commonly known as the Philippine apple or bayabas, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by edible, sweet, and juicy berries. Syzygium albayense thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its fruit is valued for its flavor and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
