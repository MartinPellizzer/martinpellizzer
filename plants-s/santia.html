Santia

Santia, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, buoyant stems. The plant is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and circular, floating on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it to the substrate.

The sacred lotus holds cultural and religious significance in various parts of the world.
