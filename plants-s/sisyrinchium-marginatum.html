Sisyrinchium marginatum

Sisyrinchium marginatum, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, blue to violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is part of the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related species.

Sisyrinchium marginatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
