Senecio leucanthothamnus

Senecio leucanthothamnus, commonly known as the white-flowered groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually grey-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and white, often arranged in dense clusters.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
