Saussurea globosa

Saussurea globosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue or purple flower heads that resemble a globe, surrounded by spiny, silver-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Saussurea globosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking appearance and hardy nature.
