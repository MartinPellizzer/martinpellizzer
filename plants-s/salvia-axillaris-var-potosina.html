Salvia axillaris var. potosina

Salvia axillaris var. potosina, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
