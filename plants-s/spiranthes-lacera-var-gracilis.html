Spiranthes lacera var. gracilis

Spiranthes lacera var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender ladies' tresses, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiraled inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and meadows. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, often appearing in late summer.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is often found in the eastern United States.
