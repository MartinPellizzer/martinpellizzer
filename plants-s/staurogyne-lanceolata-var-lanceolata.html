Staurogyne lanceolata var. lanceolata

Staurogyne lanceolata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
