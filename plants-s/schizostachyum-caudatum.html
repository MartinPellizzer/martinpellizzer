Schizostachyum caudatum

Schizostachyum caudatum, commonly known as the purple bamboo or the bamboo of the Philippines, is a tall, clumping grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a distinctive purplish hue on its leaves and stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base of the plant. It produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in panicles, which are typically found at the top of the plant.

Schizostachyum caudatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional construction and crafts in its native regions.
