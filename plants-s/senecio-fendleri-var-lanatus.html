Senecio fendleri var. lanatus

Senecio fendleri var. lanatus, commonly known as the Desert Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing, mat-forming habit, characterized by its silver-gray foliage and striking blue or purple composite flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically lined with fine hairs, reducing water loss and enhancing drought tolerance.

The flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters, making it a visually distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
