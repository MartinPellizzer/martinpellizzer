Sedum semiglobosum

Sedum semiglobosum, commonly known as the globular sedum, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its resilience and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
