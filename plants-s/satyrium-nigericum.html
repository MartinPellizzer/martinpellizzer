Satyrium nigericum

Satyrium nigericum, commonly known as the black-eyed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, petals and sepals, with a contrasting white or yellow center. It typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks.

The flowers bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees and wasps.
