Silene sericea var. minor

Silene sericea var. minor, commonly known as the silky pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
