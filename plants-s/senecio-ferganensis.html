Senecio ferganensis

Senecio ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana Senecio, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Tajikistan and Uzbekistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters.

Senecio ferganensis is often found in mountainous regions and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche.
