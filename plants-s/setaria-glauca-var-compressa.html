Setaria glauca var. compressa

Setaria glauca var. compressa, commonly known as blue foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, compressed stems and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, bristly spikelets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
