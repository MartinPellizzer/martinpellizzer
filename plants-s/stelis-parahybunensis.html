Stelis parahybunensis

Stelis parahybunensis, commonly known as the Parahyba orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and is typically found growing on trees in tropical rainforests. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in dense clusters. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
