Salvia recognita

Salvia recognita, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia recognita is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
