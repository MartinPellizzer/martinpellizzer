Solanum piluliferum

Solanum piluliferum, commonly known as the "piluliferum nightshade," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum piluliferum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
