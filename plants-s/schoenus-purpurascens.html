Schoenus purpurascens

Schoenus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple flowering sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and clusters of purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in three rows around the stem.

Schoenus purpurascens is often found in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
