Senecio erioneuron

Senecio erioneuron, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white hairs on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family.

Senecio erioneuron is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique texture and drought tolerance.
