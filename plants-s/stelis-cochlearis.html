Stelis cochlearis

Stelis cochlearis, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are small and often pale in color, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Stelis cochlearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
