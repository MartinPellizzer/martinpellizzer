Senecio diversipinnus

Senecio diversipinnus, commonly known as the Desert Sand Verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

Senecio diversipinnus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small insects.
