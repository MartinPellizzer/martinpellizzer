Salvia secunda

Salvia secunda, commonly known as the slender sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Salvia secunda is also valued for its aromatic foliage and its role in supporting pollinators.
