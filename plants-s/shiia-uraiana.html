Shiia uraiana

Shiia uraiana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that provide a lush green canopy. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Shiia uraiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
