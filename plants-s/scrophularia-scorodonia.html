Scrophularia scorodonia

Scrophularia scorodonia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and along riverbanks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scrophularia scorodonia is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
