Sideritis ciliata

Sideritis ciliata, commonly known as the fringed germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, gray-green leaves with hairy margins. Its flowers are typically pale blue to violet, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Sideritis ciliata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its medicinal properties.
