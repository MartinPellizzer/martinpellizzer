Senecio cuspidatus var. macrodon

Senecio cuspidatus var. macrodon, commonly known as the giant spear thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, conical inflorescences, which bloom from late summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its striking appearance, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
