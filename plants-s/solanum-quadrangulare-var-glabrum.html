Solanum quadrangulare var. glabrum

Solanum quadrangulare var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth four-angle nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its square, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by berries that mature to a bright yellow or orange color. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
