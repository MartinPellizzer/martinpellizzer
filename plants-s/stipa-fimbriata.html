Stipa fimbriata

Stipa fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed grass or fimbriated feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fluffy, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, while its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters.

Stipa fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
