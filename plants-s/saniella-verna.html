Saniella verna

Saniella verna, commonly known as the spring sanicle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring, forming clusters at the ends of the stems.

Saniella verna is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
