Schefflera nesopanax

Schefflera nesopanax, commonly known as the giant schefflera, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that are arranged in a distinctive umbrella-like pattern, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
