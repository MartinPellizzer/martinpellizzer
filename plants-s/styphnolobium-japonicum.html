Styphnolobium japonicum

Styphnolobium japonicum, commonly known as the Chinese honeylocust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that are bipinnate, with each leaflet being small and oval-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by flat, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
