Staurogyne polybotrya var. humilis

Staurogyne polybotrya var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters and is characterized by its dense, floating foliage. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used in aquariums.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
