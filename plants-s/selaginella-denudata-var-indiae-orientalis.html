Selaginella denudata var. indiae-orientalis

Selaginella denudata var. indiae-orientalis, commonly known as the Indian spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobila. Native to regions of South and Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant exhibits a unique ability to survive desiccation, making it highly resilient to environmental stress.

Its reproductive structures are typically clustered at the tips of its stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
