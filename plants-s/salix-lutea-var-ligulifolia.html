Salix lutea var. ligulifolia

Salix lutea var. ligulifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow on the underside. Its catkins are yellowish and appear in early spring, preceding the leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
