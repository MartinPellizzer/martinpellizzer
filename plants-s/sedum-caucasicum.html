Sedum caucasicum

Sedum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in the summer, typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum caucasicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
