Solanum fraxinifolium var. protaxanthum

Solanum fraxinifolium var. protaxanthum, commonly known as the Florida nightshade, is a variant of the common wild tomato.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, edible berries that are yellow when ripe.

This species is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and shares some botanical characteristics with other members of the genus Solanum.
