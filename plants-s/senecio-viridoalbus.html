Senecio viridoalbus

Senecio viridoalbus, commonly known as the greenish groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are generally covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Senecio viridoalbus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
