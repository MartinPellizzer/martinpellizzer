Sonchus pensylvanicus

Sonchus pensylvanicus, commonly known as Pennsylvania sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive white underside, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
