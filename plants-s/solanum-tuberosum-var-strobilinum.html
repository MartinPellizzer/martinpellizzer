Solanum tuberosum var. strobilinum

Solanum tuberosum var. strobilinum, commonly known as the purple potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet to purple skin and flesh, which gives it a unique appearance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. It produces tubers that are often used in culinary applications due to their rich flavor and nutritional profile.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
