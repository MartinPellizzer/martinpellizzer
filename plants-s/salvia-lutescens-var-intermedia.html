Salvia lutescens var. intermedia

Salvia lutescens var. intermedia, commonly known as the golden sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, velvety gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and striking blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardens.
