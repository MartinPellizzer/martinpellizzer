Styrandra petiolaris

Styrandra petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lily, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Styrandra petiolaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
