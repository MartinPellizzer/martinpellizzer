Symphytum × beckii

Symphytum × beckii, commonly known as the hybrid comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically rough and hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces tall, upright stems with clusters of bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
