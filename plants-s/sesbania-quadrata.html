Sesbania quadrata

Sesbania quadrata, commonly known as the four-angle sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with four leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its pods are cylindrical and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional agriculture.

Sesbania quadrata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
