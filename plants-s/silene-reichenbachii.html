Silene reichenbachii

Silene reichenbachii, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

Silene reichenbachii is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is an important component of its native desert ecosystems.
