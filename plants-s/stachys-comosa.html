Stachys comosa

Stachys comosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white to grayish hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Stachys comosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
