Sabina squamata

Sabina squamata, commonly known as the squamata juniper, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are tightly packed around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Sabina squamata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
