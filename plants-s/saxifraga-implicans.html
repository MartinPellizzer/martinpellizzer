Saxifraga implicans

Saxifraga implicans, commonly known as the rockfoil saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in clusters at the tips of its stems. Saxifraga implicans thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found growing in crevices or on slopes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments where it can survive extreme temperature fluctuations.
