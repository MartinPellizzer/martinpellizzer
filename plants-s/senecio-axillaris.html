Senecio axillaris

Senecio axillaris, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
