Syzygium metrosideros

Syzygium metrosideros, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Syzygium metrosideros is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
