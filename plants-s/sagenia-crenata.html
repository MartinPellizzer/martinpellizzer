Sagenia crenata

Sagenia crenata, commonly known as the crested sagena, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Sagenia crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
