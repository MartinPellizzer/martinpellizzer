Stachys tymphaea

Stachys tymphaea, commonly known as the meadow deadnettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants and square stems. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple or pink. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Stachys tymphaea is often used in traditional medicine and as a nectar source for pollinators.
