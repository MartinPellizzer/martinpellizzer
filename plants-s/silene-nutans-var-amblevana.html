Silene nutans var. amblevana

Silene nutans var. amblevana, commonly known as the nodding catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that droop downward, giving it a nodding appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The species is known for its short stature and is often considered a rare or endangered plant in certain regions.
