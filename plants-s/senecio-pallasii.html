Senecio pallasii

Senecio pallasii, commonly known as Pallas's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

Senecio pallasii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
