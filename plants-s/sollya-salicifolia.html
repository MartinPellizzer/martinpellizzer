Sollya salicifolia

Sollya salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf sollya, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in the spring. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative plant in shaded areas.

Sollya salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, woodland environments.
