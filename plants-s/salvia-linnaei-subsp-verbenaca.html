Salvia linnaei subsp. verbenaca

Salvia linnaei subsp.

verbenaca, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
