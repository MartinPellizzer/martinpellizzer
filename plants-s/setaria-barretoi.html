Setaria barretoi

Setaria barretoi, commonly known as slender false millet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are often used in traditional agriculture, and it is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop.

Setaria barretoi is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its delicate appearance.
