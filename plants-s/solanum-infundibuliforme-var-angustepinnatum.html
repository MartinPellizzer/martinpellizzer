Solanum infundibuliforme var. angustepinnatum

Solanum infundibuliforme var. angustepinnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its berries are usually green when immature and turn red when ripe, though they are toxic and not edible.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
