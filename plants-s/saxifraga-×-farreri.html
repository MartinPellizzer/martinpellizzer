Saxifraga × farreri

Saxifraga × farreri, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirculus, characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga × farreri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
