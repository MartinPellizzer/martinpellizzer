Sclerocarya gillettii

Sclerocarya gillettii, commonly known as the African bullthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Sclerocarya gillettii plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
