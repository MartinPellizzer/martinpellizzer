Senecio crassipes

Senecio crassipes, commonly known as the thick-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Senecio crassipes is drought-tolerant and adapted to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
