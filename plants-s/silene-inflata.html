Silene inflata

Silene inflata, commonly known as the balloonflower, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, inflated, balloon-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Silene inflata is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
