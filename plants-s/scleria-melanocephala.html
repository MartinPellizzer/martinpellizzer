Scleria melanocephala

Scleria melanocephala, commonly known as the black-headed sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, with a narrow, linear shape, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.
