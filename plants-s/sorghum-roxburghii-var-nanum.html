Sorghum roxburghii var. nanum

Sorghum roxburghii var. nanum, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its short stature and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, dense spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it suitable for use in ornamental landscaping and as a forage crop.
