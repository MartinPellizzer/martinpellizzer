Senecio tripinnatifidus

Senecio tripinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
