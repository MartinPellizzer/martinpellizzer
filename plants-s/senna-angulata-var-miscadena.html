Senna angulata var. miscadena

Senna angulata var. miscadena, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and contain a toxic alkaloid called sennoside, which is used in laxative medications.

The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
