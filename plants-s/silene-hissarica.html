Silene hissarica

Silene hissarica, commonly known as the Armenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, temperate climates. It has slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Silene hissarica is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
