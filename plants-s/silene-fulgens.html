Silene fulgens

Silene fulgens, commonly known as the blazing campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene fulgens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
