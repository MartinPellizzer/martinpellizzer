Solidago gracilis

Solidago gracilis, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its slender stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers in late summer to early fall.

Solidago gracilis plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
