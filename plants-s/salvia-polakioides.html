Salvia polakioides

Salvia polakioides, commonly known as the Polakioides sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy texture.

Salvia polakioides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
