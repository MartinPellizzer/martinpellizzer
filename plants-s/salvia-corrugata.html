Salvia corrugata

Salvia corrugata, commonly known as the wrinkled sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly textured surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Salvia corrugata is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
