Scirpus chloroticus

Scirpus chloroticus, commonly known as the yellow bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, lakes, and along riverbanks. The plant has tall, slender stems that are often tinged with yellow or greenish hues, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are usually pink or reddish in color.

Scirpus chloroticus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
