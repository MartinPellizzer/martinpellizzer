Senecio santiagoensis

Senecio santiagoensis, commonly known as Santiago's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
