Selaginella soyauxii

Selaginella soyauxii, commonly known as the Soyaux's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its creeping stems and clustered, needle-like leaves. Its sporophytes produce spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella soyauxii is noted for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
