Solanum xanti var. spencerae

Solanum xanti var. spencerae, commonly known as the Spencerae nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
