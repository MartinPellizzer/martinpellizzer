Stipa litwinowiana

Stipa litwinowiana, commonly known as the Litwinowian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fluffy, silver-gray plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Stipa litwinowiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, feathery foliage.
