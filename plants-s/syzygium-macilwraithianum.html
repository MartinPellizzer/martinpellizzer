Syzygium macilwraithianum

Syzygium macilwraithianum, commonly known as the Macilwraith's tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow when mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
