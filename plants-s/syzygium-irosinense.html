Syzygium irosinense

Syzygium irosinense, commonly known as the Irosin apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are yellow or orange when ripe.

The fruits are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
