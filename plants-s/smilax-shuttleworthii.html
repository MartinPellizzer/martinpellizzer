Smilax shuttleworthii

Smilax shuttleworthii, commonly known as the shuttleworth's smilax, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Smilax shuttleworthii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
