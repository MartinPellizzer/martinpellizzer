Salicornia fruticosa var. andina

Salicornia fruticosa var. andina, commonly known as Andean glassweed, is a succulent plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It thrives in arid, saline environments and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low water availability.

This plant is valued for its unique appearance and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental species.
