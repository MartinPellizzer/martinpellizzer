Silphium radula var. radula

Silphium radula var. radula, commonly known as the common silphium, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a robust, branching stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Silphium radula var. radula is known for its medicinal properties and has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
