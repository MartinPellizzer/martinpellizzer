Saurauia scabra var. prainiana

Saurauia scabra var. prainiana, commonly known as the rough-leaved saurauia, is a variant of the saurauia genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves that are typically pubescent and have a distinctive, uneven surface. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are fleshy berries that mature to a reddish color, attracting various wildlife species.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
