Silene cambessedesii

Silene cambessedesii, commonly known as the Cambessedes' catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the island's unique climate. It has small, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
