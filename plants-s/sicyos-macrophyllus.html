Sicyos macrophyllus

Sicyos macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant false cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that are followed by elongated, spiny fruits. Its stems are covered in soft, pubescent hairs, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Sicyos macrophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
