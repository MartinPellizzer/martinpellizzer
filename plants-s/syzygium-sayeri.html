Syzygium sayeri

Syzygium sayeri, commonly known as the Sayer's apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia. The plant is a small evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The flowers are white or pale pink, clustered in inflorescences, and are followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.
