Saurauia scabra f. schlimiana

Saurauia scabra f. schlimiana, commonly known as the Schlim's roughleaf saurauia, is a variant of the saurauia genus native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its botanical features include woody stems and elliptical leaves with a serrated margin.

This form is notable for its horticultural value and adaptability to cultivation in suitable conditions.
