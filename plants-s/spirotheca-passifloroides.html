Spirotheca passifloroides

Spirotheca passifloroides, commonly known as the Passionflower Vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, complex flowers that resemble a crown, with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive reproductive structure called a gynostemium. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and a subject of botanical study.
