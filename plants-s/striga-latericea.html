Striga latericea

Striga latericea, commonly known as the desert witchweed, is a parasitic flowering plant that primarily infests grasses and other herbaceous plants.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and lacks chlorophyll, relying entirely on its host for nutrients. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a highly branched root system that attaches to the roots of its host. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and is considered a significant agricultural pest.

Due to its parasitic nature and ability to thrive in harsh environments, it poses a challenge for sustainable farming in affected areas.
