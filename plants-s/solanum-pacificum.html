Solanum pacificum

Solanum pacificum, commonly known as the Pacific nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the western Pacific islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by yellow berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum pacificum is adapted to coastal and tropical environments, often found in moist, shaded areas.
