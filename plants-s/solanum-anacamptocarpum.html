Solanum anacamptocarpum

Solanum anacamptocarpum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers and produces berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
