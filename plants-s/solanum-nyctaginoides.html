Solanum nyctaginoides

Solanum nyctaginoides, commonly known as the nightshade or blue morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in gardens and wild areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its vibrant flowers, though some parts of the plant contain toxic compounds.
