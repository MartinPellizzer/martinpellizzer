Styrax nicaraguensis subsp. nicaraguensis

Styrax nicaraguensis subsp.

nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan styrax, is a flowering plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy upper surface.

The bark is smooth and light brown, often exuding a resinous sap that has been used traditionally for various purposes.
