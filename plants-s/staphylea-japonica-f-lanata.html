Staphylea japonica f. lanata

Staphylea japonica f. lanata, commonly known as the woolly Japanese staphylea, is a unique cultivar of the staphylea plant.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. It is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The foliage is covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps to distinguish it from other varieties of the species.
