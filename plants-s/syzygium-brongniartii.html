Syzygium brongniartii

Syzygium brongniartii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a fleshy, reddish drupe that contains a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
