Schizachyrium sanguineum subvar. domingense

Schizachyrium sanguineum subvar. domingense, commonly known as the Mexican switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences that often display a reddish or pinkish hue. The plant typically grows in dry to moist soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
