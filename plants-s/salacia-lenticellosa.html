Salacia lenticellosa

Salacia lenticellosa, commonly known as the roughleaf salacia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salaciacae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are typically small, round, and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Salacia lenticellosa is often grown for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and floral display.
