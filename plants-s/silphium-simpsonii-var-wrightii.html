Silphium simpsonii var. wrightii

Silphium simpsonii var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's compassplant, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a dense, flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Silphium simpsonii var. wrightii is valued for its ecological importance and historical use by indigenous peoples.
