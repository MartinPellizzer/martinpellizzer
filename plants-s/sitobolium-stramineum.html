Sitobolium stramineum

Sitobolium stramineum, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Sitobolium stramineum is also valued for its role in soil enrichment due to its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen.
