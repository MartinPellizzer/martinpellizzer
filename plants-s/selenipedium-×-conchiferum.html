Selenipedium × conchiferum

Selenipedium × conchiferum, commonly known as the hybrid giant spider-orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Selenipedium albornozii and Selenipedium conchiferum, characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders. The plant is native to the cloud forests of Central America, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. It has thick, fleshy roots that store water, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
