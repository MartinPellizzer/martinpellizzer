Salicornia perennans subsp. altaica

Salicornia perennans subsp.

altaica, commonly known as sea asparagus, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments. It thrives in coastal areas and salt marshes, where it tolerates high levels of salt in the soil and water.

The plant has succulent, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, scale-like leaves. It produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional content and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
