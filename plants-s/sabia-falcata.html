Sabia falcata

Sabia falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive sickle-shaped appearance, and its yellow flowers are arranged in racemes. Sabia falcata produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in open woodlands and savannas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
