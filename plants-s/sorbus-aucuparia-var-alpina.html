Sorbus aucuparia var. alpina

Sorbus aucuparia var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine rowan, is a variant of the common rowan tree.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange berries that mature in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring.

The species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
