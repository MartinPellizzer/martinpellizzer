Scorzonera tuberosa var. canescens

Scorzonera tuberosa var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and produces yellowish to brownish flowers with a distinctive tubular shape. The plant is notable for its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Scorzonera tuberosa var. canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
