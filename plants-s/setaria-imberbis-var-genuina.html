Setaria imberbis var. genuina

Setaria imberbis var. genuina, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually hairless and have a bluish-green color, while the flowers are small and wind-pollinated.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
