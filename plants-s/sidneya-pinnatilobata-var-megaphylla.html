Sidneya pinnatilobata var. megaphylla

Sidneya pinnatilobata var. megaphylla, commonly known as the giant lobed sidneya, is a distinctive evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that can reach up to 12 inches in length, giving it a dramatic, fern-like appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal gardens. Its foliage remains dense and vibrant throughout the year, providing ornamental value in both landscape and container settings.

While it is not typically cultivated for flowers, its unique foliage makes it a standout addition to specialized plant collections.
