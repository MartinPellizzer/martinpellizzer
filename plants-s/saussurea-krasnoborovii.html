Saussurea krasnoborovii

Saussurea krasnoborovii, commonly known as the Krasnoborov's saffron, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces feathery, bract-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is adapted to grow in high-altitude, arid environments.

Saussurea krasnoborovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
