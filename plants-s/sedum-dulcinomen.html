Sedum dulcinomen

Sedum dulcinomen, commonly known as the sweet stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant in arid climates.
