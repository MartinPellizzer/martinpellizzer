Scirpus congruus

Scirpus congruus, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brown in color. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Scirpus congruus is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.
