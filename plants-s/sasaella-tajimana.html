Sasaella tajimana

Sasaella tajimana, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a species of bamboo that belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its structural strength, while its culms are hollow and segmented.

Sasaella tajimana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
