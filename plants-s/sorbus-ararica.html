Sorbus ararica

Sorbus ararica, commonly known as the white-fruited rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Sorbus.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, yellow berries that ripen in the fall and are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

The tree is often found in moist, shaded environments and can grow up to 10 meters tall.
