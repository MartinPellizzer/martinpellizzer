Siphocampylus betulifolius

Siphocampylus betulifolius, commonly known as the birchleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant features alternate, birch-like leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a serrated margin. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping in arid regions.
