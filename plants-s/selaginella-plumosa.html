Selaginella plumosa

Selaginella plumosa, commonly known as the feather moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant in the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features delicate, feather-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It is characterized by its ability to retain water, which helps it survive in humid conditions.

Selaginella plumosa plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
