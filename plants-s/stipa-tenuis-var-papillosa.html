Stipa tenuis var. papillosa

Stipa tenuis var. papillosa, commonly known as the silky needlegrass, is a variant of the slender needlegrass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and slender, cylindrical spikelets that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its inflorescence consists of compact, grass-like clusters that are often adorned with soft, silky hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in low-maintenance environments.
