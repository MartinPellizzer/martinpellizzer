Solanum baylisii

Solanum baylisii, commonly known as the Baylis's nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, with hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces small, toxic berries that are edible when ripe.

Solanum baylisii is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
