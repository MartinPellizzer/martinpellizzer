Solanum longiconicum

Solanum longiconicum, commonly known as the long-stemmed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features hairy, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
