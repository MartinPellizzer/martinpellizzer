Symphyopappus polystachyus

Symphyopappus polystachyus, commonly known as the many-stemmed goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in dense, branched clusters, often in late summer. Its structure includes a woody stem with a distinctive symphyopappus, a unique feature where the pappus is fused to the flower head.

This plant is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
