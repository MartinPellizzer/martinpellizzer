Symplocos adenophylla var. merrittii

Symplocos adenophylla var. merrittii, commonly known as the Merritt's waxleaf.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
