Swertia recurva

Swertia recurva, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and dark green, velvety leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Swertia recurva is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
