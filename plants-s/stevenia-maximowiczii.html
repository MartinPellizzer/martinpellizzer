Stevenia maximowiczii

Stevenia maximowiczii, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained, acidic soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant, low-maintenance plants.
