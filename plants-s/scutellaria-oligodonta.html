Scutellaria oligodonta

Scutellaria oligodonta, commonly known as narrowleaf skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the central and eastern regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, with two lips that aid in pollination by insects.

The genus Scutellaria is known for its medicinal properties, and S. oligodonta is no exception, containing compounds with potential therapeutic uses.
