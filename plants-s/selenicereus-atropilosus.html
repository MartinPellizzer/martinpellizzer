Selenicereus atropilosus

Selenicereus atropilosus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale pink. Its flowers are short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.
