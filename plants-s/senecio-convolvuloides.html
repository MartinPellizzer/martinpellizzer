Senecio convolvuloides

Senecio convolvuloides, commonly known as the blueweed or blueweed-like plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and blue or purple in color.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
