Spigelia rubiifolia

Spigelia rubiifolia, commonly known as the redroot st. john's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown roots and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in terminal clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas.

Spigelia rubiifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
