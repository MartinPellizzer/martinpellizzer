Senecio hastulatus

Senecio hastulatus, commonly known as the swordleaf groundsels, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a conical inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
