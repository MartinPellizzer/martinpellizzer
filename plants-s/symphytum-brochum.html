Symphytum brochum

Symphytum brochum, commonly known as the Brazilian comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a robust root system and lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
