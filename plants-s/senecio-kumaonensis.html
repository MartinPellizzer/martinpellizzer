Senecio kumaonensis

Senecio kumaonensis, commonly known as the Kumaon groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes. It features slender stems, narrow leaves, and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
