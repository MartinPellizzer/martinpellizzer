Sporobolus aeneus f. parviflorus

Sporobolus aeneus f. parviflorus, commonly known as the silverleaf spikerush, is a variant of the spikerush genus native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to silver flowers. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

This plant is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, showcasing resilience through its drought tolerance and efficient water use.
