Spergula arvensis subsp. pseudomorisonii

Spergula arvensis subsp.

pseudomorisonii, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and riverbanks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is often mistaken for true oats due to its similar appearance.

Botanically, it is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
