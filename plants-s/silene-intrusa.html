Silene intrusa

Silene intrusa, commonly known as the invasive redflower catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe but has become an invasive species in parts of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking pink to red flowers with five petals. The plant has a robust root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it competitive in disturbed habitats. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread.

Silene intrusa is often found in open areas such as roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.
