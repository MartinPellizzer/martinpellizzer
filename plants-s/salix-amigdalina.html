Salix amigdalina

Salix amigdalina, commonly known as the almond willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its flowers, and it is often found in moist or wetland environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
