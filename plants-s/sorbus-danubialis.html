Sorbus danubialis

Sorbus danubialis, commonly known as the Danube rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found along riverbanks and in forested areas.

Sorbus danubialis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
