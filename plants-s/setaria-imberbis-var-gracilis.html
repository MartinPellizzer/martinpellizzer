Setaria imberbis var. gracilis

Setaria imberbis var. gracilis, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
