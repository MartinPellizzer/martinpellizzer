Syzygium watutense

Syzygium watutense, commonly known as the New Guinea apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces large, round fruits with a thick, yellowish skin and a sweet, juicy pulp that contains numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Syzygium watutense is also valued for its potential in agroforestry systems due to its ability to grow in diverse soil conditions.
