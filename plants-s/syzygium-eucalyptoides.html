Syzygium eucalyptoides

Syzygium eucalyptoides, commonly known as the satinwood or white myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils.

Syzygium eucalyptoides is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and carving.
