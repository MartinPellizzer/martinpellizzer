Sisymbrium laciniatum

Sisymbrium laciniatum, commonly known as the cutleaf babylon wheat, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in wastelands. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is an annual herb that plays a role in ecological succession and supports various pollinators.
