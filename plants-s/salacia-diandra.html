Salacia diandra

Salacia diandra, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Salacia diandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
