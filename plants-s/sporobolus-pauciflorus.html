Sporobolus pauciflorus

Sporobolus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Sporobolus pauciflorus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
