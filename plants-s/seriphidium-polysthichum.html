Seriphidium polysthichum

Seriphidium polysthichum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky and arid environments, where it has adapted to thrive in harsh, dry conditions. Its foliage is narrow and silvery-gray, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Seriphidium polysthichum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
