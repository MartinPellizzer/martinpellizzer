Sisymbrium dareifolium

Sisymbrium dareifolium, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
