Senecio crassissimus

Senecio crassissimus, commonly known as the giant ragwort, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, woolly leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its invasive nature, it can pose a threat to native plant species in certain regions.
