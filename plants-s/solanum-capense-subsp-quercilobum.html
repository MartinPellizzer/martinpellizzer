Solanum capense subsp. quercilobum

Solanum capense subsp.

quercilobum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features simple, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant bears orange-red berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Solanaceae family.
