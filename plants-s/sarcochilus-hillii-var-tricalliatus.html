Sarcochilus hillii var. tricalliatus

Sarcochilus hillii var. tricalliatus, commonly known as the Tricalliatus Sarcochilus, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its humid environment.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity.
