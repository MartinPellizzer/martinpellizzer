Salsola souda

Salsola souda, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant has small, green, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a high tolerance for drought.

Salsola souda is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.
