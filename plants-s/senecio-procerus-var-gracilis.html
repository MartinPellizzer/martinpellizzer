Senecio procerus var. gracilis

Senecio procerus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender stems and delicate foliage make it a striking addition to rock gardens.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
