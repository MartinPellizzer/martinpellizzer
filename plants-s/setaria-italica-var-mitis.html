Setaria italica var. mitis

Setaria italica var. mitis, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a small-grained cereal grass native to Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, bristly inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail, hence its common name. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop. Its seeds are rich in carbohydrates and protein, and are used in various traditional foods.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to prevent soil erosion.
