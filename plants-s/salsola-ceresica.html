Salsola ceresica

Salsola ceresica, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its spiny stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive nature in many areas.

Salsola ceresica is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and wastelands.
