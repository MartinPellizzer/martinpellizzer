Solanum chacoense f. plurijugum

Solanum chacoense f. plurijugum, commonly known as the Chaco nightshade, is a rare and unique botanical variant native to the Chaco region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. This particular form is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring multiple ovary chambers and a unique arrangement of stamens. It typically grows in dry, arid environments and has adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a plant of conservation interest.
