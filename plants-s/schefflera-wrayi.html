Schefflera wrayi

Schefflera wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's umbrella plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that are arranged in a distinctive umbrella-like pattern. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are usually glossy and have a deep green color, with a prominent midrib.

Schefflera wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
