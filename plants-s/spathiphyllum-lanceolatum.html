Spathiphyllum lanceolatum

Spathiphyllum lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf peace lily, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, waxy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces striking white spathes that enclose small, greenish-white flowers, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in low-light conditions and requires moderate moisture and well-draining soil.

Spathiphyllum lanceolatum is popular as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive indoors.
