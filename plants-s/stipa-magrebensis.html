Stipa magrebensis

Stipa magrebensis, commonly known as the Maghreb needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Stipa magrebensis is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of local vegetation in its native range.
