Solanum aquartia var. luxurians

Solanum aquartia var. luxurians, commonly known as the luxuriant nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow as it matures.
