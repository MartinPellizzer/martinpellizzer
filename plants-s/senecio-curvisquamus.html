Senecio curvisquamus

Senecio curvisquamus, commonly known as the curlyleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, curly leaves that give it its common name, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.

Senecio curvisquamus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
