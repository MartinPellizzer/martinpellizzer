Saussurea pseudolingulata

Saussurea pseudolingulata, commonly known as the Tibetan yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and southwestern China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations above 3,000 meters. Due to its unique habitat and medicinal properties, it is considered a rare and valuable species in traditional medicine.
