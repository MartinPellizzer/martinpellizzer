Stickmannia perforans

Stickmannia perforans, commonly known as the perforate stickman, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and covered in small, perforated leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are characterized by their distinctive, irregularly shaped holes, giving it a unique appearance.

Stickmannia perforans is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
