Silene gallica var. pauciflora

Silene gallica var. pauciflora, commonly known as the lesser campion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Silene gallica var. pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
