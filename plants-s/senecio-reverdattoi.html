Senecio reverdattoi

Senecio reverdattoi, commonly known as the Mountain Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
