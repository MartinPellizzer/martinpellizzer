Serjania trifoliolata

Serjania trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaved serjania, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves. The plant typically has trifoliate leaves, meaning each leaf is composed of three leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
