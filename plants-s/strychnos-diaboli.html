Strychnos diaboli

Strychnos diaboli, commonly known as the devil's bean, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. The seeds are enclosed in a hard, smooth capsule and are often used in traditional medicine.

However, the plant is toxic, containing compounds like strychnine, which can be harmful to humans and animals.
