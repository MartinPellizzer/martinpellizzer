Saccolabium pallens

Saccolabium pallens, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically oval and have a slightly hairy texture.

Saccolabium pallens is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.
