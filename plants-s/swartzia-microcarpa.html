Swartzia microcarpa

Swartzia microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited swartzia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in furniture and construction.

The species also has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and potential for use in horticulture.
