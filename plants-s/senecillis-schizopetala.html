Senecillis schizopetala

Senecillis schizopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
