Solanum suaveolens var. chalarophyes

Solanum suaveolens var. chalarophyes, commonly known as the sweet-smelling nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often emitting a sweet fragrance at night. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures, containing several seeds within its fleshy pulp.
