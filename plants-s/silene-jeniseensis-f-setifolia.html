Silene jeniseensis f. setifolia

Silene jeniseensis f. setifolia, commonly known as the hairy silene, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pinkish to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions, particularly in eastern Asia. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
