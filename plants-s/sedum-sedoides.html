Sedum sedoides

Sedum sedoides, commonly known as the silver rose, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, pale pink to white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is widely cultivated in regions with mild, dry climates.
