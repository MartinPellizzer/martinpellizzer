Symplocos spicata

Symplocos spicata, commonly known as the spicate beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces striking, dark purple berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Symplocos spicata typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its fragrant foliage and vibrant fruit display.
