Sabina pachyphlaea

Sabina pachyphlaea, commonly known as the desert juniper, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces blue-gray berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Its dense growth habit makes it useful for erosion control and as a ornamental plant in xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its aromatic wood, which has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples.
