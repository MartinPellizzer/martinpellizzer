Sesbania cannabina var. sericea

Sesbania cannabina var. sericea, commonly known as silky sesbania, is a variant of the sesbania genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
