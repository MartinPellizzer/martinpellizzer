Schima wallichii var. superba

Schima wallichii var. superba, commonly known as the Himalayan stone pine, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are leathery and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in catkins. Its cones are woody and contain edible seeds that are often used in local cuisines.

Schima wallichii var. superba is well adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates.
