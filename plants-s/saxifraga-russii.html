Saxifraga russii

Saxifraga russii, commonly known as the Russian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in small, fleshy leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom in early summer.

Saxifraga russii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a garden specimen.
