Smilax kraussiana var. morsoniana

Smilax kraussiana var. morsoniana, commonly known as the Morson's smilax, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of spines, which are typical features of the Smilax genus. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas within tropical forests, where it thrives in the understory vegetation.
