Salvia procurrens var. bicolor

Salvia procurrens var. bicolor, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, two-toned flowers with purple upper petals and white lower ones, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This species is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and pollinator attraction.
