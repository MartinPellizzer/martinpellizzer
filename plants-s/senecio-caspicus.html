Senecio caspicus

Senecio caspicus, commonly known as the Caspian senecio, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. It has linear, hairy leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
