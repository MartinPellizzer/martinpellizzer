Scirpus grossheimii

Scirpus grossheimii, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers at the top of its stem, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are broad and flat, often with a waxy coating that helps it thrive in waterlogged environments.

Scirpus grossheimii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
