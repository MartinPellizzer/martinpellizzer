Silene colorata var. benoistii

Silene colorata var. benoistii, commonly known as the Benoist's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, clustered flowers that are pollinated primarily by insects. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
