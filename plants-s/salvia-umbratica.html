Salvia umbratica

Salvia umbratica, commonly known as the shade sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and traditional medicinal uses.
