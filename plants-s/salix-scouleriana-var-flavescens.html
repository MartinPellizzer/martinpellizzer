Salix scouleriana var. flavescens

Salix scouleriana var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and small, yellowish-green leaves that are oval in shape. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils near streams or wetlands. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to riparian habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing streambanks.
