Solanum densiflorum

Solanum densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
