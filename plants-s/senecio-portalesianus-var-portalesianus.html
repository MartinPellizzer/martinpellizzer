Senecio portalesianus var. portalesianus

Senecio portalesianus var. portalesianus, commonly known as the Portalesian groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun and exhibiting drought tolerance. Its leaves are usually linear to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and the flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in dense clusters.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
