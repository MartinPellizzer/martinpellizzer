Selaginella caesia

Selaginella caesia, commonly known as the silver spruce or lesser clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dense, grass-like foliage and erect, spore-bearing strobila that resemble small cones. Selaginella caesia is characterized by its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
