Sapium paucinervium

Sapium paucinervium, commonly known as the narrow-veined sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with few veins. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. Its bark is often deeply furrowed and has a rough texture.

Sapium paucinervium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
