Sauropus gardnerianus

Sauropus gardnerianus, commonly known as the gardenia hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
