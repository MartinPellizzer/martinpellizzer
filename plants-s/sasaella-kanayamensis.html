Sasaella kanayamensis

Sasaella kanayamensis, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a shrub-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Sasaella kanayamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Japanese culture.
