Styrax vulcanicola

Styrax vulcanicola, commonly known as the volcanic styrax, is a unique plant species native to specific regions with volcanic soil.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its white blossoms.

Styrax vulcanicola thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas near volcanic activity.
