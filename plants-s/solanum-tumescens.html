Solanum tumescens

Solanum tumescens, commonly known as the swollen nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe, contain toxic alkaloids and should not be consumed.

Solanum tumescens is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
