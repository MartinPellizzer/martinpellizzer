Streptocarpus glandulosissimus var. longiflorus

Streptocarpus glandulosissimus var. longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered streptocarpus, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

This species is noted for its ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.
