Solanum sambuciflorum

Solanum sambuciflorum, commonly known as the black nightshade or wild tobacco, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it bears berries that are typically black when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its glandular hairs and the presence of alkaloids, which are common in the Solanaceae family.
