Selaginella guatemalensis

Selaginella guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique life cycle involving both a sporophyte and a gametophyte stage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its morphology includes a creeping stem with small, scale-like leaves and produces cone-like structures for spore dispersal.

Selaginella guatemalensis is valued for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability and its potential in botanical research.
