Salix helvetica var. subconcolor

Salix helvetica var. subconcolor, commonly known as the white willow.

This variant of the white willow is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and slender, drooping branches. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth margin, often turning yellow in the fall.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
