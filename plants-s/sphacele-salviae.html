Sphacele salviae

Sphacele salviae, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, forming whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Sphacele salviae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
