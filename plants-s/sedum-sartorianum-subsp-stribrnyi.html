Sedum sartorianum subsp. stribrnyi

Sedum sartorianum subsp.

stribrnyi, commonly known as the striped stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.
