Sabia purpurea subsp. dumicola

Sabia purpurea subsp.

dumicola, commonly known as the purple sabia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky and calcareous soils, often in dry, sunny environments.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, usually ranging in color from pink to purple.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
