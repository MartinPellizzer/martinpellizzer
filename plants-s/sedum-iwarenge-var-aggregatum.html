Sedum iwarenge var. aggregatum

Sedum iwarenge var. aggregatum, commonly known as the clustered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems that often form dense clusters, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
