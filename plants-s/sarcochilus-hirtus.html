Sarcochilus hirtus

Sarcochilus hirtus, commonly known as the hairy leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a robust, leafy rhizome and produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of white to pale green flowers. The plant's leaves are sword-shaped and often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Sarcochilus hirtus is known for its ornamental value and is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
