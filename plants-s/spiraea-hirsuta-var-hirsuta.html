Spiraea hirsuta var. hirsuta

Spiraea hirsuta var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, upright stems covered in short, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on elongated, slender inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture and a distinctive hairy underside.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
