Sedum anglicum var. rivulare

Sedum anglicum var. rivulare, commonly known as the English stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Sedum anglicum var. rivulare is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
