Strychnos mitscherlichii

Strychnos mitscherlichii, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
