Schizachne purpurascens var. pubescens

Schizachne purpurascens var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy purple grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and inflorescence that consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant color and unique texture.
