Sedum quadrifidum subsp. coccineum

Sedum quadrifidum subsp.

coccineum, commonly known as the red-flowered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It features fleshy, upright stems and leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of vibrant red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and striking floral display make it both ornamental and relatively easy to care for.
