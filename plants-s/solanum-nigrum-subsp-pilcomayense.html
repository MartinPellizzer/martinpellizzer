Solanum nigrum subsp. pilcomayense

Solanum nigrum subsp.

pilcomayense, commonly known as the black nightshade variant, is a herbaceous plant native to South America. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the more widely known Solanum nigrum.
