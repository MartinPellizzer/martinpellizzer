Sisyrinchium pedunculatum

Sisyrinchium pedunculatum, commonly known as the slender blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It is part of the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related plants.

Sisyrinchium pedunculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
