Salix caspica

Salix caspica, commonly known as the Caspian willow, is a species of willow native to Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins as its flowers. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated edge and are often covered in a fine down.

Salix caspica is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in riparian areas.
