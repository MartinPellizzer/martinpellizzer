Stylochaeton lobatum

Stylochaeton lobatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Stylochaeton lobatum is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization due to its deep root system.
