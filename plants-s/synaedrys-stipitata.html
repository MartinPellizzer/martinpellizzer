Synaedrys stipitata

Synaedrys stipitata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Synaedrys stipitata is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some regions.
