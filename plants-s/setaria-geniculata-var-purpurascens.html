Setaria geniculata var. purpurascens

Setaria geniculata var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple finger millet, is a variant of the finger millet species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-colored bracts and elongated, finger-like spikelets. The plant is an annual grass that thrives in well-drained soils and warm climates. Its seeds are small and oval, typically harvested when fully matured.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to marginal agricultural conditions.
