Senecio subpubescens

Senecio subpubescens, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
