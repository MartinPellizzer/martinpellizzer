Solanum bachmannii

Solanum bachmannii, commonly known as Bachmann's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals, and it produces small, bright red berries that are edible but can be toxic in large quantities.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
