Siparuna amplifolia

Siparuna amplifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or pink hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with distinctive pink or red veins. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects and birds, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.

Siparuna amplifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
