Scutellaria stewartii

Scutellaria stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's blueflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Scutellaria stewartii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
