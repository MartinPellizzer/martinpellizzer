Scirpus triangularis

Scirpus triangularis, commonly known as the triangular bulrush, is a tall, perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It has narrow, triangular leaves that are typically green and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that appear in late summer. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus triangularis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
