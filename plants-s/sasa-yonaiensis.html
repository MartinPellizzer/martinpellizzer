Sasa yonaiensis

Sasa yonaiensis, commonly known as the Yonai bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly bluish-green color.

Sasa yonaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest understories.
