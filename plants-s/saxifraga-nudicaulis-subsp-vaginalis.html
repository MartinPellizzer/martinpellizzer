Saxifraga nudicaulis subsp. vaginalis

Saxifraga nudicaulis subsp.

vaginalis, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically features rosettes of closely packed, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer, arranged in a terminal cluster. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for high-altitude habitats where few plants can survive.
