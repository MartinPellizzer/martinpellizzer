Spongotrichum suffruticosum

Spongotrichum suffruticosum, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, often spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Spongotrichum suffruticosum is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
