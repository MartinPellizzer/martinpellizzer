Syngonanthus xeranthemoides var. alpinus

Syngonanthus xeranthemoides var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine synogonanthus, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine tundra habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
