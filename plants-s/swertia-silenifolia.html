Swertia silenifolia

Swertia silenifolia, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, while the flowers are tubular with five lobes.

Swertia silenifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
