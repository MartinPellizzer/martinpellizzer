Senna sophera var. purpurea

Senna sophera var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pairs of opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a subtle purple tinge. It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are typically used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
