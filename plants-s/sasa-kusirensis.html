Sasa kusirensis

Sasa kusirensis, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or Japanese sasa, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often used in traditional Japanese landscapes for its ornamental value. Sasa kusirensis thrives in shaded, moist environments and is commonly found in forested areas.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense thickets.
