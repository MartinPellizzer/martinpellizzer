Solidago virgaurea proles minuta

Solidago virgaurea proles minuta, commonly known as the lesser goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
