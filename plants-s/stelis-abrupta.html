Stelis abrupta

Stelis abrupta, commonly known as the abrupt stelis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and often produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, showcasing its epiphytic nature. Its leaves are usually clustered at the base, and the inflorescence is short, with flowers emerging from the axils of the leaves.

Stelis abrupta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
