Stemonurus crassipes

Stemonurus crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved stemonurus, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and possesses broad, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
