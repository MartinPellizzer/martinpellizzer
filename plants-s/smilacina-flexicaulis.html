Smilacina flexicaulis

Smilacina flexicaulis, commonly known as the flexible greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically alternate and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Smilacina flexicaulis thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with other understory plants.

Its botanical features make it an important component of forest ecosystems.
