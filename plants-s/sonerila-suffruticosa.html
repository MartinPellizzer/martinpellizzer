Sonerila suffruticosa

Sonerila suffruticosa, commonly known as the forest ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical rainforests, often as an epiphyte on trees. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, and it has a creeping root system that helps it spread.

Sonerila suffruticosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
