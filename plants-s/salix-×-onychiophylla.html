Salix × onychiophylla

Salix × onychiophylla, commonly known as the silver willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus, Salix.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture, while its catkins are a key feature in its reproductive cycle.

Salix × onychiophylla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
