Solanum nigrum var. rigidum

Solanum nigrum var. rigidum, commonly known as black nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

The flowers are typically yellow with a white or purple corolla, and the plant is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
