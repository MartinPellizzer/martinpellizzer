Schrebera platyphylla

Schrebera platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf schrebera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a flat, fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by ornamental, round fruits. Schrebera platyphylla is often cultivated for its unique foliage and ornamental value in shaded garden settings.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
