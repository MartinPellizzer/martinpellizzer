Setaria setosa var. leiophylla

Setaria setosa var. leiophylla, commonly known as silky foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence with long, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

This variety is often cultivated as a forage crop due to its nutritional value for livestock.
