Seruneum lanceolatum

Seruneum lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate seruneum, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Seruneum lanceolatum prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a desirable ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
