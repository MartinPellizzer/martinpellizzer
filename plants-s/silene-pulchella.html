Silene pulchella

Silene pulchella, commonly known as the showy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene pulchella produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
