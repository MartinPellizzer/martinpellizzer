Sebestena indica

Sebestena indica, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, citrus-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. Its botanical features include a woody stem, hairy young shoots, and a distinctive aroma.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement due to its high nutritional value.
