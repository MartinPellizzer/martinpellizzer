Searsia simii

Searsia simii, commonly known as the silver tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Searsia simii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture.
