Sorghastrum stipoides subsp. agrostoides

Sorghastrum stipoides subsp.

agrostoides, commonly known as blue grama, is a grass species native to the central and southern United States. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a common component of native grasslands.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or blue hues.

Blue grama is well adapted to drought conditions and is an important forage plant for wildlife and livestock.
