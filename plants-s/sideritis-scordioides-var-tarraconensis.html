Sideritis scordioides var. tarraconensis

Sideritis scordioides var. tarraconensis, commonly known as the Tarraconense Sideritis, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
