Silene caffra

Silene caffra, commonly known as the African catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Silene caffra has slender stems and small, alternate leaves, and it produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

It is often found in grasslands and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
