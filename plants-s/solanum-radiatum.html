Solanum radiatum

Solanum radiatum, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinct, often lobed shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, fleshy berries, which are typically orange or red when ripe. Solanum radiatum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It contains toxic compounds such as solanine, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals if ingested.
