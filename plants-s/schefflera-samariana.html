Schefflera samariana

Schefflera samariana, commonly known as the Samarian umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like crown formed by its clustered, palmate leaves. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets radiating from a central point, giving it a lush, full appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Schefflera samariana is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of light conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
