Saxifraga magellanica var. peruviana

Saxifraga magellanica var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
