Salvia officinalis var. rubriflora

Salvia officinalis var. rubriflora, commonly known as red-flowered sage, is a cultivar of common sage characterized by its striking red-purple flower spikes.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has some culinary and medicinal applications.
