Stellera incana

Stellera incana, commonly known as the hoary bellflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in woodland or open areas. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Stellera incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
