Satureja alpina subsp. granatensis

Satureja alpina subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as Alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its strong, peppery scent.

This subspecies is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
