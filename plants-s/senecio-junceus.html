Senecio junceus

Senecio junceus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Senecio junceus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
