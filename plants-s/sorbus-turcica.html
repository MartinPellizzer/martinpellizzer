Sorbus turcica

Sorbus turcica, commonly known as the Turkish whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Sorbus turcica is often found in forest edges, rocky areas, and along riverbanks.
