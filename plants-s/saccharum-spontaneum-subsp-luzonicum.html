Saccharum spontaneum subsp. luzonicum

Saccharum spontaneum subsp.

luzonicum, commonly known as the Luzon grass, is a perennial grass native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies is often found in wetland and riparian habitats, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
