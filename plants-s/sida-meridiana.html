Sida meridiana

Sida meridiana, commonly known as desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fibrous stems and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Sida meridiana is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in dry landscapes.
