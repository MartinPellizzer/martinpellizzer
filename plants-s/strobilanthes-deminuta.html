Strobilanthes deminuta

Strobilanthes deminuta, commonly known as the "dwarf flame of the forest," is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright orange-red flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and seasonal blooming, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
