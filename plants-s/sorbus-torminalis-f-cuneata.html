Sorbus torminalis f. cuneata

Sorbus torminalis f. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a cultivar of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
