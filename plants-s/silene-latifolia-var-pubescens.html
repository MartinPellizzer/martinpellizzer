Silene latifolia var. pubescens

Silene latifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Silene latifolia var. pubescens is an annual plant, completing its life cycle within one growing season.
