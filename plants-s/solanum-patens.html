Solanum patens

Solanum patens, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by egg-shaped berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Solanum patens is often found in disturbed areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
