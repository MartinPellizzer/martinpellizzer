Stelis corae

Stelis corae, commonly known as the coral spike orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient water retention.

Stelis corae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
