Salvia campanulata var. fissa

Salvia campanulata var. fissa, commonly known as the split bell sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are typically upright and branching. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The 'fissa' variety is distinguished by its split petals, which give it a unique and ornamental appearance.
