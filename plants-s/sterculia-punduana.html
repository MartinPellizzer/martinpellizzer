Sterculia punduana

Sterculia punduana, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing flat, oval seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.

The tree is also culturally significant in many African communities.
