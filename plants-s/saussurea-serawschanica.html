Saussurea serawschanica

Saussurea serawschanica, commonly known as the Karakoram saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flower heads composed of numerous small florets. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
