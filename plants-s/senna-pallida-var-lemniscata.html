Senna pallida var. lemniscata

Senna pallida var. lemniscata, commonly known as the golden senna, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy habitats.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
