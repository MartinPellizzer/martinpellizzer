Smilax sarsaparilla

Smilax sarsaparilla, commonly known as yellow smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, hooked thorns and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its root system is extensive and can be used in traditional medicine.

The plant is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in partial shade.
