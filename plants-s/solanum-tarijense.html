Solanum tarijense

Solanum tarijense, commonly known as the Tarijense nightshade, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is adapted to grow in dry, mountainous regions and has a relatively short growing season.

Due to its potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and ethnobotry researchers.
