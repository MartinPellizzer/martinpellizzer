Salvia sarawschanica

Salvia sarawschanica, commonly known as the Sarawak sage, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Salvia sarawschanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
