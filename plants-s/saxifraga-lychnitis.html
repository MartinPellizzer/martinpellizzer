Saxifraga lychnitis

Saxifraga lychnitis, commonly known as the candle wax saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are oval to ovate, with a waxy texture and a slightly serrated margin.

Saxifraga lychnitis thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
