Salvadora glabra

Salvadora glabra, commonly known as the Indian toothbrush plant, is a shrub native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is well adapted to dry climates, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy soils. It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is known for its medicinal properties.

Salvadora glabra is often used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
