Senecio farfarifolius var. ramosus

Senecio farfarifolius var. ramosus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of soft, fuzzy, silver-green leaves that form a dense, low-growing structure. The plant produces showy clusters of yellow flowers with prominent white centers, which bloom in spring and summer. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, also known as the daisy or sunflower family.

This variety is particularly noted for its branched growth habit, which distinguishes it from other varieties of Senecio farfarifolius.
