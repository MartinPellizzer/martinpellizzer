Sterculia stipularis

Sterculia stipularis, commonly known as the African mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring a rough, bark-like trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.
