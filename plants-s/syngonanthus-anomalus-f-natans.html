Syngonanthus anomalus f. natans

Syngonanthus anomalus f. natans, commonly known as the floating bamboo, is a unique variant of the bamboo plant that exhibits a distinct aquatic adaptation.

This fern-like plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to float on the surface of water, making it well-suited for wetland environments. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that aids in water retention. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshes or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
