Saussurea gilesii

Saussurea gilesii, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron.

It is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Tibet, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense clusters of yellowish-orange flower heads. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.

Saussurea gilesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some Asian cultures.
