Sida interstans

Sida interstans, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Sida interstans is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
