Setaria pratensis

Setaria pratensis, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive, finger-like inflorescences that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, golden-yellow spikelets that are often used as forage for livestock. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Setaria pratensis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open spaces, making it both a valuable forage crop and a potential weed.
