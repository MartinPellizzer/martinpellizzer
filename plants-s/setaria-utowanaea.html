Setaria utowanaea

Setaria utowanaea, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grass species. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
