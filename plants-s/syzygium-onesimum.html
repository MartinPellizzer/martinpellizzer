Syzygium onesimum

Syzygium onesimum, commonly known as the white cempaka, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and glossy in appearance. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Syzygium onesimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
