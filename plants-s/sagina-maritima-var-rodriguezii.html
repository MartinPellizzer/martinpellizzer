Sagina maritima var. rodriguezii

Sagina maritima var. rodriguezii, commonly known as the Rodriguez's sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure. The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces tiny white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in dunes and along shorelines, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
