Sorbus parumlobata

Sorbus parumlobata, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries that are edible for birds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions, showcasing its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
