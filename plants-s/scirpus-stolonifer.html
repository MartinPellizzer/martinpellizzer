Scirpus stolonifer

Scirpus stolonifer, commonly known as the creeping bulrush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into brown, cylindrical seed heads. It spreads primarily through rhizomes and stolons, allowing it to colonize large areas quickly.

Scirpus stolonifer plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic wildlife.
