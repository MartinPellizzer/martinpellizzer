Saxifraga styriaca

Saxifraga styriaca, commonly known as the Styrian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.

Saxifraga styriaca is also noted for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
