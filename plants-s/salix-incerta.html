Salix incerta

Salix incerta, commonly known as the uncertain willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender branches, heart-shaped leaves, and catkin-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, often shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Salix incerta is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
