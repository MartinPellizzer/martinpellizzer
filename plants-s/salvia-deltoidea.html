Salvia deltoidea

Salvia deltoidea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia deltoidea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
