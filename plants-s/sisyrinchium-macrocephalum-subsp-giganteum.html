Sisyrinchium macrocephalum subsp. giganteum

Sisyrinchium macrocephalum subsp.

giganteum, commonly known as the giant blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features showy, blue to violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which are arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and native plant restoration projects.
