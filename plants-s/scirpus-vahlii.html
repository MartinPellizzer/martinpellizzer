Scirpus vahlii

Scirpus vahlii, commonly known as the Vahl's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Scirpus vahlii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
