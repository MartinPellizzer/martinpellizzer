Sambucus callicarpa

Sambucus callicarpa, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant belongs to the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are typically red or purple in color.

The berries are toxic when raw but can be used in jams and wines after proper preparation.
