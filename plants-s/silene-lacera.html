Silene lacera

Silene lacera, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ability to retain moisture.

Silene lacera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
