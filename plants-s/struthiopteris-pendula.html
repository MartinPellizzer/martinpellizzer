Struthiopteris pendula

Struthiopteris pendula, commonly known as the hanging fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with fertile fronds that produce spores on the underside. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Struthiopteris pendula thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
