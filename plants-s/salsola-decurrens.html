Salsola decurrens

Salsola decurrens, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is an annual plant native to Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and saline environments due to its deep root system and water-storing tissues.

Salsola decurrens is often considered a weed but plays a role in stabilizing soils in disturbed areas.
