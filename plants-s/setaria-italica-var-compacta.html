Setaria italica var. compacta

Setaria italica var. compacta, commonly known as millet grass, is a compact, annual grass species native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces small, pale green or golden spikelets that contain the edible grains. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy crop in arid regions.

Due to its nutritional value and adaptability, it is cultivated for both food and forage.
