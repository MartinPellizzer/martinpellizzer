Senecio hispidulus

Senecio hispidulus, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the cooler months.

Senecio hispidulus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
