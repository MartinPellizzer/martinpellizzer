Senegalia sericea

Senegalia sericea, commonly known as silky mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy foliage and long, slender pods that contain seeds. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy or rocky soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.

Senegalia sericea is also valued for its ecological resilience and traditional uses in various indigenous cultures.
