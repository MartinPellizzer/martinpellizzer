Scutellaria orientalis var. severzovii

Scutellaria orientalis var. severzovii, commonly known as the Eastern skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features erect, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
