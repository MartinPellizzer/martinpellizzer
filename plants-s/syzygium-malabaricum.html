Syzygium malabaricum

Syzygium malabaricum, commonly known as the Java apple or Malabar chestnut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small apples. The fruit has a thin, leathery skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp with a mild flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
