Sorbus hupehensis var. hupehensis

Sorbus hupehensis var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers in the spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to urban environments and resistance to pollution.
