Statice pedicellata

Statice pedicellata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in spikes, with each flower having four petals and a central cluster of stamens.

Statice pedicellata is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and vibrant color.
