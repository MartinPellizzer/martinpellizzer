Spiraea betulifolia f. glabra

Spiraea betulifolia f. glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf weeping willow spirea, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches that bear clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth, glabrous surface, giving the plant its specific epithet "glabra.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
