Scorzonera lasiospora

Scorzonera lasiospora, commonly known as the hairy scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates.

Scorzonera lasiospora is often found in meadows and rocky areas, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
