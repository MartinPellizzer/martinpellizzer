Sphaeranthus peduncularis subsp. rogersii

Sphaeranthus peduncularis subsp.

rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual with distinctive globular flower heads that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
