Savia actephila

Savia actephila, commonly known as the mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in alpine meadows and is part of the Cyperaceae family.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
