Solidago polyphylla

Solidago polyphylla, commonly known as the rough goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are alternate, rough in texture, and have a distinctive serrated margin.

Solidago polyphylla plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
