Salix hastata f. macrostachya

Salix hastata f. macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stamened willow, is a variant of the silky willow.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves and its showy, yellowish-green catkins. This plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks. Its botanical features include slender, often arching stems and a preference for temperate climates.

The variety is notable for its larger stamens, which contribute to its unique appearance and ecological role.
