Streptocarpus paniculatus

Streptocarpus paniculatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Streptocarpus paniculatus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
