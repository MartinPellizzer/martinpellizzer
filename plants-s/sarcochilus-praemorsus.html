Sarcochilus praemorsus

Sarcochilus praemorsus, commonly known as the Death's-head orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in arid environments. The plant produces striking, dark purple to black flowers with bright yellow sepals and petals. It blooms during the summer months, typically from November to February.

Sarcochilus praemorsus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
