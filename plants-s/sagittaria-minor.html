Sagittaria minor

Sagittaria minor, commonly known as lesser arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the mud. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in a spike, often appearing in late summer.

Sagittaria minor plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
