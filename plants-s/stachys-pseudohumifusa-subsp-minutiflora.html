Stachys pseudohumifusa subsp. minutiflora

Stachys pseudohumifusa subsp.

minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered betony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the upper stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
