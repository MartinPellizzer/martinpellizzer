Schoenus × intermedius

Schoenus × intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate sedge, is a hybrid plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes are usually arranged in dense clusters, and the flowers are small and inconspicuous, typical of many sedges.

Schoenus × intermedius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
