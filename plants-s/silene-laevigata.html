Silene laevigata

Silene laevigata, commonly known as the smooth catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds.

Silene laevigata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
