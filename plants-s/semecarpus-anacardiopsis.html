Semecarpus anacardiopsis

Semecarpus anacardiopsis, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes other economically important plants like the cashew and mango. The tree is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnately arranged. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in panicles, and it produces woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
