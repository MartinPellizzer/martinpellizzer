Solanum coxii

Solanum coxii, commonly known as the Coxi's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round berries that are usually yellow or orange when mature. It is often found in rocky or forested habitats at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
