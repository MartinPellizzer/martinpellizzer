Stachys tenuifolia var. hispida

Stachys tenuifolia var. hispida, commonly known as hispid stachys, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its overall rugged, drought-tolerant appearance.

It is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
