Sinningia × cerina

Sinningia × cerina, commonly known as the flamingo flower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to South America and is prized for its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its natural habitat. It produces trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Sinningia × cerina is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
