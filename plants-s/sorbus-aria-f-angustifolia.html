Sorbus aria f. angustifolia

Sorbus aria f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a botanical variant of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called pomes, which are usually red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
