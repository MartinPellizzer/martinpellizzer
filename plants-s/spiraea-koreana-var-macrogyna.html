Spiraea koreana var. macrogyna

Spiraea koreana var. macrogyna, commonly known as Korean weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It features showy, fluffy panicles of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a weeping appearance, making it ornamental. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and turn yellow in autumn.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.
