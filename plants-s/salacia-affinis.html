Salacia affinis

Salacia affinis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has elliptical leaves with a smooth, dark green surface and produces small, white flowers in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional properties.

The bark and leaves are also used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.
