Syzygium aromaticum

Syzygium aromaticum, commonly known as clove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dark red flower buds, which are harvested and dried to produce the spice cloves. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its aromatic properties. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Clove is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
