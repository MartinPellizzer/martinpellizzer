Silphium quadrangulum

Silphium quadrangulum, commonly known as the four-cornered silphium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Silphium quadrangulum is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
