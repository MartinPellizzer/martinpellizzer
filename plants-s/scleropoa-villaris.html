Scleropoa villaris

Scleropoa villaris, commonly known as the Villar's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, showing drought tolerance and a deep root system.

Scleropoa villaris is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
