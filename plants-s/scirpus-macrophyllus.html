Scirpus macrophyllus

Scirpus macrophyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Scirpus macrophyllus thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomes contribute to soil stabilization and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
