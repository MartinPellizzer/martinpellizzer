Semecarpus paucinervius

Semecarpus paucinervius, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or bael tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with a few veins, giving it the species name *paucinervius*. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed.
