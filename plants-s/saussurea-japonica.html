Saussurea japonica

Saussurea japonica, commonly known as Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
