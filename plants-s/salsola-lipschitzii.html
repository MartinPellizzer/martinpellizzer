Salsola lipschitzii

Salsola lipschitzii, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low precipitation and high temperatures. Its succulent leaves store water, aiding in drought tolerance.

Salsola lipschitzii plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
