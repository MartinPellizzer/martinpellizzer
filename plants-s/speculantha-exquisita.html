Speculantha exquisita

Speculantha exquisita, commonly known as the rare velvet orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

This plant is characterized by its velvety, dark purple petals and a unique, intricate floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often found in cloud forest ecosystems. The plant's flowers bloom in late spring, producing a subtle fragrance that enhances its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, Speculantha exquisita is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
