Senecio stanleyi

Senecio stanleyi, commonly known as Stanley's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Stanley's groundsel is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
