Sisyranthus compactus

Sisyranthus compactus, commonly known as the compact spider orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wet tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and intricate, spider-like flower structure. The plant typically produces small, pale green to white flowers that are highly specialized for pollination by specific insect species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or near water bodies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
