Serapias orientalis var. siciliensis

Serapias orientalis var. siciliensis, commonly known as the Sicilian marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, thriving in moist, calcareous soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive hooded structure and spurred petals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
