Solanum aturense

Solanum aturense, commonly known as the Aturenese nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
