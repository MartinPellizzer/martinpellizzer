Sedum pentandrum

Sedum pentandrum, commonly known as the five-stamen sedum, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually green and slightly fleshy, aiding in water retention.

Sedum pentandrum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
