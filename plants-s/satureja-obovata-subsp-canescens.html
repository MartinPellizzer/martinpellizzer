Satureja obovata subsp. canescens

Satureja obovata subsp.

canescens, commonly known as hairy woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Satureja obovata subsp.

canescens is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
