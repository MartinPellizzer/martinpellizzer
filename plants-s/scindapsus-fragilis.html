Scindapsus fragilis

Scindapsus fragilis, commonly known as the silver vine, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing or trailing plant characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by bright red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers moderate to high humidity.

Scindapsus fragilis is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
