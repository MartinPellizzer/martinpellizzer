Syzygium clavellatum

Syzygium clavellatum, commonly known as the satinwood or white sand apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is small, round, and has a smooth, waxy skin, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has economic importance in certain regions.
