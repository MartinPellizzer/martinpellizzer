Sarcochilus bancroftii

Sarcochilus bancroftii, commonly known as the Banks' sarcochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, narrow leaf and produces striking, fragrant flowers with yellow petals and a deep red lip. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil. It is known for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Sarcochilus bancroftii is a rare species that is sensitive to environmental changes.
