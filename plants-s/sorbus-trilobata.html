Sorbus trilobata

Sorbus trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, three-lobed leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange to red berries in the autumn. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it often grows in rocky or wooded areas.

Sorbus trilobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
