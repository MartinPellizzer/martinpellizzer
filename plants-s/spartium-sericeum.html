Spartium sericeum

Spartium sericeum, commonly known as silverbush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Spartium sericeum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
