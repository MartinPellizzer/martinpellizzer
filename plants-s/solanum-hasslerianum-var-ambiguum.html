Solanum hasslerianum var. ambiguum

Solanum hasslerianum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy stems and leaves. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral appearance.
