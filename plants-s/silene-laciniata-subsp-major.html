Silene laciniata subsp. major

Silene laciniata subsp.

major, commonly known as the greater fringed gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins, and it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It is pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
