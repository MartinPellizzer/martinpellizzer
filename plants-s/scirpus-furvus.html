Scirpus furvus

Scirpus furvus, commonly known as the brown bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are usually reddish-brown in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they often have a fibrous texture.

Scirpus furvus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
