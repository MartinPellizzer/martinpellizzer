Selaginella heteromorpha

Selaginella heteromorpha, commonly known as the variable spikemoss, is a member of the family Selaginellaceae and is native to various regions in North America.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique life cycle involving both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a creeping stem with whorled leaves. Its sporophyte produces spores on specialized structures called strobila, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

This species is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it an important component of certain ecosystems.
