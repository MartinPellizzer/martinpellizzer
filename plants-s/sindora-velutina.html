Sindora velutina

Sindora velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
