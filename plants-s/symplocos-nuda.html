Symplocos nuda

Symplocos nuda, commonly known as the naked symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
