Scutellaria oxyphylla

Scutellaria oxyphylla, commonly known as the mountain blueflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with two lips, typical of the genus Scutellaria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Scutellaria oxyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
