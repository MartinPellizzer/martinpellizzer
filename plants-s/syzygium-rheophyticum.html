Syzygium rheophyticum

Syzygium rheophyticum, commonly known as the cliff apple or rainforest apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky slopes or cliffs. It produces small, white flowers followed by edible, yellow or red fruits.

Syzygium rheophyticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in rainforest ecosystems.
