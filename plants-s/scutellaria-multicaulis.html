Scutellaria multicaulis

Scutellaria multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Scutellaria multicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
