Shawia glutinosa

Shawia glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that produce a sticky resin, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a glossy green surface and serrated edges.

Shawia glutinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
