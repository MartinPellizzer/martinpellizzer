Statice scabrida

Statice scabrida, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant is highly salt-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh coastal environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Statice scabrida is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
