Senecio repandus

Senecio repandus, commonly known as the trailing senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio repandus is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its low-growing habit and ornamental appeal.
