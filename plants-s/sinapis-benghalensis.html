Sinapis benghalensis

Sinapis benghalensis, commonly known as Indian mustard or wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pods that contain several rounded seeds. It is native to South and Southeast Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in many regions but also has traditional medicinal uses.
