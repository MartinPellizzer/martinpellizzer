Sideritis aranensis

Sideritis aranensis, commonly known as the Canary Island germander, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Sideritis aranensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
