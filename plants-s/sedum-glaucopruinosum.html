Sedum glaucopruinosum

Sedum glaucopruinosum, commonly known as the silver-stem sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems covered in a powdery white coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
