Styrax austromexicanus

Styrax austromexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing ornamental value in gardens.

Styrax austromexicanus is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
