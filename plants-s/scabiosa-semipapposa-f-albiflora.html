Scabiosa semipapposa f. albiflora

Scabiosa semipapposa f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower scabious, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a soft, ethereal appearance to gardens. Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
