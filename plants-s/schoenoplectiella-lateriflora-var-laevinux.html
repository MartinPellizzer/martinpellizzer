Schoenoplectiella lateriflora var. laevinux

Schoenoplectiella lateriflora var. laevinux, commonly known as the slender flowering bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, reed-like stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and more delicate appearance compared to other varieties of Schoenoplectiella.
