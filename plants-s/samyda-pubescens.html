Samyda pubescens

Samyda pubescens, commonly known as the silky senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant features compound leaves with a soft, hairy texture, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated, fibrous pods that split open when mature to release the seeds.
