Stephanostachys tepejilote

Stephanostachys tepejilote, commonly known as the Mexican grass or tepesilote, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, arching stems. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
