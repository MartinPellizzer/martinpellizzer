Salvia × fuschii

Salvia × fuschii, commonly known as the scarlet salvia, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States, particularly Texas and New Mexico, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color. Its most striking feature is its showy spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers, typically in shades of red, purple, or pink.

Salvia × fuschii is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
