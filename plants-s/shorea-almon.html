Shorea almon

Shorea almon, commonly known as almon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and develops hard, heavy fruits with winged seeds. Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
