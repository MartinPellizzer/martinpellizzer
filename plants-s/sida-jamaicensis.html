Sida jamaicensis

Sida jamaicensis, commonly known as white soda or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by a distinctive leaf shape with serrated edges. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Sida jamaicensis is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
