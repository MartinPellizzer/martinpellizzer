Statice gussoneana

Statice gussoneana, commonly known as the sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, saline soils and is often found in dune environments. Its ability to tolerate salt spray and strong winds makes it a hardy species suitable for coastal landscaping.

Statice gussoneana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in floral arrangements.
