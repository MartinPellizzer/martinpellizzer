Salvia modesta var. brachyantha

Salvia modesta var. brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine down. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet or blue, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it both ornamental and beneficial to pollinators.
