Smilacina fusca var. pilosa

Smilacina fusca var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Smilacina fusca var. pilosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
