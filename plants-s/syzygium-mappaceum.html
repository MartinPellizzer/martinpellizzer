Syzygium mappaceum

Syzygium mappaceum, commonly known as the red sanders, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has a dense, interlocked grain that makes it highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soils.

Due to overharvesting, it is now listed as an endangered species, prompting conservation efforts to protect its natural habitat.
