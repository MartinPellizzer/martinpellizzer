Salix × rubens f. basfordiana

Salix × rubens f. basfordiana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its distinctive cascading branches and delicate, narrow leaves.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found near water bodies. The plant's botanical features include its catkin-like flowers, which appear in early spring, and its ability to produce extensive root systems. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and erosion control.
