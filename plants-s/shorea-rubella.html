Shorea rubella

Shorea rubella, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its durable, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are woody capsules that release seeds when mature.

Shorea rubella is valued for its high-quality timber and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
