Solidago scaberrima

Solidago scaberrima, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color, and it thrives in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. It is a hardy species that is often used in native plant gardens due to its ecological benefits and ornamental value.

Solidago scaberrima plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
