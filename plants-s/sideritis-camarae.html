Sideritis camarae

Sideritis camarae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sideritis camarae is valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
