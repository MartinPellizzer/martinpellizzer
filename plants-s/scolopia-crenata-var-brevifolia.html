Scolopia crenata var. brevifolia

Scolopia crenata var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a waxy coating and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural research.
