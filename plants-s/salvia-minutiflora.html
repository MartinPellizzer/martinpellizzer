Salvia minutiflora

Salvia minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf sage or Mexican mint, is a perennial herb native to Central America and the southern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardening.
