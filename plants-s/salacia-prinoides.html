Salacia prinoides

Salacia prinoides, commonly known as the golden flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is climbing and has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.
