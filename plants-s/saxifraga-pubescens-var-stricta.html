Saxifraga pubescens var. stricta

Saxifraga pubescens var. stricta, commonly known as the hairy saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pink. The leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in mountainous areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
