Senecio johnstonii subsp. cheranganiensis

Senecio johnstonii subsp.

cheranganiensis, commonly known as the Cherangani's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to East Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
