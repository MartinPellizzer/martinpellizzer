Senecio leibergii

Senecio leibergii, commonly known as the ice plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

Senecio leibergii is also known for its ability to form dense mats, making it useful for erosion control in sandy soils.
