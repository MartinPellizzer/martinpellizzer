Senecio lopollensis

Senecio lopollensis, commonly known as the Loppi's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
