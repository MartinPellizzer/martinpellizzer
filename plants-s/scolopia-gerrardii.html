Scolopia gerrardii

Scolopia gerrardii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine white or gray hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Scolopia gerrardii is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks, making it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
