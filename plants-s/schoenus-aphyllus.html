Schoenus aphyllus

Schoenus aphyllus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It lacks true leaves, hence the name "aphyllus," and has a fibrous root system.

Schoenus aphyllus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
