Sedum telephium var. rhodanense

Sedum telephium var. rhodanense, commonly known as the red stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion.
