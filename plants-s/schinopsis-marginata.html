Schinopsis marginata

Schinopsis marginata, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance, while its wood is highly valued for its durability and workability.

Schinopsis marginata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and savannas.
