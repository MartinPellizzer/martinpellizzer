Sarcochilus anceps

Sarcochilus anceps, commonly known as the Bornean helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique helmet-shaped flower, which is typically green with a yellowish or reddish lip. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, erect stem with a few leaves. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Sarcochilus anceps is often found in forest undergrowth, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments.
