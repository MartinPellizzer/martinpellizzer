Selaginella jamaicensis

Selaginella jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican spikemoss, is a vascular fern that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, ground-covering plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with upright, spiky stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate sporophylls, which are specialized leaves.

Selaginella jamaicensis is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
