Scirpus cespitosus subsp. austriacus

Scirpus cespitosus subsp.

austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines.

The plant has tall, cylindrical stems with narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. Its inflorescence consists of spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in water filtration and habitat provision for aquatic life.
