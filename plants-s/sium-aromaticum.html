Sium aromaticum

Sium aromaticum, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to wetlands and moist areas of North America and Eurasia. The plant has a distinct, aromatic scent and features compound leaves with a fern-like appearance. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

The root is edible but often has a strong, unpleasant taste.
