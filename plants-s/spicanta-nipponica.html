Spicanta nipponica

Spicanta nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Spicanta nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
