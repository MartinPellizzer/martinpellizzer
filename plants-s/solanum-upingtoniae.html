Solanum upingtoniae

Solanum upingtoniae, commonly known as the Upington's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its berries, which are toxic, are usually red when mature and contain seeds.

Solanum upingtoniae is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
