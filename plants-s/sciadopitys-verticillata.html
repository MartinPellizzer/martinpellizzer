Sciadopitys verticillata

Sciadopitys verticillata, commonly known as the Japanese umbrella pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its unique, umbrella-like crown formed by clusters of long, drooping branches. The tree has scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its cones are woody and typically borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Sciadopitys verticillata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
