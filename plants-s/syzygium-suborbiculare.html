Syzygium suborbiculare

Syzygium suborbiculare, commonly known as the red-berried tamarind.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Syzygium suborbiculare thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
