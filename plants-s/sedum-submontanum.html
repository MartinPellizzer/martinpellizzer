Sedum submontanum

Sedum submontanum, commonly known as the mountain sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sedum submontanum is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
