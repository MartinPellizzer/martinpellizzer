Sulcorebutia mizquensis

Sulcorebutia mizquensis, commonly known as the Mizquense Cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed structure. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, featuring succulent stems that store water and spines for protection.

Sulcorebutia mizquensis is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
