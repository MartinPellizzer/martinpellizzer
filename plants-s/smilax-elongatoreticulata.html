Smilax elongatoreticulata

Smilax elongatoreticulata, commonly known as the reticulate smilax, is a vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, alternate leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swamps and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ecological role in providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
