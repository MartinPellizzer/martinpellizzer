Senna hirsuta var. acuminata

Senna hirsuta var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Senna hirsuta var. acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
