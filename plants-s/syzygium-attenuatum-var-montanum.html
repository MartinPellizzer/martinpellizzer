Syzygium attenuatum var. montanum

Syzygium attenuatum var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain apple or mountain syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
