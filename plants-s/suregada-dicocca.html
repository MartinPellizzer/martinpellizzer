Suregada dicocca

Suregada dicocca, commonly known as the two-seed fig, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, green fruits. The fruit turns reddish when ripe and contains two seeds, hence its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
