Sasa tanzawana var. hirtella

Sasa tanzawana var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa tanzawana species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming evergreen bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems with a distinctive hairy texture on its leaves, giving it its common name. Its foliage is dark green and densely arranged, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to shaded conditions.
