Serratula pedunculata

Serratula pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered serratula, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Serratula pedunculata is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
