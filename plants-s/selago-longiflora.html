Selago longiflora

Selago longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered selago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Selago longiflora is typically found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
