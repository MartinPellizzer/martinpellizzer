Scilla rigidifolia var. gerrardii

Scilla rigidifolia var. gerrardii, commonly known as the Gerrard's squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its rigid, upright stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is sometimes cultivated in controlled environments.
