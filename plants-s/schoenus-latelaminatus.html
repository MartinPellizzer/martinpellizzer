Schoenus latelaminatus

Schoenus latelaminatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedgeland grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it forms part of the vegetation community. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet with small, inconspicuous flowers.

Schoenus latelaminatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
