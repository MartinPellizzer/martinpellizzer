Salix lanata f. depolita

Salix lanata f. depolita, commonly known as the silky willow or woolly willow, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-white pubescent leaves that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
