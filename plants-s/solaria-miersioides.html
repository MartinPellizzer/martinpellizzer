Solaria miersioides

Solaria miersioides, commonly known as the Miers' sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solaria miersioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
