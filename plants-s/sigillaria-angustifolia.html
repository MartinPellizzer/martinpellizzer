Sigillaria angustifolia

Sigillaria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sigillaria, is a fossilized plant from the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, and segmented structure, which suggests it was a type of ancient fern-like plant. The plant's leaves were likely arranged in a spiral pattern, and its stems showed a complex system of vascular tissue. Sigillaria angustifolia is often found in coal deposits and is used by paleobotanists to study the evolution of early land plants.

Its morphology provides insights into the ecological conditions of the Carboniferous environment.
