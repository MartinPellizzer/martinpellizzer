Salix cinerea var. catalaunica

Salix cinerea var. catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalaunica willow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its grayish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands. It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are important for pollination.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
