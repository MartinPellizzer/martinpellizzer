Salix macrocarpa var. argentea

Salix macrocarpa var. argentea, commonly known as the silver weeping willow, is a variety of the weeping willow tree characterized by its striking silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

This plant is native to China and is known for its cascading branches that create a graceful, flowing appearance. The leaves are elongated, narrow, and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist environments and is often planted near water features or in wetland areas.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to stabilize soil in riparian zones.
