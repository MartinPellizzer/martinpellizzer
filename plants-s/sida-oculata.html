Sida oculata

Sida oculata, commonly known as the eyeplant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a distinctive white or pale green marking resembling an eye. Sida oculata has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
