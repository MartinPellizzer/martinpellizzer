Schanginia aegyptiaca

Schanginia aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapting to harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water in its dry environment.

Schanginia aegyptiaca is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
