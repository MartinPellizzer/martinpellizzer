Scirpus reclinatus

Scirpus reclinatus, commonly known as the drooping bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often curve downward, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively and colonize moist soils effectively.

Scirpus reclinatus plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for wetland wildlife.
