Solanum melongena var. angustum

Solanum melongena var. angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eggplant, is a variant of the common eggplant characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and slightly pointed fruits.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, producing purple or white fruits that are used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it distinct from the standard eggplant, offering a unique appearance and culinary potential.
