Scirpus anceps

Scirpus anceps, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in marshes, pond edges, and wet meadows, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical clusters of small, greenish flowers. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Scirpus anceps plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and aiding in water filtration.
