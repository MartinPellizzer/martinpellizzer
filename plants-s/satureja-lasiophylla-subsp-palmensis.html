Satureja lasiophylla subsp. palmensis

Satureja lasiophylla subsp.

palmensis, commonly known as Palmens' woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in whorls along the upper stems. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
