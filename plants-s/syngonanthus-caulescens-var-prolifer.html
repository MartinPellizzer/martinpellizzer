Syngonanthus caulescens var. prolifer

Syngonanthus caulescens var. prolifer, commonly known as the creeping marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in dense mats, with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. The plant features small, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are clustered in panicle-like arrangements. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ability to spread vegetatively makes it an important colonizer of disturbed wetland habitats.
