Sclerolaena stylosa

Sclerolaena stylosa, commonly known as the slender-flowered rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Sclerolaena stylosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic features.
