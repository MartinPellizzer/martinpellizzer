Schoenoplectus oryzetorum

Schoenoplectus oryzetorum, commonly known as the rice marsh reed, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has hollow, jointed stems that are often used in basket weaving and traditional crafts. Its flowering spikes are usually green or reddish and appear in late summer to early fall.

Schoenoplectus oryzetorum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
