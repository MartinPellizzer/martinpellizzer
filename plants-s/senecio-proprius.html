Senecio proprius

Senecio proprius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Senecio proprius is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
