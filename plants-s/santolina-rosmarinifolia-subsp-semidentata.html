Santolina rosmarinifolia subsp. semidentata

Santolina rosmarinifolia subsp.

semidentata, commonly known as narrow-leaved santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features compact, aromatic foliage with narrow, gray-green leaves that have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
