Saldanhaea lateriflora

Saldanhaea lateriflora, commonly known as the coastal lily, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure. It has linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in dune environments.

Saldanhaea lateriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing coastal dunes.
