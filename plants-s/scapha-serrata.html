Scapha serrata

Scapha serrata, commonly known as the serrated scapha, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to violet. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their shape and nectar-rich structure.

Scapha serrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
