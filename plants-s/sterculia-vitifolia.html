Sterculia vitifolia

Sterculia vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
