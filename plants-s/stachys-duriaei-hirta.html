Stachys duriaei-hirta

Stachys duriaei-hirta, commonly known as the Sierra Madre betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces spikes of violet-blue flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and drought tolerance.
