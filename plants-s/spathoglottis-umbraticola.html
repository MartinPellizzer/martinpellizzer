Spathoglottis umbraticola

Spathoglottis umbraticola, commonly known as the dark-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, orchid-like flowers with a distinctive yellow and purple coloration. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing in forest understories. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
