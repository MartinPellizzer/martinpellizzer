Solanum maroniense

Solanum maroniense, commonly known as the Maroni nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it thrives in humid, warm climates.

Solanum maroniense is often found in moist, shaded areas within its native habitat.
