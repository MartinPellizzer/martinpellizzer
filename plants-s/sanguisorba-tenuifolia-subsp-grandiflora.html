Sanguisorba tenuifolia subsp. grandiflora

Sanguisorba tenuifolia subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in moist, woodland habitats. It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade. Sanguisorba tenuifolia subsp.

grandiflora is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions, making it a versatile garden plant.
