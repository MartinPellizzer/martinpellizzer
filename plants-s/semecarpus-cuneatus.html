Semecarpus cuneatus

Semecarpus cuneatus, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or kathal, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Hippocastanaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits. The plant is often found in moist, tropical regions and thrives in shaded environments. Its seeds, enclosed in a hard, woody capsule, are used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
