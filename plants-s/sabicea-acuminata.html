Sabicea acuminata

Sabicea acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sycamore, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, flat pods that contain seeds.

Sabicea acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
