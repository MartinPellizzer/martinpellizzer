Sida emarginata

Sida emarginata, commonly known as sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in saline or brackish environments. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a distinctive notched or heart-shaped base. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Sida emarginata is adapted to withstand salt spray and sandy soils, making it a common species in coastal ecosystems.
