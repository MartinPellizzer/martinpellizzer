Silybum eburneum

Silybum eburneum, commonly known as the white cardoon, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, silvery-green, lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring temperate climates.

Silybum eburneum is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
