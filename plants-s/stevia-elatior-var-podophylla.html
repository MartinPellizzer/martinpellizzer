Stevia elatior var. podophylla

Stevia elatior var. podophylla, commonly known as the red-veined stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with striking red veins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. It is cultivated for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides.

This variety is often used as a natural sweetener and in traditional herbal medicine.
