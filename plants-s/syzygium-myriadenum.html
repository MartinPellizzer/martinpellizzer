Syzygium myriadenum

Syzygium myriadenum, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and highly valued for their sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and orchards.
