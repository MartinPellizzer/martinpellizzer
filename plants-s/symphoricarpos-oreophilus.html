Symphoricarpos oreophilus

Symphoricarpos oreophilus, commonly known as the mountain snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Symphoricarpos oreophilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and small mammals.
