Sisyrinchium plicatulum

Sisyrinchium plicatulum, commonly known as the zigzagged blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. Its leaves are narrow and often have a zigzag pattern, contributing to its common name.

Sisyrinchium plicatulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
