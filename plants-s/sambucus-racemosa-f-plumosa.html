Sambucus racemosa f. plumosa

Sambucus racemosa f. plumosa, commonly known as the silky elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, plume-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The leaves are alternate, compound, and have a soft, silvery underside. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation.

This plant thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
