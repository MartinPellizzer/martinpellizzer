Spiraea × fulvescens

Spiraea × fulvescens, commonly known as the golden spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy, cone-shaped clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically blooms in early summer, producing copious amounts of nectar that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin, and turn reddish in the fall.

Spiraea × fulvescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
