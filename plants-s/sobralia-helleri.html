Sobralia helleri

Sobralia helleri, commonly known as the Heller's sobralia, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that feature a mix of purple, yellow, and green hues, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually thick and leathery, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Sobralia helleri blooms during the warmer months, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
