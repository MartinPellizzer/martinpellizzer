Struthiola pentheri

Struthiola pentheri, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and fleshy, alternate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central column. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Struthiola pentheri is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
