Statice cephalotes

Statice cephalotes, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean, it thrives in saline and well-drained soils. The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy shorelines, making it ecologically significant in coastal ecosystems.
