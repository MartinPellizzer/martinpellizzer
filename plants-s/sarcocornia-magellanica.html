Sarcocornia magellanica

Sarcocornia magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan glassworts, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is native to coastal regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding survival in arid and salty conditions. It produces small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

Sarcocornia magellanica plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
