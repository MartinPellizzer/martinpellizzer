Sticherus rufinervis

Sticherus rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined sticherus, is a member of the family Sticheraceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves with prominent red veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Sticherus rufinervis is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
