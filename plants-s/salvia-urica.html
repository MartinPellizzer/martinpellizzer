Salvia urica

Salvia urica, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Salvia urica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies due to its aromatic and medicinal properties.
