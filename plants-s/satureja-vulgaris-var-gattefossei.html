Satureja vulgaris var. gattefossei

Satureja vulgaris var. gattefossei, commonly known as Corsican thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils contain thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
