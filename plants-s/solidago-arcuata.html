Solidago arcuata

Solidago arcuata, commonly known as the curvedleaf goldenrod, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled at the edges, giving it its common name.

Solidago arcuata plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
