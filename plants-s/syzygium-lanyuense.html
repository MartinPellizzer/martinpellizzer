Syzygium lanyuense

Syzygium lanyuense, commonly known as Lanyu tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe that turns reddish when mature.

Syzygium lanyuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
