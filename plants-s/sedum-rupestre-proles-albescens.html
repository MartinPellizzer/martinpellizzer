Sedum rupestre proles albescens

Sedum rupestre proles albescens, commonly known as the White-flowered Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, white or pale green leaves that help it store water. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, white flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
