Sida spinosa

Sida spinosa, commonly known as prickly sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides. The plant features hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and often have a rough texture.

Sida spinosa is known for its prickly bracts that surround its flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance.
