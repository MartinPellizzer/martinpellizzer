Smilax regelii

Smilax regelii, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a climbing shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and sharp, spiny thorns that cover its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Smilax regelii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
