Symphytum tauricum

Symphytum tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and tall, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to promote wound healing. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
