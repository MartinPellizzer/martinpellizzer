Sempervivum pisidicum

Sempervivum pisidicum, commonly known as the Caucasian houseleek, is a species of succulent plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in well-drained soil.

Sempervivum pisidicum is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries.
