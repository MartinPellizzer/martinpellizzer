Serratula centauroides subsp. komarovii

Serratula centauroides subsp.

komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's sickleweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
