Syzygium megacarpum

Syzygium megacarpum, commonly known as the satinwood or red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark reddish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by large, fleshy, red or orange berries. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and furniture.

Syzygium megacarpum is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
