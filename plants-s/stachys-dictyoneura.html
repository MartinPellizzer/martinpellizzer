Stachys dictyoneura

Stachys dictyoneura, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its hairy, silver-gray foliage gives it a distinctive appearance, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Stachys dictyoneura is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
