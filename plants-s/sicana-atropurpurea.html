Sicana atropurpurea

Sicana atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf sida, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically purple or reddish in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Sicana atropurpurea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in warmer climates.
