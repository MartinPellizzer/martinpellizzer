Schizochilus trilobus

Schizochilus trilobus, commonly known as the three-lobed marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, three-lobed floral structure. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in humid and nutrient-rich environments. Its flowers are usually small and pale green or white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that aids in pollination.
