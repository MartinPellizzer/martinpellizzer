Selinum spinosum

Selinum spinosum, commonly known as prickly selenium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Selinum spinosum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
