Senecio incertus

Senecio incertus, commonly known as the uncertain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces small, dry fruits known as cypsela.

Senecio incertus is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
