Smelowskia alba var. tilingii

Smelowskia alba var. tilingii, commonly known as the Tilingii snowbell, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its elegant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a compact, shrub-like growth habit with smooth, gray-green foliage. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
