Scabiosa graminifolia subsp. arizagae

Scabiosa graminifolia subsp.

arizagae, commonly known as Arizona ladies' mantle, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with a distinctive silver-blue hue.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
