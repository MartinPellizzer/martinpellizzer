Scorodonia botrys

Scorodonia botrys, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. Scorodonia botrys is often found in rocky or coastal environments, where it demonstrates a high degree of drought tolerance.
