Sadiria eugeniifolia var. burmanica

Sadiria eugeniifolia var. burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is evergreen, with glossy, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
