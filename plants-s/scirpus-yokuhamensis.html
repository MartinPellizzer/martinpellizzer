Scirpus yokuhamensis

Scirpus yokuhamensis, commonly known as the Japanese bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands.

Scirpus yokuhamensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
