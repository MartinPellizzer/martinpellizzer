Stachys heterophylla

Stachys heterophylla, commonly known as narrowleaf hedge-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Stachys heterophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
