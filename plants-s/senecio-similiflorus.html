Senecio similiflorus

Senecio similiflorus, commonly known as the similar-flower senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture.

Senecio similiflorus is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique type of shrubland ecosystem.
