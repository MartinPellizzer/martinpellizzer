Sabal palmetto var. bahamensis

Sabal palmetto var. bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian palmetto, is a variant of the cabbage palm native to the Bahamas and parts of Florida.

It is a robust, evergreen shrub or small tree with a trunk that can reach up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces large, fan-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered in spadices, and it produces edible, coconut-like fruits.

This species is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found in dunes and sandy soils.
