Senecio pulicarioides var. auriculatus

Senecio pulicarioides var. auriculatus, commonly known as the auriculate groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and hairy, gray-green leaves with distinctive auriculate (ear-like) lobes at the base. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
