Sida rhombifolia subsp. maderensis

Sida rhombifolia subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as Mader's horsegram, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean. It is a herbaceous annual with alternate, ovate to rhombic leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, indehiscent pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its drought tolerance and has been used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
