Salvia lutescens

Salvia lutescens, commonly known as the yellow sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. Salvia lutescens typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
