Swertia connata

Swertia connata, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Swertia connata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid floral display.
