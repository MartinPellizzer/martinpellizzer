Solanum violifolium f. chacoense

Solanum violifolium f. chacoense, commonly known as the Chaco bluetail, is a unique variant of the blueberry nightshade.

It is a perennial shrub native to the Chaco region of South America, characterized by its striking violet leaves and small, blue-black berries. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in dry forests and savannas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
