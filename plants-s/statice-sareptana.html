Statice sareptana

Statice sareptana, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its narrow, linear leaves are gray-green in color, and it is highly tolerant of wind and salt spray.

Statice sareptana is a popular ornamental plant due to its resilience and visual appeal in gardens.
