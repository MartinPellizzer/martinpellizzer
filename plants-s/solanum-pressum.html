Solanum pressum

Solanum pressum, commonly known as the purple potato, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots that produce edible, purple-fleshed potatoes. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves, and it produces small, white or purple flowers that develop into berries.

Solanum pressum is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content, making it a rare and sought-after crop in certain regions.
