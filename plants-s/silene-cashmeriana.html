Silene cashmeriana

Silene cashmeriana, commonly known as the Kashmir pink, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to magenta flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often upright and branched.

Silene cashmeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
