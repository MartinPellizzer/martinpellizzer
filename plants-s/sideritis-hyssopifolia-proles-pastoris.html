Sideritis hyssopifolia proles pastoris

Sideritis hyssopifolia proles pastoris, commonly known as the pastoral sidewort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a mint-like fragrance.

This species is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
