Solanum tuberosum var. chilotanum

Solanum tuberosum var. chilotanum, commonly known as the Chilote potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato native to South America.

It is characterized by its tubers, which are typically smaller and have a distinctive skin color ranging from red to purple. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is cultivated for its edible underground storage organs. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional profile, making it a significant crop in certain regions.
