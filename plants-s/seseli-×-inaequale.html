Seseli × inaequale

Seseli × inaequale, commonly known as the hybrid milk vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the genus Seseli. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
