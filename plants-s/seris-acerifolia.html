Seris acerifolia

Seris acerifolia, commonly known as the mapleleaf tree, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that resemble those of maple trees, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while its fruits are winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
