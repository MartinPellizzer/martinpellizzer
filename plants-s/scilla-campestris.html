Scilla campestris

Scilla campestris, commonly known as the meadow scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Scilla campestris is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
