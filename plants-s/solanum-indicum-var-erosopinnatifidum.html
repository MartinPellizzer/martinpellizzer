Solanum indicum var. erosopinnatifidum

Solanum indicum var. erosopinnatifidum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade variant, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin, giving it its specific variety name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic alkaloids.

Due to its potential toxicity, it is often avoided in horticulture and requires caution in cultivation.
