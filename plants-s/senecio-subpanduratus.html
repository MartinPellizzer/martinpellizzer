Senecio subpanduratus

Senecio subpanduratus, commonly known as the panduratus groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
