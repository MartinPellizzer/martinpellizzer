Salsola florida

Salsola florida, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or subshrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by succulent, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are usually small, oblanceolate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Salsola florida is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, with deep roots and a capacity to store water in its tissues.
