Salicaria graefferi

Salicaria graefferi, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterplantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Salicaria graefferi plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
