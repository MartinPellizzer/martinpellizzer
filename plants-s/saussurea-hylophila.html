Saussurea hylophila

Saussurea hylophila, commonly known as the Himalayan corydalis, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered with fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Saussurea hylophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
