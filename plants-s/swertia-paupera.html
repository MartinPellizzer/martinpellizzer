Swertia paupera

Swertia paupera, commonly known as the poor man's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Swertia paupera is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
