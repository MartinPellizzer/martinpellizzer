Steriphoma menispermifolium

Steriphoma menispermifolium, commonly known as the yellow woodbine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and dark, woody stems. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
