Spiraea faurieana

Spiraea faurieana, commonly known as the Faurie's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant has elliptical to ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden use.

Spiraea faurieana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
