Sixalix libyca

Sixalix libyca, commonly known as the Libyan boxwood, is a rare and ancient plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, glossy evergreen leaves. The plant is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deep underground. Its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a unique example of desert flora.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, Sixalix libyca is considered endangered and is of significant botanical interest.
