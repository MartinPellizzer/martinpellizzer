Sida glutinosa var. cinerea

Sida glutinosa var. cinerea, commonly known as sticky sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that produce a sticky substance. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its small white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
