Salvia carnosa subsp. pilosa

Salvia carnosa subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, woolly hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a unique appearance.
