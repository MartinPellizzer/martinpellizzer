Sciophila cephalida

Sciophila cephalida, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are characterized by their distinctive hooded structure, with five lobes that form a bell-shaped corolla.

Sciophila cephalida prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rock garden settings.
