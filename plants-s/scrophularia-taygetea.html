Scrophularia taygetea

Scrophularia taygetea, commonly known as the Greek figwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny habitats. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.
