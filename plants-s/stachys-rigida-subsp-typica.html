Stachys rigida subsp. typica

Stachys rigida subsp.

typica, commonly known as rigid hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of stiff, bristly hairs on its stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface and a serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
