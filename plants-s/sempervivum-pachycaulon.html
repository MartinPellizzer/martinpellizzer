Sempervivum pachycaulon

Sempervivum pachycaulon, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and yellow, typically in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in harsh conditions, including frost and drought.
