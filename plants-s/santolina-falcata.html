Santolina falcata

Santolina falcata, commonly known as Spanish santolina, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Santolina falcata is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its compact growth and ornamental appeal.
