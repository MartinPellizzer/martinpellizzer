Senna septemtrionalis var. pubescens

Senna septemtrionalis var. pubescens, commonly known as the Northern senna, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

The hairy pubescence on its leaves and stems is a distinguishing feature of this variety.
