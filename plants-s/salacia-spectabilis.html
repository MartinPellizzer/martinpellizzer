Salacia spectabilis

Salacia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy salacia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its striking, large, and colorful flowers that attract pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or a climbing vine, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a glossy surface, and the flowers are borne in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
