Stanfordia

Stanfordia, commonly known as the "moonflower," is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon basin. The plants are characterized by their large, showy flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet fragrance. Stanfordia species are typically shrubs or small trees with thick, waxy leaves.

They are known for their unique ecological role as pollinators attract nocturnal insects and bats.
