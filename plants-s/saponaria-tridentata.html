Saponaria tridentata

Saponaria tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed soapwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often lobed at the base, giving it its common name. Saponaria tridentata is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers produce a mild soap-like substance, which has been historically used for cleaning purposes.
