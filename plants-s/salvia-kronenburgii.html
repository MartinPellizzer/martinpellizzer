Salvia kronenburgii

Salvia kronenburgii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia kronenburgii is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience in well-drained soils.
