Silene nocturna proles brachypetala

Silene nocturna proles brachypetala, commonly known as the night-flowering catchfly, is a unique plant species characterized by its bioluminescent petals that emit a faint glow in the dark.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features delicate, pinkish-white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its native habitat.
