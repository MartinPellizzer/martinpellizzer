Symplocos stewardii

Symplocos stewardii, commonly known as Stewart's waxleaf.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Appalachian Mountains in the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that remain largely unchanged throughout the growing season. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in the fall, attracting various bird species.
