Senecio sylvaticus subsp. viscidulus

Senecio sylvaticus subsp.

viscidulus, commonly known as the sticky groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Its subspecies status reflects subtle morphological differences from the main species, particularly in leaf texture and hair density.
