Sida dumosa var. glanduligera

Sida dumosa var. glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular sida, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
