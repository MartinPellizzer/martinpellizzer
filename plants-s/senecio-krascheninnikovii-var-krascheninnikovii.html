Senecio krascheninnikovii var. krascheninnikovii

Senecio krascheninnikovii var. krascheninnikovii, commonly known as the Krascheninnikov's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a fine layer of hairs, providing protection against intense sunlight and drought.

This species is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a common sight in semi-arid landscapes.
