Sapium aubletianum

Sapium aubletianum, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain three seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming invasive.
