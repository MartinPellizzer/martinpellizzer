Sisymbrium amarum

Sisymbrium amarum, commonly known as bitter rocket, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features slender, branching stems and pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is known for its pungent, peppery taste, which is due to the presence of glucosinolates.
