Salvia rypara subsp. rypara

Salvia rypara subsp.

rypara, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers.

The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender leaves and compact growth habit compared to other Salvia species.
