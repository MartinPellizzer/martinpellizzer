Senna unijuga

Senna unijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant features compound leaves with small, narrow leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Senna unijuga is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
