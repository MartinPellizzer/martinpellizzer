Spatholobus purpureus

Spatholobus purpureus, commonly known as the purple wisteria, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
