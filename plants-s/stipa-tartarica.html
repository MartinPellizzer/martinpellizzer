Stipa tartarica

Stipa tartarica, commonly known as the Tartar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are highly visible during the flowering season. It thrives in dry, sandy, or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Stipa tartarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
