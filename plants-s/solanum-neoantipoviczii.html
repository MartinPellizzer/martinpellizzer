Solanum neoantipoviczii

Solanum neoantipoviczii, commonly known as the Antipoviczii nightshade, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions in South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically features erect stems with ovate leaves that have a rough texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive calyx.

Solanum neoantipoviczii is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
