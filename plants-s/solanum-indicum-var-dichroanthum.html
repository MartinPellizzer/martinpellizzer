Solanum indicum var. dichroanthum

Solanum indicum var. dichroanthum, commonly known as the two-colored nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive two-colored flowers, typically with purple and white petals. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
