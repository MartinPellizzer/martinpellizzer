Solanum talarense

Solanum talarense, commonly known as the Talaren nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the island of Tenerife in the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its native range. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are an important food source for certain bird species.

Solanum talarense is classified as an endemic species, highlighting its unique ecological role in the Canary Islands.
