Saxifraga fortunei var. tricolor

Saxifraga fortunei var. tricolor, commonly known as the tricolored stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense, compact foliage with leaves that are typically blue-green, pink, and red, creating a striking tricolor effect. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a favored plant among gardeners seeking drought-resistant, colorful landscaping options.
