Senecio doria subsp. kirghisicus

Senecio doria subsp.

kirghisicus, commonly known as the Kirghiz Senecio, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan.

This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in composite heads.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
