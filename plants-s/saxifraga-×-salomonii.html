Saxifraga × salomonii

Saxifraga × salomonii, commonly known as the Solomon's Seal, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its tall, arching stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing white or pale green flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Saxifraga × salomonii is often cultivated for its delicate appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
