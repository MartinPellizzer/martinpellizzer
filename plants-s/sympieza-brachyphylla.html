Sympieza brachyphylla

Sympieza brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved sympieza, is a rare and specialized plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to its often arid or semi-arid habitat. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered near the base of the stems. Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental health.
