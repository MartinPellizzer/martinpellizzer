Solanum uncinellum var. atrosanguineum

Solanum uncinellum var. atrosanguineum, commonly known as the black blood nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which contain toxic alkaloids. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments and has small, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in cultivation and requires caution when handled.
