Senecio limprichtii

Senecio limprichtii, commonly known as the Cape mountain snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
