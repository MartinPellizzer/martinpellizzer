Saxifraga hirculus var. alpina

Saxifraga hirculus var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.
