Silene tergestina

Silene tergestina, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. Silene tergestina is perennial and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
