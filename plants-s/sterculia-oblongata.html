Sterculia oblongata

Sterculia oblongata, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, oblong leaves and showy, red or purple flowers. The tree produces woody pods containing seeds surrounded by a fatty, edible pulp. It is valued for its shea nuts, which are used to make shea butter, and for its ecological and economic importance in local communities.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
