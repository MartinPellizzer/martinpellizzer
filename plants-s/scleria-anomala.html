Scleria anomala

Scleria anomala, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Scleria anomala is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
