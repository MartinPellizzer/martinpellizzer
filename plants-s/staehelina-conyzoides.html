Staehelina conyzoides

Staehelina conyzoides, commonly known as the blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a rough texture.

Staehelina conyzoides is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed or arid environments.
