Saxifraga moschata subsp. lamottei

Saxifraga moschata subsp.

lamottei, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its aromatic foliage and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
