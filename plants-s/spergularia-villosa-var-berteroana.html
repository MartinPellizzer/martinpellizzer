Spergularia villosa var. berteroana

Spergularia villosa var. berteroana, commonly known as the silky sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has fleshy, lobed leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It produces capsules that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is adapted to sandy soils and often thrives in disturbed or open habitats.
