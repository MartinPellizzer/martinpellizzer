Sasaella musashiensis

Sasaella musashiensis, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
