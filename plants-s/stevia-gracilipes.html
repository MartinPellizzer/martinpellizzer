Stevia gracilipes

Stevia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender stevia, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Stevia gracilipes is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
