Senna timoriensis

Senna timoriensis, commonly known as the Timor senna, is a shrub or small tree native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Senna timoriensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
