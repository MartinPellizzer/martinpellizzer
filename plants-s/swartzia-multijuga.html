Swartzia multijuga

Swartzia multijuga, commonly known as the yellow wood or African yellowwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts within its native habitats.
