Spiraea betulifolia subsp. aemiliana

Spiraea betulifolia subsp.

aemiliana, commonly known as the narrowleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early summer.

The leaves are oval-shaped, with a pointed tip and a serrated margin, and they turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its graceful appearance and hardy nature, thriving in colder climates.
