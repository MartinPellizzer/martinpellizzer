Solidago gigantea subsp. serotina

Solidago gigantea subsp.

serotina, commonly known as late goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, robust stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces abundant yellow flowers in late summer to early autumn, which are arranged in panicle inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides.
