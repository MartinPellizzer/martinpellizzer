Streptanthus sparsiflorus

Streptanthus sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparsiflorous whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as coastal bluffs and grasslands.
