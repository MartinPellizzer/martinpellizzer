Scirpus multicaulis var. viviparus

Scirpus multicaulis var. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense stands along the edges of ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant produces numerous small, triangular leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. One of its notable features is the presence of viviparous shoots, which are capable of rooting and growing into new plants while still attached to the parent stem.

This trait aids in its rapid colonization of wetland habitats and contributes to its ecological significance in stabilizing shorelines.
