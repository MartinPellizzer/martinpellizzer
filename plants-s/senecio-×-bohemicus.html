Senecio × bohemicus

Senecio × bohemicus, commonly known as the hybrid ragwort, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of blue-green, woolly leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
