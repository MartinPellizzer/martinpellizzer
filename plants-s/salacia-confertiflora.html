Salacia confertiflora

Salacia confertiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus *Salacia*. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
