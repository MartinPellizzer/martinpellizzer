Solanum grandiflorum var. leiocarpum

Solanum grandiflorum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the pink star apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its large, showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is typically round and has a smooth, leathery skin, with a sweet, juicy pulp containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a glossy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
