Scleranthus seticeps

Scleranthus seticeps, commonly known as the hairy thorned sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant has erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and often hairy as well. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Scleranthus seticeps is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
