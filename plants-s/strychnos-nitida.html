Strychnos nitida

Strychnos nitida, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers followed by bright red berries. The plant has smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a waxy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal and inland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
