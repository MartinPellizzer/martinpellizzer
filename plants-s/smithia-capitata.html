Smithia capitata

Smithia capitata, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson, typically blooming in late summer. Its flowers are arranged in a dense, terminal cluster, giving it a bell-like appearance.

Smithia capitata is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
