Sophora microphylla var. chathamica

Sophora microphylla var. chathamica, commonly known as the Chatham Island Sophora, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Chatham Islands of New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to the cool, maritime climate of its native habitat and is often found in coastal scrub or forest margins. It has small, hard, dark brown pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and endemic species.
