Salvia rubiginosa var. hebephylla

Salvia rubiginosa var. hebephylla, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
