Stapeliopsis saxatilis subsp. stayneri

Stapeliopsis saxatilis subsp.

stayneri, commonly known as the rock stapeliopsis, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with a strong scent, which are often mistaken for insects due to their appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
