Stenanthera pluriflora

Stenanthera pluriflora, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and green, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine substrates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
