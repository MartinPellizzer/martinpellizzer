Schoenus paradoxus

Schoenus paradoxus, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous roots. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Schoenus paradoxus is often found in moist, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments.
