Silene ouensae

Silene ouensae, commonly known as the Ouensan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Silene ouensae is often found in tundra and subalpine zones, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
