Scabiosa diandra

Scabiosa diandra, commonly known as the Fairy Ring or Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Scabiosa diandra is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
