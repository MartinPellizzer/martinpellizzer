Saxifraga aizoon rosea

Saxifraga aizoon rosea, commonly known as the pink saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
