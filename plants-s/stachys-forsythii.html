Stachys forsythii

Stachys forsythii, commonly known as yellow betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are upright and branched.

Stachys forsythii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
