Syzygium mimicum

Syzygium mimicum, commonly known as the mimic guava, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries that are similar in appearance to guavas. Syzygium mimicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
