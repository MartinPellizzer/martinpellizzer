Syzygium roseomarginatum

Syzygium roseomarginatum, commonly known as the pink-marginated syllabium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive pink margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
