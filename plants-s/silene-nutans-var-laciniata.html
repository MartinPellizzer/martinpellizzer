Silene nutans var. laciniata

Silene nutans var. laciniata, commonly known as the fragrant evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
