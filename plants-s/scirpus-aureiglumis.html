Scirpus aureiglumis

Scirpus aureiglumis, commonly known as the golden club, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often adorned with golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, typically found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Scirpus aureiglumis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
