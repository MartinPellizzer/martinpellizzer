Sorbus cashmiriana f. jaeschkei

Sorbus cashmiriana f. jaeschkei, commonly known as the Kashmir whitebeam, is a rare and ornamental shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, persistent berries. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn reddish in autumn. This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
