Salvia loxensis

Salvia loxensis, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Salvia loxensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
