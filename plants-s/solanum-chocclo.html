Solanum chocclo

Solanum chocclo, commonly known as the coffee plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark red color. These berries contain the seeds used to make coffee, which are processed and roasted to develop the characteristic flavor.

Its botanical features include simple leaves, clustered flowers, and a woody stem.
