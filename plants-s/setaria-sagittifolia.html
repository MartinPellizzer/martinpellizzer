Setaria sagittifolia

Setaria sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Setaria sagittifolia is used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
