Stachys leptopoda

Stachys leptopoda, commonly known as narrow-leaved hedge-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Stachys leptopoda is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland edges and rocky slopes.
