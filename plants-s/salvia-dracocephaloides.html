Salvia dracocephaloides

Salvia dracocephaloides, commonly known as dragon's head, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
