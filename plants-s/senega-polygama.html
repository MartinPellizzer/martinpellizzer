Senega polygama

Senega polygama, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, pinkish-purple pouch-like flower that resembles a lady's slipper, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in shaded environments. Senega polygama has a short flowering period in late spring to early summer, and its unique floral structure plays a role in its pollination strategy.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered endangered in many parts of its range.
