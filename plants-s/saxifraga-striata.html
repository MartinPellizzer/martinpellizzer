Saxifraga striata

Saxifraga striata, commonly known as the striped saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and tundra environments. The plant features upright, branched stems covered with small, oval-shaped leaves that have distinctive white or pale green stripes. Its flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Saxifraga striata is adapted to cold climates and is known for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
