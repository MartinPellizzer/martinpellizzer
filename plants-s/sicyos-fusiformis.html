Sicyos fusiformis

Sicyos fusiformis, commonly known as the spindle gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces elongated, spindle-shaped fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

Sicyos fusiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some cultures for food and medicinal purposes.
