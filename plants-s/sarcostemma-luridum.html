Sarcostemma luridum

Sarcostemma luridum, commonly known as the yellow star vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. Its botanical features include milky sap and hairy surfaces, which are typical of many species in the Apocynaceae family.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.
