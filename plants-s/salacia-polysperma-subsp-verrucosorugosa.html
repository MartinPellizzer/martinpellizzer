Salacia polysperma subsp. verrucosorugosa

Salacia polysperma subsp.

verrucosorugosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved salacia, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a climbing shrub characterized by its thick, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, warty texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
