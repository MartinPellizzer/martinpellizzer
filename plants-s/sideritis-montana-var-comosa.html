Sideritis montana var. comosa

Sideritis montana var. comosa, commonly known as the mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds in its fruit.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
