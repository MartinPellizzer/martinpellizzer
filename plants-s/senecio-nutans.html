Senecio nutans

Senecio nutans, commonly known as the drooping groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Senecio nutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
