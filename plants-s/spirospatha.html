Spirospatha

Spirospatha, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and ethereal flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy leaves and delicate, white or pale pink flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its root system is specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered in many areas.
