Saussurea yanagisawae var. elegans

Saussurea yanagisawae var. elegans, commonly known as the Japanese yarrow or elegant saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing white to pale yellow florets surrounded by green bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers cool and moist conditions.
