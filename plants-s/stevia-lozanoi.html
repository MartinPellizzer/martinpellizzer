Stevia lozanoi

Stevia lozanoi, commonly known as the Lozan stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its sweet properties, it is often used as a natural alternative to sugar in various food products.
