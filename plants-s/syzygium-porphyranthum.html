Syzygium porphyranthum

Syzygium porphyranthum, commonly known as the red sandalwood or porphyranthum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and ovate in shape. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

The tree is valued for its hard, aromatic wood, which has been used historically in traditional crafts and as a source of incense.
