Sarcophrynium macrophyllum

Sarcophrynium macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Sarcophrynium macrophyllum is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
