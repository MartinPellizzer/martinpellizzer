Salix pauciflora

Salix pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silky hair. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Salix pauciflora is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
