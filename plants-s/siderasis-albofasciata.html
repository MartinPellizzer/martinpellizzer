Siderasis albofasciata

Siderasis albofasciata, commonly known as the white-banded ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Siderasis albofasciata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
