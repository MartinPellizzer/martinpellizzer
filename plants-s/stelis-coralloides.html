Stelis coralloides

Stelis coralloides, commonly known as the coral-like orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant features a compact, branching structure with pale green to white flowers that resemble coral in appearance. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Stelis coralloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, well-drained conditions.
