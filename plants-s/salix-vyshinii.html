Salix vyshinii

Salix vyshinii, commonly known as the Vyshin's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed structure and elongated leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and along streams.

Salix vyshinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
