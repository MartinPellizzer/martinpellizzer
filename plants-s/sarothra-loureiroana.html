Sarothra loureiroana

Sarothra loureiroana, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Sarothra loureiroana is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
