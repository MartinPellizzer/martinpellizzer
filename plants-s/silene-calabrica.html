Silene calabrica

Silene calabrica, commonly known as the Calabrian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces showy pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Silene calabrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
