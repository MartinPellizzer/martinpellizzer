Saxifraga lopesiana

Saxifraga lopesiana, commonly known as the Lopes' saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit with fleshy, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
