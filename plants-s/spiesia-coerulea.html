Spiesia coerulea

Spiesia coerulea, commonly known as the blue flax or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clusters. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing a delicate, mist-like appearance.

Spiesia coerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
