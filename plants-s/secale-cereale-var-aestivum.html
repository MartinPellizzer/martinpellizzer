Secale cereale var. aestivum

Secale cereale var. aestivum, commonly known as winter rye, is a variant of rye that is primarily cultivated in temperate climates.

It is a perennial grass with tall, slender stalks and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-rowed grains. The plant is characterized by its deep root system, which helps in soil conservation and erosion control. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often used as a cover crop or for forage.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
