Scleria buchananii var. latifolia

Scleria buchananii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
