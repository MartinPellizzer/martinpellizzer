Strobilanthes macrostachya

Strobilanthes macrostachya, commonly known as the giant flame flower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that resemble flowers, which are often brightly colored in red or orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowering period occurs during the rainy season, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
