Salacia togoica

Salacia togoica, commonly known as Togo's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of West Africa.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by opposite, ovate leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
