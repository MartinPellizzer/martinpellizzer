Senecillis kaialpina

Senecillis kaialpina, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Senecillis kaialpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
