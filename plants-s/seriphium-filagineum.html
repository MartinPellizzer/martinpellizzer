Seriphium filagineum

Seriphium filagineum, commonly known as the silky tarplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, bristly seeds.

Seriphium filagineum is often found in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
