Senecio amphibolus

Senecio amphibolus, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are pale blue to white and are arranged in clusters.

Senecio amphibolus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
