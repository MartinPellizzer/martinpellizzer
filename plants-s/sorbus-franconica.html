Sorbus franconica

Sorbus franconica, commonly known as the Franconian rowan, is a species of shrub or small tree native to central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.

Sorbus franconica is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
