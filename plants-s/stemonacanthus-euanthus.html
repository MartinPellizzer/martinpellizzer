Stemonacanthus euanthus

Stemonacanthus euanthus, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The golden rain tree is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
