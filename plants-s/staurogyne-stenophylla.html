Staurogyne stenophylla

Staurogyne stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in South America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, aiding in efficient light absorption in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with moderate to high light exposure.

Staurogyne stenophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
