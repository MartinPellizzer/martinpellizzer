Senecio platycodon

Senecio platycodon, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Senecio platycodon is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
