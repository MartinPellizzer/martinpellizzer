Streptocarpus tanala var. prostratus

Streptocarpus tanala var. prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate streptocarpus, is a hybrid species within the Gesneriaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically features broad, ovate leaves with a waxy texture and a dark green coloration. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high humidity for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and compact growth form.
