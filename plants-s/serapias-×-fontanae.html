Serapias × fontanae

Serapias × fontanae, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Serapias acaulis and Serapias microchila, characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or damp habitats, such as marshes and meadows, and is native to southern Europe. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of narrow leaves and a single flowering stem that bears several showy, spurred flowers.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is considered a rare and delicate plant in its native range.
