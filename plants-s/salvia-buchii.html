Salvia buchii

Salvia buchii, commonly known as New Zealand mint, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Salvia buchii is often used in herbal teas and has a mint-like fragrance.
