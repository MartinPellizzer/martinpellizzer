Solidago salicifolia

Solidago salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and ornamental appeal.
