Sterculia rubiginosa var. rubiginosa

Sterculia rubiginosa var. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stained sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release smooth, round seeds.

The tree is often valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
