Steptorhamphus chaffanjonii

Steptorhamphus chaffanjonii, commonly known as the Chaffanjon's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in arid environments. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations.
