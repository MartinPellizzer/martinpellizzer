Salix retusa subsp. kitaibeliana

Salix retusa subsp.

kitaibeliana, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth surface, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
