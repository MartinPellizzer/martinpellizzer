Symphyotrichum tenuifolium

Symphyotrichum tenuifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Narrowleaf ironweed is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
