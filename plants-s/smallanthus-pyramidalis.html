Smallanthus pyramidalis

Smallanthus pyramidalis, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pyramidal shape and bright yellow ray florets that resemble sunflowers. The plant produces small, tubular disk flowers surrounded by leaf-like bracts. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Smallanthus pyramidalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Andean cultures for various purposes.
