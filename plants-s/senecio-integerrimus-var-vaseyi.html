Senecio integerrimus var. vaseyi

Senecio integerrimus var. vaseyi, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
