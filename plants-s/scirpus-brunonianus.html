Scirpus brunonianus

Scirpus brunonianus, commonly known as the Brunonian bulrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly in countries like India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. This herbaceous perennial grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, often forming dense stands. Its tall, cylindrical inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
