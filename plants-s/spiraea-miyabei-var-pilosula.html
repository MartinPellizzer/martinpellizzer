Spiraea miyabei var. pilosula

Spiraea miyabei var. pilosula, commonly known as the Japanese weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its arching stems that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The plant produces oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate floral display and graceful form.
