Scrophularia sambucifolia var. hirsuta

Scrophularia sambucifolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy wood betony, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular and emit a mild, aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Scrophulariaceae family.
