Sedum bodinieri

Sedum bodinieri, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, helping to store water and reduce transpiration.

Sedum bodinieri is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
