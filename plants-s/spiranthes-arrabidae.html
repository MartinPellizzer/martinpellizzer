Spiranthes arrabidae

Spiranthes arrabidae, commonly known as the Arrabida spire orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that produces delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescences with small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
