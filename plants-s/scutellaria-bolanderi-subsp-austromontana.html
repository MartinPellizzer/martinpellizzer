Scutellaria bolanderi subsp. austromontana

Scutellaria bolanderi subsp.

austromontana, commonly known as Bolander's blueflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
