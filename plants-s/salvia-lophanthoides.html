Salvia lophanthoides

Salvia lophanthoides, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often gray-green, with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Salvia lophanthoides is valued in both ornamental gardening and traditional herbal medicine.
