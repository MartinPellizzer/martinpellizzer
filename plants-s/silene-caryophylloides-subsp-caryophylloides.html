Silene caryophylloides subsp. caryophylloides

Silene caryophylloides subsp.

caryophylloides, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular.

The flowers are arranged in racemes and are pollinated primarily by insects.
