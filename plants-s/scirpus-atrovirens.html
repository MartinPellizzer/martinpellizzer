Scirpus atrovirens

Scirpus atrovirens, commonly known as blackstem bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that range from green to dark purple, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically found at the top of the stems. Scirpus atrovirens thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its dense growth helps stabilize soil and provides habitat for various wetland organisms.
