Senecio aquaticus var. flosculosus

Senecio aquaticus var. flosculosus, commonly known as the water groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments. The plant features slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
