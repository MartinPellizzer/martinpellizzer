Saxifraga cortusifolia var. variegata

Saxifraga cortusifolia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated rockfoil, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
