Sisymbrium gracile

Sisymbrium gracile, commonly known as slender broomrape, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant lacks true leaves, instead having small, scale-like structures called phyllodes. It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on host plants for nutrients through its root system.

Sisymbrium gracile is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
