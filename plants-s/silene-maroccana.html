Silene maroccana

Silene maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene maroccana is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
