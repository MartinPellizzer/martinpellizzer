Simsia megacephala

Simsia megacephala, commonly known as the giant-headed simia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small yellow flowers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Simsia megacephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
