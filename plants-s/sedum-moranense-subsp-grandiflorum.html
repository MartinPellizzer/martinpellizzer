Sedum moranense subsp. grandiflorum

Sedum moranense subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the Moran's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically thick and green, adapted to store water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
