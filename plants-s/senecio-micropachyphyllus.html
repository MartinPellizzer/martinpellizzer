Senecio micropachyphyllus

Senecio micropachyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, narrow, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
