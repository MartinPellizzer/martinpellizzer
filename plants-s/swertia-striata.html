Swertia striata

Swertia striata, commonly known as the Indian blue gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Swertia striata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
