Schima khasiana var. sericans

Schima khasiana var. sericans, commonly known as the Khasi schima, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
