Solena capitata

Solena capitata, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a slightly hairy texture.

Solena capitata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens and rockeries.
