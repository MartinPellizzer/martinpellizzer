Senna dariensis var. hypoglauca

Senna dariensis var. hypoglauca, commonly known as the white senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically shrub-like, with smooth, greyish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
