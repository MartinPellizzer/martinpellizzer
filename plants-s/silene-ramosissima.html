Silene ramosissima

Silene ramosissima, commonly known as the common catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Silene ramosissima is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent leaves.
