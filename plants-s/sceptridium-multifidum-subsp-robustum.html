Sceptridium multifidum subsp. robustum

Sceptridium multifidum subsp.

robustum, commonly known as the robust clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family. It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices.

Its reproductive structures include small, cone-like strobili that produce spores, typical of its primitive evolutionary lineage.
