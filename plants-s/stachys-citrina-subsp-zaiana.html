Stachys citrina subsp. zaiana

Stachys citrina subsp.

zaiana, commonly known as the lemon yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
