Salacia erythrocarpa

Salacia erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to other genera within the family. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its most distinctive feature is its bright red berries, which are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
