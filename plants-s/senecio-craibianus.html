Senecio craibianus

Senecio craibianus, commonly known as the Cape Town groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Senecio craibianus is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
