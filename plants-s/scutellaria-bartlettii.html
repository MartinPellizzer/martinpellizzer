Scutellaria bartlettii

Scutellaria bartlettii, commonly known as Bartlett's blueflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Scutellaria bartlettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
