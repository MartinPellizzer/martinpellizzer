Symplocos badia

Symplocos badia, commonly known as the red-flowered beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of deep purple to red berries that appear in late summer. The plant has small, oval leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

Symplocos badia is often cultivated for its vibrant berries and attractive foliage.
