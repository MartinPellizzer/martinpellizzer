Stevia lavandulifolia

Stevia lavandulifolia, commonly known as sweetleaf or honey plant, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lavender-scented leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages.

Stevia lavandulifolia is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses due to its intense sweetness and health benefits.
