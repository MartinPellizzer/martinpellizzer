Scilla carunculifera

Scilla carunculifera, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in Mediterranean cuisine.

Scilla carunculifera thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
