Silene kenyana

Silene kenyana, commonly known as the Kenyan campion, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually opposite and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene kenyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
