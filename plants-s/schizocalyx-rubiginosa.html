Schizocalyx rubiginosa

Schizocalyx rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Schizocalyx rubiginosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
