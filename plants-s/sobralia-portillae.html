Sobralia portillae

Sobralia portillae, commonly known as the Portilla's sobralia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that feature brightly colored, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Sobralia portillae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
