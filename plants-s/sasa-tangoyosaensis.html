Sasa tangoyosaensis

Sasa tangoyosaensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa tangoyosaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Japanese culture.
