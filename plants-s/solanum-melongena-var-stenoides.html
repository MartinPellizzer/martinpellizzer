Solanum melongena var. stenoides

Solanum melongena var. stenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and smaller, more elongated fruits.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1-2 meters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and the fruit is a fleshy, egg-shaped berry that can range in color from purple to white or green depending on the variety.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes in some regions.
