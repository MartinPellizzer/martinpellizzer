Silene burchellii var. maschonica

Silene burchellii var. maschonica, commonly known as the Malawi catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Silene burchellii var. maschonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
