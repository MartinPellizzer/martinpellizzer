Solanum subhastatum

Solanum subhastatum, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that start green and turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and peppers.
