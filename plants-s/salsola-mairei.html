Salsola mairei

Salsola mairei, commonly known as sandmat or Maire's saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in saline and sandy soils, making it a common species in desert environments. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, with a mat-like structure that helps it conserve water.

Salsola mairei plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in phytoremediation due to its ability to tolerate heavy metals.
