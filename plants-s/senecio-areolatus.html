Senecio areolatus

Senecio areolatus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant features a hairy, succulent stem and narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio areolatus is also known for its ability to produce a large number of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season.
