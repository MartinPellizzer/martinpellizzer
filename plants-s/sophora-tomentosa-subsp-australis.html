Sophora tomentosa subsp. australis

Sophora tomentosa subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian silky oaks, is a deciduous tree native to Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and open forest habitats.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable timber and its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
