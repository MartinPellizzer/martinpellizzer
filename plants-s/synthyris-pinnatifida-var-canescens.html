Synthyris pinnatifida var. canescens

Synthyris pinnatifida var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy synthyris, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnatifid leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky, white hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its hairy foliage provides a unique adaptation for reducing water loss in arid environments.
