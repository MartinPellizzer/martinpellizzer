Stylolepis gracilis var. glabra

Stylolepis gracilis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth slender stylolepis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and in wetlands. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, linear leaves that are smooth and hairless. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This variety is characterized by its lack of pubescence, distinguishing it from other varieties within the species.
