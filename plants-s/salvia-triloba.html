Salvia triloba

Salvia triloba, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring a bushy, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Salvia triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
