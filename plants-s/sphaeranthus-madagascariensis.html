Sphaeranthus madagascariensis

Sphaeranthus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar globeflower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive globular flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a compact, spherical shape that gives it its common name.

It is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of the island, showcasing the biodiversity of Madagascar's flora.
