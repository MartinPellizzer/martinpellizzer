Salvia tonalensis

Salvia tonalensis, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial shrub native to the central highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and vibrant violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Salvia tonalensis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
