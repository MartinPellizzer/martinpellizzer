Scepasma buxifolia

Scepasma buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf scepasma, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for birds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
