Seriphidium tridentatum

Seriphidium tridentatum, commonly known as the three-toothed wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features slender, branched stems and small, gray-green leaves with a distinctive tripartite base.

Its flowers are yellow and appear in late spring, attracting various pollinators.
