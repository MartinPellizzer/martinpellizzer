Sinningia araneosa

Sinningia araneosa, commonly known as the spider plant, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or purple. The plant's leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Sinningia araneosa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
