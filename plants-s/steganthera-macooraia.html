Steganthera macooraia

Steganthera macooraia, commonly known as the Macooraia mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces white, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through haustoria that attach to its host. It plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat by supporting wildlife and contributing to the nutrient cycle.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
