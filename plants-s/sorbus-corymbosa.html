Sorbus corymbosa

Sorbus corymbosa, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically odd-pinnate with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by birds. Its fruit, known as a pomes, is an important food source for wildlife.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
