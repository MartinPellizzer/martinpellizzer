Solanum crenatodentatum var. ramossissium

Solanum crenatodentatum var. ramossissium, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves with wavy, toothed margins. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a dark blue or black color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
