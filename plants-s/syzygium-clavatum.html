Syzygium clavatum

Syzygium clavatum, commonly known as the rose apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits with a pinkish-red skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

Syzygium clavatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is popular in local cuisines.
