Stachys reinertii subsp. velezensis

Stachys reinertii subsp.

velezensis, commonly known as the Velezensis Betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
