Solidago aureola

Solidago aureola, commonly known as golden tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and its stems are often hairy.

Solidago aureola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
