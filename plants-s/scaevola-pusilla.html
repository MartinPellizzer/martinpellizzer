Scaevola pusilla

Scaevola pusilla, commonly known as the small fanflower, is a small, compact shrub native to Australia.

It features small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters that resemble fans. The plant has narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Scaevola pusilla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
