Stylanthus thwaitesii

Stylanthus thwaitesii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a red or purple stripe. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Stylanthus thwaitesii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.
