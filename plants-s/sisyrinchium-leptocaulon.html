Sisyrinchium leptocaulon

Sisyrinchium leptocaulon, commonly known as the slender-stemmed blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Iris family (Iridaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, star-shaped blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sisyrinchium leptocaulon is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
