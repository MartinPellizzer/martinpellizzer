Sasa guangxiensis

Sasa guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are typically green or slightly bluish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in panicles, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slight sheen. Sasa guangxiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its ability to form dense, attractive clumps.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
