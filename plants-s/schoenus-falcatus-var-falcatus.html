Schoenus falcatus var. falcatus

Schoenus falcatus var. falcatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of lakes.

Schoenus falcatus var. falcatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
