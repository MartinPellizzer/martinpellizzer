Spiraea crenata f. puberula

Spiraea crenata f. puberula, commonly known as the Downy Spirea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features finely toothed, ovate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to ornamental horticulture.
