Spiraea hypericifolia proles obovata

Spiraea hypericifolia proles obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that produce clusters of white, fluffy flowers in early summer. The leaves are elliptical to obovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
