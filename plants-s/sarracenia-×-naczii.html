Sarracenia × naczii

Sarracenia × naczii, commonly known as the hybrid pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant that combines traits from its parent species, Sarracenia leucophylla and Sarracenia rubra.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in wet, acidic soils and sunny environments. The plant's distinctive red, tubular leaves function as pitfall traps, secreting digestive enzymes to break down captured insects. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with a unique structure that aids in pollination.

Sarracenia × naczii is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and unique ecological adaptations.
