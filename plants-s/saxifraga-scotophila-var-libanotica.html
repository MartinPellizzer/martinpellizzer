Saxifraga scotophila var. libanotica

Saxifraga scotophila var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, shaded environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically basal, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in woodland settings.
