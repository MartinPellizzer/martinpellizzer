Silene laciniata subsp. greggii

Silene laciniata subsp.

greggii, commonly known as Gregg's filaree, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with finely toothed margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
