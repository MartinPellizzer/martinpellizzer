Senecio clivicola var. clivicola

Senecio clivicola var. clivicola, commonly known as the cliff sedum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
