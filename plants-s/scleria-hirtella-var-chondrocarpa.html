Scleria hirtella var. chondrocarpa

Scleria hirtella var. chondrocarpa, commonly known as the hairy bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of streams. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of wetland vegetation.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
