Sorghastrum nutans subvar. canescens

Sorghastrum nutans subvar. canescens, commonly known as the grayleaf dropseed, is a grass species native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a drought-tolerant ornamental grass.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
