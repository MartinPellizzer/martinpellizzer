Sideritis scordioides subsp. laniculata

Sideritis scordioides subsp.

laniculata, commonly known as the woolly sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers.

The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, and this subspecies is no exception.
