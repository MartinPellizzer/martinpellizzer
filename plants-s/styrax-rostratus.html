Styrax rostratus

Styrax rostratus, commonly known as the narrowleaf styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been used historically for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas and can reach heights of up to 6 feet.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
