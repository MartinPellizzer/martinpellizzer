Scleranthus hirsutus

Scleranthus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy sicklegrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its hairy leaves and distinctive seed pods contribute to its ability to thrive in harsh environments.

Scleranthus hirsutus is known for its resilience and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
