Stenophyllus wilsonii

Stenophyllus wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's narrow-leafed plant, is a rare and endemic species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and compact growth habit, often found in humid, shaded environments. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and conservation studies due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements.

However, it faces threats from habitat loss and environmental changes, highlighting the need for further research and protection efforts.
