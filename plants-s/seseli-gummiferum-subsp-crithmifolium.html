Seseli gummiferum subsp. crithmifolium

Seseli gummiferum subsp.

crithmifolium, commonly known as the Crimean seseli, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Crimean Peninsula. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage.

The plant features slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient and distinctive member of the local flora.
