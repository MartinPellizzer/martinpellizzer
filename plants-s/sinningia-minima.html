Sinningia minima

Sinningia minima, commonly known as the miniature gloxinia, is a small flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, showy blossoms in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

Sinningia minima is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
