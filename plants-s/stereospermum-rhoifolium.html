Stereospermum rhoifolium

Stereospermum rhoifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red cedar, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces large, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
