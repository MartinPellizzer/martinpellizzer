Stipulicida setacea var. lacerata

Stipulicida setacea var. lacerata, commonly known as the fringed stipule plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a serrated or fringed edge that gives the plant its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate, lace-like leaves that add a refined texture to gardens.
