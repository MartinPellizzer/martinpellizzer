Scirpus yosemitanus

Scirpus yosemitanus, commonly known as Yosemitian bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Sierra Nevada region of California.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in wetland habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in terminal clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to the structure and stability of wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus yosemitanus plays a vital role in providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
