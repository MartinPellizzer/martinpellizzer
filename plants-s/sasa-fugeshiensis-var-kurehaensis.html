Sasa fugeshiensis var. kurehaensis

Sasa fugeshiensis var. kurehaensis, commonly known as the Kureha bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a mild, sweet aroma.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and cultural significance in Japanese horticulture.
