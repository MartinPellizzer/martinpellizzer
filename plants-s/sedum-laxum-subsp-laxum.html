Sedum laxum subsp. laxum

Sedum laxum subsp.

laxum, commonly known as the looseleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a loose, spreading rosette.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid climates.
