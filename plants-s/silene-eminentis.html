Silene eminentis

Silene eminentis, commonly known as the Mount Emei silene, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene eminentis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its mountainous habitat.
