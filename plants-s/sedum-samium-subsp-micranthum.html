Sedum samium subsp. micranthum

Sedum samium subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
