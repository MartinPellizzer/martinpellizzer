Sclerolaena alata

Sclerolaena alata, commonly known as the winged sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves that are often winged or ribbed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on short, upright stems. Sclerolaena alata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
