Sedum obtusatum var. boreale

Sedum obtusatum var. boreale, commonly known as Arctic sedum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in cold climates with minimal care.
