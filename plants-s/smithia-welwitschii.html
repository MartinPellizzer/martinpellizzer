Smithia welwitschii

Smithia welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's Smithia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
