Selaginella brevispicata

Selaginella brevispicata, commonly known as the dwarf spiky selaginella, is a species of spiky, low-growing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant features small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Selaginella brevispicata is known for its ability to tolerate drought and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
