Syzygium tenuirame

Syzygium tenuirame, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sour fruits similar to tamarind. Syzygium tenuirame is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and medicine.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
