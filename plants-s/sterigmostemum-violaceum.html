Sterigmostemum violaceum

Sterigmostemum violaceum, commonly known as the violet-stemmed aster, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features slender, violet-hued stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Sterigmostemum violaceum is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
