Stachys thracica

Stachys thracica, commonly known as the Thrace betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

Stachys thracica is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky habitats, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
