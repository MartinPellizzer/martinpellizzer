Salix × euryadenia

Salix × euryadenia, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous tree characterized by its drooping branches and narrow, elongated leaves.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically male and female on separate trees. Its bark is often smooth and light gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Salix × euryadenia is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in moist environments.
