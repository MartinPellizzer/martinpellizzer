Sideritis × xavieri

Sideritis × xavieri, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Sideritis scordata and Sideritis glabra, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Sideritis × xavieri is native to regions of the Mediterranean and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
