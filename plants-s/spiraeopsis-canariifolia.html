Spiraeopsis canariifolia

Spiraeopsis canariifolia, commonly known as the Canary Island spiraeo, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
