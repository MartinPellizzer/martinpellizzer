Senecio guerkei

Senecio guerkei, commonly known as the Guerke's groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
