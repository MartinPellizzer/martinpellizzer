Strongylodon lastellianus

Strongylodon lastellianus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fissured bark and produces small, round, red fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and often have a rough texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
