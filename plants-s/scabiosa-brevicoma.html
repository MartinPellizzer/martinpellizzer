Scabiosa brevicoma

Scabiosa brevicoma, commonly known as the dwarf scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features wiry stems and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers with five petals. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Scabiosa brevicoma is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
