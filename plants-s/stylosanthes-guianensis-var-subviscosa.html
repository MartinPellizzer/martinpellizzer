Stylosanthes guianensis var. subviscosa

Stylosanthes guianensis var. subviscosa, commonly known as the sticky stylosanthes, is a perennial leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system, enabling it to thrive in poor soil conditions and arid environments. It is often used as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
