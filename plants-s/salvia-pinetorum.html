Salvia pinetorum

Salvia pinetorum, commonly known as the pineapple sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of showy, violet-red flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong, spicy scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Salvia pinetorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
