Solanum invictum

Solanum invictum, commonly known as the nightshade or the common nightshade, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring woody stems and ovate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. Solanum invictum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
