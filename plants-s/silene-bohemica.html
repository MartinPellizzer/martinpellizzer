Silene bohemica

Silene bohemica, commonly known as Bohemian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It has hairy stems and leaves with a somewhat succulent texture, and its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

Silene bohemica is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
