Smilacina streptopoides

Smilacina streptopoides, commonly known as the creeping smilax, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial vine that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features stout, thorned stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries.

Smilacina streptopoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
