Sideritis canariensis

Sideritis canariensis, commonly known as Canary Island sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sideritis canariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
