Sesbania vesicaria

Sesbania vesicaria, commonly known as the bladder sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Sesbania vesicaria is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
