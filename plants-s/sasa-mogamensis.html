Sasa mogamensis

Sasa mogamensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

Sasa mogamensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
