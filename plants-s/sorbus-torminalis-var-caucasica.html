Sorbus torminalis var. caucasica

Sorbus torminalis var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is often cultivated for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal in temperate climates.
