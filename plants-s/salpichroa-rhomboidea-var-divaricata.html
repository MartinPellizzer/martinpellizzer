Salpichroa rhomboidea var. divaricata

Salpichroa rhomboidea var. divaricata, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often reddish in color, adding visual interest to its appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
