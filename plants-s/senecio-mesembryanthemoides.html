Senecio mesembryanthemoides

Senecio mesembryanthemoides, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and features small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant has fleshy, thick leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio mesembryanthemoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
