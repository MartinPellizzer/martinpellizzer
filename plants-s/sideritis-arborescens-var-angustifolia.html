Sideritis arborescens var. angustifolia

Sideritis arborescens var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This species is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
