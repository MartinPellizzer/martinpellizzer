Solanum torreyi

Solanum torreyi, commonly known as the Torrey's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually purple or blue in color. Its berries are toxic and contain solanine, a glycoalkaloid that can be harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum torreyi is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
