Silene macrosperma

Silene macrosperma, commonly known as the giant catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Silene macrosperma is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
