Salvia whitefoordiae

Salvia whitefoordiae, commonly known as the Whitefoord sage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
