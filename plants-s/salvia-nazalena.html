Salvia nazalena

Salvia nazalena, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a fuzzy texture.

Salvia nazalena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
