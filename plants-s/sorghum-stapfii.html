Sorghum stapfii

Sorghum stapfii, commonly known as the African millet, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy crop in challenging climates.

Its seeds are used for food, animal feed, and traditional brewing, highlighting its agricultural and cultural significance.
