Sedum pinoyi

Sedum pinoyi, commonly known as the Philippine stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum pinoyi is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in its natural habitat.
