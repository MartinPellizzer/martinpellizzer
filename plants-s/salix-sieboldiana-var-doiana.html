Salix sieboldiana var. doiana

Salix sieboldiana var. doiana, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
