Syzygium cinnamomeum

Syzygium cinnamomeum, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, cinnamon-scented leaves and small, white flowers. The tree produces edible, aromatic fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Syzygium cinnamomeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and economic importance in the spice trade.
