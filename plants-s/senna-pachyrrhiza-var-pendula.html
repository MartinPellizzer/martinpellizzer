Senna pachyrrhiza var. pendula

Senna pachyrrhiza var. pendula, commonly known as the hanging senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and bipinnate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its fruits are legumes that split open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The varietal form pendula is distinguished by its drooping or hanging inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance.
