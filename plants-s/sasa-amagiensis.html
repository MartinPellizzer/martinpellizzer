Sasa amagiensis

Sasa amagiensis, commonly known as the Amami bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the Amami Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, clumping growth habit. Sasa amagiensis is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
