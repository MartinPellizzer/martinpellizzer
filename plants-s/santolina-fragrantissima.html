Santolina fragrantissima

Santolina fragrantissima, commonly known as sweet santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, silver-gray foliage that is densely packed and emits a fresh, herbaceous fragrance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in spring, which are borne on short, upright stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Santolina fragrantissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
