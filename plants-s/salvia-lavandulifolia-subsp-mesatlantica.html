Salvia lavandulifolia subsp. mesatlantica

Salvia lavandulifolia subsp.

mesatlantica, commonly known as Mexican lavender, is a perennial herb native to Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple or violet flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and camphor, which contribute to its fragrance and medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and antiseptic effects.
