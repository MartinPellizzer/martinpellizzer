Sorghum macrochaetum

Sorghum macrochaetum, commonly known as the long-hair sorghum, is a tall, robust grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its coarse, serrated leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, two-lobed flowers that are typically pale green to reddish in color. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its grain and forage.

Its fibrous stalks are also used in traditional crafts and construction.
