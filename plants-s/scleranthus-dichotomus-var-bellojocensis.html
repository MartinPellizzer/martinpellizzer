Scleranthus dichotomus var. bellojocensis

Scleranthus dichotomus var. bellojocensis, commonly known as the Bellojocensis thornweed, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has a hairy texture on its leaves and stems, which helps in reducing water loss in arid environments. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
