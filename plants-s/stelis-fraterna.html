Stelis fraterna

Stelis fraterna, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, forming a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

Stelis fraterna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
