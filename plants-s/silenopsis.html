Silenopsis

Silenopsis, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Silenopsis species are often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
