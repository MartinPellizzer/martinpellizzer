Scirpus schmidii

Scirpus schmidii, commonly known as Schmid's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Scirpus schmidii thrives in moist to aquatic habitats and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of freshwater ecosystems.
