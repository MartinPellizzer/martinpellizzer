Salvia barrelieri var. pluripartita

Salvia barrelieri var. pluripartita, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically violet to purple in color. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
