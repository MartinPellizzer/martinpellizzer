Sarcanthus carinatus

Sarcanthus carinatus, commonly known as the spiny holly fern, is a unique member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its stiff, spiny leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its fronds are usually arching and can reach lengths of up to several feet.

Sarcanthus carinatus is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive appearance.
