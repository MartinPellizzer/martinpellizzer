Sedum laconicum subsp. insulare

Sedum laconicum subsp.

insulare, commonly known as the island sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
