Statice alberti

Statice alberti, commonly known as Albert's sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and purple, making it a popular choice for garden use.

Statice alberti is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
