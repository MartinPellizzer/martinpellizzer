Scorzonera mackmeliana

Scorzonera mackmeliana, commonly known as the Mackmel's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in the summer months. Scorzonera mackmeliana is known for its tuberous roots, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
