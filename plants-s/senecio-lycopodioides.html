Senecio lycopodioides

Senecio lycopodioides, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like appearance with yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Senecio lycopodioides is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resilience to drought conditions.
