Spiraea bumalda

Spiraea bumalda, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching stems covered in small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers in early to mid-summer, which are borne on the ends of the branches. Its foliage turns a striking gold in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Spiraea bumalda thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
