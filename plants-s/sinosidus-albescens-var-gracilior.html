Sinosidus albescens var. gracilior

Sinosidus albescens var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender white sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikelets. Its botanical structure is characterized by a rhizomatous root system and elongated stems.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
