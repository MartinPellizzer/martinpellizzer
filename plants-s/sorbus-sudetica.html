Sorbus sudetica

Sorbus sudetica, commonly known as the Carpathian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rounded, often multi-stemmed form. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and turn yellow in the fall.

Sorbus sudetica is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
