Setaria megaphylla var. chevalieri

Setaria megaphylla var. chevalieri, commonly known as the giant foxtail grass, is a tall annual grass species native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and distinctive spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows to heights of 1 to 3 meters, making it a prominent feature in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are often hairy, and the seeds are small, with a distinctive three-angled shape.

This species is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types, contributing to its ecological and agricultural significance.
