Schoenus tendo var. laevinux

Schoenus tendo var. laevinux, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
