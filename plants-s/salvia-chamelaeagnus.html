Salvia chamelaeagnus

Salvia chamelaeagnus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
