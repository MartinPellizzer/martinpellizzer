Schnella

Schnella, commonly known as the quick-growing shrub, is a member of the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

Its fruit, which is typically green and edible, contains a high amount of vitamin C. Schnella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
