Sanguisorba muricata subsp. ertugrulii

Sanguisorba muricata subsp.

ertugrulii, commonly known as the Turkish bloodwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey. It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its basal leaves are lanceolate with serrated edges.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
