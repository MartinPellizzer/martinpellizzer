Serratula purpurata

Serratula purpurata, commonly known as purple ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name.

Serratula purpurata is often considered a weed but can be found in various ecological settings.
