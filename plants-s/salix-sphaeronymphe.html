Salix sphaeronymphe

Salix sphaeronymphe, commonly known as the spherical willow, is a rare and unique species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical growth form, which sets it apart from other willow species. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
