Syringa affinis

Syringa affinis, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Syringa affinis is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
