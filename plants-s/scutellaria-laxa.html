Scutellaria laxa

Scutellaria laxa, commonly known as the lax scutellaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple. Its flowers are borne in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scutellaria laxa is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.
