Smilax bona-nox var. hastata

Smilax bona-nox var. hastata, commonly known as the blackberry vine, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in sharp, curved spines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinctive, bitter taste.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
