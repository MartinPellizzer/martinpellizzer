Salsola septentrionalis

Salsola septentrionalis, commonly known as northern saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in saline soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, green to yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and succulent, which help it conserve water.

Salsola septentrionalis plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
