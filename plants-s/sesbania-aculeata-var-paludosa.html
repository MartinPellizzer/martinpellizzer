Sesbania aculeata var. paludosa

Sesbania aculeata var. paludosa, commonly known as swamp sesbania, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow, pod-like flowers. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in floodplains and along riverbanks. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

It is also valued for its use in forage and erosion control in aquatic habitats.
