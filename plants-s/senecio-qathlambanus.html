Senecio qathlambanus

Senecio qathlambanus, commonly known as the Ethiopian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
