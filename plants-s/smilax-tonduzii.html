Smilax tonduzii

Smilax tonduzii, commonly known as the red-rooted smilax, is a climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its thorny stems and waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Smilax tonduzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
