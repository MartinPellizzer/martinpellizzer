Senecio zapalae

Senecio zapalae, commonly known as the Zapala groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of Argentina.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Senecio zapalae is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
