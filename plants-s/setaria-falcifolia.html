Setaria falcifolia

Setaria falcifolia, commonly known as the sword grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Setaria falcifolia is known for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
