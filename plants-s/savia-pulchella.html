Savia pulchella

Savia pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Native to parts of South America, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its latex sap is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it a plant to handle with care.
