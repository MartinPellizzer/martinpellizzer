Stelis ferrelliae

Stelis ferrelliae, commonly known as the Farrell's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with vibrant colors ranging from white to pink and purple.

Stelis ferrelliae prefers warm, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests or rainforests.
