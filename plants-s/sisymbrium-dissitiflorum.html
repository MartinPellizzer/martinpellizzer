Sisymbrium dissitiflorum

Sisymbrium dissitiflorum, commonly known as the hairy brookweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are pinnately divided with hairy surfaces. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.

Sisymbrium dissitiflorum is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich environments.
