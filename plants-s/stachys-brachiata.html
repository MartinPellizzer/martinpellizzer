Stachys brachiata

Stachys brachiata, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of violet or blue flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
