Sinapis nigra var. laevigata

Sinapis nigra var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth mustard, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

Sinapis nigra var. laevigata is also known for its potential medicinal properties and historical use in traditional remedies.
