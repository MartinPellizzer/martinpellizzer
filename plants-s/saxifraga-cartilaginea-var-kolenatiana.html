Saxifraga cartilaginea var. kolenatiana

Saxifraga cartilaginea var. kolenatiana, commonly known as the Kolenatian saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Saxifragaceae family.
