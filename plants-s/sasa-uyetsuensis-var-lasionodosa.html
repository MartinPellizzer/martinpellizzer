Sasa uyetsuensis var. lasionodosa

Sasa uyetsuensis var. lasionodosa, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or silver bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa genus native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like culms that are often covered with a silvery, powdery bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
