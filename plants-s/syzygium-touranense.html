Syzygium touranense

Syzygium touranense, commonly known as the Touranese tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, sour fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarind, though they are less commonly consumed. Syzygium touranense thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and potential horticultural purposes.
