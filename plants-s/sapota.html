Sapota

Sapota, commonly known as chiku, is a tropical fruit tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is typically oval or round, with a smooth, thin skin that turns from green to yellow or orange when ripe. Inside, it has a creamy, sweet flesh that is rich in sugars and contains a single large seed.

Sapota is valued for its nutritional content, including vitamins A and C, and is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
