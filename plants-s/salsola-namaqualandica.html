Salsola namaqualandica

Salsola namaqualandica, commonly known as the Namib Desert goosefoot, is a hardy shrub native to the hyperarid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Salsola namaqualandica plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
