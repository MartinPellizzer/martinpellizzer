Senecio brittonianus

Senecio brittonianus, commonly known as Britton's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the spring.

Senecio brittonianus is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
