Spermacoce hyssopifolia

Spermacoce hyssopifolia, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the early morning. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release small, shiny seeds.

Spermacoce hyssopifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
