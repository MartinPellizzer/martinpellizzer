Salix × subnuda

Salix × subnuda, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces catkins as its primary reproductive structures, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside.

It thrives in moist environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.
