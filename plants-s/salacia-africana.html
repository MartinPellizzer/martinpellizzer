Salacia africana

Salacia africana, commonly known as the African salacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is not closely related to them. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The bark and leaves of Salacia africana have been used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
