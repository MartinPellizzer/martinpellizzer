Saxifraga inconspicua

Saxifraga inconspicua, commonly known as the inconspicuous saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
