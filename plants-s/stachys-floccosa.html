Stachys floccosa

Stachys floccosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as limestone soils and open woodlands. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its fibrous roots and compact growth habit make it well adapted to arid environments.
