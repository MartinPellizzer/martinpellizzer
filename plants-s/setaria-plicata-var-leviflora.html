Setaria plicata var. leviflora

Setaria plicata var. leviflora, commonly known as smooth bristlegrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
