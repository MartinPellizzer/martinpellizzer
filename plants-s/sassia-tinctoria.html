Sassia tinctoria

Sassia tinctoria, commonly known as the dye plant or yellow wood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its ability to produce a yellow dye from its leaves, which was historically used in textile production. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Sassia tinctoria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
