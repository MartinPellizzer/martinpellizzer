Scutellaria nipponica

Scutellaria nipponica, commonly known as Japanese skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Scutellaria nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
