Sida martiusiana

Sida martiusiana, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its rough, often hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Sida martiusiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
