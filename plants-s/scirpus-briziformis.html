Scirpus briziformis

Scirpus briziformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-sided flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Scirpus briziformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
