Sapium klotzschianum var. glaziovii

Sapium klotzschianum var. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's cupuacu, is a species native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed, red or orange fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
