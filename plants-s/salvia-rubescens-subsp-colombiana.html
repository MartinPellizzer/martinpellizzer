Salvia rubescens subsp. colombiana

Salvia rubescens subsp.

colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian scarlet sage, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of Colombia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
