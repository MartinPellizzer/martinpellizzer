Senecio crassifolius subsp. transiens

Senecio crassifolius subsp.

transiens, commonly known as the Transient Senecio, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and large, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white powder.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, terminal clusters, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
