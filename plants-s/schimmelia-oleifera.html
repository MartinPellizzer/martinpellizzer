Schimmelia oleifera

Schimmelia oleifera, commonly known as the oil plant, is a species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a valuable essential oil, which is extracted from its leaves and used in various industrial applications. Schimmelia oleifera thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and economic contexts.
