Scirpus erectus

Scirpus erectus, commonly known as the tall bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features tall, stiff, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and grow in dense clusters at the base. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikes of pale brown to yellowish flowers.

Scirpus erectus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
