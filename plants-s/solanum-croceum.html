Solanum croceum

Solanum croceum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum croceum is often found in dry, open habitats and is characterized by its distinctive yellow coloration.
