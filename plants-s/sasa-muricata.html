Sasa muricata

Sasa muricata, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its rhizomes form a network that allows it to spread vegetatively and establish dense thickets.

Sasa muricata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
