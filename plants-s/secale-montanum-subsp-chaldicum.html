Secale montanum subsp. chaldicum

Secale montanum subsp.

chaldicum, commonly known as the Armenian ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
