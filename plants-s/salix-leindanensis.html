Salix leindanensis

Salix leindanensis, commonly known as the Leindan willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically found in Nepal and Bhutan. This shrub or small tree typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside, and it produces catkin-like inflorescences.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
