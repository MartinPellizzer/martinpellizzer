Stelis lappacea

Stelis lappacea, commonly known as the hairy stelis, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in a raceme, with a distinctive white or pale pink coloration.

Stelis lappacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
