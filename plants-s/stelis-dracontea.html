Stelis dracontea

Stelis dracontea, commonly known as the dragon's tail orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the flowers often have a distinctive, elongated shape.

Stelis dracontea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique appearance.
