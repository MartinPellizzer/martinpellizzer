Senkenbergia crassifolia

Senkenbergia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf senckenbergia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and extensive, enabling it to access groundwater.

Senkenbergia crassifolia is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils.
