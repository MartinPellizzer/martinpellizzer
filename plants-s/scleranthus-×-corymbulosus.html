Scleranthus × corymbulosus

Scleranthus × corymbulosus, commonly known as the hybrid meadow sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Scleranthus × corymbulosus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
