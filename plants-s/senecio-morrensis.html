Senecio morrensis

Senecio morrensis, commonly known as Morren's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
