Sonerila erecta var. discolor

Sonerila erecta var. discolor, commonly known as the Variegated Screw Tree, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that display a striking variegation of green and white. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by distinctive blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
