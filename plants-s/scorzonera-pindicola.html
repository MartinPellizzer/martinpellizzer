Scorzonera pindicola

Scorzonera pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Scorzonera pindicola is known for its edible roots, which have a nutty flavor and are used in traditional cuisine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
