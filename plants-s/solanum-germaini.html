Solanum germaini

Solanum germaini, commonly known as the Germain's nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Brazil. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals, and the fruit is a fleshy, globose berry that contains several seeds.
