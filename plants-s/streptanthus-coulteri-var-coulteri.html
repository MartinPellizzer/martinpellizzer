Streptanthus coulteri var. coulteri

Streptanthus coulteri var. coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's whitlow grass, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience to arid conditions.
