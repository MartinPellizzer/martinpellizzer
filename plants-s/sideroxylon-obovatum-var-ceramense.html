Sideroxylon obovatum var. ceramense

Sideroxylon obovatum var. ceramense, commonly known as the Ceramese ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Ceram.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.

This species is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
