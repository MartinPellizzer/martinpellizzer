Seseli carinatum

Seseli carinatum, commonly known as the horned milk-vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Seseli carinatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
