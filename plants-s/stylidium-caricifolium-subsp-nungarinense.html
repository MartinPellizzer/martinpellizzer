Stylidium caricifolium subsp. nungarinense

Stylidium caricifolium subsp.

nungarinense, commonly known as the Nungarin slender triggerplant, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that are highly attractive to insects, particularly bees and flies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is part of the genus Stylidium, which is known for its unique trigger mechanism in flower pollination.
