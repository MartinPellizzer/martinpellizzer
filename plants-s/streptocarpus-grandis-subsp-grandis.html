Streptocarpus grandis subsp. grandis

Streptocarpus grandis subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the giant streptocarpus, is a species native to South Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of violet and white.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
