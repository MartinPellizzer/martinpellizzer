Saxifraga stellaris var. hispidula

Saxifraga stellaris var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy star saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine, hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in cold, harsh environments. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine tundra or mountainous regions.

This plant is well adapted to extreme conditions, showcasing resilience and unique botanical characteristics.
