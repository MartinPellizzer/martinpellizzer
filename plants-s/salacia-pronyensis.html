Salacia pronyensis

Salacia pronyensis, commonly known as the Prony's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often brightly colored fruits. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense canopy of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in humid environments.

The flowers are small and clustered, often greenish-white in color, and are followed by the showy, edible fruits that are a feature of the species.
