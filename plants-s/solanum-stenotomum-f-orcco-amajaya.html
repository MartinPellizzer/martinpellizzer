Solanum stenotomum f. orcco-amajaya

Solanum stenotomum f. orcco-amajaya, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
