Solanum carautae

Solanum carautae, commonly known as the Carauta nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Solanum carautae is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
