Stipa siciliensis

Stipa siciliensis, commonly known as Sicilian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces slender spikelets that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Stipa siciliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining biodiversity in its native environment.
