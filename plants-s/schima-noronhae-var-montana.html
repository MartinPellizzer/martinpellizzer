Schima noronhae var. montana

Schima noronhae var. montana, commonly known as the mountain schima, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that turns reddish-brown during the winter. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in subtropical and temperate forest ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
