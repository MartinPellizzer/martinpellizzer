Senecio candidus

Senecio candidus, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio candidus is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
