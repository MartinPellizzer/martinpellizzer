Sideritis hyssopifolia f. alpina

Sideritis hyssopifolia f. alpina, commonly known as alpine sidewort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is typically found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude conditions and is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
