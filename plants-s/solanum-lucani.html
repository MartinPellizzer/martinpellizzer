Solanum lucani

Solanum lucani, commonly known as the Lucanian nightshade, is a species native to southern Europe, particularly Italy.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are typically white or pale purple, with five petals and a short corolla, and they bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
