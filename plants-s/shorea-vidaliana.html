Shorea vidaliana

Shorea vidaliana, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, often greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a broad ovate shape with a pointed tip.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and red or orange fruits that contain one or two seeds.
