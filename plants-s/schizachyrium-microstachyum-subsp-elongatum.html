Schizachyrium microstachyum subsp. elongatum

Schizachyrium microstachyum subsp.

elongatum, commonly known as tall false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States. It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
