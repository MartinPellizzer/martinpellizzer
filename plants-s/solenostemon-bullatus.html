Solenostemon bullatus

Solenostemon bullatus, commonly known as the silver mint or woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Solenostemon bullatus is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
