Syzygium quadrangulare

Syzygium quadrangulare, commonly known as the quadrangular clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its smooth, quadrangular stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark red, edible fruits. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

Syzygium quadrangulare is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary and medicinal uses.
