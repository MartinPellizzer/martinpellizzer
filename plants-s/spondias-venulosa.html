Spondias venulosa

Spondias venulosa, commonly known as the red mombin, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-colored fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
