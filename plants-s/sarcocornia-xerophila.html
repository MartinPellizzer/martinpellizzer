Sarcocornia xerophila

Sarcocornia xerophila, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water. The plant has small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits containing seeds. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other arid or saline soils.

Its ability to thrive in high-salinity conditions makes it an important component of salt-tolerant ecosystems.
