Scrophularia scorodonia var. glabrescens

Scrophularia scorodonia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, with a distinctive purple or reddish coloration. Its flowers are tubular and arranged in spikes, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is characterized by its reduced pubescence compared to other varieties of Scrophularia scorodonia.
