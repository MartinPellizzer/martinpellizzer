Senecio punduahensis

Senecio punduahensis, commonly known as the Punduahensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
