Spinacia oleracea var. erfurtensis

Spinacia oleracea var. erfurtensis, commonly known as the Erfurt spinach, is a lesser-known variety of spinach native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, tender leaves and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for small garden spaces. The plant thrives in cool climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. It is typically harvested before the leaves become too large, ensuring a crisp texture and mild flavor.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various growing conditions.
