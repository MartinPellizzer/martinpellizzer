Salvia inconspicua

Salvia inconspicua, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and native plant gardens.
