Syzygium gustavioides

Syzygium gustavioides, commonly known as the New Guinea apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, pinkish-red fruits that are sweet and juicy. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Syzygium gustavioides is also valued for its potential in agroforestry systems due to its adaptability and resilience.
