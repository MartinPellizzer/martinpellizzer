Sedum rhodiola var. ovatum

Sedum rhodiola var. ovatum, commonly known as the Rosy Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in xeriscaping.
