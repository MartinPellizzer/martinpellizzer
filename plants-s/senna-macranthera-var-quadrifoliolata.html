Senna macranthera var. quadrifoliolata

Senna macranthera var. quadrifoliolata, commonly known as the four-leaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features palmately divided leaves with four leaflets, giving it its distinctive appearance. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
