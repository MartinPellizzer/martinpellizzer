Salvia espirito-santensis

Salvia espirito-santensis, commonly known as the Espirito Santo sage, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
