Symphytum variegatum

Symphytum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated comfrey, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces tall spikes of violet or blue flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Due to its rapid growth and medicinal properties, it is often used in herbal remedies and as a soil amendment.
