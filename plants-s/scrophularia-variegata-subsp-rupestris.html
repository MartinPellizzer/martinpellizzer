Scrophularia variegata subsp. rupestris

Scrophularia variegata subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky habitats in Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as limestone cliffs and gravelly soils.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
