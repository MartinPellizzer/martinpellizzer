Silene glaucina

Silene glaucina, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Silene glaucina is a perennial species that is relatively easy to grow in gardens with well-drained soil.
