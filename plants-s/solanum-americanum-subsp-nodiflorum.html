Solanum americanum subsp. nodiflorum

Solanum americanum subsp.

nodiflorum, commonly known as the American nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and roadside areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
