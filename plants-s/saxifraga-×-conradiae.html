Saxifraga × conradiae

Saxifraga × conradiae, commonly known as the Conrad's saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is often found in mountainous areas such as the Alps and Pyrenees.

Saxifraga × conradiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
