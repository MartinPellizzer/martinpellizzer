Strempelia indulgens

Strempelia indulgens, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the genus Strempelia in the family Cactaceae, although it is sometimes classified under Schlumbergera. The plant is characterized by its segmented stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in winter. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Strempelia indulgens is popular as a decorative plant due to its long blooming period and ease of care.
