Saussurea nagensis

Saussurea nagensis, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and cold environments, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
