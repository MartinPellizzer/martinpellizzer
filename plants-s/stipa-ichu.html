Stipa ichu

Stipa ichu, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense tussocks. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is well adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Stipa ichu plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
