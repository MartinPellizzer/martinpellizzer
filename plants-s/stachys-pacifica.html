Stachys pacifica

Stachys pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Stachys pacifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
