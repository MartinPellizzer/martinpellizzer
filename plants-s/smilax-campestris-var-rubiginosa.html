Smilax campestris var. rubiginosa

Smilax campestris var. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and heart-shaped, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural landscapes.
