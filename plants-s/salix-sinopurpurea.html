Salix sinopurpurea

Salix sinopurpurea, commonly known as the purple willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and clusters of bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a silvery purple underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing riverbanks.
