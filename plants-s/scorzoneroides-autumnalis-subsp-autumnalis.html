Scorzoneroides autumnalis subsp. autumnalis

Scorzoneroides autumnalis subsp.

autumnalis, commonly known as autumnal scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing small, daisy-like inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fall blooms.
