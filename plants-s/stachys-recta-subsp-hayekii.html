Stachys recta subsp. hayekii

Stachys recta subsp.

hayekii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy as well.

The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
