Spathoglottis grandifolia

Spathoglottis grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, and it prefers well-drained, moist soils.

Spathoglottis grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
