Stipa megapotamia var. juncoides

Stipa megapotamia var. juncoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
