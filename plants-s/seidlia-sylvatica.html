Seidlia sylvatica

Seidlia sylvatica, commonly known as the forest seidlia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth margin and produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers in late spring. Its flowering period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Seidlia sylvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
