Stomatium pyrodorum

Stomatium pyrodorum, commonly known as the flameleaf spirea, is a flowering plant characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or orange, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically pink or red, blooming in early summer, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
