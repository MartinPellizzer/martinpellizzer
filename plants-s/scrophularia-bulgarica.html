Scrophularia bulgarica

Scrophularia bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Balkans.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. The plant features upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in spikes and are usually pale purple to pinkish in color, with five petals.

The leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
