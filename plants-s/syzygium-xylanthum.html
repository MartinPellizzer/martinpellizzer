Syzygium xylanthum

Syzygium xylanthum, commonly known as the red syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, edible seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
