Stachys lamioides

Stachys lamioides, commonly known as narrowleaf hedge-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Stachys lamioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
