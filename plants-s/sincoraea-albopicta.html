Sincoraea albopicta

Sincoraea albopicta, commonly known as the white-tailed ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

Sincoraea albopicta plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
