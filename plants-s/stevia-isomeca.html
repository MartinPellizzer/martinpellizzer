Stevia isomeca

Stevia isomeca, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from compounds called steviol glycosides, which are found in its leaves. Stevia isomeca thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its use as a natural sweetener has gained popularity due to its low calorie content and health benefits.
