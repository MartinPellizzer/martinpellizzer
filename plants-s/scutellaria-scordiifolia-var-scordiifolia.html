Scutellaria scordiifolia var. scordiifolia

Scutellaria scordiifolia var. scordiifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a compact, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a square stem, opposite leaves, and a distinctive calyx that persists after flowering.

It is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
