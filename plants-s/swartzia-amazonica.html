Swartzia amazonica

Swartzia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Swartzia amazonica plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
