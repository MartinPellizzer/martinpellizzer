Sorbus villosa f. laevis

Sorbus villosa f. laevis, commonly known as the smooth rowan, is a cultivar of the rowan tree characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and fruits.

It belongs to the genus Sorbus, which includes a wide variety of shrubs and small trees known for their ornamental value. The plant typically produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

This form is prized for its compact growth habit and seasonal display of foliage and fruit.
