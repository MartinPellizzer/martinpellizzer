Salvia acutata

Salvia acutata, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional medicine.

Salvia acutata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
