Senecio brachylepis

Senecio brachylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and fleshy, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
