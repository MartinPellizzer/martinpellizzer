Styrax macarenensis

Styrax macarenensis, commonly known as the Malayan styrax, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds. Its growth habit includes a compact canopy and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
