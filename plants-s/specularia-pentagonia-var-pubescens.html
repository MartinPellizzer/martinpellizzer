Specularia pentagonia var. pubescens

Specularia pentagonia var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent pentagon bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal-shaped flowers, which are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that give it its varietal name, "pubescens.". It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
