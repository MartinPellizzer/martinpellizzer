Schizachyrium scoparium subvar. flexile

Schizachyrium scoparium subvar. flexile, commonly known as eastern bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features fine, blue-green blades that are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from green to red and then to a soft blue-purple in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
