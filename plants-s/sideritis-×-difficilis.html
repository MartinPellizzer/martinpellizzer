Sideritis × difficilis

Sideritis × difficilis, commonly known as the Greek heath or mountain tea, is a hybrid species within the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Greece and the Balkans, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple, and its hairy, narrow leaves. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Sideritis × difficilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
