Santolina macrophylla

Santolina macrophylla, commonly known as silver woolly lamb's ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its aromatic leaves are often used in herbal teas and as a decorative element in gardens.

Santolina macrophylla is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract beneficial insects.
