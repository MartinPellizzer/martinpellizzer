Sarcoglottis umbrosa

Sarcoglottis umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy sarcoglottis, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
