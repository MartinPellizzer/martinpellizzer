Salix × paludum

Salix × paludum, commonly known as the swamp willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are elongated and have a finely serrated margin, often appearing dark green with a silvery underside.

Salix × paludum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
