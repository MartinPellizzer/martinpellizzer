Stereospermum neuranthum

Stereospermum neuranthum, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
