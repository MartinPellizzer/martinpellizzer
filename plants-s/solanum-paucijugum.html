Solanum paucijugum

Solanum paucijugum, commonly known as the few-leafed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
