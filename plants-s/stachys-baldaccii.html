Stachys baldaccii

Stachys baldaccii, commonly known as the baldacci's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from purple to pink, attracting various pollinators.

Stachys baldaccii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit.
