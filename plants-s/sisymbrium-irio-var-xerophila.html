Sisymbrium irio var. xerophila

Sisymbrium irio var. xerophila, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and feathery, pinnate leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

Its ability to tolerate drought conditions makes it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
