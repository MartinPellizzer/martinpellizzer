Styrax betongensis

Styrax betongensis, commonly known as the Betong styrax, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The bark and leaves of Styrax betongensis contain aromatic compounds, which have been used in traditional medicine and for crafting incense.
