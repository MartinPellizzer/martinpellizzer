Selaginella rupestris f. wrightii

Selaginella rupestris f. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, scale-like leaves arranged in tight, spiraled clusters. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically found at the tips of its stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological resilience.
