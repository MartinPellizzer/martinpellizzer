Syzygium versteegii

Syzygium versteegii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, edible drupes that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in carpentry.

Syzygium versteegii thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
