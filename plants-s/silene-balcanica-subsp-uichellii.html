Silene balcanica subsp. uichellii

Silene balcanica subsp.

uichellii, commonly known as the Balkan campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and shorter pedicels compared to the typical form of Silene balcanica. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant has lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
