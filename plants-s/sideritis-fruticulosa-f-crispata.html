Sideritis fruticulosa f. crispata

Sideritis fruticulosa f. crispata, commonly known as the crinkled ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It is typically found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
