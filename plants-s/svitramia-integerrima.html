Svitramia integerrima

Svitramia integerrima, commonly known as the hardy geranium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Svitramia integerrima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
