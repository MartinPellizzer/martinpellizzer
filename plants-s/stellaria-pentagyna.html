Stellaria pentagyna

Stellaria pentagyna, commonly known as the five-angled starwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Stellaria pentagyna is often found in moist, disturbed soils in meadows and along roadsides.
