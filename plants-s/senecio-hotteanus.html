Senecio hotteanus

Senecio hotteanus, commonly known as the Hotte's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster, and bloom during the summer months.

Senecio hotteanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
