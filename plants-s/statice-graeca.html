Statice graeca

Statice graeca, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like flower heads composed of numerous small, blue or purple petals. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas and dunes. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are typically gray-green in color, and it blooms from late summer into autumn.

Statice graeca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in dried floral arrangements.
