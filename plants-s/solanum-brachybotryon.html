Solanum brachybotryon

Solanum brachybotryon, commonly known as the short-fruited nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Solanum brachybotryon is also notable for its nocturnal blooming habit, with flowers opening in the evening.
