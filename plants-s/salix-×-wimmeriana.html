Salix × wimmeriana

Salix × wimmeriana, commonly known as the Wimmer's willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its weeping habit, with long, slender branches that cascade downward, creating a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It thrives in moist environments, such as streambanks and wetlands, and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring, and it is valued for its adaptability to wet soil conditions.
