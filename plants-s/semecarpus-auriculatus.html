Semecarpus auriculatus

Semecarpus auriculatus, commonly known as the ear-shaped chestnut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, ear-like leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its bark and seeds contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their astringent and antifungal properties.
