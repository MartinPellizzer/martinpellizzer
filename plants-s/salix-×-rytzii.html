Salix × rytzii

Salix × rytzii, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Salicaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix alba (white willow) and Salix triandra (southern willow), resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The leaves are typically elongated and serrated, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for wind pollination.

This species is often found in wetland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
