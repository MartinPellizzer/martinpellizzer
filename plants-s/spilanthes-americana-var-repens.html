Spilanthes americana var. repens

Spilanthes americana var. repens, commonly known as stinkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a dense rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its pungent, mustard-like odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
