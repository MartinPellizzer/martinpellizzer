Sorbus aucuparia var. subserrata

Sorbus aucuparia var. subserrata, commonly known as the serrated rowan, is a variant of the European rowan tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves and small, orange-red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often grows in woodland or mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
