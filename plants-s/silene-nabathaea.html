Silene nabathaea

Silene nabathaea, commonly known as the Nabatean catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Silene nabathaea is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and historical significance in ancient cultures.
