Solanum andigenum convar. microstigmatum

Solanum andigenum convar. microstigmatum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, and it produces berries that range in color from yellow to red.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
