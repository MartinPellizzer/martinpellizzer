Satureja camphorata

Satureja camphorata, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like camphor and thymol, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
