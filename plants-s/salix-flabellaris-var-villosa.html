Salix flabellaris var. villosa

Salix flabellaris var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy willow, is a variant of the flabellaris willow native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. It produces catkins as its primary floral structure, which are essential for its reproduction.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a soft, downy texture.
