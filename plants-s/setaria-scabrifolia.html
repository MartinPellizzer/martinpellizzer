Setaria scabrifolia

Setaria scabrifolia, commonly known as roughleaf barnyardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many regions where it is not native.
