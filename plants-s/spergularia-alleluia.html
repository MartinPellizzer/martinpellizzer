Spergularia alleluia

Spergularia alleluia, commonly known as the "Alleluia sandwort," is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has succulent leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along shorelines.

Spergularia alleluia is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it a hardy species in coastal ecosystems.
