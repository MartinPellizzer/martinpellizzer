Senecillis carpathica

Senecillis carpathica, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in a single head, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Senecillis carpathica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
