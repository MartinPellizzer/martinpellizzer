Senecio chodatianus

Senecio chodatianus, commonly known as the Chodat's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a succulent stem that stores water. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio chodatianus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
