Senecio pseudodiscoideus

Senecio pseudodiscoideus, commonly known as the Cape Daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and has small, narrow leaves. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio pseudodiscoideus is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
