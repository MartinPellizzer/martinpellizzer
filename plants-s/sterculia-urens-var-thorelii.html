Sterculia urens var. thorelii

Sterculia urens var. thorelii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also important in traditional medicine and ecological systems.
