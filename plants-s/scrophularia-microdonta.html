Scrophularia microdonta

Scrophularia microdonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are tubular, pink to purple in color, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia microdonta is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with similar floral structures and ecological preferences.
