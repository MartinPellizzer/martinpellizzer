Sorbus teodori f. cinerae

Sorbus teodori f. cinerae, commonly known as the silverleaf whitebeam, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the whitebeam genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are typically small, oval-shaped, and have a leathery texture.

The fruit is a small, aggregate berry that matures to a dark red or black color, providing a food source for various wildlife species.
