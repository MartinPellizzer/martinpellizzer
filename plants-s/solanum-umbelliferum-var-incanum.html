Solanum umbelliferum var. incanum

Solanum umbelliferum var. incanum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces smooth, spherical berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in arid to semi-arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
