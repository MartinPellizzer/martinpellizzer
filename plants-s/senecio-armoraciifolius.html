Senecio armoraciifolius

Senecio armoraciifolius, commonly known as the African iron plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
