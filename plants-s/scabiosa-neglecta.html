Scabiosa neglecta

Scabiosa neglecta, commonly known as the common scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

Scabiosa neglecta is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to wildflower gardens.
