Solanum phureja f. chocclo

Solanum phureja f. chocclo, commonly known as the Choclo pepper, is a botanical variant of the potato plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated tubers that resemble small, round potatoes. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andean regions of South America. Its tubers are typically white or pale yellow in color and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor.

This variety is valued for its unique taste and is often used in traditional Andean cuisine.
