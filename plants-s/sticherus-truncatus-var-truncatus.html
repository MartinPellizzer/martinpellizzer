Sticherus truncatus var. truncatus

Sticherus truncatus var. truncatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sticherus, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, linear leaves that are often slightly notched at the tips. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
