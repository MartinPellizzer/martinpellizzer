Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale, commonly known as common comfrey, is a perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and tall spikes of violet or blue flowers. The plant contains a high concentration of allantoin, which is known for its soothing properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for healing wounds and reducing inflammation.
