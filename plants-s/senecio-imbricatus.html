Senecio imbricatus

Senecio imbricatus, commonly known as the stonecrop sedum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to store water in its leaves.

Senecio imbricatus is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
