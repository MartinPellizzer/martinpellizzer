Senecio pulcher

Senecio pulcher, commonly known as the "Beautiful Senecio," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Senecio pulcher is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
