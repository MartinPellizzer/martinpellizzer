Sageraea nitida

Sageraea nitida, commonly known as the silky sagera, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
