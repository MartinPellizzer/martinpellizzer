Syngonanthus widgrenianus var. puberulifolius

Syngonanthus widgrenianus var. puberulifolius, commonly known as the pubescent-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a pubescent appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.
