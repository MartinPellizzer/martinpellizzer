Suprago spicata

Suprago spicata, commonly known as the spiky flameflower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright red, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are pollinated by birds and insects. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
