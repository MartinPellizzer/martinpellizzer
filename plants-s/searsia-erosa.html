Searsia erosa

Searsia erosa, commonly known as the white-leaf bramble, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its pale green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Searsia erosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
