Symplocos ferruginea var. polystachya

Symplocos ferruginea var. polystachya, commonly known as the rusty waxleaf symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and rusty red or brown underneath, providing striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color, attracting various bird species.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a range of soil types.
