Silene virgata

Silene virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's pink, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene virgata is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
