Schedonorus caldasii

Schedonorus caldasii, commonly known as the Caldasian meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude grasslands of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
