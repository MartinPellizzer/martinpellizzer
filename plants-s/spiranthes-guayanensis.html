Spiranthes guayanensis

Spiranthes guayanensis, commonly known as the Guianan spiral orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spirally arranged flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitat.
