Semiramisia alata

Semiramisia alata, commonly known as the winged bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant features showy, winged bracts that give it its common name and contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Semiramisia alata is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively easy to care for.
