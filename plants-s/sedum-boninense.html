Sedum boninense

Sedum boninense, commonly known as the Bonin sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, leafy stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, adapting to harsh, saline conditions. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in water retention.

Sedum boninense is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid or semi-arid climates.
