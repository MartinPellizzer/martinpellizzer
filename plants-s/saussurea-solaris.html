Saussurea solaris

Saussurea solaris, commonly known as the sunflower of the mountains, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stem and large, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in full sunlight and well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Saussurea solaris is often regarded as a symbol of resilience in harsh environments.
