Stephania erecta

Stephania erecta, commonly known as the climbing arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright red berries that contain toxic seeds. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

However, the plant is poisonous and should not be ingested without proper guidance.
