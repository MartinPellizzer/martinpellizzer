Salix mazzettiana

Salix mazzettiana, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are elongated, serrated, and turn yellow in the autumn. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
