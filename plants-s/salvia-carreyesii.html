Salvia carreyesii

Salvia carreyesii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Salvia carreyesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
