Syzygium malagsam

Syzygium malagsam, commonly known as the white apple or white guava, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including the Philippines, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale green to white bark and its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round, yellowish-green berry that turns red when ripe, often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

Syzygium malagsam is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage and floral display.
