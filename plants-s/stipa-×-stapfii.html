Stipa × stapfii

Stipa × stapfii, commonly known as the hybrid feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a hybrid between *Stipa capillata* and *Stipa pennata*, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant forms dense, tufted clumps with slender, wiry stems and fine, thread-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which are often adorned with silky, feathery bristles.

Stipa × stapfii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and erosion control.
