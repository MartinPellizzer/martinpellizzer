Solanum hawkesii

Solanum hawkesii, commonly known as Hawkes' nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Solanum hawkesii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
