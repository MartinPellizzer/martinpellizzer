Senecio albifolius

Senecio albifolius, commonly known as the whiteleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety leaves and yellow ray flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
