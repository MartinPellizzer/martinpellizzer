Syzygium kutubuense

Syzygium kutubuense, commonly known as the Kutubu apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Papua New Guinea, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions. The plant produces distinctive, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are evergreen and have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are small and white.

Syzygium kutubuense is valued not only for its fruit but also for its role in traditional ecological systems.
