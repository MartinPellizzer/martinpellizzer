Simsia lupo

Simsia lupo, commonly known as the Lupo's mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its root system is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Simsia lupo is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
