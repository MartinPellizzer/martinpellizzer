Senecio jacquinianus

Senecio jacquinianus, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Senecio jacquinianus is relatively drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
