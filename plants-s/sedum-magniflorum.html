Sedum magniflorum

Sedum magniflorum, commonly known as the showy sedum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of star-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it an ideal plant for arid environments.
