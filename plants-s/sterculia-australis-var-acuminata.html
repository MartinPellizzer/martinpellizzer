Sterculia australis var. acuminata

Sterculia australis var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sterculia, is a tropical rainforest tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, narrow leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of dense forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
