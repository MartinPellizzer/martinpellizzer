Sanderella leopoldinensis

Sanderella leopoldinensis, commonly known as the Leopold's sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or violet. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions through its succulent leaves and deep root system, which help it conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

Sanderella leopoldinensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
