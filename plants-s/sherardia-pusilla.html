Sherardia pusilla

Sherardia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf sandwort, is a small, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Sherardia pusilla is adapted to harsh conditions and plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.
