Succisa graeca

Succisa graeca, commonly known as Greek stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Succisa graeca is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
