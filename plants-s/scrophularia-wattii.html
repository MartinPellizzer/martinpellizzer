Scrophularia wattii

Scrophularia wattii, commonly known as the Tibetan figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale purple to white, arranged in spikes, and bloom in the summer months.

Scrophularia wattii is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
