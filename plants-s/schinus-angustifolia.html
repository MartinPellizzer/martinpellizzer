Schinus angustifolia

Schinus angustifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
