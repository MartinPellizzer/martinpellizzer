Sphaerothylax bemarivensis

Sphaerothylax bemarivensis, commonly known as the Madagascar rock fig, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers, which are typically followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its growth habit is slow, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered rare and is often cultivated in controlled environments.
