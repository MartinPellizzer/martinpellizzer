Statice juniperina

Statice juniperina, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and seaside cliffs. It is drought-tolerant and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
