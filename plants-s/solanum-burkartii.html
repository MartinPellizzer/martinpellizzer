Solanum burkartii

Solanum burkartii, commonly known as the Burkart's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple, and it produces small, yellow berries that are edible but have a bitter taste.

Solanum burkartii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
