Senecio juniperinus

Senecio juniperinus, commonly known as the silverleaf senecio, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
