Solidago nemoralis subsp. longipetiolata

Solidago nemoralis subsp.

longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It typically grows in woodland and open habitats, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with long petioles, and its showy yellow inflorescences bloom in late summer. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
