Saussurea Maximowiczii F. Serrata

Saussurea maximowiczii f. serrata is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is known for its unique, serrated leaf structure and vibrant, cone-like flower heads. This plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for local wildlife and its contribution to alpine biodiversity. Due to habitat loss and climate change, it faces increasing threats to its survival.
