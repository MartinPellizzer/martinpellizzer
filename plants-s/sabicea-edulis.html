Sabicea edulis

Sabicea edulis, commonly known as the edible sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Sabicea edulis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
