Salvia gracilipes

Salvia gracilipes, commonly known as slender sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Salvia gracilipes is widely cultivated in gardens across the United States.
