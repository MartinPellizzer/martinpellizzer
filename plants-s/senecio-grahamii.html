Senecio grahamii

Senecio grahamii, commonly known as Graham's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
