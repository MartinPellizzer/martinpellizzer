Salvia zacuapanensis

Salvia zacuapanensis, commonly known as the Zacuapan sage, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts.
