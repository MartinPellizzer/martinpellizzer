Symphytum punctatum

Symphytum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves with a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Symphytum punctatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
