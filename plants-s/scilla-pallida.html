Scilla pallida

Scilla pallida, commonly known as the pale scilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically growing in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue or white flowers in early spring. Its bulbs are perennial, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Scilla pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate spring blooms.
