Scleronema moorei

Scleronema moorei, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant features dense, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scleronema moorei is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
