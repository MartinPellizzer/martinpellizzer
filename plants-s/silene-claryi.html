Silene claryi

Silene claryi, commonly known as Clary's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many showy, herbaceous plants. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces pink to purple flowers with five petals, and its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy.

Silene claryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
