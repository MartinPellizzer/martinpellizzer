Sinephropteris delavayi

Sinephropteris delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, delicate fronds that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the water. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
