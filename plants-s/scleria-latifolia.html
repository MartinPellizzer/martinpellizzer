Scleria latifolia

Scleria latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Scleria latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
