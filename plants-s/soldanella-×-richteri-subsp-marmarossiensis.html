Soldanella × richteri subsp. marmarossiensis

Soldanella × richteri subsp.

marmarossiensis, commonly known as the Marmarossian bellflower, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is a low-growing perennial herb with distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant features hairy stems and lanceolate leaves, often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species that exemplifies the genetic diversity within the Soldanella genus.
