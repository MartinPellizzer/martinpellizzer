Simmondsia

Simmondsia, commonly known as jojoba, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is most notable for its unique seed oil, which is chemically similar to human sebum and is widely used in skincare and cosmetic products. Jojoba oil is also valued for its stability and long shelf life, making it a popular alternative to petroleum-based oils.

Additionally, the plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
