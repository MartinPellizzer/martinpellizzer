Scilla bipartita

Scilla bipartita, commonly known as the two-part scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained conditions. Its bulbs are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.

Scilla bipartita is also valued for its early blooming habit, adding color to gardens in late winter or early spring.
