Sasa tsuboiana f. hatchoensis

Sasa tsuboiana f. hatchoensis, commonly known as the Hatcho bamboo, is a compact, clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It features slender, dark green culms that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their elegant appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in summer, followed by dark blue-black berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

Its slow growth rate makes it suitable for small garden spaces and bonsai cultivation.
