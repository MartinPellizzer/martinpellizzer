Sapindus saponaria var. thurstonii

Sapindus saponaria var. thurstonii, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
