Saussurea larionowii

Saussurea larionowii, commonly known as the Altai snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
