Solanum vellereum

Solanum vellereum, commonly known as the velvet nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the evening, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. Its botanical features include a woody stem, hairy foliage, and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

Solanum vellereum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
