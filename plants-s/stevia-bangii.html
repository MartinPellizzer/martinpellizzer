Stevia bangii

Stevia bangii, commonly known as sweetleaf or sugar leaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically blooming in late summer.

Stevia bangii is valued for its intense sweetness and is widely used as a natural alternative to sugar in food and beverages.
