Solidago puncticulata

Solidago puncticulata, commonly known as the dotted sunflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its most distinctive feature is the bright yellow flower head, which consists of numerous small disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray flowers.

It typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
