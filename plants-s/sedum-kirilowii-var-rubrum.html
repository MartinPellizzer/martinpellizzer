Sedum kirilowii var. rubrum

Sedum kirilowii var. rubrum, commonly known as the Crimson Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically red or pink in color, especially under full sun exposure. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
