Salvia polystachia var. potosiana

Salvia polystachia var. potosiana, commonly known as the Potosi sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces showy, violet-blue flower clusters in the summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and aromatic foliage.
