Stachys confertiflora

Stachys confertiflora, commonly known as the denseflower hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Stachys confertiflora is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
