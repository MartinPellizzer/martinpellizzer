Syzygium melliodorum

Syzygium melliodorum, commonly known as the sweet apple or the golden apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet, aromatic flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruit.

Syzygium melliodorum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
