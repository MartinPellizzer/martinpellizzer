Smilax menispermoidea subsp. randaiensis

Smilax menispermoidea subsp.

randaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese catbriar, is a vigorous climbing shrub native to East Asia. It features stout, thorny stems that can climb and entangle other plants, often forming dense thickets.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor.

This subspecies is valued in traditional medicine and is also used as a decorative plant in gardens.
