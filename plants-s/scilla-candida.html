Scilla candida

Scilla candida, commonly known as the white squill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grass-like basal leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round, blue-black berries that contain the seeds. Scilla candida thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
