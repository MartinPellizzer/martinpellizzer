Stachys candida

Stachys candida, commonly known as white hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
