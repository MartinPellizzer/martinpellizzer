Swertia zayuensis

Swertia zayuensis, commonly known as the Yarlung Valley swertia, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Yarlung Tsangpo River Valley in Tibet.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with a fine layer of pubescence.

Swertia zayuensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
