Sclerolobium paniculatum var. subvelutinum

Sclerolobium paniculatum var. subvelutinum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, softly hairy foliage that gives it a velvety appearance. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slight pubescence.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
