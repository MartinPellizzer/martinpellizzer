Senecio rivularis subsp. pseudocrispus

Senecio rivularis subsp.

pseudocrispus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
