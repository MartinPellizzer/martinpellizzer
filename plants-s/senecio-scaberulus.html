Senecio scaberulus

Senecio scaberulus, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
