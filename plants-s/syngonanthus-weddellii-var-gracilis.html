Syngonanthus weddellii var. gracilis

Syngonanthus weddellii var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender Weddell's grass.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in grassland and shrubland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

This variant is noted for its delicate appearance and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
