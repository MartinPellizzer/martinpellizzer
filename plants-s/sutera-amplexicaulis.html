Sutera amplexicaulis

Sutera amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clinging bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
