Solanum papaverifolium

Solanum papaverifolium, commonly known as the papaverifolium nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the poppy, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum papaverifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
