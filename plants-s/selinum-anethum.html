Selinum anethum

Selinum anethum, commonly known as Indian cumin or bishop's weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems and finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds, which are small and oblong, are harvested for their pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

Selinum anethum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a versatile crop in temperate climates.
