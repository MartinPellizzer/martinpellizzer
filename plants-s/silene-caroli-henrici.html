Silene caroli-henrici

Silene caroli-henrici, commonly known as the Carolinian catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the northeastern United States and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene caroli-henrici is often found in association with other early successional plant species.
