Scabiosa cinerea subsp. cinerea

Scabiosa cinerea subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the pale scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and open woodlands.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, silvery appearance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and ecological benefits to pollinators.
