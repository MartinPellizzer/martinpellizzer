Spartium spinosum

Spartium spinosum, commonly known as prickly sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils.

Spartium spinosum is often used in herbal medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
