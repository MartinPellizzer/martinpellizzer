Spiranthes albescens

Spiranthes albescens, commonly known as the white lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraled inflorescence that resembles a twisted ribbon, with pale white to cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, greenish leaves that are lance-shaped.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
