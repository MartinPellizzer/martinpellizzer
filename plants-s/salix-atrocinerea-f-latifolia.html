Salix atrocinerea f. latifolia

Salix atrocinerea f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf weeping willow, is a unique variant of the weeping willow species.

It is characterized by its wide, broad leaves that give it a distinctive appearance compared to typical willow foliage. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often found in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its botanical features include a slender, weeping habit with long, drooping branches.

The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, contributing to its ornamental value.
