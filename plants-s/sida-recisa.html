Sida recisa

Sida recisa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Sida recisa is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields due to its adaptability.

It is also known for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
