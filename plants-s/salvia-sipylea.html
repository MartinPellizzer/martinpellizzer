Salvia sipylea

Salvia sipylea, commonly known as the Sipylea sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense clusters of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia sipylea is also valued for its aromatic properties and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
