Styrax yutajensis

Styrax yutajensis, commonly known as Yutajensis frankincense, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Styracaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, resinous wood that has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by hard, woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued for its unique chemical composition, which includes various volatile compounds with potential therapeutic properties.
