Silene macrostyla

Silene macrostyla, commonly known as the tall catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene macrostyla is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
