Sedum senanense

Sedum senanense, commonly known as the Senanense stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum senanense is also valued for its low maintenance and ability to prevent soil erosion.
