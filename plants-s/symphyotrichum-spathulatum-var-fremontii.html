Symphyotrichum spathulatum var. fremontii

Symphyotrichum spathulatum var. fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in the late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed areas, and it has lance-shaped to spatulate leaves with a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
