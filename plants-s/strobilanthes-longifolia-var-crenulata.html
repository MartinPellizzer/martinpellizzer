Strobilanthes longifolia var. crenulata

Strobilanthes longifolia var. crenulata, commonly known as the crested flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elongated and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
