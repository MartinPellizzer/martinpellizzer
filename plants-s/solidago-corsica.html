Solidago corsica

Solidago corsica, commonly known as the Corsican goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a grayish-green hue.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
