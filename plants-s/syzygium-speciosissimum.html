Syzygium speciosissimum

Syzygium speciosissimum, commonly known as the satinwood or satin-leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by edible, sweet-smelling fruits that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are thick and leathery, which helps it retain moisture in humid environments.

Syzygium speciosissimum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
