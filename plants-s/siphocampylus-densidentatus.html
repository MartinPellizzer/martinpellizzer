Siphocampylus densidentatus

Siphocampylus densidentatus, commonly known as the dense-toothed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species.
