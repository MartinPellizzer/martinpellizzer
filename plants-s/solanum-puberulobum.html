Solanum puberulobum

Solanum puberulobum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, typically containing several seeds. Native to parts of South America, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in cultivation and requires caution when handling.
