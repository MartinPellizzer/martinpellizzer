Solidago multiradiata var. arctica

Solidago multiradiata var. arctica, commonly known as Arctic goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It thrives in cold, moist environments such as tundra and rocky slopes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant produces showy, clustered yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in freezing conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in extreme environments.
