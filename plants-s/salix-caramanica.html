Salix caramanica

Salix caramanica, commonly known as the Caramanica willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters, featuring slender, flexible branches and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice in landscaping.
