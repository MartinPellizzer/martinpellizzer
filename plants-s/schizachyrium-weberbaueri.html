Schizachyrium weberbaueri

Schizachyrium weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like flower clusters that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Schizachyrium weberbaueri is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
