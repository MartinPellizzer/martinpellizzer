Senecio eenii

Senecio eenii, commonly known as the mountain glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. Senecio eenii is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
