Spatholobus roxburghii

Spatholobus roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian wisteria, is a climbing legume native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and produces distinctive, pendulous inflorescences of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in ecological systems and its potential pharmacological applications.
