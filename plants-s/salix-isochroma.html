Salix isochroma

Salix isochroma, commonly known as the white willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are elongated, serrated, and typically have a bluish-green hue on the upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
