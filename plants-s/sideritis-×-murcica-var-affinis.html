Sideritis × murcica var. affinis

Sideritis × murcica var. affinis, commonly known as the Murcian ironwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive minty aroma.

This variety is often found in rocky or calcareous soils and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
