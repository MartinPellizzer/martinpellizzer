Stipa brachychaetoides

Stipa brachychaetoides, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are often covered in short, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, awned lemmas. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
