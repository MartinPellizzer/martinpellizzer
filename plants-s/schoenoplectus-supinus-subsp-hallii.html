Schoenoplectus supinus subsp. hallii

Schoenoplectus supinus subsp.

hallii, commonly known as Hall's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines.

The plant has cylindrical, grass-like leaves that are usually reddish or purplish in color, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often submerged or floating. It is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomes contribute to the plant's ability to spread and form extensive colonies in suitable habitats.
