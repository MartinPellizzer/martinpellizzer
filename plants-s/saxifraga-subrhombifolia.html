Saxifraga subrhombifolia

Saxifraga subrhombifolia, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant features succulent, rhombic-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem.

Saxifraga subrhombifolia is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
