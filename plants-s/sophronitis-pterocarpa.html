Sophronitis pterocarpa

Sophronitis pterocarpa, commonly known as the orchid with a unique floral structure, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a distinctive lip or labellum. Its growth habit is typically epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic.

The botanical interest in Sophronitis pterocarpa lies in its intricate floral morphology and its role in orchid taxonomy.
