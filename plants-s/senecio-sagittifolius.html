Senecio sagittifolius

Senecio sagittifolius, commonly known as the arrowleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are arrow-shaped, which gives it its common name, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer months.

Senecio sagittifolius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and drought tolerance.
