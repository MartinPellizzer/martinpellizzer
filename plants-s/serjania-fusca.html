Serjania fusca

Serjania fusca, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian redwood, is a member of the Sapotaceae family.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to Brazil, particularly found in the Atlantic Forest region. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody drupe that turns bright red when mature.

Serjania fusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
