Schizophragma amplum

Schizophragma amplum, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, red berries that mature in the fall and are edible for humans and wildlife.

Schizophragma amplum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.
