Senecio hajastanicus

Senecio hajastanicus, commonly known as the Armenian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
