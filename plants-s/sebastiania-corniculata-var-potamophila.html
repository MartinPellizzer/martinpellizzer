Sebastiania corniculata var. potamophila

Sebastiania corniculata var. potamophila, commonly known as the water sycamore, is a flowering plant native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy surface. Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate wet conditions make it a popular choice for wetland landscaping.
