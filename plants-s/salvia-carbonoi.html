Salvia carbonoi

Salvia carbonoi, commonly known as the carbonari sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorls along the stems, which bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Salvia carbonoi is also valued for its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance.
