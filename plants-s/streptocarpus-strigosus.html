Streptocarpus strigosus

Streptocarpus strigosus, commonly known as the woolly streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Streptocarpus strigosus is often grown as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
