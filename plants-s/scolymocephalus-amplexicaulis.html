Scolymocephalus amplexicaulis

Scolymocephalus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping scolymus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are clasping, meaning they wrap around the stem, and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
