Silene inflata subsp. prostrata

Silene inflata subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate bladderpod, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or decumbent growth habit.

The plant features hairy stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically opposite and lanceolate, with a bluish-green hue. Silene inflata subsp.

prostrata is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
