Sporobolus indicus var. pellucidus

Sporobolus indicus var. pellucidus, commonly known as the translucent speargrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that have a distinctive translucent appearance due to the presence of silica deposits. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Sporobolus indicus var. pellucidus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
