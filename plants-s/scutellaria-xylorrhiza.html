Scutellaria xylorrhiza

Scutellaria xylorrhiza, commonly known as the rough skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Scutellaria genus. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

Scutellaria xylorrhiza is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
