Solanum paniculatum f. canescens

Solanum paniculatum f. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, star-shaped white flowers. The plant produces berries that range in color from green to red, depending on maturity. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
