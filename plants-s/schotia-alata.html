Schotia alata

Schotia alata, commonly known as the weeping boerhaave, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches that give it a weeping appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Schotia alata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
