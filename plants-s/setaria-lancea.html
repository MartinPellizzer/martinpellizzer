Setaria lancea

Setaria lancea, commonly known as slender foxtail, is a grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often loosely arranged along the stem. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

Setaria lancea is an annual plant that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
