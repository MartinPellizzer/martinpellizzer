Solanum valadares

Solanum valadares, commonly known as the Valadares nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing numerous seeds. Solanum valadares is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is considered a minor weed in agricultural settings but has limited economic importance compared to other solanaceous crops.
