Saldinia pycnophylla

Saldinia pycnophylla, commonly known as the dense-leaved sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in nutrient-poor soils and standing water. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact, bushy appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, a common feature among sedges.

Saldinia pycnophylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
