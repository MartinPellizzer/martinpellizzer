Solanum miniatum var. glabriusculum

Solanum miniatum var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a small herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and potential toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
