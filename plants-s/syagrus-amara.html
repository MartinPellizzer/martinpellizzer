Syagrus amara

Syagrus amara, commonly known as the royal palm, is a species of palm native to the coastal regions of northeastern Brazil.

It is a tall, slender tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal dunes and forested areas. Its fruit, which is a bright yellow-orange drupe, contains a single seed and is an important food source for various wildlife.

Syagrus amara is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in landscaping.
