Shorea siamensis

Shorea siamensis, commonly known as the Siamese ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Shorea siamensis plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems and is also a source of traditional timber and medicinal uses.
