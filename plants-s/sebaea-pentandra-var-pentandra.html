Sebaea pentandra var. pentandra

Sebaea pentandra var. pentandra, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which are typically five-petaled and showy. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
