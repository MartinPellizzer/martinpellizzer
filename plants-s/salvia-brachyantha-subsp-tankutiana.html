Salvia brachyantha subsp. tankutiana

Salvia brachyantha subsp.

tankutiana, commonly known as the Tankut's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking violet to purple flowers in terminal spikes.

The plant features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
