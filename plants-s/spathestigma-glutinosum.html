Spathestigma glutinosum

Spathestigma glutinosum, commonly known as the sticky-stigma plant, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sticky secretions on its floral structures, which play a role in pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a mix of white and green hues.
