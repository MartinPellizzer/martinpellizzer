Senecio franchetianus

Senecio franchetianus, commonly known as the Franchet's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a disc-like cluster.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
