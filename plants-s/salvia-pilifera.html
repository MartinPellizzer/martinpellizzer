Salvia pilifera

Salvia pilifera, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Salvia pilifera is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses including treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
