Solanum platanifolium var. lagoense

Solanum platanifolium var. lagoense, commonly known as the Lagoense nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It features large, broad leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or purple in color. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and medicinal significance.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.
