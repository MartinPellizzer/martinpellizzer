Sedum delicum

Sedum delicum, commonly known as the noble sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in small, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow-orange flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for gardens.

Sedum delicum is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
