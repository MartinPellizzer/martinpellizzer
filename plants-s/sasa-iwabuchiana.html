Sasa iwabuchiana

Sasa iwabuchiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa iwabuchiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
