Symplocos leucocarpa

Symplocos leucocarpa, commonly known as white-fruited wax tree, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically white or pale yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
