Solanum lycopersicum

Solanum lycopersicum, commonly known as the tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to western South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems, alternate leaves, and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing numerous seeds. Its fruit is widely cultivated for culinary use and is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The botanical structure of the tomato supports its role as both a vegetable and a fruit in various cuisines worldwide.
