Symplocos spicata f. xanthophylla

Symplocos spicata f. xanthophylla, commonly known as the yellow-leafed evergreen holly, is a variant of the symplocos genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, yellowish-green leaves that contrast with its red or orange berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
