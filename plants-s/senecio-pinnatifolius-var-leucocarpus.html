Senecio pinnatifolius var. leucocarpus

Senecio pinnatifolius var. leucocarpus, commonly known as the white-fruit senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, pinnately divided leaves. Its most distinctive trait is the production of white, fleshy fruits that are unique among its species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
