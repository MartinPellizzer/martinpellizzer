Silene kotschyi

Silene kotschyi, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Silene kotschyi has linear to lanceolate leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
