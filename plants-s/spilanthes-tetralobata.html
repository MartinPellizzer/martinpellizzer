Spilanthes tetralobata

Spilanthes tetralobata, commonly known as the four-lobed cowplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the genus Spilanthes within the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers. The plant produces small, yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Spilanthes tetralobata is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal remedies.
