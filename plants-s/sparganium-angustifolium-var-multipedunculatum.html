Sparganium angustifolium var. multipedunculatum

Sparganium angustifolium var. multipedunculatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved bur reed, is a variant of the bur reed species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and dense clusters of spiky, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes are often used in floral arrangements due to their distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in aquatic environments and contribute to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.
