Solanum baccatum

Solanum baccatum, commonly known as the "llanera" or "chicory apple," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its fruits are edible when ripe and have a slightly tart flavor, often used in traditional culinary practices.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
