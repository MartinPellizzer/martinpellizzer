Setaria faberi

Setaria faberi, commonly known as Chinese silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-gray plumes that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Setaria faberi is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
