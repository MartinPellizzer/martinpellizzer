Scorzonera humilis var. macrorrhiza

Scorzonera humilis var. macrorrhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has a thick, fleshy root system that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Scorzonera humilis var. macrorrhiza is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
