Senecio desiderabilis

Senecio desiderabilis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are hairy and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Senecio desiderabilis is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
