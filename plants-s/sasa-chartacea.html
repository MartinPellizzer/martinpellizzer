Sasa chartacea

Sasa chartacea, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or common sasa, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, bamboo-like stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by triangular, papery bracts that are often mistaken for leaves. Sasa chartacea is valued for its ornamental foliage, which consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-white underside.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
