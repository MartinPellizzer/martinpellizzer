Senecio beckii

Senecio beckii, commonly known as the Beck's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a silver or grayish coating that helps reduce water loss.

Senecio beckii produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
