Sageretia theezans var. tomentosa

Sageretia theezans var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sageretia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a soft, grayish-white pubescence.

The woody stems are typically reddish-brown and become more pronounced with age.
