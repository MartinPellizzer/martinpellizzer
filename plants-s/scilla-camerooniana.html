Scilla camerooniana

Scilla camerooniana, commonly known as the Cameroon squill, is a bulbous plant native to the Cameroon region of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Scilla camerooniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its delicate floral display.
