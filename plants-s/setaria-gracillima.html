Setaria gracillima

Setaria gracillima, commonly known as slender millet, is a species of grass native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a small, annual plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often used as a forage crop for livestock.

Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
