Sarcoca icosandra

Sarcoca icosandra, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters along its stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
