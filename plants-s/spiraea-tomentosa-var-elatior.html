Spiraea tomentosa var. elatior

Spiraea tomentosa var. elatior, commonly known as the silver-leaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, rounded growth with silvery-white, fluffy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it suitable for borders, rock gardens, or as a specimen plant.
