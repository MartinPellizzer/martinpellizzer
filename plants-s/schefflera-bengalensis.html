Schefflera bengalensis

Schefflera bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like arrangement of leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability to indoor conditions makes it a popular choice for houseplants.
