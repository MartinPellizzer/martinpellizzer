Samara floribunda

Samara floribunda, commonly known as the flowering samara, is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces small, winged fruits called samaras, which are adapted for wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with four to five petals and numerous stamens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
