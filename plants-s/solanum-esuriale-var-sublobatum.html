Solanum esuriale var. sublobatum

Solanum esuriale var. sublobatum, commonly known as the sublobate bitter nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing toxic alkaloids. Due to its toxic properties, it is not cultivated for food or ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
