Selaginella subfimbriata var. polyura

Selaginella subfimbriata var. polyura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and small, cone-like sporophytes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a distinct, toothed margin.

Selaginella subfimbriata var. polyura is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
