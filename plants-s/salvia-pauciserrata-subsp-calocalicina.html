Salvia pauciserrata subsp. calocalicina

Salvia pauciserrata subsp.

calocalicina, commonly known as the narrow-lobed sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with sparse, shallow serrations along the margins.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
