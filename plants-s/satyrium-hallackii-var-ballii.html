Satyrium hallackii var. ballii

Satyrium hallackii var. ballii, commonly known as the Ball's satyrium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats, preferring cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The flowers are small, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale pink, and are often clustered in loose inflorescences.
