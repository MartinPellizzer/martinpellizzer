Setaria globoidea

Setaria globoidea, commonly known as the globe millet, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Setaria globoidea is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop.
