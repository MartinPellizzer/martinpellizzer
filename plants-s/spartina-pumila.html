Spartina pumila

Spartina pumila, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass found in coastal salt marshes.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt, which helps it tolerate saline environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Spartina pumila plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ability to thrive in brackish water makes it an important component of estuarine ecosystems.
