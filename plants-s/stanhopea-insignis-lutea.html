Stanhopea insignis lutea

Stanhopea insignis lutea, commonly known as the yellow stanhopea, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a strong, spicy scent. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal rainfall conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
