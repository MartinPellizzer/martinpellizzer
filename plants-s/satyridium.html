Satyridium

Satyridium, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and emit a sweet fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Despite its beauty, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
