Stipa kungeica

Stipa kungeica, commonly known as the Kungei needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces long, narrow leaves with a bluish-gray hue. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Stipa kungeica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid landscapes for erosion control.
