Scutellaria russelioides

Scutellaria russelioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and stream banks, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers from June to August. Its genus, Scutellaria, is characterized by the presence of a scutellum, a specialized structure in the flower that aids in pollination.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
