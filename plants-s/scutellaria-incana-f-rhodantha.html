Scutellaria incana f. rhodantha

Scutellaria incana f. rhodantha, commonly known as the pink skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, two-lipped, pink to purple flowers. Its botanical structure includes a taproot system and opposite leaf arrangement, typical of many members of its family.

The flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
