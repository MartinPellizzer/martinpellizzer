Solanum johnsonianum

Solanum johnsonianum, commonly known as the Johnson's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface and serrated edges.
