Syzygium tenellum

Syzygium tenellum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are simple, alternate, and glossy, with a lanceolate shape. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
