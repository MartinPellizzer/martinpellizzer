Sinocalamus affinis var. viridiflavus

Sinocalamus affinis var. viridiflavus, commonly known as the green-yellow bamboo, is a variant of the sinocalamus affinis species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, green-yellow culms that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This bamboo variety is valued for both its aesthetic qualities and its sustainable use in various applications.
