Sasaella minomarsa

Sasaella minomarsa, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that often form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Sasaella minomarsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
