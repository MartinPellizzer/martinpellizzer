Senecio fuchsii subsp. jacquinianus

Senecio fuchsii subsp.

jacquinianus, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cascading inflorescence that resembles a ladder.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
