Schoenoplectus pulchellus

Schoenoplectus pulchellus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers in the summer. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help stabilize the soil, making it an important species for wetland restoration.

Schoenoplectus pulchellus is also valued for its ability to filter water and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
