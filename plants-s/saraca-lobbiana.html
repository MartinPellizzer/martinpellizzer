Saraca lobbiana

Saraca lobbiana, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces woody pods that contain flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Saraca lobbiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
