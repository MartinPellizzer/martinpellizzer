Sarcanthus tridentatus

Sarcanthus tridentatus, commonly known as the three-toothed saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has three tooth-like lobes on its leaves, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in alpine and arctic tundra environments.

Sarcanthus tridentatus is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season, flowering early in the summer.
