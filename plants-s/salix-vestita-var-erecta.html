Salix vestita var. erecta

Salix vestita var. erecta, commonly known as the erect willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, cone-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
