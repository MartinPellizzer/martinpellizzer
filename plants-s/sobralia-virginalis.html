Sobralia virginalis

Sobralia virginalis, commonly known as the Virgin's Crown, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Sobralia virginalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
