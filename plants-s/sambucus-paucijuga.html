Sambucus paucijuga

Sambucus paucijuga, commonly known as the narrowleaf elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. These berries are edible when fully ripe but contain toxic seeds.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
