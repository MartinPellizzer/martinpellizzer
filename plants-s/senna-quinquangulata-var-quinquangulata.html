Senna quinquangulata var. quinquangulata

Senna quinquangulata var. quinquangulata, commonly known as the five-angled senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five angular leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a slender pod that splits open to release small, flat seeds.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
