Scrophularia digitalifolia

Scrophularia digitalifolia, commonly known as the digital leaf figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive digital (finger-like) appearance. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia digitalifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
