Saxifraga burseriana magna

Saxifraga burseriana magna, commonly known as the giant saxifrage, is a rare and striking alpine plant native to the high mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or silver hue, adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
