Sloanea multiflora

Sloanea multiflora, commonly known as the Andean sloanea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture and are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Sloanea multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
