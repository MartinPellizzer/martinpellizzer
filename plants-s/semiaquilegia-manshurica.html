Semiaquilegia manshurica

Semiaquilegia manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant features showy, red or orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Semiaquilegia manshurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
