Scaevola taccada var. fauriei

Scaevola taccada var. fauriei, commonly known as the Blue Fan Flower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
