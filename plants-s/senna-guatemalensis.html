Senna guatemalensis

Senna guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan senna, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Senna guatemalensis is part of the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and leguminous properties.
