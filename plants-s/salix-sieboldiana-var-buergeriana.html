Salix sieboldiana var. buergeriana

Salix sieboldiana var. buergeriana, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, brown fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist soils.
