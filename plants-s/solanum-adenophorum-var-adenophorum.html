Solanum adenophorum var. adenophorum

Solanum adenophorum var. adenophorum, commonly known as horseweed or starfruit bush, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, star-shaped berries. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in pastures and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an invasive species in many areas.
