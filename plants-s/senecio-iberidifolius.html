Senecio iberidifolius

Senecio iberidifolius, commonly known as the Iberian senecio, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, dry environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and often have a woolly texture, while its flowers are small and yellow.

It is a relatively rare species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
