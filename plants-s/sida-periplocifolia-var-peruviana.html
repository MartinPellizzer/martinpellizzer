Sida periplocifolia var. peruviana

Sida periplocifolia var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
