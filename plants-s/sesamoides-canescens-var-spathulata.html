Sesamoides canescens var. spathulata

Sesamoides canescens var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved sesame.

It is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes. Its seeds are encased in a hard, dry shell, typical of the Polygonaceae family.

The variety spathulata is distinguished by its broader leaf blades compared to the typical form.
