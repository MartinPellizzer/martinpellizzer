Siderasis spectabilis

Siderasis spectabilis, commonly known as the showy ironbark, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark that ranges in color from dark brown to reddish tones. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in showy inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, silvery hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Siderasis spectabilis is well adapted to drought and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in dry woodlands.
