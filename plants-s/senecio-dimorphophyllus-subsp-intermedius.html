Senecio dimorphophyllus subsp. intermedius

Senecio dimorphophyllus subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the Intermediate Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like inflorescences.

The plant features succulent leaves that are often blue-green in color, adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
