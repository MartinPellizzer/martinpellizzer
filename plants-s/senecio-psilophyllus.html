Senecio psilophyllus

Senecio psilophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Senecio psilophyllus is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value.
