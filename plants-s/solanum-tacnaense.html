Solanum tacnaense

Solanum tacnaense, commonly known as Tacna nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Bolivia. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or arid soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers and red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
