Silene bergiana

Silene bergiana, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in cold, high-altitude environments. The plant features showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged alternately along the stem.

Silene bergiana is adapted to harsh alpine conditions, with a compact growth form and a deep root system for moisture retention.
