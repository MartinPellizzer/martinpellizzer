Silene apetala var. pallidiflora

Silene apetala var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its low-growing habit and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
