Sophoclesia ovata

Sophoclesia ovata, commonly known as the large-flowered false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, violet-blue flowers in raceme clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Sophoclesia ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
