Saccharum officinarum var. brevipedicellatum

Saccharum officinarum var. brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the dwarf sugarcane variety, is a tropical grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to standard sugarcane, with shorter stalks and reduced internode length. This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and manageable size. It prefers warm, humid climates and well-drained soils, similar to other sugarcane species.

The plant is also valued for its potential in biofuel production and as a source of sugar under specific cultivation conditions.
