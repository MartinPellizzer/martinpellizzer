Sieruela allamani

Sieruela allamani, commonly known as the Allamani thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its leaves are spiny and often covered in a thick layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which appear in late summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
