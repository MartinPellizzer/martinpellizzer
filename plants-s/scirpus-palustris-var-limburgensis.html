Scirpus palustris var. limburgensis

Scirpus palustris var. limburgensis, commonly known as the Limburg sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
