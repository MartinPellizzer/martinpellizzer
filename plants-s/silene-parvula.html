Silene parvula

Silene parvula, commonly known as the dwarf catchfly, is a small flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene parvula is often found in disturbed or moist soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological habitats.
