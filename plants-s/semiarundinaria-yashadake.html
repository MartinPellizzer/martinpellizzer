Semiarundinaria yashadake

Semiarundinaria yashadake, commonly known as the Yashadake bamboo, is a unique species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit, forming compact clusters that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast against the green canopy.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
