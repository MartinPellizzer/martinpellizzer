Styrax japonicus f. angustifolius

Styrax japonicus f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Japanese styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically narrower than those of the species. The plant produces fragrant, white, clustered flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
