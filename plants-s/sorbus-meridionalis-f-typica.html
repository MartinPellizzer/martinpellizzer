Sorbus meridionalis f. typica

Sorbus meridionalis f. typica, commonly known as the southern whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
