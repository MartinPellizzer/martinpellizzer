Solidago juncea var. ramosa

Solidago juncea var. ramosa, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often branched, giving it its varietal name "ramosa," meaning branched. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often slightly hairy, while its flowers are arranged in panicles of yellow, composite flower heads.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterflies.
