Serjania aculeata

Serjania aculeata, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus or spiny serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central column.

Serjania aculeata is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
