Syzygium obtusum

Syzygium obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Syzygium obtusum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a versatile choice for landscaping in warm climates.
