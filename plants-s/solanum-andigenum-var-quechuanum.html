Solanum andigenum var. quechuanum

Solanum andigenum var. quechuanum, commonly known as the Andean potato plant, is a tuberous perennial native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its starchy, edible tubers that are cultivated for food. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are usually blue or purple in color.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to harsh environments and its nutritional content.
