Spiraea japonica var. acuta

Spiraea japonica var. acuta, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer. The plant has finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea japonica var. acuta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
