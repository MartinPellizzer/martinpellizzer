Satureja troodi subsp. vardarana

Satureja troodi subsp.

vardarana, commonly known as Balkan savory, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine for their pungent, savory aroma.
