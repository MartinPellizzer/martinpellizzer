Scirpus foliatus

Scirpus foliatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color, which develop into seeds for propagation. Scirpus foliatus thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its rapid growth and dense foliage help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
