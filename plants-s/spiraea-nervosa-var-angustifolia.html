Spiraea nervosa var. angustifolia

Spiraea nervosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The leaves are narrow and serrated, providing a delicate texture to the plant. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
