Silene vaga

Silene vaga, commonly known as the wandering catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Silene vaga has a low, sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.
