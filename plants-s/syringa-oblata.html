Syringa oblata

Syringa oblata, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Syringa oblata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features include showy racemes of white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators.
