Scirpus morrisonensis

Scirpus morrisonensis, commonly known as Morrison's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. This plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers.

Scirpus morrisonensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
