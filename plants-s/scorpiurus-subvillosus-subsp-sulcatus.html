Scorpiurus subvillosus subsp. sulcatus

Scorpiurus subvillosus subsp.

sulcatus, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has opposite, hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies sulcatus is distinguished by its finely serrated leaf margins and slightly hairy stems.
