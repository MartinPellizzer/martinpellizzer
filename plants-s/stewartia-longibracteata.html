Stewartia longibracteata

Stewartia longibracteata, commonly known as the Chinese stewartia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
