Sagina nevadensis var. gredensis

Sagina nevadensis var. gredensis, commonly known as Gredens' sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils, often found in alpine or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
