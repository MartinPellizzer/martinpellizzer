Sideritis endressii subsp. provincialis

Sideritis endressii subsp.

provincialis, commonly known as the Alboraya mountain sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often found in mountainous regions.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized in traditional herbal practices.
