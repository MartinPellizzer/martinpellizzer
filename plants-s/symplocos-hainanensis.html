Symplocos hainanensis

Symplocos hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan waxleaf.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of southern China, particularly Hainan Island. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically small and white or pale yellow, clustered in umbels, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Symplocos hainanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
