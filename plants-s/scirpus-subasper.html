Scirpus subasper

Scirpus subasper, commonly known as the rough sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its stiff, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus subasper is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
