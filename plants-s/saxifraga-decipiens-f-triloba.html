Saxifraga decipiens f. triloba

Saxifraga decipiens f. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed stonecrop, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically three-lobed, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact form and vibrant floral display.
