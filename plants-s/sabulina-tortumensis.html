Sabulina tortumensis

Sabulina tortumensis, commonly known as the Tortumense sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the rocky and sandy soils of the Caucasus region.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer, arranged in a tight cluster at the center of the rosette. Its flowering stems are typically shorter than the leaves, and the plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils.
