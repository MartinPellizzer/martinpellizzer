Salvia leucocalyx

Salvia leucocalyx, commonly known as whiteflower sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, white to pale lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and emit a mild minty scent when crushed.

Salvia leucocalyx thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
