Sasa elegans

Sasa elegans, commonly known as the elegant bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and dense, feather-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps and spread vegetatively.

Sasa elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
