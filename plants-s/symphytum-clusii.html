Symphytum clusii

Symphytum clusii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and velvety to the touch. The plant produces tall spikes of violet or blue-purple flowers from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland margins or along stream banks.

Symphytum clusii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing qualities.
