Salix × alopecuroides

Salix × alopecuroides, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins during the spring, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
