Saxifraga × superba

Saxifraga × superba, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga × superba thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
