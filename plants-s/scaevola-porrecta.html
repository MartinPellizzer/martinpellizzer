Scaevola porrecta

Scaevola porrecta, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes, where it is adapted to withstand saline conditions.

Scaevola porrecta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
