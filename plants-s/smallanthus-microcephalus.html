Smallanthus microcephalus

Smallanthus microcephalus, commonly known as the Andean mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its small, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the early morning. The plant has hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
