Saxifraga × berolinensis

Saxifraga × berolinensis, commonly known as the Berlin saxifrage, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirta.

It is a low-growing perennial with distinctive rosettes of fleshy, rounded leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
