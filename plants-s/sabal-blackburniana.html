Sabal blackburniana

Sabal blackburniana, commonly known as the Florida palmetto, is a hardy, cold-tolerant palm native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stiff, fan-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its slow growth rate and ability to withstand urban environments make it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal regions.
