Syzygium merokense

Syzygium merokense, commonly known as the Malayan apple or wild apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. Syzygium merokense is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
