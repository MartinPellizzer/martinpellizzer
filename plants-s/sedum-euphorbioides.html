Sedum euphorbioides

Sedum euphorbioides, commonly known as the orange stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purplish tint. The plant produces bright orange or yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
