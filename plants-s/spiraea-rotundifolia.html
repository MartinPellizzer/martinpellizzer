Spiraea rotundifolia

Spiraea rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features arching, willow-like branches that bear clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The leaves are rounded and serrated, providing a lush green foliage throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
