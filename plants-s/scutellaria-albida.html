Scutellaria albida

Scutellaria albida, commonly known as the white skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded environments.

Scutellaria albida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
