Sempervivum × luisae

Sempervivum × luisae, commonly known as the 'Luisae' or 'Luisa's' hens and chicks, is a hybrid succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues, especially in cooler temperatures. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear clusters of small, star-shaped flowers, usually in shades of pink or white. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

Sempervivum × luisae is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
