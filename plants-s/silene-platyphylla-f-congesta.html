Silene platyphylla f. congesta

Silene platyphylla f. congesta, commonly known as the clustered bladderpod, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
