Sesbania madagascariensis

Sesbania madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to Madagascar.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural systems.
