Stipa spicaventi

Stipa spicaventi, commonly known as the spicata grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Stipa spicaventi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
