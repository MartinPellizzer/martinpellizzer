Sorbus aria var. velutina

Sorbus aria var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet tree or velvet rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and clusters of small, yellowish-orange berries that ripen in the fall. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
