Silene conica var. australis

Silene conica var. australis, commonly known as the Australian bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features distinctive pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
