Suaeda indica

Suaeda indica, commonly known as sea spurge, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats, showcasing its ability to tolerate high salinity. Its root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in coastal ecosystems.

Suaeda indica is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
