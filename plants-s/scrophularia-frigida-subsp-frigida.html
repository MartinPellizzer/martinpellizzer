Scrophularia frigida subsp. frigida

Scrophularia frigida subsp.

frigida, commonly known as the alpine figwort, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations.

The plant features erect, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous habitats.
