Stachys galpinii

Stachys galpinii, commonly known as Galpin's hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features erect stems with opposite leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually pink to purple in color, attracting various pollinators.

Stachys galpinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
