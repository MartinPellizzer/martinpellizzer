Solanum canaosense

Solanum canaosense, commonly known as the Canaosense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas in the understory of tropical forests.
