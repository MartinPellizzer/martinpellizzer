Scirpus tenuiculus

Scirpus tenuiculus, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, cylindrical stems are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps in identifying the species.

Scirpus tenuiculus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
