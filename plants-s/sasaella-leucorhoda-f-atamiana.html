Sasaella leucorhoda f. atamiana

Sasaella leucorhoda f. atamiana, commonly known as the Atamian form of the white-veined sasa, is a rare variant of the sasa genus native to specific regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive white veins on dark green leaves, which give it a striking visual appearance. The plant is typically found in shaded, humid environments such as mountain forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanically significant and ecologically specialized species.
