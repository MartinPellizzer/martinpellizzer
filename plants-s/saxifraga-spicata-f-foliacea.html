Saxifraga spicata f. foliacea

Saxifraga spicata f. foliacea, commonly known as the leafy saxifrage, is a variant of the common saxifrage, characterized by its dense, leafy foliage.

It is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features basal leaves that are typically broad and lobed, with a rosette-like arrangement. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, which are clustered in a spiciform inflorescence.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings.
