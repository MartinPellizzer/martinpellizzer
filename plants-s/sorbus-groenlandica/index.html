Sorbus Groenlandica

Sorbus groenlandica is a rare and hardy shrub native to the Arctic regions of Greenland and northern Canada. It thrives in harsh, cold climates and is well adapted to permafrost and rocky soils. This plant is known for its small, yellowish flowers and red berries that provide food for local wildlife. Its resilience makes it an important indicator of climate change in polar ecosystems. Despite its tough environment, Sorbus groenlandica remains a fascinating subject for botanical study.
