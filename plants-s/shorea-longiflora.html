Shorea longiflora

Shorea longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its durable, heavy timber used in construction and furniture. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically yellow or pale green in color.

Shorea longiflora plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
