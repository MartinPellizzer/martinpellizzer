Sedum indicum var. genuinum

Sedum indicum var. genuinum, commonly known as the Indian sedum, is a succulent plant native to parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, often blue-green or pale yellow flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, aiding in water retention.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
