Solanum antillarum

Solanum antillarum, commonly known as the Antillean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species such as tomatoes and potatoes.
