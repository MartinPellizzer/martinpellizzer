Sabina saltuaria

Sabina saltuaria, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep soil moisture.

Sabina saltuaria is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
