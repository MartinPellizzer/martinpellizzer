Serratula tinctoria var. dissecta

Serratula tinctoria var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and can grow up to 1 meter tall.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
