Salacia mauritioides

Salacia mauritioides, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

However, it is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential pharmacological properties.
