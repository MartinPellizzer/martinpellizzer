Stenogrammitis saffordii

Stenogrammitis saffordii, commonly known as the Safford's stenogrammitis, is a rare fern native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.
