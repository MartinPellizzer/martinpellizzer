Stillingia salpingadenia subsp. saxatilis

Stillingia salpingadenia subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the rock-stemmed stinging root, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, dark brown seed pods. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.
