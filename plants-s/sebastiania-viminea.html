Sebastiania viminea

Sebastiania viminea, commonly known as the weeping sebastiania, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
