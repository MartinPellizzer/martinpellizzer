Spiraea thyrsiflora

Spiraea thyrsiflora, commonly known as the thrysothorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea thyrsiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
