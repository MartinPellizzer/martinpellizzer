Streptachne ramosissima

Streptachne ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Streptachne ramosissima thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain ecological systems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
