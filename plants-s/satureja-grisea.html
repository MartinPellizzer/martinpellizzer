Satureja grisea

Satureja grisea, commonly known as gray sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its gray-green leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often grown for its fragrant foliage and ornamental value in gardens.

Satureja grisea is also used in traditional herbal remedies for its purported medicinal properties.
