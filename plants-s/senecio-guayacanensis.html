Senecio guayacanensis

Senecio guayacanensis, commonly known as the Guayacanensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which appear in clusters, are yellow and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
