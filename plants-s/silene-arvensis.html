Silene arvensis

Silene arvensis, commonly known as field catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape. Silene arvensis has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also a host plant for certain insect species, contributing to local biodiversity.
