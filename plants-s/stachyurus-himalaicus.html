Stachyurus himalaicus

Stachyurus himalaicus, commonly known as the Himalayan weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features long, pendulous branches that create a graceful, weeping appearance, with small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces yellowish-green catkins, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

Stachyurus himalaicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
