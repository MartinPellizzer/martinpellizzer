Stipa boliviensis

Stipa boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and fine, wiry leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence is a compact, spike-like structure that contains small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Stipa boliviensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
