Stemonurus membranaceus

Stemonurus membranaceus, commonly known as the membranous stemonurus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Stemonurus membranaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
