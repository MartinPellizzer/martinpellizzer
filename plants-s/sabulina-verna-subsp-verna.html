Sabulina verna subsp. verna

Sabulina verna subsp.

verna, commonly known as spring sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically features basal rosettes of linear, often hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine and coastal environments.
