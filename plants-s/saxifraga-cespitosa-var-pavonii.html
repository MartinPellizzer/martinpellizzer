Saxifraga cespitosa var. pavonii

Saxifraga cespitosa var. pavonii, commonly known as the pink saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
