Statice echioides subsp. exaristata

Statice echioides subsp.

exaristata, commonly known as the desert statice, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.

The subspecies exaristata is distinguished by its more elongated flower heads compared to other subspecies of Statice echioides.
