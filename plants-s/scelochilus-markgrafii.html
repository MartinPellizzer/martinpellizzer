Scelochilus markgrafii

Scelochilus markgrafii, commonly known as the Markgraf's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Scelochilus markgrafii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
