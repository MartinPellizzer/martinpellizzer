Salsola roshevitzii

Salsola roshevitzii, commonly known as Roshevitz's saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments. It often forms dense shrubs and plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh conditions.

Salsola roshevitzii is also used in some traditional medicinal practices.
