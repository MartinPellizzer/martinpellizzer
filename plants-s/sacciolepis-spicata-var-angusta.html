Sacciolepis spicata var. angusta

Sacciolepis spicata var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sacciolepis, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in dune environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
