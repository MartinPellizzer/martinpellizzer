Secale cereale var. spontaneum

Secale cereale var. spontaneum, commonly known as wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings but can also serve as forage for livestock.
