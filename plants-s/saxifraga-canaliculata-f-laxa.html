Saxifraga canaliculata f. laxa

Saxifraga canaliculata f. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments in East Asia.

It features small, clustered leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive notched or grooved appearance. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine arrangements.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to horticultural collections.
