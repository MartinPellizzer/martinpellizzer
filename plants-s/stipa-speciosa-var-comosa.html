Stipa speciosa var. comosa

Stipa speciosa var. comosa, commonly known as the silky needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with feathery, silver-green leaves. The plant produces dense, cylindrical seed heads that are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of native plant communities and its use in ecological restoration projects.
