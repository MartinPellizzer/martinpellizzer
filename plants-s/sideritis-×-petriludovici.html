Sideritis × petriludovici

Sideritis × petriludovici, commonly known as the hybrid lavender hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Sideritis × petriludovici is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
