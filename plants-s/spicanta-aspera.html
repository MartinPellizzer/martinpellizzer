Spicanta aspera

Spicanta aspera, commonly known as the rough spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Spicanta aspera is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.
