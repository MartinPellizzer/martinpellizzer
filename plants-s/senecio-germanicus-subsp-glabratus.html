Senecio germanicus subsp. glabratus

Senecio germanicus subsp.

glabratus, commonly known as the smoothleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are generally smooth and glabrous, giving it its subspecies name.

It produces yellow flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
