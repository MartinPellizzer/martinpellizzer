Silene muscipula subsp. corymbifera

Silene muscipula subsp.

corymbifera, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, corymb-like inflorescence and hairy stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
