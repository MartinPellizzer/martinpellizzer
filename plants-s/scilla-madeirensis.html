Scilla madeirensis

Scilla madeirensis, commonly known as the Madeira squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the island of Madeira.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers in spring. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in mild, temperate climates.

Scilla madeirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
