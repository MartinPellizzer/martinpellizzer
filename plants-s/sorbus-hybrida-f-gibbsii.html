Sorbus hybrida f. gibbsii

Sorbus hybrida f. gibbsii, commonly known as the Gibbs' whitebeam, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow berries that persist through winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a glossy upper surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or hedgerow habitats.
