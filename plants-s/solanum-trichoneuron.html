Solanum trichoneuron

Solanum trichoneuron, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and orange flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and spurred structures. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and are covered in fine hairs.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
