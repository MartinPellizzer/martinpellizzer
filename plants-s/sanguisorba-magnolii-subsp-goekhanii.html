Sanguisorba magnolii subsp. goekhanii

Sanguisorba magnolii subsp.

goekhanii, commonly known as the Turkish rose of the mountains, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey. It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces reddish-brown seed capsules that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
