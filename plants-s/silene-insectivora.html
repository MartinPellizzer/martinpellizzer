Silene insectivora

Silene insectivora, commonly known as the insect-eating catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs, which aid in trapping small insects. Silene insectivora is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it can derive some nutrients from its host plants while also performing photosynthesis.

Its unique adaptations make it an interesting subject for studying plant-predator interactions.
