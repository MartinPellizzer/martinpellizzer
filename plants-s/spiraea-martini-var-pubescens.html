Spiraea martini var. pubescens

Spiraea martini var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent meadow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features arching stems covered in fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
