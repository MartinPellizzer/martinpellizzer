Syngonium triphyllum

Syngonium triphyllum, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spadices, which are clusters of tiny berries. Syngonium triphyllum is often grown as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

It is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ease of care.
