Senecio integerrimus f. ochroleucus

Senecio integerrimus f. ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow ragwort, is a variant of the common ragwort, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the western United States. The plant typically grows in dry, open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has rough, hairy leaves and produces a compact cluster of yellow flowers at the top of the stem.

This subspecies is often found in arid environments and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
