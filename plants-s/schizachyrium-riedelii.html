Schizachyrium riedelii

Schizachyrium riedelii, commonly known as Riedel's little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves that turn coppery in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. It produces small, spike-like flower heads that are usually brown or tan in color.

Schizachyrium riedelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in native plant gardens.
