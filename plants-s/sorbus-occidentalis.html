Sorbus occidentalis

Sorbus occidentalis, commonly known as the western whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into 5-7 oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it often has a twisted or gnarled appearance.

Sorbus occidentalis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
