Sedum crassiflorum

Sedum crassiflorum, commonly known as the broadflower stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments. It is a hardy perennial that is relatively low maintenance and drought tolerant.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
