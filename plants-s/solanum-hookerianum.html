Solanum hookerianum

Solanum hookerianum, commonly known as Hooker's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, often purple or greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are usually yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic compounds.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
