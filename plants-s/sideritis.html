Sideritis

Sideritis, commonly known as ironweed, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, with many species growing in rocky or mountainous areas. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, often pink or purple flowers. Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Some species are cultivated for their ornamental value and for the production of essential oils.
