Stevia salicifolia var. integra

Stevia salicifolia var. integra, commonly known as the green leaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
