Scrophularia pilosa

Scrophularia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Scrophularia pilosa is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.
