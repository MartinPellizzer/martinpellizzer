Serjania cubensis

Serjania cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban serjania, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Serjania cubensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
