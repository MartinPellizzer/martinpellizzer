Smilax saxicola

Smilax saxicola, commonly known as the rock smilax, is a perennial climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves, which are typically alternate and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or wooded areas and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Smilax saxicola is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
