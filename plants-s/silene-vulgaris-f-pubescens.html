Silene vulgaris f. pubescens

Silene vulgaris f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy catchfly, is a flowering plant characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene vulgaris f. pubescens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
