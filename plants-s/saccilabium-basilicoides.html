Saccilabium basilicoides

Saccilabium basilicoides, commonly known as the sacred basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a strong, spicy scent reminiscent of basil.

Saccilabium basilicoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
