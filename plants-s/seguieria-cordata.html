Seguieria cordata

Seguieria cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves with a heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Seguieria cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
