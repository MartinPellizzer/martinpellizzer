Sophora microcarpa

Sophora microcarpa, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pendulous flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, blooming in the spring. Its seed pods are elongated and contain small, hard seeds.

Sophora microcarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
