Sorbus aria f. zolyomii

Sorbus aria f. zolyomii, commonly known as the Zolyom whitebeam, is a botanical variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale pink flowers that appear in dense clusters. The leaves are typically elliptical with a serrated margin, and the fruit is a small, aggregate berry. This subspecies is native to Hungary and parts of central Europe, thriving in woodland and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
