Salacia semlikiensis var. subcordatifolia

Salacia semlikiensis var. subcordatifolia, commonly known as the subcordate salacia, is a rare and striking plant native to the montane forests of East Africa.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in botanical collections.
