Salvia nudicaulis

Salvia nudicaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Salvia nudicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicinal practices.
