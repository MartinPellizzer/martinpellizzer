Syzygium montanum

Syzygium montanum, commonly known as the mountain clove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a capsule containing aromatic seeds.

Syzygium montanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
