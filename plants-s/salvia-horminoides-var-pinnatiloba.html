Salvia horminoides var. pinnatiloba

Salvia horminoides var. pinnatiloba, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a mint-like scent and is often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a popular choice in both gardens and herbal practices.
