Salix lasiolepis var. velutina

Salix lasiolepis var. velutina, commonly known as the silky willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a soft, fuzzy texture.

This species is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
