Satureja × thymifolia

Satureja × thymifolia, commonly known as hybrid thyme, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Satureja hortensis and Thymus species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant typically features small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, Satureja × thymifolia is often used in both horticulture and herbal applications.
