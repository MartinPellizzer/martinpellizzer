Smilax sinclairii

Smilax sinclairii, commonly known as the Sinclair's smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a climbing habit with spiny stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
