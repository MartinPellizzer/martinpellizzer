Symphyotrichum parviflorum

Symphyotrichum parviflorum, commonly known as the plains heath aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with purple to pink petals. It blooms from late summer to early fall, contributing to the fall floral display.

Symphyotrichum parviflorum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
