Schoenus delicatulus

Schoenus delicatulus, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to southern Africa, this plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The flowers are small and usually greenish or brownish, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
