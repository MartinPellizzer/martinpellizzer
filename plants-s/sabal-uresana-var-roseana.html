Sabal uresana var. roseana

Sabal uresana var. roseana, commonly known as the Florida rosewood, is a deciduous palm native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and hammocks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
