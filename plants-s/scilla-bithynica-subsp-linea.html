Scilla bithynica subsp. linea

Scilla bithynica subsp.

linea, commonly known as the lineated scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers well-drained conditions.

The plant produces clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in late winter.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to garden settings.
