Sarcanthus walkerianus

Sarcanthus walkerianus, commonly known as the Walker's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Sarcanthus walkerianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and resilience.
