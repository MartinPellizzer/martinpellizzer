Silene webbiana

Silene webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene webbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
