Senecio doronicum proles gerardi

Senecio doronicum proles gerardi, commonly known as the Gerard's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
