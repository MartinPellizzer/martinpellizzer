Smilax menispermoidea

Smilax menispermoidea, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny stems, which help it to climb and protect itself from herbivores. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Smilax menispermoidea is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its hardy nature.
