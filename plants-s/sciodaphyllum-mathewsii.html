Sciodaphyllum mathewsii

Sciodaphyllum mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees, primarily in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It has small, leathery leaves and produces white or pale yellow berries that are dispersed by birds. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which connect to the host's vascular system. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for birds and insects.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
