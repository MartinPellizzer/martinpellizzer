Schismatoglottis crispa

Schismatoglottis crispa, commonly known as the crinkled schismatoglottis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, crinkled, waxy leaves that are often a vibrant green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typical of many orchids. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Schismatoglottis crispa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ease of care.
