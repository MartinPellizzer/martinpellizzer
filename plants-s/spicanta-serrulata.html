Spicanta serrulata

Spicanta serrulata, commonly known as the serrated spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features slender, triangular stems with sharp, serrated edges along its leaves, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish or brownish, and it produces distinctive spike-like inflorescences.

Spicanta serrulata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
