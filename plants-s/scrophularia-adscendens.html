Scrophularia adscendens

Scrophularia adscendens, commonly known as ascending figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features upright stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes, attracting various pollinators.

Scrophularia adscendens is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
