Sambucus cerulea f. vestita

Sambucus cerulea f. vestita, commonly known as the white elderberry, is a compact shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its growth habit is typically upright and bushy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
