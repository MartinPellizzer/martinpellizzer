Silene douglasii var. macounii

Silene douglasii var. macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, such as slopes and dry meadows. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
