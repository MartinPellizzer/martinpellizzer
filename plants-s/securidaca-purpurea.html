Securidaca purpurea

Securidaca purpurea, commonly known as purple senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include stipules, a distinctive leaf venation pattern, and a unique floral structure.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
