Symplocos wikstroemiifolia

Symplocos wikstroemiifolia, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters, followed by dark purple to black berries. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves with smooth margins and a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside habitats.

The berries are a food source for various birds and mammals, contributing to its ecological significance.
