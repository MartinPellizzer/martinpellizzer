Sida urens var. prostrata

Sida urens var. prostrata, commonly known as prostrate morning glory, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, five-petaled flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming throughout the growing season. The plant has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along trails.
