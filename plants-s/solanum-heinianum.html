Solanum heinianum

Solanum heinianum, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, white flowers and smooth, often hairy stems. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing toxic compounds such as solasodine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
