Sebaea cordata var. intermedia

Sebaea cordata var. intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid sebaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
