Symplocos cochinchinensis subsp. sessifolia

Symplocos cochinchinensis subsp.

sessifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evergreen holly, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It features elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
