Selaginella incurvata

Selaginella incurvata, commonly known as the zigzag spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features whorled leaves that curve inward, giving it its distinctive zigzag appearance. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella incurvata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
