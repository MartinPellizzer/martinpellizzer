Scilla ramburii subsp. beirana

Scilla ramburii subsp.

beirana, commonly known as the Corsican squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers in early spring.

The plant is adapted to rocky and limestone soils, often growing in coastal and mountainous areas. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental value.

It is an important species for biodiversity in its native habitats.
