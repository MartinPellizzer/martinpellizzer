Sararanga sinuosa

Sararanga sinuosa, commonly known as the sinuous sararanga, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
