Salacia perakensis

Salacia perakensis, commonly known as the Bornean salacia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a glossy surface.

Salacia perakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
