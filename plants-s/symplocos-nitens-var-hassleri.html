Symplocos nitens var. hassleri

Symplocos nitens var. hassleri, commonly known as the hairy wax myrtle, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
