Shorea macrophylla

Shorea macrophylla, commonly known as the yellow meranti, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and heavy, durable wood that is highly valued in the timber industry. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces woody, elongated fruits.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
