Silene vulgaris subsp. thorei

Silene vulgaris subsp.

thorei, commonly known as red campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its pink to red flowers and linear, often hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Silene vulgaris subsp.

thorei is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.
