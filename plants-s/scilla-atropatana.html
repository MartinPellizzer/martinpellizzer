Scilla atropatana

Scilla atropatana, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scilla atropatana is valued in horticulture for its striking floral display and adaptability to various soil conditions.
