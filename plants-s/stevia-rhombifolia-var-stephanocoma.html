Stevia rhombifolia var. stephanocoma

Stevia rhombifolia var. stephanocoma, commonly known as the silver leaf stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

This variety is valued for its potential use in herbal remedies and as a natural sweetener.
