Stellaria cherleriae var. typica

Stellaria cherleriae var. typica, commonly known as the typical starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within its native habitat.
