Silene gallica var. anglica

Silene gallica var. anglica, commonly known as the English campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant produces slender, branching stems that can grow up to 60 cm tall, often with hairy surfaces. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded locations.

Silene gallica var. anglica is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
