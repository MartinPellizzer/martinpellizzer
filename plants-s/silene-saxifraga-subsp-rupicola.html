Silene saxifraga subsp. rupicola

Silene saxifraga subsp.

rupicola, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and alpine environments. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often found on limestone or sandstone substrates.

The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to conserve water.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and high altitudes.
