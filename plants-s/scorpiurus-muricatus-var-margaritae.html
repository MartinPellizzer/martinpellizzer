Scorpiurus muricatus var. margaritae

Scorpiurus muricatus var. margaritae, commonly known as the Margarita mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
