Satyrium maculatum

Satyrium maculatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to various regions in Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often spotted or streaked with dark markings. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and typically have a distinctive coloration with purple or brown hues.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
