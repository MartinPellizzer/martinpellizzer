Salix lutea var. famelica

Salix lutea var. famelica, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils near rivers or in woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
