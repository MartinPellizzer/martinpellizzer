Salmia subacaulis

Salmia subacaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved salvia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Salmia subacaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
