Serapias spiralis

Serapias spiralis, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a unique spiral pattern that gives it its common name.

Serapias spiralis is often found in alpine and coastal areas, where it adapts well to harsh climatic conditions.
