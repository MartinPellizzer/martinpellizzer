Salicornia lignosa

Salicornia lignosa, commonly known as sea beet, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, green flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils.

Salicornia lignosa is valued for its nutritional content and is used in both culinary and agricultural applications.
