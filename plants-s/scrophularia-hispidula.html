Scrophularia hispidula

Scrophularia hispidula, commonly known as the hairy figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are tubular and pink or purple, clustered in spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia hispidula is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
