Setiacis

Setiacis, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Southeast Asia, is a striking aquatic plant characterized by its large, showy flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments, often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has a complex root system that anchors it in the substrate while absorbing nutrients from the water. Its flowers, which can range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom in a sequential pattern, with the lower flowers opening first.

Setiacis is also notable for its ability to purify water and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
