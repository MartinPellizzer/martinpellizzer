Swartzia flaemingii var. cognata

Swartzia flaemingii var. cognata, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
