Salsola abarghuensis

Salsola abarghuensis, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months.

Salsola abarghuensis is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with low precipitation and high temperatures.
