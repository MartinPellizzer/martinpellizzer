Sabina convallium var. microsperma

Sabina convallium var. microsperma, commonly known as the dwarf juniper, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features compact, scale-like leaves that are typically bluish-gray in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating drought and poor soil conditions.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
