Solanum farinaceum

Solanum farinaceum, commonly known as the farinaceum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Solanum farinaceum is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and has been used traditionally in some regions for its medicinal properties.
