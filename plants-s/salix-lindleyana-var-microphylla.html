Salix lindleyana var. microphylla

Salix lindleyana var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and weeping branches that give it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are catkins, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
