Sedum erythraeum

Sedum erythraeum, commonly known as the Red Sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant color make it a visually striking addition to gardens.
