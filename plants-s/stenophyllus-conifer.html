Stenophyllus conifer

Stenophyllus conifer, commonly known as the narrow-leaf conifer, is a type of coniferous tree characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves.

It typically grows in temperate to subarctic climates, where it forms dense forests or shrublands. The plant's cones are usually small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its root system is adapted to poor soil conditions, allowing it to thrive in harsh environments.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
