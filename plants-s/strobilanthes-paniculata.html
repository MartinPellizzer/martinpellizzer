Strobilanthes paniculata

Strobilanthes paniculata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

The flowers bloom in clusters, forming paniculate inflorescences that attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
