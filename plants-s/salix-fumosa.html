Salix fumosa

Salix fumosa, commonly known as the smoky willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it blooms in early spring before its leaves emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in its native environment.
