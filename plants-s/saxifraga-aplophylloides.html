Saxifraga aplophylloides

Saxifraga aplophylloides, commonly known as the rockfoam saxifrage, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, white to pinkish flowers during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are generally basal, with a rosette structure that helps conserve moisture.

Saxifraga aplophylloides is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it adapts to cold and harsh climatic conditions.
