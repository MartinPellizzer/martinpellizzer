Sorbus fedorovii

Sorbus fedorovii, commonly known as the Fedorov's rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological importance. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Sorbus fedorovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
