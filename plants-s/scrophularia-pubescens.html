Scrophularia pubescens

Scrophularia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with toothed margins. Its flowers are tubular, arranged in clusters, and are usually pink or purple in color.

Scrophularia pubescens is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
