Stachyurus oblongifolius

Stachyurus oblongifolius, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique floral display and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.
