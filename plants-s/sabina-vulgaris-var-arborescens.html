Sabina vulgaris var. arborescens

Sabina vulgaris var. arborescens, commonly known as the creeping juniper, is a shrub-like evergreen with dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

It produces small, spherical berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for various garden environments.
