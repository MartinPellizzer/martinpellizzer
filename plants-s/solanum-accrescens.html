Solanum accrescens

Solanum accrescens, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum accrescens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common subject in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
