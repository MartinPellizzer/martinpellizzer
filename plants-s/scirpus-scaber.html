Scirpus scaber

Scirpus scaber, commonly known as stiff bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clustered spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus scaber is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
