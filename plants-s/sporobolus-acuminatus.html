Sporobolus acuminatus

Sporobolus acuminatus, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Sporobolus acuminatus is an important component of native grasslands and is adapted to withstand drought and grazing.
