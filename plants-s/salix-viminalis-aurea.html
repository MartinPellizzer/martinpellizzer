Salix viminalis aurea

Salix viminalis aurea, commonly known as golden willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which provides ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The plant has slender, weeping branches that give it a distinctive, graceful appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

Its flowers are catkins, typical of the Salicaceae family, and it is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
