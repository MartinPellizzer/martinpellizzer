Symplocos phaeoclados var. acuminata

Symplocos phaeoclados var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped evergreen holly, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with glossy, dark green leaves that have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
