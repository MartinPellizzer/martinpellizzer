Salvia officinalis subsp. gallica

Salvia officinalis subsp.

gallica, commonly known as French sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue.

The plant produces dense clusters of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its aromatic leaves are widely used in cooking, traditional medicine, and aromatherapy.
