Sedum rosea var. atropurpureum

Sedum rosea var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and leaves that are typically blue-green or purple in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
