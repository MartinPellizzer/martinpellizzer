Strumaria unguiculata

Strumaria unguiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, which helps in reducing water loss in its arid habitat. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable species in local flora.
