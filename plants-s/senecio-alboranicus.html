Senecio alboranicus

Senecio alboranicus, commonly known as the white-haired groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is typically found in rocky or sandy habitats. It has small, narrow leaves and produces numerous tiny florets arranged in a composite head.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
