Scholtzia kalbarri

Scholtzia kalbarri, commonly known as the Kalbarri sunflower, is an annual plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Scholtzia kalbarri is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
