Solanum humboldtii

Solanum humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming at night. Its fruit is a fleshy berry that contains seeds, and the plant is part of the nightshade family, which includes many toxic species.

Solanum humboldtii is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate saline conditions, making it a resilient plant in coastal environments.
