Scirpus lacustris var. custoris

Scirpus lacustris var. custoris, commonly known as the marsh club-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has narrow, cylindrical leaves that are densely packed around a central stem. Its flowering spikes consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
