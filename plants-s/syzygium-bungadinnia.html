Syzygium bungadinnia

Syzygium bungadinnia, commonly known as the Bungadinnia apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thin, edible skin and a sweet, tart flesh. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
