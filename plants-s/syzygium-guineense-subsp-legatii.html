Syzygium guineense subsp. legatii

Syzygium guineense subsp.

legatii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae. It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers.

The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
