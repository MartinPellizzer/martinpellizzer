Salix mezeroides

Salix mezeroides, commonly known as the Siberian willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold regions of Eastern Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed structure and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh climates.

Due to its resilience, it is also valued for erosion control in wetland areas.
