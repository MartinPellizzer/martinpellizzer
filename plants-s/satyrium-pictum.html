Satyrium pictum

Satyrium pictum, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display intricate patterns resembling painted designs. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a complex structure typical of orchids, including a labellum that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Satyrium pictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for various pollinators.
