Sterculia guianensis

Sterculia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese sterculia, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain smooth, round seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and a dense canopy, making it a significant component of the rainforest ecosystem.
