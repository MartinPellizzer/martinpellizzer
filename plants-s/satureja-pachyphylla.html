Satureja pachyphylla

Satureja pachyphylla, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and antiseptic properties.

Satureja pachyphylla is also valued in culinary applications for its distinctive, slightly peppery flavor.
