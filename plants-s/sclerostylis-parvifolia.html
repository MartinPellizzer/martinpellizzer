Sclerostylis parvifolia

Sclerostylis parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved screw-pine, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree with distinctive, narrow, strap-like leaves that are usually blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in coastal and saline environments, often growing on sandy or rocky soils.

Sclerostylis parvifolia is well adapted to harsh coastal conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
