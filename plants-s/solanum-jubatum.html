Solanum jubatum

Solanum jubatum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum jubatum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and can be invasive in some regions.
