Saccolabium coeleste

Saccolabium coeleste, commonly known as the sky-blue saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Its unique coloration and structure make it a notable species in its native habitat.
