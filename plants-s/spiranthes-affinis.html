Spiranthes affinis

Spiranthes affinis, commonly known as the pink spire orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer.

Spiranthes affinis is known for its ability to thrive in acidic soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
