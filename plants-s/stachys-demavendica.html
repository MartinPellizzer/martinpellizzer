Stachys demavendica

Stachys demavendica, commonly known as the Persian betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple to blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Stachys demavendica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
