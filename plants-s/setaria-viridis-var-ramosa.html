Setaria viridis var. ramosa

Setaria viridis var. ramosa, commonly known as green millet or slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.

Its rapid growth and high tolerance to drought make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
