Staurogyne kamerunensis subsp. kamerunensis

Staurogyne kamerunensis subsp.

kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon water hyssop, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams.

The plant has whorled leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small fish and invertebrates.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for water gardens.
