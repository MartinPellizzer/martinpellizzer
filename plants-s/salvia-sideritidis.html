Salvia sideritidis

Salvia sideritidis, commonly known as ironweed sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia sideritidis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid climates.
