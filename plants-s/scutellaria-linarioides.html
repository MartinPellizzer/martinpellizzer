Scutellaria linarioides

Scutellaria linarioides, commonly known as blue beakviolet, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces distinctive, tubular flowers with a prominent lower lip. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Scutellaria linarioides is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is reflected in its aromatic foliage and floral structure.
