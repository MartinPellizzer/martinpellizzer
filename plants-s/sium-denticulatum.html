Sium denticulatum

Sium denticulatum, commonly known as water parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and can be found across much of North America.
