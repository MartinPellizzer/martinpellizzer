Streptanthera cuprea

Streptanthera cuprea, commonly known as the copper orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape. The plant blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
