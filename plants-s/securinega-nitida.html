Securinega nitida

Securinega nitida, commonly known as the white coral tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in spring, producing clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
