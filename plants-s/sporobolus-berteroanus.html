Sporobolus berteroanus

Sporobolus berteroanus, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of bristle-like awns, which are typical of the genus Sporobolus. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
