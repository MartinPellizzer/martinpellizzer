Sempervivum adenotrichum

Sempervivum adenotrichum, commonly known as the snowflake plant, is a species of succulent in the family Crassulaceae.

It is native to the alpine regions of Europe and is characterized by its rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it a hardy and resilient plant.
