Solanum schwackei

Solanum schwackei, commonly known as the Schwacke's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible but can be toxic in large quantities. Solanum schwackei is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, alternate leaves, and a distinct chemical composition that includes various alkaloids.
