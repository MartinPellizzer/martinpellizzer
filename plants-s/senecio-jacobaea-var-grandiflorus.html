Senecio jacobaea var. grandiflorus

Senecio jacobaea var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Great Coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant has upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, spherical seed heads that are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
