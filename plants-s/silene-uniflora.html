Silene uniflora

Silene uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces a single, showy pink or purple flower per stem. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Silene uniflora is an important component of alpine and tundra ecosystems.
