Senecio karaguensis

Senecio karaguensis, commonly known as the Karagwan daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy yellow flowers.

Senecio karaguensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
