Spirochaeta

Spirochaeta, commonly known as a genus of spiral-shaped bacteria, is not a plant but a group of bacteria.

These microorganisms are characterized by their helical or spiral morphology, which distinguishes them from other bacterial forms. They are typically found in aquatic environments, soil, and sometimes in the gastrointestinal tracts of animals. Spirochaeta species are chemotrophic and often thrive in anaerobic conditions.

While they are not photosynthetic and do not contribute to plant life directly, they play a role in nutrient cycling in ecosystems.
