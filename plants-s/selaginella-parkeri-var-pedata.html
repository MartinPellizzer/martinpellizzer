Selaginella parkeri var. pedata

Selaginella parkeri var. pedata, commonly known as the walking fern, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its ability to produce new plantlets at the tips of its stolons, which can "walk" across the soil surface, giving it its common name. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spirally arranged leaves and a creeping growth habit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique reproductive structures and adaptive growth patterns make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
