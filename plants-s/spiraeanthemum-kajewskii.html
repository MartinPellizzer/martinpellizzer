Spiraeanthemum kajewskii

Spiraeanthemum kajewskii, commonly known as the Kajewskii's spirea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in the spring, which are borne on elongated inflorescences. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
