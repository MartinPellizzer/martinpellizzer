Salix × fragilis var. furcata

Salix × fragilis var. furcata, commonly known as weeping willow, is a hybrid willow species characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, often found near water sources such as rivers and ponds. The plant has long, slender, drooping twigs that create a graceful, flowing appearance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a serrated margin.

It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal.
