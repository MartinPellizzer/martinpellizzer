Sambucus suaveolens

Sambucus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features compound leaves with 5–9 elliptical leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like drupes in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
