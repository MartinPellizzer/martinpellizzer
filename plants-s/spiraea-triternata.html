Spiraea triternata

Spiraea triternata, commonly known as the three-part spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Spiraea triternata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
