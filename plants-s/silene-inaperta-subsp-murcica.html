Silene inaperta subsp. murcica

Silene inaperta subsp.

murcica, commonly known as the Murcian sand-plant, is a rare and endemic species native to southern Spain. It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grayish leaves and small, pinkish flowers.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in open habitats such as dunes or coastal areas.
