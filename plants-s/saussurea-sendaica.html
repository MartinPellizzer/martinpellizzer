Saussurea sendaica

Saussurea sendaica, commonly known as the Yunnan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery foliage. The plant produces yellow-orange flower heads that resemble saffron, hence its common name. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
