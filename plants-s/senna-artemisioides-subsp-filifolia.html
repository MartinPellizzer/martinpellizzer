Senna artemisioides subsp. filifolia

Senna artemisioides subsp.

filifolia, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
