Solanum lignicaule

Solanum lignicaule, commonly known as the tree tomato or carambola tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces yellow or orange berries that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Solanum lignicaule is valued both for its fruit and its ornamental qualities.
