Smilax herbacea var. oldhamii

Smilax herbacea var. oldhamii, commonly known as Oldham's greenbriar, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its stems are typically spiny, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, contributing to the understory vegetation.
