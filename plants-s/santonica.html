Santonica

Santonica, commonly known as sweet William, is a flowering plant belonging to the genus Dianthus in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered blooms that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a fragrant scent. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to Europe and the Mediterranean, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Santonica is also used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
