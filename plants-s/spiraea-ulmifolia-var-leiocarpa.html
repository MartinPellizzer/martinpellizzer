Spiraea ulmifolia var. leiocarpa

Spiraea ulmifolia var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smoothleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, arching stems and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are elliptical, smooth, and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is prized for its compact growth and long-lasting floral display.
