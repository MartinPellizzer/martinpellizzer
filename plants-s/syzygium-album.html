Syzygium album

Syzygium album, commonly known as the white apple or white sylph, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, white, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. Syzygium album is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
