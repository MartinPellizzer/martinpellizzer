Senna australis

Senna australis, commonly known as the coastal senna, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Senna australis thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
