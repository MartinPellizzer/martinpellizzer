Saxifraga codyana

Saxifraga codyana, commonly known as Cody's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga codyana is often found in alpine or subalpine environments, where it adapts to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
