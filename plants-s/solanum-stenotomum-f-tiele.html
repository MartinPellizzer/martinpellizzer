Solanum stenotomum f. tiele

Solanum stenotomum f. tiele, commonly known as the Tiele's nightshade, is a botanical variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its small, usually green or purple berries that contain toxic compounds. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological roles.
