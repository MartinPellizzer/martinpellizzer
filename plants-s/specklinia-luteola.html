Specklinia luteola

Specklinia luteola, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the forest canopy.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
