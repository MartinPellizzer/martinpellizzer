Scheelea quadrisperma

Scheelea quadrisperma, commonly known as the four-seeded palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by clustered, woody fruits containing four seeds each.

Scheelea quadrisperma is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
