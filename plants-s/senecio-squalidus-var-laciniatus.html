Senecio squalidus var. laciniatus

Senecio squalidus var. laciniatus, commonly known as the ragged ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its wiry, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically deeply lobed and toothed, giving the plant a distinctive, rugged appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its adaptability to arid environments.
