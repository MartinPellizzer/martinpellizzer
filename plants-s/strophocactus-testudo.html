Strophocactus testudo

Strophocactus testudo, commonly known as the testudinaria cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Overall, Strophocactus testudo is a hardy, drought-tolerant cactus well adapted to desert environments.
