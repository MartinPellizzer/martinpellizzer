Selaginella junghuhniana

Selaginella junghuhniana, commonly known as the Junghuhn's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a creeping stem with whorls of small, needle-like leaves, and it produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobila. It is characterized by its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.

Selaginella junghuhniana is also notable for its capacity to undergo dormancy, allowing it to withstand periods of environmental stress.
