Sabal glabra

Sabal glabra, commonly known as the American palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to 15 feet in length. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swamps and along the edges of water bodies.

Sabal glabra plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
