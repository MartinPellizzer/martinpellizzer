Solanum huancavelicae

Solanum huancavelicae, commonly known as the Huancavelica nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of Peru.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into berries. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
