Sameraria stylophora

Sameraria stylophora, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange floral bracts that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered within the colorful bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Sameraria stylophora is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
