Suteria macrantha

Suteria macrantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved suteria, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Suteria macrantha is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
