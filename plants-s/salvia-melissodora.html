Salvia melissodora

Salvia melissodora, commonly known as lemon bee bush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features striking, tubular violet-blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture and a strong, aromatic scent reminiscent of lemon. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for native gardens.
