Sida fraseri

Sida fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

The plant has fibrous roots and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.
