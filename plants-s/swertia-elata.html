Swertia elata

Swertia elata, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

Swertia elata is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan medicine.
