Senecio vaingaindrani

Senecio vaingaindrani, commonly known as the Van Gogh's Folly, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems that branch out into clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, cone-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
