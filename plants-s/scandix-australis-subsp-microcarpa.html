Scandix australis subsp. microcarpa

Scandix australis subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited sandbur, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit and produces small, spiny burrs that contain seeds.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
