Swida sanguinea subsp. australis

Swida sanguinea subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian red swida, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
