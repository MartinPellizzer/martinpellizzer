Salvia concolor var. concolor

Salvia concolor var. concolor, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
