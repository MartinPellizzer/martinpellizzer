Syzygium novoguineense

Syzygium novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Myrtaceae family, native to the rainforests of New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, typically arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a smooth, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or red when ripe, containing a single large seed.
