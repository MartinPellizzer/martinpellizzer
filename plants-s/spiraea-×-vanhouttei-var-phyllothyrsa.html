Spiraea × vanhouttei var. phyllothyrsa

Spiraea × vanhouttei var. phyllothyrsa, commonly known as the golden spiraea, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy inflorescences.

It features small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, arranged in flat-topped clusters known as corymbs. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and naturalized areas.
