Scirpus lacustris subsp. validus

Scirpus lacustris subsp.

validus, commonly known as the lake sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

The plant has tall, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that aids in water retention.

This subspecies plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
