Senecio jonesii

Senecio jonesii, commonly known as Jones' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Senecio jonesii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
