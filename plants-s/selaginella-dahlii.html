Selaginella dahlii

Selaginella dahlii, commonly known as the Himalayan clubmoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its creeping stems and small, scale-like leaves that form dense mats. Selaginella dahlii is known for its ability to thrive in high-altitude conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
