Scabiosa parnassae

Scabiosa parnassae, commonly known as the Parnassus scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Scabiosa parnassae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
