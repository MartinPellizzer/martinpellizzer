Salvia Longiflora

Salvia longiflora, also known as the long-flowered sage, is a striking plant native to the western regions of the United States. It is recognized for its vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. This herbaceous perennial thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. With its beauty and ecological benefits, Salvia longiflora is a valuable addition to any native plant garden.
