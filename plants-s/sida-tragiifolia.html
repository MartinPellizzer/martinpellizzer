Sida tragiifolia

Sida tragiifolia, commonly known as the prickly sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and covered in fine, stiff hairs. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and the plant produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Sida tragiifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
