Silene furcata

Silene furcata, commonly known as the forked catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Silene furcata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
