Sarcopteryx acuminata

Sarcopteryx acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
