Sinapis longirostra

Sinapis longirostra, commonly known as the long-beaked mustard, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers and has pinnately divided leaves.

Sinapis longirostra is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
