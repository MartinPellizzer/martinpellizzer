Salvia flexuosa

Salvia flexuosa, commonly known as meadow sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
