Souroubea pileata

Souroubea pileata, commonly known as the woolly souroubea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Souroubea pileata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
