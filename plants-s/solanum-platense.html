Solanum platense

Solanum platense, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and glandular.

Solanum platense is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
