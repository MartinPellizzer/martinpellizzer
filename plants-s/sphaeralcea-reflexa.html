Sphaeralcea reflexa

Sphaeralcea reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow, globular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Sphaeralcea reflexa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
