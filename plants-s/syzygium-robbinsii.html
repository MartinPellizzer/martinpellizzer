Syzygium robbinsii

Syzygium robbinsii, commonly known as the Robbins's apple or the red apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. Syzygium robbinsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Indigenous Australian medicine.
