Salacia frutiplatensis

Salacia frutiplatensis, commonly known as the tropical evergreen shrub, is a member of the Salaceae family.

It is native to the humid forests of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its dense foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, which is edible, is covered in a thick, leathery rind and contains several seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
