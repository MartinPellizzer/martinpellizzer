Salix kaptarae

Salix kaptarae, commonly known as the Kaptara willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly found in Afghanistan and Pakistan. This shrub or small tree typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery-white underside that reflects the sunlight.

The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
