Sanguisorba hakusanensis var. japonensis

Sanguisorba hakusanensis var. japonensis, commonly known as Japanese loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
