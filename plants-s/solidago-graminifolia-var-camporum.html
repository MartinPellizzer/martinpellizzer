Solidago graminifolia var. camporum

Solidago graminifolia var. camporum, commonly known as the Camp's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer to early fall. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
