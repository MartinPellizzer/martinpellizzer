Sorbus pekarovae

Sorbus pekarovae, commonly known as the Pekarova rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Sorbus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters and its distinctive, small, red berries that appear in the fall. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often at high altitudes.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
