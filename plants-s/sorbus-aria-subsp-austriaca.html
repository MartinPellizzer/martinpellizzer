Sorbus aria subsp. austriaca

Sorbus aria subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and the plant is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
