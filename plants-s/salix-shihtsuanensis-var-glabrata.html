Salix shihtsuanensis var. glabrata

Salix shihtsuanensis var. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a graceful, cascading appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glabrous surface. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
