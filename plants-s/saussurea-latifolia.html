Saussurea latifolia

Saussurea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy composite flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Saussurea latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
