Sterculia sangirensis

Sterculia sangirensis, commonly known as the Sangirese Sterculia, is a tropical tree native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional practices. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
