Spatholobus parviflorus

Spatholobus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in racemes. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for native pollinators and stabilizing soil in coastal areas.
