Senecio segmentatus

Senecio segmentatus, commonly known as the segmented senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, branching stem structure with narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wool. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
