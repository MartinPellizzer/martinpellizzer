Spuricianthus

Spuricianthus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the wetlands of Florida and Cuba.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves and its striking, delicate white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant relies on specific fungal associations for nutrient uptake, making it highly dependent on its environment. Its unique morphology and limited distribution have made it a subject of botanical interest and conservation concern.

Due to habitat destruction, Spuricianthus is considered endangered, highlighting the need for continued research and protection efforts.
