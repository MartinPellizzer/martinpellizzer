Selaginella boomii

Selaginella boomii, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its whorled arrangement of narrow, needle-like leaves. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Selaginella boomii is notable for its ability to survive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
