Senecio pinceus

Senecio pinceus, commonly known as the Pinceus groundsels, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Senecio pinceus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive appearance.
