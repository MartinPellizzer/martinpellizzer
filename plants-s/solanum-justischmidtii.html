Solanum justischmidtii

Solanum justischmidtii, commonly known as the Schischmidt's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom at night. The plant produces small, round berries that are yellow when mature, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique blooming habit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
