Scilla linearifolia

Scilla linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
