Stelis trullilabia

Stelis trullilabia, commonly known as the orchid with the unique labellum shape, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and relatively small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, elongated labellum that sets it apart from other orchid species.

Stelis trullilabia is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
