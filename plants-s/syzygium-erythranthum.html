Syzygium erythranthum

Syzygium erythranthum, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
