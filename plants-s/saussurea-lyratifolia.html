Saussurea lyratifolia

Saussurea lyratifolia, commonly known as the Tibetan anthem plant, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to extreme conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Saussurea lyratifolia is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its cultural and medicinal significance in traditional Tibetan medicine.
