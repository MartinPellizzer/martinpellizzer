Semecarpus borneensis

Semecarpus borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean breadnut, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes other notable plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The tree is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spiny, woody fruits. The seeds inside the fruits are encased in a hard, fibrous coat, which is a distinctive botanical feature.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
