Smilax ovalifolia var. parvigloba

Smilax ovalifolia var. parvigloba, commonly known as the small-leaf devil's backbone, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorned stems and heart-shaped, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swamps and along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
