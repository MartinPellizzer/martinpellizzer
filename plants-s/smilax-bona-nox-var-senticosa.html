Smilax bona-nox var. senticosa

Smilax bona-nox var. senticosa, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense thickets.
