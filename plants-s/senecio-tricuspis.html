Senecio tricuspis

Senecio tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow, lobed, and have a hairy texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in the summer months.

Senecio tricuspis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
