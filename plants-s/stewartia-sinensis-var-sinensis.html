Stewartia sinensis var. sinensis

Stewartia sinensis var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese Stewartia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental leaves, which are ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue, spherical berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
