Solanum mannii var. compactum

Solanum mannii var. compactum, commonly known as the dwarf potato vine, is a compact, trailing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dense foliage and colorful blooms.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers in gardens.
