Senecio andrzejowskyi

Senecio andrzejowskyi, commonly known as the Andrzejowski's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
