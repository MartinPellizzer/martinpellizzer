Solanum coerulescens var. manophyes

Solanum coerulescens var. manophyes, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates.
