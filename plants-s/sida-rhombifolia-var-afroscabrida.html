Sida rhombifolia var. afroscabrida

Sida rhombifolia var. afroscabrida, commonly known as African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its fibrous roots and drought tolerance make it adaptable to arid environments.
