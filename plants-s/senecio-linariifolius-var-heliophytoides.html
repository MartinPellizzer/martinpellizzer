Senecio linariifolius var. heliophytoides

Senecio linariifolius var. heliophytoides, commonly known as the sun-like groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and linear, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This variant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
