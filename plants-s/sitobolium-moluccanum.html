Sitobolium moluccanum

Sitobolium moluccanum, commonly known as the Moluccan wild bean, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Sitobolium moluccanum is also noted for its potential in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
