Sesbania aculeata var. erubescens

Sesbania aculeata var. erubescens, commonly known as the red-stem sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a leguminous species characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod fruits containing lens-shaped seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
