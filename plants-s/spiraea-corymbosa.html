Spiraea corymbosa

Spiraea corymbosa, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, multi-branched stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
