Symphyotrichum ciliolatum var. wilsonii

Symphyotrichum ciliolatum var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's woodland aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features showy, composite flowers that bloom in the fall, with purple to pink ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have finely toothed margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
