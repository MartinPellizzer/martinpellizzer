Salvia galeottii

Salvia galeottii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Salvia galeottii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
