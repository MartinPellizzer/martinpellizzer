Sorbus umbellata var. taurica

Sorbus umbellata var. taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its white, fluffy inflorescences that appear in late spring, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly serrated margin. The fruit is a small, aggregate berry that ripens to a dark red or black color in autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
