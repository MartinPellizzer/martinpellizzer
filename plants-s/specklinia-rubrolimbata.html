Specklinia rubrolimbata

Specklinia rubrolimbata, commonly known as the red-limbed pigmy orchid, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and strikingly colored, lance-shaped leaves that often display a reddish or maroon hue along the edges. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity in the wild, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts in controlled conditions.
