Syzygium tsoongii

Syzygium tsoongii, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a sour taste, often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

Syzygium tsoongii is also cultivated for its ability to provide shade and its ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
