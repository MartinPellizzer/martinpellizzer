Solanum diflorum var. pulverulentum

Solanum diflorum var. pulverulentum, commonly known as the dustflower or dwarf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, showy white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, toxic berries that are typically yellow when mature. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
