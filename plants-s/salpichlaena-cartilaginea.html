Salpichlaena cartilaginea

Salpichlaena cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilaginous fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
