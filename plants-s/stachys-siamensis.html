Stachys siamensis

Stachys siamensis, commonly known as Siam betony, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces spikes of purple or pink flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Stachys siamensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
