Saxifraga cotyledon var. minor

Saxifraga cotyledon var. minor, commonly known as the "Baby's Breath Saxifrage," is a low-growing succulent native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or pinkish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative plant.
