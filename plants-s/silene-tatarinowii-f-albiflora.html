Silene tatarinowii f. albiflora

Silene tatarinowii f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered tatarian catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to regions of Central Asia. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
