Shorea bakoensis

Shorea bakoensis, commonly known as Bako ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the lowland forests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a high oil content. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with smooth, grey bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface.

Shorea bakoensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
