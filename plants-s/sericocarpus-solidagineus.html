Sericocarpus solidagineus

Sericocarpus solidagineus, commonly known as the Canadian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant produces small, dry fruits called achenes, which are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Sericocarpus solidagineus is often found in the northern United States and southern Canada.
