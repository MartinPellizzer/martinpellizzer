Senecio macropus

Senecio macropus, commonly known as the large-flowered senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow-orange flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Senecio macropus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
