Striga passargei

Striga passargei, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red cedar, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its aromatic, reddish bark and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and unique scent, making it a significant resource in traditional crafts and medicine.

Striga passargei plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
