Secamone rhopalophora

Secamone rhopalophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf secamone, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its slender, often ascending stems. The plant features opposite, narrow, lanceolate leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are small, clustered in cymes, and typically have four to five petals.
