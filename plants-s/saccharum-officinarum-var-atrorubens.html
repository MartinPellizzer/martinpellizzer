Saccharum officinarum var. atrorubens

Saccharum officinarum var. atrorubens, commonly known as the red bamboo or red sugarcane, is a variant of sugarcane characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown coloration.

This plant is native to tropical regions of Asia and is cultivated for its high sugar content and ornamental value. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a deep green color that contrasts with the reddish stems. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.

It is often used in landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and can also be processed for sugar production.
