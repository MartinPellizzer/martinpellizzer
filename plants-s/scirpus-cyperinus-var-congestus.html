Scirpus cyperinus var. congestus

Scirpus cyperinus var. congestus, commonly known as the clustered bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering structures, called spikelets, are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters at the top of the plant.

This variety is often found in marshes, ponds, and other wet environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
