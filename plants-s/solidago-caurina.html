Solidago caurina

Solidago caurina, commonly known as the white goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and roadside ditches. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Solidago caurina is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
