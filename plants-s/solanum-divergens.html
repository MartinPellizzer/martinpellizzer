Solanum divergens

Solanum divergens, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features trailing stems that produce small, greenish-white flowers followed by violet or purple berries. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species like tomatoes and peppers. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Solanum divergens is toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
