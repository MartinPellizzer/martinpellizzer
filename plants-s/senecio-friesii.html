Senecio friesii

Senecio friesii, commonly known as the Fries' groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
