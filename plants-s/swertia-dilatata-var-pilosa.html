Swertia dilatata var. pilosa

Swertia dilatata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy swertia, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pilosa.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
