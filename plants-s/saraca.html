Saraca

Saraca, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South and Southeast Asia, with species such as Saraca asoca being particularly notable. The plant is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in spring, creating a vibrant display. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feathery leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Saraca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
