Salvia confertiflora var. angustifolia

Salvia confertiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a grayish-green hue with a fuzzy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
