Spiraea sublobata

Spiraea sublobata, commonly known as the narrowleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea sublobata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
