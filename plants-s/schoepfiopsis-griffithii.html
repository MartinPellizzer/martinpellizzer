Schoepfiopsis griffithii

Schoepfiopsis griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir blue poplar, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking blue-gray bark. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by yellowish-green berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
