Senecio appendiculatus

Senecio appendiculatus, commonly known as the silver cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and compact size, it is often used as a decorative indoor plant.
