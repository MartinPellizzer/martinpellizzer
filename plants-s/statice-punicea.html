Statice punicea

Statice punicea, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the western Atlantic.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its profuse display of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its flowers are typically tubular and arranged in dense spikes, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Statice punicea is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
