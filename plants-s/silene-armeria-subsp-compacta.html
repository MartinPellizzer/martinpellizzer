Silene armeria subsp. compacta

Silene armeria subsp.

compacta, commonly known as the compact sea pink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces pink to purple flowers in late summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
