Schizophragma integrifolium var. glaucescens

Schizophragma integrifolium var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blueleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
