Sisyrinchium iridioides

Sisyrinchium iridioides, commonly known as blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It is part of the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related plants.

Its name reflects both its grass-like appearance and the color of its flowers.
