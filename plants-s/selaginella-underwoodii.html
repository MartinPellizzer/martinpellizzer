Selaginella underwoodii

Selaginella underwoodii, commonly known as the Underwood's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often bright green and have a waxy coating. It produces small, cone-like structures called strobila that contain spores, aiding in its reproduction.

Selaginella underwoodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
