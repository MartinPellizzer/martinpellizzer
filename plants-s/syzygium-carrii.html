Syzygium carrii

Syzygium carrii, commonly known as the Carri's apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble apples in appearance. Syzygium carrii is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Indigenous practices.

Its growth is typically found in humid, well-drained soils under shaded conditions.
