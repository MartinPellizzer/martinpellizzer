Solanum aviculare var. typicum

Solanum aviculare var. typicum, commonly known as the scarlet sage, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its vibrant red flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
