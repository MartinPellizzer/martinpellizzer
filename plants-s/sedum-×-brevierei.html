Sedum × brevierei

Sedum × brevierei, commonly known as the stonecrop hybrid, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically in late summer. It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Sedum acre and Sedum hispanicum.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
