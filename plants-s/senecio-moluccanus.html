Senecio moluccanus

Senecio moluccanus, commonly known as the Moluccan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Guinea.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
