Salvia agrestis

Salvia agrestis, commonly known as field sage, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. Its flowers are violet to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Salvia agrestis is known for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
