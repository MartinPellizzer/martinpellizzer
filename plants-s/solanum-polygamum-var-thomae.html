Solanum polygamum var. thomae

Solanum polygamum var. thomae, commonly known as the Thomae's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces berries that turn from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
