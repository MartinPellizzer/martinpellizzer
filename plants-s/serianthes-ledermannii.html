Serianthes ledermannii

Serianthes ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's ironwood, is a rare and ancient tree species native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and durable wood, which has been used historically for construction and tool-making. The plant has a unique morphology, featuring a thick, gnarled trunk and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to the arid climate of its habitat. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by winged fruits, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, Serianthes ledermannii is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
