Sphacele clinopodioides

Sphacele clinopodioides, commonly known as slender woodruff, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its botanical features make it well adapted to moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
