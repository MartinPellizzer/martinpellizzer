Sasamorpha tobaeana var. pilosa

Sasamorpha tobaeana var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy sasamorpha, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "pilosa.". The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
