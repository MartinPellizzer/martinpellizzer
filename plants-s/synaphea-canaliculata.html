Synaphea canaliculata

Synaphea canaliculata, commonly known as the canyon thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically arranged in a single terminal head. Synaphea canaliculata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and canyon areas.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is an important species in its native ecosystem.
