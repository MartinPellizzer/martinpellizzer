Scytanthus bambusarum

Scytanthus bambusarum, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble bamboo. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Scytanthus bambusarum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
