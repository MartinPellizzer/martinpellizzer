Sclerorhachis binaludensis

Sclerorhachis binaludensis, commonly known as the Binalud spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Iranian plateau.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often milky sap. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate and simple, with a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Sclerorhachis binaludensis is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
