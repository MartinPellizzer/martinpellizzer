Syzygium longipedicellatum

Syzygium longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruit that resembles a starfruit or carambola. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a bright yellow or orange, indicating maturity.
