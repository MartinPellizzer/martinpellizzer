Stapfochloa ciliata

Stapfochloa ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bamboo, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silky leaves with fine, hair-like structures along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Stapfochloa ciliata is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to form dense, low-growing clumps.
