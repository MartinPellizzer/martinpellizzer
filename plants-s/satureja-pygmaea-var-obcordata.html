Satureja pygmaea var. obcordata

Satureja pygmaea var. obcordata, commonly known as narrowleaf savory, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tiny, aromatic leaves with a distinctive, peppery scent, and produces small, pale pink to white flowers in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its compact size and hardy nature make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

It is also valued in culinary and medicinal uses for its pungent, aromatic properties.
