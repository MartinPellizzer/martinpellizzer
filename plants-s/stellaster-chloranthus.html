Stellaster chloranthus

Stellaster chloranthus, commonly known as the golden starflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small stars. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, meaning they contain both male and female reproductive organs.

Stellaster chloranthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
