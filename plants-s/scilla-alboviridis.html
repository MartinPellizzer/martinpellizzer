Scilla alboviridis

Scilla alboviridis, commonly known as the white-flowered scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.

Scilla alboviridis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
