Styloncerus drummondii

Styloncerus drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's longhorn beetle, is a species of beetle belonging to the family Cerambycidae.

It is characterized by its elongated body and prominent antennae, which are often longer than its body. The beetle is typically found in forested areas, where it feeds on decaying wood. Its life cycle involves a larval stage that develops within dead or decaying timber.
