Sayeria euryantha

Sayeria euryantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sayeria, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the cooler months.

Sayeria euryantha is well-suited to rocky soils and can thrive in low-water conditions.
