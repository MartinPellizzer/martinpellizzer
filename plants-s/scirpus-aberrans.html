Scirpus aberrans

Scirpus aberrans, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas, pond edges, and along streams, thriving in saturated soils. The plant has tall, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, and it produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or reddish flowers. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

Scirpus aberrans plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
