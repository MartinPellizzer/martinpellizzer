Sigesbeckia

Sigesbeckia, commonly known as the common fleabane, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow to orange composite flowers that resemble daisies. The leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs. Sigesbeckia species are often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

They are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
