Sisymbrium erysimoides var. arenarium

Sisymbrium erysimoides var. arenarium, commonly known as sand mustard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sand mustard is often found in disturbed areas and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
