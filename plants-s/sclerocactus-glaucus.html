Sclerocactus glaucus

Sclerocactus glaucus, commonly known as the blue prickly pear, is a cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically blue-green in color, covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and having a shallow root system.

Sclerocactus glaucus is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
