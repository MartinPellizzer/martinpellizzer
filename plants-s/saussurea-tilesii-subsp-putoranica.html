Saussurea tilesii subsp. putoranica

Saussurea tilesii subsp.

putoranica, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a dense covering of fine hairs.
