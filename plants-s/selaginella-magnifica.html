Selaginella magnifica

Selaginella magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a vascular plant characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobila. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. Its rhizomes form dense mats, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Selaginella magnifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil health.
