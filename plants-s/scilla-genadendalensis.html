Scilla genadendalensis

Scilla genadendalensis, commonly known as the Genadendal mountain squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates with mild winters. Its bulbs are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.

Scilla genadendalensis is an important species in the Amaryllidaceae family, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
