Saussurea bicolor

Saussurea bicolor, commonly known as the white gromwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saussurea bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
