Senecio ellenbergii

Senecio ellenbergii, commonly known as the Ellenberg's groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio ellenbergii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
