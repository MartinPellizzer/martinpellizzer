Setaria glauca var. purpurascens

Setaria glauca var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple foxtail, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues, giving it its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Purple foxtail is an annual plant that typically blooms from late spring to early summer.
