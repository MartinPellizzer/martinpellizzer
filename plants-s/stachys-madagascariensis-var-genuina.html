Stachys madagascariensis var. genuina

Stachys madagascariensis var. genuina, commonly known as the Madagascar betony, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of purple or pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within forest understories.

Its ornamental value and unique appearance make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
