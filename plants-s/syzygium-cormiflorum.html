Syzygium cormiflorum

Syzygium cormiflorum, commonly known as the knobwood or knobthorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive knobby, woody stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe.

Syzygium cormiflorum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
