Saccolabium wendlandianum

Saccolabium wendlandianum, commonly known as the Wendland's saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its botanical features include a woody stem, trifoliate leaves, and pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
