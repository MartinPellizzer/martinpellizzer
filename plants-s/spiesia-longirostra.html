Spiesia longirostra

Spiesia longirostra, commonly known as the long-beaked spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its smooth, green stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the spurge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Spiesia longirostra is known for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
