Stanleya pinnata var. inyoensis

Stanleya pinnata var. inyoensis, commonly known as the Inyo's penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in high-elevation environments. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for pollinators and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
