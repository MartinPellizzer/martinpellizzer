Sedum carinthiacum

Sedum carinthiacum, commonly known as the Carinthian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Sedum carinthiacum is often used in rock gardens and as a low-maintenance ornamental plant.
