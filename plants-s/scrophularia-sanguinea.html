Scrophularia sanguinea

Scrophularia sanguinea, commonly known as redstem figwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge on the underside. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually pink to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia sanguinea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
