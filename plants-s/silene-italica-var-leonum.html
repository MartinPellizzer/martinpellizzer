Silene italica var. leonum

Silene italica var. leonum, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and dry meadows.

Silene italica var. leonum is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy inflorescences and ecological importance to pollinators.
