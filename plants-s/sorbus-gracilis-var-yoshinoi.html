Sorbus gracilis var. yoshinoi

Sorbus gracilis var. yoshinoi, commonly known as Yoshino cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and delicate, white-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and turn vibrant yellow in autumn.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.
