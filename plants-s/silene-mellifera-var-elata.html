Silene mellifera var. elata

Silene mellifera var. elata, commonly known as the tall sweet William, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
