Schoenocaulon tenue

Schoenocaulon tenue, commonly known as slender mountain-mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences during the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional herbal remedies.

Schoenocaulon tenue is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
