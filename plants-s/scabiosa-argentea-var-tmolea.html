Scabiosa argentea var. tmolea

Scabiosa argentea var. tmolea, commonly known as silver scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features silvery, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
