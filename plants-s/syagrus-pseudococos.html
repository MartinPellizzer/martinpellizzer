Syagrus pseudococos

Syagrus pseudococos, commonly known as the palm heart or "pequi" in some regions, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fruits containing edible seeds. Its trunk is valued for its fibrous material, which has been traditionally used in making ropes and mats.

Syagrus pseudococos thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
