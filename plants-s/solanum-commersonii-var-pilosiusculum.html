Solanum commersonii var. pilosiusculum

Solanum commersonii var. pilosiusculum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces berries that turn from green to red as they mature, often containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
