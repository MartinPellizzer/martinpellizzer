Scilla siberica taurica

Scilla siberica taurica, commonly known as the Tauric scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in rocky or forested areas. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can survive harsh winters.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and early blooming habit.
