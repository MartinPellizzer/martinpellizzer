Semecarpus cochinchinensis

Semecarpus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or simply chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Saraceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces hard, woody fruits that contain a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and seeds have been utilized for their astringent properties in various herbal remedies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental features and ecological significance in its native habitats.
