Serjania allenii

Serjania allenii, commonly known as the Allen's serjania, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaneae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Serjania allenii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for various wildlife species.
