Scutellaria nigrocardia

Scutellaria nigrocardia, commonly known as blackheart skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Scutellaria nigrocardia is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
