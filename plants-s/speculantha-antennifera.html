Speculantha antennifera

Speculantha antennifera, commonly known as the feathered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and its stems are often covered in fine, hair-like structures.

Speculantha antennifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
