Sagina nodosa var. borealis

Sagina nodosa var. borealis, commonly known as the northern sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense mats of small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces tiny, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in tundra or alpine environments.

Its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh conditions make it an important component of boreal and Arctic ecosystems.
