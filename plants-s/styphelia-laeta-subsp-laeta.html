Styphelia laeta subsp. laeta

Styphelia laeta subsp.

laeta, commonly known as the slender snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the cool, moist forests of eastern Australia. It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate beauty to its foliage.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in shaded, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas. Styphelia laeta subsp.

laeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable garden conditions.
