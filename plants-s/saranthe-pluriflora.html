Saranthe pluriflora

Saranthe pluriflora, commonly known as the many-flowered saranthe, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lush, broad leaves and clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions. Saranthe pluriflora typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
