Saussurea triptera var. hisauchii

Saussurea triptera var. hisauchii, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has fibrous roots and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments with well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
