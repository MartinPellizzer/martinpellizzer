Silene viscosa f. multifida

Silene viscosa f. multifida, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
