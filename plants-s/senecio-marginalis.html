Senecio marginalis

Senecio marginalis, commonly known as the Marginal Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Senecio marginalis is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
