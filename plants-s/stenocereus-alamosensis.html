Stenocereus alamosensis

Stenocereus alamosensis, commonly known as the Alamos hedgehog cactus, is a flowering cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its structure provides habitat for various desert animals, and it plays a role in local ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
