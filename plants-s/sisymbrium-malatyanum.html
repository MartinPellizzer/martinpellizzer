Sisymbrium malatyanum

Sisymbrium malatyanum, commonly known as Malatya mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and often covered in fine hairs.

Sisymbrium malatyanum is valued for its potential medicinal properties and as a source of oilseed.
