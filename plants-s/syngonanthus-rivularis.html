Syngonanthus rivularis

Syngonanthus rivularis, commonly known as the riverbank lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist woodland areas, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Syngonanthus rivularis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
