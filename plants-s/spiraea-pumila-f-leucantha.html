Spiraea pumila f. leucantha

Spiraea pumila f. leucantha, commonly known as the whiteform weeping spiraea, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The foliage is typically dark green, with a finely textured appearance that adds visual interest. This form is a naturally occurring variation of Spiraea pumila, characterized by its pale, almost white floral display.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
