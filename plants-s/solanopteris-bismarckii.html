Solanopteris bismarckii

Solanopteris bismarckii, commonly known as the Bismarck solanopteris, is a rare fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
