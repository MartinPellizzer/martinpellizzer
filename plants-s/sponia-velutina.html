Sponia velutina

Sponia velutina, commonly known as the velvet spongeplant, is a unique species within the genus Sponia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety texture and dense, spongy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are often clustered at the base, forming a rosette shape, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Sponia velutina is notable for its ability to retain moisture, making it well adapted to humid climates.
