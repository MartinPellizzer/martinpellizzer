Spiraea cavanillesii

Spiraea cavanillesii, commonly known as the golden spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has elliptic to ovate leaves that are dark green and finely toothed. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and graceful form.
