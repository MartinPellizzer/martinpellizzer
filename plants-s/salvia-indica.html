Salvia indica

Salvia indica, commonly known as blue sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and aromatic, gray-green leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other well-known herbs like mint and basil. Salvia indica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
