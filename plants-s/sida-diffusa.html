Sida diffusa

Sida diffusa, commonly known as seaside mallow, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in sandy or saline soils. The plant features hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Sida diffusa is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, coastal environments due to its salt tolerance.
