Sasa yahikoensis var. mogamensis

Sasa yahikoensis var. mogamensis, commonly known as the Mogami bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa yahikoensis species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often a deep green color. Its culms are slender and exhibit a distinctive pale coloration, especially when young.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as part of traditional Japanese landscapes.
