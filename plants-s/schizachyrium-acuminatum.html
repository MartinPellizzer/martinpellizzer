Schizachyrium acuminatum

Schizachyrium acuminatum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass species that thrives in prairie and savanna habitats.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and distinctive, hairless, spike-like inflorescence. The plant is drought-tolerant and plays a vital role in soil stabilization and wildlife habitat. It blooms in late summer with small, spikelet-like flowers that are often inconspicuous.

Little bluestem is a key component of native grassland ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
