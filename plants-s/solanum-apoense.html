Solanum apoense

Solanum apoense, commonly known as the Philippine nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom at night. The plant produces small, round berries that are usually green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
