Scorzonera humifusa

Scorzonera humifusa, commonly known as the creeping scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish-brown in color. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scorzonera humifusa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
