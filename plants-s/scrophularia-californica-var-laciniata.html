Scrophularia californica var. laciniata

Scrophularia californica var. laciniata, commonly known as the California figwort, is a perennial herb native to coastal areas of California.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
