Stachys arachnoidea

Stachys arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-like stachys, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, creeping stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in native and decorative plants.
