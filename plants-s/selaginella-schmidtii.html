Selaginella schmidtii

Selaginella schmidtii, commonly known as the Schmidt's spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile.

Selaginella schmidtii is known for its ability to survive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and adaptive species.
