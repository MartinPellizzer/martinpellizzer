Solanum indicum var. recurvatum

Solanum indicum var. recurvatum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade or wild tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. Its fruit is a berry that changes color from green to yellow or red as it matures.

The plant contains various alkaloids and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
