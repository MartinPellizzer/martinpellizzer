Senecio verticillatus var. trichophorus

Senecio verticillatus var. trichophorus, commonly known as the curly dock, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green, wavy-edged leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its unique foliage.
