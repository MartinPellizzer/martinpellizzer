Saccharum bataviense

Saccharum bataviense, commonly known as the Batavian sugarcane.

It is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and high sugar content, making it valuable for commercial sugarcane production. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a prominent midrib, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

The plant plays an important role in both agricultural and ecological systems in its native habitat.
