Statice diegoi

Statice diegoi, commonly known as Diego's sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the summer months. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well adapted to sandy and coastal soils.

Statice diegoi is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in full sun.
