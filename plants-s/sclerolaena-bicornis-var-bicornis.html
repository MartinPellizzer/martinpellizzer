Sclerolaena bicornis var. bicornis

Sclerolaena bicornis var. bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned sclerolaena, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, two-lobed seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
