Saussurea alatipes

Saussurea alatipes, commonly known as the winged safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant has feathery, branched leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in arid environments. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Saussurea alatipes thrives in high-altitude areas and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
