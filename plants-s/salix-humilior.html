Salix humilior

Salix humilior, commonly known as the low willow, is a small shrub belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its flowers are catkins, which are grouped in clusters and appear in early spring.

Salix humilior plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
