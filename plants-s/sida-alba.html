Sida alba

Sida alba, commonly known as silverleaf cotton or white mallow, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-white fuzzy leaves and small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. Sida alba is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used in traditional medicine and for textile purposes.
