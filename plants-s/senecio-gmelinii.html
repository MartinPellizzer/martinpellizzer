Senecio gmelinii

Senecio gmelinii, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Senecio gmelinii plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert environments and provides nectar for pollinators.
