Schinus pampeana

Schinus pampeana, commonly known as the pampas rose or copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina, Chile, and Uruguay, where it thrives in dry, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that give way to red, berry-like fruits.

The tree is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
