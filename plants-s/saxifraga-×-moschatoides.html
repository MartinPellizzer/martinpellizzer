Saxifraga × moschatoides

Saxifraga × moschatoides, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirculus, which gives it unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. It produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
