Stylophorum lactucoides

Stylophorum lactucoides, commonly known as the common cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a mild, slightly bitter taste.

The flowers bloom in early spring, producing yellow disc florets surrounded by white ray florets.
