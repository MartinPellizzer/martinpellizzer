Salix hallaisanensis f. elongata

Salix hallaisanensis f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated weeping willow, is a unique variant of the willow genus native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, thriving in humid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery-green underside that adds to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscape design for its graceful form.
