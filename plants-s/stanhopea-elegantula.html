Stanhopea elegantula

Stanhopea elegantula, commonly known as the elegant stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in a spiral arrangement along the stem. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often covered in a waxy coating to retain moisture.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
