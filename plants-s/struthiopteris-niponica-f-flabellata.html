Struthiopteris niponica f. flabellata

Struthiopteris niponica f. flabellata, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a variant of the Japanese shield fern, characterized by its distinctive frond morphology.

It is a fern species native to Japan, typically found in shaded, moist forest environments. The fronds are large, leathery, and have a unique, shield-like shape with a prominent midrib. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that help in vegetative propagation.

Its foliage is often used in traditional Japanese gardens for its ornamental value and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
