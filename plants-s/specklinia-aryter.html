Specklinia aryter

Specklinia aryter, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with distinctive silver veins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually small and pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance.

Specklinia aryter is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
