Seymeria virgata

Seymeria virgata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Seymeria virgata produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
