Syzygium klampok

Syzygium klampok, commonly known as the Klam pok or Klamboek, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Syzygium klampok is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
