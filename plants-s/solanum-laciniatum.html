Solanum laciniatum

Solanum laciniatum, commonly known as the scarlet sage, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically red or orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Solanum laciniatum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
