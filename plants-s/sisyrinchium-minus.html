Sisyrinchium minus

Sisyrinchium minus, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

Sisyrinchium minus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
