Saussurea glauca

Saussurea glauca, commonly known as blue saffron or blue gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Saussurea glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
