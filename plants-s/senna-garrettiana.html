Senna garrettiana

Senna garrettiana, commonly known as the golden senna, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking addition to gardens. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several seeds.

Senna garrettiana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental beauty.
