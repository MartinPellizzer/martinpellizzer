Saussurea oxyodonta

Saussurea oxyodonta, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other mountainous regions in Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to cold and arid environments, with thick, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The botanical features of Saussurea oxyodonta make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
