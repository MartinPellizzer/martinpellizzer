Sida vagans

Sida vagans, commonly known as the wandering sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by triangular, three-lobed seed pods. Sida vagans thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in many parts of the world.
