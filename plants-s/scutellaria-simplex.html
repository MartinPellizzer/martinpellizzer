Scutellaria simplex

Scutellaria simplex, commonly known as blue skullcap, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Scutellaria simplex is often found in woodland edges and wetlands, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
