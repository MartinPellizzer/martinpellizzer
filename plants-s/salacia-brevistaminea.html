Salacia brevistaminea

Salacia brevistaminea, commonly known as the red salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its striking red flowers and woody stems, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

Salacia brevistaminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
