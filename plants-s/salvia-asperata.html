Salvia asperata

Salvia asperata, commonly known as rough sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Salvia asperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
