Saccolabium guttatum

Saccolabium guttatum, commonly known as the spotted bushveld legume, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in small, glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
