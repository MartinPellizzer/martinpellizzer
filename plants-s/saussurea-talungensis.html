Saussurea talungensis

Saussurea talungensis, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in Tibet and Nepal.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh mountainous climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive, flattened shape.

Saussurea talungensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
