Senecio formosus var. subruncinatus

Senecio formosus var. subruncinatus, commonly known as the Orange-Flowered Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or grayish down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
