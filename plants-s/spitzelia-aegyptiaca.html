Spitzelia aegyptiaca

Spitzelia aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian spitzelia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Spitzelia aegyptiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
