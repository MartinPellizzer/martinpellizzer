Serratula albescens

Serratula albescens, commonly known as white serrated centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Serratula albescens is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
