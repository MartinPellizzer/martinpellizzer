Serapias parviflora

Serapias parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Serapias parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
