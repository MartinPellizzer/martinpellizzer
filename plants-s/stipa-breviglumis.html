Stipa breviglumis

Stipa breviglumis, commonly known as the short-bracted feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green leaves. The plant produces fluffy, branched inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
