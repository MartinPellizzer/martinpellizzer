Spiesia rosea

Spiesia rosea, commonly known as the rosewood tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and beauty. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Spiesia rosea is often cultivated for both its timber and its aesthetic qualities in horticulture.
