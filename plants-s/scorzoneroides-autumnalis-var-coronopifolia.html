Scorzoneroides autumnalis var. coronopifolia

Scorzoneroides autumnalis var. coronopifolia, commonly known as the autumnal scorzoneroid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowering stems bear small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive foliage, making it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
