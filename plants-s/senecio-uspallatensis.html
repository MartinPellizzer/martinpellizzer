Senecio uspallatensis

Senecio uspallatensis, commonly known as the Uspallata senecio, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in the Andes region of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to high-altitude conditions. Senecio uspallatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
