Schedonorus poiformis

Schedonorus poiformis, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, particularly when viewed in sunlight. The plant typically grows in clumps, forming dense mats that can spread slowly through rhizomes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, with a soft texture that distinguishes it from other grasses.

Schedonorus poiformis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
