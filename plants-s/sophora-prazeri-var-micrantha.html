Sophora prazeri var. micrantha

Sophora prazeri var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, appearing in spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
