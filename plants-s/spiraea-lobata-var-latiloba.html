Spiraea lobata var. latiloba

Spiraea lobata var. latiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and they turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
