Setaria glauca var. nodiflora

Setaria glauca var. nodiflora, commonly known as blue foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with blue or purple hues. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and common weed in agricultural settings.
