Saussurea taquetii

Saussurea taquetii, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron or the purple saffron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of purple to pinkish flowers. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Tibetan medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
