Sorghum orientale

Sorghum orientale, commonly known as the oriental sorghum, is a tall, robust grass native to southern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant is characterized by its sturdy stalks and is often cultivated for its grain, which is used in food and animal feed. It thrives in warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid regions.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a complex inflorescence that supports its reproductive cycle.
