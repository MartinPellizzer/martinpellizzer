Sesbania goetzei

Sesbania goetzei, commonly known as the velvetleaf sesbania, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Sesbania goetzei also serves as a forage crop and has ecological significance in soil enrichment.
