Solanum dulcamara f. hirsutum

Solanum dulcamara f. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a variant of the common bindweed.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, disturbed soils.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
