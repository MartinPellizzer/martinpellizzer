Sinapis campestris

Sinapis campestris, commonly known as field mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually pinnately lobed and its yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds. It is native to Europe and has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

Sinapis campestris is often found in disturbed soils and is commonly used as a green manure or in crop rotation systems.
