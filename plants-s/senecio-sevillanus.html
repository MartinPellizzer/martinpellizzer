Senecio sevillanus

Senecio sevillanus, commonly known as the Seville groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
