Sarothamnus scoparius var. leiostylos

Sarothamnus scoparius var. leiostylos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thymebrush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

The variety leiostylos is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Sarothamnus scoparius.
