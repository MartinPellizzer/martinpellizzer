Symplocos kwangtungensis

Symplocos kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong holly, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Symplocos kwangtungensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
