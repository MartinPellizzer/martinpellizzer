Symphyotrichum lucayanum

Symphyotrichum lucayanum, commonly known as the Florida aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and marshes. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
