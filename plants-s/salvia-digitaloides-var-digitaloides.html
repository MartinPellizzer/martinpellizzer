Salvia digitaloides var. digitaloides

Salvia digitaloides var. digitaloides, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional remedies.
