Sphaerocionium crispum

Sphaerocionium crispum, commonly known as the curlyleaf waterweed, is a aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often curled or twisted.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has a branching stem system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or white in color. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of mucilage, which helps it retain moisture and protect against herbivores.

Sphaerocionium crispum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water clarity through its dense growth.
