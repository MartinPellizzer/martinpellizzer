Saussurea quercifolia

Saussurea quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of an oak tree. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing at high elevations with poor, rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
