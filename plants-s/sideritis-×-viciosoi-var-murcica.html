Sideritis × viciosoi var. murcica

Sideritis × viciosoi var. murcica, commonly known as the Murcian ironweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
