Schotia romii

Schotia romii, commonly known as the silver tree, is a unique evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-blue leaves, which are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Schotia romii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and hardy.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
