Scabiosa ochroleuca subsp. rhodopea

Scabiosa ochroleuca subsp.

rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
