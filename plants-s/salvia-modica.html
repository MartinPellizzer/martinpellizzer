Salvia modica

Salvia modica, commonly known as the dwarf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, arranged in dense clusters.

Salvia modica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
