Semnostachya alboviridis

Semnostachya alboviridis, commonly known as the white-veined grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and distinctive white veins on its leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in panicle-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
