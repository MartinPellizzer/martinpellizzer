Stapelia fuscata

Stapelia fuscata, commonly known as the brown starfish plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers with a strong, unpleasant odor, which attracts pollinators like flies. Its leaves are usually reduced to small spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with bright, indirect light.

Stapelia fuscata is a low-maintenance plant that is popular among succulent enthusiasts.
