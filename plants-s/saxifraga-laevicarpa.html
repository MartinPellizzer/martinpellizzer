Saxifraga laevicarpa

Saxifraga laevicarpa, commonly known as the smooth-fruited saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. The plant features basal leaves that are typically rounded or ovate, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late summer.

Saxifraga laevicarpa is characterized by its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
