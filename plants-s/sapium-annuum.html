Sapium annuum

Sapium annuum, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots can form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various parts of the world.
