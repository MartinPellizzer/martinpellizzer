Sasaella sasakiana

Sasaella sasakiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Sasakawa bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, Korea, and China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant forms dense clumps of slender, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasaella sasakiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
