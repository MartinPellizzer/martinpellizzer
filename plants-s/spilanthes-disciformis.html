Spilanthes disciformis

Spilanthes disciformis, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that are often clustered in a disc-like arrangement. Its leaves are typically basal, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Spilanthes disciformis is known for its medicinal properties, with some species in the genus being used traditionally for their potential health benefits.
