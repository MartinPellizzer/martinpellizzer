Solanum taitense

Solanum taitense, commonly known as the Taiti nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the island of Taiti in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that change color from green to red as they mature. Solanum taitense is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
