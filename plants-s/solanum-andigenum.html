Solanum andigenum

Solanum andigenum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or purple in color. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are sometimes used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Solanum andigenum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
