Scutellaria rubicunda subsp. subvelutina

Scutellaria rubicunda subsp.

subvelutina, commonly known as the velvet skullcup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
