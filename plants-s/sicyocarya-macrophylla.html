Sicyocarya macrophylla

Sicyocarya macrophylla, commonly known as the giant sicyocarya, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its robust root system allows it to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical climates.
