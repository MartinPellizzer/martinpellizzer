Spennera hydrophila

Spennera hydrophila, commonly known as the water spennera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Spennera hydrophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in drought conditions.
