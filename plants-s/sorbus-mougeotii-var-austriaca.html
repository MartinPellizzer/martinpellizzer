Sorbus mougeotii var. austriaca

Sorbus mougeotii var. austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall.

The bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded woodland habitats.
