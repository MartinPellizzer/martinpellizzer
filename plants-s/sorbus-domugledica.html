Sorbus domugledica

Sorbus domugledica, commonly known as the Mongolian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries that ripen to a dark red or black color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture.
