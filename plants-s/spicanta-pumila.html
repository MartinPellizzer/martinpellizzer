Spicanta pumila

Spicanta pumila, commonly known as the dwarf spikerush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly rough texture.

Spicanta pumila plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
