Stipa luehmannii

Stipa luehmannii, commonly known as Luehmann's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, slender inflorescences composed of numerous spikelets, which are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Stipa luehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, feathery appearance.
