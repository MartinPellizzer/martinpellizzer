Senecio potaninii

Senecio potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

Senecio potaninii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
