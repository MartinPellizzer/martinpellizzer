Stachys melampyroides

Stachys melampyroides, commonly known as the flame violet, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to red, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Stachys melampyroides is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
