Shorea bracteata

Shorea bracteata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
