Steiroglossa humilis

Steiroglossa humilis, commonly known as the dwarf spider orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It has slender, hairy stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often clustered at the base of the plant.

Steiroglossa humilis is a rare and endangered species, with limited geographic distribution and specific ecological requirements.
