Silene reuteriana

Silene reuteriana, commonly known as the Reuter's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Silene reuteriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
