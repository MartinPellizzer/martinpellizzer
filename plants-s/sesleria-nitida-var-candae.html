Sesleria nitida var. candae

Sesleria nitida var. candae, commonly known as the slender wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing clumps.
