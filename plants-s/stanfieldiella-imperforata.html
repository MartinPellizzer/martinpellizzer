Stanfieldiella imperforata

Stanfieldiella imperforata, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique member of the Osmundaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often pale green or almost white, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are not covered by a peristome, a feature that distinguishes it from other ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations for efficient spore dispersal and survival in humid conditions.
