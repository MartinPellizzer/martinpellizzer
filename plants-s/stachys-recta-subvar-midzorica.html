Stachys recta subvar. midzorica

Stachys recta subvar. midzorica, commonly known as the Armenian betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses make it a notable species in botanical gardens and herbal practices.
