Silene nutans var. subverticillata

Silene nutans var. subverticillata, commonly known as the nodding campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
