Solanum physalifolium var. nitidibaccatum

Solanum physalifolium var. nitidibaccatum, commonly known as the glossy bladderleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and waxy in appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by inflated, bladder-like fruits that are green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
