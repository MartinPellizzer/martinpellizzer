Sideritis endressii subsp. emporitana

Sideritis endressii subsp.

emporitana, commonly known as the Emir's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
