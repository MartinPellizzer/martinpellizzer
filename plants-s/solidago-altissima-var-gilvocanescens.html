Solidago altissima var. gilvocanescens

Solidago altissima var. gilvocanescens, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a grayish-green appearance. This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in meadows and open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
