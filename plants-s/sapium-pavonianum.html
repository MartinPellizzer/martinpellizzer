Sapium pavonianum

Sapium pavonianum, commonly known as the pavonian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and often unique floral structures. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
