Spatholobus merguensis

Spatholobus merguensis, commonly known as the Madagascar wisteria, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

Spatholobus merguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
