Solanum lanceifolium var. volubile

Solanum lanceifolium var. volubile, commonly known as the climbing nightshade, is a perennial vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures, making it a common garden weed. Its berries, which turn from green to red, are toxic and should not be consumed.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas and is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
