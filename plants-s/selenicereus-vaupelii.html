Selenicereus vaupelii

Selenicereus vaupelii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a cactus belonging to the Cactaceae family, characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water. The plant is notable for its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. These flowers typically bloom only once a year, making the plant a rare and striking sight.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and low-light conditions.
