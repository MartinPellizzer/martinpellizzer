Sauropus paucifolius

Sauropus paucifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is adaptable to various soil types and can thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.
