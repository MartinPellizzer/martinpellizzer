Syzygium glenum

Syzygium glenum, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
