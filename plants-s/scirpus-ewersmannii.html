Scirpus ewersmannii

Scirpus ewersmannii, commonly known as the Ewersmann's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets at the top of the stems, which contain both male and female flowers, making it monoecious. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in aquatic habitats.

Scirpus ewersmannii is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions and plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
