Solanum goniocalyx var. caeruleum

Solanum goniocalyx var. caeruleum, commonly known as the blue-flowered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has oval, glossy leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers.
