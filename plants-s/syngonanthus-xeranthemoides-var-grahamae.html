Syngonanthus xeranthemoides var. grahamae

Syngonanthus xeranthemoides var. grahamae, commonly known as the Graham's ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, pale blue to violet flowers that resemble small, delicate stars. The plant produces showy, tubular blooms that are typically borne in clusters on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of succulent plants.
