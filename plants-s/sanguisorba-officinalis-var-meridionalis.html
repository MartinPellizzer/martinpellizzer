Sanguisorba officinalis var. meridionalis

Sanguisorba officinalis var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern sweetflag, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its erect stems and dense clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
