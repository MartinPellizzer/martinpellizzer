Seriphidium heptapotamicum

Seriphidium heptapotamicum, commonly known as the Seven Potamids Thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the family Asteraceae and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
