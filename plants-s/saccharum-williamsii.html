Saccharum williamsii

Saccharum williamsii, commonly known as wild sugarcane, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, elongated leaves. This species is often found in wetland and marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural sugar.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
