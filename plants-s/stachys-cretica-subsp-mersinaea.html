Stachys cretica subsp. mersinaea

Stachys cretica subsp.

mersinaea, commonly known as the Mersinaea betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
