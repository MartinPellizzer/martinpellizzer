Singchia

Singchia, commonly known as the "false arum," is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped.

Singchia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their striking foliage and dramatic floral structures.
