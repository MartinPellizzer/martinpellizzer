Solanum villaricense var. paraguariense

Solanum villaricense var. paraguariense, commonly known as the Paraguayan nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into rounded, red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is typically avoided for consumption but is of interest in botanical studies.
