Solanum dubium var. dolichoplocalyx

Solanum dubium var. dolichoplocalyx, commonly known as the long-fruited nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, violet flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, yellowish fruits. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a potentially harmful plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.
