Sempervivum × roseum var. fimbriatum

Sempervivum × roseum var. fimbriatum, commonly known as the Fimbriated Rosy Stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth habit and vibrant, colorful foliage that often displays shades of pink, red, and purple. The leaves are typically fimbriate, meaning they have a fringed or toothed edge, which gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This plant is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests, making it an ideal addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
