Selaginella kraussiana var. poulteri

Selaginella kraussiana var. poulteri, commonly known as the Poulter's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with erect, spirally arranged leaves that are usually dark green and have a waxy coating. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are typically borne on the upper part of the plant.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in humid, indoor environments.
