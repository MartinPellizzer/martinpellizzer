Salvia austromelissodora

Salvia austromelissodora, commonly known as the Australian honey sage, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its foliage has a subtle mint-like scent, adding to its ornamental value.
