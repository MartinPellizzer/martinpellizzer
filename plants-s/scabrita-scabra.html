Scabrita scabra

Scabrita scabra, commonly known as the rough scabious, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caprifoliaceae.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Scabrita scabra is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
