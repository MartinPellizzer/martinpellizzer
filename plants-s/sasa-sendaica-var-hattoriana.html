Sasa sendaica var. hattoriana

Sasa sendaica var. hattoriana, commonly known as the Hattori's bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa sendaica species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant that belongs to the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests and is often found in the understory of temperate deciduous forests. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by triangular-shaped seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
