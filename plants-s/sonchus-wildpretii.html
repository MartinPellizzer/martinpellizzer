Sonchus wildpretii

Sonchus wildpretii, commonly known as the wild prettiest sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is a short-lived perennial that produces small, achene fruits for seed dispersal.
