Stereospermum dentatum

Stereospermum dentatum, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
