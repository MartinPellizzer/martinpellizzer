Saussurea fuboensis

Saussurea fuboensis, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its pale yellow to white composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Saussurea fuboensis is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine, particularly in Tibetan and Chinese herbal practices.
