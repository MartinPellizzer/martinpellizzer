Stipa geniculata

Stipa geniculata, commonly known as the bentgrass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Stipa geniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
