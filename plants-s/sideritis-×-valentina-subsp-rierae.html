Sideritis × valentina subsp. rierae

Sideritis × valentina subsp.

rierae, commonly known as the Valentina riera sainfoin, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family. It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Spain and northern Africa.

The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, typical of many members of the mint family.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
