Senega mariana

Senega mariana, commonly known as the Carolina horsebean, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped leaflet. Its pale pink to purple flowers bloom in early spring, followed by the development of hairy, legume-like pods.

Senega mariana is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
