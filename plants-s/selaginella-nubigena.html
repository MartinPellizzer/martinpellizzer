Selaginella nubigena

Selaginella nubigena, commonly known as the cloud fern, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in rocky or mountainous regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its compact, upright fronds and a unique reproductive structure called a strobilus. It is characterized by its ability to survive in nutrient-poor soils and its capacity for vegetative propagation.

Selaginella nubigena plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
