Solanum turiamoense

Solanum turiamoense, commonly known as the Turiamo nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its fruits are berries that change color from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Solanum turiamoense is adapted to humid, tropical environments and plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various animals.
