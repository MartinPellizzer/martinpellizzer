Silene incisa

Silene incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.
