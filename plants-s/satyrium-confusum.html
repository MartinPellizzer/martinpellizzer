Satyrium confusum

Satyrium confusum, commonly known as the confused satyrium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Botanically, it is notable for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
