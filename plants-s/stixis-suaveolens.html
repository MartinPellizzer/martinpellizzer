Stixis suaveolens

Stixis suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented stixis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub, preferring moist, shaded environments in tropical forests. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing properties.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
