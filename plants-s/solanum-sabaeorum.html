Solanum sabaeorum

Solanum sabaeorum, commonly known as the Socotra starfruit, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to withstand drought conditions. Its fruits, which are small and yellow, are edible and have a tangy flavor.

Solanum sabaeorum is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
