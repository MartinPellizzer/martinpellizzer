Strobilanthes sexennis

Strobilanthes sexennis, commonly known as the "six-winged flame flower," is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange bracts that resemble delicate, winged petals, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a sought-after species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
