Salix burjatica

Salix burjatica, commonly known as the Central Asian willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along rivers and lakeshores. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, which are typical of willow species.

Salix burjatica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
