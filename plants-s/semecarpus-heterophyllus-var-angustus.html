Semecarpus heterophyllus var. angustus

Semecarpus heterophyllus var. angustus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Indian chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptic or ovate leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and paler beneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
