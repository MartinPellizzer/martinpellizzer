Senecio gunnisii

Senecio gunnisii, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Senecio gunnisii produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
