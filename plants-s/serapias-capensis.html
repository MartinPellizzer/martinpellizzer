Serapias capensis

Serapias capensis, commonly known as the Cape serapias, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the genus Serapias in the family Orchidaceae, which includes various orchid-like species. The plant features showy, brightly colored flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typically in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in open grasslands and fynbos habitats.

Serapias capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
