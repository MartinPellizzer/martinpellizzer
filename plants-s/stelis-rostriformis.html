Stelis rostriformis

Stelis rostriformis, commonly known as the rostriform orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated flower structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that resembles a beak.

Stelis rostriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
