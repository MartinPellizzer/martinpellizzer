Sisymbrium toxophyllum

Sisymbrium toxophyllum, commonly known as the poison rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, containing compounds that can cause severe illness if ingested.
