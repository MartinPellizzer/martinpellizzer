Solanum kaitisis

Solanum kaitisis, commonly known as the Kaitis Island nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Kaitis Islands in the Pacific.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant produces small, purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive trumpet-shaped corolla.
