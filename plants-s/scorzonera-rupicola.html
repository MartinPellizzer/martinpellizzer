Scorzonera rupicola

Scorzonera rupicola, commonly known as the rock scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. Its roots are tuberous and can be edible, though they are not widely cultivated.

Scorzonera rupicola thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.
