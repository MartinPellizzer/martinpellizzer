Senecio purtschelleri

Senecio purtschelleri, commonly known as the Purtzeller's groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features upright stems with narrow, silvery-green leaves that have a fuzzy texture. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Senecio purtschelleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.
