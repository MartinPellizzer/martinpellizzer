Stachys germanica var. penicillata

Stachys germanica var. penicillata, commonly known as German hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
