Schlumbergera truncata subsp. kautskyi

Schlumbergera truncata subsp.

kautskyi, commonly known as the Kautsky holiday cactus, is a species native to Brazil. It is a type of epiphytic cactus characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that store water.

The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its long blooming period and ease of care.
