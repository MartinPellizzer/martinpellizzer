Schefflera clavigera

Schefflera clavigera, commonly known as the broadleaf schefflera, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like arrangement of large, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded gardens.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes due to its attractive, evergreen appearance.
