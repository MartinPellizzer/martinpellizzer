Senecio repollensis

Senecio repollensis, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Senecio repollensis is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
