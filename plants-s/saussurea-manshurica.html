Saussurea manshurica

Saussurea manshurica, commonly known as the Korean yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features a robust root system and tall, flowering stalks that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called corymbs. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with a fine layer of hairs.

Saussurea manshurica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese and Korean medicine.
