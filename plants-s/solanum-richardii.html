Solanum richardii

Solanum richardii, commonly known as Richard's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow berries that ripen in the fall. Solanum richardii is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
