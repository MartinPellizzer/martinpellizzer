Senna silvestris var. unifaria

Senna silvestris var. unifaria, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
