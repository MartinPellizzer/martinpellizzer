Senecio cerberoanus var. lanatus

Senecio cerberoanus var. lanatus, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant.
