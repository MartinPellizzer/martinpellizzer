Solanum dulcamara lusus atroviolaceum

Solanum dulcamara lusus atroviolaceum, commonly known as the black nightshade variant, is a variant of the common bindweed, characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and foliage.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants known for their toxic properties. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded areas and is often considered an invasive species in certain regions. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Despite its toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
