Senecio stuebelii

Senecio stuebelii, commonly known as Stuebel's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
