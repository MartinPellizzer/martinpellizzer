Styphelia serratifolia

Styphelia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in heathland and coastal environments. The plant produces white, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, giving it a delicate appearance. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its common name.

Styphelia serratifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
