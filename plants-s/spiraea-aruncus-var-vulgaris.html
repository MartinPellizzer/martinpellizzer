Spiraea aruncus var. vulgaris

Spiraea aruncus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the goat willow or meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, with a serrated edge. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland edges or stream banks.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in partial to full shade.
