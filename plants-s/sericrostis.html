Sericrostis

Sericrostis, commonly known as the "golden spider orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a distinctive yellow and green coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly specialized, often relying on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Sericrostis is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
