Symphyotrichum falcatum var. falcatum

Symphyotrichum falcatum var. falcatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, lavender to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
