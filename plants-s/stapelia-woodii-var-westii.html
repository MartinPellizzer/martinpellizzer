Stapelia woodii var. westii

Stapelia woodii var. westii, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
