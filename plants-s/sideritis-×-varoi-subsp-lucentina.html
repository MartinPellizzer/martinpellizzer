Sideritis × varoi subsp. lucentina

Sideritis × varoi subsp.

lucentina, commonly known as the Lucentina ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy fruits.

This subspecies is noted for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
