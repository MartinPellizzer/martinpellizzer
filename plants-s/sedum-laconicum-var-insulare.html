Sedum laconicum var. insulare

Sedum laconicum var. insulare, commonly known as the island stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, hardy plants.
