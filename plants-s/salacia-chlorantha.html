Salacia chlorantha

Salacia chlorantha, commonly known as the green salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and elongated, narrow leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Salacia chlorantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
