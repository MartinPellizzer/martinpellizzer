Sabicea stenantha

Sabicea stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sabicea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Sabicea stenantha is often found in moist, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
