Scleranthus glomerifer

Scleranthus glomerifer, commonly known as the clustered sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are narrow and hairy. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Scleranthus glomerifer is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
