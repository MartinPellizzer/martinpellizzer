Sabicea neglecta

Sabicea neglecta, commonly known as the small-leaved senna, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes. Sabicea neglecta is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, making it a common understory plant in tropical forests.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
