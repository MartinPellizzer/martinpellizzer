Sideritis incana var. atlantica

Sideritis incana var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Sideritis incana var. atlantica is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.
