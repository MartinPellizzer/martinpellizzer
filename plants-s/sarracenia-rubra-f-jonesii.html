Sarracenia rubra f. jonesii

Sarracenia rubra f. jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' red pitcher plant, is a striking carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vivid red, tubular leaves that function as pitfall traps to capture insects. The plant's pitchers are often adorned with fine, hair-like structures that aid in trapping prey. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor wetlands, requiring consistent moisture and full sun exposure.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in carnivorous plants.
