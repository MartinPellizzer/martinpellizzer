Senecio pleistophyllus

Senecio pleistophyllus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features sturdy, upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio pleistophyllus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
