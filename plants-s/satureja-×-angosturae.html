Satureja × angosturae

Satureja × angosturae, commonly known as the hybrid savory, is a perennial herb native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant features square, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, four-lobed fruits.

Satureja × angosturae is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
