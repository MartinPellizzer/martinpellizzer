Sempervivum meyerheimii

Sempervivum meyerheimii, commonly known as the Meyerheim's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with red or purple markings. The plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, storing water in its thick foliage. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months.

Sempervivum meyerheimii is a hardy, low-maintenance plant suitable for rock gardens and containers.
