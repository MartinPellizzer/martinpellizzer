Sorghum distachyon

Sorghum distachyon, commonly known as green foxtail, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched inflorescence and small, greenish flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides due to its adaptability. It has a fibrous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Sorghum distachyon is sometimes used in ecological studies due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
