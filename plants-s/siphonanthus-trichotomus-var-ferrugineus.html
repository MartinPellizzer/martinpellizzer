Siphonanthus trichotomus var. ferrugineus

Siphonanthus trichotomus var. ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty siphonanthus, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a reddish or rusty tinge. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
