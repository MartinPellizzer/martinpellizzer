Sorbus melanocarpa

Sorbus melanocarpa, commonly known as black mountain ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its bark is typically grayish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
