Solidago hintoniorum

Solidago hintoniorum, commonly known as Hinton's goldenrod, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hinton's goldenrod plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the autumn season.
