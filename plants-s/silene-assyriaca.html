Silene assyriaca

Silene assyriaca, commonly known as Assyrian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. Silene assyriaca is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers, making it a distinctive species in its native habitat.

It is also sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
