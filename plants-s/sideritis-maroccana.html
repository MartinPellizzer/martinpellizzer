Sideritis maroccana

Sideritis maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Sideritis maroccana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
