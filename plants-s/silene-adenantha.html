Silene adenantha

Silene adenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a fibrous root system.

Silene adenantha is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
