Solidago albopilosa

Solidago albopilosa, commonly known as white pilose goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The showy, yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
