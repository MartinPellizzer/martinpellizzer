Symphyotrichum cordifolium var. racemiflorum

Symphyotrichum cordifolium var. racemiflorum, commonly known as the showy golden-rod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its showy blooms make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
