Stachys arguta

Stachys arguta, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and antispasmodic properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
