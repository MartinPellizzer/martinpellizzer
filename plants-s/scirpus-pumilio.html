Scirpus pumilio

Scirpus pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf bulrush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to reddish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts at the base.

Scirpus pumilio plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
