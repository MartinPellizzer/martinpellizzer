Solanum juzepczukii var. parco

Solanum juzepczukii var. parco, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
