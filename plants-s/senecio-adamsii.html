Senecio adamsii

Senecio adamsii, commonly known as the Adams' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio adamsii is often found in open habitats like grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
