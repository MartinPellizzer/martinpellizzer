Senecio valeriana

Senecio valeriana, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, white to grayish hairs. The plant produces striking, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a central yellow disk, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Senecio valeriana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
