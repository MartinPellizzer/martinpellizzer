Saussurea leucantha

Saussurea leucantha, commonly known as the white safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, achene-like fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
