Sarcomphalus reticulatus

Sarcomphalus reticulatus, commonly known as the nettleleaf or nettleleaf daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with stolons that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Sarcomphalus reticulatus is often found in temperate regions of North America and is known for its ecological value to pollinators.
