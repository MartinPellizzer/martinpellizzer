Stelis hammelii

Stelis hammelii, commonly known as the Hawaiian stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic orchid, typically found growing on tree trunks or branches in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has slender, often clustered stems and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Stelis hammelii is native to Hawaii and is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
