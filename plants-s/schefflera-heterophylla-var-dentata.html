Schefflera heterophylla var. dentata

Schefflera heterophylla var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically palmately arranged with serrated margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
