Silene cucubalus var. glareosa

Silene cucubalus var. glareosa, commonly known as the showy catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
