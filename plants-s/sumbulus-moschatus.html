Sumbulus moschatus

Sumbulus moschatus, commonly known as the musk cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant fruits that resemble small cucumbers and emit a strong, musky aroma. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is typically harvested when it is still green and unripe, though it can be eaten when mature.

The plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses in traditional practices.
