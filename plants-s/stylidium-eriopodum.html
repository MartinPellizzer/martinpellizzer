Stylidium eriopodum

Stylidium eriopodum, commonly known as the hairy sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
