Silene gallica

Silene gallica, commonly known as the Bladder Campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and its distinctive bladder-like seed pods. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils. Silene gallica is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in temperate climates.

Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
