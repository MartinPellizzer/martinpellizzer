Salvia cypria

Salvia cypria, commonly known as Cyprus sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing spikes of violet to purple flowers.

Salvia cypria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
