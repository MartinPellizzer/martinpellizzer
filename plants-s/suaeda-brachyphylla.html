Suaeda brachyphylla

Suaeda brachyphylla, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh conditions. The plant produces small yellow flowers, typically in clusters, which are adapted for wind pollination. Suaeda brachyphylla thrives in coastal areas and saline soils, making it an important component of salt marsh ecosystems.
