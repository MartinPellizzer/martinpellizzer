Sorbus torminalis f. stenoloba

Sorbus torminalis f. stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitebeam, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, spherical berries that are attractive to birds. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often found in woodland areas with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
