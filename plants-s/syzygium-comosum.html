Syzygium comosum

Syzygium comosum, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit covering, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and glossy, while the fruit is typically hairy and varies in color from red to yellow.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and minerals.
