Senecio subruncinatus

Senecio subruncinatus, commonly known as the blue ice plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and produces striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
