Stylidium chinense

Stylidium chinense, commonly known as the Chinese triggerplant, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spurred petals that resemble a trigger mechanism, which is used to attract specific pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Stylidium chinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
