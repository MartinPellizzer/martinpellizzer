Sempervivum × hayekii

Sempervivum × hayekii, commonly known as the hybrid houseleek, is a succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Sempervivum arachnoideum and Sempervivum tectorum, characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green or bluish-green, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

Sempervivum × hayekii is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
