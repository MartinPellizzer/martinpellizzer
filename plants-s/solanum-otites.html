Solanum otites

Solanum otites, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum otites belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
