Spiranthes lindleyana

Spiranthes lindleyana, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraling inflorescence of pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like and form a dense rosette at the base of the plant.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
