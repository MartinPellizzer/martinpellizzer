Sesbania sesban var. bicolor

Sesbania sesban var. bicolor, commonly known as the twin-color sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored leaves, which typically feature a green base and a red or purple upper surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

Additionally, it is used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
