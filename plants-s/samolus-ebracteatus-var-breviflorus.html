Samolus ebracteatus var. breviflorus

Samolus ebracteatus var. breviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered samolus, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland undergrowth.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological studies in its native habitat.
