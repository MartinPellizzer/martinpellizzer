Syngonanthus gracilis var. aureus

Syngonanthus gracilis var. aureus, commonly known as the golden threadleaf ironwood, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
