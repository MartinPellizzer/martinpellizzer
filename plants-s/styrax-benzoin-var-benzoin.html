Styrax benzoin var. benzoin

Styrax benzoin var. benzoin, commonly known as benzoin, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces a resinous gum, known as benzoin, which is exuded from the bark and twigs. This resin has been historically used in incense, perfumes, and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmaceutical applications.
