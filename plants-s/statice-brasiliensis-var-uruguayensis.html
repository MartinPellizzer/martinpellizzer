Statice brasiliensis var. uruguayensis

Statice brasiliensis var. uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay statice, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically produces dense clusters of small, showy blooms in shades of pink, red, and purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
