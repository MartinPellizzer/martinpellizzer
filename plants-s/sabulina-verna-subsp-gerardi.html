Sabulina verna subsp. gerardi

Sabulina verna subsp.

gerardi, commonly known as the spring sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in open, sun-exposed areas such as disturbed sites or along roadsides.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing in a rosette at the base of the plant.

The species is characterized by its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
