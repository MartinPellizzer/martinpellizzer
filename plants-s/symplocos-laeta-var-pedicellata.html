Symplocos laeta var. pedicellata

Symplocos laeta var. pedicellata, commonly known as the fragrant wax tree, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, waxy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
