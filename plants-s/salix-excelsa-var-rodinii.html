Salix excelsa var. rodinii

Salix excelsa var. rodinii, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that drape like curtains, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly planted in gardens and along water features.
