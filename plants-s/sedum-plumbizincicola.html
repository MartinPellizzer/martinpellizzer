Sedum plumbizincicola

Sedum plumbizincicola, commonly known as the zinc sedum, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. It is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
