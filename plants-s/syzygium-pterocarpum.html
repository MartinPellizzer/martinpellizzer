Syzygium pterocarpum

Syzygium pterocarpum, commonly known as the African cherry or red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a distinctive red, hairy drupe that contains a single seed, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties. The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
