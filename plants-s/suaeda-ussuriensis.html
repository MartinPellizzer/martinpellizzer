Suaeda ussuriensis

Suaeda ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to the coastal and saline environments of eastern Asia.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in areas with high salt content in the soil. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain water in harsh conditions. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Suaeda ussuriensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
