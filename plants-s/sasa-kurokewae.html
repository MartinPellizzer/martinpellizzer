Sasa kurokewae

Sasa kurokewae, commonly known as black bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dark, almost black-colored stems. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, forming thick mats that can reach several feet in height. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sasa kurokewae is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
