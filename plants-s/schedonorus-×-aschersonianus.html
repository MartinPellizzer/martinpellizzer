Schedonorus × aschersonianus

Schedonorus × aschersonianus, commonly known as the fine fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, upright blades and dense growth habit, making it suitable for low-maintenance lawns and turfgrass applications. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and has a clump-forming growth pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Botanically, it is a hybrid species within the genus Schedonorus, belonging to the family Poaceae.
