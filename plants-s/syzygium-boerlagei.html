Syzygium boerlagei

Syzygium boerlagei, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, particularly the KwaZulu-Natal province, where it thrives in subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round berries that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

Syzygium boerlagei is often found in coastal forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
