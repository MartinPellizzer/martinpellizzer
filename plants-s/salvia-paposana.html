Salvia paposana

Salvia paposana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Salvia paposana is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
