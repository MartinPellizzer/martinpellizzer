Smithia kotschyi

Smithia kotschyi, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapting well to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Smithia kotschyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
