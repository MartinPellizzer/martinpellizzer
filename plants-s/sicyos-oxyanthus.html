Sicyos oxyanthus

Sicyos oxyanthus, commonly known as the giant horsebean vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow or orange flowers. Its vigorous growth habit makes it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

However, it can become invasive if not properly managed.
