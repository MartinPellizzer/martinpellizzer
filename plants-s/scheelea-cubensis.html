Scheelea cubensis

Scheelea cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban horse tail, is a perennial grass native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Scheelea cubensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
