Senecio drepanophyllus

Senecio drepanophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish or grayish hue, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senecio drepanophyllus is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
