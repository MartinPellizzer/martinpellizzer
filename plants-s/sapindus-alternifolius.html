Sapindus alternifolius

Sapindus alternifolius, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, spiny fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
