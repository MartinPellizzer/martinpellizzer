Sterculia caudata

Sterculia caudata, commonly known as the longpod sterculia, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland rainforest areas.

The species is valued for its hard wood and has traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
