Scrophularia serratifolia

Scrophularia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia serratifolia is often found in rocky or clay soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
