Saxifraga virginiana

Saxifraga virginiana, commonly known as white sand saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga virginiana is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils and is often found in areas with high humidity.
