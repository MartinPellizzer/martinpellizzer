Selaginella pectinata var. curvifolia

Selaginella pectinata var. curvifolia, commonly known as the zigzag spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, spirally arranged leaves that are often curved, giving it its characteristic appearance. Its sporophyte stage produces spores in structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
