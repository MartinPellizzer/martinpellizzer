Symplocos sonoharae var. sonoharae

Symplocos sonoharae var. sonoharae, commonly known as the Sonohara's evergreen holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
