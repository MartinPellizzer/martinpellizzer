Sida philippinensis

Sida philippinensis, commonly known as Philippine mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Sida philippinensis is also known for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
