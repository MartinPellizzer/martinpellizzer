Samuelssonia verrucosa

Samuelssonia verrucosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved samuelssonia, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in small, raised bumps, giving them a textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Samuelssonia verrucosa is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops, where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil.
