Solidago ericamerioides

Solidago ericamerioides, commonly known as the eastern goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Solidago ericamerioides is an important nectar source for bees and other pollinators.
