Sapium longipes

Sapium longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed Chinese lantern, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy red bracts that resemble lanterns, which are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant in various landscapes.
