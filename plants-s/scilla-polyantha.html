Scilla polyantha

Scilla polyantha, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Scilla polyantha is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread readily in suitable conditions.
