Selaginella chiquitana

Selaginella chiquitana, commonly known as the Chiquitana spikemoss, is a low-growing, evergreen fern ally native to South America.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobila. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitats.

Selaginella chiquitana plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various invertebrates.
