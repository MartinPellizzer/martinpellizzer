Scabiosa albocincta

Scabiosa albocincta, commonly known as the white-banded scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a distinctive pinkish or reddish central disc. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the stems are often branched and upright.

Scabiosa albocincta is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
