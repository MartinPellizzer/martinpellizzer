Spiraea humboldtii

Spiraea humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has slender, arching branches covered in fine, silvery hair. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy green surface and a slightly hairy underside.

Spiraea humboldtii is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
