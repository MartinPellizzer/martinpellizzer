Saxifraga fragilis var. valentina

Saxifraga fragilis var. valentina, commonly known as Valentina's fragile saxifrage, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially in cooler weather. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
