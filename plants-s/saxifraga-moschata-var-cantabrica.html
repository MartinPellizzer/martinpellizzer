Saxifraga moschata var. cantabrica

Saxifraga moschata var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky and mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained, calcareous soils. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. It is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, including cold and drought.

This variety is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
