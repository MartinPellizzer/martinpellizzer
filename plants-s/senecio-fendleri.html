Senecio fendleri

Senecio fendleri, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs to reduce water loss.

Senecio fendleri plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as habitat for various desert wildlife.
