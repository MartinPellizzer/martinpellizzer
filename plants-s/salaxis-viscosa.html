Salaxis viscosa

Salaxis viscosa, commonly known as sticky salaxis, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that often have a sticky or waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers, typically in shades of yellow or orange. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
