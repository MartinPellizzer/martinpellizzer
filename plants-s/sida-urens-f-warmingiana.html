Sida urens f. warmingiana

Sida urens f. warmingiana, commonly known as the hairy sida, is a variant of the sida plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often covered in fine pubescence, which contributes to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas or along roadsides.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
