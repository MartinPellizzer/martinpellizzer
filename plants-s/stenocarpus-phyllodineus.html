Stenocarpus phyllodineus

Stenocarpus phyllodineus, commonly known as the broad-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest areas.

Stenocarpus phyllodineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
