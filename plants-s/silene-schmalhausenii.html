Silene schmalhausenii

Silene schmalhausenii, commonly known as the Schmalhausen's catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid climates.

Silene schmalhausenii is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in folk remedies for various ailments.
