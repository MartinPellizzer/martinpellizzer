Setaria atrata

Setaria atrata, commonly known as black dropset grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Setaria atrata is primarily valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
