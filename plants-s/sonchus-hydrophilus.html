Sonchus hydrophilus

Sonchus hydrophilus, commonly known as water lettuce, is a floating aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has dark green, waxy leaves that are broadly ovate and covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, which bloom during the warmer months. It is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in non-native habitats, outcompeting native vegetation.
