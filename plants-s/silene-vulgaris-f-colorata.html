Silene vulgaris f. colorata

Silene vulgaris f. colorata, commonly known as the redstem catchfly, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers and hairy stems.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The varietal form 'colorata' is distinguished by its more intense and vivid flower color compared to the typical form.
