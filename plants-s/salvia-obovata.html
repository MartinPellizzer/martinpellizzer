Salvia obovata

Salvia obovata, commonly known as narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces spikes of violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Salvia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
