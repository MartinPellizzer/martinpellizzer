Staurogyne glauca var. siamensis

Staurogyne glauca var. siamensis, commonly known as Siam's blue waterweed, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically a vibrant blue-green color. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic life.

It is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
