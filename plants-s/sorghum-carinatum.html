Sorghum carinatum

Sorghum carinatum, commonly known as kafir corn, is a tall, annual grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant produces hard, triangular grains that are used in traditional foods and as animal feed. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a resilient crop.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other cereal grasses.
