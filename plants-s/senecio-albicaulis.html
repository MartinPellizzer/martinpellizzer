Senecio albicaulis

Senecio albicaulis, commonly known as the white ghost plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its pale green to white, woolly foliage and its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It has a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or clumps.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
