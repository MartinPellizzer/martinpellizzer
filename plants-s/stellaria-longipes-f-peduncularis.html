Stellaria longipes f. peduncularis

Stellaria longipes f. peduncularis, commonly known as the slender starwort, is a variant of the starwort species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of diverse plant communities and its potential ecological value to pollinators.
