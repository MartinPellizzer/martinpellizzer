Siparuna verticillata

Siparuna verticillata, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces bright blue berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Siparuna verticillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
