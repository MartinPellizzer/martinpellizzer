Scrophularia pantocsekii

Scrophularia pantocsekii, commonly known as the Pantocsek's figwort, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes various species of figworts and related plants. The plant is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters.

Scrophularia pantocsekii is often found in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in open, sunny habitats.
