Salvia clandestina var. obtusata

Salvia clandestina var. obtusata, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
