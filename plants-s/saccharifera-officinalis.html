Saccharifera officinalis

Saccharifera officinalis, commonly known as the medicinal sugar plant, is a herbaceous species native to temperate regions.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, clustered white flowers. The plant is notable for its high sucrose content in its stems, which can be harvested and processed into a natural sweetener. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its historical use in traditional medicine highlights its significance in herbal practices.
