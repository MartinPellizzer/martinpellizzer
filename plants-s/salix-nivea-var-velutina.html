Salix nivea var. velutina

Salix nivea var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-white bark and slender, weeping branches that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrowly elliptic, with a soft, velvety texture on both sides, contributing to its common name. It produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
