Satyrium stolzii

Satyrium stolzii, commonly known as the Stolz's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or high-altitude grasslands. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, a feature common among epiphytic orchids.

The genus Satyrium is known for its intricate floral structures, which play a role in attracting specific pollinators.
