Spathoglottis kimballiana

Spathoglottis kimballiana, commonly known as the Kimball's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive yellow or green throat. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a cluster of showy, resupinate flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Spathoglottis kimballiana thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
