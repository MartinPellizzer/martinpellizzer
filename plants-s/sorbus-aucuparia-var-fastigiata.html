Sorbus aucuparia var. fastigiata

Sorbus aucuparia var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright rowan, is a compact, bushy shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and small, dark red berries that persist through winter. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a serrated margin. It produces yellowish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the distinctive berries.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in gardens.
