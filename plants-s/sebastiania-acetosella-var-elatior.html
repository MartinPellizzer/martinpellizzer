Sebastiania acetosella var. elatior

Sebastiania acetosella var. elatior, commonly known as the red-veined hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
