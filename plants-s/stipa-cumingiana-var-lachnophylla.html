Stipa cumingiana var. lachnophylla

Stipa cumingiana var. lachnophylla, commonly known as the hairy feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy inflorescences that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in arid conditions.
