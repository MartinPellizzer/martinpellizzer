Syzygium salignum

Syzygium salignum, commonly known as the sandpaper fig, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, sandpapery bark and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, round, brown fruits resembling figs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
