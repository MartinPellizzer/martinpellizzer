Saussurea paucijuga

Saussurea paucijuga, commonly known as the slender yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
