Sisymbrium apetalum

Sisymbrium apetalum, commonly known as the common vetch, is an annual legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. The plant produces slender, branching stems that often grow along the ground, and its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods. Sisymbrium apetalum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in agricultural settings.

It is also valued for its role in ecological restoration due to its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
