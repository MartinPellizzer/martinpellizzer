Selenicereus hondurensis

Selenicereus hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Central America.

It is a climbing plant with a succulent stem that can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers are large, white, and fragrant, opening at night and closing by morning. The plant produces yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
