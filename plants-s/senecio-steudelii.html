Senecio steudelii

Senecio steudelii, commonly known as the yellow owl's clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble owl eyes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
