Solidago alleghaniensis

Solidago alleghaniensis, commonly known as Allegheny goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer and early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Allegheny goldenrod is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
