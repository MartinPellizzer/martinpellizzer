Solanum papillosum

Solanum papillosum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, toxic berries that are typically yellow when ripe. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
