Symplocarpus renifolius

Symplocarpus renifolius, commonly known as the skunk cabbage, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive mottled purple and green spatulate leaves that resemble a skunk's tail. The plant produces a spadix, a fleshy spike of tiny flowers, enclosed within a showy, hood-like bract. It thrives in wet, shaded environments such as swamps and bogs.

Symplocarpus renifolius is notable for its ability to generate heat through thermogenesis, which helps melt through snow and attract pollinators.
