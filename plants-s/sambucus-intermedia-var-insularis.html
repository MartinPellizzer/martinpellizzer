Sambucus intermedia var. insularis

Sambucus intermedia var. insularis, commonly known as the insular elderberry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
