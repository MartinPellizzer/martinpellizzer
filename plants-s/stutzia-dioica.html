Stutzia dioica

Stutzia dioica, commonly known as the two-flowered stutzia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, or grass family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers on separate spikelets. Stutzia dioica plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
