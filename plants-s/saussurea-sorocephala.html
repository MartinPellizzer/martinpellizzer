Saussurea sorocephala

Saussurea sorocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Saussurea sorocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking floral display.
