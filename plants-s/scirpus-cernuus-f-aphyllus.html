Scirpus cernuus f. aphyllus

Scirpus cernuus f. aphyllus, commonly known as the hairless bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush characterized by its reduced foliage.

It is a perennial wetland plant that thrives in marshy and aquatic environments. The plant features a tall, slender stem that often grows in dense clumps. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This form is often observed in disturbed wetlands and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.
