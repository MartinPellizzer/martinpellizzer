Salsola flavovirens

Salsola flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor in loose, sandy soils.

Salsola flavovirens is often found in deserts, coastal regions, and salt marshes, playing a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.
