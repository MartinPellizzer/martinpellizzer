Spiranthes sancta

Spiranthes sancta, commonly known as the sacred spiraea, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it blooms in late summer to early fall.

Spiranthes sancta is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
