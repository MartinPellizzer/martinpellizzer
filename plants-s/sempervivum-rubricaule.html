Sempervivum rubricaule

Sempervivum rubricaule, commonly known as the red-leaved houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features compact rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with red or purple hues on the upper surfaces. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in well-drained soil.

Sempervivum rubricaule is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
