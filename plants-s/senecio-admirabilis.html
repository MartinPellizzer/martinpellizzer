Senecio admirabilis

Senecio admirabilis, commonly known as the Desert Star, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features a short, woody stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio admirabilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
