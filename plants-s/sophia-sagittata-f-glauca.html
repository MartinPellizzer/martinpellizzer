Sophia sagittata f. glauca

Sophia sagittata f. glauca, commonly known as the blue-flowered arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its striking blue flowers.

This plant is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan. It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The blue flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
