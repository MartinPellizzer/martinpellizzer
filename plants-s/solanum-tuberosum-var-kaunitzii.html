Solanum tuberosum var. kaunitzii

Solanum tuberosum var. kaunitzii, commonly known as the Kaunitz potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, often featuring a higher starch content and a distinct flavor profile compared to standard potato varieties. This variety originates from Central and South America, where it has been cultivated for its culinary and agricultural value. The plant produces herbaceous stems with alternate, ovate leaves and bears white or pale purple flowers.

Its tubers are typically smaller and have a longer storage life, making it a valuable crop in certain regions.
