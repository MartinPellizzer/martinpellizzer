Serratula martini

Serratula martini, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in alpine meadows.
