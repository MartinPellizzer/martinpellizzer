Sclerolobium sericeum

Sclerolobium sericeum, commonly known as silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, silvery-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

Sclerolobium sericeum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
