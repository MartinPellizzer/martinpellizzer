Sideritis incana subsp. glauca

Sideritis incana subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the glaucous rock sedge, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, sunny habitats. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties.

Sideritis species are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
