Symphytum × uplandicum f. densiflorum

Symphytum × uplandicum f. densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower comfrey, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
