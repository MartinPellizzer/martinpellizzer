Salsola parviflora

Salsola parviflora, commonly known as dwarf Russian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Mediterranean.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, succulent leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish flowers. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in dry, sandy soils and extreme temperatures. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in degraded areas due to its deep root system.

Salsola parviflora is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a resilient weed and a potential indicator of environmental stress.
