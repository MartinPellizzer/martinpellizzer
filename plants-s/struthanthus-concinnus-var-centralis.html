Struthanthus concinnus var. centralis

Struthanthus concinnus var. centralis, commonly known as the Central California struthanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has linear to lance-shaped leaves and a slender, branching stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
