Senecio launayifolius

Senecio launayifolius, commonly known as the Launay's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a grayish-blue hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Senecio launayifolius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and resilience in arid conditions.
