Scirpus fimbriatus

Scirpus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces fluffy, white or brown inflorescences that appear at the top of the stems during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive fringed edge along the margins.

Scirpus fimbriatus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
