Senecio benthamianus

Senecio benthamianus, commonly known as the Bentham's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
