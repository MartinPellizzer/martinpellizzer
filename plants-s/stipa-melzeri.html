Stipa melzeri

Stipa melzeri, commonly known as Melzer's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces inflorescences composed of narrow, spike-like clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowering structures are usually pale green to brownish.

Stipa melzeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine, upright foliage.
