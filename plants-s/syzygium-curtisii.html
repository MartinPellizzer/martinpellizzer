Syzygium curtisii

Syzygium curtisii, commonly known as the Kurris' apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe.

Syzygium curtisii is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit.
