Silene saponariifolia

Silene saponariifolia, commonly known as the soaproot silene, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woolly leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms. Silene saponariifolia is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Its common name reflects the presence of saponins in its roots, which can produce a soapy lather when crushed.
