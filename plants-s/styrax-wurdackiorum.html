Styrax wurdackiorum

Styrax wurdackiorum, commonly known as the Wurdacki styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, hard, aromatic seeds enclosed in a woody capsule. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

Styrax wurdackiorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
