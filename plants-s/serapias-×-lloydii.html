Serapias × lloydii

Serapias × lloydii, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in wet or damp habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators like bees and flies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, Serapias × lloydii is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
