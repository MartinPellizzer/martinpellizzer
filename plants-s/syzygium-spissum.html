Syzygium spissum

Syzygium spissum, commonly known as the thick-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarind but have a different flavor profile. Syzygium spissum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
