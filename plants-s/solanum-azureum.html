Solanum azureum

Solanum azureum, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and potatoes. Solanum azureum typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy blossoms that attract pollinators.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
