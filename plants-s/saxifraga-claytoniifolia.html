Saxifraga claytoniifolia

Saxifraga claytoniifolia, commonly known as the Clayton's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. The plant features dense, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, clustered in upright inflorescences, and are usually pink or white in color.

Saxifraga claytoniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
