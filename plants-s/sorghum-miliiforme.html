Sorghum miliiforme

Sorghum miliiforme, commonly known as wild millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, hard grains that are often used as a food source in some regions. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
