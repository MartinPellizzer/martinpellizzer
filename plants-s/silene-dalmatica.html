Silene dalmatica

Silene dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its stems are usually hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene dalmatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
