Schizoglossum barberae

Schizoglossum barberae, commonly known as the Mexican feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring.

Schizoglossum barberae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
