Salvia expansa

Salvia expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salvia expansa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
