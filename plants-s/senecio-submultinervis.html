Senecio submultinervis

Senecio submultinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
