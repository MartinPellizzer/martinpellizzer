Solanum haleakalaense

Solanum haleakalaense, commonly known as Haleakala nightshade, is a rare plant native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky, coastal areas and is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of the Hawaiian highlands. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers, followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is listed as endangered due to habitat loss and invasive species.

Botanically, it is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and exhibits distinctive morphological features typical of its ecological niche.
