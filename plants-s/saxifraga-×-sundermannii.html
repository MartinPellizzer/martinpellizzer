Saxifraga × sundermannii

Saxifraga × sundermannii, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually rosette-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Saxifraga × sundermannii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
