Scirpus aemulans

Scirpus aemulans, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Scirpus aemulans is characterized by its ability to form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

It is widely distributed across temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
