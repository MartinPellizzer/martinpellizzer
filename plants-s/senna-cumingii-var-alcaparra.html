Senna cumingii var. alcaparra

Senna cumingii var. alcaparra, commonly known as the Alcaparra senna, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and coastal areas, exhibiting a compact growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feathery leaflets, and the plant is adapted to arid and saline environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
