Schaueria maximiliani

Schaueria maximiliani, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a raceme of small, colorful flowers.

Schaueria maximiliani is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
