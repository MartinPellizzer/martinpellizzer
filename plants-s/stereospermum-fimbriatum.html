Stereospermum fimbriatum

Stereospermum fimbriatum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

The botanical features of S. fimbriatum make it well-suited for warm climates and landscaped environments.
