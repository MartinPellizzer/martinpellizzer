Saxifraga serpyllifolia var. purpurea

Saxifraga serpyllifolia var. purpurea, commonly known as purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with succulent, often reddish stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
