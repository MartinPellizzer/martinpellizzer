Senecio carnulentus

Senecio carnulentus, commonly known as the woolly senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of fine white hairs on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
