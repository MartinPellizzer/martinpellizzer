Solanum tuberosum var. sipancachi

Solanum tuberosum var. sipancachi, commonly known as the Sipancachi potato, is a botanical variety native to the Andean region of South America.

This variety is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which often exhibits a distinct coloration and texture compared to common potato varieties. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and shares the same genus as the common potato, Solanum tuberosum. The plant typically features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white or purple flowers.

It is valued for its culinary uses and has been cultivated for its nutritional content and adaptability to high-altitude environments.
