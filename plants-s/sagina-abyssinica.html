Sagina abyssinica

Sagina abyssinica, commonly known as African silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Africa.

It features small, prostrate stems that form dense mats, often carpeting rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces tiny, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped, hairy, and arranged in pairs along the stems.

Sagina abyssinica is adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
