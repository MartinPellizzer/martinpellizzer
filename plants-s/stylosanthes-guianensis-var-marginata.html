Stylosanthes guianensis var. marginata

Stylosanthes guianensis var. marginata, commonly known as the Guianan stylo, is a perennial leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often used as forage for livestock.

Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and sustainable agriculture.
