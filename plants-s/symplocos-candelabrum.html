Symplocos candelabrum

Symplocos candelabrum, commonly known as the candlewood or candelabra plant, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, which resembles a candlestick, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a rough texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Symplocos candelabrum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
