Saccolabium griffithii

Saccolabium griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Saccolabium griffithii is well-suited to harsh environments due to its drought-resistant properties and deep root system.
