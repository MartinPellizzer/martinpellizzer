Seruneum insulare

Seruneum insulare, commonly known as the island seruneum, is a rare flowering plant native to the remote archipelagos of the Southern Ocean.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue petals and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand strong coastal winds. The plant produces small, clustered flowers during the short summer season, relying on wind pollination due to the absence of pollinators in its harsh environment. Its root system is highly specialized, allowing it to anchor in nutrient-poor, rocky soils.

Seruneum insulare is considered an indicator species of the unique and fragile ecosystems found in subantarctic regions.
