Spiraea lanceolata

Spiraea lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea lanceolata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ease of maintenance.
