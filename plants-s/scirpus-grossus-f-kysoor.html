Scirpus grossus f. kysoor

Scirpus grossus f. kysoor, commonly known as the Kysoor sedge, is a variant of the common sedge found in wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it thrives in marshy or waterlogged soils.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
