Sida crispifolia

Sida crispifolia, commonly known as the crinkled sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often crinkled or wavy in appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Sida crispifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
