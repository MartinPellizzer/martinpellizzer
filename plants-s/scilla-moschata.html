Scilla moschata

Scilla moschata, commonly known as the stinking squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soil.

The plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
