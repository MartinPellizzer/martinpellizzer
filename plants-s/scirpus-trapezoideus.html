Scirpus trapezoideus

Scirpus trapezoideus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often growing in dense stands. The plant has broad, flat leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base, and it produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers. Scirpus trapezoideus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its rhizomes are often used in phytoremediation efforts due to their ability to absorb heavy metals from contaminated water.
