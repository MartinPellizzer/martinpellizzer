Saxifraga × youngiana

Saxifraga × youngiana, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Saxifraga cernua and Saxifraga oppositifolia, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parents. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga × youngiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
