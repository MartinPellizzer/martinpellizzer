Sieglingia elongata

Sieglingia elongata, commonly known as the elongated sieve grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, pale green leaves. Its flowers are inconspicuous and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Sieglingia elongata is characterized by its unique sieve-like structure in its stems, which is a distinguishing botanical feature.
