Scilla petitiana

Scilla petitiana, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountains of Turkey and the Caucasus region.

It produces clusters of striking, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Scilla petitiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its early blooming period.
