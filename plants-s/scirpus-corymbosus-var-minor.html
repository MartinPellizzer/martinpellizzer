Scirpus corymbosus var. minor

Scirpus corymbosus var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

The species is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels.
