Solanum pruniforme

Solanum pruniforme, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that open in the early morning. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, with heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems. Its flowers are tubular and often have a pleasant fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solanum pruniforme is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
