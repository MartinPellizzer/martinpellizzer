Senecio johnstonii subsp. elgonensis

Senecio johnstonii subsp.

elgonensis, commonly known as the Elgon senecio, is a succulent plant native to the highland regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
