Schedonorus pumilus

Schedonorus pumilus, commonly known as the meadow fescue, is a low-growing grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically forms dense, fine-textured turf and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and as a turfgrass.

Schedonorus pumilus is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
