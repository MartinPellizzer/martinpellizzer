Scorzonera tau-saghyz subsp. tautaryensis

Scorzonera tau-saghyz subsp.

tautaryensis, commonly known as the Tautaryensis scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typical of the genus Scorzonera. It is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is of interest for its potential medicinal properties and resilience to harsh conditions.
