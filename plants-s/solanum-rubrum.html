Solanum rubrum

Solanum rubrum, commonly known as the red tomato, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its hairy stems, simple alternate leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries. The plant is native to the Americas and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its fruit is widely cultivated for culinary use and is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

Solanum rubrum plays an important role in both agriculture and nutrition worldwide.
