Stenostomum hotteanum

Stenostomum hotteanum, commonly known as the golden star grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
