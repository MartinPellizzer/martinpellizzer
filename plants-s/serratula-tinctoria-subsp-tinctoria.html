Serratula tinctoria subsp. tinctoria

Serratula tinctoria subsp.

tinctoria, commonly known as the dyer's mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its historical use in dyeing fabrics, it has cultural significance in various regions.
