Solanum delagoense var. karagweanum

Solanum delagoense var. karagweanum, commonly known as the Karagwe nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. It produces berries that range in color from yellow to red, often containing toxic alkaloids.

This species is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky areas.
