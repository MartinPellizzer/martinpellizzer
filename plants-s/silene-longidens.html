Silene longidens

Silene longidens, commonly known as the long-stamen catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pinkish or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers have five petals and numerous stamens, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Silene longidens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
