Salacia bipindensis f. brevifolia

Salacia bipindensis f. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved salacia, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Salaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, short, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are a feature of the genus. It is native to the tropical rainforests of western Africa, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
