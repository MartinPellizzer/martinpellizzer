Syringa spontanea

Syringa spontanea, commonly known as the wild lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Syringa spontanea is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
