Sauropus discocalyx

Sauropus discocalyx, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
