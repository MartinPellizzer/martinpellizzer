Sida paniculata var. compacta

Sida paniculata var. compacta, commonly known as the compact sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a hairy surface.

It is used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber in some regions.
