Spigelia japonica

Spigelia japonica, commonly known as Japanese spigelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Spigelia japonica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
