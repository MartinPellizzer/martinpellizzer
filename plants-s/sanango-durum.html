Sanango durum

Sanango durum, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is native to West and Central Africa, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces large, round fruits that resemble breadfruit, with a starchy, edible pulp enclosed in a fibrous husk. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree is valued for its versatile fruit, which can be cooked, fermented, or dried for long-term storage.
