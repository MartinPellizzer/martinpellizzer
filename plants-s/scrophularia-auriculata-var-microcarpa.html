Scrophularia auriculata var. microcarpa

Scrophularia auriculata var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf heartworts, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
