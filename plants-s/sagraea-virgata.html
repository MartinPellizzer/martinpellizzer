Sagraea virgata

Sagraea virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf sagraea, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Sagraea virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
