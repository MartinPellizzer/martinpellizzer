Salix abscondita

Salix abscondita, commonly known as the hidden willow, is a rare and elusive species of willow native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, inconspicuous leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces catkin-like flowers, which are typically greenish-yellow and appear in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Salix abscondita is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
