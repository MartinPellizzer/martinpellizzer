Staphylea colchica var. kochiana

Staphylea colchica var. kochiana, commonly known as the Caucasian hazel, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its deciduous leaves, which are elliptical and have a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible, hazelnut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
