Serjania grazielae

Serjania grazielae, commonly known as the Brazilian angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a smooth margin.

Serjania grazielae is also known for its fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
