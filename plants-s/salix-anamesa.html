Salix anamesa

Salix anamesa, commonly known as the Anamesa willow, is a shrub-like plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as moist meadows and mountainous regions, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Salix anamesa is also noted for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it an important species in its native ecosystem.
