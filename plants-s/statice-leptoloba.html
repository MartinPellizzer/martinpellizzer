Statice leptoloba

Statice leptoloba, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Statice leptoloba is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and ability to add vibrant color to gardens.
