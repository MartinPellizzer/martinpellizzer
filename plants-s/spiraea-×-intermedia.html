Spiraea × intermedia

Spiraea × intermedia, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is a hybrid between Spiraea prairie and Spiraea japonica, resulting in its distinctive cascading form. The plant features small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding ornamental value.

Spiraea × intermedia is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance and hardiness in various climates.
