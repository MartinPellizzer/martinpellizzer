Scrophularia subhastata

Scrophularia subhastata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are tubular, usually in shades of pink or purple, and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Scrophularia subhastata is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
