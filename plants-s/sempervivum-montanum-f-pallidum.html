Sempervivum montanum f. pallidum

Sempervivum montanum f. pallidum, commonly known as the pale alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant characterized by its low-growing, rosette-forming structure.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green to bluish-gray in color, which help it store water and reduce transpiration. Native to mountainous regions of Europe, this variety thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

The plant is also noted for its ability to survive in harsh alpine conditions, showcasing its resilience and adaptability.
