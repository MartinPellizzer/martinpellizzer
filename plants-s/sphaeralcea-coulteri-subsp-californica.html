Sphaeralcea coulteri subsp. californica

Sphaeralcea coulteri subsp.

californica, commonly known as Coulter's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces spherical seed pods that split open to release seeds, a feature typical of the genus Sphaeralcea. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
