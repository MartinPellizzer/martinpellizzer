Silene mogadorensis var. obtusifolia

Silene mogadorensis var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the obtuse leaf silene, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually broad and rounded, with a distinctive obtuse tip, giving it its specific epithet.

It plays an important role in the local flora and is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to the island's climate.
