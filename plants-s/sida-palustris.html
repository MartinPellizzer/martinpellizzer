Sida palustris

Sida palustris, commonly known as marsh sida, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Sida palustris is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding and is often found in disturbed wetland habitats.
