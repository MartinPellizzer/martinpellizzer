Salsola angularis

Salsola angularis, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, triangular stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often considered a weed. Salsola angularis has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in many regions.
