Solanum plurifurcipilum

Solanum plurifurcipilum, commonly known as the many-toothed nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often found in disturbed habitats and is not commonly cultivated.
