Sternbergia citrina

Sternbergia citrina, commonly known as the lemon bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, elongated capsules that contain numerous seeds. Sternbergia citrina typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun.

It is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
