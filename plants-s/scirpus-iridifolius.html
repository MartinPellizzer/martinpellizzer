Scirpus iridifolius

Scirpus iridifolius, commonly known as the purple leaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive purple-tinged leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Scirpus iridifolius plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
