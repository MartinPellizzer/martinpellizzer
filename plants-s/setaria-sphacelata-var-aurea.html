Setaria sphacelata var. aurea

Setaria sphacelata var. aurea, commonly known as golden millet, is a variant of the grass species Setaria sphacelata.

It is characterized by its striking golden hue, which is most prominent in its inflorescence. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering structures are spike-like and often branched.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional agricultural practices.
