Salix × pustoriae

Salix × pustoriae, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination, and has simple, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
