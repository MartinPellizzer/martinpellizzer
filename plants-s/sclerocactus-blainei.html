Sclerocactus blainei

Sclerocactus blainei, commonly known as Blaine's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a rounded, ribbed stem that often develops a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
