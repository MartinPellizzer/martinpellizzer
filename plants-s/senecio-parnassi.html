Senecio parnassi

Senecio parnassi, commonly known as Parnassian groundsels, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Senecio parnassi produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
