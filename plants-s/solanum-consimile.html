Solanum consimile

Solanum consimile, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum consimile is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
