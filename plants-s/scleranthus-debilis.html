Scleranthus debilis

Scleranthus debilis, commonly known as the slender sicklegrass, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight pubescence, and it thrives in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Scleranthus debilis is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to colonize open, sunny habitats.
