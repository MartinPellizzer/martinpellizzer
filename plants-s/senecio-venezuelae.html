Senecio venezuelae

Senecio venezuelae, commonly known as the Venezuelan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Venezuela.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry climates. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
