Stipa tenuissima var. curamalalensis

Stipa tenuissima var. curamalalensis, commonly known as the Curramalal grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
