Sida cordata var. nasirii

Sida cordata var. nasirii, commonly known as Nasiri's heartleaf sida, is a variant of the heartleaf sida species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
