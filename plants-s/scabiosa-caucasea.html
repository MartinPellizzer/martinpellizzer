Scabiosa caucasea

Scabiosa caucasea, commonly known as the Caucasian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Scabiosa caucasea is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
