Setaria chapmanii

Setaria chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's beardgrass, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Setaria chapmanii is adapted to withstand drought and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant in arid landscapes.
