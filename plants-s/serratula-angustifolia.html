Serratula angustifolia

Serratula angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Serratula angustifolia is also known for its medicinal properties, though it can be toxic in high doses.
