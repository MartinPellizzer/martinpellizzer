Serruria furcellata

Serruria furcellata, commonly known as the Mountain Catchfly, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Serruria furcellata is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in dry, sunny environments.
