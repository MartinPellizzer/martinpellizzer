Sasa pygmaea var. disticha

Sasa pygmaea var. disticha, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its short, stiff culms that grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are narrow and densely packed. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.

Due to its slow growth and hardiness, it is a popular choice for erosion control and landscape design in temperate climates.
