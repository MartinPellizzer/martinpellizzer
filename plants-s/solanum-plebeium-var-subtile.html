Solanum plebeium var. subtile

Solanum plebeium var. subtile, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns red when mature.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance.
