Salix lutea var. desolata

Salix lutea var. desolata, commonly known as the desert willow, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure and its striking yellowish-green catkins that appear in early spring. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Despite its delicate appearance, it is remarkably drought-tolerant and can survive in harsh environmental conditions.
