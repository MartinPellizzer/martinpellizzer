Scirpus rigidus

Scirpus rigidus, commonly known as rigid flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its stiff, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense spikes, typically found near the base of the stems. It thrives in marshy areas and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Scirpus rigidus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
