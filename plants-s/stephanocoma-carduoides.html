Stephanocoma carduoides

Stephanocoma carduoides, commonly known as the desert hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy foliage and deep root systems to conserve water. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh desert conditions.
