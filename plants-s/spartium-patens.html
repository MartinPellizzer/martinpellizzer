Spartium patens

Spartium patens, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a silvery bloom, giving it its common name. Spartium patens thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological settings.
