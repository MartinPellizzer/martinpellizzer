Stachys palustris f. baicalensis

Stachys palustris f. baicalensis, commonly known as the Baical Stachys, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and they tend to be somewhat succulent. It produces spikes of small, pale purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist, boggy environments.
