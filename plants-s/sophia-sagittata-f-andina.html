Sophia sagittata f. andina

Sophia sagittata f. andina, commonly known as the Andean arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it produces small, white flowers during the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific humidity and temperature requirements.
