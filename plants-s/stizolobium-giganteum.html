Stizolobium giganteum

Stizolobium giganteum, commonly known as the giant legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, bipinnate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
