Saxifraga × desetangsii

Saxifraga × desetangsii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its hybrid origin results from crosses between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga moschata, giving it unique morphological traits.

Saxifraga × desetangsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in alpine gardens and rockeries.
