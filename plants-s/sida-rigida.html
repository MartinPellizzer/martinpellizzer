Sida rigida

Sida rigida, commonly known as rigid sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by distinctive, triangular seed pods. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Sida rigida is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
