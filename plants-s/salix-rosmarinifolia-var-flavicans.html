Salix rosmarinifolia var. flavicans

Salix rosmarinifolia var. flavicans, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a variant of the rosmarinifolia willow species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues. Its flowers are catkins, typical of the Salicaceae family, and it typically blooms in early spring.

The leaves are aromatic, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and fragrance.
