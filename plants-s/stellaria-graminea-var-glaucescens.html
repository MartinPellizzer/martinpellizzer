Stellaria graminea var. glaucescens

Stellaria graminea var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue starweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
