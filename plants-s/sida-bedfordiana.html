Sida bedfordiana

Sida bedfordiana, commonly known as Bedford's mallard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 3 feet tall, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from summer to fall. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Sida bedfordiana is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
