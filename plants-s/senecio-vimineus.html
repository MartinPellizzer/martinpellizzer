Senecio vimineus

Senecio vimineus, commonly known as the creeping groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, often used in gardens for its ornamental value. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread, it can become invasive in certain environments.
