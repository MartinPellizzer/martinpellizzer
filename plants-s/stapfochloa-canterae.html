Stapfochloa canterae

Stapfochloa canterae, commonly known as the Cape grass or white speckled grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Stapfochloa canterae thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
