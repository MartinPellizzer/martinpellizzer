Scutellaria calcarata

Scutellaria calcarata, commonly known as the mountain skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Scutellaria calcarata is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
