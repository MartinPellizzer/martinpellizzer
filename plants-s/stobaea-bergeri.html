Stobaea bergeri

Stobaea bergeri, commonly known as the Bergier's stobaea, is a rare and endangered plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, dense foliage and striking, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and requires a specific microclimate to flourish. Its reproductive cycle is closely tied to seasonal rainfall patterns, making it highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
