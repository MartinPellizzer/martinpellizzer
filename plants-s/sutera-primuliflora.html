Sutera primuliflora

Sutera primuliflora, commonly known as the white violets, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Sutera primuliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
