Scirpus cyperinus f. condensatus

Scirpus cyperinus f. condensatus, commonly known as the dense club rush, is a variant of the club rush genus Scirpus.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that gives it its common name. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense mat near the water's surface.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
