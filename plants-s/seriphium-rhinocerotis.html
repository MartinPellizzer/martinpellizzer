Seriphium rhinocerotis

Seriphium rhinocerotis, commonly known as the rhinoceros hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Seriphium rhinocerotis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
