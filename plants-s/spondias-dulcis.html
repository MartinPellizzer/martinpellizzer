Spondias dulcis

Spondias dulcis, commonly known as the sweet sop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the distinctive, spiny fruit. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a creamy, aromatic pulp that is sweet and juicy.

It is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
