Saxifraga vaginalis

Saxifraga vaginalis, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It typically features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Saxifraga vaginalis is often found in mountainous areas and is known for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
