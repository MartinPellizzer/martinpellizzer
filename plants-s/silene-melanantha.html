Silene melanantha

Silene melanantha, commonly known as the black-stalked catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene melanantha is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
