Sida urens

Sida urens, commonly known as "common sida," is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, green to brown seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Sida urens is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
