Salsola angolensis

Salsola angolensis, commonly known as Angola saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its thick, waxy leaf cuticle. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Salsola angolensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
