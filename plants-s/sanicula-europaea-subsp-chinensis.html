Sanicula europaea subsp. chinensis

Sanicula europaea subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as Chinese sanicle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of East Asia. It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and stems, which contain volatile oils. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The fruit is a dry capsule that releases tiny seeds, aiding in its propagation.
