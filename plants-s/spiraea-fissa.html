Spiraea fissa

Spiraea fissa, commonly known as the fragrant spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers partial to full sun with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Spiraea fissa is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and easy maintenance.
