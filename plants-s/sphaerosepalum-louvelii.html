Sphaerosepalum louvelii

Sphaerosepalum louvelii, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often pale yellow or greenish-white, with a unique structure that includes specialized sepals and petals.

Sphaerosepalum louvelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories.
