Stipa arguens

Stipa arguens, commonly known as the silvergrass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are often seen in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its slender, upright stems are covered in a dense layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Stipa arguens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
