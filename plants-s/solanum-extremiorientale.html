Solanum extremiorientale

Solanum extremiorientale, commonly known as the Eastern Solanum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
