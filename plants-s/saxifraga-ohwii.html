Saxifraga ohwii

Saxifraga ohwii, commonly known as the Japanese saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Saxifraga ohwii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
