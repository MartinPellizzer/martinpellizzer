Sida angulosa

Sida angulosa, commonly known as angular sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features slender, angular stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have serrated edges.

Sida angulosa is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
