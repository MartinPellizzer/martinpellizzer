Saxifraga pseudocaesia

Saxifraga pseudocaesia, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs that help reduce water loss.

Saxifraga pseudocaesia is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high elevations where few other plants can survive.
