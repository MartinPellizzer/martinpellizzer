Senecio adglacialis

Senecio adglacialis, commonly known as the glacier senecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, harsh climates. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in a dense layer of fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Senecio adglacialis is also notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a resilient species in extreme conditions.
