Saxifraga hirculus var. indica

Saxifraga hirculus var. indica, commonly known as the Indian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It typically grows in alpine and rocky environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool temperatures. The plant features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early summer. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
