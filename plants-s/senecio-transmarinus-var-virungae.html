Senecio transmarinus var. virungae

Senecio transmarinus var. virungae, commonly known as the Virunga senecio, is a flowering plant native to the Albertine Rift region of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This variant is notable for its limited distribution and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
