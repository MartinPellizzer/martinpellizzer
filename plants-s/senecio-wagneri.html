Senecio wagneri

Senecio wagneri, commonly known as Wagner's groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to South Africa and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained environments.

Senecio wagneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
