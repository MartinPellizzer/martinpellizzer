Solidago nemoralis var. typica

Solidago nemoralis var. typica, commonly known as the common goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, composite flowers arranged in panicles. The plant typically grows in full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Solidago nemoralis var. typica plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
