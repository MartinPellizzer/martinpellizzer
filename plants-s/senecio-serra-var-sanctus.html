Senecio serra var. sanctus

Senecio serra var. sanctus, commonly known as the sacred mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
