Schefflera multiflora

Schefflera multiflora, commonly known as the Southern Schefflera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. Schefflera multiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
