Scaevola arenaria

Scaevola arenaria, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Australia.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with distinctive white markings. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Scaevola arenaria is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
