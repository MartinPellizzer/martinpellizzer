Silene nangqenensis

Silene nangqenensis, commonly known as the Nangqen sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau in China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and pinkish or white flowers. The plant typically thrives in high-altitude, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, helping to conserve water in its harsh habitat.

Silene nangqenensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
