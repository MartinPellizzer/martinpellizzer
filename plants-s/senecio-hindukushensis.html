Senecio hindukushensis

Senecio hindukushensis, commonly known as the Hindukush senecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to high-altitude conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with narrow, serrated leaves that help reduce water loss.

Senecio hindukushensis is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
