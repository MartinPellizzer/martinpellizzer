Selaginella neblinae

Selaginella neblinae, commonly known as the misty spikemoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is native to the cloud forests of Mexico and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and spore-producing structures. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates or in moist, well-drained soil. Selaginella neblinae is notable for its ability to survive in high-altitude conditions, which is a testament to its adaptability.
