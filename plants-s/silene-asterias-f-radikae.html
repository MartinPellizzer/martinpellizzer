Silene asterias f. radikae

Silene asterias f. radikae, commonly known as the radikate form of the common campion, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive root structure.

This subspecies exhibits a more robust and fibrous root system compared to the typical form, which enhances its ability to absorb nutrients from the soil. The plant belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces pink or white flowers with five petals, and its leaves are linear and slightly hairy.

The subspecies is noted for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy plant in various habitats.
