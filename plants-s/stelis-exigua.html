Stelis exigua

Stelis exigua, commonly known as the small-stemmed orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with slender, erect stems that bear a few lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typically fragrant. Stelis exigua thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical studies.
