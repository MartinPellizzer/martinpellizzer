Sesleria caerulea

Sesleria caerulea, commonly known as blue sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Sesleria caerulea is also valued for its ability to thrive in rocky or sandy environments.
