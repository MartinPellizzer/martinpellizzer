Scrophularia scorodonifolia

Scrophularia scorodonifolia, commonly known as the heartwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue. Its flowers are tubular, usually pink or purple, and bloom in early summer.

The plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
