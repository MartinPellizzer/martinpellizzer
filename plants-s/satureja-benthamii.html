Satureja benthamii

Satureja benthamii, commonly known as Bentham's savory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of tiny, pale purple flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Satureja benthamii is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
