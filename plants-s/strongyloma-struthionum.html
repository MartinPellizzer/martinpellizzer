Strongyloma struthionum

Strongyloma struthionum, commonly known as the ostrich fern, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fiddlehead-like young fronds that unfurl as the plant matures. The plant belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is known for its robust, upright growth habit. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a soft, fuzzy texture on the underside.

Strongyloma struthionum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
