Sempervivum tectorum var. diplogynum

Sempervivum tectorum var. diplogynum, commonly known as the "Coral Bells" or "Hens and Chicks," is a succulent plant native to the Alps and Pyrenees.

It features rosettes of fleshy, grass-like leaves that are typically green with reddish or pinkish hues, especially in full sun. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers on tall stems during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
