Sutera gossweileri

Sutera gossweileri, commonly known as the desert sutera, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sutera gossweileri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
