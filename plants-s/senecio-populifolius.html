Senecio populifolius

Senecio populifolius, commonly known as the Poplar-leaved Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of a poplar tree, giving it its common name. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio populifolius is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
