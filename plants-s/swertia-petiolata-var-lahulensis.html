Swertia petiolata var. lahulensis

Swertia petiolata var. lahulensis, commonly known as Lahul Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,500 and 4,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
