Salvia argentea subsp. patula

Salvia argentea subsp.

patula, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces spikes of showy blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to both gardens and botanical collections.
