Sanguisorba verrucosa

Sanguisorba verrucosa, commonly known as rough betony, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers in late summer, which are surrounded by bracts. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Sanguisorba verrucosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
