Spathiphyllopsis minahassae

Spathiphyllopsis minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa spathiphyllum, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spatulate-shaped inflorescence that gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to tree bark in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a subtle fragrance, and it produces small, elongated leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
