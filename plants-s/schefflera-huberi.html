Schefflera huberi

Schefflera huberi, commonly known as the Hawaiian Schefflera, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, palm-like foliage with large, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, umbrella-like shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Schefflera huberi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
