Solanum stellatum var. puberulum

Solanum stellatum var. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy star apple, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, though they may have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Due to its hairy foliage, it is sometimes referred to as the "hairy star apple" in horticultural references.
