Stephanomeria exigua var. carotifera

Stephanomeria exigua var. carotifera, commonly known as the narrowleaf stephanomeria, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.
