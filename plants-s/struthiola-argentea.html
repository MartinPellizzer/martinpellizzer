Struthiola argentea

Struthiola argentea, commonly known as silverleaf struthiola, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features silver-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss through reflection of sunlight. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pale yellow or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Struthiola argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and hardy nature.
