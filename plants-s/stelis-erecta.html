Stelis erecta

Stelis erecta, commonly known as the upright stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short, horizontal rhizome and a cluster of erect, slender pseudobulbs. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Stelis erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
