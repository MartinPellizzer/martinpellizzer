Silene cheiranthifolia

Silene cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the alpine catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene cheiranthifolia is often found in open meadows and is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
