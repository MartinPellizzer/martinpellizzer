Shorea quadrinervis

Shorea quadrinervis, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or oblong in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
