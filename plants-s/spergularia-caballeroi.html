Spergularia caballeroi

Spergularia caballeroi, commonly known as the Mexican sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits that release tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically succulent, helping it retain moisture in dry environments.

Spergularia caballeroi is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is a common component of desert flora.
