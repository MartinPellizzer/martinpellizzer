Scutellaria lacei

Scutellaria lacei, commonly known as the lace scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, two-lipped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, delicate appearance.

Scutellaria lacei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
