Syzygium jambolanum var. axillare

Syzygium jambolanum var. axillare, commonly known as the Java apple or wild mango, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
