Solidago chlorolepis

Solidago chlorolepis, commonly known as the greenleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small, clustered flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. Solidago chlorolepis thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is an important nectar source for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
