Salix phlomoides

Salix phlomoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often swampy habitats, such as riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, zigzagging stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its catkins are an important feature, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
