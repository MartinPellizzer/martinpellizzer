Schisandra henryi subsp. henryi

Schisandra henryi subsp.

henryi, commonly known as Chinese five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and red berries that turn dark purple when mature.

The plant is notable for its aromatic, five-flavored berries used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
