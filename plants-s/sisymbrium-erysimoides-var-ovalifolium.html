Sisymbrium erysimoides var. ovalifolium

Sisymbrium erysimoides var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mustard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is an annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow and oval-shaped, which distinguishes it from other varieties of the species. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
