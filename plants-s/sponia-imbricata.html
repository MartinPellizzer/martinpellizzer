Sponia imbricata

Sponia imbricata, commonly known as the velvetleaf sponia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Sponia imbricata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and its role in local ecosystems.
