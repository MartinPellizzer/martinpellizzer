Saribus brevifolius

Saribus brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leafed palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact stature and distinctive, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, starchy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Saribus brevifolius is well adapted to coastal and mangrove environments, making it an important component of these ecosystems.
