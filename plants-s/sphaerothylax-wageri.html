Sphaerothylax wageri

Sphaerothylax wageri, commonly known as the Wager's globe cactus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a small, globular cactus with a compact, rounded shape and a dense covering of spines. The plant features areolae, which are small, raised bumps on its surface, and it produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
