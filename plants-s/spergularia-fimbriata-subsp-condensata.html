Spergularia fimbriata subsp. condensata

Spergularia fimbriata subsp.

condensata, commonly known as the dense-fruited sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to sandy and coastal habitats. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure.

The plant produces small, pinkish or white flowers with five petals, and its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or rocky coastal areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense inflorescences and compact growth habit.
