Seseli caespitosum

Seseli caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, mat-forming growth habit. Its small, white to pale pink flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Seseli caespitosum is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.
