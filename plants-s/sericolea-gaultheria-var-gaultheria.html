Sericolea gaultheria var. gaultheria

Sericolea gaultheria var. gaultheria, commonly known as the red-flowered sericolea, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy texture with serrated edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
