Solanum douvillei

Solanum douvillei, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its berries are orange-red when ripe and contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum douvillei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance.
