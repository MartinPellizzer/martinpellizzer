Saxifraga groenlandica

Saxifraga groenlandica, commonly known as the Greenland saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Saxifraga groenlandica is often found in tundra and mountainous environments.
