Scutellaria reptans

Scutellaria reptans, commonly known as creeping skullcap, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Scutellaria reptans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
