Saxifraga sarmentosa var. aptera

Saxifraga sarmentosa var. aptera, commonly known as the creeping saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with numerous stolons that root at the nodes. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a waxy surface and a bluish-green hue. It produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This species is well adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
