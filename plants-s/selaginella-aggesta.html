Selaginella aggesta

Selaginella aggesta, commonly known as the African spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to parts of Africa.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spiky, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the reproductive organs.

Selaginella aggesta is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
