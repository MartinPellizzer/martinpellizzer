Senecio drummondii

Senecio drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio drummondii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
