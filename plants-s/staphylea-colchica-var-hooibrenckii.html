Staphylea colchica var. hooibrenckii

Staphylea colchica var. hooibrenckii, commonly known as the Caucasian horned poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy orange-red flowers that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
