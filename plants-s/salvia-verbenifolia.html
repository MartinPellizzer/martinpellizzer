Salvia verbenifolia

Salvia verbenifolia, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late summer. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia verbenifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
