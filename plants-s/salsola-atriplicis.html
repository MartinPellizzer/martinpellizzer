Salsola atriplicis

Salsola atriplicis, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its succulent stems and fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments due to its deep root system and ability to store water.

Salsola atriplicis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
