Syzygium unipunctatum

Syzygium unipunctatum, commonly known as the star fruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed, yellow-orange fruit. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical with a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit develops from a floral cluster and is known for its unique shape and tangy, acidic flavor.
