Schizachyrium gracilipes

Schizachyrium gracilipes, commonly known as slender threadgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or white. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Schizachyrium gracilipes plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
