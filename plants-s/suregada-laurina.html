Suregada laurina

Suregada laurina, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange-red pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically leathery and can be used in traditional medicine in some regions.
