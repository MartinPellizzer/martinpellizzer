Saxifraga sarmentosa var. tricolor

Saxifraga sarmentosa var. tricolor, commonly known as the three-color saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky environments in Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically green, white, and pink, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a valued addition to alpine gardens and botanical collections.
