Scandix trichosperma

Scandix trichosperma, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is a small, deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by large, round fruits that resemble breadfruit. The fruit contains a starchy, edible pulp that is used in various traditional dishes.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
