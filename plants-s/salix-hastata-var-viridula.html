Salix hastata var. viridula

Salix hastata var. viridula, commonly known as the green willow, is a variant of the slender willow native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green, with a smooth, slightly glossy surface. Its catkins are prominent in early spring, contributing to its role in pollination and seed dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
