Stipa pulcherrima

Stipa pulcherrima, commonly known as the purple grass or purple needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense clusters of purple or pinkish flower spikes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often hairy and slightly curled at the edges.

Stipa pulcherrima is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive color and drought tolerance.
