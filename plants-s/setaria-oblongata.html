Setaria oblongata

Setaria oblongata, commonly known as slender barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to Africa and now widely distributed in temperate regions.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that grows up to 1 meter tall, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, typical of the Poaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in hard, straw-colored glumes, which help in seed dispersal.

Setaria oblongata is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural fields.
