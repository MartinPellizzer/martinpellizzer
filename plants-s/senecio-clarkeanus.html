Senecio clarkeanus

Senecio clarkeanus, commonly known as the Clarke's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features erect stems with opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Senecio clarkeanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
