Scandix pecten-veneris var. brevirostris

Scandix pecten-veneris var. brevirostris, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky, blue or purple flower heads that resemble the shell of the Venus flower. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in dunes and rocky shores.

Scandix pecten-veneris var. brevirostris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats.
