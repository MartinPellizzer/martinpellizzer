Serjania macrocarpa var. glabricarpa

Serjania macrocarpa var. glabricarpa, commonly known as the smooth-fruited serjania, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland rainforests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
