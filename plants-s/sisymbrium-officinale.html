Sisymbrium officinale

Sisymbrium officinale, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a taproot system and erect, branched stems that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by slender, elongated pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly pubescent surface.

Sisymbrium officinale is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
