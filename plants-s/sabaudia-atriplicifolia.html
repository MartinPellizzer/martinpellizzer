Sabaudia atriplicifolia

Sabaudia atriplicifolia, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with fleshy, gray-green leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

Sabaudia atriplicifolia plays an important role in pollination and is an indicator species for cold climates.
