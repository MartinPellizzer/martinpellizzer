Scorzonera rosea subsp. peristerica

Scorzonera rosea subsp.

peristerica, commonly known as the Persian melon or Persian cucumber, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountains of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and large, lobed leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by edible, cucumber-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and edible qualities make it a valued plant in both horticulture and local cuisines.
