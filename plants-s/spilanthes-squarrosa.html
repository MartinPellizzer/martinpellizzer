Spilanthes squarrosa

Spilanthes squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa pungentweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that emit a strong, pungent odor. The plant belongs to the genus Spilanthes, which includes several species known for their medicinal properties. Its small, white to yellow flowers are typically clustered in a rounded head, and the plant thrives in dry, open habitats.

Spilanthes squarrosa is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
