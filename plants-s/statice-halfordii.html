Statice halfordii

Statice halfordii, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

Statice halfordii is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to add texture and color to garden landscapes.
