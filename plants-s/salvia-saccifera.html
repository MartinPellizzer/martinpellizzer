Salvia saccifera

Salvia saccifera, commonly known as the bladder sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Salvia saccifera thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique seed pods, which resemble small bladders, give it its common name and add to its ornamental appeal.
