Senecio tripetaloides

Senecio tripetaloides, commonly known as the three-petaled groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
