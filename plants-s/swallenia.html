Swallenia

Swallenia, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Swallenia thrives in moist, sunny conditions and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
