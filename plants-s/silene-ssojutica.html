Silene ssojutica

Silene ssojutica, commonly known as the Narrowleaf Catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene ssojutica is a perennial species that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
