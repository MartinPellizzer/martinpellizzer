Spinacia oleracea var. helvetica

Spinacia oleracea var. helvetica, commonly known as Swiss chard, is a leafy green vegetable belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, colorful stalks and broad, tender leaves that can range in color from white to deep red. The plant is typically cultivated for its edible leaves and stems, which are rich in nutrients such as vitamins A, C, and K. It thrives in cool climates and prefers well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional profile, making it a popular choice in gardens and kitchens alike.
