Salvia pratensis var. caerulea

Salvia pratensis var. caerulea, commonly known as blue mountain sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
