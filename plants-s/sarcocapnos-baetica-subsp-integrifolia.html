Sarcocapnos baetica subsp. integrifolia

Sarcocapnos baetica subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the Iberian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with entire margins.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
