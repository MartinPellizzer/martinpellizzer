Senecio lacinifolius

Senecio lacinifolius, commonly known as the needleleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
