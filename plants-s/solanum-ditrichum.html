Solanum ditrichum

Solanum ditrichum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows in low, dense mats, with small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale green flowers. Its berries are small, yellowish, and toxic, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum ditrichum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
