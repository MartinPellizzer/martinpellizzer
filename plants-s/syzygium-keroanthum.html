Syzygium keroanthum

Syzygium keroanthum, commonly known as the red lily.

This plant is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to New Guinea. It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
