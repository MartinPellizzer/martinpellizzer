Salvia selleana

Salvia selleana, commonly known as the blue salvia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-blue underside. The plant produces striking spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Salvia selleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
