Schima khasiana var. macrocarpa

Schima khasiana var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited schima, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by large, woody, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
