Sapindus rarak var. velutinus

Sapindus rarak var. velutinus, commonly known as velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits that split open to release hairy, winged seeds. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it is often found in moist, tropical environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
