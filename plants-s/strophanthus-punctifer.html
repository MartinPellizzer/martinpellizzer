Strophanthus punctifer

Strophanthus punctifer, commonly known as the spotted blood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is also noted for its milky sap, which contains toxic cardiac glycosides.
