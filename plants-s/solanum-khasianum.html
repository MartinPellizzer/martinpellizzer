Solanum khasianum

Solanum khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi tomato, is a herbaceous plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, red berries, similar to tomatoes. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental and potential medicinal value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
