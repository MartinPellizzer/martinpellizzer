Stachys mialhesii

Stachys mialhesii, commonly known as the purpleflower betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Stachys mialhesii is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
