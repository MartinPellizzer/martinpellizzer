Symonanthus

Symonanthus, commonly known as the star anise plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruits. The most well-known species, Symonanthus aromaticus, produces small, capsule-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. These plants typically grow in moist, shaded environments and have simple, alternate leaves.
