Sopubia conferta

Sopubia conferta, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems covered in soft, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a tight, compact rosette.
