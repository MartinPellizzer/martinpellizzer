Scrophularia aquatica subsp. cinerea

Scrophularia aquatica subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the gray willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in areas with standing water or along the edges of ponds and streams. Scrophularia aquatica subsp.

cinerea is a valuable plant for wetland restoration and supports a variety of pollinators.
