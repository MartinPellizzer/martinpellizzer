Scabiosa longifolia

Scabiosa longifolia, commonly known as the "pincushion flower," is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, spherical flower heads composed of small, pink or white petals. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Scabiosa longifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
