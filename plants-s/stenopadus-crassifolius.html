Stenopadus crassifolius

Stenopadus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf stenopadus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late spring or early summer. Its foliage is noted for its thick, waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Stenopadus crassifolius is often found in rocky or disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade environments.
