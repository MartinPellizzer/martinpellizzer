Syzygium scortechinii

Syzygium scortechinii, commonly known as the Java apple or *Lansium* species, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible and have a sweet, acidic flavor. Syzygium scortechinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in its native habitat.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
