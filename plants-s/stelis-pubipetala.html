Stelis pubipetala

Stelis pubipetala, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive hairy lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually oval and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Stelis pubipetala is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological dependence on symbiotic relationships.
