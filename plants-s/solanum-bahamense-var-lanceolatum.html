Solanum bahamense var. lanceolatum

Solanum bahamense var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the Bahamian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its lanceolate-shaped leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom at night. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

The fruit, which is typically green and berry-like, contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
