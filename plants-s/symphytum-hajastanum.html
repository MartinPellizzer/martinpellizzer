Symphytum hajastanum

Symphytum hajastanum, commonly known as the Caucasian comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces violet-blue flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
