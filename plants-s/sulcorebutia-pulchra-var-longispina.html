Sulcorebutia pulchra var. longispina

Sulcorebutia pulchra var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It features a compact, globular shape with a dense covering of spines, which are typically longer and more pronounced than in other varieties. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
