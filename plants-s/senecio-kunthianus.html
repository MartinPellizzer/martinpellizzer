Senecio kunthianus

Senecio kunthianus, commonly known as the Kunth's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senecio kunthianus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
