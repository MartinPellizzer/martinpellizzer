Saxifraga michauxii

Saxifraga michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, demonstrating resilience to cold and poor soil conditions.

Saxifraga michauxii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
