Sida abutilon

Sida abutilon, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. Sida abutilon is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production contribute to its invasive potential in certain ecosystems.
