Scrophularia calycina

Scrophularia calycina, commonly known as the lesser scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, typically pink or purple, and appear in clusters at the leaf axils.

Scrophularia calycina thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.
