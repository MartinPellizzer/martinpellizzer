Stipa pennata subsp. cerariorum

Stipa pennata subsp.

cerariorum, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The inflorescence consists of a dense panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
