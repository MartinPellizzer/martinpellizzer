Sutera crassicaulis

Sutera crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed sutera, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its sturdy, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Sutera crassicaulis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
