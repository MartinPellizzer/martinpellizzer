Shorea mangachapoi

Shorea mangachapoi, commonly known as the mangachapoi or yellow meranti, is a tropical tree species belonging to the Dipterocarpaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The tree is characterized by its heavy, durable timber, which is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth surface, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits.

Shorea mangachapoi plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
