Selaginella wallacei

Selaginella wallacei, commonly known as the Wallace's spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a stem with spirally arranged leaves that are usually narrow and pointed. Selaginella wallacei reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and it has a unique ability to survive extreme environmental conditions.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of certain ecosystems.
