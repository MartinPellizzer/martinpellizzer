Satureja montana subsp. kitaibelii

Satureja montana subsp.

kitaibelii, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in cooking for their peppery, thyme-like flavor, and the plant is also valued for its medicinal properties.
