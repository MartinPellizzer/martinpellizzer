Satureja cuneifolia subsp. obovata

Satureja cuneifolia subsp.

obovata, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, giving it a strong, pungent fragrance.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
