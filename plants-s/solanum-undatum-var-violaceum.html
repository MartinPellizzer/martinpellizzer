Solanum undatum var. violaceum

Solanum undatum var. violaceum, commonly known as the violet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The species features violet to purple petals, often with a white or yellow throat, and produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along supports.
