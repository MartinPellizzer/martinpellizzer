Swartzia opacifolia

Swartzia opacifolia, commonly known as the dark-leaved swartzia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a straight trunk with a smooth, dark bark and produces long, flat pods containing flat, winged seeds.

It is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
