Symphyotrichum porteri

Symphyotrichum porteri, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branching.

The showy, blue-violet flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
