Spachelodes

Spachelodes, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white, daisy-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Spachelodes is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
