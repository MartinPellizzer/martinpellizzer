Statice laxiflora

Statice laxiflora, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of salt, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Statice laxiflora is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
