Stachys macrantha

Stachys macrantha, commonly known as the purple flowering betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Stachys macrantha is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
