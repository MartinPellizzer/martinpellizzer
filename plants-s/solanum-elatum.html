Solanum elatum

Solanum elatum, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, spiny stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
