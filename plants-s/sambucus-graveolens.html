Sambucus graveolens

Sambucus graveolens, commonly known as European elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered white or greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
