Stillingia saxatilis var. salicifolia

Stillingia saxatilis var. salicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stillingia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of flora adapted to arid environments.
