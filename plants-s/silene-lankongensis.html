Silene lankongensis

Silene lankongensis, commonly known as the Lankong catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene lankongensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its harsh environment.
