Staphylea holocarpa var. holocarpa

Staphylea holocarpa var. holocarpa, commonly known as the Eurasian hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant bears edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
