Stanleya glauca

Stanleya glauca, commonly known as bluecurls, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery blue bloom. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

Stanleya glauca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
