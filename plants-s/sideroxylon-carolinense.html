Sideroxylon carolinense

Sideroxylon carolinense, commonly known as the Carolina ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and hard, heavy wood that is highly resistant to decay. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is considered a valuable component of its native ecosystems.
