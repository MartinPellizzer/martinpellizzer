Sundamomum hastilabium

Sundamomum hastilabium, commonly known as the hairy leaf grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, typical of many grass species.

Sundamomum hastilabium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
