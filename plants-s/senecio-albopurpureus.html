Senecio albopurpureus

Senecio albopurpureus, commonly known as the purplestem sedum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that are often tinged with purple, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its foliage is typically succulent, with thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water.
