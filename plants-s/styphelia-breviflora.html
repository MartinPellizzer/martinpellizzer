Styphelia breviflora

Styphelia breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of western Australia.

It features dense, leathery foliage and produces delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it well-suited to coastal environments. Styphelia breviflora is often found in heathlands and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
