Solanum crassipes

Solanum crassipes, commonly known as the roughleaf nightshade, is a shrub native to the Americas, particularly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic compounds that can be harmful to humans and animals. Solanum crassipes belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural studies.
