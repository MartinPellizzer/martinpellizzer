Streptocarpus albiflorus

Streptocarpus albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered streptocarpus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features attractive, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with a distinctive spurred structure, typical of the Gesneriaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Streptocarpus albiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in a home setting.
