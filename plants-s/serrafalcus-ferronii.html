Serrafalcus ferronii

Serrafalcus ferronii, commonly known as the golden spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with yellow or green hues. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Serrafalcus ferronii is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
