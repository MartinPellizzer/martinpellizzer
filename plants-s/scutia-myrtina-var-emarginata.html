Scutia myrtina var. emarginata

Scutia myrtina var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate scutia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive wavy margin, which gives it its botanical name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal floral display.
