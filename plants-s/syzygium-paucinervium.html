Syzygium paucinervium

Syzygium paucinervium, commonly known as the slender-veined clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

Syzygium paucinervium is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions.
