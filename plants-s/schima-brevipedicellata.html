Schima brevipedicellata

Schima brevipedicellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved schima, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicle inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical forest understories.

Schima brevipedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
