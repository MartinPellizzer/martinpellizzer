Sedum chrysanthemifolium

Sedum chrysanthemifolium, commonly known as the golden leaf sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and fleshy, silver-green leaves that are adorned with a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ability to withstand harsh conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid environments.
