Solanum ellipticum

Solanum ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring elliptical, waxy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Following flowering, it develops dark purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in the Solanaceae family, known for its ecological and chemical significance.
