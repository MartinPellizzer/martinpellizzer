Senecio erechthitoides

Senecio erechthitoides, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many regions outside its native range.
