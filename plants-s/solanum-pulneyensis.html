Solanum pulneyensis

Solanum pulneyensis, commonly known as the Pulney nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.

Solanum pulneyensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
