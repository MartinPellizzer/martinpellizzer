Stapelia parvipuncta

Stapelia parvipuncta, commonly known as the small-punctured stapelia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces unique flowers that are often described as resembling dead animals, a feature that aids in attracting pollinators like flies. Its leaves are usually small and pointed, and it blooms in shades of yellow and orange.

Stapelia parvipuncta is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
