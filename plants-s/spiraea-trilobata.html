Spiraea trilobata

Spiraea trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems and serrated, trifoliate leaves that are typically green and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea trilobata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
