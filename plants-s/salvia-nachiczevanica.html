Salvia nachiczevanica

Salvia nachiczevanica, commonly known as the Nachikzevan sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often fragrant and can be used in traditional herbal remedies.

Salvia nachiczevanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
