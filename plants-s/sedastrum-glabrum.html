Sedastrum glabrum

Sedastrum glabrum, commonly known as the smooth sedastrum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant produces distinctive, three-lobed leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Sedastrum glabrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
