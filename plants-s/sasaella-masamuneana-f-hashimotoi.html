Sasaella masamuneana f. hashimotoi

Sasaella masamuneana f. hashimotoi, commonly known as the Hashimoto's sasa, is a unique variant of the sasa genus native to Japan.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, bamboo-like growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its foliage is often a deep green, with a slightly glossy texture.

This form is distinguished by its subtle morphological differences from the typical species, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
