Salix inamoena

Salix inamoena, commonly known as the velvetleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves fully develop. Salix inamoena thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as streams and wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with a temperate climate.
