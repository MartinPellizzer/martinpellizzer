Sabicea velutina

Sabicea velutina, commonly known as the velvet sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its bark is often covered in a dense, velvety layer of fine hairs, giving it its common name.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and the plant produces small, flat pods that contain its seeds.
