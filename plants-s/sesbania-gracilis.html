Sesbania gracilis

Sesbania gracilis, commonly known as the slender sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
