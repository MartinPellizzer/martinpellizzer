Solanum baidoense

Solanum baidoense, commonly known as the Baidoense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, usually white or pale yellow flowers, and its berries are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in forest understories and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for agricultural purposes.
