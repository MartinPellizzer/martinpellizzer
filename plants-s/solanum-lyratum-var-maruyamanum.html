Solanum lyratum var. maruyamanum

Solanum lyratum var. maruyamanum, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is native to East Asia and thrives in moist, shady environments. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often contain toxic compounds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive if not managed properly.
