Syzygium sakalavarum

Syzygium sakalavarum, commonly known as the Kandyan rose apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The fruit is a round, fleshy drupe with a sweet and tangy flavor, often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a significant species in both horticulture and local agriculture.
