Spiraea japonica var. japonica

Spiraea japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a weeping appearance, and its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for creating a soft, flowing visual effect in landscaping.
