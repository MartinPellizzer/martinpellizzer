Stackhousia brunonis var. typica

Stackhousia brunonis var. typica, commonly known as the West Australian hakea.

It is a shrub native to Western Australia, characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
