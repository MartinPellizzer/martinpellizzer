Setaria lutescens f. pallens

Setaria lutescens f. pallens, commonly known as the pale leaved foxtail, is a grass species characterized by its slender, pale-green foliage.

It is a variant of Setaria lutescens, distinguished by its reduced pigmentation and lighter coloration compared to the typical form. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose, cylindrical panicle, with pale lemma and palea.

This ornamental grass is valued for its unique appearance and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
