Sedum hexapetalum

Sedum hexapetalum, commonly known as the six-petaled stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.

Sedum hexapetalum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
