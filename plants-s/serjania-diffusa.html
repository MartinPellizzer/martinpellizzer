Serjania diffusa

Serjania diffusa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink serjania, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive reddish hue when mature.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
