Salvia mandarinorum

Salvia mandarinorum, commonly known as Chinese sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
