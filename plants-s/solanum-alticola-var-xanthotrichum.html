Solanum alticola var. xanthotrichum

Solanum alticola var. xanthotrichum, commonly known as the yellow-haired nightshade, is a perennial herb native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and potential ecological roles.
