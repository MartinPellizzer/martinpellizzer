Staurogyne longicuneata

Staurogyne longicuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water clover, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, floating foliage composed of small, heart-shaped leaves that form a mat on the water surface. The plant has thin, wiry stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly in still or slow-moving waters. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

Staurogyne longicuneata is often used in pond and aquarium settings for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
