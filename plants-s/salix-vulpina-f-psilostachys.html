Salix vulpina f. psilostachys

Salix vulpina f. psilostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a shrub characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and lanceolate leaves.

It is native to the northern hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wetlands. The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a smooth margin. It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination and seed dispersal.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
