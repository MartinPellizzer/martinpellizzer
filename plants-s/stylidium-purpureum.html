Stylidium purpureum

Stylidium purpureum, commonly known as the purple spurred orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that are often violet or purple in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and wasps. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Stylidium purpureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
