Spiraea brahuica

Spiraea brahuica, commonly known as the Chinese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features arching branches covered in small, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer, which are borne on the ends of the stems. Its foliage turns yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.

Spiraea brahuica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
