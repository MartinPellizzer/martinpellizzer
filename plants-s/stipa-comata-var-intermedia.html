Stipa comata var. intermedia

Stipa comata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and long, feathery plumes that give it a delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
