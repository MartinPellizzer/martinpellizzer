Solanum gourlayi

Solanum gourlayi, commonly known as the Gourlay's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
