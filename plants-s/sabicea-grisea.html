Sabicea grisea

Sabicea grisea, commonly known as the gray sabicea, is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its bark is often covered in a thin, grayish layer, which contributes to its common name.

Sabicea grisea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
