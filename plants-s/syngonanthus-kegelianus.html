Syngonanthus kegelianus

Syngonanthus kegelianus, commonly known as the Kegelian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant's leaves are typically small, oval, and glossy, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are important for local wildlife.

Due to its parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
