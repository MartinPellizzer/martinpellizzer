Sempervivum crassifolium

Sempervivum crassifolium, commonly known as the stonecrop or houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features compact rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a wide range of climates, including cold winters.
