Scutellaria eplingii

Scutellaria eplingii, commonly known as Epling's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scutellaria eplingii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at higher elevations.
