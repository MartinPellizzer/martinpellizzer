Siphoneugena glabrata

Siphoneugena glabrata, commonly known as the smooth bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic prey, primarily tiny crustaceans and insects. The plant has slender, branching stems and is typically found in still or slow-moving water. Its leaves are reduced to scale-like structures, and it lacks true roots, relying instead on rhizoids for anchorage.

Siphoneugena glabrata plays a unique role in its ecosystem by contributing to nutrient cycling through its predatory behavior.
