Smilacina stellata var. crassa

Smilacina stellata var. crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Smilacina stellata var. crassa is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in partial shade.
