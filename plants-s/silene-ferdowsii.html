Silene ferdowsii

Silene ferdowsii, commonly known as the Ferdows's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene ferdowsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
