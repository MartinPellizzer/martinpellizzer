Saccolabium plebeium

Saccolabium plebeium, commonly known as the common saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain dormant for extended periods.

Saccolabium plebeium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
