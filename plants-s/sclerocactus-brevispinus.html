Sclerocactus brevispinus

Sclerocactus brevispinus, commonly known as the short-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a short, spiny stem that typically grows to about 10 centimeters in height. The plant has pale green to bluish-green skin and produces yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

Sclerocactus brevispinus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and unique appearance.
