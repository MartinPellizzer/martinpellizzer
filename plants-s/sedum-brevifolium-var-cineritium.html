Sedum brevifolium var. cineritium

Sedum brevifolium var. cineritium, commonly known as the silverleaf stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular plant among gardeners in arid regions.
