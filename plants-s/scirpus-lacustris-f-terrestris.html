Scirpus lacustris f. terrestris

Scirpus lacustris f. terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial marsh grass, is a variant of the common bulrush.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes and the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, often reaching several feet in height.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
