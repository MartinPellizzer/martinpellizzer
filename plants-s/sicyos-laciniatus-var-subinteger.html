Sicyos laciniatus var. subinteger

Sicyos laciniatus var. subinteger, commonly known as the smoothleaf morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory genus characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and less lobed compared to the typical Sicyos laciniatus. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in the early morning. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
