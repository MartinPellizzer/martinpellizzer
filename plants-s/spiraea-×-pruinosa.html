Spiraea × pruinosa

Spiraea × pruinosa, commonly known as the snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Spiraea × pruinosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
