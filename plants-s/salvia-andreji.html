Salvia andreji

Salvia andreji, commonly known as the Andreevi sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant traits.

Salvia andreji is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
