Sclerolobium guianense var. radlkoferi

Sclerolobium guianense var. radlkoferi, commonly known as the Guianan ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often glandular and can be quite fragrant. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and appear in clusters, while the fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds.
