Salmea parviceps

Salmea parviceps, commonly known as the small-headed salmea, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has fibrous roots that help it survive in dry conditions, and its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
