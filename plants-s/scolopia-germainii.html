Scolopia germainii

Scolopia germainii, commonly known as the Florida bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Scolopia germainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
