Spiraea filipendula glabra

Spiraea filipendula glabra, commonly known as white meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that are often covered with white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, gray-green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Spiraea filipendula glabra is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
