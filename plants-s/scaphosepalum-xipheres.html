Scaphosepalum xipheres

Scaphosepalum xipheres, commonly known as the purple-stemmed orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often display a distinctive purple or reddish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with pale pink to white petals and a prominent, spurred lip. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
