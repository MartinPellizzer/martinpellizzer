Sodada decidua

Sodada decidua, commonly known as the deciduous soda plant, is a unique species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its ability to shed its leaves seasonally, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in saline soils, showcasing its tolerance to high levels of sodium. Its stems are succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently.

Sodada decidua plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
