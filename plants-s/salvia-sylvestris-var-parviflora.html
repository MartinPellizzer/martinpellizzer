Salvia sylvestris var. parviflora

Salvia sylvestris var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved woodland sage, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
