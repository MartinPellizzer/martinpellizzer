Sasa diabolica

Sasa diabolica, commonly known as the devil's bamboo, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by triangular, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its robust root system allows it to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
