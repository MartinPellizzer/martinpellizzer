Senna pallida var. brachyrrhachis

Senna pallida var. brachyrrhachis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets, and its flowers are yellow, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
