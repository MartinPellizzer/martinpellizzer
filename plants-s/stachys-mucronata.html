Stachys mucronata

Stachys mucronata, commonly known as the blunt-tipped hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer.

Stachys mucronata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
