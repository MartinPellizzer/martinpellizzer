Symplocos patens f. elmeri

Symplocos patens f. elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's waxleaf.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in terminal panicles, which bloom in the spring. The fruits are small, fleshy drupes that ripen to a red or orange color, attracting various bird species.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to different soil types.
