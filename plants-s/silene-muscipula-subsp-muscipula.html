Silene muscipula subsp. muscipula

Silene muscipula subsp.

muscipula, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often with a hairy texture.

The species is known for its showy, globe-shaped inflorescence, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
