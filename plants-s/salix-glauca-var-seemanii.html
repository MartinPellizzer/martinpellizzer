Salix glauca var. seemanii

Salix glauca var. seemanii, commonly known as the white willow.

This variety is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind.

It is often found in moist or wetland habitats, thriving in environments with high humidity and well-drained soils.
