Scaphosepalum ximenae

Scaphosepalum ximenae, commonly known as the Ximenae orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Scaphosepalum ximenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
