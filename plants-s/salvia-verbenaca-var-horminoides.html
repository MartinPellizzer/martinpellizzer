Salvia verbenaca var. horminoides

Salvia verbenaca var. horminoides, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in both garden and herbal applications.
