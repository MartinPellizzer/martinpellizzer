Sphaerocoryne blanfordiana

Sphaerocoryne blanfordiana, commonly known as the Blanford's spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in its native desert environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring or early summer. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Sphaerocoryne blanfordiana is known for its resilience in harsh, dry conditions, making it a hardy species in xerophytic ecosystems.
