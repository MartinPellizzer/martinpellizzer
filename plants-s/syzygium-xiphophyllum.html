Syzygium xiphophyllum

Syzygium xiphophyllum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a small evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Syzygium xiphophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
