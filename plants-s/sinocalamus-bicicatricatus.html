Sinocalamus bicicatricatus

Sinocalamus bicicatricatus, commonly known as the two-scar bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that often exhibit distinctive scar patterns on the surface. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, bushy form. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sinocalamus bicicatricatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and construction.
