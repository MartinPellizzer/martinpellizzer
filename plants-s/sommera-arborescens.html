Sommera arborescens

Sommera arborescens, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Sommera arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
