Senecio berteroanus var. obtusus

Senecio berteroanus var. obtusus, commonly known as the Cape flower, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with woody stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid environments.
