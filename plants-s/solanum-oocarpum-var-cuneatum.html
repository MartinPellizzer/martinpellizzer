Solanum oocarpum var. cuneatum

Solanum oocarpum var. cuneatum, commonly known as the Cuneate Nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into globose, fleshy berries. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain numerous seeds.

Like other species in the Solanaceae family, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
