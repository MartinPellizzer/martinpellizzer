Sauromatum paramjitii

Sauromatum paramjitii, commonly known as the Paramjit's arrowhead, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
