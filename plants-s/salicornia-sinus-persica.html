Salicornia sinus-persica

Salicornia sinus-persica, commonly known as Persian glasswort, is a halophyte plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, jointed stems that often appear glassy due to their waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It is native to regions with coastal or inland saline soils, such as parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

Salicornia sinus-persica is often used in traditional medicine and can also be cultivated as a vegetable in salt-tolerant agriculture.
