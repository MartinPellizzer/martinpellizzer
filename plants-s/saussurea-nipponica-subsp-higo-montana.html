Saussurea nipponica subsp. higo-montana

Saussurea nipponica subsp.

higo-montana, commonly known as the Japanese mountain safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered with dense white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
