Solanum maglia var. brevifrons

Solanum maglia var. brevifrons, commonly known as the short-fruited nightshade, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is not suitable for human consumption and should be handled with care.
