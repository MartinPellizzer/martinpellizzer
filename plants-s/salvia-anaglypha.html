Salvia anaglypha

Salvia anaglypha, commonly known as the silver leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and tall spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
