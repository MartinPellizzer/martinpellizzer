Sambucus pubens f. calva

Sambucus pubens f. calva, commonly known as the white elderberry, is a variant of the elderberry plant characterized by its white flowers and smaller berries.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America, particularly found in the eastern and central United States. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The berries, though edible when ripe, are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
