Salacia pancheri

Salacia pancheri, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy green leaves and small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of stipules at the base of the leaves.

The berries are typically harvested when fully mature, and the plant is valued for its medicinal and nutritional properties.
