Sporobolus poiretii

Sporobolus poiretii, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bristly awns, which are adaptations for wind dispersal of its seeds. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Sporobolus poiretii is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and degraded grasslands.
