Seymeria bipinnatisecta var. texana

Seymeria bipinnatisecta var. texana, commonly known as the Texas sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features a dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or prairie habitats.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to dry, sunny environments.
