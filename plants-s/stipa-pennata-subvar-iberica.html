Stipa pennata subvar. iberica

Stipa pennata subvar. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted inflorescences that resemble soft, feathery plumes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
