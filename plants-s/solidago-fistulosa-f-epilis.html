Solidago fistulosa f. epilis

Solidago fistulosa f. epilis, commonly known as the silverleaf goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bright yellow composite flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for garden landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
