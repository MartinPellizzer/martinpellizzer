Saxifraga × racemiflora

Saxifraga × racemiflora, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant in the family Saxifragaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirculus. This plant typically grows in alpine and arctic regions, where it thrives in rocky and gravelly soils. It produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its botanical characteristics include rosettes of basal leaves and elongated stems bearing clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.
