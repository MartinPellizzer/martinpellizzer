Salvia bicolor var. barrelieri

Salvia bicolor var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
