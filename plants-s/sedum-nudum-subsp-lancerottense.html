Sedum nudum subsp. lancerottense

Sedum nudum subsp.

lancerottense, commonly known as the Lancerot sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
