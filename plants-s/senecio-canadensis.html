Senecio canadensis

Senecio canadensis, commonly known as Canadian ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio canadensis is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along stream banks.
