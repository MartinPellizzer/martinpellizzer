Silene vulgaris var. marginata

Silene vulgaris var. marginata, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are linear and often have a reddish or purplish margin, which gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

This plant is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
