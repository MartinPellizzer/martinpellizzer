Swainsona laxa var. rigida

Swainsona laxa var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid swainsona, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
