Solidago × krotkovii

Solidago × krotkovii, commonly known as the hybrid goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a cross between Solidago odora and Solidago rugosa, resulting in a plant with distinctive foliage and flowering characteristics. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purplish hue, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in panicles. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
