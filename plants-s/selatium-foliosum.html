Selatium foliosum

Selatium foliosum, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom in clusters.

Selatium foliosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
