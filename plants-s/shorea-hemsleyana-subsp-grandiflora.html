Shorea hemsleyana subsp. grandiflora

Shorea hemsleyana subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the Hemsley's rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The tree produces heavy, aromatic hardwood that is highly valued in the timber industry. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and requires a humid, warm climate.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.
