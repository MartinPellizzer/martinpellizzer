Senecio hachanus

Senecio hachanus, commonly known as the Hachan's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in clusters, followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and botanical collections.
