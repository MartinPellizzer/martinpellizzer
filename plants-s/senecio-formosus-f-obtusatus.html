Senecio formosus f. obtusatus

Senecio formosus f. obtusatus, commonly known as the obtuse-leaved African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, violet-colored flowers. The leaves are typically broad and rounded, with a waxy texture, and the plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a cluster. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This cultivar is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
