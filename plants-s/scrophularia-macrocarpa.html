Scrophularia macrocarpa

Scrophularia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-flowered figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
