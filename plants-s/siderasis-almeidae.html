Siderasis almeidae

Siderasis almeidae, commonly known as the Almeida's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, metallic-blue flowers that bloom in the late summer. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Due to its unique coloration and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
