Stipa nutans var. quinqueciliata

Stipa nutans var. quinqueciliata, commonly known as the five-ribbed basket grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical seed heads that are often curved and drooping, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
