Stevia samaipatensis

Stevia samaipatensis, commonly known as the "Peru sweet herb," is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for containing high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are typically harvested for use in food and beverages as a sugar substitute.

Stevia samaipatensis thrives in cool, humid climates and is often cultivated in shaded environments.
