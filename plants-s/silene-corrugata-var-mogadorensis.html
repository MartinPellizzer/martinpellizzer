Silene corrugata var. mogadorensis

Silene corrugata var. mogadorensis, commonly known as the Mogador carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late summer. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
