Silaum

Silaum, commonly known as silybum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive thistle-like appearance with spiny leaves and purple flowers. The plant is notable for containing compounds such as silymarin, which is known for its potential liver-protective properties. Silaum is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and medicinal use.

Its botanical features include a robust root system and a flowering period that typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
