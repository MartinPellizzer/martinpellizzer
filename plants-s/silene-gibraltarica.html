Silene gibraltarica

Silene gibraltarica, commonly known as the Gibraltar rockpink, is a perennial flowering plant native to the rocky areas of the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to thrive in arid and sunny conditions. It is often found growing on limestone and in coastal habitats.

Silene gibraltarica is a relatively rare species, making it a notable subject of botanical interest.
