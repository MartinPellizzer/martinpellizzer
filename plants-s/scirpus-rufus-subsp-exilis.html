Scirpus rufus subsp. exilis

Scirpus rufus subsp.

exilis, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters in shallow water or moist soil.

The plant produces small, triangular spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in environments with standing water or saturated soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
