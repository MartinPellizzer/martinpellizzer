Solanum tuberosum f. latum

Solanum tuberosum f. latum, commonly known as the broad-leaved potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its larger, broader leaves compared to the typical potato plant. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to the Andes region of South America. It produces tubers that are often used in culinary applications similar to the standard potato.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening as well.
