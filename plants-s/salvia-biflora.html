Salvia biflora

Salvia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming from summer to fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia biflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
