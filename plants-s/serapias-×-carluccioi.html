Serapias × carluccioi

Serapias × carluccioi, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a cross between Serapias aucubifolia and Serapias microchila.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Italy and southern France. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in wet, calcareous soils and is often found in marshy or boggy habitats.

Serapias × carluccioi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist conditions.
