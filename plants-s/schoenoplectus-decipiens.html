Schoenoplectus decipiens

Schoenoplectus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its roots help stabilize soil in wetland environments, making it ecologically significant.

Schoenoplectus decipiens is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.
