Sipapoa stipularis

Sipapoa stipularis, commonly known as the stipular orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with bright, indirect light.

Sipapoa stipularis is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
