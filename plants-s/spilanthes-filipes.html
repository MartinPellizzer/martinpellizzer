Spilanthes filipes

Spilanthes filipes, commonly known as the threadleaf pungentweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, thread-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Spilanthes filipes is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is known for its pungent odor and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
