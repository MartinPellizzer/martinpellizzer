Syzygium buxifolium

Syzygium buxifolium, commonly known as the box-leaf tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that resemble those of boxwood, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour-tasting fruits similar to tamarind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Syzygium buxifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
