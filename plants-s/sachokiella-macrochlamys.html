Sachokiella macrochlamys

Sachokiella macrochlamys, commonly known as the large-chalmed orchid, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a prominent lip. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. It grows as an epiphyte, often found on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a complex floral structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.
