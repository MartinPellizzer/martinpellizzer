Syzygium wrightii

Syzygium wrightii, commonly known as Wright's apple or the white apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, resembling a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Syzygium wrightii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and fragrant blossoms.
