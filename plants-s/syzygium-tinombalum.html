Syzygium tinombalum

Syzygium tinombalum, commonly known as the Bornean apple or Borneo apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo, where it grows in lowland and hill forests. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are yellow when ripe.
