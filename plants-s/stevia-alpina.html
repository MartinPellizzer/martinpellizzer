Stevia alpina

Stevia alpina, commonly known as the alpine sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intensely sweet leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. Stevia alpina thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
