Silene flavescens

Silene flavescens, commonly known as yellow catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are opposite and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene flavescens is often found in temperate climates and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
