Spergula × salontana

Spergula × salontana, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a perennial grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Spergula × salontana is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its use in sustainable landscaping.
