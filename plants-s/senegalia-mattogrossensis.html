Senegalia mattogrossensis

Senegalia mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian legume tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, which benefits surrounding vegetation.
