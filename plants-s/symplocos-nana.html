Symplocos nana

Symplocos nana, commonly known as dwarf beautyberry, is a small shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a reddish-brown underside.

Symplocos nana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and ability to attract wildlife.
