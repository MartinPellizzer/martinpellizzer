Spathacanthus simplicifolius

Spathacanthus simplicifolius, commonly known as the simple-leaved spathacanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, or grass family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
