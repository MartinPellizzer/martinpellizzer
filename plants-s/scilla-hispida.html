Scilla hispida

Scilla hispida, commonly known as the rough scilla, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. Scilla hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
