Sansevieria sordida

Sansevieria sordida, commonly known as the "Black Gold" snake plant, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, upright leaves that often have a purplish tinge, especially under bright light. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stalks, which are typically followed by dark purple berries. Sansevieria sordida is highly adaptable, thriving in low light conditions and requiring minimal water, making it an excellent choice for indoor cultivation.

Its air-purifying qualities also make it a popular houseplant.
