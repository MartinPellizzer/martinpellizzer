Salvia secunda var. tomentella

Salvia secunda var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a desirable ornamental plant.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
