Scabiosa columbaria var. rubella

Scabiosa columbaria var. rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered sweet William, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
