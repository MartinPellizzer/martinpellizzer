Spartina intermedia

Spartina intermedia, commonly known as cordgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt. This plant thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and preventing erosion. Spartina intermedia is also known for its ability to form dense stands, which can outcompete native vegetation in some cases.

Its rhizomes spread rapidly, allowing it to colonize new areas quickly and adapt to varying environmental conditions.
