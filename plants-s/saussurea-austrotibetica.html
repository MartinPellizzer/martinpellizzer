Saussurea austrotibetica

Saussurea austrotibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense, yellowish-orange flower heads. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic compound known as saffron-like resin, which has been traditionally used in Tibetan medicine. Its growth is adapted to cold, arid conditions, making it a hardy species found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique chemical composition and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and pharmacologists alike.
