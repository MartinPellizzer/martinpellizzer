Senecio weberbaueri

Senecio weberbaueri, commonly known as Weberbauer's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Senecio weberbaueri is often found in open, sunny habitats where it thrives in well-drained soils.
