Sigmatostalix lunata

Sigmatostalix lunata, commonly known as the moonlight starling, is a rare flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that emit a faint bioluminescent glow at night. The plant's leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reflect moonlight. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
