Saussurea cinerea

Saussurea cinerea, commonly known as hoary saffron or ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its pale, woolly foliage and its striking yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring cool and dry conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers surrounded by bracts.

Saussurea cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
