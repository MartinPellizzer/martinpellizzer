Stevia claussenii

Stevia claussenii, commonly known as the mountain stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Stevia claussenii is often cultivated as a natural sweetener and ornamental plant.
