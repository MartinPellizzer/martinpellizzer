Salix pentandra var. macrophylla

Salix pentandra var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
