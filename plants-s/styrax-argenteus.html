Styrax argenteus

Styrax argenteus, commonly known as silver styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, white, clustered flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Styrax argenteus prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
