Solidago curtisii var. flaccidifolia

Solidago curtisii var. flaccidifolia, commonly known as the flaccidifoliate goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wet meadows. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of bright yellow, rayless flowers.

The species is characterized by its slender stems and is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.
