Syzygium argyrocalyx

Syzygium argyrocalyx, commonly known as the silver-veined syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that have a distinctive silver vein pattern. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
