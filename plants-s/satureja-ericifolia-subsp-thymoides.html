Satureja ericifolia subsp. thymoides

Satureja ericifolia subsp.

thymoides, commonly known as dwarf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and flowers.

The plant typically forms dense mats, with small, narrow leaves that are gray-green in color and emit a strong thyme scent. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in small clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is well adapted to rocky and poor soils, making it a hardy plant in alpine and subalpine environments.
