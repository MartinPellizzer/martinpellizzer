Senecio mutisii var. congestus

Senecio mutisii var. congestus, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its striking blue and purple flowers. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
