Solanum lanceolatum var. sinuatum

Solanum lanceolatum var. sinuatum, commonly known as the Sinuous Nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive sinuous margin, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition from white to purple as they mature. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic alkaloids.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
