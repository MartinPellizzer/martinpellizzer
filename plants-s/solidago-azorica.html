Solidago azorica

Solidago azorica, commonly known as the Azorean goldenrod, is a herbaceous plant native to the Azores, a group of islands in the North Atlantic Ocean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Solidago azorica typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
