Sasa septentrionalis f. kuzakaiana

Sasa septentrionalis f. kuzakaiana, commonly known as the Kuzakaian bamboo, is a variant of the northern sasa bamboo native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming, evergreen grass that produces slender, cylindrical culms with a smooth, pale green surface. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in mountainous regions with acidic soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a glossy texture and a pointed tip, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and is often used in horticulture for landscaping and erosion control.
