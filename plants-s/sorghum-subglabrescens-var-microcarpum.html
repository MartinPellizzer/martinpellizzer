Sorghum subglabrescens var. microcarpum

Sorghum subglabrescens var. microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf sorghum, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, hard seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and potential agricultural value.
