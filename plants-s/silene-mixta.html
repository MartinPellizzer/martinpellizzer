Silene mixta

Silene mixta, commonly known as the mixed campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and other open habitats. Silene mixta has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often found in regions with temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
