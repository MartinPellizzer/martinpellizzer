Syzygium gladiatum

Syzygium gladiatum, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are encased in a fibrous husk, containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Syzygium gladiatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
