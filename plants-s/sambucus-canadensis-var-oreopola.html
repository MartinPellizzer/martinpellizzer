Sambucus canadensis var. oreopola

Sambucus canadensis var. oreopola, commonly known as the Allegheny blackberry, is a variant of the common elderberry.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant produces clusters of small, dark purple to black berries that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are compound, with three to five ovate leaflets, and it has fragrant, white flowers in umbels.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
