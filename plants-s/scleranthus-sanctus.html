Scleranthus sanctus

Scleranthus sanctus, commonly known as the sacred thorn, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated leaves and produces small, dry fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides or in waste areas.

Scleranthus sanctus is considered a valuable plant in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in supporting local insect populations.
