Symphyopappus cuneatus f. intermedius

Symphyopappus cuneatus f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate wedgeleaf sunflower, is a variant of the wedgeleaf sunflower native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. It exhibits a compact growth habit with multiple stems and produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

This form is distinguished by its intermediate characteristics between the typical and variegated forms of the species.
