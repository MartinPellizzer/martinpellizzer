Syringa emodi f. aurea

Syringa emodi f. aurea, commonly known as the golden spire viburnum, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its striking golden-yellow inflorescences that appear in early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
