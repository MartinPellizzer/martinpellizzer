Senecio inconstans

Senecio inconstans, commonly known as the variable groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Senecio inconstans is also known for its variable appearance, which contributes to its common name.
