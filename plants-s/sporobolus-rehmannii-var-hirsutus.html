Sporobolus rehmannii var. hirsutus

Sporobolus rehmannii var. hirsutus, commonly known as hairy sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a clumping growth habit and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in dryland ecosystems and a valuable component of native plant communities.
