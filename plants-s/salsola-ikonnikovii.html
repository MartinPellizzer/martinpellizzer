Salsola ikonnikovii

Salsola ikonnikovii, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, which are often mistaken for tumbleweeds. Native to Central Asia, it has adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in poor soils and high sunlight conditions.

Salsola ikonnikovii is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it a common invasive species in many regions.
