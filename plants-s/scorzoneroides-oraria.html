Scorzoneroides oraria

Scorzoneroides oraria, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, violet-blue, and clustered in dense inflorescences. Scorzoneroides oraria is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in horticulture.
