Smilax moranensis

Smilax moranensis, commonly known as the Morán’s smilax, is a climbing shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny foliage. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers that are clustered in spadices, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Smilax moranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
