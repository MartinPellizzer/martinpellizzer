Sapindus fraxinifolius

Sapindus fraxinifolius, commonly known as the Indian soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is thick and rough, often peeling in strips, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local cultures.
