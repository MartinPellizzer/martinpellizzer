Saxifraga cherlerioides var. rebunshirensis

Saxifraga cherlerioides var. rebunshirensis, commonly known as the Rebun Island saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the island of Rebun in the northern Japanese archipelago.

It thrives in cool, humid environments and is typically found in rocky or mossy habitats. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are usually gray-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. Its flowers are pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late summer.

This variety is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and endemic species.
