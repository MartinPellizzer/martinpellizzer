Selenia aurea var. aperta

Selenia aurea var. aperta, commonly known as the open-gold selenia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps that help in moisture retention.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural collections.
