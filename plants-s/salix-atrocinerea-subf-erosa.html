Salix atrocinerea subf. erosa

Salix atrocinerea subf.

erosa, commonly known as the black willow subspecies with a serrated margin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with finely serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are male and female, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often found in riparian zones and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
