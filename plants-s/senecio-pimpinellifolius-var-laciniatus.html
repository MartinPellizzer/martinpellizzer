Senecio pimpinellifolius var. laciniatus

Senecio pimpinellifolius var. laciniatus, commonly known as the laceleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticultural settings.
