Surenus mexicana

Surenus mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican suranus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardens in dry climates.
