Sorbus aestivalis

Sorbus aestivalis, commonly known as the summer whitebeams, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval or elliptic, with serrated edges, and it has a rounded, bushy growth habit.

Sorbus aestivalis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
