Solanum dulcamara subsp. pusztarum

Solanum dulcamara subsp.

pusztarum, commonly known as the Hungarian bindweed, is a perennial vine native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that transition from white to pink as they mature. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange, are toxic but serve as a food source for certain birds.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
