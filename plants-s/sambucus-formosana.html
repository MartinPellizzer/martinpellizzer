Sambucus formosana

Sambucus formosana, commonly known as the Formosan elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in traditional medicine.

Sambucus formosana is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
