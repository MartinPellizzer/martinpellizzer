Schoenoplectus validus subsp. luxurians

Schoenoplectus validus subsp.

luxurians, commonly known as the tall bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often growing in dense stands in marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in late summer, which are typically pale green to brown in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming extensive colonies in wetland habitats.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
