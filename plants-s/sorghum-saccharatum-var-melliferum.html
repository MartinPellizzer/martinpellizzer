Sorghum saccharatum var. melliferum

Sorghum saccharatum var. melliferum, commonly known as sweet sorghum, is a variant of sorghum characterized by its high sugar content in its stalks.

This plant is native to Africa and thrives in warm, arid climates, making it well-suited for cultivation in semi-arid regions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while its stems are thick and fibrous, containing a significant amount of sucrose. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in panicles, which are typical of the grass family.

Sweet sorghum is valued not only for its sugar content but also for its adaptability and potential as a bioenergy crop.
