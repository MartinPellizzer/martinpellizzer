Salacia celebica

Salacia celebica, commonly known as the Celebes salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
