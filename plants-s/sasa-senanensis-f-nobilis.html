Sasa senanensis f. nobilis

Sasa senanensis f. nobilis, commonly known as the noble bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded environments in eastern Asia. The plant features slender, green culms that are often used in traditional crafts and ornamental gardening. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Sasa senanensis f. nobilis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in forested areas.
