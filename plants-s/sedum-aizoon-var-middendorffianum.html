Sedum aizoon var. middendorffianum

Sedum aizoon var. middendorffianum, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it store water efficiently. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
