Sedum × lorenzoi

Sedum × lorenzoi, commonly known as the hybrid stonecrop, is a succulent plant that belongs to the Crassulaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Sedum leucanthum and Sedum kamchatcense, resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The species is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, and it produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white.

Sedum × lorenzoi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
