Saccharum cylindricum var. longifolium

Saccharum cylindricum var. longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf sugarcane.

This variant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soils. The plant is a perennial grass that produces tall, sturdy stalks, which are used for sugar production.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
