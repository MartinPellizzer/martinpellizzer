Shorea obovoidea

Shorea obovoidea, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The tree is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. It typically grows in lowland tropical rainforests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
