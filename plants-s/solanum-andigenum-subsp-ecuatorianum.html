Solanum andigenum subsp. ecuatorianum

Solanum andigenum subsp.

ecuatorianum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian nightshade, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
