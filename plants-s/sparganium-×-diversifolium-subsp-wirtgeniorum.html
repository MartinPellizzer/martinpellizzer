Sparganium × diversifolium subsp. wirtgeniorum

Sparganium × diversifolium subsp.

wirtgeniorum, commonly known as the Wirtgen's bur-reed, is a hybrid plant species native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that often grow in wetlands and shallow water bodies.

The plant produces fluffy, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are elongated and lanceolate, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps it thrive in moist environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in supporting aquatic and semi-aquatic wildlife.
