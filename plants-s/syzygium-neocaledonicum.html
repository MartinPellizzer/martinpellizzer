Syzygium neocaledonicum

Syzygium neocaledonicum, commonly known as the New Caledonian apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family, native to the island of New Caledonia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

Syzygium neocaledonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
