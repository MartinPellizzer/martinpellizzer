Senecio serpentini

Senecio serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's tail, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Senecio serpentini is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
