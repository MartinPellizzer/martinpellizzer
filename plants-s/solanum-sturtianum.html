Solanum sturtianum

Solanum sturtianum, commonly known as Sturt's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

Solanum sturtianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by Indigenous Australians.
