Sherwoodia sinensis

Sherwoodia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the evergreen rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves that are often hairy underneath. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The botanical features of Sherwoodia sinensis make it a distinctive and valued plant in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
