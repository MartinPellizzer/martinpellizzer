Stanhopea lietzei

Stanhopea lietzei, commonly known as the Lietze's Stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a complex, intricate structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its humid environment.

Stanhopea lietzei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
