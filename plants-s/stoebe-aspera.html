Stoebe aspera

Stoebe aspera, commonly known as rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Stoebe aspera is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
