Stemodia microphylla

Stemodia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved stemodia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth form with hairy stems and small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called cypsela.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
