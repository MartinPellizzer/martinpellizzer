Solanum pycnotrichum

Solanum pycnotrichum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
