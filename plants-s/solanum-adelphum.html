Solanum adelphum

Solanum adelphum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum adelphum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
