Sindora galedupa

Sindora galedupa, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and the tree is often found in moist, lowland forests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
