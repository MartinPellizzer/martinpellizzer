Styrax paralleloneurus f. inutilis

Styrax paralleloneurus f. inutilis, commonly known as the useless styrax, is a rare plant species belonging to the Styracaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
