Stachys chamissonis var. chamissonis

Stachys chamissonis var. chamissonis, commonly known as creeping betony, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers in the summer, which are arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native plant gardens.
