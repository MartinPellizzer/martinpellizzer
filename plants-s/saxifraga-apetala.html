Saxifraga apetala

Saxifraga apetala, commonly known as the barren saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often reddish stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces numerous flowers in early summer, usually in shades of white or pale pink, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Saxifraga apetala plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme climates.
