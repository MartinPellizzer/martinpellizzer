Solidago deflexa

Solidago deflexa, commonly known as the drooping goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, often with a distinctive drooping or reflexed appearance.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
