Spiraea multijuga

Spiraea multijuga, commonly known as the broad-leaved spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features upright, multi-branched stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has oval to heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Spiraea multijuga is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
