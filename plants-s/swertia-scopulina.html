Swertia scopulina

Swertia scopulina, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

Swertia scopulina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
