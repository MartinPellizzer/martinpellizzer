Solanum albiflorum

Solanum albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers and smooth, hairy stems. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow when mature.

Solanum albiflorum is often found in dry, open habitats and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
