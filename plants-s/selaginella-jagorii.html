Selaginella jagorii

Selaginella jagorii, commonly known as the jagorii spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or forest floors. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are linear and often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are borne on the upper part of the stem and contain spores.

Selaginella jagorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
