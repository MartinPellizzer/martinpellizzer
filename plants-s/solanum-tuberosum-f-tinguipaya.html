Solanum tuberosum f. tinguipaya

Solanum tuberosum f. tinguipaya, commonly known as the Tinguipaya potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato with distinct morphological characteristics.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it has been traditionally cultivated by indigenous communities. The plant features tubers that are typically smaller and have a unique skin coloration, often ranging from purple to red. Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, sharing traits with other nightshade plants.

Its genetic diversity makes it an important subject for agricultural research and crop improvement programs.
