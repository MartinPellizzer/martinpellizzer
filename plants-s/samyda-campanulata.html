Samyda campanulata

Samyda campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Samyda campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
