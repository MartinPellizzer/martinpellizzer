Setaria membranifolia

Setaria membranifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
