Silene davidii

Silene davidii, commonly known as David's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene davidii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
