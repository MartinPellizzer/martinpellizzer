Solanum brevidens

Solanum brevidens, commonly known as the short-leaved nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small, spherical berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
