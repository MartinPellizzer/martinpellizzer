Scirpus globiceps

Scirpus globiceps, commonly known as the globular bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense, spiky inflorescences that are typically brown or purple in color. Its roots help stabilize soil and improve water filtration in aquatic environments.

Scirpus globiceps is an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
