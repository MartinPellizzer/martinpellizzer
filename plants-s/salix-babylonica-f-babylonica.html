Salix babylonica f. babylonica

Salix babylonica f. babylonica, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and typically have a bluish-green hue on top and a pale white underside. It thrives in moist soils and is often planted near water bodies.

The plant is noted for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
