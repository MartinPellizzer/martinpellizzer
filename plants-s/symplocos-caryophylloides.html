Symplocos caryophylloides

Symplocos caryophylloides, commonly known as the fragrant wax tree, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, waxy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
