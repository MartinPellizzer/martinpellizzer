Swertia rotata

Swertia rotata, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Swertia rotata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the gentian family.
