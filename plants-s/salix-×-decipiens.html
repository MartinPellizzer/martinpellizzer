Salix × decipiens

Salix × decipiens, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that create a drooping canopy, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and small, inconspicuous female flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a smooth surface and a serrated edge.

Native to the northern hemisphere, it thrives in moist environments and is often found near water bodies.
