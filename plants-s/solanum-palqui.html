Solanum palqui

Solanum palqui, commonly known as the Palqui nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the evening. Its fruit is a small, green berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.

Solanum palqui is often found in moist, shaded environments at higher elevations.
