Saxifraga hispidula

Saxifraga hispidula, commonly known as the hairy saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and tundra environments. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Saxifraga hispidula plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
