Sesleria brevifolia

Sesleria brevifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sesleria, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, pale green to reddish spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Sesleria brevifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
