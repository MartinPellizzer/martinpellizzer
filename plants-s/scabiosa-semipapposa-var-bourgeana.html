Scabiosa semipapposa var. bourgeana

Scabiosa semipapposa var. bourgeana, commonly known as Bourgean's scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.
