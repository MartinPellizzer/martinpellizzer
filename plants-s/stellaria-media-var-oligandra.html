Stellaria media var. oligandra

Stellaria media var. oligandra, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers with five petals that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are typically oval or kidney-shaped with a hairy texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in gardens, lawns, and along paths. It has a shallow root system and spreads primarily through stolons, making it a common weed in cultivated areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
