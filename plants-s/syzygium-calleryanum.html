Syzygium calleryanum

Syzygium calleryanum, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense or Chinese sandalwood, is a flowering plant species native to southern China.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruits are small, round drupes that turn red when mature, containing a single seed.

Syzygium calleryanum is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
