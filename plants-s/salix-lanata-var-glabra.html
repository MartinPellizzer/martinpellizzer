Salix lanata var. glabra

Salix lanata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth silver willow, is a variant of the silver willow native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and long, drooping branches. The leaves are typically broad, lance-shaped, and have a silvery underside due to fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
