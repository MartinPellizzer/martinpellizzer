Sida guyanensis

Sida guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana mallow, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, and its leaves are ovate with serrated edges. It has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.
