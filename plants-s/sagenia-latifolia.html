Sagenia latifolia

Sagenia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers arranged in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
