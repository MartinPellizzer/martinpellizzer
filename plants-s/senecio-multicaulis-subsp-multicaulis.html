Senecio multicaulis subsp. multicaulis

Senecio multicaulis subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy purple to violet flower heads that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to full sun and is drought-tolerant once established.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
