Solanum indicum var. jaegeri

Solanum indicum var. jaegeri, commonly known as the Jaeger's nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically features hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in cultivation and is considered a weed in some regions.
