Senecio discifolius

Senecio discifolius, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio discifolius is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
