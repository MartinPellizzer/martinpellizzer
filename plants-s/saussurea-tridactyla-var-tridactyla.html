Saussurea tridactyla var. tridactyla

Saussurea tridactyla var. tridactyla, commonly known as the Tibetan yellowhead, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense, yellowish inflorescence that resembles a cluster of small yellow heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, thriving at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, with both male and female reproductive structures, and it relies on wind pollination.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan medicine.
