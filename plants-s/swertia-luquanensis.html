Swertia luquanensis

Swertia luquanensis, commonly known as the Luquan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 3,500 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched and hairy.

Swertia luquanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
