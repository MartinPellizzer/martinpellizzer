Sasaella ogamiensis

Sasaella ogamiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Ogami bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, grassy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasaella ogamiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
