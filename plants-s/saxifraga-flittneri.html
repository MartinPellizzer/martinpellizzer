Saxifraga flittneri

Saxifraga flittneri, commonly known as Flittner's saxifrage, is a flowering plant species native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant typically produces small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga flittneri thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
