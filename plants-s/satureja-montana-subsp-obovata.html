Satureja montana subsp. obovata

Satureja montana subsp.

obovata, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features aromatic, lanceolate to obovate leaves with a distinctive mint-like fragrance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies and culinary applications.
