Salix sinica var. dentata

Salix sinica var. dentata, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in gardens and parks.
