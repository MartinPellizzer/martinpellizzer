Saxifraga signata var. lancepetala

Saxifraga signata var. lancepetala, commonly known as the lance-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
