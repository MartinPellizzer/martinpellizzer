Sheadendron pisoniiflorum var. brachystachyum

Sheadendron pisoniiflorum var. brachystachyum, commonly known as the short-stalked flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The short-stalked variety is distinguished by its shorter flower clusters compared to other varieties of the species.
