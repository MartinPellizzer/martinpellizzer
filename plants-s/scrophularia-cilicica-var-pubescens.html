Scrophularia cilicica var. pubescens

Scrophularia cilicica var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent dog's mercury, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It is a perennial herb that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
