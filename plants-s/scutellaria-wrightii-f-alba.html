Scutellaria wrightii f. alba

Scutellaria wrightii f. alba, commonly known as the white skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while the root system is fibrous and shallow.

This plant is often found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
