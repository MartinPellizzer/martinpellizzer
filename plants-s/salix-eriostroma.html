Salix eriostroma

Salix eriostroma, commonly known as the silver willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along streams.

The silver willow is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
