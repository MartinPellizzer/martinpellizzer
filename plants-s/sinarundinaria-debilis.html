Sinarundinaria debilis

Sinarundinaria debilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Sinarundinaria debilis is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in moist environments.
