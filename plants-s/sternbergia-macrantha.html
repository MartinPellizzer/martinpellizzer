Sternbergia macrantha

Sternbergia macrantha, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sternbergia macrantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
