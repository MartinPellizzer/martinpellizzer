Senecio × hybridus var. holtzeri

Senecio × hybridus var. holtzeri, commonly known as the hybrid senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that often branch at the base, forming a compact, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
