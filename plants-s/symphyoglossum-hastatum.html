Symphyoglossum hastatum

Symphyoglossum hastatum, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as swamps and pine flatwoods. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and branched.

The flowers feature prominent spurred petals, which are adapted for pollination by specific insect species.
