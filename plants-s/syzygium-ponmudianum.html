Syzygium ponmudianum

Syzygium ponmudianum, commonly known as the Ponmudianum apple or Malabar apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India and is characterized by its small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a fragrant aroma, while its flowers are white and clustered.

Syzygium ponmudianum is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
