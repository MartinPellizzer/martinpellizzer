Spiraea opulifolia var. lutea

Spiraea opulifolia var. lutea, commonly known as goldenweeds, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
