Salvia atropaenulata

Salvia atropaenulata, commonly known as the dark purple sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet and purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia atropaenulata is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
