Sasa kanayamensis

Sasa kanayamensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or kanayamensis sasa, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is a clump-forming shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine and crafts.

Sasa kanayamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining forest ecosystems.
