Sedum maximum var. atropurpureum

Sedum maximum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the Purple Giant Sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with purple, especially under sunny conditions. The plant produces tall, branched stems that can reach several feet in height, bearing clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
