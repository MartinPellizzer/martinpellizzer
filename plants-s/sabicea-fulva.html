Sabicea fulva

Sabicea fulva, commonly known as the golden sabicea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Sabicea fulva is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
