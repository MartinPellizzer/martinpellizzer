Sieruela silvatica

Sieruela silvatica, commonly known as the forest sieruela, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest understories. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the top of the stems.

Sieruela silvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local insect populations.
