Spilanthes ciliata

Spilanthes ciliata, commonly known as the toothed false mustards, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent aroma. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
