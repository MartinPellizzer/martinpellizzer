Stachys rehmannii

Stachys rehmannii, commonly known as redstem betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Stachys rehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
