Salacia whytei f. ophiurella

Salacia whytei f. ophiurella, commonly known as the coral tree or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its distinctive coral-like flowers and woody structure. The plant typically grows as a tree or shrub, with bark that can be smooth or fissured depending on the species. Its leaves are alternate and simple, often with a glossy surface, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
