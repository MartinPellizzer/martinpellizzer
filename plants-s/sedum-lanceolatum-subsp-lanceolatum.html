Sedum lanceolatum subsp. lanceolatum

Sedum lanceolatum subsp.

lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
