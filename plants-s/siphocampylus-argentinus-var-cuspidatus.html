Siphocampylus argentinus var. cuspidatus

Siphocampylus argentinus var. cuspidatus, commonly known as the Argentine mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes of South America.

It features erect, slender stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
