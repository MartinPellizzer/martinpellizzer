Sinningia rupicola

Sinningia rupicola, commonly known as the rock nightshade, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
