Sorbus croceocarpa

Sorbus croceocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by yellow, spherical fruits that are edible for certain bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
