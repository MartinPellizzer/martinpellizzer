Sebaea semialata

Sebaea semialata, commonly known as the silverleaf sebaea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or white.

Sebaea semialata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
