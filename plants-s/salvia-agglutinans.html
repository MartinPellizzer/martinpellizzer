Salvia agglutinans

Salvia agglutinans, commonly known as the sticky sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, sticky seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Salvia agglutinans is often used in traditional medicine and is a popular plant among xeriscaping enthusiasts.
