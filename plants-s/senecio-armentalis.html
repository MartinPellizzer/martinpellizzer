Senecio armentalis

Senecio armentalis, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio armentalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
