Sida galheirensis var. subgracilis

Sida galheirensis var. subgracilis, commonly known as the slender sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, erect stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
