Smilax ilicifolia

Smilax ilicifolia, commonly known as catbrier, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, spiny growth, and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to some animals. Smilax ilicifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native habitat.
