Satureja mexicana

Satureja mexicana, commonly known as Mexican thyme, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and oregano. The plant features small, pink or white flowers and has narrow, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
