Senecio pomacochanus

Senecio pomacochanus, commonly known as the Peruvian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
