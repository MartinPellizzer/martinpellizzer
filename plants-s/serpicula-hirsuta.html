Serpicula hirsuta

Serpicula hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy serpentine, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, serpentine-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought conditions. Its small, clustered flowers are often pink or red, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Serpicula hirsuta is known for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
