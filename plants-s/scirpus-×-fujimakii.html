Scirpus × fujimakii

Scirpus × fujimakii, commonly known as the hybrid bulrush, is a wetland plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spadix-like flowers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Scirpus californicus and Scirpus acutifolius, showcasing genetic traits from both parents. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form extensive underground networks, contributing to soil stabilization.

Scirpus × fujimakii plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
