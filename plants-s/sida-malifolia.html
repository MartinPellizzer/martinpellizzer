Sida malifolia

Sida malifolia, commonly known as the sour fig or wild cotton, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. Sida malifolia is typically found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
