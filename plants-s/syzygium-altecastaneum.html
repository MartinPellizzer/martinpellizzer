Syzygium altecastaneum

Syzygium altecastaneum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown wood. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in many regions.
