Saussurea brunneopilosa

Saussurea brunneopilosa, commonly known as the brown-haired safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in a fine, brownish pubescence. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry climates.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest in both botanical research and traditional medicine.
