Schima mertensiana

Schima mertensiana, commonly known as the Chinese teak or Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red berries that attract various bird species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some regions.

Schima mertensiana thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.
