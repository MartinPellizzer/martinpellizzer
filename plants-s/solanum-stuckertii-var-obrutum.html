Solanum stuckertii var. obrutum

Solanum stuckertii var. obrutum, commonly known as the Obrutum nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves with a distinctive, serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by berries that mature to a deep blue-black. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
