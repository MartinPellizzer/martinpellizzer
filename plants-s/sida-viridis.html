Sida viridis

Sida viridis, commonly known as green sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Sida viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
