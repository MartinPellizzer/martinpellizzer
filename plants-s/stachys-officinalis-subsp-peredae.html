Stachys officinalis subsp. peredae

Stachys officinalis subsp.

peredae, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and wildflower meadows.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is attributed to its purported calming and healing properties.
