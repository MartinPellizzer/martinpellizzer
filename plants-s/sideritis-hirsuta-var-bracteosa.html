Sideritis hirsuta var. bracteosa

Sideritis hirsuta var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
