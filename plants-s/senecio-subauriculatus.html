Senecio subauriculatus

Senecio subauriculatus, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
