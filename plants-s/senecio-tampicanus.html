Senecio tampicanus

Senecio tampicanus, commonly known as the Tampicane Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The yellow flowers bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
