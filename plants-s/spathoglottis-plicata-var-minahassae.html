Spathoglottis plicata var. minahassae

Spathoglottis plicata var. minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fleshy, and often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in humid, tropical climates.

The flowers are usually borne on branched inflorescences and can vary in color from white to pink or yellow, depending on the specific variety.
