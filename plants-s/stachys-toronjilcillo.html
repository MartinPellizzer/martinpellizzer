Stachys toronjilcillo

Stachys toronjilcillo, commonly known as lemon mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct lemony scent when crushed.

Stachys toronjilcillo is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.
