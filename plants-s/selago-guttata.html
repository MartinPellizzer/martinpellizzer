Selago guttata

Selago guttata, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Selago guttata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
