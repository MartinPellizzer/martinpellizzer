Stelis spathosa

Stelis spathosa, commonly known as the orchid with a spatulate lip, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with a single, lanceolate leaf. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically arranged in a raceme, with a distinctive spatulate lip that aids in pollination.

Stelis spathosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
