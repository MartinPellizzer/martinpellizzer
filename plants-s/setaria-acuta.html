Setaria acuta

Setaria acuta, commonly known as rough dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open prairies and disturbed areas.

Setaria acuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
