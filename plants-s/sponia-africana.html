Sponia africana

Sponia africana, commonly known as the African sponia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Sponia africana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
