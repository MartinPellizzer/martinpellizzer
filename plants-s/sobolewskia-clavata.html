Sobolewskia clavata

Sobolewskia clavata, commonly known as the clustered clubgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Sobolewskia clavata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
