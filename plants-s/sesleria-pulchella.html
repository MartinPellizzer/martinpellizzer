Sesleria pulchella

Sesleria pulchella, commonly known as the pink tufted grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

Sesleria pulchella is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
