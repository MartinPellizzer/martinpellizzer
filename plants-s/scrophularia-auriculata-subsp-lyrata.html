Scrophularia auriculata subsp. lyrata

Scrophularia auriculata subsp.

lyrata, commonly known as the lyrated figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly wavy margin. Its flowers are tubular and typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in horticulture.
