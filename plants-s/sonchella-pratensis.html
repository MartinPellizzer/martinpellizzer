Sonchella pratensis

Sonchella pratensis, commonly known as the meadow sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
