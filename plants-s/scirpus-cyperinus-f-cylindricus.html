Scirpus cyperinus f. cylindricus

Scirpus cyperinus f. cylindricus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it helps stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration projects.
