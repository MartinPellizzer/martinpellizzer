Saxifraga intricata subvar. nana

Saxifraga intricata subvar. nana, commonly known as the dwarf saxifrage, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to alpine and rocky habitats in Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, decorative plants.
