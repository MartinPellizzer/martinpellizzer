Setaria setosa f. interrupta

Setaria setosa f. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted beardgrass, is a variant of the common beardgrass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous bristly spikelets. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for ecological studies and soil stabilization.
