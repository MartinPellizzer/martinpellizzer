Setaria jubiflora

Setaria jubiflora, commonly known as the golden panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are typically pale yellow to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Setaria jubiflora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soils.
