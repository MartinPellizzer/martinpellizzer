Selago dinteri subsp. pseudodinteri

Selago dinteri subsp.

pseudodinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's selago, is a rare and endemic species native to the arid regions of Namibia. It is a succulent shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered vulnerable to conservation status.
