Scindapsus cuscuaria

Scindapsus cuscuaria, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage.

Scindapsus cuscuaria is also known for its ability to climb and trail, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or shelves.
