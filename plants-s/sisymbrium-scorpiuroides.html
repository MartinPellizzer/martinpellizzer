Sisymbrium scorpiuroides

Sisymbrium scorpiuroides, commonly known as the scorpionweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a distinctive, branching stem.

Sisymbrium scorpiuroides is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its hardy, resilient nature.
