Statice welwitschii var. cinerea

Statice welwitschii var. cinerea, commonly known as silver statice, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and spikes of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and coastal environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in xeriscaping and rock gardens.

The variety 'cinerea' is distinguished by its grayish foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance compared to other statice species.
