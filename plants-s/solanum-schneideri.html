Solanum schneideri

Solanum schneideri, commonly known as Schneider's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to yellow when mature, often containing several seeds.
