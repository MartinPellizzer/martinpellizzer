Smyrnium lineare

Smyrnium lineare, commonly known as the cow parsnip, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and can grow up to 2 meters tall. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

The plant is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and disturbed soils.
