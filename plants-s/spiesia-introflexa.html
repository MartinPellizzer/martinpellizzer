Spiesia introflexa

Spiesia introflexa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with white ray florets.

Spiesia introflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
