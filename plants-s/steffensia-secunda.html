Steffensia secunda

Steffensia secunda, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers. Steffensia secunda is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low moisture, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important plant for pollinators and local biodiversity.
