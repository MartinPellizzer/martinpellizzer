Sucrea monophylla

Sucrea monophylla, commonly known as the desert cactus, is a unique plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its single, thick stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Sucrea monophylla plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert species.
