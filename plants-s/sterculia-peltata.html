Sterculia peltata

Sterculia peltata, commonly known as the broad-leaf sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
