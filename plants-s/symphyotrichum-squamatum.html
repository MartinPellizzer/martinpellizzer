Symphyotrichum squamatum

Symphyotrichum squamatum, commonly known as the squamate heath aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in the fall. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive leaf scales, which are a key identifying characteristic.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
