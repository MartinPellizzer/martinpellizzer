Senecio hesperius

Senecio hesperius, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent leaves and a deep root system to conserve water. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio hesperius is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in regions with limited rainfall.
