Spartina maritima subvar. radii

Spartina maritima subvar. radii, commonly known as the Atlantic cordgrass, is a perennial halophyte that thrives in coastal salt marshes.

It is characterized by its dense, rhizomatous growth and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to tolerate saline and brackish conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its roots form extensive networks that help stabilize tidal soils and prevent erosion.

Spartina maritima subvar. radii plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
