Sapindus emarginatus

Sapindus emarginatus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with shallow fissures.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
