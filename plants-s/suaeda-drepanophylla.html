Suaeda drepanophylla

Suaeda drepanophylla, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in saline and drought-prone environments. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Its ability to tolerate extreme conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
