Speranskia tuberculata var. pekinensis

Speranskia tuberculata var. pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking rose or Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of many modern garden roses.
