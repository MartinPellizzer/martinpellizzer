Selaginella blatteri

Selaginella blatteri, commonly known as the Blatter's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. Its sporophyte produces strobila, which are cone-like structures containing spores.

Selaginella blatteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
