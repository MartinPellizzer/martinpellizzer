Salvia reuteriana

Salvia reuteriana, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia reuteriana is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
