Scabiosa crenata var. glabrescens

Scabiosa crenata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the softleaf scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves that become smooth and glabrous as the plant matures. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
