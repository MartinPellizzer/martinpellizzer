Salvia selguapensis

Salvia selguapensis, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Salvia selguapensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
