Scilla bifolia var. subnivalis

Scilla bifolia var. subnivalis, commonly known as the alpine scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is typically found in alpine meadows and subnival zones. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh winter conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of high-altitude flora.
