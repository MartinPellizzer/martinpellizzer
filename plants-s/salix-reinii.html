Salix reinii

Salix reinii, commonly known as the Mongolian willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces catkin-like flowers, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Salix reinii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in challenging climates.
