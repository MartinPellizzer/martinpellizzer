Saxifraga federici-augusti subsp. grisebachii

Saxifraga federici-augusti subsp.

grisebachii, commonly known as the gray saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in a grayish or whitish pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in cymes, and are usually pink or purple in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
