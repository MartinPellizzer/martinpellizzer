Schizostachyum alopecurus

Schizostachyum alopecurus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved palmyra palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are narrow and feather-like. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fruits that are used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove areas.

Schizostachyum alopecurus plays an important role in both ecological and economic systems in its native regions.
