Shorea worthingtonii

Shorea worthingtonii, commonly known as Worton's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a broad, buttressed trunk and compound leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, and it produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Shorea worthingtonii plays an important ecological role in its native forests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
