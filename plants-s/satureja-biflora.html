Satureja biflora

Satureja biflora, commonly known as twin-flowered savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers in pairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary applications, while the plant also has medicinal properties.

Satureja biflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.
