Salvia marquandii

Salvia marquandii, commonly known as Marquand's sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and striking purple-blue flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
