Scabiosa columbaria var. corsica

Scabiosa columbaria var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican Scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal teas. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is also noted for its purported calming and digestive properties.
