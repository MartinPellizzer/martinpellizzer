Specklinia tabacina

Specklinia tabacina, commonly known as the tobacco fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often lobed fronds that resemble the leaves of the tobacco plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
