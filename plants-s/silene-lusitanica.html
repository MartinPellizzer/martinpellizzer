Silene lusitanica

Silene lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and slender stems. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene lusitanica is often found in dry, sunny locations and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
