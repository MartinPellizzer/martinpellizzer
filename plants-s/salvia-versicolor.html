Salvia versicolor

Salvia versicolor, commonly known as the variable sage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and fragrant leaves. The plant produces showy, violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia versicolor is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
