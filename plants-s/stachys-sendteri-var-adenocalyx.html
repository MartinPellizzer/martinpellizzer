Stachys sendteri var. adenocalyx

Stachys sendteri var. adenocalyx, commonly known as the腺萼鼠尾草, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
