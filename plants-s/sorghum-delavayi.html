Sorghum delavayi

Sorghum delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's sorghum, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential agricultural uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
