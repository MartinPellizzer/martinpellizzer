Spathoglottis trivalvis

Spathoglottis trivalvis, commonly known as the three-valved orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are zygomorphic, with three distinct petals and a specialized lip that aids in pollination.

The rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its natural habitat.
