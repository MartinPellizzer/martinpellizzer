Sasa

Sasa, commonly known as bamboo grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to the temperate regions of East Asia, including Japan, China, and Korea. Sasa species are characterized by their clump-forming growth habit and slender, hollow stems. They often grow in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and traditional uses in basketry and papermaking.
