Syringa vulgaris var. purpurea

Syringa vulgaris var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in shades of purple and pink. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored choice for landscaping and floral arrangements.
