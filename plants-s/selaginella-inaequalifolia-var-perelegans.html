Selaginella inaequalifolia var. perelegans

Selaginella inaequalifolia var. perelegans, commonly known as the variable spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, linear leaves arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces spores in small, cone-like structures called strobili, which are borne on the upper parts of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
