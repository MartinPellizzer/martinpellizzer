Sisymbrium polyceratium var. pilosum

Sisymbrium polyceratium var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual or biennial herb characterized by its branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The leaves are usually hairy, giving the plant its common name, and the flowers are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
