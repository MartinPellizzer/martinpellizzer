Sauvetrea grandimentum

Sauvetrea grandimentum, commonly known as the grandiflora sauvetrea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and have a strong, spicy aroma. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and aromatic properties.
