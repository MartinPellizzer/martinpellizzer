Serjania clematidea var. acuminata

Serjania clematidea var. acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
