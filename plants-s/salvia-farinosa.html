Salvia farinosa

Salvia farinosa, commonly known as desert sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh desert conditions. Its fibrous root system helps it survive in poor soil and low moisture conditions.

Salvia farinosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
