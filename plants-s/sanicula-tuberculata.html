Sanicula tuberculata

Sanicula tuberculata, commonly known as the rough sanicle, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in early summer.

Sanicula tuberculata is valued for its ecological role, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
