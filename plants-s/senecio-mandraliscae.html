Senecio mandraliscae

Senecio mandraliscae, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact rosette of thick, bluish-gray leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking spikes of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not controlled properly.
