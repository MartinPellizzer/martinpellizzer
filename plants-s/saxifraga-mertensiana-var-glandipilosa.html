Saxifraga mertensiana var. glandipilosa

Saxifraga mertensiana var. glandipilosa, commonly known as the glandular-pilose saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It features rosettes of fleshy, hairy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its unique morphology and ability to withstand harsh climates make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
