Silene coeli-rosa var. aspera

Silene coeli-rosa var. aspera, commonly known as the rough silene, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish to purplish flowers. The leaves are typically linear and covered with fine, rough hairs, giving the plant its "aspera" designation. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
