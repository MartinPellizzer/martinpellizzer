Solanum brevistylum

Solanum brevistylum, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features small, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
