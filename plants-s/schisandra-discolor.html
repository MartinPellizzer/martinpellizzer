Schisandra discolor

Schisandra discolor, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible and used in traditional medicine. Schisandra discolor is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in herbal remedies for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
