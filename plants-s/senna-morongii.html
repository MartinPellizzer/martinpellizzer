Senna morongii

Senna morongii, commonly known as the Morong senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Senna morongii is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
