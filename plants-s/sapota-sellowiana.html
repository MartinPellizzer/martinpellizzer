Sapota sellowiana

Sapota sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian sapote, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The fruit is typically a smooth, green or yellowish berry with a sweet, creamy pulp. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
