Saxifraga camposii subsp. leptophylla

Saxifraga camposii subsp.

leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette.

The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
