Simira cesariana

Simira cesariana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark red to black heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The tree is often found in lowland forests and can grow up to 30 meters tall.
