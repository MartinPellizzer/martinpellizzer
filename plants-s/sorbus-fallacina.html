Sorbus fallacina

Sorbus fallacina, commonly known as the false whitebeam, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called pomes, which are often red or yellow when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and parks.
