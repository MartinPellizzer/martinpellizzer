Sisymbrium heteromallum f. dahuricum

Sisymbrium heteromallum f. dahuricum, commonly known as Siberian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Siberia.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring slender stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in racemose inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds upon maturity.

The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, temperate climates.
