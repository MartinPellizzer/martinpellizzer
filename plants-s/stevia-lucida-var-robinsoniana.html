Stevia lucida var. robinsoniana

Stevia lucida var. robinsoniana, commonly known as the Hawaiian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, silver-green leaves that are highly aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and dry environments.

This variety is notable for its use in traditional Hawaiian medicine and as a natural sweetener.
