Sesbania aculeata var. parviflora

Sesbania aculeata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system that can fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful forage crop and a potential weed.
