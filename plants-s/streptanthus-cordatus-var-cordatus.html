Streptanthus cordatus var. cordatus

Streptanthus cordatus var. cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has basal leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name. It produces yellow or white flowers in late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in California and Oregon, where it thrives in open, sunny environments.
