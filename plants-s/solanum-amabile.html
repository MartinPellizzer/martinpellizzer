Solanum amabile

Solanum amabile, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible tuberous roots, which are rich in carbohydrates and nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Solanum amabile is also valued for its ability to improve soil structure through its extensive root system.
