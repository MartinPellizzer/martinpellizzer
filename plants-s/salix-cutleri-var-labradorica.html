Salix cutleri var. labradorica

Salix cutleri var. labradorica, commonly known as Labrador willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and coastal areas. The plant has slender, flexible stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, woody fruits.

This species is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
