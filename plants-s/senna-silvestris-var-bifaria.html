Senna silvestris var. bifaria

Senna silvestris var. bifaria, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
