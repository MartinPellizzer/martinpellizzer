Sorbus aria proles acutifolia

Sorbus aria proles acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, lanceolate leaves that are sharply pointed. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
