Syringodea fascicularis

Syringodea fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered syringodea, is a rare and specialized plant species found in specific tropical environments.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure, which consists of tightly clustered flowers arranged in a fascicle pattern. The plant exhibits a sympodial growth habit, with multiple shoots arising from the base, contributing to its compact appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a distinct midrib.

Syringodea fascicularis is notable for its ecological niche, often growing in shaded, moist habitats where it plays a role in local biodiversity.
