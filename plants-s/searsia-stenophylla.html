Searsia stenophylla

Searsia stenophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf bramble, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically white or pale green underneath. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and coastal areas, and is often found in woodland and shrubland environments.

Searsia stenophylla plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
