Senna cardiosperma subsp. microphylla

Senna cardiosperma subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnate and have small, ovate leaflets.

The plant produces yellow, fluffy flowers that are typical of the Fabaceae family, followed by pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
