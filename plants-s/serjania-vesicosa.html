Serjania vesicosa

Serjania vesicosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals.
