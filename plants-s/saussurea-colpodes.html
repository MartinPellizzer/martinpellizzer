Saussurea colpodes

Saussurea colpodes, commonly known as the narrowleafed saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are similar in appearance to those of other saffron plants. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing at high elevations with poor soil conditions.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
