Sedum lycopodiodes

Sedum lycopodiodes, commonly known as the mossy stonecrop, is a succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is native to rocky and arid environments in Europe and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Sedum lycopodiodes is often used in rock gardens due to its low maintenance and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
