Scorzonera coronopifolia var. undulata

Scorzonera coronopifolia var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust, fibrous root system and produces basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant blooms with yellow, daisy-like flowers that have a distinctive central disc and radiating petals. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
