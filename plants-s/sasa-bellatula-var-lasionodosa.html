Sasa bellatula var. lasionodosa

Sasa bellatula var. lasionodosa, commonly known as the broadleaf sasa, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form thick, attractive ground cover.
