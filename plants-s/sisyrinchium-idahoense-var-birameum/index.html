Sisyrinchium Idahoense Var. Birameum

Sisyrinchium idahoense var. birameum is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Idaho. It belongs to the daisy family and is known for its bright yellow blooms that appear in late spring. This variant is distinguished by its unique two-lobed petals, setting it apart from other members of its species. Due to its limited habitat and declining population, it is considered a conservation concern. The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems and is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
