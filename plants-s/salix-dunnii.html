Salix dunnii

Salix dunnii, commonly known as the Dunns willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the high Andes of South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It produces small, catkin-like flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, appearing in early spring.

Salix dunnii is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
