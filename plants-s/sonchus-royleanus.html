Sonchus royleanus

Sonchus royleanus, commonly known as the Indian sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, similar to dandelions, which are followed by small, spiky seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its alternate leaf arrangement and milky sap.
