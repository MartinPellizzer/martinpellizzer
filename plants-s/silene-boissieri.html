Silene boissieri

Silene boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene boissieri is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
