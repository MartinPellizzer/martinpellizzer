Schollera hispidula

Schollera hispidula, commonly known as the rough schollera, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Schollera hispidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
