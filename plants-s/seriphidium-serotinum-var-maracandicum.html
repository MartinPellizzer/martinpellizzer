Seriphidium serotinum var. maracandicum

Seriphidium serotinum var. maracandicum, commonly known as the Karakoram wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-gray hue. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
