Salvia lachnostachys

Salvia lachnostachys, commonly known as the wooly sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Salvia lachnostachys is also valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
