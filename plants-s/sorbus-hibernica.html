Sorbus hibernica

Sorbus hibernica, commonly known as the Irish whitebeam, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, white bark and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas and along riverbanks.

The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and turn bright yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value.
