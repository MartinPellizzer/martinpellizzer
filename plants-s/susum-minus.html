Susum minus

Susum minus, commonly known as the lesser sumach, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with 5 to 11 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Susum minus is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
