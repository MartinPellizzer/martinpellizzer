Stylidium thesioides

Stylidium thesioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, sun-like flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive, spurred lip.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
