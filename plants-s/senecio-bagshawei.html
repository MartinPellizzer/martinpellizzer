Senecio bagshawei

Senecio bagshawei, commonly known as the Bagshaw's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, dry fruits called cypselae.

Senecio bagshawei is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
