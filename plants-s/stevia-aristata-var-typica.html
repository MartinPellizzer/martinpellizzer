Stevia aristata var. typica

Stevia aristata var. typica, commonly known as the Andean stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in various food products. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it a valuable plant in traditional and modern herbal practices.
