Statice graeca var. sieberi

Statice graeca var. sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's sea thrift, is a hardy perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom in late summer and early autumn, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens and floral arrangements.
