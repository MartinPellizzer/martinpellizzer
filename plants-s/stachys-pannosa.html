Stachys pannosa

Stachys pannosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Stachys pannosa is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
