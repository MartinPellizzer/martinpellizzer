Schomburgkia × rechingeriana var. superba

Schomburgkia × rechingeriana var. superba, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a complex system of pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its striking floral display.
