Scrophularia galilaea

Scrophularia galilaea, commonly known as the Jerusalem figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, including Israel and surrounding areas.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, hairy stems that can reach up to 60 centimeters in height. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple in color.

Scrophularia galilaea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
