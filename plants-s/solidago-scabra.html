Solidago scabra

Solidago scabra, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Solidago scabra is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
