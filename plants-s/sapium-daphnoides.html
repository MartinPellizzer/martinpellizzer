Sapium daphnoides

Sapium daphnoides, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a reddish or bronze hue, especially in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by distinctive, inflated, orange to red seed pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and seeds contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.
