Schizanthus humilis

Schizanthus humilis, commonly known as the common beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Schizanthus humilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
