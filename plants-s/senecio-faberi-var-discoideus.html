Senecio faberi var. discoideus

Senecio faberi var. discoideus, commonly known as the "Saucer Daisy," is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact rosette of silver-gray, spoon-shaped leaves that form a dense, rounded structure. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
