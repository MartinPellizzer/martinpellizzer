Solidago guiradonis

Solidago guiradonis, commonly known as the Guiradoni goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Solidago guiradonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
