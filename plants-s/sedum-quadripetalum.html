Sedum quadripetalum

Sedum quadripetalum, commonly known as the four-petaled stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to rocky habitats in Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid environments. It is often found growing on cliffs, rocks, and in well-drained soils.

Sedum quadripetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
