Senecio nudibasis

Senecio nudibasis, commonly known as the naked-stem senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions. Its foliage is usually narrow and may have a slightly succulent texture.

Senecio nudibasis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
