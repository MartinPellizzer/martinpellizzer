Stigmaphyllon bonariense

Stigmaphyllon bonariense, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
