Sophora formosa

Sophora formosa, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces showy, pendulous racemes of pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for various pollinators.
