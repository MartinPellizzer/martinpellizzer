Scilla × allenii

Scilla × allenii, commonly known as the hybrid scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Scilla × allenii is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
