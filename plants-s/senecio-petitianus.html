Senecio petitianus

Senecio petitianus, commonly known as the Little's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, sandy soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
