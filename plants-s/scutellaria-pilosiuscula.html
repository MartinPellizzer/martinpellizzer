Scutellaria pilosiuscula

Scutellaria pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features small, tubular flowers that are often blue or purple in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its scientific name reflects its characteristic skullcap-like flower structure and hairy foliage.

Scutellaria pilosiuscula is valued in herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
