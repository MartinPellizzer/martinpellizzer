Saccifolium bandeirae

Saccifolium bandeirae, commonly known as the Brazilian saccifolium, is a rare flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, sac-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Saccifolium bandeirae is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
