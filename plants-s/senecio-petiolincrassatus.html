Senecio petiolincrassatus

Senecio petiolincrassatus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed senecio, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
