Solanum georgicum

Solanum georgicum, commonly known as the Georgian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its purple flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky areas and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it has been studied for potential uses in traditional medicine.
