Saccharum pappiferum

Saccharum pappiferum, commonly known as the hairy bamboo grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, hair-like structures along the nodes, which give it its common name. The plant is often found in wetland and riparian habitats, where it thrives in moist, tropical environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a slight waxy coating.

Saccharum pappiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for use in landscaping or traditional medicine.
