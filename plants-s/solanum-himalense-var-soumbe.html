Solanum himalense var. soumbe

Solanum himalense var. soumbe, commonly known as the Himalayan nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
