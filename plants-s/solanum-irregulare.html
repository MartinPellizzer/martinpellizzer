Solanum irregulare

Solanum irregulare, commonly known as the irregular nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and oval, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or edible purposes.
