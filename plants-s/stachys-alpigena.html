Stachys alpigena

Stachys alpigena, commonly known as alpine betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stachys alpigena is often used in rock gardens and alpine gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
