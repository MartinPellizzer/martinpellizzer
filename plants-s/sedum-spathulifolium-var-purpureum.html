Sedum spathulifolium var. purpureum

Sedum spathulifolium var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe.

It features fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically tinged with purple or reddish hues, especially under sunny conditions. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, pale yellow to orange flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
