Silene alexandri

Silene alexandri, commonly known as Alexandrian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene alexandri is often found in open fields and along coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
