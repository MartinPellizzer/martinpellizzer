Spiraea fortunei var. superflorens

Spiraea fortunei var. superflorens, commonly known as the snowball bush or Japanese snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy inflorescences. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to attract pollinators.
