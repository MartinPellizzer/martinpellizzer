Sinapis trilocularis

Sinapis trilocularis, commonly known as the three-chambered mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and it produces a strong, pungent odor when crushed.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological features and ecological adaptability.
