Sophronitis wittigiana var. longifolia

Sophronitis wittigiana var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchid species.
