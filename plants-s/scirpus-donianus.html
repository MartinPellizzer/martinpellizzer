Scirpus donianus

Scirpus donianus, commonly known as Donian's bulrush, is a tall, perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow waters, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

The flowering spikes are usually unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate spikes, a characteristic known as dioecious.
