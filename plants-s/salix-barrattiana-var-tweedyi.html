Salix barrattiana var. tweedyi

Salix barrattiana var. tweedyi, commonly known as Tweedy's willow, is a shrub-like species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or near water sources.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
