Stellaria graminea lusus erecta

Stellaria graminea lusus erecta, commonly known as the upright starwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and often recurved. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to wet environments, with a compact growth form and efficient water use.
