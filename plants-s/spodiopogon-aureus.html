Spodiopogon aureus

Spodiopogon aureus, commonly known as golden spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are yellow-green in color. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
