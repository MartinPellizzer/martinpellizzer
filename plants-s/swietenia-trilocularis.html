Swietenia trilocularis

Swietenia trilocularis, commonly known as the Honduras mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value and durable hardwood. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense, broad canopy. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release flat, winged seeds.
