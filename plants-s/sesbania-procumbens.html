Sesbania procumbens

Sesbania procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found growing in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments due to its drought tolerance. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Sesbania procumbens also plays a role in soil improvement due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
