Salacia tuberculata var. tuberculata

Salacia tuberculata var. tuberculata, commonly known as the mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in small tubercles. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, red berries that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
