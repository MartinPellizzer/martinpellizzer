Syngonanthus humboldtii var. parvus

Syngonanthus humboldtii var. parvus, commonly known as the dwarf marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in marshy or swampy habitats. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers clustered in a spike, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native wetland ecosystems.
