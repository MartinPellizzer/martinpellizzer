Secale cereale var. setosocaducum

Secale cereale var. setosocaducum, commonly known as the hairy wheatgrass, is a variant of wheatgrass characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and robust growth habit.

It is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its foliage is broad and serrated, with a bluish-green hue that contributes to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in marginal environments.
