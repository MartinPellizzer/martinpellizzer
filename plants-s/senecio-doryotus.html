Senecio doryotus

Senecio doryotus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with white ray florets. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

Senecio doryotus prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.
