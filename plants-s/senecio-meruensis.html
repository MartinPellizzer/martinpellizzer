Senecio meruensis

Senecio meruensis, commonly known as the Meru groundsel, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Senecio meruensis is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
