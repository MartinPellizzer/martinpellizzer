Stachys albanica

Stachys albanica, commonly known as the Balkan betony, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces spikes of purple or pink flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Stachys albanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
