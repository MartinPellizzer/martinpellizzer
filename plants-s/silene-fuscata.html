Silene fuscata

Silene fuscata, commonly known as the brown-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it often grows in moist, calcareous soils.

Silene fuscata is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
