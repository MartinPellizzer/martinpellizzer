Secale sylvestre

Secale sylvestre, commonly known as woodland ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
