Schrenkia fasciculata

Schrenkia fasciculata, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions through its deep root system and waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Schrenkia fasciculata is often found growing in rocky soils and is a symbol of resilience in extreme environments.
