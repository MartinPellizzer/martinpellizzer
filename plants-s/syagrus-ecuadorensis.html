Syagrus ecuadorensis

Syagrus ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuador palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the coastal regions of Ecuador.

It typically grows to a height of 10 to 15 meters and has a straight trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are large, feathery, and arranged in a crown at the top of the plant. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces dark blue, berry-like fruits that are edible.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and tropical climates, thriving in sandy soils and full sun.
