Scirpus savii f. todaroanus

Scirpus savii f. todaroanus, commonly known as the Todaro's sedge, is a variant of the sedge species Scirpus savii.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow waters, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
