Sorghum halepense var. minus

Sorghum halepense var. minus, commonly known as lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a weed in many regions.
