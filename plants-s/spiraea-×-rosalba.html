Spiraea × rosalba

Spiraea × rosalba, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with slender, arching branches that give it a graceful appearance. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Spiraea × rosalba thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
