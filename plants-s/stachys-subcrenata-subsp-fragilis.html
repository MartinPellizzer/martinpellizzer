Stachys subcrenata subsp. fragilis

Stachys subcrenata subsp.

fragilis, commonly known as silverweed or fragrant stachys, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are aromatic and can be used in culinary or medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
