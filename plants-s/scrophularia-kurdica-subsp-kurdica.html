Scrophularia kurdica subsp. kurdica

Scrophularia kurdica subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
