Sarracenia × melanorhoda

Sarracenia × melanorhoda, commonly known as the black-red swamp lantern, is a hybrid carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red and black-colored pitchers that are highly effective at trapping insects. The plant's leaves are modified into specialized trapping structures that secrete digestive enzymes to break down prey. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires high humidity and bright light for optimal growth.

Sarracenia × melanorhoda is a popular choice among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and fascinating ecological adaptations.
