Spathodea comosa

Spathodea comosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Spathodea comosa make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
