Securigera cretica

Securigera cretica, commonly known as Cretan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Securigera cretica is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
