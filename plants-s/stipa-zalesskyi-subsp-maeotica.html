Stipa zalesskyi subsp. maeotica

Stipa zalesskyi subsp.

maeotica, commonly known as the Crimean needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern regions of Europe and southwestern Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, steppes, and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
