Serapias lingua f. tunetana

Serapias lingua f. tunetana, commonly known as the Tunisian tongue orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. Native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Tunisia, this plant thrives in rocky and arid environments. The species is notable for its horticultural value and is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a delicate and endangered plant in its native range.
