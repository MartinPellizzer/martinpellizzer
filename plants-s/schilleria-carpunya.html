Schilleria carpunya

Schilleria carpunya, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Schilleria carpunya is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for respiratory ailments.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
