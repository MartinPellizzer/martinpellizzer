Senecio jacobaea var. cinereus

Senecio jacobaea var. cinereus, commonly known as the cinereous ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and grayish-green foliage. Native to Europe, this variant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic to livestock.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species.
