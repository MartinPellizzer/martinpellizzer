Sinapis pekinensis var. pe-tsai

Sinapis pekinensis var. pe-tsai, commonly known as Chinese mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a robust taproot and upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often have a slightly bitter taste.

The seeds are small, round, and used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.
