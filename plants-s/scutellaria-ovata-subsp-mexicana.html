Scutellaria ovata subsp. mexicana

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as Mexican skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, often arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity among gardeners and botanists alike.
