Spiranthes graminea var. walteri

Spiranthes graminea var. walteri, commonly known as the Walter's ladies' tresses, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiral arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant, white to pale green inflorescence.

This variety is often found in the eastern United States and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
