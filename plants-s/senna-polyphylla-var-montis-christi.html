Senna polyphylla var. montis-christi

Senna polyphylla var. montis-christi, commonly known as the Montezuma senna, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration in desert ecosystems.
