Senecio spathulifolius var. pratensis

Senecio spathulifolius var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. The leaves are typically spoon-shaped, which is reflected in its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
