Solanum lechnoviczii var. latifolium

Solanum lechnoviczii var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. It produces berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
