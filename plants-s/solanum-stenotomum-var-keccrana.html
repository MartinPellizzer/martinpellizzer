Solanum stenotomum var. keccrana

Solanum stenotomum var. keccrana, commonly known as the Keccrana nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. Its fruits are small berries that mature to a dark purple color, often containing toxic compounds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is typically avoided for consumption but studied for its potential medicinal compounds.
