Senegalia menabeensis

Senegalia menabeensis, commonly known as the Madagascar mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in dry forests and scrublands.

Senegalia menabeensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
