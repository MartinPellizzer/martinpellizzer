Scutellaria pinnatifida subsp. mucida

Scutellaria pinnatifida subsp.

mucida, commonly known as the sticky scutellaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal plants.

The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with a fine, sticky coating. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
