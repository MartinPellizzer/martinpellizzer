Sibthorpia triandra

Sibthorpia triandra, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in terminal panicles.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
