Shorea martiniana

Shorea martiniana, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has smooth, dark-brown bark and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

Shorea martiniana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
