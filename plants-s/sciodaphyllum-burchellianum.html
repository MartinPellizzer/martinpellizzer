Sciodaphyllum burchellianum

Sciodaphyllum burchellianum, commonly known as the Burchell's shade plant, is a unique evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and compact growth habit, which makes it well-suited for shaded environments. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, red berries.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with partial shade.
