Stenolobium caeruleum var. sericeum

Stenolobium caeruleum var. sericeum, commonly known as the silver-leaved glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
