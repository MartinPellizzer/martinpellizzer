Symphyotrichum lateriflorum var. horizontale

Symphyotrichum lateriflorum var. horizontale, commonly known as the horizontal blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that often lie horizontally along the ground, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
