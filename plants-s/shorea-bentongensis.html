Shorea bentongensis

Shorea bentongensis, commonly known as the Bentong rosewood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown timber. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Due to its high commercial value, it is also considered an endangered species.
