Syringa potaninii

Syringa potaninii, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. Syringa potaninii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
