Salvia tenuiflora

Salvia tenuiflora, commonly known as lemon grass or Mexican mint, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and spikes of purple or violet flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its fragrant foliage and ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Salvia tenuiflora is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
