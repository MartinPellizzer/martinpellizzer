Silene vittata

Silene vittata, commonly known as the spotted evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Silene vittata is a herbaceous perennial that produces capsules containing numerous small seeds.
