Schimpera arabica subsp. persica

Schimpera arabica subsp.

persica, commonly known as the Persian coffee plant, is a species native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its potential use in coffee production, though it is less commonly cultivated than other coffee species.
