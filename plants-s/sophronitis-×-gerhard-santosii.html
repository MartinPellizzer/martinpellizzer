Sophronitis × gerhard-santosii

Sophronitis × gerhard-santosii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Sophronitis clevelandii and Sophronitis stenophylla.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the humid cloud forests of Central America. The plant typically produces compact, upright pseudobulbs with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

This hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
