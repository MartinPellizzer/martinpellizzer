Sideritis salviifolia

Sideritis salviifolia, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a serrated margin and a fragrant aroma.

Sideritis salviifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
