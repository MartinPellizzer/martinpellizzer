Salvia mouretii

Salvia mouretii, commonly known as the Mouret's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Salvia mouretii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
