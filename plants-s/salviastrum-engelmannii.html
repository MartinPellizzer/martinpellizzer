Salviastrum engelmannii

Salviastrum engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's sage, is a member of the Lamiaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes and native plant gardens.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
