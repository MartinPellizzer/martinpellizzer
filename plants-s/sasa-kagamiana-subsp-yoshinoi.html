Sasa kagamiana subsp. yoshinoi

Sasa kagamiana subsp.

yoshinoi, commonly known as Yoshino bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring, followed by feathery seed heads.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
