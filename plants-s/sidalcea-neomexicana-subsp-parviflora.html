Sidalcea neomexicana subsp. parviflora

Sidalcea neomexicana subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered checkerspot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands or along streambanks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This subspecies is part of the legume family and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
