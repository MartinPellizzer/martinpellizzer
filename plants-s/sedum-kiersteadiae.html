Sedum kiersteadiae

Sedum kiersteadiae, commonly known as the Kierstead's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in arid conditions with minimal water requirements.
