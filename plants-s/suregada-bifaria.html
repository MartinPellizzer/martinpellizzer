Suregada bifaria

Suregada bifaria, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add visual interest to shaded areas.
