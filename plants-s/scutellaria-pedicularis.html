Scutellaria pedicularis

Scutellaria pedicularis, commonly known as the sticky scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a compact, low-growing habit. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stem. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Scutellaria pedicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
