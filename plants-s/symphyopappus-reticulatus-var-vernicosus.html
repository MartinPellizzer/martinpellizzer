Symphyopappus reticulatus var. vernicosus

Symphyopappus reticulatus var. vernicosus, commonly known as the roughleaf symphyopappus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, rough-textured leaves with a distinctive reticulate pattern, contributing to its common name. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
