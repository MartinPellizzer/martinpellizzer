Syzygium plumbeum

Syzygium plumbeum, commonly known as the blue-pear or blue-leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-blue bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

Syzygium plumbeum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
