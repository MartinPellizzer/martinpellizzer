Scrophularia olivieri

Scrophularia olivieri, commonly known as the olive scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia olivieri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
