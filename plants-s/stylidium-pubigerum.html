Stylidium pubigerum

Stylidium pubigerum, commonly known as the hairy sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble small sunflowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.
