Scirpus okuyamae

Scirpus okuyamae, commonly known as Okuyama's bulrush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Scirpus okuyamae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
