Schefflera minahasae

Schefflera minahasae, commonly known as the Minahasa umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like arrangement of large, leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by black, spherical berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
