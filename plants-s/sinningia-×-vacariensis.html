Sinningia × vacariensis

Sinningia × vacariensis, commonly known as the Violet Queen, is a hybrid orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking violet and purple flowers with intricate patterns resembling a queen's crown. The plant typically grows as a compact herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
