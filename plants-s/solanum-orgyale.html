Solanum orgyale

Solanum orgyale, commonly known as the Ethiopian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum orgyale is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It has been traditionally used in local medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
