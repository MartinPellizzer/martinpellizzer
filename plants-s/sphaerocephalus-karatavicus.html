Sphaerocephalus karatavicus

Sphaerocephalus karatavicus, commonly known as the Karatavicus sphaerocephalus, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, fleshy fruit that resembles a small, round berry. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water. Its leaves are often small and thick, reducing surface area for evaporation.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its distinct morphology and ecological niche.
