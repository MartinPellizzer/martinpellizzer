Succisa atropurpurea

Succisa atropurpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and a tall, branching stem that supports its showy inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its dark purple coloration, which is prominent in both its flowers and bracts.
