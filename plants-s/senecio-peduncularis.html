Senecio peduncularis

Senecio peduncularis, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.
