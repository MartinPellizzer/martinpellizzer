Syntherisma sanguinalis var. extensa

Syntherisma sanguinalis var. extensa, commonly known as the extended bloodroot, is a variant of the bloodroot plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a distinctive red-orange sap that exudes from its rhizome when cut. The plant features a single, palmate leaf that is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.

However, due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
