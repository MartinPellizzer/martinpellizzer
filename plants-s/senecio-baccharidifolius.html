Senecio baccharidifolius

Senecio baccharidifolius, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring and are followed by distinctive seed heads that resemble small stars.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
