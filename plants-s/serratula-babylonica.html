Serratula babylonica

Serratula babylonica, commonly known as Babylonian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Serratula babylonica is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
