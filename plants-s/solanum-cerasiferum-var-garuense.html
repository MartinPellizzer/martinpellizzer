Solanum cerasiferum var. garuense

Solanum cerasiferum var. garuense, commonly known as the Garuense cherry tomato, is a variant of the cherry tomato plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, round fruits resembling cherries, typically ranging in color from red to orange. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also edible, often used in local cuisines for its sweet and tangy flavor.
