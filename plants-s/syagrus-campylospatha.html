Syagrus campylospatha

Syagrus campylospatha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed palm, is a species of palm native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and pinnate leaves that are narrow and often drooping. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. Syagrus campylospatha is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species and is often found in conservation areas.
