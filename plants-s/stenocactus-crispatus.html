Stenocactus crispatus

Stenocactus crispatus, commonly known as the brittle cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and protect it from herbivores.

Stenocactus crispatus is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
