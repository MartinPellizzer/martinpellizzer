Sisymbrium crassifolium f. bicornis

Sisymbrium crassifolium f. bicornis, commonly known as the two-lobed broomrape, is a botanical variant of the annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its reduced leaves and a highly branched, yellow-flowered inflorescence. The plant is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially relies on host plants for nutrients through its root system. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically pollinated by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations and parasitic lifestyle.
