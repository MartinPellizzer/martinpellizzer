Solanum gracile var. microphyllum

Solanum gracile var. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of South America.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, giving it its varietal name "microphyllum.". The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellowish as they mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
