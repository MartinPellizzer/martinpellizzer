Senghasia maculosa

Senghasia maculosa, commonly known as the spotted apple or Malabar apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with a distinctive white or yellowish spot near the base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are green when unripe and turn red when mature.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
