Silene bornmuelleri

Silene bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Silene bornmuelleri is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
