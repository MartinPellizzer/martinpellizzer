Silene italica subsp. mellifera

Silene italica subsp.

mellifera, commonly known as the sweetweed or honeyplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils. Silene italica subsp.

mellifera is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
