Sorghum nutans subsp. linnaeanum

Sorghum nutans subsp.

linnaeanum, commonly known as the slender sorghum, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often used in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop for livestock.
