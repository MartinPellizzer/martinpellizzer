Spiraea myrtilloides

Spiraea myrtilloides, commonly known as the sweetmeadow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It features arching stems that produce clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Spiraea myrtilloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various pollinators.
