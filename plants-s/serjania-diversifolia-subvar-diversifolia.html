Serjania diversifolia subvar. diversifolia

Serjania diversifolia subvar. diversifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-mato, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
