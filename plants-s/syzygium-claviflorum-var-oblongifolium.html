Syzygium claviflorum var. oblongifolium

Syzygium claviflorum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf clove.

This variant is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving off a strong clove-like aroma. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species within the Syzygium genus.
