Saxifraga scardica subsp. korabensis

Saxifraga scardica subsp.

korabensis, commonly known as the Korab saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing in crevices and on steep slopes.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, typically between June and August. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and alpine plant collections.
