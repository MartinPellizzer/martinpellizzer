Sporobolus amaliae

Sporobolus amaliae, commonly known as Amalia's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Poaceae family and exhibits adaptations for drought tolerance and efficient water use.
