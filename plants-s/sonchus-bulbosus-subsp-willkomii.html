Sonchus bulbosus subsp. willkomii

Sonchus bulbosus subsp.

willkomii, commonly known as the bulbous sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a short, stout stem that bears a single flower head.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is known for its hairy leaves and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
