Salvia fusca

Salvia fusca, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.

Salvia fusca is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
