Scabiosa persica var. rosea

Scabiosa persica var. rosea, commonly known as the Persian scabious or rosea scabious, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Persia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white to rose-colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are typically hairy and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa persica var. rosea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
