Stenocactus lexarzae

Stenocactus lexarzae, commonly known as the Mexican spineless cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is a small, cylindrical cactus with a ribbed structure and is typically covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
