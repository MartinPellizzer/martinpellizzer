Staehelina rosmarinifolia

Staehelina rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rock rose or rosemary plant, is a succulent shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, often in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves are sometimes used in culinary and medicinal applications.
