Stephanotis volubilis

Stephanotis volubilis, commonly known as the sweet screwbean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green, bean-like fruits that turn yellow when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Stephanotis volubilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and pleasant fragrance.
