Sempervivum tectorum subsp. tectorum

Sempervivum tectorum subsp.

tectorum, commonly known as the houseleek, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe. It features dense rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are thick and covered in a white powdery coating called farina.

The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks bearing small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or container planting.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including cold winters.
