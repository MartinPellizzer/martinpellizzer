Scleria hirtella var. pauciciliata

Scleria hirtella var. pauciciliata, commonly known as the woolly spearplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is often found in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
