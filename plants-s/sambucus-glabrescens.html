Sambucus glabrescens

Sambucus glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It typically grows in moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Sambucus glabrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
