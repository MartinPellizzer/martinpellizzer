Stelis phaeantha

Stelis phaeantha, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Stelis phaeantha is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
