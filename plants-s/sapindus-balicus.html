Sapindus balicus

Sapindus balicus, commonly known as the soapberry tree, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries containing toxic seeds.

Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
