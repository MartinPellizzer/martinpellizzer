Solanum decorticatum

Solanum decorticatum, commonly known as the naked-stem nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often hairless stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, shiny seeds.

Solanum decorticatum is typically found in dry, open habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
