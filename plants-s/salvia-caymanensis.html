Salvia caymanensis

Salvia caymanensis, commonly known as the Cayman sage, is a flowering plant native to the Cayman Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value and unique appearance make it a popular choice for gardens in its native region.
