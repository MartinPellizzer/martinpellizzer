Sapranthus violaceus

Sapranthus violaceus, commonly known as the violet tansy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Sapranthus violaceus is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
