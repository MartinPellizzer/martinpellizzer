Sorbaria assurgens

Sorbaria assurgens, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in a silvery-gray pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Sorbaria assurgens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local wildlife.
