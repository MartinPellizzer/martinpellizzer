Samanea simabifolia

Samanea simabifolia, commonly known as the yellow shower tree, is a tropical legume native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and its striking yellow flowers that bloom in drooping clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Samanea simabifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
