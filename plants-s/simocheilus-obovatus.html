Simocheilus obovatus

Simocheilus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-ovate water plant, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem.

It is native to freshwater environments in southern Africa, where it thrives in slow-moving streams and ponds. The plant has a branched stem that can grow up to several meters in length, supporting its floating or submerged growth habit. Its leaves are smooth and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its aquatic habitat.

The flowers, when present, are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in the summer months.
