Scirpus bicolor var. virens

Scirpus bicolor var. virens, commonly known as the green-flowered bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are typically found in the upper part of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing from the base of the plant.

Scirpus bicolor var. virens thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes and pond edges.
