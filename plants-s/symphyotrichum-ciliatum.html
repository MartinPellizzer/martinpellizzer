Symphyotrichum ciliatum

Symphyotrichum ciliatum, commonly known as the silky aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a silvery sheen, while its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

It blooms in the fall, producing clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
