Senecio trifurcatus

Senecio trifurcatus, commonly known as the three-branched groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio trifurcatus is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to spread through stolons, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
