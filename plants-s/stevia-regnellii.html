Stevia regnellii

Stevia regnellii, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from compounds called steviol glycosides, which are found in its leaves. Stevia regnellii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.

It is widely used as a natural sweetener and has gained popularity as a sugar alternative in health-conscious diets.
