Sida hastata

Sida hastata, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces bright orange to red composite flowers, which are actually clusters of smaller florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Sida hastata is also valued for its medicinal properties and ability to attract pollinators.
