Salvia rubropunctata

Salvia rubropunctata, commonly known as the red-spotted sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with red spots, which are clustered in whorls along the stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia rubropunctata is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
