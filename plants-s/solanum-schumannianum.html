Solanum schumannianum

Solanum schumannianum, commonly known as the Schumacher's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, and the plant can grow up to 2 meters in height.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
