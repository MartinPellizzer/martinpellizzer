Synostemon kakadu

Synostemon kakadu, commonly known as the Kakadu plum, is a small evergreen shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used for centuries by Indigenous Australians for food and medicinal purposes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
