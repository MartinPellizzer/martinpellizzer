Stylidium piliferum var. minor

Stylidium piliferum var. minor, commonly known as the small hairy sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its compact, hairy stems and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in well-drained conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive hood-like structure, and it blooms during the spring and summer months.

It is a short-lived perennial and is often found in coastal or inland regions of Australia.
