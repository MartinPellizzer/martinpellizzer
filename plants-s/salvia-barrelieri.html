Salvia barrelieri

Salvia barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's sage, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
