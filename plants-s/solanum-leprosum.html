Solanum leprosum

Solanum leprosum, commonly known as the leprose nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its berries are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe, containing toxic alkaloids.

Due to its poisonous properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
