Salix bicolor subsp. bicolor

Salix bicolor subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the white willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth margin and a pale underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers, streams, and wetlands.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
