Salix fissa

Salix fissa, commonly known as the Sierra willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typically male and female on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and mountainous areas.

Salix fissa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
