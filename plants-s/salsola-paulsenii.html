Salsola paulsenii

Salsola paulsenii, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Salsola paulsenii is well adapted to harsh environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and tolerate high temperatures.

It often grows on sandy soils and is sometimes used for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
