Sorghum papyrascens

Sorghum papyrascens, commonly known as the paper sorghum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically green or pale in color. Its fibrous stem is highly valued for its strength and flexibility, making it useful in traditional crafts and construction.

Sorghum papyrascens is also an important species for studying plant adaptation to arid environments.
