Stipa mayeri

Stipa mayeri, commonly known as Mayer's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fine texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Stipa mayeri is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant, airy foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
