Saxifraga cotyledon var. maculata

Saxifraga cotyledon var. maculata, commonly known as the "peacock plant," is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves with a distinctive pattern of red and purple markings, giving it its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its compact size and vibrant foliage, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
