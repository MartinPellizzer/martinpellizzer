Sericolea werneri

Sericolea werneri, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Sericolea werneri thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
