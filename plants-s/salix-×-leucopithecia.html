Salix × leucopithecia

Salix × leucopithecia, commonly known as the white willow, is a hybrid species within the willow family (Salicaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and finely toothed leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white bloom. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The bark is smooth and pale, and the plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its graceful appearance.
