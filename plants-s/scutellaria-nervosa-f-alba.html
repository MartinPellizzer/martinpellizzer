Scutellaria nervosa f. alba

Scutellaria nervosa f. alba, commonly known as white nerve scutellaria, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, visible veins, which give it its common name.

It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.
