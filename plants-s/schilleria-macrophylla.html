Schilleria macrophylla

Schilleria macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved schilleria, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in subtropical and tropical regions.
