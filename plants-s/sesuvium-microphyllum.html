Sesuvium microphyllum

Sesuvium microphyllum, commonly known as dwarf sesuvium, is a succulent plant native to arid and coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
