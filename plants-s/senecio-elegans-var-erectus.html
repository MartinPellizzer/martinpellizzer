Senecio elegans var. erectus

Senecio elegans var. erectus, commonly known as the upright blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
