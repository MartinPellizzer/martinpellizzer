Stemmadenia tomentosa

Stemmadenia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly stemmadenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany.
