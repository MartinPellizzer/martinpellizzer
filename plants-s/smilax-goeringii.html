Smilax goeringii

Smilax goeringii, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries, which are an important food source for wildlife. Smilax goeringii thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a distinctive member of the Smilacaceae family.
