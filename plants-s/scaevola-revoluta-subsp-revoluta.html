Scaevola revoluta subsp. revoluta

Scaevola revoluta subsp.

revoluta, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of eastern Australia. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy surface.

The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments.

This species is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
