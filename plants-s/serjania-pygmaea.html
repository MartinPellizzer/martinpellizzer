Serjania pygmaea

Serjania pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf serjania, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
