Serruria subsericea

Serruria subsericea, commonly known as the silver cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, silver-blue leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
