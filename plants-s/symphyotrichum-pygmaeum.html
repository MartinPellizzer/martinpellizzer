Symphyotrichum pygmaeum

Symphyotrichum pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. This low-growing perennial typically reaches a height of only a few inches, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small blue to purple flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and sandy soils.

The plant is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
