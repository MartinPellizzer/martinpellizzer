Smilax aspera var. maculata

Smilax aspera var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted smilax, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in sharp, spiny projections. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough, textured surface.

This species is often found in woodland edges and moist, shaded environments.
