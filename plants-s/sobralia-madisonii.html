Sobralia madisonii

Sobralia madisonii, commonly known as Madison's sobralia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, specifically Jamaica.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences. The plant features vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
