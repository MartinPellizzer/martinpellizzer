Scirpus eriophorus

Scirpus eriophorus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it spread vegetatively through wetland habitats.

Scirpus eriophorus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
