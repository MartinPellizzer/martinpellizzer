Scutellaria wrightii

Scutellaria wrightii, commonly known as Wright's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval and serrated, and it often grows in prairie or open woodland habitats.

Wright's skullcap is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
