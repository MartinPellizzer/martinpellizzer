Sida amplexicaulis

Sida amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Sida amplexicaulis is known for its fibrous stems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
