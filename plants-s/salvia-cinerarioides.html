Salvia cinerarioides

Salvia cinerarioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia cinerarioides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
