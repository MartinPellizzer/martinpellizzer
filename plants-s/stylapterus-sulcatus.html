Stylapterus sulcatus

Stylapterus sulcatus, commonly known as the sulcate leaf beetle, is a species of leaf beetle belonging to the family Chrysomelidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated body and intricate patterns on its elytra, which often feature a series of longitudinal grooves. This beetle is typically found in forested areas where it feeds on the leaves of specific host plants, primarily within the family Rosaceae. Its life cycle includes egg, larval, pupal, and adult stages, with the larvae often exhibiting different coloration compared to the adults.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a host for this beetle, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
