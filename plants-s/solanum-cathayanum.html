Solanum cathayanum

Solanum cathayanum, commonly known as the Chinese nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
