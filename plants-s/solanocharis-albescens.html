Solanocharis albescens

Solanocharis albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or pale flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as woodlands or stream banks. Its flowers bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators like moths.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
