Setaria vulpina

Setaria vulpina, commonly known as foxsetail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spikelike inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often considered a weed. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray sheen, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Setaria vulpina is an annual plant that completes its life cycle within a single growing season.
