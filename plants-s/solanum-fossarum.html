Solanum fossarum

Solanum fossarum, commonly known as the Italian nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature, containing toxic compounds such as solanine. Solanum fossarum thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
