Spiraea blumei var. rotundifolia

Spiraea blumei var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to round in shape, with a smooth, glossy surface, giving the plant a refined appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
