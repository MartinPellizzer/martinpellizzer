Saponaria bucharica

Saponaria bucharica, commonly known as the Bukharan soapwort, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its roots contain saponins, which give it its name and contribute to its historical use in soap-making.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.
