Schizonotus arboreus var. glabratus

Schizonotus arboreus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smoothleaf tree heath, is a shrub or small tree native to certain regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
