Senecio neoheimii

Senecio neoheimii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Senecio neoheimii is generally hardy and adapted to cold, dry environments.
