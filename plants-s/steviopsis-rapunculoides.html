Steviopsis rapunculoides

Steviopsis rapunculoides, commonly known as the mountain stevia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves, which contain compounds similar to those found in the well-known stevia plant. It typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

Steviopsis rapunculoides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
