Sorghum nitidum subsp. dichroanthum

Sorghum nitidum subsp.

dichroanthum, commonly known as the two-colored sorghum, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of Africa. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its distinctive two-colored inflorescences, which typically display a mix of red and yellow hues.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open grasslands or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and agricultural studies.
