Siphonoglossa sessilis var. calcarea

Siphonoglossa sessilis var. calcarea, commonly known as the calcareous siphonoglossa, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This variety is distinguished by its calcareous (lime-rich) tissues, which contribute to its unique ecological niche.
