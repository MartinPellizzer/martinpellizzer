Santiria ebo

Santiria ebo, commonly known as African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and produces yellow to orange flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, elongated pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Santiria ebo is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
