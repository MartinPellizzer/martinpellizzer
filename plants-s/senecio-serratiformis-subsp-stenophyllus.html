Senecio serratiformis subsp. stenophyllus

Senecio serratiformis subsp.

stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features narrow, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
