Senecio invalidus

Senecio invalidus, commonly known as the invalid's sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for treating respiratory ailments.
