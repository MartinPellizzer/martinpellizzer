Solanum setosum

Solanum setosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name, and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. The berries are usually green when unripe and turn red when mature, containing seeds inside.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
