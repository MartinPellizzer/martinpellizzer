Stapelia paniculata subsp. scitula

Stapelia paniculata subsp.

scitula, commonly known as the Ghost Stapelia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its unique, waxy, and often pale green or yellowish leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive, almost fleshy appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
