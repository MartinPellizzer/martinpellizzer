Stemonurus scorpioides

Stemonurus scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpionweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping or as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
