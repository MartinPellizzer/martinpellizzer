Syngonanthus fertilis var. glandulosus

Syngonanthus fertilis var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular-flowered tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and aid in water retention. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woods.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
