Sanguisorba parviflora var. coccinea

Sanguisorba parviflora var. coccinea, commonly known as red dock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
