Stipa brevipes

Stipa brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted inflorescence and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are small and dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Stipa brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
