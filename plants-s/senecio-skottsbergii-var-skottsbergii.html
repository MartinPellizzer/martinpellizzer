Senecio skottsbergii var. skottsbergii

Senecio skottsbergii var. skottsbergii, commonly known as the Skottsberg's groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
