Silene tatarica var. foliosa

Silene tatarica var. foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its showy, showy inflorescences.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
