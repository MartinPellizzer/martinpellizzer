Schoenoxiphium fragile

Schoenoxiphium fragile, commonly known as the fragrant mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
