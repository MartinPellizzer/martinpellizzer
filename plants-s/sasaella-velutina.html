Sasaella velutina

Sasaella velutina, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, matted growth habit and its distinctive, soft, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and swampy areas.

Sasaella velutina plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
