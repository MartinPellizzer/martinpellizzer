Saussurea schischkinii

Saussurea schischkinii, commonly known as the Siberian mountain thistle, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Saussurea schischkinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
