Stelis sanguinea

Stelis sanguinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with a reddish hue, especially during the flowering season. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and specialized root system.
