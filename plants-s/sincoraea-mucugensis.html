Sincoraea mucugensis

Sincoraea mucugensis, commonly known as the Amazonian grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small fauna and contributing to the structural complexity of the forest understory.
