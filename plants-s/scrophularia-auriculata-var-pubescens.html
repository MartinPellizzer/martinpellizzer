Scrophularia auriculata var. pubescens

Scrophularia auriculata var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent auriculate scrophularia, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine pubescence, which gives the variety its common name.

This species is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
