Sinocalamus affinis

Sinocalamus affinis, commonly known as the bamboo of China, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, linear leaves. This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its culms are often used in construction and as a source of fiber.

The plant plays an important role in traditional Chinese culture and is also valued for its ecological benefits.
