Strophanthus puberulus

Strophanthus puberulus, commonly known as the velvet milkvine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, clustered in loose panicles, and are followed by distinctive, inflated, seed-containing pods.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
