Scorzoneroides autumnalis subsp. pratensis

Scorzoneroides autumnalis subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as the meadow scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flowers arranged in a loose raceme.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
