Syzygium wilsonii subsp. epigaeum

Syzygium wilsonii subsp.

epigaeum, commonly known as the mountain clove, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, clove-like flowers that are typically pink to red in color.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for horticultural and ecological purposes.
