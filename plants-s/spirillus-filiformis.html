Spirillus filiformis

Spirillus filiformis, commonly known as the spiral filament moss, is a unique species of liverwort characterized by its spiral-shaped growth pattern.

Spirillus filiformis is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its resilience to desiccation.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for studying microhabitat conditions in temperate regions.
