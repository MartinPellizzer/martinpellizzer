Solanum melancholicum

Solanum melancholicum, commonly known as the melancholy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated but is of interest in botanical studies.
