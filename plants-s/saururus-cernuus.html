Saururus cernuus

Saururus cernuus, commonly known as threadleaf bladdernut, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a distinctive thread-like appearance on the underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in early spring.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, winged seeds.
