Scutellaria orientalis subsp. santolinoides

Scutellaria orientalis subsp.

santolinoides, commonly known as the Santolinoides blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
