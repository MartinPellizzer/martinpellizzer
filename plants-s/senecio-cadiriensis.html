Senecio cadiriensis

Senecio cadiriensis, commonly known as the Cape Town groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Senecio cadiriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
