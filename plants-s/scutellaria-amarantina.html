Scutellaria amarantina

Scutellaria amarantina, commonly known as the scarlet skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with two lips, and are typically a striking blue or violet color.

Scutellaria amarantina is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
