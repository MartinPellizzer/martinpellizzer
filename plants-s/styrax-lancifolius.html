Styrax lancifolius

Styrax lancifolius, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and small, dark purple fruits that contain seeds.

Styrax lancifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
