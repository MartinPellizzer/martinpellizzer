Setaria pachystachya

Setaria pachystachya, commonly known as giant foxtail, is a perennial grass native to Africa but widely naturalized in other parts of the world.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stature, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and its dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, beige to brown florets that are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Setaria pachystachya is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth and adaptability.
