Solidago speciosa var. typica

Solidago speciosa var. typica, commonly known as showy goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, composite flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woods, meadows, and along stream banks. Its showy inflorescences attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
