Stylosanthes guianensis

Stylosanthes guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan stylo, is a perennial legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinctive shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, flat pods containing several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a valuable forage plant in dry regions.

Stylosanthes guianensis is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
