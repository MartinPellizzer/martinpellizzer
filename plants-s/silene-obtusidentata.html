Silene obtusidentata

Silene obtusidentata, commonly known as the blunt-toothed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive bluish-green color.

Silene obtusidentata is an herbaceous perennial that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.
