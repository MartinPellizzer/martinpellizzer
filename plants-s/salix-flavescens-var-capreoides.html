Salix flavescens var. capreoides

Salix flavescens var. capreoides, commonly known as the yellow willow or capreoid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, often riparian habitats, such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant has slender, flexible branches and is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage, which turns reddish in the fall. Its catkins are prominent, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
