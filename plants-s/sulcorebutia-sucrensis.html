Sulcorebutia sucrensis

Sulcorebutia sucrensis, commonly known as the Sucrensis Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom from the areoles along its stems. It is a drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens and collections.
