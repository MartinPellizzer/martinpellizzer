Stipa pringlei var. lemmonii

Stipa pringlei var. lemmonii, commonly known as the Lemmon's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in dry landscapes.
