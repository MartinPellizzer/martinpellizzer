Sorbus gauckleri

Sorbus gauckleri, commonly known as the Sierra snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The Sierra snowberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
