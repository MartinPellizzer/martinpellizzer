Sasa velutinosa

Sasa velutinosa, commonly known as velvet bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stalks covered in a fine, silky layer of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, velvety texture that gives it its common name.

Sasa velutinosa is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and structural form.
