Salix × paradaphnoides

Salix × paradaphnoides, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, often giving it a graceful and ornamental look. The leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, or wetlands.

This species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to stabilize soil along waterways.
