Scutellaria russeliifolia

Scutellaria russeliifolia, commonly known as the Russel's blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Scutellaria russeliifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
