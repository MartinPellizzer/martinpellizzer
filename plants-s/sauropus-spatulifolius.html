Sauropus spatulifolius

Sauropus spatulifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved sauropus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender peduncles. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
