Senecio kahuzicus

Senecio kahuzicus, commonly known as the Kahuzi Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
