Syzygium calcimontanum

Syzygium calcimontanum, commonly known as the limestone apple or calcimontanum apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round, and have a crisp, juicy texture with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Syzygium calcimontanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in local communities.
