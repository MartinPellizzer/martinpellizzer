Serratula lanata

Serratula lanata, commonly known as the woolly sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Serratula lanata is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
