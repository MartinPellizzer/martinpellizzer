Stipa orientalis var. azutavica

Stipa orientalis var. azutavica, commonly known as the Azotavica feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its elegant, wind-blown foliage.
