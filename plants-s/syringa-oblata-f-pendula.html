Syringa oblata f. pendula

Syringa oblata f. pendula, commonly known as weeping bush honeysuckle, is a cultivar of the shrub Syringa oblata.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer. The plant is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its unique, drooping form makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and hedges.
