Satureja guamaniensis

Satureja guamaniensis, commonly known as the Guamani thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Satureja guamaniensis is a hardy species that thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soil.
