Solanum coagulans var. ochraceum

Solanum coagulans var. ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species characterized by its distinctive yellow to orange fruiting bodies.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit, which resembles small, elongated eggs, turns from green to yellow or orange as it matures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique coloration and culinary potential.
