Spiranthes sunii

Spiranthes sunii, commonly known as the sun spire orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its unique, spiral-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
