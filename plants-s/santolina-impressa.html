Santolina impressa

Santolina impressa, commonly known as blue fescue, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, grass-like foliage that is typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by fluffy seed heads. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Santolina impressa is also valued for its aromatic scent and ability to form compact, decorative clumps.
