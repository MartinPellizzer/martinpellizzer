Scorzonera codringtonii

Scorzonera codringtonii, commonly known as the Codrington's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in the summer months. Scorzonera codringtonii is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
