Sideritis × puiggariana var. brevispicata

Sideritis × puiggariana var. brevispicata, commonly known as the short-spurred ironweed, is a hybrid plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as coastal areas or scrublands. It is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit and is well adapted to dry and sunny conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
