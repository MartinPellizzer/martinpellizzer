Spilanthes arnicoides var. grisea

Spilanthes arnicoides var. grisea, commonly known as gray spilanthes, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grayish-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late summer. It is known for its strong, pungent odor and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

The species is part of the genus Spilanthes, which includes several plants with similar characteristics and uses.
