Silene microsperma

Silene microsperma, commonly known as the small-seeded catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
