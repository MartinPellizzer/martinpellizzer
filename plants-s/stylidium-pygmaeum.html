Stylidium pygmaeum

Stylidium pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf stick orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact stature and unique floral structure. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and usually yellow or greenish, with a distinctive spurred petal.

Stylidium pygmaeum is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions, showcasing specialized root systems and water-conserving mechanisms.
