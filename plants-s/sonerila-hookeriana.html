Sonerila hookeriana

Sonerila hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's sonerila, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually small, clustered in inflorescences, and display a range of colors from white to pink or violet.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal in the natural habitat.
