Salix × sugayana

Salix × sugayana, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and has simple, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers, streams, or wetlands.

Salix × sugayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
