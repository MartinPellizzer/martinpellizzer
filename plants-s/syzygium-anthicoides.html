Syzygium anthicoides

Syzygium anthicoides, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. Syzygium anthicoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
