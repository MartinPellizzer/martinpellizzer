Salmia incisa

Salmia incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf salvia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. Salmia incisa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
