Solidago rugosa var. sphagnophila

Solidago rugosa var. sphagnophila, commonly known as the sphagnophila goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs or wetlands.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
