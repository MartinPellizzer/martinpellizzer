Saxifraga × pseudokotschyi

Saxifraga × pseudokotschyi, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Saxifraga × pseudokotschyi is often cultivated in rock gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
