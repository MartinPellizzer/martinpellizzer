Sisymbrium rubescens

Sisymbrium rubescens, commonly known as the redstem shepherd's purse, is an annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and can be hairy, while the fruit is a distinctive triangular pod.

Sisymbrium rubescens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
