Schisandra japonica

Schisandra japonica, commonly known as Japanese five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, red berries that turn dark purple when mature. The plant is known for its aromatic, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Schisandra japonica thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Its berries are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their complex flavor profile and potential health benefits.
