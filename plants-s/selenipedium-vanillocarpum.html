Selenipedium vanillocarpum

Selenipedium vanillocarpum, commonly known as the vanilla orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and large, showy flowers. The plant is notable for its unique vanilla-scented flowers, which give it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to bloom.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species in its native range.
