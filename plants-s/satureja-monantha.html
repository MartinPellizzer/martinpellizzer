Satureja monantha

Satureja monantha, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain savory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Satureja monantha is valued for its medicinal properties and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
