Salsola androssowii subsp. deserticola

Salsola androssowii subsp.

deserticola, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it access underground water. Its leaves are often coated with a waxy layer to reduce water loss, a feature common in xerophytic plants.

It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
