Samolus africanus

Samolus africanus, commonly known as the African samolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

Samolus africanus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
