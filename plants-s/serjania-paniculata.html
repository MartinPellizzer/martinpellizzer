Serjania paniculata

Serjania paniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or *pau-rosa*, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaneae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, aromatic wood. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The species is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
