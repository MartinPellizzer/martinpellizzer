Schizophragma integrifolium var. denticulatum

Schizophragma integrifolium var. denticulatum, commonly known as the denticulate honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name, and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white to pink. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade. It is also valued for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
