Salix turczaninowii

Salix turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow willow, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are followed by narrow, elongated fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
