Stipa barchanica

Stipa barchanica, commonly known as the Barchan grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted clumps and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate extreme drought conditions.

Stipa barchanica plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
