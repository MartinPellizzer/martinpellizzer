Solanum acuminatum var. viridiflorum

Solanum acuminatum var. viridiflorum, commonly known as the greenflower nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant features elongated, hairy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically small berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
