Saxifraga schleicheri

Saxifraga schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Saxifraga schleicheri is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
