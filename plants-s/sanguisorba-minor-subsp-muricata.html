Sanguisorba minor subsp. muricata

Sanguisorba minor subsp.

muricata, commonly known as the redroot dropwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces distinctive, spiky seed heads that persist into autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. Sanguisorba minor subsp.

muricata is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
