Sambucus cochinchinensis

Sambucus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Adoxaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.

Sambucus cochinchinensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
