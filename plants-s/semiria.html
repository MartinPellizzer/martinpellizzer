Semiria

Semiria, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and can grow up to 15 meters tall. Its flowers are rich in nectar, attracting various pollinators like bees and birds.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.
