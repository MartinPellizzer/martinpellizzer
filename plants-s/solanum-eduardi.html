Solanum eduardi

Solanum eduardi, commonly known as Eduardo's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum eduardi is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique fruiting habit.
