Senecio longipetiolatus

Senecio longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branched stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
