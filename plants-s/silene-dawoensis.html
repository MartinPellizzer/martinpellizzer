Silene dawoensis

Silene dawoensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian campion, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 2,500 meters. It has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
