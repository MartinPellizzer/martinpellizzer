Sinapistrum lanatum

Sinapistrum lanatum, commonly known as the woolly sinapistrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, fuzzy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage.

Due to its low maintenance requirements, it is popular among gardeners seeking to add texture and visual interest to their landscapes.
