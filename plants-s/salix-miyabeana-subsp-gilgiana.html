Salix miyabeana subsp. gilgiana

Salix miyabeana subsp.

gilgiana, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green on top and pale underneath.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is commonly used in landscaping and riparian restoration projects.
