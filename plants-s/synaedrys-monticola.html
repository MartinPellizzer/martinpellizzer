Synaedrys monticola

Synaedrys monticola, commonly known as the mountain synedrella, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a compact cluster, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
