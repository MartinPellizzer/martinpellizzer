Scorzonella glauca

Scorzonella glauca, commonly known as blue scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, blue or purplish flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Scorzonella glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
