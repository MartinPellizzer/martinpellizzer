Spilotantha graminea

Spilotantha graminea, commonly known as the narrowleaf spilotantha, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
