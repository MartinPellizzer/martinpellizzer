Solanum andigenum f. nodosum

Solanum andigenum f. nodosum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and is often studied for its potential medicinal properties.
