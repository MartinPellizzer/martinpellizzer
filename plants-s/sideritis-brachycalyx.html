Sideritis brachycalyx

Sideritis brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx ironwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

Sideritis brachycalyx is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
