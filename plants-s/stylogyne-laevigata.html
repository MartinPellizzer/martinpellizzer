Stylogyne laevigata

Stylogyne laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has slender, often branching stems that are covered in a smooth, waxy coating. Its leaves are small, oval, and arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.

Stylogyne laevigata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
