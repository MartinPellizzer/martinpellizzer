Sideritis lurida var. relegata

Sideritis lurida var. relegata, commonly known as the Balkan ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
