Senecio momordicifolius

Senecio momordicifolius, commonly known as the bitterleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble those of a bitter melon. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
