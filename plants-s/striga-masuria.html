Striga masuria

Striga masuria, commonly known as the common witch-hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits that split open to release seeds. Striga masuria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
