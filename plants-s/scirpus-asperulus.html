Scirpus asperulus

Scirpus asperulus, commonly known as rough bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered with small, backward-pointing bristles. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
