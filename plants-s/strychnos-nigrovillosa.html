Strychnos nigrovillosa

Strychnos nigrovillosa, commonly known as the black hairy strangler, is a woody shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of traditional medicine.
