Sida rhombifolia var. serratifolia

Sida rhombifolia var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species with alternate, ovate to rhombic leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, round capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
