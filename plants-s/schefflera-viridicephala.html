Schefflera viridicephala

Schefflera viridicephala, commonly known as the Emerald-headed Schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and distinctive foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite or whorled in arrangement. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its lush appearance.

Schefflera viridicephala is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
