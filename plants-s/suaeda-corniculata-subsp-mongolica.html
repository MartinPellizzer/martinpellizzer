Suaeda corniculata subsp. mongolica

Suaeda corniculata subsp.

mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian sea barley, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments. It is native to parts of Central Asia, including Mongolia and northern China.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt lakes, and saline soils, showcasing its tolerance to high salinity. Its small, fleshy leaves and clustered flowers are characteristic of the Amaranthaceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is of interest for ecological and agricultural studies.
