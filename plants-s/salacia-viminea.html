Salacia viminea

Salacia viminea, commonly known as the climbing salacia, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that climb using tendrils, and its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

Salacia viminea is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
