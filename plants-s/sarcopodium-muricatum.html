Sarcopodium muricatum

Sarcopodium muricatum, commonly known as the passion fruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It produces vibrant, edible berries with a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces striking flowers with a unique structure resembling a heart. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a complex floral morphology.

The fruit is rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making it both nutritionally valuable and commercially important.
