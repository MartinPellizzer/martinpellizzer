Smodingium andrieuxii

Smodingium andrieuxii, commonly known as the Andrieux's smudging plant, is a rare species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often found in specialized botanical gardens.
