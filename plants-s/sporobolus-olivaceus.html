Sporobolus olivaceus

Sporobolus olivaceus, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with fine, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in summer, which are arranged in loose panicles. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or well-drained soils.

Sporobolus olivaceus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
