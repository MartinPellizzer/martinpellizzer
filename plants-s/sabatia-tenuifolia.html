Sabatia tenuifolia

Sabatia tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features upright stems covered with fine, hairy hairs and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Sabatia tenuifolia is known for its attractive appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
