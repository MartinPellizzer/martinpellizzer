Scabiosa corsica

Scabiosa corsica, commonly known as the Corsican scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, and its stems are usually erect and branched. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scabiosa corsica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
