Sabicea umbrosa

Sabicea umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruits are small, globose berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Sabicea umbrosa thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
