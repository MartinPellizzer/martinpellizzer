Scilla fistulosa

Scilla fistulosa, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which is adapted for survival in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Scilla fistulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
