Scirpus × carinatus

Scirpus × carinatus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a hybrid wetland plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and broad, flat leaves that are often used for thatching or weaving. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers, making it monoecious.

Scirpus × carinatus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
