Spiranthes weberbaueri var. aurantiaca

Spiranthes weberbaueri var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange spire orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its striking, spirally arranged orange or reddish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological specificity.
