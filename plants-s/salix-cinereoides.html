Salix cinereoides

Salix cinereoides, commonly known as the ash-gray willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, grayish-green leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves fully develop. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
