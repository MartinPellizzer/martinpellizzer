Schizoglossum striatum

Schizoglossum striatum, commonly known as the striped lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, striped petals that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Schizoglossum striatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
