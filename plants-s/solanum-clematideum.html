Solanum clematideum

Solanum clematideum, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Solanum clematideum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers.
