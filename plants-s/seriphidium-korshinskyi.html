Seriphidium korshinskyi

Seriphidium korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's tansy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying xerophytic flora.
