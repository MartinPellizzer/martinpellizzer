Senecio pauciflorus var. croceus

Senecio pauciflorus var. croceus, commonly known as the yellow senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as a bloom.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
