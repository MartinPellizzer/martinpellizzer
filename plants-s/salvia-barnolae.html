Salvia barnolae

Salvia barnolae, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to the central regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slight woolly texture, while the stems are erect and branching.

Salvia barnolae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
