Serrafalcus squarrosus var. villosus

Serrafalcus squarrosus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy serrafalcus, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves.

The leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy pubescence, which gives the plant its varietal name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, adding ornamental value to gardens. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
