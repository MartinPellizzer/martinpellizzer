Scutellaria anomala

Scutellaria anomala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and hairy surfaces.

Scutellaria anomala is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
