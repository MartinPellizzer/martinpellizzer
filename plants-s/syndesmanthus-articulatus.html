Syndesmanthus articulatus

Syndesmanthus articulatus, commonly known as the articular syndesmanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, articulated stem structure, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are alternate and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Syndesmanthus articulatus is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
