Syncolostemon parviflorus var. dissitiflorus

Syncolostemon parviflorus var. dissitiflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered syncolostemon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in greenhouses.
