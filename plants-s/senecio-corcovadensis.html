Senecio corcovadensis

Senecio corcovadensis, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Senecio. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio corcovadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
