Schismatoglottis samarensis

Schismatoglottis samarensis, commonly known as the Samarensis arrowroot, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
