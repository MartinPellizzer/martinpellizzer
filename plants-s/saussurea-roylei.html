Saussurea roylei

Saussurea roylei, commonly known as the Himalayan spurge, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, often at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Saussurea roylei is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
