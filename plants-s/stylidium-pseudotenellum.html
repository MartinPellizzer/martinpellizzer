Stylidium pseudotenellum

Stylidium pseudotenellum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a spike, often resembling a sun-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is typically found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

Stylidium pseudotenellum is known for its rapid growth and short lifespan, often blooming during the warmer months.
