Serjania albida

Serjania albida, commonly known as the white serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its showy, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, grayish bark and elongated leaves. Its flowers have a subtle fragrance and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Serjania albida is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
