Swertia lactea

Swertia lactea, commonly known as the milk gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Swertia lactea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
