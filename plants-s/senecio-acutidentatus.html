Senecio acutidentatus

Senecio acutidentatus, commonly known as the sharp-toothed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves with sharp, tooth-like margins. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
