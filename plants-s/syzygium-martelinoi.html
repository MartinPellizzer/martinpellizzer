Syzygium martelinoi

Syzygium martelinoi, commonly known as the star fruit or carambola, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed, star-shaped fruit. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical with a glossy surface.

The fruit develops from an inflorescence of small white flowers and is known for its tart-sweet flavor.
