Sagraea sessiliflora

Sagraea sessiliflora, commonly known as the Cape rose or Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
