Sterculia cordifolia

Sterculia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically leathery and glossy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
