Sinapis pubescens

Sinapis pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, a type of dry fruit typical of the mustard family.

Sinapis pubescens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
