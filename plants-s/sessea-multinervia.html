Sessea multinervia

Sessea multinervia, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces long, narrow leaves with a prominent vein structure. Its bark is thick and often covered in a layer of white, powdery lichen.

Sessea multinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
