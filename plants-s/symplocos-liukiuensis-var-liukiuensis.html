Symplocos liukiuensis var. liukiuensis

Symplocos liukiuensis var. liukiuensis, commonly known as the Liuqu Island symplocos, is a shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that turn red when mature.

This species is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest in botanical studies due to its limited distribution.
