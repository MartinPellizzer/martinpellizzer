Syringa villosa f. glabra

Syringa villosa f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth lilac, is a cultivar of the common lilac that exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to its parent species.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and smaller, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white to pale pink. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in temperate climates. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

The plant is also noted for its resilience and resistance to some common lilac diseases.
