Salvia cuneifolia

Salvia cuneifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. Salvia cuneifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native plant gardens.
