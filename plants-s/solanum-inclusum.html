Solanum inclusum

Solanum inclusum, commonly known as the narrowleaf nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has simple, narrow leaves and produces small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its berries, which are toxic to humans and livestock, are an important food source for certain wildlife species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
