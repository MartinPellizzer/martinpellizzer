Salvia japonica f. filicifolia

Salvia japonica f. filicifolia, commonly known as Japanese sage with fern-like leaves, is a cultivar of the Salvia genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and elongated, fern-like foliage that gives it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is valued for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
