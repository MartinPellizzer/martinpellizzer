Sida jamaicensis subsp. hermannioides

Sida jamaicensis subsp.

hermannioides, commonly known as Hermann's sida, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Hermann's sida is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and along roadsides.
