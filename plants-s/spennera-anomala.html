Spennera anomala

Spennera anomala, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland areas with rich, moist soil.

Spennera anomala is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
