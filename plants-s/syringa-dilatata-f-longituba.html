Syringa dilatata f. longituba

Syringa dilatata f. longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed lilac, is a cultivar of the common lilac.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, pale lavender to white flowers. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its showy spring blooms and attractive foliage.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and resistance to many common lilac diseases.
