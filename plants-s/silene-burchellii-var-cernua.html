Silene burchellii var. cernua

Silene burchellii var. cernua, commonly known as the hanging catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically opposite and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
