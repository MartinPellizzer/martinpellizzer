Senecio calocephalus

Senecio calocephalus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth pattern and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid native environment. It produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in spring and summer.

Senecio calocephalus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
