Solanum nipponense

Solanum nipponense, commonly known as the Japanese nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though caution is advised due to its toxic properties.
