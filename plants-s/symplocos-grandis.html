Symplocos grandis

Symplocos grandis, commonly known as the largeleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces vibrant purple berries that mature in the fall, attracting various birds and wildlife. Symplocos grandis typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color during the growing season.
