Stylocoryna pubiflora

Stylocoryna pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered stylocoryna, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage that provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Stylocoryna pubiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
