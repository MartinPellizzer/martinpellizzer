Satureja ericifolia subsp. canariensis

Satureja ericifolia subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as Canary thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas with a Mediterranean climate. Its leaves are small, oval, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in whorls.

It is valued for its fragrant properties and is sometimes used in local culinary or medicinal traditions.
