Salvia officinalis var. absinthites

Salvia officinalis var. absinthites, commonly known as lemon sage, is a variant of the common sage plant characterized by its distinctive lemon-scented foliage.

This plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and emit a strong, aromatic fragrance when crushed. It produces striking violet-blue flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in culinary and herbal applications.
