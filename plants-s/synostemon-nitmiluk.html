Synostemon nitmiluk

Synostemon nitmiluk, commonly known as the Northern River Fig, is a species of flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near watercourses.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental potential in subtropical gardens.
