Syzygium sieberianum

Syzygium sieberianum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically evergreen, with glossy, lance-shaped leaves that provide a lush canopy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a small to medium-sized tree.

Syzygium sieberianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
