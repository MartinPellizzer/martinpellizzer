Scrophularia duplicatoserrata f. nudipes

Scrophularia duplicatoserrata f. nudipes, commonly known as the naked-stemmed figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes a variety of flowering plants often found in temperate and alpine environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, leafless stems that rise from the base, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically pale purple or pink, arranged in clusters.

This plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.
