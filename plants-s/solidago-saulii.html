Solidago saulii

Solidago saulii, commonly known as the Saul's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Solidago saulii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
