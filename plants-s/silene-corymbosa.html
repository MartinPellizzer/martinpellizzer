Silene corymbosa

Silene corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous species that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers in dense, branched clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene corymbosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
