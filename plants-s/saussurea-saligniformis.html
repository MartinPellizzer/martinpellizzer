Saussurea saligniformis

Saussurea saligniformis, commonly known as the Himalayan celery or false cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in a cylindrical shape. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and traditional medicine practitioners alike.
