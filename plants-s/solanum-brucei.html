Solanum brucei

Solanum brucei, commonly known as Bruce's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, featuring simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are berries that change color as they mature, often starting green and turning red or yellow.

Solanum brucei contains various alkaloids, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
