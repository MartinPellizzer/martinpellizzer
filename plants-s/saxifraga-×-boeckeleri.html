Saxifraga × boeckeleri

Saxifraga × boeckeleri, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a hybrid between Saxifraga oppositifolia and Saxifraga hirculus, resulting in a plant with distinctive white and pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The species is characterized by its rosette-forming foliage and ability to thrive in harsh, rocky environments. It is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its hardy and attractive appearance.
