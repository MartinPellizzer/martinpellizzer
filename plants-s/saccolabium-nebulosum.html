Saccolabium nebulosum

Saccolabium nebulosum, commonly known as the clouded sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Saccolabium nebulosum is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments, making it an important component of local flora.
