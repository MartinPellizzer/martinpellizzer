Siphocampylus polycladus var. rubicundus

Siphocampylus polycladus var. rubicundus, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features erect, hairy stems that are typically reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
