Scirpus atacamensis var. exscapus

Scirpus atacamensis var. exscapus, commonly known as the Atacama reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to the extreme conditions of the Atacama Desert.

Its fibrous roots contribute to soil stabilization in its harsh environment.
