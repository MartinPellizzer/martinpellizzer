Sideritis comosa

Sideritis comosa, commonly known as the woolly sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Sideritis comosa is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
