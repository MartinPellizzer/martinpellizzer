Suaeda spicata

Suaeda spicata, commonly known as sea spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments such as coastal areas and salt marshes. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid conditions. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Suaeda spicata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
