Sclerocactus whipplei subsp. cloverae

Sclerocactus whipplei subsp.

cloverae, commonly known as the Whipple's Sclerocactus, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled settings.
