Sclerolaena stelligera

Sclerolaena stelligera, commonly known as the coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sclerolaena stelligera is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
