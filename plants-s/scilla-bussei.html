Scilla bussei

Scilla bussei, commonly known as the Mediterranean squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features lance-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

Scilla bussei is valued for its ornamental appeal and early blooming habit.
