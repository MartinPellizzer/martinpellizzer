Solanum evolvuloides

Solanum evolvuloides, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Solanum evolvuloides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
