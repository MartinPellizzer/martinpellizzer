Scandalida siliquosa, commonly known as the silique scandens, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods called siliques, which contain the seeds. Scandalida siliquosa is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
