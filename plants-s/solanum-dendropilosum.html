Solanum dendropilosum

Solanum dendropilosum, commonly known as the hairy tree tomato, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and have a tangy, acidic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or secondary growth habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
