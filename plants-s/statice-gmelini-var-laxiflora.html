Statice gmelini var. laxiflora

Statice gmelini var. laxiflora, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray.

Statice gmelini var. laxiflora is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a cut flower.
