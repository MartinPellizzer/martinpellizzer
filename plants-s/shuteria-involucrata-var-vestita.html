Shuteria involucrata var. vestita

Shuteria involucrata var. vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf shuteria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
