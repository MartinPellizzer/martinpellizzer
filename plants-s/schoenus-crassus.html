Schoenus crassus

Schoenus crassus, commonly known as the broadleaf sedgeland grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with broad, flat leaves that often form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its botanical structure and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
