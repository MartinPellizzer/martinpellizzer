Sieversia rosea

Sieversia rosea, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink and white petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Sieversia rosea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
