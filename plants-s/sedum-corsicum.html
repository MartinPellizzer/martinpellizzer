Sedum corsicum

Sedum corsicum, commonly known as Corsican stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The leaves are typically green and arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing the plant to store water efficiently. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This hardy species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
