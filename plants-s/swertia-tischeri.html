Swertia tischeri

Swertia tischeri, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,500 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Swertia tischeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
