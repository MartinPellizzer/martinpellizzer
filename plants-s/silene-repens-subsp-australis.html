Silene repens subsp. australis

Silene repens subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian sand-sage, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal and inland regions.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native insects.
