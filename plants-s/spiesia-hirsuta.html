Spiesia hirsuta

Spiesia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in soft, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Spiesia hirsuta is typically found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to arid environments.
