Stipa cordobensis var. tuberculata

Stipa cordobensis var. tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of tightly packed, spike-like clusters that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
