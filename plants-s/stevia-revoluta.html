Stevia revoluta

Stevia revoluta, commonly known as the Brazilian sweet leaf, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring compounds. Stevia revoluta typically grows in shaded, humid environments and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

It is often cultivated as a natural sweetener alternative to sugar.
