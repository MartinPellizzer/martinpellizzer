Stachys elegans

Stachys elegans, commonly known as velvetleaf sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in soft, velvety leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from lavender to pink, blooming in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Stachys elegans is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
