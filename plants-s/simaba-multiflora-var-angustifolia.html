Simaba multiflora var. angustifolia

Simaba multiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf simaba, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
