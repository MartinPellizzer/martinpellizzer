Saussurea kurentzoviae

Saussurea kurentzoviae, commonly known as the Kurentzov's saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
