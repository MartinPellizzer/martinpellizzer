Stelis falcatiloba

Stelis falcatiloba, commonly known as the cat's tail orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated inflorescence that resembles a cat's tail, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforest environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Stelis falcatiloba thrives in humid conditions with high light exposure, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
