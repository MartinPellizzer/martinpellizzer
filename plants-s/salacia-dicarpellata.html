Salacia dicarpellata

Salacia dicarpellata, commonly known as the two-seeded salacia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy in appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces fruit that is elliptical and contains two seeds.

Salacia dicarpellata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
