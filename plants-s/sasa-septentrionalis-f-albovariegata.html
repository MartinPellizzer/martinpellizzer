Sasa septentrionalis f. albovariegata

Sasa septentrionalis f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-variegated bamboo, is a variant of the northern bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white and green variegation on its leaves, which sets it apart from the typical green foliage of its parent species. This plant is a clumping bamboo that grows in dense stands, often in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky slopes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form thickets, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.

The variegation is due to genetic mutations affecting chlorophyll distribution, resulting in a unique aesthetic appeal.
