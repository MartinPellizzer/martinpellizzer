Sideritis javalambrensis

Sideritis javalambrensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis javalambrensis is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
