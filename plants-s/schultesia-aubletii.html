Schultesia aubletii

Schultesia aubletii, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. Schultesia aubletii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
