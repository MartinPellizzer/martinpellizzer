Solidago virgaurea subsp. nudiflora

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-stem goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant features a tall, unbranched stem that is often hairless, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered with fine hairs.

It produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies.
