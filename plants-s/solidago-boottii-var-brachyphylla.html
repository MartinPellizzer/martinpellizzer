Solidago boottii var. brachyphylla

Solidago boottii var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the shortleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
