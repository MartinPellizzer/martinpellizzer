Salix shandanensis

Salix shandanensis, commonly known as the Shandong willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and is often found in moist, temperate regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
