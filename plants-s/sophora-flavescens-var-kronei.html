Sophora flavescens var. kronei

Sophora flavescens var. kronei, commonly known as the Krone's Sophora, is a variant of the Sophora flavescens species native to parts of East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Its root system is known for containing bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

However, it is less commonly cultivated than other Sophora species due to its specific habitat requirements.
