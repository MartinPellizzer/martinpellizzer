Saurolluma furta

Saurolluma furta, commonly known as the ghostplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a subtle ornamental touch. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
