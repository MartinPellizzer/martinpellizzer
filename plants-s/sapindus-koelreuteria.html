Sapindus koelreuteria

Sapindus koelreuteria, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which turn red in autumn. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries that are edible for some bird species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant seasonal display.
