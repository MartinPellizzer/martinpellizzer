Solanum lycocarpum var. decalvatum

Solanum lycocarpum var. decalvatum, commonly known as the "Brazilian nightshade," is a variant of the species native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, often hairless bark. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, clustered in umbels, and the fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

The plant contains various alkaloids and has been used in traditional medicine, though it is toxic in large quantities.
