Scirpus maritimus var. megastachyus

Scirpus maritimus var. megastachyus, commonly known as the giant marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It thrives in saline or brackish wetlands, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
