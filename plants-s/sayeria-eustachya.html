Sayeria eustachya

Sayeria eustachya, commonly known as the slender panic grass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Sayeria eustachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
