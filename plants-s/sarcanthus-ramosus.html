Sarcanthus ramosus

Sarcanthus ramosus, commonly known as the branched glassworts, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in saline or coastal environments, where it thrives in high-salt conditions.

Sarcanthus ramosus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh climates.
