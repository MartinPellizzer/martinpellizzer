Solanum dissimile

Solanum dissimile, commonly known as the variable nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its diverse morphological traits. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with five petals, and it produces small, oval berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

Solanum dissimile is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
