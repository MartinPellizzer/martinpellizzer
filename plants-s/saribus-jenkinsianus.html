Saribus jenkinsianus

Saribus jenkinsianus, commonly known as the Jenkins' palm, is a species of palm native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-colored fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
