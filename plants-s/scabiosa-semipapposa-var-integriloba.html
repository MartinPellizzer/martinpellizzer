Scabiosa semipapposa var. integriloba

Scabiosa semipapposa var. integriloba, commonly known as the pink scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flower heads that resemble small pom-poms. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to both wildflower gardens and ecological landscapes.
