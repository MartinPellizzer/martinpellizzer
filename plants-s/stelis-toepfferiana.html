Stelis toepfferiana

Stelis toepfferiana, commonly known as the Toepffer's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Stelis toepfferiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
