Silene praemixta

Silene praemixta, commonly known as the mixed silene, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branching and erect.

Silene praemixta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
