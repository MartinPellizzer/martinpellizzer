Sondera auriculata

Sondera auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate sondera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive ear-like lobes at the base, giving it its common name. It produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming during the warmer months.

Sondera auriculata is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
