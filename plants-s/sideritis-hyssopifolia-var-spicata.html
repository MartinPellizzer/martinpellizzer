Sideritis hyssopifolia var. spicata

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. spicata, commonly known as spiky ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
