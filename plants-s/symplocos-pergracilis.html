Symplocos pergracilis

Symplocos pergracilis, commonly known as slender waxleaf.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Symplocos pergracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
