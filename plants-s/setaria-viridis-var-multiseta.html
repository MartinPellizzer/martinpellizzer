Setaria viridis var. multiseta

Setaria viridis var. multiseta, commonly known as green foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are typically green to golden in color. The plant has a robust root system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

This variant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
