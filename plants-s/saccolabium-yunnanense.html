Saccolabium yunnanense

Saccolabium yunnanense, commonly known as the Yunnan saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates. It is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
