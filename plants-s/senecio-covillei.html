Senecio covillei

Senecio covillei, commonly known as Coville's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. It is often found in areas with low precipitation and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
