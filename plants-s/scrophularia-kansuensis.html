Scrophularia kansuensis

Scrophularia kansuensis, commonly known as Kansu figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows.

Scrophularia kansuensis is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
