Salvia costaricensis

Salvia costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican sage, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a minty scent when crushed.

Salvia costaricensis is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
