Sabulina attica subsp. idaea

Sabulina attica subsp.

idaea, commonly known as the Greek sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically thrives in rocky, sandy, or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

It is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
