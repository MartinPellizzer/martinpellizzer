Scabiosa uniflora

Scabiosa uniflora, commonly known as the singleflower scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa uniflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
