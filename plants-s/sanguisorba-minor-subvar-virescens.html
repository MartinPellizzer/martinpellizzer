Sanguisorba minor subvar. virescens

Sanguisorba minor subvar. virescens, commonly known as green-stem sorrel, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features upright, hairy stems that are typically green to reddish in color, distinguishing it from other subspecies. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
