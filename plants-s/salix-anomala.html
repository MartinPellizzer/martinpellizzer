Salix anomala

Salix anomala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it blooms early in the spring. Salix anomala thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
