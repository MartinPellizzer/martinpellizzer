Stevia satureiifolium

Stevia satureiifolium, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with tiny, clear glands, which produce a sweet, intense flavor. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained popularity as a sugar alternative worldwide.
