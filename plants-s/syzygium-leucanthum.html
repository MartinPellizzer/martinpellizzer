Syzygium leucanthum

Syzygium leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
