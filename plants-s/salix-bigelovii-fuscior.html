Salix bigelovii fuscior

Salix bigelovii fuscior, commonly known as the darkleaf willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, often hairy leaves and slender, flexible branches. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

The bark and leaves of this willow contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in botanical and medicinal studies.
