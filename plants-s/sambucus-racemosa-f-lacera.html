Sambucus racemosa f. lacera

Sambucus racemosa f. lacera, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a flowering shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible when fully ripe but contain toxins when unripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
