Sophora affinis

Sophora affinis, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Sophora affinis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice in gardens and reforestation projects.
