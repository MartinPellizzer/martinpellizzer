Selinum uliginosum

Selinum uliginosum, commonly known as swamp sandwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing close to the ground.

Selinum uliginosum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
