Synisoon

Synisoon, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Synisoon thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
