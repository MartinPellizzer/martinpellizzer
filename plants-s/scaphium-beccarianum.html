Scaphium beccarianum

Scaphium beccarianum, commonly known as the Beccari's spurge, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically alternate and ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Scaphium beccarianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
