Sparganium affine subsp. borderi

Sparganium affine subsp.

borderi, commonly known as the border bur reed, is a wetland plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences that are covered in dense, spiky bracts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

This subspecies is often found in shallow water or moist soils along the edges of lakes, ponds, and marshes.
