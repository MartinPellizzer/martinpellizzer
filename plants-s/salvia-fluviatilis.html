Salvia fluviatilis

Salvia fluviatilis, commonly known as river sage, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of showy violet to blue-purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and wetlands.

Its aromatic foliage and striking blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
