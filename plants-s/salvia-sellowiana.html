Salvia sellowiana

Salvia sellowiana, commonly known as the Sowden's sage, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a rough, fuzzy texture.

Salvia sellowiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
