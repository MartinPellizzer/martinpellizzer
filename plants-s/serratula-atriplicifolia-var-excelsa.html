Serratula atriplicifolia var. excelsa

Serratula atriplicifolia var. excelsa, commonly known as the tall sickleweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and serrated, with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear yellow composite flowers, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
