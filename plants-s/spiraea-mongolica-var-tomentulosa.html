Spiraea mongolica var. tomentulosa

Spiraea mongolica var. tomentulosa, commonly known as the Mongolian spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, grayish foliage that becomes more pronounced as it matures, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant flowers and attractive winter silhouette.
