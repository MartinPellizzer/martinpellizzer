Salvia phaenostemma

Salvia phaenostemma, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia phaenostemma is valued in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
