Sporobolus ioclados

Sporobolus ioclados, commonly known as the sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions makes it a valuable species for erosion control and habitat restoration.

Sporobolus ioclados plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
