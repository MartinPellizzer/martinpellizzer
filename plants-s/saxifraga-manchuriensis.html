Saxifraga manchuriensis

Saxifraga manchuriensis, commonly known as the Manchurian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually basal, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga manchuriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
