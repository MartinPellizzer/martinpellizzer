Stemmatella congesta

Stemmatella congesta, commonly known as the clustered stemmatella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has a short, upright stem that bears several pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale green, and are borne in dense inflorescences.

Stemmatella congesta is often found in association with other woodland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
