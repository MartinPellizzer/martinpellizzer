Salsola congesta

Salsola congesta, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in deserts and coastal areas.

Salsola congesta is also known for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisine in some regions.
