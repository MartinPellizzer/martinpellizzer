Siphocampylus tupaeformis var. stenophyllus

Siphocampylus tupaeformis var. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf desert bell, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America.

It features a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and grayish-green. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
