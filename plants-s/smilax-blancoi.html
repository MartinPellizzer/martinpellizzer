Smilax blancoi

Smilax blancoi, commonly known as the Mexican catbriar, is a woody vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces bright yellow berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Smilax blancoi is known for its hardy nature and ability to climb or trail along the ground.
