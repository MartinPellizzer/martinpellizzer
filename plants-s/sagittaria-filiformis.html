Sagittaria filiformis

Sagittaria filiformis, commonly known as the slender arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It features narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with roots submerged in the mud. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshes and ponds.

Sagittaria filiformis plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for insects and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
