Saxifraga × saleixiana

Saxifraga × saleixiana, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers in early summer, which are followed by red or orange berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

Saxifraga × saleixiana is also noted for its ability to tolerate cold climates and poor soil conditions.
