Solidago spathulata var. neomexicana

Solidago spathulata var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico flatleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
