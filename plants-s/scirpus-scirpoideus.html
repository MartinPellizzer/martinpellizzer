Scirpus scirpoideus

Scirpus scirpoideus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, appearing in late summer to early fall. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Scirpus scirpoideus plays an important role in providing habitat for various aquatic and avian species.
