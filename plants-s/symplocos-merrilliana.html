Symplocos merrilliana

Symplocos merrilliana, commonly known as the Malayan wax tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Symplocos merrilliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
