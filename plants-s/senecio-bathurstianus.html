Senecio bathurstianus

Senecio bathurstianus, commonly known as the Bathurst groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy.

Senecio bathurstianus is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
