Saldinia mandracensis

Saldinia mandracensis, commonly known as the mandracenian salsify, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its roots are fleshy and tuberous, which are edible and have a slightly sweet taste.

Saldinia mandracensis is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid environments.
