Sasaella kogasensis

Sasaella kogasensis, commonly known as the Japanese sasa, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine, silvery bloom. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Sasaella kogasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
