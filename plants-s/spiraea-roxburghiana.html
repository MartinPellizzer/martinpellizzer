Spiraea roxburghiana

Spiraea roxburghiana, commonly known as the Chinese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It features clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has arching branches covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Spiraea roxburghiana is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and floral display.
