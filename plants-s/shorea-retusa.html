Shorea retusa

Shorea retusa, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 50 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The tree plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
