Silene foetida subsp. foetida

Silene foetida subsp.

foetida, commonly known as stinking roselight, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, while its stems are erect and hairy.

The subspecies is notable for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is a key feature in its common name.
