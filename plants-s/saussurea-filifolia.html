Saussurea filifolia

Saussurea filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
