Saxifraga moschata f. pygmaea

Saxifraga moschata f. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf musk saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky habitats in Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive musky scent. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often forming dense mats in rocky crevices.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine arrangements.
