Sagraea lanata

Sagraea lanata, commonly known as the woolly sagraea, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among gardeners.

Sagraea lanata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
