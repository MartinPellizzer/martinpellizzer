Sorbus tiantangensis

Sorbus tiantangensis, commonly known as the Tiantang whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its white to pale yellow flowers and persistent fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, while its fruits are aggregate berries that turn red in autumn.

Sorbus tiantangensis is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
