Sideritis fruticulosa f. cavanillesii

Sideritis fruticulosa f. cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' fruticose sainfoin, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its drought tolerance and visual appeal.
