Saccolabium longifolium

Saccolabium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
