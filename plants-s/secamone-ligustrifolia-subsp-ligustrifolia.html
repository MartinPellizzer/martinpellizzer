Secamone ligustrifolia subsp. ligustrifolia

Secamone ligustrifolia subsp.

ligustrifolia, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, resembling bells.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverine thickets. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
