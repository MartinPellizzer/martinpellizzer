Sesamum orientale

Sesamum orientale, commonly known as the Indian sesame or ben oil plant, is an annual herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its erect stem, alternate leaves, and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, hard seeds used for oil extraction. Sesamum orientale thrives in warm, dry climates and is cultivated for its oil-rich seeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in both culinary and medicinal applications.
