Senecio vulgaris proles motelayi

Senecio vulgaris proles motelayi, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor environments.

The species is also known for its ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind.
