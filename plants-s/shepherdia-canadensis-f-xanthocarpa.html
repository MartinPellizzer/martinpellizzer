Shepherdia canadensis f. xanthocarpa

Shepherdia canadensis f. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer, followed by bright orange to yellow berries that persist into winter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
