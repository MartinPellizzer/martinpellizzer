Scorzonera montana

Scorzonera montana, commonly known as the mountain scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a robust root system, which is often thick and fleshy, and produces yellow flowers with five petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Scorzonera montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
