Setaria viridis var. nodiflora

Setaria viridis var. nodiflora, commonly known as slender false rice, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetland habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
