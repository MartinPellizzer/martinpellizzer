Scirpus pseudosetaceus

Scirpus pseudosetaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus pseudosetaceus is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate a wide range of water depths and soil types.
