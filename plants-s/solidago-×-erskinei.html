Solidago × erskinei

Solidago × erskinei, commonly known as the Eversman's goldenrod, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Solidago nemoralis and Solidago decurrens.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in yellow to orange inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, disk-like flowers. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in regions with temperate climates.

Solidago × erskinei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
