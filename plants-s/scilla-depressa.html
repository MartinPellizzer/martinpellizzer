Scilla depressa

Scilla depressa, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. Scilla depressa is a hardy species that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
