Symphonia melleri

Symphonia melleri, commonly known as the African tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
