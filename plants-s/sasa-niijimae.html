Sasa niijimae

Sasa niijimae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or black bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa niijimae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
