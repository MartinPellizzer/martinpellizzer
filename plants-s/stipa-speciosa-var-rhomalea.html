Stipa speciosa var. rhomalea

Stipa speciosa var. rhomalea, commonly known as the silvergrass or purple wheatgrass, is a variant of the slender wheatgrass species.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive silver-blue foliage. The plant produces plume-like inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
