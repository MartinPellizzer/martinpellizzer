Sorbus tilingii

Sorbus tilingii, commonly known as the Tilingii whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, specifically found in regions of Nepal and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red berries that ripen in the autumn. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sorbus tilingii is typically found in moist, mountainous forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
