Spathiphyllum lacustre

Spathiphyllum lacustre, commonly known as the lake peace lily, is a rare aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in tropical regions.

It features broad, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in water. The plant produces striking white spathes that enclose its small, inconspicuous flowers, which are pollinated by insects. Its rhizomes help anchor it in muddy substrates, while its roots absorb nutrients from the surrounding water.

Due to its unique habitat and ornamental appeal, it is cultivated in specialized aquatic gardens and botanical collections.
