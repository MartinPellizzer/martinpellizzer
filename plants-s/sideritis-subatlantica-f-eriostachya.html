Sideritis subatlantica f. eriostachya

Sideritis subatlantica f. eriostachya, commonly known as the Atlantic sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in coastal regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
