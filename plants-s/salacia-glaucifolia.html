Salacia glaucifolia

Salacia glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue-leaved salacia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating, contributing to its unique appearance.

Salacia glaucifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
