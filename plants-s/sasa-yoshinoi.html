Sasa yoshinoi

Sasa yoshinoi, commonly known as the Yoshino bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching culms that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its rhizomes are capable of rapid vegetative propagation. Sasa yoshinoi is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and the way it forms dense, lush clumps over time.
