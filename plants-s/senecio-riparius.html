Senecio riparius

Senecio riparius, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

Senecio riparius is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance.
