Salvia tubiformis

Salvia tubiformis, commonly known as the tubular sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and aromatic. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia tubiformis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
