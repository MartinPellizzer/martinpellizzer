Samanea coripatensis

Samanea coripatensis, commonly known as the Coripate Samanea, is a tropical tree native to parts of South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its roots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
