Sinapis alba var. hispida

Sinapis alba var. hispida, commonly known as hairy mustard, is a variant of the white mustard plant.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its seeds are small and can be used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.
