Salvia kuntzeana

Salvia kuntzeana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia kuntzeana is often grown for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
