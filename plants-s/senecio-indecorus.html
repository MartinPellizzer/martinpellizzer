Senecio indecorus

Senecio indecorus, commonly known as the naked stem senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has a slender, unbranched stem that is often leafless, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Senecio indecorus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
