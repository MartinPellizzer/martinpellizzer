Sataria

Sataria, commonly known as the false indigo, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. Sataria species are characterized by their upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and showy, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plants are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

They also serve as a host plant for certain butterfly species, contributing to local biodiversity.
