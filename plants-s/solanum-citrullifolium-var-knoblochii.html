Solanum citrullifolium var. knoblochii

Solanum citrullifolium var. knoblochii, commonly known as the knoblochii variant of the citrullifolium species, is a lesser-known member of the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that resemble those of a citron, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that change color as they mature. It is native to specific regions in South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts.
