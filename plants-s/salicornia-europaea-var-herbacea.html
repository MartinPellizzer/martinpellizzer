Salicornia europaea var. herbacea

Salicornia europaea var. herbacea, commonly known as sea beans or glasswort, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and estuaries where it tolerates high salt concentrations. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid or saline conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or reddish and are produced in clusters.

This species is often used in culinary applications and has ecological importance in stabilizing coastal soils.
