Stereospermum strigilosum

Stereospermum strigilosum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
