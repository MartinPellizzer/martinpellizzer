Syringa oblata var. dilatata

Syringa oblata var. dilatata, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to landscaping in temperate climates.
