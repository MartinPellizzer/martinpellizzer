Spiraea expansa rubra

Spiraea expansa rubra, commonly known as the redleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The foliage turns a striking red or orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
