Scrophularia smithii

Scrophularia smithii, commonly known as Smith's figwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces clusters of violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Smith's figwort is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
