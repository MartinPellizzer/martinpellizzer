Stipa comata

Stipa comata, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with long, narrow leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its fibrous roots make it a valuable plant for soil stabilization and erosion control.

Stipa comata is also cultivated as an ornamental grass for its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
