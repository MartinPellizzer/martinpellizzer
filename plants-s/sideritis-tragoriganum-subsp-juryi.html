Sideritis tragoriganum subsp. juryi

Sideritis tragoriganum subsp.

juryi, commonly known as the Balkan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems, narrow leaves, and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
