Sida leprosa var. sagittifolia

Sida leprosa var. sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf sida, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead form, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in association with other wetland flora.

The species is characterized by its fibrous roots and its ability to tolerate periodic flooding.
