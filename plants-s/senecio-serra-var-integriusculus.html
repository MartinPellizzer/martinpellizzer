Senecio serra var. integriusculus

Senecio serra var. integriusculus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
