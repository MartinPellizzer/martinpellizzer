Stachytarpheta scaberrima var. pilosa

Stachytarpheta scaberrima var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy blue mistflower, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of bright blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
