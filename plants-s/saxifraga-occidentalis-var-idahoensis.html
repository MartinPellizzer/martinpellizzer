Saxifraga occidentalis var. idahoensis

Saxifraga occidentalis var. idahoensis, commonly known as the Idaho mountain saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, often found in mountainous regions of Idaho and western Montana. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed, with a rosette structure that helps it conserve moisture. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions, showcasing resilience to cold and low-nutrient soils.
