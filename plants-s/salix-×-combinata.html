Salix × combinata

Salix × combinata, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Salix fragilis and Salix triandra, characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces catkins in early spring, with male and female flowers occurring on separate branches. Its leaves are elongated, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salix × combinata is often used in landscaping for its fast growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
