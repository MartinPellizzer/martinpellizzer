Solandra hirsuta

Solandra hirsuta, commonly known as the desert rose or hirsute solandra, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, showy flowers. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in the evening. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture with hairy surfaces.

Solandra hirsuta is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
