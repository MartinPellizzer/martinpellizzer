Sonchus radicatus subsp. gummifer

Sonchus radicatus subsp.

gummifer, commonly known as the gumweed, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields.

Its latex-rich sap has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
