Solanum pusillum

Solanum pusillum, commonly known as the small nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in dry, arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, toxic berries.

Solanum pusillum is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
