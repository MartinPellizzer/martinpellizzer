Sporobolus artus

Sporobolus artus, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which are adapted for survival in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Sporobolus artus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
