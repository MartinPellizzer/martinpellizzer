Scilla autumnalis f. dumetorum

Scilla autumnalis f. dumetorum, commonly known as the autumn squill, is a botanical variant of the common squill.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers in the autumn, typically from September to November. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge in the spring and wither by late summer.
