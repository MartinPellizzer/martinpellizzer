Sesamum rigidum subsp. rigidum

Sesamum rigidum subsp.

rigidum, commonly known as the rigid sesame, is a species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system.

The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
