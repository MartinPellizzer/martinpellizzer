Sesleria tenuifolia var. juncifolia

Sesleria tenuifolia var. juncifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like leaves that are narrow and grass-green, often with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
