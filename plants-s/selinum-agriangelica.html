Selinum agriangelica

Selinum agriangelica, commonly known as angelica root, is a biennial herb belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Its root is the primary part used in herbal medicine, known for its strong, earthy scent.

Selinum agriangelica thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and woodland areas.
