Silene artemisetorum

Silene artemisetorum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems that are typically purplish in color, and its leaves are linear with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and arranged in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Silene artemisetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
