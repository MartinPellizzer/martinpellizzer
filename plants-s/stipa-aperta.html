Stipa aperta

Stipa aperta, commonly known as the open feather grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and feathery, silver-green foliage that gives it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle that produces small, pale flowers.

Stipa aperta is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
