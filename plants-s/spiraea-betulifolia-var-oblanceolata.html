Spiraea betulifolia var. oblanceolata

Spiraea betulifolia var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that produce clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. The leaves are elongated and oval-shaped, with a distinctive silvery-white underside that adds visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
