Sonchus cavanillesii

Sonchus cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are generally rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be considered an invasive species in some regions.
