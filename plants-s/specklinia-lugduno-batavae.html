Specklinia lugduno-batavae

Specklinia lugduno-batavae, commonly known as the Batavian specklinia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and possesses lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
