Saussurea virgata

Saussurea virgata, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron or the golden saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and dense, yellowish-orange inflorescences. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Saussurea virgata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
