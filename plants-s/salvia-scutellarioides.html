Salvia scutellarioides

Salvia scutellarioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia scutellarioides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
