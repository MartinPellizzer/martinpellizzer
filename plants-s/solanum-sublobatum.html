Solanum sublobatum

Solanum sublobatum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries, which are toxic, are typically green when immature and turn red when mature.

Solanum sublobatum is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
