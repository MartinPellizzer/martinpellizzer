Shorea brevipetiolaris

Shorea brevipetiolaris, commonly known as the short-peduncle mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in coastal and brackish water environments. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and thick, waxy leaves that help it tolerate saline conditions. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.

Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
