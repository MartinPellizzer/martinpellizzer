Sinapistrum

Sinapistrum, commonly known as the "Chinese lantern plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, papery, orange-red bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is typically grown as an ornamental due to its vibrant foliage and unique appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Sinapistrum species are often cultivated for their decorative value in gardens and indoor spaces.
