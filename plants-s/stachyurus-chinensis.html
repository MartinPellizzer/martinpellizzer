Stachyurus chinensis

Stachyurus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese weeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a weeping appearance, and its leaves are ovate with a glossy green surface. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Stachyurus chinensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
