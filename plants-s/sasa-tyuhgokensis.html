Sasa tyuhgokensis

Sasa tyuhgokensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery foliage. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Sasa tyuhgokensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
