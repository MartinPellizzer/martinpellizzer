Scorzonera laciniata var. integrifolia

Scorzonera laciniata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate with a smooth margin, distinguishing it from other varieties. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.
