Senecio breanus

Senecio breanus, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small spheres. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers bloom in clusters of white to pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio breanus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
