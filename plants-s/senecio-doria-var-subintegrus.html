Senecio doria var. subintegrus

Senecio doria var. subintegrus, commonly known as the Doria's senecio, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers arranged in a conical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
