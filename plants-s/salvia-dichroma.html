Salvia dichroma

Salvia dichroma, commonly known as the two-colored sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that contrast with its silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its lance-shaped leaves are aromatic and have a slightly minty scent.

Salvia dichroma is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
