Senecio brevilorus

Senecio brevilorus, commonly known as the shortleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in the spring, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Senecio brevilorus is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
