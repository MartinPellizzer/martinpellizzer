Schedonorus calamarius

Schedonorus calamarius, commonly known as the meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often used in turfgrass mixtures. The plant produces spikelets with two to three florets, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Schedonorus calamarius is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand heavy foot traffic.
