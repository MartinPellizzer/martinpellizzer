Saxifraga × fontqueri

Saxifraga × fontqueri, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Appalachian Mountains of eastern North America.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with rounded, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in a compact inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and woodland floors.

Saxifraga × fontqueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging habitats.
