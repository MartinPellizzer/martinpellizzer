Stolzia cupuligera

Stolzia cupuligera, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, resembling a small lantern. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nutritious fruit.
