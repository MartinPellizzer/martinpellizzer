Shorea lepidota

Shorea lepidota, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
