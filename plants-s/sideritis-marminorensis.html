Sideritis marminorensis

Sideritis marminorensis, commonly known as the Marminorensis sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Sideritis marminorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
