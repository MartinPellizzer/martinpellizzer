Solanum pseudomaglia

Solanum pseudomaglia, commonly known as the false nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
