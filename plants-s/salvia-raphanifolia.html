Salvia raphanifolia

Salvia raphanifolia, commonly known as the radish leaf sage, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
