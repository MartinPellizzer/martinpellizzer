Silene microsperma subsp. cypria

Silene microsperma subsp.

cypria, commonly known as the Cyprian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and dry meadows. Silene microsperma subsp.

cypria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
