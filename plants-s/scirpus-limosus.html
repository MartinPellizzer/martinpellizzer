Scirpus limosus

Scirpus limosus, commonly known as the soft bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in muddy or aquatic habitats.

Scirpus limosus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
