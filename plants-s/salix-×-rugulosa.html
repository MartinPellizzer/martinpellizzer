Salix × rugulosa

Salix × rugulosa, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow-green in color.

Salix × rugulosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens or near water features.
