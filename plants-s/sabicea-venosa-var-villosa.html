Sabicea venosa var. villosa

Sabicea venosa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy sabicea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The varietal name 'villosa' refers to the dense, hairy covering on its stems and leaves.
