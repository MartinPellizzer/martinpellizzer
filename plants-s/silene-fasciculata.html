Silene fasciculata

Silene fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered campion, is a flowering plant species native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene fasciculata is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
