Senecio cotuloides

Senecio cotuloides, commonly known as the blueweed or African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in spring and are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Senecio cotuloides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
