Saxifraga fortunei f. alpina

Saxifraga fortunei f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
