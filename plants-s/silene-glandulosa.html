Silene glandulosa

Silene glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular campion, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

Silene glandulosa is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides.
