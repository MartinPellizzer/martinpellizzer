Schizachyrium lactiflorum

Schizachyrium lactiflorum, commonly known as milkgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of its genus. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Schizachyrium lactiflorum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
