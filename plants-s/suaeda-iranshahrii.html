Suaeda iranshahrii

Suaeda iranshahrii, commonly known as the Iranian sea blite, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and saline soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its leaves are often covered with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it exhibits a C4 photosynthetic pathway, enhancing its efficiency in hot, dry conditions.

Suaeda iranshahrii is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
