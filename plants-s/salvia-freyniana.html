Salvia freyniana

Salvia freyniana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces striking spikes of purple or blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Salvia freyniana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
