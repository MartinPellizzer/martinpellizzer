Swertia multicaulis var. umbellifera

Swertia multicaulis var. umbellifera, commonly known as the Umbellate Swertia, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
