Scirpus brunneus

Scirpus brunneus, commonly known as the brown bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers.

Scirpus brunneus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
