Schizolegnia oxyphylla

Schizolegnia oxyphylla, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a complex structure, typically in shades of white and green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Schizolegnia oxyphylla is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
