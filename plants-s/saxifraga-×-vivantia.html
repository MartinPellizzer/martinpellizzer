Saxifraga × vivantia

Saxifraga × vivantia, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it adapts to cold and harsh conditions.

Saxifraga × vivantia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
