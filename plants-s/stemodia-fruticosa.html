Stemodia fruticosa

Stemodia fruticosa, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features woody stems covered in silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits.

Stemodia fruticosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
