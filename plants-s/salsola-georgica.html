Salsola georgica

Salsola georgica, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Salsola georgica is often considered a weed but has some medicinal and ecological uses.
