Syngonanthus glandulosus var. epapillosus

Syngonanthus glandulosus var. epapillosus, commonly known as the hairy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers, followed by berries that are often consumed by birds. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
