Senecio longiflorus subsp. scottii

Senecio longiflorus subsp.

scottii, commonly known as the Scott's longflower senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow ray florets and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
