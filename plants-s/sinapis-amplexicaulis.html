Sinapis amplexicaulis

Sinapis amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Sinapis amplexicaulis is an annual herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
