Sagina ambigua

Sagina ambigua, commonly known as common sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and tiny white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in moist, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit and is often found in coastal areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a hardy species well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
