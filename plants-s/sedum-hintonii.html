Sedum hintonii

Sedum hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its adaptability to arid conditions and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
