Scaphyglottis fusiformis

Scaphyglottis fusiformis, commonly known as the slender-tongue orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, tongue-shaped lip.

Scaphyglottis fusiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
