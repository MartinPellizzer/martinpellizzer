Scrophularia densifolia

Scrophularia densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branching stems that are often covered in dense, glandular hairs. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

Scrophularia densifolia is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
