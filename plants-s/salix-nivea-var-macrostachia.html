Salix nivea var. macrostachia

Salix nivea var. macrostachia, commonly known as the white willow with large inflorescences, is a variant of the white willow species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and white, fluffy catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils near rivers or streams.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
