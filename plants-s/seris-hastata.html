Seris hastata

Seris hastata, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, disk-like flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Seris hastata is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
