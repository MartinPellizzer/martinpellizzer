Solidago purshii var. gillmanii

Solidago purshii var. gillmanii, commonly known as Gillman's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well.

Gillman's goldenrod is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
