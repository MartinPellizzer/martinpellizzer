Sasa megalophylla var. argillacea

Sasa megalophylla var. argillacea, commonly known as the clay-leaf sasa, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
