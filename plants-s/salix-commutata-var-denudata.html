Salix commutata var. denudata

Salix commutata var. denudata, commonly known as the naked willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and clusters of small, yellowish-green catkins. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a glossy surface. This variety is notable for its early spring flowering and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its graceful appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
