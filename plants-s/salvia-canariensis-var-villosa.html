Salvia canariensis var. villosa

Salvia canariensis var. villosa, commonly known as the Canary Islands sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
