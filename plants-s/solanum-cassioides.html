Solanum cassioides

Solanum cassioides, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with smooth, often thornless stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to black as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant contains various alkaloids and is used in traditional medicine, though it is toxic in large quantities.
