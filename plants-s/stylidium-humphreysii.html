Stylidium humphreysii

Stylidium humphreysii, commonly known as the purple-stemmed spurred orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique spurred flowers and hairy, often purple-stemmed foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive spur that extends from the base of the petals.

Stylidium humphreysii is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
