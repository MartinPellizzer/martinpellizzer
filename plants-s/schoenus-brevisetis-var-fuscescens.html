Schoenus brevisetis var. fuscescens

Schoenus brevisetis var. fuscescens, commonly known as the dark-stemmed flat sedge, is a grass-like plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, broad leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense mats that stabilize shoreline areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.

The dark coloration of its stems is a distinguishing feature, setting it apart from other sedge species.
