Sacciolepis polymorpha var. grandis

Sacciolepis polymorpha var. grandis, commonly known as the giant sacciolepis, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for coastal gardens and rockeries.
