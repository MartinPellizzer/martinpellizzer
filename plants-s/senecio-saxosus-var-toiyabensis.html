Senecio saxosus var. toiyabensis

Senecio saxosus var. toiyabensis, commonly known as the Toiyabene stonecress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
