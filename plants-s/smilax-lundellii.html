Smilax lundellii

Smilax lundellii, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a climbing habit with thorned stems and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as swamps and hammocks. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall and is an important food source for various wildlife.

Smilax lundellii is characterized by its persistent, warty, and often hairy stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.
