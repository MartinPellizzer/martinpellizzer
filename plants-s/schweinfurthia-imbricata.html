Schweinfurthia imbricata

Schweinfurthia imbricata, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a tight, overlapping pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Schweinfurthia imbricata is often found in alpine meadows and mountain tundra, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
