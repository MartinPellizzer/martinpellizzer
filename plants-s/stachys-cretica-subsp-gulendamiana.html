Stachys cretica subsp. gulendamiana

Stachys cretica subsp.

gulendamiana, commonly known as the Persian betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
