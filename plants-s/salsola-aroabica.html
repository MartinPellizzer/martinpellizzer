Salsola aroabica

Salsola aroabica, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are succulent and adapted to conserve water, making it well-suited for harsh desert environments.

Salsola aroabica is often found in sandy soils and can play a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
