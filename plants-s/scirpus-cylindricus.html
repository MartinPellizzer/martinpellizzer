Scirpus cylindricus

Scirpus cylindricus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a tall, wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense stands in shallow water or moist soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its root system helps stabilize soil and is important for wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus cylindricus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to filter water and support diverse wildlife.
