Scleria vaginata

Scleria vaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbous sedge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers in dense clusters, typically during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies in wet or moist environments.

Scleria vaginata is often found in marshy areas, along riverbanks, and in other waterlogged soils.
