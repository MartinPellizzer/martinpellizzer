Salvia purpurea

Salvia purpurea, commonly known as purple sage, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces striking spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Salvia purpurea is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
