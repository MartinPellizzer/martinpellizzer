Seringia integrifolia

Seringia integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf seringia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems that often branch near the base. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have smooth margins, contributing to its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color.

Seringia integrifolia is adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.
