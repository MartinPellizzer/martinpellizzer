Spiraea filipendula var. vulgaris

Spiraea filipendula var. vulgaris, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin, and they turn yellow in the fall.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
