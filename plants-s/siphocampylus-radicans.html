Siphocampylus radicans

Siphocampylus radicans, commonly known as the creeping siphocampylus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of white and purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Siphocampylus radicans thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
