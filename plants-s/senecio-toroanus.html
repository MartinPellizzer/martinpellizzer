Senecio toroanus

Senecio toroanus, commonly known as the Toran's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by tufted seeds.

Its foliage is usually grayish-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.
