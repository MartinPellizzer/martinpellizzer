Sapindus peruvianus

Sapindus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian soapberry, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The tree produces spherical, woody fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used traditionally in some cultures for their cleansing properties.

Its bark and leaves have also been utilized in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic effects.
