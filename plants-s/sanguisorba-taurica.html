Sanguisorba taurica

Sanguisorba taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor due to their high oxalic acid content.

Sanguisorba taurica is often found in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
