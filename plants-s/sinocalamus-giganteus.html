Sinocalamus giganteus

Sinocalamus giganteus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of over 20 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually green and smooth on the upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it has been used in construction and as a source of biomass energy.
