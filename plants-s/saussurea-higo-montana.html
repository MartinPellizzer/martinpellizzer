Saussurea higo-montana

Saussurea higo-montana, commonly known as the mountain fig saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features a robust root system and a tall, unbranched stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy composite flower heads that are typically yellow or brown in color. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered with dense, white, woolly hairs.

Saussurea higo-montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its striking appearance.
