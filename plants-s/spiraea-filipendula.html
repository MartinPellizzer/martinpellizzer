Spiraea filipendula

Spiraea filipendula, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching branches covered in clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, fluffy flower clusters that attract pollinators.

This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
