Sporobolus cryptandrus var. vaginatus

Sporobolus cryptandrus var. vaginatus, commonly known as the blackbush, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in arid and disturbed environments.

This variety is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
