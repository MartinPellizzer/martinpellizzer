Satureja laevigata

Satureja laevigata, commonly known as smooth mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a distinctive spicy and minty fragrance.

Satureja laevigata is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
