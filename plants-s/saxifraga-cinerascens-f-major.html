Saxifraga cinerascens f. major

Saxifraga cinerascens f. major, commonly known as the large-flowered cinerascens saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
