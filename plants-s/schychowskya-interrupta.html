Schychowskya interrupta

Schychowskya interrupta, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub that typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 feet. The plant features small, waxy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. Its flowers are showy, bell-shaped, and typically range in color from white to pink or red.

Schychowskya interrupta is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
