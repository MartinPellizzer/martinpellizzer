Salpichroa rhomboidea var. mollis

Salpichroa rhomboidea var. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems.
