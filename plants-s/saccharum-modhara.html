Saccharum modhara

Saccharum modhara, commonly known as wild sugarcane, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and coarse leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its rhizomes and roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Saccharum modhara thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found near water bodies.
