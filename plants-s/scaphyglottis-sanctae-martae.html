Scaphyglottis sanctae-martae

Scaphyglottis sanctae-martae, commonly known as the Santa Marta skyflower, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a pale blue or white color with intricate patterns. The plant grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in humid and shaded conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
