Sasa pachyphylla

Sasa pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved bamboo, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a thick, waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its often arid habitat. Sasa pachyphylla typically grows in shaded, forested areas and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
