Salix laevigata var. wardii

Salix laevigata var. wardii, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to China.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth, dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
