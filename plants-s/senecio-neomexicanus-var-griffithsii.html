Senecio neomexicanus var. griffithsii

Senecio neomexicanus var. griffithsii, commonly known as Griffiths' yellowcrown, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
