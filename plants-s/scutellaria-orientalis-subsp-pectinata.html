Scutellaria orientalis subsp. pectinata

Scutellaria orientalis subsp.

pectinata, commonly known as the oriental beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It features lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of violet or blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are tubular and have a distinctive beard-like structure at the base, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is part of the mint family, Lamiaceae.
