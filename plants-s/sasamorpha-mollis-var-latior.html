Sasamorpha mollis var. latior

Sasamorpha mollis var. latior, commonly known as the broadleaf sasamorpha, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are soft to the touch and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest for botanists studying tropical flora.
