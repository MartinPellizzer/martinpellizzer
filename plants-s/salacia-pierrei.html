Salacia pierrei

Salacia pierrei, commonly known as the Malabar gooseberry, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by oval, fleshy berries that are edible. Its botanical features make it notable for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
