Sarcocornia carinata

Sarcocornia carinata, commonly known as the arrowleaf marsh grass, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and salt, aiding in its survival in coastal and estuarine areas. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It has a shallow root system that helps stabilize sediments in tidal zones.

Sarcocornia carinata plays an important role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
