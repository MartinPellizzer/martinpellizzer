Sisymbrium nitidulum

Sisymbrium nitidulum, commonly known as the smooth shepherd's purse, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant produces distinctive triangular seed pods that are often used in herbal medicine. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
