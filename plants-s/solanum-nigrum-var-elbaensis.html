Solanum nigrum var. elbaensis

Solanum nigrum var. elbaensis, commonly known as the Elba black nightshade, is a variant of the common black nightshade.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Solanaceae family, characterized by its spiny stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
