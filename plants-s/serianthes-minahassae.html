Serianthes minahassae

Serianthes minahassae, commonly known as the Java ironwood, is a rare and critically endangered tree species native to the tropical rainforests of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived component of its ecosystem. Its leaves are compound and evergreen, while its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in yellow or green hues.

Due to habitat loss and overexploitation, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
