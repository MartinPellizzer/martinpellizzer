Schedonorus arundinaceus subsp. orientalis

Schedonorus arundinaceus subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as oriental couchgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread readily, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed.
