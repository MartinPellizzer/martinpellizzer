Syagrus stratincola

Syagrus stratincola, commonly known as the palm tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, spiky fruits. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Syagrus stratincola is adapted to humid, tropical climates and plays a role in local ecosystems.
