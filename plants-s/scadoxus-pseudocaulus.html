Scadoxus pseudocaulus

Scadoxus pseudocaulus, commonly known as the African calla or white calla lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its large, showy spathe that surrounds a central spadix. The plant typically grows as a rhizomatous herb, with a short, stocky stem and broad, arrow-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Scadoxus pseudocaulus is often cultivated for its elegant appearance and is used in gardens and as a cut flower.
