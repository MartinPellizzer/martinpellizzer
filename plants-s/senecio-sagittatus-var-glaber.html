Senecio sagittatus var. glaber

Senecio sagittatus var. glaber, commonly known as the arrowleaf senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, though the 'glaber' variety is hairless. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to its habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various regions with a Mediterranean climate.
