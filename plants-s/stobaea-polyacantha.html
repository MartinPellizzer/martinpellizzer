Stobaea polyacantha

Stobaea polyacantha, commonly known as the many-branched stobaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like surface and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stobaea polyacantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique form and floral display.
