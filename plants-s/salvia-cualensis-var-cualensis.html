Salvia cualensis var. cualensis

Salvia cualensis var. cualensis, commonly known as the Cualensis sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive scent.

It is valued in both horticulture and traditional medicine for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
