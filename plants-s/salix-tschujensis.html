Salix tschujensis

Salix tschujensis, commonly known as the Tschujinskaya willow, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to poor, rocky soils.

Salix tschujensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid regions.
