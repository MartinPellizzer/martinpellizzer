Saussurea seoulensis

Saussurea seoulensis, commonly known as Seoul gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
