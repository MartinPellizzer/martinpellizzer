Silene lasiantha

Silene lasiantha, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, adapted to the arid environments it inhabits. Silene lasiantha is a perennial herb that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Its botanical features reflect its ecological role in desert ecosystems, where it contributes to biodiversity and provides nectar for specific pollinators.
