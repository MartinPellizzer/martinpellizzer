Specularia falcata

Specularia falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
