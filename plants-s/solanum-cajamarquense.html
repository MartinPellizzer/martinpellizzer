Solanum cajamarquense

Solanum cajamarquense, commonly known as the Peruvian nightshade, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and red, berry-like fruits that are edible but contain toxic compounds. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and has a unique chemical composition.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in its native habitat.
