Saxifraga serrula

Saxifraga serrula, commonly known as the serrulate saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to white, and arranged in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga serrula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
