Salacia riedeliana

Salacia riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's salacia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and smooth, often reddish bark. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
