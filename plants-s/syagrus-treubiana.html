Syagrus treubiana

Syagrus treubiana, commonly known as the royal palm, is a tall, slender palm native to Brazil.

It features a straight, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Syagrus treubiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and as a symbol of prosperity in some cultures.
