Secale cereale var. tuberculatofuscum

Secale cereale var. tuberculatofuscum, commonly known as the rough wheatgrass, is a variant of wheatgrass characterized by its distinctive, tuberculate (knobbed) and fuscous (dusky) foliage.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers. It is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variant is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices and as a forage crop.
