Solanum papita

Solanum papita, commonly known as the papita tomato, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its small, yellow-orange fruits are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Solanum papita is valued not only for its flavor but also for its ornamental appeal.
