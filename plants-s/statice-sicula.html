Statice sicula

Statice sicula, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant is typically found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and sandy soils, where it thrives in saline conditions. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple, and they bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Statice sicula is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and ornamental value.
