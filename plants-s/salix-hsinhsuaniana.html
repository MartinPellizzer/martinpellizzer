Salix hsinhsuaniana

Salix hsinhsuaniana, commonly known as the Hsinhsuan mountain willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

This species is typically found in moist, mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
