Solanum coquimbense

Solanum coquimbense, commonly known as the Coquimbo nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
