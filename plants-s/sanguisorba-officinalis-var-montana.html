Sanguisorba officinalis var. montana

Sanguisorba officinalis var. montana, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in panicle inflorescences. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Sanguisorba officinalis var. montana is often used in ornamental gardening and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
