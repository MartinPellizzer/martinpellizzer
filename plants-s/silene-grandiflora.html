Silene grandiflora

Silene grandiflora, commonly known as the showy campion, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Silene grandiflora is an important plant for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
