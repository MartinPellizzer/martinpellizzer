Solanum andigenum convar. brachistylum

Solanum andigenum convar. brachistylum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with hairy stems and small, often glandular leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. Its fruits are berries that mature to a deep blue or black color, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals.

This species is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
