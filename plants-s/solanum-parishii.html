Solanum parishii

Solanum parishii, commonly known as the Paraguay nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or moist environments. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Solanum parishii is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
