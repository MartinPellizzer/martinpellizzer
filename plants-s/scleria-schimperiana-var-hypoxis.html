Scleria schimperiana var. hypoxis

Scleria schimperiana var. hypoxis, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog plantings.
