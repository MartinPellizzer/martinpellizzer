Sapindus pubescens

Sapindus pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. The fruit's outer layer contains a soapy substance, which has been traditionally used for washing and cleaning.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
