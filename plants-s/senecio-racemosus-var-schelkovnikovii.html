Senecio racemosus var. schelkovnikovii

Senecio racemosus var. schelkovnikovii, commonly known as Schelkovnikov's upright senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

This variant is notable for its ecological adaptation to harsh climates and is of interest to botanists studying plant resilience.
