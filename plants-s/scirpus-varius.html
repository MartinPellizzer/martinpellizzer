Scirpus varius

Scirpus varius, commonly known as the variable bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomes are often used in wetland restoration projects due to their ability to stabilize soil.

Scirpus varius thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
