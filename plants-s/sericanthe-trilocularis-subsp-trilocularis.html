Sericanthe trilocularis subsp. trilocularis

Sericanthe trilocularis subsp.

trilocularis, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic if ingested.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
