Senecio lavandulifolius

Senecio lavandulifolius, commonly known as blueweed or lavenderleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, aromatic leaves that resemble lavender foliage, giving it its common name. It produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months.

Senecio lavandulifolius is known for its drought tolerance and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
