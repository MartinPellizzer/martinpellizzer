Saxifraga lhasana

Saxifraga lhasana, commonly known as the Lhasa saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Tibet and Nepal.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga lhasana is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
