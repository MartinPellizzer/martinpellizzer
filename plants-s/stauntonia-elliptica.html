Stauntonia elliptica

Stauntonia elliptica, commonly known as the Chinese lantern vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of orange-red, lantern-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, with smooth, green stems and hairy young shoots. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, tropical-looking plants.
