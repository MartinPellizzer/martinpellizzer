Symplocos iteophylla var. elliptica

Symplocos iteophylla var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
