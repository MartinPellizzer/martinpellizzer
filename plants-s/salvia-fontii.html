Salvia fontii

Salvia fontii, commonly known as the Armenian sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and well-developed.

Salvia fontii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
