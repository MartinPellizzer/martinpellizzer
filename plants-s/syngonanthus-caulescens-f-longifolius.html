Syngonanthus caulescens f. longifolius

Syngonanthus caulescens f. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved marsh grass, is a variant of the marsh grass species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that can reach several centimeters in length. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, forming a low, herbaceous ground cover. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
