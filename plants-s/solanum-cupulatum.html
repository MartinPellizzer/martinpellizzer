Solanum cupulatum

Solanum cupulatum, commonly known as the cupula tomato or the Brazilian cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive red, cherry-like fruits. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. Solanum cupulatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which have a sweet, tangy flavor.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
