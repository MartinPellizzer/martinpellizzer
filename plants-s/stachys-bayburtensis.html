Stachys bayburtensis

Stachys bayburtensis, commonly known as Bayburt horseweed, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
