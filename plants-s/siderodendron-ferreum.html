Siderodendron ferreum

Siderodendron ferreum, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is resistant to decay, making it valuable for construction and furniture. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in greenish-yellow hues.

Siderodendron ferreum plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
