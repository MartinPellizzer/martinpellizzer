Senecio carstenszensis

Senecio carstenszensis, commonly known as the Karoo Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
