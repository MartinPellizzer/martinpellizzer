Scirpus subterminalis f. terrestris

Scirpus subterminalis f. terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial sedge, is a variant of the sedge genus Scirpus.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. Its botanical features include fibrous roots, which help stabilize soil in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
