Selinum coriandrum

Selinum coriandrum, commonly known as sweet cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems and finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds, which are small and oblong, are harvested for their distinctive flavor and are widely used in various cuisines.

Selinum coriandrum is also valued for its medicinal properties, including its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
