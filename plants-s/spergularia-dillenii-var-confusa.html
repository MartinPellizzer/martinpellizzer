Spergularia dillenii var. confusa

Spergularia dillenii var. confusa, commonly known as the slender sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to sandy and coastal environments.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It typically grows in open habitats such as dunes, rocky soils, and coastal grasslands.

Its adaptations make it well-suited for harsh environments, contributing to its ecological significance in stabilizing sandy soils.
