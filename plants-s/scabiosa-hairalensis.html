Scabiosa hairalensis

Scabiosa hairalensis, commonly known as the Caucasian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces a distinctive, globular inflorescence that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa hairalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
