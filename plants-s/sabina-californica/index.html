Sabina Californica

Sabina californica, commonly known as the California juniper, is a hardy evergreen shrub native to the western United States. This resilient plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions. Its scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones make it easily identifiable in its natural habitat. The plant plays a vital role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species. With its adaptability and ecological importance, Sabina californica remains a significant part of the region's natural landscape.
