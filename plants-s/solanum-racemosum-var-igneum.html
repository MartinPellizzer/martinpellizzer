Solanum racemosum var. igneum

Solanum racemosum var. igneum, commonly known as the red-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the fruits are small, berry-like, and often contain toxic alkaloids.

It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
