Sterculia digitata

Sterculia digitata, commonly known as the finger tree, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in some cultures.
