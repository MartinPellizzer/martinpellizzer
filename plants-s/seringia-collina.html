Seringia collina

Seringia collina, commonly known as the mountain seringia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it forms dense, low-growing mats. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early summer. Seringia collina is adapted to cold climates and has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
