Selaginella hornei

Selaginella hornei, commonly known as the Horne's spikemoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its delicate, needle-like leaves and compact, branching structure. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, a characteristic feature of the spore-bearing plants.

Selaginella hornei is also notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its role in ecological systems as a pioneer species in certain habitats.
