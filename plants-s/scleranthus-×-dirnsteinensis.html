Scleranthus × dirnsteinensis

Scleranthus × dirnsteinensis, commonly known as the hybrid common cocklebur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Scleranthus × dirnsteinensis is notable for its adaptability and ability to hybridize with other Scleranthus species.
