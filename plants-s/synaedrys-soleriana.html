Synaedrys soleriana

Synaedrys soleriana, commonly known as the sun orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its flowers are arranged in a raceme, with each bloom featuring three sepals and three petals.

Synaedrys soleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, sun-like appearance.
