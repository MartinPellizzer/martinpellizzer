Symplocos ramosissima var. ramosissima

Symplocos ramosissima var. ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy waxtree, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
