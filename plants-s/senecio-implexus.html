Senecio implexus

Senecio implexus, commonly known as the entangled groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent perennial with a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in spring and summer.

Senecio implexus is adapted to arid environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
