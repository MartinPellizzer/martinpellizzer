Salix tschuktschorum

Salix tschuktschorum, commonly known as the Tschuktschorum willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It thrives in harsh, cold climates and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures.

Salix tschuktschorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
