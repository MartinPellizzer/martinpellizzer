Selago thomsonii var. coerulea

Selago thomsonii var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue star or blue star grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
