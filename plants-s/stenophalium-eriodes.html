Stenophalium eriodes

Stenophalium eriodes, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.

Stenophalium eriodes is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
