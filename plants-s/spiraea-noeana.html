Spiraea noeana

Spiraea noeana, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems that are often arching or spreading. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Spiraea noeana thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
