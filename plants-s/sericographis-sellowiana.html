Sericographis sellowiana

Sericographis sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
