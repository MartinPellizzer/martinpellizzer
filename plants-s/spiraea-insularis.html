Spiraea insularis

Spiraea insularis, commonly known as the island spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, arching branches covered in small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. The plant has alternate, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Spiraea insularis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
