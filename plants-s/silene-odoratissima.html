Silene odoratissima

Silene odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet willow or fragrant catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, preferring full sun.

Silene odoratissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its subtle, sweet fragrance.
