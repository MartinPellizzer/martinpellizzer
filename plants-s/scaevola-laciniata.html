Scaevola laciniata

Scaevola laciniata, commonly known as the lace flower or coastal laceflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery blue or purple flowers that are arranged in clusters. The plant has small, narrow, and often lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Scaevola laciniata is often used in coastal landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
