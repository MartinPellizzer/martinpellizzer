Scindapsus schlechteri

Scindapsus schlechteri, commonly known as the silver mottled philodendron, is a climbing tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that often display striking silver or white mottling. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and spread along supports, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or shelves.

Scindapsus schlechteri is relatively low-maintenance, making it an ideal houseplant for beginners.
