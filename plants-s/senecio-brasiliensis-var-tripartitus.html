Senecio brasiliensis var. tripartitus

Senecio brasiliensis var. tripartitus, commonly known as the tripartite groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a triangular pattern, giving it its specific name "tripartitus.". The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
