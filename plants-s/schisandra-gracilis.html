Schisandra gracilis

Schisandra gracilis, commonly known as the slender five-flavor vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and red berries that mature in the fall. The plant is notable for its aromatic, resinous fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Schisandra gracilis thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
