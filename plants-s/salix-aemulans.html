Salix aemulans

Salix aemulans, commonly known as the mimic willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination, and its flowers are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely used in horticulture.
