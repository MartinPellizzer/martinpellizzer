Silene drummondii var. kruckebergii

Silene drummondii var. kruckebergii, commonly known as Kruckeberg's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pinkish-purple flowers and hairy, glandular stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native environment.
