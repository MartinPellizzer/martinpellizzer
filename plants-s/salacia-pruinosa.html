Salacia pruinosa

Salacia pruinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Salacia pruinosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
