Sinoadina

Sinoadina, commonly known as the "Mexican orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family, native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Sinoadina has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitats.
