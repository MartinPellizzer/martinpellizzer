Syzygium saliciforme

Syzygium saliciforme, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Syzygium saliciforme is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
