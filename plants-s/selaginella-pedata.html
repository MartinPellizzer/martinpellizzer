Selaginella pedata

Selaginella pedata, commonly known as the stair-step moss or common spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a creeping or ascending stem with whorled leaves that are often spirally arranged. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are borne on the upper part of the plant and contain sporangia.

Selaginella pedata is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
