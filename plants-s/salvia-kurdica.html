Salvia kurdica

Salvia kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia kurdica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
