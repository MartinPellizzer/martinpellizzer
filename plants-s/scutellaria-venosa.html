Scutellaria venosa

Scutellaria venosa, commonly known as the veined skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Scutellaria venosa is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
