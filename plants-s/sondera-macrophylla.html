Sondera macrophylla

Sondera macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf sondera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
