Salvia potentillifolia

Salvia potentillifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of violet to blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal practices.
