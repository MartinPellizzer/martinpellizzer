Serratula mucronata

Serratula mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sickleweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Serratula mucronata is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is considered a hardy species that can thrive in poor soil conditions.
