Secale cereale var. ramosum

Secale cereale var. ramosum, commonly known as ramosum rye, is a variant of rye characterized by its branching inflorescence.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. It produces small, two-lobed grains that are used in traditional baking and as animal feed.

Its botanical features make it distinct from standard rye, particularly in its growth habit and floral structure.
