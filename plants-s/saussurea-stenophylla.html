Saussurea stenophylla

Saussurea stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single flower head at the top of each stem.

Saussurea stenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
