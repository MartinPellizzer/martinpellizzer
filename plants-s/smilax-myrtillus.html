Smilax myrtillus

Smilax myrtillus, commonly known as greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in sharp, spiny projections. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Smilax myrtillus is often found in woodland areas and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in partial shade.
