Stapelia limosa

Stapelia limosa, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. These flowers emit a strong, unpleasant odor to attract pollinators like flies.

Stapelia limosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
