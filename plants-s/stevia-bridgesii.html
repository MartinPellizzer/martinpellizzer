Stevia bridgesii

Stevia bridgesii, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from compounds called steviol glycosides, which are found in the leaves. Stevia bridgesii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Its use as a natural sweetener has gained global recognition due to its low calorie content and health benefits.
