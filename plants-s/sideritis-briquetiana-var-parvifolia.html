Sideritis briquetiana var. parvifolia

Sideritis briquetiana var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
