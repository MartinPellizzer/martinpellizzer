Sesamum senecioides subsp. senecioides

Sesamum senecioides subsp.

senecioides, commonly known as the African sesame or wild sesame, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the family Pedaliaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny, ovoid seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
