Schismatoglottis dulosa

Schismatoglottis dulosa, commonly known as the dwarf violet orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its humid environment.

Schismatoglottis dulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its compact size and vibrant blooms.
