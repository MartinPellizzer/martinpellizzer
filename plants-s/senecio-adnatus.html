Senecio adnatus

Senecio adnatus, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Senecio adnatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant yellow flowers.
