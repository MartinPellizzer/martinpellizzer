Sinarundinaria reticulata

Sinarundinaria reticulata, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds and rhizomes.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's fronds are typically pinnately compound, with a reticulate venation pattern that gives it its scientific name. It is often found growing on rocky slopes or in mountainous regions.

Sinarundinaria reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
