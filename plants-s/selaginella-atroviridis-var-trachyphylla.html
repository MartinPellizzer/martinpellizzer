Selaginella atroviridis var. trachyphylla

Selaginella atroviridis var. trachyphylla, commonly known as the rough-scaled spikemoss, is a vascular fern ally native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often forming dense mats or cushions. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a rough, scaly texture, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. It produces sporophylls that bear spores, and it is well adapted to survive in harsh, low-nutrient conditions.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological resilience.
