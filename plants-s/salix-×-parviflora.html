Salix × parviflora

Salix × parviflora, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers, ponds, and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a silvery underside that adds to its ornamental value.

Salix × parviflora is also noted for its early spring flowering, producing small, fluffy catkins that contribute to its ecological importance.
