Sanguisorba dictyocarpa proles guestphalica

Sanguisorba dictyocarpa proles guestphalica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweetflag, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
