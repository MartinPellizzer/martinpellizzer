Stipa richteriana var. dasyphylla

Stipa richteriana var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the rough feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form compact clumps. The leaves are narrow, stiff, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, rugged appearance. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically brown or reddish in color.

This plant is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species often found in rocky or sandy soils.
