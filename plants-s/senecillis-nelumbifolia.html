Senecillis nelumbifolia

Senecillis nelumbifolia, commonly known as the water lily of the east, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade on the water's surface, while its flowers are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive central stigma. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are aquatic, anchoring it to the substrate, and it plays a role in water purification by absorbing nutrients.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
