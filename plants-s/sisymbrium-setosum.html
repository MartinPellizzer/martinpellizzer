Sisymbrium setosum

Sisymbrium setosum, commonly known as hoary crowsfoot, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces slender, branching stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in small, pod-like fruits that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Sisymbrium setosum is often found in disturbed soils and can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
