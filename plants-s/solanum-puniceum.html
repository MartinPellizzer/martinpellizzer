Solanum puniceum

Solanum puniceum, commonly known as the red tomato bush, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant red berries that resemble small tomatoes. The plant features thick, hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces fruits that are edible when ripe.

Solanum puniceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses in some regions.
