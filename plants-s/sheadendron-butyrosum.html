Sheadendron butyrosum

Sheadendron butyrosum, commonly known as the buttercup tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble those of the buttercup, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces fruit that contains a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
