Satureja tragoriganum

Satureja tragoriganum, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, pink to purple flowers appear in clusters and are visited by various pollinators.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
