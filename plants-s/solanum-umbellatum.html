Solanum umbellatum

Solanum umbellatum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale green flowers, which are followed by smooth, round berries that turn red when mature. Solanum umbellatum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and can be invasive in some regions.
