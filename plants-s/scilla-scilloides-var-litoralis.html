Scilla scilloides var. litoralis

Scilla scilloides var. litoralis, commonly known as sea scilla, is a coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal areas. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple bell-shaped flowers in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in the spring.

It is adapted to tolerate salt spray and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species for maritime environments.
