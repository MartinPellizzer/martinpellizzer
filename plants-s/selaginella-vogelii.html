Selaginella vogelii

Selaginella vogelii, commonly known as the Vogel's spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. The plant features creeping stems with whorled leaves that are linear and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the plant's spores.

Due to its unique adaptations, Selaginella vogelii is an important species in the study of plant survival in harsh climates.
