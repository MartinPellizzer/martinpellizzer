Sideritis stachydioides

Sideritis stachydioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, pink or purple flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Sideritis species are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
