Saussurea corymbosa

Saussurea corymbosa, commonly known as the Indian saffron or the Himalayan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic properties. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to saffron.

The botanical features of Saussurea corymbosa make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
