Sida alamosana

Sida alamosana, commonly known as desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Sida alamosana is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
