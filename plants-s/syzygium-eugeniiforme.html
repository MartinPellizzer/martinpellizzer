Syzygium eugeniiforme

Syzygium eugeniiforme, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, sour-tasting fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Syzygium eugeniiforme is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
