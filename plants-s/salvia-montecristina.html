Salvia montecristina

Salvia montecristina, commonly known as the Montecristina sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Salvia montecristina is relatively drought-tolerant, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
