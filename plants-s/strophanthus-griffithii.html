Strophanthus griffithii

Strophanthus griffithii, commonly known as the red-flowered African bloodroot, is a climbing plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers and waxy, yellow-orange berries. The plant has thick, succulent stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is also noted for its toxic properties, containing cardiac glycosides that have been used in traditional medicine.
