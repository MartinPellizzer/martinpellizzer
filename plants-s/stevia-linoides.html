Stevia linoides

Stevia linoides, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring sweeteners. Its leaves are harvested and processed to extract the sweet compounds used in food and beverages.

Stevia linoides thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
