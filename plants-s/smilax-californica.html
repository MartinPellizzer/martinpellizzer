Smilax californica

Smilax californica, commonly known as California smilax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and spiny, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall and are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Smilax californica is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including woodlands and chaparral.
