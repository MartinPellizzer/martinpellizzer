Spathelia rhoifolia

Spathelia rhoifolia, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands or along streambanks. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive spatulate lip and are pollinated by insects.

Spathelia rhoifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
