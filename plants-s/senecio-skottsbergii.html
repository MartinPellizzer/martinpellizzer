Senecio skottsbergii

Senecio skottsbergii, commonly known as the Skottsberg's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
