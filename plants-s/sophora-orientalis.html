Sophora orientalis

Sophora orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese pagoda tree, is a deciduous legume native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several flat, winged seeds.

Sophora orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
