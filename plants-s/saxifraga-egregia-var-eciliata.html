Saxifraga egregia var. eciliata

Saxifraga egregia var. eciliata, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. The leaves are typically basal, fleshy, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. This species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountain slopes.

It is a hardy plant that is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.
