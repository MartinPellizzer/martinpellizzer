Silene campanulata

Silene campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower of the mountains, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well.

Silene campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
