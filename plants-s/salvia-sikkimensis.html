Salvia sikkimensis

Salvia sikkimensis, commonly known as Sikkim sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fragrant, mint-like aroma.

Salvia sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
