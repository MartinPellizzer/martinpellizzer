Satureja thymifolia

Satureja thymifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf savory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Satureja thymifolia is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
