Salvia monadelpha

Salvia monadelpha, commonly known as Texas sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking violet-blue flowers in spikes during the spring and summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability to arid conditions contribute to its widespread use in landscaping.
