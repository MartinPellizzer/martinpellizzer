Silene velutina var. tomentosa

Silene velutina var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Silene velutina var. tomentosa is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
