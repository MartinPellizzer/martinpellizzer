Smilax herbacea var. acuminata

Smilax herbacea var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems covered in spiny, yellowish-brown tubers, which are characteristic of the Smilacaceae family. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a rough texture with a slightly serrated margin.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks.
