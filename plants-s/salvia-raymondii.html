Salvia raymondii

Salvia raymondii, commonly known as blue sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Salvia raymondii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
