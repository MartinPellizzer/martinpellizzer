Solanum heterogonum

Solanum heterogonum, commonly known as the blueberry vine, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Solanum heterogonum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
