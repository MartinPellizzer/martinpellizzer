Sorghum filipendulum

Sorghum filipendulum, commonly known as the silky sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, silky, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Sorghum filipendulum is also valued for its resilience to drought and heat, making it a useful plant in arid landscapes.
