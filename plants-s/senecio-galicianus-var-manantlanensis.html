Senecio galicianus var. manantlanensis

Senecio galicianus var. manantlanensis, commonly known as the Galician Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Galicia in northwestern Spain.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience to harsh alpine conditions.
