Symplocos singuliflora

Symplocos singuliflora, commonly known as the single-flower evergreen, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are often borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Symplocos singuliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
