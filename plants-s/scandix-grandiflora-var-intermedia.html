Scandix grandiflora var. intermedia

Scandix grandiflora var. intermedia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cow parsley, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white, umbrella-like inflorescences. The plant has heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often hairy and emit a mild scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
