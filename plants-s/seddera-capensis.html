Seddera capensis

Seddera capensis, commonly known as the Cape seddera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a cluster.

Seddera capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
