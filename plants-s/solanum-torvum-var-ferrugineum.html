Solanum torvum var. ferrugineum

Solanum torvum var. ferrugineum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are typically dark green and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas due to its adaptability.

Botanically, it is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
