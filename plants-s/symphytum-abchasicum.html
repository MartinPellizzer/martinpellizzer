Symphytum abchasicum

Symphytum abchasicum, commonly known as the Abchazian comfrey, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in clusters during the summer months. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, which is characterized by hairy stems and often medicinal properties.

Symphytum abchasicum is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
