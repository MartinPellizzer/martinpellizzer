Salacia racemosa

Salacia racemosa, commonly known as the white salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is not closely related to them. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Salacia racemosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
