Sesleria caerulea f. micrantha

Sesleria caerulea f. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood sedge, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like leaves that are narrow and dark green, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes in shades of pink to purple during the summer months. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
