Sympagis maculata

Sympagis maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spotted petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Sympagis maculata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
