Serratula boetica subsp. legionensis

Serratula boetica subsp.

legionensis, commonly known as the "Legionary wormwood," is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It contains various volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Due to its historical use in herbal medicine, it has been studied for potential therapeutic applications.
