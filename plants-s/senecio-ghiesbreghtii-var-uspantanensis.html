Senecio ghiesbreghtii var. uspantanensis

Senecio ghiesbreghtii var. uspantanensis, commonly known as the Uspantanensis Groundsel, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a low-growing habit.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
