Scilla tricolor

Scilla tricolor, commonly known as three-color scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of blue, white, and pink flowers in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Scilla tricolor is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable conditions.
