Solanum seretii

Solanum seretii, commonly known as the Seretii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and it has a woody stem that becomes more branched with age.

Solanum seretii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
