Solanum calycinum

Solanum calycinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that transition from green to red as they mature, often containing toxic compounds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum calycinum is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.
