Spiraea sikokualpina

Spiraea sikokualpina, commonly known as the Sierra spiralwood, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has finely cut, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscapes.

Spiraea sikokualpina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local pollinators.
