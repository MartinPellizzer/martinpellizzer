Stapelia marientalensis

Stapelia marientalensis, commonly known as the Mariental Stapelia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, stem-like structures that store water. The plant produces distinctive flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a waxy texture, often resembling a corpse to attract pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Stapelia marientalensis is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
