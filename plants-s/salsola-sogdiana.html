Salsola sogdiana

Salsola sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian saltbush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia, including regions such as Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and Tajikistan. The plant is adapted to arid and saline environments, often growing in desert or semi-desert regions. Its leaves are succulent and salt-tolerant, which helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Salsola sogdiana produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.
