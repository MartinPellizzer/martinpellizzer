Symphoricarpos orbiculatus f. variegatus

Symphoricarpos orbiculatus f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated snowberry, is a cultivar of the snowberry plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a distinctive variegated pattern of white and green, adding visual interest to the plant. It produces small, bright red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used as ornamental features in gardens.

This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
