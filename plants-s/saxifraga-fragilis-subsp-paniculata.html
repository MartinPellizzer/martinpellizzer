Saxifraga fragilis subsp. paniculata

Saxifraga fragilis subsp.

paniculata, commonly known as the panicled saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It thrives in rocky and barren environments, often found in alpine tundras and mountainous areas.

The plant features delicate, hairy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its growth habit is characterized by a rosette of leaves with upright flowering stems.

This species is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
