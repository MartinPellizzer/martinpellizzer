Sarcocornia lagascae

Sarcocornia lagascae, commonly known as the desert marsh grass, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas and salt marshes, where it tolerates high levels of salt in the soil and water. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or reddish and bloom during the wet season.

Sarcocornia lagascae plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various organisms.
