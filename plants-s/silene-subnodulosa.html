Silene subnodulosa

Silene subnodulosa, commonly known as the nodular catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene subnodulosa is an herbaceous perennial that is often found in disturbed soils and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
