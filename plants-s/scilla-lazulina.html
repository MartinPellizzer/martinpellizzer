Scilla lazulina

Scilla lazulina, commonly known as blue scilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in early spring. Scilla lazulina typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
