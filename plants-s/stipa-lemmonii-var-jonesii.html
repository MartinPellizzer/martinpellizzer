Stipa lemmonii var. jonesii

Stipa lemmonii var. jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually brown or reddish in hue. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
