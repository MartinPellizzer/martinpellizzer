Spergula platensis var. platensis

Spergula platensis var. platensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
