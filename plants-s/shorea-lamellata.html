Shorea lamellata

Shorea lamellata, commonly known as the Malacca ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a high density and a rich, dark color. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter, often pubescent underside.

The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.
