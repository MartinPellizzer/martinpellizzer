Saxifraga pedemontana incudinensis

Saxifraga pedemontana incudinensis, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit with fleshy, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in rocky or alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy species found in high-altitude habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant ecology and alpine botany.
