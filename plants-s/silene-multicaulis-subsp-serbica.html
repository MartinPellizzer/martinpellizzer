Silene multicaulis subsp. serbica

Silene multicaulis subsp.

serbica, commonly known as the Serbica campion, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces pink to purple flowers in racemes. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the typical form of Silene multicaulis.
