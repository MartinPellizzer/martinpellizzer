Symphyotrichum dumosum var. gracilipes

Symphyotrichum dumosum var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slenderleaf heath aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The species produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in the fall, typically in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along forest edges.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
