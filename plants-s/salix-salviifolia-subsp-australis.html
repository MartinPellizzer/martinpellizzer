Salix salviifolia subsp. australis

Salix salviifolia subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It typically grows along watercourses and in moist, well-drained soils, showcasing its adaptability to wetland environments.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for its pollination process.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for native wildlife.
