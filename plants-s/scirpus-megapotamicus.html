Scirpus megapotamicus

Scirpus megapotamicus, commonly known as the South American bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in wetland environments such as marshes and pond edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus megapotamicus is also valued for its ability to filter water and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.
