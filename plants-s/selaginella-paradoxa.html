Selaginella paradoxa

Selaginella paradoxa, commonly known as the "Reverse Spike Moss," is a unique vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is notable for its ability to survive extreme desiccation, entering a state of dormancy when conditions become unfavorable. The plant features a creeping, branched stem system with small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its reproductive structures, including microsporangia and megasporangia, are located on specialized leaves, making it a fascinating example of spore-producing plant adaptation.
