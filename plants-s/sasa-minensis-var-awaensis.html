Sasa minensis var. awaensis

Sasa minensis var. awaensis, commonly known as the Awa bamboo, is a variant of the Minensis bamboo species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass that grows in dense stands, often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are typically green to reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional Japanese gardens.

The plant's growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for erosion control and as a decorative plant in humid climates.
