Searsia ruspolii

Searsia ruspolii, commonly known as the red-stemmed bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple stems and leaves that are usually opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Searsia ruspolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
