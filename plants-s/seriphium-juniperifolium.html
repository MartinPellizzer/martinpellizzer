Seriphium juniperifolium

Seriphium juniperifolium, commonly known as the juniper-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and resemble those of juniper, giving it its common name.

Seriphium juniperifolium produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.
