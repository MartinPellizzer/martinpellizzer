Swertia fwambensis

Swertia fwambensis, commonly known as the African gentian, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Swertia fwambensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
