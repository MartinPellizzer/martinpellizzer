Sporobolus pectinellus

Sporobolus pectinellus, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including prairies, disturbed soils, and sandy areas.

Sporobolus pectinellus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
