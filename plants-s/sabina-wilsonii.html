Sabina wilsonii

Sabina wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones that mature to a dark blue color. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. Its compact form and drought tolerance make it a hardy ornamental plant.

Sabina wilsonii plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
