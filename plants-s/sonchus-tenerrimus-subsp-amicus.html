Sonchus tenerrimus subsp. amicus

Sonchus tenerrimus subsp.

amicus, commonly known as the friendly sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head. It produces small, elongated achenes that are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
