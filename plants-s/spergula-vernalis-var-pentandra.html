Spergula vernalis var. pentandra

Spergula vernalis var. pentandra, commonly known as spring sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
