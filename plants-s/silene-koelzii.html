Silene koelzii

Silene koelzii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Silene koelzii is adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its high-altitude habitat.
