Salvia coccinea f. pseudococcinea

Salvia coccinea f. pseudococcinea, commonly known as the red salvia or scarlet sage, is a strikingly ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant red bracts.

It is a cultivar of the common salvia, Salvia coccinea, and is prized for its showy, long-lasting flowers that resemble small, clustered blossoms. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can be grown as an annual or perennial depending on the climate.

Its botanical features include opposite, lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for container gardening.
