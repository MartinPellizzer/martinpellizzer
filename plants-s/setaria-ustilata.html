Setaria ustilata

Setaria ustilata, commonly known as the common foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and agricultural areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

The species is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential in bioenergy and forage production.
