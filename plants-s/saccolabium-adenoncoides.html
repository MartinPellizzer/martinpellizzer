Saccolabium adenoncoides

Saccolabium adenoncoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and dense, woody growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, followed by legumes that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Its hardy nature makes it valuable for soil stabilization and as a source of browse for wildlife.
