Stevia mascotensis

Stevia mascotensis, commonly known as the Mascot stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green leaves that are used for their sweet, herbal flavor. The plant produces tiny yellow flowers and is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Stevia mascotensis is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
