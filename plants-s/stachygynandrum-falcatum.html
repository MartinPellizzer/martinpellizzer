Stachygynandrum falcatum

Stachygynandrum falcatum, commonly known as the banana plant, is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with large, broad leaves that are typically green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of bananas, which are the edible fruit, and it flowers with inflorescences that develop into the familiar banana bunches.

Its rapid growth and high yield make it an important crop in tropical agriculture.
