Stipa megapotamia var. typica

Stipa megapotamia var. typica, commonly known as the Andean needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in high-altitude environments, such as the Andes, where it is adapted to cold and arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and steppe ecosystems.
