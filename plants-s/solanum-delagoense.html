Solanum delagoense

Solanum delagoense, commonly known as the Delagoense nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with simple, opposite, and ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, yellow berries that contain the toxic alkaloids characteristic of the genus Solanum.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing some degree of drought tolerance.
