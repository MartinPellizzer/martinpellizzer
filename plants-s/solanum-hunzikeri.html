Solanum hunzikeri

Solanum hunzikeri, commonly known as the Hunziker's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or mountainous areas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are generally ovate or elliptical, with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum hunzikeri belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
