Scutellaria mexicana

Scutellaria mexicana, commonly known as Mexican skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Scutellaria mexicana is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
