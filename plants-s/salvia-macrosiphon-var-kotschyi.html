Salvia macrosiphon var. kotschyi

Salvia macrosiphon var. kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage, and its striking violet-blue flowers bloom in whorled clusters during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, bilaterally symmetrical blossoms typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
