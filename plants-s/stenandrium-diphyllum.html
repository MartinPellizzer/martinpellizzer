Stenandrium diphyllum

Stenandrium diphyllum, commonly known as the two-leaved stenandrium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually glabrous. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with adventitious roots, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Stenandrium diphyllum is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
