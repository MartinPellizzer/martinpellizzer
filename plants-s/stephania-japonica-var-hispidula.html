Stephania japonica var. hispidula

Stephania japonica var. hispidula, commonly known as Japanese stephania, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The roots of this plant have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
