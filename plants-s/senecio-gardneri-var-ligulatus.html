Senecio gardneri var. ligulatus

Senecio gardneri var. ligulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Senecio, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and ligulate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
