Stonesiella selaginoides

Stonesiella selaginoides, commonly known as the rockweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white flowers. It is adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions, making it a resilient species.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its ability to thrive in challenging, coastal habitats.
