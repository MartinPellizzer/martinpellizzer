Solanum polyanthemum

Solanum polyanthemum, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces berries that range in color from yellow to red, which are edible when ripe but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Solanaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
