Silene longiflora var. alpigena

Silene longiflora var. alpigena, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates.

Silene longiflora var. alpigena is often found in rocky soils and is a characteristic species of high-altitude ecosystems.
