Senecio nebrodensis

Senecio nebrodensis, commonly known as the Sicilian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a grayish or blue-green hue, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio nebrodensis is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and limestone formations.
