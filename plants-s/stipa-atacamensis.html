Stipa atacamensis

Stipa atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal desert environments.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
