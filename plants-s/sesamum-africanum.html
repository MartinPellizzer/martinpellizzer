Sesamum africanum

Sesamum africanum, commonly known as African sesame, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stem and alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous ellipsoidal seeds. Its seeds are rich in oil and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Sesamum africanum thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and economic importance.
