Solidago serotina var. salebrosa

Solidago serotina var. salebrosa, commonly known as late goldenrod, is a variant of the late goldenrod species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stature and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
