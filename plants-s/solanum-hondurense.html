Solanum hondurense

Solanum hondurense, commonly known as the Honduran nightshade, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features hairy, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening. Its berries are typically red when ripe and contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
