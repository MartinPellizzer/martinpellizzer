Spiraea glabrescens

Spiraea glabrescens, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

Spiraea glabrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
