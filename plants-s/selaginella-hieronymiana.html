Selaginella hieronymiana

Selaginella hieronymiana, commonly known as the Hartmann's spikemoss, is a member of the spikemoss family (Selaginellaceae).

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dense, needle-like leaves and upright, branching stems. It produces tiny, cone-like structures called strobila that contain spores for reproduction.

Selaginella hieronymiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
