Schoenoplectiella subbisetosa

Schoenoplectiella subbisetosa, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and contribute to wetland ecosystems.

Schoenoplectiella subbisetosa is valued for its ability to filter water and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
