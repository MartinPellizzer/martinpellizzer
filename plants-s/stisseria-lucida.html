Stisseria lucida

Stisseria lucida, commonly known as the brightleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are bright green and covered in fine, silky hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the early spring.

Stisseria lucida plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
