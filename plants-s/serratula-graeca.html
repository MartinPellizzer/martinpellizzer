Serratula graeca

Serratula graeca, commonly known as the Greek serrated wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
