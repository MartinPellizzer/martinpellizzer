Saraca confusa

Saraca confusa, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Saraca confusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
