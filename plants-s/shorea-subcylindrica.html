Shorea subcylindrica

Shorea subcylindrica, commonly known as the narrow-cylindric tamanu, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

It is valued for its durable wood and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
