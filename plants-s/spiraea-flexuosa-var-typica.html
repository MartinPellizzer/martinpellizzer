Spiraea flexuosa var. typica

Spiraea flexuosa var. typica, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
