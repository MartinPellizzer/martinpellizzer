Schisandra verrucosa

Schisandra verrucosa, commonly known as the rough schisandra, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. These berries are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Schisandra verrucosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
