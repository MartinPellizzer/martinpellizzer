Schizanthus fallax

Schizanthus fallax, commonly known as the "poison ivy vine," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.

Schizanthus fallax is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
