Schoenus acuminatus var. evolutus

Schoenus acuminatus var. evolutus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often rolled or folded at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, forming dense stands that help stabilize aquatic ecosystems. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in clusters, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in water filtration and habitat provision for aquatic life.
