Silene secundiflora var. macrotheca

Silene secundiflora var. macrotheca, commonly known as the showy silene, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
