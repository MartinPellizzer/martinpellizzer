Siphocampylus regelii var. umbrosus

Siphocampylus regelii var. umbrosus, commonly known as the dark umbrosus bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface and a dark green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in hue, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological niche, it is occasionally cultivated by plant enthusiasts and conservationists.
