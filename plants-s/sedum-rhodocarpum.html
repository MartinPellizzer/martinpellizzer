Sedum rhodocarpum

Sedum rhodocarpum, commonly known as the red-stemmed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum rhodocarpum is known for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
