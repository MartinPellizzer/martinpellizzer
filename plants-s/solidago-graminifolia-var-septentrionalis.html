Solidago graminifolia var. septentrionalis

Solidago graminifolia var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a reddish or purplish hue on the underside.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
