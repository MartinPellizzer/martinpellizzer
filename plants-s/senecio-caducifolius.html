Senecio caducifolius

Senecio caducifolius, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.

Senecio caducifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant.
