Samolus caulescens

Samolus caulescens, commonly known as the slender samolus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with thin, upright stems that often produce clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Samolus caulescens is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in wet meadows and along the edges of ponds.
