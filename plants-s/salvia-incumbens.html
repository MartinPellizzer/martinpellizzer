Salvia incumbens

Salvia incumbens, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and showy, tubular violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and fragrant leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Salvia incumbens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
