Salix vagans occidentalis

Salix vagans occidentalis, commonly known as the western willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of wind pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wetlands.

Its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil make it ecologically significant in riparian ecosystems.
