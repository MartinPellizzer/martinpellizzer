Stauracanthus nepa f. webbiana

Stauracanthus nepa f. webbiana, commonly known as the swamp bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in nutrient absorption. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are reduced to scales, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
