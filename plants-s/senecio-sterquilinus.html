Senecio sterquilinus

Senecio sterquilinus, commonly known as the cow dung lettuce, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, dandelion-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually fleshy and covered in a fine, white coating.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
