Solanum tuberosum var. elongatum

Solanum tuberosum var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated potato, is a variant of the potato plant characterized by its longer, more slender tubers compared to standard potato varieties.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. The plant produces starchy, edible tubers that are typically used in similar ways to regular potatoes.

It is valued for its unique shape and texture, making it a cultivar of interest in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
