Silene venosa var. commutata

Silene venosa var. commutata, commonly known as the Commutated Bladder Campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branching.

The flowers are borne in clusters and bloom during the summer months.
