Sphaeralcea munroana var. subrhomboidea

Sphaeralcea munroana var. subrhomboidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces spherical seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
