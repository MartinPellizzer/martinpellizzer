Senecio somalensis

Senecio somalensis, commonly known as the Somali groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces yellow flowers that are often surrounded by pale green bracts, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

Senecio somalensis is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
