Senecio manshuricus

Senecio manshuricus, commonly known as the Korean groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio manshuricus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
