Suaeda caespitosa

Suaeda caespitosa, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophyte shrub adapted to arid and saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and inland saline soils, demonstrating remarkable tolerance to high salinity. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss and store moisture. Its yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Suaeda caespitosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
