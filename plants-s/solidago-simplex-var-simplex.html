Solidago simplex var. simplex

Solidago simplex var. simplex, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open areas such as meadows and wetlands. Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in diverse environmental conditions.
