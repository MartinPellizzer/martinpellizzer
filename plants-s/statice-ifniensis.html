Statice ifniensis

Statice ifniensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands statice, is a flowering plant native to the island of Fuerteventura in the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, papery flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. Its flowers range in color from pale pink to white, and it is often found in coastal areas and open grasslands.

Statice ifniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
