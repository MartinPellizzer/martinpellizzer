Sesleria calcarea var. tenuifolioides

Sesleria calcarea var. tenuifolioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
