Salsola hispidula

Salsola hispidula, commonly known as sandmat or desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual or perennial shrub with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed soils and sandy environments.

Salsola hispidula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in desert ecosystems.
