Sarcotheca varians

Sarcotheca varians, commonly known as the variable sarcococca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Sarcococceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form and its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, containing several seeds. It is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and winter interest.

The leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate flowers.
