Sinningia aghensis

Sinningia aghensis, commonly known as the Aghen's gloxinia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of violet or pink blooms during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Sinningia aghensis is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
