Setaria latifolia

Setaria latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf foxtail, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that resemble a foxtail. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a bluish-green hue and often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is an annual plant that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, followed by hard, bristly seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
