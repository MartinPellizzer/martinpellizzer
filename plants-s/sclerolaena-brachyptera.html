Sclerolaena brachyptera

Sclerolaena brachyptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

Sclerolaena brachyptera plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
