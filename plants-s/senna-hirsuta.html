Senna hirsuta

Senna hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and contain a significant amount of saponins, making it useful in traditional medicine.

Senna hirsuta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
