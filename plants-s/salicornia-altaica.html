Salicornia altaica

Salicornia altaica, commonly known as sea beet, is a halophyte plant that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves, which are typically red or pink in color. This plant is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its small, green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it reproduces through both self-pollination and cross-pollination.

Salicornia altaica is also valued for its nutritional content and is used in some culinary traditions.
