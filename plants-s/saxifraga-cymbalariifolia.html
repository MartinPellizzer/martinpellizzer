Saxifraga cymbalariifolia

Saxifraga cymbalariifolia, commonly known as the crested saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Saxifraga cymbalariifolia is a hardy species that demonstrates adaptability to extreme climatic conditions.
