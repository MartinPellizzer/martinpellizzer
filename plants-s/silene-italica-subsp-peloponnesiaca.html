Silene italica subsp. peloponnesiaca

Silene italica subsp.

peloponnesiaca, commonly known as the Peloponnesian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece. It is a herbaceous species with slender stems and small, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
