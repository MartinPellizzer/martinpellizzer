Silene aeoniopsis

Silene aeoniopsis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pale pink or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its arid environment. It blooms during the summer months, producing clusters of small, delicate flowers.

Silene aeoniopsis is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its unique and ethereal appearance.
