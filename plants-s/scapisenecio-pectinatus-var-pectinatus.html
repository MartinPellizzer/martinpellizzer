Scapisenecio pectinatus var. pectinatus

Scapisenecio pectinatus var. pectinatus, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grassland areas. It has a low-growing habit with creeping stems that produce numerous flowering shoots.

The species is adapted to arid conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.
