Scindapsus salomoniensis

Scindapsus salomoniensis, commonly known as the Salomon's arrowroot, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that often exhibit variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
