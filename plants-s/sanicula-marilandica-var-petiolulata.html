Sanicula marilandica var. petiolulata

Sanicula marilandica var. petiolulata, commonly known as Maryland ginseng, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant features compound leaves with long petioles, giving it its varietal name. Its small, white flowers bloom in early summer, followed by dark blue berries that are a food source for wildlife.

The roots of this plant are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
