Sorbus torminalis var. kabylica

Sorbus torminalis var. kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylica whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits called pomes, which are typically orange to red in color and contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests or on rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
