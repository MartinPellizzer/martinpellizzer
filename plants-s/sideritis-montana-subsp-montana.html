Sideritis montana subsp. montana

Sideritis montana subsp.

montana, commonly known as mountain ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves and flowers used in traditional herbal remedies.
