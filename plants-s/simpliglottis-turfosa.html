Simpliglottis turfosa

Simpliglottis turfosa, commonly known as the Turfosa bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
