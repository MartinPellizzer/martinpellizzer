Sedum quevae

Sedum quevae, commonly known as the Queva sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, aiding in water retention and drought tolerance.

Sedum quevae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
