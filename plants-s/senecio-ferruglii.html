Senecio ferruglii

Senecio ferruglii, commonly known as the rusty groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as desert plains and rocky soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio ferruglii is generally drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun conditions.
