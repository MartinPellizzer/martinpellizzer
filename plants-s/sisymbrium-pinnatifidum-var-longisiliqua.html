Sisymbrium pinnatifidum var. longisiliqua

Sisymbrium pinnatifidum var. longisiliqua, commonly known as the longpod wild radish, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and segmented pods.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides. The plant features linear, pinnately divided leaves and produces elongated, slender pods that contain several small, round seeds. Its flowers are yellow and have four petals, typical of the mustard family.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
