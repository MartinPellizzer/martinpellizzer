Solandra longiflora

Solandra longiflora, commonly known as the sweet William fruit or the everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Solandra longiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
