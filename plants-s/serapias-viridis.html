Serapias viridis

Serapias viridis, commonly known as the green orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are often arranged in a loose inflorescence.

Serapias viridis is a perennial herb that blooms in spring, adding a unique touch of color to its natural habitat.
