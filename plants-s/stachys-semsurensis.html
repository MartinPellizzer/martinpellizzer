Stachys semsurensis

Stachys semsurensis, commonly known as Persian hedge parsley, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Stachys semsurensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
