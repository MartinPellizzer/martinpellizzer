Saxifraga moschata var. glabra

Saxifraga moschata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features rosettes of rounded, leathery leaves that are smooth and often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
