Sempervivum subalpinum

Sempervivum subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine houseleek, is a low-growing succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purple tint. The plant is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in rocky soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are small, star-shaped, and usually pink or red, appearing in early summer.

Sempervivum subalpinum is known for its hardiness and ability to survive in cold climates, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
