Scabiosa lagodechiana

Scabiosa lagodechiana, commonly known as the Italian sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa lagodechiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
