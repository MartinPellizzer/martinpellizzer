Senecio sakamaliensis

Senecio sakamaliensis, commonly known as the Sakamaliensis groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, seed-filled spheres.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
