Solanum immane

Solanum immane, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. Solanum immane produces berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
