Solanum nigrum var. guineense

Solanum nigrum var. guineense, commonly known as African black nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into spherical, black berries when mature. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas and along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in many regions.
