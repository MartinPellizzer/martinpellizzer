Scutellaria parviflora

Scutellaria parviflora, commonly known as small-flower blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Scutellaria parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
