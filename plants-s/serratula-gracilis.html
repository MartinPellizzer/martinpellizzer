Serratula gracilis

Serratula gracilis, commonly known as slender serrata, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a serrated edge, giving it its scientific name.

Serratula gracilis is known for its delicate yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.
