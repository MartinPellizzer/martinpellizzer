Senecio xerophilus var. deserticola

Senecio xerophilus var. deserticola, commonly known as the desert senecio, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in spring. It grows low to the ground, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in xeriscaping and rock gardens.
