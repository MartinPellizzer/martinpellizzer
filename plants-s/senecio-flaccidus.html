Senecio flaccidus

Senecio flaccidus, commonly known as the drooping groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senecio flaccidus is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
