Spartina stricta var. townsendii

Spartina stricta var. townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's cordgrass, is a perennial grass that thrives in brackish and salt marsh environments.

It is characterized by its stiff, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt-excreting crystals. This plant plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and preventing erosion. It is particularly adapted to tidal zones, where it tolerates periodic flooding and high salinity.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, contributing to the structure and biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
