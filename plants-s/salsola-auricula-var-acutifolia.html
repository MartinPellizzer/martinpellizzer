Salsola auricula var. acutifolia

Salsola auricula var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf Russian thorn, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and has fleshy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. It is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Salsola genus, which are known for their resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
