Salsola patentipilosa

Salsola patentipilosa, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prickly leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly drought-tolerant, adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils and extreme temperatures.

Salsola patentipilosa is often considered a weed due to its invasive nature and ability to spread rapidly.
