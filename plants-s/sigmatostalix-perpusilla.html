Sigmatostalix perpusilla

Sigmatostalix perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of various coniferous trees.

It produces small, white, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. The plant lacks true leaves and instead absorbs nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host tree's vascular system. It is native to parts of North America and is often found in forested areas.

Its small size and specialized growth habit make it a unique and ecologically significant component of its habitat.
