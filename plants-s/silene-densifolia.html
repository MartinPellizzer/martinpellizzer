Silene densifolia

Silene densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are densely arranged and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Silene densifolia is often found in alpine and arid environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.
