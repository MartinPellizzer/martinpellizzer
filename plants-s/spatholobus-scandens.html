Spatholobus scandens

Spatholobus scandens, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Spatholobus scandens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
