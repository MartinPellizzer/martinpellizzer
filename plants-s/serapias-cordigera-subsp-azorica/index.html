Serapias Cordigera Subsp. Azorica

Azorean serapias, scientifically known as Serapias cordigera subsp. azorica, are rare flowering plants endemic to the Azores archipelago. These unique orchids thrive in humid, shaded environments on volcanic rock and forest floors. With their striking orange and yellow petals, they display a vibrant splash of color in the otherwise lush green landscapes. Their delicate structure and specialized habitat make them a symbol of the region's rich biodiversity. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect these enchanting blooms from threats like habitat loss and climate change.
