Silene marginata

Silene marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are linear and often have a reddish margin, giving it its scientific name.

Silene marginata is an annual or biennial plant, depending on the climate, and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance to pollinators.
