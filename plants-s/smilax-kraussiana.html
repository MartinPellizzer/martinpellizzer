Smilax kraussiana

Smilax kraussiana, commonly known as the Krauss's catbrier, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Smilax kraussiana is valued for its ornamental potential and has traditional uses in some cultures.
