Sida viscosa

Sida viscosa, commonly known as hairy sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features slender, branched stems covered in fine, stiff hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and also densely hairy. Small, yellow flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.
