Senecio tibatiensis

Senecio tibatiensis, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

Senecio tibatiensis produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, contributing to the biodiversity of its alpine habitat.
