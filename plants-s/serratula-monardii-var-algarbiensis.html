Serratula monardii var. algarbiensis

Serratula monardii var. algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algarbiense goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to native plant collections.
