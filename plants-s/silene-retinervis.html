Silene retinervis

Silene retinervis, commonly known as the net-veined catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive net-like venation on its leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring.

Silene retinervis is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.
