Scleria pachyrrhyncha

Scleria pachyrrhyncha, commonly known as the thick-beaked sedgeland, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its robust, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its thick, fleshy rhizomes allow it to thrive in wetland and marshy environments.

Scleria pachyrrhyncha is often found in damp soils and is adapted to seasonal flooding.
