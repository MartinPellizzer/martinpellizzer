Schizachyrium scoparium subvar. gracile

Schizachyrium scoparium subvar. gracile, commonly known as little bluestem, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often copper or rust-colored.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and support for wildlife.
