Solanum siphonobasis

Solanum siphonobasis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
