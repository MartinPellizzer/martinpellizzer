Salvia strictiflora

Salvia strictiflora, commonly known as autumn sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, tube-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to ecological diversity.

Salvia strictiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
