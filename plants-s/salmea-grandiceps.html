Salmea grandiceps

Salmea grandiceps, commonly known as the giant salmea, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Salmea grandiceps is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
