Steiractinia lucidula

Steiractinia lucidula, commonly known as the shining spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy, shiny surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Steiractinia lucidula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
