Schoenoplectiella × kainanensis

Schoenoplectiella × kainanensis, commonly known as the Kainan sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic bridge between its parent species, offering insights into hybridization in wetland ecosystems.
