Scorzonera subintegra

Scorzonera subintegra, commonly known as the Italian salsify, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a robust, fleshy taproot that is often harvested for its edible, slightly sweet and nutty flavor. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Scorzonera subintegra is often cultivated for both its culinary value and ornamental appeal.
