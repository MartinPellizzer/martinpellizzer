Strumaria tenella subsp. orientalis

Strumaria tenella subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
