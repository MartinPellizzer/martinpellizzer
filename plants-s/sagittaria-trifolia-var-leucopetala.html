Sagittaria trifolia var. leucopetala

Sagittaria trifolia var. leucopetala, commonly known as the white arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive white underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically above the water surface. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
