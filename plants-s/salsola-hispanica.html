Salsola hispanica

Salsola hispanica, commonly known as Spanish gooseberry, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellowish-green berries. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and salt resistance. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salsola hispanica plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
