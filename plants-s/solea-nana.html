Solea nana

Solea nana, commonly known as the dwarf sea hare, is a small marine gastropod mollusk belonging to the family Aplysiidae.

It is characterized by its soft, gelatinous body and a distinctive, often brightly colored mantle. This species is typically found in shallow coastal waters, where it feeds on algae and other plant matter. Its botanical relevance lies in its role as a herbivorous organism that contributes to the ecological balance of marine ecosystems.

The plant-like structures it interacts with, such as algae, are essential components of its habitat and food source.
