Salsola bottae

Salsola bottae, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bluish-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its succulent stems and leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in sandy and rocky soils.

Salsola bottae plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing forage for wildlife.
