Satureja weberbaueri

Satureja weberbaueri, commonly known as Weberbauer's savory, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and it produces small, aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine.

Satureja weberbaueri is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in local herbal remedies.
