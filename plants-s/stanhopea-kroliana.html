Stanhopea kroliana

Stanhopea kroliana, commonly known as the Kroliana orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that bloom in a spiral arrangement. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often yellow or orange with red or purple markings, and it produces a distinctive, elongated pseudobulb.

Stanhopea kroliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
