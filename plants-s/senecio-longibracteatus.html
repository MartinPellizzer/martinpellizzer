Senecio longibracteatus

Senecio longibracteatus, commonly known as the Long-bract Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
