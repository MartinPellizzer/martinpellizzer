Solidago juliae

Solidago juliae, commonly known as the Sierra goldflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Solidago juliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
