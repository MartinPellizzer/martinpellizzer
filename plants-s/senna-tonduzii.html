Senna tonduzii

Senna tonduzii, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Senna tonduzii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
