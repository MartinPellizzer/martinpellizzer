Senecio doronicum subsp. transylvanicus

Senecio doronicum subsp.

transylvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian groundsel, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in composite heads.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
