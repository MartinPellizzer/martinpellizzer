Sisyrinchium exilifolium

Sisyrinchium exilifolium, commonly known as slenderleaf blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, showy blue or purple flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand arid conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native environment.
