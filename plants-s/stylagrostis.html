Stylagrostis

Stylagrostis, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are typically white or pink, with a distinctive shape that resembles a cup, and they bloom at night. Stylagrostis thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for healthy aquatic ecosystems.
