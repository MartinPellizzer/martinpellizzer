Salix arctica subvar. pusilla

Salix arctica subvar. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf Arctic willow, is a low-growing shrub native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and catkin flowers, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. This plant thrives in harsh environments, often growing on rocky soils and in tundra habitats. Its growth is adapted to cold climates, with a short growing season and winter dormancy.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for arctic wildlife.
