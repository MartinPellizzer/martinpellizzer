Salvia tenella var. albiflora

Salvia tenella var. albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, grey-green foliage and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
