Schwenckia americana var. hirta

Schwenckia americana var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and fluffy, yellow to golden inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant features finely hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a grayish-green appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Schwenckia americana var. hirta is often found in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
