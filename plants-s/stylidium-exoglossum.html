Stylidium exoglossum

Stylidium exoglossum, commonly known as the broad-leaved sun-orchid, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators including bees and birds.

Stylidium exoglossum is also noted for its ability to produce a unique, sticky secretion from its labellum, which plays a role in pollination.
