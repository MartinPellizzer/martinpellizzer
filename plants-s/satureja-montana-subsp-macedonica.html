Satureja montana subsp. macedonica

Satureja montana subsp.

macedonica, commonly known as Macedonian savory, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous areas. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, ovoid fruits.

The leaves are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine for their pungent, savory aroma.
