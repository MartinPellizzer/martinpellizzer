Salvia ceratophylla var. viridifolia

Salvia ceratophylla var. viridifolia, commonly known as greenleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and medicinal plant collections.
