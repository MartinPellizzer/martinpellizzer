Sedum blochmaniae

Sedum blochmaniae, commonly known as the Bolivian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, upright stems that often bear small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, dry conditions with well-draining soil. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, which helps it store water and resist drought.

Sedum blochmaniae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
