Symphyotrichum novi-belgii var. laevigatus

Symphyotrichum novi-belgii var. laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a smooth, waxy texture, contributing to its common name.

The variety laevigatus is distinguished by its smoother leaf surfaces compared to other varieties of Symphyotrichum novi-belgii.
