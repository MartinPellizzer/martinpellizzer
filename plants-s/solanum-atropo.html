Solanum atropo

Solanum atropo, commonly known as black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to various regions in the Americas.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are usually purple or violet in color.

The fruit, which is toxic when mature, resembles a small tomato and contains alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
