Salvia blepharochlaena

Salvia blepharochlaena, commonly known as the golden beard or sacred sage, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features striking, velvety blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique foliage.
