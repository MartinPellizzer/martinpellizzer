Salvia hierosolymitana f. chlorocalycina

Salvia hierosolymitana f. chlorocalycina, commonly known as the Jerusalem sage or white-flowered sage, is a cultivar of the Salvia genus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and features striking white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and the plant is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, thriving in warm, dry climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its unique color and hardy nature.
