Sorghum dochna var. atrum

Sorghum dochna var. atrum, commonly known as black sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves, which give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It is adapted to grow in poor soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
