Sorostachys pulchellus

Sorostachys pulchellus, commonly known as the elegant feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and delicate, feathery plumes that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture, while its inflorescence consists of multiple spikelets arranged in a panicle.

Sorostachys pulchellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
