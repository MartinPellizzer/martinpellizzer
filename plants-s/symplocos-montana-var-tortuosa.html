Symplocos montana var. tortuosa

Symplocos montana var. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted mountain waxplant, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often twisted or curled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are typically red or orange berries that contain several seeds.

Symplocos montana var. tortuosa is often found in shaded, moist forest environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
