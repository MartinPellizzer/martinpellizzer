Salix barclayi var. pseudomonticola

Salix barclayi var. pseudomonticola, commonly known as the Sierra willow, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as foothills and desert valleys. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are often narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
