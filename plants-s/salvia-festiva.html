Salvia festiva

Salvia festiva, commonly known as the festive sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in velvety gray-green foliage and produces striking violet-blue flower clusters. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Salvia festiva thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
