Salacia decussata

Salacia decussata, commonly known as the mountain pepper or Indian pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture. Salacia decussata is valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
