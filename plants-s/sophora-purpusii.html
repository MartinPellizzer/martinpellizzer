Sophora purpusii

Sophora purpusii, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous flat, winged seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
