Solanum tacnaense var. sandemanii

Solanum tacnaense var. sandemanii, commonly known as the Tacna nightshade, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a pubescent surface. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, with five petals and a distinct calyx.

The plant produces small, yellowish berries that contain the toxic alkaloids typical of the Solanaceae family.
