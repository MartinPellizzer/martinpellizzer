Sasa hashimotoi

Sasa hashimotoi, commonly known as the Hashimoto bamboo, is a shrub-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by slender, cylindrical seed pods.

Sasa hashimotoi is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form thickets in shaded environments.
