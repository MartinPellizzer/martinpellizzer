Smilax pinfaensis

Smilax pinfaensis, commonly known as the Chinese smilax, is a species of climbing shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its berries are dark red or black when mature and are often used in traditional medicine.

Smilax pinfaensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
