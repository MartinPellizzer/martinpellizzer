Spartium sphaerocarpum

Spartium sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globehead sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by distinctive spherical seed pods. Spartium sphaerocarpum is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
