Silene graminifolia lusus subglabra

Silene graminifolia lusus subglabra, commonly known as the smooth meadow cranesbill, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is known for its ornamental value and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.
