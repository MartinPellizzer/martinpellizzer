Sempervivum versicolor

Sempervivum versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red, pink, and purple. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Sempervivum versicolor is also known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, including frost and drought.
