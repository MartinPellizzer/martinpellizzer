Stipa damascena

Stipa damascena, commonly known as the Damask grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive, airy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.

Stipa damascena is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
