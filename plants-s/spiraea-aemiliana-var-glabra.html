Spiraea aemiliana var. glabra

Spiraea aemiliana var. glabra, commonly known as white meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early summer. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
