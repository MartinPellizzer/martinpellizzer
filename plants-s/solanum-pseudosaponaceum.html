Solanum pseudosaponaceum

Solanum pseudosaponaceum, commonly known as the African eggplant or bitter apple, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its spiny stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, berry-like fruits. The fruits contain several seeds and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
