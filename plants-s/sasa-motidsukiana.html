Sasa motidsukiana

Sasa motidsukiana, commonly known as the Alaska sasa, is a shrub-like bamboo native to the northern regions of North America, particularly Alaska and western Canada.

It is a perennial plant that forms dense clumps through its rhizomatous growth system, which allows it to spread and colonize moist, shaded environments. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used by local Indigenous communities for construction and tool-making. Its leaves are lance-shaped and evergreen, providing year-round coverage in its cold climate.

Sasa motidsukiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
