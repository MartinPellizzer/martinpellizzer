Scheeria mexicana var. coerulescens

Scheeria mexicana var. coerulescens, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and fragrant foliage. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and aromatic properties.
