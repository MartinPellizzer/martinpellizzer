Scrophularia sambucifolia subsp. mellifera

Scrophularia sambucifolia subsp.

mellifera, commonly known as the honey-leaved scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy hair.

The plant produces clusters of tubular, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
