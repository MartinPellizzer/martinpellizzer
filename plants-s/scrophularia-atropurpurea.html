Scrophularia atropurpurea

Scrophularia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive purple or reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically purple or pink, with a spurred lower lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Scrophularia atropurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
