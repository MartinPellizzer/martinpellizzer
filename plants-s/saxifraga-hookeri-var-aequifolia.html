Saxifraga hookeri var. aequifolia

Saxifraga hookeri var. aequifolia, commonly known as the silver saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with bright silver-gray foliage due to the presence of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its compact form and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
