Sideritis gomeraea

Sideritis gomeraea, commonly known as Gomera sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands, specifically Tenerife.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, small purple flowers, and hairy leaves. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the island's unique climate.

Sideritis gomeraea is also valued for its medicinal properties and is considered a rare endemic species.
