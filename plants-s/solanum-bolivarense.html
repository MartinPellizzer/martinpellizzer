Solanum bolivarense

Solanum bolivarense, commonly known as the Bolivian nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, fleshy berries. These berries are typically orange to red in color and contain numerous seeds.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is important to handle the plant with care.
