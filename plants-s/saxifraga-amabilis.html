Saxifraga amabilis

Saxifraga amabilis, commonly known as the pink saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga amabilis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
