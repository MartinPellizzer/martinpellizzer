Senecio pentactinus

Senecio pentactinus, commonly known as the Five-Finger Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
