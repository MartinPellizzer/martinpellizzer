Saussurea tobitae

Saussurea tobitae, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and yellowish to reddish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and valuable species in alpine ecosystems.
