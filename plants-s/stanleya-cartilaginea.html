Stanleya cartilaginea

Stanleya cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilaginous aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Stanleya cartilaginea thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
