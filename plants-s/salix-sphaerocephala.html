Salix sphaerocephala

Salix sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical or globose inflorescences, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with a fine layer of silky hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
