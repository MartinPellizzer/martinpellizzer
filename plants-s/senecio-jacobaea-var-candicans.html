Senecio jacobaea var. candicans

Senecio jacobaea var. candicans, commonly known as the showy ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it produces a large number of seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is also known for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
