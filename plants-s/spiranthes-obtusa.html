Spiranthes obtusa

Spiranthes obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive spiral arrangement of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It produces small, greenish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Spiranthes obtusa is a perennial herb that blooms from June to August, adding a delicate floral display to its natural habitat.
