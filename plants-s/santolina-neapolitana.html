Santolina neapolitana

Santolina neapolitana, commonly known as Naples santolina, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fluffy yellow flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its silver-gray foliage is aromatic and often used in ornamental gardening.

Santolina neapolitana is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
