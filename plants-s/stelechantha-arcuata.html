Stelechantha arcuata

Stelechantha arcuata, commonly known as the curling lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, curling leaves that resemble tightly wound ribbons. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
