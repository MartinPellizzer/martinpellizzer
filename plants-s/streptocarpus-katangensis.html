Streptocarpus katangensis

Streptocarpus katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms in shades of purple and blue, often with contrasting yellow markings. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth surface and prominent veins.

Streptocarpus katangensis is typically grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
