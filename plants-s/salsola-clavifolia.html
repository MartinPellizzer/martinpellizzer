Salsola clavifolia

Salsola clavifolia, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the southwestern United States and parts of Mexico. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish flowers. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and the ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Salsola clavifolia plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
