Solanum oceanicum

Solanum oceanicum, commonly known as the sea tomato, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils near the shoreline. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange fruits that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it has showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer.

Solanum oceanicum is adapted to salty air and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it a resilient coastal species.
