Sarcostemma cuspidatum

Sarcostemma cuspidatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and southern regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky areas.

Sarcostemma cuspidatum is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
