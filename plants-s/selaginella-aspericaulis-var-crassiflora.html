Selaginella aspericaulis var. crassiflora

Selaginella aspericaulis var. crassiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its distinctive, somewhat rough-textured leaves. The plant produces strobili, or cone-like structures, which contain the reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
