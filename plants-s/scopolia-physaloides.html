Scopolia physaloides

Scopolia physaloides, commonly known as the balloonflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, greenish-yellow flowers that resemble small balloons. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that transition from green to purple as they mature. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Scopolia physaloides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
