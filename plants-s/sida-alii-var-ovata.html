Sida alii var. ovata

Sida alii var. ovata, commonly known as Hawaiian coast morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing along sandy shores and rocky cliffs. It has a trailing growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Sida alii var. ovata is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of Hawaii and plays a role in the local ecosystem.
