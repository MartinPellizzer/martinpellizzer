Spiraea canescens var. glabra

Spiraea canescens var. glabra, commonly known as smooth spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage is often bright green, turning yellow or red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It is valued for its compact growth habit and moderate water requirements.
