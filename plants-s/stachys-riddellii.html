Stachys riddellii

Stachys riddellii, commonly known as Riddell's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. Its hairy foliage and distinctive flowering structure make it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.

Stachys riddellii is often found in rocky or grassy slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
