Solanum ochroneurum

Solanum ochroneurum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and toxic berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit, which is poisonous, ripens to a bright orange or yellow color and attracts various wildlife.
