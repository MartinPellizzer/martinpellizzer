Scabiosa columbaria subsp. pratensis

Scabiosa columbaria subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as the meadow scabious, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
