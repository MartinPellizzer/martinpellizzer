Sanguisorba ancistroides var. castellorum

Sanguisorba ancistroides var. castellorum, commonly known as the rock sanguisorba, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and dense clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
