Scutellaria indica var. typica

Scutellaria indica var. typica, commonly known as Indian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in moist soils near streams or in shaded woodland areas.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
