Souroubea guianensis var. cylindrica

Souroubea guianensis var. cylindrica, commonly known as the Guianese souroubea, is a variant of the souroubea tree native to South America.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface, while the bark is thick and reddish-brown in color. This plant thrives in humid tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
