Stachys obtusicrena

Stachys obtusicrena, commonly known as blunt-leaved hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a blunt tip, and it produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its hairy stems and leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance and aid in its identification.
