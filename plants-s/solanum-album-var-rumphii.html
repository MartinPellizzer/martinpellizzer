Solanum album var. rumphii

Solanum album var. rumphii, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale green to white flowers and elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is closely related to other toxic plants in the genus Solanum.
