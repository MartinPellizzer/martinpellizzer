Sterculia diversifolia

Sterculia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and mountain forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
