Spartina juncea

Spartina juncea, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to tolerate salty and brackish water conditions. This plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and preventing erosion. Spartina juncea is also an important habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its rhizomes form dense mats that help filter sediments and improve water quality in tidal wetlands.
