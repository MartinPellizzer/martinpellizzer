Spondias glabra

Spondias glabra, commonly known as the white sand apple, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces edible, round, yellow or orange fruits that are often consumed fresh or processed into jams and wines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
