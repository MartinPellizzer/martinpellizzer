Senecio neomexicanus var. metcalfei

Senecio neomexicanus var. metcalfei, commonly known as the metcalfe's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
