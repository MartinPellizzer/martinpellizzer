Silene albescens

Silene albescens, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, pink, or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. Silene albescens is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

It is also a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar morphological features.
