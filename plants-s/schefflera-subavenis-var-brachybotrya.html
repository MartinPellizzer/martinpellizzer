Schefflera subavenis var. brachybotrya

Schefflera subavenis var. brachybotrya, commonly known as the compact schefflera, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a dense, rounded growth habit with multiple stems arising from the base. The leaves are typically thick, glossy, and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by orange-red berries.

This variety is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its slow growth and tolerance of low light conditions.
