Salvia lyrata var. obovata

Salvia lyrata var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features lanceolate to obovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces striking spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and native plant landscapes.
