Sideritis athoa

Sideritis athoa, commonly known as Greek catnip, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a mint-like aroma when crushed.

Sideritis athoa is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
