Solanum chimborazense

Solanum chimborazense, commonly known as the Chimborazo nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark blue or black color, and they are edible but contain toxic compounds.

Solanum chimborazense is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.
