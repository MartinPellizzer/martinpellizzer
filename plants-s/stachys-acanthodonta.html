Stachys acanthodonta

Stachys acanthodonta, commonly known as the rough-stemmed betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces spikes of violet or blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
