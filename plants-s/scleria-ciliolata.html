Scleria ciliolata

Scleria ciliolata, commonly known as the fringed bulbous sedge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact, bulbous base and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Scleria ciliolata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
