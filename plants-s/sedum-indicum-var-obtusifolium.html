Sedum indicum var. obtusifolium

Sedum indicum var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the blunt-leaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to regions of Asia, including China and Korea.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually pale yellow to orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in gardens and rockeries.
