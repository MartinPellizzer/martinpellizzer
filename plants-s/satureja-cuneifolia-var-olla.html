Satureja cuneifolia var. olla

Satureja cuneifolia var. olla, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, with a strong, peppery aroma.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, arid environments.
