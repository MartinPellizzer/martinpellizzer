Silene capillipes

Silene capillipes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed campion, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks, often forming dense clusters. Its stems are slender and often branching, while its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs.

Silene capillipes is also noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a common sight in wetland environments.
