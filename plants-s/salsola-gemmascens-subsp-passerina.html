Salsola gemmascens subsp. passerina

Salsola gemmascens subsp.

passerina, commonly known as sandmat or desert spinach, is a halophytic plant adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in regions with low rainfall.

Its edible leaves are used in traditional cuisines and have nutritional value.
