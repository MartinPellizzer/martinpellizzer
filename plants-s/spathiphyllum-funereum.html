Spathiphyllum funereum

Spathiphyllum funereum, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and a striking white spathe that encloses the small, yellowish spadix. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often cultivated as a houseplant. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed.

The plant is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and is popular in indoor gardening.
