Squilla serotina

Squilla serotina, commonly known as the late squill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, blue-green leaves and showy, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its blooming period occurs in late summer to early autumn, making it a distinctive feature of Mediterranean landscapes.

Squilla serotina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
