Salix neofuscata

Salix neofuscata, commonly known as the black willow, is a species of willow native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and riparian zones. The plant has slender, grayish branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it relies on wind pollination.

Salix neofuscata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
