Syzygium medium

Syzygium medium, commonly known as the small satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Syzygium medium thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
