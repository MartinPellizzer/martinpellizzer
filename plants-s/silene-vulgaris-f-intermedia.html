Silene vulgaris f. intermedia

Silene vulgaris f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate common campion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its variability in flower color and growth form, which contributes to its adaptability in different environments.
