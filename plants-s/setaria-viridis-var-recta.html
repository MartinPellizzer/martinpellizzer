Setaria viridis var. recta

Setaria viridis var. recta, commonly known as green foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescence. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its rapid growth rate makes it both a weed and a potential bioenergy crop.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its erect, branching stems and the presence of bracts that enclose the flowering structures.
