Silene delectabilis

Silene delectabilis, commonly known as the sweet-scented catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Silene delectabilis is noted for its sweet, fragrant scent, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
