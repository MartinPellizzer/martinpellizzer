Setaria viridis subsp. pachystachys

Setaria viridis subsp.

pachystachys, commonly known as green foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a C4 photosynthetic pathway, making it efficient in hot and dry environments.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a common weed and a potential bioenergy crop.
