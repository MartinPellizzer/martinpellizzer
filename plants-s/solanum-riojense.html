Solanum riojense

Solanum riojense, commonly known as the Rioja nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five petals and yellow stamens.

The berries it produces are small, green, and contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
