Salvia hallieri

Salvia hallieri, commonly known as narrowleaf blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant produces tubular flowers that are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia hallieri thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
