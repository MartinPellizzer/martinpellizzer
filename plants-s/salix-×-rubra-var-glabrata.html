Salix × rubra var. glabrata

Salix × rubra var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and lance-shaped leaves that are smooth on both sides. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. It produces catkins in early spring, which are the primary means of pollen dispersal.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
