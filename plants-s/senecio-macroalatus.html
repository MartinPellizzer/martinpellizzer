Senecio macroalatus

Senecio macroalatus, commonly known as the broadleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, blue-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though its ornamental value is its primary appeal.
