Solanum martinisii

Solanum martinisii, commonly known as the Martin's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum martinisii is known for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
