Senecio jacobaea subsp. dunensis

Senecio jacobaea subsp.

dunensis, commonly known as the Scottish groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact growth compared to the typical Senecio jacobaea.
