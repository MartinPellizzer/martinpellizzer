Spiraea contorta

Spiraea contorta, commonly known as meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial to full sun. Its leaves are oval-shaped and turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.

Spiraea contorta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
