Silene corrugata var. adusta

Silene corrugata var. adusta, commonly known as the dark red catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges, and it has hairy stems and leaves that are often tinged with red or purple.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
