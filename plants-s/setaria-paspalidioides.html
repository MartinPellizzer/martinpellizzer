Setaria paspalidioides

Setaria paspalidioides, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive spikelets that resemble a foxtail. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers in late summer, which are followed by bristly, awned seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, fields, and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
