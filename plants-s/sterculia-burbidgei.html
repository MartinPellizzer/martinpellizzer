Sterculia burbidgei

Sterculia burbidgei, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain flat, edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It is valued for its durable timber and is also cultivated for its ornamental appearance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
