Sambucus racemosa f. plumosoaurea

Sambucus racemosa f. plumosoaurea, commonly known as the golden feathery elderberry, is a cultivar of the elderberry plant characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are elongated, serrated, and display a vibrant golden hue, particularly in the autumn season. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
