Salvia purpurea var. pubens

Salvia purpurea var. pubens, commonly known as purplestem sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often have a grayish-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
