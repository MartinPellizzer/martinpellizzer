Schizolobium excelsum

Schizolobium excelsum, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. Schizolobium excelsum is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in construction and furniture-making.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
