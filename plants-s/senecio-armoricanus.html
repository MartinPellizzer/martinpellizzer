Senecio armoricanus

Senecio armoricanus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio armoricanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
