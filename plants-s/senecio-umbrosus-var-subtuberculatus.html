Senecio umbrosus var. subtuberculatus

Senecio umbrosus var. subtuberculatus, commonly known as the subtuberculate senecio, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
