Serapias flava

Serapias flava, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Rockcress (Capparaceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky, sunny habitats and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
