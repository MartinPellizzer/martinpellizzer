Samyda pitumba

Samyda pitumba, commonly known as the African wild fig, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for both wildlife and humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and sustenance for various species in its native environment.
