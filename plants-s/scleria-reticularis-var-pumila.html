Scleria reticularis var. pumila

Scleria reticularis var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf netleaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, triangular stems with a distinctive reticulate pattern on its surface, giving it a unique appearance. Its small, grass-like leaves are usually green, and it produces spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is notable for its compact size and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
