Sium ferulifolium

Sium ferulifolium, commonly known as wild parsnip, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a robust taproot and tall, hollow stems that are often branched. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with finely divided, fern-like lobes.

It is commonly found in moist meadows, stream banks, and disturbed soils.
