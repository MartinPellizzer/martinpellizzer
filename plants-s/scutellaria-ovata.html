Scutellaria ovata

Scutellaria ovata, commonly known as the oval skullcap, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Scutellaria ovata is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
