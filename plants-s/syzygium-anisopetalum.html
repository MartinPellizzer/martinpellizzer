Syzygium anisopetalum

Syzygium anisopetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled apple.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, resembling a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor.

Syzygium anisopetalum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
