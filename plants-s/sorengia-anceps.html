Sorengia anceps

Sorengia anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively.

Sorengia anceps plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
