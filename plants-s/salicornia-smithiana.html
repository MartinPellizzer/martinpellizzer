Salicornia smithiana

Salicornia smithiana, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in coastal and arid regions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are reduced to scale-like structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Salicornia smithiana is often found in salt marshes and tidal flats, contributing to stabilizing coastal soils.
