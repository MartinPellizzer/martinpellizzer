Schefflera indivisa

Schefflera indivisa, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like arrangement of large, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens.

Its adaptability and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
