Symplocos spicata var. laurina

Symplocos spicata var. laurina, commonly known as the red-berried snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

The berries are an important food source for birds and wildlife during the colder months.
