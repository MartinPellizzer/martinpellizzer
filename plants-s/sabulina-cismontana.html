Sabulina cismontana

Sabulina cismontana, commonly known as the western sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in loose, well-drained substrates.

Sabulina cismontana is adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
