Solanum commutatum

Solanum commutatum, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is a perennial shrub native to the Americas, particularly found in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries. Its berries are toxic when ripe but may be used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

Solanum commutatum is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
