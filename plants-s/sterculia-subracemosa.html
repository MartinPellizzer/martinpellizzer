Sterculia subracemosa

Sterculia subracemosa, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, fertile soils and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
