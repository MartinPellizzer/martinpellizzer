Setaria oligochaete

Setaria oligochaete, commonly known as slender barnyardgrass, is a grass species native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright leaves and spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stem. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. Its flowering occurs in late summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Setaria oligochaete is an annual plant that can be a weed in crops such as corn and rice.
