Sorbus portae-bohemicae

Sorbus portae-bohemicae, commonly known as the Bohemian whitebeam, is a species of rowan tree native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, persistent berries. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. This plant prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and forest edges.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
