Senecio subradiatus

Senecio subradiatus, commonly known as the radiate senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
