Seriphidium huguetii

Seriphidium huguetii, commonly known as the desert chicory, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a robust root system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, followed by small, bract-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinct, bitter taste, which contributes to its medicinal properties.

Seriphidium huguetii is well adapted to extreme environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
