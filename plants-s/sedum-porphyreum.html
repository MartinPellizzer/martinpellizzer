Sedum porphyreum

Sedum porphyreum, commonly known as the Porphyry Sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a dense, low-growing rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Sedum porphyreum is often used in rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
