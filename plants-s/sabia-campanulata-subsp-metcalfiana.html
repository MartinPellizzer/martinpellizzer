Sabia campanulata subsp. metcalfiana

Sabia campanulata subsp.

metcalfiana, commonly known as the Metcalf's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
