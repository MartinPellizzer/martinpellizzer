Sansevieria gracillima

Sansevieria gracillima, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with subtle variegation. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it an excellent choice for indoor environments. Its air-purifying qualities have also made it a popular houseplant.

Sansevieria gracillima is also appreciated for its resilience and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
