Securigera varia subsp. hirta

Securigera varia subsp.

hirta, commonly known as the hairy goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, branched inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its small, yellowish-white flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves.

It is not commonly cultivated but can be found in some botanical gardens and natural areas.
