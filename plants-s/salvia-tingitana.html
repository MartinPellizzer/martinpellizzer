Salvia tingitana

Salvia tingitana, commonly known as the Moroccan sage, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Salvia tingitana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
