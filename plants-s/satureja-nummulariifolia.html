Satureja nummulariifolia

Satureja nummulariifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, round, gray-green leaves and spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun. It is often used in culinary applications for its aromatic, peppery flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and rosemary.
