Salix × acuminata

Salix × acuminata, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green during the growing season. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

Salix × acuminata is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize soil along waterways.
