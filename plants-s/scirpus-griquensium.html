Scirpus griquensium

Scirpus griquensium, commonly known as the Grootfontein bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, which develop into fluffy seed heads. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help in stabilizing the soil in aquatic environments.

Scirpus griquensium plays an important ecological role in filtering water and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
