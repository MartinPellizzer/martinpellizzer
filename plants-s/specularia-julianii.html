Specularia julianii

Specularia julianii, commonly known as Julian's speculum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing vivid pink or white petals that reflect light in a shimmering manner. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty growers.
