Sarcocornia pulvinata

Sarcocornia pulvinata, commonly known as the sea lavender, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is native to coastal areas of the Americas and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system helps stabilize coastal soils, preventing erosion.

Sarcocornia pulvinata plays an important ecological role in salt marsh ecosystems.
