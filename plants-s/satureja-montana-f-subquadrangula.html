Satureja montana f. subquadrangula

Satureja montana f. subquadrangula, commonly known as narrow-leaf mountain savory, is a subspecies of the mountain savory plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, and often gray-green leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
