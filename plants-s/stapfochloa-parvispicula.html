Stapfochloa parvispicula

Stapfochloa parvispicula, commonly known as the small-eyed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface and a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Stapfochloa parvispicula is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
