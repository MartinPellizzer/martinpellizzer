Solanum ovigerum subsp. sinuatorepandum

Solanum ovigerum subsp.

sinuatorepandum, commonly known as the sinuately lobed nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are deeply sinuate and lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
