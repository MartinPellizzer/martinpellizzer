Symphyotrichum oblongifolium

Symphyotrichum oblongifolium, commonly known as New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy purple or pink flower heads that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Symphyotrichum oblongifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
