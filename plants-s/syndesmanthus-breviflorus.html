Syndesmanthus breviflorus

Syndesmanthus breviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered syndesmanthus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, oblong fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often used in arid landscape designs.
