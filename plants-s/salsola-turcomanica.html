Salsola turcomanica

Salsola turcomanica, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability.

Its ability to thrive in such conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
