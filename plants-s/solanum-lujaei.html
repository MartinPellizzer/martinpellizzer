Solanum lujaei

Solanum lujaei, commonly known as the Lujan's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing various alkaloids. Solanum lujaei is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
