Sorghum conspicuum var. usaramense

Sorghum conspicuum var. usaramense, commonly known as the Usaramense sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and large, showy bracts that give it a striking appearance. The plant typically thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial grass that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it a notable ornamental and potential forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and agricultural studies.
