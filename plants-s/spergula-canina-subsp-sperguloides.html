Spergula canina subsp. sperguloides

Spergula canina subsp.

sperguloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically forms dense mats of slender, hairy leaves that are often less than 2 cm in length.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in spike-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
