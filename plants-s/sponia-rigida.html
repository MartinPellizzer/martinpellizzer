Sponia rigida

Sponia rigida, commonly known as the rigid sponge plant, is a unique species characterized by its spongy, fibrous structure.

It belongs to the family Sponiaceae and is native to specific arid regions. The plant's leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, while its stems are thick and water-storing. Sponia rigida thrives in harsh environments, showcasing adaptations for water retention and drought resistance.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
