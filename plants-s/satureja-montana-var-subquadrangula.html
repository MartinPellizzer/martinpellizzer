Satureja montana var. subquadrangula

Satureja montana var. subquadrangula, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent, often used in traditional herbal remedies. The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its botanical classification places it within the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
