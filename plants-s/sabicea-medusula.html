Sabicea medusula

Sabicea medusula, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
