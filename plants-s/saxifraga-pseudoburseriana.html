Saxifraga pseudoburseriana

Saxifraga pseudoburseriana, commonly known as the Burserian saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
