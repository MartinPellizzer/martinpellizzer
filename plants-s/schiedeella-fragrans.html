Schiedeella fragrans

Schiedeella fragrans, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and emit a strong aromatic scent when crushed.

Schiedeella fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.
