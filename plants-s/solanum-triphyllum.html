Solanum triphyllum

Solanum triphyllum, commonly known as the triphyllium nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
