Scindapsus argyraeus

Scindapsus argyraeus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking variegated leaves. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and a distinctive silver or white vein pattern. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
