Saxifraga muscoides var. laxa

Saxifraga muscoides var. laxa, commonly known as the loose moss saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Saxifraga muscoides var. laxa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its cold, high-altitude habitats.
