Scutellaria violacea var. glabrior

Scutellaria violacea var. glabrior, commonly known as the glabrous blue skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny woodland areas.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
