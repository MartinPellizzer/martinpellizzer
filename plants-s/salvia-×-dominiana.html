Salvia × dominiana

Salvia × dominiana, commonly known as the hybrid sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it is a cross between Salvia nemorosa and Salvia gregaria, resulting in a robust and hardy hybrid.
