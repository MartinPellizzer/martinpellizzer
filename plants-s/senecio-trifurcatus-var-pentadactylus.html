Senecio trifurcatus var. pentadactylus

Senecio trifurcatus var. pentadactylus, commonly known as the five-fingered senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, finger-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
