Salvia incerta

Salvia incerta, commonly known as the uncertain sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salvia incerta is valued in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
