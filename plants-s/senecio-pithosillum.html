Senecio pithosillum

Senecio pithosillum, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
