Scrophularia fedtschenkoi

Scrophularia fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Siberian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The species is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in some regions.
