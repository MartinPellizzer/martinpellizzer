Sorindeia falcata

Sorindeia falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sorindeia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

Sorindeia falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
