Spartium hirsutum

Spartium hirsutum, commonly known as red brome, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Spartium hirsutum is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
