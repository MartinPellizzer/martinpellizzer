Scleria vogelii

Scleria vogelii, commonly known as the Vogel's sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and clustered growth habit.

It typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers in spikelets. The plant is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Scleria vogelii is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
