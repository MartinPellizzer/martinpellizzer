Solanum verbascifolium

Solanum verbascifolium, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar species like tomatoes and peppers. Solanum verbascifolium is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its berries are toxic and should not be consumed.
