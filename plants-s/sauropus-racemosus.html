Sauropus racemosus

Sauropus racemosus, commonly known as the clustered sauropus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers, followed by small, round berries.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
