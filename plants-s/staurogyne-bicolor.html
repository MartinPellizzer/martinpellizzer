Staurogyne bicolor

Staurogyne bicolor, commonly known as the pink waterwheel plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique, whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are usually bright green with a pinkish or reddish underside, giving it a striking appearance.

Staurogyne bicolor is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in controlled aquatic settings.
