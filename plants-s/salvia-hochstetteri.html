Salvia hochstetteri

Salvia hochstetteri, commonly known as Hochstetter's sage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Salvia hochstetteri is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to well-drained soils.
