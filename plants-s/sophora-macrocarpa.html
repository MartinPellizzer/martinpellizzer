Sophora macrocarpa

Sophora macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous legume native to China.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

Sophora macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
