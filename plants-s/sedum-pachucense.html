Sedum pachucense

Sedum pachucense, commonly known as the Mexican stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soil environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months.

Sedum pachucense is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
