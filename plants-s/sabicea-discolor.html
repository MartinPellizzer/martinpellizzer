Sabicea discolor

Sabicea discolor, commonly known as the variable senna, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
