Stipa pennata subsp. lejophylla

Stipa pennata subsp.

lejophylla, commonly known as the silver feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and panicles of fluffy, white or pale yellow flowers.

This subspecies thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with tall, upright stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
