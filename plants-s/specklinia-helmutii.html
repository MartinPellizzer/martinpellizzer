Specklinia helmutii

Specklinia helmutii, commonly known as the Helmut's tillandsia, is a species of air plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of pink or white, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
