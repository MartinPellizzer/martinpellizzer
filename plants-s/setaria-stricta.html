Setaria stricta

Setaria stricta, commonly known as green millet or rigid foxtail, is an annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are often mistaken for weeds in agricultural settings. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and fallow lands.

Setaria stricta is a hardy plant that can tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions.
