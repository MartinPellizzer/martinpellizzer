Sarcostemma odontolepis

Sarcostemma odontolepis, commonly known as the toothed starling vine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found along rocky outcrops and in dry woodlands.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
