Scirpus klingei

Scirpus klingei, commonly known as the dwarf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps with tall, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown, depending on the season. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Scirpus klingei is valued for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
