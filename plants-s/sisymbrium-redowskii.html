Sisymbrium redowskii

Sisymbrium redowskii, commonly known as the Redowski's broomrape, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically attaching itself to the roots of other species. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a highly reduced structure compared to its non-parasitic relatives. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the growth and survival of its host plants.
