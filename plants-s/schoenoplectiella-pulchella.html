Schoenoplectiella pulchella

Schoenoplectiella pulchella, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a herbaceous wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds and lakes, thriving in saturated soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Schoenoplectiella pulchella is also valued for its ability to filter water and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
