Stanhopea tigrina var. nigroviolacea

Stanhopea tigrina var. nigroviolacea, commonly known as the Black Violet Stanhopea, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking dark purple and violet-colored flowers, which often feature intricate patterns resembling a tiger's stripes. The plant typically grows as a compact, terrestrial orchid with a short, thick rhizome and a few narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, producing a fragrant, nectar-rich bloom that attracts various pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
