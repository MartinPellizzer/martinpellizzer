Symplocos horsfieldiana

Symplocos horsfieldiana, commonly known as the Horsfield's waxleaf.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos horsfieldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
