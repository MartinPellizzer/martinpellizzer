Sisymbrium tanacetifolium

Sisymbrium tanacetifolium, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance.

Sweet rocket is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
