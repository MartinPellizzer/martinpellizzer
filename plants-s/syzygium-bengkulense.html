Syzygium bengkulense

Syzygium bengkulense, commonly known as the Bengkulu apple or Bintangor, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits resembling small apples.

Syzygium bengkulense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
