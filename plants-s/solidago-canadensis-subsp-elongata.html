Solidago canadensis subsp. elongata

Solidago canadensis subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces showy, yellow composite flowers in late summer and early fall, which are composed of many small florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
