Solidago rigida var. canescens

Solidago rigida var. canescens, commonly known as stiff goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has rigid, fibrous stems that are often covered in a fine layer of white, silky hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough, textured surface.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woods, meadows, and along roadsides.
