Serpocaulon attenuatum

Serpocaulon attenuatum, commonly known as the slender serpocaulon, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or yellowish in color. It is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and cliffs.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
