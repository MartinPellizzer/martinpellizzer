Semiramisia pulcherrima

Semiramisia pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its striking appearance and historical significance in horticulture.
