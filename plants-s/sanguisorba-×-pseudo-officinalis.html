Sanguisorba × pseudo-officinalis

Sanguisorba × pseudo-officinalis, commonly known as the Japanese teasel, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pinkish-red flowers that attract pollinators. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile production.

Sanguisorba × pseudo-officinalis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
