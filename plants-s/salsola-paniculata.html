Salsola paniculata

Salsola paniculata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is an annual or biennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has succulent, spine-tipped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in panicles. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots and a high tolerance for salt and heat.

Salsola paniculata is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
