Scutellaria rugosa

Scutellaria rugosa, commonly known as rough blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Scutellaria rugosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
