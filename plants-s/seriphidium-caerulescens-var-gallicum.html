Seriphidium caerulescens var. gallicum

Seriphidium caerulescens var. gallicum, commonly known as the French tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
