Salix babylonica var. szechuanica

Salix babylonica var. szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly used in landscaping and garden settings.
