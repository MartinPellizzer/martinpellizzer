Solanum pseudocapsicum f. pilulosum

Solanum pseudocapsicum f. pilulosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and stems.

It is native to regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, often green or purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its hairy texture is a notable botanical feature, distinguishing it from other members of the species.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.
