Satureja micromerioides

Satureja micromerioides, commonly known as dwarf mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Satureja micromerioides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
