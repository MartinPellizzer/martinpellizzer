Statice taxanthema

Statice taxanthema, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of tiny, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its salt tolerance makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Statice taxanthema is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardy nature and long-lasting blooms.
