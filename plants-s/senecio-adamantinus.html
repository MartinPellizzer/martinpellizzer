Senecio adamantinus

Senecio adamantinus, commonly known as the diamondleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, diamond-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
