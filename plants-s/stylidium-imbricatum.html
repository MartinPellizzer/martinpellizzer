Stylidium imbricatum

Stylidium imbricatum, commonly known as the variable spurred orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive spur, and it produces small, rounded seed pods.

Stylidium imbricatum is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained environments.
