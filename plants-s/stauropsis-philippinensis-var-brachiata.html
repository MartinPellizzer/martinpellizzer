Stauropsis philippinensis var. brachiata

Stauropsis philippinensis var. brachiata, commonly known as the Philippine stauropsis, is a rare climbing fern native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
