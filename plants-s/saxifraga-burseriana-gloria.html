Saxifraga burseriana gloria

Saxifraga burseriana gloria, commonly known as the glory saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and is often found in rocky, alpine environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in late summer. It has hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh, arid habitat.

Saxifraga burseriana gloria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
