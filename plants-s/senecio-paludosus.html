Senecio paludosus

Senecio paludosus, commonly known as the swamp senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It has a low-growing habit with a rosette of basal leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
