Smilacina liliacea

Smilacina liliacea, commonly known as the white false Solomon's seal, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asparagales order and is part of the Smilacaceae family. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emerge in a rosette pattern before the stems elongate.

Smilacina liliacea is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in partial shade.
