Strychnos guineensis

Strychnos guineensis, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red or orange berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is notable for containing toxic alkaloids, such as strychnine, which are present in its seeds and roots.
