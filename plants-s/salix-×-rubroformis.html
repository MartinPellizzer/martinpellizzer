Salix × rubroformis

Salix × rubroformis, commonly known as the redform hybrid willow, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Salix alba and Salix triandra.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in moist soils along rivers, streams, and wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments. It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination and seed dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
