Schotia kirunduensis

Schotia kirunduensis, commonly known as the Kirundo acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern African region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Schotia kirunduensis is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuelwood.
