Salacia panamensis

Salacia panamensis, commonly known as the Panama salacia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
