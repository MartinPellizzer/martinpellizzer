Sabina poblana

Sabina poblana, commonly known as Mexican juniper, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Sabina poblana is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
