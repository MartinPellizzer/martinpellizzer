Scabiosa subalpina

Scabiosa subalpina, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained, sunny habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five petals arranged in a cup-like shape, typically in shades of pink, white, or purple.

Scabiosa subalpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early summer blooms.
