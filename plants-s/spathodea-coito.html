Spathodea coito

Spathodea coito, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, flame-colored bracts that surround its small, clustered flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Spathodea coito is also known for its rapid growth and ability to produce a profusion of blooms during the rainy season.
