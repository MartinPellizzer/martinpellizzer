Stylidium glaucum

Stylidium glaucum, commonly known as the blue-stemmed sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a unique structure with a spurred lip that serves as a nectar source for pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny habitats.

Stylidium glaucum is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand dry conditions.
