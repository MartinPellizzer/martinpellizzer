Scilla concinna

Scilla concinna, commonly known as the spring snowflake, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and mild climates.

Scilla concinna is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
