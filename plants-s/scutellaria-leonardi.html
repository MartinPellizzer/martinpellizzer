Scutellaria leonardi

Scutellaria leonardi, commonly known as Leonard's skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, which bloom during the summer months.

Scutellaria leonardi is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
