Sasa saitoana

Sasa saitoana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Sasa bamboo, is a shrub-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasa saitoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
