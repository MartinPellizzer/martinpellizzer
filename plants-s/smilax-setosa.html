Smilax setosa

Smilax setosa, commonly known as the greenbriar or catbriar, is a perennial vine native to the eastern United States.

It features a woody stem covered with sharp, thorn-like projections, which help in climbing and defending against herbivores. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, spiny berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Smilax setosa is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a common sight in woodland areas.
