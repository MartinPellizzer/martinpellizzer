Satureja pilosa var. slavjankae

Satureja pilosa var. slavjankae, commonly known as Slavjanka savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional herbal medicine and cuisine.

This variety is distinguished by its unique morphology and habitat preferences compared to other satureja species.
