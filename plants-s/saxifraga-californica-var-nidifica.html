Saxifraga californica var. nidifica

Saxifraga californica var. nidifica, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial plant native to California.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and rock gardens.
