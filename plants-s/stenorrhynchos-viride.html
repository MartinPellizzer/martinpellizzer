Stenorrhynchos viride

Stenorrhynchos viride, commonly known as the green stenorrhynchos, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Stenorrhynchos viride is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
