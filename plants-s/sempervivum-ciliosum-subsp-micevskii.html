Sempervivum ciliosum subsp. micevskii

Sempervivum ciliosum subsp.

micevskii, commonly known as the Armenian houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Caucasus region. It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in arid environments.
