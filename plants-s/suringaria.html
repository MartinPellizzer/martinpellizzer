Suringaria

Suringaria, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gesneriaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Madagascar, and the Arabian Peninsula. The plants are characterized by their compact, rosette-forming growth habit and showy, velvety flowers that come in a variety of colors. Suringaria species typically thrive in bright, indirect light and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

They are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
