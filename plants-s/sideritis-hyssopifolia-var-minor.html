Sideritis hyssopifolia var. minor

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. minor, commonly known as lesser hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and slightly aromatic, contributing to its medicinal uses.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Lamiaceae family, sharing similar structural and chemical characteristics.
