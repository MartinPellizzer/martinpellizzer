Scleranthus tenuissimus

Scleranthus tenuissimus, commonly known as slender sand-plant, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It features slender, branching stems and small, pale green or yellowish leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Scleranthus tenuissimus plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
