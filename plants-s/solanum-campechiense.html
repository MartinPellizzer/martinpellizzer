Solanum campechiense

Solanum campechiense, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and leaves, and it is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

Solanum campechiense is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
