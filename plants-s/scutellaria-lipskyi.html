Scutellaria lipskyi

Scutellaria lipskyi, commonly known as the Litsky's blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
