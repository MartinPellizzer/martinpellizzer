Senecio rosulifer

Senecio rosulifer, commonly known as the Rosulate Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rosettes of narrow, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a flat, disk-like structure, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Senecio rosulifer is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
