Salix argyrodendron

Salix argyrodendron, commonly known as the silver-leaved willow, is a species of willow native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, woody fruits.

Salix argyrodendron is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
