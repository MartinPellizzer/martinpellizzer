Solanum tejucense

Solanum tejucense, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn red when mature. Solanum tejucense is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it has a long history of traditional use in local cultures.
