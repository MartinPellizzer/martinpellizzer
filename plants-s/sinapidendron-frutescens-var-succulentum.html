Sinapidendron frutescens var. succulentum

Sinapidendron frutescens var. succulentum, commonly known as the succulent kudzuvine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
