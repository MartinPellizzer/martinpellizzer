Selago multispicata

Selago multispicata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red and yellow. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in montane forests.

Selago multispicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
