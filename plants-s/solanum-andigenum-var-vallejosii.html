Solanum andigenum var. vallejosii

Solanum andigenum var. vallejosii, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators. Its leaves are usually alternate and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cooler and more humid conditions.
