Senecio aureus var. aurantiacus

Senecio aureus var. aurantiacus, commonly known as the orange lion's tail, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red bracts that resemble a lion's tail. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique and eye-catching appearance.
