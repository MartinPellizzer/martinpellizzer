Schizocasia

Schizocasia, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into round, yellow fruits resembling small melons. Schizocasia is valued for its edible fruit, which is consumed both raw and cooked.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional agriculture and local diets.
