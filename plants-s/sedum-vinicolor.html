Sedum vinicolor

Sedum vinicolor, commonly known as purple stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, visually appealing plants.
