Salvia calycina

Salvia calycina, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Salvia calycina thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
