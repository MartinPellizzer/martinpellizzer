Senecio talinoides subsp. cylindricus

Senecio talinoides subsp.

cylindricus, commonly known as the cylindrical senecio, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa. It features cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water, adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, with a bluish-green hue that helps reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
