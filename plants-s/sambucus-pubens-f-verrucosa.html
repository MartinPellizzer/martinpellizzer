Sambucus pubens f. verrucosa

Sambucus pubens f. verrucosa, commonly known as the rough-fruited elderberry, is a variant of the elderberry plant characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured berries.

It is a deciduous shrub native to North America, typically found in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early summer, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
