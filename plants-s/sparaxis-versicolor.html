Sparaxis versicolor

Sparaxis versicolor, commonly known as the rain lily, is a striking South African plant characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the coastal regions of South Africa. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
