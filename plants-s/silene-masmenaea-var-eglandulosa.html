Silene masmenaea var. eglandulosa

Silene masmenaea var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular silene, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. This variety is often found in grassy areas and along woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Caryophyllaceae family.
