Solidago virgaurea proles hartmanniana

Solidago virgaurea proles hartmanniana, commonly known as the Hartmann's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
