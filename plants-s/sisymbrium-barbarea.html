Sisymbrium barbarea

Sisymbrium barbarea, commonly known as wild radish, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features erect, branched stems with alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The plant produces round, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
