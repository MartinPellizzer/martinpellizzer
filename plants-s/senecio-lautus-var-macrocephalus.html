Senecio lautus var. macrocephalus

Senecio lautus var. macrocephalus, commonly known as the giant ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and roadsides.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
