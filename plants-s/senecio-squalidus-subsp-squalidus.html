Senecio squalidus subsp. squalidus

Senecio squalidus subsp.

squalidus, commonly known as the sticky sedum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
