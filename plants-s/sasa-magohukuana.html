Sasa magohukuana

Sasa magohukuana, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a shrub-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often a vibrant yellow-green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa magohukuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in Japanese culture.
