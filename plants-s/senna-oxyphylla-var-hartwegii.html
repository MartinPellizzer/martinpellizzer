Senna oxyphylla var. hartwegii

Senna oxyphylla var. hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in regions with suitable climates.
