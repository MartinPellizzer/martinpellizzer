Senecio silvarum

Senecio silvarum, commonly known as the forest senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Senecio silvarum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
