Schinnongia ciliata

Schinnongia ciliata, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Schinnongia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden settings.
