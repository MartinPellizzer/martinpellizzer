Salix monandra var. angustifolia

Salix monandra var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 cm wide. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, often riparian environments, making it a common sight near streams and wetlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
