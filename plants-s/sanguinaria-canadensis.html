Sanguinaria canadensis

Sanguinaria canadensis, commonly known as bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a single, palmately lobed leaf and a single, white, bell-shaped flower that appears in early spring. The plant is characterized by its reddish-orange sap, which gives it its common name and has been used historically for various purposes. Bloodroot grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and medicinal properties have made it both ecologically and culturally significant.
