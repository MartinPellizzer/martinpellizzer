Salix × pendulina nothof. salamonii

Salix × pendulina nothof.

salamonii, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Salix alba and Salix babylonica. It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, typical of many willow species.

The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often a bright green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in gardens and along waterways.
