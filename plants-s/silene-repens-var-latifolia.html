Silene repens var. latifolia

Silene repens var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bluets, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on moist, calcareous soils. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in wet meadows and along the edges of ponds and streams.
