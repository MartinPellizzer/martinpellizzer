Salix udensis subsp. siuzevii

Salix udensis subsp.

siuzevii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and catkin flowers, typical of the Salicaceae family.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in riparian zones. It has a relatively short lifespan and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, temperate climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
