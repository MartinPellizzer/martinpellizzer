Sorghum dubium

Sorghum dubium, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in loose panicles, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Sorghum dubium is also valued for its potential as a forage crop and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
