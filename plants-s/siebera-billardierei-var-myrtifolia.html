Siebera billardierei var. myrtifolia

Siebera billardierei var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the Myrtle-leaved Siebera, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understorey habitats. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in native ecosystems.
