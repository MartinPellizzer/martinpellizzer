Senecio pterophorus var. apterus

Senecio pterophorus var. apterus, commonly known as the winged groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow and are arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
