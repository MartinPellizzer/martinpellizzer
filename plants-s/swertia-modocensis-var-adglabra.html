Swertia modocensis var. adglabra

Swertia modocensis var. adglabra, commonly known as the Modoc gentian, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
