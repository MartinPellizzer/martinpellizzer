Solanum glanduliferum

Solanum glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which bear small glandular structures. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Solanum glanduliferum is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and contains toxic compounds that make it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
