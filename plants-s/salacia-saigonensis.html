Salacia saigonensis

Salacia saigonensis, commonly known as Vietnamese gooseberry, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. These berries are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soils.
