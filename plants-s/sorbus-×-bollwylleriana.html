Sorbus × bollwylleriana

Sorbus × bollwylleriana, commonly known as the Bollwyller's whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, characterized by its ornamental value and ecological significance.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in spring, followed by small, red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

It is native to Europe and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental foliage and fruit.
