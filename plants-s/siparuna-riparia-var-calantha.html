Siparuna riparia var. calantha

Siparuna riparia var. calantha, commonly known as the pink-flowered siparuna, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red bracts that enclose small, white flowers, giving it a dramatic visual appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
