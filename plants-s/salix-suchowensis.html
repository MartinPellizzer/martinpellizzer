Salix suchowensis

Salix suchowensis, commonly known as the Suchow willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species for stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
