Salix × permixta

Salix × permixta, commonly known as the hybrid weeping willow, is a deciduous tree that results from the cross between Salix alba (white willow) and Salix babylonica (Babylon willow).

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance, often drooping to the ground. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, silvery underside that shimmers in the wind.

Salix × permixta is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is often found in parks and along waterways.
