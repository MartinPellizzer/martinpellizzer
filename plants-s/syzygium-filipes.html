Syzygium filipes

Syzygium filipes, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals but not typically for humans. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Syzygium filipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its floral display.
