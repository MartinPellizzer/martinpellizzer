Sambucus Glauca Var. Neomexicana

Sambucus glauca var. neomexicana is a rare and striking plant native to the southwestern United States. Known for its vibrant blue-green foliage and clusters of white flowers, it adds a unique visual element to its natural habitat. This variant of the elderberry plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing remarkable resilience. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for pollinators and wildlife. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
