Salomonia longiciliata

Salomonia longiciliata, commonly known as the long-beak salvia, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
