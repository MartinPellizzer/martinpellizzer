Suaeda kulundensis

Suaeda kulundensis, commonly known as the Kulund Desert spinach, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding its survival in dry conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish or yellowish, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Suaeda kulundensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
