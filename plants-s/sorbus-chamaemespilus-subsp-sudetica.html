Sorbus chamaemespilus subsp. sudetica

Sorbus chamaemespilus subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the Bohemian whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and small, glossy leaves that turn red in autumn.

The plant produces clusters of white flowers in spring, which are followed by small, red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
