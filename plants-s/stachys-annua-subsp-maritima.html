Stachys annua subsp. maritima

Stachys annua subsp.

maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and striking spires of blue or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and rocky shores. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, four-lobed fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens along the coast.
