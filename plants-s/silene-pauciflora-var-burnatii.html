Silene pauciflora var. burnatii

Silene pauciflora var. burnatii, commonly known as the Burnat's few-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

This variety is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native range.
