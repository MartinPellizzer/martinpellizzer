Solandra brachycalyx

Solandra brachycalyx, commonly known as the small-calyx solandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal regions, characterized by its dense, woody structure. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Solandra brachycalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
