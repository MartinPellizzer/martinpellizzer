Sclerocactus uncinatus var. crassihamatus

Sclerocactus uncinatus var. crassihamatus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed hooked cactus, is a rare and specialized cactus native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, adapted to its arid environment. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators. Its spines are stout and hooked, providing protection from herbivores.

This variety is particularly sensitive to environmental changes, making it a valuable indicator species for ecosystem health.
