Sieversia peckii

Sieversia peckii, commonly known as the Peck's sievegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Sieversia peckii typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
