Scirpus reptans

Scirpus reptans, commonly known as the creeping bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, and it produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-brown flowers. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that help stabilize soil.

Scirpus reptans plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
