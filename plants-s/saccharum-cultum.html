Saccharum cultum

Saccharum cultum, commonly known as cultivated sugarcane.

It is a tall, perennial grass native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stalks that contain a high concentration of sucrose. Its growth is typically supported by warm climates and abundant rainfall.

The botanical features of Saccharum cultum make it an essential crop in the production of sugar and biofuel.
