Saxifraga grandipetala

Saxifraga grandipetala, commonly known as the large-petaled saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, broad leaves and produces clusters of showy, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in mountainous areas where it can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Saxifraga grandipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
