Statice occidentalis

Statice occidentalis, commonly known as western glassflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and blue. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often used in floral arrangements due to their durability.

Statice occidentalis is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants.
