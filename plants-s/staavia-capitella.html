Staavia capitella

Staavia capitella, commonly known as the clustered starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in dense, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering period usually occurs during the cooler months, and it is often found in grassland or shrubland habitats.

Staavia capitella is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
