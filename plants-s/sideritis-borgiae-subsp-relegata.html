Sideritis borgiae subsp. relegata

Sideritis borgiae subsp.

relegata, commonly known as the Borgia's sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants.

The species is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
