Sowerbaea juncea

Sowerbaea juncea, commonly known as the Christmas fern, is a perennial evergreen fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arching fronds that resemble a Christmas tree, hence its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces spore-bearing leaves in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Sowerbaea juncea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide year-round interest in the garden.
