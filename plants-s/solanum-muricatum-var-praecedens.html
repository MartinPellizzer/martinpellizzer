Solanum muricatum var. praecedens

Solanum muricatum var. praecedens, commonly known as the purple passionfruit, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to the Andean regions of South America and is characterized by its vibrant purple to red fruits with a thick, spiny rind. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is valued for its aromatic, sweet-tart pulp, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
