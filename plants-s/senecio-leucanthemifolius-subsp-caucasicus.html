Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp. caucasicus

Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp.

caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian whiteleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
