Solanum albicans

Solanum albicans, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Solanum albicans is typically found in moist, shaded environments and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
