Senecio lugens var. exaltatus

Senecio lugens var. exaltatus, commonly known as the tall senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
