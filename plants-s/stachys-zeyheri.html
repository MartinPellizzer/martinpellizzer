Stachys zeyheri

Stachys zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's hedge parsley, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
