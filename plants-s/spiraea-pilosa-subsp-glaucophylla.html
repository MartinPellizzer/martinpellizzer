Spiraea pilosa subsp. glaucophylla

Spiraea pilosa subsp.

glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla spiraea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive glaucous (bluish-gray) underside, giving the plant its subspecies name. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
