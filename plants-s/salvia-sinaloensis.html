Salvia sinaloensis

Salvia sinaloensis, commonly known as Sinaloan sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow leaves, and spikes of violet to blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
