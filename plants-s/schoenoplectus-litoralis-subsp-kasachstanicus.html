Schoenoplectus litoralis subsp. kasachstanicus

Schoenoplectus litoralis subsp.

kasachstanicus, commonly known as the Caspian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and saline environments of Central Asia. It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its adaptability to saline conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems in the region.
