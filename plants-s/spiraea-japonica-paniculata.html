Spiraea japonica paniculata

Spiraea japonica paniculata, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically oval to ovate in shape, with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early summer, which are arranged in panicles. These flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
