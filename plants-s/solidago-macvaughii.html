Solidago macvaughii

Solidago macvaughii, commonly known as Macvaugh's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Solidago macvaughii plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during its flowering period.
