Scutellaria costaricana

Scutellaria costaricana, commonly known as the Costa Rican skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Scutellaria costaricana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some indigenous communities.
