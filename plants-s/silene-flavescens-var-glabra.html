Silene flavescens var. glabra

Silene flavescens var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth silene, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, often hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a distinctive, showy inflorescence.
