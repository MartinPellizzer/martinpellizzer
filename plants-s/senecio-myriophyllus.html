Senecio myriophyllus

Senecio myriophyllus, commonly known as the mountain ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in some traditional uses.

However, it can be toxic to livestock if ingested in large quantities.
