Spodiopogon tainanensis

Spodiopogon tainanensis, commonly known as Tainan grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern China and Taiwan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
