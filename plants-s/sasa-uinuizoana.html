Sasa uinuizoana

Sasa uinuizoana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it a common component of forest understories.

Sasa uinuizoana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Japanese culture.
