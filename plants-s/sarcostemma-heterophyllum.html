Sarcostemma heterophyllum

Sarcostemma heterophyllum, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its latex sap is milky and has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
