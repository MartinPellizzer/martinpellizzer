Salvia muelleri

Salvia muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia muelleri is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
