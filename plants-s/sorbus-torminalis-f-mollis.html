Sorbus torminalis f. mollis

Sorbus torminalis f. mollis, commonly known as the velvet rowan, is a variant of the rowan tree characterized by its distinctive, velvety foliage.

This cultivar features thick, soft leaves that give it a unique tactile quality, making it visually appealing. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens. It produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

The botanical features of Sorbus torminalis f. mollis make it a valuable addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
