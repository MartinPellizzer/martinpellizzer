Solanum stenotomum f. alkka-phinu

Solanum stenotomum f. alkka-phinu, commonly known as the Alkka-phinu tomato, is a wild tomato species native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, typical of many solanaceous species. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique flavor and nutritional value, it has gained interest for potential cultivation and use in local cuisines.
