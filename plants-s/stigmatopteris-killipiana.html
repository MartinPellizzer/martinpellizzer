Stigmatopteris killipiana

Stigmatopteris killipiana, commonly known as the Killip's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into small, rounded segments. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas with rich, organic soil. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Stigmatopteris killipiana plays a role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native habitat through its ecological interactions and habitat provision.
