Scutellaria potosina subsp. platyphylla

Scutellaria potosina subsp.

platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the mint family, Lamiaceae.
