Sphaeralcea filicaulis var. setosa

Sphaeralcea filicaulis var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental horticulture.
