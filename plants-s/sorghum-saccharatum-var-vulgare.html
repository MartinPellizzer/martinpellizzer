Sorghum saccharatum var. vulgare

Sorghum saccharatum var. vulgare, commonly known as sweet sorghum, is a tall, warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its high sugar content in its stalks, making it a valuable crop for biofuel and sugar production. The plant has deep roots that help prevent soil erosion and improve soil structure. Its leaves are narrow and sword-shaped, and it produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Sweet sorghum is drought-tolerant and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile agricultural crop.
