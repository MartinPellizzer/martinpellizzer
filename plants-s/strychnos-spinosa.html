Strychnos spinosa

Strychnos spinosa, commonly known as the spiny antirrhinum, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces woody, spindle-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Strychnos spinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
