Streptocarpus cyaneus subsp. cyaneus

Streptocarpus cyaneus subsp.

cyaneus, commonly known as the blue streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

This species is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
