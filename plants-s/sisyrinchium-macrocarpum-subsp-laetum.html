Sisyrinchium macrocarpum subsp. laetum

Sisyrinchium macrocarpum subsp.

laetum, commonly known as the showy blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces showy, blue to violet flowers with a yellow center.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in partial to full sun.

The subspecies laetum is distinguished by its more vibrant flower color and slightly larger inflorescences compared to other subspecies.
