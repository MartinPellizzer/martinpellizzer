Sambucus pubens var. melanocarpa

Sambucus pubens var. melanocarpa, commonly known as black elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in the fall. These berries are edible when fully ripe and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
