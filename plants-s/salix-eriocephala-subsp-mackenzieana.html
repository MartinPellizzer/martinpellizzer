Salix eriocephala subsp. mackenzieana

Salix eriocephala subsp.

mackenzieana, commonly known as Mackenzie's willow, is a shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of North America. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, catkin-like flowers that appear in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its stems are often covered with a dense layer of short, soft hairs.

This subspecies plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment and provides habitat for various arctic and alpine organisms.
