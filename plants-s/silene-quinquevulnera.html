Silene quinquevulnera

Silene quinquevulnera, commonly known as the five-wound catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Silene quinquevulnera is also known for its medicinal uses by indigenous peoples, who have historically used it to treat wounds and skin ailments.
