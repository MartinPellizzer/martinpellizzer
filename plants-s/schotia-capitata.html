Schotia capitata

Schotia capitata, commonly known as the silver tree, is a distinctive South African plant characterized by its unique, umbrella-like crown of branches.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Western and Northern Cape provinces. The plant features small, waxy, pale green leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its branches. Its flowers are small and yellow, and it produces white, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Schotia capitata is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky outcrops and coastal areas.
