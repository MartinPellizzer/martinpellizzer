Silene dahurica

Silene dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene dahurica is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to cold climates.
