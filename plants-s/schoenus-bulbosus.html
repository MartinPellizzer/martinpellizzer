Schoenus bulbosus

Schoenus bulbosus, commonly known as the bulbous sedge, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland conservation.
