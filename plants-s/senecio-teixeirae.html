Senecio teixeirae

Senecio teixeirae, commonly known as the Teixeira's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
