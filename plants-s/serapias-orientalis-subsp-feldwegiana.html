Serapias orientalis subsp. feldwegiana

Serapias orientalis subsp.

feldwegiana, commonly known as the Feldweg's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It belongs to the genus Serapias, which is part of the family Orchidaceae, commonly known as the orchid family.

The plant typically features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with distinctive markings. It thrives in rocky and limestone soils, often growing in open habitats such as slopes and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
