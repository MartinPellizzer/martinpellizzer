Scutellaria campestris

Scutellaria campestris, commonly known as field scullion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, two-lipped, and usually blue or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Scutellaria campestris is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.
