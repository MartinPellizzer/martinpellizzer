Sedum lushanense

Sedum lushanense, commonly known as the Lushan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and star-shaped, usually appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Sedum lushanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
