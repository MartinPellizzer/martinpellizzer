Sarcochilus fitzgeraldii

Sarcochilus fitzgeraldii, commonly known as Fitzgerald's saxifrage, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a slender, upright stem with a few leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is known for its hardy nature.

Sarcochilus fitzgeraldii is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological significance in New Zealand's flora.
