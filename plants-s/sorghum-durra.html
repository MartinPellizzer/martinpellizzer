Sorghum durra

Sorghum durra, commonly known as durra, is a tall, annual grass native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce edible grains. The plant is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Durra is primarily cultivated for its grain, which is used to make flour, porridge, and beverages like sorghum beer.
