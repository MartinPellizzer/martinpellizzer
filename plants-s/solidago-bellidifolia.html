Solidago bellidifolia

Solidago bellidifolia, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Solidago bellidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
