Scorpiurus subvillosus var. laevigatus

Scorpiurus subvillosus var. laevigatus, commonly known as the smoothleaf scorpiurus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, hairy stems that are often prostrate or ascending. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth texture that distinguishes it from other varieties.

Small white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
