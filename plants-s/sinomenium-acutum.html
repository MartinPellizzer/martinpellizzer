Sinomenium acutum

Sinomenium acutum, commonly known as Chinese wisteria, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in early summer. Its fruits are elongated, green pods that mature into red-brown capsules.

Sinomenium acutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
