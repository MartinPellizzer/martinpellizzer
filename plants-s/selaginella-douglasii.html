Selaginella douglasii

Selaginella douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern ally that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a rosette of spirally arranged leaves that are often green or reddish in color, with a distinctive, spiky appearance. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate sporophylls, which are specialized leaves for spore production.

Selaginella douglasii is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
