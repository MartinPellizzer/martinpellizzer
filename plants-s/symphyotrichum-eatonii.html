Symphyotrichum eatonii

Symphyotrichum eatonii, commonly known as Eaton's heath aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
