Salvia lagochila

Salvia lagochila, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia lagochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
