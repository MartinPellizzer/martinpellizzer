Silenanthe

Silenanthe, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that display a mix of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a sweet, intoxicating fragrance. Silenanthe thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
