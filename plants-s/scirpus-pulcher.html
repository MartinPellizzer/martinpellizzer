Scirpus pulcher

Scirpus pulcher, commonly known as the elegant bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery inflorescences that give it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its leaves are narrow and sword-shaped, typically ranging from 1 to 3 meters in height.

Scirpus pulcher plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
