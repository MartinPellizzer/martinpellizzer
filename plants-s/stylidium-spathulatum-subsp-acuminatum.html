Stylidium spathulatum subsp. acuminatum

Stylidium spathulatum subsp.

acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated spurred orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Australia. It features a slender, leafless stem with a single, erect flower that has a distinctive spurred lip.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a narrow, elongated spur.

It is a short-lived perennial, often flowering in response to rainfall.
