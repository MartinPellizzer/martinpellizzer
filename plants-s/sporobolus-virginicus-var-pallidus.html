Sporobolus virginicus var. pallidus

Sporobolus virginicus var. pallidus, commonly known as coastal flatsedge, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or brown. This plant thrives in wet, coastal environments and is often found in marshes and tidal flats. Its ability to tolerate saltwater makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
