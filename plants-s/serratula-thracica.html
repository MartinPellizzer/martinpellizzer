Serratula thracica

Serratula thracica, commonly known as the Thrace goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are serrated and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Serratula thracica is known for its medicinal properties, though it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
