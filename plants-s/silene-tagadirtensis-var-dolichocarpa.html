Silene tagadirtensis var. dolichocarpa

Silene tagadirtensis var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
