Swertia californica

Swertia californica, commonly known as California gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often branching and upright.

Swertia californica is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
