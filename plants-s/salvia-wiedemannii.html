Salvia wiedemannii

Salvia wiedemannii, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Salvia wiedemannii is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
