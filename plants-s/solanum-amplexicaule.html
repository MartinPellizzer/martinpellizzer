Solanum amplexicaule

Solanum amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it its common name. Its small, star-shaped flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it produces berries that range from yellow to red in color.

Solanum amplexicaule is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
