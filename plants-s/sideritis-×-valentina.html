Sideritis × valentina

Sideritis × valentina, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and flowering stems.

It is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, where it thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Sideritis × valentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
