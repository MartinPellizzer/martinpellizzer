Syringa pubescens var. hirsuta

Syringa pubescens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, hairy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Syringa pubescens var. hirsuta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability.
