Schisandra tomentella

Schisandra tomentella, commonly known as the woolly five-flower, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and dark, glossy berries. The plant is notable for its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. Schisandra tomentella is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
