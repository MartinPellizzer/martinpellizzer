Stachys albiflora

Stachys albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Stachys albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
