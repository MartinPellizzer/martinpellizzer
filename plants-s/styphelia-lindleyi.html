Styphelia lindleyi

Styphelia lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's snowbell, is a small evergreen shrub native to the wet heathlands of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and covered in a fine, silvery bloom. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires cool, moist conditions.

Styphelia lindleyi is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the unique biodiversity of heathland ecosystems.
