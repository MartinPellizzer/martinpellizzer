Stenosiphonium zeylanicum

Stenosiphonium zeylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fragrant, aromatic wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now considered an endangered species.
