Solanum papa

Solanum papa, commonly known as the papa tomato or wild tomato, is a species native to South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and typically grows as a shrub or small tree. The plant produces small, yellow or red berries that contain the edible seeds. Its leaves are usually ovate with a hairy texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Solanum papa is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
