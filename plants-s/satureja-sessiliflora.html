Satureja sessiliflora

Satureja sessiliflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Satureja sessiliflora is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its antiseptic and digestive properties.
