Silene sveae

Silene sveae, commonly known as the Swedish catchfly, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Silene sveae is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
