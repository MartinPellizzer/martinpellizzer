Saxifraga cespitosa var. palmata

Saxifraga cespitosa var. palmata, commonly known as the clustered saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant forms dense, mat-like clusters with short, upright stems and small, palmately arranged leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pink, appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
