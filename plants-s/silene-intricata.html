Silene intricata

Silene intricata, commonly known as the intricate cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces slender, branching stems. Silene intricata is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Caryophyllaceae family.
