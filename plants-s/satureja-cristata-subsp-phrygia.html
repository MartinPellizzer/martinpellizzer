Satureja cristata subsp. phrygia

Satureja cristata subsp.

phrygia, commonly known as Phrygian savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies due to its pungent, peppery scent.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like rosemary and thyme.
