Salvia × simonkaiana

Salvia × simonkaiana, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and tall, sturdy stems that rise above the foliage. The plant produces showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a long blooming period that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
