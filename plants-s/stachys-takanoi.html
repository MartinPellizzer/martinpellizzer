Stachys takanoi

Stachys takanoi, commonly known as the Japanese hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, violet-colored flowers arranged in whorled clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
