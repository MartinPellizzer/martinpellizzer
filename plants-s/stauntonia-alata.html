Stauntonia alata

Stauntonia alata, commonly known as the winged sterculia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often followed by elongated, woody pods. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Stauntonia alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
