Scaphosepalum standleyi

Scaphosepalum standleyi, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive spurred lip, which is a key feature in orchid pollination. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its habitat.
