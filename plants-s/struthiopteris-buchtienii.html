Struthiopteris buchtienii

Struthiopteris buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtien's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are often divided into multiple segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as along stream banks or in dense woodland areas. Its spore-producing structures, called soruses, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
