Scorzonera cana var. jacquiniana

Scorzonera cana var. jacquiniana, commonly known as the Jacob's squill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, tuberous root system that is often harvested for its edible qualities. The plant has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically pale blue or white, with five petals and a distinctive central yellow gland.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
