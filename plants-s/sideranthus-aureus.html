Sideranthus aureus

Sideranthus aureus, commonly known as goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual or perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Sideranthus aureus is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in open fields.
