Shorea pinanga

Shorea pinanga, commonly known as the mangrove ironwood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, often in brackish or saline water, making it an important component of mangrove ecosystems. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth surface, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Shorea pinanga plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
