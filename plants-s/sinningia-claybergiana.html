Sinningia claybergiana

Sinningia claybergiana, commonly known as the claybergiana hybrid, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among collectors and growers of gesneriad plants.
