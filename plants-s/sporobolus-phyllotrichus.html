Sporobolus phyllotrichus

Sporobolus phyllotrichus, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, and it produces light, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sporobolus phyllotrichus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
