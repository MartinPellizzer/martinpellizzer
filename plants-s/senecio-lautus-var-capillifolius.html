Senecio lautus var. capillifolius

Senecio lautus var. capillifolius, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-covered stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
