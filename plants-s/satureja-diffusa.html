Satureja diffusa

Satureja diffusa, commonly known as Spanish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their antiseptic and carminative properties.

Satureja diffusa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and versatile garden plant.
