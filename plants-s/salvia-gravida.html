Salvia gravida

Salvia gravida, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and have a mint-like fragrance.

Salvia gravida is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
