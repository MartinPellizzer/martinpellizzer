Sesleria insularis

Sesleria insularis, commonly known as the island sesleria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Sesleria insularis is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a rare and locally distributed species.
