Securigera atlantica

Securigera atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic salsify, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a unique, elongated seed pod that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it holds interest for both ecological and pharmacological research.
