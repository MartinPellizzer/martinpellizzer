Sophia leptophylla

Sophia leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf sophia, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a compact growth habit.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
