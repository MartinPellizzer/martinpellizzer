Salpichroa ramosissima

Salpichroa ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often branch out extensively. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
