Sinapis arvensis subsp. allionii

Sinapis arvensis subsp.

allionii, commonly known as yellow mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is native to Europe and western Asia.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a biennial life cycle, with the first year being a rosette of leaves and the second year producing flowers and seeds.

The seeds are small and contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
