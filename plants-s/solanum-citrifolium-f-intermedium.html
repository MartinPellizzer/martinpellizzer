Solanum citrifolium f. intermedium

Solanum citrifolium f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate citron tree, is a variant of the citron tree native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its intermediate leaf morphology between the typical citron tree and its close relatives. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
