Scutia buxifolia

Scutia buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf scutia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that resembles the leaves of boxwood, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Scutia buxifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
