Silene maritima var. glareosa

Silene maritima var. glareosa, commonly known as the sea pink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
