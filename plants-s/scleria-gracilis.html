Scleria gracilis

Scleria gracilis, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Africa.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Scleria gracilis produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are often borne on tall, slender inflorescences.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
