Saxifraga pekinensis

Saxifraga pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features upright stems with clustered, small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.

Saxifraga pekinensis is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate frost, making it a hardy species in temperate regions.
