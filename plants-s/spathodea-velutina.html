Spathodea velutina

Spathodea velutina, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces striking orange to red inflorescences that attract various pollinators. Its trunk is covered in a dense, velvety layer of brownish fibers, giving it its common name.

Spathodea velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
