Symplocos macrophylla subsp. cordifolia

Symplocos macrophylla subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and India. It is characterized by its elliptical to heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, red berries that attract various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
