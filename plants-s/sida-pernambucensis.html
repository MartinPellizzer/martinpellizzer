Sida pernambucensis

Sida pernambucensis, commonly known as Brazilian mallow, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, heart-shaped leaves, and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils, thriving in warm, humid climates.

Sida pernambucensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional medicine.
