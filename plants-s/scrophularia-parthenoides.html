Scrophularia parthenoides

Scrophularia parthenoides, commonly known as the lesser scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, characterized by its bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred lower petal.

Scrophularia parthenoides is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in poor soils due to its adaptability.
