Sauropus bicolor

Sauropus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored sterculia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored leaves, which typically display a combination of green and white or silver patterns. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine in various regions.
