Setaria platycaulis

Setaria platycaulis, commonly known as broad-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, broad leaves and dense, grass-like foliage that often forms low-growing clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop in certain regions.
