Scirpus anomalus

Scirpus anomalus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has tall, slender stems that are often used for erosion control in wetland environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are wind-pollinated.

Scirpus anomalus plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems.
