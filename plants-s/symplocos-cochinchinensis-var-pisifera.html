Symplocos cochinchinensis var. pisifera

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. pisifera, commonly known as the large-leafed evergreen tea plant, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, fleshy berries. The berries are typically consumed raw or used in traditional remedies.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
