Sideritis incana var. altiatlantica

Sideritis incana var. altiatlantica, commonly known as the Canary Islands ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
