Scutellaria nigrescens

Scutellaria nigrescens, commonly known as black scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Scutellaria nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
