Sabia densiflora

Sabia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered sabia, is a member of the Malvaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
