Solanum unguiculatum

Solanum unguiculatum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. Solanum unguiculatum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is characterized by its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
