Symplocos pyrifolia

Symplocos pyrifolia, commonly known as Chinese waxapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The fruit is a woody, spiny drupe that turns red when mature, containing several seeds. This plant is often found in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
