Salvia popenoei

Salvia popenoei, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a striking perennial shrub native to the highlands of Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and full sun, making it a resilient addition to xeriscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
