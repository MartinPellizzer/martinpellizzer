Saxifraga brunoniana

Saxifraga brunoniana, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, usually blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette shape, and are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga brunoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
