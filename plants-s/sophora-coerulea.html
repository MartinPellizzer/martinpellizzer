Sophora coerulea

Sophora coerulea, commonly known as the blue pea tree, is a deciduous legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers, which are actually a result of anthocyanin pigments. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its pods contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The tree also has cultural significance in traditional medicine and is used in various local practices.
