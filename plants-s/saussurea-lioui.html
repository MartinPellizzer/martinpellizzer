Saussurea lioui

Saussurea lioui, commonly known as the Tibetan yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are often slender and branched.

Saussurea lioui is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
