Stelis papillipetala

Stelis papillipetala, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a hairy, spurred lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

Stelis papillipetala thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, porous growing media for optimal growth.
