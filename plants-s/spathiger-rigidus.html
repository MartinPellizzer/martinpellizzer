Spathiger rigidus

Spathiger rigidus, commonly known as the rigid spathiger, is a unique plant species characterized by its stiff, upright growth habit.

It typically features elongated, narrow leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or white in color, often blooming in late spring or early summer. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Spathiger rigidus is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
