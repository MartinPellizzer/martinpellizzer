Sidalcea elegans

Sidalcea elegans, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers and fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a shallow root system that helps it thrive in arid conditions.

Sidalcea elegans is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
