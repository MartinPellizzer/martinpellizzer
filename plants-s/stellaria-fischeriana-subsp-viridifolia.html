Stellaria fischeriana subsp. viridifolia

Stellaria fischeriana subsp.

viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leaved starwort, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, star-shaped white flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive green color, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
