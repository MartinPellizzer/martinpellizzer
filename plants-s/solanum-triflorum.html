Solanum triflorum

Solanum triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters of three, giving it its common name. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum triflorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
