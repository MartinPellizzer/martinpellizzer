Salix raddeana var. liangshuiensis

Salix raddeana var. liangshuiensis, commonly known as the Liangshui willow, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
