Satureja varia subsp. thymoides

Satureja varia subsp.

thymoides, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in traditional herbal medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller leaves and more compact growth habit compared to other satureja species.
