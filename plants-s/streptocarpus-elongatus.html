Streptocarpus elongatus

Streptocarpus elongatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved streptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of violet or blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Streptocarpus elongatus is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
