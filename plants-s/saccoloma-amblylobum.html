Saccoloma amblylobum

Saccoloma amblylobum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed saccoloma, is a fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists studying fern diversity and adaptation.
