Sison amomus

Sison amomus, commonly known as the Indian ginger or wild ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous underground stems that produce clusters of narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in spike-like inflorescences, and are often fragrant.

The plant is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
