Smilax populnea var. angustata

Smilax populnea var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, woody stems with thorned nodes and compound leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and berries provide food for various wildlife species, including birds and small mammals.
