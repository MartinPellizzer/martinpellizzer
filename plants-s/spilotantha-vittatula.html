Spilotantha vittatula

Spilotantha vittatula, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
