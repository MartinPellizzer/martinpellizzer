Sauropus parviflorus

Sauropus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered sauropus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Sauropus parviflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and traditional medicine.
