Smilax ecirrhata var. biltmoreana

Smilax ecirrhata var. biltmoreana, commonly known as the Biltmore smilax, is a variant of the smilax genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its berries, which are typically red or yellow, are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
