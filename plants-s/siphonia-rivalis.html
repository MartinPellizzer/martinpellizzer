Siphonia rivalis

Siphonia rivalis, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a flowering plant native to tropical coastal regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive red pneumatophores, which are specialized root structures that help the plant breathe in waterlogged soil. The plant thrives in saline environments and plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to reduce water loss in high-salinity conditions.

Siphonia rivalis is also known for its ability to filter pollutants from the water, making it an important species in mangrove forests.
