Solena dumetorum

Solena dumetorum, commonly known as "devil's weed" or "angel's trumpet," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant produces clusters of trumpet-shaped, bell-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow or orange. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a strong, unpleasant odor.

Solena dumetorum contains psychoactive alkaloids, making it both botanically intriguing and potentially hazardous.
