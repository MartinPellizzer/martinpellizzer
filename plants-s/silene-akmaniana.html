Silene akmaniana

Silene akmaniana, commonly known as the Akmanian campion, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene akmaniana is often found in mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
