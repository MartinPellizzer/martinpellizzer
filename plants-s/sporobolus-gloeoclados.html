Sporobolus gloeoclados

Sporobolus gloeoclados, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton. This plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with upright stems that can reach heights of up to 30 inches.

Sporobolus gloeoclados plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
