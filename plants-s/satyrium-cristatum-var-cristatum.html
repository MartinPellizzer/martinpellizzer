Satyrium cristatum var. cristatum

Satyrium cristatum var. cristatum, commonly known as the crested orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy leaves and a unique inflorescence that bears a cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high elevations. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
