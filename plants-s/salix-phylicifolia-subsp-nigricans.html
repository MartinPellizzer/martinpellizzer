Salix phylicifolia subsp. nigricans

Salix phylicifolia subsp.

nigricans, commonly known as black willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male and female on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
