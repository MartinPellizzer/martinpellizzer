Seguiera perfoliata var. compacta

Seguiera perfoliata var. compacta, commonly known as the compact form of the common seguiera, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
