Senecio davidii

Senecio davidii, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Senecio davidii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
