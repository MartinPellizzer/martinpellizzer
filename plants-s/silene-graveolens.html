Silene graveolens

Silene graveolens, commonly known as the stinking roselin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Silene graveolens is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more noticeable when the plant is in bloom.
