Suberanthus canellifolius

Suberanthus canellifolius, commonly known as the canellifolius suberanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Suberanthus canellifolius is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
