Senecio congestus

Senecio congestus, commonly known as the dense senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers atop slender, branched stems. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Senecio congestus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
