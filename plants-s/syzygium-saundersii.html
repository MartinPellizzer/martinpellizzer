Syzygium saundersii

Syzygium saundersii, commonly known as the Singapore tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, purple-black berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium saundersii thrives in humid, warm climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
