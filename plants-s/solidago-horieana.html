Solidago horieana

Solidago horieana, commonly known as the Japanese goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Solidago horieana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
