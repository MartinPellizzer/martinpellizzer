Salvia lorentii

Salvia lorentii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, mint-like aroma.

Salvia lorentii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
