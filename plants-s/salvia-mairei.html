Salvia mairei

Salvia mairei, commonly known as Maire's sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly minty scent, adding to its ornamental value.

Salvia mairei is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness in temperate climates.
