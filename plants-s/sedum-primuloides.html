Sedum primuloides

Sedum primuloides, commonly known as the "Baby's Breath Sedum," is a succulent plant native to the southern United States.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green or tinged with red, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
