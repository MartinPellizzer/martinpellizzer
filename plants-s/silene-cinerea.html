Silene cinerea

Silene cinerea, commonly known as hoary silene, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grayish-white foliage, which is covered in fine, powdery hairs. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Silene cinerea is a perennial herb that is often found in the eastern United States.
