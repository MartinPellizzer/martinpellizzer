Solanum muricatum var. teleutogenum

Solanum muricatum var. teleutogenum, commonly known as the purple passionfruit, is a variant of the passionfruit plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp with a tangy flavor. The plant is a climbing vine with opposite, ovate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. Its fruit develops from the flower and typically ripens in late summer to early autumn.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and peppers.
