Senna macranthera var. micans

Senna macranthera var. micans, commonly known as the velvet senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is known for its glandular hairs that give it a velvety texture, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
