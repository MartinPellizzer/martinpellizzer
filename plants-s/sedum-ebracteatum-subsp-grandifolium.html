Sedum ebracteatum subsp. grandifolium

Sedum ebracteatum subsp.

grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to North America. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions with minimal care.
