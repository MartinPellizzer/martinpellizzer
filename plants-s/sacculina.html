Sacculina

Sacculina, commonly known as the parasitic barnacle, is a genus of crustaceans that exhibits unique botanical-like features due to its parasitic lifestyle.

It lacks a distinct body structure and instead forms a sac-like, gelatinous body that attaches to its host, typically a crab. The plant-like appearance of Sacculina is due to its ability to absorb nutrients directly from the host's hemolymph. It reproduces through a complex life cycle involving both sexual and asexual stages.

This organism is often studied for its fascinating adaptations and its role in marine ecosystems.
