Sorghum Arundinaceum

Sorghum grass, scientifically known as Sorghum arundinaceum, is a hardy perennial plant native to Africa and widely cultivated for its versatile uses. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, making it a crucial crop for food security and environmental resilience. The plant is valued for its robust stalks, which are used in construction, thatching, and as a source of fiber. Additionally, Sorghum grass plays a vital role in soil conservation and livestock feed. Its adaptability and sustainability make it an important resource in agricultural systems worldwide.
