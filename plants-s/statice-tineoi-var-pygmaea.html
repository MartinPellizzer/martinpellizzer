Statice tineoi var. pygmaea

Statice tineoi var. pygmaea, commonly known as dwarf statice, is a compact, low-growing flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tightly packed spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Statice tineoi var. pygmaea is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like butterflies and bees.
