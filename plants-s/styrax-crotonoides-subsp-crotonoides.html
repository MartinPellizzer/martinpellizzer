Styrax crotonoides subsp. crotonoides

Styrax crotonoides subsp.

crotonoides, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China. It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on top.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in spring, which are followed by red or orange berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.

The species is also noted for its aromatic wood, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
