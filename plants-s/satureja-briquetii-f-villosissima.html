Satureja briquetii f. villosissima

Satureja briquetii f. villosissima, commonly known as the woolly woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
