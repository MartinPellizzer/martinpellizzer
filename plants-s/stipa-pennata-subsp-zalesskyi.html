Stipa pennata subsp. zalesskyi

Stipa pennata subsp.

zalesskyi, commonly known as the Zalessky's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth and produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that are often adorned with fine, silky hairs.

The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it conserve moisture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
