Syzygium millsii

Syzygium millsii, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apples, with a sweet, aromatic pulp. Syzygium millsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
