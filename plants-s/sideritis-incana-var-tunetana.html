Sideritis incana var. tunetana

Sideritis incana var. tunetana, commonly known as the Tunisian hairymouth shrub, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in North Africa and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or violet, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

Sideritis species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
