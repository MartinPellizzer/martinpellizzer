Stelis ophiodontodes

Stelis ophiodontodes, commonly known as the dragon's tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
