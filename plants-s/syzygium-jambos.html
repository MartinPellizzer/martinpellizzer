Syzygium jambos

Syzygium jambos, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruit exterior. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible, hairy fruits. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
