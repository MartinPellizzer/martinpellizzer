Stellaria humifusa var. suberecta

Stellaria humifusa var. suberecta, commonly known as the upright starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

This plant is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
