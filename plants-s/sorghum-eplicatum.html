Sorghum eplicatum

Sorghum eplicatum, commonly known as the white sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of pale yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in regions with limited rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an important resource for both forage and grain production.
