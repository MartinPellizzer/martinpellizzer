Sasa subglabra

Sasa subglabra, commonly known as the smooth bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are smooth and often slightly woody at the base. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glabrous surface, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its graceful appearance.
