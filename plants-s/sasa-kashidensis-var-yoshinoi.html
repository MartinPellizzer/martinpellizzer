Sasa kashidensis var. yoshinoi

Sasa kashidensis var. yoshinoi, commonly known as Yoshino bamboo, is a deciduous grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically greenish or pale brown. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
