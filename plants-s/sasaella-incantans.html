Sasaella incantans

Sasaella incantans, commonly known as the enchanted bamboo, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Sasaella incantans is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in traditional herbal medicine.
