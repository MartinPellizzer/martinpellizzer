Selaginella pouzolziana var. brevifolia

Selaginella pouzolziana var. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, creeping herb with closely spaced, scale-like leaves that form a dense, mat-like growth. The plant produces strobila, or cones, which contain spores for reproduction. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of certain ecosystems.
