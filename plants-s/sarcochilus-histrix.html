Sarcochilus histrix

Sarcochilus histrix, commonly known as the rough leaved hibiscus, is a tropical orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy structures. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a dark throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Sarcochilus histrix is known for its resilience and is often cultivated in warm climates for its ornamental value.
