Solenangis scandens

Solenangis scandens, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with aerial roots that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

Solenangis scandens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
