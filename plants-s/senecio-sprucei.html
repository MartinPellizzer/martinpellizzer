Senecio sprucei

Senecio sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce's groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Senecio sprucei is often found in mountainous areas, where it contributes to the unique flora of these ecosystems.
