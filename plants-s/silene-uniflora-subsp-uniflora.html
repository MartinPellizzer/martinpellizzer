Silene uniflora subsp. uniflora

Silene uniflora subsp.

uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent-like leaves.
