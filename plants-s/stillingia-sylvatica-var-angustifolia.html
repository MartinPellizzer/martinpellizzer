Stillingia sylvatica var. angustifolia

Stillingia sylvatica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf stellaria, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that split open to release tiny seeds.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
