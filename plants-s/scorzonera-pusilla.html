Scorzonera pusilla

Scorzonera pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf scorzonera, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scorzonera pusilla is relatively hardy and can be found in alpine and rocky habitats.
