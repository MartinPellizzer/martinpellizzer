Stramonium globosum

Stramonium globosum, commonly known as the globular thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive globular-shaped fruits. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant can grow up to 2 meters tall.

Stramonium globosum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
