Schisandra elongata

Schisandra elongata, commonly known as the long-fruited five-flavor vine, is a member of the Schisandraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that contain a single seed. Schisandra elongata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
