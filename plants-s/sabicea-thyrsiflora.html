Sabicea thyrsiflora

Sabicea thyrsiflora, commonly known as the thrysis tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

Its growth habit and floral structure make it an important species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
