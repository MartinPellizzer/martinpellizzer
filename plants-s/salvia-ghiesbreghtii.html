Salvia ghiesbreghtii

Salvia ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Ghiesbreght's sage, is a perennial herb native to the United States, specifically found in California.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features violet to blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Salvia ghiesbreghtii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native gardens.
