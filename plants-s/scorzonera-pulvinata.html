Scorzonera pulvinata

Scorzonera pulvinata, commonly known as the slender scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and basal leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and can be somewhat tuberous, which is a notable feature in the genus Scorzonera.

This plant thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
