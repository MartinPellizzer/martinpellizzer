Senecio veitchianus

Senecio veitchianus, commonly known as the Veitch's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and bright yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Senecio veitchianus is also known for its ability to thrive in arid environments with minimal water requirements.
