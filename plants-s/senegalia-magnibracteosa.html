Senegalia magnibracteosa

Senegalia magnibracteosa, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are typically reduced to spines in mature plants, and its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are a valuable food source for wildlife and livestock. Senegalia magnibracteosa is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in dry environments.

Its deep root system helps prevent soil erosion and makes it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
