Stevia apensis

Stevia apensis, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweet taste, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. Stevia apensis is often cultivated as a natural sweetener and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its leaves are typically harvested and processed to extract the sweet compounds used in food and beverages.
