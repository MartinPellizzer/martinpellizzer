Solanum palenquense

Solanum palenquense, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are edible when ripe. Solanum palenquense belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is related to other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
