Sedum guttatum

Sedum guttatum, commonly known as the "Guttate stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that often bear translucent, water-filled glands. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum guttatum is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
