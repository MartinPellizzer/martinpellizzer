Syzygium sabangense

Syzygium sabangense, commonly known as the Sabang apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its small, edible, apple-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy green surface with a slightly serrated margin.

The flowers are white with a pinkish tinge and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
