Solanum neumanni var. schoense

Solanum neumanni var. schoense, commonly known as the Neumann's nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and oval, pointed leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale purple, with five petals and a distinctive calyx. It produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids.

This plant is native to Central America and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
