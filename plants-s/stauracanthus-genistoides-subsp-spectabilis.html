Stauracanthus genistoides subsp. spectabilis

Stauracanthus genistoides subsp.

spectabilis, commonly known as the showy genistoid bush, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
