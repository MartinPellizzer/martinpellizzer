Silene inclinata

Silene inclinata, commonly known as the leaning catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene inclinata is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
