Saxifraga transsilvanica

Saxifraga transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in cymes, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Saxifraga transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
