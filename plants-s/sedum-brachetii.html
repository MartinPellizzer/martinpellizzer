Sedum brachetii

Sedum brachetii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Sedum brachetii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in harsh, arid environments with minimal water and care.
