Sedum margaritae

Sedum margaritae, commonly known as the blue stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the rocky habitats of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, Sedum margaritae is widely cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
