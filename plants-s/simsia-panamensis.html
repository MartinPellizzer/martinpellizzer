Simsia panamensis

Simsia panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian Simsia, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Panama.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Simsia panamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
