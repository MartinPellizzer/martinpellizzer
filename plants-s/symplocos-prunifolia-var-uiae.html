Symplocos prunifolia var. uiae

Symplocos prunifolia var. uiae, commonly known as the red-leaf symplocos, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that often display a striking red or maroon coloration, especially during the dry season. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as montane forests and is often found in undergrowth layers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
