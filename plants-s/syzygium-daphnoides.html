Syzygium daphnoides

Syzygium daphnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. It produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sour taste, similar to tamarind.

Syzygium daphnoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines.
