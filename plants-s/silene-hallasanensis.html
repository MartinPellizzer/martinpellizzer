Silene hallasanensis

Silene hallasanensis, commonly known as the Hallasan mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the island of Jeju in South Korea.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, coastal areas and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its high-altitude habitat. Silene hallasanensis is a rare species, with a limited distribution, and is considered an endemic plant of Jeju Island.

Due to its unique ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
