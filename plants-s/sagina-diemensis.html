Sagina diemensis

Sagina diemensis, commonly known as the dwarf pearlwort, is a low-growing evergreen plant native to southern Africa.

It typically forms dense mats of small, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces tiny white flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Sagina diemensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
