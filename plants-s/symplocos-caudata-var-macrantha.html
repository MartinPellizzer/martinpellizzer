Symplocos caudata var. macrantha

Symplocos caudata var. macrantha, commonly known as the showy blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, though the variety macrantha is noted for its larger, showier fruits. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist woodlands and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various wildlife species.
