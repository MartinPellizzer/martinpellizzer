Salvia floriana

Salvia floriana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

Salvia floriana thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
