Solanum simplicifolium subsp. venturii

Solanum simplicifolium subsp.

venturii, commonly known as the Venturi's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe, contain toxic alkaloids and are not edible.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
