Salix eriocaulos

Salix eriocaulos, commonly known as the silver willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams, wetlands, and other water bodies.

Salix eriocaulos is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
