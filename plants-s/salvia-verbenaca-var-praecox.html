Salvia verbenaca var. praecox

Salvia verbenaca var. praecox, commonly known as early purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a conical shape at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and native plant landscapes.
