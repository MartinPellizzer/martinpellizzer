Schoenus limosus

Schoenus limosus, commonly known as the swamp grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps it retain moisture. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Schoenus limosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
