Salix babylonica var. crispa

Salix babylonica var. crispa, commonly known as the crinkled weeping willow, is a cultivar of the willow tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cascading branches that droop gracefully from the main trunk. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a crinkled or wavy edge, giving the plant a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often planted near water features.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shade in gardens and landscapes.
