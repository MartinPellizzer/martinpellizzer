Silene fernandezii

Silene fernandezii, commonly known as the Fernandez's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on island endemism and ecological adaptation.
