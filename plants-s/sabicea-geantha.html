Sabicea geantha

Sabicea geantha, commonly known as the African coral tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Sabicea geantha is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils.
