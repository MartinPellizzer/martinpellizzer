Senecio claryae

Senecio claryae, commonly known as Clary's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Senecio claryae is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
