Syngonanthus lisowskii subsp. marunguensis

Syngonanthus lisowskii subsp.

marunguensis, commonly known as the Marunguensis grassveld lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is adapted to well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
