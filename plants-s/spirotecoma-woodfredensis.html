Spirotecoma woodfredensis

Spirotecoma woodfredensis, commonly known as the golden spiral fern, is a rare and unique plant native to the misty highlands of the Woodfred Basin.

It is characterized by its intricate, spiral-shaped fronds that unfurl in a mesmerizing pattern, resembling a natural helix. The plant's leaves are covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps it retain moisture in its humid, shaded environment. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are small and yellow, clustered in tight, spiral formations.

Botanists are particularly interested in its genetic makeup, as it exhibits unusual adaptations to its specific ecological niche.
