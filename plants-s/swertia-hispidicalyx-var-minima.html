Swertia hispidicalyx var. minima

Swertia hispidicalyx var. minima, commonly known as the small-hairy swertia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
