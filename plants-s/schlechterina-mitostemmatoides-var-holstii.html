Schlechterina mitostemmatoides var. holstii

Schlechterina mitostemmatoides var. holstii, commonly known as the Holst's Schlumbergera, is a unique cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the cooler months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a slow growth rate, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is often grown in controlled conditions.
