Stauntonia maculata

Stauntonia maculata, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and often have a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and can vary in size.

Stauntonia maculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to warm climates.
