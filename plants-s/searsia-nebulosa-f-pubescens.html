Searsia nebulosa f. pubescens

Searsia nebulosa f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wild pear, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red when mature, which are consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The hairy variant distinguishes it from other forms of Searsia nebulosa through its dense, soft pubescence on the leaves and stems.
