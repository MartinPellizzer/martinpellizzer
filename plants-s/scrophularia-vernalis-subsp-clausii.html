Scrophularia vernalis subsp. clausii

Scrophularia vernalis subsp.

clausii, commonly known as the spring scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and grassy habitats.

The plant features lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flowers and shorter stems compared to other members of the Scrophularia genus.
