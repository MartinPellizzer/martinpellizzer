Stizolobium macrophyllum

Stizolobium macrophyllum, commonly known as the Mexican silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring. Its fruit is a flat, woody pod that splits open to release seeds.

Stizolobium macrophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
