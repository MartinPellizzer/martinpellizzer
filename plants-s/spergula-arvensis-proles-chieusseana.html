Spergula arvensis proles chieusseana

Spergula arvensis proles chieusseana, commonly known as the Chieusean field grass, is a variant of the common field grass, characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic and ecological traits.
