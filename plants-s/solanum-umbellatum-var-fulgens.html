Solanum umbellatum var. fulgens

Solanum umbellatum var. fulgens, commonly known as the flameflower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from orange to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a velvety texture, and the flowers are borne in umbel-like clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
