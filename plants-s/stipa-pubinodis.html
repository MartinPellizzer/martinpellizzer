Stipa pubinodis

Stipa pubinodis, commonly known as the woolly feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its overall rugged and adaptive morphology.

Stipa pubinodis is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
