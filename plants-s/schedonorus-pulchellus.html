Schedonorus pulchellus

Schedonorus pulchellus, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, silver-blue leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

Schedonorus pulchellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various insect and bird species.
