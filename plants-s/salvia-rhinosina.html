Salvia rhinosina

Salvia rhinosina, commonly known as the rhino sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Salvia rhinosina is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
