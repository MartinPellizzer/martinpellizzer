Sagenia mexicana

Sagenia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of purple or blue flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions.

Sagenia mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in local herbal medicine.
