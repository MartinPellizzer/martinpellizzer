Solanum cerasiferum var. kerense

Solanum cerasiferum var. kerense, commonly known as the Kerens wild tomato, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and edible berries that resemble small tomatoes. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments and has adapted to thrive in regions with limited water availability. Its leaves are usually hairy and oval-shaped, while its stems are woody and branched.

The fruit, which ripens to a red or yellow color, is used in traditional cuisines and has potential economic value for local communities.
