Scirpus asiaticus

Scirpus asiaticus, commonly known as the Asian bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, covered with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually greenish or reddish and appear at the top of the stems.

Scirpus asiaticus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
