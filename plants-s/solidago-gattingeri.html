Solidago gattingeri

Solidago gattingeri, commonly known as Gattinger's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features serrated, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a pollinator attractor and its ecological adaptability.
