Salix drummondiana subsp. subcoerulea

Salix drummondiana subsp.

subcoerulea, commonly known as the blueleaf willow, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth margin, while its catkins are yellow and appear in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riparian habitats.
