Scabiosa brigantiaca

Scabiosa brigantiaca, commonly known as the Italian clover or mountain scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Scabiosa brigantiaca is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
