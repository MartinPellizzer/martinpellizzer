Senecio woytkowskii

Senecio woytkowskii, commonly known as the Woytkowski's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
