Stachys jacquinii

Stachys jacquinii, commonly known as the purple flowered hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Stachys jacquinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
