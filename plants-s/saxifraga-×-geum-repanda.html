Saxifraga × geum repanda

Saxifraga × geum repanda, commonly known as the creeping saxifrage, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant features fleshy, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens, blooming in early summer.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
