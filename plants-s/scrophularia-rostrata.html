Scrophularia rostrata

Scrophularia rostrata, commonly known as the beakweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
