Solanum campylocladum

Solanum campylocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical berries that change from green to yellow or red as they mature. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Solanum campylocladum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
