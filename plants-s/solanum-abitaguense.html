Solanum abitaguense

Solanum abitaguense, commonly known as the Abitagua nightshade, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes many economically important plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while its flowers are usually white or purple, with five petals.

The fruit is a yellowish berry that contains several seeds.
