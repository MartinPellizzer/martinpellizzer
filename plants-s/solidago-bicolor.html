Solidago bicolor

Solidago bicolor, commonly known as the Purpletop Goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flower heads that are surrounded by purple bracts, creating a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its showy flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solidago bicolor is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
