Sasa kammurensis

Sasa kammurensis, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its culms are slender, hollow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Sasa kammurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts and as a food source in certain regions.
