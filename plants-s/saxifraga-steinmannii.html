Saxifraga steinmannii

Saxifraga steinmannii, commonly known as the Steinmann's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb with compact, rosette-like growth habit, featuring dark green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing in crevices or on steep slopes.

Saxifraga steinmannii is adapted to cold climates and is typically found in areas with high levels of sunlight and well-drained soils.
