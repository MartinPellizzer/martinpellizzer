Scorzonera austriaca var. lanigera

Scorzonera austriaca var. lanigera, commonly known as the Austrian scurvygrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features a robust, fleshy root system that is often harvested for its edible tubers. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family and is valued for both its culinary and medicinal properties.
