Scorzonera laciniata proles decumbens

Scorzonera laciniata proles decumbens, commonly known as the creeping scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a low-growing, decumbent stem that lies flat on the ground, often with a trailing habit. The plant produces basal leaves that are deeply lobed and can reach up to 20 cm in length. Its flowers are yellow and daisy-like, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

Scorzonera laciniata proles decumbens is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.
