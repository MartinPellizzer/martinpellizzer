Scirpus acicularis

Scirpus acicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Scirpus acicularis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
