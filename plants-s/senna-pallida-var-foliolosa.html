Senna pallida var. foliolosa

Senna pallida var. foliolosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and bipinnate leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange floral clusters that are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its flowers are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Senna pallida var. foliolosa is adapted to drought conditions and is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant.
