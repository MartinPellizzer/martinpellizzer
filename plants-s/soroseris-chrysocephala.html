Soroseris chrysocephala

Soroseris chrysocephala, commonly known as the goldenhead sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer, with a distinctive golden hue. The plant has upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
