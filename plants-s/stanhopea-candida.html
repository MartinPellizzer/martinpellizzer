Stanhopea candida

Stanhopea candida, commonly known as the white stanhopea, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. Its flowering period usually occurs during the dry season, and it is often found growing in shaded, humid environments.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
