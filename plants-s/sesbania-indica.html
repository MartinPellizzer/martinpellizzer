Sesbania indica

Sesbania indica, commonly known as the Indian sesbania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agroforestry and as a forage crop.

Sesbania indica is also cultivated for its ornamental value and in traditional medicine.
