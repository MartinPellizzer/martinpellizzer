Silene sciaphila

Silene sciaphila, commonly known as the shade-loving catchfly, is a flowering plant that thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland or shaded areas. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Silene sciaphila is adapted to low-light conditions, making it a characteristic species of shaded habitats.
