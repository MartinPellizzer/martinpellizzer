Sphaeralcea bonariensis var. laciniata

Sphaeralcea bonariensis var. laciniata, commonly known as the fringed globemallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
