Sedum telephium var. aurigeranum

Sedum telephium var. aurigeranum, commonly known as the golden sedum, is a succulent plant native to Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. This variety is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and prolonged flowering period.
