Salvia pansamalensis

Salvia pansamalensis, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia pansamalensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
