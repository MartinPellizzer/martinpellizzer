Simsia sanguinea var. triloba

Simsia sanguinea var. triloba, commonly known as the red-flowered trilobed simsia, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its vibrant red petals and distinctive three-lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are borne in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
