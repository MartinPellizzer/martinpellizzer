Synaedrys elizabethae

Synaedrys elizabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a short, slender stem and produces few, small leaves that are often obscured by dense foliage. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, typically from April to June, and are pollinated by specific species of beetles.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Synaedrys elizabethae is considered critically endangered and is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
