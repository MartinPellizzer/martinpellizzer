Scorzonera seidlitzii

Scorzonera seidlitzii, commonly known as the Siberian scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features a robust, fleshy root system that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Scorzonera seidlitzii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.
