Senecio pinnatus

Senecio pinnatus, commonly known as the featherleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio pinnatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
