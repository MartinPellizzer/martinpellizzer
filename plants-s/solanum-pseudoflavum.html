Solanum pseudoflavum

Solanum pseudoflavum, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries that change color as they mature. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum pseudoflavum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
