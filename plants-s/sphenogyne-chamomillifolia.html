Sphenogyne chamomillifolia

Sphenogyne chamomillifolia, commonly known as the yellow mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and sturdy.

Sphenogyne chamomillifolia plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
