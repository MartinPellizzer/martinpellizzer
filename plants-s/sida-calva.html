Sida calva

Sida calva, commonly known as white sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.

Sida calva is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
