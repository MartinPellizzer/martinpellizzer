Sorghum polyatherum

Sorghum polyatherum, commonly known as the Sudanese millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and green, while its flowers are typically pale green to brown.

Sorghum polyatherum is valued for its use in forage, grain production, and traditional food systems.
