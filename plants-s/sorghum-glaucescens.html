Sorghum glaucescens

Sorghum glaucescens, commonly known as the blue panicgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bluish-green foliage and erect, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to golden florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.

Sorghum glaucescens is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
