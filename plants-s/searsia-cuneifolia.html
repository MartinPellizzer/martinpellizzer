Searsia cuneifolia

Searsia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sugarbush, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers in spring, which are followed by orange to red spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Searsia cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
