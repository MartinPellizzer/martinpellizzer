Solanum placitum

Solanum placitum, commonly known as the pleasant nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, violet flowers and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that contains toxic compounds, making it harmful to humans and animals.

Despite its toxicity, Solanum placitum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain wildlife species.
