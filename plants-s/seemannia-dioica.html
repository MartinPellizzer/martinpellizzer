Seemannia dioica

Seemannia dioica, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
