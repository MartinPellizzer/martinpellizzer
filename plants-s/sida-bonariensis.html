Sida bonariensis

Sida bonariensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Sida bonariensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
