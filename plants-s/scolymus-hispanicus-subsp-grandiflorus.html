Scolymus hispanicus subsp. grandiflorus

Scolymus hispanicus subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the Spanish scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
