Salsola caesia

Salsola caesia, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with succulent, spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters, and it produces seeds that are easily dispersed by wind.

Salsola caesia is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies.
