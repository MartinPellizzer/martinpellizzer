Salvia integerrima

Salvia integerrima, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, sky-blue flower clusters that appear in late summer and persist into fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia integerrima is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
