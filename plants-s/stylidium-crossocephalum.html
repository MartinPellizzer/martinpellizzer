Stylidium crossocephalum

Stylidium crossocephalum, commonly known as the helmeted sun-orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower that resembles a miniature helmet. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It produces a single, showy flower that is usually yellow or orange in color.

Stylidium crossocephalum is known for its ability to attract specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
