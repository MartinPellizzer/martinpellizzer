Saxifraga magellanica subf. viridiflora

Saxifraga magellanica subf.

viridiflora, commonly known as the Magellan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, greenish-gray leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
