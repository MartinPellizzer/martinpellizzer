Suaeda × genesiana

Suaeda × genesiana, commonly known as the hybrid saltbush, is a halophyte native to arid and coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features succulent leaves that store water, and its small, yellow flowers bloom in summer. It is often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
