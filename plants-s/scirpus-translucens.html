Scirpus translucens

Scirpus translucens, commonly known as the translucent bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a translucent or waxy appearance.

Scirpus translucens thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important species in aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
