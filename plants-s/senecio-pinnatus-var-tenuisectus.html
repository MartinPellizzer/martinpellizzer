Senecio pinnatus var. tenuisectus

Senecio pinnatus var. tenuisectus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, feather-like leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
