Sabicea sanguinosa subsp. viridis

Sabicea sanguinosa subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the green-stemmed sabicea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its slender, green stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
