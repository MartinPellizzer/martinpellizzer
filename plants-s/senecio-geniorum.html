Senecio geniorum

Senecio geniorum, commonly known as the Blue Dustplant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio geniorum is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
