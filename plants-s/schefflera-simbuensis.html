Schefflera simbuensis

Schefflera simbuensis, commonly known as the Papua New Guinea umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the highlands of Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown composed of multiple leaflets arranged in a radiating pattern. The plant features thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries.

Schefflera simbuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
