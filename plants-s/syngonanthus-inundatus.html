Syngonanthus inundatus

Syngonanthus inundatus, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to tropical regions.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it forms dense underwater colonies. The plant has finely divided, feathery fronds that are adapted for efficient gas exchange in water. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, anchoring it to the substrate and facilitating vegetative propagation.

Syngonanthus inundatus plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by reducing erosion and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
