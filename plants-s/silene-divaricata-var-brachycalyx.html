Silene divaricata var. brachycalyx

Silene divaricata var. brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the evening primrose family, Onagraceae.
