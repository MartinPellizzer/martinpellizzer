Serapias xiphophyllum

Serapias xiphophyllum, commonly known as the sword-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and sword-shaped, giving it its common name, while its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers.

Serapias xiphophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to drought conditions.
