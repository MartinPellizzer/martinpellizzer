Stevia origanoides

Stevia origanoides, commonly known as sweetleaf or honey plant, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used as alternatives to sugar. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky environments.

Stevia origanoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
