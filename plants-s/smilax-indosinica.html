Smilax indosinica

Smilax indosinica, commonly known as the Vietnamese arrowroot or Indosinica smilax, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny projections. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Smilax indosinica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
