Stevia myriadenia

Stevia myriadenia, commonly known as the giant stevia, is a species native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its medicinal properties and sweetness, it is gaining interest for both culinary and therapeutic uses.
