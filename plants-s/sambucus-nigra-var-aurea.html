Sambucus nigra var. aurea

Sambucus nigra var. aurea, commonly known as the golden elderberry, is a variant of the elderberry plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which turns red in the fall, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its attractive features, it is often cultivated in gardens for both its aesthetic value and its ecological benefits.
