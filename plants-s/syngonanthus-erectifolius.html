Syngonanthus erectifolius

Syngonanthus erectifolius, commonly known as the upright-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces white, clustered flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by small, fleshy berries that attract birds. Its roots form specialized structures called haustoria, which attach to the host plant and extract water and nutrients.
