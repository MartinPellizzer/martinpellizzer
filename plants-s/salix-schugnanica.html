Salix schugnanica

Salix schugnanica, commonly known as the Schugnan willow, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous and alpine environments. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are elongated and have a serrated edge, adapting to the harsh climatic conditions of its habitat.

Salix schugnanica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
