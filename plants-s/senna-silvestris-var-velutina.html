Senna silvestris var. velutina

Senna silvestris var. velutina, commonly known as velvet senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
