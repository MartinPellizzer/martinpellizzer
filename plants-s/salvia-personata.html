Salvia personata

Salvia personata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking violet-blue flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia personata thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for garden cultivation.
