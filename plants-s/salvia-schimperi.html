Salvia schimperi

Salvia schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian sage, is a perennial shrub native to Ethiopia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces striking violet to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaf arrangement, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Salvia schimperi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
