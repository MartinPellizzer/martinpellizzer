Sporobolus actinocladus

Sporobolus actinocladus, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. The plant produces slender, three-awned spikelets that are characteristic of the genus Sporobolus. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a common adaptation in its harsh environment.

The species is notable for its resilience to drought and high temperatures, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
