Salvia benthamiana var. macaheensis

Salvia benthamiana var. macaheensis, commonly known as the Macaheensis sage, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to blue flowers, which are typically arranged in whorled clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and scientific research.
