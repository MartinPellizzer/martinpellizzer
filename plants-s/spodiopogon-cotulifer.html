Spodiopogon cotulifer

Spodiopogon cotulifer, commonly known as the golden beard grass, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, golden-colored inflorescences. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of florets that are often arranged in a panicle structure. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Spodiopogon cotulifer is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
