Scilla monophyllos var. tingitana

Scilla monophyllos var. tingitana, commonly known as the Algerian squill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of striking blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.

However, it can be invasive in some areas if not managed properly.
