Saccharodendron hispidum

Saccharodendron hispidum, commonly known as the rough sugar tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The plant has been used traditionally for its edible fruits and has some medicinal applications in local communities.
