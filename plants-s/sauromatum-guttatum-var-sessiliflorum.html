Sauromatum guttatum var. sessiliflorum

Sauromatum guttatum var. sessiliflorum, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, purple to violet flowers that resemble those of the passion fruit, with a distinctive corona of filaments. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
