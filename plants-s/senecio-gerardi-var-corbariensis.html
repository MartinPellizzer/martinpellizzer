Senecio gerardi var. corbariensis

Senecio gerardi var. corbariensis, commonly known as the Corbarien's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, numerous florets arranged in a composite head.

This variety is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
