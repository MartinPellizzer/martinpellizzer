Sesbania drummondii

Sesbania drummondii, commonly known as the yellow sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.

Sesbania drummondii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in restoration projects.
