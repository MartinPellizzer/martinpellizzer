Stevia scabrella var. scabrella

Stevia scabrella var. scabrella, commonly known as the woolly stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves contain steviol glycosides, which are used as natural sweeteners.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
