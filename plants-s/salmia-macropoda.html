Salmia macropoda

Salmia macropoda, commonly known as the giant salvia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that range in color from purple to violet. The plant produces numerous bracts that resemble petals, giving it a dramatic floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Salmia macropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
