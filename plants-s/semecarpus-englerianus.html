Semecarpus englerianus

Semecarpus englerianus, commonly known as the Engler's satin-ash, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Saraceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, and the bark is thick and fissured.
