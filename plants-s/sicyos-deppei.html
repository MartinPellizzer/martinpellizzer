Sicyos deppei

Sicyos deppei, commonly known as the giant burr cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, trailing vines and distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in moist, fertile soils. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Sicyos deppei is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and vigorous growth habit.
