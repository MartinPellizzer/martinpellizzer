Solanum laevigatum

Solanum laevigatum, commonly known as the velvet leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum laevigatum is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.
