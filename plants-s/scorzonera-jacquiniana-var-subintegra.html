Scorzonera jacquiniana var. subintegra

Scorzonera jacquiniana var. subintegra, commonly known as the Jacob's root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is notable for being thick and fleshy, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Scorzonera.

This variety is often found in rocky and arid environments, adapting well to harsh climatic conditions.
