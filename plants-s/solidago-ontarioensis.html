Solidago ontarioensis

Solidago ontarioensis, commonly known as Ontario goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ontario goldenrod is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
