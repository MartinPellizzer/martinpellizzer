Swertia coloradensis

Swertia coloradensis, commonly known as Colorado gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Swertia coloradensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
