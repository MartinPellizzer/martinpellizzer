Sida elliptica

Sida elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its elliptical, often hairy leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed areas and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.
