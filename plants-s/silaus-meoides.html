Silaus meoides

Silaus meoides, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Silaus meoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and unique floral structure.
