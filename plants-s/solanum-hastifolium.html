Solanum hastifolium

Solanum hastifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, violet flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic compounds similar to other members of its family. Solanum hastifolium is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
