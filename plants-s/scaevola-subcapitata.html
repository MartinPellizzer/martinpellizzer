Scaevola subcapitata

Scaevola subcapitata, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves and produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found along shorelines.

Scaevola subcapitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize dunes.
