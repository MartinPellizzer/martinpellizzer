Schizolegnia indica

Schizolegnia indica, commonly known as the Indian false indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Schizolegnia indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
