Satureja graeca subsp. garganica

Satureja graeca subsp.

garganica, commonly known as Greek thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, oval leaves that are densely packed on square stems.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
