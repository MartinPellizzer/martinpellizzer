Scabiosa crenata subsp. pseudisetensis

Scabiosa crenata subsp.

pseudisetensis, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of pink or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, seed-like fruits known as achenes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
