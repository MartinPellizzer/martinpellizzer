Senecio atropurpureus subsp. frigidus

Senecio atropurpureus subsp.

frigidus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada and other mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, silvery-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is a perennial species that is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in many areas.
