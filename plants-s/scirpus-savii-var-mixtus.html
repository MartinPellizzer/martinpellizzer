Scirpus savii var. mixtus

Scirpus savii var. mixtus, commonly known as the mixed sedge, is a variant of the sedge genus Scirpus, characterized by its distinctive morphological features.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in wetland ecosystems.
