Sinosidus albescens var. pilosus

Sinosidus albescens var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy white sinosidus, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its pale white to pale yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and covered with a fine, hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. This variety is notable for its unique floral structure and subtle fragrance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
