Salix kamanica

Salix kamanica, commonly known as the Kaman willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to parts of Central Asia, including regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. This shrub or small tree typically grows in riparian zones, along rivers and streams, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth margin, and it produces catkin-like flowers in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
