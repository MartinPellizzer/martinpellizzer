Semiarundinaria scopula

Semiarundinaria scopula, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense clumps.

Semiarundinaria scopula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
