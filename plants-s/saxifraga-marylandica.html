Saxifraga marylandica

Saxifraga marylandica, commonly known as Maryland saxifrage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with toothed margins and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers in the spring. It has a short stem and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Saxifraga marylandica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
