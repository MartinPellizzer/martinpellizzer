Salix pamirica

Salix pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including the Pamir and Tien Shan ranges. This shrub or small tree typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cold, rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces catkins that are important for pollination.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its native ecosystems.
