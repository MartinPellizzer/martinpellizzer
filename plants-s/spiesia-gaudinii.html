Spiesia gaudinii

Spiesia gaudinii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in umbels. It is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

Spiesia gaudinii is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.
