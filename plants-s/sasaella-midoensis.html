Sasaella midoensis

Sasaella midoensis, commonly known as the Mido Island bamboo, is a grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
