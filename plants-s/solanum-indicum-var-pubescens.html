Solanum indicum var. pubescens

Solanum indicum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, purple to white flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
