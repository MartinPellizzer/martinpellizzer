Sida lepidota var. sagittifolia

Sida lepidota var. sagittifolia, commonly known as arrowleaf sida, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead form, and it produces small, white to pale purple flowers. Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are characteristic of the genus Sida.

It is often found in wet or disturbed soils and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.
