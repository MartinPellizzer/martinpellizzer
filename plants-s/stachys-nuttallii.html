Stachys nuttallii

Stachys nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to greenish flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly fuzzy texture.

Stachys nuttallii is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
