Senecio pedunculosus

Senecio pedunculosus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, purple-pink flower heads that resemble small coneflowers. The plant produces ray flowers around the edge of the head, which are often more prominent than the central disk flowers. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
