Scirpus ellipticus

Scirpus ellipticus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

Scirpus ellipticus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
