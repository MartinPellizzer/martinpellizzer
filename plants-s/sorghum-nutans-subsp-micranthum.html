Sorghum nutans subsp. micranthum

Sorghum nutans subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as the slender sorghum, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, two-lipped flowers arranged in panicles.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, pale brown grains that are often used for forage.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for livestock feed.
