Solanum juglandifolium var. lehmannianum

Solanum juglandifolium var. lehmannianum, commonly known as the Lehmann's huckleberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
