Saguerus undulatifolius

Saguerus undulatifolius, commonly known as the wavy-leaf saguerus, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, undulating leaves that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
