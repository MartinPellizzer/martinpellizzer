Selaginella wendtii

Selaginella wendtii, commonly known as the Wendt's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its creeping, branched stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Selaginella wendtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
