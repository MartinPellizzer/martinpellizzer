Senna weddelliana

Senna weddelliana, commonly known as Weddell's senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in slender, curved pods and are often used in traditional medicine.
