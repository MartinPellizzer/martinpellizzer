Sedum doratocarpum

Sedum doratocarpum, commonly known as the golden sedum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It blooms in summer, producing small, star-shaped flowers that attract pollinators.

Sedum doratocarpum is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
