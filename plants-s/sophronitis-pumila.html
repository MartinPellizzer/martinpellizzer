Sophronitis pumila

Sophronitis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, pale green leaves and produces tiny, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and flies.

Due to its size and beauty, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
