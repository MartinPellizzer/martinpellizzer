Stizolobium meyeri

Stizolobium meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's legume, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellow to orange flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that split open when mature.

Stizolobium meyeri is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
