Shorea teysmanniana

Shorea teysmanniana, commonly known as Taysmann's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
