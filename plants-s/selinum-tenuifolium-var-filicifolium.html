Selinum tenuifolium var. filicifolium

Selinum tenuifolium var. filicifolium, commonly known as slenderleaf wormwood, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Botanically, it is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is part of the Asteraceae family.
