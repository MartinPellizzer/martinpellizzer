Santolina subclausa

Santolina subclausa, commonly known as narrowleaf santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina subclausa is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
