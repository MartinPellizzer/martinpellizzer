Stachys zoharyana

Stachys zoharyana, commonly known as the Persian betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stachys zoharyana is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
