Sideritis massoniana var. crassifolia

Sideritis massoniana var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
