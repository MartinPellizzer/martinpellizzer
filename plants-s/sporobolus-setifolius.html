Sporobolus setifolius

Sporobolus setifolius, commonly known as silverleaf threeawn, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Sporobolus setifolius is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
