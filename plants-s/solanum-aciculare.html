Solanum aciculare

Solanum aciculare, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum aciculare is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It is considered a minor weed in some regions but has potential ecological and medicinal significance.
