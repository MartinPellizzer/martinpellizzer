Silene odontopetala subsp. congesta

Silene odontopetala subsp.

congesta, commonly known as the clustered toothed campion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its densely packed inflorescences, which give it its common name. The leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these specialized ecosystems.
