Scorzonera popovii

Scorzonera popovii, commonly known as the Caucasian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features a robust, tuberous root system that is often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with a distinct central stem. Its flowers are yellow, with five petals and a prominent central disk, typically blooming in late summer.

Scorzonera popovii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.
