Sasa kurilensis var. jotanii

Sasa kurilensis var. jotanii, commonly known as the Kuril bamboo, is a temperate evergreen shrub native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine layer of silvery hair. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form thick, erosion-resistant ground cover.
