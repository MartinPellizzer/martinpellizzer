Sesbania aculeata var. cannabina

Sesbania aculeata var. cannabina, commonly known as the hairy sesbania, is a variant of the sesbania genus native to tropical regions.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
