Scilla haemorrhoidalis

Scilla haemorrhoidalis, commonly known as the red scilla, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along forest edges, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Scilla haemorrhoidalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its early seasonal display.
