Scaphochlamys multifolia

Scaphochlamys multifolia, commonly known as the many-leaved scaphochlamys, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in botanical gardens and ecological studies.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.
