Scaevola caespitosa

Scaevola caespitosa, commonly known as the coastal wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub that thrives in coastal environments. The plant features small, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds.

Scaevola caespitosa is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
