Stemona angusta

Stemona angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf stemona, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Stemona angusta thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
