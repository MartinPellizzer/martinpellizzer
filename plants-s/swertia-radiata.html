Swertia radiata

Swertia radiata, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its botanical classification places it in the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers.

Swertia radiata is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties.
