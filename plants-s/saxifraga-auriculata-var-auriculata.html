Saxifraga auriculata var. auriculata

Saxifraga auriculata var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Saxifraga auriculata var. auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
