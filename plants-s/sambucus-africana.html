Sambucus africana

Sambucus africana, commonly known as the African elderberry, is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit and adaptability make it a common species in various habitats across its native range.

The berries are also a food source for birds and other wildlife.
