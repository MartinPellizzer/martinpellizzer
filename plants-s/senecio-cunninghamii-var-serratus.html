Senecio cunninghamii var. serratus

Senecio cunninghamii var. serratus, commonly known as the serrated groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
