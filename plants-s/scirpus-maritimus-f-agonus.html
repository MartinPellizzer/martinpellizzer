Scirpus maritimus f. agonus

Scirpus maritimus f. agonus, commonly known as the saltmarsh bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that thrives in brackish or saline wetlands. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and can grow up to 2 meters in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
