Syzygium schlechteri

Syzygium schlechteri, commonly known as the Malabar cherry or white syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.
