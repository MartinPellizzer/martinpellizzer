Scilla melaina

Scilla melaina, commonly known as black scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black bulbs, which are adapted for storing nutrients and surviving harsh conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Scilla melaina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early blooming habit.
