Spinulum

Spinulum, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, waxy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Spinulum is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in gardens and agricultural fields.
