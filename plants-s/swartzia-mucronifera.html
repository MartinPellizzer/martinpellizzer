Swartzia mucronifera

Swartzia mucronifera, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It is often found in moist lowland forests and can grow up to 30 meters tall.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
