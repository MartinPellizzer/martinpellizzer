Salvia alariformis

Salvia alariformis, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Salvia alariformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
