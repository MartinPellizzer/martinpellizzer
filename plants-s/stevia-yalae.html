Stevia yalae

Stevia yalae, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring sweeteners. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters during the growing season.

Stevia yalae is widely cultivated for its use in natural sweetener production and herbal medicine.
