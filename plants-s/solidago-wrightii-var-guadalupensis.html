Solidago wrightii var. guadalupensis

Solidago wrightii var. guadalupensis, commonly known as Guadalupe goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small, disk-like flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
