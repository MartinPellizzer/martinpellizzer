Sabicea fulvovenosa

Sabicea fulvovenosa, commonly known as the golden-barked sabelia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its distinctive golden-brown bark, which becomes more pronounced with age. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
