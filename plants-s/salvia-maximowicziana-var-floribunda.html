Salvia maximowicziana var. floribunda

Salvia maximowicziana var. floribunda, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which emits a mint-like scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
