Spirea

Spirea, commonly known as meadow spirea, is a genus of flowering plants in the rose family.

These shrubs are characterized by their clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. They have slender, often arching stems and are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Spirea species vary in height and growth habit, ranging from low-growing ground covers to taller shrubs.

Their foliage is often finely textured, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.
