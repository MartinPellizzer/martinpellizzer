Senecio hickenii

Senecio hickenii, commonly known as the Hicken's groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woolly, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.
