Syzygium tenue

Syzygium tenue, commonly known as the slender tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. Syzygium tenue is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a distinctive species within its native habitat.
