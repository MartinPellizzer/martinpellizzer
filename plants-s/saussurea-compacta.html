Saussurea compacta

Saussurea compacta, commonly known as the Chinese yarrow or the Himalayan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

Saussurea compacta is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used in ornamental gardening.
