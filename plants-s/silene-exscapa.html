Silene exscapa

Silene exscapa, commonly known as the sky pilot, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that often reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose raceme, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Silene exscapa is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
