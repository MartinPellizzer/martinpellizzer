Spergula decumbens

Spergula decumbens, commonly known as bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often lie flat on the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Spergula decumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
