Senecio tehuelches

Senecio tehuelches, commonly known as the Tehuelche groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Patagonian region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal in certain regions.
