Saussurea odontophylla

Saussurea odontophylla, commonly known as the giant cardamom, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, aromatic seeds enclosed in a spiky, woody capsule. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
