Styrax bracteolatus

Styrax bracteolatus, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Styracaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Styrax bracteolatus thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in suitable climates.
