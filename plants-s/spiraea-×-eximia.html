Spiraea × eximia

Spiraea × eximia, commonly known as the weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading branches that create a weeping appearance, with small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage turns a striking gold in the fall, adding ornamental value throughout the growing season.

Spiraea × eximia is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
