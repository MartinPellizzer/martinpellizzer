Schizostachyum diffusum

Schizostachyum diffusum, commonly known as the spreading bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically yellow or brown in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Schizostachyum diffusum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
