Stachys boraginoides

Stachys boraginoides, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features hairy, square stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its showy blue to purple flowers appear in whorls along the stem, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Stachys boraginoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
