Schismus fasciculatus var. flaccidus

Schismus fasciculatus var. flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for survival in desert ecosystems with limited water availability.
