Scutellaria orientalis subsp. karatschaica

Scutellaria orientalis subsp.

karatschaica, commonly known as the Karatschaic skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in steppe and semi-desert environments.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and are characteristic of the Scutellaria genus.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
