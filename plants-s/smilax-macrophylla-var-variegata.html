Smilax macrophylla var. variegata

Smilax macrophylla var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, variegated leaves with a mix of green, white, and yellow colors, which make it visually striking. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic, tropical effect.
