Solanum corymbiferum

Solanum corymbiferum, commonly known as the golden nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, hence its common name. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and the fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

The plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
