Saussurea poljakowii

Saussurea poljakowii, commonly known as the Karakoram saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant thrives in arid, rocky environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
