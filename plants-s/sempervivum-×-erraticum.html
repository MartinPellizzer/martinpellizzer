Sempervivum × erraticum

Sempervivum × erraticum, commonly known as the wandering jew or stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, often blue or silver-green leaves that are adapted to store water and withstand harsh conditions. The plant produces vibrant clusters of pink or red flowers in late spring or early summer. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Sempervivum × erraticum is popular in rock gardens and as a decorative indoor plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
