Senecio sarracenicus var. flosculosus

Senecio sarracenicus var. flosculosus, commonly known as the blueweed or bluebells, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
