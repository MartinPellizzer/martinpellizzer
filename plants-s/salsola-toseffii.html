Salsola toseffii

Salsola toseffii, commonly known as Toseff's saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments. Salsola toseffii is often found in steppes, dunes, and along the edges of salt lakes.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
