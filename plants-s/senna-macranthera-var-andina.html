Senna macranthera var. andina

Senna macranthera var. andina, commonly known as Andean senna, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils at high altitudes. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are bipinnately compound with numerous small leaflets. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
