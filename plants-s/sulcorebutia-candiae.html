Sulcorebutia candiae

Sulcorebutia candiae, commonly known as the Canary Island cactus or the sun rose, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a short, thick stem with numerous woolly, spiny stems that branch out in a rosette formation. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which bloom from the axils of the spines. Its succulent leaves are fleshy and covered in a dense layer of white, fuzzy hair, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Sulcorebutia candiae is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
