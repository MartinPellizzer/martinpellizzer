Symphyotrichum dumosum var. pergracile

Symphyotrichum dumosum var. pergracile, commonly known as the slender bushy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, shrub-like form with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale purple, typically blooming in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

This variety is distinguished by its more slender growth habit compared to other varieties within the Symphyotrichum genus.
