Smithia racemosa

Smithia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Smithia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, clustered blooms.
