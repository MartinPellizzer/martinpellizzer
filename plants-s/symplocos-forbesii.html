Symplocos forbesii

Symplocos forbesii, commonly known as Forbes' waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Symplocos forbesii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
