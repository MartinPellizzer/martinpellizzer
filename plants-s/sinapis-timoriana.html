Sinapis timoriana

Sinapis timoriana, commonly known as Timor mustard, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats and is often found in tropical regions.

Sinapis timoriana is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of bioactive compounds.
