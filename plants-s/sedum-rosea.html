Sedum rosea

Sedum rosea, commonly known as the pink stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, fuchsia-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

This hardy plant is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
