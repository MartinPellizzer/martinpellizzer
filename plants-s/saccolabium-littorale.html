Saccolabium littorale

Saccolabium littorale, commonly known as sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are typically alternate and lanceolate, often with a rough texture.

Saccolabium littorale plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various coastal wildlife.
