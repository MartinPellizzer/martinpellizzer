Salvia caracasana

Salvia caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas sage, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

Salvia caracasana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
