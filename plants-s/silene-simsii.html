Silene simsii

Silene simsii, commonly known as the Tibetan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and arctic environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Silene simsii plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
