Stilbe albiflora

Stilbe albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Stilbe albiflora is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
