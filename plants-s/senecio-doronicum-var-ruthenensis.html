Senecio doronicum var. ruthenensis

Senecio doronicum var. ruthenensis, commonly known as the Ruthenian groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its foliage is generally silver-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
