Stylogyne standleyi

Stylogyne standleyi, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a shallow root system that helps it anchor in rocky soils.

Stylogyne standleyi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
