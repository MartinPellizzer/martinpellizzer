Senecio rosmarinus

Senecio rosmarinus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
