Speirantha gardenii

Speirantha gardenii, commonly known as the California lily, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, white to pale pink bell-shaped flowers. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Speirantha gardenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
