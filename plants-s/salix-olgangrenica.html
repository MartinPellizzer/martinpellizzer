Salix olgangrenica

Salix olgangrenica, commonly known as the Olga's willow, is a species of willow native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring before its leaves fully emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along streams and in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
