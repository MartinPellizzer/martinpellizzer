Scutellaria aurata var. sulphurea

Scutellaria aurata var. sulphurea, commonly known as golden beard vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged blooming period.
