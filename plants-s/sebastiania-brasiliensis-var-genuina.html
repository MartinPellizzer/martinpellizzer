Sebastiania brasiliensis var. genuina

Sebastiania brasiliensis var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or golden starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers and elliptical, fleshy fruits that resemble small star-shaped berries. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests. Its foliage consists of opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a glossy texture.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible qualities.
