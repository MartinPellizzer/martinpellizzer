Sorbus × kawashiroi

Sorbus × kawashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red or orange berries. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit display.
