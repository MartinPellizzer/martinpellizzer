Satureja lepida var. argagae

Satureja lepida var. argagae, commonly known as Armenian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a strong, pungent scent, and produces tiny pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique chemical composition compared to other satureja species.
