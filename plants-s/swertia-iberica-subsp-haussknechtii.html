Swertia iberica subsp. haussknechtii

Swertia iberica subsp.

haussknechtii, commonly known as the Iberian swertia, is a member of the Gentianaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in Mediterranean flora.
