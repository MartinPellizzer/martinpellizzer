Sorbus hybrida

Sorbus hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid whitebeam, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red or orange berries in the fall. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and they turn yellow or red in autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
