Stachys puberula

Stachys puberula, commonly known as velvetleaf or woolly betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its spikes of purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.

Stachys puberula is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
