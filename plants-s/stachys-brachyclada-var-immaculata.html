Stachys brachyclada var. immaculata

Stachys brachyclada var. immaculata, commonly known as the white-hairy betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
