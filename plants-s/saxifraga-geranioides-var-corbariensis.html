Saxifraga geranioides var. corbariensis

Saxifraga geranioides var. corbariensis, commonly known as the Corbari saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the limestone regions of the British Isles.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rock crevices. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, pinkish-white flowers are clustered in cymes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
