Staurogyne angustifolia

Staurogyne angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide, arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, dark blue to black seeds. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Staurogyne angustifolia plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
