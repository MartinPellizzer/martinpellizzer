Sasa tanahashiana

Sasa tanahashiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Sasa tanahashiana is often used in traditional landscaping and as a decorative plant due to its graceful appearance.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it a useful plant for erosion control.
