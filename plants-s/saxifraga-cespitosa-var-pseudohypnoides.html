Saxifraga cespitosa var. pseudohypnoides

Saxifraga cespitosa var. pseudohypnoides, commonly known as the mossy saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and has small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of moss-like vegetation. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers in early summer, which are borne on short, hairy stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices and woodland floors.

Its unique appearance and ability to colonize rocky habitats make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
