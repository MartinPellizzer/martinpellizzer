Seddera arabica f. brevipedunculata

Seddera arabica f. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked seddera, is a botanical variant of the genus Seddera, native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and reduced peduncles, which are the stalks that support its inflorescences. The plant typically produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, showcasing its ornamental potential. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
