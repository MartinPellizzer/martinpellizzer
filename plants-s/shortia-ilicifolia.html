Shortia ilicifolia

Shortia ilicifolia, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Shortia ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
