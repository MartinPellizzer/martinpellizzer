Salvia gracilis

Salvia gracilis, commonly known as slender sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Salvia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
