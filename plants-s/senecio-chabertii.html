Senecio chabertii

Senecio chabertii, commonly known as the Chabert's groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

Senecio chabertii is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
