Saccharum longifolium

Saccharum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaf sugarcane.

It is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and robust stalks, which are used for sugar production. It thrives in warm climates with high humidity and requires abundant sunlight.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its natural habitat.
