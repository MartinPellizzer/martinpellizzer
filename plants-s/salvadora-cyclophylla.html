Salvadora cyclophylla

Salvadora cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclophylla date palm, is a species native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered, edible fruits that resemble dates. The plant is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can thrive with minimal water. Its fruit is typically consumed fresh or processed into various food products.

The plant also has cultural and economic significance in its native regions.
