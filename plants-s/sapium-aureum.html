Sapium aureum

Sapium aureum, commonly known as golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with lobes that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native plant with both ecological and aesthetic benefits.
