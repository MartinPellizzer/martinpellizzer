Sesleria caerulea var. albicans

Sesleria caerulea var. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.
