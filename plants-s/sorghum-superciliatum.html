Sorghum superciliatum

Sorghum superciliatum, commonly known as the silky sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, silky, silver-gray hairs on its leaves and stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soils and is often used for forage or ornamental purposes.

Due to its unique appearance, it is also cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic value in gardens.
