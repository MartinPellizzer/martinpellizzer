Scaryomyrtus hexamera

Scaryomyrtus hexamera, commonly known as the six-petaled myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
