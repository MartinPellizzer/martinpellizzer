Sisyrinchium intihuatanense

Sisyrinchium intihuatanense, commonly known as the Andean blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue to violet-colored flowers with prominent yellow centers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
