Senecio subdentatus var. ramosus

Senecio subdentatus var. ramosus, commonly known as the branched blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green hue.

It is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas, where it thrives in full sun.
