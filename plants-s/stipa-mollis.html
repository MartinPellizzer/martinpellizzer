Stipa mollis

Stipa mollis, commonly known as silky needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and panicle-like inflorescence that produces small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. Its leaves are covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Stipa mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
