Sarcopodium macropodum

Sarcopodium macropodum, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as marshes and ponds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other well-known plants like taro and water lilies.
