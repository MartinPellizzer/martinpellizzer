Syzygium ochneocarpum

Syzygium ochneocarpum, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarind.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically reddish when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
