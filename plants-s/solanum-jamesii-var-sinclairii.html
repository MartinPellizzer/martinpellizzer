Solanum jamesii var. sinclairii

Solanum jamesii var. sinclairii, commonly known as the Sinclair's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphological features and ecological significance in its native habitat.
