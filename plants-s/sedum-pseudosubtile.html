Sedum pseudosubtile

Sedum pseudosubtile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a member of the Crassulaceae family.

It is a low-growing succulent plant native to rocky and arid environments in Europe. The plant features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the summer months.

Sedum pseudosubtile is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
