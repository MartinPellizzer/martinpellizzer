Spiraea chamaedryfolia var. pubescens

Spiraea chamaedryfolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wood spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are elliptical, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
