Saccharum giganteum

Saccharum giganteum, commonly known as giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It can grow up to 20 meters in height and has long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces inflorescences that are both ornamental and used in cultural practices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and have been historically used for making ropes and textiles.

Saccharum giganteum is also valued for its rapid growth and potential in sustainable agriculture.
