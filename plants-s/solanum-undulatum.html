Solanum undulatum

Solanum undulatum, commonly known as the undulating nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are ovate with wavy edges, giving it its common name.

Solanum undulatum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
