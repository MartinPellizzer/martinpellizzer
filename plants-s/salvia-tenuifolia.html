Salvia tenuifolia

Salvia tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of violet to blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils and volatile compounds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure supports its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
