Stachys salviae

Stachys salviae, commonly known as woodland betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the woodlands of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces spikes of violet or purple flowers. Stachys salviae is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat ailments such as inflammation and digestive issues.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and herbal contexts.
