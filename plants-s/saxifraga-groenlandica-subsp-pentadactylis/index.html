Saxifraga Groenlandica Subsp. Pentadactylis

Saxifraga groenlandica subsp. pentadactylis is a hardy plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It thrives in rocky and coastal environments where few other species can survive. This subspecies is known for its distinctive five-lobed leaves that resemble tiny hands. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, adding a delicate touch to its harsh habitat. It plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native regions, supporting local wildlife and contributing to biodiversity.
