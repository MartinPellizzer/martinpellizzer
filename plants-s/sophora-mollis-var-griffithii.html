Sophora mollis var. griffithii

Sophora mollis var. griffithii, commonly known as the silky senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its soft, silky-hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
