Solidago microglossa var. ramosa

Solidago microglossa var. ramosa, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It blooms in late summer to early fall, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
