Silene boryi var. mesatlantica

Silene boryi var. mesatlantica, commonly known as the Mexican catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
