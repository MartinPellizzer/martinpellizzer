Stipa pratensis

Stipa pratensis, commonly known as smooth wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green hue, and it is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Stipa pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
