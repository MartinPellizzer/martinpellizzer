Salvia linnaei

Salvia linnaei, commonly known as Linnaean sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. Salvia linnaei thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
