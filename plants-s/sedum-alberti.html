Sedum alberti

Sedum alberti, commonly known as the Albert's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum alberti is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
