Securidaca longepedunculata var. parvifolia

Securidaca longepedunculata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved devil's bit, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
