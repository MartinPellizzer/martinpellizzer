Styrax burchellii

Styrax burchellii, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Styrax burchellii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
