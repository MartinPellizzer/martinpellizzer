Sinarundinaria polytricha

Sinarundinaria polytricha, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a fern native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are often divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, thriving in humid conditions. Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Sinarundinaria polytricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
