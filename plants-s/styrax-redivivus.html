Styrax redivivus

Styrax redivivus, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a unique species of flowering plant belonging to the Styracaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean. The plant is notable for its ability to survive extreme drought conditions by entering a state of desiccation, where it appears dead but can revive when water becomes available. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, and it produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its resilience and its role in ecological systems where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
