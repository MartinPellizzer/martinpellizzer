Silene vallesia

Silene vallesia, commonly known as the Vallesia catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Silene vallesia is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
