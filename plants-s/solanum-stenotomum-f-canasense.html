Solanum stenotomum f. canasense

Solanum stenotomum f. canasense, commonly known as the Canasense nightshade, is a rare botanical variant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that change color as they mature, often from green to red.

Due to its limited distribution and unique genetic traits, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
