Solanum medians var. majorifrons

Solanum medians var. majorifrons, commonly known as the large-fruited nightshade, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important species such as tomatoes and potatoes.
