Spiranthes castillonii

Spiranthes castillonii, commonly known as the Castillon's spirea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a tight spiral of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Spiranthes castillonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
