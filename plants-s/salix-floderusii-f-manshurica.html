Salix floderusii f. manshurica

Salix floderusii f. manshurica, commonly known as the Manshurian willow, is a shrub-like plant native to the Far East, particularly found in regions of Russia and China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they turn yellow in the autumn. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
