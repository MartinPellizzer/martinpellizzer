Stipa setigera var. hispidula

Stipa setigera var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces dense panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
