Sechium pittieri

Sechium pittieri, commonly known as the pitahaya or dragon fruit, is a cactus species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems and vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces a unique, fleshy fruit with a thick rind and a sweet, sometimes tart pulp. Its botanical classification places it in the family Cactaceae, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
