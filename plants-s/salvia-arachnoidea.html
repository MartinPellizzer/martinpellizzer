Salvia arachnoidea

Salvia arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal among gardeners.

Salvia arachnoidea is also valued for its aromatic leaves and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
