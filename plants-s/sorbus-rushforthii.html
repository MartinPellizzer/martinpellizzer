Sorbus rushforthii

Sorbus rushforthii, commonly known as the Rushforth's whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by distinctive berries that are edible for birds. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the tree has a rounded, compact growth habit.

Sorbus rushforthii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
