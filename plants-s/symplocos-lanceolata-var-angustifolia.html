Symplocos lanceolata var. angustifolia

Symplocos lanceolata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf wax myrtle, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, narrow leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or purple berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
