Schizoglossum strictum

Schizoglossum strictum, commonly known as the rigid schizoglossum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Schizoglossum strictum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens that feature drought-tolerant plants.
