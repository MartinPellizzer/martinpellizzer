Spiraea hypoleuca

Spiraea hypoleuca, commonly known as white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching branches covered in clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Spiraea hypoleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its elegant floral display.
