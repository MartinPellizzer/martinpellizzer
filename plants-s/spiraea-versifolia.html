Spiraea versifolia

Spiraea versifolia, commonly known as the variable spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
