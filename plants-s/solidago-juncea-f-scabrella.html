Solidago juncea f. scabrella

Solidago juncea f. scabrella, commonly known as the rough goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its stiff, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to golden flower heads that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its distinctive rough texture and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
