Stapelia rugosa

Stapelia rugosa, commonly known as the carrion flower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, meaty, and often foul-smelling花朵 that resemble decaying flesh, a feature that attracts pollinators such as flies. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically spine-tipped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Stapelia rugosa is a popular choice for succulent gardens due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
