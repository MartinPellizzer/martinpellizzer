Sideritis arborescens subsp. lusitanica

Sideritis arborescens subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as Portuguese heather, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
