Scleranthus borborygius

Scleranthus borborygius, commonly known as the European cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual herb characterized by its prickly, spiny leaves and distinctive, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with five lobed petals, and it produces a strong, unpleasant odor to attract its pollinators.

Scleranthus borborygius is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread rapidly in suitable environments.
