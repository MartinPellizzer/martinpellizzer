Sphaeranthus neglectus

Sphaeranthus neglectus, commonly known as the common sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb with a mat-forming habit, often found in disturbed soils and sandy environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a globular inflorescence, which gives it its genus name "Sphaeranthus," meaning "sphere-bearing.". Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

The species is adapted to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
