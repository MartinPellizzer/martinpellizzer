Sesban

Sesban, commonly known as sesbania, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions and are characterized by their compound leaves and often showy flowers. Sesbania species are typically shrubs or small trees, with some varieties growing as annuals or perennials. They are known for their nitrogen-fixing ability, making them valuable in soil enrichment.

The seeds of certain sesban species are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
