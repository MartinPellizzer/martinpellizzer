Sphenostylis erecta subsp. obtusifolia

Sphenostylis erecta subsp.

obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved African violet, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa. It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences, with showy, tubular petals that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
