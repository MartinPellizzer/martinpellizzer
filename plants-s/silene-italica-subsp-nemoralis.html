Silene italica subsp. nemoralis

Silene italica subsp.

nemoralis, commonly known as the alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in harsh climates. Silene italica subsp.

nemoralis is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in alpine ecosystems.
