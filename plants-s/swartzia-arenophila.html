Swartzia arenophila

Swartzia arenophila, commonly known as the sand swartzia, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by hard, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments, demonstrating adaptability to coastal conditions.

Swartzia arenophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
