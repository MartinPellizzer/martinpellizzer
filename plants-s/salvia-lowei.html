Salvia lowei

Salvia lowei, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, and red. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its square stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
