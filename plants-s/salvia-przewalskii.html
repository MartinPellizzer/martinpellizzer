Salvia przewalskii

Salvia przewalskii, commonly known as Chinese sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and China.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
