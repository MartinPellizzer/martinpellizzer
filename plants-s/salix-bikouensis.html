Salix bikouensis

Salix bikouensis, commonly known as the Bikou willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces catkin-like flowers, which are typical of willow species, and its leaves are typically oval with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
