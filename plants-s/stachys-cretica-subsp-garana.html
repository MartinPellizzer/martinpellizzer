Stachys cretica subsp. garana

Stachys cretica subsp.

garana, commonly known as redstem betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features slender, hairy stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, while its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls along the stem.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
