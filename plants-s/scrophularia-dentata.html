Scrophularia dentata

Scrophularia dentata, commonly known as the toothed figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins, and it produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Scrophularia dentata belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with similar floral structures.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is considered a valuable plant for ecological habitats.
