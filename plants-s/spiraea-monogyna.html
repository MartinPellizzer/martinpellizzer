Spiraea monogyna

Spiraea monogyna, commonly known as the common spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring arching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea monogyna is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
