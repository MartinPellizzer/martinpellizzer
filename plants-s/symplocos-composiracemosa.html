Symplocos composiracemosa

Symplocos composiracemosa, commonly known as the clustered evergreen holly, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Symplocos composiracemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
