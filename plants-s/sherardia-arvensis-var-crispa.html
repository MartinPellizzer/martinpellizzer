Sherardia arvensis var. crispa

Sherardia arvensis var. crispa, commonly known as the curly-leaved evening-primrose, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled, and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Sherardia arvensis var. crispa is an annual herb that plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
