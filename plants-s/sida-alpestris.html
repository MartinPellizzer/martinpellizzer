Sida alpestris

Sida alpestris, commonly known as alpine sida, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing herb with small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Sida alpestris plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and supports local insect populations.
