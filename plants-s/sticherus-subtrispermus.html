Sticherus subtrispermus

Sticherus subtrispermus, commonly known as the three-seeded sticherus, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Sticheraceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, clustered inflorescences with small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains three seeds, giving it the specific epithet "subtrispermus.".

This species is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
