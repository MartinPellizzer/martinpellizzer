Solanum asteropilodes

Solanum asteropilodes, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
