Scleranthus madonius

Scleranthus madonius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sand-plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a rough texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Scleranthus madonius is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
