Syzygium kriegeri

Syzygium kriegeri, commonly known as the Krieger's tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a woody pod containing several flat, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
