Solanum felinum

Solanum felinum, commonly known as the cat's claw plant, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive, claw-like spines on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy berries, which are edible when ripe. Solanum felinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a challenge to local ecosystems.
