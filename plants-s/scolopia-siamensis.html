Scolopia siamensis

Scolopia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam spurge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, which are specialized structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Scolopia siamensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its ability to store water in its thick leaves.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging conditions.
