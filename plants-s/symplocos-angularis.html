Symplocos angularis

Symplocos angularis, commonly known as the angular waxtree, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its angular, often thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically pubescent. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by small, red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Symplocos angularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
