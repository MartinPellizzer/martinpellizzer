Solanum confertiseriatum

Solanum confertiseriatum, commonly known as the dense-flowered nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the fruits are small berries that turn red when mature.

Solanum confertiseriatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
