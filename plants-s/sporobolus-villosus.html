Sporobolus villosus

Sporobolus villosus, commonly known as silky grama, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silky hairs that cover its leaves and stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Sporobolus villosus is an important forage plant for grazing animals and contributes to soil stabilization in arid environments.
