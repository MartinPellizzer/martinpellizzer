Sida montana var. longearistata

Sida montana var. longearistata, commonly known as the long-stamen sida, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves with a rough, serrated texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its unique stamen structure, which contributes to its common name.
