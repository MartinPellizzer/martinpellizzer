Senecio subumbellatus var. microcephalus

Senecio subumbellatus var. microcephalus, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, and the plant is often found in open, sunny habitats.

It has a compact growth habit with short, stiff stems and hairy leaves.
