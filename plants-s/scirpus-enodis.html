Scirpus enodis

Scirpus enodis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Scirpus enodis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
