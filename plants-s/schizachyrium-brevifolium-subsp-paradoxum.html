Schizachyrium brevifolium subsp. paradoxum

Schizachyrium brevifolium subsp.

paradoxum, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States. It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and pale green to brown in color.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
