Soldanella pusilla

Soldanella pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It produces delicate, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit, typically reaching only a few centimeters in height. Its leaves are usually basal, simple, and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Soldanella pusilla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
