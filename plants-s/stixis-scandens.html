Stixis scandens, commonly known as the climbing stixis, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender, twining stems that climb using tendrils. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Stixis scandens is often found in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
