Spodiopogon hogoensis

Spodiopogon hogoensis, commonly known as the Hogo grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
