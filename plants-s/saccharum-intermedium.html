Saccharum intermedium

Saccharum intermedium, commonly known as hybrid sugarcane, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a hybrid between Saccharum spontaneum and Saccharum officinarum, exhibiting traits that make it adaptable to various environmental conditions. The plant is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and high sugar content, making it valuable for agricultural purposes. It thrives in wetland areas and requires a warm climate with ample rainfall.

Its botanical features include broad, elongated leaves and a fibrous root system that aids in its survival in moist soils.
