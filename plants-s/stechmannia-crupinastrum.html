Stechmannia crupinastrum

Stechmannia crupinastrum, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for drought-resistant gardens.
