Sonchus perfoliatus

Sonchus perfoliatus, commonly known as common groundsel, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa but has become naturalized in many parts of the world.

Sonchus perfoliatus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
