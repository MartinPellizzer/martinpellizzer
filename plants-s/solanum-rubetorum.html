Solanum rubetorum

Solanum rubetorum, commonly known as the red tomato bush, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates.

Solanum rubetorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
