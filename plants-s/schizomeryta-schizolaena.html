Schizomeryta schizolaena

Schizomeryta schizolaena, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandmat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Schizomeryta schizolaena is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate periods of drought.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
