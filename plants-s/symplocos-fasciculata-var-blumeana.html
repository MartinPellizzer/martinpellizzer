Symplocos fasciculata var. blumeana

Symplocos fasciculata var. blumeana, commonly known as the Blume's waxtree, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
