Symplocos crassipes var. rufomarginata

Symplocos crassipes var. rufomarginata, commonly known as the red-margin honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a glossy surface and red margins, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
