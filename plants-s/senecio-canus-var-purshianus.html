Senecio canus var. purshianus

Senecio canus var. purshianus, commonly known as the Pursh's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple ray florets and yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a creeping or ascending growth habit. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and it produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is often found in open areas such as grasslands and along roadsides.
