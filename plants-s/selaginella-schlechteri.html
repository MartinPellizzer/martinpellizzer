Selaginella schlechteri

Selaginella schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's spikemoss, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a prostrate stem with whorled leaves that are often narrow and linear. It produces strobilae, which are cone-like structures containing spores, a characteristic feature of its reproductive system.

Selaginella schlechteri is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
