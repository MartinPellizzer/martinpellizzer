Sedum sempervivoides

Sedum sempervivoides, commonly known as the Hen and Chicks, is a succulent plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features a rosette-shaped stem with fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers.

Its ability to propagate through offsets makes it easy to cultivate and maintain.
