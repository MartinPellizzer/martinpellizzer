Syringa pubescens var. flavoanthera

Syringa pubescens var. flavoanthera, commonly known as the golden anther honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, with distinctive golden anthers. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has slender, arching branches covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and turn yellow in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
