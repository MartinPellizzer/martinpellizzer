Sahagunia mexicana

Sahagunia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican safflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Sahagunia mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in local remedies.
