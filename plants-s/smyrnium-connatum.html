Smyrnium connatum

Smyrnium connatum, commonly known as the common cow parsley, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall and thrives in moist, disturbed soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in various regions.

Smyrnium connatum is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.
