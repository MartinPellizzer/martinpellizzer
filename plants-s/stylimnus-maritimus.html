Stylimnus maritimus

Stylimnus maritimus, commonly known as the coastal sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in maritime climates where it tolerates salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in coastal flora studies.
