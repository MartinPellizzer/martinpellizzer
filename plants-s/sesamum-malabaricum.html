Sesamum malabaricum

Sesamum malabaricum, commonly known as Malabar sesame, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical climates.
