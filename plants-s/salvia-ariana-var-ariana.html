Salvia ariana var. ariana

Salvia ariana var. ariana, commonly known as the Armenian sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features upright, branched stems covered with grayish-white fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and also covered with fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
