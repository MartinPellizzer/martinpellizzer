Senecio balsamitae var. crawfordii

Senecio balsamitae var. crawfordii, commonly known as Crawford's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
