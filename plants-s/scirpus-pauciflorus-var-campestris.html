Scirpus pauciflorus var. campestris

Scirpus pauciflorus var. campestris, commonly known as the field club-rush, is a wetland plant characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into hard, three-angled seeds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus pauciflorus var. campestris is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and water filtration.
