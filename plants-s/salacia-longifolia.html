Salacia longifolia

Salacia longifolia, commonly known as the long-stemmed salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are elongated and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Salacia longifolia is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
