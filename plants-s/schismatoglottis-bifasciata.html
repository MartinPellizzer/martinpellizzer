Schismatoglottis bifasciata

Schismatoglottis bifasciata, commonly known as the bicolor lady's slipper, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that resemble a lady's slipper, with a distinctive bicolor pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environment.

Schismatoglottis bifasciata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
