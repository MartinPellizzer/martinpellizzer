Silene italica var. mellifera

Silene italica var. mellifera, commonly known as the sweet-scented catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Silene italica var. mellifera is also noted for its attractive, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
