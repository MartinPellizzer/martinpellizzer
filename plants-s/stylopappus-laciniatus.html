Stylopappus laciniatus

Stylopappus laciniatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stylopappus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a delicate yellowish color that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
