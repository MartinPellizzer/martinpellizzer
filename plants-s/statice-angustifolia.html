Statice angustifolia

Statice angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf glasswort, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, spike-like clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal marshes. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other salt-tolerant species.

Statice angustifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
