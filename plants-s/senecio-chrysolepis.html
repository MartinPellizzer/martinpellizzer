Senecio chrysolepis

Senecio chrysolepis, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio chrysolepis is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
