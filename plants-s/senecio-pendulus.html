Senecio pendulus

Senecio pendulus, commonly known as the hanging senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its daisy-like flowers. The plant features long, trailing stems that often hang from rock faces or cliffs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
