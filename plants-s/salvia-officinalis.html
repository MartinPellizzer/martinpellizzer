Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, velvety leaves and spikes of purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties. Sage contains essential oils, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its therapeutic uses.

It is widely cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
