Senecio krameri

Senecio krameri, commonly known as Krammer's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky or sandy soils, often found in desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, adapted to reduce water loss.

Senecio krameri is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
