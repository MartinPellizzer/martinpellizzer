Scutellaria umbratilis

Scutellaria umbratilis, commonly known as the shade scullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.

Scutellaria umbratilis is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and medicinal properties.
