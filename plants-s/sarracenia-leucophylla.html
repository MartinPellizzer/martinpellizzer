Sarracenia leucophylla

Sarracenia leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaved pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking white, tubular leaves that function as pitfall traps to capture insects. The plant produces bright red flowers that attract pollinators, and its leaves secrete a sticky digestive fluid to break down prey. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires high humidity and bright light for optimal growth.

Sarracenia leucophylla is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations.
