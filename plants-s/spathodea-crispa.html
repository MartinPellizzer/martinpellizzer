Spathodea crispa

Spathodea crispa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-red bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its flowers are typically arranged in panicle inflorescences, and it produces woody pods containing several seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
