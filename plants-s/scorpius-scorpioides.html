Scorpius scorpioides

Scorpius scorpioides, commonly known as the false scorpion plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly succulent texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Scorpius scorpioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
