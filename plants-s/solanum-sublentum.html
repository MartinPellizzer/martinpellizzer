Solanum sublentum

Solanum sublentum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple in color. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum sublentum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
