Syngonanthus crispus var. itacambirensis

Syngonanthus crispus var. itacambirensis, commonly known as the Itacambira palm, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon basin.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered growth habit. The plant features pinnate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in humid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits.
