Synthyris missurica subsp. major

Synthyris missurica subsp.

major, commonly known as the largeleaf synthyris, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small, white or greenish-white, arranged in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is characterized by its relatively large leaves compared to other members of the genus.
