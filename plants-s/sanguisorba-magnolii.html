Sanguisorba magnolii

Sanguisorba magnolii, commonly known as the Japanese teaberry, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers resembling tiny berries. The plant produces distinctive, oval-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a slightly serrated edge. Sanguisorba magnolii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
