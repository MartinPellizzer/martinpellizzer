Strophanthus gossweileri

Strophanthus gossweileri, commonly known as the African milkvine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces berries that contain a milky sap, which is toxic and has been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
