Sporobolus sarmentosus

Sporobolus sarmentosus, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and dense, creeping growth habit, which helps it thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Sporobolus sarmentosus also plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
