Spathe rhoifolia

Spathe rhoifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Spathe rhoifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
