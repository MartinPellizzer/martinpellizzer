Stipa × saurica

Stipa × saurica, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, two-lobed spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Stipa × saurica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant with potential for use in landscaping and erosion control.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
