Streptanthus lasiophyllus var. utahensis

Streptanthus lasiophyllus var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah streptanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments, such as Utah's deserts and mountain ranges. It has a compact, low-growing habit with narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.
