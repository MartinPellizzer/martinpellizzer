Secale cereale var. hybernum

Secale cereale var. hybernum, commonly known as intermediate rye, is a variant of rye characterized by its intermediate growth habit between rye and wheat.

It is a grass species native to Europe and is often found in temperate regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets. It is typically cultivated for forage or as a cover crop due to its hardiness and adaptability.

The botanical features of this variety make it valuable in agricultural and ecological contexts.
