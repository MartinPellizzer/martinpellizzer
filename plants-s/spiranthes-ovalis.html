Spiranthes ovalis

Spiranthes ovalis, commonly known as the oval spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, spiky inflorescences. The flowers are small, white or greenish-white, and often have a subtle fragrance. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions.

The plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
