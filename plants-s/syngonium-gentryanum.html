Syngonium gentryanum

Syngonium gentryanum, commonly known as the Gentry's arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often exhibit variegation with white or silver markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Syngonium gentryanum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
