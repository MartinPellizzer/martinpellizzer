Sporobolus laetevirens

Sporobolus laetevirens, commonly known as the greenbunch grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, cylindrical seed heads that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form extensive clumps and stabilize soil in disturbed areas.

Sporobolus laetevirens is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological resilience and ability to prevent erosion.
