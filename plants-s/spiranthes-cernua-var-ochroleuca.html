Spiranthes cernua var. ochroleuca

Spiranthes cernua var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow spiraea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a basal rosette of foliage.

This variety is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers, which contrast with the darker green foliage.
