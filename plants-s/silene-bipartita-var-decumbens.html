Silene bipartita var. decumbens

Silene bipartita var. decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent two-parted catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems that often lie flat on the ground. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.
