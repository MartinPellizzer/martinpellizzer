Salvia albimaculata

Salvia albimaculata, commonly known as the white-spotted sage, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by the distinctive white spots on its leaves.
