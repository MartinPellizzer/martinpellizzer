Spiraea longigemmis

Spiraea longigemmis, commonly known as the longstem spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often noted for its attractive, serrated leaves that turn reddish in the fall.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
