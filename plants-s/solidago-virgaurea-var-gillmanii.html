Solidago virgaurea var. gillmanii

Solidago virgaurea var. gillmanii, commonly known as Gillman's goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
