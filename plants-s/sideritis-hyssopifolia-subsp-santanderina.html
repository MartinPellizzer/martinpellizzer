Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. santanderina

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

santanderina, commonly known as the Santander germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color.

The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic foliage and showy flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and adaptability to various soil conditions.
