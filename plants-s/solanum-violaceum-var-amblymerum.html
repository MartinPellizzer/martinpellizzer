Solanum violaceum var. amblymerum

Solanum violaceum var. amblymerum, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged foliage and small, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and the fruits mature to a deep purple or black color.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
