Smilax watsonii

Smilax watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features a woody stem covered in sharp, spiny bracts and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it bears bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Smilax watsonii thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

It is a hardy plant that contributes to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
