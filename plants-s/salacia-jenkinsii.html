Salacia jenkinsii

Salacia jenkinsii, commonly known as the Jenkins' salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant features elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
