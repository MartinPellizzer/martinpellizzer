Salicornia rubra

Salicornia rubra, commonly known as red glasswort, is a halophyte plant adapted to grow in saline environments such as coastal areas and salt marshes.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that are typically red or pink in color, which help it store water and nutrients. The plant produces small, green flowers that bloom in spikes, and its leaves are often reduced to scale-like structures. Salicornia rubra is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained, salty soils and is often found in estuarine habitats.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
