Scirpus jardinei

Scirpus jardinei, commonly known as the Jardine's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of pale brown flowers, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil in marshy areas, making it ecologically significant.

Scirpus jardinei is often found in shallow water or saturated soils, contributing to the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
