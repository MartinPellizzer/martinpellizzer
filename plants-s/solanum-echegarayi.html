Solanum echegarayi

Solanum echegarayi, commonly known as the Echegaray's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, tubular flowers that are usually violet or purple in color.

Solanum echegarayi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often thriving in rocky or sandy soils.
