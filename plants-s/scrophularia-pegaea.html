Scrophularia pegaea

Scrophularia pegaea, commonly known as the Persian fig or woolly scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are generally hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scrophularia pegaea is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
