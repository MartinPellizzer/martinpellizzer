Stelis thecoglossa

Stelis thecoglossa, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like structure that encloses the flower, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Stelis thecoglossa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
