Sasa scaberula

Sasa scaberula, commonly known as the rough bamboo, is a shrub-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are covered with rough, scaly nodes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Sasa scaberula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
