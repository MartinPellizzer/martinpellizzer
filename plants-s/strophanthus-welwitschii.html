Strophanthus welwitschii

Strophanthus welwitschii, commonly known as the African blood vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. Its fruit is a fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Strophanthus welwitschii is also noted for containing cardioactive alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine.
