Scirpus cernuus var. australiensis

Scirpus cernuus var. australiensis, commonly known as the Australian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as swamps, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense stands.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
