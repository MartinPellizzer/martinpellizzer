Scutellaria nervosa var. typica

Scutellaria nervosa var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scutellaria nervosa var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
