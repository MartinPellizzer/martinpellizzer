Sedum laxum var. eastwoodiae

Sedum laxum var. eastwoodiae, commonly known as Eastwood's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an adaptable and low-maintenance plant for various garden settings.
