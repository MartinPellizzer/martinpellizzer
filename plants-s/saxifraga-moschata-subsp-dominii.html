Saxifraga moschata subsp. dominii

Saxifraga moschata subsp.

dominii, commonly known as the fragrant saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant features rosettes of dark green, aromatic leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its strong, pleasant fragrance, which is most noticeable when the leaves are crushed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
