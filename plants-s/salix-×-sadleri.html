Salix × sadleri

Salix × sadleri, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, giving it a distinctive and elegant look. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a soft, velvety texture, typically turning yellow in the autumn. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers, lakes, and ponds.

The plant is also notable for its rapid growth rate and its ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
