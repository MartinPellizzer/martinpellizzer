Solanum pachimatium

Solanum pachimatium, commonly known as the purple eggplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive purple, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit, which ripens to a deep purple, is edible and used in various culinary applications in its native regions.
