Sporobolus versicolor

Sporobolus versicolor, commonly known as the variable dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it exhibits a range of color variations in its foliage.

Sporobolus versicolor is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
