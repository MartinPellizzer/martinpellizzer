Senecio alniphilus

Senecio alniphilus, commonly known as the alderleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.
