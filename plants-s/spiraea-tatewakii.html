Spiraea tatewakii

Spiraea tatewakii, commonly known as the Japanese spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, arching branches and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and flowering display. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Spiraea tatewakii is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded areas, enhancing its versatility in different garden settings.
