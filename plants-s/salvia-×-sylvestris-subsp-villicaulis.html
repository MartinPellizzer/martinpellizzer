Salvia × sylvestris subsp. villicaulis

Salvia × sylvestris subsp.

villicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long flowering period contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
