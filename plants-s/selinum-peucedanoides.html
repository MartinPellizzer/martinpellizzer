Selinum peucedanoides

Selinum peucedanoides, commonly known as love-in-a-mist, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical name reflects its close relation to the carrot family, the Apiaceae.
