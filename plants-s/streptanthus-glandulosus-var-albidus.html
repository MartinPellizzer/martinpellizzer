Streptanthus glandulosus var. albidus

Streptanthus glandulosus var. albidus, commonly known as the white-stemmed winter cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open areas, often in coastal regions, and has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge, while its fruit is a dry, segmented capsule.
