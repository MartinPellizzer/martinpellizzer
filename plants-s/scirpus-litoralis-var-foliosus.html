Scirpus litoralis var. foliosus

Scirpus litoralis var. foliosus, commonly known as salt marsh bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal wetlands.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often growing in dense clumps. The leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms in its native environment.
