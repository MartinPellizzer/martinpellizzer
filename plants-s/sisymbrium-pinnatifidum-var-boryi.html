Sisymbrium pinnatifidum var. boryi

Sisymbrium pinnatifidum var. boryi, commonly known as the Bory's narrowleaf mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately divided, giving the plant a delicate, fern-like appearance. It is native to the western United States and thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

This annual plant plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in arid regions.
