Satureja debilis

Satureja debilis, commonly known as creeping savory, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in cooking for their mild, peppery flavor, and the plant is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.

Satureja debilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
