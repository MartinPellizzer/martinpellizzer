Senecio pteridophyllus

Senecio pteridophyllus, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue and violet flower heads that resemble snowflakes. The plant has narrow, silvery-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
