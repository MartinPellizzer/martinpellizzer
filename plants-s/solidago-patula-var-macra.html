Solidago patula var. macra

Solidago patula var. macra, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with clusters of bright yellow flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

This plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during the late summer and early autumn.
