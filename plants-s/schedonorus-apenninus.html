Schedonorus apenninus

Schedonorus apenninus, commonly known as the Italian meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Schedonorus apenninus is also used in some horticultural settings for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
