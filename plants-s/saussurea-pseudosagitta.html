Saussurea pseudosagitta

Saussurea pseudosagitta, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau.

The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand cold and strong winds. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Saussurea pseudosagitta is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
