Sedum okuyamae

Sedum okuyamae, commonly known as Okuyama's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soil. Its leaves are often blue-green in color and can store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Sedum okuyamae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
