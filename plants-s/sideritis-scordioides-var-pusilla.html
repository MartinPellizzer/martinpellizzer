Sideritis scordioides var. pusilla

Sideritis scordioides var. pusilla, commonly known as the small dwarf mountain tea, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal collections.
