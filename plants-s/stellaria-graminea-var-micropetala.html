Stellaria graminea var. micropetala

Stellaria graminea var. micropetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The species is notable for its delicate structure and is often found in damp, nutrient-rich soils.
