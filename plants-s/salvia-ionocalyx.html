Salvia ionocalyx

Salvia ionocalyx, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of tiny, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia ionocalyx is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
