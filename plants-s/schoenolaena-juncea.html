Schoenolaena juncea

Schoenolaena juncea, commonly known as the golden grass or sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and narrow, golden-green leaves that are often used in traditional ceremonies. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and ceremonial purposes.
