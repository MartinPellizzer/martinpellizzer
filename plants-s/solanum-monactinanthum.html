Solanum monactinanthum

Solanum monactinanthum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, toxic berries that contain alkaloids.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided for consumption but is of interest in botanical and ecological studies.
