Saxifraga paniculata subsp. cartilaginea

Saxifraga paniculata subsp.

cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilaginous saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense, woolly layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Saxifragaceae family.
