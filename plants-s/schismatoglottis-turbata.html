Schismatoglottis turbata

Schismatoglottis turbata, commonly known as the Turbata Schismatoglottis, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts in controlled greenhouse conditions.
