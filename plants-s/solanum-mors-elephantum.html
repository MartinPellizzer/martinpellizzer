Solanum mors-elephantum

Solanum mors-elephantum, commonly known as the elephant's death plant, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and globose, waxy fruits. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to both animals and humans. Its common name reflects its historical use as a poison, particularly in traditional practices.

The botanical features of Solanum mors-elephantum include its hairy stems, lanceolate leaves, and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
