Salvia rouyana

Salvia rouyana, commonly known as the Maltese sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue and produces striking violet to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaf arrangement, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia rouyana is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
