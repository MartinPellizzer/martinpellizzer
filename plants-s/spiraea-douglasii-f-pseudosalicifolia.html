Spiraea douglasii f. pseudosalicifolia

Spiraea douglasii f. pseudosalicifolia, commonly known as the Douglas spirea or false willow spirea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters along the stems. The foliage is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves, which are often a deep green and have a slightly glossy texture. This variety is noted for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens where its delicate appearance and seasonal blooms are appreciated.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a range of garden conditions.
