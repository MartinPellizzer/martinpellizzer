Schistophragma polystachyum

Schistophragma polystachyum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that resemble bells. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add vibrant color and texture to their landscapes.
