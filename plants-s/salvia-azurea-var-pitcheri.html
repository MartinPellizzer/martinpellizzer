Salvia azurea var. pitcheri

Salvia azurea var. pitcheri, commonly known as the Pitcher's blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the Great Lakes region of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along lake shores and wetlands. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
