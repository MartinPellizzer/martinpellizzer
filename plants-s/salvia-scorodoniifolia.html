Salvia scorodoniifolia

Salvia scorodoniifolia, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Salvia scorodoniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
