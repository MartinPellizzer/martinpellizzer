Schwenckia paniculata

Schwenckia paniculata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems that often grow in a spreading or creeping manner. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs.
