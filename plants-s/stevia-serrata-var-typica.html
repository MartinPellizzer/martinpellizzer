Stevia serrata var. typica

Stevia serrata var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian sweet leaf, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its use in natural sweeteners.

Its leaves are typically harvested and processed to extract the sweet compounds used in food and beverages.
