Salacia nemorosa

Salacia nemorosa, commonly known as the forest salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a distinct midrib, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
