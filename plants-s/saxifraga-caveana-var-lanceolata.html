Saxifraga caveana var. lanceolata

Saxifraga caveana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the cave saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to subterranean environments such as limestone caves.

It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adapted to the dim light conditions of its habitat. The leaves are typically lance-shaped, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss in its moist, cool surroundings. This variety is often found in crevices and rock formations, where it thrives in humid, shaded conditions.

Its unique morphology and specialized adaptations make it a fascinating example of plant life in extreme environments.
