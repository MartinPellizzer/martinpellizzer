Syringa oblata var. affinis

Syringa oblata var. affinis, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

Syringa oblata var. affinis is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
