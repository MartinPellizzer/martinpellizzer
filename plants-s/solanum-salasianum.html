Solanum salasianum

Solanum salasianum, commonly known as the Salasian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub with hairy, glandular leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
