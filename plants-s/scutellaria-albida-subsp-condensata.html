Scutellaria albida subsp. condensata

Scutellaria albida subsp.

condensata, commonly known as the dense-flowered blue skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
