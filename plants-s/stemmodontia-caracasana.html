Stemmodontia caracasana

Stemmodontia caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas bristlepod, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
