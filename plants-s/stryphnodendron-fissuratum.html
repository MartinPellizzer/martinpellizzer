Stryphnodendron fissuratum

Stryphnodendron fissuratum, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its tonal qualities in musical instruments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, red fruits that contain seeds.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
