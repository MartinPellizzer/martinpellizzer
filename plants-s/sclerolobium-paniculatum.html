Sclerolobium paniculatum

Sclerolobium paniculatum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that appear in panicles during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
