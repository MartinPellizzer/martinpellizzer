Salix × tetrapla

Salix × tetrapla, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix discolor and Salix interior, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and bright green. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated.

Salix × tetrapla is often found in moist soils near streams, wetlands, and along riverbanks.
