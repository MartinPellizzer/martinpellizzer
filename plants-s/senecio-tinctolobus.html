Senecio tinctolobus

Senecio tinctolobus, commonly known as the red-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Senecio tinctolobus is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
