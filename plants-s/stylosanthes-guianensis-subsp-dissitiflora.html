Stylosanthes guianensis subsp. dissitiflora

Stylosanthes guianensis subsp.

dissitiflora, commonly known as the hairy stylo, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers.

The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and deep root system, which helps in soil stabilization. It is widely used as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value and adaptability to poor soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important component in sustainable agricultural systems.
