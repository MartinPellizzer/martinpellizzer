Silene rosea

Silene rosea, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Silene rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
