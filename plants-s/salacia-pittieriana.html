Salacia pittieriana

Salacia pittieriana, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a member of the Salaciacae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling stars. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tart, citrus-like flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Salacia pittieriana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
