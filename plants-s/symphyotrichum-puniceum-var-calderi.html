Symphyotrichum puniceum var. calderi

Symphyotrichum puniceum var. calderi, commonly known as the Calder's blazing aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces a distinctive, glandular-scented foliage.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
