Stenolobium cochabambense

Stenolobium cochabambense, commonly known as the Cochabamba burr bean, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, woody pods, which contribute to its common name.

Stenolobium cochabambense is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays a role in local ecosystems.
