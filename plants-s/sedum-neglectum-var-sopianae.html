Sedum neglectum var. sopianae

Sedum neglectum var. sopianae, commonly known as the rock sedum, is a succulent plant native to rocky habitats in Europe.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green, adapted for water storage. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
