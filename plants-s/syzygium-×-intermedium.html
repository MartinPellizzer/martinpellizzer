Syzygium × intermedium

Syzygium × intermedium, commonly known as the Java apple or Java plum, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Syzygium jambos and Syzygium samarangense.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically sour or tart, with a high acidity level and a distinctive aroma. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, often with a glossy surface.

Syzygium × intermedium is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in tropical and subtropical climates.
