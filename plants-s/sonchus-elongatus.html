Sonchus elongatus

Sonchus elongatus, commonly known as the slender sowthistle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family.

Sonchus elongatus is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain regions.
