Senecio durbanensis

Senecio durbanensis, commonly known as the Durban groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
