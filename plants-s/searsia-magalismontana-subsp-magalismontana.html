Searsia magalismontana subsp. magalismontana

Searsia magalismontana subsp.

magalismontana, commonly known as the Natal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
