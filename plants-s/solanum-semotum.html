Solanum semotum

Solanum semotum, commonly known as the wild tomato or mountain tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing several seeds. Solanum semotum is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to cooler climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
