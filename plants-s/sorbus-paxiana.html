Sorbus paxiana

Sorbus paxiana, commonly known as the Pacific mountain ash, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits called pomes, which are typically red or orange in color and contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a cool, moist climate.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and a popular ornamental plant.
