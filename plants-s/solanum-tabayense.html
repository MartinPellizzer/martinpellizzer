Solanum tabayense

Solanum tabayense, commonly known as the Tabayen nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its fruits are berries that mature to a deep purple color.

Solanum tabayense is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
