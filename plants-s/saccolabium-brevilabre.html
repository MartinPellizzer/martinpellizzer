Saccolabium brevilabre

Saccolabium brevilabre, commonly known as the narrow-lipped saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and grasslands.
