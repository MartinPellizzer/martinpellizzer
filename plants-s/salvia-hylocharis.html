Salvia hylocharis

Salvia hylocharis, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia hylocharis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
