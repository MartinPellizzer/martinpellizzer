Suaeda malacosperma

Suaeda malacosperma, commonly known as saltbush or coastal saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to grow in saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize coastal and inland saline soils.

Suaeda malacosperma plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in arid regions.
