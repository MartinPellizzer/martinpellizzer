Sisymbrium chrysanthum

Sisymbrium chrysanthum, commonly known as golden mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its small, elongated pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Sisymbrium chrysanthum is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
