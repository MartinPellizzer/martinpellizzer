Swintonia pierrei

Swintonia pierrei, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems that resemble the branches of a tree. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the holiday season. Swintonia pierrei thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
