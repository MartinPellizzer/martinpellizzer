Serratula integrifolia

Serratula integrifolia, commonly known as the common serrated centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Serratula integrifolia is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
