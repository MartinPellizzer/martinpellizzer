Spiraea alba f. paniculata

Spiraea alba f. paniculata, commonly known as white spiraea or panicled spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has arching branches covered in fine, silvery-green foliage. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its showy blooms and is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
