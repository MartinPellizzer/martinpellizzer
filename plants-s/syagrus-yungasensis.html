Syagrus yungasensis

Syagrus yungasensis, commonly known as the Yungas palm, is a species of palm native to the humid forests of northern Argentina and southern Bolivia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that form a dense crown. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in the understorey of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife.

Syagrus yungasensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and providing food and shelter for numerous species.
