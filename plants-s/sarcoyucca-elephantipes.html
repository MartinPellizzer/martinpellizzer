Sarcoyucca elephantipes

Sarcoyucca elephantipes, commonly known as the elephant's foot yucca, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, elephant-like roots that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant features sword-shaped, evergreen leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a unique visual contrast. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
