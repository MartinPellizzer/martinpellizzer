Seriphium cinereum

Seriphium cinereum, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Seriphium cinereum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
