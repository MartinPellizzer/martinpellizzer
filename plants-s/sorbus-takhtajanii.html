Sorbus takhtajanii

Sorbus takhtajanii, commonly known as the Takhtajan rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge.

Sorbus takhtajanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
