Salix concolor

Salix concolor, commonly known as the white willow, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark and slender, drooping branches. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a silvery underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and stream sides.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
