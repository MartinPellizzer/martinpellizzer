Symplocos nitida

Symplocos nitida, commonly known as glossy teak or smooth waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries. Symplocos nitida thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
