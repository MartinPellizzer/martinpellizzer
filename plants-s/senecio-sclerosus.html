Senecio sclerosus

Senecio sclerosus, commonly known as the stiff senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rigid, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which are followed by distinctive, spiky seed heads.

Senecio sclerosus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
