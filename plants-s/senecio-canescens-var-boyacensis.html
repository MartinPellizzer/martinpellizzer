Senecio canescens var. boyacensis

Senecio canescens var. boyacensis, commonly known as the Boyacensis ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and dry conditions. Its leaves are usually grayish-green and covered with fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
