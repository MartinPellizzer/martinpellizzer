Silene cretica subsp. tenuiflora

Silene cretica subsp.

tenuiflora, commonly known as slender-flowered redweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pink or red flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. Silene cretica subsp.

tenuiflora is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
