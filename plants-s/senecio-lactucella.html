Senecio lactucella

Senecio lactucella, commonly known as the milk thistle.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its latex-like sap, which can cause skin irritation. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a silvery underside, while the stems are hairy and upright.

Senecio lactucella is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including roadsides and meadows.
