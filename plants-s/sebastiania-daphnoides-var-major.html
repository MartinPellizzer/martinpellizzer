Sebastiania daphnoides var. major

Sebastiania daphnoides var. major, commonly known as the silverleaf sebastiania, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which features a striking silvery-white underside on the leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its ornamental value comes from its unique leaf coloration and compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to tropical and subtropical climates.
