Scorzonera pseudodivaricata var. leiocarpa

Scorzonera pseudodivaricata var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-seeded scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by small, smooth-seeded fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
