Solanum repandifrons

Solanum repandifrons, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture. Solanum repandifrons belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.
