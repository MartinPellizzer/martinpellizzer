Solanum lacteum

Solanum lacteum, commonly known as the milky nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, white to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are ovate and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Solanum lacteum produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
