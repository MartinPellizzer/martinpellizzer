Scirpus ustulatus

Scirpus ustulatus, commonly known as the burnet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pink or reddish, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus ustulatus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
