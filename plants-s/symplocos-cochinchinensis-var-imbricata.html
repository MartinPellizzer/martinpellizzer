Symplocos cochinchinensis var. imbricata

Symplocos cochinchinensis var. imbricata, commonly known as the broad-leaved sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, imbricated leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
