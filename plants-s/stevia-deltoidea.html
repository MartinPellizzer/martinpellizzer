Stevia deltoidea

Stevia deltoidea, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its intensely sweet-tasting leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides. These compounds are used as natural sweeteners in food and beverages.

Stevia deltoidea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and commercial cultivation.
