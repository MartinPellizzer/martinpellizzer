Sonchus kirkii

Sonchus kirkii, commonly known as Kirk's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
