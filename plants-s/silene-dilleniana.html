Silene dilleniana

Silene dilleniana, commonly known as the Dillen's campion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Silene dilleniana is an herbaceous perennial that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
