Saccharum rubicundum

Saccharum rubicundum, commonly known as the red bamboo grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves with a reddish tinge, especially at the base. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in marshy or swampy areas.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
