Solanum cordioides

Solanum cordioides, commonly known as the heartleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, featuring dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that start green and turn red when mature. Solanum cordioides contains alkaloids, which can be toxic, making it important to handle with care.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Solanaceae family.
