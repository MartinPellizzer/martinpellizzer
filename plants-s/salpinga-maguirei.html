Salpinga maguirei

Salpinga maguirei, commonly known as the Maguire's orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to its high-altitude environment.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Salpinga maguirei is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
