Sida juncea

Sida juncea, commonly known as cottonseed plant, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Sida juncea is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in animal feed and for the production of oil.

It thrives in warm climates and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
