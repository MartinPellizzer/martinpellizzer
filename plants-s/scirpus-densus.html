Scirpus densus

Scirpus densus, commonly known as dense bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, upright clusters of sword-shaped leaves and its flowering spikes that produce small, clustered flowers. This plant thrives in saline or brackish wetlands, often found in coastal areas and estuaries. Scirpus densus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its fibrous roots help filter water and reduce erosion in marshy environments.
