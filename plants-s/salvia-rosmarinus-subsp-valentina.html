Salvia rosmarinus subsp. valentina

Salvia rosmarinus subsp.

valentina, commonly known as Greek lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grey-green leaves with a strong aromatic scent, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is also cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.

This subspecies is a compact form of Salvia rosmarinus, making it ideal for rock gardens and container planting.
