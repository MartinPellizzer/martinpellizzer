Salisia

Salisia, commonly known as the European mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Salisia thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

It has historical uses in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
