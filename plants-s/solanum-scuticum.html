Solanum scuticum

Solanum scuticum, commonly known as the scarlet sage, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its fruits are berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Solanum scuticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
