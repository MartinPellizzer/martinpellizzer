Salix × charrieri

Salix × charrieri, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Salix × charrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
