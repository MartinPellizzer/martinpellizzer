Selaginella disticha

Selaginella disticha, commonly known as the common spike moss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spiral arrangement of leaves and a spore-producing structure called a strobilus at the tip of its stem. Selaginella disticha is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, including drought and poor soil, due to its specialized adaptations.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it an important component of many ecosystems.
