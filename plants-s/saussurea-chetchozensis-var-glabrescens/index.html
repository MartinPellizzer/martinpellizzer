Saussurea Chetchozensis Var. Glabrescens

Glabrescens saussurea, scientifically known as Saussurea chetchozensis var. glabrescens, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Asteraceae family. Native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, this plant thrives in cold, arid environments with limited sunlight. Its unique morphology includes a compact rosette structure and pale, woolly leaves that help it withstand harsh climatic conditions. Despite its ecological significance, it remains under-researched and vulnerable to habitat loss. This article explores its botanical characteristics, habitat, and conservation status.
