Smilax anceps

Smilax anceps, commonly known as the climbing smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and its ability to climb using tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped with a rough texture.

Smilax anceps is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
