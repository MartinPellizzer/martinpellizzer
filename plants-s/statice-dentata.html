Statice dentata

Statice dentata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically erect and somewhat woody. Statice dentata thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and ornamental appeal.
