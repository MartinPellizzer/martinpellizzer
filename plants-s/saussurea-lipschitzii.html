Saussurea lipschitzii

Saussurea lipschitzii, commonly known as the Karakorum saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and the Karakoram.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flower heads that resemble saffron threads, giving it its common name. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and conservation efforts.
