Solidago sempervirens f. viminea

Solidago sempervirens f. viminea, commonly known as the orange-scented goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange-pink flower heads. The plant's foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

The flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
