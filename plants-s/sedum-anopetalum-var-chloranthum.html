Sedum anopetalum var. chloranthum

Sedum anopetalum var. chloranthum, commonly known as the blue sedum, is a succulent plant native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, compact growth with small, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer, adding a delicate touch to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular selection for landscaping in arid climates.
