Silene plicata

Silene plicata, commonly known as the wrinkled catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Silene plicata is an herbaceous perennial with a low-growing habit, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
