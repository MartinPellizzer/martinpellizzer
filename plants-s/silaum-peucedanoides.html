Silaum peucedanoides

Silaum peucedanoides, commonly known as Siberian cowparsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and Kazakhstan. The plant features slender stems and compound leaves with a finely divided structure, typical of the carrot family. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in umbrella-like clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The roots and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
