Scutellaria angustifolia var. micrantha

Scutellaria angustifolia var. micrantha, commonly known as narrow-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or violet in color. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
