Scrophularia lyrata

Scrophularia lyrata, commonly known as the lyreleaf figwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a lyre-like pattern, giving it its common name. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia lyrata is often found in shaded areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
