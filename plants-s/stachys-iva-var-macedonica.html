Stachys iva var. macedonica

Stachys iva var. macedonica, commonly known as Macedonian betony, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorls along the upper stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
