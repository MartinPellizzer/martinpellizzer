Saussurea oblongifolia

Saussurea oblongifolia, commonly known as the Indian saffron or the greater corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin known for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Saussurea oblongifolia is valued for its therapeutic uses, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
