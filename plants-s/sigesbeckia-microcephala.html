Sigesbeckia microcephala

Sigesbeckia microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has hairy, sometimes glandular leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands.

Sigesbeckia microcephala is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
