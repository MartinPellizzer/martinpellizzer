Solanum andigenum f. guatemalense

Solanum andigenum f. guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan potato plant, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial shrub native to the highlands of Central America, including Guatemala. The plant features hairy, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berry-like fruits. Its tubers, similar to potatoes, are edible and have been used traditionally by indigenous communities.

The species is noted for its adaptability to high-altitude environments and its potential agricultural value.
