Streptachne calamagrostis

Streptachne calamagrostis, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
