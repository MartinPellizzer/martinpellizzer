Schisandra ovalifolia

Schisandra ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf schisandra, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and dark red berries. The plant is valued for its aromatic, medicinal fruits, which contain compounds with potential health benefits. Schisandra ovalifolia typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories.

Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.
