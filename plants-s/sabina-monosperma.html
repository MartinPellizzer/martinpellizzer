Sabina monosperma

Sabina monosperma, commonly known as the Indian juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include aromatic foliage and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

Sabina monosperma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
