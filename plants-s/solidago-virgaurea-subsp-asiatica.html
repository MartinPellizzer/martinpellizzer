Solidago virgaurea subsp. asiatica

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as Asian goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It features bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish or purplish.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
