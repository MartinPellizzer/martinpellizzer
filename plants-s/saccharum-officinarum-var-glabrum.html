Saccharum officinarum var. glabrum

Saccharum officinarum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth bamboo grass, is a variant of the sugarcane species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and sturdy, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative element due to their aesthetic appeal.

While not typically cultivated for sugar production, it holds cultural and ecological significance in its native habitats.
