Stelis deuterodewildei

Stelis deuterodewildei, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered pseudobulbs. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically during the wet season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
