Serapias × ambigua subsp. panormosana

Serapias × ambigua subsp.

panormosana, commonly known as the Panormosan orchid, is a hybrid plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display a mix of yellow and orange hues.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, erect stem that bears several flower spikes.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and unique floral display.
