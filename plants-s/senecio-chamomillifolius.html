Senecio chamomillifolius

Senecio chamomillifolius, commonly known as the chamomile-leaved senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are aromatic and resemble those of chamomile, giving it its common name.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring and early summer.
