Salvia rugosissima

Salvia rugosissima, commonly known as the roughleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia rugosissima is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
