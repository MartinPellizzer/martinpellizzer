Syzygium barringtonioides var. quadrisepalum

Syzygium barringtonioides var. quadrisepalum, commonly known as the four-sepal syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while the fruit is a small, round drupe.

This variant is notable for its unique floral morphology, featuring four distinct sepals that are often mistaken for petals.
