Stemodiacra peduncularis

Stemodiacra peduncularis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces showy, often brightly colored blooms during the rainy season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
