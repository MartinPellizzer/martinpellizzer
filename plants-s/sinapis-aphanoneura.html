Sinapis aphanoneura

Sinapis aphanoneura, commonly known as the ghost mustard, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes referred to as the ghost mustard because of its ethereal look.
