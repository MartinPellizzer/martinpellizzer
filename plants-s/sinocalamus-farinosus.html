Sinocalamus farinosus

Sinocalamus farinosus, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks covered in a fine, hairy layer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a soft, fuzzy texture.

Sinocalamus farinosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
