Schizachyrium occultum

Schizachyrium occultum, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wetland environments and its role in stabilizing soil.

Schizachyrium occultum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
