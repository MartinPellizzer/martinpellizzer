Sarracenia rubra f. viridescens

Sarracenia rubra f. viridescens, commonly known as the Green-veined Pitcher Plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, tubular pitchers that are typically green with prominent red veins, which help attract insects. The plant's leaves are modified into specialized traps that secrete digestive enzymes to break down prey. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires high humidity and bright light for optimal growth.

This variety is prized by horticulturists for its striking appearance and unique ecological adaptations.
