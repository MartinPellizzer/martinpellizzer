Scirpus welwitschii

Scirpus welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its stiff, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stems. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in wetland environments.

Scirpus welwitschii is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of thatching material.
