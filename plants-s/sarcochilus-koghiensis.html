Sarcochilus koghiensis

Sarcochilus koghiensis, commonly known as the Koghiens orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Sarcochilus koghiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
