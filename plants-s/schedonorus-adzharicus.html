Schedonorus adzharicus

Schedonorus adzharicus, commonly known as the Caucasian meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many other grass species.
