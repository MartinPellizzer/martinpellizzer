Satureja ericoides

Satureja ericoides, commonly known as dwarf savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties. Satureja ericoides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

It is valued for its fragrant foliage and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
