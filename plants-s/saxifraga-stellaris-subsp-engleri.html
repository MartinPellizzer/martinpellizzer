Saxifraga stellaris subsp. engleri

Saxifraga stellaris subsp.

engleri, commonly known as the star saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
