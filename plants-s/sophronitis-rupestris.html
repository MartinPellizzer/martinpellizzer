Sophronitis rupestris

Sophronitis rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spike, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive.

Sophronitis rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
