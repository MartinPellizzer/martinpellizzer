Scutellaria grossecrenata

Scutellaria grossecrenata, commonly known as the broad-crenated skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Scutellaria grossecrenata is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native habitat.
