Senna racemosa var. coalcomanica

Senna racemosa var. coalcomanica, commonly known as the coalcomanica senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
