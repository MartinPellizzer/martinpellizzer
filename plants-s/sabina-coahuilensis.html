Sabina coahuilensis

Sabina coahuilensis, commonly known as the Coahuila juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing remarkable drought tolerance.

Its hardy nature and unique appearance make it a notable species in desert botany.
