Sasa tashiroi

Sasa tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often used in traditional Japanese crafts and as ornamental plants.

Sasa tashiroi is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
