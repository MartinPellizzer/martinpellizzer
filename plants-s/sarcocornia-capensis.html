Sarcocornia capensis

Sarcocornia capensis, commonly known as the Cape goosegrass, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is native to coastal regions of southern Africa. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, and its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in salt marshes and tidal flats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Sarcocornia capensis is also used in phytoremediation to reduce soil salinity.
