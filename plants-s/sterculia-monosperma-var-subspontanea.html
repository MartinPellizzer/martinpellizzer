Sterculia monosperma var. subspontanea

Sterculia monosperma var. subspontanea, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five petals, typically in shades of yellow or red. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

The leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
