Solanum olivaceum

Solanum olivaceum, commonly known as the oliveleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly in regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by egg-shaped berries that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature.

Solanum olivaceum belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
