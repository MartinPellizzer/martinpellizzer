Sorbus aucuparia var. variegata

Sorbus aucuparia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated rowan, is a cultivar of the rowan tree known for its striking foliage.

It features leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but not as prominent as those of the common rowan. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
