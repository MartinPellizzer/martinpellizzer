Salvia reitzii

Salvia reitzii, commonly known as the blue salvia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia reitzii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
