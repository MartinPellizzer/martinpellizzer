Solanum tuberosum f. amajaya

Solanum tuberosum f. amajaya, commonly known as the Amajaya potato, is a botanical variant of the potato plant characterized by its unique tuber morphology and coloration.

This cultivar is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, where it has adapted to cooler and more temperate climates. The tubers of Solanum tuberosum f. amajaya are typically smaller and have a distinct purple or violet hue, which is attributed to higher levels of anthocyanins. It is valued for its flavor and nutritional content, making it a cultivar of interest in both culinary and agricultural contexts.

The plant exhibits typical solanaceous features, including hairy stems and yellow flowers, and is relatively resistant to certain pests and diseases.
