Samyda viridiflora

Samyda viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered samyda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Samyda viridiflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
