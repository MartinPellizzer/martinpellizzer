Sideritis libanotica var. incana

Sideritis libanotica var. incana, commonly known as the white-haired ironwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy foliage and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its aromatic leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their mild sedative and antispasmodic properties.

The species is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and sage.
