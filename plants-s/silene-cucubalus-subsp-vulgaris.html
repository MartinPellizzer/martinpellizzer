Silene cucubalus subsp. vulgaris

Silene cucubalus subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces distinctive bladder-like seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. Silene cucubalus subsp.

vulgaris is often found in moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
