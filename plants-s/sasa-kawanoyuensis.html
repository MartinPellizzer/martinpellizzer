Sasa kawanoyuensis

Sasa kawanoyuensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its culms are slender and often have a bluish-green hue, while its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Sasa kawanoyuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscapes.
