Solanum concepcionis var. typicum

Solanum concepcionis var. typicum, commonly known as the Concepcion morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a pulp. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in tropical regions.
