Sauromatum hirsutum

Sauromatum hirsutum, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
