Styrax casearifolius

Styrax casearifolius, commonly known as the golden-leaved styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn a striking golden yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, white, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes contribute to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
