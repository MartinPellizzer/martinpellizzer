Sapium ilicifolium

Sapium ilicifolium, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a fragrant, waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red berries. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it an invasive species in many regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structures and latex-producing properties.
