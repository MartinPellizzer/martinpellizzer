Sanguisorba tenuifolia var. tenuifolia

Sanguisorba tenuifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf burnet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are finely divided, linear, and often tinged with red or purple, giving the plant its characteristic appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators.
