Sophorocapnos pallida

Sophorocapnos pallida, commonly known as the pale sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Sophorocapnos pallida is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
