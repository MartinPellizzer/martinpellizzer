Salix drummondiana var. subcoerulea

Salix drummondiana var. subcoerulea, commonly known as the blueleaf willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the summer. The plant has slender, flexible branches that often grow in a cascading manner, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its flowers are catkins, typical of willow species, and it produces small, round fruits.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
