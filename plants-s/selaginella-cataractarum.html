Selaginella cataractarum

Selaginella cataractarum, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique spiral arrangement of its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant has a specialized root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically small, scale-like, and arranged in a whorl pattern around the stem.

Selaginella cataractarum is often found growing on the surfaces of rocks or in damp, shaded areas, where it can absorb moisture efficiently.
