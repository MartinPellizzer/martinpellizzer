Salix × cernua

Salix × cernua, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Salix × cernua produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind.
