Solanum incisum

Solanum incisum, commonly known as the incised nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its serrated, lobed leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruits are typically yellow or orange berries that mature in late summer.

Solanum incisum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
