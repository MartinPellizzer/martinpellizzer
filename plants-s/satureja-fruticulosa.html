Satureja fruticulosa

Satureja fruticulosa, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Satureja fruticulosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
