Synandrospadix

Synandrospadix, commonly known as the spadix of the genus Syngonium, is a unique floral structure found in certain arum family plants.

It is a dense, spikelike cluster of small, usually unisexual flowers, often surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The synandrospadix typically contains both male and female flowers, with the male flowers clustered at the base and the female flowers near the apex. This arrangement facilitates efficient pollination through a specialized mechanism involving insect attraction and movement.

The structure is an important feature in the reproductive biology of these plants.
