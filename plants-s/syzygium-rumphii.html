Syzygium rumphii

Syzygium rumphii, commonly known as the white clydesdale, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a small, round, reddish-purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Syzygium rumphii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
