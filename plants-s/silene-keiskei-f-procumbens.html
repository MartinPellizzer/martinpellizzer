Silene keiskei f. procumbens

Silene keiskei f. procumbens, commonly known as the carpet silene, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in cold climates with limited nutrients.
