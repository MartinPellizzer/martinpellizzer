Senecio phaeanthus

Senecio phaeanthus, commonly known as the Blue-Eyed Grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
