Silene tempskyana

Silene tempskyana, commonly known as the Tempskyana campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene tempskyana is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
