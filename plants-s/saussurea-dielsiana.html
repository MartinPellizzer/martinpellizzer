Saussurea dielsiana

Saussurea dielsiana, commonly known as the Himalayan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent.

Saussurea dielsiana is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
