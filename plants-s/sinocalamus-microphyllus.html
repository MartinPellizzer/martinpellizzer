Sinocalamus microphyllus

Sinocalamus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its growth habit is clumping, forming dense stands that contribute to the structure of its native habitat.

Sinocalamus microphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and construction in some regions.
