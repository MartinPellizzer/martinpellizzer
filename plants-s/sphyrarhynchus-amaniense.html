Sphyrarhynchus amaniense

Sphyrarhynchus amaniense, commonly known as the Amani thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude grasslands of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the cooler, drier conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its resilience and ecological role, it is an important component of the local grassland ecosystems.
