Senecio paraguariensis

Senecio paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
