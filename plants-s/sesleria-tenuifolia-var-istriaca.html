Sesleria tenuifolia var. istriaca

Sesleria tenuifolia var. istriaca, commonly known as the Istrian narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe, particularly the Istrian Peninsula.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a dark green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
