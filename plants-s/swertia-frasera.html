Swertia frasera

Swertia frasera, commonly known as Fraser's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Swertia frasera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
