Santolina magonica subsp. magonica

Santolina magonica subsp.

magonica, commonly known as Spanish santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with a compact growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice in Mediterranean landscapes.
