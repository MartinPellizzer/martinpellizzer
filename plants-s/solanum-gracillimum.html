Solanum gracillimum

Solanum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in dry, arid environments. The plant features small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, and the fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its unique ecological adaptations, Solanum gracillimum is of interest to botanists studying plant responses to extreme climates.
