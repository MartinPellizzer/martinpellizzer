Silene kaulfussii

Silene kaulfussii, commonly known as the Kaulfuss's campion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Silene kaulfussii has small, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
