Schizonotus argenteus var. griseus

Schizonotus argenteus var. griseus, commonly known as the silverleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along waterways. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery or grayish bloom, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Schizonotus argenteus var. griseus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
