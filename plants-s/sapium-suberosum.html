Sapium suberosum

Sapium suberosum, commonly known as the roughleaf stinkwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, corky bark that provides resistance to insect damage and fire. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods.

Sapium suberosum is often used in traditional medicine and for its durable wood.
