Saponaria bellidifolia

Saponaria bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellflower soapwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its clusters of pink or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

The species is notable for its saponin content, which gives it a soapy texture when crushed.
