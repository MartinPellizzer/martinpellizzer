Satureja subspicata var. macedonica

Satureja subspicata var. macedonica, commonly known as Macedonian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous areas. Its small, pink or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The leaves are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
