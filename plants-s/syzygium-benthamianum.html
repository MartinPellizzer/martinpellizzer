Syzygium benthamianum

Syzygium benthamianum, commonly known as the Bentham's apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface.

Syzygium benthamianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
