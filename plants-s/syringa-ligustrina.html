Syringa ligustrina

Syringa ligustrina, commonly known as the Korean lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and turn yellow in the autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Syringa ligustrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its showy blooms.
