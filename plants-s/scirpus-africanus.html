Scirpus africanus

Scirpus africanus, commonly known as African bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Scirpus africanus is often used in traditional medicine and for erosion control in wetland areas.
