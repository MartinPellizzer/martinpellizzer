Seriphidium algeriense

Seriphidium algeriense, commonly known as Algerian tansy, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, yellow to brown flowers that attract various pollinators.

Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.
