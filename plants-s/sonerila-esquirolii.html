Sonerila esquirolii

Sonerila esquirolii, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a compact rosette pattern.

Sonerila esquirolii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
