Salvia perlonga

Salvia perlonga, commonly known as long-stemmed sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Salvia perlonga thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
