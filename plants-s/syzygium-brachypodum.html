Syzygium brachypodum

Syzygium brachypodum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Syzygium brachypodum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide shade in arid environments.
