Senegalia tamarindifolia

Senegalia tamarindifolia, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate, and its long, woody pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a valuable species for reforestation and soil stabilization. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces a sweet, sticky resin that has been used traditionally in various cultures.

The tamarind tree is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in degraded landscapes.
