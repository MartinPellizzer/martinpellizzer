Sanguisorba officinalis var. nana

Sanguisorba officinalis var. nana, commonly known as dwarf Korean dock, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
