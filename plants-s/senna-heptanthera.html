Senna heptanthera

Senna heptanthera, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. Senna heptanthera belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
