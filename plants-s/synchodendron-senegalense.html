Synchodendron senegalense

Synchodendron senegalense, commonly known as the Senegalese tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry savanna regions.
