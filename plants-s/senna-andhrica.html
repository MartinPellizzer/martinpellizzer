Senna andhrica

Senna andhrica, commonly known as Indian senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy, triangular pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Senna andhrica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
