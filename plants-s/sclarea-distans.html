Sclarea distans

Sclarea distans, commonly known as the distant angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in complex, branched clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The roots of Sclarea distans are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.
