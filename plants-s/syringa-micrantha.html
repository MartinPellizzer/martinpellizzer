Syringa micrantha

Syringa micrantha, commonly known as the Japanese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Syringa micrantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping for its showy floral display.
