Stevia coronifera

Stevia coronifera, commonly known as the crowned stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

Stevia coronifera is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and for its medicinal and culinary applications.
