Sporobolus seineri

Sporobolus seineri, commonly known as the sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its seed heads are distinctive for their ability to disperse seeds by wind, a feature that aids in its propagation across open landscapes.

Sporobolus seineri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
