Scutellaria ovata subsp. rugosa

Scutellaria ovata subsp.

rugosa, commonly known as the rough-leaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade.

The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, and the plant is often found in rich, loamy soils. Scutellaria ovata subsp.

rugosa is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
