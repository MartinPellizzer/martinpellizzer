Selaginella velutina

Selaginella velutina, commonly known as the velvet spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with dense, scale-like leaves that give it a velvety appearance. Selaginella velutina is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its capacity to retain moisture.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
