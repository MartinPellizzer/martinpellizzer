Schisandra grandiflora var. cathayensis

Schisandra grandiflora var. cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese five-flavor tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that ripen in late summer, containing a high concentration of lignans and other bioactive compounds. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
