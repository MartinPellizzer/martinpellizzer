Schizanthus gracilis var. angustifolius

Schizanthus gracilis var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf common scurfpea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trailing growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, colorful ground cover.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ability to attract pollinators.
