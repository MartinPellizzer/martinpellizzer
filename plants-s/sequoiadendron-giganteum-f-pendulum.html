Sequoiadendron giganteum f. pendulum

Sequoiadendron giganteum f. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping giant sequoia, is a unique variant of the coast redwood.

It features long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, distinguishing it from the typical upright form of the species. This form is a natural mutation that occurs in certain populations, often found in moist, shaded environments. The foliage remains dense and green, maintaining the characteristic appearance of the species.

It is primarily cultivated in botanical gardens and arboreta for its ornamental value and ecological interest.
