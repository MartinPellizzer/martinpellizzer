Syngonanthus welwitschii

Syngonanthus welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Syngonanthus welwitschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
