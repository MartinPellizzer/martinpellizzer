Sinapis exotica

Sinapis exotica, commonly known as the Indian mustard or wild radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia, including India. The plant features alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow, cross-shaped flowers. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several seeds.

Sinapis exotica is often grown as a cover crop or for its edible seeds and leaves.
