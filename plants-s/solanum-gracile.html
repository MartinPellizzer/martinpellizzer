Solanum gracile

Solanum gracile, commonly known as the slender nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Solanum gracile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
