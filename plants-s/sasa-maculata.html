Sasa maculata

Sasa maculata, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems covered with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue, particularly on the lower parts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, followed by triangular, three-angled fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sasa maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
