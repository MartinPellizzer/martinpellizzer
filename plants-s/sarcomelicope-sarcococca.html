Sarcomelicope sarcococca

Sarcomelicope sarcococca, commonly known as the Christmas fern, is a perennial evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arching fronds that resemble the shape of a Christmas tree, hence its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are bioluminescent in the dark, a unique feature that aids in its survival by deterring herbivores.

Sarcomelicope sarcococca thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
