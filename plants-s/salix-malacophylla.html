Salix malacophylla

Salix malacophylla, commonly known as the Malacophylla willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in the early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
