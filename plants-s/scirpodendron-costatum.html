Scirpodendron costatum

Scirpodendron costatum, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often ribbed or ridged, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces dark brown, three-angled berries.

Scirpodendron costatum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
