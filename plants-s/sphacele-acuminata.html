Sphacele acuminata

Sphacele acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed sphacele, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
