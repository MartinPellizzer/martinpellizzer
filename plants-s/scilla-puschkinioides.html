Scilla puschkinioides

Scilla puschkinioides, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant has a short stem and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.

Scilla puschkinioides is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable climates.
