Solanum tuberosum var. julianum

Solanum tuberosum var. julianum, commonly known as the Julian potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which includes elongated, finger-like shapes and a distinct purple skin. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. Its tubers are typically smaller than standard potatoes and have a higher starch content.

This variety is valued for its culinary versatility and has been cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
