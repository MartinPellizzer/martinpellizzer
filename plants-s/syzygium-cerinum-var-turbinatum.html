Syzygium cerinum var. turbinatum

Syzygium cerinum var. turbinatum, commonly known as the turban fruit or turbinated apple.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a distinctive, elongated, turban-shaped berry that turns red when mature. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, which has a tart, acidic flavor.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both its beauty and culinary uses.
