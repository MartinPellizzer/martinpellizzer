Sponia pallida

Sponia pallida, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble a ghostly apparition. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It has a unique root system that allows it to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its habitat specificity and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered endangered and highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
