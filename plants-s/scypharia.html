Scypharia

Scypharia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and showy, often blue or purple, composite flower heads. Scypharia species are typically found in coastal areas, where they thrive in salty, well-drained soils.
