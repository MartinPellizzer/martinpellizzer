Struthiola erecta var. ambigua

Struthiola erecta var. ambigua, commonly known as the variable struthiola, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
