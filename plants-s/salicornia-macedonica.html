Salicornia macedonica

Salicornia macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian glasswort, is a halophytic plant native to the wetlands and salt marshes of the Balkans.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, showcasing its adaptability to high-sodium conditions. The plant features succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or reddish, appearing in clusters near the tips of the stems.

Salicornia macedonica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal and wetland soils while also serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
