Scrophularia incisa var. pamirica

Scrophularia incisa var. pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir dog's mercury, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often found in rocky soils and meadows at high elevations. The plant has slender stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late summer.

This variety is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses in traditional herbal practices.
