Senegalia hildebrandtii

Senegalia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Senegalia hildebrandtii is often used for forage by livestock and has traditional uses in local communities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
