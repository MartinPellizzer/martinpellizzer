Stickmannia radicalis

Stickmannia radicalis, commonly known as the radical stickman plant, is a unique member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often twisted stems that resemble a stick figure.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with rich, loamy soil. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Stickmannia radicalis is valued in horticulture for its unusual appearance and ability to add visual interest to shaded garden spaces.
