Spiraea hypericifolia var. nutans

Spiraea hypericifolia var. nutans, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin, and turn yellow in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
