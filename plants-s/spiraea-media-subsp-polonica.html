Spiraea media subsp. polonica

Spiraea media subsp.

polonica, commonly known as the Polish meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features arching stems covered in small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces numerous inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, providing a lush green backdrop to the floral display. Spiraea media subsp.

polonica is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
