Scrophularia subsessilis

Scrophularia subsessilis, commonly known as narrowleaf figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem and are characterized by their two-lipped structure, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
