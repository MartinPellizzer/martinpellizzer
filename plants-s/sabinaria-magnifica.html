Sabinaria magnifica

Sabinaria magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent sabinaria, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue and purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems. Its unique coloration and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is considered endangered in its natural range.
