Sapium itzanum

Sapium itzanum, commonly known as the Itzam tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some indigenous communities.
