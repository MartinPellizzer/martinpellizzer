Spermolepis rubra

Spermolepis rubra, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, red or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Spermolepis rubra is often found in rocky areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
