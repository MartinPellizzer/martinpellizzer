Serapias epidendraea

Serapias epidendraea, commonly known as the Mediterranean sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It has a short-lived, herbaceous growth habit and produces its flowers during the spring and early summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
