Satureja cercocarpoides

Satureja cercocarpoides, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Satureja cercocarpoides is valued for its pungent aroma and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
