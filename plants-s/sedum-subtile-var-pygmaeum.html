Sedum subtile var. pygmaeum

Sedum subtile var. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf sedum, is a low-growing succulent native to the rocky areas of the southwestern United States.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
