Scutellaria squarrosa

Scutellaria squarrosa, commonly known as the square-stemmed skullcap, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Scutellaria squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
