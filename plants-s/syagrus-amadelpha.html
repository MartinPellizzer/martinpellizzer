Syagrus amadelpha

Syagrus amadelpha, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that are typically a bright yellow-green color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into orange-red fruits, which are edible for both humans and animals. Syagrus amadelpha thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
