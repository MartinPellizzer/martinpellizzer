Setaria onurus f. grandiflora

Setaria onurus f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers, which are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
