Sasa uii

Sasa uii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sasa uii thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas across Japan and Korea.
