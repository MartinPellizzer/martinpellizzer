Savignya parviflora subsp. longistyla

Savignya parviflora subsp.

longistyla, commonly known as the slender-styled savignya, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated styles, which are a key morphological feature. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in the Cape Province region.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
