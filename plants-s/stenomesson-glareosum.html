Stenomesson glareosum

Stenomesson glareosum, commonly known as the golden tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and often covered in a whitish bloom, which helps reduce water loss.

Stenomesson glareosum is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
