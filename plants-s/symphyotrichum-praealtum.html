Symphyotrichum praealtum

Symphyotrichum praealtum, commonly known as the tall blue mistflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Symphyotrichum praealtum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
