Senecio lanigerus

Senecio lanigerus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that give it a feathery appearance. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Senecio lanigerus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
