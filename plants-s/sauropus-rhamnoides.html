Sauropus rhamnoides

Sauropus rhamnoides, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with smooth, often reddish stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and have a velvety texture with prominent veins. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
