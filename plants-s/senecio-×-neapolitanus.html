Senecio × neapolitanus

Senecio × neapolitanus, commonly known as the Neapolitan groundsels, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that resemble daisies. Native to South Africa, this species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
