Smilax platycentron

Smilax platycentron, commonly known as the flat-centre smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Smilax platycentron is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ability to climb or trail along the ground.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the local ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
