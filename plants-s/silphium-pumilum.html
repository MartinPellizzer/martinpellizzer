Silphium pumilum

Silphium pumilum, commonly known as dwarf silphium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Silphium pumilum is adapted to moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

It has historical significance as a medicinal plant used by Native American tribes.
