Sideritis attica

Sideritis attica, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in cooking, particularly in Greek cuisine, for their aromatic and slightly minty flavor.

Sideritis attica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
