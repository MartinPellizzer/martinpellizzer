Sedum amplibracteatum var. emarginatum

Sedum amplibracteatum var. emarginatum, commonly known as the Emarginate Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, usually in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
