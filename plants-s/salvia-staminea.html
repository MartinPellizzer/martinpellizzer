Salvia staminea

Salvia staminea, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Salvia staminea is also a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
