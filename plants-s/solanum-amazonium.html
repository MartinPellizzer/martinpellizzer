Solanum amazonium

Solanum amazonium, commonly known as the Amazonian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. Its fruits are berries that contain toxic compounds, making them dangerous if ingested.

Solanum amazonium plays a role in its ecosystem as both a food source for certain animals and a potential habitat for insects.
