Swintonia acuta

Swintonia acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Swintonia acuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm climates.
