Stipa zalesskyi subsp. turcomanica

Stipa zalesskyi subsp.

turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan needlegrass, is a grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and tough, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
