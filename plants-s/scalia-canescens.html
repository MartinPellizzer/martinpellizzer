Scalia canescens

Scalia canescens, commonly known as the white scalia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, grayish-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Scalia canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
