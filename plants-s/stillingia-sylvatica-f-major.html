Stillingia sylvatica f. major

Stillingia sylvatica f. major, commonly known as the giant stellaria, is a variant of the stellaria plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its larger leaves and stems compared to the typical form, with lance-shaped foliage that can reach lengths of up to 15 cm. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often sought-after plant in horticultural collections.
