Sprucella

Sprucella, commonly known as the spruce tree, is a coniferous evergreen plant belonging to the family Pinaceae.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, needle-like leaves, and cones that contain its seeds. Sprucella typically grows in temperate and subarctic regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing nutrient absorption.

The plant is also valued for its timber and is often used in reforestation efforts.
