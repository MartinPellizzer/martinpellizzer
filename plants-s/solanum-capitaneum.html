Solanum capitaneum

Solanum capitaneum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces spherical, orange-red berries that are edible and have a tangy, sweet flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Solanum capitaneum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
