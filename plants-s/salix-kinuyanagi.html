Salix kinuyanagi

Salix kinuyanagi, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches that hang gracefully, creating a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green on top and lighter on the underside. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
