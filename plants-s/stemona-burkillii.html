Stemona burkillii

Stemona burkillii, commonly known as Burkill's stemona, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, cylindrical stem that is often branched and covered with small, rough hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The roots of Stemona burkillii contain bioactive compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
