Sphaerolobium fornicatum

Sphaerolobium fornicatum, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Sphaerolobium fornicatum is an important species for coastal stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.
