Streblosa bullata

Streblosa bullata, commonly known as the bullate streblosa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features erect stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a distinctive bulge at the base of the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
