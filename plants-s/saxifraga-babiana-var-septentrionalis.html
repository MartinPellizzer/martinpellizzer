Saxifraga babiana var. septentrionalis

Saxifraga babiana var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the Northern Cape saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
