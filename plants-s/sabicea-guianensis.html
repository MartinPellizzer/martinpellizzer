Sabicea guianensis

Sabicea guianensis, commonly known as Guianese sabicea, is a woody shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and produces small, often clustered flowers in shades of white or pink.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
