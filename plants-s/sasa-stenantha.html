Sasa stenantha

Sasa stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its ability to form dense stands.

Sasa stenantha is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in stabilizing soil.
