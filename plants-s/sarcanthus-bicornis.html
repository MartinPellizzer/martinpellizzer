Sarcanthus bicornis

Sarcanthus bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments. Its unique morphology includes two prominent bracts that resemble horns, giving it its common name.

Sarcanthus bicornis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
