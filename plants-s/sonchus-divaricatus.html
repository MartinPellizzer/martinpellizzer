Sonchus divaricatus

Sonchus divaricatus, commonly known as the common sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
