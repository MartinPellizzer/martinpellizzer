Solanum trepidans

Solanum trepidans, commonly known as the trembling nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, oval-shaped leaves, and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange, contain toxic alkaloids and should not be consumed.

Solanum trepidans is often found in moist, shaded areas and is known for its rapid growth and short lifespan.
