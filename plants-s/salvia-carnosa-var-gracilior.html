Salvia carnosa var. gracilior

Salvia carnosa var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and pollinator-friendly landscapes.
