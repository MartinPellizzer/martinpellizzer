Syzygium wrayi

Syzygium wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, round drupe, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

Syzygium wrayi is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
