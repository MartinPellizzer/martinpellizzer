Silene rosiflora

Silene rosiflora, commonly known as the roseleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene rosiflora is often found in open areas such as slopes and gravelly soils, where it thrives in full sun.
