Sida Crenatiflora

Sida crenatiflora is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is known for its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas. Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in popular horticulture.
