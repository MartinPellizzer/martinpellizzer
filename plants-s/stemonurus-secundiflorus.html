Stemonurus secundiflorus

Stemonurus secundiflorus, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with slender, hairy stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

Stemonurus secundiflorus is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in alpine environments.
