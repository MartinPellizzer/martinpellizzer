Sagenia semibipinnata

Sagenia semibipinnata, commonly known as the broadleaf burrbean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves that are bipinnate, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing edible seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional agriculture and ecological studies.
