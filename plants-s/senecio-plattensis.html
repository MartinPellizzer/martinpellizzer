Senecio plattensis

Senecio plattensis, commonly known as Platt's groundsels, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
