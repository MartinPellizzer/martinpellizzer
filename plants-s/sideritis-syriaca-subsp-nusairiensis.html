Sideritis syriaca subsp. nusairiensis

Sideritis syriaca subsp.

nusairiensis, commonly known as the Syrian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Syria. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
