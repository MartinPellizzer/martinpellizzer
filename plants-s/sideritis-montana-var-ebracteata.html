Sideritis montana var. ebracteata

Sideritis montana var. ebracteata, commonly known as the mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface.

Sideritis montana var. ebracteata is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
