Solidago deamii

Solidago deamii, commonly known as Deam's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Deam's goldenrod plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
