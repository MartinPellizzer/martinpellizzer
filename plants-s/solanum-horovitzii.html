Solanum horovitzii

Solanum horovitzii, commonly known as the Horovitz's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. It produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Solanum horovitzii is often found in humid, lowland areas and is adapted to thrive in tropical climates.
