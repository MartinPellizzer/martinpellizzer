Solanum paniculatum var. integrifolium

Solanum paniculatum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
