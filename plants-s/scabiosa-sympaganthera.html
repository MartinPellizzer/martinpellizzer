Scabiosa sympaganthera

Scabiosa sympaganthera, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb with a compact, bushy growth habit, often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and are arranged in a basal rosette.

Scabiosa sympaganthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, daisy-like flowers.
