Stellaria borealis subsp. bongardiana

Stellaria borealis subsp.

bongardiana, commonly known as the Arctic star, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions. It has small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August.

The plant possesses a fibrous root system and produces small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is often found in tundra and mountainous areas where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
