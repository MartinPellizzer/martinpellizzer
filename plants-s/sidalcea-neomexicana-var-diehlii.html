Sidalcea neomexicana var. diehlii

Sidalcea neomexicana var. diehlii, commonly known as the Mexican checkerbloom, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
