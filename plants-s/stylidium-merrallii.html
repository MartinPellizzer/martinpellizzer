Stylidium merrallii

Stylidium merrallii, commonly known as the Merrall's sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome and produces delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Stylidium merrallii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.
