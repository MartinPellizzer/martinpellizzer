Symplocos superba

Symplocos superba, commonly known as the red-flowered beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that transition into vibrant purple to red berries. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its berries are a valuable food source for birds and other wildlife.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
