Senecio thianschanicus

Senecio thianschanicus, commonly known as the Tian Shan Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
