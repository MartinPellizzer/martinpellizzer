Serapias lingua subsp. oxyglottis

Serapias lingua subsp.

oxyglottis, commonly known as the "tongue orchid," is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, spurred flower that resembles a tongue, hence its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It produces showy, yellow-orange flowers during the spring and early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its elongated, narrow petals and a unique floral structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.
