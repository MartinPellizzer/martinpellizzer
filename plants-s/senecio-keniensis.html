Senecio keniensis

Senecio keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Senecio keniensis is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
