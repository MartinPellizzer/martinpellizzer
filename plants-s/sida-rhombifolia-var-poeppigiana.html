Sida rhombifolia var. poeppigiana

Sida rhombifolia var. poeppigiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. It has a hairy stem and is often found in dune ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
