Solanum caldasii var. glabrescens

Solanum caldasii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Caldasii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, though the variety glabrescens has a smoother, glabrous surface. It produces small, purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature.

This species is often found in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
