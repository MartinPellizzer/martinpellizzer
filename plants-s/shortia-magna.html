Shortia magna

Shortia magna, commonly known as the large shortia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are relatively small but are often fragrant, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Shortia magna is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
