Smilax ovata var. buckleyi

Smilax ovata var. buckleyi, commonly known as Buckley's arrowwood, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its stout, thorny stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant ecosystems.
