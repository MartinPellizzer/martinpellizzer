Salix Olivacea Var. Thuilleriana

Salix olivacea var. thuilleriana is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. This variant of the willow species is known for its unique, narrow leaves and delicate, catkin-like flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges. Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts. Due to its limited natural range, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful protection.
