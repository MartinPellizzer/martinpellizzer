Sauropus suberosus

Sauropus suberosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved sauropus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and ovate, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and coastal landscaping.
