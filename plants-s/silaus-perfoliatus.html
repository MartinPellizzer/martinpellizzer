Silaus perfoliatus

Silaus perfoliatus, commonly known as the leafy perfoliate, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Silaus perfoliatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
