Schizostachyum gracile

Schizostachyum gracile, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a tropical grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture, and it thrives in moist, tropical environments.

Schizostachyum gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional construction and crafts in its native regions.
