Solanum spectabile var. fissum

Solanum spectabile var. fissum, commonly known as the fissured blueberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy blue-violet flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
