Sabularia brevifolia

Sabularia brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and small, fleshy, often blue or greenish leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Sabularia brevifolia is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the ecological stability of such environments.
