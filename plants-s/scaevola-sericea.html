Scaevola sericea

Scaevola sericea, commonly known as the silver scabiosa, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.

Scaevola sericea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
