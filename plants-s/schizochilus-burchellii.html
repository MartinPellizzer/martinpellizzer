Schizochilus burchellii

Schizochilus burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's rock orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has a short, erect stem with a few leaves and produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive yellowish-green lip. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and is often found in regions of southern Africa.
