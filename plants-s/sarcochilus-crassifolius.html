Sarcochilus crassifolius

Sarcochilus crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers with prominent, showy sepals and petals. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
