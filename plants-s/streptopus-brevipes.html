Streptopus brevipes

Streptopus brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf moonwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spiral pattern, which is a unique feature among fern allies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Streptopus brevipes is closely related to other members of the water fern family, but it is distinguished by its compact size and specific habitat preferences.
