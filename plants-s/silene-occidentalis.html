Silene occidentalis

Silene occidentalis, commonly known as western campion, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces showy pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local ecosystems.
