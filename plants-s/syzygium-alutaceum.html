Syzygium alutaceum

Syzygium alutaceum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slight aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, providing food for native wildlife.
