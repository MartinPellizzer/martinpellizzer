Scolopendrium cordatum

Scolopendrium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf mossy clubmoss, is a species of lycopod that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of moss-like structures. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

Scolopendrium cordatum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
