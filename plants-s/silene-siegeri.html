Silene siegeri

Silene siegeri, commonly known as the Sieger's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Silene siegeri is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
