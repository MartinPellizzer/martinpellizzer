Stomatochaeta cymbifolia

Stomatochaeta cymbifolia, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, pale bark and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Stomatochaeta cymbifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
