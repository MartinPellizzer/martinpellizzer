Symphyotrichum carolinianum

Symphyotrichum carolinianum, commonly known as Carolina aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It produces showy purple to pink flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
