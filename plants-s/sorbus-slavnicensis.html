Sorbus slavnicensis

Sorbus slavnicensis, commonly known as the Slavonian whitebeam, is a species of rowan tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous and forested areas. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a bright red color in the autumn.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing food for various bird species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
