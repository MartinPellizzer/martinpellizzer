Spiraea × oblongifolia

Spiraea × oblongifolia, commonly known as the hybrid meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, upright stems covered in small, ovate to elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of fluffy, white to pinkish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its foliage is often a striking bronze or red in the autumn, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Spiraea × oblongifolia is well-suited for garden use, particularly in borders or as a specimen plant due to its compact growth habit and showy blooms.
