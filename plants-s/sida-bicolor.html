Sida bicolor

Sida bicolor, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Sida bicolor is known for its fibrous stems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
