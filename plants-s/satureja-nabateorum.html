Satureja nabateorum

Satureja nabateorum, commonly known as Nabatean thyme, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it useful in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Satureja nabateorum is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and desert environments.
