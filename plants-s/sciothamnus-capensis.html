Sciothamnus capensis

Sciothamnus capensis, commonly known as the Cape goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sciothamnus capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
