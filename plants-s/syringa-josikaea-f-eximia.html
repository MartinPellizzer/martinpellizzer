Syringa josikaea f. eximia

Syringa josikaea f. eximia, commonly known as the Siberian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
