Swertia engleri var. woodii

Swertia engleri var. woodii, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hair.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
