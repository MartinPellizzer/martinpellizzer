Salpianthus pyriformis

Salpianthus pyriformis, commonly known as the roundleaf salpianthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
