Shorea thiseltonii

Shorea thiseltonii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a rough, fissured bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
