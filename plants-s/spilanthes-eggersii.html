Spilanthes eggersii

Spilanthes eggersii, commonly known as the Indian mustard or yellow rocket, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
