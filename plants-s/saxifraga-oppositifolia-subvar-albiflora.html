Saxifraga oppositifolia subvar. albiflora

Saxifraga oppositifolia subvar. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered rockfoil, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in harsh environments with short growing seasons. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found on rocky slopes or tundra.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient species in extreme ecological conditions.
