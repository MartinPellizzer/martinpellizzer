Symplocos crataegoides f. major

Symplocos crataegoides f. major, commonly known as the large-fruited wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
