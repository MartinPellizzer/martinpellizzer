Sinapis orientalis

Sinapis orientalis, commonly known as oriental mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant features pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into siliques containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth.

The plant is also known for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
