Scabiosa calocephala

Scabiosa calocephala, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of northern Africa.

It is characterized by its fluffy, spherical flower heads that resemble small woolly balls, typically ranging in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa calocephala is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
