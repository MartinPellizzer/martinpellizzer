Stanhopea grandiflora var. jenischiana

Stanhopea grandiflora var. jenischiana, commonly known as the Jenischian orchid, is a rare and striking species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their intricate structure and long-lasting bloom, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
