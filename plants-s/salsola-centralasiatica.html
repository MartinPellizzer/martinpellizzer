Salsola centralasiatica

Salsola centralasiatica, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often surrounded by bracts. Salsola centralasiatica is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and adaptability have led to its classification as an invasive species in many regions.
