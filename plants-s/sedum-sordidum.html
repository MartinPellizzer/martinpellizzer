Sedum sordidum

Sedum sordidum, commonly known as the stinking sedum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Crassulaceae.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Sedum sordidum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to withstand drought.
