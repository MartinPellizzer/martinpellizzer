Silene dorrii

Silene dorrii, commonly known as the Dorris' campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky areas and has a short, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves. Silene dorrii is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it well-adapted to its native habitat.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
