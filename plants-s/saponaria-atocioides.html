Saponaria atocioides

Saponaria atocioides, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has dark green, fleshy leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Saponaria atocioides is known for its ability to produce a mild soap-like substance when the leaves are crushed.
