Solanum schomburgkii

Solanum schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, violet flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Solanum schomburgkii is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in regions with distinct seasonal changes.
