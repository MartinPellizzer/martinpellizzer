Sedum ursi

Sedum ursi, commonly known as stonecrop or rockcrop, is a succulent plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically in the summer months. It is often found growing on cliffs, ledges, and in dry, well-drained soils.

Sedum ursi is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
