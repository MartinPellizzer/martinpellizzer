Sundamomum laxesquamosum

Sundamomum laxesquamosum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble a misty cloud, hence its common name. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Sundamomum laxesquamosum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
