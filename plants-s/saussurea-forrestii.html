Saussurea forrestii

Saussurea forrestii, commonly known as the Tibetan ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Asia, including Tibet, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saussurea forrestii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
