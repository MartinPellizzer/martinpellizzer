Scilla

Scilla, commonly known as the windflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Amaryllidaceae family.

These plants are native to Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, often found in woodland and meadow habitats. Scilla species are characterized by their narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. They typically bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

Many species are cultivated for their ornamental value and are also used in traditional herbal medicine.
