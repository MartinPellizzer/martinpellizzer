Salix laevigata f. araquipa

Salix laevigata f. araquipa, commonly known as the Andean willow, is a unique form of the silky willow native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler, mountainous climates. Its flowers are catkins, which are prominent in early spring, and it produces small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
