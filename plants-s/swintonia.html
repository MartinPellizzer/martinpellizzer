Swintonia

Swintonia, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Schlumbergera and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that form a bushy shape. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. Swintonia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
