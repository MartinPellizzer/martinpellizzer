Senecio transmarinus var. sycephyllus

Senecio transmarinus var. sycephyllus, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, silver-blue leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a frosty appearance. The plant produces showy, star-shaped yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are borne on tall, branched stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
