Shorea assamica f. globifera

Shorea assamica f. globifera, commonly known as the globifera variant of the Indian rosewood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood with a rich, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is valued for its high-quality timber. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in catkins.

This variant is notable for its unique fruiting structures, which are spherical and contain numerous seeds.
