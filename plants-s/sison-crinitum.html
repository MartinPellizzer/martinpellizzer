Sison crinitum

Sison crinitum, commonly known as the silky sison, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The silky sison is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
