Sida dinteriana

Sida dinteriana, commonly known as the desert willow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its slender, often twisted branches. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in summer. Its leaves are small, narrow, and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Sida dinteriana is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
