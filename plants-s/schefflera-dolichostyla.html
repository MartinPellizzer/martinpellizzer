Schefflera dolichostyla

Schefflera dolichostyla, commonly known as the long-styled umbrella plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that resemble an umbrella, with long petioles and a distinctive leaflet arrangement. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Schefflera dolichostyla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
