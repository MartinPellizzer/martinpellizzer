Sasa atagoensis

Sasa atagoensis, commonly known as the Atago bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Sasa atagoensis is often used in traditional Japanese landscapes and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
