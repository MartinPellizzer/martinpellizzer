Senecio erraticus subsp. barbareifolius

Senecio erraticus subsp.

barbareifolius, commonly known as the Barbaric Leaf Senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often lobed leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to South Africa and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
