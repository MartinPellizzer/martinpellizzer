Stoebe pachyclada

Stoebe pachyclada, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Stoebe pachyclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
