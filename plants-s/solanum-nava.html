Solanum nava

Solanum nava, commonly known as the Navajo nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, violet flowers that bloom in the evening, and its berries are toxic to humans and animals. Solanum nava is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
