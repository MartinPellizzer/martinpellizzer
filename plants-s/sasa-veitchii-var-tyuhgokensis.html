Sasa veitchii var. tyuhgokensis

Sasa veitchii var. tyuhgokensis, commonly known as the Tyungokensis bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa veitchii species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is a clump-forming, evergreen bamboo characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in shaded forest understories. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing ornamental value in gardens.

It is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental bamboo cultivation.
