Senecillis persica

Senecillis persica, commonly known as Persian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Persia and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in temperate climates for both its beauty and potential medicinal uses.
