Senecio regularis

Senecio regularis, commonly known as the regular senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Senecio regularis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
