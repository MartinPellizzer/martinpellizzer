Senecio heterophyllus

Senecio heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which vary depending on environmental conditions, a trait known as heterophylly. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
