Scilla glandulosa f. major

Scilla glandulosa f. major, commonly known as the large-flowered scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features lance-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its glandular surfaces, which give it its specific epithet "glandulosa.". It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and delicate appearance.
