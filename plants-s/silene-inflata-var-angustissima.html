Silene inflata var. angustissima

Silene inflata var. angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces inflated, bladder-like pods that contain seeds, a feature typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
