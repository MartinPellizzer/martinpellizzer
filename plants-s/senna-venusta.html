Senna venusta

Senna venusta, commonly known as the golden senna, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Senna venusta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm, arid climates.
