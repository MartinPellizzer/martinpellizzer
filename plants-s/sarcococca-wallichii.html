Sarcococca wallichii

Sarcococca wallichii, commonly known as the Indian boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features dense, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that remain throughout the year, providing year-round foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Sarcococca wallichii is also valued for its ability to tolerate acidic soils and its low maintenance requirements.
