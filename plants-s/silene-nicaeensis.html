Silene nicaeensis

Silene nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaea catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are narrow and hairy. Silene nicaeensis is characterized by its short stature and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
