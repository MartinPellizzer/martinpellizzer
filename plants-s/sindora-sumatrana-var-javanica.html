Sindora sumatrana var. javanica

Sindora sumatrana var. javanica, commonly known as Java sindora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both horticultural and traditional uses.
