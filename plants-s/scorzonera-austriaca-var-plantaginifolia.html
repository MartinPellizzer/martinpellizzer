Scorzonera austriaca var. plantaginifolia

Scorzonera austriaca var. plantaginifolia, commonly known as the Austrian scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features a robust, tuberous root system that is often harvested for its edible qualities. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with a hairy underside and produces yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family, sharing similar structural and ecological traits.
