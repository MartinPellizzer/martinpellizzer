Silene subconica

Silene subconica, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish-purple, and arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem.

Silene subconica produces capsules as its fruit, containing numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.
