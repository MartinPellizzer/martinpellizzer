Sasa shikotanensis var. pseudobrachyphylla

Sasa shikotanensis var. pseudobrachyphylla, commonly known as the Shikotan bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa genus native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded environments in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often densely clustered at the base. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and structural characteristics.
