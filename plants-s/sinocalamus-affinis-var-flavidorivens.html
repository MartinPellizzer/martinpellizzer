Sinocalamus affinis var. flavidorivens

Sinocalamus affinis var. flavidorivens, commonly known as the yellow-ringed bamboo, is a variant of the sinocalamus genus native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinct yellow or golden-colored bands on the culms, which distinguish it from other bamboo species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its culms are slender and hollow, with a smooth, sometimes ridged surface.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.
