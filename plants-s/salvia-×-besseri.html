Salvia × besseri

Salvia × besseri, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
