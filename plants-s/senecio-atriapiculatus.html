Senecio atriapiculatus

Senecio atriapiculatus, commonly known as the black-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
