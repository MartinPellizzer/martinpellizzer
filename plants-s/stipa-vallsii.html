Stipa vallsii

Stipa vallsii, commonly known as the Valls' needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Stipa vallsii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native range.
