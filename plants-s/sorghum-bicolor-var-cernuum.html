Sorghum bicolor var. cernuum

Sorghum bicolor var. cernuum, commonly known as the woolly sorghum, is a flowering plant native to Africa and cultivated in warm climates.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and dense, woolly inflorescence that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like flowers that are typically pale green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its unique visual appeal.
