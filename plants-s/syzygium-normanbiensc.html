Syzygium normanbiensc

Syzygium normanbiensc, commonly known as the Normanby's apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Syzygium normanbiensc thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native region.
