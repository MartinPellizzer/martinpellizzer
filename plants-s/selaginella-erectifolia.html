Selaginella erectifolia

Selaginella erectifolia, commonly known as the upright spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features upright stems covered with small, scale-like leaves that are tightly packed. Selaginella erectifolia reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called strobila.

This plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
