Seriphidium porrectum

Seriphidium porrectum, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Seriphidium porrectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
