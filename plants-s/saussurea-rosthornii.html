Saussurea rosthornii

Saussurea rosthornii, commonly known as the Tibetan snow lotus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other mountainous regions in Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, where it thrives in cold, arid conditions. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
