Scrophularia scorodonia subsp. multiflora

Scrophularia scorodonia subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the purple marsh willow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of purple or pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, wetland habitats such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
