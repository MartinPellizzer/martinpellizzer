Silene circumcarmanica

Silene circumcarmanica, commonly known as the broad-leafed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are broad and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene circumcarmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
