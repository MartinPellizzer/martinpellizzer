Shorea obtusoides

Shorea obtusoides, commonly known as the Malacca ironwood, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody fruits that contain winged seeds.

Shorea obtusoides plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
