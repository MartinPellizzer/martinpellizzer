Smilacina macrophylla

Smilacina macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf greenbriar, is a perennial vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Smilacina macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
