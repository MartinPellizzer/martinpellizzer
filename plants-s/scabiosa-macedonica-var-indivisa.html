Scabiosa macedonica var. indivisa

Scabiosa macedonica var. indivisa, commonly known as the Macedonian scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It features delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
