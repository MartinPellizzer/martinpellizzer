Selaginella congoensis

Selaginella congoensis, commonly known as the Congo spikemoss, is a small, evergreen fern ally native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a compact, mat-forming structure with closely packed, needle-like leaves. Its reproductive structures include spore-producing cones, which are borne on specialized branches.

Selaginella congoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
