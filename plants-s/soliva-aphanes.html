Soliva aphanes

Soliva aphanes, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Soliva aphanes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
