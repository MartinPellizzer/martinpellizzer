Syzygium circumscissum

Syzygium circumscissum, commonly known as the golden apple or the yellow apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The fruit is a round, golden-yellow berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor, often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and edible fruit make it an important species in both horticulture and local ecosystems.
