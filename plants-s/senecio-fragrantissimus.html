Senecio fragrantissimus

Senecio fragrantissimus, commonly known as the fragrant senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aromatic scent.

Senecio fragrantissimus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in Mediterranean climates.
