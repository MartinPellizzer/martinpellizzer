Symplocos macrophylla subsp. grandiflora

Symplocos macrophylla subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered inkberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that are often densely arranged along the stems.

The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, leathery berries that turn red as they mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
