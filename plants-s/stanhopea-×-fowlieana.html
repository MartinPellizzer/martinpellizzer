Stanhopea × fowlieana

Stanhopea × fowlieana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Stanhopea and a related species, typically Stanhopea coccinea.

It is a tropical orchid native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are often pink or red in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It is known for its unique morphology, including long, slender stems and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in the horticultural trade.
