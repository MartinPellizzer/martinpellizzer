Stephanopholis pinnata

Stephanopholis pinnata, commonly known as the common stephanopholis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
