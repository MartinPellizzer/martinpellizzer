Spilanthes americana f. ciliatifolia

Spilanthes americana f. ciliatifolia, commonly known as the fringed bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant features hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
