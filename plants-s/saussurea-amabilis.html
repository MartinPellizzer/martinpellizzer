Saussurea amabilis

Saussurea amabilis, commonly known as the Himalayan saffron or the Indian saffron, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
