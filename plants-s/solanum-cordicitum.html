Solanum cordicitum

Solanum cordicitum, commonly known as the heartleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, violet flowers that bloom in the evening. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant.

It is often found in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks.
