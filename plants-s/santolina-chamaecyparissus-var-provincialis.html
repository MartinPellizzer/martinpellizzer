Santolina chamaecyparissus var. provincialis

Santolina chamaecyparissus var. provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial santolina, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage that is densely packed and emits a pleasant, herbaceous scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in the spring, which are borne on short, upright stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in mild climates.
