Serruria eriocephala

Serruria eriocephala, commonly known as the woolly serruria, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise subdued appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
