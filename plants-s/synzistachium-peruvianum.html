Synzistachium peruvianum

Synzistachium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
