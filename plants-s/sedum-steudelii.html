Sedum steudelii

Sedum steudelii, commonly known as the yellow stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
