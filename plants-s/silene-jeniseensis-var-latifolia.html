Silene jeniseensis var. latifolia

Silene jeniseensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface. This variety is noted for its adaptability to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to plant biodiversity.
