Silene glareosa

Silene glareosa, commonly known as the glaucous catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, bluish-gray foliage and produces small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Silene glareosa is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
