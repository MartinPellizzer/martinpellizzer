Serjania ochroclada

Serjania ochroclada, commonly known as the yellow serjania, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae, which includes several species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and long, slender leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in spring and summer.

Serjania ochroclada is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
