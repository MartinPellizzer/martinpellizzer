Satyrium lindleyanum

Satyrium lindleyanum, commonly known as the Lindley's satyrium, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, meaning it attaches itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
