Schisandra glabra

Schisandra glabra, commonly known as the smooth schisandra, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries, which are edible and have a unique, complex flavor. Schisandra glabra is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
