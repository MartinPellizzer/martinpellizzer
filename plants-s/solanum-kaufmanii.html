Solanum kaufmanii

Solanum kaufmanii, commonly known as Kaufman's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are usually white or purple, and it produces small, bright red berries that are edible but can be toxic in large quantities.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
