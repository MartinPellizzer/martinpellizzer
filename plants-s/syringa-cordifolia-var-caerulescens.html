Syringa cordifolia var. caerulescens

Syringa cordifolia var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
