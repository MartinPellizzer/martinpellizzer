Silene pubescens

Silene pubescens, commonly known as the hairy campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Silene pubescens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
