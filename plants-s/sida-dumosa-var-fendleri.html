Sida dumosa var. fendleri

Sida dumosa var. fendleri, commonly known as the hairy sida, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
