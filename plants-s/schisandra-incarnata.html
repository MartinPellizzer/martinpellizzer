Schisandra incarnata

Schisandra incarnata, commonly known as red sanders, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, five-lobed, red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with five to nine elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are greenish-yellow with five petals. Schisandra incarnata thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
