Stellaria dichotoma var. lanceolata

Stellaria dichotoma var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a prostrate or ascending stem with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a succulent texture. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
