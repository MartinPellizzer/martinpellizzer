Senecio trachylaenus

Senecio trachylaenus, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, bluish-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is relatively low-maintenance, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
