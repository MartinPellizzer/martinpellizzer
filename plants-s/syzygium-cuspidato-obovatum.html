Syzygium cuspidato-obovatum

Syzygium cuspidato-obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are drupes, typically reddish when mature, containing a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
