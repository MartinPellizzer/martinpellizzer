Syzygium racemosum subsp. racemosum

Syzygium racemosum subsp.

racemosum, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy on the underside, and its raceme-like inflorescences that bear small, fragrant white flowers.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to reveal several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some applications in local cuisines and herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Myrtaceae family.
