Smilax calophylla var. concolor

Smilax calophylla var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed smilax, is a species of vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitats.
