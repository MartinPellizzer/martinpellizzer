Stylosanthes guianensis var. longiseta

Stylosanthes guianensis var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-hairy stylo, is a perennial legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with long, hairy stipules. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is highly effective at fixing atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable forage crop.

It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in pasturelands and soil conservation efforts.
