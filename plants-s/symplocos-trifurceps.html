Symplocos trifurceps

Symplocos trifurceps, commonly known as the three-branched beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright purple to violet berries that mature in the fall, attracting various bird species. Symplocos trifurceps typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.

Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.
