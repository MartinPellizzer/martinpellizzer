Selaginella browneana

Selaginella browneana, commonly known as the Brown's spikemoss, is a rare and endangered species of spikemoss native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae, characterized by its creeping, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophylls that bear microspores and megaspores, typical of its evolutionary lineage. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats, often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is listed as endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
