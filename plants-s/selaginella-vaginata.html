Selaginella vaginata

Selaginella vaginata, commonly known as the slender spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and sporophylls.

It is a vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant exhibits a unique life cycle with distinct gametophyte and sporophyte stages. Its sporophyte produces microspores and megaspores on separate structures, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Selaginella vaginata is also noted for its ability to survive in low-light conditions, making it a resilient species in diverse habitats.
