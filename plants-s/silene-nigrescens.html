Silene nigrescens

Silene nigrescens, commonly known as the blackening catchfly, is a flowering plant species native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Silene nigrescens is adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.
