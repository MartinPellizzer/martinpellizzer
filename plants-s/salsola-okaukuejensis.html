Salsola okaukuejensis

Salsola okaukuejensis, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan and surrounding areas. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and clustered in dense inflorescences, adapted to attract specific pollinators.

Salsola okaukuejensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
