Sindora siamensis

Sindora siamensis, commonly known as the Siam tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves have been utilized for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
