Salacca vermicularis

Salacca vermicularis, commonly known as the snake fruit plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that grow in a dense, cascading manner, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that eventually develop into the edible, spiky fruits known as snake fruits. These fruits are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Salacca vermicularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its fruit.
