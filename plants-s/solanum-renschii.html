Solanum renschii

Solanum renschii, commonly known as the Rensch's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Solanum renschii belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
