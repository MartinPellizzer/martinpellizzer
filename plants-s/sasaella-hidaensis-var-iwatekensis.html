Sasaella hidaensis var. iwatekensis

Sasaella hidaensis var. iwatekensis, commonly known as the Iwate sasa, is a variant of the Hida sasa, native to Japan.

It is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the bamboo family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in Japanese culture.
