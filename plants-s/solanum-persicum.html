Solanum persicum

Solanum persicum, commonly known as the Persian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually purple or violet in color. Its berries, which are toxic, are often mistaken for edible fruits.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
