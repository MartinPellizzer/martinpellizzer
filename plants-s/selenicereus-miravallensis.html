Selenicereus miravallensis

Selenicereus miravallensis, commonly known as the Miravallensis moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a thick, fleshy stem and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant is typically epiphytic, growing on trees or rocks rather than in soil. It thrives in warm, humid climates with bright, indirect light.

Selenicereus miravallensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
