Senecio exaltatus var. uniflosculus

Senecio exaltatus var. uniflosculus, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
