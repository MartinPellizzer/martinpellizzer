Solidago virgaurea var. gigantea

Solidago virgaurea var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant goldenrod, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies enhances its ecological value.
