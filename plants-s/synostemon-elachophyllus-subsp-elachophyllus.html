Synostemon elachophyllus subsp. elachophyllus

Synostemon elachophyllus subsp.

elachophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf synostemon, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are densely covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in arid regions.
