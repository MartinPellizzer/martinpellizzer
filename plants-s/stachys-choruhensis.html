Stachys choruhensis

Stachys choruhensis, commonly known as the Karakachan mountain betony, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or limestone soils in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
