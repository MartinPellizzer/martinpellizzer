Statice × abnormis

Statice × abnormis, commonly known as Sea Lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences of blue to purple flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color.

Statice × abnormis is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
