Swertia crassiuscula subsp. crassiuscula

Swertia crassiuscula subsp.

crassiuscula, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, often at elevations above 3,000 meters.

The plant features a slender, branched stem and lanceolate, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are blue to violet, arranged in cymes, and bloom during the summer months.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antipyretic effects.
