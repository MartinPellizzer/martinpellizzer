Solanum andigenum var. sihuanum

Solanum andigenum var. sihuanum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its potential medicinal properties, it has been the subject of botanical and pharmacological studies.
