Stachys thirkei

Stachys thirkei, commonly known as the Thirke's hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with opposite leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple.

Stachys thirkei is often found in rocky or woodland habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
