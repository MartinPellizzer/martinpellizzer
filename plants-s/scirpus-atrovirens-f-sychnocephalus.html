Scirpus atrovirens f. sychnocephalus

Scirpus atrovirens f. sychnocephalus, commonly known as the purple club rush, is a botanical variant of the common club rush.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that is typically purple to reddish in color. The plant is a perennial sedge that thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dark green to reddish hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
