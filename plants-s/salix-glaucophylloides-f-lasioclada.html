Salix glaucophylloides f. lasioclada

Salix glaucophylloides f. lasioclada, commonly known as the glaucophylloides willow with a lasioclada form, is a variant of the glaucophylloides willow.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a bluish-green hue and a dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found near streams or in wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
