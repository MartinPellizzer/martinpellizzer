Silene germana

Silene germana, commonly known as the German catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Silene germana is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often pink or red flowers.
