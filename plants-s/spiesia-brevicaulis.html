Spiesia brevicaulis

Spiesia brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, often clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Spiesia brevicaulis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
