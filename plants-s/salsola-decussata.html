Salsola decussata

Salsola decussata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spiny heads. Its extensive root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Salsola decussata is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
