Stipa cernua

Stipa cernua, commonly known as nodding grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and drooping inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Stipa cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
