Solanum tarderemotum

Solanum tarderemotum, commonly known as the ghost pepper plant, is a species of nightshade native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, green, bell-shaped flowers that transition to a red hue as they mature. The plant produces tiny, fiery red berries that contain a high concentration of capsaicin, making them extremely spicy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its potency, it is often cultivated for its unique culinary and medicinal properties.
