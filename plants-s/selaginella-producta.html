Selaginella producta

Selaginella producta, commonly known as the spore-bearing clubmoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves.

It is a vascular plant that produces spores rather than seeds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a short, upright stem with spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Selaginella producta is also notable for its ability to survive extreme environmental conditions, including desiccation.

Its unique morphology and reproductive strategy make it an interesting subject in the study of ancient plant lineages.
