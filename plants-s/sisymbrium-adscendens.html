Sisymbrium adscendens

Sisymbrium adscendens, commonly known as the ascending winter cress, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features basal rosettes of pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruits are short, cylindrical pods that release seeds upon maturity.

Sisymbrium adscendens is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
