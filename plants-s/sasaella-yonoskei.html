Sasaella yonoskei

Sasaella yonoskei, commonly known as the Yonoske's sasa, is a shrub-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Sasaella yonoskei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
