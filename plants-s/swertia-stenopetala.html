Swertia stenopetala

Swertia stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled swertia, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are slender and branched.

Swertia stenopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
