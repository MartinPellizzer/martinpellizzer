Sison haenkei

Sison haenkei, commonly known as the yellow bell or yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
