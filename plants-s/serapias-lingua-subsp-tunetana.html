Serapias lingua subsp. tunetana

Serapias lingua subsp.

tunetana, commonly known as the Sicilian orchid, is a rare and endemic species found primarily in southern Italy. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny and well-drained environments. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological significance.
