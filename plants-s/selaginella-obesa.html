Selaginella obesa

Selaginella obesa, commonly known as the fat spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to arid regions of Central America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in shaded environments. The plant features a rosette of fleshy, scale-like leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and produce spores for propagation.

Selaginella obesa is valued for its resilience and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental appearance.
