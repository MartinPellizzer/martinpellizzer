Solanum plicatile

Solanum plicatile, commonly known as the zigzag nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum plicatile is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
