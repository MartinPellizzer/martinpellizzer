Sasa hannoensis

Sasa hannoensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Hanno bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a deep green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Sasa hannoensis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It is also valued in traditional Japanese culture for its aesthetic and practical uses.
