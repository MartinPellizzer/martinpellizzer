Spathia

Spathia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, spathaceous bracts that resemble a single, large petal, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers within the bracts, which are typically white or pale pink in color. Spathia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.
