Senecio integrifolius subsp. capitatus

Senecio integrifolius subsp.

capitatus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of blue-green, hairy leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species in its native range.
