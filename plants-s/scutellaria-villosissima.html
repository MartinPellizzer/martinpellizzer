Scutellaria villosissima

Scutellaria villosissima, commonly known as the hairy skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Scutellaria villosissima is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
