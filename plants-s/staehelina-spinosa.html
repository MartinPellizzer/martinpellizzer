Staehelina spinosa

Staehelina spinosa, commonly known as the spiny cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in sharp, spiny tubercles that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stem to survive in dry conditions.

Staehelina spinosa is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and unique member of the cactus family.
