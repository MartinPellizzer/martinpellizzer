Symplocos huegeliana

Symplocos huegeliana, commonly known as the red-berried beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for various bird species.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking fruit display.
