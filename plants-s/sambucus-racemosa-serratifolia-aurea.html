Sambucus racemosa serratifolia-aurea

Sambucus racemosa serratifolia-aurea, commonly known as the golden elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a golden-yellow hue, particularly noticeable in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
