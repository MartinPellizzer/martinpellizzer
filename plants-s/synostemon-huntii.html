Synostemon huntii

Synostemon huntii, commonly known as the Florida boxwood, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
