Sedum spathulifolium subsp. yosemitense

Sedum spathulifolium subsp.

yosemitense, commonly known as the Yosemite sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California. It is characterized by its fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to rocky and alpine environments.

This subspecies is often found in open meadows and along streambanks in its native habitat.
