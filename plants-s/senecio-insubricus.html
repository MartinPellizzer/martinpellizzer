Senecio insubricus

Senecio insubricus, commonly known as the Insubricus groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture.

It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators.
