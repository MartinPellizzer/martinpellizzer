Sersalisia usambarensis

Sersalisia usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambaras papyrus, is a unique aquatic plant native to the Usambara Mountains in Tanzania.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats, often found in marshes and along the edges of lakes. The plant has long, narrow leaves that float on the water surface, with a fibrous, spongy structure that aids in buoyancy. Its flowering stems emerge from the water, bearing small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color.

Sersalisia usambarensis plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
