Scoparia ericacea

Scoparia ericacea, commonly known as the heath scoparia, is a low-growing shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact, mat-like appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters during the late spring to early summer.

Scoparia ericacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
