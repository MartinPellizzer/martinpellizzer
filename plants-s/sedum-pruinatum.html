Sedum pruinatum

Sedum pruinatum, commonly known as the silver stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to North America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
