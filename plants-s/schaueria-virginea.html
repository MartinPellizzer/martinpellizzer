Schaueria virginea

Schaueria virginea, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Schaueria virginea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
