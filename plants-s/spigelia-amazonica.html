Spigelia amazonica

Spigelia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian candlewood, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
