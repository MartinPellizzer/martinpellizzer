Setaria viridis subsp. glareosa

Setaria viridis subsp.

glareosa, commonly known as green foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural research.
