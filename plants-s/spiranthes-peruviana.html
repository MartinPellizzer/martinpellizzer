Spiranthes peruviana

Spiranthes peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers.

Spiranthes peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
