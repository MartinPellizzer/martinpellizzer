Scleranthus longidens

Scleranthus longidens, commonly known as the long-stemmed thornweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, often pale green or reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Scleranthus longidens is adapted to thrive in temperate climates and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
