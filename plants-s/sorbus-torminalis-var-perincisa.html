Sorbus torminalis var. perincisa

Sorbus torminalis var. perincisa, commonly known as the whitebeams, is a variety of the rowan tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, bright red berries in the fall. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge. This plant is often found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
