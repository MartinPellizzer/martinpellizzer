Sagina procumbens var. praecox

Sagina procumbens var. praecox, commonly known as early common sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, closely spaced leaves that are typically succulent and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom early in the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in rocky areas.

This species is known for its hardiness and ability to colonize disturbed sites.
