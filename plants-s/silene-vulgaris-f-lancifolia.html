Silene vulgaris f. lancifolia

Silene vulgaris f. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluemink, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, with five petals and a prominent central yellow spot. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
