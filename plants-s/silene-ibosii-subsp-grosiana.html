Silene ibosii subsp. grosiana

Silene ibosii subsp.

grosiana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or open habitats.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to the arid conditions of its environment. Silene ibosii subsp.

grosiana is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
