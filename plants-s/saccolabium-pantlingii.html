Saccolabium pantlingii

Saccolabium pantlingii, commonly known as the pantling's saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often found in open habitats such as savannas and forest edges, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
