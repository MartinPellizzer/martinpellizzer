Stachys officinalis lusus peloria

Stachys officinalis lusus peloria, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and pairs of opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-pink flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its scientific name reflects its historical use in herbal medicine for its oil-rich seeds.

The lusus peloria variant is notable for its unique floral coloration and variability in flower structure.
