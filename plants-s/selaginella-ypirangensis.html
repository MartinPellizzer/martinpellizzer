Selaginella ypirangensis

Selaginella ypirangensis, commonly known as the Ypiranga spikerush, is a species of spikemoss found in the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small, terrestrial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, spiky stems and small, scale-like leaves. It is characterized by its ability to produce sporangia on specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella ypirangensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
