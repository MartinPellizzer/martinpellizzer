Stylidium limbatum

Stylidium limbatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, sun-like flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Stylidium limbatum is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of Australia.
