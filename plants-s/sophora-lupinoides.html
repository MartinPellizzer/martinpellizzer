Sophora lupinoides

Sophora lupinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
