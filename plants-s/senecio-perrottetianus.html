Senecio perrottetianus

Senecio perrottetianus, commonly known as the African ragwort, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces numerous small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
