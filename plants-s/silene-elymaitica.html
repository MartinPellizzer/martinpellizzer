Silene elymaitica

Silene elymaitica, commonly known as the Elymian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Silene elymaitica has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
