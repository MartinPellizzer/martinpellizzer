Solanum barbulatum

Solanum barbulatum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Its berries, which are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, contain toxic compounds and should not be consumed.

Solanum barbulatum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
