Sideritis fruticulosa subsp. tarraconensis

Sideritis fruticulosa subsp.

tarraconensis, commonly known as the Tarraconensis sainfoin, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly serrated edge.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
