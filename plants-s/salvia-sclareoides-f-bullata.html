Salvia sclareoides f. bullata

Salvia sclareoides f. bullata, commonly known as the bullate sage, is a cultivar of the flowering plant Salvia sclareoides.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, and rounded leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
