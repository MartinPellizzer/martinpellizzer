Solanum yabari var. cuzcoense

Solanum yabari var. cuzcoense, commonly known as the Cuzco nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is native to the highlands of Peru and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant of both ecological and medicinal interest.

It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
