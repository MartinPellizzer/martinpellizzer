Salix eriocephala var. amplifolia

Salix eriocephala var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and small, inconspicuous female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
