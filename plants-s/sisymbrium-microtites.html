Sisymbrium microtites

Sisymbrium microtites, commonly known as the small-flowered mustard, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
