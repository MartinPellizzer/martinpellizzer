Salvia fragifera

Salvia fragifera, commonly known as the fragrant sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom in the summer. Salvia fragifera is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal remedies.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
