Syzygium amieuense

Syzygium amieuense, commonly known as the white-veined tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with prominent white veins. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour fruits similar to tamarind. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and economic significance.

Syzygium amieuense thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
