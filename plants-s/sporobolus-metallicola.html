Sporobolus metallicola

Sporobolus metallicola, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a common weed in agricultural and urban landscapes.
