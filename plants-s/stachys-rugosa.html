Stachys rugosa

Stachys rugosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Stachys rugosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
