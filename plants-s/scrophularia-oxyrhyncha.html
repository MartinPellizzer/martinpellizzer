Scrophularia oxyrhyncha

Scrophularia oxyrhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular, arranged in spikes, and are usually pale purple to pink in color.

Scrophularia oxyrhyncha is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
