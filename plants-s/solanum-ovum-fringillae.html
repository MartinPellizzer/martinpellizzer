Solanum ovum-fringillae

Solanum ovum-fringillae, commonly known as the golden apple or bird's egg plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals. Its fruit is a small, round berry that changes color from green to yellow or orange as it matures.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
