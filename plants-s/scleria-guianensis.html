Scleria guianensis

Scleria guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes during the growing season.

Scleria guianensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in wetland gardens.
