Saxifraga exarata subsp. carniolica

Saxifraga exarata subsp.

carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
