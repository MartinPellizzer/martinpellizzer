Salix × fontqueri

Salix × fontqueri, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Salix nigra and Salix discolor, characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter green underneath, with a finely toothed margin. It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination and seed dispersal.

Salix × fontqueri is often found in moist, shaded areas such as stream banks and woodland edges.
