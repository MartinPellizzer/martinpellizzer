Siphocampylus megalanthus var. orthosepalus

Siphocampylus megalanthus var. orthosepalus, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its habitat.

This variant is noted for its unique floral structure and is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
