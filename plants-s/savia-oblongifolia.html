Savia oblongifolia

Savia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
