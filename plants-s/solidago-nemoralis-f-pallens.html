Solidago nemoralis f. pallens

Solidago nemoralis f. pallens, commonly known as the pallid goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to whitish composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological importance, it is often found in conservation areas and native plant gardens.
