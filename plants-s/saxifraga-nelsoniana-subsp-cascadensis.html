Saxifraga nelsoniana subsp. cascadensis

Saxifraga nelsoniana subsp.

cascadensis, commonly known as the Cascade saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically thrives in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and forest floors.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in dense inflorescences, adding ornamental value to its natural environment.

It is well adapted to cool, humid climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
