Senecio suavis

Senecio suavis, commonly known as sweet Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Senecio suavis is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
