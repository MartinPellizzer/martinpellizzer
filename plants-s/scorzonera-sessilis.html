Scorzonera sessilis

Scorzonera sessilis, commonly known as the sessile scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive, with thick, fleshy roots that are often harvested for their edible qualities.

Scorzonera sessilis is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
