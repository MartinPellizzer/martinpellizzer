Sporobolus vryburgensis

Sporobolus vryburgensis, commonly known as the Vryburg grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Sporobolus vryburgensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
