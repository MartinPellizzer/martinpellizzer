Seruneum calendulaceum

Seruneum calendulaceum, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Seruneum calendulaceum is also noted for its medicinal properties, having been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
