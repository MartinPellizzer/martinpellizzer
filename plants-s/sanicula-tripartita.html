Sanicula tripartita

Sanicula tripartita, commonly known as three-part sanicle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae, which includes buttercups and other flowering plants. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a follicle that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sanicula tripartita is often found in moist meadows and woodlands, where it thrives in partial shade.
