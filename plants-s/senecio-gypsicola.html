Senecio gypsicola

Senecio gypsicola, commonly known as the gypsum groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, often on gypsum soils, which influence its unique adaptations. It produces small, yellow daisy-like flowers and has linear, succulent leaves that help it conserve water.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
