Symphytum officinale f. rakosiense

Symphytum officinale f. rakosiense, commonly known as the Rakosi's comfrey, is a variant of the common comfrey plant.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and tall flowering stalks that bear clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to promote wound healing.

However, due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
