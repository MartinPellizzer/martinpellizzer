Saussurea lanata

Saussurea lanata, commonly known as the velvet tarragon or the woolly saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
