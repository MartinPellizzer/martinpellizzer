Saxifraga gatogombensis

Saxifraga gatogombensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian saxifrage, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered rosettes of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its alpine habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
