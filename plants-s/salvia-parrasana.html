Salvia parrasana

Salvia parrasana, commonly known as the Parras sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Salvia parrasana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
