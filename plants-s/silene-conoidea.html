Silene conoidea

Silene conoidea, commonly known as the "spearwort," is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink to white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that split open to release seeds. Silene conoidea thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
