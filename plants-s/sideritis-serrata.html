Sideritis serrata

Sideritis serrata, commonly known as Greek ladies' fingers, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Sideritis serrata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
