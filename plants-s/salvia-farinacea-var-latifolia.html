Salvia farinacea var. latifolia

Salvia farinacea var. latifolia, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features attractive, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements contribute to its widespread cultivation.
