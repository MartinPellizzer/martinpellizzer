Scirpus hippolyti

Scirpus hippolyti, commonly known as the purple bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in marshy or muddy habitats. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are usually purple or reddish in color.

Scirpus hippolyti plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
