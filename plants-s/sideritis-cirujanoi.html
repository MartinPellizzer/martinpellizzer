Sideritis cirujanoi

Sideritis cirujanoi, commonly known as Spanish heather, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Sideritis cirujanoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
