Salvia pamplonitana

Salvia pamplonitana, commonly known as the Pamplona sage, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, minty aroma.

Salvia pamplonitana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and Mediterranean landscapes.
