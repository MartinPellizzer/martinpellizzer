Sideritis taurica subsp. catillaris

Sideritis taurica subsp.

catillaris, commonly known as the Caucasian ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its aromatic properties. Sideritis taurica subsp.

catillaris is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
