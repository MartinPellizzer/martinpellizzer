Spiraea prunifolia var. plena

Spiraea prunifolia var. plena, commonly known as the double meadow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of double, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems and finely cut, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its showy flowers and compact growth habit.
