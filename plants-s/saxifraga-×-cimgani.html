Saxifraga × cimgani

Saxifraga × cimgani, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
