Scrophularia scorodonia var. longepetiolata

Scrophularia scorodonia var. longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves with long petioles. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple to pink in color. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often found in open habitats like grasslands and woodland edges.
