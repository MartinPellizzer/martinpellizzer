Spiranthes costaricensis

Spiranthes costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican spiral orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral structure.
