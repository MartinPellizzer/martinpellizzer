Santolina intricata

Santolina intricata, commonly known as the Mexican santolina, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant has aromatic, silver-gray foliage that is often used in ornamental gardening for its visual appeal and texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina intricata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
