Simira podocarpa

Simira podocarpa, commonly known as the red cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by elliptical, leathery fruits that contain winged seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and durable wood, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural and timber purposes.
