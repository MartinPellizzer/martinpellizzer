Sieversia × macrantha

Sieversia × macrantha, commonly known as the giant hyacinth, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Sieversia humilis and Sieversia macrophylla.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with long, strap-like leaves and a robust root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is not found in the wild and is primarily cultivated for its striking floral display.
