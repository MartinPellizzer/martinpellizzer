Sterculia cuspidata

Sterculia cuspidata, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, oval seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and traditional medicine.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
