Senecio homogyniphyllus

Senecio homogyniphyllus, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow-orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
