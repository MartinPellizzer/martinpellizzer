Salvia viridis var. horminum

Salvia viridis var. horminum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it both ornamental and valuable to pollinators.
