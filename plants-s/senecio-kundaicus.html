Senecio kundaicus

Senecio kundaicus, commonly known as the Kunda's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Senecio kundaicus is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
