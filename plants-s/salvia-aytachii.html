Salvia aytachii

Salvia aytachii, commonly known as the Aytach sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Salvia aytachii is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
