Solanum parmentieri

Solanum parmentieri, commonly known as the Parmentier's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by oval, red berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum parmentieri is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
