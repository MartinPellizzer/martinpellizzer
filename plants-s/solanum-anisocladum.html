Solanum anisocladum

Solanum anisocladum, commonly known as the variable nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its variable leaf shapes and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that change color as they mature, often from green to red.

Solanum anisocladum is adapted to a variety of habitats, including dry and moist environments, and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.
