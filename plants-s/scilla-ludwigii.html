Scilla ludwigii

Scilla ludwigii, commonly known as the Ludwig's scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Scilla ludwigii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
