Stachys foeniculum

Stachys foeniculum, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, pink or violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Stachys foeniculum is often found in grassy areas, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its fragrant properties and use in traditional herbal medicine.
