Sophia viscosa

Sophia viscosa, commonly known as the rough sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or rocky areas.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
