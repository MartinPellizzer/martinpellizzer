Stachys trapezuntea

Stachys trapezuntea, commonly known as the Trapezuntian hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Stachys trapezuntea is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
