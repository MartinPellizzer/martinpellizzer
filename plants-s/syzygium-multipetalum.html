Syzygium multipetalum

Syzygium multipetalum, commonly known as the white-flowered syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while the fruit is a small, round drupe with a smooth skin.

Syzygium multipetalum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a source of aromatic foliage.
