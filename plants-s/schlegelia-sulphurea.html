Schlegelia sulphurea

Schlegelia sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a pale green or yellowish hue. Schlegelia sulphurea thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
