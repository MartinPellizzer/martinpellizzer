Stryphnodendron inaequale

Stryphnodendron inaequale, commonly known as the yellow rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, highly valued wood that ranges from yellow to reddish-brown in color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and used in traditional medicine.
