Sideritis hyssopifolia var. aranensis

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. aranensis, commonly known as the Armenian hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to rocky and arid environments.
