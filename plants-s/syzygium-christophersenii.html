Syzygium christophersenii

Syzygium christophersenii, commonly known as the Kauri apple, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to the tropical rainforests of New Guinea.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are yellow or orange when ripe.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
