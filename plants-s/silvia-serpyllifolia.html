Silvia serpyllifolia

Silvia serpyllifolia, commonly known as common mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spoon-shaped leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and rocky areas.

Silvia serpyllifolia is a hardy species that is well adapted to temperate climates.
