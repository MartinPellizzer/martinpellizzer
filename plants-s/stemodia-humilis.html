Stemodia humilis

Stemodia humilis, commonly known as the low stemodia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Africa.

It has a prostrate or ascending growth habit with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered in fine pubescence. Small, white to pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters at the leaf axils.

This plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
