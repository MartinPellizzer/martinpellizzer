Spiranthes oestrifera

Spiranthes oestrifera, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of pale green to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a slender, creeping rhizome and produces small, greenish-brown seed pods after flowering.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in clusters along stream banks and in pine forests.
