Senna intermedia

Senna intermedia, commonly known as golden senna, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in herbal medicine for its laxative properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family.
