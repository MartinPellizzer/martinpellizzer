Selinum japonicum

Selinum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese parsley or Japanese carrot, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often used in traditional Asian cuisine. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Selinum japonicum is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.
