Siraitia grosvenorii

Siraitia grosvenorii, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its large, round fruits that can weigh over 500 pounds. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are typically separate from the fruit-bearing ones. Its fruit is primarily cultivated for its size and is often used in festivals and as a decorative element.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season to reach its full potential.
