Steriphoma paradoxum subsp. macranthum

Steriphoma paradoxum subsp.

macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of xerophytic plants.
