Senecio minimus

Senecio minimus, commonly known as the dwarf African daisy, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
