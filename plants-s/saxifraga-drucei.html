Saxifraga drucei

Saxifraga drucei, commonly known as the Druce's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are characterized by their serrated edges.

Saxifraga drucei is often found in high-altitude habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
