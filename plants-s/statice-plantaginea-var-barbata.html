Statice plantaginea var. barbata

Statice plantaginea var. barbata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its profuse display of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with hairy stems that help reduce water loss.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand salty air, making it well-suited for maritime environments.
