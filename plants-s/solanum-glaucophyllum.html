Solanum glaucophyllum

Solanum glaucophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Solanum glaucophyllum belongs to the nightshade family, which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.
