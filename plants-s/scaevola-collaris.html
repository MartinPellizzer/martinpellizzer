Scaevola collaris

Scaevola collaris, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a velvety texture, and its flowers are arranged in dense, showy clusters. Scaevola collaris thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
