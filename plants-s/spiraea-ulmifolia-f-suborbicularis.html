Spiraea ulmifolia f. suborbicularis

Spiraea ulmifolia f. suborbicularis, commonly known as the roundedleaf weeping spirea, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, creating a delicate, cascading effect. The leaves are ovate to suborbicular in shape, with a smooth, slightly wavy margin, giving the plant a unique, rounded appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting floral display.
