Schoenus natans

Schoenus natans, commonly known as the floating grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and lakes. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and floating leaves that remain just above the water surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in panicles and are wind-pollinated.

Schoenus natans plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
