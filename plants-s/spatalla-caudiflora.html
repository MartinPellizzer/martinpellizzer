Spatalla caudiflora

Spatalla caudiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered spathiflora, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Spatalla caudiflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and greenhouses.
