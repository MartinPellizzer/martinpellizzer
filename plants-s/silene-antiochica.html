Silene antiochica

Silene antiochica, commonly known as the Antioch pink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Silene antiochica is often found in areas with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
