Salicornia senegalensis

Salicornia senegalensis, commonly known as Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are encased in a papery, persistent husk. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
