Salix nyiwensis

Salix nyiwensis, commonly known as the Bhutanese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region.

It typically grows in moist, cool environments such as stream banks and forest edges. The plant has slender, often zigzagging branches and produces catkins during the spring season. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Salix nyiwensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
