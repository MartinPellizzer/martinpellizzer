Sphinga

Sphinga, commonly known as the sphinx moth plant, is a type of flowering plant native to various regions in the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers that resemble those of orchids or other exotic blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant flowers.

Sphinga is also valued for its ecological role, attracting pollinators like moths and butterflies.
