Synnema origanoides

Synnema origanoides, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its fragrant leaves and unique appearance make it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens.
