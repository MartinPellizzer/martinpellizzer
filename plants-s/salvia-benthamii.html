Salvia benthamii

Salvia benthamii, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia benthamii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
