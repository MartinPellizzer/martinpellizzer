Sedum scaphiophyllum

Sedum scaphiophyllum, commonly known as the rock sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically thick and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Sedum scaphiophyllum is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
