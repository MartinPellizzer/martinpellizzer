Silene scabriflora subsp. megacalycina

Silene scabriflora subsp.

megacalycina, commonly known as the roughleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open meadows and sagebrush steppe.

Its subspecies status highlights its unique adaptations to arid environments.
