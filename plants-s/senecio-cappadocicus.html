Senecio cappadocicus

Senecio cappadocicus, commonly known as the Cappadocia groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio cappadocicus is often found in limestone soils and is considered a characteristic species of the region's unique ecosystem.
