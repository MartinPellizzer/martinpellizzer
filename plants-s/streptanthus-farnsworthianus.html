Streptanthus farnsworthianus

Streptanthus farnsworthianus, commonly known as the Farnsworth's bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. Its unique morphology includes a short stem with several pairs of pinnately divided leaves.

The species is named in honor of the botanist and author Robert Farnsworth.
