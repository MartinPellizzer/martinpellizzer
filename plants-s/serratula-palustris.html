Serratula palustris

Serratula palustris, commonly known as swamp sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in moist soils and thrives in marshy or boggy environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Serratula palustris is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
