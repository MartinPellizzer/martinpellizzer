Salix aurita f. uliginosa

Salix aurita f. uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp willow, is a shrub native to wetland habitats in northern regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, yellow-green catkins that appear in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

This variety is a valuable component of riparian ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
