Scutellaria purpurascens var. obtusifolia

Scutellaria purpurascens var. obtusifolia, commonly known as purple scutellaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a purplish tinge. The plant produces spikes of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
