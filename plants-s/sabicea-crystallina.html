Sabicea crystallina

Sabicea crystallina, commonly known as the crystal vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, waxy, white or pale green leaves that often have a crystalline appearance due to the presence of minute calcium oxalate crystals. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
