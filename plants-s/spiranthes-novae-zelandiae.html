Spiranthes novae-zelandiae

Spiranthes novae-zelandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand ladies' tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, white or pale green flowers that spiral around the stem, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. It has strap-like leaves and produces a single, elongated spike of flowers.

Spiranthes novae-zelandiae is a rare and protected species, valued for its unique floral structure and ecological significance.
