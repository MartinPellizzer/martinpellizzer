Saccolabium fimbriatum

Saccolabium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which are often surrounded by soft, hairy structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in grassland or open woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
