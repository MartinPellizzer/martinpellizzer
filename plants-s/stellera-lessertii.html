Stellera lessertii

Stellera lessertii, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are usually covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair.

Stellera lessertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
