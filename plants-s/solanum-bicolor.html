Solanum bicolor

Solanum bicolor, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum bicolor is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
