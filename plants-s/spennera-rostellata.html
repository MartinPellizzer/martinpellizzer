Spennera rostellata

Spennera rostellata, commonly known as the red-tipped spennera, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils.

Spennera rostellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
