Scholtzia pentamera subsp. collina

Scholtzia pentamera subsp.

collina, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features showy, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Scholtzia pentamera subsp.

collina is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
