Salvia incarnata

Salvia incarnata, commonly known as scarlet salvia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia incarnata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and low maintenance requirements.
