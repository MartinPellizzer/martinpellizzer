Schizachyrium jeffreysii

Schizachyrium jeffreysii, commonly known as Jeffreys' sideblades, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, and it is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Schizachyrium jeffreysii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
