Scleria wichurae

Scleria wichurae, commonly known as the white-eyed bulrush, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Scleria wichurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
