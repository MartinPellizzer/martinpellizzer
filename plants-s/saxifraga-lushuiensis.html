Saxifraga lushuiensis

Saxifraga lushuiensis, commonly known as the Lushui saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous environments, thriving in cool, humid conditions. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in high-altitude regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
