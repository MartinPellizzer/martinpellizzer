Setaria pradana

Setaria pradana, commonly known as false millet, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Setaria pradana is an important plant in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in wetland ecosystems.
