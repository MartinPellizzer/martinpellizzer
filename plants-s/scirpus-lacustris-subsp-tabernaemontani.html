Scirpus lacustris subsp. tabernaemontani

Scirpus lacustris subsp.

tabernaemontani, commonly known as the water clubrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats. It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and lake margins. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

The species is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
