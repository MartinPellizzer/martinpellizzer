Salix × brachypurpurea

Salix × brachypurpurea, commonly known as the purple osier dogwood, is a hybrid willow species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems that produce clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly the striking purplish-red color of its young branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found along stream banks and wetlands.

Salix × brachypurpurea is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
