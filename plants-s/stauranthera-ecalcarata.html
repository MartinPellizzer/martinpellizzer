Stauranthera ecalcarata

Stauranthera ecalcarata, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its flowers typically bloom from late winter to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The species is characterized by its distinctive seed pods that split open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
