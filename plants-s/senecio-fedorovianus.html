Senecio fedorovianus

Senecio fedorovianus, commonly known as the Fedorov's groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
