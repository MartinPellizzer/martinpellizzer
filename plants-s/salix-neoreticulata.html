Salix neoreticulata

Salix neoreticulata, commonly known as the Chinese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, typically appearing bright green in summer. It produces catkins in early spring, which are the primary means of seed dispersal.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
