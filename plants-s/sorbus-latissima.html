Sorbus latissima

Sorbus latissima, commonly known as the broadleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often glabrous on the upper surface and hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
