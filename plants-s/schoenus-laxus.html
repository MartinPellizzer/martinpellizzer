Schoenus laxus

Schoenus laxus, commonly known as the loose sedgeland sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense mats near water bodies. Its leaves are grass-like, with narrow, linear blades, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Schoenus laxus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
