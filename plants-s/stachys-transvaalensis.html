Stachys transvaalensis

Stachys transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and pink.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
