Senecio berteroanus

Senecio berteroanus, commonly known as the Bertero's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually grayish-green, covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio berteroanus is often found in open, sunny areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
