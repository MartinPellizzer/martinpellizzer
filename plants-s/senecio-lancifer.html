Senecio lancifer

Senecio lancifer, commonly known as the lancifer dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and hairy leaves that are lance-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by fluffy seed heads similar to those of dandelions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Senecio lancifer is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
