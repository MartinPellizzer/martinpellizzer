Santolina pecten

Santolina pecten, commonly known as sea fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that bloom in the summer months. Santolina pecten is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in coastal environments.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
