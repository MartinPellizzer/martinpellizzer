Scilla peruviana var. livida

Scilla peruviana var. livida, commonly known as the Peruvian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South America.

It features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters at the top of slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
