Stevia eupatoria

Stevia eupatoria, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers and its aromatic, slightly sweet-smelling leaves. Stevia eupatoria is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and herbal collections.
