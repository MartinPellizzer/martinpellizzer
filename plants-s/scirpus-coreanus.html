Scirpus coreanus

Scirpus coreanus, commonly known as the Korean bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

Scirpus coreanus produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellowish-brown flowers that bloom in the summer months.
