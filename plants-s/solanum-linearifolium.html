Solanum linearifolium

Solanum linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. Its fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum linearifolium is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
