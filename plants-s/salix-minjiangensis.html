Salix minjiangensis

Salix minjiangensis, commonly known as the Minjiang willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
