Senegalia guadalupensis

Senegalia guadalupensis, commonly known as Guadalupine acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures in mature plants. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

Senegalia guadalupensis plays an important ecological role by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in desert landscapes.
