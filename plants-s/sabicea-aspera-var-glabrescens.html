Sabicea aspera var. glabrescens

Sabicea aspera var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth sabicea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and has lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth or slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels. Its fruits are small, spherical pods that split open to release seeds.

This species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in traditional medicine.
