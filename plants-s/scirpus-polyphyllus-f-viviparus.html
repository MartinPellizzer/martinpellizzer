Scirpus polyphyllus f. viviparus

Scirpus polyphyllus f. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush characterized by its unique reproductive strategy.

This plant is a wetland species that thrives in shallow water and saturated soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. One of its most notable features is the production of viviparous shoots, which are capable of rooting and growing into new plants while still attached to the parent. These shoots contribute to its rapid propagation and ecological success in aquatic environments.

The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated.
