Silene vespertina var. pubicalycina

Silene vespertina var. pubicalycina, commonly known as the evening silene, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers have prominent, showy calyces that are hairy, giving it its varietal name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
