Scorzonera limnophila

Scorzonera limnophila, commonly known as the water scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine.

Scorzonera limnophila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
