Spiraea × semperflorens f. spicata

Spiraea × semperflorens f. spicata, commonly known as the spicata form of the everblooming spirea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its floral display. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is hardy in USDA zones 4 through 8.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability in garden settings.
