Sphondylium marginatum

Sphondylium marginatum, commonly known as the margined spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white margin, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Sphondylium marginatum is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
