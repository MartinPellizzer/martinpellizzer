Selenicereus monacanthus

Selenicereus monacanthus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces a fragrant, tubular bloom that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its growth habit includes a woody stem with areoles, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions with minimal water requirements.
