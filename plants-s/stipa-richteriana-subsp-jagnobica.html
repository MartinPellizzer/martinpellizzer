Stipa richteriana subsp. jagnobica

Stipa richteriana subsp.

jagnobica, commonly known as the Jagnobica needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often clustered in dense, spike-like formations. This subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low precipitation.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems in its native range.
