Seridia

Seridia, commonly known as the golden grass or seridia grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to various regions of Africa, particularly in the southern part of the continent. The plants are typically perennial, characterized by their slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. They produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Seridia species are often found in dry, open habitats and are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological roles in supporting local wildlife.
