Seaforthia coronata

Seaforthia coronata, commonly known as the crown seaforthia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescence that resembles a crown, consisting of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense, spherical cluster. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight bluish-gray hue.

Seaforthia coronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
