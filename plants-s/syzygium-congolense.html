Syzygium congolense

Syzygium congolense, commonly known as the Congo rose or African rose, is a tropical evergreen tree native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
