Sorbus oblongifolia var. virescens

Sorbus oblongifolia var. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that remain densely packed throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
