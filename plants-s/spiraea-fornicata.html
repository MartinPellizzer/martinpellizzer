Spiraea fornicata

Spiraea fornicata, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin.

Spiraea fornicata prefers moist, well-drained soil and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
