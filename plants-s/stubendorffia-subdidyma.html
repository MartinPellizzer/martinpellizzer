Stubendorffia subdidyma

Stubendorffia subdidyma, commonly known as the narrow-leafed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its small yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and it produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
