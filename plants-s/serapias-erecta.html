Serapias erecta

Serapias erecta, commonly known as the upright sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring well-drained environments. The plant features a basal rosette of linear, gray-green leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded shape. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Serapias erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
