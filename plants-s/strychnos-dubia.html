Strychnos dubia

Strychnos dubia, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine despite their harmful properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
