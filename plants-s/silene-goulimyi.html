Silene goulimyi

Silene goulimyi, commonly known as the mountain catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene goulimyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
