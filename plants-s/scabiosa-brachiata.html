Scabiosa brachiata

Scabiosa brachiata, commonly known as the clustered scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Scabiosa brachiata is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
