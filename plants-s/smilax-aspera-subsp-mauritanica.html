Smilax aspera subsp. mauritanica

Smilax aspera subsp.

mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania devil's backbone, is a climbing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa. It features spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with low soil moisture.

Its morphology reflects its evolutionary response to harsh, desert-like conditions.
