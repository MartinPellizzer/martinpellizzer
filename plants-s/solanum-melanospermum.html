Solanum melanospermum

Solanum melanospermum, commonly known as the blackseed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that start green and mature to a deep black. Its fruits contain small, dark seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its toxic properties, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
