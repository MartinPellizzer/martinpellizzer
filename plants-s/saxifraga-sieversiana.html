Saxifraga sieversiana

Saxifraga sieversiana, commonly known as the Sievers' saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are often basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Saxifraga sieversiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
