Smilax mexicana

Smilax mexicana, commonly known as Mexican catclaw, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in sharp, hooked spines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a rough texture.

Smilax mexicana is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
