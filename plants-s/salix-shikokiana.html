Salix shikokiana

Salix shikokiana, commonly known as the Shikoku willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow to golden-green foliage that turns red in the fall. The plant produces catkins, which are its male flowers, and small, inconspicuous female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Salix shikokiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
