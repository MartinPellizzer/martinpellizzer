Senecio fruticosus

Senecio fruticosus, commonly known as the woolly goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
