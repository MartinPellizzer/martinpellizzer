Syzygium micklethwaitii var. subcordatum

Syzygium micklethwaitii var. subcordatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed clove.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential in essential oil production.
