Stachys aimerici

Stachys aimerici, commonly known as Aimeric betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Stachys aimerici is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
