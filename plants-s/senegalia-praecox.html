Senegalia praecox

Senegalia praecox, commonly known as the early senegalia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, feathery leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments. Senegalia praecox plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its ecological significance makes it a valuable species in desert ecosystems.
