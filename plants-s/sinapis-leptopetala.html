Sinapis leptopetala

Sinapis leptopetala, commonly known as the slender-flowered mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine.
