Sasaella

Sasaella, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or sasa, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and the presence of both leafy shoots and flowering stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions of East Asia. Sasaella species are known for their slender, hollow stems and their ability to form thickets, which provide habitat for various wildlife.

The genus includes several species, each with slight variations in leaf shape and flowering structures.
