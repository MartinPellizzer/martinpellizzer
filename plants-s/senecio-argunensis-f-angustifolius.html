Senecio argunensis f. angustifolius

Senecio argunensis f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senecio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with a fibrous root system adapted to rocky soils. The leaves are lance-shaped, narrow, and often covered with a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
