Scutellaria microphysa

Scutellaria microphysa, commonly known as the small-leaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

Scutellaria microphysa is often found in the understory of forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
