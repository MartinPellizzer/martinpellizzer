Suaeda maritima var. macrocarpa

Suaeda maritima var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited sea blite, is a halophytic plant adapted to coastal environments.

It thrives in saline soils and is often found in salt marshes and dunes. The plant has succulent leaves that store water, and its flowers are small and typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are inflated, three-lobed capsules that release seeds when mature.

This species plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various organisms.
