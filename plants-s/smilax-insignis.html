Smilax insignis

Smilax insignis, commonly known as the red stem smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
