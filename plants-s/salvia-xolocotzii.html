Salvia xolocotzii

Salvia xolocotzii, commonly known as the Xolocotzi sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
