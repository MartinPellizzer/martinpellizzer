Senecio pentanthus

Senecio pentanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio pentanthus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience to drought.
