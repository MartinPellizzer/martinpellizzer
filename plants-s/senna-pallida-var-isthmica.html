Senna pallida var. isthmica

Senna pallida var. isthmica, commonly known as the Isthmian senna, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of northern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a slender legume containing several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
