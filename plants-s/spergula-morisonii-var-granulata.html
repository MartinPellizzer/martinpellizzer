Spergula morisonii var. granulata

Spergula morisonii var. granulata, commonly known as the granular spergula, is a flowering plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture. Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
