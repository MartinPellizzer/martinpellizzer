Schefflera subavenis

Schefflera subavenis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. Schefflera subavenis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
