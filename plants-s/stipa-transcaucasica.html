Stipa transcaucasica

Stipa transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Stipa transcaucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
