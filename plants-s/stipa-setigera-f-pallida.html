Stipa setigera f. pallida

Stipa setigera f. pallida, commonly known as the pale threadgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its slender, pale green blades and compact growth habit. The inflorescence consists of a narrow, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, pale florets. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

This plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
