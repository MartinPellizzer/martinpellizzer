Spiraea laeta var. subpubescens

Spiraea laeta var. subpubescens, commonly known as the silver-leaf weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-white leaves that provide striking visual contrast. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers in late spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
