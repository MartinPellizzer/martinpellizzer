Sesleria argentea var. hispanica

Sesleria argentea var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-white leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its graceful, feathery foliage and drought tolerance.
