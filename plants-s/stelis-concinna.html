Stelis concinna

Stelis concinna, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a slender, erect stem with few leaves, and its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale green. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique inflorescence and specialized root system.

Stelis concinna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
