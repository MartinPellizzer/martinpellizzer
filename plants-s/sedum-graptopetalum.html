Sedum graptopetalum

Sedum graptopetalum, commonly known as the "Blue Spruce Sedum," is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette pattern, which helps it store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it an excellent choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it a popular plant among gardeners in dry climates.
