Scilla gracillima

Scilla gracillima, commonly known as slender scilla, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

Scilla gracillima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early blooming habit.
