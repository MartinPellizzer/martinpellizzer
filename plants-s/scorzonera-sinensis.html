Scorzonera sinensis

Scorzonera sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features a robust, fleshy root system that is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Scorzonera sinensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
