Struthanthus cajamarcanus

Struthanthus cajamarcanus, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
