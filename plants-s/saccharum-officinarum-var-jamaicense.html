Saccharum officinarum var. jamaicense

Saccharum officinarum var. jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican sugarcane, is a variant of sugarcane native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks that contain high concentrations of sucrose. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and ample rainfall. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a prominent midrib, and it produces clusters of small, greenish flowers.

This variety is widely cultivated for sugar production and has significant economic importance in regions like Jamaica.
