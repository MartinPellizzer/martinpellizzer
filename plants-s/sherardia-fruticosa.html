Sherardia fruticosa

Sherardia fruticosa, commonly known as the African rose or red bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and produces clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

The shrub is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
