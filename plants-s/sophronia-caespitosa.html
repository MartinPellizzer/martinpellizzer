Sophronia caespitosa

Sophronia caespitosa, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which provides protection against harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces purple to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
