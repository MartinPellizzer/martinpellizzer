Sugerokia orientalis

Sugerokia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental sugarcane plant, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant is known for its high sugar content, making it economically significant in regions where it is cultivated. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with ample sunlight and water.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in both agricultural and ornamental settings.
