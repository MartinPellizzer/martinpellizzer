Senecio icoglossus var. gigantophyllus

Senecio icoglossus var. gigantophyllus, commonly known as the giant-leaf senecio, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
