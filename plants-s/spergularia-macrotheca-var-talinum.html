Spergularia macrotheca var. talinum

Spergularia macrotheca var. talinum, commonly known as the coastal sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Europe.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

The species is often found in dunes and other coastal habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.
