Syringa dilatata f. violacea

Syringa dilatata f. violacea, commonly known as the purple-leaf weigela, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its clusters of fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and floral display, which enhances its appeal in ornamental horticulture.
