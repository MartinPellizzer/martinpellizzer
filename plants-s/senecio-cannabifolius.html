Senecio cannabifolius

Senecio cannabifolius, commonly known as the marijuana leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of Cannabis sativa, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of its stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal and ornamental potential.
