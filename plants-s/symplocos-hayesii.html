Symplocos hayesii

Symplocos hayesii, commonly known as Hayes' waxleaf.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central America and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the late spring and early summer.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
