Salvia gabrieli

Salvia gabrieli, commonly known as Gabrieli sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gabrieli sage is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
