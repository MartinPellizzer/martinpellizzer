Scilla autumnalis subsp. fallax

Scilla autumnalis subsp.

fallax, commonly known as autumn squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It produces clusters of pale blue or lilac flowers in the autumn, typically from September to November.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in open woodlands, scrublands, and along coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
