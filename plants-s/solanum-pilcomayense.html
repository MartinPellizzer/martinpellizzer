Solanum pilcomayense

Solanum pilcomayense, commonly known as the Peruvian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats at high altitudes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
