Sasa chiyomurensis

Sasa chiyomurensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a bluish-green hue and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Japanese landscaping.

Sasa chiyomurensis is also known for its ability to form dense thickets, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
