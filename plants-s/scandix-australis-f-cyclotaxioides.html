Scandix australis f. cyclotaxioides

Scandix australis f. cyclotaxioides, commonly known as the cyclocyclic scandix, is a rare botanical variant native to southern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, featuring a distinctive arrangement of lobes that form a near-circular pattern. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense foliage, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring, producing small, clustered white flowers.
