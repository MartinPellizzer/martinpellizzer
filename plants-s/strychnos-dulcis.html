Strychnos dulcis

Strychnos dulcis, commonly known as the bitter apple, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
