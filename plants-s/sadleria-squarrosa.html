Sadleria squarrosa

Sadleria squarrosa, commonly known as the silver spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive purple throat. The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland gardens and stream banks.

Sadleria squarrosa is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
