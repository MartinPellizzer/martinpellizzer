Schefflera buxifolioides

Schefflera buxifolioides, commonly known as the glossy bucktooth plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features a dense, compact growth habit with thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its attractive foliage and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
