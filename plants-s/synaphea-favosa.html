Synaphea favosa

Synaphea favosa, commonly known as the fairy moss, is a small, cryptogenic moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is often found in shaded, moist environments such as rock crevices and forest floors. It produces small, inconspicuous capsules for spore dispersal, which are often surrounded by a peristome.

Synaphea favosa is well adapted to arid conditions, demonstrating resilience through desiccation tolerance and efficient water retention.
