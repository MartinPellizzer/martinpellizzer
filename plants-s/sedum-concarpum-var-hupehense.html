Sedum concarpum var. hupehense

Sedum concarpum var. hupehense, commonly known as the Hubei stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
