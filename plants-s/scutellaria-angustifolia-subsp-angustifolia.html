Scutellaria angustifolia subsp. angustifolia

Scutellaria angustifolia subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf skullcap, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and small, tubular flowers that are usually blue to purple in color. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
