Saccharum tinctorium

Saccharum tinctorium, commonly known as wild sugarcane or red grass, is a tall perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust stalks and vibrant red or purple pigmentation in its leaves and stems. The plant is notable for its high sugar content, which has historically been used for both culinary and medicinal purposes. Its fibrous structure makes it valuable for paper and textile production.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and carbon sequestration in its native habitats.
