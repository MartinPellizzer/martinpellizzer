Smilax blumei

Smilax blumei, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a climbing shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Smilax blumei is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
