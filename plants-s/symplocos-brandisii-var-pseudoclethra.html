Symplocos brandisii var. pseudoclethra

Symplocos brandisii var. pseudoclethra, commonly known as the Indian clethra, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
