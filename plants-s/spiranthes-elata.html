Spiranthes elata

Spiranthes elata, commonly known as the tall lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spiral-like arrangements of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and wet meadows. It has a tuberous root system and produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and moths.

Spiranthes elata is a perennial herb that plays a role in wetland ecosystems and is often found in areas with rich, organic soil.
