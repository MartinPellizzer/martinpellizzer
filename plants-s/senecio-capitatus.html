Senecio capitatus

Senecio capitatus, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a single, terminal head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Senecio capitatus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
