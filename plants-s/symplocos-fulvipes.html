Symplocos fulvipes

Symplocos fulvipes, commonly known as the yellow-flowered evergreen, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
