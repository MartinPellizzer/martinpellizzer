Solanum trizygum

Solanum trizygum, commonly known as the purple leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple foliage and small, showy flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, and they contain toxic compounds such as solanine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
