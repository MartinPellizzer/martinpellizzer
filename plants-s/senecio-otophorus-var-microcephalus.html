Senecio otophorus var. microcephalus

Senecio otophorus var. microcephalus, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
