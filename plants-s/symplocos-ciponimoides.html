Symplocos ciponimoides

Symplocos ciponimoides, commonly known as the Indian snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
