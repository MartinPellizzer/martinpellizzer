Solenidiopsis tigroides

Solenidiopsis tigroides, commonly known as the tiger milk mushroom, is a species of fungus that belongs to the family Tricholomataceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sticky, yellowish-orange cap that resembles milk droplets, hence its common name. The gills are initially white but turn pale yellow as the mushroom matures. It typically grows in association with oak and other broadleaf trees in temperate regions.

This edible mushroom is prized for its mild flavor and is often used in various culinary dishes.
