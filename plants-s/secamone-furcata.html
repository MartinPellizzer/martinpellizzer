Secamone furcata

Secamone furcata, commonly known as the African sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems. The plant produces small, fragrant orchid-like flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
