Sinapis serrata

Sinapis serrata, commonly known as the serrated mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Sinapis serrata is known for its potential as a forage crop and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
