Sabina corneyana

Sabina corneyana, commonly known as the Mexican juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family, which includes cypress and juniper species. The plant has scale-like leaves that are closely packed, giving it a dense, compact appearance. Its small, spherical blue-gray berries are produced in clusters and are an important food source for birds.

Sabina corneyana is adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
