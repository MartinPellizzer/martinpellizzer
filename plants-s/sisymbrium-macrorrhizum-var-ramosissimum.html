Sisymbrium macrorrhizum var. ramosissimum

Sisymbrium macrorrhizum var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods and dense, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It has small white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The variety ramosissimum is distinguished by its more branched and hairy stems compared to the typical form.
