Sasa iwamiana

Sasa iwamiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental plants. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, providing a delicate appearance. Sasa iwamiana thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
