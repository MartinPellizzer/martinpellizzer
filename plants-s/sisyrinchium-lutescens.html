Sisyrinchium lutescens

Sisyrinchium lutescens, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals arranged in a globular shape, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
