Sinocalamus flagellifer

Sinocalamus flagellifer, commonly known as the long-branch bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and long, narrow leaves. This bamboo species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its culms are flexible and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
