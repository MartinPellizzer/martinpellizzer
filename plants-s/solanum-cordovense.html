Solanum cordovense

Solanum cordovense, commonly known as the red velvet tamarillo, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor. Its leaves are thick and waxy, with a deep green color, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Solanum cordovense is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
