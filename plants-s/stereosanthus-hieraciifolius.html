Stereosanthus hieraciifolius

Stereosanthus hieraciifolius, commonly known as the hairy leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

This species is native to parts of Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
