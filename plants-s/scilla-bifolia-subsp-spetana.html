Scilla bifolia subsp. spetana

Scilla bifolia subsp.

spetana, commonly known as the Spanish squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet, bell-shaped flowers in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its small, spherical bulbs and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal. Scilla bifolia subsp.

spetana is typically grown in gardens for its striking floral display.
