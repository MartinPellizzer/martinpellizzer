Setaria glauca var. macrocarpa

Setaria glauca var. macrocarpa, commonly known as bluegrass dropseed, is a native grass species found in the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, beige to brown seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
