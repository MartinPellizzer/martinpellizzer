Stevia fiebrigii var. jujuiensis

Stevia fiebrigii var. jujuiensis, commonly known as the Jujuy stevia, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it is gaining interest as a sustainable alternative to artificial sweeteners.
