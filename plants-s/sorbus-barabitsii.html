Sorbus barabitsii

Sorbus barabitsii, commonly known as the Transcaucasian whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region, including parts of Georgia, Armenia, and Azerbaijan. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Sorbus barabitsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
