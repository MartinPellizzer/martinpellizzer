Schefflera quadripetala

Schefflera quadripetala, commonly known as the four-petaled umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, umbrella-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets arranged in a whorled pattern, and it produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Schefflera quadripetala is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate partial shade.
