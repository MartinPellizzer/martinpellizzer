Solanum cyanophyllum

Solanum cyanophyllum, commonly known as the blueberry nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are toxic to humans but are used in traditional medicine by some indigenous groups. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
