Sasa tatewakiana var. glabrifolia

Sasa tatewakiana var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved bamboo, is a variant of the sasa bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and construction. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture, distinguishing it from other sasa varieties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
