Sasa praeclusa

Sasa praeclusa, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating.

Sasa praeclusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
