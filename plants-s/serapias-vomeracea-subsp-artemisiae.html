Serapias vomeracea subsp. artemisiae

Serapias vomeracea subsp.

artemisiae, commonly known as the Artemisia-like orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred orchid-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive lip.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
