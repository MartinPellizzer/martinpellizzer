Swartzia calva

Swartzia calva, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the rainy season. The tree has a straight trunk and can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a dense canopy of dark green leaves. Its bark is smooth and light-colored, and it produces small, round fruits that split open to release seeds.

Swartzia calva is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks for its striking floral display.
