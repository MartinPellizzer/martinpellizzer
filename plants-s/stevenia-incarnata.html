Stevenia incarnata

Stevenia incarnata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant produces cone-shaped seed heads that persist through winter, adding visual interest to the garden. Stevenia incarnata prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to native plant gardens.

Its foliage is typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture, enhancing its ornamental value.
