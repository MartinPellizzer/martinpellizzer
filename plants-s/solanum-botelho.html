Solanum botelho

Solanum botelho, commonly known as the Botelho nightshade, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom in the evening.

Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer and is edible when fully mature.
