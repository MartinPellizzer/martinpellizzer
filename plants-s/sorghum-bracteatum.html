Sorghum bracteatum

Sorghum bracteatum, commonly known as the purple top millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple bracts that surround the inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in dry, sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Sorghum bracteatum is often cultivated for forage, ornamental purposes, and as a source of grain in some regions.
