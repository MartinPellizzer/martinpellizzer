Senecio lividovulgaris

Senecio lividovulgaris, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
