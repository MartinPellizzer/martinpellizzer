Spicanta fendleri

Spicanta fendleri, commonly known as the Desert Evening Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has succulent leaves and stems, which help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Spicanta fendleri is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with low rainfall.
