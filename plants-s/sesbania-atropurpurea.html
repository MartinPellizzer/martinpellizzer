Sesbania atropurpurea

Sesbania atropurpurea, commonly known as purple sesbania, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its striking purple flowers and trifoliate leaves. It grows as an annual herb or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

Sesbania atropurpurea is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural and ecological contexts.
