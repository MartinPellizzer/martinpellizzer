Spiraea salicifolia var. regeliana

Spiraea salicifolia var. regeliana, commonly known as the Regel's weeping willow spirea, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, cascading clusters of small, white flowers.

It features narrow, willow-like leaves that are typically dark green and emit a subtle, fresh fragrance. The plant produces elongated, drooping inflorescences that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is often used in gardens for its elegant, weeping form and early spring blooms.
