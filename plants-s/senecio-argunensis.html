Senecio argunensis

Senecio argunensis, commonly known as the Argun Mountain groundsell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters.

Senecio argunensis is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
