Silene fimbriata

Silene fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Silene fimbriata is an important species for pollinators and is often found in early successional habitats.
