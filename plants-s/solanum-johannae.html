Solanum johannae

Solanum johannae, commonly known as the Johann's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and leaves with a waxy, leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by violet or purple berries. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native habitat.

Solanum johannae is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
