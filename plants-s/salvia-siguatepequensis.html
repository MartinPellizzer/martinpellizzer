Salvia siguatepequensis

Salvia siguatepequensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian sage, is a perennial shrub native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are hairy and woody.

Salvia siguatepequensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
