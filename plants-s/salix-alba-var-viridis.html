Salix alba var. viridis

Salix alba var. viridis, commonly known as the green willow, is a variant of the white willow that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers and streams.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
