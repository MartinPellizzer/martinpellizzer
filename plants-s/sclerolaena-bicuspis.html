Sclerolaena bicuspis

Sclerolaena bicuspis, commonly known as the two-toothed screw-tongue, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are often hidden among the foliage. Its fruits are dry, three-angled capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Sclerolaena bicuspis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
