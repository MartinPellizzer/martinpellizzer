Stachys spathulata

Stachys spathulata, commonly known as broadleaf betony, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this herbaceous perennial thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
