Senecio tanzaniensis

Senecio tanzaniensis, commonly known as the Tanzanian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
