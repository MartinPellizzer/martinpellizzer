Salvia sucrensis

Salvia sucrensis, commonly known as the Sucrensis sage, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces vibrant purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a minty scent, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Salvia sucrensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
