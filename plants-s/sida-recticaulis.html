Sida recticaulis

Sida recticaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the wet season.

Sida recticaulis is characterized by its fibrous stems and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
