Stemona aphylla

Stemona aphylla, commonly known as the stemona without leaves, is a unique plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, which distinguishes it from other members of the Stemona genus. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and medicinal value, it is considered an endangered species in its natural habitat.
