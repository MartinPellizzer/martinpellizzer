Sycomorus dahro

Sycomorus dahro, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to parts of Africa.

It is a large, deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, lobed leaves. The tree produces small, greenish flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its roots are deep and strong, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.

Sycomorus dahro is often used in reforestation projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide shade.
