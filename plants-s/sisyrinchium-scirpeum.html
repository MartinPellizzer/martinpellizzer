Sisyrinchium scirpeum

Sisyrinchium scirpeum, commonly known as the marsh blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sisyrinchium scirpeum plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in wetland ecosystems.
