Scleria motleyi subsp. rostrata

Scleria motleyi subsp.

rostrata, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale blue flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life. Scleria motleyi subsp.

rostrata is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
