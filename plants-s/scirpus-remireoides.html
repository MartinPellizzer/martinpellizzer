Scirpus remireoides

Scirpus remireoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers that develop into brown, cylindrical seed heads.

Scirpus remireoides plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
