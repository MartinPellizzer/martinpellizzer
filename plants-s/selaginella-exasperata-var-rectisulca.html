Selaginella exasperata var. rectisulca

Selaginella exasperata var. rectisulca, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along its stems. The plant produces strobila, which are cone-like structures that contain the sporangia for spore production. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

This variety is distinguished by its narrow, elongated leaf ribs, which give it its specific epithet "rectisulca.".
