Scaphochlamys rubescens

Scaphochlamys rubescens, commonly known as the red-winged orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant red and green floral structure. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, showy orchid-like flower that has a distinctive, winged lip. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific humidity and light requirements.
