Silene colorata var. crassifolia

Silene colorata var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its stems are often sprawling or ascending, and the flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Silene colorata var. crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
