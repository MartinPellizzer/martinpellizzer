Salacia fimbrisepala

Salacia fimbrisepala, commonly known as the hairy-lobed salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to other members of the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves with lobed margins and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitats.
