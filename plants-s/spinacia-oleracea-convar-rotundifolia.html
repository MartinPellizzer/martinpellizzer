Spinacia oleracea convar. rotundifolia

Spinacia oleracea convar. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf spinach, is a botanical variant of the common spinach.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are typically smooth and slightly waxy in texture. This variety is often cultivated for its tender foliage, which is prized for its mild flavor and nutritional value. The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both home gardens and commercial agriculture.
