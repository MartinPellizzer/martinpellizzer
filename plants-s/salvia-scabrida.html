Salvia scabrida

Salvia scabrida, commonly known as rough sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and spikes of purple to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Salvia scabrida also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
