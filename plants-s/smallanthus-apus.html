Smallanthus apus

Smallanthus apus, commonly known as the Andean mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and climbing.

Smallanthus apus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms.
