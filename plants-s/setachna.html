Setachna

Setachna, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray florets and a central disk of yellow flowers. Setachna thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and naturalized landscapes.
