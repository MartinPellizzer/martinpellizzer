Schefflera rubiginosa

Schefflera rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-veined schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound, palmate leaves that are typically dark green with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor decoration.
