Senna aversiflora

Senna aversiflora, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

Senna aversiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
