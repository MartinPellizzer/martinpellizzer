Scorzonera gracilis

Scorzonera gracilis, commonly known as slender scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a slender, hairy stem that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Scorzonera gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
