Sloanea sterculiacea

Sloanea sterculiacea, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its tonal qualities in musical instruments. The plant has elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red berries.

Sloanea sterculiacea is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a rare and sought-after species.
