Scirpus trispiculatus

Scirpus trispiculatus, commonly known as the three-spined bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of green to brown. Its root system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus trispiculatus plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
