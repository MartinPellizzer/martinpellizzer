Spodiopogon tainanensis var. takeoi

Spodiopogon tainanensis var. takeoi, commonly known as the Tainan grass, is a perennial herb native to southern China and Taiwan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it thrive in shaded or moist environments.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
