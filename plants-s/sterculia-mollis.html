Sterculia mollis

Sterculia mollis, commonly known as the silky apple or silky tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.
