Syngonanthus lagopodioides f. minor

Syngonanthus lagopodioides f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf rabbit's foot fern, is a compact, low-growing fern species native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, hairy, grass-like leaves that resemble rabbit feet, giving it its common name. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

This fern is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
