Scirpus erectus var. wallichii

Scirpus erectus var. wallichii, commonly known as the tall bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant characterized by its tall, slender, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

It is native to regions of Asia, including India and China, and thrives in marshy or aquatic environments. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
