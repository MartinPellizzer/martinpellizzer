Schizostachyum lutescens

Schizostachyum lutescens, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and golden-yellow sheaths. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Schizostachyum lutescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional basketry and thatching.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a valuable resource in reforestation and erosion control efforts.
