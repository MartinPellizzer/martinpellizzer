Senecio bicolor

Senecio bicolor, commonly known as the Blazing Star, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescence that resembles a burning flame. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Senecio bicolor is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
