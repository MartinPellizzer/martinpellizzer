Stevia yaconensis var. saltensis

Stevia yaconensis var. saltensis, commonly known as salt marsh stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It thrives in saline and coastal environments, exhibiting a robust root system adapted to high-sodium soils. The plant produces small, white flowers and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly sweet aroma. Its leaves contain steviol glycosides, making it a natural sweetener.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and its potential in sustainable agriculture.
