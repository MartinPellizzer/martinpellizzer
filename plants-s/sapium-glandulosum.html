Sapium glandulosum

Sapium glandulosum, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves with glandular dots along the veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkin-like inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots can form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
