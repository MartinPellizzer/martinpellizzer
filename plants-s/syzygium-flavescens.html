Syzygium flavescens

Syzygium flavescens, commonly known as the yellow syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
