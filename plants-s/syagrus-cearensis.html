Syagrus cearensis

Syagrus cearensis, commonly known as the Cearense palm, is a species of palm tree native to the northeastern regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that form a broad crown. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Syagrus cearensis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
