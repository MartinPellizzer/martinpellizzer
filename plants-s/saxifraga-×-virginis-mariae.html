Saxifraga × virginis-mariae

Saxifraga × virginis-mariae, commonly known as the Virgin's Mary saxifrage, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and clustered flowers. The plant typically produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
