Sempervivum × barbulatum var. delasoieii

Sempervivum × barbulatum var. delasoieii, commonly known as the Delasoie's houseleek, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features compact rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues, especially under sunlight. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, leafless stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, alpine conditions.
