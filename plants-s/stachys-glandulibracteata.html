Stachys glandulibracteata

Stachys glandulibracteata, commonly known as sticky betony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with glandular dots. The plant produces clusters of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticultural settings.
