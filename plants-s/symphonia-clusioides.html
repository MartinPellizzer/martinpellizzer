Symphonia clusioides

Symphonia clusioides, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
