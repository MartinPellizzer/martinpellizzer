Siphocampylus onagrius

Siphocampylus onagrius, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The seeds are enclosed in a capsule and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in pollination and seed dispersal.
