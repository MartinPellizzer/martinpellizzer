Sycopsis laurifolia

Sycopsis laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel-leaf sycopsis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically leathery and have a glossy appearance. Sycopsis laurifolia produces small, clustered white flowers that are often followed by dark, spherical berries.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative shrub.
