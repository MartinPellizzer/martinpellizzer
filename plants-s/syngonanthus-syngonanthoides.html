Syngonanthus syngonanthoides

Syngonanthus syngonanthoides, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its foliage is often velvety and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture.

Syngonanthus syngonanthoides is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
