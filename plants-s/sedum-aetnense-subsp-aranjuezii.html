Sedum aetnense subsp. aranjuezii

Sedum aetnense subsp.

aranjuezii, commonly known as the Aranjuez sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white.

The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xerophytic environments. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and may develop a powdery coating to reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
