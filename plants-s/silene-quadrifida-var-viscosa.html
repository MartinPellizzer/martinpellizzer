Silene quadrifida var. viscosa

Silene quadrifida var. viscosa, commonly known as sticky catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Silene quadrifida var. viscosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
