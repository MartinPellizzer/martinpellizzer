Sutera atropurpurea

Sutera atropurpurea, commonly known as purple evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Onagraceae and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
