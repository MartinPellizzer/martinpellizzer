Silene pratensis proles divaricata

Silene pratensis proles divaricata, commonly known as the broad-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

Silene pratensis proles divaricata is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of wet grasslands.
