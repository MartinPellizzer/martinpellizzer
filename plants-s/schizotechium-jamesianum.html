Schizotechium jamesianum

Schizotechium jamesianum, commonly known as the Jamesian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

Schizotechium jamesianum plays a significant ecological role in its habitat by influencing host tree health and supporting avian populations.
