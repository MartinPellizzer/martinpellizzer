Scilla lusitanica

Scilla lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scilla lusitanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
