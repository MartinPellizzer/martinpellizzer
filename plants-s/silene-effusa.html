Silene effusa

Silene effusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched.

Silene effusa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
