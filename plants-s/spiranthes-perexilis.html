Spiranthes perexilis

Spiranthes perexilis, commonly known as the slender spiral orchid, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flower spikes that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as wetlands and forest floors.

Spiranthes perexilis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and ecological significance.
