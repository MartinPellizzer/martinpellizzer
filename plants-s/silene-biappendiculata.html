Silene biappendiculata

Silene biappendiculata, commonly known as the two-appendaged catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with succulent stems and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Silene biappendiculata is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
