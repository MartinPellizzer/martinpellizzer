Solanum hindsianum

Solanum hindsianum, commonly known as the Hindsian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it is typically avoided by livestock and humans.
