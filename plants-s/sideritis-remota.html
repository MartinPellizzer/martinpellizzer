Sideritis remota

Sideritis remota, commonly known as the remote sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Sideritis remota is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
