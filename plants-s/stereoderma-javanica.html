Stereoderma javanica

Stereoderma javanica, commonly known as the Javanese spiky scale, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, and branched structure, which resembles a small tree or shrub. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered with a waxy coating.

Stereoderma javanica is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
