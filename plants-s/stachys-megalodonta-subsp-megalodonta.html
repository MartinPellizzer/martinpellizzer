Stachys megalodonta subsp. megalodonta

Stachys megalodonta subsp.

megalodonta, commonly known as giant horse mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of purple or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and have a strong, minty aroma, which is often used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to well-drained soils.
