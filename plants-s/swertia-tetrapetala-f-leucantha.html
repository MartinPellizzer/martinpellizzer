Swertia tetrapetala f. leucantha

Swertia tetrapetala f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
