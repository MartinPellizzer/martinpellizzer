Senecio fuertesii

Senecio fuertesii, commonly known as the Fuertes' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
