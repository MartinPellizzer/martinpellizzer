Stachys zuvandica

Stachys zuvandica, commonly known as the Zuvandica hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
