Stipa arundinacea

Stipa arundinacea, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and panicle-like inflorescence composed of numerous spikelets. The plant is perennial, often forming dense clumps that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough, hairy texture.

Stipa arundinacea is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and disturbed areas.
