Scirpus thomsonii

Scirpus thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets at the top of the stems, which are typically green to reddish in color. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil in marshy environments.

Thomson's bulrush is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and lakes.
