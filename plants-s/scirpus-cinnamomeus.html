Scirpus cinnamomeus

Scirpus cinnamomeus, commonly known as cinnamon grass, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a reddish or cinnamon-colored hue, giving it its common name.

Scirpus cinnamomeus thrives in moist or waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
