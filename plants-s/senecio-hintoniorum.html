Senecio hintoniorum

Senecio hintoniorum, commonly known as Hinton's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in shades of yellow and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
