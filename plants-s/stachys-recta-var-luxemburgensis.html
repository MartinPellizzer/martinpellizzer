Stachys recta var. luxemburgensis

Stachys recta var. luxemburgensis, commonly known as the Luxembourg betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features upright, square stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, two-lipped blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often hairy on the upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Stachys recta var. luxemburgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
