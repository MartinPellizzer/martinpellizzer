Sabal texana

Sabal texana, commonly known as the Texas needle-leaf palm, is a hardy, cold-tolerant plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade. Its resilience makes it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal and urban environments.

The palm is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh weather conditions.
