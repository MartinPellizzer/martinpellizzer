Swartzia katawa

Swartzia katawa, commonly known as the Natal ironwood, is a tropical tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and montane forests.

The tree is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
