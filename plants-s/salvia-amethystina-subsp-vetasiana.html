Salvia amethystina subsp. vetasiana

Salvia amethystina subsp.

vetasiana, commonly known as the violet salvia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
