Solanum achorum

Solanum achorum, commonly known as the mountain nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that mature in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum achorum is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous and alpine environments.
