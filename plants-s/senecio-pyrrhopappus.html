Senecio pyrrhopappus

Senecio pyrrhopappus, commonly known as the redleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow and are arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio pyrrhopappus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and ease of care.
