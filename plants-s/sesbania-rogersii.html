Sesbania rogersii

Sesbania rogersii, commonly known as the white sesban, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings. It produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Sesbania rogersii is often used as a forage crop and in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and environmental benefits.
