Symplocos ophirensis var. cumingiana

Symplocos ophirensis var. cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's waxleaf.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
