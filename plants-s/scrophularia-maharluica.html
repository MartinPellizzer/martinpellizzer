Scrophularia maharluica

Scrophularia maharluica, commonly known as the Maharluica figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
