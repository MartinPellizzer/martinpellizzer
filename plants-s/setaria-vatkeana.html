Setaria vatkeana

Setaria vatkeana, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed florets.

Setaria vatkeana is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
