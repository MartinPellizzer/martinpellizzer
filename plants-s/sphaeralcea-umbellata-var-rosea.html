Sphaeralcea umbellata var. rosea

Sphaeralcea umbellata var. rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
