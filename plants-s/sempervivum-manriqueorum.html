Sempervivum manriqueorum

Sempervivum manriqueorum, commonly known as the Manriquean houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its rosettes of fleshy, hairy leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Sempervivum manriqueorum is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens and alpine settings.
