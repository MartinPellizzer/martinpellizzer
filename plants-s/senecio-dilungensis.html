Senecio dilungensis

Senecio dilungensis, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, daisy-like flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing both yellow disk flowers and white to pale blue ray flowers.

Senecio dilungensis is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
