Sporobolus bequaertii

Sporobolus bequaertii, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its deep root system allows it to survive in drought conditions, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.

Sporobolus bequaertii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
