Satureja alpina var. pyrenaea

Satureja alpina var. pyrenaea, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary applications. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal remedies.
