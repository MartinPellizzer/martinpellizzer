Sinapis cheiranthos var. rectangularis

Sinapis cheiranthos var. rectangularis, commonly known as the broadleaf mustard, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are broad and lobed, with a rough, hairy texture, and upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are typically black or dark brown in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

The species is valued in some regions for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
