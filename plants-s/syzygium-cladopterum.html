Syzygium cladopterum

Syzygium cladopterum, commonly known as the woolly apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe that is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
