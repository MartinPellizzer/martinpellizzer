Salvia novoleontis

Salvia novoleontis, commonly known as the Leontes sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to blue flowers in whorls along the upper stem, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Salvia novoleontis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
