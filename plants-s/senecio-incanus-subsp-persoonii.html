Senecio incanus subsp. persoonii

Senecio incanus subsp.

persoonii, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
