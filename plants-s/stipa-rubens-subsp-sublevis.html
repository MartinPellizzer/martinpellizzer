Stipa rubens subsp. sublevis

Stipa rubens subsp.

sublevis, commonly known as the slender redgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, cylindrical spikelets that are typically reddish in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
