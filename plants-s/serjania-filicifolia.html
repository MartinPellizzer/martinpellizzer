Serjania filicifolia

Serjania filicifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved serjania, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble ferns, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is typically found in humid tropical forests and thrives in shady, moist environments.

Serjania filicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
