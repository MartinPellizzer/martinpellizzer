Schoenus articulatus

Schoenus articulatus, commonly known as the jointed grass or marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often have distinct jointed nodes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clumps.

Schoenus articulatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
