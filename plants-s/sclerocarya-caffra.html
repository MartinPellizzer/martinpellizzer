Sclerocarya caffra

Sclerocarya caffra, commonly known as the African hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and clusters of small, pale green leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by hard, woody fruits containing edible nuts. The nuts are rich in oils and have been traditionally used for food and medicinal purposes.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
