Satureja punctata subsp. ovata

Satureja punctata subsp.

ovata, commonly known as roundleaf mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a seasoning due to their pungent, minty aroma.

The subspecies ovata is distinguished by its more rounded leaves compared to other forms of Satureja punctata.
