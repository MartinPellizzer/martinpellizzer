Stevia fiebrigii var. typica

Stevia fiebrigii var. typica, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant is renowned for its intense sweetness, derived from compounds called steviol glycosides, which are found in the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal use.

Stevia fiebrigii var. typica is also valued for its low-calorie sweetening properties and traditional use in herbal medicine.
