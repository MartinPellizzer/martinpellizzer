Saphesia

Saphesia, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Acoraceae.

It is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and flowering spikes that produce small, white or greenish flowers. The plant is native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America and parts of Asia. Saphesia has a rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in waterlogged soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
