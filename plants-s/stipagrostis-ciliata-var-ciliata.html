Stipagrostis ciliata var. ciliata

Stipagrostis ciliata var. ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in compact clumps and has narrow, linear leaves that are often rolled or folded. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged.

This grass is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
