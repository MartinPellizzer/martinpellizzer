Swertia abyssinica

Swertia abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian gentian, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Swertia abyssinica is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its traditional medicinal uses.
