Sidalcea hickmanii subsp. hickmanii

Sidalcea hickmanii subsp.

hickmanii, commonly known as Hickman's soapwort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. It has a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture in dry soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
