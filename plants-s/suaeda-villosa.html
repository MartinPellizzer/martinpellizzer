Suaeda villosa

Suaeda villosa, commonly known as the silky sea-blite, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments, and possesses specialized adaptations to retain water and tolerate high salt concentrations. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves covered in fine, silky hairs that reduce water loss and reflect sunlight. Its small, yellowish flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Suaeda villosa plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
