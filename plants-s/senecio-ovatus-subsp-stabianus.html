Senecio ovatus subsp. stabianus

Senecio ovatus subsp.

stabianus, commonly known as the Stabian's owl's eye, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange daisy-like flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
