Solanum villosum

Solanum villosum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Solanum villosum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
