Senecio magnificus

Senecio magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy composite flowers.

It is native to South Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowering stems bear clusters of yellow daisy-like blooms, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio magnificus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
