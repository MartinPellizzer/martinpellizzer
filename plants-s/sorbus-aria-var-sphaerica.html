Sorbus aria var. sphaerica

Sorbus aria var. sphaerica, commonly known as the globular rowan, is a variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its spherical, compact growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are a food source for birds. Its leaves are typically oval and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is often found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.
