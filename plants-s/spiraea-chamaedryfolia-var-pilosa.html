Spiraea chamaedryfolia var. pilosa

Spiraea chamaedryfolia var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy wood spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in early summer. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
