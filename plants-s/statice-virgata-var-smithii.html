Statice virgata var. smithii

Statice virgata var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's glasswort, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in saline and sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
