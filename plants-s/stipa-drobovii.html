Stipa drobovii

Stipa drobovii, commonly known as the Drobovii needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to temperate climates.

Stipa drobovii is often found in regions with well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
