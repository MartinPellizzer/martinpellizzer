Scirpus komarovii

Scirpus komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in wetland environments.

Scirpus komarovii is adapted to moist to aquatic habitats, making it a common species in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
