Solanum megistacrolobum

Solanum megistacrolobum, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers and toxic foliage. The plant produces berries that are bright red when ripe, though they are poisonous to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxicity, it is typically handled with care in horticultural and botanical settings.
