Scorzonera jacquiniana

Scorzonera jacquiniana, commonly known as the Jacob's root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stem and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, which bloom in the spring. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scorzonera jacquiniana thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, moist conditions.
