Scirpus seoanei

Scirpus seoanei, commonly known as the Seoane's bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in marshy or aquatic environments, often forming dense stands that stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help in nutrient absorption from water.

Scirpus seoanei plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and carbon sequestration.
