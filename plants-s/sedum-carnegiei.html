Sedum carnegiei

Sedum carnegiei, commonly known as the Carnegie stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a dense, mat-like growth. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum carnegiei is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid environments.
