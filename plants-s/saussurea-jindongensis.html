Saussurea jindongensis

Saussurea jindongensis, commonly known as the Jindong safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy composite flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
