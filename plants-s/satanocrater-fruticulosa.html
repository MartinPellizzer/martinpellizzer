Satanocrater fruticulosa

Satanocrater fruticulosa, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking, red-orange flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically small and spiny, providing some protection from herbivores.

Satanocrater fruticulosa is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and visually distinctive plant.
