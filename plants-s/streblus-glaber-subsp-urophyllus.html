Streblus glaber subsp. urophyllus

Streblus glaber subsp.

urophyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf strangler fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are typically consumed by various animals.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb and envelop other trees make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
