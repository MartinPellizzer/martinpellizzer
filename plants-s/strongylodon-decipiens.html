Strongylodon decipiens

Strongylodon decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive beauty or the false orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that resemble those of other orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, slender leaves and a unique inflorescence that can produce multiple blooms.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is relatively rare and requires specific conditions to thrive.
