Schlegelia cornuta

Schlegelia cornuta, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated floral spurs that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance.

Schlegelia cornuta is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
