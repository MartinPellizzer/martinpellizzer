Sasa ontakensis

Sasa ontakensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green coloration.

Sasa ontakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
