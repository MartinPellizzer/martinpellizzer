Scorzonera runcinata

Scorzonera runcinata, commonly known as the Italian salsify, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant has lobed, hairy leaves and produces a long, cylindrical root that is often used in cooking. Scorzonera runcinata prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
