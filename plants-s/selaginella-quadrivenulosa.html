Selaginella quadrivenulosa

Selaginella quadrivenulosa, commonly known as the four-veined spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its slender, branching stems and tiny, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are clustered at the tips of the stems and produce spores.

Selaginella quadrivenulosa is also notable for its ability to survive in adverse conditions, showcasing resilience through its adaptive features.
