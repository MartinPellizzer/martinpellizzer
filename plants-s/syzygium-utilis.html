Syzygium utilis

Syzygium utilis, commonly known as the rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy, spiny fruit that turns red when mature. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is cultivated for its edible, sweet-tart fruit. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and thrives in humid, warm climates.
