Sapindus stenopterus

Sapindus stenopterus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces fruit that resembles a soapberry, which contains a saponin-rich pulp used in traditional soap-making. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
