Suregada meliocarpa

Suregada meliocarpa, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and capsule-like fruits that contain winged seeds. The plant thrives in saline or brackish water environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough, hairy texture.

Suregada meliocarpa plays a role in coastal stabilization and provides habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
