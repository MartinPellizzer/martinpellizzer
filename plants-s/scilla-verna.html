Scilla verna

Scilla verna, commonly known as the spring scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Scilla verna is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable conditions.
