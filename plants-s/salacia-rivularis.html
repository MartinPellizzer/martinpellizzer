Salacia rivularis

Salacia rivularis, commonly known as the river salacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Salacia rivularis is typically found near water sources, thriving in moist, shaded environments.
