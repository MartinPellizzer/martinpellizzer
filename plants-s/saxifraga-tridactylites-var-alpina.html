Saxifraga tridactylites var. alpina

Saxifraga tridactylites var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features rosettes of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five pink or red petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.
