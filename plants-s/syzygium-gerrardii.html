Syzygium gerrardii

Syzygium gerrardii, commonly known as the black bean tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces elliptical, woody pods that contain flat, black seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
