Senecio antucensis var. lanuginosus

Senecio antucensis var. lanuginosus, commonly known as the lanuginous groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats. It is a perennial herb that produces small, daisy-like blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
