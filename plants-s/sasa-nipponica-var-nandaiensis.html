Sasa nipponica var. nandaiensis

Sasa nipponica var. nandaiensis, commonly known as the Nandaiensis bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
