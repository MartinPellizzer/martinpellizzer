Stylophorum × crugianum

Stylophorum × crugianum, commonly known as the hybrid golden saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Stylophorum × crugianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
