Stipa pennata subsp. joannis

Stipa pennata subsp.

joannis, commonly known as the narrowleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, awned spikelets. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and sustainable land management practices.
