Spilanthes urens var. nervosa

Spilanthes urens var. nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf toothache plant, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branched stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It is known for its strong, pungent odor and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported analgesic properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
