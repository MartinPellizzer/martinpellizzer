Stipa styriaca var. melzeri

Stipa styriaca var. melzeri, commonly known as the Melzer's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, silver-gray inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
