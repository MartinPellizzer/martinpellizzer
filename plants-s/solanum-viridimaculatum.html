Solanum viridimaculatum

Solanum viridimaculatum, commonly known as the green-spotted nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually green with distinctive yellow or green spots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically green when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Its unique appearance and toxic properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
