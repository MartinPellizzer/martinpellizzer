Steyerbromelia nukakii

Steyerbromelia nukakii, commonly known as the Nukak bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding structure. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in the humid, tropical environment.

Steyerbromelia nukakii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
