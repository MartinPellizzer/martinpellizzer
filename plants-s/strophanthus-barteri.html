Strophanthus barteri

Strophanthus barteri, commonly known as the red milk-vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and woody stems, which often have a milky sap. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, red aril. Strophanthus barteri is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
