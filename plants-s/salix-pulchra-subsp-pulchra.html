Salix pulchra subsp. pulchra

Salix pulchra subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the Sierra willow, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and riparian zones. It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are important for pollination by wind.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing streambanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
