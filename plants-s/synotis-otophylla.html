Synotis otophylla

Synotis otophylla, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower that resembles a small crown. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in the undergrowth of rainforests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Synotis otophylla is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
