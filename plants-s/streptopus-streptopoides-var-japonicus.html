Streptopus streptopoides var. japonicus

Streptopus streptopoides var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese starwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a cluster at the stem's tip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae (carrot) family.
