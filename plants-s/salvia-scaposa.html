Salvia scaposa

Salvia scaposa, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow lance-shaped leaves, and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia scaposa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
