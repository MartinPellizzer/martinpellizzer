Sanguisorba applanata

Sanguisorba applanata, commonly known as the flatleaf sorrel, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, which include astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.

Sanguisorba applanata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
