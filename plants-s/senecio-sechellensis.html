Senecio sechellensis

Senecio sechellensis, commonly known as the Seychelles groundsel, is an endemic flowering plant native to the Seychelles archipelago.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with numerous small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to the island's unique climate. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Due to its restricted range and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
