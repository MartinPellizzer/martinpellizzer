Schrebera orientalis

Schrebera orientalis, commonly known as the oriental schrebera, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in an alternate pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red or orange berries. Schrebera orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
