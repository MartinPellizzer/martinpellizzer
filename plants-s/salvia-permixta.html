Salvia permixta

Salvia permixta, commonly known as the hybrid sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic quality.

Salvia permixta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
