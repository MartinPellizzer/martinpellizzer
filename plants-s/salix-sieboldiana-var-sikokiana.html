Salix sieboldiana var. sikokiana

Salix sieboldiana var. sikokiana, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches that drape gracefully, creating a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a silvery underside, giving the plant a striking visual effect. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
