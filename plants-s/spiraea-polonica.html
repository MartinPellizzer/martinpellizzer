Spiraea polonica

Spiraea polonica, commonly known as the Polish spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea polonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
