Stemonurus secundiflorus var. secundiflorus

Stemonurus secundiflorus var. secundiflorus, commonly known as the slender stemless bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky streambanks. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
