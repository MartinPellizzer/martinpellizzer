Selaginella schatteburgiana

Selaginella schatteburgiana, commonly known as the Schatteburg's spikemoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a stem that produces upright, spirally arranged leaves, which are often scale-like and closely clustered. It is characterized by its ability to reproduce via spores, a trait common among spore-bearing plants.

This species is native to parts of Africa and is often found in humid, tropical or subtropical regions.
