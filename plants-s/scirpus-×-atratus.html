Scirpus × atratus

Scirpus × atratus, commonly known as the black rush, is a hybrid plant species within the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems, which are often used in floral arrangements and as a decorative element. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Scirpus × atratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
