Siphonanthus triphyllus

Siphonanthus triphyllus, commonly known as the three-leaved honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, trifoliate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its showy, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and bloom in late spring.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.
