Senecio porphyresthes

Senecio porphyresthes, commonly known as the purple-stemmed groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its hairy, purple-stemmed foliage and yellow flowering heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, typical of the daisy family.

Senecio porphyresthes is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
