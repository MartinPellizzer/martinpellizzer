Stipa filiformis

Stipa filiformis, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a species of grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in diameter. The plant produces slender, spikelike inflorescences that are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

Stipa filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
