Scheelea macrocarpa

Scheelea macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The inflorescence consists of a large, branched structure that bears both male and female flowers.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
