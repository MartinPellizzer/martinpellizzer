Stachys dolichodeira

Stachys dolichodeira, commonly known as the long-stemmed hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Stachys dolichodeira is often found in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and stream sides.

Its fibrous roots and upright growth habit make it a resilient plant in suitable conditions.
