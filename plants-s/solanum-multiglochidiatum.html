Solanum multiglochidiatum

Solanum multiglochidiatum, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
