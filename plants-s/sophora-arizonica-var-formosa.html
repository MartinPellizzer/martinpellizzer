Sophora arizonica var. formosa

Sophora arizonica var. formosa, commonly known as the Arizona mountain mahogany, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It produces small, hard pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The plant has historical significance for indigenous peoples and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
