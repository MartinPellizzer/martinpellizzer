Stemmacantha satzyperovii

Stemmacantha satzyperovii, commonly known as the Zambian candlestick plant, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a tall, unbranched stem that resembles a candlestick, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing a defense mechanism against herbivores.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
