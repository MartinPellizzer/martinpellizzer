Stravadium coccineum

Stravadium coccineum, commonly known as the scarlet balm, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
