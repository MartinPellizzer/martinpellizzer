Silene inflata var. major

Silene inflata var. major, commonly known as the giant bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its inflated, bladder-like seed pods are a distinctive botanical feature, aiding in seed dispersal.

Silene inflata var. major thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
