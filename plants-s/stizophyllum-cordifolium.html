Stizophyllum cordifolium

Stizophyllum cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved stizophyllum, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are usually a deep green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
