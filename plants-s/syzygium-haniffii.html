Syzygium haniffii

Syzygium haniffii, commonly known as the Malayan apple or the white apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, which is typically small and yellow or red in color, is edible and has a sweet, slightly tart flavor.

Syzygium haniffii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in local communities.
