Scilla hyacinthoides

Scilla hyacinthoides, commonly known as the windflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland borders.

Scilla hyacinthoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in floral arrangements.
