Stelis wilhelmii

Stelis wilhelmii, commonly known as the Wilhelm's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features a short, erect stem with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding environment, and it thrives in humid, shaded conditions.

Stelis wilhelmii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
