Serruria balanocephala

Serruria balanocephala, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Rhamnaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry environments. It produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by animals.

Serruria balanocephala is often found in rocky outcrops and coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
