Sabina chinensis f. pendula

Sabina chinensis f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese juniper, is a unique variant of the Chinese juniper characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

This evergreen shrub or small tree features scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast against its pendulous form. It is native to China and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its distinctive shape and hardiness.

Its slow growth rate makes it suitable for small spaces and rock gardens.
