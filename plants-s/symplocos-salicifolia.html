Symplocos salicifolia

Symplocos salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in shaded woodlands. The plant features simple, alternate, willow-like leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, fleshy berries.

Symplocos salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
