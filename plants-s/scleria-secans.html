Scleria secans

Scleria secans, commonly known as the slender marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and colonize moist soils effectively. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear small, spike-like clusters of white to pale blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and are arranged in a basal rosette.

Scleria secans plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
