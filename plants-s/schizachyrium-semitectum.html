Schizachyrium semitectum

Schizachyrium semitectum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass species characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and distinctive silver-gray inflorescence.

It is a warm-season grass that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats. The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects. Its foliage turns a striking copper color in the fall, adding visual interest to landscapes.

Schizachyrium semitectum plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and small mammals.
