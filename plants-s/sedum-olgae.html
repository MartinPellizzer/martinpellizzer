Sedum olgae

Sedum olgae, commonly known as the Olgas' stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum olgae is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to prevent soil erosion.
