Salsola prostrata var. flavescens

Salsola prostrata var. flavescens, commonly known as yellow jointweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features prostrate or decumbent stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, typically covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in branched inflorescences.

This plant is adapted to harsh environments, with a succulent structure that helps it store water and withstand drought.
