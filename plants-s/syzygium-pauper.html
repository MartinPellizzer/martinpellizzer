Syzygium pauper

Syzygium pauper, commonly known as the poor syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, round drupe that is typically red when ripe.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
