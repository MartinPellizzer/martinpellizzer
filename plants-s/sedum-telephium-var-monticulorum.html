Sedum telephium var. monticulorum

Sedum telephium var. monticulorum, commonly known as the mountain stonecrop, is a succulent perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and prolonged blooming period.
