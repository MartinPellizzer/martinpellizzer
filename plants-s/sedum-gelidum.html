Sedum gelidum

Sedum gelidum, commonly known as the alpine stonecrop, is a hardy succulent plant native to cold, mountainous regions.

It thrives in rocky and arctic environments, showcasing its ability to survive in harsh climates. The plant has fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to endure long periods of drought. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in late summer.

Sedum gelidum is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
