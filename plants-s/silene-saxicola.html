Silene saxicola

Silene saxicola, commonly known as the rock catchfly, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments in North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Silene saxicola plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and provides nectar for pollinators.
