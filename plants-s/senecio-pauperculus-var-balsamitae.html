Senecio pauperculus var. balsamitae

Senecio pauperculus var. balsamitae, commonly known as the balsamita senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces delicate yellow flowers in the spring, which are arranged in a cluster at the ends of the stems.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
