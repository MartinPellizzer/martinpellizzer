Sasa hiyeiana

Sasa hiyeiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Sasa hiyeiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its ability to form dense, attractive foliage.
