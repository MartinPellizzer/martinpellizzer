Scirpus subcapitatus subsp. celebicus

Scirpus subcapitatus subsp.

celebicus, commonly known as the Celebes sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that consists of numerous small, three-angled spikelets.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
