Silene diurniflora

Silene diurniflora, commonly known as the morning glory of the desert, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday, adapting to the heat of the day. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Silene diurniflora is an important species for pollinators and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
