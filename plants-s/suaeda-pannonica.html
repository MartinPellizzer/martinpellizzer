Suaeda pannonica

Suaeda pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to the Pannonian Basin in Central Europe.

It thrives in saline and arid environments, often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and dry soils. The plant has succulent leaves that store water, and its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.

Suaeda pannonica is also valued for its resilience to extreme environmental conditions.
