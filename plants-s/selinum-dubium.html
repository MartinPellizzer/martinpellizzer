Selinum dubium

Selinum dubium, commonly known as the common fennel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and carrots. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels. It is often cultivated for its licorice-scented foliage and seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Selinum dubium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbalists.
