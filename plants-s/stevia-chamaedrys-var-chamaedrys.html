Stevia chamaedrys var. chamaedrys

Stevia chamaedrys var. chamaedrys, commonly known as creeping sage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of pale blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild sedative properties.

Stevia chamaedrys var. chamaedrys is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
