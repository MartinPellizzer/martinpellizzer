Salix helvetica var. discolor

Salix helvetica var. discolor, commonly known as the white willow.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is typically gray and smooth, with a distinctive white or pale mottling.

It produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination and seed dispersal.
