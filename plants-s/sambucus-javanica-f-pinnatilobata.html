Sambucus javanica f. pinnatilobata

Sambucus javanica f. pinnatilobata, commonly known as the Javan dogwood, is a variant of the native dogwood species found in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinnately lobed leaves, which give it a unique foliage appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
