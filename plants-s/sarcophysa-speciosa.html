Sarcophysa speciosa

Sarcophysa speciosa, commonly known as the showy sarcophysa, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which serve to attract pollinators. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Sarcophysa speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
