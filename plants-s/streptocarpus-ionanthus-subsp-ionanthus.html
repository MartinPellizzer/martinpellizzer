Streptocarpus ionanthus subsp. ionanthus

Streptocarpus ionanthus subsp.

ionanthus, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical Africa. It features fleshy, dark green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender stems and are pollinated by various insects, including bees and butterflies.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
