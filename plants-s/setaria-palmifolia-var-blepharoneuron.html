Setaria palmifolia var. blepharoneuron

Setaria palmifolia var. blepharoneuron, commonly known as the broad-leaf foxtail, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
