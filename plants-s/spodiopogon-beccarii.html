Spodiopogon beccarii

Spodiopogon beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively.

Spodiopogon beccarii plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
