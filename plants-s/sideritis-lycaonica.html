Sideritis lycaonica

Sideritis lycaonica, commonly known as Lycaonian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often found at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Sideritis lycaonica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Greek medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
