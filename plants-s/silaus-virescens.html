Silaus virescens

Silaus virescens, commonly known as the green sprout plant, is a herbaceous species characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant's stems are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its humid habitat. Its leaves are rich in chlorophyll, contributing to its vibrant green coloration.

Silaus virescens plays a role in supporting local insect populations and maintaining soil stability in its native ecosystem.
