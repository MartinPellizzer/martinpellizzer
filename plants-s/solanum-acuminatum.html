Solanum acuminatum

Solanum acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminatum nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum acuminatum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
