Salix ramifusca

Salix ramifusca, commonly known as the red willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and riparian areas. The plant has slender, often reddish stems that are covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are catkins, which are typically yellow-green and appear in early spring.
