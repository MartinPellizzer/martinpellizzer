Stelis dirigens

Stelis dirigens, commonly known as the directed stelis, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees in tropical rainforests, where it derives moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features a short, compact stem with numerous small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Stelis dirigens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
