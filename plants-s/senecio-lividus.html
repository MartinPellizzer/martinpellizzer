Senecio lividus

Senecio lividus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-green, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio lividus is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
