Solanum lignicaule var. longistylum

Solanum lignicaule var. longistylum, commonly known as the long-stemmed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it produces small, toxic berries that are yellow when ripe.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
