Semenovia lasiocarpa subsp. lasiocarpa

Semenovia lasiocarpa subsp.

lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy bracted bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy bracts and bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine, appressed hairs, while the flowers are blue to violet in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
