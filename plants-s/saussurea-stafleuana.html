Saussurea stafleuana

Saussurea stafleuana, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and other mountainous regions in Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root systems. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust adaptation to harsh climatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster, which bloom in the summer months.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
