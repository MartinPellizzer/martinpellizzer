Saussurea sagittifolia

Saussurea sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowhead saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
