Scolymocephalus rupicola

Scolymocephalus rupicola, commonly known as the rock scolymus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss.

Scolymocephalus rupicola is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
