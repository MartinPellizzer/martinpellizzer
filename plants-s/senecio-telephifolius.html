Senecio telephifolius

Senecio telephifolius, commonly known as the "Goat's Beard," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, branched stem and large, feathery leaves that resemble the beard of a goat. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Senecio telephifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
