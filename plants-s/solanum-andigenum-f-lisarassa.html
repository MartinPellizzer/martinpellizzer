Solanum andigenum f. lisarassa

Solanum andigenum f. lisarassa, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a variant of the Andean tomato, native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy, branched stems and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, red berries, which are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Due to its unique genetic traits, it is of interest in botanical research and plant breeding programs.
