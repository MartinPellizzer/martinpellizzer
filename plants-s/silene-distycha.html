Silene distycha

Silene distycha, commonly known as the two-rowed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Silene distycha is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like carnations and moss campion.
