Syzygium rockii

Syzygium rockii, commonly known as the Rock Island apple.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters and are followed by edible, red-orange berries.

Syzygium rockii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and fruit.
