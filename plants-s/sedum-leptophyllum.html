Sedum leptophyllum

Sedum leptophyllum, commonly known as stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum leptophyllum is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion and its low maintenance requirements.
