Sporobolus virginicus var. gaditanus

Sporobolus virginicus var. gaditanus, commonly known as Gulf Coast sand grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and dense, fibrous root system, which helps stabilize sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in coastal dunes and disturbed sandy habitats, tolerating salt spray and drought.

Its ecological importance includes preventing erosion and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
