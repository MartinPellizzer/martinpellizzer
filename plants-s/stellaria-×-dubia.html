Stellaria × dubia

Stellaria × dubia, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or white dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure, and are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
