Senecio paarlensis

Senecio paarlensis, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or ozymandias.

It is a succulent shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange to red berries, which are edible and have a tangy flavor. Senecio paarlensis thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky outcrops or in sandy soils.

It is well adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its succulent structure and efficient water storage capabilities.
