Sibiraea laevigata var. croatica

Sibiraea laevigata var. croatica, commonly known as the Croatian smooth sibiraea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and dark green on top. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
