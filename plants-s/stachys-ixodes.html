Stachys ixodes

Stachys ixodes, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Stachys ixodes is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils, typically in woodland or grassy areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
