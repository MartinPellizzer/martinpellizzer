Salix × seringeana

Salix × seringeana, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a bluish-green hue on top with a white or silvery underside. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
