Scelochilus carinatus

Scelochilus carinatus, commonly known as the ridge-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Scelochilus carinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
