Spiraea schneideriana var. schneideriana

Spiraea schneideriana var. schneideriana, commonly known as the Schneider's weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red or orange autumn foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a compact, rounded form. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a medium to dark green color.

This ornamental plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders or as a specimen plant.
