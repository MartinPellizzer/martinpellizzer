Stylogyne bracteolata

Stylogyne bracteolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed stylogyne, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in swampy or wetland habitats. The plant features slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Stylogyne bracteolata plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
