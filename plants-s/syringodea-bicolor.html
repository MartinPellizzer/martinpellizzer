Syringodea bicolor

Syringodea bicolor, commonly known as the pink and white orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, dichotomous inflorescence with alternating pink and white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with showy petals and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Syringodea bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouse settings.
