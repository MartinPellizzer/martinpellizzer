Sabicea umbellata var. genuina

Sabicea umbellata var. genuina, commonly known as the white-flowered sabicea, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
