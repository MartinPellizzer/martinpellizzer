Sarcomphalus obtusifolius var. canescens

Sarcomphalus obtusifolius var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy-leaved sarcomphalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features upright stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a rounded tip, with a leathery texture and a grayish-green coloration. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
