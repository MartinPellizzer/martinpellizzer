Stachycarpus ferrugineus

Stachycarpus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty stachys, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in harsh, coastal environments.
