Stephanomeria runcinata var. myrioclada

Stephanomeria runcinata var. myrioclada, commonly known as the many-bracted stephanomeria, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a fuzzy texture, and it produces numerous small, bracted flower clusters.

This variety is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain insects.
