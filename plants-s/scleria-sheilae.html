Scleria sheilae

Scleria sheilae, commonly known as the Sheilas' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Scleria sheilae typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
