Spiraeopsis aglaiiformis

Spiraeopsis aglaiiformis, commonly known as the white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its showy clusters of white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, serrated leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Spiraeopsis aglaiiformis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
