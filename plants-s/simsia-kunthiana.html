Simsia kunthiana

Simsia kunthiana, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and gray-green, hairy foliage adapted to conserve water. Simsia kunthiana typically grows in rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptability make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
