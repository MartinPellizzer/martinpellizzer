Sabicea laurentii var. angustifolia

Sabicea laurentii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with multiple branches forming a dense canopy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
