Solidago brachyphylla

Solidago brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have a distinct hairy texture.

The showy yellow inflorescences appear in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
