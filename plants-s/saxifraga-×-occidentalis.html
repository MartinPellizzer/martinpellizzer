Saxifraga × occidentalis

Saxifraga × occidentalis, commonly known as the Western saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Saxifraga × occidentalis is well adapted to cold climates and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
