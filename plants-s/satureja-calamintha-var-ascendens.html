Satureja calamintha var. ascendens

Satureja calamintha var. ascendens, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of pale purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its pungent, peppery aroma.

The variety ascendens is distinguished by its more upright growth habit compared to other varieties of satureja.
