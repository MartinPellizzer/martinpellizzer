Salix × furcata

Salix × furcata, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a distinctive drooping appearance. The plant produces catkins as its primary flowering structure, with separate male and female flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a serrated margin.

The weeping willow is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
