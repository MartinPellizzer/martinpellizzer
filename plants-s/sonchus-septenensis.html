Sonchus septenensis

Sonchus septenensis, commonly known as the seven-angled sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are usually heart-shaped with a serrated margin. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is an annual herb that can be invasive in certain regions due to its rapid growth and seed production.
