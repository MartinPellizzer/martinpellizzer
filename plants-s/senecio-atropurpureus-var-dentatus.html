Senecio atropurpureus var. dentatus

Senecio atropurpureus var. dentatus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features striking purple to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical name reflects its dark purple coloration and the toothed margins of its leaves.
