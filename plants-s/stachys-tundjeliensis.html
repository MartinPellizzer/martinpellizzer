Stachys tundjeliensis

Stachys tundjeliensis, commonly known as the Tundjeli mint, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Arc Mountains in Tanzania.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are used traditionally in local remedies for their medicinal properties.

Stachys tundjeliensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in horticultural cultivation.
