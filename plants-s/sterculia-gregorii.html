Sterculia gregorii

Sterculia gregorii, commonly known as the Gregor's sterculia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a hairy coating.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
