Sinarundinaria jainiana

Sinarundinaria jainiana, commonly known as the Jain's bamboo, is a species of grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, grass-like formation. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense clumps.

Sinarundinaria jainiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
