Salvia verticillata subsp. verticillata

Salvia verticillata subsp.

verticillata, commonly known as meadow sage, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces dense clusters of violet to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
