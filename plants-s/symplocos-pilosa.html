Symplocos pilosa

Symplocos pilosa, commonly known as roughleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright purple berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Symplocos pilosa typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
