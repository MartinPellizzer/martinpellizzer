Spiraea opulifolia

Spiraea opulifolia, commonly known as the rose willow or shrub willow, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features slender, arching branches and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically serrated and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Spiraea opulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
