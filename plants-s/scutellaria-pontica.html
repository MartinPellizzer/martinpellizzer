Scutellaria pontica

Scutellaria pontica, commonly known as the Pontic skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Scutellaria pontica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
