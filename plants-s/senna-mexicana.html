Senna mexicana

Senna mexicana, commonly known as Mexican senna, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Senna mexicana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
