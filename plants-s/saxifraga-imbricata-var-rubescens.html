Saxifraga imbricata var. rubescens

Saxifraga imbricata var. rubescens, commonly known as the red saxifrage, is a flowering plant species native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in extreme environments.
