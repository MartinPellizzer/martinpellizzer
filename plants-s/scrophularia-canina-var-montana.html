Scrophularia canina var. montana

Scrophularia canina var. montana, commonly known as the mountain dog's mouth, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

This variety is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
