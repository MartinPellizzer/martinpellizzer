Solanum armourense

Solanum armourense, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that turn orange-yellow when mature. These fruits are edible and have a tangy, slightly tart flavor.

Solanum armourense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
