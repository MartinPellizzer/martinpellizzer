Saussurea sugimurai

Saussurea sugimurai, commonly known as the Japanese snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its fibrous roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also appreciated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
