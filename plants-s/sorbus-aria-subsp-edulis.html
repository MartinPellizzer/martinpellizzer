Sorbus aria subsp. edulis

Sorbus aria subsp.

edulis, commonly known as the edible rowan, is a shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red berries, which are edible when fully ripe.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and appear in clusters.

The edible berries are a food source for birds and have been used by humans in various culinary and medicinal traditions.
