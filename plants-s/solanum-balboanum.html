Solanum balboanum

Solanum balboanum, commonly known as the Balbo's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
