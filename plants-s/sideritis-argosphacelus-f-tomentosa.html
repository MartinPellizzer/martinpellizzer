Sideritis argosphacelus f. tomentosa

Sideritis argosphacelus f. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, grayish-green foliage and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
