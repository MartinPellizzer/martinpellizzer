Seriphium ambiguum

Seriphium ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gravelly soils. It produces yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Seriphium ambiguum is often found in the southwestern United States and parts of Mexico.
