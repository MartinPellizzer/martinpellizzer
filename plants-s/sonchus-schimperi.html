Sonchus schimperi

Sonchus schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian sowthistle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are generally lobed and have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often considered a weed but has some traditional uses in local medicine.
