Sideritis microchlamys

Sideritis microchlamys, commonly known as the small-chalmed rock sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Sideritis microchlamys is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
