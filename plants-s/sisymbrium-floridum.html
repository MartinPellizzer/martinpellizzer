Sisymbrium floridum

Sisymbrium floridum, commonly known as Florida mustangweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a branched stem that grows up to 60 cm tall, covered with fine hairs. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from May to September. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a glandular surface.

Sisymbrium floridum is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
