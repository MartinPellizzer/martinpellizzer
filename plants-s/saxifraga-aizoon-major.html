Saxifraga aizoon major

Saxifraga aizoon major, commonly known as the giant saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often considered a striking example of alpine flora.
