Solea heterophylla

Solea heterophylla, commonly known as the variable water starwort, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its ability to exhibit morphological variation in leaf shape and size depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and can form dense mats on the water surface or submerged vegetation. Its leaves are often elongated and may have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Solea heterophylla plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
