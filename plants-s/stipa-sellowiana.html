Stipa sellowiana

Stipa sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Stipa sellowiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and structure.
