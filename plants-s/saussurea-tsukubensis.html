Saussurea tsukubensis

Saussurea tsukubensis, commonly known as the Tsukubensis yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pale blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous regions and has narrow, linear leaves. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
