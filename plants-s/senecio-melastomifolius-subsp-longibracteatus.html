Senecio melastomifolius subsp. longibracteatus

Senecio melastomifolius subsp.

longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the bracts are elongated and persistent.

It is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.
