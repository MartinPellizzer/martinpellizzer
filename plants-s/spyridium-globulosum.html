Spyridium globulosum

Spyridium globulosum, commonly known as the globular spyridium, is a flowering plant native to south-eastern Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the dry conditions of its habitat.

The species is notable for its compact growth form and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
