Spadonia polymorpha var. populifolia

Spadonia polymorpha var. populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spadonia, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that resemble those of maple or ash trees, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, thriving in humid, shaded environments such as tropical rainforests. Its foliage is often a vibrant green, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns.

The inflorescence consists of small, clustered white or yellow flowers, followed by dark red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.
