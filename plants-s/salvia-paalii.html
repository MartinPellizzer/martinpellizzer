Salvia paalii

Salvia paalii, commonly known as the Hawaiian salvia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Hawaii.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Salvia paalii is typically found in coastal and wet forest habitats, growing in well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in Hawaiian flora.
