Shorea falciferoides subsp. falciferoides

Shorea falciferoides subsp.

falciferoides, commonly known as the Malacca ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hard, and durable wood, which is highly valued for construction and furniture.

The plant has simple, alternate, and often elliptic to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and typically clustered in spikes, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
