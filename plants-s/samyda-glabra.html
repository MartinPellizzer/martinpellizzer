Samyda glabra

Samyda glabra, commonly known as the smooth samyda, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a broad ovate shape and a pale green underside.

Samyda glabra is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
