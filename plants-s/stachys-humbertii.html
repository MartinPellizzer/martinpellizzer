Stachys humbertii

Stachys humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge.

Stachys humbertii is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
