Silene acaulis var. parviflora

Silene acaulis var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf catchfly, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features dense clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
