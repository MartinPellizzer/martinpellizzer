Sempervivum marmoreum var. erythraeum

Sempervivum marmoreum var. erythraeum, commonly known as the Red-veined Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and its striking foliage, which features red-veined leaves that contrast with its green or blue-gray tones. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
