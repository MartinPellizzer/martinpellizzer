Spiraeanthemum bullatum

Spiraeanthemum bullatum, commonly known as the golden dewdrop, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has finely textured, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine white powder, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The species is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
