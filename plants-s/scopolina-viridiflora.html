Scopolina viridiflora

Scopolina viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered scopolia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the wet season. Scopolina viridiflora is often found in shaded, humid environments such as rainforests and undergrowth areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually appealing addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
