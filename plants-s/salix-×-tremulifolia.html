Salix × tremulifolia

Salix × tremulifolia, commonly known as the quaking aspen.

It is a hybrid deciduous tree or shrub native to North America, characterized by its trembling leaves that rustle in the wind. The plant belongs to the Salicaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas. Its bark is smooth and gray, while its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin.

It produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, contributing to its ecological importance as a food source for various wildlife species.
