Sambucus nigra pendula-nova

Sambucus nigra pendula-nova, commonly known as the weeping elderberry, is a cultivar of the elderberry plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces dark purple berries in the fall, which are edible but should be cooked before consumption. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental shrub is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and historical medicinal uses.
