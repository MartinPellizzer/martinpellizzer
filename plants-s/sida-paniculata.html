Sida paniculata

Sida paniculata, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture.

Sida paniculata is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open fields.
