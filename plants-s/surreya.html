Surreya

Surreya, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes. The plant is often found in shallow water or damp soil, and it has a strong, aromatic scent. Surreya is also known for its medicinal properties, with its rhizomes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical name, Acorus calamus, reflects its historical significance in herbal remedies.
