Schoberia maritima var. asparagoides

Schoberia maritima var. asparagoides, commonly known as the coastal spurge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a succulent perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red or purple, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the plant is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and is often found in maritime environments.
