Silene exsudans

Silene exsudans, commonly known as the weeping silene, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a distinctive feature of exuding a clear, sticky substance from its stems, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Silene exsudans is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.
