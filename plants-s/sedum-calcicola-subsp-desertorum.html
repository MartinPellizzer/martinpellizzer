Sedum calcicola subsp. desertorum

Sedum calcicola subsp.

desertorum, commonly known as the desert sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal, it is often used in xeriscaping and rock gardens.
