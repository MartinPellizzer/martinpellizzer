Saxifraga coriophylla

Saxifraga coriophylla, commonly known as the pink saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, creating a striking visual contrast.

Saxifraga coriophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
