Syzygium quadrangulare var. quadrangulare

Syzygium quadrangulare var. quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-angled flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular (four-sided) stems and leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically leathery and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
