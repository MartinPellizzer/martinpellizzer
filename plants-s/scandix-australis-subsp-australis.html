Scandix australis subsp. australis

Scandix australis subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian salsify, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It features a stout, ribbed stem that is often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.
