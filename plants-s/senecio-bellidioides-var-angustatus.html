Senecio bellidioides var. angustatus

Senecio bellidioides var. angustatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cluster at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
