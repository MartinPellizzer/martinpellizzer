Symphytum stenophyllum

Symphytum stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved comfrey, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and tall spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its roots contain allantoin, which has been traditionally used to promote wound healing.
