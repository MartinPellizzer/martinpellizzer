Sasa chartacea f. iwakiensis

Sasa chartacea f. iwakiensis, commonly known as the Iwaki bamboo, is a variant of the broadleaf bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are typically green and covered with a thin, papery sheath. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth, glossy surface and a pointed tip. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
