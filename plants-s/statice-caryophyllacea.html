Statice caryophyllacea

Statice caryophyllacea, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, often pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are arranged in dense spikes.

Statice caryophyllacea is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
