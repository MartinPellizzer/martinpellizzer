Serjania moritziana

Serjania moritziana, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

The flowers are usually white or yellowish, with five petals, and are produced in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
