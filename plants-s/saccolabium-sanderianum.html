Saccolabium sanderianum

Saccolabium sanderianum, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and visually striking plant in its native habitat.
