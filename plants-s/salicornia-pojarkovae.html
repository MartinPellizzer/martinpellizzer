Salicornia pojarkovae

Salicornia pojarkovae, commonly known as the Pojarkov's glasswort, is a halophytic plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in desert and coastal environments. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions. Its small, scale-like leaves are adapted to reduce water loss, and it produces tiny, greenish flowers.

Salicornia pojarkovae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
