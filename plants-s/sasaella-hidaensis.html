Sasaella hidaensis

Sasaella hidaensis, commonly known as the Hida bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Japanese gardens.

Sasaella hidaensis is also notable for its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
