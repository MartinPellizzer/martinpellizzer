Senecio coriaceus

Senecio coriaceus, commonly known as the stonecrop sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its drought tolerance makes it an ideal choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
