Saxifraga champutungensis

Saxifraga champutungensis, commonly known as the Champutung saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
