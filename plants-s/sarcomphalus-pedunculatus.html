Sarcomphalus pedunculatus

Sarcomphalus pedunculatus, commonly known as the slender starwort, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, star-shaped flowers in late spring. Its inflorescence is a cluster of white or pale blue blooms, which attract various pollinators.

Sarcomphalus pedunculatus is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in riparian habitats.
