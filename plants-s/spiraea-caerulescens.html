Spiraea caerulescens

Spiraea caerulescens, commonly known as the blue spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, delicate, blue-lilac flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely cut, dark green foliage that turns red or bronze in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and landscapes.

Spiraea caerulescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
