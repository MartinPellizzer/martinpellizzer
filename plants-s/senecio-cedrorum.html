Senecio cedrorum

Senecio cedrorum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring.

Senecio cedrorum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
