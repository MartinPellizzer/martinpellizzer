Silene campanulata var. petrophila

Silene campanulata var. petrophila, commonly known as the Sierra bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
