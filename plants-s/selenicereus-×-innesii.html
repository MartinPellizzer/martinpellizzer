Selenicereus × innesii

Selenicereus × innesii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a hybrid cactus native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces waxy, spiny leaves and has a rapid growth rate under optimal conditions. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance and are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

Selenicereus × innesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
