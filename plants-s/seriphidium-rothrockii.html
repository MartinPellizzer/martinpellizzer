Seriphidium rothrockii

Seriphidium rothrockii, commonly known as the Rothrock's wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The small yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
