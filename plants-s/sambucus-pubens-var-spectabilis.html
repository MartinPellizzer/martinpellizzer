Sambucus pubens var. spectabilis

Sambucus pubens var. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pubescent and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The showy elderberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
