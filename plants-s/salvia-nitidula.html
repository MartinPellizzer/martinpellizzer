Salvia nitidula

Salvia nitidula, commonly known as the silver sage or white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet to white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia nitidula is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal practices.
