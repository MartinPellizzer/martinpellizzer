Sesuvium spathulatum

Sesuvium spathulatum, commonly known as seaside daisy, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It has fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically opposite and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is well adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a hardy species for coastal environments.

Its ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
