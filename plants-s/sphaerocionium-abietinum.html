Sphaerocionium abietinum

Sphaerocionium abietinum, commonly known as the mountain pine or white pine, is a coniferous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and long, slender needles arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that mature in the second year, and its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
