Stryphnodendron consimile

Stryphnodendron consimile, commonly known as the yellow tala, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, narrow leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody pods containing flat, papery seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
