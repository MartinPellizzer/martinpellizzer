Scrophularia scorodonia subvar. viridiflora

Scrophularia scorodonia subvar. viridiflora, commonly known as the greenflower scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as gravelly slopes and rocky outcrops.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
