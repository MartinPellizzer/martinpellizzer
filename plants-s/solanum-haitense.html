Solanum haitense

Solanum haitense, commonly known as Haitian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red as they mature. Solanum haitense belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

It is characterized by its adaptability to tropical climates and its potential use in traditional medicine.
