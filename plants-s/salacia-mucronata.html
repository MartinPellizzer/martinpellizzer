Salacia mucronata

Salacia mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Salacia mucronata is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
