Salix wolfii var. pseudolapponum

Salix wolfii var. pseudolapponum, commonly known as the Arctic willow, is a shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and catkins, which are typical of willow species. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
