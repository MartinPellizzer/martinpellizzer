Saponaria × pulchella

Saponaria × pulchella, commonly known as the carnation or garden carnation, is a flowering plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Saponaria ocymifolia and Saponaria macedonica. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, and purple. It typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Saponaria × pulchella is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
