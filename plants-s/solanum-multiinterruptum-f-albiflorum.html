Solanum multiinterruptum f. albiflorum

Solanum multiinterruptum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflowered groundcherry, is a variant of the groundcherry species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and its unique, white or pale yellow flowers that contrast with the typical red or orange blooms of the species. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral display, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
