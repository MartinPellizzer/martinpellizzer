Salvia montbretii

Salvia montbretii, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Salvia montbretii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
