Serratula gmelinii

Serratula gmelinii, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have serrated edges, giving it its common name.

Serratula gmelinii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
