Sedum telephium var. buxicola

Sedum telephium var. buxicola, commonly known as the stonecrop sedum, is a low-growing succulent native to Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted for water storage, which is characteristic of many succulent species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
