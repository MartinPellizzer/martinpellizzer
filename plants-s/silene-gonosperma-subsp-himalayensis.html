Silene gonosperma subsp. himalayensis

Silene gonosperma subsp.

himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, adapting to the harsh conditions of high altitudes. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in cold, arid environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
