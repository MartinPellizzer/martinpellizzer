Schizopepon dioicus

Schizopepon dioicus, commonly known as the Chinese bitter melon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinct, ribbed, and often bitter-tasting fruits, which are typically green when mature. The plant produces separate male and female flowers, a trait that is typical of dioecious species. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and trailing.

Native to East Asia, it is cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
