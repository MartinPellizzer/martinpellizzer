Sorbus torminalis f. glaberrima

Sorbus torminalis f. glaberrima, commonly known as the smoothleaf whitebeam, is a rare and distinctive variant of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and smaller, more rounded leaves compared to the typical species. This form is native to southern Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for study in plant morphology and horticulture.
