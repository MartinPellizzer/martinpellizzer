Solanum delpierrei

Solanum delpierrei, commonly known as the Delpierre's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are berries that turn red when mature.

Solanum delpierrei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
