Semiarundinaria albostriata var. glabrata

Semiarundinaria albostriata var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are covered in a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and structural qualities.
