Stipa smirnovii

Stipa smirnovii, commonly known as Smirnov's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brown or golden in color. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Stipa smirnovii plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
