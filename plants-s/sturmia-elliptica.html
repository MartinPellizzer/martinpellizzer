Sturmia elliptica

Sturmia elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical sturmia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive leaf structure.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features compound leaves with a serrated margin and small, umbellate flowers that bloom in early summer. Its roots are often harvested for medicinal purposes due to their bioactive properties.

Sturmia elliptica is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
