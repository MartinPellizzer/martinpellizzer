Sasa suzukii

Sasa suzukii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Suzukii bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts and as a source of material for papermaking.

Sasa suzukii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions of Japan.
