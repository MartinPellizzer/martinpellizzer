Stachys angustifolia

Stachys angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Stachys angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
