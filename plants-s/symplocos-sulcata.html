Symplocos sulcata

Symplocos sulcata, commonly known as the roughleaf evergreen huckleberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, dark purple berries in the fall. Symplocos sulcata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
