Salvia ovalifolia var. ovalifolia

Salvia ovalifolia var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
