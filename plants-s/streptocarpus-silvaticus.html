Streptocarpus silvaticus

Streptocarpus silvaticus, commonly known as the forest streptocarpus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its fleshy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Streptocarpus silvaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
