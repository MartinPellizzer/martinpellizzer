Sasa kurilensis f. yezo-alpina

Sasa kurilensis f. yezo-alpina, commonly known as the Yezo alpine sasa, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, waxy layer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine zones.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions in its native habitat.
