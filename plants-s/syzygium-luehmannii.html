Syzygium luehmannii

Syzygium luehmannii, commonly known as the luehmann's apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland rainforests.

Syzygium luehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
