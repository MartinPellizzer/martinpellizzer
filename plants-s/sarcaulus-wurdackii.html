Sarcaulus wurdackii

Sarcaulus wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's broomrape, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plant for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of other species. The plant is characterized by its yellowish-green stems and small, scale-like leaves. It is native to southern Africa and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem by influencing the growth and distribution of its host plants.
