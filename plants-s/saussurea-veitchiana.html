Saussurea veitchiana

Saussurea veitchiana, commonly known as the Veitch's spikenard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and dense, fluffy flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spherical cluster, which are surrounded by bracts. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Saussurea veitchiana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
