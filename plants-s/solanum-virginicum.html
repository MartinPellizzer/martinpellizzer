Solanum virginicum

Solanum virginicum, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces smooth, oval berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
