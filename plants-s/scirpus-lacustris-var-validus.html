Scirpus lacustris var. validus

Scirpus lacustris var. validus, commonly known as the marsh clubrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, greenish-brown spikelets that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help stabilize soil in marshy areas, contributing to wetland ecology.

It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian habitats.
