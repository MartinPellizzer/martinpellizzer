Scilla corsica

Scilla corsica, commonly known as the Corsican squill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. Scilla corsica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
