Scrophularia buergeriana var. quelpartensis

Scrophularia buergeriana var. quelpartensis, commonly known as the Korean figwort, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features erect, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are clustered in spikes, producing showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
