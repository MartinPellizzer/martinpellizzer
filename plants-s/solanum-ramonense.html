Solanum ramonense

Solanum ramonense, commonly known as Ramon's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
