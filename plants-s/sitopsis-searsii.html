Sitopsis searsii

Sitopsis searsii, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sitopsis searsii is known for its resilience and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
