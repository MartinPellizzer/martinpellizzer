Silene dyris

Silene dyris, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Silene dyris is typically found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some Native American tribes.
