Sclerolobium paniculatum var. polyphyllum

Sclerolobium paniculatum var. polyphyllum, commonly known as the African broom, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and compound leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening and for erosion control.
