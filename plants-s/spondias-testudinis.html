Spondias testudinis

Spondias testudinis, commonly known as the "Chestnut Apple" or "Brazil Nut Tree," is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, chestnut-like flavor.

Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various parts of its native range.
