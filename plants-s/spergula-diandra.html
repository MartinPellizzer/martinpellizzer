Spergula diandra

Spergula diandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets and bloom in early summer.

Spergula diandra plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
