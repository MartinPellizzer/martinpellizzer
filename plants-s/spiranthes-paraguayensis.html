Spiranthes paraguayensis

Spiranthes paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
