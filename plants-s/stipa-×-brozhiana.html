Stipa × brozhiana

Stipa × brozhiana, commonly known as the hybrid false wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its foliage and seeds are sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
