Styloglossum rhodochilum

Styloglossum rhodochilum, commonly known as the pink-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen habit with fleshy, succulent-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
