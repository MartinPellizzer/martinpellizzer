Stipa schmidtii

Stipa schmidtii, commonly known as Schmidt's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperature fluctuations. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Stipa schmidtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
