Senecio late-alatopetiolatus

Senecio late-alatopetiolatus, commonly known as the late-winged petioled senecio, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring broad, wing-like structures on the petioles. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
