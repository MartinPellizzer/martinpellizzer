Sida frutescens

Sida frutescens, commonly known as marsh mallow or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is an annual or perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils. The plant features alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its showy, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the warm season.

Sida frutescens is known for its fibrous stems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
