Salvia fruticetorum f. macrophylla

Salvia fruticetorum f. macrophylla, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies due to its aromatic properties.
