Solanum hederiradiculum

Solanum hederiradiculum, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows close to the ground, with trailing stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to both humans and animals.
