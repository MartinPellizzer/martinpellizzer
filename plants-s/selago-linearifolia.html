Selago linearifolia

Selago linearifolia, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Selago linearifolia thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
