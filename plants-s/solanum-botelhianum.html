Solanum botelhianum

Solanum botelhianum, commonly known as the Botelho's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, purple berries that are typically toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and lobed leaves, making it easily identifiable in its native habitat.
