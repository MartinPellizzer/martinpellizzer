Stachys penanevada

Stachys penanevada, commonly known as the Peninsular Stachys, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the upper stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
