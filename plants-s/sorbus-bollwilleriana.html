Sorbus bollwilleriana

Sorbus bollwilleriana, commonly known as the Bollwiller mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red berries that are clustered in small, spherical inflorescences. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
