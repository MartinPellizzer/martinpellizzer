Selaginella helicoclada

Selaginella helicoclada, commonly known as the spiral moss or spiral clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant in the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, creeping plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinct spiral arrangement of its leaves, which gives it its common name. It possesses a short, erect stem with whorls of spirally arranged leaves.

Selaginella helicoclada is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures.
