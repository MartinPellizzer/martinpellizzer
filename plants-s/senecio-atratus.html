Senecio atratus

Senecio atratus, commonly known as the Blackfoot Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow with a dark center, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio atratus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
