Santolina trifurcata

Santolina trifurcata, commonly known as fringed santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, low-growing stems covered in small, silver-gray leaves that are often edged with fine fringes. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in both garden and landscape design.
