Senecio clematoides

Senecio clematoides, commonly known as the blueweed or blueweed aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Senecio clematoides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
