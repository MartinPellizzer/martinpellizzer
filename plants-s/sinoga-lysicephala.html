Sinoga lysicephala

Sinoga lysicephala, commonly known as the golden head orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange inflorescence that resembles a head, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a complex structure that includes specialized petals and sepals.

Sinoga lysicephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique morphological features.
