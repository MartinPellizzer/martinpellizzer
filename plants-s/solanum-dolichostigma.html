Solanum dolichostigma

Solanum dolichostigma, commonly known as the long-stemmed nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, yellowish berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its native habitat.
