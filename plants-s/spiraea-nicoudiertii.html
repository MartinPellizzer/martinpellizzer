Spiraea nicoudiertii

Spiraea nicoudiertii, commonly known as the Nicoudier's weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant has finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Spiraea nicoudiertii is valued for its graceful appearance and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
