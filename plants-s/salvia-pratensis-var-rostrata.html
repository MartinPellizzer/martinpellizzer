Salvia pratensis var. rostrata

Salvia pratensis var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrata blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
