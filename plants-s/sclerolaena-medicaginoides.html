Sclerolaena medicaginoides

Sclerolaena medicaginoides, commonly known as the African medick, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
