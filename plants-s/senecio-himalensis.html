Senecio himalensis

Senecio himalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

Senecio himalensis produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
