Senecio epidendricus

Senecio epidendricus, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, silvery-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Senecio epidendricus is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
