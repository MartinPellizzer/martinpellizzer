Shorea polita

Shorea polita, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with smooth, greyish bark. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with prominent veins.

The flowers are small and yellowish, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.
