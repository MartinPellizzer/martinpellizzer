Sideritis hyssopifolia var. somedana

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. somedana, commonly known as the Sueda's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered with fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. somedana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
