Salix hippophaeifolia

Salix hippophaeifolia, commonly known as the Chinese willow or the sand willow, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating drought, wind, and poor soil conditions.

Salix hippophaeifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
