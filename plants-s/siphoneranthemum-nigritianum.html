Siphoneranthemum nigritianum

Siphoneranthemum nigritianum, commonly known as the Black River sandmat, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has hairy leaves and a low-growing habit, often forming dense mats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
