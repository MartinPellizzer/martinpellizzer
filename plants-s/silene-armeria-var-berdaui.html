Silene armeria var. berdaui

Silene armeria var. berdaui, commonly known as the Armenian campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers.
