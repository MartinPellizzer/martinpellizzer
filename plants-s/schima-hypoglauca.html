Schima hypoglauca

Schima hypoglauca, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain sticky seeds. Schima hypoglauca is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

It is notable for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions, making it adaptable to different habitats.
