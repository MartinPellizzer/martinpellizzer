Streptocarpus suffruticosus var. hirtellus

Streptocarpus suffruticosus var. hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy streptocarpus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
