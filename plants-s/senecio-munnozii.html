Senecio munnozii

Senecio munnozii, commonly known as Munnoz's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Senecio munnozii is often found in scrubland and chaparral habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
