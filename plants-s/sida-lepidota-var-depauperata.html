Sida lepidota var. depauperata

Sida lepidota var. depauperata, commonly known as the sparseleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
