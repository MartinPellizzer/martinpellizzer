Swida alba var. flaviramea

Swida alba var. flaviramea, commonly known as the yellow-flowered white swida, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Swida genus.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with its white or pale green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest regions. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Botanically, it is noted for its unique floral structure and limited geographic distribution.
