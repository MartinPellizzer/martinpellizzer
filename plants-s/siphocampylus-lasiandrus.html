Siphocampylus lasiandrus

Siphocampylus lasiandrus, commonly known as the hairy leaf lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces striking white to pale pink flowers with six petals, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.
