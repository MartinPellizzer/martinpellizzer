Stipa caucasica subsp. iskanderkulica

Stipa caucasica subsp.

iskanderkulica, commonly known as the Iskanderkul grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, with a rough, hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
