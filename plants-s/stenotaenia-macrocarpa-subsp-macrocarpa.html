Stenotaenia macrocarpa subsp. macrocarpa

Stenotaenia macrocarpa subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited sedge, is a perennial herb native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its robust, grass-like foliage and produces large, elongated, cylindrical inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

The subspecies is distinguished by its relatively large, showy fruits compared to other members of the genus.
