Solanopsis

Solanopsis, commonly known as the "deadly nightshade," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are characterized by their spiny stems, showy flowers, and often toxic berries. Solanopsis species are native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas. They typically thrive in dry, arid environments and are often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to their toxic properties, they are considered dangerous to both humans and animals.
