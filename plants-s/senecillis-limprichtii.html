Senecillis limprichtii

Senecillis limprichtii, commonly known as the Limpricht's senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
