Senecio attenuatus var. microphyllus

Senecio attenuatus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the narrowleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant in dry landscapes.
