Sisymbrium meyeri

Sisymbrium meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs.

Sisymbrium meyeri is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
