Schizachyrium scoparium subvar. lolioides

Schizachyrium scoparium subvar. lolioides, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass that thrives in prairies and dry, open habitats.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. It is drought-tolerant and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
