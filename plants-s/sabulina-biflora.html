Sabulina biflora

Sabulina biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces two to four white or pinkish flowers per stem, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountain environments.

Sabulina biflora is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its succulent leaves and showy flowers.
