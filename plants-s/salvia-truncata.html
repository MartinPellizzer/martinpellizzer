Salvia truncata

Salvia truncata, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia truncata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
