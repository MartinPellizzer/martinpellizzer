Salix wenchuanica

Salix wenchuanica, commonly known as the Wenchuan willow, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Salix.

It is native to China, specifically the Sichuan province, and is adapted to grow in mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and small, yellowish-green leaves. It produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and is often found near rivers and streams.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
