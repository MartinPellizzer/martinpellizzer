Scleria viridifolia

Scleria viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leafed umbrella sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers that are usually green or reddish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.

Scleria viridifolia is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
