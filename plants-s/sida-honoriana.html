Sida honoriana

Sida honoriana, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically green and have a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Sida honoriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
