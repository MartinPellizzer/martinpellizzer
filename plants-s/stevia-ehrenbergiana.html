Stevia ehrenbergiana

Stevia ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the Paraguay sweet leaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has gained attention as a sustainable alternative to sugar.
