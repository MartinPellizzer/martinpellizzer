Setaria maximowiczii

Setaria maximowiczii, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a grass species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a foxtail, hence its common name. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in warm climates, making it a valuable crop in arid regions. Its seeds are used for food, animal feed, and even as a source of fiber.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and high yield potential.
