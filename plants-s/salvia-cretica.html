Salvia cretica

Salvia cretica, commonly known as Greek sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.
