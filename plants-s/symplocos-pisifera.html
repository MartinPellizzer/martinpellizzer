Symplocos pisifera

Symplocos pisifera, commonly known as the sweetgum or Chinese sweetgum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn vibrant colors in autumn. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by distinctive, spiky, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Symplocos pisifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
