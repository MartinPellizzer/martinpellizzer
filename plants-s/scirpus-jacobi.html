Scirpus jacobi

Scirpus jacobi, commonly known as the Jacob's sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to wetland habitats in North America, it thrives in moist to aquatic environments. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Scirpus jacobi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
