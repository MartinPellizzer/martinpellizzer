Solanum daphnophyllum

Solanum daphnophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its ornamental value and toxic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
