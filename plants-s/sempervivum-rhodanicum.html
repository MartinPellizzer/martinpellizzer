Sempervivum rhodanicum

Sempervivum rhodanicum, commonly known as the French rose or red sedum, is a succulent plant native to the Alps and Pyrenees.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white powdery wax. The leaves are usually a vibrant red or pinkish hue, especially when exposed to full sun. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This hardy plant is well adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
