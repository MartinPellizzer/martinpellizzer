Satyrium ligulatum

Satyrium ligulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Satyrium ligulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
