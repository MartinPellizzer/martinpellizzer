Senecio × siegfriedii

Senecio × siegfriedii, commonly known as the Siegfried's groundsel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
