Scabiosa crenata subsp. breviscapa

Scabiosa crenata subsp.

breviscapa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
