Salix pulchra subsp. anadyrensis

Salix pulchra subsp.

anadyrensis, commonly known as the Anadyrensis willow, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and thrives in well-drained soils. It is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
