Saxifraga ferdinandi-coburgi

Saxifraga ferdinandi-coburgi, commonly known as the Coburg saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. It is a perennial species that is relatively hardy, though it requires cool, moist conditions to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable microclimates.
