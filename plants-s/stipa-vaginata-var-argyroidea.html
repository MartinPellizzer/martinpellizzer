Stipa vaginata var. argyroidea

Stipa vaginata var. argyroidea, commonly known as silver-stemmed needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-haired stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that is often tinged with a pale or silver hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
