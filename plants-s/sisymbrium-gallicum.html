Sisymbrium gallicum

Sisymbrium gallicum, commonly known as French broom, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a feathery appearance, and it produces slender pods that contain several seeds.

Sisymbrium gallicum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
