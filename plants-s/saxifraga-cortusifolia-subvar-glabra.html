Saxifraga cortusifolia subvar. glabra

Saxifraga cortusifolia subvar. glabra, commonly known as the smooth saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. The plant features upright, branching stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually smooth and hairless. Its small, white to pinkish flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
