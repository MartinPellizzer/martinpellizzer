Sarcanthus sagittatus

Sarcanthus sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Sarcanthus sagittatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
