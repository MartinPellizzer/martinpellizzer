Sterculia hymenocalyx

Sterculia hymenocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a pleasant fragrance. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
