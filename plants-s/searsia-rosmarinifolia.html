Searsia rosmarinifolia

Searsia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Searsia rosmarinifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
