Sabatia grandiflora

Sabatia grandiflora, commonly known as the showy yellow catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, often pink or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are opposite and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Sabatia grandiflora blooms from June to September, attracting pollinators with its vibrant petals.
