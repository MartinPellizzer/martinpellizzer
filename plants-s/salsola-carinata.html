Salsola carinata

Salsola carinata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, deltoid-shaped leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common weed in disturbed soils. Its extensive root system allows it to survive in poor soil conditions and resist drought.

Salsola carinata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
