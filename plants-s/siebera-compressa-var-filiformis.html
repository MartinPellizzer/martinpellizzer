Siebera compressa var. filiformis

Siebera compressa var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender siebera, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often described as filamentous, giving it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive without direct sunlight. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or white, and it produces tiny, dark berries as its fruit.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
