Saussurea chrysotricha

Saussurea chrysotricha, commonly known as the golden hair grass, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its fibrous roots help stabilize the soil in these harsh conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
