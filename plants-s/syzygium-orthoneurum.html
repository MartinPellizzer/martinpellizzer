Syzygium orthoneurum

Syzygium orthoneurum, commonly known as the satinwood or white sand apple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or orange when mature. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Syzygium orthoneurum is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
