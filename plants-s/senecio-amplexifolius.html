Senecio amplexifolius

Senecio amplexifolius, commonly known as the clasping leaf senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that clasp the stem, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
