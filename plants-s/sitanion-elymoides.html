Sitanion elymoides

Sitanion elymoides, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Sitanion elymoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
