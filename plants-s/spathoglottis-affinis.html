Spathoglottis affinis

Spathoglottis affinis, commonly known as the white lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and tall, erect inflorescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors.

Its unique floral structure and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
