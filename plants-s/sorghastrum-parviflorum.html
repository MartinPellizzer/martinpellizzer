Sorghastrum parviflorum

Sorghastrum parviflorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
