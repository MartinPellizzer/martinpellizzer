Seymeria glandulosa

Seymeria glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular seymeria, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves that bear small, glandular structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Seymeria glandulosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
