Streptocarpus wendlandii

Streptocarpus wendlandii, commonly known as the Cape violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Streptocarpus wendlandii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
