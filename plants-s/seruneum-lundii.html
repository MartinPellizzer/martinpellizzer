Seruneum lundii

Seruneum lundii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with upright stems and produces vibrant blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Seruneum lundii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
