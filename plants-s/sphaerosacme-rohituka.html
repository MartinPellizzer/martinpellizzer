Sphaerosacme rohituka

Sphaerosacme rohituka, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clustered, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically borne in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
