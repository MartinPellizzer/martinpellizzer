Scleranthus comatus

Scleranthus comatus, commonly known as the hairy sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Scleranthus comatus is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments due to its robust and resilient structure.
