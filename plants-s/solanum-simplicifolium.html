Solanum simplicifolium

Solanum simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, toxic berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
