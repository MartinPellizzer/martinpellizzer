Smilax odoratissima

Smilax odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and its ability to climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, distinctive odor.

Smilax odoratissima is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and historical uses.
