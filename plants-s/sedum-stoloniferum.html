Sedum stoloniferum

Sedum stoloniferum, commonly known as the creeping sedum, is a succulent plant native to Eurasia.

It features fleshy, upright stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly along the ground. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. Its leaves are typically green and succulent, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.
