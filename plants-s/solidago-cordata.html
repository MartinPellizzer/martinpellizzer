Solidago cordata

Solidago cordata, commonly known as heartleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often heart-shaped at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Solidago cordata is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
