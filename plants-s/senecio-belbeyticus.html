Senecio belbeyticus

Senecio belbeyticus, commonly known as the Turkish groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
