Serratula suffulta

Serratula suffulta, commonly known as the tall yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are serrated and alternate, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Serratula suffulta is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in open habitats.
