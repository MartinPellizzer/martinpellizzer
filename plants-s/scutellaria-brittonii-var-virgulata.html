Scutellaria brittonii var. virgulata

Scutellaria brittonii var. virgulata, commonly known as bluehead mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and often blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
