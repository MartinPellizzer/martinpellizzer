Salvia hunzikeri

Salvia hunzikeri, commonly known as the Hunziker's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and silver-gray foliage, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists.
