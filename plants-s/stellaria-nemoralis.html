Stellaria nemoralis

Stellaria nemoralis, commonly known as common mouse-ear, is a low-growing annual or biennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically reaches heights of 5 to 20 centimeters and features small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several tiny seeds.

Stellaria nemoralis thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and along roadsides.
