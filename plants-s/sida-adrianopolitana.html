Sida adrianopolitana

Sida adrianopolitana, commonly known as the Adrianople sida, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features small, woolly leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Sida adrianopolitana has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
