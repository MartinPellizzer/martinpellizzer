Spiranthes autumnalis var. parviflora

Spiranthes autumnalis var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered autumn spider-orchid, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling arrangement of pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces a single spike of small, fragrant blooms.

This variety is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
