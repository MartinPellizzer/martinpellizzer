Spergularia colorata proles crassipes

Spergularia colorata proles crassipes, commonly known as the thick-stemmed sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems that store water, aiding in survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually small and scale-like, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Spergularia colorata proles crassipes is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high salinity and temperature fluctuations.
