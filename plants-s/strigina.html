Strigina

Strigina, commonly known as the "ghost grass," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

These plants are characterized by their slender, often pale or whitish appearance, which gives them their ethereal look. Due to their low-growing habit and unique appearance, Strigina species are often considered weedy and can be invasive in certain regions.
