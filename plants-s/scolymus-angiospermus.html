Scolymus angiospermus

Scolymus angiospermus, commonly known as the common scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Scolymus angiospermus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its edible roots and leaves.
