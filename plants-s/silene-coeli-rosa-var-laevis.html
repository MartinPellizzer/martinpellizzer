Silene coeli-rosa var. laevis

Silene coeli-rosa var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces upright stems bearing clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
