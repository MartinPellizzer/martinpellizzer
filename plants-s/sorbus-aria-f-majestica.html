Sorbus aria f. majestica

Sorbus aria f. majestica, commonly known as the whitebeam tree, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its large, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

This cultivar is prized for its showy inflorescences, which bloom in late spring and early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticulture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Its foliage turns a striking red in autumn, enhancing its ornamental appeal throughout the year.
