Schoenus brachyphyllus

Schoenus brachyphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in waterlogged soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated.

Schoenus brachyphyllus plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
