Sorbus crantzii

Sorbus crantzii, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
