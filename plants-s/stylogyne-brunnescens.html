Stylogyne brunnescens

Stylogyne brunnescens, commonly known as the brown stylogynum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It typically grows in swampy or seasonally flooded areas, where it thrives in the moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a basal rosette, and it is known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.

Stylogyne brunnescens plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
