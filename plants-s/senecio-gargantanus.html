Senecio gargantanus

Senecio gargantanus, commonly known as the giant senecio, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its towering height, often reaching up to 5 meters, and its large, showy yellow flower heads. The plant features a thick, woody stem with broad, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a striking landscape plant.
