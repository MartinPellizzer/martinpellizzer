Schisandra henryi subsp. hoatii

Schisandra henryi subsp.

hoatii, commonly known as the Hoat's five-flavor fruit, is a member of the Schisandraceae family. It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and produces bright red berries that contain a mix of aromatic compounds. The berries are traditionally used in Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
