Schizochilus sulphureus

Schizochilus sulphureus, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Schizochilus sulphureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
