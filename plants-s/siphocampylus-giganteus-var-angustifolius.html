Siphocampylus giganteus var. angustifolius

Siphocampylus giganteus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed giant siphocampylus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach several feet in height, supporting large, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinct, angular appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
