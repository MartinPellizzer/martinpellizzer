Scleranthus seladonius

Scleranthus seladonius, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Scleranthus seladonius is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
