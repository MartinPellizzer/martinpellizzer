Stachys durandiana var. grantii

Stachys durandiana var. grantii, commonly known as the Kashmir violet, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
