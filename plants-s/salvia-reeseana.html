Salvia reeseana

Salvia reeseana, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for gardens and native plant landscapes.
