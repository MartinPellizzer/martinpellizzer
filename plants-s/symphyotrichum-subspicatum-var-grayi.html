Symphyotrichum subspicatum var. grayi

Symphyotrichum subspicatum var. grayi, commonly known as Gray's heath aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its clustered, small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is distinguished by its grayish-green foliage and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
