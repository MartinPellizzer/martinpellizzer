Styphelia rufa

Styphelia rufa, commonly known as the red-stemmed wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, waxy coating. Styphelia rufa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and coastal heath environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
