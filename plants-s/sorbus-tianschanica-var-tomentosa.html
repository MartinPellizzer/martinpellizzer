Sorbus tianschanica var. tomentosa

Sorbus tianschanica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the Tianshan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval or elliptical with a serrated edge and a hairy texture.

This species is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
