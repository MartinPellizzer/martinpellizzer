Struthiola argentea var. oblongata

Struthiola argentea var. oblongata, commonly known as the silverleaf struthiola, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features silver-gray, ovate leaves that are densely covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and compact growth habit.
