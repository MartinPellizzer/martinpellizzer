Solanum multiinterruptum var. machaytambinum

Solanum multiinterruptum var. machaytambinum, commonly known as the Machaytambinum nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinctive, serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its fruits are berries that mature to a deep red, containing several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
