Senecio lautus var. lanceolatus

Senecio lautus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features linear, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
