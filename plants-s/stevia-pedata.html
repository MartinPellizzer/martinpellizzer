Stevia pedata

Stevia pedata, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Stevia pedata is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
