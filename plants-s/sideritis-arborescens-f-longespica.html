Sideritis arborescens f. longespica

Sideritis arborescens f. longespica, commonly known as the long-spurred sidewort, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, hairy leaves that are often glandular. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers with distinctive, elongated corollas that give it its common name. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
