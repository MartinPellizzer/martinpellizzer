Saxifraga × amabilis

Saxifraga × amabilis, commonly known as the pink saxifrage, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces clusters of showy, pink or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Saxifraga × amabilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
