Salvia micrantha var. blodgettii

Salvia micrantha var. blodgettii, commonly known as Blodgett's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
