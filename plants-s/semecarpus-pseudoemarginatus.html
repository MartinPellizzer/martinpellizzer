Semecarpus pseudoemarginatus

Semecarpus pseudoemarginatus, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or hill chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
