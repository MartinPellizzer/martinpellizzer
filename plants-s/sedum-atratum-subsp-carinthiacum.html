Sedum atratum subsp. carinthiacum

Sedum atratum subsp.

carinthiacum, commonly known as the Carinthian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Alps. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted for water storage.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, usually blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
