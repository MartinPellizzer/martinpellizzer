Syzygium legatii

Syzygium legatii, commonly known as the Lord's tamarind, is a flowering plant native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Syzygium legatii is often found in moist, shaded forest areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
