Silene viridis

Silene viridis, commonly known as green catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in moist, grassy habitats. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Silene viridis is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partially shaded conditions.
