Salvia japonica var. erythrophylla

Salvia japonica var. erythrophylla, commonly known as the Japanese scarlet sage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to pollinator-friendly landscapes.
