Scolopia henryi

Scolopia henryi, commonly known as Henry's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to China.

It is characterized by its spine-tipped stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible fruits, although they are generally small and not widely cultivated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Scolopia henryi is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh climates.
