Secula

Secula, commonly known as the sacred fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grey bark. The tree produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible figs that are rich in nutrients. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
