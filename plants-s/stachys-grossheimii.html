Stachys grossheimii

Stachys grossheimii, commonly known as the woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
