Salvia ocimoides

Salvia ocimoides, commonly known as lemon beebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to white. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Salvia ocimoides is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
