Syzygium chandrasekharanii

Syzygium chandrasekharanii, commonly known as the Kanyakumari flame apple, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, bright red fruits that resemble small apples. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, while its flowers are small and white with a slight pinkish hue.

The fruit develops in clusters and is known for its unique, fiery coloration, making it a popular ornamental species.
