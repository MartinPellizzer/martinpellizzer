Swertia esquirolii

Swertia esquirolii, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stem is erect and branched.

Swertia esquirolii is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
