Stereochilus pachyphyllus

Stereochilus pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leafed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the warmer months. Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soil make it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The species is also noted for its resilience to extreme temperatures and minimal water requirements.
