Symphytosiphon

Symphytosiphon, commonly known as the spiral siphon, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its spiral-shaped structure.

It belongs to the order Siphonales and is found in freshwater environments. The plant's morphology includes a central axis with spirally arranged filaments that enhance its ability to capture light and nutrients. Symphytosiphon exhibits a simple yet efficient photosynthetic system adapted to low-light conditions.

Its ecological role includes contributing to water clarity and providing habitat for microorganisms.
