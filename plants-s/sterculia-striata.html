Sterculia striata

Sterculia striata, commonly known as the Striated Sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. Its bark is thick and corky, often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood in local crafts and construction.
