Senecio lautus var. esperensis

Senecio lautus var. esperensis, commonly known as the Esperance sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces numerous small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
