Salvia × rociana

Salvia × rociana, commonly known as the hybrid sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Salvia × rociana is also valued for its aromatic leaves and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
