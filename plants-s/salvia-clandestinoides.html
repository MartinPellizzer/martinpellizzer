Salvia clandestinoides

Salvia clandestinoides, commonly known as the secretive sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
