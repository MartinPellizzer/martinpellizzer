Senecio paulensis

Senecio paulensis, commonly known as the Cape gold flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces bright yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio paulensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
