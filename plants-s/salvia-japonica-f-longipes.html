Salvia japonica f. longipes

Salvia japonica f. longipes, commonly known as the Japanese sage with long stamens, is a cultivar of the Salvia genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stems. The plant features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its extended stamens give it a unique appearance, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
