Stachys × tarnensis

Stachys × tarnensis, commonly known as the purple mint or common stachys, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Stachys × tarnensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
