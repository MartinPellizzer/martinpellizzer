Schinus procera

Schinus procera, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Schinus procera is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and seasonal fruit display.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
