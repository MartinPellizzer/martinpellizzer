Silene cespitosa

Silene cespitosa, commonly known as the tufted catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Silene cespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
