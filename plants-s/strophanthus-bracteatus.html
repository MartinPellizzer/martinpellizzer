Strophanthus bracteatus

Strophanthus bracteatus, commonly known as the African blood lily, is a striking plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces vibrant orange or red flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Strophanthus bracteatus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
