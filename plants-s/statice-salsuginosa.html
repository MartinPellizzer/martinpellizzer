Statice salsuginosa

Statice salsuginosa, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and showy, violet-blue flower heads. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes, salt marshes, and coastal grasslands. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Statice salsuginosa is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
