Sasa pycnotricha

Sasa pycnotricha, commonly known as the dense-hair sasa, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is a rhizomatous perennial plant that forms dense clumps, often found in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant has slender, arching stems covered in short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer.

Sasa pycnotricha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in partial shade.
