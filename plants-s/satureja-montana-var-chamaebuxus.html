Satureja montana var. chamaebuxus

Satureja montana var. chamaebuxus, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in the summer months, which are clustered in loose spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
