Selaginella lemairei

Selaginella lemairei, commonly known as the spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is characterized by its small, spore-bearing structures called strobila.

It is a vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, branching stems and scale-like leaves. Selaginella lemairei is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions due to its desiccation tolerance.

Its reproductive structures are essential for its propagation and ecological role in various habitats.
