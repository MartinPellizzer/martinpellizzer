Symphyotrichum phlogifolium

Symphyotrichum phlogifolium, commonly known as the fireleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open woodlands and shaded areas. The plant features showy purple to pink floral heads that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are characterized by their lobed shape and reddish-brown margins, which give it its common name.

Symphyotrichum phlogifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
