Salvia aliciae

Salvia aliciae, commonly known as the Alice sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and hairy. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Salvia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
