Stachys candida subsp. chrysantha

Stachys candida subsp.

chrysantha, commonly known as the golden hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers with yellow stamens and pale blue or white petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy areas.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
