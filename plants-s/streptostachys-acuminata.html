Streptostachys acuminata

Streptostachys acuminata, commonly known as the slender glassworts, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline and coastal environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in coastal dune ecosystems.

Streptostachys acuminata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate high salt concentrations and prevent erosion.
