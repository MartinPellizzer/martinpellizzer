Solanum leontopodium

Solanum leontopodium, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white foliage and showy yellow-centered white flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a symbol of resilience and beauty in mountainous regions.

Due to its ecological significance and limited distribution, it is also considered a protected species in many areas.
