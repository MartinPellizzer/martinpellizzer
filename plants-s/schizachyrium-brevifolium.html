Schizachyrium brevifolium

Schizachyrium brevifolium, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass that thrives in prairie and woodland habitats.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-gray foliage and distinctive, slender, spike-like inflorescence. The plant is drought-tolerant and often forms dense, mat-like growths that stabilize soil and prevent erosion. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Little bluestem is an important component of native plant communities and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
