Symphyotrichum lanceolatum var. interior

Symphyotrichum lanceolatum var. interior, commonly known as the interior blue aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in sandy or loamy soils.

The interior blue aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
