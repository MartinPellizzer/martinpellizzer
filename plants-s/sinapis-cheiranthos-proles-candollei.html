Sinapis cheiranthos proles candollei

Sinapis cheiranthos proles candollei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are elongated, pod-like structures.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its potential in agricultural systems.
