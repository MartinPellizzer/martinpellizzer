Solanum andigenum f. pallidum

Solanum andigenum f. pallidum, commonly known as the Andean pale tomato, is a botanical variant of the Andean tomato.

It is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America. The plant produces small, pale yellow to red berries that are typically used in local cuisines. Its leaves are ovate and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its unique flavor and adaptability to harsh mountainous environments.
