Sabicea hirsuta

Sabicea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Sabicea hirsuta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
