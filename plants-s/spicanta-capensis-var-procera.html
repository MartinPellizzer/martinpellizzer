Spicanta capensis var. procera

Spicanta capensis var. procera, commonly known as the Cape spurge, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, spiny seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
