Scrophularia variegata subsp. depauperata

Scrophularia variegata subsp.

depauperata, commonly known as the poorflower, is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, usually pale green or yellowish flowers.

The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare plant in certain regions.
