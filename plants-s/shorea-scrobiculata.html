Shorea scrobiculata

Shorea scrobiculata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody fruits that contain winged seeds.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various forms of wildlife.
