Solanum conanthum

Solanum conanthum, commonly known as the rock nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Solanum conanthum is part of the nightshade family, which includes many other toxic plants.
