Sicyos baderoa var. baderoa

Sicyos baderoa var. baderoa, commonly known as the Bader's gourd, is a variant of the genus Sicyos, which belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae.

This plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, lobed leaves that are typically green with a rough texture. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a feature common in many cucurbit species. The fruit is a spherical or oval gourd that can vary in color from green to yellow or orange depending on maturity.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional cuisines when mature.
