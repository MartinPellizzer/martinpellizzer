Sechium edule

Sechium edule, commonly known as the chayote, is a tropical vine native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It produces a pear-shaped fruit with a white, edible flesh that is low in calories and high in water content. The plant has compound leaves and bears yellow flowers that develop into the characteristic fruit. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

The chayote is often cultivated for its nutritional value and versatility in culinary use.
