Sacoila apetala

Sacoila apetala, commonly known as the naked-flowered sacoila, is a unique plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that lack petals, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Sacoila apetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive floral display.
