Secale villosum var. multiflorum

Secale villosum var. multiflorum, commonly known as the hairy wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
