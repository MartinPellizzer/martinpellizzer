Samyda decandra

Samyda decandra, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Samyda decandra is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
