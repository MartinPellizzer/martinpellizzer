Silene infidelium

Silene infidelium, commonly known as the infidelium catchfly, is a species of flowering plant in the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region, and thrives in moist, wooded habitats. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a short, upright stem.

Silene infidelium is often found in shaded areas and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
