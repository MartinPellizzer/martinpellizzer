Sophronitis perrinii

Sophronitis perrinii, commonly known as the Perin's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning it often grows on trees or rocks rather than in soil.

Sophronitis perrinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
