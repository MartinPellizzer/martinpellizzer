Sabatia macrophylla

Sabatia macrophylla, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a distinct white or silver underside.
