Sterculia incana

Sterculia incana, commonly known as the white sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, white, papery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
