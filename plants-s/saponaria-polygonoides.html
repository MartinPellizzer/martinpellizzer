Saponaria polygonoides

Saponaria polygonoides, commonly known as "Chinese soapwort," is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Saponaria polygonoides is also noted for its mild saponin content, which can produce a soapy lather when crushed.
