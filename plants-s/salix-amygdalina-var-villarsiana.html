Salix amygdalina var. villarsiana

Salix amygdalina var. villarsiana, commonly known as the white willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive white or pale greenish hue.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
