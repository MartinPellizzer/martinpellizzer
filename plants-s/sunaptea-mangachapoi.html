Sunaptea mangachapoi

Sunaptea mangachapoi, commonly known as the mangachapoi tree, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
