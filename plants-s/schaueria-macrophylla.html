Schaueria macrophylla

Schaueria macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, which float on the water's surface. The plant's showy white flowers bloom in the early morning and emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Schaueria macrophylla thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with abundant sunlight.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
