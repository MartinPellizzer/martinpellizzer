Silene cucubalus subsp. thorei

Silene cucubalus subsp.

thorei, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red.

The plant has small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats. Silene cucubalus subsp.

thorei is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
