Salsola damascena

Salsola damascena, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for survival in harsh environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor.

Salsola damascena plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in desert ecosystems.
