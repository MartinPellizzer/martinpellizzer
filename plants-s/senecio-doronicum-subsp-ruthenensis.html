Senecio doronicum subsp. ruthenensis

Senecio doronicum subsp.

ruthenensis, commonly known as the Ruthenian groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in composite heads. It has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family and is noted for its ecological adaptability and ornamental value.
