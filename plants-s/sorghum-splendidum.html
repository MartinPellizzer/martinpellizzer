Sorghum splendidum

Sorghum splendidum, commonly known as the splendid sorghum, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and striking, colorful inflorescences that range from red to purple. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Sorghum splendidum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its visual appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
