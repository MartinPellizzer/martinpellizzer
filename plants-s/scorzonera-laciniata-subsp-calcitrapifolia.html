Scorzonera laciniata subsp. calcitrapifolia

Scorzonera laciniata subsp.

calcitrapifolia, commonly known as the limestone scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in dry and sunny conditions.

The plant features dark green, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent, hairy stamens. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been historically used in traditional cuisine.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in local biodiversity.
