Salvia sclarea

Salvia sclarea, commonly known as clary sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Clary sage is known for its aromatic essential oil, which is used in perfumes and aromatherapy.

Its botanical features make it a valued plant in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
