Schizonepeta multifida

Schizonepeta multifida, commonly known as Chinese skullcap, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its branched stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. Schizonepeta multifida is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its aerial parts are used to make teas and tinctures, valued for their purported health benefits.
