Sorbus altaica

Sorbus altaica, commonly known as the Siberian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
