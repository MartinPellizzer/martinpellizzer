Salix ludingensis

Salix ludingensis, commonly known as Luding willow, is a species of willow native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. It produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction.

Salix ludingensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in ecological restoration projects.
