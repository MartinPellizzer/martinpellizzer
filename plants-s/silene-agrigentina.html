Silene agrigentina

Silene agrigentina, commonly known as the Argentine campion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has simple, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene agrigentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
