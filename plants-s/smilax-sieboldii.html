Smilax sieboldii

Smilax sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese devil's backbone, is a climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It features woody stems with sharp, spiny thorns and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Smilax sieboldii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
