Syzygium nienkui

Syzygium nienkui, commonly known as the Nienkui tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

Syzygium nienkui is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
