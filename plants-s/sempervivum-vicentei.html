Sempervivum vicentei

Sempervivum vicentei, commonly known as the Vicente's houseleek, is a species of succulent plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Sempervivum vicentei is a low-maintenance plant that is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
