Sarcocapnos crassifolia subsp. atlantis

Sarcocapnos crassifolia subsp.

atlantis, commonly known as the Atlantis rockrose, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows on rocky slopes and gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
