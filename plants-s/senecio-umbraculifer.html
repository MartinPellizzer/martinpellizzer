Senecio umbraculifer

Senecio umbraculifer, commonly known as the shadowy groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton or wool. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Senecio umbraculifer is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
