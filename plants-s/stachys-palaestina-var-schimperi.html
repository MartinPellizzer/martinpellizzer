Stachys palaestina var. schimperi

Stachys palaestina var. schimperi, commonly known as the Jordanian hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
