Sarcochilus thycola

Sarcochilus thycola, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive greenish-yellow color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas. It has a unique morphology with a short, stout stem and a spurred lip that is characteristic of the Sarcochilus genus.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
