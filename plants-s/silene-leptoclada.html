Silene leptoclada

Silene leptoclada, commonly known as the slender-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Silene leptoclada is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
