Syzygium longissimum

Syzygium longissimum, commonly known as the long-stemmed starfruit, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, star-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The flowers are small, white, and fragrant, often appearing in clusters, followed by the characteristic star-shaped fruit that matures to a yellow or orange color.
