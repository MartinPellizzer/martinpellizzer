Senecio pinifolius var. macrocephalus

Senecio pinifolius var. macrocephalus, commonly known as the giant yellow ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
