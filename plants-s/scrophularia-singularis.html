Scrophularia singularis

Scrophularia singularis, commonly known as the singular scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. The plant features erect stems that are usually hairy, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have serrated edges. Its flowers are tubular, typically pink or purple, and bloom in the summer months.

Scrophularia singularis is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.
