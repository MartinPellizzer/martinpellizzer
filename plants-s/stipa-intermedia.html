Stipa intermedia

Stipa intermedia, commonly known as intermediate feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to form dense clumps. The plant produces slender, awned spikelets that are often tinged with red or brown, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Stipa intermedia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
