Silene armeria f. albiflora

Silene armeria f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and showy white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals and prominent stamens, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is notable for its pale, almost porcelain-like flowers, which contrast with the green foliage.
