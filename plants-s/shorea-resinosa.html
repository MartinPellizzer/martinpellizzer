Shorea resinosa

Shorea resinosa, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. It thrives in humid, lowland rainforest environments and plays a vital role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to the forest canopy.

The plant is also noted for its ability to produce a valuable resin, which has been used historically in various applications.
