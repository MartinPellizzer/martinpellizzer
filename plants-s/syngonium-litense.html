Syngonium litense

Syngonium litense, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that vary in color from light green to deep maroon. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Syngonium litense is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb trellises or other supports.
