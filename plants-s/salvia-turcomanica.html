Salvia turcomanica

Salvia turcomanica, commonly known as Turcomanian sage, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a minty scent.

Salvia turcomanica is often grown for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
