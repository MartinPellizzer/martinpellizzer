Stemodia lobelioides

Stemodia lobelioides, commonly known as the swamp bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear whorled leaves, which are often lance-shaped and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
