Solidago spathulata var. gillmanii

Solidago spathulata var. gillmanii, commonly known as Gillman's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or marshy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive basal rosette, while its stems are covered in fine, hairy hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
