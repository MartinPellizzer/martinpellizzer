Strigilia leiophylla

Strigilia leiophylla, commonly known as the smooth-leaf strigil, is a small, delicate herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, often with a slight pink hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species.
