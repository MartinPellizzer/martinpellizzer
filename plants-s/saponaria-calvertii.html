Saponaria calvertii

Saponaria calvertii, commonly known as Calvert's soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Saponaria calvertii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
