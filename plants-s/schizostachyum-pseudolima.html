Schizostachyum pseudolima

Schizostachyum pseudolima, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, woody grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Schizostachyum pseudolima is often used in landscaping and as a source of sustainable building materials.
