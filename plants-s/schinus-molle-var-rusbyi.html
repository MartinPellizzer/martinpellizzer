Schinus molle var. rusbyi

Schinus molle var. rusbyi, commonly known as the rusby's velvet tamarack, is a variant of the velvet tamarack, native to parts of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and distinctive red berries. The leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface, and the plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
