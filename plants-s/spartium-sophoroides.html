Spartium sophoroides

Spartium sophoroides, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Spartium sophoroides has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
