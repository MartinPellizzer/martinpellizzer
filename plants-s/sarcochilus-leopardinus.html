Sarcochilus leopardinus

Sarcochilus leopardinus, commonly known as the leopard orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves and striking, showy flowers that resemble leopard spots, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in spring or early summer.

Sarcochilus leopardinus is a hardy species that requires well-drained soil and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
