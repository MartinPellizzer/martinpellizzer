Silphium ternatum var. atropurpureum

Silphium ternatum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as purple silphium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its historical significance and rarity, it is considered a valuable species in ecological and botanical studies.
