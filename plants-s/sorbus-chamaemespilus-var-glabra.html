Sorbus chamaemespilus var. glabra

Sorbus chamaemespilus var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and alternate, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries that attract birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
