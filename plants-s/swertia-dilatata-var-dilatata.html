Swertia dilatata var. dilatata

Swertia dilatata var. dilatata, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes at the tips of the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
