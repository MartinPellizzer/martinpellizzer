Spiraea lindleyana

Spiraea lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Spiraea lindleyana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
