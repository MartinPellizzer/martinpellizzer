Solidago integerrima

Solidago integerrima, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are rough to the touch, hence the common name, and its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Solidago integerrima plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
