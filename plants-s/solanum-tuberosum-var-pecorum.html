Solanum tuberosum var. pecorum

Solanum tuberosum var. pecorum, commonly known as the Pecorum potato, is a variant of the common potato with distinct botanical characteristics.

It features a robust root system and tubers that are typically larger and more elongated compared to standard potato varieties. The plant produces violet-colored flowers, typical of the Solanaceae family, and has hairy stems and leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile and potential agricultural adaptability.
