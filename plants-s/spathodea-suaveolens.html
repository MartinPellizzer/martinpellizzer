Spathodea suaveolens

Spathodea suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces distinctive bracts that enclose the actual flowers, giving it a unique appearance. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Spathodea suaveolens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
