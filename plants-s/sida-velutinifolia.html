Sida velutinifolia

Sida velutinifolia, commonly known as velvetleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Sida velutinifolia is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential weed in agricultural settings.
