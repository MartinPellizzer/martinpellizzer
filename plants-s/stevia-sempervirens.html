Stevia sempervirens

Stevia sempervirens, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant is renowned for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are naturally occurring sweeteners. Stevia sempervirens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its use as a natural sweetener has gained popularity due to its low caloric content and health benefits.
