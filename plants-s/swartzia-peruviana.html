Swartzia peruviana

Swartzia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian blackwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes.

Swartzia peruviana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in its natural habitat.
