Spermacoce semiamplexicaulis

Spermacoce semiamplexicaulis, commonly known as the creeping spurred morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
