Swertia iberica var. albida

Swertia iberica var. albida, commonly known as white swertia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in loose clusters.

Swertia iberica var. albida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
