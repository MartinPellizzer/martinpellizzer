Symplocos elegans var. elegans

Symplocos elegans var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant wax myrtle, is a species native to parts of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it its common name. Its flowers are clustered in small, spherical inflorescences and are usually white or pale yellow, while the fruit is a small, dark blue to black drupe.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
