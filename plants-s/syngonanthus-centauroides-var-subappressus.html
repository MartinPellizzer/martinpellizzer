Syngonanthus centauroides var. subappressus

Syngonanthus centauroides var. subappressus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or yellow and contain sticky seeds that adhere to birds for dispersal.

This species is found primarily in tropical and subtropical regions of South America.
