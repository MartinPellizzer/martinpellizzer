Stachys × cryptadenia

Stachys × cryptadenia, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Stachys × cryptadenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
