Senegalia moggii

Senegalia moggii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves, which are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, and it is often used for soil stabilization and as a source of browse for livestock.

Senegalia moggii is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in traditional medicine.
