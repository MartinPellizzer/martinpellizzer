Sabulina armena

Sabulina armena, commonly known as Armenian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant has small, clustered leaves and produces delicate white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including cold temperatures and limited soil nutrients.

Sabulina armena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
