Senecio dryophyllus

Senecio dryophyllus, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and drought conditions.

Senecio dryophyllus plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
