Sideritis hyssopifolia var. longicaulis

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it typically grows in rocky or dry soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
