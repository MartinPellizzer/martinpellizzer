Sedum sinoglaciale

Sedum sinoglaciale, commonly known as the alpine stonecrop, is a perennial succulent plant native to high-altitude regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sedum sinoglaciale is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
