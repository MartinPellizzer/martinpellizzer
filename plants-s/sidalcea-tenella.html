Sidalcea tenella

Sidalcea tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf checker-mallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. The plant has slender, branching stems and produces small, showy pink or purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive hairy texture.
