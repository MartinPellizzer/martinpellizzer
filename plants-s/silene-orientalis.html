Silene orientalis

Silene orientalis, commonly known as the eastern campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and woodland edges. Silene orientalis has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers.
