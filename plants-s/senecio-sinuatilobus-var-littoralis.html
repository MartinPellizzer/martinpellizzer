Senecio sinuatilobus var. littoralis

Senecio sinuatilobus var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal senecio, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing along sandy or rocky coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
