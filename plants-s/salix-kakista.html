Salix kakista

Salix kakista, commonly known as the Kakista willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
