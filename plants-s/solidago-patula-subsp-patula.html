Solidago patula subsp. patula

Solidago patula subsp.

patula, commonly known as the eastern goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
