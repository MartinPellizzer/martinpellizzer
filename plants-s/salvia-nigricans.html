Salvia nigricans

Salvia nigricans, commonly known as black sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Salvia nigricans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
