Saxifraga tombeanensis

Saxifraga tombeanensis, commonly known as the Tombean saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Saxifraga tombeanensis is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
