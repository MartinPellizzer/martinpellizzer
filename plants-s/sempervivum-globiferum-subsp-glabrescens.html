Sempervivum globiferum subsp. glabrescens

Sempervivum globiferum subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the Woolly Houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features dense, fleshy rosettes of pale green to bluish-gray foliage covered in a fine layer of white woolly hairs.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens or container planting.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
