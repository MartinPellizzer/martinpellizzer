Senecio decaisnei

Senecio decaisnei, commonly known as the Cape Harebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio decaisnei is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
