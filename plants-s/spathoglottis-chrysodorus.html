Spathoglottis chrysodorus

Spathoglottis chrysodorus, commonly known as the golden root orchid, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination by insects.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
