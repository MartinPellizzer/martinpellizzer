Syzygium masukuense subsp. pachyphyllum

Syzygium masukuense subsp.

pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved satinash, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
