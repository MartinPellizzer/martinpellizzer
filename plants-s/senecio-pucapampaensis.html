Senecio pucapampaensis

Senecio pucapampaensis, commonly known as the Pucapampa groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Senecio pucapampaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
