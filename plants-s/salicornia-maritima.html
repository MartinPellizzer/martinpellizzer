Salicornia maritima

Salicornia maritima, commonly known as sea beans or glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems that store water. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and estuaries, where it tolerates high salt concentrations. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or red, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Salicornia maritima plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
