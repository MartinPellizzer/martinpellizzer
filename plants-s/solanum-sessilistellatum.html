Solanum sessilistellatum

Solanum sessilistellatum, commonly known as the purple nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for consumption or cultivation in edible gardens.
