Styrax americanus f. pulverulentus

Styrax americanus f. pulverulentus, commonly known as the powdered styrax, is a rare and unique variant of the American styrax.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white, powdery appearance on its foliage, which gives it its distinguishing name. This form is typically found in specific microhabitats, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, woody capsules.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
