Senecio schaffneri

Senecio schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wooly, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive seed heads.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
