Senecio huaquilicus

Senecio huaquilicus, commonly known as the Huaquilic Daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Senecio huaquilicus is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
