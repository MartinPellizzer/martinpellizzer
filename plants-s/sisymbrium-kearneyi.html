Sisymbrium kearneyi

Sisymbrium kearneyi, commonly known as Kearney's winter cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in a siliqua, a dry, pod-like fruit typical of the mustard family.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in the western United States.
