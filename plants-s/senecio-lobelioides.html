Senecio lobelioides

Senecio lobelioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodland edges. It produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio lobelioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
