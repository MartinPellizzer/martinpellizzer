Stenostomum albobrunneum

Stenostomum albobrunneum, commonly known as the white-barked stenostomum, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pale green to yellow in color.

Stenostomum albobrunneum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
