Salvia bonplandiana

Salvia bonplandiana, commonly known as the Mexican sage or Bonpland's sage, is a striking perennial shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and vibrant purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowering stems are often branched, supporting clusters of tubular flowers. Salvia bonplandiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
