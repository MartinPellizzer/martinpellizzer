Specklinia crenata

Specklinia crenata, commonly known as the crested staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, branching structure that resembles a staghorn, with a central stem and multiple fiddlehead-like offshoots. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the wild. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are rarely seen.

Specklinia crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in terrariums.
