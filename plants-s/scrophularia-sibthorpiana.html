Scrophularia sibthorpiana

Scrophularia sibthorpiana, commonly known as the Sibthorpian figwort, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It is part of the Plantaginaceae family, which includes many species with similar morphological traits.

Scrophularia sibthorpiana is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
