Saussurea rufotricha

Saussurea rufotricha, commonly known as the red-haired saussurea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and dense clusters of yellowish to brownish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered with a fine, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Saussurea rufotricha is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
