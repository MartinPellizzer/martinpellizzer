Spondias globosa

Spondias globosa, commonly known as the globe spondias, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are typically alternate along the branches. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, round, orange-red fruits resembling small globes. Its fruit is highly valued for its sweet, tangy flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary preparations.

Spondias globosa is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
