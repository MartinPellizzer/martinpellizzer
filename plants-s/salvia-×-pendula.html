Salvia × pendula

Salvia × pendula, commonly known as the weeping sage, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. Native to the western United States, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
