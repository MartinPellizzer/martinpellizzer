Saussurea arctecapitulata

Saussurea arctecapitulata, commonly known as the Arctic gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is often found in meadows and on mountain slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
