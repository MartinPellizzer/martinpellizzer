Scutellaria atriplicifolia

Scutellaria atriplicifolia, commonly known as blue beadvetch, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria atriplicifolia is often found in moist, wooded areas and has a preference for partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
