Sasa kurilensis var. hirta

Sasa kurilensis var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy dwarf bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles, and its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and hardy nature.
