Sedum × nicaeense var. saguntinum

Sedum × nicaeense var. saguntinum, commonly known as the Saguntinum stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
