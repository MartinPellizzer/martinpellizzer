Sida brasila

Sida brasila, commonly known as Brazilian mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Sida brasila is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
