Scilla lepida

Scilla lepida, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Scilla lepida is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread readily in suitable conditions.
