Sorghum dulcicaule

Sorghum dulcicaule, commonly known as sweet sorghum, is a tall, warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall stalks and large, loose panicles that produce abundant seeds. The plant is notable for its high sugar content in its juice, making it valuable for biofuel and syrup production. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in dry, sunny conditions.

Sorghum dulcicaule is also used as forage for livestock and has cultural significance in many regions.
