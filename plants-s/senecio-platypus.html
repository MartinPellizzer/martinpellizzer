Senecio platypus

Senecio platypus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio platypus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
