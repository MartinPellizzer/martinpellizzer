Sasa kurilensis f. shimashimafuri

Sasa kurilensis f. shimashimafuri, commonly known as the Shima Shima Furi bamboo, is a unique form of the Japanese bamboo species.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and finely textured foliage. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often hidden within its thick foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions of Japan.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural landscapes.
