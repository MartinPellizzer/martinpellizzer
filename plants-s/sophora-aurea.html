Sophora aurea

Sophora aurea, commonly known as golden sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, and it produces elongated pods containing flat, dark brown seeds.

Sophora aurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
