Staphylea japonica f. eburnea

Staphylea japonica f. eburnea, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a cultivar of the Staphylea genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its elegant floral display and is relatively low maintenance.
