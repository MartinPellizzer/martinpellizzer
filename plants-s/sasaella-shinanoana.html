Sasaella shinanoana

Sasaella shinanoana, commonly known as the Shinano bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that form dense clusters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are usually a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Sasaella shinanoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to shady, moist environments.

It plays a role in traditional Japanese gardens and is valued for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
