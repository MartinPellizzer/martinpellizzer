Scirpus chen-moui

Scirpus chen-moui, commonly known as the Chinese bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically found at the top of the stem. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form dense colonies in muddy or wet soils.

Scirpus chen-moui plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
