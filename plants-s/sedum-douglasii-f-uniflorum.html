Sedum douglasii f. uniflorum

Sedum douglasii f. uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered Douglas sedum, is a succulent plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces a single, upright flower stem that bears several yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is a compact, low-maintenance plant that is well adapted to arid environments.
