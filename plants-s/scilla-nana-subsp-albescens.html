Scilla nana subsp. albescens

Scilla nana subsp.

albescens, commonly known as the white scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its small size, typically reaching heights of only a few inches. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
