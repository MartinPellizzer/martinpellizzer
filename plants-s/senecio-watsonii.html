Senecio watsonii

Senecio watsonii, commonly known as Watson's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by tufts of silky, white to grayish seeds.

Senecio watsonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
