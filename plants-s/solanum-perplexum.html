Solanum perplexum

Solanum perplexum, commonly known as the perplexing nightshade, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its fruits are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals.

Solanum perplexum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or thickets.
