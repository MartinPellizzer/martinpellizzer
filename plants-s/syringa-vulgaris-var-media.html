Syringa vulgaris var. media

Syringa vulgaris var. media, commonly known as the weeping syringa, is a cultivar of the common lilac that is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, pale purple flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet, with a broad, rounded form. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, and it is known for its showy, early summer blooms.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
