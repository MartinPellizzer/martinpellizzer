Stevia seemanioides

Stevia seemanioides, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing high levels of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Stevia seemanioides is also cultivated as a low-maintenance ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
