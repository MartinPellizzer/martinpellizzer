Saxifraga parvicalyx

Saxifraga parvicalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often densely packed along its stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in early summer.

Saxifraga parvicalyx is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in rock gardens and alpine settings.
