Senecio alabugensis

Senecio alabugensis, commonly known as the Alabuga groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and serrated, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
