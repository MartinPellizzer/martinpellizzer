Scutellaria macrostegia

Scutellaria macrostegia, commonly known as the broad-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a rough, hairy texture.

Scutellaria macrostegia is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
