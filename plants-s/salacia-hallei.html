Salacia hallei

Salacia hallei, commonly known as the Halle's salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and elliptical leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
