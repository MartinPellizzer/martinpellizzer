Scirpus amentaceus

Scirpus amentaceus, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in shallow water or moist soil. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in a spike that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

Scirpus amentaceus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
