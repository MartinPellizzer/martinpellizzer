Solanum lepidotum

Solanum lepidotum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and they contain toxic compounds belonging to the solanine family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for ornamental or edible purposes.
