Salsola vermiformis

Salsola vermiformis, commonly known as sandmat or desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has a low-growing, sprawling habit, which helps it conserve moisture and avoid desiccation.

Salsola vermiformis plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitats.
