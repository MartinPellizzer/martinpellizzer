Sasa arikae var. nasuensis

Sasa arikae var. nasuensis, commonly known as the Nasu bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa arikae species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and cuisine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
