Sclerochaetium koenigii

Sclerochaetium koenigii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, citrus-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its botanical significance lies in its medicinal properties and use in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

The fruit's thick, astringent skin contains high levels of antioxidants and is widely used in herbal remedies.
