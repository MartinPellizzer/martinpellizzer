Sempervivum leucoblepharum

Sempervivum leucoblepharum, commonly known as the White-eyed Houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Alps of Europe.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or silver markings, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in well-drained soil.

Sempervivum leucoblepharum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
