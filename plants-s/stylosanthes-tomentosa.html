Stylosanthes tomentosa

Stylosanthes tomentosa, commonly known as hairy stylo, is a perennial leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions. It is widely used as a forage crop for livestock due to its high nutritional value and hardiness.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility in agricultural systems.
