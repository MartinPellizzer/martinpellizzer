Sarcococca ruscifolia var. chinensis

Sarcococca ruscifolia var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late winter or early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is prized for its compact form and year-round ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
