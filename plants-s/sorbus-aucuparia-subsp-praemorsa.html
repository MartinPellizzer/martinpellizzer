Sorbus aucuparia subsp. praemorsa

Sorbus aucuparia subsp.

praemorsa, commonly known as the white-thorned rowan, is a subspecies of the rowan tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
