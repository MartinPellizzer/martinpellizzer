Senecio iranicus

Senecio iranicus, commonly known as Persian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a woolly texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer months.

Senecio iranicus is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
