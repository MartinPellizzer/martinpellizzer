Scrophularia gaubae

Scrophularia gaubae, commonly known as the African figwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal uses.

Scrophularia gaubae is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
