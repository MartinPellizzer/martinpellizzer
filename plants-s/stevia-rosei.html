Stevia rosei

Stevia rosei, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is renowned for its intense sweet flavor, which comes from compounds called steviol glycosides. Its leaves are often used as a natural sweetener in food and beverages.

Stevia rosei thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
