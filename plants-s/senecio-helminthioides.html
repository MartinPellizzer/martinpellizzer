Senecio helminthioides

Senecio helminthioides, commonly known as the bladderleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant has small, inflated leaves that resemble bladders, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Senecio helminthioides is adapted to arid conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
