Senecio amplectens var. holmii

Senecio amplectens var. holmii, commonly known as the Holm's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It is a perennial herb that produces small, daisy-like flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
