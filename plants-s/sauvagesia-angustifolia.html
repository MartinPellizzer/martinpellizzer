Sauvagesia angustifolia

Sauvagesia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sauvagesia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slight pubescence on the underside.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
