Sloetia

Sloetia, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Sloetia is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and succulent leaves that help it conserve water.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in dry landscapes.
