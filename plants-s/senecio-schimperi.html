Senecio schimperi

Senecio schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

Senecio schimperi is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in local herbal remedies.
