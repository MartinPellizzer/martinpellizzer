Stevia grazielae

Stevia grazielae, commonly known as the Brazilian sweet leaf, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and it is cultivated for its intensely sweet leaves, which contain high levels of steviol glycosides. Stevia grazielae is often used as a natural sweetener and has gained popularity in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a valuable plant in both traditional and modern botanical applications.
