Scorzonera acaulis

Scorzonera acaulis, commonly known as the naked stem scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant features a short, thick rootstock and lacks true stems, with leaves that are typically basal and oblanceolate in shape. Its flowers are yellow and daisy-like, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Scorzonera acaulis is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
