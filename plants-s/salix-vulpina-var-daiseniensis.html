Salix vulpina var. daiseniensis

Salix vulpina var. daiseniensis, commonly known as the Daiseniensis willow, is a variant of the common willow that thrives in moist environments.

It is characterized by its slender, often weeping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in wetlands or along rivers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
