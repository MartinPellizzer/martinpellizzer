Scorzonera lanata var. latifolia

Scorzonera lanata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features a robust taproot that is often harvested for its edible tubers, which have a nutty flavor. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in late summer.

Scorzonera lanata var. latifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
