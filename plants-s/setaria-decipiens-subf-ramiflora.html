Setaria decipiens subf. ramiflora

Setaria decipiens subf.

ramiflora, commonly known as the false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often mistaken for true panicgrass due to their similar appearance. Its flowers are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
