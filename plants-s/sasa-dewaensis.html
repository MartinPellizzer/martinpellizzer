Sasa dewaensis

Sasa dewaensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense stands, often growing in shaded, moist environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional Japanese gardens.

Sasa dewaensis is also known for its resilience and ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.
