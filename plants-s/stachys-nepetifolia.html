Stachys nepetifolia

Stachys nepetifolia, commonly known as caraway leaved betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs and produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, often with a distinctive caraway-like aroma when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
