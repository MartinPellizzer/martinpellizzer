Scilla africana

Scilla africana, commonly known as the African scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to naturalize in gardens.

Scilla africana is also valued for its resilience and ability to bloom reliably in temperate climates.
