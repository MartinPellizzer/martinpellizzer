Symplocarpus foetidus f. variegatus

Symplocarpus foetidus f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated skunk cabbage, is a variant of the skunk cabbage plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves with white and green variegation, which distinguish it from the typical solid green leaves of the species. The plant produces a single, large, purple-tinged spathe that encloses a spadix, a feature typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors, often in areas with rich organic soil.

The plant is known for its unique ability to generate heat through thermogenesis, aiding in the attraction of pollinators.
