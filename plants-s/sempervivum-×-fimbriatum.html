Sempervivum × fimbriatum

Sempervivum × fimbriatum, commonly known as the variegated houseleek, is a hybrid succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, rosette-forming leaves that are often variegated with white, yellow, or pink markings. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
