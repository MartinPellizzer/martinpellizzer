Solanum ochrophyllum

Solanum ochrophyllum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. It produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution.

Solanum ochrophyllum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.
