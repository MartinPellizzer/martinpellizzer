Sarracenia × maddisoniana

Sarracenia × maddisoniana, commonly known as the Maddison's pitcher plant, is a hybrid species within the Sarraceniaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, pitcher-shaped leaves that are typically red or orange with dark red veins. These leaves function as pitfall traps, secreting a digestive enzyme-laden liquid to break down captured insects. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor wetlands.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous nature make it a popular specimen among plant enthusiasts and botanists alike.
