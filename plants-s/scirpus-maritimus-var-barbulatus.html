Scirpus maritimus var. barbulatus

Scirpus maritimus var. barbulatus, commonly known as the soft-stemmed bulrush, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, cylindrical clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often forming dense stands that stabilize shorelines. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, velvety texture.

This variety is adapted to tidal environments and plays a crucial role in providing habitat for various aquatic and avian species.
