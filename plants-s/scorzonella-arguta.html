Scorzonella arguta

Scorzonella arguta, commonly known as the slender scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, narrow leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Scorzonella arguta is valued in some regions for its edible roots, which are used in traditional cuisine.
