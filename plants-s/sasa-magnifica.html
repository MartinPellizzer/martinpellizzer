Sasa magnifica

Sasa magnifica, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the forests of East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and bright yellow-green foliage. The plant forms dense clumps through rhizome growth, creating a thick ground cover. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often reaching lengths of up to 1 meter.

Sasa magnifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
