Seruneum macrodontum

Seruneum macrodontum, commonly known as the giant toothed fern, is a strikingly unique plant characterized by its large, toothed fronds.

Native to tropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with rich, moist soil. The plant's fronds can grow up to several meters in length, featuring a distinctive serrated edge that sets it apart from other fern species. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing cones, are positioned along the fronds, contributing to its botanical complexity.

Due to its size and appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens and private collections.
