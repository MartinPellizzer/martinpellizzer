Salix gordejevii

Salix gordejevii, commonly known as the Gorbachev willow, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
