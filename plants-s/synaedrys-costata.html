Synaedrys costata

Synaedrys costata, commonly known as the serrated thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
