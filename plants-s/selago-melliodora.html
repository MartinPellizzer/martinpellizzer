Selago melliodora

Selago melliodora, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Selago melliodora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
