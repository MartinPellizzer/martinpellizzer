Saguaster gracilis

Saguaster gracilis, commonly known as the slender saguaro, is a rare and delicate cactus species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that grow slowly and can reach heights of up to 10 feet. Unlike the more common saguaro (Carnegiea gigantea), it has a more fragile structure and is less robust. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bats and birds.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Saguaster gracilis is considered an endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
