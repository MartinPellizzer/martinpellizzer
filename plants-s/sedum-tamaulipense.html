Sedum tamaulipense

Sedum tamaulipense, commonly known as the Tamaulipan sedum, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of northeastern Mexico.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint, adapted for water storage in its native habitat. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
