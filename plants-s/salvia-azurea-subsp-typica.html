Salvia azurea subsp. typica

Salvia azurea subsp.

typica, commonly known as the blue sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
