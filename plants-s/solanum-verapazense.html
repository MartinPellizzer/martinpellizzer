Solanum verapazense

Solanum verapazense, commonly known as the Verapaz nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and less-studied species.
