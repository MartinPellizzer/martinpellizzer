Silene swertiifolia var. straussiana

Silene swertiifolia var. straussiana, commonly known as the Strauss's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
