Sambucus pubens var. xanthocarpa

Sambucus pubens var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that transition from green to yellow, which are edible when ripe but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
