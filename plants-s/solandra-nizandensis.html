Solandra nizandensis

Solandra nizandensis, commonly known as the Nizandean lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its fruits are fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds.

Solandra nizandensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
