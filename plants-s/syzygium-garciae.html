Syzygium garciae

Syzygium garciae, commonly known as the Garcia's tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, sour-tasting fruits that are similar in appearance to tamarinds. Syzygium garciae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
