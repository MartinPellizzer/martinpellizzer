Statice frederici

Statice frederici, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its showy, spike-like flower heads that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its narrow, linear leaves are covered in a fine layer of salt-excreting glands, which help it tolerate harsh maritime conditions.

Statice frederici is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
