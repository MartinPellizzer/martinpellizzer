Scilla taurica

Scilla taurica, commonly known as the Tauric scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scilla taurica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
