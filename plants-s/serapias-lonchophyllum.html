Serapias lonchophyllum

Serapias lonchophyllum, commonly known as the long-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single flowering stem that bears several flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Serapias lonchophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
