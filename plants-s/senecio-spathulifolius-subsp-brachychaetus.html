Senecio spathulifolius subsp. brachychaetus

Senecio spathulifolius subsp.

brachychaetus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
