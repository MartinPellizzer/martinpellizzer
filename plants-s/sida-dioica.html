Sida dioica

Sida dioica, commonly known as yellow mallow, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are usually hairy as well. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Sida dioica is known for its fibrous roots and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
