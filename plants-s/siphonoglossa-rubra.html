Siphonoglossa rubra

Siphonoglossa rubra, commonly known as the red-tongued orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and green floral structures, which are adapted for attracting specific pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are known for their intricate morphology, featuring specialized labellum structures that aid in pollination.
