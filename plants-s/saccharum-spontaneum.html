Saccharum spontaneum

Saccharum spontaneum, commonly known as wild sugarcane, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stalks and long, narrow leaves, which can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces inflorescences that are often panicle-like, containing small, spikelet-based flowers. It is a member of the Poaceae family and is closely related to the genus Saccharum, which includes commercially important sugarcane varieties.

Saccharum spontaneum is known for its high sugar content and is often used in breeding programs to improve the resilience and productivity of cultivated sugarcane.
