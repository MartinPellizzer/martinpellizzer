Silene dasyphylla

Silene dasyphylla, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Silene dasyphylla is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.
