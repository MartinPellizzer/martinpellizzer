Stuckertiella peregrina

Stuckertiella peregrina, commonly known as the wandering bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Stuckertiella peregrina is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
