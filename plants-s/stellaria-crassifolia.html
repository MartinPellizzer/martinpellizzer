Stellaria crassifolia

Stellaria crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf starwort, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, broad, ovate leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.
