Sabina saxicola

Sabina saxicola, commonly known as the rock juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast against its rugged habitat. The plant produces small, spherical blue berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, Sabina saxicola is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
