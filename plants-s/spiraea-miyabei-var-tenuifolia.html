Spiraea miyabei var. tenuifolia

Spiraea miyabei var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its graceful, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
