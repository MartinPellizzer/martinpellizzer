Salmonopuntia schickendantzii

Salmonopuntia schickendantzii, commonly known as the red-tipped ponytail cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-tipped, cylindrical stems that resemble a ponytail, hence its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
