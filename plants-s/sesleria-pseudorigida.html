Sesleria pseudorigida

Sesleria pseudorigida, commonly known as the rigid false oat, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sesleria pseudorigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact, decorative appearance.
