Syngonanthus densifolius var. brachyphyllus

Syngonanthus densifolius var. brachyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, dense, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small white or pink flowers, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fleshy, red berries.

This species is native to parts of South America and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
