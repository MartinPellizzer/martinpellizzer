Syzygium laetum subsp. jugorum

Syzygium laetum subsp.

jugorum, commonly known as the red-fruited tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers.

The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and have a tart, sour flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish fruit color and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the main species.
