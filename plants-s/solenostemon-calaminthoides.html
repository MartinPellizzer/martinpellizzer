Solenostemon calaminthoides

Solenostemon calaminthoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features soft, fuzzy blue-gray foliage and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that resemble mist. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is often used in landscape design for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Solenostemon calaminthoides is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic leaves.
