Solanum citrifolium var. ochrandrum

Solanum citrifolium var. ochrandrum, commonly known as the yellow-leafed nightshade, is a variant of the citrusleaf nightshade species.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow foliage, which contrasts with the typical green leaves of the species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to berries that can range in color from yellow to orange. It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
