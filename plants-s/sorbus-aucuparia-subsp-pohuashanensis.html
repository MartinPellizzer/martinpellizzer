Sorbus aucuparia subsp. pohuashanensis

Sorbus aucuparia subsp.

pohuashanensis, commonly known as the Pohuashan whitebeams, is a subspecies native to China, specifically found in the Pohuashan area. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions with rocky soils.

The plant is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that appear in spring, followed by red or orange berries in autumn. Its leaves are elliptical, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
