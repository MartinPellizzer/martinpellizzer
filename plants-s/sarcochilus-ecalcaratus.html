Sarcochilus ecalcaratus

Sarcochilus ecalcaratus, commonly known as the black-veined helleborine, is an epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, leathery leaf and produces dark purple to black flowers with white veins. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spike and bloom during the wet season.

Sarcochilus ecalcaratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
