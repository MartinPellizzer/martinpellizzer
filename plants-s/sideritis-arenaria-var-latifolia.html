Sideritis arenaria var. latifolia

Sideritis arenaria var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pink or purple flowers in spikes. It is often found in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Sideritis arenaria var. latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
