Salacistis rubicunda

Salacistis rubicunda, commonly known as the red-stemmed salacida, is a striking plant native to the temperate forests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in the spring. Its foliage is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional healing practices.

Salacistis rubicunda thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
