Silene stricta

Silene stricta, commonly known as the upright campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are linear and arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene stricta is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
