Smyrnium rotundifolium

Smyrnium rotundifolium, commonly known as round-leaved cow-wheat, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and umbellate inflorescence.

It features heart-shaped, rounded leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive, slightly lobed appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Smyrnium rotundifolium is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
