Sinobambusa maculata

Sinobambusa maculata, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often display a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern. It grows in clusters and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Sinobambusa maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
