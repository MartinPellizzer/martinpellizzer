Salvia sigchosica

Salvia sigchosica, commonly known as the Sigchos sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for various health purposes.

Salvia sigchosica is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological and cultural significance.
