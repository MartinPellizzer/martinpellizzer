Solidago canadensis var. scabra

Solidago canadensis var. scabra, commonly known as rough goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
