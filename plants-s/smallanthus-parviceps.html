Smallanthus parviceps

Smallanthus parviceps, commonly known as the Andean spinach, is a leafy plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, edible leaves that are often used in traditional Andean cuisine. The plant thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a short stem, hairy foliage, and small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is closely related to other members of the Chenopodium family, which includes quinoa and spinach.
