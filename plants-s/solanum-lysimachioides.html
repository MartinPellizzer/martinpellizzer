Solanum lysimachioides

Solanum lysimachioides, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features slender, trailing stems that produce small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or purple. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.
