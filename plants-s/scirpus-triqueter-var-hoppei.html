Scirpus triqueter var. hoppei

Scirpus triqueter var. hoppei, commonly known as the Hoppe's three-angled bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, cylindrical inflorescences that rise above the water. The plant thrives in shallow water and moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that aids in its structural integrity.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic life.
