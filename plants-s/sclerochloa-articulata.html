Sclerochloa articulata

Sclerochloa articulata, commonly known as the jointed grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important component of desert and grassland ecosystems.

Sclerochloa articulata is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
