Scilla Flexuosa

Scilla flexuosa is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family known for its delicate, bell-shaped flowers. Native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East, this plant thrives in rocky and woodland habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care. Despite its beauty, it remains a relatively obscure species among gardeners.
