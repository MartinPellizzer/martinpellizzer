Sinapis cheiranthos var. corbariensis

Sinapis cheiranthos var. corbariensis, commonly known as the Corbariense mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard species. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
