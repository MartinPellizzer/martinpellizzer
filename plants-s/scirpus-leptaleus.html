Scirpus leptaleus

Scirpus leptaleus, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and cylindrical inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Scirpus leptaleus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
