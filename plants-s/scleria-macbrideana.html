Scleria macbrideana

Scleria macbrideana, commonly known as the MacBride's sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Scleria macbrideana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
