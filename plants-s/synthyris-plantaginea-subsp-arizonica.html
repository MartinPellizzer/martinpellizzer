Synthyris plantaginea subsp. arizonica

Synthyris plantaginea subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as Arizona synthyris, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable component of arid ecosystems.
