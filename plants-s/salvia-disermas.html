Salvia disermas

Salvia disermas, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing nutrients from the soil.

Salvia disermas is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
