Saxifraga barbuloides

Saxifraga barbuloides, commonly known as the woolly saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Saxifraga barbuloides is adapted to cold climates and is a hardy species that can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
