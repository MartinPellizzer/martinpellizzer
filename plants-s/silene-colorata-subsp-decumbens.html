Silene colorata subsp. decumbens

Silene colorata subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit, with stems that lie flat on the ground.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
