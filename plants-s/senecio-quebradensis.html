Senecio quebradensis

Senecio quebradensis, commonly known as the Quebrada Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to arid conditions with its succulent leaves. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
