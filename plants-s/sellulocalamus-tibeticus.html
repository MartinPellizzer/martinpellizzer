Sellulocalamus tibeticus

Sellulocalamus tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a hollow, segmented structure typical of bamboo species. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, adapting to the cold and arid conditions of its native habitat. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species in its environment.

Due to its resilience and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
