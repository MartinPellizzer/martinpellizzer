Solanum aridicola

Solanum aridicola, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries and hairy, glandular leaves adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often blooming at night. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh environments.

Solanum aridicola plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various animals and insects.
