Scorzonella maxima

Scorzonella maxima, commonly known as the giant scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small, oval-shaped seeds.

Scorzonella maxima is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.
