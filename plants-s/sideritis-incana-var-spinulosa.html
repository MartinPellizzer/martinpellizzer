Sideritis incana var. spinulosa

Sideritis incana var. spinulosa, commonly known as the hairy rock sedge, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
