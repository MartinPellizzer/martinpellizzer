Sideritis brevibracteata

Sideritis brevibracteata, commonly known as the short-bract ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Sideritis brevibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
