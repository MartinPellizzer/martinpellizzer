Sideritis algarviensis

Sideritis algarviensis, commonly known as Algarviensis sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in dry, sunny habitats.

Sideritis algarviensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
