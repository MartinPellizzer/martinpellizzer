Sonchus ustulatus subsp. maderensis

Sonchus ustulatus subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the Maderan sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is often found in temperate climates. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by achene seeds.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a distinct, slightly bitter taste.
