Sorbus aria var. typica

Sorbus aria var. typica, commonly known as whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow berries that persist through winter. The leaves are typically oval or ovate, with serrated edges, and turn bright red in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
