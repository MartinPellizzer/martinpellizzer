Styrax faberi

Styrax faberi, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and oval in shape, with a smooth texture.

Styrax faberi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
