Stenogyne rugosa var. pubens

Stenogyne rugosa var. pubens, commonly known as the rough-hairy stenogyne, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in native forests and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
