Stobaea cirsiifolia

Stobaea cirsiifolia, commonly known as the woolly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Stobaea cirsiifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
