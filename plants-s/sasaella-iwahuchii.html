Sasaella iwahuchii

Sasaella iwahuchii, commonly known as the Iwahuchii bamboo, is a unique grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
