Searsia scytophylla var. dentata

Searsia scytophylla var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated bristle bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to dry conditions.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.
