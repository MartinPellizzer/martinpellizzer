Specklinia ximenae

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to its host, while its leaves are typically divided into numerous narrow segments.

Specklinia ximenae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
