Stemmacantha lyrata

Stemmacantha lyrata, commonly known as the lyreleaf stemmacantha, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive lyre-shaped leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a striking appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub, with woody stems that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in terminal inflorescences, and come in shades of white or pale yellow.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
