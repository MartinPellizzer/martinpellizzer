Sedum pruinatum var. virescens

Sedum pruinatum var. virescens, commonly known as the blue sedum, is a low-growing succulent native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense clusters of blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an adaptable and ornamental plant for various garden settings.
