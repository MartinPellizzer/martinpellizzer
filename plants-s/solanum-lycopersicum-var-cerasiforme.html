Solanum lycopersicum var. cerasiforme

Solanum lycopersicum var. cerasiforme, commonly known as the cherry tomato, is a variant of the common tomato plant characterized by its small, round, and often brightly colored fruits.

The plant is a herbaceous perennial in its native habitat but is typically grown as an annual in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces flowers that are typically yellow or white. The fruit develops from the flower and is prized for its sweet flavor and vibrant appearance.

This variety is popular in both home gardens and commercial agriculture due to its adaptability and culinary versatility.
