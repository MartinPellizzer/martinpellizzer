Sideritis candicans var. candicans

Sideritis candicans var. candicans, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
