Sempervivum bentejui

Sempervivum bentejui, commonly known as the Bentejui houseleek, is a low-growing succulent belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is native to the Pyrenees region and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
