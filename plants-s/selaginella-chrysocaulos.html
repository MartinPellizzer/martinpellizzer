Selaginella chrysocaulos

Selaginella chrysocaulos, commonly known as the golden-cushion spikemoss, is a terrestrial moss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The plant features small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, with a distinctive yellowish or golden coloration on the stems and leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Selaginella chrysocaulos is a short-lived perennial, with its life cycle involving both sexual and asexual reproduction.
