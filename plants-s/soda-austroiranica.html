Soda austroiranica

Soda austroiranica, commonly known as the Iranian soda plant, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, saline environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates. It produces small, edible seeds that are sometimes used in local cuisines.

Its ability to thrive in poor soils makes it an important plant for ecological restoration in degraded landscapes.
