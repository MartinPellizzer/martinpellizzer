Senecio lazicus

Senecio lazicus, commonly known as the Lazik's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with gray-green, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique adaptations, Senecio lazicus is often found in rocky or sandy soils where it thrives in full sun.
