Smilax walteri

Smilax walteri, commonly known as Walter's greenbriar, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
