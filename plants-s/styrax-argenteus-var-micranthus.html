Styrax argenteus var. micranthus

Styrax argenteus var. micranthus, commonly known as silver flame or silver styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white variegated foliage, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, dark red berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its unique appearance.
