Saxifraga rufidula f. minor

Saxifraga rufidula f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky habitats in Europe.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-red flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
