Scolopia guerkeana

Scolopia guerkeana, commonly known as the Mexican arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scolopia guerkeana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
