Senecio canovirens

Senecio canovirens, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has hairy leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Senecio canovirens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
