Solanum torvum var. lanatum

Solanum torvum var. lanatum, commonly known as the rough nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed and can be harmful if ingested.
