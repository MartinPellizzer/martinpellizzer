Schoepfia evenia

Schoepfia evenia, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
