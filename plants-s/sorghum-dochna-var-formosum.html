Sorghum dochna var. formosum

Sorghum dochna var. formosum, commonly known as the Chinese broomcorn millet, is a grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles of small, bract-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy crop in regions with limited rainfall. Its seeds are used for food, animal feed, and traditional crafts.

The plant's botanical features include fibrous roots, narrow leaves, and a flowering structure that resembles a broom.
