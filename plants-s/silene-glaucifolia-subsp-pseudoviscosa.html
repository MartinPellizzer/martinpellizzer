Silene glaucifolia subsp. pseudoviscosa

Silene glaucifolia subsp.

pseudoviscosa, commonly known as the sticky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture, and its blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and adaptations to arid environments.
