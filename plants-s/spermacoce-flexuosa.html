Spermacoce flexuosa

Spermacoce flexuosa, commonly known as the creeping randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Spermacoce flexuosa is known for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant or used in traditional medicine.
