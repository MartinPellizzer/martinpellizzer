Silene gallica var. agrestina

Silene gallica var. agrestina, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces striking pink to red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Silene gallica var. agrestina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
