Statice dodartii

Statice dodartii, commonly known as the sea lavender or coastal statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas and dunes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of salt.

Statice dodartii is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to add color to garden landscapes.
