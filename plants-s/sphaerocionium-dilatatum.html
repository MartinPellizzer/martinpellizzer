Sphaerocionium dilatatum

Sphaerocionium dilatatum, commonly known as the inflated bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized bladder-like structures.

These bladders are used to trap small aquatic organisms, enabling the plant to obtain nutrients through a form of carnivory. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. It has a delicate, branching stem with tiny, scale-like leaves.

Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, often relying on water currents for seed dispersal.
