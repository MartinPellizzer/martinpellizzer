Saxifraga brachypetala

Saxifraga brachypetala, commonly known as the short-petaled saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, alpine environments where it forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Saxifraga brachypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
