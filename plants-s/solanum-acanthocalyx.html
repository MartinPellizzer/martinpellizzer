Solanum acanthocalyx

Solanum acanthocalyx, commonly known as the horned nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped calyx that persists on the fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with smooth, often hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, and the fruit is a globose berry that turns red when mature.

The species is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
