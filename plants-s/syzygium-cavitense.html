Syzygium cavitense

Syzygium cavitense, commonly known as the hollow-fruit guava, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are small and yellowish in color. Syzygium cavitense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its fruit is valued for its unique flavor and is sometimes used in local cuisines.
