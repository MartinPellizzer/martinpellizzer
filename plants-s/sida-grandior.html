Sida grandior

Sida grandior, commonly known as the largeleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Sida grandior is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it a notable presence in its native habitats.
