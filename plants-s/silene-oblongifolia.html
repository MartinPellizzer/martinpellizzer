Silene oblongifolia

Silene oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Silene oblongifolia is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent-like stems.
