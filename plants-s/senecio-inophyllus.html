Senecio inophyllus

Senecio inophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Senecio inophyllus is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
