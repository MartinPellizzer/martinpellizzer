Setaria compressa

Setaria compressa, commonly known as slender barnyardgrass, is an annual grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become a weed in agricultural fields.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important cereal crops.
