Statice peregrina

Statice peregrina, commonly known as the wandering plume, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, fluffy, pink or white flowers that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy inflorescences.

Statice peregrina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant qualities.
