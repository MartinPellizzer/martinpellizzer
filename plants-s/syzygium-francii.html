Syzygium francii

Syzygium francii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Syzygium francii is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
