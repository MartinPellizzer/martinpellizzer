Saxifraga flagellaris var. komarovii

Saxifraga flagellaris var. komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features basal leaves that are elongated and sometimes hairy, with small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. Its flowering stems are often glandular and may have a reddish or purplish hue.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
