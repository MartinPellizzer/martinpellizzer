Solanum houstonii

Solanum houstonii, commonly known as Houston's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often in Texas and adjacent regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or blue in color. Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids similar to those found in other nightshade species.

Solanum houstonii is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes potatoes, tomatoes, and tobacco.
