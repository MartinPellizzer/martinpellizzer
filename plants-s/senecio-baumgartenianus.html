Senecio baumgartenianus

Senecio baumgartenianus, commonly known as the Cape mountain snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has hairy, narrow leaves and a short, upright stem.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
