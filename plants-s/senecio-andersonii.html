Senecio andersonii

Senecio andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mojave Desert in the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water availability. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio andersonii is often found in crevices and on slopes, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.
