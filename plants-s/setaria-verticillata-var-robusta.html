Setaria verticillata var. robusta

Setaria verticillata var. robusta, commonly known as rough dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikelets that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
