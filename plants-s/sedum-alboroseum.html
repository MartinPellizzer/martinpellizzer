Sedum alboroseum

Sedum alboroseum, commonly known as the "White Rose Sedum," is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, blue-gray leaves that form a low-growing rosette, which helps it conserve water in its natural habitat. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, pale pink to white flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it an excellent choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and delicate blooms.
