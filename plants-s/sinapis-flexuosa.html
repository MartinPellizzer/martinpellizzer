Sinapis flexuosa

Sinapis flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features slender, branching stems that often grow in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the spring.

Sinapis flexuosa produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
