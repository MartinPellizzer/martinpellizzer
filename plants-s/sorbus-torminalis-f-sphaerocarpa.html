Sorbus torminalis f. sphaerocarpa

Sorbus torminalis f. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-berried whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree characterized by its distinctive, rounded, berry-like fruits.

The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color. The fruits, which are typically red or orange, are clustered and serve as a food source for various birds and mammals.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in garden and natural settings.
