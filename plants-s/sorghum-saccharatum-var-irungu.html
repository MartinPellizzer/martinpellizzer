Sorghum saccharatum var. irungu

Sorghum saccharatum var. irungu, commonly known as Irungu sugarcane, is a variety of sugarcane native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and high sugar content, making it valuable for both commercial and traditional uses. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and ample sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while the stems are fibrous and contain a significant amount of sucrose.

This variety is also known for its adaptability to various soil types, contributing to its widespread cultivation in regions with limited agricultural resources.
