Salsola implicata

Salsola implicata, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in desert and coastal environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Salsola implicata plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts due to its ability to tolerate heavy metals.
