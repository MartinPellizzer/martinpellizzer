Spiranthes ecallosa

Spiranthes ecallosa, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spirally arranged stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish itself in suitable habitats.

The species is also known for its fragrant flowers, which attract various pollinators.
