Syzygium kanneliyense

Syzygium kanneliyense, commonly known as the Kanneliyen sand apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits with a crisp, juicy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Syzygium kanneliyense is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
