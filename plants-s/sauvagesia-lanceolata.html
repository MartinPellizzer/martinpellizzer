Sauvagesia lanceolata

Sauvagesia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well-adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
