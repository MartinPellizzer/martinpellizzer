Senecio tonduzii

Senecio tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduz's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
