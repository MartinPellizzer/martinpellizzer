Syzygium fastigiatum

Syzygium fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sour yellow fruits. Its botanical features include a woody trunk with smooth bark and a dense canopy.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit in warm climates.
