Saurauia cauliflora

Saurauia cauliflora, commonly known as the climbing saurauia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage, often growing in shaded forest environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Saurauia cauliflora is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded conditions and its attractive foliage.
