Scabiosa tomentosa var. grosii

Scabiosa tomentosa var. grosii, commonly known as the Woolly Scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy, gray-green foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.
