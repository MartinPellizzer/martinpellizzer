Scleria pauciflora var. effusa

Scleria pauciflora var. effusa, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, greenish-brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
