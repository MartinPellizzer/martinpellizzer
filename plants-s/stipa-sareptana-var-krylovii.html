Stipa sareptana var. krylovii

Stipa sareptana var. krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, silver-gray inflorescences that resemble a delicate plume. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variety is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
