Scirpus acicularis var. giganteus

Scirpus acicularis var. giganteus, commonly known as giant narrowleaf bulrush, is a tall, grass-like plant native to wetland habitats.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically found in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Its rapid growth and dense growth habit make it an effective stabilizer of shoreline ecosystems.
