Streptocarpus montanus

Streptocarpus montanus, commonly known as the mountain streptocarpus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its native habitat. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in well-drained soil with moderate light conditions.
