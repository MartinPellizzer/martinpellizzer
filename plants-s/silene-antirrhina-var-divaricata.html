Silene antirrhina var. divaricata

Silene antirrhina var. divaricata, commonly known as the pink campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy fields and along roadsides. The plant features upright stems that are often branched, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

The species is characterized by its showy blooms and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
