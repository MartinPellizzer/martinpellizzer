Sarcocaulon patersonii subsp. curvatum

Sarcocaulon patersonii subsp.

curvatum, commonly known as the Curved Sarcocaulon, is a unique and rare subshrub native to South Australia. It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and dense, woody stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
