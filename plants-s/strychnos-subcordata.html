Strychnos subcordata

Strychnos subcordata, commonly known as the African strychnos, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruiting habit.
