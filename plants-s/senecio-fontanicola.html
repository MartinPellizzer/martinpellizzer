Senecio fontanicola

Senecio fontanicola, commonly known as the waterfall senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender, branching stems and small, daisy-like flowers make it a striking addition to rock gardens.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
