Silene primuliflora

Silene primuliflora, commonly known as the primrose campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Silene primuliflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
