Selaginella dregei var. hildebrandtiana

Selaginella dregei var. hildebrandtiana, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's spikemoss, is a perennial vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit and small, scale-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces strobila, which are cone-like structures containing spores, a feature typical of its evolutionary lineage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.

This variety is notable for its resilience and adaptability to various soil conditions.
