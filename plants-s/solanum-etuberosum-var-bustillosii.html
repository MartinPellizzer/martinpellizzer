Solanum etuberosum var. bustillosii

Solanum etuberosum var. bustillosii, commonly known as the Bustillosii nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
