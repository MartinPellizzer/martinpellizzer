Senna neglecta

Senna neglecta, commonly known as the Indian senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It features compound leaves with 8 to 16 pairs of ovate leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters during the rainy season. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, with the leaves and flowers used in traditional medicine for their laxative effects. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Senna neglecta is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of organic fertilizer.
