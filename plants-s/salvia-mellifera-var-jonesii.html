Salvia mellifera var. jonesii

Salvia mellifera var. jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' hummingbird sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
