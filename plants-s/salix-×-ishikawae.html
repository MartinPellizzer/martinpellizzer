Salix × ishikawae

Salix × ishikawae, commonly known as the Ishikawa willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its graceful appearance.
