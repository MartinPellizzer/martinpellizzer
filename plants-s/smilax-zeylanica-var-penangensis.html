Smilax zeylanica var. penangensis

Smilax zeylanica var. penangensis, commonly known as Penang smilax, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and produces sharp, spiny stems that help it climb and anchor itself to supports. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in spikes. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along riverbanks.

The fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that is edible and attracts various wildlife.
