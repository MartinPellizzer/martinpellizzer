Salacia scandens

Salacia scandens, commonly known as the climbing gooseberry, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by orange to red berries. Its berries are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
