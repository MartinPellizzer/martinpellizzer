Sesleria juncifolia

Sesleria juncifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Sesleria juncifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing clumps.
