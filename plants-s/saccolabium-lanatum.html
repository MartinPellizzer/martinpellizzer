Saccolabium lanatum

Saccolabium lanatum, commonly known as the woolly milkbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its latex sap is milky and can be used for various traditional purposes.
