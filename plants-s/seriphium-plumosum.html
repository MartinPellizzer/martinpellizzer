Seriphium plumosum

Seriphium plumosum, commonly known as the downy hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, yellow composite flower heads that resemble a hawk's beak. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Seriphium plumosum is a hardy perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
