Scleranthus

Scleranthus, commonly known as thistleweed, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are typically annual or perennial herbs with erect, often hairy stems. Their leaves are usually lance-shaped and may have serrated edges, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences. Scleranthus species are found in various habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

They are characterized by their tough, woody stems and are often adapted to dry or sandy environments.
