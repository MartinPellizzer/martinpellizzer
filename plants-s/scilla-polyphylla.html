Scilla polyphylla

Scilla polyphylla, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, temperate climates.

Scilla polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
