Salix gilgiana var. pendula

Salix gilgiana var. pendula, commonly known as weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is prized for its graceful form and is commonly used in landscaping and riparian restoration.
