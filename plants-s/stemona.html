Stemona

Stemona, commonly known as the "Chinese bellflower," is a genus of flowering plants native to East Asia.

It features slender, often branched stems that are typically covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue in color. Stemona species are often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

They are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
