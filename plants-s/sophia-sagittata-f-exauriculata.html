Sophia sagittata f. exauriculata

Sophia sagittata f. exauriculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves.

This cultivar exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to the typical form, with leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Its botanical features make it a desirable choice for those looking to add texture and interest to shaded garden areas.
