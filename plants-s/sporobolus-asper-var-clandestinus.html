Sporobolus asper var. clandestinus

Sporobolus asper var. clandestinus, commonly known as the rough spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences and rough, bristly texture along its stems and leaves. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. It reproduces through wind-dispersed seeds and has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its ecosystem, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
