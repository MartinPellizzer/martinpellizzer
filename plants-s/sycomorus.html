Sycomorus

Sycomorus, commonly known as the sycamore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Moraceae.

These trees are characterized by their broad, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, often grey or white bark. They produce fig-like fruits that are enclosed in a woody capsule, which opens to release the seeds. Sycomorus species are native to various regions across Africa, Asia, and the Middle East.

They are often valued for their ornamental beauty and historical significance in ancient cultures.
