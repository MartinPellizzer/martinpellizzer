Salvia tetramerioides

Salvia tetramerioides, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, mint-like fragrance.

Salvia tetramerioides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
