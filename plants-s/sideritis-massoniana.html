Sideritis massoniana

Sideritis massoniana, commonly known as Masson's ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, hairy leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Sideritis massoniana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
