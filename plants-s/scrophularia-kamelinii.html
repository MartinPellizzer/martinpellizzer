Scrophularia kamelinii

Scrophularia kamelinii, commonly known as Kamelin's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia kamelinii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
