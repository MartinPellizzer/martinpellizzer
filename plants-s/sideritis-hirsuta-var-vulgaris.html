Sideritis hirsuta var. vulgaris

Sideritis hirsuta var. vulgaris, commonly known as rough hirsute ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive ailments.
