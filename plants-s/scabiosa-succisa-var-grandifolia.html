Scabiosa succisa var. grandifolia

Scabiosa succisa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the sweet William or garden scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters atop slender, branched stems. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are opposite and covered in soft, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
