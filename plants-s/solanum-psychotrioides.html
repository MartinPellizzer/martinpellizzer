Solanum psychotrioides

Solanum psychotrioides, commonly known as the sacred pepper or the hallucinogenic nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and spiny, oval-shaped berries. The plant contains various alkaloids, including dimethyltryptamine (DMT), which contributes to its psychoactive properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its potent chemical composition, it is both culturally significant and botanically intriguing.
