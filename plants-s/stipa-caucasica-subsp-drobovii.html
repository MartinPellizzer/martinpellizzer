Stipa caucasica subsp. drobovii

Stipa caucasica subsp.

drobovii, commonly known as the Caucasian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and fine, wiry leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It has a clump-forming growth habit and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of its native environment.
