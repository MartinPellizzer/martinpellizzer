Stachys recta var. lisae

Stachys recta var. lisae, commonly known as the narrowleaf hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers clustered in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to gardens.
