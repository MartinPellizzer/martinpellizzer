Scutellaria verecunda

Scutellaria verecunda, commonly known as the purple skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria verecunda thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used by Native American tribes for various health purposes.
