Symplocos ramentacea

Symplocos ramentacea, commonly known as the hairy waxleaf.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically thick and leathery, with a waxy texture that gives it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
