Senna guatemalensis var. oligantha

Senna guatemalensis var. oligantha, commonly known as the Guatemalan senna, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
