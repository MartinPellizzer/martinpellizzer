Sericocalyx phyllostachyus

Sericocalyx phyllostachyus, commonly known as the silver-haired bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish bloom, giving it its common name. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.

Sericocalyx phyllostachyus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
