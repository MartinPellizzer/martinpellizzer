Seaforthia ptychosperma

Seaforthia ptychosperma, commonly known as the coastal bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southern coast of Australia.

It is a small annual herb with erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its seed pods are inflated and bladder-like, giving it its common name.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and heathlands.
