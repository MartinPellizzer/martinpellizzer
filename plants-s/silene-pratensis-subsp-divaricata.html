Silene pratensis subsp. divaricata

Silene pratensis subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the creeping catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with stems that often lie along the ground and produce numerous flowering stems.

The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically linear to oblanceolate in shape. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. Silene pratensis subsp.

divaricata is adapted to temperate climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
