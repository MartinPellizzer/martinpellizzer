Senecio primulifolius

Senecio primulifolius, commonly known as the springleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
