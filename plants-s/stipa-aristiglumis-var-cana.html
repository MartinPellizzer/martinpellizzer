Stipa aristiglumis var. cana

Stipa aristiglumis var. cana, commonly known as the silvergrass or cana grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and panicles that are often tinged with pink or red. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
