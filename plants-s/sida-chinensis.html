Sida chinensis

Sida chinensis, commonly known as Chinese sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, alternate leaves with serrated edges, and small, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Sida chinensis is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
