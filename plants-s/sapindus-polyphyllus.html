Sapindus polyphyllus

Sapindus polyphyllus, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a strikingly ornamental appearance. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, spiny fruits. The fruits contain a starchy, edible seed, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
