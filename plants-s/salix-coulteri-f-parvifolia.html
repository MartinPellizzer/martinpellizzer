Salix coulteri f. parvifolia

Salix coulteri f. parvifolia, commonly known as Coulter's weeping willow, is a unique cultivar of the willow tree characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and cascading branches.

It is a deciduous plant native to the southwestern United States, particularly in Texas and New Mexico. The leaves are smaller and more finely textured compared to typical willow species, giving it a more refined appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its graceful form.

This cultivar is valued for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
