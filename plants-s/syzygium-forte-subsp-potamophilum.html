Syzygium forte subsp. potamophilum

Syzygium forte subsp.

potamophilum, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red.

The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by water, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams. Syzygium forte subsp.

potamophilum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
