Stackhousia tenuissima

Stackhousia tenuissima, commonly known as the slender stackhousia, is a small shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
