Scrophularia alata subsp. ehrahrtii

Scrophularia alata subsp.

ehrahrtii, commonly known as the broadleaf figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
