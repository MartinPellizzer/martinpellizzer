Salvia robusta

Salvia robusta, commonly known as Mexican bush sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features upright, woody stems and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a strong, mint-like fragrance.

Salvia robusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
