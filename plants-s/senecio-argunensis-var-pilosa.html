Senecio argunensis var. pilosa

Senecio argunensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy senecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
