Stapelia incarnata

Stapelia incarnata, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, and often reddish or orange-colored stems that resemble the shape of a ghost or a human face. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in late summer. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Stapelia incarnata is a popular choice for succulent gardens due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
