Stemodia durantifolia var. chilensis

Stemodia durantifolia var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand harsh environmental conditions, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
