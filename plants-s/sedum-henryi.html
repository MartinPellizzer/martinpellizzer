Sedum henryi

Sedum henryi, commonly known as the Henry's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that store water, typical of the Crassulaceae family. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
