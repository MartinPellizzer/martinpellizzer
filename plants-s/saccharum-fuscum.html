Saccharum fuscum

Saccharum fuscum, commonly known as black cane grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dark-colored leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is often found in wetlands and along water bodies due to its preference for moist soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

While not widely cultivated for sugar production like its relative Saccharum officinarum, it plays a role in ecological systems and has been studied for its potential in phytoremediation.
