Scelochiloides

Scelochiloides, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and ethereal flowering plant native to the wet tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its roots for nutrient absorption. The plant produces delicate, pale green or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered in many regions.

Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this rare botanical species.
