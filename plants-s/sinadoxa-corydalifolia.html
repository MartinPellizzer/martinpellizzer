Sinadoxa corydalifolia

Sinadoxa corydalifolia, commonly known as the common false indigo, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Sinadoxa corydalifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
