Salix brachystachys var. scouleriana

Salix brachystachys var. scouleriana, commonly known as Scouler's willow, is a shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streams, wetlands, and forest edges. Its catkins are yellowish-green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

This variety is known for its adaptability to wet conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
