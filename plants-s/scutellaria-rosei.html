Scutellaria rosei

Scutellaria rosei, commonly known as the rose scutellaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing shades of blue to purple that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scutellaria rosei is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
