Spathandra caerulea

Spathandra caerulea, commonly known as blue star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, star-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Spathandra caerulea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
