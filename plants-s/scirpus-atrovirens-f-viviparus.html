Scirpus atrovirens f. viviparus

Scirpus atrovirens f. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush characterized by its unique viviparous reproduction.

This plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its stems are tall and cylindrical, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dark green coloration.

The plant produces specialized structures called viviparous shoots, which develop on the stems and can root upon contact with water, facilitating vegetative propagation.
