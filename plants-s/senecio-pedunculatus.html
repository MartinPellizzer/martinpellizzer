Senecio pedunculatus

Senecio pedunculatus, commonly known as the upright groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to many horticultural settings.
