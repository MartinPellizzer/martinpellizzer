Sclerostylis buxifolia

Sclerostylis buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf sclerostylis, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
