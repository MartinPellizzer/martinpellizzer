Syngonanthus restingensis

Syngonanthus restingensis, commonly known as the Restinga grass.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of Brazil, particularly in the Atlantic Forest biome. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the restinga ecosystem.

Syngonanthus restingensis plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
