Salvia biserrata

Salvia biserrata, commonly known as the Cape sage, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces striking violet or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Salvia biserrata is also valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal.
