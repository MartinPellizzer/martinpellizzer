Setaria scalaris

Setaria scalaris, commonly known as slender bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows as an annual or perennial herb. The plant has narrow, upright leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, and the plant is adapted to thrive in dry, disturbed environments.

Setaria scalaris is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize bare soil.
