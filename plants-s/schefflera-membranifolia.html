Schefflera membranifolia

Schefflera membranifolia, commonly known as the paper-leaf umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are thin and papery in texture. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its compound leaves are arranged in a whorl at the ends of the branches, giving it a distinctive umbrella-like appearance.

Schefflera membranifolia is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
