Senecio ualtata

Senecio ualtata, commonly known as the Blue-Eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Senecio ualtata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
