Saraca asoca

Saraca asoca, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, creating a vibrant display. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the tree can grow up to 15 meters tall.

Saraca asoca is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
