Senecio valerianella

Senecio valerianella, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features soft, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
