Sagenia melanocaulos

Sagenia melanocaulos, commonly known as the black-stemmed sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sagenia melanocaulos is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
