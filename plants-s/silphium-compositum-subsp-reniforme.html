Silphium compositum subsp. reniforme

Silphium compositum subsp.

reniforme, commonly known as the broadleaf compass plant, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often ribbed and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a flat-topped cluster, typically blooming from July to September. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Silphium compositum subsp.

reniforme is known for its medicinal properties and historical use by Native American tribes.
