Senecio toluccanus

Senecio toluccanus, commonly known as the Toluca groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster.

Senecio toluccanus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
