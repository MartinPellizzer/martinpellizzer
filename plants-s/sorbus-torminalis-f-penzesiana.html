Sorbus torminalis f. penzesiana

Sorbus torminalis f. penzesiana, commonly known as the Penzesian whitebeam, is a rare and unique horticultural form of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow fruits and distinctive, finely textured leaves. This form is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value, making it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, exhibiting good resistance to pests and diseases.

The Penzesian whitebeam is a valuable addition to conservation efforts due to its genetic uniqueness and ecological significance.
