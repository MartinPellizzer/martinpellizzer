Silene viscariifolia

Silene viscariifolia, commonly known as the stickyleaf catchfly, is a species of flowering plant in the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that exude a sticky substance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
