Senecio pectinatus var. leptocarpus

Senecio pectinatus var. leptocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-fruit senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive pectinate (toothed) margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
