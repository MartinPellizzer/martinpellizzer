Syngonanthus gracilis var. glabriusculus

Syngonanthus gracilis var. glabriusculus, commonly known as the slender hairless spike-rush, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland environments in eastern North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in shallow, still water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it reproduces primarily through rhizomes.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
