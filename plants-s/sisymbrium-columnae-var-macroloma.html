Sisymbrium columnae var. macroloma

Sisymbrium columnae var. macroloma, commonly known as the long-seed sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

The variety macroloma is distinguished by its elongated seed pods, which contribute to its unique botanical appearance.
