Saussurea tangutica var. bayankaraensis

Saussurea tangutica var. bayankaraensis, commonly known as the Bayankara safflower, is a perennial herb native to the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange ray florets. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine environments.
