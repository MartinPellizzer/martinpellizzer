Staurogyne argentea var. angustifolia

Staurogyne argentea var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved silverleaf, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish bloom, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color.

Staurogyne argentea var. angustifolia is valued in aquariums for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
