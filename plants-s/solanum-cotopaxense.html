Solanum cotopaxense

Solanum cotopaxense, commonly known as the Cotopaxi nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it bears red, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
