Sulcorebutia pirhuaniensis

Sulcorebutia pirhuaniensis, commonly known as the Pizarro's rock rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
