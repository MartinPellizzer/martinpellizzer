Salvia blancoana var. hegelmaieri

Salvia blancoana var. hegelmaieri, commonly known as the Hegelmaier's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and showy, violet-blue floral spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaped landscapes. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant plants.

This variety is also valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
