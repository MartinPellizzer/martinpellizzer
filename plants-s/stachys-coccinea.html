Stachys coccinea

Stachys coccinea, commonly known as redstem betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Stachys coccinea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
