Serratula cichoracea subsp. mucronata

Serratula cichoracea subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sickleweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant features elongated, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a loose, branching inflorescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
