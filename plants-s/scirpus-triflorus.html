Scirpus triflorus

Scirpus triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts at the base of the plant.

Scirpus triflorus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
