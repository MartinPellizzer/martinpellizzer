Senecillis intermedia

Senecillis intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecillis intermedia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
