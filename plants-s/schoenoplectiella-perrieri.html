Schoenoplectiella perrieri

Schoenoplectiella perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. This plant is typically found in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a rare or endangered species in certain regions.
