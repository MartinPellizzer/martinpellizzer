Schoenoxiphium bracteosum

Schoenoxiphium bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with five lobes and a distinct throat. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are hairy and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
