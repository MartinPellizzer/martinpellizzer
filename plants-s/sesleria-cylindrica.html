Sesleria cylindrica

Sesleria cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue or purple flowers, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Sesleria cylindrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscapes.
