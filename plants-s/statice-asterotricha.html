Statice asterotricha

Statice asterotricha, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant is well adapted to saline and well-drained soils, often growing in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Statice asterotricha is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
