Senecio quinquelepis

Senecio quinquelepis, commonly known as the five-leaved senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, narrow leaves arranged in a whorled pattern along its stems. Its flowering period typically occurs in spring, producing clusters of yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
