Salix pyrenaica var. serrata

Salix pyrenaica var. serrata, commonly known as the Pyrenean willow with serrated leaves, is a shrub native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and its lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or alpine environments.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
