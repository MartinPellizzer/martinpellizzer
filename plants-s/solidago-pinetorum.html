Solidago pinetorum

Solidago pinetorum, commonly known as the pine barrens goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils within pine forests and open woodlands. The plant features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago pinetorum plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often used in native plant gardens.
