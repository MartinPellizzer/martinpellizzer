Stelis clematis

Stelis clematis, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and its ability to climb using aerial roots. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. Its leaves are usually alternate and lance-shaped, with a smooth or hairy surface.

Stelis clematis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
