Salpichroa scandens

Salpichroa scandens, commonly known as the climbing bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and grows by twining around supports. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Salpichroa scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
