Syzygium jasminifolium

Syzygium jasminifolium, commonly known as the jasmine leaf tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that resemble jasmine. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, often emitting a sweet scent. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
