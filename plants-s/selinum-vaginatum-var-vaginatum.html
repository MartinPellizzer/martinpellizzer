Selinum vaginatum var. vaginatum

Selinum vaginatum var. vaginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed selenium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert grassland habitats.

The species is also known for its aromatic foliage, which can emit a distinctive scent when crushed.
