Silene macrocarpa

Silene macrocarpa, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that bear clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are notable for their unique, pouch-like structure. Silene macrocarpa thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
