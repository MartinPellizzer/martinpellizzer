Sagraea polystachya

Sagraea polystachya, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Sagraea polystachya is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
