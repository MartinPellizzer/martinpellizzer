Stemmacantha ficifolia

Stemmacantha ficifolia, commonly known as the giant fig tree, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has a dense canopy of broad, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its unique feature is the presence of aerial roots that help it climb and anchor itself to surrounding vegetation.

The tree produces fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
