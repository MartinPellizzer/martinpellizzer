Synexemia

Synexemia, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both a notable weed and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
