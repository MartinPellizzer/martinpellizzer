Siparuna pilosolepidota

Siparuna pilosolepidota, commonly known as the velvetleaf siphonandra, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Siparunaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically green when immature and turn red upon ripening. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Siparuna pilosolepidota is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
