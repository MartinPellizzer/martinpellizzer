Sarcomphalus cyclocardius

Sarcomphalus cyclocardius, commonly known as the cyclocardian bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, which it then digests for nutrients. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor water bodies, where its carnivorous adaptations provide a crucial survival advantage. Its leaves are thin and flexible, allowing them to move and entrap prey efficiently.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and evolutionary adaptations.
