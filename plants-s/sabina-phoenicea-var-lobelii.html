Sabina phoenicea var. lobelii

Sabina phoenicea var. lobelii, commonly known as the Lobel's juniper, is a shrub-like evergreen plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, berry-like cones that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
