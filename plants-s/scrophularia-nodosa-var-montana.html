Scrophularia nodosa var. montana

Scrophularia nodosa var. montana, commonly known as mountain scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features erect, branched stems that are often hairy, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are tubular, typically pink or purple, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is part of the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in alpine gardens.
