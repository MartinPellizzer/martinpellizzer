Salacia khasiana

Salacia khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi salacia, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, shedding its leaves seasonally, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits in local habitats.
