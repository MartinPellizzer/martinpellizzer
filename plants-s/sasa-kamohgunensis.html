Sasa kamohgunensis

Sasa kamohgunensis, commonly known as the Korean bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer months.

Sasa kamohgunensis is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in Korean culture.
