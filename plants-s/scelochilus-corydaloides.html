Scelochilus corydaloides

Scelochilus corydaloides, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually yellow or golden in color, with a distinctive, thread-like appearance.

Scelochilus corydaloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
