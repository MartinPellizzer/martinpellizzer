Senecio phylicifolius

Senecio phylicifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
