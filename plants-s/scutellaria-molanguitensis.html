Scutellaria molanguitensis

Scutellaria molanguitensis, commonly known as the Molango skullcap, is a species of flowering plant native to the humid forests of Central America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scutellaria molanguitensis is often found growing in shaded, moist environments, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
