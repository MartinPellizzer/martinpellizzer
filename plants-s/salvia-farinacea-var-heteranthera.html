Salvia farinacea var. heteranthera

Salvia farinacea var. heteranthera, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stems. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its unique flower structure and vibrant color make it a valuable addition to pollinator gardens.
