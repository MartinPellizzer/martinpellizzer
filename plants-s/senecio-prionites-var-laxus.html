Senecio prionites var. laxus

Senecio prionites var. laxus, commonly known as the softleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a soft, fuzzy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
