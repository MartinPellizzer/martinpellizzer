Solanum wallacei

Solanum wallacei, commonly known as Wallace's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia, and typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Solanum wallacei is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
