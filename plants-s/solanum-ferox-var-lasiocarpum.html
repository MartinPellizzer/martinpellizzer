Solanum ferox var. lasiocarpum

Solanum ferox var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the rough-fruited nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into globose, greenish fruits covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its fruits are typically toxic but have been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.

The species is also noted for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
