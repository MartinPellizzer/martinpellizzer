Senecio candicans

Senecio candicans, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio candicans is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique appearance.
