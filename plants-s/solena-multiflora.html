Solena multiflora

Solena multiflora, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers throughout the summer months. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Solena multiflora is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
