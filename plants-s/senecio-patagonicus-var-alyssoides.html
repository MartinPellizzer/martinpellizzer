Senecio patagonicus var. alyssoides

Senecio patagonicus var. alyssoides, commonly known as the Patagonian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent leaves to conserve water. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons.

The variety alyssoides is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and smaller foliage compared to other varieties of Senecio patagonicus.
