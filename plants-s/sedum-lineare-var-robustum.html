Sedum lineare var. robustum

Sedum lineare var. robustum, commonly known as the robust sedum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for both gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
