Scrophularia adenopa

Scrophularia adenopa, commonly known as the woolly scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Scrophularia adenopa is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
