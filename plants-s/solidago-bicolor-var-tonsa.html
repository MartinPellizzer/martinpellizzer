Solidago bicolor var. tonsa

Solidago bicolor var. tonsa, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bright yellow and orange flower heads, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and grayish-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its showy flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
