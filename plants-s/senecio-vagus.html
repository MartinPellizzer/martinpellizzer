Senecio vagus

Senecio vagus, commonly known as the wandering senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is a perennial herb native to South Africa, typically growing in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, producing small, wind-pollinated seeds.

Senecio vagus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
