Stephanolirion narcissoides

Stephanolirion narcissoides, commonly known as the golden bulb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that resemble those of the daffodil. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its blooming period typically occurs in spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its native habitat.

The golden bulb is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
