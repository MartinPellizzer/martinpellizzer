Senecio dracunculoides

Senecio dracunculoides, commonly known as the dragon's head or false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a dragon's head. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
