Saionia

Saionia, commonly known as the desert willow, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Saionia has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
