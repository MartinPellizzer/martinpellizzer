Scrophularia hypericifolia

Scrophularia hypericifolia, commonly known as the hairy scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Scrophularia hypericifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
