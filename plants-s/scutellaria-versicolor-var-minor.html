Scutellaria versicolor var. minor

Scutellaria versicolor var. minor, commonly known as the lesser blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.

The species is closely related to other members of the Scutellaria genus, which are characterized by their unique calyces and spurred petals.
