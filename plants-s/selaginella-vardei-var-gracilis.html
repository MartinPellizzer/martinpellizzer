Selaginella vardei var. gracilis

Selaginella vardei var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender spikemoss, is a low-growing, evergreen fern ally native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the reproductive organs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh environments make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
