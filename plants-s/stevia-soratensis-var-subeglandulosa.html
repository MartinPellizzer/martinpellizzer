Stevia soratensis var. subeglandulosa

Stevia soratensis var. subeglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular leaf stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are covered in small, glandular dots that secrete a sweet compound. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. It is valued for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are used as natural sweeteners.

Due to its unique botanical features and potential medicinal uses, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
