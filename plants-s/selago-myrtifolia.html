Selago myrtifolia

Selago myrtifolia, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Selago myrtifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
