Sida hirsuta

Sida hirsuta, commonly known as rough sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields.

Sida hirsuta is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
