Solidago multiradiata

Solidago multiradiata, commonly known as the multibract goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its numerous yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems are usually tall and sturdy, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

Solidago multiradiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
