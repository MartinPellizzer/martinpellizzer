Syringa ligustrina var. pendula

Syringa ligustrina var. pendula, commonly known as weeping lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has long, slender branches that droop gracefully, giving it a distinctive weeping appearance. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
