Sarcobodium lobbii

Sarcobodium lobbii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers during the rainy season. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable species for studying desert flora.
