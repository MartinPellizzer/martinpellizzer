Silene clavaecarpa

Silene clavaecarpa, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Silene clavaecarpa is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.
