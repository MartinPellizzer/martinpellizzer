Symplocos lanceolata var. rhamnifolia

Symplocos lanceolata var. rhamnifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf evergreen holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
