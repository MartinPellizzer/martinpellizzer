Salvia coahuilensis

Salvia coahuilensis, commonly known as Coahuila sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. It is often found in rocky slopes and limestone soils, showcasing its resilience to drought.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in desert flora.
