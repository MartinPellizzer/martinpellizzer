Silene yildirimlii

Silene yildirimlii, commonly known as the Turkish catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene yildirimlii is also noted for its small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
