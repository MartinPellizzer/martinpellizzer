Sarothrostachys multiramea

Sarothrostachys multiramea, commonly known as the multibranched grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a hairy texture.
