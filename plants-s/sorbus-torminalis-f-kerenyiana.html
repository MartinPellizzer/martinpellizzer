Sorbus torminalis f. kerenyiana

Sorbus torminalis f. kerenyiana, commonly known as the Kerenyian whitebeams, is a rare horticultural form of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often variegated leaves that display a mix of white, green, and pink hues. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries in the autumn. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
