Sesleria dactyloides

Sesleria dactyloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer. It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to humid, temperate climates.

Sesleria dactyloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
