Scorzonera castulonensis

Scorzonera castulonensis, commonly known as the Castulo scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a taproot that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
