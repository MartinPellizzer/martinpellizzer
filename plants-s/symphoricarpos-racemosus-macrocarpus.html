Symphoricarpos racemosus macrocarpus

Symphoricarpos racemosus macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible, bright red berries that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.
