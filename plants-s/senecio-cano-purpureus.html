Senecio cano-purpureus

Senecio cano-purpureus, commonly known as the purplestem senecio, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with hairy, often purple-tinged stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.
