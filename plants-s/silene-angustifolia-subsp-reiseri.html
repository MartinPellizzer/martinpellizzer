Silene angustifolia subsp. reiseri

Silene angustifolia subsp.

reiseri, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
