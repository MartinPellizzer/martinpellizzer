Senecio angustilobatus

Senecio angustilobatus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
