Syzygium mekongense

Syzygium mekongense, commonly known as the Mekong tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in local remedies.

Syzygium mekongense thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
