Solanum matadori

Solanum matadori, commonly known as the Matador nightshade, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or purple and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a small, shiny berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
