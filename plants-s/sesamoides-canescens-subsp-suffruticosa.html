Sesamoides canescens subsp. suffruticosa

Sesamoides canescens subsp.

suffruticosa, commonly known as the silverleaf sesamoid, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody stems and thick leaves are adapted for survival in nutrient-poor soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber in local communities.
