Symplocos spicata f. acuminata

Symplocos spicata f. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated spicata beautyberry, is a variant of the beautyberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright purple berries in the fall, which are a striking feature of its botanical appearance. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth texture, while the stems are often hairy.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
