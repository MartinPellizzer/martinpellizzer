Sobralia liliastrum var. alba

Sobralia liliastrum var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered sobralia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store moisture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
