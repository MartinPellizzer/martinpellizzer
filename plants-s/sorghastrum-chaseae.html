Sorghastrum chaseae

Sorghastrum chaseae, commonly known as Chase's bluestem, is a tallgrass prairie species native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, purplish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry to mesic soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides. It plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its drought tolerance makes it a valuable component of restoration ecology projects.
