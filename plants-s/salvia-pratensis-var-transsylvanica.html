Salvia pratensis var. transsylvanica

Salvia pratensis var. transsylvanica, commonly known as Transylvanian blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces spikes of purple to violet flowers from June to August. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and conservation areas.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, though it is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic value.
