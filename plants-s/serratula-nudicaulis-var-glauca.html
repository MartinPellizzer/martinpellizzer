Serratula nudicaulis var. glauca

Serratula nudicaulis var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. Its leaves are narrowly lance-shaped and covered in a bluish-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
