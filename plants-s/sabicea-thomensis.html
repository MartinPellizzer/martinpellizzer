Sabicea thomensis

Sabicea thomensis, commonly known as the Tomineza or Tomineza tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Mozambique.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
