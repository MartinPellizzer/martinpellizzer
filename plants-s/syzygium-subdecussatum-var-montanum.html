Syzygium subdecussatum var. montanum

Syzygium subdecussatum var. montanum, commonly known as mountain clove, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules containing aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal research.
