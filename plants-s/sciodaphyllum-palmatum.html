Sciodaphyllum palmatum

Sciodaphyllum palmatum, commonly known as the moonlight palm, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that exhibit a unique silvery-blue coloration under moonlight. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bats and insects. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-draining soil.

Due to habitat loss, Sciodaphyllum palmatum is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
