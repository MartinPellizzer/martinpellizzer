Salacia pierlotii

Salacia pierlotii, commonly known as the Malabar gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Salaceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Salacia pierlotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
