Solanum pogogenum

Solanum pogogenum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries. The berries are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, containing seeds inside.

It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in a variety of soil types.
