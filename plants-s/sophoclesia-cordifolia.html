Sophoclesia cordifolia

Sophoclesia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, thriving in humid environments. Its botanical features include hairy stems and capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Sophoclesia cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
