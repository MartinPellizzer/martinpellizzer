Stylidium articulatum

Stylidium articulatum, commonly known as the articulated sun-orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spurred petals and a compact, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive, elongated spur that houses the nectar.

Stylidium articulatum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
