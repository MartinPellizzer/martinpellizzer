Stachyarpagophora

Stachyarpagophora, commonly known as the giant waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, circular leaves that float on the water's surface, supporting a dense network of roots that hang below. The plant's leaves are typically green and can grow up to several meters in diameter, creating a habitat for various aquatic organisms. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes.

Stachyarpagophora is notable for its ability to trap and hold water, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
