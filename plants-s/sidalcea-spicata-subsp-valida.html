Sidalcea spicata subsp. valida

Sidalcea spicata subsp.

valida, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in native plant gardens.
