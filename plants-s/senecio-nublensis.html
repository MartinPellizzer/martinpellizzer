Senecio nublensis

Senecio nublensis, commonly known as the cloud mountain groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio nublensis is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these unique ecosystems.
