Seriphium gnaphaloides

Seriphium gnaphaloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, grayish foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Seriphium gnaphaloides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
