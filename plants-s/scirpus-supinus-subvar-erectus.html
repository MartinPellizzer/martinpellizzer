Scirpus supinus subvar. erectus

Scirpus supinus subvar. erectus, commonly known as the upright bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in marshy areas.

Scirpus supinus subvar. erectus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate and thrive in waterlogged conditions.
