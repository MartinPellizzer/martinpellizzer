Salvia blancoana var. amethystea

Salvia blancoana var. amethystea, commonly known as the amethyst sage, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.
