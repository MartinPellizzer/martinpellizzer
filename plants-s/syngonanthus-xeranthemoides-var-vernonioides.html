Syngonanthus xeranthemoides var. vernonioides

Syngonanthus xeranthemoides var. vernonioides, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

The variety vernonioides is notable for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
