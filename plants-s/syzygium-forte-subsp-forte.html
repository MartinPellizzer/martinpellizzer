Syzygium forte subsp. forte

Syzygium forte subsp.

forte, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae. It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of white, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces edible, round fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.

The leaves are lance-shaped, evergreen, and have a glossy surface.
