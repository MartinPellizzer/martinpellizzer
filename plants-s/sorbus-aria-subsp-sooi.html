Sorbus aria subsp. sooi

Sorbus aria subsp.

sooi, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, persistent berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
