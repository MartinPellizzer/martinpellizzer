Scirpus dichromenoides

Scirpus dichromenoides, commonly known as the redstem bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often display a reddish hue, especially at the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. Scirpus dichromenoides thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
