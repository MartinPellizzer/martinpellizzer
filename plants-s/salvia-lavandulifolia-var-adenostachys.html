Salvia lavandulifolia var. adenostachys

Salvia lavandulifolia var. adenostachys, commonly known as lemon sage, is a compact, aromatic perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a distinct lemony fragrance, which is released when the leaves are crushed. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
