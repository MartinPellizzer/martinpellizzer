Selaginella incana

Selaginella incana, commonly known as the hairy spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or rocky crevices. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy structures, which help reduce water loss in its habitat. Its leaves are linear and often arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Selaginella incana is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
