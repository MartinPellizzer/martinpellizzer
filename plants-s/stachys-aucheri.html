Stachys aucheri

Stachys aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian hedge-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Stachys aucheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
