Senecio doryphorus

Senecio doryphorus, commonly known as the Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio doryphorus is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
