Syzygium kokomo

Syzygium kokomo, commonly known as the Kokomo apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Syzygium kokomo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and a warm, humid climate to thrive.
