Salvia lampsanifolia

Salvia lampsanifolia, commonly known as the sacred sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, while its scent is often described as minty and refreshing.

Salvia lampsanifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
