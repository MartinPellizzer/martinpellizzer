Salvia lamiifolia

Salvia lamiifolia, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a minty aroma. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia lamiifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional herbal remedies.
