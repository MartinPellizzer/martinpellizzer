Salacia gagnepainiana

Salacia gagnepainiana, commonly known as the golden salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has elongated, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
