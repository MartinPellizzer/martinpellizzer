Scleranthus chaitophyllus

Scleranthus chaitophyllus, commonly known as the woolly sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Scleranthus chaitophyllus is typically found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and coastal dunes.
