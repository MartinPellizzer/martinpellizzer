Scrophularia lunariifolia

Scrophularia lunariifolia, commonly known as moonflower scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, erect stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the evening. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Scrophularia lunariifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
