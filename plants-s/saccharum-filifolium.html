Saccharum filifolium

Saccharum filifolium, commonly known as the grassy bamboo or filifolium bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in landscaping and as a decorative element in gardens.
