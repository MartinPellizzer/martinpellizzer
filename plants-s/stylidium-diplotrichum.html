Stylidium diplotrichum

Stylidium diplotrichum, commonly known as the two-branched spurred orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique spurred structure on its flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spur that extends from the base of the petals.

Stylidium diplotrichum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
