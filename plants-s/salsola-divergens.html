Salsola divergens

Salsola divergens, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. Salsola divergens is well adapted to harsh environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and tolerate high temperatures.

It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife.
