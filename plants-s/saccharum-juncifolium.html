Saccharum juncifolium

Saccharum juncifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass or sweetgrass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes produce small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color.

Saccharum juncifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional practices in some regions.
