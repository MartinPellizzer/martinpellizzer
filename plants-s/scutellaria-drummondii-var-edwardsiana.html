Scutellaria drummondii var. edwardsiana

Scutellaria drummondii var. edwardsiana, commonly known as Edwards' bluehead, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Edwards' bluehead is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
