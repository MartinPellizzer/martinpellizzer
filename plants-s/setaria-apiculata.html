Setaria apiculata

Setaria apiculata, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed bracts.

Setaria apiculata is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
