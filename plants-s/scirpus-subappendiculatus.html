Scirpus subappendiculatus

Scirpus subappendiculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spike-like flowers. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its rhizomes are often used for stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in wetland habitats.

Scirpus subappendiculatus plays an important ecological role in supporting diverse wetland flora and fauna.
