Senecio hirtellus

Senecio hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Senecio hirtellus is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
