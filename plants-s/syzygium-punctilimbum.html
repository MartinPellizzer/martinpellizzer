Syzygium punctilimbum

Syzygium punctilimbum, commonly known as the Java apple or *Pomelo*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. The fruit is typically round or oval, with a thick, rough rind and a juicy, segmented interior. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
