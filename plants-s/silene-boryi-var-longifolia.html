Silene boryi var. longifolia

Silene boryi var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf silene, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged alternately along the stem.

Silene boryi var. longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
