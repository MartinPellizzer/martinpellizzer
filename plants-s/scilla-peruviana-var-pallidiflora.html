Scilla peruviana var. pallidiflora

Scilla peruviana var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its foliage emerges in early spring, followed by flowering in late spring or early summer.

The variety is noted for its delicate appearance and adaptability to temperate climates.
