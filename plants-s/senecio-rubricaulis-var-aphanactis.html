Senecio rubricaulis var. aphanactis

Senecio rubricaulis var. aphanactis, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden among the foliage. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a ghostly appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
