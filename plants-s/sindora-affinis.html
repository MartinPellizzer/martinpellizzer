Sindora affinis

Sindora affinis, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing edible, sour fruits that are used in various culinary applications. Its bark and leaves have also been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their medicinal properties.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.
