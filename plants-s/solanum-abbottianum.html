Solanum abbottianum

Solanum abbottianum, commonly known as Abbott's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential hazard in its native habitat.
