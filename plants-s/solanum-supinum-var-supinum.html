Solanum supinum var. supinum

Solanum supinum var. supinum, commonly known as the prostrate nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It typically grows in a sprawling, low-lying form, with stems that often lie flat on the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a deep red color. It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with slightly hairy surfaces.

Solanum supinum var. supinum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
