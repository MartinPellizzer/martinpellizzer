Simbuleta arabica

Simbuleta arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia and Yemen.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds used to make coffee. Simbuleta arabica thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as one of the primary sources of Arabica coffee, a globally cherished beverage.
