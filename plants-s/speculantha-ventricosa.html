Speculantha ventricosa

Speculantha ventricosa, commonly known as the swollen bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms.

It is native to freshwater habitats in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in still or slow-moving water. The plant's specialized leaves, which are modified into bladder-like appendages, aid in nutrient absorption and predation. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters along the stems.

Speculantha ventricosa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
