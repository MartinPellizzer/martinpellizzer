Sagus sylvestris

Sagus sylvestris, commonly known as the wild saguaro, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem with prominent ribs and spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb water efficiently after rare rains.

Sagus sylvestris plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert species.
