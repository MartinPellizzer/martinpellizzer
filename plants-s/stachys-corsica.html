Stachys corsica

Stachys corsica, commonly known as Corsican hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Stachys corsica is notable for its hairy foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
