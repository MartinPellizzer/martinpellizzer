Serrafalcus mollis var. velutinus

Serrafalcus mollis var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf serrafalcus, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its soft, velvety foliage.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive tactile quality. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical structure makes it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
