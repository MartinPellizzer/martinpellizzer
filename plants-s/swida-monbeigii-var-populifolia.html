Swida monbeigii var. populifolia

Swida monbeigii var. populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf swida, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a tendency to peel in thin layers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
