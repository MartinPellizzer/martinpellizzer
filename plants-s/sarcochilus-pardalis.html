Sarcochilus pardalis

Sarcochilus pardalis, commonly known as the Black Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, dark red to black flowers with yellow markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in woodland areas. It is a perennial herb that blooms during the cooler months, usually from August to November.

The flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, including insects and birds.
