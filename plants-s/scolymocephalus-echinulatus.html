Scolymocephalus echinulatus

Scolymocephalus echinulatus, commonly known as the spiny-headed water plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its dense, spiny inflorescence.

It typically grows in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it thrives in muddy or sandy substrates. The plant's leaves are small and often submerged, while its flowers are borne in compact, cone-like clusters at the water's surface. Its spiny bracts are adapted to protect the reproductive structures from herbivores and water currents.

This species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
