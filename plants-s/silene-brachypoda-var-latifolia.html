Silene brachypoda var. latifolia

Silene brachypoda var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

Due to its delicate appearance and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
