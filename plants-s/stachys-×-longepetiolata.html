Stachys × longepetiolata

Stachys × longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled hedge nettle, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with long petioles. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Stachys × longepetiolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
