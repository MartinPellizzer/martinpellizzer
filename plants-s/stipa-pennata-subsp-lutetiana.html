Stipa pennata subsp. lutetiana

Stipa pennata subsp.

lutetiana, commonly known as the Paris grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various invertebrates.
