Saxifraga komarovii

Saxifraga komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its small, white or pink flowers are arranged in a cyme and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Saxifraga komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
