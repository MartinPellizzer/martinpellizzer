Sapium laurocerasus var. ellipticum

Sapium laurocerasus var. ellipticum, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or variegated boxwood, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with variegated patterns of white or yellow, which adds ornamental value to gardens. The leaves are typically elliptical in shape, hence the species name *ellipticum*, and are arranged in compact clusters. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds.

This plant is valued for its durability, adaptability, and aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
