Spiraea japonica var. leucantha

Spiraea japonica var. leucantha, commonly known as the white spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance and seasonal interest.
