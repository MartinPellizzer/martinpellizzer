Soroseris pumila

Soroseris pumila, commonly known as the dwarf soroseris, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. It has a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
