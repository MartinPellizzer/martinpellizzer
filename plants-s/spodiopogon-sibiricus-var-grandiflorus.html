Spodiopogon sibiricus var. grandiflorus

Spodiopogon sibiricus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Siberian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
