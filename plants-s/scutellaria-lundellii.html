Scutellaria lundellii

Scutellaria lundellii, commonly known as Lundell's bluebug, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria lundellii is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in partial to full shade and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
