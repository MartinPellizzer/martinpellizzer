Sempervivum preissianum

Sempervivum preissianum, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers on tall, slender stems during the summer months. It is a perennial species, capable of surviving harsh winters due to its hardy nature.

Sempervivum preissianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and resilience.
