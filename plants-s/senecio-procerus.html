Senecio procerus

Senecio procerus, commonly known as the tall senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its height can reach up to 1.5 meters, making it a striking addition to gardens.

It produces yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
