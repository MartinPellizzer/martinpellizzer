Syzygium capitatum

Syzygium capitatum, commonly known as the satinwood or mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Syzygium capitatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in tropical ecosystems.
