Stellaria dianthifolia

Stellaria dianthifolia, commonly known as the fairy clock or starwort, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist grasslands and woodland edges. The plant produces tiny, star-shaped white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters.

Stellaria dianthifolia is known for its delicate appearance and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
