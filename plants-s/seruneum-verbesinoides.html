Seruneum verbesinoides

Seruneum verbesinoides, commonly known as the golden chalice, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and botanical collections.
