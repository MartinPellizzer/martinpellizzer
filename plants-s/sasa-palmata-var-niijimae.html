Sasa palmata var. niijimae

Sasa palmata var. niijimae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo palm, is a variant of the Sasa palmata species native to Japan.

It is a type of grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and slightly curled at the edges. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
