Senyumia granitica

Senyumia granitica, commonly known as the Granite Smile Plant, is a rare flowering species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy white petals that resemble a smile, hence its common name. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sunlight to flourish. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Senyumia granitica is known for its brief blooming period, typically during the late spring and early summer months.
