Solidago sparsiflora

Solidago sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparseflower goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
