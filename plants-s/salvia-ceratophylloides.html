Salvia ceratophylloides

Salvia ceratophylloides, commonly known as the woolly sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia ceratophylloides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
