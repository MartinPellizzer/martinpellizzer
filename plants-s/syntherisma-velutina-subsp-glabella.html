Syntherisma velutina subsp. glabella

Syntherisma velutina subsp.

glabella, commonly known as the velvetleaf syntherisma, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and have a distinctive waxy texture.

The subspecies glabella is distinguished by its relatively smooth, less hairy appearance compared to other subspecies.
