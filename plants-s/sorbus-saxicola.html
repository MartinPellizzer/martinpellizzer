Sorbus saxicola

Sorbus saxicola, commonly known as the rock whitebeam, is a small deciduous shrub or tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Sorbus saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
