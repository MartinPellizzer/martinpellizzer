Sedum telephium var. praeruptorum

Sedum telephium var. praeruptorum, commonly known as red stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
