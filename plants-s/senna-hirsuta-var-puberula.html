Senna hirsuta var. puberula

Senna hirsuta var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, ovate leaflets that are also hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
