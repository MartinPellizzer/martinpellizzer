Sanicula japonica

Sanicula japonica, commonly known as Japanese sanicle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Sanicula japonica is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
