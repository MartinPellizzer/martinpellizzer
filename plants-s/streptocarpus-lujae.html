Streptocarpus lujae

Streptocarpus lujae, commonly known as the Lujan's streptocarpus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and attractive, showy flowers. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white throat. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Streptocarpus lujae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in moderate climates.
