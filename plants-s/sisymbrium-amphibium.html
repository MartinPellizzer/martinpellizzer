Sisymbrium amphibium

Sisymbrium amphibium, commonly known as the water pepper, is a flowering plant belonging to the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its roots are known to exude a peppery substance, which gives it its common name.

Sisymbrium amphibium is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.
