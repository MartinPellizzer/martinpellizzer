Sambucus nigra var. pulla

Sambucus nigra var. pulla, commonly known as the black elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white to greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the autumn. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant is also notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
