Sporobolus distichophyllus

Sporobolus distichophyllus, commonly known as the two-leaf spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a spiky cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in two rows, giving it its scientific name. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its ability to store water and tolerate high temperatures.

Sporobolus distichophyllus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
