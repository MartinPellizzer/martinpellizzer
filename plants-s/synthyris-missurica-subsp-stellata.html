Synthyris missurica subsp. stellata

Synthyris missurica subsp.

stellata, commonly known as the starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its striking star-shaped flowers, which typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

The subspecies stellata is distinguished by its more pronounced floral structure compared to other subspecies of Synthyris.
