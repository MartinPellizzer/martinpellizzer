Serpocaulon appressum

Serpocaulon appressum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved serpocaulon, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with a single, erect stem that bears several narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and pale greenish-yellow, often clustered in a loose spike.

It is a rare and delicate species, often requiring specific environmental conditions to thrive.
