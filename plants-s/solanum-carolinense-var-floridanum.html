Solanum carolinense var. floridanum

Solanum carolinense var. floridanum, commonly known as the Florida nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in disturbed soils and sunny environments.

The species is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and peppers.
