Selaginella durvillaei

Selaginella durvillaei, commonly known as the African spikeweed, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spiky, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It is known for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Selaginella durvillaei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
