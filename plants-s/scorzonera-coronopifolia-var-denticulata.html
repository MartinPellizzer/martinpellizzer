Scorzonera coronopifolia var. denticulata

Scorzonera coronopifolia var. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust taproot system, which is often harvested for its edible tubers. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive toothed margin. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central tube.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
