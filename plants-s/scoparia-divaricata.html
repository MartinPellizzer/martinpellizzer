Scoparia divaricata

Scoparia divaricata, commonly known as the variable scoparia, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Scoparia divaricata produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.
