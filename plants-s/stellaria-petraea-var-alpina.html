Stellaria petraea var. alpina

Stellaria petraea var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Stellaria petraea var. alpina is adapted to cold climates and is typically found at elevations above 1,500 meters.
