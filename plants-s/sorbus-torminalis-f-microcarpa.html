Sorbus torminalis f. microcarpa

Sorbus torminalis f. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a compact shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits in the autumn. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soils.
