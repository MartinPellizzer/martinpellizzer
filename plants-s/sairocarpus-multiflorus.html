Sairocarpus multiflorus

Sairocarpus multiflorus, commonly known as the multibloom spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
