Senna pistaciifolia var. picta

Senna pistaciifolia var. picta, commonly known as the painted senna, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply divided into five to seven lobes, often with a bright green or variegated appearance. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seed pods are elongated and contain numerous flat, bean-like seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
