Specklinia medinae

It belongs to the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, antler-like fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, anchoring itself with its rhizomes while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Due to its rarity and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
