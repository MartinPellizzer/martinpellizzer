Senecio gonocladus

Senecio gonocladus, commonly known as the yellow starbursts, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical fruiting structures called cymes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
