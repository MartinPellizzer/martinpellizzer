Selinum peregrinum

Selinum peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering jew, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Selinum peregrinum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
