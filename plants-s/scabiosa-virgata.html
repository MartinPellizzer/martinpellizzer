Scabiosa virgata

Scabiosa virgata, commonly known as the common scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.

Scabiosa virgata is also known for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
