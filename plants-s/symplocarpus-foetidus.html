Symplocarpus foetidus

Symplocarpus foetidus, commonly known as skunk cabbage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, mottled purple and green leaves that often persist through the winter. The plant produces a unique, foul-smelling flower that resembles a small cabbage, which helps attract pollinators. It thrives in wet, boggy habitats and is known for its ability to generate heat around its flower to melt snow and attract insects.

Symplocarpus foetidus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems and is a popular subject in botanical studies.
