Solanum andigenum subsp. tarmense

Solanum andigenum subsp.

tarmense, commonly known as the Tarmes nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
