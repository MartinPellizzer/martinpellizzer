Styrax dasyanthus var. hypoleucus

Styrax dasyanthus var. hypoleucus, commonly known as the white styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
