Sarothamnus glaucus

Sarothamnus glaucus, commonly known as whitethorn or sea holly, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features gray-green, woolly foliage and produces yellow flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Sarothamnus glaucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
