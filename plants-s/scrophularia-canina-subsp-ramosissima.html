Scrophularia canina subsp. ramosissima

Scrophularia canina subsp.

ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, woodlands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features opposite, hairy, ovate leaves and produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

The species is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and sometimes medicinal uses.
