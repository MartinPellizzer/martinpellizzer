Stipa soukupii

Stipa soukupii, commonly known as the Soukup's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is often grayish-green, with narrow leaves that help reduce water loss.

Stipa soukupii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
