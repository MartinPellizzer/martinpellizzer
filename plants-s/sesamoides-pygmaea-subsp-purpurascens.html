Sesamoides pygmaea subsp. purpurascens

Sesamoides pygmaea subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered dwarf sesamoid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive purple petals that bloom in late spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of its native region.
