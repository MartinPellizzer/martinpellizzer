Selinum wallichianum var. elatum

Selinum wallichianum var. elatum, commonly known as the Himalayan parsley or tall selinum, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of parsley, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
