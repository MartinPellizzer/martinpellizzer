Silene armeria var. serpentini

Silene armeria var. serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's head, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are usually upright.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
