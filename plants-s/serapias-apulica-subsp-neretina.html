Serapias apulica subsp. neretina

Serapias apulica subsp.

neretina, commonly known as the Neretine marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive hooded lip.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, thriving in moist, calcareous soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of several showy, spurred flowers.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
