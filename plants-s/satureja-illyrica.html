Satureja illyrica

Satureja illyrica, commonly known as Illyrian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in the summer, and the leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Satureja illyrica is valued for its fragrant properties and is sometimes used in cooking and medicine.
