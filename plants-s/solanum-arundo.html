Solanum arundo

Solanum arundo, commonly known as the wild tomato or nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing several seeds. Solanum arundo is often found in dry, open habitats and can thrive in disturbed soils.

It is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
