Scariola triquetra

Scariola triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features distinctive three-angled stems and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. Scariola triquetra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
