Streblus pendulinus

Streblus pendulinus, commonly known as the hanging fig, is a tropical tree belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous fruit clusters that hang from the branches. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences. The fruit is a fig-like structure that is typically green when immature and turns yellow or red when ripe.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various animals and birds.
