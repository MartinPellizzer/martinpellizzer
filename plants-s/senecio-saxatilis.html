Senecio saxatilis

Senecio saxatilis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces yellow, dandelion-like flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing succulent traits to conserve water. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flowers.

Senecio saxatilis is often found in crevices and on rocky outcrops, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
