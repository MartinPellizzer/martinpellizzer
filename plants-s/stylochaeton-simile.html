Stylochaeton simile

Stylochaeton simile, commonly known as the slender leaved acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds.

Stylochaeton simile is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
