Schoepfia brasiliensis

Schoepfia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Schoepfia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
