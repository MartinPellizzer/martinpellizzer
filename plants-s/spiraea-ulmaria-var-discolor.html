Spiraea ulmaria var. discolor

Spiraea ulmaria var. discolor, commonly known as white meadow spirea, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are oval and have a slightly serrated edge, turning red in the fall.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
