Scaevola restiacea subsp. divaricata

Scaevola restiacea subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the year.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
