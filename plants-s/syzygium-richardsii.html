Syzygium richardsii

Syzygium richardsii, commonly known as the Richards' apple or the mountain apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by round, red or orange fruits resembling apples. Syzygium richardsii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
