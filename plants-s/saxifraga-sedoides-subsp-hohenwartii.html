Saxifraga sedoides subsp. hohenwartii

Saxifraga sedoides subsp.

hohenwartii, commonly known as the Hohenwart's saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features upright, branched stems covered with small, rounded leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, producing white or pale pink petals that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
