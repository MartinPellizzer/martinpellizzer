Saxifraga neopropagulifera

Saxifraga neopropagulifera, commonly known as the rockfoam saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its botanical features include hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche.
