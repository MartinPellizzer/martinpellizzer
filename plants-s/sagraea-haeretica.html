Sagraea haeretica

Sagraea haeretica, commonly known as the rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has aromatic, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Sagraea haeretica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
