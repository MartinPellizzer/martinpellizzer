Styphelia foliosa

Styphelia foliosa, commonly known as the fairy moss or slender snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, delicate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Styphelia foliosa thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
