Stackhousia subterranea

Stackhousia subterranea, commonly known as the underground stackhouse, is a unique plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its subterranean growth habit, with most of its structure hidden beneath the soil surface. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Stackhousia subterranea is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
