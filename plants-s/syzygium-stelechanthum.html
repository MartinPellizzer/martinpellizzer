Syzygium stelechanthum

Syzygium stelechanthum, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include a dense canopy, woody bark, and a complex root system adapted to wet tropical climates.

Syzygium stelechanthum is valued for its ecological role and economic importance in local communities.
