Sidotheca trilobata

Sidotheca trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers that are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to coastal regions of southern Africa, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline environments.

Sidotheca trilobata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
