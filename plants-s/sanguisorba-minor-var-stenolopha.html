Sanguisorba minor var. stenolopha

Sanguisorba minor var. stenolopha, commonly known as narrow-leaved sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
