Setaria utowanaea var. ophiticola

Setaria utowanaea var. ophiticola, commonly known as the narrow-leaf foxtail, is a grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and agricultural areas. It has a C4 photosynthetic pathway, which allows it to efficiently utilize water and thrive in hot, dry environments.

Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.
