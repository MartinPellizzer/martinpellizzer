Spicanta osmundodes

Spicanta osmundodes, commonly known as the marsh fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that emerge from rhizomes growing beneath the water's surface. The plant thrives in shallow, still water and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Spicanta osmundodes plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
