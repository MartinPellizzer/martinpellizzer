Spiraea salicifolia var. latifolia

Spiraea salicifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf weeping willow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features cascading clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a serrated edge, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
