Stachys vulcanica

Stachys vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic betony, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

Stachys vulcanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
