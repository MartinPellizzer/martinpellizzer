Salix morii

Salix morii, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a graceful, cascading appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by small, round fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along waterways.
