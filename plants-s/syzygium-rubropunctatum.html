Syzygium rubropunctatum

Syzygium rubropunctatum, commonly known as the red-spotted tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, elongated fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its fruit contains a sweet, sticky pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer, making it a valuable food source.

Syzygium rubropunctatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
