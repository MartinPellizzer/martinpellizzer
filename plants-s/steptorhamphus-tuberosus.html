Steptorhamphus tuberosus

Steptorhamphus tuberosus, commonly known as the tuberous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming in the summer months. Its tuberous roots are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive harsh winter conditions.

Steptorhamphus tuberosus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
