Simsia annectens

Simsia annectens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple petals and a distinctive central cone-like structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Simsia annectens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.
