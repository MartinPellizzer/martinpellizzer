Selinum falcaria

Selinum falcaria, commonly known as Narrowleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is known for its milky sap, which is toxic to some animals.

Selinum falcaria thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.
