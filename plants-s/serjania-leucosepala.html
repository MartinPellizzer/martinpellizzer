Serjania leucosepala

Serjania leucosepala, commonly known as white-flowered serjania, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae, characterized by its woody structure and showy flowers. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
