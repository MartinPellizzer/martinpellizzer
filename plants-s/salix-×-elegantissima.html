Salix × elegantissima

Salix × elegantissima, commonly known as the Weeping Willow, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that give it a distinctive, graceful appearance. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its rapid growth and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
