Stravadium rheedei

Stravadium rheedei, commonly known as the Kew's orchid, is a rare and fascinating orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically display a mix of vibrant colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, meaning it grows in segments rather than from a single stem, which allows it to spread and establish itself in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment, and it often thrives in shaded, moist forest understories.

Due to its rarity and unique floral structure, Stravadium rheedei is of great interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
