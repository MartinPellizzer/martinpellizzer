Senecio herreanus

Senecio herreanus, commonly known as the Herre's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

Senecio herreanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
