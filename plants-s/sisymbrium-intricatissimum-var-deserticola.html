Sisymbrium intricatissimum var. deserticola

Sisymbrium intricatissimum var. deserticola, commonly known as the desert mustangweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often covered in fine hairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small insects in its native environment.
