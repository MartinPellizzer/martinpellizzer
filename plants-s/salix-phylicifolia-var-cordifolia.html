Salix phylicifolia var. cordifolia

Salix phylicifolia var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf willow, is a variant of the willow family native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically broad and serrated. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in riparian ecosystems.
