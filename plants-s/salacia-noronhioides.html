Salacia noronhioides

Salacia noronhioides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving the appearance of a fiery display. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its bark is smooth and light-colored.

Salacia noronhioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
