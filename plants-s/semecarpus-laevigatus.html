Semecarpus laevigatus

Semecarpus laevigatus, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
