Silene laxantha

Silene laxantha, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Silene laxantha is a perennial species that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
