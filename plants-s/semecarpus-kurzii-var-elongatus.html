Semecarpus kurzii var. elongatus

Semecarpus kurzii var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-fruited Indian chestnut, is a member of the Sapindaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas and is characterized by its elongated, woody fruits that hang in clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by the distinctive, spiny fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.
