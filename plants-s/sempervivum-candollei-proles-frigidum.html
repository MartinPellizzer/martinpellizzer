Sempervivum candollei proles frigidum

Sempervivum candollei proles frigidum, commonly known as the alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant thrives in cool, well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its flowers, which appear in early summer, are typically pink or red and are borne on tall, slender stalks.

This species is known for its hardiness and ability to survive in harsh, cold climates.
