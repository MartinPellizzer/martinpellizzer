Specklinia bipapularis

Specklinia bipapularis, commonly known as the two-leafed specklinia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a distinctive speckled pattern, and it has a unique growth habit that makes it a popular choice for epiphytic cultivation.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to grow in non-tropical conditions.
