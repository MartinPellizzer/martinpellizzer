Statice gummifera

Statice gummifera, commonly known as gum arabic statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance known as gum arabic, which is harvested from its stems and used in various industrial applications. Statice gummifera thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in coastal or sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
