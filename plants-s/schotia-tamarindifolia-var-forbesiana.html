Schotia tamarindifolia var. forbesiana

Schotia tamarindifolia var. forbesiana, commonly known as the Forbes' schotia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are simple, alternate, and pinnately divided, giving the plant a feathery appearance. It produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
