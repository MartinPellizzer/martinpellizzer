Solanum multiflorae

Solanum multiflorae, commonly known as the many-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically features clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the evening. Its leaves are usually ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
