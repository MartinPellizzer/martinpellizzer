Salvia doliostachys

Salvia doliostachys, commonly known as the purple sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia doliostachys is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
