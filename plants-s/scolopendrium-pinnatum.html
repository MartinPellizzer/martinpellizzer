Scolopendrium pinnatum

Scolopendrium pinnatum, commonly known as the creeping fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to form dense, low-growing mats. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Scolopendrium pinnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
