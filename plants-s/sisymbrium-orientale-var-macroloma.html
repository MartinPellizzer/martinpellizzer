Sisymbrium orientale var. macroloma

Sisymbrium orientale var. macroloma, commonly known as the broad-leaved shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It blooms with small white or pale yellow flowers from late spring to early summer.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
