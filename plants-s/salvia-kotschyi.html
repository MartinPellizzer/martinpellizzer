Salvia kotschyi

Salvia kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi sage, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer. It is adapted to dry environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Salvia kotschyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
