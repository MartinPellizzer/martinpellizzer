Salix tristis f. festiva

Salix tristis f. festiva, commonly known as the silver willow or weeping willow, is a unique variant of the weeping willow species.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white bark and long, cascading branches that give it a graceful appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a pale green underside that adds to its visual appeal. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
