Syzygium courtallense

Syzygium courtallense, commonly known as the Kaffir lime, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-shaped fruits with a thick, bumpy rind. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often used in traditional medicine and cooking.

The fruit is valued for its aromatic zest and juice, widely used in culinary applications.
