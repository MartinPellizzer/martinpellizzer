Seubertia obscura

Seubertia obscura, commonly known as the dark seubertia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowers are small and pale, often appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Seubertia obscura is noted for its resilience to drought and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
