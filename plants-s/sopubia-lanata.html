Sopubia lanata

Sopubia lanata, commonly known as the woolly sopubia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique texture and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
