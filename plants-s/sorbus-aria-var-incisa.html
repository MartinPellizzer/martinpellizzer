Sorbus aria var. incisa

Sorbus aria var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical *Sorbus aria*. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting a range of wildlife.
