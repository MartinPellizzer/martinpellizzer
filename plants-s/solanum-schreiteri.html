Solanum schreiteri

Solanum schreiteri, commonly known as the Schreiter's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum schreiteri is often found in humid, mountainous habitats and is adapted to thrive in cooler climates.
