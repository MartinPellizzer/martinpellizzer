Solanum ovigerum var. ovum-rubens

Solanum ovigerum var. ovum-rubens, commonly known as the red eggplant, is a variant of the eggplant species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black skin that develops a reddish hue when mature. The plant produces small, egg-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they are still firm and unripe. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of this variety make it distinct from other eggplant cultivars, particularly in its fruit coloration and plant structure.
