Solanum brasilianum

Solanum brasilianum, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
