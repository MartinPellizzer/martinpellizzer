Senecio pinnatus var. achalensis

Senecio pinnatus var. achalensis, commonly known as the Achalensis Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern India.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
