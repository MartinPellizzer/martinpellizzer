Sarcanthus micranthus

Sarcanthus micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered sarcanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and white. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits known as cypselae.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
