Sasa magnifica subsp. fujitae

Sasa magnifica subsp.

fujitae, commonly known as the Fujita's bamboo, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
