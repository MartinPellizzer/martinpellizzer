Silene brachypoda var. minor

Silene brachypoda var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf catchfly, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Silene brachypoda var. minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
