Sauropus micrasterias

Sauropus micrasterias, commonly known as the small-leaf sauropus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has simple, opposite, and usually elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
