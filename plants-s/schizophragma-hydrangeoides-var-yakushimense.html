Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. yakushimense

Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. yakushimense, commonly known as the Yakushimense honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, dark green upper surface. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and attractive foliage.
