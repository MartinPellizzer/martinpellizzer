Sesleria latifolia var. serpentinica

Sesleria latifolia var. serpentinica, commonly known as the serpent's tongue grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, calcareous soils, often on serpentine soils, which are rich in heavy metals. It produces small, pale green or white spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
