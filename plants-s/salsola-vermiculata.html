Salsola vermiculata

Salsola vermiculata, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments due to its deep root system and succulent leaves. It produces a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in new regions.

Salsola vermiculata is often found in disturbed soils and can colonize areas quickly, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural landscapes.
