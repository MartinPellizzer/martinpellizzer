Saccharum caducum

Saccharum caducum, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its culms are hollow and jointed, a typical feature of grasses, and it can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

Saccharum caducum is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber in some regions.
