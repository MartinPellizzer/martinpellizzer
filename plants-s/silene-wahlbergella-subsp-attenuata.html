Silene wahlbergella subsp. attenuata

Silene wahlbergella subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, and it has a slender, branching stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
