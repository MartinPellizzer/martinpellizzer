Stylidium fissilobum

Stylidium fissilobum, commonly known as the fissured sun-orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred labellum and bright yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and can reach up to 30 centimeters in height.

Stylidium fissilobum is a short-lived perennial, with its life cycle closely tied to seasonal rainfall patterns.
