Sorbus commixta var. takasui

Sorbus commixta var. takasui, commonly known as the Takasui rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and vibrant fruit display.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
