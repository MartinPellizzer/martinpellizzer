Schizophragma megalocarpum

Schizophragma megalocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant bears large, showy berries that are typically orange or red in color, attracting birds and other wildlife. Schizophragma megalocarpum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also noted for its ability to climb and thrive in partial shade.
