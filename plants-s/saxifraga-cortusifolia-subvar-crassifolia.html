Saxifraga cortusifolia subvar. crassifolia

Saxifraga cortusifolia subvar. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features basal rosettes of broad, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and usually appear in shades of white or pale pink.

This subvariety is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
