Sinarundinaria polystachya

Sinarundinaria polystachya, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and prefers moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound with a distinctive, serrated margin.

Sinarundinaria polystachya is often found in rocky or forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
