Stelis reptata

Stelis reptata, commonly known as the creeping stelis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic orchid, typically found growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a short, creeping stem with numerous roots that help it attach to its substrate. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow.

Stelis reptata thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light.
