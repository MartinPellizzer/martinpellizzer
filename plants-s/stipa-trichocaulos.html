Stipa trichocaulos

Stipa trichocaulos, commonly known as the curly hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, tufted inflorescences that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its common name, while its seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Stipa trichocaulos is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
