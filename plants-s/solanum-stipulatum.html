Solanum stipulatum

Solanum stipulatum, commonly known as the stipule nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Solanum stipulatum produces small, yellowish berries that mature to a dark purple color.
