Sideritis subatlantica f. heterostachya

Sideritis subatlantica f. heterostachya, commonly known as the Atlantic sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
