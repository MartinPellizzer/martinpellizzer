Salix cinerea f. subintegra

Salix cinerea f. subintegra, commonly known as the smooth willow or white willow, is a variant of the common willow.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and slender, weeping branches. The leaves are typically elongated, with a serrated margin and a pale underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
