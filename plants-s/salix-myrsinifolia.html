Salix myrsinifolia

Salix myrsinifolia, commonly known as the silky willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
