Scutellaria nepetoides

Scutellaria nepetoides, commonly known as blueweed or narrowleaf skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or violet, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Scutellaria nepetoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
