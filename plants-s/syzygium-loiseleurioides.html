Syzygium loiseleurioides

Syzygium loiseleurioides, commonly known as the red-fruit syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, which is typically red when ripe, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

Syzygium loiseleurioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
