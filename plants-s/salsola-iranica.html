Salsola iranica

Salsola iranica, commonly known as Iranian saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant is well adapted to saline and drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. It produces small greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Salsola iranica is often found in rocky soils and can play a role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
