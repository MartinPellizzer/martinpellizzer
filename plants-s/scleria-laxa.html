Scleria laxa

Scleria laxa, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Scleria laxa typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as riverbanks and swamps, where it can thrive in saturated soils.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its native environment.
