Solanum canasense

Solanum canasense, commonly known as the Canasense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching up to 1 meter in height. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
