Spiraea pachystachys

Spiraea pachystachys, commonly known as the Japanese spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Spiraea pachystachys is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
