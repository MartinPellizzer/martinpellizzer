Sclarea × sylvestris

Sclarea × sylvestris, commonly known as the wild chervil, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland edges and grassy areas. Its aromatic foliage is used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.

Sclarea × sylvestris is valued for its mild, anise-like fragrance and its historical use in both food and apothecary.
