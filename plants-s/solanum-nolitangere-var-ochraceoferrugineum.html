Solanum nolitangere var. ochraceoferrugineum

Solanum nolitangere var. ochraceoferrugineum, commonly known as the ochraceoferrugineum nightshade, is a rare and distinctive variant of the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its striking, rust-colored stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that transition from white to a vibrant orange-red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare plants.
