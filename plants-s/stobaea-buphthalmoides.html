Stobaea buphthalmoides

Stobaea buphthalmoides, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in a showy inflorescence. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in arid environments.
