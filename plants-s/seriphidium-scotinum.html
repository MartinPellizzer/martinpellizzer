Seriphidium scotinum

Seriphidium scotinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Seriphidium scotinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
