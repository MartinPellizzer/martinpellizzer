Semeiandra grandiflora

Semeiandra grandiflora, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces a strong, pleasant fragrance.

Semeiandra grandiflora is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
