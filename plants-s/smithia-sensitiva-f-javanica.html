Smithia sensitiva f. javanica

Smithia sensitiva f. javanica, commonly known as the Javanese sensitive plant, is a variant of the sensitive plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rapid leaf movement in response to touch or light. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape.

The flowers are small, usually white or pale yellow, and appear in clusters.
