Salvia carnea var. punicans

Salvia carnea var. punicans, commonly known as the coral salvia, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It features striking coral-pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
