Schizachyrium brevifolium var. maclaudii

Schizachyrium brevifolium var. maclaudii, commonly known as Maclaud's little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for native plant gardens. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects.
