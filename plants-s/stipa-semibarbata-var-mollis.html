Stipa semibarbata var. mollis

Stipa semibarbata var. mollis, commonly known as soft beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture that distinguishes it from other grass varieties.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
