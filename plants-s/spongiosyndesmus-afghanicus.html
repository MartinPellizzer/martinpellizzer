Spongiosyndesmus afghanicus

Spongiosyndesmus afghanicus, commonly known as the Afghan sponge moss, is a rare and specialized liverwort species.

It is characterized by its intricate, spongy thallus structure, which provides a unique habitat for microorganisms. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions of Central Asia. Its reproductive structures are small and clustered, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
