Sciadotenia paraensis

Sciadotenia paraensis, commonly known as the Paraensis Cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to dry conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers, which can be yellow or red, bloom during the warmer months and attract various pollinators.

Sciadotenia paraensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
