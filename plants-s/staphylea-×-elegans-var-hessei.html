Staphylea × elegans var. hessei

Staphylea × elegans var. hessei, commonly known as the Hessey's sweet pepper tree, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and showy flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
