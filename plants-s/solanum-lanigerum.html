Solanum lanigerum

Solanum lanigerum, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is generally considered a weed with limited horticultural value.
