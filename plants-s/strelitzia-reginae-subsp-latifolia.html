Strelitzia reginae subsp. latifolia

Strelitzia reginae subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed bird of paradise, is a striking tropical plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its large, banana-like leaves and distinctive, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble a bird in flight.

The plant produces pendulous, orange-and-blue flowers that are pollinated by sunbirds and other nectar-feeding birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This subspecies is popular in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and resilience in warm climates.
