Senecio germanicus

Senecio germanicus, commonly known as the German groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer.

The species is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
