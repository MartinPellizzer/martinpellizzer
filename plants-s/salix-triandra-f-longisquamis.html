Salix triandra f. longisquamis

Salix triandra f. longisquamis, commonly known as the longscale willow, is a botanical variant of the silver willow.

It is characterized by its distinctive long, scaly leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant is native to parts of Asia and thrives in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its bark is often smooth and may have a silvery or grayish hue.

This willow species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
