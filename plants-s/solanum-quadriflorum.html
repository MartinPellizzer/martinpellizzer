Solanum quadriflorum

Solanum quadriflorum, commonly known as the four-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, opposite, and ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing several seeds.

The plant is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known species such as tomatoes and potatoes.
