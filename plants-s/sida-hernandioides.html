Sida hernandioides

Sida hernandioides, commonly known as Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and streambanks. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sida hernandioides is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value in gardens.
