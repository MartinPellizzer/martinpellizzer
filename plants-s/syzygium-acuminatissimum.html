Syzygium acuminatissimum

Syzygium acuminatissimum, commonly known as the narrow-pointed tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and Australia, where it thrives in humid climates. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

Syzygium acuminatissimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
