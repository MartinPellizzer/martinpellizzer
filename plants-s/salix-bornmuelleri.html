Salix bornmuelleri

Salix bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it blooms in early spring. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
