Syzygium nomoa

Syzygium nomoa, commonly known as the Java apple or *Cerbera odollam*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. Syzygium nomoa thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
