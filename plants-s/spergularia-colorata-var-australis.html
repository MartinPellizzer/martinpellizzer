Spergularia colorata var. australis

Spergularia colorata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas and arid regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It has small, fleshy leaves and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Spergularia genus, which are known for their ecological importance in stabilizing sandy soils.
