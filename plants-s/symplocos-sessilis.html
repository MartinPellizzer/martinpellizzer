Symplocos sessilis

Symplocos sessilis, commonly known as the sessile waxberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. Symplocos sessilis thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded garden settings.
