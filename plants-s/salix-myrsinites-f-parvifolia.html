Salix myrsinites f. parvifolia

Salix myrsinites f. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a shrub belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it is often found in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks or in wetlands. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
