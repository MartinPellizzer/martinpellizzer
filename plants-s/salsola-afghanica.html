Salsola afghanica

Salsola afghanica, commonly known as Afghan saltwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh, saline environments. It often grows in sandy or rocky soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.

Its ability to thrive in extreme conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
