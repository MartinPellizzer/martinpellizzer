Solanum crispum var. ligustrinum

Solanum crispum var. ligustrinum, commonly known as the purple leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces berries that transition from green to red as they mature, and these berries are toxic to humans and animals. Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its hairy stems and lobed leaves.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
