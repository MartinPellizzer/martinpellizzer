Solanum hoffmanseggii

Solanum hoffmanseggii, commonly known as Hoffman's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic compounds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
