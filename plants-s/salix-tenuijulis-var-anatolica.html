Salix tenuijulis var. anatolica

Salix tenuijulis var. anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian weeping willow, is a shrub or small tree native to regions of Turkey and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden planting in suitable climates.
