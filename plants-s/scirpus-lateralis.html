Scirpus lateralis

Scirpus lateralis, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are broad and flat, often with a waxy coating that helps repel water.

Scirpus lateralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
