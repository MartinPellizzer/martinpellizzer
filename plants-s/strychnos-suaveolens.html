Strychnos suaveolens

Strychnos suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented strychnos, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually elliptical in shape.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds when mature.
