Smilax herbacea var. peduncularis

Smilax herbacea var. peduncularis, commonly known as the slender greenbriar, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with spines and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in hammocks and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
