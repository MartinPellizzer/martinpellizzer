Semele pterygophora

Semele pterygophora, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically golden yellow with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Semele pterygophora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
