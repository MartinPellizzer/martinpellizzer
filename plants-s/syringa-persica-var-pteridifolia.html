Syringa persica var. pteridifolia

Syringa persica var. pteridifolia, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value comes from both its showy flowers and its aromatic foliage.
