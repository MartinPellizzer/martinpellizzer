Sedum × luteolum subsp. hegnaueri

Sedum × luteolum subsp.

hegnaueri, commonly known as the yellow stonecrop, is a hybrid succulent plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically green, sometimes with a slight reddish tinge, and are arranged in a tight rosette pattern. This species is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
