Selinum

Selinum, commonly known as rock samphire, is a hardy perennial plant that thrives in coastal and saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils and can tolerate high levels of salt. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine, and it is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

Selinum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing soil erosion.
