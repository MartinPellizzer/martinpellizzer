Sebastiania integra

Sebastiania integra, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Sebastiania integra is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
