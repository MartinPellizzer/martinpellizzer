Saccharum hirsutum

Saccharum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy bamboo grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stalks and dense, hairy leaves that give it its common name. This plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense stands that can stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its fibrous structure makes it useful for thatching and basket weaving in traditional practices.

Additionally, it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
