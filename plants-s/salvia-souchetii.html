Salvia souchetii

Salvia souchetii, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to dry conditions.

Salvia souchetii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
