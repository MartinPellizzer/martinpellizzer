Solanum subspatulatum

Solanum subspatulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet, followed by berries that mature to a deep blue or black.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments in its native habitat.
