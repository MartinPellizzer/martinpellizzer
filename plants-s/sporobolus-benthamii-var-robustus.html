Sporobolus benthamii var. robustus

Sporobolus benthamii var. robustus, commonly known as the robust spurtsgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and coarse, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in panicles, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
