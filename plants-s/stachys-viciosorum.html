Stachys viciosorum

Stachys viciosorum, commonly known as the creeping betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Stachys viciosorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
