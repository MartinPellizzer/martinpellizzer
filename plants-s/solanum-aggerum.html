Solanum aggerum

Solanum aggerum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, fleshy berries containing several seeds. Solanum aggerum is known for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for various ailments.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
