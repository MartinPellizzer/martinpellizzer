Soliva valdiviana

Soliva valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian soliva, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in the temperate rainforests of Chile and Argentina.

It is a perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Soliva valdiviana has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various invertebrates.
