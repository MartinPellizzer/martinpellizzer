Sideritis tragoriganum subsp. reverchonii

Sideritis tragoriganum subsp.

reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
