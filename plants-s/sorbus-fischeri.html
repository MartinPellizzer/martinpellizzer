Sorbus fischeri

Sorbus fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's whitebeams, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. Its fruit is a small, aggregate berry that matures to a dark red or black color.

Sorbus fischeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
