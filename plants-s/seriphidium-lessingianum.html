Seriphidium lessingianum

Seriphidium lessingianum, commonly known as the lesser fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and coastal areas. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well adapted to arid environments.
