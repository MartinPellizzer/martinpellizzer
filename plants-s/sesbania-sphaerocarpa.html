Sesbania sphaerocarpa

Sesbania sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, inflated pods that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, ovate leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Sesbania sphaerocarpa is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment.
