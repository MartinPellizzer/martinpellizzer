Stylocoryna pavettoides

Stylocoryna pavettoides, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Stylocoryna pavettoides is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
