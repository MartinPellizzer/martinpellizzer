Samara pentandra

Samara pentandra, commonly known as the five-angled samara, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its distinctive five-angled fruit, which is a type of dry, dehiscent fruit called a samara. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The samara fruit disperses seeds by wind, contributing to its spread in natural habitats.
