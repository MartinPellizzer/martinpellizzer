Solanum fraternorum

Solanum fraternorum, commonly known as the "fraternal nightshade," is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and lobed, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with five petals, and it produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
