Setaria autumnalis

Setaria autumnalis, commonly known as autumn panicgrass, is a warm-season grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, gray-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and disturbed areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
