Scleranthus cancellatus

Scleranthus cancellatus, commonly known as the net-veined sandparsley, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and disturbed ground. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

The species is characterized by its distinctive net-like venation on the leaves, which gives it its scientific name.
