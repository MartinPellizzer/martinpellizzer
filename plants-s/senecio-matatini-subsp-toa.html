Senecio matatini subsp. toa

Senecio matatini subsp.

toa, commonly known as the Matatini groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
