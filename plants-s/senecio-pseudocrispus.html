Senecio pseudocrispus

Senecio pseudocrispus, commonly known as the serrated groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often serrated and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
