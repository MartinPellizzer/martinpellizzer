Saxifraga adscendens var. aprica

Saxifraga adscendens var. aprica, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats, often thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and mountain slopes.
