Salvia digyna

Salvia digyna, commonly known as mountain sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems that often branch near the base. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in whorls along the stems, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

Salvia digyna is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
