Sorghum mannii

Sorghum mannii, commonly known as the tall millet, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is drought-resistant and thrives in warm, arid climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its seeds are small and typically pale yellow or brown.

Sorghum mannii is often used for forage and has historical significance in traditional agriculture.
