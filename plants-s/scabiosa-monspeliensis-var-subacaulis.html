Scabiosa monspeliensis var. subacaulis

Scabiosa monspeliensis var. subacaulis, commonly known as the creeping scabious, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that often lie close to the ground, producing clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy and covered in a soft, downy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
