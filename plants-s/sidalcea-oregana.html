Sidalcea oregana

Sidalcea oregana, commonly known as the Oregon checkerbloom, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits.
