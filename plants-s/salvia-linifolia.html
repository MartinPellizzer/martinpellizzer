Salvia linifolia

Salvia linifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
