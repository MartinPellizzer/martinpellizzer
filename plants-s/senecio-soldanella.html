Senecio soldanella

Senecio soldanella, commonly known as the snowdrop gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits that contain tiny seeds.

Due to its habitat and appearance, it is often found in mountainous areas and is considered a rare and fragile species.
