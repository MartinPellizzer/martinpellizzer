Spiraea panigrahiana

Spiraea panigrahiana, commonly known as the Himalayan spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Spiraea panigrahiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand cold climates.
