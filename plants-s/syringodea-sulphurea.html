Syringodea sulphurea

Syringodea sulphurea, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, fragrant flowers. Syringodea sulphurea typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
