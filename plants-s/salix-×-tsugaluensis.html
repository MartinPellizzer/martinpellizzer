Salix × tsugaluensis

Salix × tsugaluensis, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the Japanese willow (Salix koreana) and the mountain willow (Salix tsugaefolia).

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and narrow, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along water features. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for wetland or pond edge planting.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
