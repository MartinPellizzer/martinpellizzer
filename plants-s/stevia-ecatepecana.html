Stevia ecatepecana

Stevia ecatepecana, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are rich in steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Stevia ecatepecana is also valued for its potential health benefits, including its use as a low-calorie sweetener alternative.
