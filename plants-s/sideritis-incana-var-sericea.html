Sideritis incana var. sericea

Sideritis incana var. sericea, commonly known as silver-haired ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color, arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Sideritis incana var. sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
