Solanum stenotomum var. poccoya

Solanum stenotomum var. poccoya, commonly known as the Poccoya nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of white or pale green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
