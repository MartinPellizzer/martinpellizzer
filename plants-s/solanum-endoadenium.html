Solanum endoadenium

Solanum endoadenium, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in groups of three, and it produces berries that contain a single seed.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
