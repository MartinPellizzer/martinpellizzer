Salix insignis

Salix insignis, commonly known as the yellow willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are narrow, elongated, and have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and riparian zones.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
