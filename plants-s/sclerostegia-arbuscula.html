Sclerostegia arbuscula

Sclerostegia arbuscula, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on short peduncles. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like surface, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which is often used in traditional medicine.
