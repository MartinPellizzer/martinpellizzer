Spinacia fera

Spinacia fera, commonly known as wild spinach, is a leafy green plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found growing in fields, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, making it a valuable forage plant for both humans and livestock.

Spinacia fera is also known for its high nutritional value, containing significant amounts of vitamins A, C, and K.
