Scolopia fragrans

Scolopia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scolopia fragrans is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and pleasant aroma.
