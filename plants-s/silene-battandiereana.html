Silene battandiereana

Silene battandiereana, commonly known as the Battandiere's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. It has simple, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
