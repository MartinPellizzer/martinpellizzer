Sumatroscirpus rupestris

Sumatroscirpus rupestris, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Sumatra.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soil environments, often in sunny locations. Its botanical features include a prostrate or upright growth habit, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Sumatroscirpus rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
