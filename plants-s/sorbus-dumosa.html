Sorbus dumosa

Sorbus dumosa, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Sorbus dumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in garden and natural settings.
