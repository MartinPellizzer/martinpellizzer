Sasa kagamiana var. kumagaiana

Sasa kagamiana var. kumagaiana, commonly known as the Kumagawa bamboo, is a clump-forming perennial grass native to Japan.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and cuisine. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
