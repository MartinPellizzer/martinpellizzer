Salvia forskohlei

Salvia forskohlei, commonly known as Indian sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of blue to violet tubular flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The essential oils derived from Salvia forskohlei contain compounds like forskolin, which have been studied for their potential health benefits.
