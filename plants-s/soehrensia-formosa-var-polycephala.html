Soehrensia formosa var. polycephala

Soehrensia formosa var. polycephala, commonly known as the golden star hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bracts that resemble stars, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant foliage and showy inflorescences.
