Schistophyllidium bifurcum

Schistophyllidium bifurcum, commonly known as the two-branched leaf fern, is a unique member of the Schistophyllidium genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive bifurcated leaves, which split into two distinct branches, giving it a fern-like appearance. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its foliage is composed of thin, delicate fronds that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it exhibits a simple rhizomatous structure, with roots and leaves growing from a central stem.
