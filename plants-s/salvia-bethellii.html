Salvia bethellii

Salvia bethellii, commonly known as Bethell's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Salvia bethellii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
