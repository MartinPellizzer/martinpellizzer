Syzygium stocksii

Syzygium stocksii, commonly known as the New Guinea apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, round fruits with a sweet, tart flavor, similar to a cross between a lychee and a persimmon. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Syzygium stocksii is also valued for its potential in agroforestry systems due to its ability to grow in various soil types.
