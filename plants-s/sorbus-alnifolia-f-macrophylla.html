Sorbus alnifolia f. macrophylla

Sorbus alnifolia f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are larger than those of the typical species, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
