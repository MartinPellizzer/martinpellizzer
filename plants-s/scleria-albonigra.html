Scleria albonigra

Scleria albonigra, commonly known as the white-stemmed sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and rushes. The plant is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems that are often covered in a white or pale bloom, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and parallel-veined, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Scleria albonigra typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist soils and often found near water bodies.
