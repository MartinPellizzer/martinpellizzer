Solanum aethiopicum var. integrifolium

Solanum aethiopicum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with entire margins and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically egg-shaped and can range in color from green to purple when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and culinary uses.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
