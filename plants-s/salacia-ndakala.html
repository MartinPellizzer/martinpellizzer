Salacia ndakala

Salacia ndakala, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal salacia, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are small and clustered in catkins.

Salacia ndakala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
