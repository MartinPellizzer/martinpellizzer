Solms-laubachia pulcherrima f. atrichophylla

Solms-laubachia pulcherrima f. atrichophylla, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a unique cultivar of the tamarind tree.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, smooth bark and its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing sweet, sticky pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a shade tree.
