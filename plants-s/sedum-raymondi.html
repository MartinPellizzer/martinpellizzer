Sedum raymondi

Sedum raymondi, commonly known as Raymond's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Sedum raymondi is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
