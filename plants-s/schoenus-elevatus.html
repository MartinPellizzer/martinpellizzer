Schoenus elevatus

Schoenus elevatus, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Schoenus elevatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems.
