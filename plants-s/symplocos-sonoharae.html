Symplocos sonoharae

Symplocos sonoharae, commonly known as the Sonohara's wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species. Symplocos sonoharae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
