Stoechadomentha macrophylla

Stoechadomentha macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf water shield, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. Its roots are adapted to thrive in shallow water, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Stoechadomentha macrophylla is an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes in wetland habitats.
