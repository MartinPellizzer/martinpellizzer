Spilanthes bicolor

Spilanthes bicolor, commonly known as the twinflower or false hellebore, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in pairs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Spilanthes bicolor is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
