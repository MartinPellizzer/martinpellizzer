Scrophularia gontscharovii

Scrophularia gontscharovii, commonly known as the Siberian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct aromatic scent. Its flowers are tubular, pale purple to pink in color, and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Scrophularia gontscharovii is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
