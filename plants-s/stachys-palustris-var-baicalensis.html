Stachys palustris var. baicalensis

Stachys palustris var. baicalensis, commonly known as the marsh woundwort, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in eastern North America.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
