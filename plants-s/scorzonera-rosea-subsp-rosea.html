Scorzonera rosea subsp. rosea

Scorzonera rosea subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the rose tarweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Scorzonera rosea subsp.
