Senecio phlomidifolius

Senecio phlomidifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to southern Africa and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
