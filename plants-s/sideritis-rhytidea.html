Sideritis rhytidea

Sideritis rhytidea, commonly known as the Greek violet or mountain violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features slender stems and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis rhytidea is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its medicinal properties.
