Serratula serrata

Serratula serrata, commonly known as the serrated hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features serrated leaves and yellow-orange ray flowers surrounding a central disc of tubular florets. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Serratula serrata is often found in open habitats and can spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
