Sphaerocionium pyramidatum

Sphaerocionium pyramidatum, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pyramidal growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Sphaerocionium pyramidatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
