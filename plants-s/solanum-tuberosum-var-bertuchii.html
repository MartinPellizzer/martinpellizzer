Solanum tuberosum var. bertuchii

Solanum tuberosum var. bertuchii, commonly known as the Bertuchii potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which often includes irregular shapes and varying skin colors. This variety is native to South America, specifically the Andean region, where it has been cultivated for centuries. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, sharing traits with other nightshade plants.

Its genetic diversity makes it valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving disease resistance and yield in potato crops.
