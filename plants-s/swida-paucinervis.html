Swida paucinervis

Swida paucinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. Swida paucinervis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
