Senecillis lapathifolia

Senecillis lapathifolia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct central stem that supports its composite flower heads. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure and medicinal properties historically used by indigenous peoples.
