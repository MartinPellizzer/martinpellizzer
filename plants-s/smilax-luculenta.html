Smilax luculenta

Smilax luculenta, commonly known as the sweet flag or yellow smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with spiny tips. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and woodlands.

Smilax luculenta is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
