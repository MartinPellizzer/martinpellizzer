Smilacina scilloidea var. rosea

Smilacina scilloidea var. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered false Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant blooms in early spring, often before its leaves fully emerge, and the flowers are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
