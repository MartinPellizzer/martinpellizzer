Selinum carvifolia

Selinum carvifolia, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a whitish coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden conditions.

Due to its strong scent, it is often used in herbal medicine and as a natural pest repellent.
