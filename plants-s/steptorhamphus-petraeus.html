Steptorhamphus petraeus

Steptorhamphus petraeus, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Steptorhamphus petraeus is typically found in rocky outcrops and dry, sandy soils.
