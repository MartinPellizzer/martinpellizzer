Sasa nana var. unoi

Sasa nana var. unoi, commonly known as the Oahu bamboo, is a clumping bamboo native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a significant plant in Hawaiian botany.
