Spiraea × difformis

Spiraea × difformis, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It is characterized by its arching, willow-like branches that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall.

Spiraea × difformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.
