Sanguisorba vulgaris subvar. mauritanica

Sanguisorba vulgaris subvar. mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and North Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
