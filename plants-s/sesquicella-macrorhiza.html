Sesquicella macrorhiza

Sesquicella macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted sesquicella, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often tuberous roots, which are adapted for storing nutrients and water. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a broad, heart-shaped or ovate form.

Sesquicella macrorhiza is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
