Sesamum trifoliatum

Sesamum trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved sesame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, oil-rich seeds. These seeds are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their nutritional value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
