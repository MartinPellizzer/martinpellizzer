Sambucus nigra f. luteomarmorata

Sambucus nigra f. luteomarmorata, commonly known as the golden marbled elderberry, is a cultivar of the elderberry plant characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

Its leaves display a unique combination of yellow and marbled green patterns, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens for its ornamental qualities.
