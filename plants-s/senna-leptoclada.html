Senna leptoclada

Senna leptoclada, commonly known as the slender senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is shrub-like, with narrow, pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, curved pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Senna leptoclada is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
