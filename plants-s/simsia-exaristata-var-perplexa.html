Simsia exaristata var. perplexa

Simsia exaristata var. perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing thistle, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
