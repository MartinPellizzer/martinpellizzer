Sonerila alata

Sonerila alata, commonly known as the winged sonerila, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its distinctive winged petioles on its leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by colorful, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
