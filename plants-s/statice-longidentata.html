Statice longidentata

Statice longidentata, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its showy, blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to withstand salty air and windy conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Statice longidentata is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting, colorful blooms.
