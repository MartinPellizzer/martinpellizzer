Statice limonium proles serotina

Statice limonium proles serotina, commonly known as sea lavender or winter sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and tidal zones. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to coastal landscapes.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
