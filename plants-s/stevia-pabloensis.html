Stevia pabloensis

Stevia pabloensis, commonly known as the Pablo's stevia, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its medicinal and commercial potential, it has attracted interest for sustainable cultivation and use in natural sweetener production.
