Salix montis-lopatinii

Salix montis-lopatinii, commonly known as the mountain willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same individual. It thrives in cold, dry environments and is adapted to high altitudes.

The bark and leaves of Salix montis-lopatinii have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.
