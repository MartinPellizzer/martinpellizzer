Scutellaria galericulata var. vulgaris

Scutellaria galericulata var. vulgaris, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, four-lobed fruits.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
