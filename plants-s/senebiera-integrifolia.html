Senebiera integrifolia

Senebiera integrifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is distinguished by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves with smooth, entire margins. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid forests.

Senebiera integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
