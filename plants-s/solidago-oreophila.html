Solidago oreophila

Solidago oreophila, commonly known as the mountain goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago oreophila is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
