Senecio costaricensis

Senecio costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican Senecio, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

Senecio costaricensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
