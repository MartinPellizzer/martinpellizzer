Stanhopea wardii var. aurea

Stanhopea wardii var. aurea, commonly known as the golden stanhopea, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

This variant is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns of darker hues. It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is known for its complex floral structure, typical of many orchid species. The plant prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its conservation status is a concern due to habitat loss and over-collection.
