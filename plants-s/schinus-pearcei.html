Schinus pearcei

Schinus pearcei, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, bright red berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slight serration along the edges.

Schinus pearcei is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
