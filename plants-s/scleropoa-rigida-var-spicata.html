Scleropoa rigida var. spicata

Scleropoa rigida var. spicata, commonly known as the rigid fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in arid and semi-arid environments.
