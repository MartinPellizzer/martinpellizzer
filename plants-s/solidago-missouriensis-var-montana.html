Solidago missouriensis var. montana

Solidago missouriensis var. montana, commonly known as the Montana goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and northern United States.

It typically grows in dry to moist prairies, meadows, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
