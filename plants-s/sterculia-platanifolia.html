Sterculia platanifolia

Sterculia platanifolia, commonly known as the sycamore fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble those of the plane tree. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
