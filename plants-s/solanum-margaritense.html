Solanum margaritense

Solanum margaritense, commonly known as the pearl apple or white eggplant, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically white or pale yellow, with a soft, fleshy texture and a mild, slightly sweet flavor. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy surface, and it often grows as a shrub or small tree.

Solanum margaritense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
