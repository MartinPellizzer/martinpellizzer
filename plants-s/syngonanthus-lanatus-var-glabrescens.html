Syngonanthus lanatus var. glabrescens

Syngonanthus lanatus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the woolly evergreen hibiscus, is a variant of the evergreen hibiscus native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that become smooth with age, giving it the common name "glabrescens.". The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and a yellow center, blooming in clusters during the warm season.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
