Salvia horminum var. viridis

Salvia horminum var. viridis, commonly known as green hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, greenish-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

It is often used in herbal medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
