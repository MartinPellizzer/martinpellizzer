Sarcocaulon marlothii

Sarcocaulon marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's rock cress, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in rosettes at the base.

Sarcocaulon marlothii is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
