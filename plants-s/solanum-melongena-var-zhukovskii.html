Solanum melongena var. zhukovskii

Solanum melongena var. zhukovskii, commonly known as the Zhukovskii eggplant, is a botanical variant of the common eggplant.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often pointed fruit shape, which differs from the typical round or oval form of standard eggplants. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and shares genetic traits with other solanum species. It is native to Central Asia and has been cultivated for its distinctive appearance and potential culinary uses.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its genetic diversity and adaptability to different growing conditions.
