Spiraea quinqueloba

Spiraea quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features showy, flat-topped clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Spiraea quinqueloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
