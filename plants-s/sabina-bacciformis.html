Sabina bacciformis

Sabina bacciformis, commonly known as the red cedar or Japanese red cedar, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain edible, sweet-tasting seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The botanical features of Sabina bacciformis make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticulture.
