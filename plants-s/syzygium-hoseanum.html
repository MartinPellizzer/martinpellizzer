Syzygium hoseanum

Syzygium hoseanum, commonly known as the Australian satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rainforest areas.

Syzygium hoseanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
