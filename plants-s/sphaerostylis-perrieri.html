Sphaerostylis perrieri

Sphaerostylis perrieri, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in its natural habitat.
