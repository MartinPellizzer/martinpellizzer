Spiranthes pubicaulis

Spiranthes pubicaulis, commonly known as the hairy spire orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-like arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are grass-like and linear, while its flowers are small, white to pale green, and often have a slight fragrance.

Spiranthes pubicaulis is known for its unique floral structure and is often found in colonies, making it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
