Salvia stachydifolia

Salvia stachydifolia, commonly known as woolly sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Salvia stachydifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
