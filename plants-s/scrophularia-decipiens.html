Scrophularia decipiens

Scrophularia decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with showy, often tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with two lips that resemble insect-like structures, aiding in pollination by insects.

Scrophularia decipiens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
