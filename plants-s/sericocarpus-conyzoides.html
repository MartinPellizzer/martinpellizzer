Sericocarpus conyzoides

Sericocarpus conyzoides, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry to moist soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It produces small, hard, dry fruits called achenes, which are often covered in fine hairs.

Sericocarpus conyzoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
