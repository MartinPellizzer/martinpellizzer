Senecio gilgianus

Senecio gilgianus, commonly known as the Gilgian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture.

Senecio gilgianus is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
