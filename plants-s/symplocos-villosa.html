Symplocos villosa

Symplocos villosa, commonly known as the hairy wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Symplocos villosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
