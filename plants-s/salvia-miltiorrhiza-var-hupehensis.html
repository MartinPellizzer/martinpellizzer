Salvia miltiorrhiza var. hupehensis

Salvia miltiorrhiza var. hupehensis, commonly known as Chinese red sage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are valued in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flower structure.
