Scirpus georgianus

Scirpus georgianus, commonly known as the Georgia bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets at the top of its stems, which are typically green to brown in color. Scirpus georgianus thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
