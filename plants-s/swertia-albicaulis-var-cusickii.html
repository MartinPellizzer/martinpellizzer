Swertia albicaulis var. cusickii

Swertia albicaulis var. cusickii, commonly known as the Sierra Whitehead, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada and other mountain ranges in western North America.

It belongs to the Gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its ecological significance in supporting local pollinators and contributing to alpine biodiversity.
