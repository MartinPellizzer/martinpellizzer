Scleranthus annuus subsp. polycarpos

Scleranthus annuus subsp.

polycarpos, commonly known as the hairy thornweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

The plant has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, as well as its growth habit and flowering patterns.
