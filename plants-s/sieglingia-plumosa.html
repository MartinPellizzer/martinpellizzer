Sieglingia plumosa

Sieglingia plumosa, commonly known as the featherleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves that are soft to the touch and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Sieglingia plumosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and drought tolerance.
