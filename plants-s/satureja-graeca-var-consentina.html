Satureja graeca var. consentina

Satureja graeca var. consentina, commonly known as Greek thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are fragrant and often used in cooking or as a herbal remedy.
