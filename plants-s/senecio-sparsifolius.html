Senecio sparsifolius

Senecio sparsifolius, commonly known as the sparse-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio sparsifolius is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
