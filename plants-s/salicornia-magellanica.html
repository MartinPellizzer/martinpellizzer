Salicornia magellanica

Salicornia magellanica, commonly known as Magellan glassweed, is a halophytic plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in estuaries and tidal flats. The plant has succulent, jointed stems that are typically covered in a layer of fine, white salt crystals. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or reddish and bloom in early spring.

Salicornia magellanica plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
