Senecio integerrimus var. ochroleucus

Senecio integerrimus var. ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow owl's clover, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that aid in wind pollination. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to native plant gardens.
