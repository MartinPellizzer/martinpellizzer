Solidago senanensis

Solidago senanensis, commonly known as the Senanese goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago senanensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
