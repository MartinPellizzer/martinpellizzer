Sophora mollis

Sophora mollis, commonly known as the velvet tamarack, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 11 pairs of oval, hairless leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in upright clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing dark, elongated seeds. Sophora mollis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
