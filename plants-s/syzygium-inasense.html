Syzygium inasense

Syzygium inasense, commonly known as the Java apple or *Sala*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, round, red or yellow berries. Syzygium inasense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
