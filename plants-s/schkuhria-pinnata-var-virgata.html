Schkuhria pinnata var. virgata

Schkuhria pinnata var. virgata, commonly known as the Virginia fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes form a dense mat, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The leaves are usually evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.
