Succisa rhodanensis

Succisa rhodanensis, commonly known as the Rhodanese succulent, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
