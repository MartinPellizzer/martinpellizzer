Statice sanjurjensis

Statice sanjurjensis, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its compact, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well adapted to sandy and saline soils.

Statice sanjurjensis is often used in ornamental gardening and is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
