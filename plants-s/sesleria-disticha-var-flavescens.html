Sesleria disticha var. flavescens

Sesleria disticha var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-haired sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a pale yellow or greenish hue. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shady conditions.
