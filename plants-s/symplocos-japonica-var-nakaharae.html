Symplocos japonica var. nakaharae

Symplocos japonica var. nakaharae, commonly known as the Nakahara's Japanese waxberry, is a shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. It thrives in acidic soils and is often found in forested areas, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
