Senecio ledifolius var. lehmannii

Senecio ledifolius var. lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance and distinctive appearance.
