Sedum tosaense

Sedum tosaense, commonly known as the Tosa stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to Japan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
