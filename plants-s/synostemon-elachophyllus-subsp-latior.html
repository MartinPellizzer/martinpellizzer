Synostemon elachophyllus subsp. latior

Synostemon elachophyllus subsp.

latior, commonly known as the broad-leaved synostemon, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant.
