Sapium insigne var. malabaricum

Sapium insigne var. malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar cudroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of southern India.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, thick, fleshy roots that store nutrients. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
