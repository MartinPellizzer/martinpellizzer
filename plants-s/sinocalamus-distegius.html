Sinocalamus distegius

Sinocalamus distegius, commonly known as the broad-leaved bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and robust culms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Sinocalamus distegius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and sustainable use in construction and crafts.
