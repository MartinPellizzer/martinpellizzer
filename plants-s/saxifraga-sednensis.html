Saxifraga sednensis

Saxifraga sednensis, commonly known as the Antarctic saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the remote regions of Antarctica.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in the extreme cold and harsh conditions of the continent. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom during the brief summer months. Its leaves are typically rosette-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs to protect against freezing temperatures.

Saxifraga sednensis is one of the few flowering plants that survive in the Antarctic environment, showcasing remarkable adaptations to its extreme habitat.
