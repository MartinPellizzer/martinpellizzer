Sulcorebutia crispata subsp. hertusii

Sulcorebutia crispata subsp.

hertusii, commonly known as the Hertusii crassula, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, blue-green to grayish leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or violet during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
