Sparaxis grandiflora subsp. grandiflora

Sparaxis grandiflora subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the orange ox-eye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features upright, grass-like foliage and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, with clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens in suitable climates.
