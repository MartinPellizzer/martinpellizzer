Saussurea alpina var. intermedia

Saussurea alpina var. intermedia, commonly known as the Alpine saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the European Alps.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving at high elevations with cool temperatures and well-drained soils. Its fibrous roots and sturdy structure allow it to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also play a role in pollination by attracting various insects.
