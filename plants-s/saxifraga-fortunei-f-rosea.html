Saxifraga fortunei f. rosea

Saxifraga fortunei f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered stonecrop, is a compact, low-growing perennial succulent native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or pinkish hue, and it produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its foliage and blooms are often used in ornamental horticulture for their attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
