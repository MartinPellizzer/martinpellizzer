Senecio cuneatus

Senecio cuneatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Senecio cuneatus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
