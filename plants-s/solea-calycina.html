Solea calycina

Solea calycina, commonly known as the calycine sole, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
