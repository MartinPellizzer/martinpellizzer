Spartocytisus filipes

Spartocytisus filipes, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Spartocytisus filipes is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture.
