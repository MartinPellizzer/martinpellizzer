Serapias ausoniae

Serapias ausoniae, commonly known as the Ausonian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow and orange flowers with a prominent lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Serapias ausoniae is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
