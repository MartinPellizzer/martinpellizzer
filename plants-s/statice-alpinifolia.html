Statice alpinifolia

Statice alpinifolia, commonly known as the alpine statice, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spike-like flower heads. The plant typically produces small, colorful flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom from summer to autumn. Statice alpinifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in harsh alpine environments with low temperatures and strong winds.
