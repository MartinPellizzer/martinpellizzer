Selaginella kanehirae

Selaginella kanehirae, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair spleenwort, is a rare and endangered species of spore-bearing plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant exhibits a unique ability to survive extreme environmental conditions, including desiccation, due to its specialized water-storing structures. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rock crevices and forest floors.

Its conservation status highlights the importance of preserving its natural habitat to prevent further decline.
