Senecio runcinifolius

Senecio runcinifolius, commonly known as the woolly goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, daisy-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
