Sinapis juncea var. napiformis

Sinapis juncea var. napiformis, commonly known as the turnip mustard, is a variant of the oilseed rape species.

It is characterized by its swollen, globe-shaped root, which is typically harvested for its edible qualities. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It produces small yellow flowers and has a robust, branched stem.

The leaves are alternate, lobed, and often covered in fine hairs.
