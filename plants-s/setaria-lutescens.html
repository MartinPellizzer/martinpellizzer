Setaria lutescens

Setaria lutescens, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and golden-yellow spikelets that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open fields and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate to warm climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Setaria lutescens is often used as a forage crop for livestock due to its high nutritional value and adaptability.
