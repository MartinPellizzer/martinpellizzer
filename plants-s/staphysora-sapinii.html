Staphysora sapinii

Staphysora sapinii, commonly known as the Alpine catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and dry grasslands.

Staphysora sapinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
