Sedum albanicum

Sedum albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the Balkans.

It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that store water efficiently. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, appearing in summer, and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Sedum albanicum is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
