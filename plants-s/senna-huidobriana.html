Senna huidobriana

Senna huidobriana, commonly known as the golden senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and the plant can grow as a shrub or small tree.

Senna huidobriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
