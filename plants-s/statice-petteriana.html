Statice petteriana

Statice petteriana, commonly known as the dwarf statice, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

Statice petteriana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
