Sophora ludovicea-decima-sexta

Sophora ludovicea-decima-sexta, commonly known as the Chinese scholar tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

The tree also has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
