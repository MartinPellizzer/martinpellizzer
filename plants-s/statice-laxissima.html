Statice laxissima

Statice laxissima, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

Statice laxissima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
