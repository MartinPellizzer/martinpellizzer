Sanguisorba vulgaris proles verrucosa

Sanguisorba vulgaris proles verrucosa, commonly known as the rough sanguisorba, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
