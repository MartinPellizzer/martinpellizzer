Scirpus rufus var. balticus

Scirpus rufus var. balticus, commonly known as the Baltic bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush found in wetland habitats.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, spiky inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
