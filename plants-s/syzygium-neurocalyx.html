Syzygium neurocalyx

Syzygium neurocalyx, commonly known as the fragrant sandpaper apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, sandpaper-like bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Syzygium neurocalyx is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
