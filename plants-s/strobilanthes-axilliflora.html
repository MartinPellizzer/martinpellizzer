Strobilanthes axilliflora

Strobilanthes axilliflora, commonly known as the "blue ghost" or "false flax," is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often appearing at night. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowering period is brief, usually occurring during the rainy season.

Strobilanthes axilliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
