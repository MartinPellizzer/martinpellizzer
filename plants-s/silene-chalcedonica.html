Silene chalcedonica

Silene chalcedonica, commonly known as the Chalcedony Silene, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Silene chalcedonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
