Sloanea hirsuta subsp. hirsuta

Sloanea hirsuta subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy sloanea, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

The species is notable for its adaptability to a variety of soil types and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
