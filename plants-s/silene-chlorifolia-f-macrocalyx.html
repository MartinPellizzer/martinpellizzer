Silene chlorifolia f. macrocalyx

Silene chlorifolia f. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered bladderwort, is a unique variant of the bladderwort plant.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, distinguishing it from other members of the genus. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, often shaded environments, such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are typically bluish-green and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is notable for its specialized reproductive structures, including small, bladder-like organs that aid in capturing small aquatic organisms.
