Salvia florida

Salvia florida, commonly known as the flowering sage, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia florida is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional remedies.
