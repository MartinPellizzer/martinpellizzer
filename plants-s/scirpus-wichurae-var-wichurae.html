Scirpus wichurae var. wichurae

Scirpus wichurae var. wichurae, commonly known as the Wichura's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
