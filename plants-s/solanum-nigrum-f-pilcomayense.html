Solanum nigrum f. pilcomayense

Solanum nigrum f. pilcomayense, commonly known as the black nightshade variant from Pilcomayo, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Paraguay. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This subspecies is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to thrive in humid environments.
