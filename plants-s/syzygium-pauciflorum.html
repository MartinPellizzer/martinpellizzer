Syzygium pauciflorum

Syzygium pauciflorum, commonly known as the rare or little-flowered clove.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruits are edible, small drupes with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Syzygium pauciflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
