Sutera integrifolia var. parvifolia

Sutera integrifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and entire along their margins. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to gardens in suitable climates.
