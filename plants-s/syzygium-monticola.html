Syzygium monticola

Syzygium monticola, commonly known as the mountain clove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, clove-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in humid, well-drained soils. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Syzygium monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
