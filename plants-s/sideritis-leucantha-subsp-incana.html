Sideritis leucantha subsp. incana

Sideritis leucantha subsp.

incana, commonly known as white-haired rock sedge, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
