Silene tenuifolia

Silene tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene tenuifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
