Salvia sagittata

Salvia sagittata, commonly known as arrowleaf sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Salvia sagittata is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
