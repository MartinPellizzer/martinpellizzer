Senecio lagopus

Senecio lagopus, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio lagopus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
