Secale strictum subsp. ciliatoglume

Secale strictum subsp.

ciliatoglume, commonly known as the slender ryegrass, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with ciliate margins.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability and is often found in temperate climates.
