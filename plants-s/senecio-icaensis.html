Senecio icaensis

Senecio icaensis, commonly known as the Ica Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

Senecio icaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
