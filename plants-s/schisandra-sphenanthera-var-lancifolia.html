Schisandra sphenanthera var. lancifolia

Schisandra sphenanthera var. lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed, red berries that turn dark purple when mature. The plant features simple, opposite leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The fruit is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
