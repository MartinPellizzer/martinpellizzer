Sideritis × candelii var. gamiri

Sideritis × candelii var. gamiri, commonly known as the Cretan sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, and this variety is no exception.
