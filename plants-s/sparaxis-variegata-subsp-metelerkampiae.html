Sparaxis variegata subsp. metelerkampiae

Sparaxis variegata subsp.

metelerkampiae, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family. It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow, tulip-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in sandy or gravelly soils. It is native to the Western and Northern Cape provinces of South Africa.
