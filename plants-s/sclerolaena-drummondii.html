Sclerolaena drummondii

Sclerolaena drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's brittlebush, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth form, with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. Sclerolaena drummondii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
