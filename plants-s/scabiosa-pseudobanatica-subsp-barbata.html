Scabiosa pseudobanatica subsp. barbata

Scabiosa pseudobanatica subsp.

barbata, commonly known as the barbed scabious, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery bracts and clusters of small, pink or white flowers.

The subspecies barbata is distinguished by its barbed, hairy bracts that give it its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The plant is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
