Stanleya pinnata var. texana

Stanleya pinnata var. texana, commonly known as the Texas penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is adapted to arid and rocky environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
