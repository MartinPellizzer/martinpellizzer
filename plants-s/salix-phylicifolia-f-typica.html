Salix phylicifolia f. typica

Salix phylicifolia f. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands, making it a common sight in riparian habitats.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
