Scaevola sericea var. taccada

Scaevola sericea var. taccada, commonly known as the velvetleaf scaevola, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to both garden landscapes and floral arrangements.
