Sauvagesia erecta var. stricta

Sauvagesia erecta var. stricta, commonly known as the upright sauvagesia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in rock gardens or as a border plant.

The species is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
