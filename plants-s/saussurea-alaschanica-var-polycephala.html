Saussurea alaschanica var. polycephala

Saussurea alaschanica var. polycephala, commonly known as the Alaska safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Alaska.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and numerous yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and the leaves are narrow and linear.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
