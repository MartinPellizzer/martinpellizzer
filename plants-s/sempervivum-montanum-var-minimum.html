Sempervivum montanum var. minimum

Sempervivum montanum var. minimum, commonly known as the miniature mountain houseleek, is a low-growing, compact succulent with rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves.

It is native to the alpine regions of Europe and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
