Solanum herbertianum

Solanum herbertianum, commonly known as Herbert's nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries, which are typically red when mature. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
