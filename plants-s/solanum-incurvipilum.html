Solanum incurvipilum

Solanum incurvipilum, commonly known as the narrow-hair nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, yellow flowers and elliptical, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and can thrive in arid conditions.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role.
