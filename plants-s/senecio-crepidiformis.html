Senecio crepidiformis

Senecio crepidiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Senecio crepidiformis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
