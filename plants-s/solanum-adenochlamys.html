Solanum adenochlamys

Solanum adenochlamys, commonly known as the purple nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that turns red when mature.

Solanum adenochlamys is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
