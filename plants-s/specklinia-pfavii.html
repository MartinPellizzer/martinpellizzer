Specklinia pfavii

Specklinia pfavii, commonly known as the Hawaiian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often clustered at the base, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
