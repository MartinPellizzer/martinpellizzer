Salvia clinopodioides

Salvia clinopodioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Salvia clinopodioides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
