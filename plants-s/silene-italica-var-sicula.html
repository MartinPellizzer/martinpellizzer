Silene italica var. sicula

Silene italica var. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are pink to purple, clustered in racemes, and bloom from summer to autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
