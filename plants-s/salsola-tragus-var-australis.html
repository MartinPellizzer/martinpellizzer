Salsola tragus var. australis

Salsola tragus var. australis, commonly known as Australian sandmat, is a low-growing, annual plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of greenish or yellowish flowers, which are typically followed by small, spherical fruits. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low water availability.

This species is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is considered a common weed in disturbed areas.
