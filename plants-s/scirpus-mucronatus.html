Scirpus mucronatus

Scirpus mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late summer. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshes and pond edges.

Scirpus mucronatus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
