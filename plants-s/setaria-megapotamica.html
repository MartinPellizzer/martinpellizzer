Setaria megapotamica

Setaria megapotamica, commonly known as the Panic grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-green color.

Setaria megapotamica is known for its adaptability to moist environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
