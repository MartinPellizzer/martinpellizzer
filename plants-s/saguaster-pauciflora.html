Saguaster pauciflora

Saguaster pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered saguaster, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a low, sprawling shrub with narrow, silvery-gray leaves adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers, which are pale yellow to white, bloom in small clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
