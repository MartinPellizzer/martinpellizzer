Sedum thomsonianum

Sedum thomsonianum, commonly known as the Thompson's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum thomsonianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to propagate through offsets.
