Scutellaria rupestris subsp. geraniana

Scutellaria rupestris subsp.

geraniana, commonly known as the rock skullcup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in sunny, well-drained habitats.

The plant features slender stems that are often prostrate or ascending, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

The species is characterized by its unique morphology and is often found in dry, open environments such as gravelly slopes and limestone outcrops.
