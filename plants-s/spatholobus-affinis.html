Spatholobus affinis

Spatholobus affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wisteria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer.
