Solanum cunninghamii

Solanum cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are berries that change color from green to red as they mature.

Solanum cunninghamii is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland and heathland environments.
