Seriphidium pauciflorum var. maikarum

Seriphidium pauciflorum var. maikarum, commonly known as the Maikarum tansy.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. The plant features a robust root system and dense, hairy foliage adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
