Senecio incisifolia

Senecio incisifolia, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-blue coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Senecio incisifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
