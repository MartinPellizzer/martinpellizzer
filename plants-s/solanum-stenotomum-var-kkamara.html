Solanum stenotomum var. kkamara

Solanum stenotomum var. kkamara, commonly known as the Kkamara nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, and it bears berries that can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
