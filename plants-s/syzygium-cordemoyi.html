Syzygium cordemoyi

Syzygium cordemoyi, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and rich reddish color, though it is also threatened by overharvesting.

Syzygium cordemoyi plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species.
