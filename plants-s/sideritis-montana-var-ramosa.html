Sideritis montana var. ramosa

Sideritis montana var. ramosa, commonly known as mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Sideritis montana var. ramosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
