Scilla monophyllos f. maculata

Scilla monophyllos f. maculata, commonly known as the spotted scilla, is a striking bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue-violet flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and delicate appearance.
