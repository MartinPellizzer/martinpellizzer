Schoberia maritima

Schoberia maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by white, spiny bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-gray, adapted to reduce water loss in saline conditions.

Schoberia maritima is an important plant for coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
