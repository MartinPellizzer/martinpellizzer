Senecio scapiger

Senecio scapiger, commonly known as the naked stem senecio, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, leafless stems that rise directly from the base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
