Steffensia scabra

Steffensia scabra, commonly known as the rough stemmed steffensia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a hairy, branching stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Steffensia scabra is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
