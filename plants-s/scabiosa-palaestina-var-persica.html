Scabiosa palaestina var. persica

Scabiosa palaestina var. persica, commonly known as Persian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are hairy to the touch, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
