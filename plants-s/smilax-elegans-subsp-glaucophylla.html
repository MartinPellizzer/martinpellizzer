Smilax elegans subsp. glaucophylla

Smilax elegans subsp.

glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla smilax, is a shrub-like plant native to the southeastern United States. It features compound leaves with spiny tips and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red berries.

The plant is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its foliage has a distinctive bluish-green hue, giving it its subspecies name.

It is a member of the Smilacaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role.
