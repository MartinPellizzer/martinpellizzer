Stachys neurocalycina

Stachys neurocalycina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved betony, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces spikes of violet or blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Stachys neurocalycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
