Sasa mikawana

Sasa mikawana, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or Mikawa bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa mikawana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese gardens.
