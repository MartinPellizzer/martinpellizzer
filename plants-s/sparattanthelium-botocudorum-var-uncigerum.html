Sparattanthelium botocudorum var. uncigerum

Sparattanthelium botocudorum var. uncigerum, commonly known as the Botocudo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often hairy.

This variety is notable for its ecological significance in supporting local biodiversity and soil stabilization.
