Senna villosa

Senna villosa, commonly known as silky senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves with soft, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Senna villosa is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It also plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
