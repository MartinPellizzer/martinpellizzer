Scutellaria fulgens

Scutellaria fulgens, commonly known as the shining skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria fulgens typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
