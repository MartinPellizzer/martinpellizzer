Spergula platensis var. balansae

Spergula platensis var. balansae, commonly known as the Balansae sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces small, three-lobed flowers in spikelets.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
