Stemona hirtella

Stemona hirtella, commonly known as the hairy stemona, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is of interest in traditional herbal medicine.
