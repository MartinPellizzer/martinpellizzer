Stramonium tatula

Stramonium tatula, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a toxic plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple berries and bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains tropane alkaloids, such as atropine and scopolamine, which are highly poisonous to humans and animals. It thrives in woodland areas and is native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
