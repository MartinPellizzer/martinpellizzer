Stipa spartea var. tweedyi

Stipa spartea var. tweedyi, commonly known as Tweedy's feathergrass, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry foliage and tufted inflorescences that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is well adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in poor soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
