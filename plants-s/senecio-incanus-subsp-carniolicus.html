Senecio incanus subsp. carniolicus

Senecio incanus subsp.

carniolicus, commonly known as the Alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native range.
