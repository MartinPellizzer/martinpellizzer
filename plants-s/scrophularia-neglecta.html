Scrophularia neglecta

Scrophularia neglecta, commonly known as common figwort, is a flowering plant in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Scrophularia neglecta is often found growing along roadsides, in meadows, and in woodland edges.
