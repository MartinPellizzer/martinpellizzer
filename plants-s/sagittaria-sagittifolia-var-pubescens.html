Sagittaria sagittifolia var. pubescens

Sagittaria sagittifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes and pond edges. Its flowering stems produce small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The hairy arrowhead is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
