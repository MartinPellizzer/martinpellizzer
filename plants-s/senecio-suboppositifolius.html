Senecio suboppositifolius

Senecio suboppositifolius, commonly known as the opposite-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are opposite in arrangement, giving it its scientific name, and it produces small yellow flowers.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
