Sasaella kogasensis var. gracillima

Sasaella kogasensis var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a shrub-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
