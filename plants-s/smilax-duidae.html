Smilax duidae

Smilax duidae, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or arrowhead vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Smilax duidae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments make it a common ornamental and invasive species in some regions.
