Skirrhophorus eriocephalus

Skirrhophorus eriocephalus, commonly known as the woolly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
