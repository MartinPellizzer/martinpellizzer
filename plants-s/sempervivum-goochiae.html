Sempervivum goochiae

Sempervivum goochiae, commonly known as the Gooch's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues along the edges. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Sempervivum goochiae is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to alpine and rocky environments.
