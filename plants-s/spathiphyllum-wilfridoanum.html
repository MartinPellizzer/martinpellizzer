Spathiphyllum wilfridoanum

Spathiphyllum wilfridoanum, commonly known as the Wilfrido's peace lily, is a tropical plant native to the understory of humid forests in Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are typically broad and lance-shaped. The plant produces striking white spathe flowers that resemble a modified leaf, with a central spadix that bears small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in low-light conditions and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular houseplant.
