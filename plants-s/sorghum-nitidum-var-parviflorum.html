Sorghum nitidum var. parviflorum

Sorghum nitidum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flower sorghum, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with narrow, pale green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
