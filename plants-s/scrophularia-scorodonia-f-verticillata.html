Scrophularia scorodonia f. verticillata

Scrophularia scorodonia f. verticillata, commonly known as the clustered figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are tubular, clustered in whorls, and usually range in color from pale pink to violet.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
