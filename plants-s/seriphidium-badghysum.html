Seriphidium badghysum

Seriphidium badghysum, commonly known as the Afghan tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as small, hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its yellowish-green flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
