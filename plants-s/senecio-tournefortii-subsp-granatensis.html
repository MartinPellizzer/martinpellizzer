Senecio tournefortii subsp. granatensis

Senecio tournefortii subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and southern France. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
