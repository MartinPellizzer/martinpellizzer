Stelis spathilabia

Stelis spathilabia, commonly known as the Orchid with a Spathe-like Labellum, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a spathelike labellum that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Stelis spathilabia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
