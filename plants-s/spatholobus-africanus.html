Spatholobus africanus

Spatholobus africanus, commonly known as the African wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in spikes. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
