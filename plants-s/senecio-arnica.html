Senecio arnica

Senecio arnica, commonly known as the leopard's bane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in spring and are followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Senecio arnica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
