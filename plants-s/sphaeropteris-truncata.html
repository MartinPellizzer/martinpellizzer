Sphaeropteris truncata

Sphaeropteris truncata, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the cycad family, characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived species. Its leaves are typically short and broad, with a distinctive, rounded shape.

Sphaeropteris truncata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
