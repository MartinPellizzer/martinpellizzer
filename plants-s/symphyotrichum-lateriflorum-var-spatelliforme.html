Symphyotrichum lateriflorum var. spatelliforme

Symphyotrichum lateriflorum var. spatelliforme, commonly known as the Spatelliform Heath aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered purple to pink flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and floral arrangement, making it a notable species in its native range.
