Sedum telephium var. cebennense

Sedum telephium var. cebennense, commonly known as the red stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and are arranged in a rosette pattern. This variety is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
