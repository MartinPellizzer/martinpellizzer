Sarcostemma solanoides

Sarcostemma solanoides, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, hairy stems, and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly pubescent surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, white seeds.
