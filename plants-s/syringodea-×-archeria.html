Syringodea × archeria

Syringodea × archeria, commonly known as the hybrid syringodea, is a rare and striking plant that combines features of its parent species, Syringodea and Archelia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a distinctive, showy inflorescence with fused petals and stamens. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a deep green color.

Syringodea × archeria is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
