Spartium valentinum

Spartium valentinum, commonly known as Valentine's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers, which bloom in the spring, are yellow and surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves.

Spartium valentinum is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
