Sorbus graeca var. danubialis

Sorbus graeca var. danubialis, commonly known as the Danube rowan, is a variant of the European rowan tree.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and Eastern Europe. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and in woodland areas.
