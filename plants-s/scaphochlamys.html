Scaphochlamys

Scaphochlamys, commonly known as the winged bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits that are elongated and have two prominent wing-like structures. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Scaphochlamys is valued for its edible seeds and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
