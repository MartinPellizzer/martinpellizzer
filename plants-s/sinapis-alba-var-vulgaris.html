Sinapis alba var. vulgaris

Sinapis alba var. vulgaris, commonly known as white mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a taproot system and erect, branched stems that are typically hairless. The leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and have a distinctive mustard-like odor when crushed. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, and it produces long, slender pods containing several seeds.

This plant is often grown as a cover crop or for its seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
