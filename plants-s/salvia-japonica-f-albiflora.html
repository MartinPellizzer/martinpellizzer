Salvia japonica f. albiflora

Salvia japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese sage, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features upright stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that are predominantly white with a pale blue or lavender hue. It blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
