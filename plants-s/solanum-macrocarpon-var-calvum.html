Solanum macrocarpon var. calvum

Solanum macrocarpon var. calvum, commonly known as the Jamaican cherry or wild tomato, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple calyx.

The fruit is edible when ripe, though it is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
