Syngonanthus ulei var. goyazensis

Syngonanthus ulei var. goyazensis, commonly known as the Goyazensis greenbriar, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
