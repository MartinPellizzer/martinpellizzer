Sterculia cymbiformis

Sterculia cymbiformis, commonly known as the African bell tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a sticky, fleshy coating.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
