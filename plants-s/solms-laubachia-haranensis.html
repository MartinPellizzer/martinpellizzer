Solms-laubachia haranensis

Solms-laubachia haranensis, commonly known as the Haran Valley grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Solms-laubachia haranensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in regions with limited rainfall.
