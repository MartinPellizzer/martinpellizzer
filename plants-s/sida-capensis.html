Sida capensis

Sida capensis, commonly known as the coastal sida, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue or purple hue. Its small, pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, and it produces small, round seeds.

Sida capensis is adapted to salt spray and is often found in dune environments along the coastline.
