Sundamomum flavoalbum

Sundamomum flavoalbum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered milkweed, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. Its milky sap, which is latex-like, contains toxic compounds and is used in traditional medicine.

Sundamomum flavoalbum is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
