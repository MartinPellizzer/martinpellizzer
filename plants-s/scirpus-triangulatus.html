Scirpus triangulatus

Scirpus triangulatus, commonly known as the triangular bulrush, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its triangular, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers that appear in late summer.

Scirpus triangulatus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
