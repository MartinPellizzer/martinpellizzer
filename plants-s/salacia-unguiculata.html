Salacia unguiculata

Salacia unguiculata, commonly known as the horned salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its unique, horn-shaped buds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its leaves are simple and alternate.

Salacia unguiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive appearance.
