Sticherus flabellatus var. flabellatus

Sticherus flabellatus var. flabellatus, commonly known as the flabellate sticherus, is a small, delicate moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sporophytes, are short and club-shaped, emerging from the leafy shoots.

This variety is characterized by its fine, intricate structure and is often found in temperate regions with high humidity.
