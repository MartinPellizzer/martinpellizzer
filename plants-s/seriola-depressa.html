Seriola depressa

Seriola depressa, commonly known as the dwarf seriola, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Seriola depressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and unique appearance.
