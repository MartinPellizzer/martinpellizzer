Scrophularia leporella

Scrophularia leporella, commonly known as the Italian figwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in woodlands and meadows. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Scrophularia leporella is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
