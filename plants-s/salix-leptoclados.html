Salix leptoclados

Salix leptoclados, commonly known as the slender willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
