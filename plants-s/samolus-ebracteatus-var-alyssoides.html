Samolus ebracteatus var. alyssoides

Samolus ebracteatus var. alyssoides, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters known as cymes. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of alpine flora.
