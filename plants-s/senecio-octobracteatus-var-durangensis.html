Senecio octobracteatus var. durangensis

Senecio octobracteatus var. durangensis, commonly known as the Durango Senecio, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, daisy-like blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
