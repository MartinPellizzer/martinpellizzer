Scirpus polyopsis

Scirpus polyopsis, commonly known as the narrow leaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. Scirpus polyopsis thrives in shallow water or moist soil, playing an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to habitat structure for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
