Sisymbrium tomentosum

Sisymbrium tomentosum, commonly known as woolly mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow, cruciform flowers and hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Sisymbrium tomentosum is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants.
