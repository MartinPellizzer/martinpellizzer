Syngonanthus heteropeploides

Syngonanthus heteropeploides, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, adding vibrant color to wetlands and prairies.

Syngonanthus heteropeploides is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
