Seriphidium terrae-albae var. massagetovii

Seriphidium terrae-albae var. massagetovii, commonly known as the Massagetovii wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing a compact growth habit. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
