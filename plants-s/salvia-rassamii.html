Salvia rassamii

Salvia rassamii, commonly known as the Persian sage, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty scent, contributing to its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.

Salvia rassamii is adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
