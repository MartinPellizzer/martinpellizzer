Stroganowia desertorum

Stroganowia desertorum, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Stroganowia desertorum plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and providing nectar for pollinators.
