Salacca secunda

Salacca secunda, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into edible, sweet fruits. Its leaves are often used for thatching and weaving, while the sap can be tapped for a sweet, syrup-like liquid.

Salacca secunda is valued both for its economic and ecological contributions in its native habitats.
