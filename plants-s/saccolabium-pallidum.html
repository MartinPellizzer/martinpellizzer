Saccolabium pallidum

Saccolabium pallidum, commonly known as the pale saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, pale yellow or greenish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its pods are typically slender and hairy.

It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems.
