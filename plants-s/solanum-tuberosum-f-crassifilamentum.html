Solanum tuberosum f. crassifilamentum

Solanum tuberosum f. crassifilamentum, commonly known as the thick-stemmed potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its thicker, more robust stems compared to the standard variety. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. It produces tubers that are typically smaller but have a denser structure.

This form is often cultivated for its unique morphology and potential culinary uses.
