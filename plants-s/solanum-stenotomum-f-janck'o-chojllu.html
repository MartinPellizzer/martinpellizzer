Solanum stenotomum f. janck'o-chojllu

Solanum stenotomum f. janck'o-chojllu, commonly known as the Janck'o-Chojllu tomato, is a botanical variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its small, cherry-like fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. Its foliage consists of simple, ovate leaves with a hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
