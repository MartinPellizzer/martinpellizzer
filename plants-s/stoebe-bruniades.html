Stoebe bruniades

Stoebe bruniades, commonly known as lesser cow-wheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a fibrous root system and produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Stoebe bruniades is often found in association with other woodland flora and plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
