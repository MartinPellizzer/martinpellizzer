Syngonanthus mucugensis subsp. riocontensis

Syngonanthus mucugensis subsp.

riocontensis, commonly known as the Rio Conta sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family. It is native to the highland regions of Colombia, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous growth habit and a preference for well-drained soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
