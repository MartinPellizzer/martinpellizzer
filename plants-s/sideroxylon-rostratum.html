Sideroxylon rostratum

Sideroxylon rostratum, commonly known as the horned ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and distinctive horn-like protuberances on its trunk and branches. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. It produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Sideroxylon rostratum is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and dry forest regions.
