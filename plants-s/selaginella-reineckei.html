Selaginella reineckei

Selaginella reineckei, commonly known as the spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, needle-like leaves and a branching, moss-like structure. Selaginella reineckei is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and for its unique reproductive structures called strobila.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
