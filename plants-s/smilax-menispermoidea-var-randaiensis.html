Smilax menispermoidea var. randaiensis

Smilax menispermoidea var. randaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese arrowroot or Japanese smilax, is a climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It features a robust root system with tubers that are often harvested for their starchy, edible qualities. The plant has compound leaves with spiny margins and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and of interest for horticultural use.
