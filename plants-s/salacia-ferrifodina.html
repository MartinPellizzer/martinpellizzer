Salacia ferrifodina

Salacia ferrifodina, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish wood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, while its flowers are small and clustered in catkins.

The tree can reach heights of up to 20 meters and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
