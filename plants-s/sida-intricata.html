Sida intricata

Sida intricata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

Sida intricata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
