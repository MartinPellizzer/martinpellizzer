Swartzia flaemingii

Swartzia flaemingii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Swartzia flaemingii is often found in moist lowland forests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
