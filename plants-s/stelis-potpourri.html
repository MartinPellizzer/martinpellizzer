Stelis potpourri

Stelis potpourri, commonly known as the "butterfly orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle, and relies on aerial roots for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are known to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Stelis potpourri is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under suitable conditions.
