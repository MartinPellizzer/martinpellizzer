Senecio southlandicus

Senecio southlandicus, commonly known as the Southland groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its low-growing habit.
