Stylidium cornuatum

Stylidium cornuatum, commonly known as the horned sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, spurred petals that resemble a horn, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Stylidium cornuatum is often found in coastal dunes and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
