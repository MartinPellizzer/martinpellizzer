Salacia pyriformis var. obtusa

Salacia pyriformis var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed salacia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but have a tart, sour flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
