Symplocos ledermannii

Symplocos ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that remains green throughout the year. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue, egg-shaped berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Symplocos ledermannii is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense thickets.
