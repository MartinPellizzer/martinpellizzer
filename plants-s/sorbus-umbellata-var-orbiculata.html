Sorbus umbellata var. orbiculata

Sorbus umbellata var. orbiculata, commonly known as the round-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its orbicular (rounded) leaves, which are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white, umbellate flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
