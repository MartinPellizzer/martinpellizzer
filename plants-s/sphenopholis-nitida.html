Sphenopholis nitida

Sphenopholis nitida, commonly known as the slender sphenophyll, is a member of the family Sphenophyllaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually clustered in dense inflorescences.

Sphenopholis nitida is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
