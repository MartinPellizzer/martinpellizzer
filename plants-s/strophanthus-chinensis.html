Strophanthus chinensis

Strophanthus chinensis, commonly known as Chinese strophanthus, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers and milky sap. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent spur at the base.

The plant contains cardiac glycosides, which have been historically used in traditional medicine.
