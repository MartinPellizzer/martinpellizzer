Sinobambusa farinosa

Sinobambusa farinosa, commonly known as the farinose bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a deciduous bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded environments in mountainous regions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Sinobambusa farinosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
