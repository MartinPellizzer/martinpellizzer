Solanum macrocarpon var. primovestitum

Solanum macrocarpon var. primovestitum, commonly known as the "yellow bell pepper," is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped fruits that mature to a bright orange-red color. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are followed by the characteristic fruit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in certain cuisines.
