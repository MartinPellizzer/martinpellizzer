Sempervivum × oligotrichium

Sempervivum × oligotrichium, commonly known as the hybrid houseleek, is a succulent plant that belongs to the Crassulaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Sempervivum tectorum and Sempervivum × humile, resulting in a plant with unique foliage characteristics. The leaves are typically fleshy, rosette-shaped, and often display a range of colors from green to blue-gray, depending on light exposure. It is adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This plant is also known for its ability to thrive in well-drained conditions and its resilience to drought.
