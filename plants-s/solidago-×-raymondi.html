Solidago × raymondi

Solidago × raymondi, commonly known as the Raymondi goldenrod, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and pond edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Solidago × raymondi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
