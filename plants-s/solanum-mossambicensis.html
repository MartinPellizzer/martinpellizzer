Solanum mossambicensis

Solanum mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, and it produces small, toxic berries that are yellow when mature.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by livestock and humans.
