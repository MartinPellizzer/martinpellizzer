Senecio sanguisorbae

Senecio sanguisorbae, commonly known as the bloodroot senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, reddish-brown stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.
