Syzygium laosense var. quocense

Syzygium laosense var. quocense, commonly known as the Quocense tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically yellow when ripe, and contains a single seed surrounded by a sweet, acidic pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
