Scutellaria altissima subsp. commutata

Scutellaria altissima subsp.

commutata, commonly known as the tall blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria altissima subsp.

commutata is often noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
