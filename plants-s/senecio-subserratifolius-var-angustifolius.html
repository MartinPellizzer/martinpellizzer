Senecio subserratifolius var. angustifolius

Senecio subserratifolius var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often hairy and have a narrow, linear shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
