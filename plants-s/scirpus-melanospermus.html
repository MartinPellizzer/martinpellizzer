Scirpus melanospermus

Scirpus melanospermus, commonly known as blackseed bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant produces dark, hard seeds that are adapted for dispersal by water. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Scirpus melanospermus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
