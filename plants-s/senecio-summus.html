Senecio summus

Senecio summus, commonly known as the African snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The plant is also noted for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
