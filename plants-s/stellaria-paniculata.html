Stellaria paniculata

Stellaria paniculata, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers with five petals that resemble stars, giving it its species name "paniculata.". The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, oval fruits called capsules. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in gardens.

Stellaria paniculata is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.
