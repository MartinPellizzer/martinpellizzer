Salix alnoides

Salix alnoides, commonly known as the Himalayan weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in mountainous regions.

Salix alnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
