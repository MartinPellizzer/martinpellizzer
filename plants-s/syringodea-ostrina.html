Syringodea ostrina

Syringodea ostrina, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and flat, often arranged in a rosette pattern to maximize light absorption.

Syringodea ostrina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
