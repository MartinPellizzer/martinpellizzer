Solidago maxima

Solidago maxima, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a tall perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Solidago maxima is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
