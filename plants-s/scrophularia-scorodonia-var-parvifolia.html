Scrophularia scorodonia var. parvifolia

Scrophularia scorodonia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length, giving it its varietal name "parvifolia.". Its flowers are tubular and usually pale purple or yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is known for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
