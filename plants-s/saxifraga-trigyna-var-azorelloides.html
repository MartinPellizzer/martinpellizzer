Saxifraga trigyna var. azorelloides

Saxifraga trigyna var. azorelloides, commonly known as the Azorean saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Azores.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
