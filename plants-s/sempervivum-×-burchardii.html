Sempervivum × burchardii

Sempervivum × burchardii, commonly known as the Burchard's houseleek, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern with fleshy, thick leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it popular among rock garden enthusiasts.

Sempervivum × burchardii is also known for its ability to survive in harsh climates, showcasing its resilience and adaptability.
