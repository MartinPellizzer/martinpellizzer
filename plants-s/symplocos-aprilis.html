Symplocos aprilis

Symplocos aprilis, commonly known as the April beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Symplocos aprilis typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and early blooming habit.
