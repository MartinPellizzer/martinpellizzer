Salix brachycarpa var. sansonii

Salix brachycarpa var. sansonii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and catkin flowers, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas. It is a deciduous species, shedding its leaves in the autumn.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
