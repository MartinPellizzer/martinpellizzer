Sedum chrysastrum

Sedum chrysastrum, commonly known as the golden sedum, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a succulent species that thrives in rocky or sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
