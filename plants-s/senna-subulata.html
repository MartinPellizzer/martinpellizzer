Senna subulata

Senna subulata, commonly known as the needle senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senna subulata is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
