Salix humilis opaca

Salix humilis opaca, commonly known as the opaque willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and grayish-brown bark that remains smooth throughout its life. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a pale green upper surface and a whitish underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
