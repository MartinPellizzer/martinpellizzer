Senecio brevidentatus

Senecio brevidentatus, commonly known as the narrow-toothed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow with short, sharp teeth, giving it its specific epithet.

It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
