Shorea palembanica

Shorea palembanica, commonly known as the Palembang ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
