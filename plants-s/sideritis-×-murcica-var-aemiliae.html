Sideritis × murcica var. aemiliae

Sideritis × murcica var. aemiliae, commonly known as the Aemilia's sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open grasslands.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its drought tolerance.
