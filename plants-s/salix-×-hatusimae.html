Salix × hatusimae

Salix × hatusimae, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and along waterways.
