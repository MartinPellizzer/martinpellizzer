Sasaella kogasensis var. yoshinoi

Sasaella kogasensis var. yoshinoi, commonly known as Yoshino's sasa, is a variant of the sasa genus native to Japan.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, bamboo-like growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions of Japan.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
