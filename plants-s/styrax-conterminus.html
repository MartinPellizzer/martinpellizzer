Styrax conterminus

Styrax conterminus, commonly known as the Florida styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by dark red, waxy berries.

Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
