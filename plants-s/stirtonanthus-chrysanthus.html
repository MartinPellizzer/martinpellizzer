Stirtonanthus chrysanthus

Stirtonanthus chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the stem. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
