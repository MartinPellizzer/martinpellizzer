Saussurea peguensis

Saussurea peguensis, commonly known as the Burmese yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
