Salacia bequaertii

Salacia bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's salacia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supporting structures. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a glossy surface.

Salacia bequaertii produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, which bloom in clusters along the stems.
