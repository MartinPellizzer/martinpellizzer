Sisymbrium andinum f. dolichocarpum

Sisymbrium andinum f. dolichocarpum, commonly known as the long-seeded field vetch, is a perennial herb native to the Andes region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has hairy stems and lanceolate leaves. Its distinctive feature is the elongated, slender pods that contain several small, rounded seeds.

This form is notable for its ecological adaptation to high-altitude environments.
