Spiraeanthemum ellipticum

Spiraeanthemum ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic spirea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface and serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
