Salvia chinensis f. pinnata

Salvia chinensis f. pinnata, commonly known as the Chinese sage with pinnate leaves, is a variant of the Salvia chinensis species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features pinnately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and adaptability to various garden conditions.
