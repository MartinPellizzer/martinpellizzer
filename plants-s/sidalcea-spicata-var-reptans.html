Sidalcea spicata var. reptans

Sidalcea spicata var. reptans, commonly known as the trailing soapwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, trailing stems that often grow along the ground, producing small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically borne in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
