Saccolabium fissum

Saccolabium fissum, commonly known as the fissured saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fissured leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
