Siphocampylus nemoralis

Siphocampylus nemoralis, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a robust, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Siphocampylus nemoralis is characterized by its spiny leaves and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
