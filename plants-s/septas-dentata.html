Septas dentata

Septas dentata, commonly known as the toothed septa, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive leaf structure.

Its leaves are serrated, with finely toothed margins that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
