Seddera capensis var. glabrescens

Seddera capensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Cape seddera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which become less prominent as the plant matures.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
