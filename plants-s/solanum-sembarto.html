Solanum sembarto

Solanum sembarto, commonly known as the Sembarto nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Solanum sembarto belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers.

It is often found in shaded, humid environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
