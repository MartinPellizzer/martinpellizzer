Senecillis fargesii

Senecillis fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
