Sideritis scordioides subsp. hirtula

Sideritis scordioides subsp.

hirtula, commonly known as the hairy sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, pink or violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a strong, peppery scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
