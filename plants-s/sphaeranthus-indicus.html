Sphaeranthus indicus

Sphaeranthus indicus, commonly known as Indian globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that resemble small globes. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are yellow or orange in color.

Sphaeranthus indicus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
