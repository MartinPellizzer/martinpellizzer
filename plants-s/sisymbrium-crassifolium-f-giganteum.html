Sisymbrium crassifolium f. giganteum

Sisymbrium crassifolium f. giganteum, commonly known as the giant bladderwort, is a variant of the bladderwort genus characterized by its enlarged, fleshy leaves and specialized structures for trapping prey.

It is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe and is adapted to wet, nutrient-poor soils. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with bladder-like structures that aid in nutrient acquisition through carnivory. Its leaves are broad and thick, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This form is considered a botanical curiosity due to its morphological variation and ecological adaptations.
