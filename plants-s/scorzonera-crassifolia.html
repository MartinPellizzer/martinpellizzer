Scorzonera crassifolia

Scorzonera crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Scorzonera crassifolia produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
