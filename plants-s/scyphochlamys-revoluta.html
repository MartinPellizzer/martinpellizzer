Scyphochlamys revoluta

Scyphochlamys revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Scyphochlamys revoluta is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
