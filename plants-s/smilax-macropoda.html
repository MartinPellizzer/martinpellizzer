Smilax macropoda

Smilax macropoda, commonly known as the giant arrowhead vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and the presence of spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat.

Smilax macropoda is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
