Stevia liebmannii var. chiapensis

Stevia liebmannii var. chiapensis, commonly known as Chiapan stevia, is a perennial shrub native to the humid forests of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing high levels of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used as a sugar substitute. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in montane cloud forests.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it is of interest for both culinary and medicinal applications.
