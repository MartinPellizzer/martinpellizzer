Sorbus acutiloba

Sorbus acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with sharply toothed margins. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by orange to red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
