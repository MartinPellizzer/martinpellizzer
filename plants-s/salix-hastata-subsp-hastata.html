Salix hastata subsp. hastata

Salix hastata subsp.

hastata, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a deciduous shrub native to North America. It typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks, wet meadows, and floodplains.

The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length. Its catkins are yellow and appear in early spring, followed by small, greenish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
