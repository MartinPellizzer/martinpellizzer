Scrophularia nabataeorum

Scrophularia nabataeorum, commonly known as the Nabatean figwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Scrophularia nabataeorum is often found in rocky habitats and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
