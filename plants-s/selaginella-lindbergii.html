Selaginella lindbergii

Selaginella lindbergii, commonly known as the Lindberg's spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage.

Selaginella lindbergii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
