Selaginella curtisii

Selaginella curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's spikemoss, is a vascular fern-like plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Selaginella curtisii is well adapted to drought conditions, with a unique ability to survive extreme environmental stress.
