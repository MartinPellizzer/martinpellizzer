Silene oliveriana

Silene oliveriana, commonly known as Oliver's campion, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Silene oliveriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
