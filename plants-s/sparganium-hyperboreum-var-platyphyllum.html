Sparganium hyperboreum var. platyphyllum

Sparganium hyperboreum var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf burr rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and distinctive, spiky seed heads that form dense clusters. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a unique, spiky appearance that aids in wind pollination.

This species plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
