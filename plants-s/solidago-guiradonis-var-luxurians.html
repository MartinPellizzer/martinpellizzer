Solidago guiradonis var. luxurians

Solidago guiradonis var. luxurians, commonly known as the luxuriant goldenrod, is a variant of the goldenrod genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
