Stachys rosmarinifolia var. burkei

Stachys rosmarinifolia var. burkei, commonly known as the Burke's wood betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable species in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
