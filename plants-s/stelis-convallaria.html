Stelis convallaria

Stelis convallaria, commonly known as the orchid bell, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs and linear leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in a raceme, and are characterized by their bell-shaped structure.

Stelis convallaria is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
