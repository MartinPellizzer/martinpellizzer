Sempervivum brachiatum

Sempervivum brachiatum, commonly known as the football plant or hen and chicks, is a succulent species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces upright flowering stems topped with clusters of star-shaped flowers, often in shades of pink or red. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

Sempervivum brachiatum is a low-maintenance plant that is popular in rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its hardy nature.
