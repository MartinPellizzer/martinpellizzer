Sorbus gongshanensis

Sorbus gongshanensis, commonly known as the Gongshan whitebeam, is a species of rowan tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.
