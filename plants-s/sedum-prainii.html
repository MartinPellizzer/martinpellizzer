Sedum prainii

Sedum prainii, commonly known as the Prain's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically green and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Sedum prainii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in gardens.
