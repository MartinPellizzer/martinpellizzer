Saxifraga carinata

Saxifraga carinata, commonly known as the mountain saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with dark green, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and range in color from white to pink, depending on the species.

Saxifraga carinata is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
