Senecio olympicus

Senecio olympicus, commonly known as Olympic groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
