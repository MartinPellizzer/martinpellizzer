Solanum tuberosum f. wila-pala

Solanum tuberosum f. wila-pala, commonly known as the Wila-Pala potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, featuring a distinctive coloration and texture. This cultivar is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, where it has adapted to specific environmental conditions. The plant exhibits typical solanaceous features, including solanum-like leaves and flowers.

Its genetic diversity makes it valuable for agricultural research and breeding programs.
