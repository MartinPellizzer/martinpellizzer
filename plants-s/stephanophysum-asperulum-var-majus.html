Stephanophysum asperulum var. majus

Stephanophysum asperulum var. majus, commonly known as the roughleaf stephanophysum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving the plant its "asperulum" designation. The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have a pale yellow to greenish hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded woodland environments.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
