Scirpus cyperoides

Scirpus cyperoides, commonly known as narrowleaf bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous wetland plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in marshy areas, pond edges, and along streams, thriving in saturated soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often covered with dense, cylindrical clusters of small, brownish flowers.

Scirpus cyperoides plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
