Salvia lucida

Salvia lucida, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a mist-like appearance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Salvia lucida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
