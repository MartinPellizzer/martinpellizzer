Swertia moetis

Swertia moetis, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and typically grows at high altitudes where temperatures are cool and sunlight is abundant. Swertia moetis is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh mountainous environments.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Tibetan medicine.
