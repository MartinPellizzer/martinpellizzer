Senecio lamarum

Senecio lamarum, commonly known as the Lamarum groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
