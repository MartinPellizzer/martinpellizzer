Sida purpurascens

Sida purpurascens, commonly known as purpleleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or subshrub that typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in the summer months.

Sida purpurascens is known for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
