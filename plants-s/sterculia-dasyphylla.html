Sterculia dasyphylla

Sterculia dasyphylla, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree bears woody, spiny capsules that contain edible, starchy seeds used as a food source. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in savanna woodlands.

The plant has cultural and nutritional significance in many African communities.
