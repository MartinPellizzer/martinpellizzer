Stizolobium championii

Stizolobium championii, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is notable for its fruiting pods, which are covered in hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. Stizolobium championii thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

It is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant in certain regions.
