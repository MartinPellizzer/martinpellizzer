Senecio pseudoarnica f. rollandii

Senecio pseudoarnica f. rollandii, commonly known as the Rolland's Senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It produces small yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
