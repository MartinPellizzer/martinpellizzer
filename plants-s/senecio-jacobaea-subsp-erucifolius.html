Senecio jacobaea subsp. erucifolius

Senecio jacobaea subsp.

erucifolius, commonly known as the tansy ragwort, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellowish to reddish composite flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
