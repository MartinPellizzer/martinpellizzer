Sesamoides purpurascens subsp. pinetorum

Sesamoides purpurascens subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the red-flowered pinegrass, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its striking violet to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
