Sarcopodium cheiri

Sarcopodium cheiri, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its succulent, segmented stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in winter. The plant belongs to the family Cactaceae and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers typically come in shades of red, pink, and white, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with moderate watering.

Sarcopodium cheiri is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
