Shorea laurifolia

Shorea laurifolia, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.

The plant is also known for its fragrant flowers and is often found in mixed dipterocarp forests.
