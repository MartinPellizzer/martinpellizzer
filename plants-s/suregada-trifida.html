Suregada trifida

Suregada trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifid (three-lobed) leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been traditionally used for their durable fibers.

Suregada trifida is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
