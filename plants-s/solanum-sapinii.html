Solanum sapinii

Solanum sapinii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forested areas, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, violet flowers that bloom in the evening.

The fruit is a globose berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
