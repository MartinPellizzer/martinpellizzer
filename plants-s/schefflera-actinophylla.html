Schefflera actinophylla

Schefflera actinophylla, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like arrangement of large, leathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by orange-yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Schefflera actinophylla is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
