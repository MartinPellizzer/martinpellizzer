Scutellaria ovata var. pseudoarguta

Scutellaria ovata var. pseudoarguta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky habitats.
