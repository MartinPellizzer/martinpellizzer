Saxifraga heteroclada var. aurantia

Saxifraga heteroclada var. aurantia, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are usually basal, with a distinctive lobed or toothed appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
