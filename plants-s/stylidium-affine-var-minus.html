Stylidium affine var. minus

Stylidium affine var. minus, commonly known as the lesser spurred sun-orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a short, erect stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
