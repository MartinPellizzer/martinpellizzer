Sorghum × niloticum var. kavirondense

Sorghum × niloticum var. kavirondense, commonly known as the Kavirondo sorghum, is a hybrid plant species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and large, pointed leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It is well-suited to arid and semi-arid climates, making it an important crop in regions with limited rainfall.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both forage and grain production in tropical and subtropical regions.
