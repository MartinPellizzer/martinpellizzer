Stevia reclinata

Stevia reclinata, commonly known as the "hanging stevia," is a perennial shrub native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is valued for its leaves, which contain high concentrations of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used in food products. Stevia reclinata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its adaptability and low maintenance make it a popular choice for both ornamental and commercial cultivation.
