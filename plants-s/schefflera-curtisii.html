Schefflera curtisii

Schefflera curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that radiate from a single point, giving it a distinctive umbrella-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a smooth texture.

Schefflera curtisii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor conditions.
