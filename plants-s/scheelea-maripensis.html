Scheelea maripensis

Scheelea maripensis, commonly known as the Mexican tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant can grow up to 10 meters in height and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports its growth and helps it thrive in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fronds are used in traditional crafts and have cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
