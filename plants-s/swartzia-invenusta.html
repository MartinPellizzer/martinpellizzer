Swartzia invenusta

Swartzia invenusta, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a straight, cylindrical trunk with smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are odd-pinnate, consisting of several pairs of elliptical leaflets.

Swartzia invenusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
