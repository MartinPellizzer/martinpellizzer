Salvia rugosa

Salvia rugosa, commonly known as Chinese sage, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice among gardeners.
