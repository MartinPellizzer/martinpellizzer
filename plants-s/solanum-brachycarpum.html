Solanum brachycarpum

Solanum brachycarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing toxic alkaloids. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
