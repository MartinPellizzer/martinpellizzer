Symplocos ophirensis subsp. ophirensis

Symplocos ophirensis subsp.

ophirensis, commonly known as the Ophirensis waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color, attracting various bird species.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
