Sebaea barbeyana

Sebaea barbeyana, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of white, woolly flowers that resemble balls, giving it its common name. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.

Sebaea barbeyana is also valued for its ability to thrive in coastal environments with high salt exposure.
