Sprengeria watsoniana

Sprengeria watsoniana, commonly known as the Watson's spider orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange with dark markings. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, and it prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
