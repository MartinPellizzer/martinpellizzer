Sasa sayekiensis

Sasa sayekiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Sayeki bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Sasa sayekiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
