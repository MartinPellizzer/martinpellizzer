Swertia pauciflora

Swertia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Swertia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
