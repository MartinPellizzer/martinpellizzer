Spatalla nivea

Spatalla nivea, commonly known as the white spathiphyllum, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white spathe, which surrounds a cluster of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often reaching several feet in length, adding a dramatic visual impact to its surroundings.

Spatalla nivea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
