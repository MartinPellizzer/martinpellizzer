Sasa kurehaensis

Sasa kurehaensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Kureha bamboo, is a clump-forming grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping.

Sasa kurehaensis is also noted for its ability to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in its natural habitat.
