Sedum purpusii

Sedum purpusii, commonly known as the purple stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant purple flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it ideal for arid or semi-arid climates.
