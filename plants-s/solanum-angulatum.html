Solanum angulatum

Solanum angulatum, commonly known as the angular nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often angular in shape, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers are typically borne in clusters and are followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Solanum angulatum is known for its potential medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in handling.
