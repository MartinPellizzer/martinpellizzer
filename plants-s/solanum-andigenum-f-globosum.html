Solanum andigenum f. globosum

Solanum andigenum f. globosum, commonly known as the Andean globe tomato, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, globe-shaped fruits that are typically red when mature. The plant is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into the edible fruits.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and potential use in both ornamental and culinary contexts.
