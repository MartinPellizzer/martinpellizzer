Silene succulenta var. cryptantha

Silene succulenta var. cryptantha, commonly known as the cryptantha bluemouth, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, a feature common in many succulents.

The species is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
