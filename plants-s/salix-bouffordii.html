Salix bouffordii

Salix bouffordii, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically male, while female flowers are found on separate plants. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks.

Salix bouffordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
