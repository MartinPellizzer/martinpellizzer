Swida austrosinensis

Swida austrosinensis, commonly known as the Chinese swida, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the spring.

Swida austrosinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
