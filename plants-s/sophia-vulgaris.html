Sophia vulgaris

Sophia vulgaris, commonly known as common sophora, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, flat pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Sophia vulgaris make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
