Sauropus reticulatus

Sauropus reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
