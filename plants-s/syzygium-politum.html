Syzygium politum

Syzygium politum, commonly known as the glossy apple or satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Syzygium politum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value and fruit make it a popular species in horticulture and local cuisine.
