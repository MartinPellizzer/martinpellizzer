Scirpus villosus

Scirpus villosus, commonly known as the soft bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems covered in soft, hairy tissue, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes help it spread and form dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus villosus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
