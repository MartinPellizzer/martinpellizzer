Scabiosa lucida subsp. calcicola

Scabiosa lucida subsp.

calcicola, commonly known as the limestone scabious, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with pale blue to pinkish flowers arranged in loose clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
