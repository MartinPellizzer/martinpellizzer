Saccolabium miniatum

Saccolabium miniatum, commonly known as the dwarf silverbush, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its compact form and drought tolerance make it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Saccolabium miniatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
