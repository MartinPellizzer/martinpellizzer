Salix clorophana

Salix clorophana, commonly known as the green willow, is a species of willow native to parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shady environments. The plant features slender, flexible branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green. Its flowers are catkins, which are inconspicuous and wind-pollinated.

Salix clorophana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
