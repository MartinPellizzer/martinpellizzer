Solanum coracinum

Solanum coracinum, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum coracinum is toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
