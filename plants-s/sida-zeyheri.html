Sida zeyheri

Sida zeyheri, commonly known as the common sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It features erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a hairy surface with prominent veins. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming from spring to summer.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
