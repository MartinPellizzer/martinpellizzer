Solanum micranthum

Solanum micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. Its small, white or pale green flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Solanum micranthum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
