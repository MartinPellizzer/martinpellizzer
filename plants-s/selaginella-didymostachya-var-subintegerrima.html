Selaginella didymostachya var. subintegerrima

Selaginella didymostachya var. subintegerrima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that produce clusters of small, scale-like leaves. The plant forms dense mats and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include strobila, which contain spores for propagation.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly rough, almost smooth leaves and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
