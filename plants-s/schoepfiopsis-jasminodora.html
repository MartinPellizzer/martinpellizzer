Schoepfiopsis jasminodora

Schoepfiopsis jasminodora, commonly known as the jasmine-like mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It possesses small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered with a fine pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble those of jasmine, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

This species is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
