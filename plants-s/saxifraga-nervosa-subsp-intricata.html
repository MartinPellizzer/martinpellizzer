Saxifraga nervosa subsp. intricata

Saxifraga nervosa subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the intricate saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically features rosettes of narrow, linear leaves with a waxy texture, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its intricate leaf patterns and delicate floral display make it a notable species in botanical gardens and alpine habitats.
