Salvia verbenaca var. multifida

Salvia verbenaca var. multifida, commonly known as the fringed sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive fringes along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and long-lasting blooms make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
