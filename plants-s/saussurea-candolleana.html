Saussurea candolleana

Saussurea candolleana, commonly known as the Himalayan gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that has been traditionally used in folk medicine. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
