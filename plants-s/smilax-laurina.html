Smilax laurina

Smilax laurina, commonly known as the evergreen smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems with sharp, recurved spines and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient component of its native ecosystem.
