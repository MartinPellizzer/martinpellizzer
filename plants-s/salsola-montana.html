Salsola montana

Salsola montana, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its succulent stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments due to its deep root system and ability to store water.

Salsola montana is often considered a weed but plays a role in stabilizing soil in degraded areas.
