Statice kraussiana

Statice kraussiana, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

Statice kraussiana is also valued for its ability to tolerate salty air and sandy soils, which makes it an excellent plant for maritime landscapes.
