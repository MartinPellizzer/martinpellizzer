Solanum ocoapense

Solanum ocoapense, commonly known as the Ocoapense nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a globose berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
