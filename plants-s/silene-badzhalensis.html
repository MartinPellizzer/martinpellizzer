Silene badzhalensis

Silene badzhalensis, commonly known as the Armenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and adjacent areas of Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and bear the clustered inflorescences.

Silene badzhalensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
