Senecio danielis

Senecio danielis, commonly known as Daniel's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Senecio danielis is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
