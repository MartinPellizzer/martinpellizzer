Saponaria sewerzowii

Saponaria sewerzowii, commonly known as the Siberian soapwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, typical of the Caryophyllaceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Saponaria sewerzowii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its saponin content.
