Silene klokovii

Silene klokovii, commonly known as the Klokov's campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Silene klokovii has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
