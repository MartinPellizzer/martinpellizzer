Sida biflora

Sida biflora, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. Sida biflora is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

It is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
