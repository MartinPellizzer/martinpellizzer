Spergula Fallax

Spergula, scientifically known as Spergula fallax, is a hardy perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pale green to blue-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Often found in moist, calcareous soils, it thrives in shaded or semi-shaded environments. This plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity in its habitat. Its unique characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
