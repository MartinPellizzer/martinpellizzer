Senecio lautus subsp. esperensis

Senecio lautus subsp.

esperensis, commonly known as the Esper's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
