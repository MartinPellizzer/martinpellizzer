Senecio campestris subsp. kirilowii

Senecio campestris subsp.

kirilowii, commonly known as the Kirilow's ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Russian Far East. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in temperate climates with adequate moisture.
