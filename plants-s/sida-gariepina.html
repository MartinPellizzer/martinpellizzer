Sida gariepina

Sida gariepina, commonly known as the Cape leadplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered with fine hairs.

Sida gariepina is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
