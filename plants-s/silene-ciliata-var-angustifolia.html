Silene ciliata var. angustifolia

Silene ciliata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. The plant features slender, ascending stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in moist soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
