Senecio javanicus

Senecio javanicus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with purple centers. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Senecio javanicus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
