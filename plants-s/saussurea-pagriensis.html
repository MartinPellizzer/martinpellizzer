Saussurea pagriensis

Saussurea pagriensis, commonly known as the Pagri saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and clusters of yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, cylindrical flower heads that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a vulnerable species.
