Syzygium euphlebium

Syzygium euphlebium, commonly known as the Java apple or water apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible, translucent, pinkish-red fruits. The fruit has a crisp, juicy texture and a sweet-tart flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
