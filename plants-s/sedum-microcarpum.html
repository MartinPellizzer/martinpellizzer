Sedum microcarpum

Sedum microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden design.
