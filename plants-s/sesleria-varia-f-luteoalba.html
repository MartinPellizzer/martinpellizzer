Sesleria varia f. luteoalba

Sesleria varia f. luteoalba, commonly known as the yellow-flowered wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically pale yellow to white in color, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring bloom.
