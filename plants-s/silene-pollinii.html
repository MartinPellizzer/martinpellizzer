Silene pollinii

Silene pollinii, commonly known as the Pollini's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Silene pollinii is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
