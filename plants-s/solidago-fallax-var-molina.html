Solidago fallax var. molina

Solidago fallax var. molina, commonly known as Molina's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other riparian habitats.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
