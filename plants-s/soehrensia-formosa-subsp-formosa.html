Soehrensia formosa subsp. formosa

Soehrensia formosa subsp.

formosa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rich soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

The plant features showy, yellowish-orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
