Salix × excelsa

Salix × excelsa, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its graceful, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

Salix × excelsa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and along waterways due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to moist soils.
