Stylidium pulchellum

Stylidium pulchellum, commonly known as the pretty sun-orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, brightly colored flowers that resemble miniature orchids. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its unique feature is the presence of a spurred labellum, which is a specialized structure that aids in pollination.

Stylidium pulchellum is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
