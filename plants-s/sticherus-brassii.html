Sticherus brassii

Sticherus brassii, commonly known as the yellow star grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its botanical structure is characterized by a basal rosette of leaves and a short, fibrous root system.

Sticherus brassii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
