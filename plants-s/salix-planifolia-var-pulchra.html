Salix planifolia var. pulchra

Salix planifolia var. pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful willow, is a variant of the narrowleaf willow.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. The plant is characterized by its slender, graceful branches and small, elongated leaves that are typically pale green and have a smooth texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in riparian zones.

The flowers are catkins, which are typically yellow-green and appear in early spring.
