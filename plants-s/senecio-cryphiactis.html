Senecio cryphiactis

Senecio cryphiactis, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in winter, giving it its common name.

Senecio cryphiactis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and unique flower structure.
