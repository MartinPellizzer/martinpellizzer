Senna foliosa

Senna foliosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters, featuring compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Senna foliosa is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its leaves and flowers used in traditional medicine for their laxative and anti-inflammatory effects.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful plant for soil stabilization and reforestation efforts.
