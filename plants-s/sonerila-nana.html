Sonerila nana

Sonerila nana, commonly known as the dwarf sonerila, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The flowers are often pink or purple, with five petals, and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits.
