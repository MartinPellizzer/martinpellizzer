Swainsona rostrata

Swainsona rostrata, commonly known as the yellow wickweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and its leaves are often pinnately divided. Swainsona rostrata has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.
