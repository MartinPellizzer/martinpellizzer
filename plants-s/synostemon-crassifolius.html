Synostemon crassifolius

Synostemon crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf synostemon, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
