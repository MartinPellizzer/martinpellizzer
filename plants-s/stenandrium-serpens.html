Stenandrium serpens

Stenandrium serpens, commonly known as the snake orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, serpentine inflorescence that resembles a snake, hence its common name. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, orchid-like flowers in shades of yellow and green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Stenandrium serpens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
