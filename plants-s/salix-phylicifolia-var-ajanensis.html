Salix phylicifolia var. ajanensis

Salix phylicifolia var. ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajanensis willow, is a variant of the willow species native to specific regions of East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender branches and distinctive leaf morphology. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a serrated margin, often with a grayish-green underside. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
