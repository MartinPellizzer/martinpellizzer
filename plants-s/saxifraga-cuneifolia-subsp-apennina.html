Saxifraga cuneifolia subsp. apennina

Saxifraga cuneifolia subsp.

apennina, commonly known as the Italian saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the Alps and surrounding regions. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often on slopes and cliffs.

The plant features small, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to reddish, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
