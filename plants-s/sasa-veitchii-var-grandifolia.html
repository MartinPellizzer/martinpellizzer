Sasa veitchii var. grandifolia

Sasa veitchii var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa veitchii species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its growth habit makes it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
