Scrophularia umbrosa subsp. neesii

Scrophularia umbrosa subsp.

neesii, commonly known as the dark scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

The subspecies neesii is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the main species.
