Silene babylonica

Silene babylonica, commonly known as the Babylonian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Silene babylonica is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
