Stixis nayarii

Stixis nayarii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thick, fleshy stems and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Stixis nayarii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
