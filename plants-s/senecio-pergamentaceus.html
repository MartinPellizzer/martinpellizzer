Senecio pergamentaceus

Senecio pergamentaceus, commonly known as the parchmentleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive, almost papery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
