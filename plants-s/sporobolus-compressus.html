Sporobolus compressus

Sporobolus compressus, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions.

Sporobolus compressus is often found in disturbed soils and is used in restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
