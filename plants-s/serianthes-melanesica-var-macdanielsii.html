Serianthes melanesica var. macdanielsii

Serianthes melanesica var. macdanielsii, commonly known as the MacDaniels' ironwood, is a rare and hardy tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived species. It thrives in coastal and lowland forest environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
