Sasa suzukii var. kosakensis

Sasa suzukii var. kosakensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese black bamboo, is a variant of the sasa genus native to East Asia.

It is a clump-forming grass-like plant that grows in dense stands, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant has slender, dark green culms that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
