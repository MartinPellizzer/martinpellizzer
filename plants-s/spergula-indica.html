Spergula indica

Spergula indica, commonly known as Indian spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Spergula indica is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
