Schoberia vera

Schoberia vera, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
