Solanum campylacanthum subsp. kondeense

Solanum campylacanthum subsp.

kondeense, commonly known as the Konde's nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are berries that mature to a deep purple color, containing several seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
