Stephania intertexta

Stephania intertexta, commonly known as the Chinese yew or the Indian yew, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Menispermaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and Nepal, and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in forested areas.

Stephania intertexta is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
