Symplocos paucinervia

Symplocos paucinervia, commonly known as the few-veined waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with a waxy texture. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth margin and a prominent midrib. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
