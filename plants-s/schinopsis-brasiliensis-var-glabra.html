Schinopsis brasiliensis var. glabra

Schinopsis brasiliensis var. glabra, commonly known as the Brazilian carob tree, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in savanna and woodland ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
