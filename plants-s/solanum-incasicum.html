Solanum incasicum

Solanum incasicum, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries, which are usually red when mature. These berries contain seeds and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
