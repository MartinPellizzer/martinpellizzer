Schismatoglottis calyptrata var. maculata

Schismatoglottis calyptrata var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted schismatoglottis, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a mix of green, white, and purple hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spathe, a common feature in the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
