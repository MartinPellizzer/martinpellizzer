Salvia compacta var. irazuensis

Salvia compacta var. irazuensis, commonly known as the Irazu sage, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within cloud forests. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
