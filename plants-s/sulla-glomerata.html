Sulla glomerata

Sulla glomerata, commonly known as clustered senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a taproot system.

Sulla glomerata is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
