Sisymbrium lippizense

Sisymbrium lippizense, commonly known as the Lippizian mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sisymbrium lippizense is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
