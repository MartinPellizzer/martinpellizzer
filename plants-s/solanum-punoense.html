Solanum punoense

Solanum punoense, commonly known as the Puno nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
