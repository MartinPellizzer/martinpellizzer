Scorzonera nova

Scorzonera nova, commonly known as the New Zealand burr.

It is a flowering plant native to New Zealand, characterized by its distinctive spiky, burr-like seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers with purple markings, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Scorzonera nova is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world as a garden plant.
