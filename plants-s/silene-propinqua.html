Silene propinqua

Silene propinqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene propinqua is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
