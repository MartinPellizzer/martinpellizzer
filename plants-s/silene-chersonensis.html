Silene chersonensis

Silene chersonensis, commonly known as the Crimean catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features simple, linear leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Silene chersonensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
