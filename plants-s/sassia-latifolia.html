Sassia latifolia

Sassia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf sassia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Sassia latifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
