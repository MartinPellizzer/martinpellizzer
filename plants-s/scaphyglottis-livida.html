Scaphyglottis livida

Scaphyglottis livida, commonly known as the blue-tongued orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue petals and a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a tongue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a mix of blue, violet, and white hues.

Scaphyglottis livida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique and colorful blossoms.
