Senecio brigalowensis

Senecio brigalowensis, commonly known as the Brigalow's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, aiding in water retention.

Senecio brigalowensis produces small yellow flowers that attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in native ecosystems.
