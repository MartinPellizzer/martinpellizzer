Scutellaria indica f. coccinea

Scutellaria indica f. coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with two lips, and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Scutellaria indica f. coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
