Saldinia hirsuta

Saldinia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy salvia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grey-green leaves and spikes of purple or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Saldinia hirsuta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
