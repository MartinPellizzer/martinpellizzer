Statice gaditana var. tingitana

Statice gaditana var. tingitana, commonly known as the Canary Islands statice, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing pink or red flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in coastal or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
