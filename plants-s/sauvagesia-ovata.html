Sauvagesia ovata

Sauvagesia ovata, commonly known as the white sauvagesia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural spread.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
