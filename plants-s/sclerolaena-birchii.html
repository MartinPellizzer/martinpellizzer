Sclerolaena birchii

Sclerolaena birchii, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient.

Sclerolaena birchii is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological value as a habitat for various insects and birds.
