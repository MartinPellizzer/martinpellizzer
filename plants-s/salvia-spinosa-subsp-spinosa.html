Salvia spinosa subsp. spinosa

Salvia spinosa subsp.

spinosa, commonly known as the spiny sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This subspecies is noted for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
