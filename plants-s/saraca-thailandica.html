Saraca thailandica

Saraca thailandica, commonly known as Thai flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Saraca thailandica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
