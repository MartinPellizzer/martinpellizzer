Sciuris officinalis

Sciuris officinalis, commonly known as the medicinal squirrel bean, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical structure of Sciuris officinalis supports its role in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
