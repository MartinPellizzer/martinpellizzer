Septas umbella

Septas umbella, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, umbrella-like leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in diameter, creating a dramatic visual effect. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Septas umbella thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant displays.
