Schizostachyum fuchsianum

Schizostachyum fuchsianum, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that often exhibit a striking purple hue, especially in younger growth. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, clumping habit. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Schizostachyum fuchsianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional landscaping and basket-making in its native regions.
