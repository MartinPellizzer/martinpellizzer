Senecio scabrosus

Senecio scabrosus, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, rough-textured stem and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
