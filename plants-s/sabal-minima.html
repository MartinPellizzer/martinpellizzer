Sabal minima

Sabal minima, commonly known as the dwarf cabbage palm, is a small, slow-growing palm native to the southeastern United States.

It typically reaches a height of less than three feet, making it one of the smallest palms in the world. The plant has a short trunk with a dense cluster of feather-like leaves at the top. Its leaves are usually a bluish-green color and can grow up to two feet in length.

Sabal minima is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in wetlands and dunes.
