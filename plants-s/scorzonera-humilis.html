Scorzonera humilis

Scorzonera humilis, commonly known as the dwarf scorzonera, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact rosette of silvery-green, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Scorzonera humilis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
