Sedum penzesii

Sedum penzesii, commonly known as the Penzes' stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Sedum penzesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
