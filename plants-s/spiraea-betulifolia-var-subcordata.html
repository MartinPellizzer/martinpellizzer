Spiraea betulifolia var. subcordata

Spiraea betulifolia var. subcordata, commonly known as the subcordate willow-leaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rounded base and finely toothed margins. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide early season floral interest in gardens.
