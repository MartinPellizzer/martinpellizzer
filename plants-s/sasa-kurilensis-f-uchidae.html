Sasa kurilensis f. uchidae

Sasa kurilensis f. uchidae, commonly known as the Uchida's sasa, is a clump-forming bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
