Sophia millefolia

Sophia millefolia, commonly known as common yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features feathery, finely divided leaves that give it its Latin name "millefolium," meaning "thousand leaves.". The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink or red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for various ailments, including wounds and digestive issues.
