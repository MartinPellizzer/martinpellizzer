Silene caramanica var. idaea

Silene caramanica var. idaea, commonly known as the Caramanica catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
