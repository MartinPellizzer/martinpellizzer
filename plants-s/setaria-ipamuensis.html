Setaria ipamuensis

Setaria ipamuensis, commonly known as the ipamuensis millet, is a grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.

Its botanical features make it useful for both ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
