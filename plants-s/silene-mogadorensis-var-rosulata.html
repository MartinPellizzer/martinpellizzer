Silene mogadorensis var. rosulata

Silene mogadorensis var. rosulata, commonly known as the Mogador rosebay, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces striking pink to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Silene mogadorensis var. rosulata is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.
