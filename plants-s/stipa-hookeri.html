Stipa hookeri

Stipa hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that are often pale green to golden in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue that adds visual interest to its habitat.

Stipa hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
