Stenaria nigricans var. floridana

Stenaria nigricans var. floridana, commonly known as Florida stenaria, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant has linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
