Salsola physophora

Salsola physophora, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Salsola physophora is well adapted to harsh environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and tolerate high temperatures.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
