Spodiopogon rhizophorus

Spodiopogon rhizophorus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and swampy environments in eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly zigzagged in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in saturated soils and is often found in marshes, along riverbanks, and in other moist habitats.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
