Symplocos maculata

Symplocos maculata, commonly known as the spotted evergreen or red sanders, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often spotted with red or purple markings. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. Symplocos maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
