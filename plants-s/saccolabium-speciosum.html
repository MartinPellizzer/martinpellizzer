Saccolabium speciosum

Saccolabium speciosum, commonly known as the showy saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is known for its vibrant yellow or orange flowers. Its leaves are compound, with small, ovate leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain the seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially grown.
