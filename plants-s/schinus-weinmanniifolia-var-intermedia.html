Schinus weinmanniifolia var. intermedia

Schinus weinmanniifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or Brazilian peppercorn, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to Brazil and parts of South America. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and have a feathery appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a peppery taste.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
