Solidago virginis-mariae

Solidago virginis-mariae, commonly known as the Virgin Mary's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental beauty in native plant gardens.
