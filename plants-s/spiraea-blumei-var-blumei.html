Spiraea blumei var. blumei

Spiraea blumei var. blumei, commonly known as Blume's weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that create a weeping appearance, and its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its cascading form and delicate floral display.
