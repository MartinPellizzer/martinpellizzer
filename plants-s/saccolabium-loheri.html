Saccolabium loheri

Saccolabium loheri, commonly known as the Lohr's saccolabium, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Saccolabium loheri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
