Spiraea nervosa subvar. angustifolia

Spiraea nervosa subvar. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The leaves are narrow and serrated, providing a fine-textured appearance throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its graceful form and seasonal interest.
