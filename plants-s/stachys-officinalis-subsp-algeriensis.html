Stachys officinalis subsp. algeriensis

Stachys officinalis subsp.

algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Algerian betony is valued for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
