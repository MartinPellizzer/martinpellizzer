Syngonanthus glandulosus

Syngonanthus glandulosus, commonly known as the sticky leaved grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. It is typically found in moist, sandy soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

Syngonanthus glandulosus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact form and attractive foliage.
