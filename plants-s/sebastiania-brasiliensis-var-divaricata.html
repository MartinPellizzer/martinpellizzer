Sebastiania brasiliensis var. divaricata

Sebastiania brasiliensis var. divaricata, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruits that resemble small, yellow-orange starfruits. The plant features smooth, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in subtropical regions.
