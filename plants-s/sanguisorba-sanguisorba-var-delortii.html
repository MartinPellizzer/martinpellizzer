Sanguisorba sanguisorba var. delortii

Sanguisorba sanguisorba var. delortii, commonly known as the Delort's bloodwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
