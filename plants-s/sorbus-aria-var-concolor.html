Sorbus aria var. concolor

Sorbus aria var. concolor, commonly known as the white-leaf rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white or pale green leaves, which are smooth and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its distinctive foliage and seasonal changes make it a visually appealing addition to landscapes.
