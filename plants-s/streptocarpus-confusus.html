Streptocarpus confusus

Streptocarpus confusus, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of velvety, dark green leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its unique flowering pattern, where flowers open in the evening and close during the day. Streptocarpus confusus is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

It prefers indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
