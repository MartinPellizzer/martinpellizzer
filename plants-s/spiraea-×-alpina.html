Spiraea × alpina

Spiraea × alpina, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring multiple stems that are often covered in grayish bark. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in white or pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated edge, turning yellow in the fall.

Spiraea × alpina is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
