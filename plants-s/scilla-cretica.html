Scilla cretica

Scilla cretica, commonly known as sea scilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, showing a strong adaptation to saline environments. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that allow it to survive in harsh conditions.

Scilla cretica is often found in maritime climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
