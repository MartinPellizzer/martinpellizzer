Scirpus phaeocarpus

Scirpus phaeocarpus, commonly known as the black fruit sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Scirpus phaeocarpus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
