Samaroceltis rhamnoides

Samaroceltis rhamnoides, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Rhamnaceae.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that matures into a dark brown seed pod.
