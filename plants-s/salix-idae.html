Salix idae

Salix idae, commonly known as the Idaea willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, catkin-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

Salix idae is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
