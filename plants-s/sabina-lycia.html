Sabina lycia

Sabina lycia, commonly known as Turkish tamarisk, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Sabina in the family Pinaceae, which includes various species of cypress and juniper. The plant is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.
