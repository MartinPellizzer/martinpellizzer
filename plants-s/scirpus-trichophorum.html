Scirpus trichophorum

Scirpus trichophorum, commonly known as the soft-stemmed bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of pale brown flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize wetland soils.

Scirpus trichophorum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
