Stachys xanthantha

Stachys xanthantha, commonly known as the yellow betony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Stachys xanthantha is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
