Saxifraga × mattfeldii

Saxifraga × mattfeldii, commonly known as the rockfoam, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in arid environments.
