Sorbus decipientiformis

Sorbus decipientiformis, commonly known as the false whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

Sorbus decipientiformis is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
