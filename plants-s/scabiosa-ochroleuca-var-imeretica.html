Scabiosa ochroleuca var. imeretica

Scabiosa ochroleuca var. imeretica, commonly known as the Caucasian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
