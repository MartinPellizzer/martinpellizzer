Schoenoplectus acutus var. acutus

Schoenoplectus acutus var. acutus, commonly known as sharp-tongued sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and pond edges. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion.

This species is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
