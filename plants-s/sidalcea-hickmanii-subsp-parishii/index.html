Sidalcea Hickmanii Subsp. Parishii

Sidalcea hickmanii subsp. parishii is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the western United States. This subspecies is known for its striking pink to purple blooms that appear in late spring. It thrives in specific habitats, often found in dry, rocky areas with well-drained soils. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts. Understanding and protecting this unique plant is crucial for preserving biodiversity in its native range.
