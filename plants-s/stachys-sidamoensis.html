Stachys sidamoensis

Stachys sidamoensis, commonly known as the Sidamo betony, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
