Serapias palmifolia

Serapias palmifolia, commonly known as the palm-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Serapias palmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
