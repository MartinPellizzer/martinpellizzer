Spiraea ariifolia var. discolor

Spiraea ariifolia var. discolor, commonly known as the whiteleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that cascade with clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The leaves are typically white to pale green, turning red or bronze in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
