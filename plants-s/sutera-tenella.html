Sutera tenella

Sutera tenella, commonly known as the silverleaf suture, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Sutera tenella thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
