Salvia fairuziana

Salvia fairuziana, commonly known as the Fairuz sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
