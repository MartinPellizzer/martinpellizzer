Spergella intermedia

Spergella intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate spikerush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Spergella intermedia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
