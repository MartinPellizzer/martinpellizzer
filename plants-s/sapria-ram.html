Sapria ram

Sapria ram, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The sapria ram is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
