Simonisia riedeliana

Simonisia riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's starry bell, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat. It is typically found in rocky soils and requires well-drained environments to thrive.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Simonisia riedeliana is considered a species of conservation concern.
