Sophronitis cernua var. endsfeldzii

Sophronitis cernua var. endsfeldzii, commonly known as the Endsfeldzii orchid, is a rare and exquisite species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often forming dense clusters that help retain moisture.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging and valuable species for orchid enthusiasts.
