Stephanotis lutchuensis

Stephanotis lutchuensis, commonly known as the Lutchu Island jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It is a woody vine characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found climbing trees or shrubs.

The flowers are typically visited by insects and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
