Setaria porphyrantha

Setaria porphyrantha, commonly known as purple foxtail, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinted inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more pronounced in the summer months.

Setaria porphyrantha plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
