Seriphidium cretaceum

Seriphidium cretaceum, commonly known as the Cretaceous thistle, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.
