Satureja acropolitana

Satureja acropolitana, commonly known as the Acropolis thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. This plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cooking, valued for their aromatic and slightly minty flavor.
