Silene scabrella

Silene scabrella, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
