Scrophularia trichopoda

Scrophularia trichopoda, commonly known as the hairy scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow environments. The plant features erect, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually pale purple or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Scrophularia trichopoda is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
