Stewartia sericea

Stewartia sericea, commonly known as silver stewartia, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by elliptical, red-brown fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Stewartia sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and parks.
