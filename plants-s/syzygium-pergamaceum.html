Syzygium pergamaceum

Syzygium pergamaceum, commonly known as the white apple or white sand apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a crisp, juicy pulp. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Its ornamental value and culinary uses make it a notable species in its native habitat.
