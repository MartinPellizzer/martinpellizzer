Stachys tibetica

Stachys tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan betony, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically blue or purple in color.

Stachys tibetica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
