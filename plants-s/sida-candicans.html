Sida candicans

Sida candicans, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Sida candicans is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
