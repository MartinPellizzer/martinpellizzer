Scutellaria brittonii

Scutellaria brittonii, commonly known as Britton's skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

Scutellaria brittonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
