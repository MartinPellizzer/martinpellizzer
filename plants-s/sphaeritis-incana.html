Sphaeritis incana

Sphaeritis incana, commonly known as the silverleaf sphaeritis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Sphaeritis incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
