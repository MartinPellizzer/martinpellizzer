Scypholepia

Scypholepia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that resemble sea urchins, hence its common name. The plants are typically found in coastal areas, where they thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Scypholepia species are known for their hardy nature and ability to tolerate salt spray and harsh maritime conditions.

Their flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to their ecological significance.
