Spiraea japonica var. mayebarae

Spiraea japonica var. mayebarae, commonly known as the Japanese Spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters along the stems. The plant has slender, arching branches and dark green, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is known for its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
