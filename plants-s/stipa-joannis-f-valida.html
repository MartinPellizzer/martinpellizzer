Stipa joannis f. valida

Stipa joannis f. valida, commonly known as the valid form of Joannis' grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Stipa joannis f. valida is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
