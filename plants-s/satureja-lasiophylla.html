Satureja lasiophylla

Satureja lasiophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, gray-green leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and other phenolic compounds, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Satureja lasiophylla is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its antiseptic and digestive properties.
