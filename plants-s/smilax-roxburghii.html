Smilax roxburghii

Smilax roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot or wild arrowroot, is a climbing shrub belonging to the family Smilacaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant features a thick, tuberous rhizome that serves as a storage organ and is often harvested for its starchy roots. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red or orange berries.

Smilax roxburghii is valued not only for its edible rhizomes but also for its use in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant.
