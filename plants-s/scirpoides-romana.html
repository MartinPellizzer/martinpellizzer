Scirpoides romana

Scirpoides romana, commonly known as the Roman bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters at the top of the stems, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Scirpoides romana is also valued for its ability to filter water and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.
