Sideritis spinulosa subsp. subspinosa

Sideritis spinulosa subsp.

subspinosa, commonly known as the spiked rock sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and cliffs. Sideritis spinulosa subsp.

subspinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
