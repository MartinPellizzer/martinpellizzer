Stachytarpheta mollis

Stachytarpheta mollis, commonly known as the sweetflag or blue mistflower, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Stachytarpheta mollis is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
