Sida digitata

Sida digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Sida digitata is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline soils.
