Sclerocactus parviflorus subsp. terrae-canyonae

Sclerocactus parviflorus subsp.

terrae-canyonae, commonly known as the canyon cholla, is a small, spiny cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It typically grows in rocky, arid environments, such as canyons and desert plains.

The plant features a compact, branching stem covered in short, cylindrical spines. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and bloom in spring.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low water availability, making it a resilient member of the cactus family.
