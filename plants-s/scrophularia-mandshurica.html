Scrophularia mandshurica

Scrophularia mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian figwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Scrophularia mandshurica is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
