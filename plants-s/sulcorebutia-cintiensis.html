Sulcorebutia cintiensis

Sulcorebutia cintiensis, commonly known as the Cinti's sun rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the dry regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and bright yellow flowers. The plant features ribbed stems covered in white to grayish spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically dispersed by animals.

Sulcorebutia cintiensis is a slow-growing, cold-hardy cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
