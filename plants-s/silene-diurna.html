Silene diurna

Silene diurna, commonly known as the dawnflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces striking pink to red flowers that open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name. Silene diurna is a perennial herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as cliffs and slopes.
