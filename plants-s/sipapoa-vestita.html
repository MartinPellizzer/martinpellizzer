Sipapoa vestita

Sipapoa vestita, commonly known as the naked orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or orange with a dark central stripe, and they emit a mild fragrance.

Sipapoa vestita is well-suited to rocky or dry environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
