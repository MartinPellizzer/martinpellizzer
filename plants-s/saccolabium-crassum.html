Saccolabium crassum

Saccolabium crassum, commonly known as the broadleaf saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater, which contributes to its resilience in dry conditions.

Saccolabium crassum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
