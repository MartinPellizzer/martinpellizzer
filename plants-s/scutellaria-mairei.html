Scutellaria mairei

Scutellaria mairei, commonly known as Maire's skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Scutellaria mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
