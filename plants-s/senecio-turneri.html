Senecio turneri

Senecio turneri, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Senecio turneri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
