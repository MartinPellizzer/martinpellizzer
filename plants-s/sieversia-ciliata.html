Sieversia ciliata

Sieversia ciliata, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often hairy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated with ciliate margins, giving it its scientific name.

Sieversia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
