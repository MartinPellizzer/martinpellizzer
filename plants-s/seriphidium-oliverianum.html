Seriphidium oliverianum

Seriphidium oliverianum, commonly known as the Oliver's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Seriphidium oliverianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
