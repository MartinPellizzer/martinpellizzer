Saussurea insularis

Saussurea insularis, commonly known as the island saffron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool temperatures and ample sunlight. Its fibrous roots and robust structure allow it to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
