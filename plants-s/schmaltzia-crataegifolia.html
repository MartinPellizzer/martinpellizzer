Schmaltzia crataegifolia

Schmaltzia crataegifolia, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and inland deserts. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers. It is adapted to arid environments with deep roots that help it survive drought conditions.

Schmaltzia crataegifolia is an important species for stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
