Sambucus racemosa f. flavescens

Sambucus racemosa f. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow elder or yellow elderberry, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or purple berries in the fall. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and showy inflorescences.

It is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
