Stemmacantha pulchra subsp. zardabii

Stemmacantha pulchra subsp.

zardabii, commonly known as the Zardabii starling plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture. This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
