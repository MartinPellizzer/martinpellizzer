Smilax kerberi

Smilax kerberi, commonly known as the prickly pear or Indian nettle, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in sharp, spiny projections. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Smilax kerberi is often found in woodland areas and is known for its aggressive growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
