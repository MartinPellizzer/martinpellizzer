Salix tetrasperma

Salix tetrasperma, commonly known as the four-seeded willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, often swampy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive four-winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, slightly glossy surface.

Salix tetrasperma is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
