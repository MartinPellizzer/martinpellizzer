Sorbus umbellata subsp. koevessii

Sorbus umbellata subsp.

koevessii, commonly known as the Koevessii whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its rounded, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, compound leaves that are alternate and have a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, white flowers in umbellate clusters, which are followed by small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
