Satureja procumbens

Satureja procumbens, commonly known as creeping savory, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are often used in cooking for their pungent, herbaceous flavor.

Satureja procumbens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
