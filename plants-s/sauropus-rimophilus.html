Sauropus rimophilus

Sauropus rimophilus, commonly known as the hairy sauropus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Sauropus rimophilus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
