Senecio layneae

Senecio layneae, commonly known as Layne's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
