Sideritis cassia

Sideritis cassia, commonly known as Cassia Sideritis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Sideritis cassia is often grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
