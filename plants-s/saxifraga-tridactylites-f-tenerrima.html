Saxifraga tridactylites f. tenerrima

Saxifraga tridactylites f. tenerrima, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. The plant features small, often white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, with three lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to survive in harsh alpine conditions.
