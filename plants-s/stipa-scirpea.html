Stipa scirpea

Stipa scirpea, commonly known as the common feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, feathery seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more prominent in the summer months.

Stipa scirpea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
