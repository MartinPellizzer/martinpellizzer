Scutellaria tatianae

Scutellaria tatianae, commonly known as the Tibetan skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountainous conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
