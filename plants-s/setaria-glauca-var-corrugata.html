Setaria glauca var. corrugata

Setaria glauca var. corrugata, commonly known as rough fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural and natural environments.
