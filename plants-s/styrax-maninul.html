Styrax maninul

Styrax maninul, commonly known as the Manchurian styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance source. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Styrax maninul thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
