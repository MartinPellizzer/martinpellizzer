Salvia fruticetorum

Salvia fruticetorum, commonly known as Mexican bush sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, narrow lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens. Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Salvia fruticetorum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
