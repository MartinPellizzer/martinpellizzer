Spilanthes anactina

Spilanthes anactina, commonly known as the toothache plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Spilanthes anactina is also noted for its medicinal properties, particularly for its ability to alleviate toothache pain.
