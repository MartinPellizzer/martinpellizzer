Shorea compressa

Shorea compressa, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and fruits that are important for local wildlife and seed dispersal.
