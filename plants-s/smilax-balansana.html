Smilax balansana

Smilax balansana, commonly known as the arrowhead smilax, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny foliage, which help it to climb and protect itself from herbivores. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are a food source for various animals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention in its native habitat.

Smilax balansana is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
