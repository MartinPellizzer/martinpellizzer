Spergula rubra var. rouyana

Spergula rubra var. rouyana, commonly known as red sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, bright red flowers. The plant produces oval, green leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Spergula rubra var. rouyana is primarily a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
