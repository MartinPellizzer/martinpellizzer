Saxifraga aizoides f. pallida

Saxifraga aizoides f. pallida, commonly known as the pale saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features succulent, pale green to bluish-gray leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
