Schinus dependens f. grandifolia

Schinus dependens f. grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or Portuguese pepper tree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that develop into bright red berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy appearance.

The fruit is an important food source for various bird species and is also used in traditional medicine.
