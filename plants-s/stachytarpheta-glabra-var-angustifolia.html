Stachytarpheta glabra var. angustifolia

Stachytarpheta glabra var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features lanceolate, glabrous leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
