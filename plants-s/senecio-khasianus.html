Senecio khasianus

Senecio khasianus, commonly known as the Khasi Senecio, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
