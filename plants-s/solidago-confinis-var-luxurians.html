Solidago confinis var. luxurians

Solidago confinis var. luxurians, commonly known as the eastern goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 meters and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear numerous small, rayless flower heads.

This variety is often found in moist to dry woodlands and open fields, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
