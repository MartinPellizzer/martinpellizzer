Symphyotrichum puniceum var. puniceum

Symphyotrichum puniceum var. puniceum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The flowers consist of a yellow central disk surrounded by ray florets, making it a popular choice for native gardens and pollinator habitats.
