Saponaria barbata

Saponaria barbata, commonly known as the spurred soaproot, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance and help in water retention. Its roots contain saponins, which give the plant its historical use in soap-making and as a natural detergent.

Saponaria barbata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.
