Setaria leonis

Setaria leonis, commonly known as the lion's tail grass, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly found in regions like Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its distinctive, tufted appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish or purplish in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found growing along roadsides or in disturbed areas.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
