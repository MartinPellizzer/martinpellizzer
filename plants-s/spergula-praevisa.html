Spergula praevisa

Spergula praevisa, commonly known as the early sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes. It is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological studies.
