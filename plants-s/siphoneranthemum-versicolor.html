Siphoneranthemum versicolor

Siphoneranthemum versicolor, commonly known as the variable siphon flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are notable for their color variation, ranging from yellow to orange and red, depending on environmental conditions.

Siphoneranthemum versicolor is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
