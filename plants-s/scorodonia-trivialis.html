Scorodonia trivialis

Scorodonia trivialis, commonly known as the three-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

Scorodonia trivialis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
