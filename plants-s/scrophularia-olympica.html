Scrophularia olympica

Scrophularia olympica, commonly known as the Olympic figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant features erect, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in certain regions.
