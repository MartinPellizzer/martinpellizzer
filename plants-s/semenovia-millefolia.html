Semenovia millefolia

Semenovia millefolia, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flower heads that are arranged in flat-topped clusters. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in well-drained soils.

Semenovia millefolia is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
