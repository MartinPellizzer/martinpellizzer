Schizostachyum latifolium

Schizostachyum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to two meters in length. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its inflorescences are typically clustered at the base of the stem, producing small, fragrant flowers.

Schizostachyum latifolium is valued for its use in furniture and handicrafts due to its strong and durable properties.
