Setariopsis

Setariopsis, commonly known as the "false panicgrass," is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plants produce small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Setariopsis species are often found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in environments with low moisture.

Due to their hardy nature, they are sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
