Salvia verbenaca var. amplifrons

Salvia verbenaca var. amplifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in herbal teas and culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
