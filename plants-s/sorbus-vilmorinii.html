Sorbus vilmorinii

Sorbus vilmorinii, commonly known as the Vilmorin's whitebeams, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by distinctive orange-red berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
