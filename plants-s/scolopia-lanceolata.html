Scolopia lanceolata

Scolopia lanceolata, commonly known as the arrowleaf groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Scolopia lanceolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
