Salsola tomentosa subsp. buhseana

Salsola tomentosa subsp.

buhseana, commonly known as silver saxifrage, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an adaptive and resilient species in extreme climates.
