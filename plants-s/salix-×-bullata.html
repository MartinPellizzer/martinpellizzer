Salix × bullata

Salix × bullata, commonly known as weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub with a distinctive weeping habit, featuring long, cascading branches that droop gracefully. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a bright green upper surface and a lighter, often pubescent underside. It produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typically male and female on separate plants.

This species is well adapted to moist environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes.
