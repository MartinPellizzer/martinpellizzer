Succisa pentaphylla

Succisa pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed succulent, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, typical of succulent plants. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
