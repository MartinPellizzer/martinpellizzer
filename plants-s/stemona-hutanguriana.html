Stemona hutanguriana

Stemona hutanguriana, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy and exhibit a pale green to reddish-brown coloration. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The roots of Stemona hutanguriana are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
