Sorghum bicolor var. arduinii

Sorghum bicolor var. arduinii, commonly known as the Italian millet, is a variant of sorghum native to southern Europe.

It is a small-grained cereal grass that thrives in warm, dry climates. The plant produces compact panicles of small, golden-yellow flowers, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its seeds are rich in nutrients, including protein and fiber, making it a valuable crop for both human consumption and animal feed.

The botanical features of this variety include its drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soil conditions.
