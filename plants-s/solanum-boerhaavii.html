Solanum boerhaavii

Solanum boerhaavii, commonly known as the Boerhaave's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters tall, with woody stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by berries that change from green to red as they mature. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, and it is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

Solanum boerhaavii is also notable for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
