Salix filistyla

Salix filistyla, commonly known as the Filistyla willow, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellowish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is noted for its resilience to coastal conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
