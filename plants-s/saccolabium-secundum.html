Saccolabium secundum

Saccolabium secundum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Saccolabium secundum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
