Symphytum elatum

Symphytum elatum, commonly known as the tall comfrey, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from purple to blue, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Symphytum elatum is often found in moist, shady areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
