Sorbus japonica f. denudata

Sorbus japonica f. denudata, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge, turning yellow in autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
