Saxifraga camposii var. leptophylla

Saxifraga camposii var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
