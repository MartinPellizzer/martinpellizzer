Stachys duriaei var. argilacea

Stachys duriaei var. argilacea, commonly known as clayplant or argilaceous stachys, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
