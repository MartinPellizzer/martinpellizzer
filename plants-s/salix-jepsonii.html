Salix jepsonii

Salix jepsonii, commonly known as Jepson's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada. This shrub or small tree typically grows in moist environments such as streambanks and wetlands. It has slender branches, narrow leaves, and produces catkin-like flowers in early spring.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
