Striga pinnatifida

Striga pinnatifida, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and pinnately compound leaves, which are typically green and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round, yellow fruits that are rich in vitamin C. Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
