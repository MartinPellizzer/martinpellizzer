Sorbus koehneana

Sorbus koehneana, commonly known as the Koehne's rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
