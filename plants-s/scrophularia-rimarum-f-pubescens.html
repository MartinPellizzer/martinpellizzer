Scrophularia rimarum f. pubescens

Scrophularia rimarum f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent scrophularia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "pubescens.". The plant typically produces clusters of tubular or bell-shaped flowers, often in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
