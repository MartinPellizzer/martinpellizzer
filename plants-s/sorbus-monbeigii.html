Sorbus monbeigii

Sorbus monbeigii, commonly known as the Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

Sorbus monbeigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
