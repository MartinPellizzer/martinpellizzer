Senna italica subsp. italica

Senna italica subsp.

italica, commonly known as the Italian senna, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are compound and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds. Senna italica subsp.

italica is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
