Syzygium tubiflorum

Syzygium tubiflorum, commonly known as the large-lobed tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Syzygium tubiflorum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
