Stachys palustris var. aspera

Stachys palustris var. aspera, commonly known as rough hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and glandular, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with purple to pink petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows and along the edges of ponds.
