Salvia micheliana

Salvia micheliana, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, mint-like scent when crushed.

Salvia micheliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
