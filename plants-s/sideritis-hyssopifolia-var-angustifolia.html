Sideritis hyssopifolia var. angustifolia

Sideritis hyssopifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in both wild and cultivated settings.
