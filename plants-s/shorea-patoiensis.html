Shorea patoiensis

Shorea patoiensis, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard timber that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves. It thrives in lowland rainforests and is often found in mixed dipterocarp forests.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
