Schoenus globosus

Schoenus globosus, commonly known as the globular sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Schoenus globosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
