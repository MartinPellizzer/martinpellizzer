Senecio ceratophyllus var. major

Senecio ceratophyllus var. major, commonly known as the giant yellow ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can be invasive in some regions.
