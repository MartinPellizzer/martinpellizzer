Stachys orientalis

Stachys orientalis, commonly known as oriental hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the mint family, Lamiaceae.
