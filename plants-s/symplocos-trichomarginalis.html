Symplocos trichomarginalis

Symplocos trichomarginalis, commonly known as the hairy-margin tamarind, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with hairy margins. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
