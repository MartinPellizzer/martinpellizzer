Schizachne purpurascens subsp. capillipes

Schizachne purpurascens subsp.

capillipes, commonly known as the purple bladed grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves that are often tinged with purple, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often pale green or reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a decorative grass in humid climates.
