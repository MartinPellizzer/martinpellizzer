Schkuhria pinnata var. wislizeni

Schkuhria pinnata var. wislizeni, commonly known as the wislizeni yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in flat-topped clusters called umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
