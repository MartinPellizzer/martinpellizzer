Sporobolus mangaloricus

Sporobolus mangaloricus, commonly known as the mangrove spikerush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is typically found in coastal areas, including mangrove swamps and salt marshes. The plant exhibits a robust rhizomatous growth system, allowing it to thrive in waterlogged and nutrient-poor soils. Its inflorescences are spike-like and contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
