Sphaeralcea ambigua var. rosacea

Sphaeralcea ambigua var. rosacea, commonly known as the roseleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often pink or purple in color. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a valuable plant for wildlife and ecological restoration.
