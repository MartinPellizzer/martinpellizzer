Salix nigricans var. punctata

Salix nigricans var. punctata, commonly known as the speckled willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, yellowish-green leaves with distinctive black or dark brown spots. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination and seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

The speckled willow is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
