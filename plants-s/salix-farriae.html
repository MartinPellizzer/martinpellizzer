Salix farriae

Salix farriae, commonly known as the Farri's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and China. The plant is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a pale green underside.

Salix farriae is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas.
