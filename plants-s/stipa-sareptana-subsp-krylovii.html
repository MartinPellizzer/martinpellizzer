Stipa sareptana subsp. krylovii

Stipa sareptana subsp.

krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's feathergrass, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
