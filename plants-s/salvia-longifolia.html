Salvia longifolia

Salvia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and aromatic properties. Salvia longifolia typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has a long history of traditional medicinal use.
