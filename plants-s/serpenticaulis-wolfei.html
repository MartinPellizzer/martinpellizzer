Serpenticaulis wolfei

Serpenticaulis wolfei, commonly known as the serpent-like stem plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, serpentine stems that can grow several meters in length, often trailing along the ground or climbing supports. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Serpenticaulis wolfei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
