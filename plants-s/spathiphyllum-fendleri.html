Spathiphyllum fendleri

Spathiphyllum fendleri, commonly known as the White Spathiphyllum, is a tropical evergreen perennial plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces elegant, spatulate white flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is a member of the Araceae family, known for its unique floral structure and air-purifying properties.

Spathiphyllum fendleri is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
