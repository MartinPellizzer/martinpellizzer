Scorzonera rumicifolia

Scorzonera rumicifolia, commonly known as the prickly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and lobed, often prickly leaves. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Scorzonera rumicifolia is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
