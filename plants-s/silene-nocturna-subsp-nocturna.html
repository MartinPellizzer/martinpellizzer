Silene nocturna subsp. nocturna

Silene nocturna subsp.

nocturna, commonly known as the evening silene, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom at night.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance, which may attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The species is known for its unique blooming time, which aligns with the twilight hours, hence its common name.
