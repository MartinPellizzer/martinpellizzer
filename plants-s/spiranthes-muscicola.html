Spiranthes muscicola

Spiranthes muscicola, commonly known as the musk orchid, is a small, perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flower spikes. The plant produces pale green to white flowers that bloom in late summer, and its roots are often covered in a dense, moss-like growth.

Spiranthes muscicola is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
