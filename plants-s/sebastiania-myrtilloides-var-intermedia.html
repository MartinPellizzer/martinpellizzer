Sebastiania myrtilloides var. intermedia

Sebastiania myrtilloides var. intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green and slightly pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and edible gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
