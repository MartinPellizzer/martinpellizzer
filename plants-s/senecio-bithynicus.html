Senecio bithynicus

Senecio bithynicus, commonly known as the Bithynian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Senecio bithynicus is often found in open woodlands and along rocky outcrops.
