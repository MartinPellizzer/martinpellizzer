Senecio sinapoides

Senecio sinapoides, commonly known as the yellow oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread.

Senecio sinapoides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
