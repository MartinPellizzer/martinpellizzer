Sinocalamus tomentosus

Sinocalamus tomentosus, commonly known as the thick-hair bamboo, is a tall, clumping bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and robust, often zigzagging stems. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas. Its culms are strong and flexible, making it suitable for construction and crafts.

Sinocalamus tomentosus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
