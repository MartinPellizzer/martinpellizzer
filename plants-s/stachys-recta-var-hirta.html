Stachys recta var. hirta

Stachys recta var. hirta, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Stachys recta var. hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
