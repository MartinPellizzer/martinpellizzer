Scirpus tabernaemontani var. uliginosus

Scirpus tabernaemontani var. uliginosus, commonly known as the marsh club-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
