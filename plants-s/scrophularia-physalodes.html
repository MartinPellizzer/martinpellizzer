Scrophularia physalodes

Scrophularia physalodes, commonly known as the common figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Scrophularia physalodes is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
