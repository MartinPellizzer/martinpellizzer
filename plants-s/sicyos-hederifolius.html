Sicyos hederifolius

Sicyos hederifolius, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are showy, trumpet-shaped, and typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces edible, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
