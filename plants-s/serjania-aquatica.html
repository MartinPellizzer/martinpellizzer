Serjania aquatica

Serjania aquatica, commonly known as water serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows near water bodies such as rivers and swamps. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in inflorescences.

Serjania aquatica is adapted to wetland environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
