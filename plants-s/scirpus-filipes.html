Scirpus filipes

Scirpus filipes, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its root system is extensive and helps stabilize soil in marshy environments.

Scirpus filipes thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
