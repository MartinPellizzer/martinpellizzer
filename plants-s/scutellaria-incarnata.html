Scutellaria incarnata

Scutellaria incarnata, commonly known as bluebeard, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Scutellaria incarnata is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade.
