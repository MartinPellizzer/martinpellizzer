Stapelia senilis

Stapelia senilis, commonly known as the old man's beard, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features long, hanging stems that produce clusters of fleshy, waxy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is characterized by its swollen, knotted stems and is often cultivated for its striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Stapelia senilis is also noted for its ability to produce offsets, which can be easily propagated to grow new plants.
