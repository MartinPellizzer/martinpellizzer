Searsia anchietae f. suffruticosa

Searsia anchietae f. suffruticosa, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
