Simsia setosa

Simsia setosa, commonly known as the slender mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. Simsia setosa is typically found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Its seeds are encased in hairy pods, which aid in seed dispersal by wind.
