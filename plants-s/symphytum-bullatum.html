Symphytum bullatum

Symphytum bullatum, commonly known as the purple knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, purple-pink flowers in clusters at the ends of the stems, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
