Scelochilus saccatus

Scelochilus saccatus, commonly known as the bladder orchid, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, sac-like structure at the base of its flower, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color and bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

Scelochilus saccatus is a perennial herb that plays a role in its local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.
