Sarothamnus malacitanus

Sarothamnus malacitanus, commonly known as the Malacitan thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to sunny, arid environments. Its botanical features include aromatic foliage and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates.

Sarothamnus malacitanus is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
