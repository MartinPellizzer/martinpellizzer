Spongiocarpella purpurea

Spongiocarpella purpurea, commonly known as the purple spongecarp, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spongy, and velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring. Spongiocarpella purpurea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
