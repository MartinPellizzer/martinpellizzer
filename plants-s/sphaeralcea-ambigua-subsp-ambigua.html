Sphaeralcea ambigua subsp. ambigua

Sphaeralcea ambigua subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has small, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
