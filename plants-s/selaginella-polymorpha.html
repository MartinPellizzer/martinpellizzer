Selaginella polymorpha

Selaginella polymorpha, commonly known as the variable spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss found in various parts of the world, particularly in temperate and tropical regions.

It is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and branching stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Selaginella polymorpha exhibits a unique ability to survive desiccation, making it a resilient species in arid conditions.

Its reproductive structures, including strobili, are essential for its propagation and ecological role.
