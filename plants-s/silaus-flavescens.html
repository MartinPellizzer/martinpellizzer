Silaus flavescens

Silaus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Silaus flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
