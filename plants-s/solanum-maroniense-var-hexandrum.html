Solanum maroniense var. hexandrum

Solanum maroniense var. hexandrum, commonly known as the six-stamen nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features small, white or greenish flowers with six stamens, giving it its varietal name. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are usually green when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral morphology.
