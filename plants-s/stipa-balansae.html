Stipa balansae

Stipa balansae, commonly known as the Balansae needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Stipa balansae plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
