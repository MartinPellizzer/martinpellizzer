Stellaria undulata

Stellaria undulata, commonly known as the wavy starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often curled or wavy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Stellaria undulata is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
