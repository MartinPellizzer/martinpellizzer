Stipa crassiflora

Stipa crassiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Stipa crassiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
