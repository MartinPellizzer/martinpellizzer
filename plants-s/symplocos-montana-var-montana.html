Symplocos montana var. montana

Symplocos montana var. montana, commonly known as mountain waxplant, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are clustered in small inflorescences and are usually white or pale green, producing a mild, aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
