Silene montana

Silene montana, commonly known as the mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Silene montana is also known for its attractive, delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
