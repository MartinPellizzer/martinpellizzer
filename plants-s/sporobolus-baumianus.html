Sporobolus baumianus

Sporobolus baumianus, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. The plant produces spikelets that are typically three-awned, giving it its common name, and it is well adapted to drought conditions. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Sporobolus baumianus is also valued for its ability to stabilize dunes and provide forage for wildlife.
