Sorbus trilocularis

Sorbus trilocularis, commonly known as the three-chambered whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges.

Sorbus trilocularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its autumn color and winter interest.
