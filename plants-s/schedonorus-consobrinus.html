Schedonorus consobrinus

Schedonorus consobrinus, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, upright blades and dense growth habit, making it a common ornamental and turfgrass species. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Schedonorus consobrinus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
