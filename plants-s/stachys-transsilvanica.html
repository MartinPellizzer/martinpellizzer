Stachys transsilvanica

Stachys transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters.

Stachys transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
