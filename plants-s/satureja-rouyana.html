Satureja rouyana

Satureja rouyana, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, aromatic seeds.

Satureja rouyana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
