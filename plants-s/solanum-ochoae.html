Solanum ochoae

Solanum ochoae, commonly known as Ochoa's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
