Sasa sacrosancta

Sasa sacrosancta, commonly known as sacred bamboo or Japanese bamboo, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, bamboo-like culms that grow in clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Sasa sacrosancta is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rhizomes and leaves are used in various herbal preparations.
