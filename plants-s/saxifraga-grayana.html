Saxifraga grayana

Saxifraga grayana, commonly known as the gray's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters. Saxifraga grayana is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
