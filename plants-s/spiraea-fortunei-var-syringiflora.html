Spiraea fortunei var. syringiflora

Spiraea fortunei var. syringiflora, commonly known as the weeping spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, white-pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
