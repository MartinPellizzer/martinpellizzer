Senecio sublutescens

Senecio sublutescens, commonly known as the whiteleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense heads, which bloom in the spring.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and drought tolerance.
