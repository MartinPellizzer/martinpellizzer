Saxifraga rhomboidea var. austrina

Saxifraga rhomboidea var. austrina, commonly known as the Australian saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Saxifraga rhomboidea var. austrina is often found in coastal and inland regions, growing in full sun.
