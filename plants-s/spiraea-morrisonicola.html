Spiraea morrisonicola

Spiraea morrisonicola, commonly known as Morrison's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often a vibrant green in summer, turning yellow in the fall.

Spiraea morrisonicola is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
