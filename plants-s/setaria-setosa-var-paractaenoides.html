Setaria setosa var. paractaenoides

Setaria setosa var. paractaenoides, commonly known as slender finger grass, is a variant of the panic grass genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with short, bristly bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
