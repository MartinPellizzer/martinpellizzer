Solanum macrocarpum

Solanum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature.

The leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant can grow up to 2 meters in height.
