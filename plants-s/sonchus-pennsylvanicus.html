Sonchus pennsylvanicus

Sonchus pennsylvanicus, commonly known as Pennsylvania sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet areas, such as marshes and stream banks, and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain insects and animals.
