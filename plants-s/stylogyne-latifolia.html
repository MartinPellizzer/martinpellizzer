Stylogyne latifolia

Stylogyne latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf stylogyne, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Stylogyne latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
