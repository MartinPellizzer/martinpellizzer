Serraea nubica

Serraea nubica, commonly known as the rock thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments.

Serraea nubica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
