Symplocos mollis

Symplocos mollis, commonly known as silverleaf evergreen holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Symplocos mollis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
