Setaria italica var. setosa

Setaria italica var. setosa, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a grass species native to warm temperate and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bristly, finger-like inflorescences that resemble a fox's tail. The plant produces small, oval-shaped grains that are harvested for their nutritional value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a resilient crop in arid environments.

Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a fibrous root system, which contribute to its adaptability.
