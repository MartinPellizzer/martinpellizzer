Salix daiseniensis

Salix daiseniensis, commonly known as the Daisen willow, is a species of willow native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant has slender, flexible branches and produces catkin-like flowers in early spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

The Daisen willow is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
