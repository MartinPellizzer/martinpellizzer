Senecio culcitioides

Senecio culcitioides, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features dense, woolly hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Senecio culcitioides produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
