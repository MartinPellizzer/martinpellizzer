Scrophularia saxatilis

Scrophularia saxatilis, commonly known as the saxatile figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments in Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are arranged in spikes and are usually purple or reddish, with tubular corollas that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Scrophularia saxatilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
