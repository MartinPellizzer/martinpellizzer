Schefflera paraensis

Schefflera paraensis, commonly known as the Amazonian umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like cluster of large, leathery leaves that provide shade and habitat for various organisms. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in its humid native habitat.

Schefflera paraensis is also notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
