Scrophularia nervosa subsp. boissieriana

Scrophularia nervosa subsp.

boissieriana, commonly known as the Boissier's dog's mercury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous areas.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in local ecosystems.
