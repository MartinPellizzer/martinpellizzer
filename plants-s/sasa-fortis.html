Sasa fortis

Sasa fortis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or hard bamboo, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its robust, woody stems that form dense clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green on top and lighter in color underneath. Sasa fortis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
