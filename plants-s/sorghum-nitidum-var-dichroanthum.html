Sorghum nitidum var. dichroanthum

Sorghum nitidum var. dichroanthum, commonly known as the two-colored sorghum, is a lesser-known variant of the sorghum genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive bicoloration, typically featuring a mix of green and silver or white hues on its foliage. The plant is native to Africa and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
