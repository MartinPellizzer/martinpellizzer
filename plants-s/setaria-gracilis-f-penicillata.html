Setaria gracilis f. penicillata

Setaria gracilis f. penicillata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a rapid growth habit, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

The species is native to various regions of Africa and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
