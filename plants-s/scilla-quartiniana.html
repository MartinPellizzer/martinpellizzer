Scilla quartiniana

Scilla quartiniana, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters of 3 to 6. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Scilla quartiniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
