Salvia mazatlanensis

Salvia mazatlanensis, commonly known as Mexican bush sage, is a perennial shrub native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It features upright stems covered in dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, helping it thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and requires well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members like its square stems and aromatic leaves.
