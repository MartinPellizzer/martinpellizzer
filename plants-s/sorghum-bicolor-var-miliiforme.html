Sorghum bicolor var. miliiforme

Sorghum bicolor var. miliiforme, commonly known as millet, is an annual grass species native to the warmer regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its seeds are rich in carbohydrates and are used as a staple food in many parts of the world.

The plant is also valued for its drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soil conditions.
