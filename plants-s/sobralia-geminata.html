Sobralia geminata

Sobralia geminata, commonly known as the twin-leafed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of multiple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem, giving it the "geminata" designation.

Sobralia geminata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
