Salsola nollothensis

Salsola nollothensis, commonly known as Nolloth saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is adapted to survive in harsh, saline environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss and are often covered in a white, waxy coating. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Salsola nollothensis is often found in sandy soils and plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
