Salix elymaitica

Salix elymaitica, commonly known as the Elymaitic willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. This shrub or small tree typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and catkin-like inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
