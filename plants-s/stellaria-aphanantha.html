Stellaria aphanantha

Stellaria aphanantha, commonly known as the ghost star, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has small, hairy leaves and produces white or pale pink blooms. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
