Syzygium petrophilum

Syzygium petrophilum, commonly known as the rock apple or mountain apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and glossy, and its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Syzygium petrophilum produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed.
