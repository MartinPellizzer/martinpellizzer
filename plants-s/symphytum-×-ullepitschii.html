Symphytum × ullepitschii

Symphytum × ullepitschii, commonly known as the Russian comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Due to its rapid growth and nutrient-rich roots, it has been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
