Sideritis incana var. longifolia

Sideritis incana var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved Heath, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, gray-green stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.
