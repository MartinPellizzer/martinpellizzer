Sanguisorba minor f. verrucosa

Sanguisorba minor f. verrucosa, commonly known as the woolly sanguisorba, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative plant.
