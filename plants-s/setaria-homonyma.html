Setaria homonyma

Setaria homonyma, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny spikelets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Setaria homonyma is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
