Solanum salamancae

Solanum salamancae, commonly known as the Salamanca nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring branched stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, which bloom in the evening and emit a faint fragrance. Its fruit is a yellowish berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum salamancae is part of the nightshade family and is characterized by its adaptability to coastal and rocky habitats.
