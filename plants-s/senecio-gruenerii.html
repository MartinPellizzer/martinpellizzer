Senecio gruenerii

Senecio gruenerii, commonly known as Gruener's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio gruenerii is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
