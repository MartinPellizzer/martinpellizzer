Serapias × garganica

Serapias × garganica, commonly known as the hybrid orchid of the serapias genus, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Serapias aperita and Serapias himalayana.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of yellow and orange hues with intricate patterns. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that often grows in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an example of natural hybridization in the orchid family.
