Salvia gachantivana

Salvia gachantivana, commonly known as the Andean sage, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia gachantivana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been traditionally used in Andean cultures for various purposes.
