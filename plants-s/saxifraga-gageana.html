Saxifraga gageana

Saxifraga gageana, commonly known as the Gage's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white or silver hairs.

Saxifraga gageana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
