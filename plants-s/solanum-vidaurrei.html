Solanum vidaurrei

Solanum vidaurrei, commonly known as the Vidaurre's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are an important food source for certain bird species.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments at moderate elevations.
