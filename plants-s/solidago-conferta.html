Solidago conferta

Solidago conferta, commonly known as the dense goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Solidago conferta typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland areas.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
