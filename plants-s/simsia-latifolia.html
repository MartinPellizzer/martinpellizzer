Simsia latifolia

Simsia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf simsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Simsia latifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
