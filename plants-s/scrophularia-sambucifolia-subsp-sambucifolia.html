Scrophularia sambucifolia subsp. sambucifolia

Scrophularia sambucifolia subsp.

sambucifolia, commonly known as the sambucifolia scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in the summer months. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct leaf shape and floral structure, which are adapted to its specific ecological niche.
