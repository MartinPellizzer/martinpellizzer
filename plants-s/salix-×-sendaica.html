Salix × sendaica

Salix × sendaica, commonly known as the hybrid willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is a cross between Salix matsudana and Salix schwerinii, resulting in a plant with distinctive, narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive form and adaptability to various soil types.

Salix × sendaica is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects.
