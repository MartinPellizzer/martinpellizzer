Scrophularia pisidica

Scrophularia pisidica, commonly known as the Crimean scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open woodlands or along riverbanks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are arranged in spikes, with tubular corollas that are pink to purple in color.

Scrophularia pisidica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
