Suttonia montana

Suttonia montana, commonly known as the mountain suttonia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures.

Suttonia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
