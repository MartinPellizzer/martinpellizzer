Sideritis caureliana

Sideritis caureliana, commonly known as the Caucasian sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, narrow leaves, and small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Sideritis caureliana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
