Smilacina stellata f. paniculata

Smilacina stellata f. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled smilacina, is a variant of the smilacina genus native to eastern North America.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, thread-like stems and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellowish-ochre berries that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological diversity in its native habitat.
