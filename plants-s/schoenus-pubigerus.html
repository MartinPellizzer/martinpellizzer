Schoenus pubigerus

Schoenus pubigerus, commonly known as the hairy marsh grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often pale green to brown in color.

Schoenus pubigerus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
