Siparuna fuchsiifolia

Siparuna fuchsiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that surround small, white flowers, creating a visually dramatic display. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Siparuna fuchsiifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
