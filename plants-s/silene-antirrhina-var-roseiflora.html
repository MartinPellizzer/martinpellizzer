Silene antirrhina var. roseiflora

Silene antirrhina var. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
