Stemotis coccineum

Stemotis coccineum, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking red stems and delicate, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in racemes and often display a range of colors from pink to red.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
