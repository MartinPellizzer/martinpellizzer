Schisandra

Schisandra, commonly known as five-flavor fruit, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is characterized by its small, red, berry-like fruits that contain a mix of bitter, sour, sweet, spicy, and umami flavors. The plant's berries are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient, valued for their unique taste and potential health benefits. Schisandra thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in the spring.
