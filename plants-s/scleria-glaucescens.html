Scleria glaucescens

Scleria glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Scleria glaucescens typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is often found in temperate regions of North America and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
