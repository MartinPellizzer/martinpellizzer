Satureja montana var. kitaibelii

Satureja montana var. kitaibelii, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and dry grasslands. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and therapeutic uses.
