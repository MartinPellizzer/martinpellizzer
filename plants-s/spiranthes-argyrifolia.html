Spiranthes argyrifolia

Spiranthes argyrifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf ladies' tresses, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is a perennial herb that produces delicate, spiraling inflorescences of white or pale blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It is native to eastern North America and is often associated with calcareous soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
