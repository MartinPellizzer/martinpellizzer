Schoenoplectiella mucronata var. antrorsispinulosa

Schoenoplectiella mucronata var. antrorsispinulosa, commonly known as the hooked spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and spike-like inflorescences.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a dense, compact form, and its flowers are small, clustered in spikes with distinctive bract structures. It is notable for its unique morphological features, including the hooked spines on its inflorescence, which aid in seed dispersal.

This variety is part of the Cyperaceae family, known for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
