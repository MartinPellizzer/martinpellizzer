Stylocoryna junghuhniana

Stylocoryna junghuhniana, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Stylocoryna junghuhniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
