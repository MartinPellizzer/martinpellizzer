Silene conglomeratica

Silene conglomeratica, commonly known as the clustered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene conglomeratica thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.
