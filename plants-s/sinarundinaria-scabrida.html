Sinarundinaria scabrida

Sinarundinaria scabrida, commonly known as the Chinese cliff brake, is a fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a rough, scaly texture. The plant typically grows on rocky slopes and cliffs, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters.

Sinarundinaria scabrida is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens and shady landscapes.
