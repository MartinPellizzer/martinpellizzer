Satureja alternipilosa

Satureja alternipilosa, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine environments. Its leaves are small, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in whorls.

Satureja alternipilosa is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
