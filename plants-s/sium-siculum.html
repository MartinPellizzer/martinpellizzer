Sium siculum

Sium siculum, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that grow on hollow, ridged stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, wet environments such as marshes and stream banks.

Its root system is fibrous, and the plant is often found in wetland habitats.
