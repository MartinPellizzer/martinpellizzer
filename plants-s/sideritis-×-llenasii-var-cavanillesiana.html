Sideritis × llenasii var. cavanillesiana

Sideritis × llenasii var. cavanillesiana, commonly known as the Cavanilles' sainfoin, is a hybrid plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
