Scutellaria procumbens

Scutellaria procumbens, commonly known as匍匐鼠尾草, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It features prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scutellaria procumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
