Schedonorus × schlickumii subsp. czarnohorensis

Schedonorus × schlickumii subsp.

czarnohorensis, commonly known as the Carpathian meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Carpathian Mountains. It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
