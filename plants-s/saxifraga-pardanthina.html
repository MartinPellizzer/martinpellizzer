Saxifraga pardanthina

Saxifraga pardanthina, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, white or pinkish flowers in the summer. It is often found in mountainous areas where it forms dense mats to retain heat and moisture.

Saxifraga pardanthina is an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
