Syzygium comorense

Syzygium comorense, commonly known as the Comoros apple or kola tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Comoros Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The fruit has a crisp, juicy texture with a sweet and tart flavor, making it a popular local food source.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.
