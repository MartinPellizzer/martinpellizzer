Syringa oblata var. giraldii

Syringa oblata var. giraldii, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Syringa oblata var. giraldii is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.
