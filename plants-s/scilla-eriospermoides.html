Scilla eriospermoides

Scilla eriospermoides, commonly known as the woolly scilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

The flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
