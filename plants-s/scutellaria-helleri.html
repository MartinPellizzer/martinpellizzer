Scutellaria helleri

Scutellaria helleri, commonly known as Heller's bluebug.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in areas of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Scutellaria helleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
