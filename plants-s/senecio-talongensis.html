Senecio talongensis

Senecio talongensis, commonly known as the Talong's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and linear.

Senecio talongensis is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
