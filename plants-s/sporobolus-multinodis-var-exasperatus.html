Sporobolus multinodis var. exasperatus

Sporobolus multinodis var. exasperatus, commonly known as the rough spikerush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences and fibrous root system. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in spikes. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils with low water availability.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
