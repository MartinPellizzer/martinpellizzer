Salvia drusica

Salvia drusica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia drusica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
