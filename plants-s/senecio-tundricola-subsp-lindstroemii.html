Senecio tundricola subsp. lindstroemii

Senecio tundricola subsp.

lindstroemii, commonly known as the Arctic groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in alpine and tundra environments.

The plant has dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters during the short summer season.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to survive harsh weather conditions.
