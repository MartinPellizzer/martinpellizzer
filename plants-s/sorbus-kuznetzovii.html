Sorbus kuznetzovii

Sorbus kuznetzovii, commonly known as the Kuznetsov's whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Russian Far East and parts of northeastern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

Sorbus kuznetzovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its winter interest and adaptability to various soil conditions.
