Salsola nudiflora

Salsola nudiflora, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is highly drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and can form dense colonies.

Its ability to thrive in extreme conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
