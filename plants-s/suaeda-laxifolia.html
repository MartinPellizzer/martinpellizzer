Suaeda laxifolia

Suaeda laxifolia, commonly known as sea blite, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, and its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in coastal areas.

Suaeda laxifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
