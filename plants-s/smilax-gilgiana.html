Smilax gilgiana

Smilax gilgiana, commonly known as the red-berried smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often armed with sharp, curved spines. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and a prominent leaflet arrangement.

Smilax gilgiana is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
