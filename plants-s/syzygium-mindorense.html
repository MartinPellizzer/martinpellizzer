Syzygium mindorense

Syzygium mindorense, commonly known as the Mindanao apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp.

Syzygium mindorense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is enjoyed both fresh and in culinary applications.
