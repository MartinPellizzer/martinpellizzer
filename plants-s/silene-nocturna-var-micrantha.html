Silene nocturna var. micrantha

Silene nocturna var. micrantha, commonly known as the night-flowering catchfly, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals that curl back as they mature. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
