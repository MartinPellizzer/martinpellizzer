Swertia pseudopetiolata

Swertia pseudopetiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Swertia pseudopetiolata is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
