Stipa humilis f. major

Stipa humilis f. major, commonly known as the giant bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green blades that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces inflorescences consisting of spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
