Stelis butcheri

Stelis butcheri, commonly known as the Butcher's stelis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit, often growing on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Stelis butcheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
