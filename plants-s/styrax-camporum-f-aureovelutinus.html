Styrax camporum f. aureovelutinus

Styrax camporum f. aureovelutinus, commonly known as the golden velvet styrax, is a botanical variant of the styrax plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This shrub typically features lance-shaped leaves that are a striking golden-green color, covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in partial shade to full sun.

The golden velvet styrax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
