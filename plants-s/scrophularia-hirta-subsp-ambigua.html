Scrophularia hirta subsp. ambigua

Scrophularia hirta subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the hairy scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces clusters of tubular, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
