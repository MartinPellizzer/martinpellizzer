Stelis subequalis

Stelis subequalis, commonly known as the equal-flowered stelis, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a short, erect stem with several closely spaced, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and are arranged in a raceme, giving it a delicate appearance.

Stelis subequalis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
