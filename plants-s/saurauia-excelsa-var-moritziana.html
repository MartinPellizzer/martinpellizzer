Saurauia excelsa var. moritziana

Saurauia excelsa var. moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's evergreen oak, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that attracts various bird species.
