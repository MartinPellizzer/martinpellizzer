Salvia × mendizabalii

Salvia × mendizabalii, commonly known as the Mendizabal sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique color, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
