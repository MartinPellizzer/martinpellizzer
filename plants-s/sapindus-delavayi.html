Sapindus delavayi

Sapindus delavayi, commonly known as the Chinese soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces spherical, spiny fruits that contain a starchy, edible seed. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the fruits have been utilized for their soap-like properties.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
