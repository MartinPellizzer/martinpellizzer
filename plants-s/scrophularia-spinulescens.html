Scrophularia spinulescens

Scrophularia spinulescens, commonly known as the rough scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value. The plant is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or grassy areas.

Scrophularia spinulescens is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
