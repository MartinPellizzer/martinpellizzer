Stevia brunetii

Stevia brunetii, commonly known as the Brazilian stevia, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, natural sweeteners used as a sugar substitute. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

Stevia brunetii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
