Senecio scitus

Senecio scitus, commonly known as the blueweed or the African blueweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Senecio scitus has narrow, linear leaves and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
