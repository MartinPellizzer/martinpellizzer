Solanum vicinum

Solanum vicinum, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are usually yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum vicinum contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals if ingested.
