Stevia purpurascens

Stevia purpurascens, commonly known as purple sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners used in food and beverages. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value, combined with its medicinal and commercial uses, makes it a versatile plant in both horticulture and natural health industries.
