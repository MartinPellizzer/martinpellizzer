Stipa pulcherrima var. alagezica

Stipa pulcherrima var. alagezica, commonly known as the Alagezic feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that produce dense, fluffy panicles of pale to golden-yellow flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
