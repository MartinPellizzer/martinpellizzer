Struthiopteris pensylvanica

Struthiopteris pensylvanica, commonly known as the eastern wood fern, is a perennial fern native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves, or fronds, are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, almost feathery appearance.

Struthiopteris pensylvanica thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
