Sauropus quadrangularis var. compressus

Sauropus quadrangularis var. compressus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled under, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
