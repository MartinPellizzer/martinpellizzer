Seorsus clavifolius

Seorsus clavifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed seorsus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a distinctively narrow and pointed appearance, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
