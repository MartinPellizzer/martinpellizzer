Syzygium sumatranum

Syzygium sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, apple-like fruits with a sweet and sour flavor. Syzygium sumatranum is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
