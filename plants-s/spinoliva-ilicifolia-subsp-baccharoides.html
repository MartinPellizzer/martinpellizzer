Spinoliva ilicifolia subsp. baccharoides

Spinoliva ilicifolia subsp.

baccharoides, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in coastal and saline environments, showing a high tolerance for drought and salt.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to rocky and coastal soils.
