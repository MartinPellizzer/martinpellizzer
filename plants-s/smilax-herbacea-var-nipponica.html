Smilax herbacea var. nipponica

Smilax herbacea var. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese smilax, is a perennial vine native to East Asia.

It features woody stems with spiny nodes and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
