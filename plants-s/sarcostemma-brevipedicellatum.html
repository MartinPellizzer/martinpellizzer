Sarcostemma brevipedicellatum

Sarcostemma brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the small-stemmed nightshade, is a climbing shrub belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn red when mature. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
