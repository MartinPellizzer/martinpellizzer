Silene uralensis subsp. apetala

Silene uralensis subsp.

apetala, commonly known as the Ural flaxflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The subspecies is notable for its lack of petals, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to other Silene species. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

This plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
