Solanum acaule subsp. punae

Solanum acaule subsp.

punae, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is adapted to cold, mountainous environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in cooler climates.
