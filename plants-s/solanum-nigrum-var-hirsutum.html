Solanum nigrum var. hirsutum

Solanum nigrum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a variant of the common nightshade plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in disturbed soils and along pathways. The plant features dark purple to black berries that are edible but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it the "hirsutum" designation, and it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

The hairy nightshade is known for its ornamental value and its role in traditional medicine in some cultures.
