Sideritis × sallentii

Sideritis × sallentii, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy, with a distinctive grayish-green color. Its small, pale blue or violet flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, typical of the genus Sideritis.

Sideritis × sallentii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
