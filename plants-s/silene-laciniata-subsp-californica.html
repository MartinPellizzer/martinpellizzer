Silene laciniata subsp. californica

Silene laciniata subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California pink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive notched or lobed appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
