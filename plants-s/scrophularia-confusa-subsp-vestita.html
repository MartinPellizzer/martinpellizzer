Scrophularia confusa subsp. vestita

Scrophularia confusa subsp.

vestita, commonly known as the hairy scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. Its flowers are pale pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
