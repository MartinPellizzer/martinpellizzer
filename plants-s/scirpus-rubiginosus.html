Scirpus rubiginosus

Scirpus rubiginosus, commonly known as red-stem bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas, pond edges, and along streams, thriving in saturated soils. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that are reddish-brown in color, often with a fibrous texture. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers in the summer.

Scirpus rubiginosus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
