Schefflera fruticosa

Schefflera fruticosa, commonly known as the evergreen holly, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, bushy form. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions contributes to its widespread cultivation.
