Sempervivum kosaninii

Sempervivum kosaninii, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues, especially under stress or in full sun. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Sempervivum kosaninii is also known for its ability to survive in harsh alpine conditions, showcasing its resilience and adaptability.
