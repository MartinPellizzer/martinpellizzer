Sorghum saccharatum var. burmanicum

Sorghum saccharatum var. burmanicum, commonly known as Burmese sweet sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellow bracts, and its leaves are broad and lance-shaped. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its high sugar content in the stalks, making it useful for both culinary and industrial applications.
