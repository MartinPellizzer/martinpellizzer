Syngonanthus williamsii

Syngonanthus williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of cylindrical stems that form a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color.

Syngonanthus williamsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a component in traditional Andean agriculture.
