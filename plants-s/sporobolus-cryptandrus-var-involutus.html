Sporobolus cryptandrus var. involutus

Sporobolus cryptandrus var. involutus, commonly known as the curled spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are tightly wrapped, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swales, and other moist soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variety is noted for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
