Solanum buettneri

Solanum buettneri, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet and purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a mild, sweet fragrance. Its fruit, which resembles a small egg, is edible and has a tart, berry-like flavor.

Solanum buettneri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
