Stelis tricardium

Stelis tricardium, commonly known as the three-heart orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, three-lobed orchid blooms that resemble heart shapes. The plant typically grows on tree branches or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with a faint pinkish tinge, and they bloom during the wet season.

Stelis tricardium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
