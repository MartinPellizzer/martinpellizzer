Sidalcea oregana var. nevadensis

Sidalcea oregana var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada checkerbloom, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions of Nevada and adjacent states.
