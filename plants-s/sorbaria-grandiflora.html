Sorbaria grandiflora

Sorbaria grandiflora, commonly known as sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Sorbaria grandiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
