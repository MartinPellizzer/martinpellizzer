Stipa chrysophylla f. modica

Stipa chrysophylla f. modica, commonly known as the Modica false wheatgrass, is a compact, clump-forming grass with narrow, bright yellow-green leaves.

It is native to southern Italy and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky florets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and the subtle, pale floral display.

It is a low-maintenance plant that adds a soft, elegant texture to garden landscapes.
