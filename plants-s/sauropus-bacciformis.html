Sauropus bacciformis

Sauropus bacciformis, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Sauropus bacciformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
