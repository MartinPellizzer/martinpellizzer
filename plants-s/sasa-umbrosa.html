Sasa umbrosa

Sasa umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow bamboo, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

Sasa umbrosa thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
