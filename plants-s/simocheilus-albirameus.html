Simocheilus albirameus

Simocheilus albirameus, commonly known as the white-flowered water lily, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface, and its flowers are white with a yellow center. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its ornamental value has also made it popular in water garden settings.
