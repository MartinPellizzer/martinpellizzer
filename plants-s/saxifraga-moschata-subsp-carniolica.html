Saxifraga moschata subsp. carniolica

Saxifraga moschata subsp.

carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps and Carpathian regions. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically pink or reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
