Serapias × ambigua subsp. sallentina

Serapias × ambigua subsp.

sallentina, commonly known as the Sardinian marsh orchid, is a hybrid species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia. It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, thriving in moist, calcareous soils. Its inflorescence consists of several flowers arranged in a raceme, with petals that are often marked with dark veins.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
