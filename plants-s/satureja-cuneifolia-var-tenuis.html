Satureja cuneifolia var. tenuis

Satureja cuneifolia var. tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic qualities. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale purple or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which are valued for their culinary and medicinal properties.
