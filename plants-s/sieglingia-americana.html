Sieglingia americana

Sieglingia americana, commonly known as the American sieglingia, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in prairie or open woodland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
