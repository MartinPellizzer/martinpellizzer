Spiraeanthemum novoguineense

Spiraeanthemum novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea spiraea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its fragrant blooms and attractive foliage.
