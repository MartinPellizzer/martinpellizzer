Stachys mardinensis

Stachys mardinensis, commonly known as Mardin hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pale purple to pink flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
