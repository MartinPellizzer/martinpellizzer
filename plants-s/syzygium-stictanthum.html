Syzygium stictanthum

Syzygium stictanthum, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties, though it is also threatened by overharvesting.

Syzygium stictanthum plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species.
