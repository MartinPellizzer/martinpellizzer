Sedum crassicaule

Sedum crassicaule, commonly known as the thick-stemmed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in dry conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum crassicaule is also valued for its low maintenance and ability to prevent soil erosion.
