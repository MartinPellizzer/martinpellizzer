Sarcococca pruniformis var. zeylanica

Sarcococca pruniformis var. zeylanica, commonly known as the Zeylanica boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Sri Lanka.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a leathery texture, and its small, fragrant flowers appear in clusters during late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange in color, which are an important food source for birds.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and hedge planting.
