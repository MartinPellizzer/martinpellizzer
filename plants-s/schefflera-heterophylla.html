Schefflera heterophylla

Schefflera heterophylla, commonly known as the variable-leaf umbrella plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like arrangement of leaves, which vary in shape and size depending on the plant's growth conditions. The plant typically has glossy, dark green foliage with a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Schefflera heterophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and adaptability to various indoor environments.
