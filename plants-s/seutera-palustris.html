Seutera palustris

Seutera palustris, commonly known as the marsh seutera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its small, white to pale pink flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Seutera palustris plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
