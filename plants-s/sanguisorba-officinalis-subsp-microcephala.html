Sanguisorba officinalis subsp. microcephala

Sanguisorba officinalis subsp.

microcephala, commonly known as dwarf lady's fingers, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, red-brown flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.

Its subspecies name, *microcephala*, refers to its small, rounded flower heads.
