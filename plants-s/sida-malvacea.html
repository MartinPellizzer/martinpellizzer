Sida malvacea

Sida malvacea, commonly known as yellow mallow, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, alternate leaves with notched margins, and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Sida malvacea is also known for its mucilaginous seeds, which can be used in traditional remedies.
