Silene venosa var. paniculata

Silene venosa var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Silene venosa var. paniculata is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
