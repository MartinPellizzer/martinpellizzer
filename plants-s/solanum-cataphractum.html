Solanum cataphractum

Solanum cataphractum, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Solanum cataphractum is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in areas with limited vegetation.
