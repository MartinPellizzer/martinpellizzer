Sideritis × costae

Sideritis × costae, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Sideritis × costae is valued for its adaptability and ornamental appeal in gardens.
