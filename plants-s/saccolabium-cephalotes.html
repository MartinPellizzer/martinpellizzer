Saccolabium cephalotes

Saccolabium cephalotes, commonly known as the mountain bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red, bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
