Silene caryophylloides

Silene caryophylloides, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, often pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Silene caryophylloides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique bloom time and color.
