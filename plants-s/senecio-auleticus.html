Senecio auleticus

Senecio auleticus, commonly known as the African snowball, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of white, woolly flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
