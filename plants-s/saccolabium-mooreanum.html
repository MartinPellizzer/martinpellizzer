Saccolabium mooreanum

Saccolabium mooreanum, commonly known as the Moorean senna, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
