Sarcocephalus cordatus var. pubescens

Sarcocephalus cordatus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The hairy texture of its foliage is a distinguishing feature that aids in its identification.
