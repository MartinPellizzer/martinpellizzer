Sonerila hirsutissima

Sonerila hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy sonerila, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.
