Sisymbrium junceum var. soongaricum

Sisymbrium junceum var. soongaricum, commonly known as the Chinese broomrape, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially depends on host plants for water and nutrients while photosynthesizing on its own. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces small, elliptical seeds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have ecological impacts on its host species and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
