Solanum andigenum var. ckello-huaccoto

Solanum andigenum var. ckello-huaccoto, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The fruit is a spherical berry that ripens to a deep purple color, often used in traditional Andean medicine and culinary practices.
