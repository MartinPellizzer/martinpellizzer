Sanguisorba albiflora

Sanguisorba albiflora, commonly known as white false spirea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, grayish-green leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and capable of forming dense colonies, making it a hardy plant suitable for moist, well-drained soils.

Sanguisorba albiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
