Sium cicutarium

Sium cicutarium, commonly known as water parsnip, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

Its roots are often harvested for use in traditional medicine and as a food source.
