Syringa villosa var. rosea

Syringa villosa var. rosea, commonly known as the rose pink mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its species name "villosa.". It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its showy blooms and is often used in landscaping for its pleasant scent and attractive appearance.
