Sedum grandidentatum

Sedum grandidentatum, commonly known as the large-toothed stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Sedum grandidentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
