Salvia itaguassuensis

Salvia itaguassuensis, commonly known as the Itaguassu sage, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a fragrant aroma.

Salvia itaguassuensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
