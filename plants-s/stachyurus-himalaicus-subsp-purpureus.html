Stachyurus himalaicus subsp. purpureus

Stachyurus himalaicus subsp.

purpureus, commonly known as the purple stachyurus, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its elegant, pendulous racemes of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
