Schizostachyum flexuosum

Schizostachyum flexuosum, commonly known as the flexuous bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged alternately along the stem. Schizostachyum flexuosum is often found in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, tropical climates.

It is valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable timber.
