Satyrium dizygoceras

Satyrium dizygoceras, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pearly-eye, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features a slender stem with a few leaves and produces small, delicate flowers with a distinctive white or pale pink coloration. Its flowers have a unique structure with a spurred lip that serves as a nectar source for pollinators.

The orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
