Sulcorebutia frankiana

Sulcorebutia frankiana, commonly known as the Frank's sun rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of Argentina and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom from areoles along the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
