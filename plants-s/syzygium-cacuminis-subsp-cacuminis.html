Syzygium cacuminis subsp. cacuminis

Syzygium cacuminis subsp.

cacuminis, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves.

The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling small apples. The fruit contains a single seed and is valued for its nutritional content and use in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.
