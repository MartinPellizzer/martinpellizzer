Senecio kukul

Senecio kukul, commonly known as the Kukul groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters.

Senecio kukul is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
