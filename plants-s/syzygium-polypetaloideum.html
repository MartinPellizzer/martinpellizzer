Syzygium polypetaloideum

Syzygium polypetaloideum, commonly known as the white apple or white syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The fruit, when mature, is a small, round, and edible berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.
