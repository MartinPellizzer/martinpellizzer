Saussurea tatsienensis

Saussurea tatsienensis, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and western Sichuan in China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its fibrous roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
