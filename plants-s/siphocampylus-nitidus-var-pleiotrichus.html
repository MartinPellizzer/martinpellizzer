Siphocampylus nitidus var. pleiotrichus

Siphocampylus nitidus var. pleiotrichus, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine, white hairs.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.
