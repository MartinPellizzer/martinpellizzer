Syzygium longiflorum

Syzygium longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered apple or the white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor, often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Syzygium longiflorum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its economic and ecological significance in its native habitats.
