Schizonotus argenteus var. mexicanus

Schizonotus argenteus var. mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
