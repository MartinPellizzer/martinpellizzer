Spiranthes ramentacea

Spiranthes ramentacea, commonly known as the threadleaf ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a dense, spiral arrangement of pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It produces small, nectar-rich flowers that attract pollinators like bees and moths.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in colonies along stream banks and in swampy areas.
