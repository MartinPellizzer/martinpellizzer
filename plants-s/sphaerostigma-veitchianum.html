Sphaerostigma veitchianum

Sphaerostigma veitchianum, commonly known as the Veitch's globe flower, is a rare and striking member of the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical flower heads that resemble small globes, composed of numerous tiny, bell-shaped flowers. Native to the high-altitude regions of China, this plant thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically evergreen.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
