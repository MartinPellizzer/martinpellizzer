Saxifraga sponhemica

Saxifraga sponhemica, commonly known as the Sponheim saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found in crevices and on slopes where soil is shallow. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. Its small, white or pink flowers bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its rocky habitat.

Saxifraga sponhemica is adapted to harsh conditions, showcasing resilience and a unique botanical structure suited for its specific ecological niche.
