Syzygium marginatum

Syzygium marginatum, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a reddish margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Syzygium marginatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Syzygium genus.
