Silene conica

Silene conica, commonly known as the pointed carnation, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant produces distinctive, cone-shaped seed capsules that split open to release its seeds. Silene conica thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
