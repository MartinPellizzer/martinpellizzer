Stenolobium brachycarpum

Stenolobium brachycarpum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or cream in color. It produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
