Sinocrassula parvifoliana

Sinocrassula parvifoliana, commonly known as the small-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted for water storage. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the spring or early summer.

Sinocrassula parvifoliana is well-suited for rock gardens and xerophytic landscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
