Senecio gariepiensis

Senecio gariepiensis, commonly known as the Gariep daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
