Solidago neurolepis

Solidago neurolepis, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with hairy surfaces, and it blooms in late summer to early autumn.

Solidago neurolepis plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the flowering season.
