Solidago multiradiata subsp. paramuschirensis

Solidago multiradiata subsp.

paramuschirensis, commonly known as the Mexican goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
