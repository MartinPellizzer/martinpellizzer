Sophronitis bahiensis

Sophronitis bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its intricate and delicate floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small but striking, featuring a unique coloration pattern that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Due to habitat loss, it is critically threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
