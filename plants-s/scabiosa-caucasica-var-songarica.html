Scabiosa caucasica var. songarica

Scabiosa caucasica var. songarica, commonly known as the Caucasian scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, pink to purple flower heads that are typically arranged in clusters, resembling a cluster of tiny bells. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
