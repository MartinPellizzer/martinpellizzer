Scirpus × rosellinii

Scirpus × rosellinii, commonly known as the Rosell's bulrush, is a hybrid wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, triangular spikelets at the top of its stems. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus × rosellinii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
