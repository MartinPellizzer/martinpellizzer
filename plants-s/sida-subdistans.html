Sida subdistans

Sida subdistans, commonly known as the slender sida, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in coastal and wetland areas. The plant features small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually ovate with a hairy texture. Sida subdistans is known for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it a common species in coastal vegetation.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and adaptability to harsh coastal conditions.
