Selaginella caesia arborea

Selaginella caesia arborea, commonly known as the tree moss or climbing spikemoss, is a unique vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, often branched stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Selaginella caesia arborea is noted for its ability to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
