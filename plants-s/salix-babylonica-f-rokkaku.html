Salix babylonica f. rokkaku

Salix babylonica f. rokkaku, commonly known as the weeping willow with a cascading form, is a cultivated variant of the weeping willow.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a flowing, elegant appearance. The plant produces catkins and small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a silvery-green underside.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often planted near water features for its ornamental value.
