Scleria pratensis var. melanocarpa

Scleria pratensis var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-root sedgeland, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, rhizomes and its slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
