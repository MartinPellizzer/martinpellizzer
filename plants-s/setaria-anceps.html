Setaria anceps

Setaria anceps, commonly known as slender millet, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its seeds are often used as a staple food in some regions due to their nutritional value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its resilience and agricultural significance.
