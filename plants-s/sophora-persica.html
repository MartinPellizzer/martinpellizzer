Sophora persica

Sophora persica, commonly known as the Persian peony, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces flat, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Sophora persica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
