Sapranthus viridiflorus

Sapranthus viridiflorus, commonly known as the greenflower sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually greenish-white to pale blue, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Sapranthus viridiflorus is known for its resilience and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.
