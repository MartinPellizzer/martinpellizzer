Sebastiania brasiliensis f. microphylla

Sebastiania brasiliensis f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf sebastiania, is a compact shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in understory layers of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is appreciated in horticulture and conservation efforts.
