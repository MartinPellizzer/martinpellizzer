Salvia mexiae

Salvia mexiae, commonly known as Mexican sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.
