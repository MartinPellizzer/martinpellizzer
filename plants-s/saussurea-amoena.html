Saussurea amoena

Saussurea amoena, commonly known as the white saffron or Himalayan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white to pale yellow ray florets. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic, and sometimes pungent scent, particularly in its flowers. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
