Spiraea atemnophylla

Spiraea atemnophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically arranged oppositely along the stems. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
