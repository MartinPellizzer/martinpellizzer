Scilla princeps

Scilla princeps, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its bulbs are also used in traditional medicine.
