Salix × myrtoides

Salix × myrtoides, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a hybrid species within the willow genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that hang in long, drooping streams. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollination. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a smooth, dark green upper surface.

Salix × myrtoides thrives in moist environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.
