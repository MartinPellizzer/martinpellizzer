Solanum pterospermum

Solanum pterospermum, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Solanum pterospermum is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive foliage.
