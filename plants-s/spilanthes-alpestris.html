Spilanthes alpestris

Spilanthes alpestris, commonly known as the alpine everlasting or mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Spilanthes alpestris thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
