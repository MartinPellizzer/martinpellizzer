Spodiopogon tenuis

Spodiopogon tenuis, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a monocot herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense colonies in marshes and pond margins. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, with seeds that are easily carried by wind or water.

Spodiopogon tenuis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
