Syzygium leptostemon

Syzygium leptostemon, commonly known as the slender-stemmed apple.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. Syzygium leptostemon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
