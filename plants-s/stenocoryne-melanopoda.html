Stenocoryne melanopoda

Stenocoryne melanopoda, commonly known as the black-stemmed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its fruit is a small, wind-dispersed seed with a pappus, aiding in its propagation.

The species is notable for its resilience and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
