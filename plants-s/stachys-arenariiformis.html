Stachys arenariiformis

Stachys arenariiformis, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in spikes along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Stachys arenariiformis is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
