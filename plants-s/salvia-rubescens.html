Salvia rubescens

Salvia rubescens, commonly known as scarlet sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking red or purplish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Salvia rubescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
