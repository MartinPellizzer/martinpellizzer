Stanhopea maculosa

Stanhopea maculosa, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature a mix of white, purple, and pink hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Stanhopea maculosa is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
