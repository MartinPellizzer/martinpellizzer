Spergularia nicaeensis var. hispanica

Spergularia nicaeensis var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish sandspurry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces pink or red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring and early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments.

This variety is native to southern Europe and is adapted to arid and saline conditions.
