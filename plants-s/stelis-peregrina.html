Stelis peregrina

Stelis peregrina, commonly known as the wandering orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, which is essential for its survival in its natural habitat.

Stelis peregrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
