Silene bipartita var. canescens

Silene bipartita var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy silene, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-parted petals and hairy, often bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
