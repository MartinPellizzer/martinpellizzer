Solidago velutina var. nevadensis

Solidago velutina var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, velvety hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a similar fuzzy texture.

It produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, contributing to its role in supporting pollinators.
