Senecio lautus subsp. lanceolatus

Senecio lautus subsp.

lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are lanceolate, with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
