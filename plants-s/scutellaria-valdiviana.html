Scutellaria valdiviana

Scutellaria valdiviana, commonly known as Valdivian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Scutellaria valdiviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
