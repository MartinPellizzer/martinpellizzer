Sedum rupestre var. arrigens

Sedum rupestre var. arrigens, commonly known as the Blue Spruce Sedum, is a succulent plant native to rocky habitats in the western United States.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are densely packed. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
