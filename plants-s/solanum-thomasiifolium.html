Solanum thomasiifolium

Solanum thomasiifolium, commonly known as the Thomasiifolium nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, violet flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a yellow, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is not typically cultivated for food or ornamental purposes.
