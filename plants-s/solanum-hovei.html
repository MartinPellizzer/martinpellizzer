Solanum hovei

Solanum hovei, commonly known as the Hove's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, spherical berries that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
