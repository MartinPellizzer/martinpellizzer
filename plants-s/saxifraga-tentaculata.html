Saxifraga tentaculata

Saxifraga tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Saxifraga tentaculata is often found in mountainous areas and is a hardy species that can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
