Stanhopea aurata

Stanhopea aurata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a sweet, spicy fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal climates.

Stanhopea aurata is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral characteristics.
