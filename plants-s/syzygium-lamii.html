Syzygium lamii

Syzygium lamii, commonly known as the red lily or Chinese lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are capsule-like and contain several seeds.

Syzygium lamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
