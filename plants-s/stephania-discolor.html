Stephania discolor

Stephania discolor, commonly known as the Chinese herb "Fupi," is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, palmate-shaped fruit that contains seeds. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
