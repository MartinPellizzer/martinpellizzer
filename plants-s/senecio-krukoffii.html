Senecio krukoffii

Senecio krukoffii, commonly known as the Krukoff's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
