Spiraea japonica var. morrisonicola

Spiraea japonica var. morrisonicola, commonly known as the Morrison's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, arching branches and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.
