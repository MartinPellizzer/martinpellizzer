Sorbus aria var. buekkensis

Sorbus aria var. buekkensis, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a rare variant of the whitebeam tree native to Hungary.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red berries. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated edge and turn a striking red or orange in the fall. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
