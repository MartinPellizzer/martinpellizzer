Serapias orientalis subsp. orientalis

Serapias orientalis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Marsh Gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, calcareous soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is part of the Gentianaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
