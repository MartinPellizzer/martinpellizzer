Sinningia lineata

Sinningia lineata, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its tubular, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Sinningia lineata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant flowers and compact size.
