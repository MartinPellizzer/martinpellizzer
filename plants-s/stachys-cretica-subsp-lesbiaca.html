Stachys cretica subsp. lesbiaca

Stachys cretica subsp.

lesbiaca, commonly known as the Lesser Betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
