Surenus febrifuga

Surenus febrifuga, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves contain compounds such as parthenolide, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Surenus febrifuga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
