Synaedrys wrayi

Synaedrys wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Synaedrys wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
