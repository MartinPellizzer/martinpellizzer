Sida linearis

Sida linearis, commonly known as the slender sida, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer or early autumn. Sida linearis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
