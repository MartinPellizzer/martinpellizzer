Sarcanthus scolopendrifolius

Sarcanthus scolopendrifolius, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling coral in appearance. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark and pinnate leaves that are compound and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Sarcanthus scolopendrifolius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
