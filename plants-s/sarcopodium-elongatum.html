Sarcopodium elongatum

Sarcopodium elongatum, commonly known as the elongated root plant, is a unique member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and striking, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spathe, a common feature among arums. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unusual appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
