Symplocos prunifolia

Symplocos prunifolia, commonly known as the cherryleaf alder, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
