Scorzonera montana var. boetica

Scorzonera montana var. boetica, commonly known as the Alpine scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a robust root system that can grow up to 30 cm in length, which is often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh alpine environment.

Scorzonera montana var. boetica is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
