Sisymbrium adenophorum

Sisymbrium adenophorum, commonly known as hoary salsify, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust taproot and erect, branched stems covered in dense white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

Sisymbrium adenophorum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
