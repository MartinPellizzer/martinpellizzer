Scleromitrion tetrandrum

Scleromitrion tetrandrum, commonly known as the four-stamen rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces four stamens in each flower, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Scleromitrion tetrandrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
