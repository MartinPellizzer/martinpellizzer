Sison fasciculatum

Sison fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered sison, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
