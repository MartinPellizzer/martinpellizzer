Scrophularia kurbanovii

Scrophularia kurbanovii, commonly known as the Kurbanov's figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers. It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.

Scrophularia kurbanovii is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
