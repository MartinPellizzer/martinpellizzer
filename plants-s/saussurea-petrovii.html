Saussurea petrovii

Saussurea petrovii, commonly known as the Karakoram saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, adapting to cold and arid conditions. Saussurea petrovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
