Scrophularia kotschyana

Scrophularia kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, hairy stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Scrophularia kotschyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
