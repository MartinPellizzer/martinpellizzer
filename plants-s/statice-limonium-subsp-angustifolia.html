Statice limonium subsp. angustifolia

Statice limonium subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and spikes of small, showy blue or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often growing in dunes or rocky coastal areas. It is a perennial species that blooms from late summer to early autumn.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for coastal gardens and floral arrangements.
