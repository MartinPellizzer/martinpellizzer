Silene rigidula

Silene rigidula, commonly known as the rigid catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Silene rigidula thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
