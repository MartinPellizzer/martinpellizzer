Sorbus yesoensis

Sorbus yesoensis, commonly known as the Yeso whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Korean Peninsula and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
