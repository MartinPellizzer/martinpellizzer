Silene bipartita var. angustifolia

Silene bipartita var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf twinflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene bipartita var. angustifolia is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
