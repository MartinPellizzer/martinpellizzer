Senna bacillaris var. benthamiana

Senna bacillaris var. benthamiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
