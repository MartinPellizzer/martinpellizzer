Scilla monophyllos f. albiflora

Scilla monophyllos f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered scilla, is a botanical variant of the scilla genus.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with the typical blue or violet hues of the species. This perennial plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils. It typically blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch to gardens and natural landscapes.

The plant's foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.
