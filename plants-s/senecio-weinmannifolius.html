Senecio weinmannifolius

Senecio weinmannifolius, commonly known as the Weinmannia-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
