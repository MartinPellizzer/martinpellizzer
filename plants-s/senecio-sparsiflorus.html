Senecio sparsiflorus

Senecio sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which are small and yellow, appear in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.
