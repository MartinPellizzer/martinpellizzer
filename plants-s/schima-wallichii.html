Schima wallichii

Schima wallichii, commonly known as the Chinese teak or Indian teak, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant has elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. Schima wallichii is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

It also plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
