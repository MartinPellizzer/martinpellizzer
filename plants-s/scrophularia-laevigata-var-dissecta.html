Scrophularia laevigata var. dissecta

Scrophularia laevigata var. dissecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
