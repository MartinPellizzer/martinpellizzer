Stachys subcrenata subsp. labiosa

Stachys subcrenata subsp.

labiosa, commonly known as the pink hedge nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate with a slightly wavy margin.

The plant produces small, pale pink to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
