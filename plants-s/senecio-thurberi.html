Senecio thurberi

Senecio thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

The leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the plant is generally drought-tolerant.
