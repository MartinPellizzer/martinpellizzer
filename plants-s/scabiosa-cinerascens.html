Scabiosa cinerascens

Scabiosa cinerascens, commonly known as the silver scabious, is a flowering plant native to central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pale blue to almost white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are oval in shape and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa cinerascens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
