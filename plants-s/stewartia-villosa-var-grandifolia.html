Stewartia villosa var. grandifolia

Stewartia villosa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered stewartia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
