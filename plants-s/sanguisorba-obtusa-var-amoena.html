Sanguisorba obtusa var. amoena

Sanguisorba obtusa var. amoena, commonly known as the pink false spirea, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to rose-colored flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, slender inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
