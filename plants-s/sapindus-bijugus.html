Sapindus bijugus

Sapindus bijugus, commonly known as the Indian soapberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a starchy, edible seed. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
