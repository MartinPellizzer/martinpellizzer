Scilla asperifolia

Scilla asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy texture on the leaves and stems, which gives it its specific epithet "asperifolia.". It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Scilla asperifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early blooming habit.
