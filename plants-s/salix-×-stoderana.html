Salix × stoderana

Salix × stoderana, commonly known as the weeping willow hybrid, is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is a natural hybrid between Salix alba (white willow) and Salix triandra (cottonwood). The plant is notable for its finely toothed, light green leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
