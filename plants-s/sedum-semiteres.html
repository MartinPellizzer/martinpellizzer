Sedum semiteres

Sedum semiteres, commonly known as the narrowleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Sedum semiteres is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
