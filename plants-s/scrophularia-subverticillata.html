Scrophularia subverticillata

Scrophularia subverticillata, commonly known as the clustered figwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers with two lips, typically in shades of pink or purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Scrophularia subverticillata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
