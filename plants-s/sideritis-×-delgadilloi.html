Sideritis × delgadilloi

Sideritis × delgadilloi, commonly known as the Delgadillo sainfoin, is a hybrid shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in Mediterranean climates.
