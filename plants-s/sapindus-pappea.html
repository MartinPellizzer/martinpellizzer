Sapindus pappea

Sapindus pappea, commonly known as the Indian soapnut tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces fruits that are covered in a thick, soapy coating, which is used in traditional soap-making. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental features and ecological benefits.
