Sterculia fulgens

Sterculia fulgens, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are large, simple, and alternate, often with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The tree produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
