Scaphosepalum ophidion

Scaphosepalum ophidion, commonly known as the snake orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, serpentine-shaped flowers. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and lanceolate leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Scaphosepalum ophidion is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
