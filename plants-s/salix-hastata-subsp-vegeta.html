Salix hastata subsp. vegeta

Salix hastata subsp.

vegeta, commonly known as the green willow, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along streambanks and in wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration projects.
