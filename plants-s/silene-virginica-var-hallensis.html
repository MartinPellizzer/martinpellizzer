Silene virginica var. hallensis

Silene virginica var. hallensis, commonly known as the Hall's catchfly, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Silene virginica var. hallensis is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
