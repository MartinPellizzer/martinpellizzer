Stelis jaramilloi

Stelis jaramilloi, commonly known as the Jaramillo's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant, typically found growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, a characteristic common among epiphytic orchids.

Stelis jaramilloi is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
