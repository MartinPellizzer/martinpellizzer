Solidago graminifolia var. polycephala

Solidago graminifolia var. polycephala, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces numerous small, yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.
