Scorzonera graminifolia var. obtusisquama

Scorzonera graminifolia var. obtusisquama, commonly known as the woolly scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of thick, woolly, and often bluish-gray leaves that are broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in a loose inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and flowers.
