Sabicea asperula

Sabicea asperula, commonly known as the roughleaf sabicea, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
