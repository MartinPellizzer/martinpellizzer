Salvia arabica

Salvia arabica, commonly known as the Arabian sage, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valued ornamental plant in xeriscaping.
