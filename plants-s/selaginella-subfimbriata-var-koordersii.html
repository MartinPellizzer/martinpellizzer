Selaginella subfimbriata var. koordersii

Selaginella subfimbriata var. koordersii, commonly known as the Koorders' spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial fern ally that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has slender, creeping stems with whorled leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are borne on the upper parts of the stems.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
