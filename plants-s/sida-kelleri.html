Sida kelleri

Sida kelleri, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual or perennial herb with hairy, succulent stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from summer to fall. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Sida kelleri is often found in desert scrub and along rocky slopes.
