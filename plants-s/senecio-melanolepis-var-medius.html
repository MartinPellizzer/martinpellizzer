Senecio melanolepis var. medius

Senecio melanolepis var. medius, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, daisy-like yellow flowers and dark green, velvety leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun. It produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
