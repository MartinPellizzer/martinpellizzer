Skimmia oblata

Skimmia oblata, commonly known as Japanese skimmia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is known for its ornamental value, with showy red berries appearing in late summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Skimmia oblata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
