Saussurea pubifolia var. pubifolia

Saussurea pubifolia var. pubifolia, commonly known as the woolly safflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
