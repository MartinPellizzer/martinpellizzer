Sagittaria calycina var. grandis

Sagittaria calycina var. grandis, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with submerged leaves that are narrower and more elongated. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, often appearing above the water.

Sagittaria calycina var. grandis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
