Sasa bellatula

Sasa bellatula, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or dwarf bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, arching culms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its rhizomes form dense mats that help prevent soil erosion. Sasa bellatula is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in forest understories.
