Scaevola fastigiata

Scaevola fastigiata, commonly known as the upright fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant has narrow, upright leaves that give it a neat, tidy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Scaevola fastigiata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
