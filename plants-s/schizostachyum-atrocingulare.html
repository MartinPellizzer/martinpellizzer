Schizostachyum atrocingulare

Schizostachyum atrocingulare, commonly known as black-streaked bamboo, is a tropical grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that often exhibit dark streaks along the surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Schizostachyum atrocingulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its elegant appearance.
