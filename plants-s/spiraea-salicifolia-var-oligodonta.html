Spiraea salicifolia var. oligodonta

Spiraea salicifolia var. oligodonta, commonly known as the narrowleaf weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching branches that bear small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a fine, silvery-white underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This variety is valued for its graceful appearance and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
