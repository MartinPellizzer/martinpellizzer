Solanum lasiocladum

Solanum lasiocladum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its glandular-hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum lasiocladum is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
