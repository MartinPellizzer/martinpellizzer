Spiesia lasiopoda

Spiesia lasiopoda, commonly known as the roughleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, giving it its common name.

Spiesia lasiopoda is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
