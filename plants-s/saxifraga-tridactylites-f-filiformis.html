Saxifraga tridactylites f. filiformis

Saxifraga tridactylites f. filiformis, commonly known as the filiform saxifrage, is a rare alpine plant characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is typically found in rocky, high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its growth form is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
