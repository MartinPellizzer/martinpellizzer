Styrax faberi var. formosanus

Styrax faberi var. formosanus, commonly known as the Formosan frangipani, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and provide a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The wood of this species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional crafts and medicine.
