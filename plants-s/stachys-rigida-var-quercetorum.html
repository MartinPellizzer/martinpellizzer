Stachys rigida var. quercetorum

Stachys rigida var. quercetorum, commonly known as oakleaf hedge-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, oak-like leaves that are deeply lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
