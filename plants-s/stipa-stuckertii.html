Stipa stuckertii

Stipa stuckertii, commonly known as the Stuckert's feathergrass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted clumps of silver-green foliage and delicate, airy inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Stipa stuckertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
