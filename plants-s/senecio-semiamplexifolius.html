Senecio semiamplexifolius

Senecio semiamplexifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are semi-amplexicaul, meaning they partially clasping the stem. It produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
