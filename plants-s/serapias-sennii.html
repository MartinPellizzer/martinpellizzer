Serapias sennii

Serapias sennii, commonly known as the Sicilian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble orchids. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually yellow with red or orange markings, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Serapias sennii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
