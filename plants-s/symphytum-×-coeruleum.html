Symphytum × coeruleum

Symphytum × coeruleum, commonly known as the blue burr or blue globemallow, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that turn brown as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.

Symphytum × coeruleum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
