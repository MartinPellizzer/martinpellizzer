Sideritis trojana

Sideritis trojana, commonly known as Trojan sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Sideritis trojana is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
