Senecio jacobaea subvar. flosculus

Senecio jacobaea subvar. flosculus, commonly known as the lesser ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of blue-green leaves and yellow flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings and can be harmful to livestock.
