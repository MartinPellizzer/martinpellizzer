Solanum ciliatum

Solanum ciliatum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, fleshy berries. Solanum ciliatum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
