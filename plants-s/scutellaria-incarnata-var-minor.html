Scutellaria incarnata var. minor

Scutellaria incarnata var. minor, commonly known as bluebeard, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom from June to September. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its resemblance to the genus Scutellaria, characterized by its unique seed structure.
