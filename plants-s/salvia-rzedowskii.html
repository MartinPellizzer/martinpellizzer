Salvia rzedowskii

Salvia rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to the Sierra Madre mountains in Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia rzedowskii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to arid environments.
