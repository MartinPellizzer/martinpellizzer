Sedum japonicum subsp. boninense

Sedum japonicum subsp.

boninense, commonly known as the Bonin stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Bonin Islands. It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showing tolerance to salt spray and well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its drought resistance and ability to thrive in marginal conditions.
