Sempervivum tectorum subsp. cebennense

Sempervivum tectorum subsp.

cebennense, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to retain water in harsh, rocky environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
