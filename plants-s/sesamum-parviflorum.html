Sesamum parviflorum

Sesamum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered sesame, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, dark brown seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and is also used in traditional medicine.

Sesamum parviflorum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.
