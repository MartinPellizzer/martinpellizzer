Silene reeseana

Silene reeseana, commonly known as the Reese's rockpink, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, basal leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Silene reeseana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
