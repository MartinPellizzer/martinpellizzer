Sideranthus australis

Sideranthus australis, commonly known as the Australian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Sideranthus australis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
