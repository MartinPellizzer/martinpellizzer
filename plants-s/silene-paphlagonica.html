Silene paphlagonica

Silene paphlagonica, commonly known as the Paphlagonian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Silene paphlagonica is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
