Selago hyssopifolia

Selago hyssopifolia, commonly known as the mountain sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Selago hyssopifolia is adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
