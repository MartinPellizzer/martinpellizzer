Sisymbrium glaciale

Sisymbrium glaciale, commonly known as glacier spinach, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky soils. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a nutritious wild vegetable.
