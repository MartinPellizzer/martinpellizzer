Solanum eremophilum

Solanum eremophilum, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant produces purple to black berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and heat resistance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
