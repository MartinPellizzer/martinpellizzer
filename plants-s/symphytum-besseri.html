Symphytum besseri

Symphytum besseri, commonly known as Bessera comfrey, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy violet or blue flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
