Solanum crepinii

Solanum crepinii, commonly known as the Crepin's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by dark purple or black berries. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Solanum crepinii belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
