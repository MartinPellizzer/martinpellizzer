Schleichera ptychocarpa

Schleichera ptychocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

Schleichera ptychocarpa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in non-native regions.
