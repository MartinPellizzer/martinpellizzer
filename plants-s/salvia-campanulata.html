Salvia campanulata

Salvia campanulata, commonly known as the bell sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and valuable to pollinators.
