Salvia linearis

Salvia linearis, commonly known as narrowleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of violet to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Salvia linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid environments.
