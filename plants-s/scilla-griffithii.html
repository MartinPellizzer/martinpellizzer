Scilla griffithii

Scilla griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's scilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in early spring, which bloom on slender, leafless stems. Its leaves are basal, grass-like, and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Scilla griffithii is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty and early blooming habit.
