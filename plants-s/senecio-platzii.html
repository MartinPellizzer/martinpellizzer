Senecio platzii

Senecio platzii, commonly known as the Klein's groundsel, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are borne on tall, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
