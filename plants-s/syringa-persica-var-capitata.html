Syringa persica var. capitata

Syringa persica var. capitata, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in dense, clustered inflorescences, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath, and it produces small, woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
