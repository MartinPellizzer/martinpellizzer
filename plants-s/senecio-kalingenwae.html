Senecio kalingenwae

Senecio kalingenwae, commonly known as the Kalingenwae groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.
