Sedum paradisum subsp. paradisum

Sedum paradisum subsp.

paradisum, commonly known as the "Paradise stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand drought and moderate frost, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
