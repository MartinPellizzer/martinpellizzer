Solanum triflorum var. pyrethrifolium

Solanum triflorum var. pyrethrifolium, commonly known as the pyrethrum-leaved nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are compound and often have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming in late summer. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
