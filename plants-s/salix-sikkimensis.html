Salix sikkimensis

Salix sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically pubescent. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Salix sikkimensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
