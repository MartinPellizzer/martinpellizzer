Senecio basipinnatus

Senecio basipinnatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsels, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette formation.

Senecio basipinnatus is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
