Salvia nemorosa subsp. pseudosylvestris

Salvia nemorosa subsp.

pseudosylvestris, commonly known as woodland sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
