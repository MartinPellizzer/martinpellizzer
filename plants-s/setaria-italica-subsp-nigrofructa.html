Setaria italica subsp. nigrofructa

Setaria italica subsp.

nigrofructa, commonly known as black foxtail millet, is a wild grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and spikelets that produce small, dark brown grains.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is well-adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are small and hardy, making them suitable for cultivation in regions with limited water resources.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it an important forage crop and a potential candidate for sustainable agriculture.
