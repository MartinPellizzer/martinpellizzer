Salvia pinardii

Salvia pinardii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Salvia pinardii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
