Spiraea hypericifolia proles typica

Spiraea hypericifolia proles typica, commonly known as the common spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for landscaping and naturalized areas.
