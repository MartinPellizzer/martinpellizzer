Symplocos decora

Symplocos decora, commonly known as the showy silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, peeling bark and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are dark red when mature, often attracting birds and other wildlife. Symplocos decora thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
