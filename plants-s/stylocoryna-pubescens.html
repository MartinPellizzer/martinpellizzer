Stylocoryna pubescens

Stylocoryna pubescens, commonly known as the hairy stylocoryna, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Stylocoryna pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
