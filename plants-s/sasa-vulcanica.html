Sasa vulcanica

Sasa vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its robust, bamboo-like culms that grow in dense clumps. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green hue that reflects its adaptation to high-altitude conditions.

Sasa vulcanica plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various forest organisms.
