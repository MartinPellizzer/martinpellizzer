Solanum laurifrons

Solanum laurifrons, commonly known as the golden star solanum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its fruits are small, berry-like, and contain toxic alkaloids.

Solanum laurifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
