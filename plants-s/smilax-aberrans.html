Smilax aberrans

Smilax aberrans, commonly known as the Florida arrowwood, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Smilax aberrans plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
