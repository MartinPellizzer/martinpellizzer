Saxifraga lingulata var. australis

Saxifraga lingulata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Australia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially in cooler conditions. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
