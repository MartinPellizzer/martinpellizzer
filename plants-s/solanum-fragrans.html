Solanum fragrans

Solanum fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its attractive, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and glossy, while its flowers are usually white or pale purple with a strong, sweet scent.

Solanum fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
