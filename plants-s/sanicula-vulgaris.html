Sanicula vulgaris

Sanicula vulgaris, commonly known as common sanicle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, dark brown fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Sanicula vulgaris is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
