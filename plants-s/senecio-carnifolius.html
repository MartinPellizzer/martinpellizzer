Senecio carnifolius

Senecio carnifolius, commonly known as the butcher's broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems that resemble a broom, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Senecio carnifolius is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
