Salvia tovariensis

Salvia tovariensis, commonly known as Tovari's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic and slightly hairy, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Salvia tovariensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
