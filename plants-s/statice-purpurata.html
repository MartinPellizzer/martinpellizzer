Statice purpurata

Statice purpurata, commonly known as sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and well-drained soils. The plant produces dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its salt tolerance makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and rockeries.

Statice purpurata is also valued for its ornamental use in floral arrangements and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
