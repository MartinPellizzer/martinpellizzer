Sclerochiton sousae

Sclerochiton sousae, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
