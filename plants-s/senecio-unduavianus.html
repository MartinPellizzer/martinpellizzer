Senecio unduavianus

Senecio unduavianus, commonly known as the Unduavian ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, bright yellow ray florets surrounded by numerous tubular disc florets. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Senecio unduavianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
