Strongylodon

Strongylodon, commonly known as the "sugar cane plant," is a tropical perennial grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant is cultivated for its sweet, fibrous stalks, which are processed into sugar and other products. Strongylodon thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its biological features make it an important agricultural crop in many tropical regions.
