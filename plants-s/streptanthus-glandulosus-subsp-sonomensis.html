Streptanthus glandulosus subsp. sonomensis

Streptanthus glandulosus subsp.

sonomensis, commonly known as the Sonoma bladderpod, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure and small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a hairy texture.

This subspecies is native to California and is often found in the northern part of the state, including the Sonoma County area.
