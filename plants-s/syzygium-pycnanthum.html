Syzygium pycnanthum

Syzygium pycnanthum, commonly known as the red apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage and showy, clustered white flowers. The tree produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and pale in color.

Syzygium pycnanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
