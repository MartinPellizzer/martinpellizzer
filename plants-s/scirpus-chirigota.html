Scirpus chirigota

Scirpus chirigota, commonly known as the marsh arrowgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Scirpus chirigota thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
