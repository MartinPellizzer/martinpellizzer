Silene yetii var. herbilegorum

Silene yetii var. herbilegorum, commonly known as the Herbie's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cooler, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to other Silene species.
