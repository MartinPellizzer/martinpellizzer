Salvia bogotensis subsp. sochensis

Salvia bogotensis subsp.

sochensis, commonly known as the Sochensia salvia, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of Colombia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually violet to purple in color, with two-lipped corollas typical of the genus Salvia.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
