Senna italica

Senna italica, commonly known as Italian senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Senna italica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
