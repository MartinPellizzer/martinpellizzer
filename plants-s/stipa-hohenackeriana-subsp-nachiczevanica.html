Stipa hohenackeriana subsp. nachiczevanica

Stipa hohenackeriana subsp.

nachiczevanica, commonly known as the Armenian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and ecological significance in its native range.
