Setaria viridis subsp. nana

Setaria viridis subsp.

nana, commonly known as dwarf green millet, is a small annual grass species native to Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical features make it suitable for use in ornamental gardening and as a potential food crop in certain regions.
