Smilax latifolia

Smilax latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by spiny, red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax latifolia is known for its aggressive growth and is considered a common invasive species in some regions.
