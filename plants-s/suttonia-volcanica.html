Suttonia volcanica

Suttonia volcanica, commonly known as the volcanic suttonia, is a rare flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and robust, woody stems adapted to withstand harsh, nutrient-poor soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the dry season, relying on wind pollination due to the scarcity of pollinators in its habitat. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently in arid conditions.

Suttonia volcanica is considered an indicator species of volcanic soil ecosystems, reflecting the unique botanical adaptations required for survival in such environments.
