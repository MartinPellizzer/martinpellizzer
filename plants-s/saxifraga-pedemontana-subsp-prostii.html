Saxifraga pedemontana subsp. prostii

Saxifraga pedemontana subsp.

prostii, commonly known as the Alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to tolerate cold climates and is considered a hardy, resilient plant.
