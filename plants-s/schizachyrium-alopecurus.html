Schizachyrium alopecurus

Schizachyrium alopecurus, commonly known as eastern grass or hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, leaf-like blades that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like clusters of brownish or reddish flowers in the summer.

Schizachyrium alopecurus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
