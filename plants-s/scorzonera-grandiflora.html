Scorzonera grandiflora

Scorzonera grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust taproot system, which is often harvested for its edible and nutritious qualities. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Scorzonera grandiflora thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
