Spiraea fritschiana

Spiraea fritschiana, commonly known as the Fritsch's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It features clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, arching stems and serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

The botanical characteristics of Spiraea fritschiana make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and ecological habitats.
