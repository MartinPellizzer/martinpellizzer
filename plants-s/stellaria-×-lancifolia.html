Stellaria × lancifolia

Stellaria × lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that resemble stars, hence its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Stellaria × lancifolia is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cool, humid climates.
