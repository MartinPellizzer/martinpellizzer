Swertia bimaculata var. impunctata

Swertia bimaculata var. impunctata, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
