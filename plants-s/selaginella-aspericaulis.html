Selaginella aspericaulis

Selaginella aspericaulis, commonly known as the rough-leaved spikemoss, is a member of the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a sporophyte-dominated life cycle, with spores produced in specialized structures called strobila. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically rough and hairy, providing protection against desiccation.

Selaginella aspericaulis is often found in rocky crevices or sandy soils, showcasing its resilience and ecological adaptability.
