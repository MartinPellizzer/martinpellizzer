Saracha guttata

Saracha guttata, commonly known as the spotted saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with five petals. Native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
