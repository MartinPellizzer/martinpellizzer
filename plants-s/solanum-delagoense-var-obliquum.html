Solanum delagoense var. obliquum

Solanum delagoense var. obliquum, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

Its flowers are usually yellowish-green with a distinctive purple calyx, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans but may be used in traditional medicine.
