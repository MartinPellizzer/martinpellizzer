Senecio candidissimus

Senecio candidissimus, commonly known as the whiteleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in rocky, arid environments and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
