Spiranthes subfiliformis

Spiranthes subfiliformis, commonly known as the slender spiral orchid, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spiral arrangement.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and ecological importance.
