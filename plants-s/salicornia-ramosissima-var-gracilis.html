Salicornia ramosissima var. gracilis

Salicornia ramosissima var. gracilis, commonly known as slender glasswort, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments such as coastal marshes and salt marshes.

It is characterized by its thin, succulent stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various salt-tolerant organisms.

Its ability to accumulate salt makes it a valuable indicator species for monitoring environmental salinity levels.
