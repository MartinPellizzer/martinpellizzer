Solanum zahlbruckneri

Solanum zahlbruckneri, commonly known as the Zahlsbruckner's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, mountainous environments. The plant produces small, usually green or purple berries that contain toxic alkaloids, similar to other members of the Solanaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a prominent central stigma.
