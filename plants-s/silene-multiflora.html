Silene multiflora

Silene multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces numerous small, clustered flowers along its stems. It has hairy leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

Silene multiflora is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
