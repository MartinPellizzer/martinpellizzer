Scabiosa ustulata

Scabiosa ustulata, commonly known as the scorched scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Scabiosa ustulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
