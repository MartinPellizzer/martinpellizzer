Syzygium panayense

Syzygium panayense, commonly known as the Panay apple or white apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Panay in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, yellowish fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Syzygium panayense is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
