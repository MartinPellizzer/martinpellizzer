Scleria biflora subsp. ferruginea

Scleria biflora subsp.

ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence, which bears small, three-lobed flowers.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are often covered in a reddish-brown powder, giving it its common name.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
