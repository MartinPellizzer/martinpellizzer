Syzygium paniculatum

Syzygium paniculatum, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

Syzygium paniculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
