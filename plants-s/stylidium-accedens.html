Stylidium accedens

Stylidium accedens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sun-orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, sun-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often has a short, erect stem.

Stylidium accedens is a short-lived perennial, often flowering in spring and summer.
