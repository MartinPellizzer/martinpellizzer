Stenanthium rhombipetalum

Stenanthium rhombipetalum, commonly known as the rhombipetalum false gentian, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The flowers are arranged in clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
