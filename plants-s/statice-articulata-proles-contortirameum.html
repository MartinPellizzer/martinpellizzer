Statice articulata proles contortirameum

Statice articulata proles contortirameum, commonly known as the twisted-stem statice, is a rare and distinctive plant characterized by its unique, contorted stem structure.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to specific regions with arid or semi-arid climates. The plant features small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its foliage is narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Due to its rarity and unusual morphology, it is of particular interest to botanists and horticulturists.
