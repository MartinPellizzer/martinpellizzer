Siphocampylus umbellatus var. willdenowianus

Siphocampylus umbellatus var. willdenowianus, commonly known as the willdenow's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a slender, upright stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its classification within the Campanulaceae family, known for its ornamental and ecological significance.
