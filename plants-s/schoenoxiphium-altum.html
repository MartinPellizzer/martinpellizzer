Schoenoxiphium altum

Schoenoxiphium altum, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Schoenoxiphium altum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
