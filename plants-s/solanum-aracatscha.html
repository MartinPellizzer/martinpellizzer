Solanum aracatscha

Solanum aracatscha, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces small, yellow, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

Solanum aracatscha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
