Santolina minor

Santolina minor, commonly known as narrowleaf santolina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, aromatic foliage with silver-gray leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Santolina minor is also valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in landscaping.
