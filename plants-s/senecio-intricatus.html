Senecio intricatus

Senecio intricatus, commonly known as the intricate groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically narrow and linear. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Senecio intricatus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
