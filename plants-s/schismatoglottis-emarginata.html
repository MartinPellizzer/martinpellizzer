Schismatoglottis emarginata

Schismatoglottis emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate schismatoglottis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The flowers are usually white or pale green, with three sepals and three petals, and are often fragrant.
