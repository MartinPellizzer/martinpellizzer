Sorbus × kitaibeliana

Sorbus × kitaibeliana, commonly known as whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive autumn foliage.
