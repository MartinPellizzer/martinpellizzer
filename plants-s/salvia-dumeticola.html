Salvia dumeticola

Salvia dumeticola, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and medicinal properties. Salvia dumeticola thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
