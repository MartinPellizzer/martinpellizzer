Silene karkaralensis

Silene karkaralensis, commonly known as the Karkaralensis campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, often hairy stems and small, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate and linear, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene karkaralensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
