Spartothamnella puberula

Spartothamnella puberula, commonly known as the silky spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Spartothamnella puberula is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
