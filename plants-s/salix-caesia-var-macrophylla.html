Salix caesia var. macrophylla

Salix caesia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters typical of willow species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along stream banks and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
