Sertula besseriana

Sertula besseriana, commonly known as the Brazilian nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many species known for their stinging hairs. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Sertula besseriana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
