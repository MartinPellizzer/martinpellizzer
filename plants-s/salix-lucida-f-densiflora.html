Salix lucida f. densiflora

Salix lucida f. densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower willow, is a shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green catkins. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and wetlands.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
