Sabulina michauxii

Sabulina michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in open habitats such as dunes, prairies, and rocky outcrops. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern.

Sabulina michauxii is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
