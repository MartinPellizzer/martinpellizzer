Solanum chacoense var. latisectum

Solanum chacoense var. latisectum, commonly known as the Chaco false nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the dry regions of South America.

It features woody stems and lanceolate, often lobed leaves that are broad and segmented, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and succulent-like leaves.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
