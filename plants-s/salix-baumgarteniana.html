Salix baumgarteniana

Salix baumgarteniana, commonly known as the Baumann's willow, is a species of shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and its production of catkin-like flowers in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in mountainous regions.
