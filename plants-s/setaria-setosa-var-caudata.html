Setaria setosa var. caudata

Setaria setosa var. caudata, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and distinctive, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as marshes and wetlands, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
