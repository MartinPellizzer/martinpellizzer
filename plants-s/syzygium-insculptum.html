Syzygium insculptum

Syzygium insculptum, commonly known as the carved syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, ridged bark and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface with a slightly serrated margin.

Syzygium insculptum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
