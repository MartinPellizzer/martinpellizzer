Satureja cremnophila subsp. anatolica

Satureja cremnophila subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped berries.

The leaves are often used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine for their pungent, peppery aroma.
