Sesleria nitida var. intermedia

Sesleria nitida var. intermedia, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically arranged in loose spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated settings.
