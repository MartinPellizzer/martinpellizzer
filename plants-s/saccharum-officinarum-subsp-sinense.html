Saccharum officinarum subsp. sinense

Saccharum officinarum subsp.

sinense, commonly known as Chinese bamboo sugar cane, is a subspecies of the sugarcane plant native to southern China. It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stalks that can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

The plant produces a sweet, fibrous juice that is used for both culinary and industrial purposes. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and is often cultivated for its sugar content and ornamental appeal.
