Sida portoricensis

Sida portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Sida portoricensis plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
