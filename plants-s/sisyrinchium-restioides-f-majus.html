Sisyrinchium restioides f. majus

Sisyrinchium restioides f. majus, commonly known as the large-flowered blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like fruits known as caryopses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
