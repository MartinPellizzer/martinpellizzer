Swertia crassiuscula

Swertia crassiuscula, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is upright and branched.

Swertia crassiuscula is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
