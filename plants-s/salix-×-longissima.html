Salix × longissima

Salix × longissima, commonly known as weeping willow, is a hybrid species of willow tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green on top and pale underneath. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers and ponds.

Salix × longissima is widely cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks.
