Saxifraga macrostigma f. longipila

Saxifraga macrostigma f. longipila, commonly known as the long-stemmed saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of broad, fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wool. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in garden settings.
