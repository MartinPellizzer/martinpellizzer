Sideritis chamaedryoides

Sideritis chamaedryoides, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Sideritis chamaedryoides is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
