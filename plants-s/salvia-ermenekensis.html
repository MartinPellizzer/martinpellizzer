Salvia ermenekensis

Salvia ermenekensis, commonly known as the Ermenek sage, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia ermenekensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in sunny conditions.
