Sporobolus indicus var. andinus

Sporobolus indicus var. andinus, commonly known as Andean needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in arid regions.
