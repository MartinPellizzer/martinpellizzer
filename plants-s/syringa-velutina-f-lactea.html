Syringa velutina f. lactea

Syringa velutina f. lactea, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a cultivar of the sweetwood plant.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, rounded growth habit with dark green, glossy leaves. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value lies in its fragrant flowers and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens.
