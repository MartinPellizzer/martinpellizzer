Syringa amurensis var. pekinensis

Syringa amurensis var. pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet and has a rounded, compact form.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with some varieties developing fissures as the plant matures.
