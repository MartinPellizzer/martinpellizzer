Syzygium eugenioides

Syzygium eugenioides, commonly known as the wild cinnamon tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their distinctive scent.

Syzygium eugenioides thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
