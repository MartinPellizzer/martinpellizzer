Scabiosa atropurpurea subsp. ambigua

Scabiosa atropurpurea subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the alpine scabious, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
