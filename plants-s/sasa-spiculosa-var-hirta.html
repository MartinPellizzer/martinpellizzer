Sasa spiculosa var. hirta

Sasa spiculosa var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, wiry culms covered in fine, hairy structures. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slightly rough texture. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
