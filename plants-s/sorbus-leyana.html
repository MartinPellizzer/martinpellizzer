Sorbus leyana

Sorbus leyana, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
