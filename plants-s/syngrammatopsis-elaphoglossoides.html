Syngrammatopsis elaphoglossoides

Syngrammatopsis elaphoglossoides, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in strobila, a typical feature of ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Its delicate structure and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded gardens.
