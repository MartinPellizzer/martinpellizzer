Solidago virgaurea var. asiatica

Solidago virgaurea var. asiatica, commonly known as Asiatic goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in panicles during late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
