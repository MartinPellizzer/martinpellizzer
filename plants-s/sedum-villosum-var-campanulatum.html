Sedum villosum var. campanulatum

Sedum villosum var. campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to North America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
