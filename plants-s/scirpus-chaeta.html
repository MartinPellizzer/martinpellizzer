Scirpus chaeta

Scirpus chaeta, commonly known as tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and is often found in wetland habitats such as marshes and pond edges. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of greenish-brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense tussocks, contributing to soil stabilization in wet environments.

Scirpus chaeta is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
