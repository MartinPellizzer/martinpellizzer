Silene viridella

Silene viridella, commonly known as the green willow herb, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Silene viridella has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is a perennial species that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
