Staurogyne subglabra

Staurogyne subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often covered with small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces small, round fruits.

Staurogyne subglabra plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
