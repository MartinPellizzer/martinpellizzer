Salvia lophantha

Salvia lophantha, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces vibrant violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia lophantha thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in suitable climates.
