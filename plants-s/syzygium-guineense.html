Syzygium guineense

Syzygium guineense, commonly known as the African cherry or guineense apple, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Syzygium guineense is also valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction.
