Selaginella toppingii

Selaginella toppingii, commonly known as the Topping's spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinct appearance with slender, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Selaginella toppingii is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its resilience to periods of drought.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
