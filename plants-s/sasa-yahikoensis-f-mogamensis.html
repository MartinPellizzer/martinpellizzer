Sasa yahikoensis f. mogamensis

Sasa yahikoensis f. mogamensis, commonly known as the Mogami bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa yahikoensis species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, culm-like stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The Mogami bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Japanese gardening and landscaping.
