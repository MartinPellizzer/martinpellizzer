Solanum cinereum

Solanum cinereum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a dark blue or black color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open areas.

Solanum cinereum contains toxic alkaloids and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
