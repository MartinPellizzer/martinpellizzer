Senecio weddellianus

Senecio weddellianus, commonly known as the Weddell's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by cylindrical seed heads.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
