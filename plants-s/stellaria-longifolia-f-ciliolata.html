Stellaria longifolia f. ciliolata

Stellaria longifolia f. ciliolata, commonly known as the hairy starwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with ciliate margins that give the plant its distinctive appearance. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
