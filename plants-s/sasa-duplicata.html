Sasa duplicata

Sasa duplicata, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or black bamboo, is a clumping perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, dark green leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cylindrical, spike-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sasa duplicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
