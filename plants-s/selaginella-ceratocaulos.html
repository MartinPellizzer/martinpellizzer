Selaginella ceratocaulos

Selaginella ceratocaulos, commonly known as the horned spikemoss, is a member of the Selaginellaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with whorled leaves that are usually narrow and linear. Its sporophyte stage produces microsporangia and megasporangia on specialized leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Selaginella ceratocaulos is noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
