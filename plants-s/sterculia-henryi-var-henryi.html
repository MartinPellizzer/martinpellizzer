Sterculia henryi var. henryi

Sterculia henryi var. henryi, commonly known as Henry's sterculia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Myanmar.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and historical significance.
