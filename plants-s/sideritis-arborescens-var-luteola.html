Sideritis arborescens var. luteola

Sideritis arborescens var. luteola, commonly known as yellow mountain sedge, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features upright, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring.

This variety is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
