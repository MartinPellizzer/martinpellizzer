Solanum bansoense subsp. sanaganum

Solanum bansoense subsp.

sanaganum, commonly known as the Sanaganum nightshade, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet.

The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
