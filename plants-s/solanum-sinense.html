Solanum sinense

Solanum sinense, commonly known as Chinese nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries. The berries are typically red when mature and contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
