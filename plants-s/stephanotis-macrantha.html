Stephanotis macrantha

Stephanotis macrantha, commonly known as the sweet scent vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, climbing stems and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The flowers have a waxy texture and emit a sweet, vanilla-like fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly glossy appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant aroma.
