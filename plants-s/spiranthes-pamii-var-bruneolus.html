Spiranthes pamii var. bruneolus

Spiranthes pamii var. bruneolus, commonly known as the Bruneolus Ladies' Tresses, is a rare orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its intricate, spiral arrangement of pale green to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands, often forming dense clumps. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a distinct basal rosette structure.

The species is listed as threatened or endangered in several states due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
