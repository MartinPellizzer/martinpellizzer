Syringa × dubia

Syringa × dubia, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant.

Syringa × dubia prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
