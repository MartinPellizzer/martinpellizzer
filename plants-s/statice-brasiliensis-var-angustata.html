Statice brasiliensis var. angustata

Statice brasiliensis var. angustata, commonly known as Brazilian glass flower, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family, known for its striking, colorful bracts that resemble glass-like structures. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant colors.

The variety angustata is distinguished by its narrower leaves and more compact growth habit compared to other varieties of Statice.
