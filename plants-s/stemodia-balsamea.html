Stemodia balsamea

Stemodia balsamea, commonly known as balsam weed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It has slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
