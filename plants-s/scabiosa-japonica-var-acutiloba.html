Scabiosa japonica var. acutiloba

Scabiosa japonica var. acutiloba, commonly known as Japanese scabious, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
