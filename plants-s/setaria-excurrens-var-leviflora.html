Setaria excurrens var. leviflora

Setaria excurrens var. leviflora, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to various regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a flowering period that often occurs during the dry season.

It is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
