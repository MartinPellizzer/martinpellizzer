Senecio argentatus

Senecio argentatus, commonly known as the silver senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
