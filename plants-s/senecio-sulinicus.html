Senecio sulinicus

Senecio sulinicus, commonly known as the silver bush or blue ice plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features dense clusters of small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Senecio sulinicus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
