Silene antirrhina f. bicolor

Silene antirrhina f. bicolor, commonly known as the pink and white catchfly, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive bi-colored flowers.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, consist of five petals with a pinkish hue on the outer petals and white on the inner ones, creating a striking visual contrast.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance and ease of care.
