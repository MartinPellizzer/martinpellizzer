Spiraea tomentosa var. virgata

Spiraea tomentosa var. virgata, commonly known as the silver-leaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, rounded growth with small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its compact form and attractive foliage, which adds visual interest throughout the growing season.
