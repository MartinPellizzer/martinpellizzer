Syringa pinnatifolia

Syringa pinnatifolia, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white to lilac, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Syringa pinnatifolia is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
