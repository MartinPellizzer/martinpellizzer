Sarcaulus brasiliensis subsp. brasiliensis

Sarcaulus brasiliensis subsp.

brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically arranged in a raceme. It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation purposes.
