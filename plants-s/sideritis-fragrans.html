Sideritis fragrans

Sideritis fragrans, commonly known as sweet sideritis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it typically grows in rocky or dry soils.

Sideritis fragrans is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming and soothing properties.
