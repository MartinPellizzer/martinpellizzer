Scrophularia flava

Scrophularia flava, commonly known as the yellow figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, featuring yellow petals with a distinctive purple or brownish throat.

The plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
