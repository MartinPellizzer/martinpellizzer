Solanum inops

Solanum inops, commonly known as the ghost pepper plant, is a species of nightshade native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, pale green leaves and produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its fruit, which is a small, red, and highly spicy chili pepper with a unique, almost ghostly appearance. Solanum inops thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its pungent fruit used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Solanaceae family.
