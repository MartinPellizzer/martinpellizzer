Syringa vulgaris var. macrantha

Syringa vulgaris var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that are typically pale purple to white, arranged in clustered panicles. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental in gardens.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to landscapes.
