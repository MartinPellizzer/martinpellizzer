Sterculia frondosa

Sterculia frondosa, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a large, tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody, spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often growing in coastal or riverine habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
