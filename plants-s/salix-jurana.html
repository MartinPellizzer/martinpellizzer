Salix jurana

Salix jurana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, zigzagging stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, shiny surface. It produces catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

Salix jurana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
