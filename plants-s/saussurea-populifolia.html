Saussurea populifolia

Saussurea populifolia, commonly known as the poplar-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of a poplar tree, giving it its common name. It thrives in alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

Saussurea populifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
