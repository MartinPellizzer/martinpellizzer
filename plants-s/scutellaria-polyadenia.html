Scutellaria polyadenia

Scutellaria polyadenia, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its small, tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Scutellaria polyadenia is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
