Staebe cineraria

Staebe cineraria, commonly known as cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the family Asteraceae, also known as the daisy family, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Staebe cineraria prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
