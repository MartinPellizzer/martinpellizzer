Sabazia elata

Sabazia elata, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for warm, humid climates and it is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
