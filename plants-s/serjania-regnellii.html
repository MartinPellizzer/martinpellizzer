Serjania regnellii

Serjania regnellii, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or pink serjania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are simple and alternate, while its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Serjania regnellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
