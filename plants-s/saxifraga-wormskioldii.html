Saxifraga wormskioldii

Saxifraga wormskioldii, commonly known as the alpine saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, white or pinkish flowers in the summer months. It is well adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh weather conditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
