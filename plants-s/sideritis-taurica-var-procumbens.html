Sideritis taurica var. procumbens

Sideritis taurica var. procumbens, commonly known as Caucasian ironweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features hairy, branched stems that often lie prostrate on the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
