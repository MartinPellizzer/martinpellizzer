Setaria verticillata var. ambigua

Setaria verticillata var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural and riparian environments.
