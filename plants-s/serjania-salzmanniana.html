Serjania salzmanniana

Serjania salzmanniana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian white cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction.
