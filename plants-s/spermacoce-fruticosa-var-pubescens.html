Spermacoce fruticosa var. pubescens

Spermacoce fruticosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spurred nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough, pubescent texture.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
