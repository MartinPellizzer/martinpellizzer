Stephanium guianense

Stephanium guianense, commonly known as the Guianan stephanium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
