Streptachne tenuis

Streptachne tenuis, commonly known as slender flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in areas with high humidity and abundant water. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its seeds are often dispersed by water, contributing to its spread in wetland ecosystems.

Streptachne tenuis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
