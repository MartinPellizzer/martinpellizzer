Salvia kuznetzovii

Salvia kuznetzovii, commonly known as Kuznetsova's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, gray-green leaves and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid garden settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing features with other members of the sage genus.
