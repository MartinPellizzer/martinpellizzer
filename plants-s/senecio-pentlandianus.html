Senecio pentlandianus

Senecio pentlandianus, commonly known as the Pentland's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Senecio pentlandianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
