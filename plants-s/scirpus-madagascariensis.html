Scirpus madagascariensis

Scirpus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to submerged soil conditions. Its cylindrical, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Scirpus madagascariensis plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
