Scrophularia schmitzii

Scrophularia schmitzii, commonly known as Schmitz's figwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes many flowering plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

Scrophularia schmitzii is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves, which are often lance-shaped and have a rough texture.
