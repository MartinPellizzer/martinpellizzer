Sesbania keniensis

Sesbania keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan sesbania, is a leguminous plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with pinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia in its root nodules. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Sesbania keniensis is also valued for its role in soil enrichment and as a forage plant for livestock.
