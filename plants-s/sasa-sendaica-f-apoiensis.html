Sasa sendaica f. apoiensis

Sasa sendaica f. apoiensis, commonly known as the Apoiensis Sasa, is a variant of the Sasa genus native to East Asia.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features slender, hollow stems that form dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
