Silene badaroi

Silene badaroi, commonly known as the Kashmiri catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky soils at high altitudes. The plant features showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

Silene badaroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
