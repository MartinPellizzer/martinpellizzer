Symplocos ramosissima var. salwinensis

Symplocos ramosissima var. salwinensis, commonly known as the Salwin gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in subtropical and tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
