Specklinia podoglossa

Specklinia podoglossa, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Specklinia podoglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
