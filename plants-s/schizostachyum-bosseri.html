Schizostachyum bosseri

Schizostachyum bosseri, commonly known as the Bornean bamboo, is a tall, evergreen grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a dense, bushy form, contributing to its ornamental value. Schizostachyum bosseri is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is also valued for its use in construction and as a source of sustainable timber in its native regions.
