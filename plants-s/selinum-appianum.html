Selinum appianum

Selinum appianum, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to onions, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Selinum appianum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically for its purported health benefits.
