Senecio argunensis f. latifolius

Senecio argunensis f. latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved argun senecio, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
