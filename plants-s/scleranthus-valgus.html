Scleranthus valgus

Scleranthus valgus, commonly known as the bristly sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and sometimes hairy, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.

The plant has small, oval-shaped fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.
