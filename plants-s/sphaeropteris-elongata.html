Sphaeropteris elongata

Sphaeropteris elongata, commonly known as the elongated cyclosorus, is a species of fern belonging to the family Cyrtomium.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in circular patterns known as sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Sphaeropteris elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
