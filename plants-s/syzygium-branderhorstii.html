Syzygium branderhorstii

Syzygium branderhorstii, commonly known as the Brandehorst tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically yellow when ripe, and contains a single seed. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
