Sapium pallens

Sapium pallens, commonly known as the white senna, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge.

Sapium pallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
