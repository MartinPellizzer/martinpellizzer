Salvia ceratophylla

Salvia ceratophylla, commonly known as the wooly blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-white fuzzy foliage and spikes of blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia ceratophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
