Scilla richardiana

Scilla richardiana, commonly known as Richard's scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of striking blue or violet flowers in early spring, typically before the leaves emerge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Scilla richardiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
