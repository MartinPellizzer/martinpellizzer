Solanum deppei

Solanum deppei, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Solanum deppei produces small, yellow berries that contain the seeds, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
