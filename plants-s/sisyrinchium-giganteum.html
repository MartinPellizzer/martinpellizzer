Sisyrinchium giganteum

Sisyrinchium giganteum, commonly known as the giant blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, blue to violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is characterized by its compact stature and is often used in native plant gardens.
