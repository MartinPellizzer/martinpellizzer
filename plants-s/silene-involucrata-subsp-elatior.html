Silene involucrata subsp. elatior

Silene involucrata subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the tall sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branched.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
