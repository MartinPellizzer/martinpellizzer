Steenhammera sibirica

Steenhammera sibirica, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear showy blue to purple flowers resembling bells, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine beds.

Steenhammera sibirica is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh climates.
