Santiria nervosa

Santiria nervosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, oblong seeds with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Santiria nervosa is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
