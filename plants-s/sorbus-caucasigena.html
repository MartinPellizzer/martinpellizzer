Sorbus caucasigena

Sorbus caucasigena, commonly known as the Caucasian rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Caucasus region, including parts of Georgia, Armenia, and Azerbaijan. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters and its bright red berries that mature in the fall. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

It typically grows in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
