Stizolobium monospermum

Stizolobium monospermum, commonly known as the African milkwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in providing habitat for wildlife.
