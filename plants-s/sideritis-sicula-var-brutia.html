Sideritis sicula var. brutia

Sideritis sicula var. brutia, commonly known as Sicilian ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sideritis species are often valued for their medicinal properties, and this variety is no exception.
