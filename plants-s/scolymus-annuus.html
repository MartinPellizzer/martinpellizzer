Scolymus annuus

Scolymus annuus, commonly known as the common scorzonera, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust, branching stem that often grows up to 1 meter in height, covered with stiff, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to September. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, which is edible and has been used in traditional cuisine.

Scolymus annuus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
