Saussurea grandifolia f. microcephala

Saussurea grandifolia f. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed safflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy stems and small, yellowish-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
