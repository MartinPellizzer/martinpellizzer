Sabina dealbata

Sabina dealbata, commonly known as the white cedar or Australian white cedar, is a member of the Cupressaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia. The plant is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are closely pressed together, giving it a dense, compact appearance. Its bark is typically reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in its natural habitat.
