Sideritis augustini

Sideritis augustini, commonly known as Augustini's ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, and Sideritis augustini is no exception, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
