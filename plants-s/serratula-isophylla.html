Serratula isophylla

Serratula isophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppes and semi-desert environments.

Serratula isophylla is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
