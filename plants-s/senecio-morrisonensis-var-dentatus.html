Senecio morrisonensis var. dentatus

Senecio morrisonensis var. dentatus, commonly known as the Morrison's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
