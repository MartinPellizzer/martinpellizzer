Scabiosa tomentosa var. arundana

Scabiosa tomentosa var. arundana, commonly known as the woolly scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy, gray-green foliage that helps it retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
