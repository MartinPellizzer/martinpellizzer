Smilax oldhamii var. daibuensis

Smilax oldhamii var. daibuensis, commonly known as the Daibu arrowroot or Daibu smilax, is a flowering plant species native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinctive yellowish underside.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
