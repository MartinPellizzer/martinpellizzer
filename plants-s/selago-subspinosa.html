Selago subspinosa

Selago subspinosa, commonly known as the spiny selago, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Restionaceae and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in winter. Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
