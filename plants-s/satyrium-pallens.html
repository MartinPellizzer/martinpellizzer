Satyrium pallens

Satyrium pallens, commonly known as the pale lichen orchid, is a rare and unique plant found primarily in the southern United States.

It is a type of lichen, which is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and an alga or cyanobacterium. The plant thrives in specific microhabitats, often on rock surfaces or tree trunks in humid environments. Its botanical structure is characterized by a thallus, which is the body of the lichen, and it lacks the typical root, stem, and leaf structures of vascular plants.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Satyrium pallens is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
