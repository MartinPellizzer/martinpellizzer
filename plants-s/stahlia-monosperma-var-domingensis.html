Stahlia monosperma var. domingensis

Stahlia monosperma var. domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican stahlia, is a rare tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of the Dominican Republic.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, and often spirally arranged leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the dry season. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
