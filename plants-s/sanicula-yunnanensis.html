Sanicula yunnanensis

Sanicula yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan sanicula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant features a stout, hairy stem that is often branched and bears opposite, pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, fern-like appearance. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

The roots of Sanicula yunnanensis are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
