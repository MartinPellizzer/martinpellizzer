Serratula furcata

Serratula furcata, commonly known as the forked sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and fields. Its leaves are serrated and have a distinctive forked shape, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Serratula furcata is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
