Syzygium micrandrum

Syzygium micrandrum, commonly known as the large-fruited tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods containing edible, sour-tasting seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Syzygium micrandrum thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
