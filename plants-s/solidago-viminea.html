Solidago viminea

Solidago viminea, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are linear with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Solidago viminea is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and roadside ditches.
