Syzygium mouanum

Syzygium mouanum, commonly known as the Cameroon apple or African star apple, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into edible, round fruits with a yellow or green skin and a white, juicy pulp.

Syzygium mouanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
