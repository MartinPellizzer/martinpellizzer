Spathiphyllum cuspidatum

Spathiphyllum cuspidatum, commonly known as the saddleleaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green underside, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and air-purifying qualities.
