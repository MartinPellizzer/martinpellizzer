Syzygium laosense

Syzygium laosense, commonly known as the Laos apple or wild apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a crisp, juicy texture and a mild, sweet flavor. Syzygium laosense thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, though it is less commonly cultivated than other Syzygium species.
