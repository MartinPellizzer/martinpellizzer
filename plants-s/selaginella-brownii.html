Selaginella brownii

Selaginella brownii, commonly known as the Brown's spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant in the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with upright, spirally arranged leaves that are often bright green and covered in fine, scale-like structures. It produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are usually found at the tips of its stems.

Selaginella brownii is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and adaptive species.
