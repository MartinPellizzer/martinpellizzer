Silene konuralpii

Silene konuralpii, commonly known as the Konuralp's campion, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and slender stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the summer months.

Silene konuralpii is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
