Stenocactus vaupelianus

Stenocactus vaupelianus, commonly known as the Vaupelian's stenocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Brazil.

It is a solitary plant with a short, cylindrical stem that is often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warm season, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for collectors and desert gardeners.
