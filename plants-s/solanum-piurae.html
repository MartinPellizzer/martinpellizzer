Solanum piurae

Solanum piurae, commonly known as the Piura nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically dark purple when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. Solanum piurae is adapted to grow in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or arid soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
