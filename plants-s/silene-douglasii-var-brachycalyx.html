Silene douglasii var. brachycalyx

Silene douglasii var. brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, and it has a compact growth habit.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to arid environments.
