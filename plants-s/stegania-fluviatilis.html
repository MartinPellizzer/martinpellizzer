Stegania fluviatilis

Stegania fluviatilis, commonly known as the river stegania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in dense inflorescences. Stegania fluviatilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and streams.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a valuable species for ecological studies.
