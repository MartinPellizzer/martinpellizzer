Sorbus cibagouensis

Sorbus cibagouensis, commonly known as the Cibagou whitebeams, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cooler, temperate climates.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.
