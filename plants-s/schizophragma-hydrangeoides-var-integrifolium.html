Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. integrifolium

Schizophragma hydrangeoides var. integrifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically smooth-edged and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences during late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange and serve as a food source for birds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
