Spiraea obovata var. sibirica

Spiraea obovata var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the colder regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its profuse display of small, white to pink flowers in early summer. The leaves are typically oval to obovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is hardy in USDA zones 3-7.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
