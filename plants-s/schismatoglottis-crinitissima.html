Schismatoglottis crinitissima

Schismatoglottis crinitissima, commonly known as the hairy schismatoglottis, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of strikingly hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often hidden among the foliage, making it less showy than other orchids. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
