Spiraea simplex

Spiraea simplex, commonly known as meadowsweet, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a soft, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
