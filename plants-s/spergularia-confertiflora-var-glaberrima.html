Spergularia confertiflora var. glaberrima

Spergularia confertiflora var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name.

The flowers are pink to reddish in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.
