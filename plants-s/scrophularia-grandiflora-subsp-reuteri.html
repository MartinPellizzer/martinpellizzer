Scrophularia grandiflora subsp. reuteri

Scrophularia grandiflora subsp.

reuteri, commonly known as the large-flowered scrophularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
