Senecio vaseyi

Senecio vaseyi, commonly known as the Desert Senecio, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant is typically shrub-like, with narrow, gray-green leaves adapted to conserve water. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio vaseyi is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.
