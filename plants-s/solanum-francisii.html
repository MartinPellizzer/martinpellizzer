Solanum francisii

Solanum francisii, commonly known as the Francis's nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. Its berries, which are typically yellow or orange when mature, are edible but contain toxic compounds.

Solanum francisii is often found in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
