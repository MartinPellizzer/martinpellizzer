Solanum myrtifolium

Solanum myrtifolium, commonly known as the myrtleleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that resemble those of myrtle, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and contain toxic compounds.

Solanum myrtifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
