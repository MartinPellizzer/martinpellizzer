Sericocarpus tortifolius

Sericocarpus tortifolius, commonly known as the hookedleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to brown flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Its leaves are often folded in a V-shape, giving it the common name "hookedleaf.".

Sericocarpus tortifolius thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
