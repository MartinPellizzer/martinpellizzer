Spiranthes sawyeri

Spiranthes sawyeri, commonly known as the Sawyer's spiral orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of flowers along the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
