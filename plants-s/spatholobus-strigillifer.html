Spatholobus strigillifer

Spatholobus strigillifer, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Spatholobus strigillifer is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional indigenous practices.
