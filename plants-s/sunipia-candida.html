Sunipia candida

Sunipia candida, commonly known as the white sunipia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a subtle and elegant appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Sunipia candida is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
