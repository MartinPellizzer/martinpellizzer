Saussurea jurineioides

Saussurea jurineioides, commonly known as the Tibetan saffron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Tibet.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and has a deep root system to access water from the rocky soil. Its flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan medicine.
