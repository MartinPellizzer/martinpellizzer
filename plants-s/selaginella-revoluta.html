Selaginella revoluta

Selaginella revoluta, commonly known as the stair-step moss or the common spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a vascular plant that exhibits a unique life cycle involving both a gametophyte and a sporophyte stage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are spirally arranged and have a distinctive revolute margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Selaginella revoluta is also noted for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its role in soil stabilization.
