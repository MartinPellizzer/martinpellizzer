Stenolobium brachycarpum var. brachystachyum

Stenolobium brachycarpum var. brachystachyum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in dry, semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is often used for its timber and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in ecological restoration projects.
