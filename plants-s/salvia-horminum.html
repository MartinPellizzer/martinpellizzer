Salvia horminum

Salvia horminum, commonly known as autumn sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, aromatic leaves that are often gray-green in appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Salvia horminum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
