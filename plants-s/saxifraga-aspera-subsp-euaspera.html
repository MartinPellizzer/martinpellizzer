Saxifraga aspera subsp. euaspera

Saxifraga aspera subsp.

euaspera, commonly known as the rough saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features upright, hairy stems and small, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its hardiness and ability to add ornamental interest to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
