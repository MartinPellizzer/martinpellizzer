Santolina ascensionis

Santolina ascensionis, commonly known as the Ascension Island santolina, is a flowering plant native to the island of Ascension in the South Atlantic Ocean.

It is a low-growing shrub with silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic leaves and is adapted to the dry, coastal climate of its native habitat. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Santolina ascensionis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
