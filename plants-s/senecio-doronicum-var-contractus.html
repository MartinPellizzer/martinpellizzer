Senecio doronicum var. contractus

Senecio doronicum var. contractus, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
