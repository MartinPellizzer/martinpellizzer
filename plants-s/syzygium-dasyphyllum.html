Syzygium dasyphyllum

Syzygium dasyphyllum, commonly known as the glossy apple or red apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by edible, round, red fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. The fruit's skin is thin and smooth, and it contains a single seed.

Syzygium dasyphyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical regions.
