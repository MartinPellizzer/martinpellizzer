Sida plumosa

Sida plumosa, commonly known as the downy sida, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields and along roadsides. It is an annual or perennial herb that thrives in warm, dry climates.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and environmental conditions.
