Senecio tussilaginoides

Senecio tussilaginoides, commonly known as the yellow oxeye daisy, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with white centers. The plant is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Senecio tussilaginoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
