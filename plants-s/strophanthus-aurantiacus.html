Strophanthus aurantiacus

Strophanthus aurantiacus, commonly known as the red flame lily, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant is a deciduous shrub with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. Its botanical features include showy bracts that resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
