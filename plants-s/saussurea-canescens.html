Saussurea canescens

Saussurea canescens, commonly known as the Tibetan snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy composite flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to survive in harsh mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically grayish-green and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Saussurea canescens is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
