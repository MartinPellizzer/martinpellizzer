Sauropus robinsonii

Sauropus robinsonii, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white powdery coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine in some regions for their purported health benefits.
