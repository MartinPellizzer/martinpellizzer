Senecio sisymbriiformis

Senecio sisymbriiformis, commonly known as the yellow senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Senecio sisymbriiformis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
