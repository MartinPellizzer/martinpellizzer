Satyrium barbatum

Satyrium barbatum, commonly known as the beard orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often hairy or bristly appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Satyrium barbatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
