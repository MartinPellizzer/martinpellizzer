Serjania cornigera

Serjania cornigera, commonly known as the red mombin or red mulberry, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible for humans and animals. Serjania cornigera is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
