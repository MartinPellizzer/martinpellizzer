Saxifraga gyacaensis

Saxifraga gyacaensis, commonly known as the Tibetan saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing on steep slopes and cliffs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
