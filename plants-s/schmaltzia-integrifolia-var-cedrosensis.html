Schmaltzia integrifolia var. cedrosensis

Schmaltzia integrifolia var. cedrosensis, commonly known as Cedros Island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Channel Islands of California.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has palmate leaves with a rough texture and a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
