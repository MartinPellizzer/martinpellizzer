Sideroxylon euphlebium var. cryptophlebium

Sideroxylon euphlebium var. cryptophlebium, commonly known as the African ebony, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dark, dense wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in some regions.
