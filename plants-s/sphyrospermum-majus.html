Sphyrospermum majus

Sphyrospermum majus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its botanical characteristics include a complex root system and the ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.

The plant is also notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
