Sporobolus pyramidalis

Sporobolus pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive pyramid-shaped seed heads. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Sporobolus pyramidalis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
