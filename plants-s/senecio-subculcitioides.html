Senecio subculcitioides

Senecio subculcitioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
