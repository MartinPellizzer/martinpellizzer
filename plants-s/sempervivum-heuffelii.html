Sempervivum heuffelii

Sempervivum heuffelii, commonly known as the Heuffel's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Alps.

It features rosettes of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with reddish or purple hues, especially in cooler temperatures. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, though it prefers full sun to partial shade.
