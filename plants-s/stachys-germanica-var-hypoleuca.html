Stachys germanica var. hypoleuca

Stachys germanica var. hypoleuca, commonly known as white betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces spikes of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

White betony is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
