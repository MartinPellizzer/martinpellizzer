Sedum carinatum

Sedum carinatum, commonly known as the blue stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions makes it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
