Solanum candidum

Solanum candidum, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Solanum candidum is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
