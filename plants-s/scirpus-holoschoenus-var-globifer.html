Scirpus holoschoenus var. globifer

Scirpus holoschoenus var. globifer, commonly known as the globular bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a globe, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize soil.

This species plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic organisms and aiding in water filtration.
