Staphylea trifolia f. pyriformis

Staphylea trifolia f. pyriformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed sweetshrub, is a cultivar of the sweetshrub species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has a bushy, upright habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and pleasant scent, which makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
