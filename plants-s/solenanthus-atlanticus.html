Solenanthus atlanticus

Solenanthus atlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic solenanthus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic Ocean.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to tolerate salt spray and sandy soils. It thrives in maritime climates with moderate temperatures and high humidity.

Solenanthus atlanticus plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
