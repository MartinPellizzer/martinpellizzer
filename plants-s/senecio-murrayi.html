Senecio murrayi

Senecio murrayi, commonly known as Murray's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
