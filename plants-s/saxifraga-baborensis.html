Saxifraga baborensis

Saxifraga baborensis, commonly known as the Baborensis saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months, typically between June and August. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows and scree slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
