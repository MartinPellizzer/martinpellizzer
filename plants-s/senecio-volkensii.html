Senecio volkensii

Senecio volkensii, commonly known as the Volkens' groundsel, is a striking African plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dense, branching structure. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in spring. Senecio volkensii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
