Sedum pamiroalaicum

Sedum pamiroalaicum, commonly known as the Pamir stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Pamir Mountains.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water, enabling it to survive in arid and cold environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found growing on slopes or in crevices.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable species for ecological studies in alpine ecosystems.
