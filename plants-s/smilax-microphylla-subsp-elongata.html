Smilax microphylla subsp. elongata

Smilax microphylla subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the slender catclaw, is a vine native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
