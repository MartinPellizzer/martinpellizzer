Sisymbrium nanum var. leiocarpum

Sisymbrium nanum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smoothpod winter cress, is a small annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, and the fruit is a slender, smooth pod that matures in the fall.

This species is often considered a weed but has some historical use in traditional medicine.
