Setaria acromelaena

Setaria acromelaena, commonly known as the narrowleaf foxtail, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelets that are typically arranged in a panicle. The plant produces small, two-lobed spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in grasslands and agricultural fields.

Setaria acromelaena is a perennial plant that can become invasive in certain regions.
