Schizaea valdiviana

Schizaea valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian clubmoss, is a species of primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Schizaeaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporangia in specialized structures called strobili, which are typically clustered at the tips of the branches. Schizaea valdiviana is native to temperate rainforests of South America, particularly in regions of Chile and Argentina.

It plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various invertebrates.
