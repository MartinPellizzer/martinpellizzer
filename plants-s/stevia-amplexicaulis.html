Stevia amplexicaulis

Stevia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that often wrap around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its sweet-tasting leaves, which contain steviol glycosides.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and research settings.
