Sarothamnus lusitanicus var. obscurum

Sarothamnus lusitanicus var. obscurum, commonly known as the dark thyme or dark sarothamnus, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage with small, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
