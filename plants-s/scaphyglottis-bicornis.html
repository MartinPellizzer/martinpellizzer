Scaphyglottis bicornis

Scaphyglottis bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive two-horned shape on the lip of its flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can receive adequate light for photosynthesis. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Scaphyglottis bicornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
