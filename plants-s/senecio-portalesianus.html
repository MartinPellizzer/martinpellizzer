Senecio portalesianus

Senecio portalesianus, commonly known as the Portalesian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
