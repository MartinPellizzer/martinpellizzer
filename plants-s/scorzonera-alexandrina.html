Scorzonera alexandrina

Scorzonera alexandrina, commonly known as the Egyptian salsify, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, fleshy root system that is often harvested for its edible, tuberous roots. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Scorzonera alexandrina is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated as a vegetable in some regions.
