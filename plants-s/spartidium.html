Spartidium

Spartidium, commonly known as the common spartidium, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which typically consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant is native to various regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often growing in meadows and grasslands. Spartidium species are generally herbaceous, with erect stems and alternate leaves.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
