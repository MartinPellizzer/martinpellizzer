Sabina horizontalis

Sabina horizontalis, commonly known as the creeping juniper, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, scale-like foliage that forms a mat-like structure, often used in rock gardens or as ground cover. The plant produces small, blue-gray berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
