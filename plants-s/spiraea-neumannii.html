Spiraea neumannii

Spiraea neumannii, commonly known as the Neumann's weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features upright, multi-branched stems and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Spiraea neumannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
