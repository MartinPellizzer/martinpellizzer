Solanum dimorphum

Solanum dimorphum, commonly known as the two-form nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Solanum dimorphum is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.
