Stickmannia longicollis

Stickmannia longicollis, commonly known as the long-necked stickman, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, segmented stems that resemble the posture of a stick figure, giving it its whimsical common name. The plant typically grows low to the ground, with small, clustered leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often greenish or pale yellow, and appear in the axils of the leaves.

Stickmannia longicollis is a succulent, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive prolonged dry periods.
