Stachys caucasica

Stachys caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian betony, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Stachys caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
