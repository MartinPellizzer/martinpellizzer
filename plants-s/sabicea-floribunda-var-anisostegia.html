Sabicea floribunda var. anisostegia

Sabicea floribunda var. anisostegia, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on elongated inflorescences. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
