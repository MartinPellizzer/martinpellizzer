Salvia aspera

Salvia aspera, commonly known as rough sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties. Salvia aspera thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its medicinal uses include treatments for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.
