Syngonanthus xeranthemoides

Syngonanthus xeranthemoides, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent leaves and deep root systems. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
