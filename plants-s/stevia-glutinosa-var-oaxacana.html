Stevia glutinosa var. oaxacana

Stevia glutinosa var. oaxacana, commonly known as Oaxacan sweetleaf, is a species native to Mexico, particularly the state of Oaxaca.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and sweetening properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both traditional medicine and modern natural sweetener production.
