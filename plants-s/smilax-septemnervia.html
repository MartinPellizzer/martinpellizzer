Smilax septemnervia

Smilax septemnervia, commonly known as the seven-veined smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves with seven prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of understory vegetation in humid, temperate regions.
