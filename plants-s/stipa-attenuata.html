Stipa attenuata

Stipa attenuata, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and tall, slender flowering stems that produce feathery panicles. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers.

Stipa attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
