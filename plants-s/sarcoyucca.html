Sarcoyucca

Sarcoyucca, commonly known as the giant yucca, is a striking plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, pendulous clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Sarcoyucca is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
