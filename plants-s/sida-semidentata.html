Sida semidentata

Sida semidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Sida semidentata is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.
