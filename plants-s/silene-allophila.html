Silene allophila

Silene allophila, commonly known as the slender catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, typically arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Silene allophila is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
