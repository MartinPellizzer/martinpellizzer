Solanum apopsilomenum

Solanum apopsilomenum, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow fruits that turn red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated by various insects, including bees and butterflies, making it an important species for biodiversity.
