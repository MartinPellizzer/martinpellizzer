Sedum telephium var. millieri

Sedum telephium var. millieri, commonly known as Mill's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
