Sideritis nemausensis

Sideritis nemausensis, commonly known as the Nemausus sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and scrub habitats.

Sideritis species are known for their medicinal properties, and Sideritis nemausensis is no exception, containing compounds with potential health benefits.
