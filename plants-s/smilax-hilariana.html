Smilax hilariana

Smilax hilariana, commonly known as the Florida smilax, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and pine flatwoods.

Smilax hilariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
