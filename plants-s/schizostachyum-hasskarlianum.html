Schizostachyum hasskarlianum

Schizostachyum hasskarlianum, commonly known as the Burmese bamboo, is a tall, woody grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It is valued for its durability and is used in construction and handicrafts.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an important resource in reforestation and sustainable agriculture.
