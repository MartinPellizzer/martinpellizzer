Sideritis syriaca

Sideritis syriaca, commonly known as Syrian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its medicinal properties.

Sideritis syriaca is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and calming effects.
