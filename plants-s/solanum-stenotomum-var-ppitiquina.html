Solanum stenotomum var. ppitiquina

Solanum stenotomum var. ppitiquina, commonly known as the red-tipped potato bush, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
