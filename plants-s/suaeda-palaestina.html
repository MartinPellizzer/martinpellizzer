Suaeda palaestina

Suaeda palaestina, commonly known as Bladderwrack, is a halophyte plant native to the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It thrives in saline and arid environments, often found in coastal areas and salt marshes. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish or yellowish, and it produces small, round fruits.

Suaeda palaestina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
