Senecio mutabilis

Senecio mutabilis, commonly known as the everlasting or everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow central disk surrounded by white or pink ray florets. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.
