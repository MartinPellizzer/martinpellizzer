Sphaeralcea subhastata var. pumila

Sphaeralcea subhastata var. pumila, commonly known as the smallleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
