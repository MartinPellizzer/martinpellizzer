Syagrus acaulis

Syagrus acaulis, commonly known as the palm tree, is a species native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in sandy soils near the coast. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible fruits that are important to local wildlife.

Syagrus acaulis plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
