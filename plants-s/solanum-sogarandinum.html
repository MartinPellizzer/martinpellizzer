Solanum sogarandinum

Solanum sogarandinum, commonly known as the nightshade or the devil's apple, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and edible berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. The berries contain toxic compounds, making them dangerous if consumed in large quantities.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.
