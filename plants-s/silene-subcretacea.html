Silene subcretacea

Silene subcretacea, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, preferring full sun. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Silene subcretacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
