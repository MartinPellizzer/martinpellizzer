Solanum peekelii

Solanum peekelii, commonly known as the Peckel's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Solanum peekelii is often found in shaded, forested areas and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
