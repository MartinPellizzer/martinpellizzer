Scrophularia rupestris var. wilhelmsii

Scrophularia rupestris var. wilhelmsii, commonly known as the Wilhelms' rock scrophularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, arid environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
