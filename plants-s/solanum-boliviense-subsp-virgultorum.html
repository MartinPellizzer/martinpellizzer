Solanum boliviense subsp. virgultorum

Solanum boliviense subsp.

virgultorum, commonly known as the Bolivian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, temperate climates.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a rough, hairy texture.

The species is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
