Solanum gourlayi subsp. vidaurrei

Solanum gourlayi subsp.

vidaurrei, commonly known as the Vidaurre's nightshade, is a species native to South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Bolivia. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, tropical, or subtropical environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
