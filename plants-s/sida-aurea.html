Sida aurea

Sida aurea, commonly known as yellow mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by pale green bracts. Sida aurea is typically found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
