Styphelia carolineae

Styphelia carolineae, commonly known as Caroline's wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged in a dense, mat-like formation. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full shade.

Styphelia carolineae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal floral display.
