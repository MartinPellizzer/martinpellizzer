Scaevola ramosissima

Scaevola ramosissima, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

Scaevola ramosissima is drought-tolerant and can withstand salt spray, making it well adapted to coastal conditions.
