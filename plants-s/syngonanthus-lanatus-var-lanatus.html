Syngonanthus lanatus var. lanatus

Syngonanthus lanatus var. lanatus, commonly known as the silky hairveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
