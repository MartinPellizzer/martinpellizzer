Swida glabrata

Swida glabrata, commonly known as the smooth swida, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Swida glabrata is often found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils with partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
