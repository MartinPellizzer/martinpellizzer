Sasa mayebarae

Sasa mayebarae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and as a source of fiber.

Sasa mayebarae thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.
