Swertia difformis

Swertia difformis, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are usually hairy and erect.

Swertia difformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
