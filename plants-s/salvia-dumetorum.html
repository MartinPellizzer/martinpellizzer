Salvia dumetorum

Salvia dumetorum, commonly known as copal or holy herb, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of violet or blue flowers. It contains psychoactive compounds, primarily tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), which contribute to its traditional and modern uses.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and culturally important in various regions.
