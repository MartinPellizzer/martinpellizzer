Sorghum papillipes

Sorghum papillipes, commonly known as the African millet, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and heat resistance. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sorghum papillipes is primarily cultivated for its grain, which is used in traditional foods and animal feed.
