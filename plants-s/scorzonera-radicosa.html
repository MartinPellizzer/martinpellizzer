Scorzonera radicosa

Scorzonera radicosa, commonly known as the common scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, taproot system that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, which is often harvested for its edible roots. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are yellow with purple veins, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Scorzonera radicosa is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils, and it is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties.
