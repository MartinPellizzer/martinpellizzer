Stipa sanluisensis var. longispicula

Stipa sanluisensis var. longispicula, commonly known as the long-spined needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This variety is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments.
