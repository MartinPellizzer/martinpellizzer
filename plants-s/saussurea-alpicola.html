Saussurea alpicola

Saussurea alpicola, commonly known as the alpine saussurea, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and pale yellow to white flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow and sometimes hairy, adapted to withstand cold and strong winds.

Saussurea alpicola is often found in alpine meadows and is considered a symbol of the rugged beauty of high mountain environments.
