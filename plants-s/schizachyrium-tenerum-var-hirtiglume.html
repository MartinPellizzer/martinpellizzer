Schizachyrium tenerum var. hirtiglume

Schizachyrium tenerum var. hirtiglume, commonly known as the hairy glume panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, blade-like leaves. Its inflorescence is a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
