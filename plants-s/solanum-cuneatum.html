Solanum cuneatum

Solanum cuneatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
