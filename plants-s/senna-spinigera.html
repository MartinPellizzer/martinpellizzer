Senna spinigera

Senna spinigera, commonly known as the spiny senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces oblong, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Senna spinigera is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in poor soils.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
