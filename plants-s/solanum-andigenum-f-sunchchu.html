Solanum andigenum f. sunchchu

Solanum andigenum f. sunchchu, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a botanical variant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or violet flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
