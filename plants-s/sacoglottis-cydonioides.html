Sacoglottis cydonioides

Sacoglottis cydonioides, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a round, yellowish berry with a sweet, juicy pulp and a single large seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
