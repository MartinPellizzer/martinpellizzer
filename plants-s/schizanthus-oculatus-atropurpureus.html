Schizanthus oculatus-atropurpureus

Schizanthus oculatus-atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple-eyed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, with distinctive purple eyespots on the petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of flowers along its stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Schizanthus oculatus-atropurpureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
