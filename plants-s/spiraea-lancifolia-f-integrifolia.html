Spiraea lancifolia f. integrifolia

Spiraea lancifolia f. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are oval-shaped with smooth margins, distinguishing it from other spiraea varieties. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
