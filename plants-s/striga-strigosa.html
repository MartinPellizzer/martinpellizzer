Striga strigosa

Striga strigosa, commonly known as the common witchgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in late summer. Striga strigosa is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

It plays a minor role in ecological systems but can become invasive in certain environments.
