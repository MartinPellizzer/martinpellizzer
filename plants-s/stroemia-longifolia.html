Stroemia longifolia

Stroemia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf stroemia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often arranged spirally along its stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and can develop into a dense network, which helps stabilize the soil in its natural habitat.

Stroemia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in forest ecosystems.
