Silene hirsutissima var. laxiflora

Silene hirsutissima var. laxiflora, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and the plant is adapted to arid environments.
