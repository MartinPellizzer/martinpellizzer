Sabatia chloroides var. flexuosa

Sabatia chloroides var. flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible swamp pink, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish tinge, while the stems are flexible and can trail along the ground.

This species is part of the Gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
