Setaria macrosperma

Setaria macrosperma, commonly known as large-seeded foxtail, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability and rapid growth.
