Swertia thomsonii

Swertia thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving at high elevations. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Swertia thomsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
