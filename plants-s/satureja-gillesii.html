Satureja gillesii

Satureja gillesii, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are small, lance-shaped, and emit a strong, pungent scent when crushed.

Satureja gillesii is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antimicrobial properties.
