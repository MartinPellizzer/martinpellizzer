Senecio kingii f. subdiscoideus

Senecio kingii f. subdiscoideus, commonly known as the subdiscoideus senecio, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact cluster, typical of the genus Senecio. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
