Spergula pentandra var. morisonii

Spergula pentandra var. morisonii, commonly known as the Morison's sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. This variety is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing pale green to yellowish flowers.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
