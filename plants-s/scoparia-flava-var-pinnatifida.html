Scoparia flava var. pinnatifida

Scoparia flava var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the yellow scoparia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
