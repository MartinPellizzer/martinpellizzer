Scabiosa japonica var. alpina

Scabiosa japonica var. alpina, commonly known as alpine scabious, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with distinctive, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, daisy-like blooms.
