Silene palinotricha

Silene palinotricha, commonly known as the hairy-fruited catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Silene palinotricha produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
