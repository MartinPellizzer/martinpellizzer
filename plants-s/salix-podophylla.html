Salix podophylla

Salix podophylla, commonly known as the woolly willow, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its grayish, hairy stems and small, inconspicuous leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in mountainous regions.
