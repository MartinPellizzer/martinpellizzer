Schizachyrium weberbaueri var. minus

Schizachyrium weberbaueri var. minus, commonly known as the narrowleaf little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flower spikes in the fall. Its slender stems and delicate appearance make it a valuable component of native plant landscapes.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
