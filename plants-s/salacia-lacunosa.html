Salacia lacunosa

Salacia lacunosa, commonly known as the hollow salacia, is a unique member of the Salaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive hollow stems, which are often segmented and resemble bamboo. Native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
