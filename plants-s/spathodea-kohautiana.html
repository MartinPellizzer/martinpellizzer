Spathodea kohautiana

Spathodea kohautiana, commonly known as the African tulip, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant produces long, narrow, strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. It is a member of the Arecaceae (palm) family and is typically grown as a landscape plant due to its vibrant appearance.

Spathodea kohautiana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
