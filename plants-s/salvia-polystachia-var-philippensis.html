Salvia polystachia var. philippensis

Salvia polystachia var. philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine sage, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flower clusters that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
