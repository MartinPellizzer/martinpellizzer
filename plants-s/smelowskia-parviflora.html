Smelowskia parviflora

Smelowskia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered smelowskia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of downy hair.

Smelowskia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
