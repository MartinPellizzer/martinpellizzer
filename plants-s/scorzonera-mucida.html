Scorzonera mucida

Scorzonera mucida, commonly known as the sticky scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a sticky texture, which gives it its common name.

Scorzonera mucida is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
