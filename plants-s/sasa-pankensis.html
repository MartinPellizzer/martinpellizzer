Sasa pankensis

Sasa pankensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often with a slight bluish-green hue.

Sasa pankensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts and landscaping.
