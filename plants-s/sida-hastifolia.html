Sida hastifolia

Sida hastifolia, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Sida hastifolia is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
