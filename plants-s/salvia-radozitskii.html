Salvia radozitskii

Salvia radozitskii, commonly known as the Russian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, slender stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy spikes of violet-blue flowers in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and striking blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
