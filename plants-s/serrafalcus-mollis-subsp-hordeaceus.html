Serrafalcus mollis subsp. hordeaceus

Serrafalcus mollis subsp.

hordeaceus, commonly known as the slender serrated grass, is a member of the Poaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with serrated edges.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
