Solidago helleri

Solidago helleri, commonly known as Heller's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are alternately arranged along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a popular choice for native plant landscaping.
