Sempervivum poculiforme

Sempervivum poculiforme, commonly known as the rosette houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy rosettes of leaves that form a compact, rounded shape. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. This plant is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in poor, well-drained soils.

It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months.
