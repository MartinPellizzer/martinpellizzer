Scabiosa columbaria var. vulgaris

Scabiosa columbaria var. vulgaris, commonly known as the "Snowball Scabious," is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
