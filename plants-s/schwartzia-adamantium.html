Schwartzia adamantium

Schwartzia adamantium, commonly known as the diamondwood, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and its ability to form thick, interlacing branches that create a lattice-like appearance. The plant's leaves are typically small and glossy, often arranged in a compact, clustered manner. It is known for its unique ability to store water in its thick, fibrous stems, which helps it survive in dry conditions.

Schwartzia adamantium is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
