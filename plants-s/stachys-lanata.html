Stachys lanata

Stachys lanata, commonly known as velvetleaf or woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its spikes of purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic properties.

Stachys lanata is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
