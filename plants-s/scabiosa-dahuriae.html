Scabiosa dahuriae

Scabiosa dahuriae, commonly known as the Siberian scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Scabiosa dahuriae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
