Solanum andigenum convar. obtusiacuminatum

Solanum andigenum convar. obtusiacuminatum, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture and a distinct serrated margin. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen.

The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are edible but contain toxic alkaloids.
