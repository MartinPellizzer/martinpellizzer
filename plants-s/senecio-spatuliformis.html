Senecio spatuliformis

Senecio spatuliformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are broad and spoon-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in a compact inflorescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
