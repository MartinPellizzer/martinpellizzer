Schoenus galpinii

Schoenus galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and lake margins, where it forms dense stands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems.

Schoenus galpinii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
