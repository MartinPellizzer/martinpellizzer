Salacia congestiflora

Salacia congestiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered salacia, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Salacia congestiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
