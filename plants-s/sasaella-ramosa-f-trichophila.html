Sasaella ramosa f. trichophila

Sasaella ramosa f. trichophila, commonly known as the hairy-leaf sasa, is a variant of the sasa plant native to the forests of East Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, rhizomatous growth habit. The leaves are typically narrow, elongated, and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various forest organisms.
