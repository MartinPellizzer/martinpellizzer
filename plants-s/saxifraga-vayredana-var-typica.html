Saxifraga vayredana var. typica

Saxifraga vayredana var. typica, commonly known as the Vayreda's saxifrage, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually basal, clustered at the base, and have a slightly succulent texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
