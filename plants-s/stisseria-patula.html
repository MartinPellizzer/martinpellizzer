Stisseria patula

Stisseria patula, commonly known as the slender stinger orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and cloud forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged spirally along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stisseria patula is valued for its unique floral structure and is often found in specialized horticultural collections.
