Scilla currorii

Scilla currorii, commonly known as the Turkish squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of striking blue or purple flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other well-known ornamental plants.

Scilla currorii is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained conditions.
