Senecio mortonii

Senecio mortonii, commonly known as the Morton's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio mortonii is adapted to arid environments and is characterized by its drought-resistant properties.
