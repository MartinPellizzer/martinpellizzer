Sida montana f. duplicipila

Sida montana f. duplicipila, commonly known as the twinleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas. Its botanical features include stellate (star-shaped) hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
