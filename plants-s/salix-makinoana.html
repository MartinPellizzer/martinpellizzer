Salix makinoana

Salix makinoana, commonly known as Makino's willow, is a species of flowering plant in the willow family, Salicaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
