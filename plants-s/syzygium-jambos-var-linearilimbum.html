Syzygium jambos var. linearilimbum

Syzygium jambos var. linearilimbum, commonly known as the long-leaf rambutan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by hairy, spiny fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic value from its unique fruit.
