Syzygium bernieri var. oblanceolata

Syzygium bernieri var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptic to oblanceolate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in home gardens.
