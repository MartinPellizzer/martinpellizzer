Solanum pennellii var. elachistus

Solanum pennellii var. elachistus, commonly known as the small-fruited nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
