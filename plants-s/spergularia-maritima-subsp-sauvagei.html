Spergularia maritima subsp. sauvagei

Spergularia maritima subsp.

sauvagei, commonly known as the coastal sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in dune environments.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
