Sosnovskya arpensis

Sosnovskya arpensis, commonly known as the prairie gentian, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny prairies and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Sosnovskya arpensis plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
