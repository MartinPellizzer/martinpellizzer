Sideritis peruviana

Sideritis peruviana, commonly known as Peruvian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Sideritis peruviana is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly minty aroma, contributing to its use in traditional medicine.
