Sphaeralcea angustifolia subsp. lobata

Sphaeralcea angustifolia subsp.

lobata, commonly known as narrowleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
