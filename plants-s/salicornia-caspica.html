Salicornia caspica

Salicornia caspica, commonly known as sea beans or glasswort, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas and salt marshes, where it tolerates high salt concentrations in the soil and water. The plant has succulent, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and contain high levels of salt. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or yellow and appear in clusters.

Salicornia caspica is also known for its nutritional value, being rich in minerals and used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
