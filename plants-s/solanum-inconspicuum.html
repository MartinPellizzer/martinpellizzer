Solanum inconspicuum

Solanum inconspicuum, commonly known as the small nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a slightly rough texture.

Solanum inconspicuum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
