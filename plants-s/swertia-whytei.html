Swertia whytei

Swertia whytei, commonly known as the Whyte's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Swertia whytei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
