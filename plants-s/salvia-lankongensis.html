Salvia lankongensis

Salvia lankongensis, commonly known as the Lankong sage, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture, while the stems are woody and branched.

Salvia lankongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential traditional medicinal uses.
