Saccolabium peninsulare

Saccolabium peninsulare, commonly known as the Peninsular saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
