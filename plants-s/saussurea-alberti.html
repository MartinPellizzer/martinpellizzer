Saussurea alberti

Saussurea alberti, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to cold climates. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
