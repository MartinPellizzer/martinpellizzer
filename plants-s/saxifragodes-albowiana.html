Saxifragodes albowiana

Saxifragodes albowiana, commonly known as the albow's saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
