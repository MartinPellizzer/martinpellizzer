Sedum pruinatum subsp. forsterianum

Sedum pruinatum subsp.

forsterianum, commonly known as the Forster's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the eastern United States. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance choice for rock gardens.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.
