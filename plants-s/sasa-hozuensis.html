Sasa hozuensis

Sasa hozuensis, commonly known as the Hozu bamboo, is a clump-forming grass found in the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its ability to form dense thickets.

Sasa hozuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Japanese culture.
