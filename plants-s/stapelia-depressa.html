Stapelia depressa

Stapelia depressa, commonly known as the "Carrion Flower," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and its unique, meaty-looking flowers that resemble decaying flesh. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with a strong, unpleasant odor, which attracts flies for pollination. Stapelia depressa is a low-growing perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its striking appearance and unusual blooming habits.
