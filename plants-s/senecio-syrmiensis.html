Senecio syrmiensis

Senecio syrmiensis, commonly known as the Sirmian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its restricted habitat, Senecio syrmiensis is considered a rare and endemic species.
