Stevia burroana

Stevia burroana, commonly known as the Burroana stevia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in horticulture and phytochemistry.
