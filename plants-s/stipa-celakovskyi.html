Stipa celakovskyi

Stipa celakovskyi, commonly known as the Celakovsky's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
