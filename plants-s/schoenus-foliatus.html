Schoenus foliatus

Schoenus foliatus, commonly known as the broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, flat leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
