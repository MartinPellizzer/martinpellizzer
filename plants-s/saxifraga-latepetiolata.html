Saxifraga latepetiolata

Saxifraga latepetiolata, commonly known as the late-petiolate saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Saxifraga latepetiolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
