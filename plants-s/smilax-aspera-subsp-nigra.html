Smilax aspera subsp. nigra

Smilax aspera subsp.

nigra, commonly known as black smilax, is a flowering plant species native to the southeastern United States. It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and spiny, compound leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for some animals. Its root system consists of tubers, which are often used in traditional medicine. Smilax aspera subsp.

nigra thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
