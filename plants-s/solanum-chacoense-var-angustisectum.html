Solanum chacoense var. angustisectum

Solanum chacoense var. angustisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Paraguay.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has small, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic compounds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for medicinal use, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though caution is advised due to its poisonous properties.
