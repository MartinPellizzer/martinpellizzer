Scirpus validus var. creber

Scirpus validus var. creber, commonly known as the broad-leaved bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often emerging from dense, rhizomatous root systems. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences composed of small, clustered flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its broad, flat leaves are arranged in two rows, contributing to its distinctive appearance in wetland environments.

Scirpus validus var. creber plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
