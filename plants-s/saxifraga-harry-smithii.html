Saxifraga harry-smithii

Saxifraga harry-smithii, commonly known as Harry-Smith's saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of southern Africa.

It typically features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh, rocky habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in mountainous environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
