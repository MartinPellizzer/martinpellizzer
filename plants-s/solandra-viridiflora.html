Solandra viridiflora

Solandra viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered solandra, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces a strong, sweet fragrance, which attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a glossy green surface.

Solandra viridiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
