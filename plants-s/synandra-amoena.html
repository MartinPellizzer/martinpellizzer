Synandra amoena

Synandra amoena, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Synandra amoena is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
