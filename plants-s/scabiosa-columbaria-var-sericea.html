Scabiosa columbaria var. sericea

Scabiosa columbaria var. sericea, commonly known as the silvery scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pink or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is a perennial species that produces seeds through its showy blooms, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety 'sericea' is distinguished by its silky, silver-tomentose foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.
