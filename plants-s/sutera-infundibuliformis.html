Sutera infundibuliformis

Sutera infundibuliformis, commonly known as the lanternflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that resemble small lanterns, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its striking violet or blue petals. It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
