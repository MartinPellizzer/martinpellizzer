Solanoana purpurascens

Solanoana purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered nightshade, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often at night, attracting specific pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
