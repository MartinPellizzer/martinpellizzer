Salsola titovii

Salsola titovii, commonly known as Titarov's saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Salsola titovii plays a role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its native habitats.

It is also used in some traditional medicinal practices in the regions where it grows.
