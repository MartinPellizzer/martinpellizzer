Selenipedium conchiferum

Selenipedium conchiferum, commonly known as the giant spiny lily, is a striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, spiky leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is notable for its unique morphology, featuring a thick, fleshy rhizome and a tall, slender stem. Selenipedium conchiferum is adapted to humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to thrive.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and study.
