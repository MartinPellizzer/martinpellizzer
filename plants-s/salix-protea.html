Salix protea

Salix protea, commonly known as the silver willow, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside that gives the tree its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
