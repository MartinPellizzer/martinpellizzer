Spiraea hypericifolia var. obovata

Spiraea hypericifolia var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are ovate to obovate in shape, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
