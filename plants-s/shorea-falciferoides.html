Shorea falciferoides

Shorea falciferoides, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
