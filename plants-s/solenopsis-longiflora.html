Solenopsis longiflora

Solenopsis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered fire ant, is a species of ant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, slender flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Solenopsis longiflora is an important component of its native ecosystems, contributing to pollination and seed dispersal.
