Salix caprea var. ishidoyana

Salix caprea var. ishidoyana, commonly known as the Japanese weeping willow, is a variant of the European weeping willow.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
