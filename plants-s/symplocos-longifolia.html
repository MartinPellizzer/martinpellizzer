Symplocos longifolia

Symplocos longifolia, commonly known as long-leaved evergreen or Indian tea, is a shrub native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, woody fruits containing several seeds. Symplocos longifolia is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
