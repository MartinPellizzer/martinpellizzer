Selenicereus mirandae

Selenicereus mirandae, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique combination of a brightly colored upper stem and a green, leafy lower portion. The plant produces night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
