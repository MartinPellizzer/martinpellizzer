Syzygium brousmichei

Syzygium brousmichei, commonly known as the Brousmiche'sSyzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
