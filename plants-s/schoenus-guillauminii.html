Schoenus guillauminii

Schoenus guillauminii, commonly known as the Guillaumin's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Schoenus guillauminii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
