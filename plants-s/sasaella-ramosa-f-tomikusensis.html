Sasaella ramosa f. tomikusensis

Sasaella ramosa f. tomikusensis, commonly known as the Tomikusensis sasa, is a variant of the sasa plant native to Japan.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, bamboo-like growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming thickets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
