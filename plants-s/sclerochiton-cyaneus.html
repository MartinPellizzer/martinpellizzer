Sclerochiton cyaneus

Sclerochiton cyaneus, commonly known as the blue shield fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, showy blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
