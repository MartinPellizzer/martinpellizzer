Stellaria angustifolia subsp. tenella

Stellaria angustifolia subsp.

tenella, commonly known as narrowleaf starwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically features slender, branching stems and small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in star-like clusters.

The leaves are linear and often hairy, adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
