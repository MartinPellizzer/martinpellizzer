Saccolabiopsis wulaokenensis

Saccolabiopsis wulaokenensis, commonly known as the Wulaoken grass, is a perennial herb native to northern China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescence. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded lands.
