Symplocos candicans

Symplocos candicans, commonly known as the shining evergreen or silverleaf symplocos, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitats.
