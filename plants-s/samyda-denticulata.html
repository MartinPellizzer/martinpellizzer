Samyda denticulata

Samyda denticulata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Samyda denticulata is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
