Sophora rapaensis

Sophora rapaensis, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its clusters of fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. Its root system is deep and strong, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Sophora rapaensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
