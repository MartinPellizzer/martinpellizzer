Scrobicaria ilicifolia

Scrobicaria ilicifolia, commonly known as the fragrant buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Scrobicaria ilicifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various cultural practices.
