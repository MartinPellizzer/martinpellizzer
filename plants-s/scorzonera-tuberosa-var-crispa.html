Scorzonera tuberosa var. crispa

Scorzonera tuberosa var. crispa, commonly known as the curly scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, tubular shape, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots are tuberous and have been historically used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Scorzonera tuberosa var. crispa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high altitudes.
