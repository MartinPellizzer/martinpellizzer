Salvia gilliesii var. lorentzii

Salvia gilliesii var. lorentzii, commonly known as Lorentz's blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and aromatic properties.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
