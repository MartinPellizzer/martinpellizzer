Schumeria viciifolia

Schumeria viciifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or wild spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, with a deep green coloration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by kidney-shaped, edible seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.
