Salacia fredericqii

Salacia fredericqii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its drooping clusters of yellow flowers that resemble showers of light. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.
