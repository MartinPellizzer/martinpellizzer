Siphocampylus asper

Siphocampylus asper, commonly known as the roughleaf daisy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Siphocampylus asper is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
