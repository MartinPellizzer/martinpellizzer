Sideritis incana subvar. depauperata

Sideritis incana subvar. depauperata, commonly known as the slender woolly sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
