Synaedrys iteaphylla

Synaedrys iteaphylla, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
