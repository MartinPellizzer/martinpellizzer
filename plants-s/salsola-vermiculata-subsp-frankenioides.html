Salsola vermiculata subsp. frankenioides

Salsola vermiculata subsp.

frankenioides, commonly known as Frankenioides gooseweed, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in desert and coastal environments.

The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces clusters of small, greenish flowers. It is a perennial species with a robust root system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in desert ecosystems.
