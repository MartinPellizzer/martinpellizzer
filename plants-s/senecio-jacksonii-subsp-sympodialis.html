Senecio jacksonii subsp. sympodialis

Senecio jacksonii subsp.

sympodialis, commonly known as the Jackson's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry climates. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
