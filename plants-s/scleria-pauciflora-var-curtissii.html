Scleria pauciflora var. curtissii

Scleria pauciflora var. curtissii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces small, spike-like clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense tufts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and botanical study.
