Sabina vulgaris

Sabina vulgaris, commonly known as the common juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, blue-black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant is evergreen, with a dense, aromatic foliage that provides habitat for various wildlife species. Its roots are deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

Sabina vulgaris plays an important role in ecological systems, particularly in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in temperate regions.
