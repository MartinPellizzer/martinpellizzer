Sericotheca

Sericotheca, commonly known as the silky sheath, is a specialized structure found in certain fungal spore cases.

It is typically composed of a thin, membranous layer that encases the spores, providing protection and aiding in their dispersal. The sericotheca is often covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss and increase durability. This structure is particularly common in members of the family Helotiaceae.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in spore survival and ecological adaptation.
