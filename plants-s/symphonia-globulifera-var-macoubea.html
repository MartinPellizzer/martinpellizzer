Symphonia globulifera var. macoubea

Symphonia globulifera var. macoubea, commonly known as the Macouba or African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree bears oval, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. Its bark and wood are valued for their durability and have been used in traditional medicine and construction.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
