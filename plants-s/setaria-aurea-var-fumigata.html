Setaria aurea var. fumigata

Setaria aurea var. fumigata, commonly known as the smoky foxtail, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive smoky-colored inflorescence.

It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a dense, cylindrical spikelet that gives it its characteristic appearance. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.

The botanical features of Setaria aurea var. fumigata make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
