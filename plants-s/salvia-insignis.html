Salvia insignis

Salvia insignis, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a strong, minty fragrance.

Salvia insignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
