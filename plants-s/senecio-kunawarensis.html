Senecio kunawarensis

Senecio kunawarensis, commonly known as the Kunawe rainforest sedum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its rosette growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat.

Senecio kunawarensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
