Sinarundinaria andropogonoides

Sinarundinaria andropogonoides, commonly known as Chinese silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and its ability to form dense clumps. The plant produces tall, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

Sinarundinaria andropogonoides is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
