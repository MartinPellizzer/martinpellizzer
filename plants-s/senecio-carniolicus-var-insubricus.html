Senecio carniolicus var. insubricus

Senecio carniolicus var. insubricus, commonly known as the Alpine groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
