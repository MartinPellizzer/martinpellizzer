Scabiosa cinerea subsp.

hladnikiana, commonly known as the gray scabious, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
