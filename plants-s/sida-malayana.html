Sida malayana

Sida malayana, commonly known as the Malay mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Sida malayana is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
