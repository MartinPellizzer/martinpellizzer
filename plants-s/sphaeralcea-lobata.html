Sphaeralcea lobata

Sphaeralcea lobata, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its glandular, hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to full sun.
