Seriphidium amoenum

Seriphidium amoenum, commonly known as the Ameno Mountain Thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and appear in dense clusters.

Seriphidium amoenum is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
