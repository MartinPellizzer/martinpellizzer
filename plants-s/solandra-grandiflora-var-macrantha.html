Solandra grandiflora var. macrantha

Solandra grandiflora var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered solandra, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, showy flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a strong, sweet fragrance, and they bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture, while its bark is smooth and pale in color.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
