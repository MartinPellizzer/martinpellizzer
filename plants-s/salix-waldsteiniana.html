Salix waldsteiniana

Salix waldsteiniana, commonly known as the Waldstein's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and clusters of small, greenish-yellow catkins. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a smooth upper surface with a fuzzy underside.

Salix waldsteiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in rugged terrain.
