Scrophularia californica subsp. floribunda

Scrophularia californica subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the California figwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
