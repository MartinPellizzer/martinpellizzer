Saxifraga muscoides var. elatior

Saxifraga muscoides var. elatior, commonly known as the greater saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its ability to withstand harsh conditions makes it a resilient and hardy species.
