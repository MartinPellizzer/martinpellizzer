Senna birostris var. arequipensis

Senna birostris var. arequipensis, commonly known as the Arequipa senna, is a shrub native to the highlands of southern Peru.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its yellow flowers are typical of the genus Senna, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves and pods are used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
