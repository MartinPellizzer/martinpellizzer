Swietenia fabrilis

Swietenia fabrilis, commonly known as the Honduras mahogany, is a tropical hardwood tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood with a fine, even texture. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with broad, ovate leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by animals.

Due to its high commercial value, it is also considered an endangered species in many regions.
