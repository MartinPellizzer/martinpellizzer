Stachyurus praecox f. leucotrichus

Stachyurus praecox f. leucotrichus, commonly known as the white-haired stachyurus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its unique, pendulous clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The foliage consists of ovate, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
