Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp. cyrenaicus

Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp.

cyrenaicus, commonly known as the Cyrenaic whiteleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
