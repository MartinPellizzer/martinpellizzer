Smilax solanifolia

Smilax solanifolia, commonly known as the purple-stemmed smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in spines, and its compound leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Smilax solanifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
