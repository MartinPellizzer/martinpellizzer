Sorghum dochna var. burmanicum

Sorghum dochna var. burmanicum, commonly known as Burmese sorghum, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in moist, lowland areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional landscaping or as a forage crop.
