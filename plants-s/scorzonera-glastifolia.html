Scorzonera glastifolia

Scorzonera glastifolia, commonly known as the alpine scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped, silvery-gray leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine habitats.

Scorzonera glastifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
