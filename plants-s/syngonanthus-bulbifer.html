Syngonanthus bulbifer

Syngonanthus bulbifer, commonly known as the bulbous bluemont, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and erect, grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among gardeners.
