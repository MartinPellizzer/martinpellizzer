Senecio araeus

Senecio araeus, commonly known as the silver ragwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Senecio araeus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
