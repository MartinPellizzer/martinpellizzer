Seddera gracilis

Seddera gracilis, commonly known as slender seddera, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. Seddera gracilis is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its succulent stems.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
