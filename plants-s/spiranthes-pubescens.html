Spiranthes pubescens

Spiranthes pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spire orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands, bogs, and forest floors. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The hairy spire orchid is known for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific soil types and moisture conditions.
