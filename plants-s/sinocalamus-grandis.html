Sinocalamus grandis

Sinocalamus grandis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, slender plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rapid growth and hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its culms are strong and flexible, making it useful in construction and traditional crafts.

Sinocalamus grandis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
