Salsola aphylla

Salsola aphylla, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub or perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in loose, sandy soils and access deep water sources.

Salsola aphylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing forage for livestock and wildlife.
