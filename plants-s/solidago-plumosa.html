Solidago plumosa

Solidago plumosa, commonly known as the downy goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its feathery, silver-gray leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its showy inflorescences attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Solidago plumosa plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
