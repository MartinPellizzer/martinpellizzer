Scutellaria jodudiana

Scutellaria jodudiana, commonly known as the Indian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Scutellaria jodudiana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
