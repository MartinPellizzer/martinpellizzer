Seriphidium prasinum

Seriphidium prasinum, commonly known as the green hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are generally narrow and gray-green in color, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Seriphidium prasinum is often found in open habitats such as steppes and coastal dunes.
