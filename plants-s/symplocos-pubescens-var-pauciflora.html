Symplocos pubescens var. pauciflora

Symplocos pubescens var. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that turn red when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical and subtropical forests.
