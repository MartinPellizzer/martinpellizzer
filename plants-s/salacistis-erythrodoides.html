Salacistis erythrodoides

Salacistis erythrodoides, commonly known as the red-leafed salacid, is a unique plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant under direct sunlight. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Salacidae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
