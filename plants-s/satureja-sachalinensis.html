Satureja sachalinensis

Satureja sachalinensis, commonly known as Sachalin thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and parts of northeastern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Satureja sachalinensis is valued for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
