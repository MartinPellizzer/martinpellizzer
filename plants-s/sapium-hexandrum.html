Sapium hexandrum

Sapium hexandrum, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant is notable for its fruiting structures, which are three-lobed capsules containing numerous seeds. Its botanical characteristics make it an ornamental plant, though it is also considered invasive in some regions.

The species belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many plants with latex-producing properties.
