Spyridium coronatum

Spyridium coronatum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences and bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

The golden wattle is an important species in Australian flora and is also the national floral emblem of Australia.
