Smilax laurifolia

Smilax laurifolia, commonly known as the evergreen arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that remain green throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It has a robust root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types, including clay and loam.

Smilax laurifolia is often found in moist woodlands and is known for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
