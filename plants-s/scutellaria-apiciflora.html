Scutellaria apiciflora

Scutellaria apiciflora, commonly known as the apiciflora skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Scutellaria apiciflora is often found in moist woodlands and stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
