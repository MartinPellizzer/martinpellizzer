Sida acuta subsp. carpinifolia

Sida acuta subsp.

carpinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant features slender, needle-like leaves that are opposite or whorled, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to thrive in moist environments.
