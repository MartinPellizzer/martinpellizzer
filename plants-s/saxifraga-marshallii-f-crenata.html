Saxifraga marshallii f. crenata

Saxifraga marshallii f. crenata, commonly known as the marsh saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and rosette-forming foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
