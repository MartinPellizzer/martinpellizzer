Senecio viscosus

Senecio viscosus, commonly known as sticky senecio, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that exude a sticky, resinous substance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
