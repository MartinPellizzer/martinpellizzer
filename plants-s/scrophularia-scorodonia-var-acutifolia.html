Scrophularia scorodonia var. acutifolia

Scrophularia scorodonia var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland margins or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinct serrated edge. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
