Searsia gracillima var. glaberrima

Searsia gracillima var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth-leaved wild olive, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and coastal areas, and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for wildlife.
