Susanna

Susanna, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Susanna is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
