Saxifraga oregana

Saxifraga oregana, commonly known as the Oregon saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. The plant features basal leaves that are rounded and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink or white, and appear in clusters at the base of the plant during the spring.

Saxifraga oregana is well adapted to cool, humid climates and is often found in regions with high rainfall.
