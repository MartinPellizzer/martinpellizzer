Sipapoa rufa

Sipapoa rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with yellow markings, blooming in late winter to early spring. Sipapoa rufa is a pseudobulbous plant that stores water in its thick stems, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is often grown as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
