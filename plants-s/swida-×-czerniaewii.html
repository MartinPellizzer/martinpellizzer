Swida × czerniaewii

Swida × czerniaewii, commonly known as the hybrid sweet cherry, is a naturally occurring cross between Prunus avium (sweet cherry) and Prunus padus (sloe).

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
