Sisymbrium austriacum var. derelictum

Sisymbrium austriacum var. derelictum, commonly known as the Austrian mustard or desert mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces slender, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in ecological succession.
