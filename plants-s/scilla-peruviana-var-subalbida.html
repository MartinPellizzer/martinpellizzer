Scilla peruviana var. subalbida

Scilla peruviana var. subalbida, commonly known as the Peruvian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The variety subalbida is distinguished by its lighter-colored flowers compared to the typical form.
