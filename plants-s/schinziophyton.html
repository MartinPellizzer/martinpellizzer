Schinziophyton

Schinziophyton, commonly known as the giant lily, is a unique and rare plant native to the Eastern Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant grows as a perennial herb with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Schinziophyton is adapted to its arid environment through its deep root system and ability to store water in its leaves.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native region.
