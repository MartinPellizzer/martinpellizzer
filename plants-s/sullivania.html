Sullivania

Sullivania, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Sullivania thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
