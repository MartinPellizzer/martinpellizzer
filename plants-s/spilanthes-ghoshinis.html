Spilanthes ghoshinis

Spilanthes ghoshinis, commonly known as the Ghoshini stinkcup, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the genus Spilanthes, which is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and pungent odor, which is a common trait among members of this genus. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open fields and along riverbanks.

Due to its unique fragrance and medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine.
