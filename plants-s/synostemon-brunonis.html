Synostemon brunonis

Synostemon brunonis, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
