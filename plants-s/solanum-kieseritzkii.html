Solanum kieseritzkii

Solanum kieseritzkii, commonly known as the Kieseritzkii nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale purple, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature, containing several seeds.

Solanum kieseritzkii is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
