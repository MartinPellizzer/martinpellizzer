Secamone cloiselii

Secamone cloiselii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

Secamone cloiselii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
