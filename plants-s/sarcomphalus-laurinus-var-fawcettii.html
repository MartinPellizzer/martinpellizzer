Sarcomphalus laurinus var. fawcettii

Sarcomphalus laurinus var. fawcettii, commonly known as the Fawcett's laurel, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
