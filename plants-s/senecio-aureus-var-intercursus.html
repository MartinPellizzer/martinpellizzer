Senecio aureus var. intercursus

Senecio aureus var. intercursus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and linear, silver-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
