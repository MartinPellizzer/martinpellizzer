Serjania matogrossensis

Serjania matogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian serjania, is a flowering plant native to Brazil, particularly found in the states of Mato Grosso and Mato Grosso do Sul.

It belongs to the family Serjaniaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often elliptical in shape, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange with five petals.

Serjania matogrossensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
