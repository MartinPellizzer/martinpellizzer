Senecio lanatifolius

Senecio lanatifolius, commonly known as the woollyleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio lanatifolius is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
