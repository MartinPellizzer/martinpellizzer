Sorbus cuspidata

Sorbus cuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

Sorbus cuspidata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
