Salvia compacta var. latifolia

Salvia compacta var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sage, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for both gardeners and herbalists.
