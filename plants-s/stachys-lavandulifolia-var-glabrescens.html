Stachys lavandulifolia var. glabrescens

Stachys lavandulifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a grayish-green color. The plant produces spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular plant in horticulture.
