Sesleria variegata

Sesleria variegata, commonly known as the variegated woodland grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Sesleria variegata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its variegated foliage, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
