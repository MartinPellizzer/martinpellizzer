Scaevola virgata

Scaevola virgata, commonly known as the fairy fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, brightly colored flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Scaevola virgata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
