Sorbus aucuparia var. maderensis

Sorbus aucuparia var. maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's rowan, is a variant of the common rowan tree.

It is native to Central Europe and is characterized by its small, red berries that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cooler climates. Its leaves are alternate and oval-shaped, with finely serrated edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
