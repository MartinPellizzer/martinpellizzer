Sherbournia bignoniiflora

Sherbournia bignoniiflora, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to grow as a shrub or small tree, depending on environmental conditions.
