Salsola turgaica

Salsola turgaica, commonly known as Torga saltwort, is a halophytic plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in saline soils and is often found in desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing dunes. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that store water, and its flowers are typically greenish-white, appearing in clusters. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is adapted to extreme temperatures and low water availability.

Salsola turgaica is an important component of desert ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for livestock.
