Saponaria griffithiana

Saponaria griffithiana, commonly known as Griffith's soapwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Saponaria griffithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
