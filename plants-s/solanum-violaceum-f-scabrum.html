Solanum violaceum f. scabrum

Solanum violaceum f. scabrum, commonly known as the rough violaceum, is a variant of the violaceum plant, characterized by its distinctive rough, scabrous texture on its stems and leaves.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant typically features small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, hairy layer, contributing to its unique appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
