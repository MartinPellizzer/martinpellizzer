Seddera latifolia var. argentea

Seddera latifolia var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf seddera, is a variant of the broader seddera plant species.

It is characterized by its broad, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage provides a striking contrast to its green stems and flowers, which are typically small and clustered.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique aesthetic appeal.
