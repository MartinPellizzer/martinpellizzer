Saussurea sawadae

Saussurea sawadae, commonly known as the Japanese snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy composite flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces a single flower head at the top of the stem.

Saussurea sawadae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some Asian countries.
