Sarcomphalus graecus

Sarcomphalus graecus, commonly known as the Greek thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Sarcomphalus graecus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical uses in traditional medicine.
