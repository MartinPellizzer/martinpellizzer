Salvia × tunica-mariae

Salvia × tunica-mariae, commonly known as the Tunica-Mariae sage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
