Stipa mandavillei

Stipa mandavillei, commonly known as the Mandaville grass, is a species of flowering grass native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stature and tufted growth habit. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically panicles, with small, spikelet-like flowers that are often pale green to brown in color. Stipa mandavillei is adapted to grow in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
