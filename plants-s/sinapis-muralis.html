Sinapis muralis

Sinapis muralis, commonly known as wall rocket, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it often grows on rocky or sandy soils.

It is a hardy annual that thrives in disturbed environments and is sometimes considered a weed.
