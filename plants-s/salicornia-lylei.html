Salicornia lylei

Salicornia lylei, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophyte plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its jointed, succulent stems that often appear in clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is typically found in coastal areas or salt marshes. Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Salicornia lylei plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
