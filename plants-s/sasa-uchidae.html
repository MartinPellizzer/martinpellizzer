Sasa uchidae

Sasa uchidae, commonly known as the Uchida bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Sasa uchidae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
