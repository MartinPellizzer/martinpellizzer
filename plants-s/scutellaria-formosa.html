Scutellaria formosa

Scutellaria formosa, commonly known as the purple skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or open habitats and is often found in dry, sunny areas.

Scutellaria formosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
