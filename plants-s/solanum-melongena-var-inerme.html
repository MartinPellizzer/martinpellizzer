Solanum melongena var. inerme

Solanum melongena var. inerme, commonly known as the wild eggplant, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, purple or white flowers. The plant produces berries that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Unlike its cultivated counterpart, it is not typically grown for food but is of interest in botanical studies and traditional medicine.
