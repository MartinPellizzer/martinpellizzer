Sarcopera anomala

Sarcopera anomala, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a small, shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
