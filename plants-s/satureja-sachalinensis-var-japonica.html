Satureja sachalinensis var. japonica

Satureja sachalinensis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese savory, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine for their pungent, peppery flavor.

The flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
