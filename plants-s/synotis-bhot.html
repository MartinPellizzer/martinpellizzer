Synotis bhot

Synotis bhot, commonly known as the Himalayan moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal blooms that open at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique blooming habit and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
