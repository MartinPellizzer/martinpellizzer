Stachytarpheta reticulata

Stachytarpheta reticulata, commonly known as the purpletop vervain, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
