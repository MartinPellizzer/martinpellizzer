Sesquicella digitata

Sesquicella digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finger-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical structure includes a sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.
