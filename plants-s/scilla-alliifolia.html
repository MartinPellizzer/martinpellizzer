Scilla alliifolia

Scilla alliifolia, commonly known as the garlic scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in spring. The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic scent, making them a popular ingredient in some cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Scilla alliifolia is also valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and early blooming habit.
