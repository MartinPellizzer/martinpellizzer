Swida × dubia

Swida × dubia, commonly known as the hybrid sweet cherry.

It is a cross between Prunus avium (sweet cherry) and Prunus dulcis (peach), resulting in a plant that exhibits characteristics of both parents. The botanical features include a deciduous tree with a rounded crown and smooth, grey bark. Its leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring.

The fruit is a small, sweet cherry with a thin skin and a firm, juicy flesh.
