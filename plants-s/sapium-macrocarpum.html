Sapium macrocarpum

Sapium macrocarpum, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features alternate, simple, ovate leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
