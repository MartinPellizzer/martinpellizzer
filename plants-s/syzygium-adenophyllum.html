Syzygium adenophyllum

Syzygium adenophyllum, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sour-tasting fruits. Syzygium adenophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for shade gardens and tropical landscapes.
