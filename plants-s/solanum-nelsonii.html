Solanum nelsonii

Solanum nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, and its berries are typically red when mature. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and can thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for consumption or cultivation in gardens.
