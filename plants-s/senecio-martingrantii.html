Senecio martingrantii

Senecio martingrantii, commonly known as the Martingrant's groundsel, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant features a short, thick stem with fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in succulent gardens.
