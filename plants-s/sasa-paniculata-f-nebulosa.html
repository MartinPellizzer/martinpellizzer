Sasa paniculata f. nebulosa

Sasa paniculata f. nebulosa, commonly known as the clouded sasa, is a variant of the sasa plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is a bamboo-like perennial, characterized by its slender, arching stems and dense clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered panicles of white to pale green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, cylindrical berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
