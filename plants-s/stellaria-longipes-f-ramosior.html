Stellaria longipes f. ramosior

Stellaria longipes f. ramosior, commonly known as the branched starwort, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological and horticultural studies.
