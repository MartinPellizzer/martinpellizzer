Spiranthes truncata

Spiranthes truncata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiral arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves and its long, flowering spike that produces small, white or pale pink flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands, bogs, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

The flowers are pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies, which are attracted by the plant's scent and nectar.
