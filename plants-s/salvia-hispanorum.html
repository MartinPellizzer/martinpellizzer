Salvia hispanorum

Salvia hispanorum, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorls along the stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
