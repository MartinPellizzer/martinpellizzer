Setaria paniciformis

Setaria paniciformis, commonly known as Panicled Foxtail, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, panicle-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Setaria paniciformis is used in some traditional practices and is also considered a potential forage crop in certain areas.
