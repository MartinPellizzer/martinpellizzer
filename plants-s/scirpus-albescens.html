Scirpus albescens

Scirpus albescens, commonly known as the white bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in moist environments such as marshes and pond edges.

Scirpus albescens plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
