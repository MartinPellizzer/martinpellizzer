Stipa zalesskyi var. glabrata

Stipa zalesskyi var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that are visually striking. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it is noted for its fine texture and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
