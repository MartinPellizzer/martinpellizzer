Sarracenia flava var. rubricorpora

Sarracenia flava var. rubricorpora, commonly known as the red-bodied sweet flag, is a striking carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to reddish-pink pitchers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive red throat. The plant's leaves are modified into tubular structures that secrete digestive enzymes to trap and digest insects. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires consistent moisture and bright light to flourish.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and unique ecological adaptations.
