Saccolabium hillii

Saccolabium hillii, commonly known as the Mexican bluebonnet, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
