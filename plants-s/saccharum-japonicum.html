Saccharum japonicum

Saccharum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese sugarcane, is a tall grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its long, fibrous stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in wetland areas. Its leaves are broad and blade-like, with a prominent midrib.

It is primarily cultivated for its high sugar content in its stalks, making it an important resource in traditional agriculture.
