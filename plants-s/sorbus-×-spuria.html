Sorbus × spuria

Sorbus × spuria, commonly known as whitebeams, is a hybrid species within the genus Sorbus, characterized by its distinctive white to pale pink flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in woodland or along stream banks. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as pomes, which are usually red or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Sorbus × spuria is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
