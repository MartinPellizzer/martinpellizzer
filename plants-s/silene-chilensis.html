Silene chilensis

Silene chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean catchfly, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
