Spiranthes inaequilatera

Spiranthes inaequilatera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiral arrangement of pale green to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a single, elongated flower spike.

Spiranthes inaequilatera is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
