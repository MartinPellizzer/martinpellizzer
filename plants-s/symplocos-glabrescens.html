Symplocos glabrescens

Symplocos glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf waxberry, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are smooth and glabrous on both surfaces. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include woody stems, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Symplocos glabrescens is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
