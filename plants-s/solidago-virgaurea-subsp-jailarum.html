Solidago virgaurea subsp. jailarum

Solidago virgaurea subsp.

jailarum, commonly known as the Jailarum goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and rocky slopes.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to golden-orange flower heads, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
