Syzygium stenurum

Syzygium stenurum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit clove.

It is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae, native to Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red when mature.

Syzygium stenurum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its aromatic foliage.
