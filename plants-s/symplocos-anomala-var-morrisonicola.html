Symplocos anomala var. morrisonicola

Symplocos anomala var. morrisonicola, commonly known as Morrison's waxleaf, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
