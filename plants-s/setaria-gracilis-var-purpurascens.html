Setaria gracilis var. purpurascens

Setaria gracilis var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple foxtail, is a grass species native to warm temperate and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and elongated, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are often tinged with purple, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental grass due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
