Stenodraba pusilla var. patagonica

Stenodraba pusilla var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian dwarf dandelion, is a small, flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellow composite flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a compact growth form, typically reaching only a few centimeters in height.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
