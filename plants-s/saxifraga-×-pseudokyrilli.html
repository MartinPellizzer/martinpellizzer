Saxifraga × pseudokyrilli

Saxifraga × pseudokyrilli, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often reddish or purple in color, which help it conserve water in its arid environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
