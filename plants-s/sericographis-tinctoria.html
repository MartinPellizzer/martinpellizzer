Sericographis tinctoria

Sericographis tinctoria, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five petals, five sepals, and a unique reproductive structure called a gynostemium. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Sericographis tinctoria is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
