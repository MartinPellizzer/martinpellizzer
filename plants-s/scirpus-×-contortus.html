Scirpus × contortus

Scirpus × contortus, commonly known as the twisted bulrush, is a hybrid wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that often twist and coil, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, thriving in marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers form dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stems.

Scirpus × contortus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
