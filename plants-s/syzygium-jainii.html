Syzygium jainii

Syzygium jainii, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by edible, round, orange-red fruits. Syzygium jainii is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and handicrafts, and for its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
