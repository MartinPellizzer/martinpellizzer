Solena pubescens

Solena pubescens, commonly known as the hairy solena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.

Solena pubescens is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
