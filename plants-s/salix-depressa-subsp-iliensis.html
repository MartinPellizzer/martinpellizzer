Salix depressa subsp. iliensis

Salix depressa subsp.

iliensis, commonly known as the Ili Valley willow, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically pubescent.

The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and wetlands.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
