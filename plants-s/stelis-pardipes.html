Stelis pardipes

Stelis pardipes, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on its petals and sepals. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
