Satureja parnassica subsp. parnassica

Satureja parnassica subsp.

parnassica, commonly known as Alpina savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and small, aromatic leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Satureja parnassica subsp.

parnassica is valued for its pungent fragrance and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
