Spiraea hypericifolia var. genuina

Spiraea hypericifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The foliage is typically dark green, with a fine, serrated leaf margin. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and graceful, weeping appearance.
