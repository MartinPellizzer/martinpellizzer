Senecio digitalifolius

Senecio digitalifolius, commonly known as the Blue-Eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that resemble eyes, hence its common name. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Senecio digitalifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
