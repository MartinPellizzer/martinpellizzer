Solanum biflorum var. kotoensis

Solanum biflorum var. kotoensis, commonly known as the Koto nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and the fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for consumption.
