Solanum caricaefolium

Solanum caricaefolium, commonly known as the roughleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features woody stems and small, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in shrublands and grasslands.
