Salvia fugax

Salvia fugax, commonly known as the ephemeral sage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments, thriving in full sun. Salvia fugax has a short blooming period, often lasting only a few weeks, which contributes to its common name.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
