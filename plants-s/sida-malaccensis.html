Sida malaccensis

Sida malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca sida, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, tropical environments. The plant has small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a slightly hairy texture.

Sida malaccensis is also known for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
