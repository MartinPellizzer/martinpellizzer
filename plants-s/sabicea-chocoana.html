Sabicea chocoana

Sabicea chocoana, commonly known as the Chocoan sabicea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its potential for medicinal uses.
