Sansevieria pinguicula subsp. disticha

Sansevieria pinguicula subsp.

disticha, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, pale green, strap-like leaves that often have a waxy texture and a distinctive light green to almost white coloration.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stems, though this is rare in cultivation.

The Ghost Plant is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and its ability to adapt to a variety of environmental conditions.
