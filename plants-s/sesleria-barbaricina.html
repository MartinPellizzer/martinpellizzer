Sesleria barbaricina

Sesleria barbaricina, commonly known as the purple false heather, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of purple or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
