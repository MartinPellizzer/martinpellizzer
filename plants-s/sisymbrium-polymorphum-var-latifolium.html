Sisymbrium polymorphum var. latifolium

Sisymbrium polymorphum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf wild radish, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions of North America. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often used in ecological restoration due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in marginal habitats.
