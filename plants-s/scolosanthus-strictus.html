Scolosanthus strictus

Scolosanthus strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, upright leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, translucent seed pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Scolosanthus strictus is known for its drought tolerance and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
