Serratula echinocephala

Serratula echinocephala, commonly known as the spinyhead groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a basal rosette of spiny, hairy leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive central disk. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Serratula echinocephala is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
