Solanum ombrophilum

Solanum ombrophilum, commonly known as the forest tomato, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tangy, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for its edible fruits.
