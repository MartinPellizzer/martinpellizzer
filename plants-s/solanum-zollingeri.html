Solanum zollingeri

Solanum zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom at night. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
