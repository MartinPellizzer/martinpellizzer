Sisyrinchium planicola

Sisyrinchium planicola, commonly known as the plains blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves and small, star-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairie and steppe habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Sisyrinchium planicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
