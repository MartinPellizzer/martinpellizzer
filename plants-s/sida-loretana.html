Sida loretana

Sida loretana, commonly known as the Loretto morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows up to 1 to 2 meters in height. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Sida loretana is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showing its adaptability to various environments.
