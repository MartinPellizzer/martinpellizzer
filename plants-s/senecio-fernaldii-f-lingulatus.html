Senecio fernaldii f. lingulatus

Senecio fernaldii f. lingulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its linear, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to rock gardens and xeriscapes.
