Scorpiurus muricatus subsp. subvillosus

Scorpiurus muricatus subsp.

subvillosus, commonly known as the silky sandbur, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, spiny seed heads that form distinctive, burr-like structures.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and an important part of local ecosystems.
