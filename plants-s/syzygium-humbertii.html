Syzygium humbertii

Syzygium humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's apple.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, fleshy fruits that resemble apples. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

Syzygium humbertii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
