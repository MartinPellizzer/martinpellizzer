Scutellaria geraniana

Scutellaria geraniana, commonly known as the geranium scutellaria, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Scutellaria geraniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
