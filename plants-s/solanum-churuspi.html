Solanum churuspi

Solanum churuspi, commonly known as the churupi nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes plants such as tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Solanum churuspi is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
