Sabicea bracteolata

Sabicea bracteolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
