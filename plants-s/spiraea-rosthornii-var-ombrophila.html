Spiraea rosthornii var. ombrophila

Spiraea rosthornii var. ombrophila, commonly known as the shade spiraea, is a variant of the spiraea plant that thrives in woodland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to flourish in partial to full shade. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early to mid-summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and remains lush throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to moist, shaded soils.
