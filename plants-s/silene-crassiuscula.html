Silene crassiuscula

Silene crassiuscula, commonly known as the dwarf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Silene crassiuscula is known for its ability to thrive in poor, sandy soils and its short life cycle.
