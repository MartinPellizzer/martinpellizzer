Serapias latifolia var. pallida

Serapias latifolia var. pallida, commonly known as the pale serapias, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color, with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant has basal leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy areas.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
