Salix amygdalina f. microphylla

Salix amygdalina f. microphylla, commonly known as the Weeping Willow with small leaves, is a unique cultivar of the willow tree characterized by its delicate, drooping branches and finely textured foliage.

It is a deciduous tree with slender, flexible branches that create a cascading appearance, making it popular in ornamental landscaping. The leaves are small, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface, turning yellow in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often used in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shade in wet areas.
