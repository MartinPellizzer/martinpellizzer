Senecio erucifolius

Senecio erucifolius, commonly known as the wooly beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
