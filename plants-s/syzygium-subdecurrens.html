Syzygium subdecurrens

Syzygium subdecurrens, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing sweet, sticky pulp. Syzygium subdecurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.
