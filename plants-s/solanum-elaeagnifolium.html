Solanum elaeagnifolium

Solanum elaeagnifolium, commonly known as silverleaf nightshade, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its toxic properties.
