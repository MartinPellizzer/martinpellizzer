Solanum filiforme

Solanum filiforme, commonly known as the threadleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, thread-like leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers. Its fruits are berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Solanum filiforme is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in certain horticultural settings.
