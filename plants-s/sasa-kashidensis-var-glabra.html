Sasa kashidensis var. glabra

Sasa kashidensis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bamboo, is a variant of the Sasa kashidensis species native to Japan.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are covered in a smooth, glabrous surface, distinguishing it from other variants. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
