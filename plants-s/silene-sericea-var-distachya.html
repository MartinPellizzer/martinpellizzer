Silene sericea var. distachya

Silene sericea var. distachya, commonly known as the silky catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

Silene sericea var. distachya is an annual or biennial species that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
