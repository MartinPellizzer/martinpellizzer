Solanum neglectum

Solanum neglectum, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Solanum neglectum belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other toxic plants like belladonna and tomato.
