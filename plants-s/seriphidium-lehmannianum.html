Seriphidium lehmannianum

Seriphidium lehmannianum, commonly known as Lehmann's tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
