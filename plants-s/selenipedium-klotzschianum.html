Selenipedium klotzschianum

Selenipedium klotzschianum, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stalks that can reach up to 3 meters in height, supporting large, showy, spider-like flowers. Native to the humid rainforests of Central and South America, this plant thrives in shaded, moist environments with high humidity. Its unique floral structure consists of a central spadix surrounded by a hood-like bract, giving it its distinctive appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is considered an endangered species in the wild.
