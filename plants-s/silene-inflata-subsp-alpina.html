Silene inflata subsp. alpina

Silene inflata subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant produces inflated, bladder-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Silene inflata subsp.

alpina is often found in mountainous areas of Europe, Asia, and North America.
