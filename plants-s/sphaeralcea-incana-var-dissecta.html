Sphaeralcea incana var. dissecta

Sphaeralcea incana var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and toothed. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
