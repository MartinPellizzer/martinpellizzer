Salix × viciosorum

Salix × viciosorum, commonly known as the black willow, is a hybrid species within the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, and its branches are often used for basket weaving.

This species is valued for its rapid growth and adaptability to various wetland environments.
