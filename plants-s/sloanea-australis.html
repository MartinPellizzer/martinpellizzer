Sloanea australis

Sloanea australis, commonly known as the Australian sloanea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and forested areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Sloanea australis produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
