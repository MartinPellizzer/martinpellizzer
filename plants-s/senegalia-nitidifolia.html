Senegalia nitidifolia

Senegalia nitidifolia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

Senegalia nitidifolia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
