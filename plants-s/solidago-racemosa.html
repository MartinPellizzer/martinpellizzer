Solidago racemosa

Solidago racemosa, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces abundant yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, elongated clusters called racemes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Solidago racemosa is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
