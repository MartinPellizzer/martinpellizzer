Silene villosa var. graveolens

Silene villosa var. graveolens, commonly known as the stinkcup, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
