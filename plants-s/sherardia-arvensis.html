Sherardia arvensis

Sherardia arvensis, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Sherardia arvensis is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain habitats.
