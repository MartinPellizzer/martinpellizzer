Salvia officinalis subsp. minor

Salvia officinalis subsp.

minor, commonly known as lemon sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet or blue flowers.

The plant is known for its distinct lemony fragrance, which comes from volatile oils present in its leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
