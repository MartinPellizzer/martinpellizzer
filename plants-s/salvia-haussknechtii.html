Salvia haussknechtii

Salvia haussknechtii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Salvia haussknechtii is also valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
