Sphacele campanulata

Sphacele campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from white to blue or purple. The plant has opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Sphacele campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
