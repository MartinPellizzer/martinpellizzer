Saxifraga pacifica

Saxifraga pacifica, commonly known as the Japanese saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest floors. The plant features rosettes of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring.

Saxifraga pacifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
