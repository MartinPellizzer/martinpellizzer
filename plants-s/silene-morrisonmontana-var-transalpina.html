Silene morrisonmontana var. transalpina

Silene morrisonmontana var. transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
