Serapias laxiflora var. columnae

Serapias laxiflora var. columnae, commonly known as the columnar serapias, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, trifoliate, and have a distinctive, glossy appearance.

Serapias laxiflora var. columnae thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
