Senecio heteroideus

Senecio heteroideus, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that resemble ghostly shapes. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its striking foliage, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
