Secale cereale subsp. aestivum

Secale cereale subsp.

aestivum, commonly known as wheatgrass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. It is a perennial plant that typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellow flowers, which are followed by small, oval-shaped seeds. Wheatgrass is known for its high nutritional value and is often used in herbal remedies and as a source of vitamins and minerals.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
