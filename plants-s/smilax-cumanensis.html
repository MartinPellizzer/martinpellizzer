Smilax cumanensis

Smilax cumanensis, commonly known as the Cumanese smilax, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax cumanensis is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
