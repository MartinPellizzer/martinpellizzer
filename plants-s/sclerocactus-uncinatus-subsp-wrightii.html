Sclerocactus uncinatus subsp. wrightii

Sclerocactus uncinatus subsp.

wrightii, commonly known as Wright's hooked cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color, with spines that are often curved or hooked.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent stems and having a slow growth rate.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
