Senecio stenodon

Senecio stenodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
