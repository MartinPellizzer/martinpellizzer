Senecio crusoei

Senecio crusoei, commonly known as the Cape St. Francis daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in the fynbos biome, where it thrives in mild, temperate climates.

Senecio crusoei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
