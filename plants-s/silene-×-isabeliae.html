Silene × isabeliae

Silene × isabeliae, commonly known as the Isabel's catchfly, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Silene armeria and Silene latifolia.

It is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Silene × isabeliae is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
