Sorbus coronaria

Sorbus coronaria, commonly known as the crown flower or whitebeams, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its white, clustered flowers that appear in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Sorbus coronaria is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
