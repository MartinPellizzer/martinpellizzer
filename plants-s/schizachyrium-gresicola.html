Schizachyrium gresicola

Schizachyrium gresicola, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage that turns coppery in the fall. This plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems. Schizachyrium gresicola is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental and ecological plant.

Its seed heads are often used in floral arrangements and provide food for birds and small mammals.
