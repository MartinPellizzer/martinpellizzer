Scleria tricholepis

Scleria tricholepis, commonly known as the wooly sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and the presence of a distinctive, wooly covering on its stems and leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster, which is a common feature among sedges.

Scleria tricholepis is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
