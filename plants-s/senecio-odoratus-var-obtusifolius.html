Senecio odoratus var. obtusifolius

Senecio odoratus var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the sweet dew or sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, velvety leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
