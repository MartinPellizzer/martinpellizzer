Syzygium scortechinii var. cuneatum

Syzygium scortechinii var. cuneatum, commonly known as the Cuneatum apple tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a thin, leathery skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

This variant is closely related to other Syzygium species, such as the rambutan and durian.
