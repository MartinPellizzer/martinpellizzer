Silene tatarica f. foliosa

Silene tatarica f. foliosa, commonly known as the leafy tatarian catchfly, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to eastern Europe and western Asia and thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
