Sophora tetraptera var. microphylla

Sophora tetraptera var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces pendulous racemes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
