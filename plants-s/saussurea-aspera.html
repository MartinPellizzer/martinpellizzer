Saussurea aspera

Saussurea aspera, commonly known as the rough saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia, including parts of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and dense, feathery foliage. The plant produces large, showy composite flower heads that are typically yellow or brown in color. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
