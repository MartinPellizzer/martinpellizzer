Sorindeia heterandra

Sorindeia heterandra, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and often have a distinctive yellowish or greenish color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its resilience and unique features, it is valued for both ecological and traditional uses.
