Sida atropurpurea

Sida atropurpurea, commonly known as purple morning glory, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically purple or bronze in color, with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, opening in the morning and closing in the evening. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Sida atropurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
