Solidago linoides

Solidago linoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and wet meadows. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Solidago linoides is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
