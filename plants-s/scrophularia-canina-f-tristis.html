Scrophularia canina f. tristis

Scrophularia canina f. tristis, commonly known as the weedy dog's mercury, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in early spring. It is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and waste areas.

This variety is distinguished by its more slender growth habit compared to the typical form of Scrophularia canina.
