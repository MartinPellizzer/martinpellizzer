Sagraea pilosa

Sagraea pilosa, commonly known as the hairy sagraea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its small, daisy-like flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in composite flower heads.

Sagraea pilosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
