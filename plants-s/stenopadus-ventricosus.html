Stenopadus ventricosus

Stenopadus ventricosus, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, balloon-like fruits that contain the seeds. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, using its tendrils to ascend supports. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white or yellowish hues.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
