Senecillis filiformis

Senecillis filiformis, commonly known as the slender senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant has thin, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
