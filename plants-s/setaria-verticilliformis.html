Setaria verticilliformis

Setaria verticilliformis, commonly known as bearded grass, is a species of annual grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open fields, roadsides, and agricultural areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spikelet-like flowers.

Setaria verticilliformis is often used as a forage plant for livestock due to its high nutritional value.
