Sorbus aria proles mougeotii

Sorbus aria proles mougeotii, commonly known as the Mougeot's whitebeam, is a subspecies of the whitebeam tree native to Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, and its small, white, aggregate fruits that mature in the autumn. It is often found in rocky or forested habitats, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
