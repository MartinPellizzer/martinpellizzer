Sedum laxum subsp. heckneri

Sedum laxum subsp.

heckneri, commonly known as the Mexican stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
