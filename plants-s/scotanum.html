Scotanum

Scotanum, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant blooms that open at night. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The flowers close during the day, making it a unique and striking addition to gardens.
