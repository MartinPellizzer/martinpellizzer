Spigelia hedyotidea

Spigelia hedyotidea, commonly known as the redroot st. john's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is notable for containing compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Spigelia hedyotidea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland understories.
