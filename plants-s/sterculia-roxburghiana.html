Sterculia roxburghiana

Sterculia roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or Himalayan chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain several edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
