Sorbus torminalis f. pendula

Sorbus torminalis f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping whitebeam, is a striking variant of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that cascade downward, creating a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fuzzy berries in the autumn. Its foliage is typically dark green with a slight blue tint, adding to its visual appeal.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its unique form and seasonal interest.
