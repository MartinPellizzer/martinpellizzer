Silene occidentalis subsp. longistipitata

Silene occidentalis subsp.

longistipitata, commonly known as the long-stemmed western campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated pedicels, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.
