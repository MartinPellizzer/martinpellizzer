Spiranthes casei

Spiranthes casei, commonly known as the pink spiraea, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. It has a delicate root system and is often found in association with other orchid species.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in many areas.
