Serapias vomeracea f. platyglottis

Serapias vomeracea f. platyglottis, commonly known as the flat-tongued orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spikes that bear pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, flattened labellum. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect species.
