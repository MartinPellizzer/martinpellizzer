Symplocos obana

Symplocos obana, commonly known as the red-flowered inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Symplocos obana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
