Statice japonica

Statice japonica, commonly known as Japanese glassweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Statice japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
