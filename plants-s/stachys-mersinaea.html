Stachys mersinaea

Stachys mersinaea, commonly known as the Mediterranean hedge woundwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spherical fruits known as schizocarps. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Stachys mersinaea is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
