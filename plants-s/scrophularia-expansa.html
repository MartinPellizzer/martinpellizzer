Scrophularia expansa

Scrophularia expansa, commonly known as broad-leaved figwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Scrophularia expansa is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
