Scilla tetraphylla

Scilla tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its four leaves, which give it its common name, and it typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in woodland margins and open meadows, where it thrives in partial shade.

Scilla tetraphylla is a hardy plant that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with suitable conditions.
