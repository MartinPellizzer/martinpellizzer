Solanum amblycalyx

Solanum amblycalyx, commonly known as the narrow-calyx nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. It produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

Solanum amblycalyx is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
