Solidago racemosa var. gillmanii

Solidago racemosa var. gillmanii, commonly known as Gillman's goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
