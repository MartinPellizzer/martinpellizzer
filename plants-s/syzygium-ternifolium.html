Syzygium ternifolium

Syzygium ternifolium, commonly known as the Indian bay leaf or tejpatta, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are aromatic and used in traditional medicine and cooking. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by capsule fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are often dried and used as a spice, contributing a distinctive flavor to various cuisines.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its culinary and medicinal properties.
