Salvia columbariae var. bernardina

Salvia columbariae var. bernardina, commonly known as the Bernard's sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and grayish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
