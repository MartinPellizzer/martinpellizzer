Solanum kurtzianum

Solanum kurtzianum, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the stems are hairy.

Solanum kurtzianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some pharmacological potential due to its bioactive compounds.
