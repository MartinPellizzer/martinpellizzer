Shortia rotata

Shortia rotata, commonly known as the Carolina wintergreen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forest understories, often in shaded environments. The plant features small, white, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture with a distinctive reddish hue on the underside.

Shortia rotata is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
