Salix nujiangensis

Salix nujiangensis, commonly known as the Nujiang willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typically yellow-green in color, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
