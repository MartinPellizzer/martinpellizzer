Solanum nitidibaccatum

Solanum nitidibaccatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is notable for its chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal use.
