Solidago lepida subsp. fallax

Solidago lepida subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the fallax goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks.

The plant features erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer to early fall, forming dense, rounded clusters at the ends of the stems.
