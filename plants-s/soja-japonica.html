Soja japonica

Soja japonica, commonly known as Japanese soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is cultivated for its high-protein seeds, which are used in a variety of food products. Soja japonica thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in both traditional and modern agriculture.
