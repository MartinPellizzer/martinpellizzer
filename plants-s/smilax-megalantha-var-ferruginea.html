Smilax megalantha var. ferruginea

Smilax megalantha var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty smilax, is a perennial climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features thick, woody stems with spines and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
