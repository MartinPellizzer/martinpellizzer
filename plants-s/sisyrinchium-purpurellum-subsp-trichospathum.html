Sisyrinchium purpurellum subsp. trichospathum

Sisyrinchium purpurellum subsp.

trichospathum, commonly known as the purple-blue gaura, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Its flowers are often arranged in clusters and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
