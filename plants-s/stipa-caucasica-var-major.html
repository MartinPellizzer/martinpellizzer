Stipa caucasica var. major

Stipa caucasica var. major, commonly known as the Caucasian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of spikelets, which are the flowering structures, and it blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and mountainous areas.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
