Symplocos wynadense

Symplocos wynadense, commonly known as Wynad evergreen or Wynad boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Symplocos wynadense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
