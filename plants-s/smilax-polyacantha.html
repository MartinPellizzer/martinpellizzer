Smilax polyacantha

Smilax polyacantha, commonly known as the prickly coral-root, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often hidden beneath its dense foliage. Its berries are red or yellow and contain several seeds.

Smilax polyacantha is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.
