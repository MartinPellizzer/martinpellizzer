Scirpus sterilis

Scirpus sterilis, commonly known as the sterile sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. This plant is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Scirpus sterilis is not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes but plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy, undisturbed wetland ecosystems.
