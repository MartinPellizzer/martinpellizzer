Setaria laevigata

Setaria laevigata, commonly known as silky millet, is an annual grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it a valuable crop in arid regions.

Silky millet is also used as forage for livestock and has been cultivated for its nutritional seeds.
