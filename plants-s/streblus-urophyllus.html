Streblus urophyllus

Streblus urophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds with silky filaments.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, tropical lowland forests.
