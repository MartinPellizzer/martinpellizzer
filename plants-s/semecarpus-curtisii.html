Semecarpus curtisii

Semecarpus curtisii, commonly known as the Indian horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its palmate leaves and spiny fruit capsules. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing hard, angular seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in Ayurvedic medicine.
