Senecio floridanus

Senecio floridanus, commonly known as the Florida senecio, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. It has slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves.

Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
