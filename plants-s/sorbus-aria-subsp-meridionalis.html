Sorbus aria subsp. meridionalis

Sorbus aria subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture with a serrated margin. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
