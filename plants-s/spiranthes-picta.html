Spiranthes picta

Spiranthes picta, commonly known as the painted ladies' tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Spiranthes picta is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and plays a role in supporting local ecosystems.
