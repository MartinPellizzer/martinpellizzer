Spodiopogon

Spodiopogon, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. They are characterized by their upright, spike-like inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. Spodiopogon species often thrive in moist, well-drained soils and are sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
