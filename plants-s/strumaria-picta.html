Strumaria picta

Strumaria picta, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Strumaria picta is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
