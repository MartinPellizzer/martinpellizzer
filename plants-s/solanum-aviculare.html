Solanum aviculare

Solanum aviculare, commonly known as the bird's-beak nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, bird-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, violet-colored flowers. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

Solanum aviculare is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.
