Smilax herbacea var. pubescens

Smilax herbacea var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate with a pointed tip and are often covered in tiny, glandular hairs.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
