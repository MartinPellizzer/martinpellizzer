Salacia pyriformioides

Salacia pyriformioides, commonly known as the lemon-scented salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a citrus-like fragrance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salacia pyriformioides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
