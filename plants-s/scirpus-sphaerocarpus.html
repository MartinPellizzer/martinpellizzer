Scirpus sphaerocarpus

Scirpus sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the globular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces distinctive spherical, dark brown inflorescences that are highly visible and often used for identification. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Scirpus sphaerocarpus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
