Silene latifolia subsp. alba

Silene latifolia subsp.

alba, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white, pink, or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its showy, showy display.

The subspecies alba is distinguished by its predominantly white flowers, which contrast with the more colorful flowers of other subspecies.
