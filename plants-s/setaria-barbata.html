Setaria barbata

Setaria barbata, commonly known as bearded dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and distinctive, drooping inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes other grasses.
