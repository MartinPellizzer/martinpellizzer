Setaria decipiens

Setaria decipiens, commonly known as false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
