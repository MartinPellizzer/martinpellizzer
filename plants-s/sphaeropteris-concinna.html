Sphaeropteris concinna

Sphaeropteris concinna, commonly known as the golden tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of bright yellow to golden fronds that give it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Sphaeropteris concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
