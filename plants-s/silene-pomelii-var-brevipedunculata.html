Silene pomelii var. brevipedunculata

Silene pomelii var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface. This variant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy plant in xerophytic environments.
