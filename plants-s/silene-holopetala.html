Silene holopetala

Silene holopetala, commonly known as the naked-petaled catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-red flowers that are nearly petal-less, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are showy and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Silene holopetala is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar morphological features.
