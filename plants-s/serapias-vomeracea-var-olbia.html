Serapias vomeracea var. olbia

Serapias vomeracea var. olbia, commonly known as the Olbia marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in wet meadows and marshy areas, where it forms dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, and it is often found in association with other wetland flora.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
