Sabicea dewildemaniana

Sabicea dewildemaniana, commonly known as the Dewildeman's sabicea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It produces legume pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
