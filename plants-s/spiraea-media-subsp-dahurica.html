Spiraea media subsp. dahurica

Spiraea media subsp.

dahurica, commonly known as the shrub willow, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense thickets.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and they turn yellow in the fall. Spiraea media subsp.

dahurica is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
