Solanum lehmannii

Solanum lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are an important food source for certain wildlife species.

Solanum lehmannii belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
