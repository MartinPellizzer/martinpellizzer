Setaria viridis var. maior

Setaria viridis var. maior, commonly known as green millet, is a variant of the green foxtail grass native to temperate regions.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is often used in ecological restoration and as a forage crop.
