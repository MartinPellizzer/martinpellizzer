Scleria levis var. pubescens

Scleria levis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy false rush, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves, from which it derives its common name, is a distinguishing feature.
