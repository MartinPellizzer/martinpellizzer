Schizostachyum cuspidatum

Schizostachyum cuspidatum, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, arching leaves that give it a tropical appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by three-winged fruits, which are dispersed by wind. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Schizostachyum cuspidatum is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
