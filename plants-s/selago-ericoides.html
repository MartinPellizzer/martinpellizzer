Selago ericoides

Selago ericoides, commonly known as the heath shrub, is a perennial evergreen plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath or heather family, and is characterized by its compact, woody growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray.

Selago ericoides thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
