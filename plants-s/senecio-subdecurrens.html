Senecio subdecurrens

Senecio subdecurrens, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
