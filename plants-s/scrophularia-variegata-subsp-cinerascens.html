Scrophularia variegata subsp. cinerascens

Scrophularia variegata subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in loose clusters.

It is part of the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
