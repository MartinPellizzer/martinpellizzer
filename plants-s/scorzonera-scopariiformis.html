Scorzonera scopariiformis

Scorzonera scopariiformis, commonly known as the Alpine scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the European Alps.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by cylindrical, spiky fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Scorzonera scopariiformis is often found in meadows and slopes at elevations above 1,500 meters.
