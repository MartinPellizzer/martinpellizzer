Saxicolella

Saxicolella, commonly known as the rock lichen, is a genus of lichens that exhibit unique botanical characteristics.

These organisms are typically found growing on rocky surfaces, where they form a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, usually a green alga or cyanobacterium. Saxicolella species are known for their ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to extreme conditions. Their thallus, or body, is often crust-like, providing structural stability on uneven rock substrates.

The genus plays an important ecological role in soil formation and nutrient cycling in rocky habitats.
