Syringodea virescens

Syringodea virescens, commonly known as the green syringa, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy green appearance. The plant prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to many landscapes.
