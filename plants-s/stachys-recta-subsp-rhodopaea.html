Stachys recta subsp. rhodopaea

Stachys recta subsp.

rhodopaea, commonly known as the Greek betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features upright, square stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

The plant produces clusters of violet to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
