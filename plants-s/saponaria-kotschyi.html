Saponaria kotschyi

Saponaria kotschyi, commonly known as the Kashmir soaproot, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits. It is known for its saponin content, which gives it a soapy texture when crushed.

Saponaria kotschyi is often found in rocky and alpine environments at high elevations.
