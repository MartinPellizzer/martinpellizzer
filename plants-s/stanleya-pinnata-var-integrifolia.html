Stanleya pinnata var. integrifolia

Stanleya pinnata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the white prairie cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a smooth, uninterrupted margin, distinguishing it from other varieties. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
