Senecio papagonius

Senecio papagonius, commonly known as the Papago's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
