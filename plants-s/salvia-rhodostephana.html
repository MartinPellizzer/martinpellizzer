Salvia rhodostephana

Salvia rhodostephana, commonly known as the scarlet sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
