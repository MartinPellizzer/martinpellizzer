Stevia ovata var. expansa

Stevia ovata var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf stevia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Stevia ovata var. expansa is valued for its high concentration of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners.

It is commonly cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of natural sweetener in food and beverages.
