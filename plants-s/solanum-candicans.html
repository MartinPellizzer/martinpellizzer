Solanum candicans

Solanum candicans, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a deep red, containing toxic seeds. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

Solanum candicans is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
