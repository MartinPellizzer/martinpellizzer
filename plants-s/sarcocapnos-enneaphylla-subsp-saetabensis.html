Sarcocapnos enneaphylla subsp. saetabensis

Sarcocapnos enneaphylla subsp.

saetabensis, commonly known as the mountain saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of white hairs, providing protection against harsh weather conditions. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered flowers with five petals, often displaying shades of pink or white.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and adaptability to high-altitude ecosystems.
