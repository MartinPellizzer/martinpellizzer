Silene cucubalus subsp. commutata

Silene cucubalus subsp.

commutata, commonly known as the common bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive urn-shaped pink or purple flowers and hairy, branched stems.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or semi-natural environments.
