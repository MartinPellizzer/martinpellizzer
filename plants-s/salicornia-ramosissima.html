Salicornia ramosissima

Salicornia ramosissima, commonly known as sea beet, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and estuaries, where it tolerates high salinity levels. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid or salty conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or yellow and are pollinated by wind or insects.

Salicornia ramosissima is also valued for its edible shoots, which are rich in minerals and often used in culinary applications.
