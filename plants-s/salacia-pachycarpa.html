Salacia pachycarpa

Salacia pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited salacia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, thick, and fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or green, and are borne on short pedicels.
