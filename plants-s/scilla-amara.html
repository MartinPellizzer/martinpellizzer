Scilla amara

Scilla amara, commonly known as bitter scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. Scilla amara is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
