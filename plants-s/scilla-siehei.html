Scilla siehei

Scilla siehei, commonly known as the Chinese squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring, which are often arranged in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
