Symplocos magnifica

Symplocos magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a fragrant aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Symplocos magnifica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
