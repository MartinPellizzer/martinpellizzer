Sterculia australis var. genuina

Sterculia australis var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sterculia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glabrous or nearly so. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
