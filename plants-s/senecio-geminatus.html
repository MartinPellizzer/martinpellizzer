Senecio geminatus

Senecio geminatus, commonly known as the twinleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features basal leaves that are opposite and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Senecio geminatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
