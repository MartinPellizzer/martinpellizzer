Statice capitella

Statice capitella, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous small, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas and dunes. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions.

Statice capitella is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
