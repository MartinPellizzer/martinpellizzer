Salvia microphylla var. wislizeni

Salvia microphylla var. wislizeni, commonly known as the wislizeni sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, narrow gray-green leaves, and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens. Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
