Silene × dubia

Silene × dubia, commonly known as the hybrid campion, is a perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Silene gallica and Silene suecica, characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene × dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological studies of hybridization.
