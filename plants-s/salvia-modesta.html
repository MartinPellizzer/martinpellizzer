Salvia modesta

Salvia modesta, commonly known as meadow sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

Salvia modesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
