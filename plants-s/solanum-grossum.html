Solanum grossum

Solanum grossum, commonly known as the large-fruited nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by berries that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and emit a mild, often unpleasant odor.

Solanum grossum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
