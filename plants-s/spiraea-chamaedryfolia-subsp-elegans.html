Spiraea chamaedryfolia subsp. elegans

Spiraea chamaedryfolia subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the elegant meadow spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, arching stems covered in dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has alternate, narrow, and serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate floral display and compact growth habit.
