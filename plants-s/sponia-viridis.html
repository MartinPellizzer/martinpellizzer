Sponia viridis

Sponia viridis, commonly known as the green sponge plant, is a unique species characterized by its spongy, leaf-like structures that resemble a sponge.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's foliage is typically a vibrant green, with a textured surface that aids in water retention. It reproduces through spores, which are released into the air and settle on moist surfaces to germinate.

Due to its unusual appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
