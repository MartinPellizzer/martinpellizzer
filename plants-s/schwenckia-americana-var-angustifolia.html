Schwenckia americana var. angustifolia

Schwenckia americana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in moist meadows and along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
