Sapium bolivianum

Sapium bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds with a tuft of hair, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, lowland areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
