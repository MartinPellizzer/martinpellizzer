Senecio polyrhizus

Senecio polyrhizus, commonly known as the clustered groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

Senecio polyrhizus is often considered an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
