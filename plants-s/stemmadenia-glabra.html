Stemmadenia glabra

Stemmadenia glabra, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed stemmadenia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, cylindrical stems that are often woody and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly rough texture.

Stemmadenia glabra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
