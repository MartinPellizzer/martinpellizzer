Smilax brevipes var. angustifolia

Smilax brevipes var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf catclaw, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features stout, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Its distinctive morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
