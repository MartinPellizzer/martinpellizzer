Securigera carinata

Securigera carinata, commonly known as the spiny senna, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features prominently thorned stems and leaves, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. Its small yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated, ribbed pods, which are typical of the legume family.
