Sigesbeckia pringlei subsp. tuerckheimii

Sigesbeckia pringlei subsp.

tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Chinese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
