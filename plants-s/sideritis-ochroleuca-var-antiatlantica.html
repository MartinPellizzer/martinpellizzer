Sideritis ochroleuca var. antiatlantica

Sideritis ochroleuca var. antiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic mountain sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
