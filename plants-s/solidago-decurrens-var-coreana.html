Solidago decurrens var. coreana

Solidago decurrens var. coreana, commonly known as Korean goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of small, bright yellow ray florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate regions.
