Struthiopteris discolor

Struthiopteris discolor, commonly known as the purple fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its distinctive fronds that display a range of colors, from green to purple and brown. Native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant's rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
