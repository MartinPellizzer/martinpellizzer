Solanum tuberosum f. surico

Solanum tuberosum f. surico, commonly known as the Surico potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, which includes elongated shapes and a distinct skin coloration. This variety is native to the Andes region and is adapted to high-altitude growing conditions. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, sharing traits with other nightshade species.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural research and potential agricultural use.
