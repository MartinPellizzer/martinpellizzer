Saussurea affinis

Saussurea affinis, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of yellow to orange flower heads. The plant has fibrous roots and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its alpine habitat.

Saussurea affinis is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine systems.
