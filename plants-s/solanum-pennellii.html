Solanum pennellii

Solanum pennellii, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in arid environments.

Solanum pennellii is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
