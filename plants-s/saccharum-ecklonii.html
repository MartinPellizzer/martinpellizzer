Saccharum ecklonii

Saccharum ecklonii, commonly known as the tall cane grass, is a tall perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, erect stems that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a rough, fibrous texture, and it produces panicles of small, whitish flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Saccharum ecklonii is valued for its strong, fibrous stalks, which have been used historically for thatching and other traditional purposes.
