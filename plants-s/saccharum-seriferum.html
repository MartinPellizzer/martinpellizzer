Saccharum seriferum

Saccharum seriferum, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to wetlands and marshy areas in the Americas.

It can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring long, narrow leaves and dense clusters of small, fluffy flowers. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. It is often found in shallow water or damp soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its fibrous stalks have been used historically for thatching and basket weaving.
