Saussurea sagitta

Saussurea sagitta, commonly known as the arrowhead saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and numerous yellowish to brownish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while the flower heads are arranged in a compact, spherical shape.

Saussurea sagitta is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
