Saccharum alopecuroides subvar. angustifolium

Saccharum alopecuroides subvar. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved sugarcane, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrower than those of other sugarcane varieties. The plant produces tall, flowering stalks that can reach several meters in height. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soils.

This subvariety is often cultivated for its potential in biofuel production and as a source of sugar.
