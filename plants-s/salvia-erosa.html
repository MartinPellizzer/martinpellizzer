Salvia erosa

Salvia erosa, commonly known as pink evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
