Salvia castanea

Salvia castanea, commonly known as the copperleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking copper-colored leaves and spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Salvia castanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
