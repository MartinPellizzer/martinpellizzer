Scorzonera crocifolia

Scorzonera crocifolia, commonly known as the creeping scorpionvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with four petals. The plant has a taproot that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Scorzonera crocifolia is also valued as a source of nectar for pollinators.
