Sarcoglottis sagittata

Sarcoglottis sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a feature common in the Araceae family. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary botanical interest lies in its morphology and growth habits.

Sarcoglottis sagittata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
