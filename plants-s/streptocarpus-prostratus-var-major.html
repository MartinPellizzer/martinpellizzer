Streptocarpus prostratus var. major

Streptocarpus prostratus var. major, commonly known as the dwarf streptocarpus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

This variety is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in moderate humidity conditions.
