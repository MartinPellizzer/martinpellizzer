Senecio scalaris

Senecio scalaris, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio scalaris is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
