Staphylea coulombieri

Staphylea coulombieri, commonly known as the African sweetshrub, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Staphyleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it produces small, round berries that are edible. Staphylea coulombieri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
