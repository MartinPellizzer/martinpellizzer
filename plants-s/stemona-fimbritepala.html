Stemona fimbritepala

Stemona fimbritepala, commonly known as the broadleaf stemona, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, branching stem that is often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, dark green leaves. Its roots are rich in alkaloids and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
