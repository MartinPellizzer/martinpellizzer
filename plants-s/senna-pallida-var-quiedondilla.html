Senna pallida var. quiedondilla

Senna pallida var. quiedondilla, commonly known as the Quiedong tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly hairy.

The Quiedong tree is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
