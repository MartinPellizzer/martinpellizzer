Senna ripleyana

Senna ripleyana, commonly known as the Ripleys senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, feather-like leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
