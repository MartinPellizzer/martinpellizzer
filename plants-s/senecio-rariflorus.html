Senecio rariflorus

Senecio rariflorus, commonly known as the rareflower senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a compact cluster, and bloom during the cooler months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
