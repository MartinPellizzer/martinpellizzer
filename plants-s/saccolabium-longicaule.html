Saccolabium longicaule

Saccolabium longicaule, commonly known as the long-stemmed saccolabium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
