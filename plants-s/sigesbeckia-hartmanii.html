Sigesbeckia hartmanii

Sigesbeckia hartmanii, commonly known as the Hartman's sigesbeckia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, herbaceous stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Sigesbeckia hartmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
