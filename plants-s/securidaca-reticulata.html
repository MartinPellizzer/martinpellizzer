Securidaca reticulata

Securidaca reticulata, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical with a rough texture and a strong, spicy scent.

Securidaca reticulata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
