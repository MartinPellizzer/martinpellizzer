Solanum flagrans

Solanum flagrans, commonly known as the spreading nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, featuring woody stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs on the leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Solanum flagrans is part of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
