Scleranthus myriocarpus

Scleranthus myriocarpus, commonly known as the many-seeded sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and seed dispersal, it can become a weed in agricultural settings.
