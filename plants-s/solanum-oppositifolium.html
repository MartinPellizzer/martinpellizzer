Solanum oppositifolium

Solanum oppositifolium, commonly known as the oppositifoliate nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and produces small, often toxic berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Due to its toxic properties, caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
