Solanum volubile

Solanum volubile, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a trailing or climbing plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to berries that change color from green to red as they mature. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in a variety of conditions.

However, its berries are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.
