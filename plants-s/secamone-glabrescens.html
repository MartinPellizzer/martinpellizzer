Secamone glabrescens

Secamone glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth secamone, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glabrous. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have a yellowish or greenish hue with prominent stamens.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
