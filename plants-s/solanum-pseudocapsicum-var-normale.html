Solanum pseudocapsicum var. normale

Solanum pseudocapsicum var. normale, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into berries containing toxic compounds. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the stems are typically hairy as well.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
