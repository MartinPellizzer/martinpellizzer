Syzygium acrophilum

Syzygium acrophilum, commonly known as the mountain apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries. These berries are rich in nutrients and have been traditionally used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in montane forests.
