Senecio gamolepis

Senecio gamolepis, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow ray florets and green disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
