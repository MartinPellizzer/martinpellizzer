Sasaella iwatekensis var. hidaensis

Sasaella iwatekensis var. hidaensis, commonly known as the Hida bamboo, is a variant of the Japanese bamboo species native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming grass that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in forested areas.
