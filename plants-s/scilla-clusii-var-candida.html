Scilla clusii var. candida

Scilla clusii var. candida, commonly known as the white scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers in early spring. The flowers are typically 2-3 cm in diameter and have a delicate, fragrant scent. This variety is closely related to other scilla species and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland or rock garden settings.
