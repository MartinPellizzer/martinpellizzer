Schizachyrium fragile var. shimadae

Schizachyrium fragile var. shimadae, commonly known as the Japanese feather grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery plumes that give it a soft, flowing appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest in both landscape and garden settings.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
