Salacia ekoka

Salacia ekoka, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, though it is more closely related to the genus Salacia. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Salacia ekoka is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
