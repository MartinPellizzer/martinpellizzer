Scaevola corsoniana

Scaevola corsoniana, commonly known as the Blue Mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a velvety texture, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Scaevola corsoniana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
