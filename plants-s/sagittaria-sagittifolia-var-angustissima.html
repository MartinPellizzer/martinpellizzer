Sagittaria sagittifolia var. angustissima

Sagittaria sagittifolia var. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It features narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive V-shaped leaf base. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, typically in summer. It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams.

Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and it is often used in wetland restoration projects.
