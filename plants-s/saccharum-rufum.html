Saccharum rufum

Saccharum rufum, commonly known as the red grass or red bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with a reddish hue in the leaves and stems, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Saccharum rufum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
