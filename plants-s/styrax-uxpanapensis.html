Styrax uxpanapensis

Styrax uxpanapensis, commonly known as the Mexican styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is thick and fissured.

Styrax uxpanapensis thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.
