Solanum chacoense f. pilosulum

Solanum chacoense f. pilosulum, commonly known as the Chaco nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the dry regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy foliage and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. It produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its potential use in traditional medicine.
