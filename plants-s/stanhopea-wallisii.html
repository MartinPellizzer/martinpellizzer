Stanhopea wallisii

Stanhopea wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis's stanhopea, is a tropical orchid native to the wet forests of Costa Rica and Panama.

It features a striking, elongated, and often colorful flower spike with numerous showy, fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often pale yellow to orange with contrasting darker veins, and it blooms during the dry season.

Stanhopea wallisii is valued in horticulture for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
