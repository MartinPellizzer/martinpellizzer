Syzygium venustum

Syzygium venustum, commonly known as the fragrant satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.

Syzygium venustum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
