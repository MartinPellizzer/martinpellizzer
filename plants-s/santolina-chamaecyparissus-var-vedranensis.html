Santolina chamaecyparissus var. vedranensis

Santolina chamaecyparissus var. vedranensis, commonly known as the Vedranensis santolina, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with a dense, mat-like growth habit, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a border plant. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, adding visual interest. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, requiring well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
