Sorbus manshuriensis

Sorbus manshuriensis, commonly known as the Manchurian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
