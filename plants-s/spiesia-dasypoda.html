Spiesia dasypoda

Spiesia dasypoda, commonly known as the roughleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its latex is milky and can be toxic, a common trait among plants in the Euphorbiaceae family.

Spiesia dasypoda is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
