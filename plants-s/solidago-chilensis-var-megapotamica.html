Solidago chilensis var. megapotamica

Solidago chilensis var. megapotamica, commonly known as the Andean goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in botanical research.
