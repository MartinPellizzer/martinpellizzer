Selenicereus tricae

Selenicereus tricae, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces white, star-shaped blooms that emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Selenicereus tricae is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, arid environments with minimal water.

Its unique blooming habit and striking appearance make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and botanical collections.
