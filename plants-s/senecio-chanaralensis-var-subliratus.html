Senecio chanaralensis var. subliratus

Senecio chanaralensis var. subliratus, commonly known as the coastal senecio, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in capitula, typical of the daisy family. It is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its growth habit is usually low and spreading, making it a hardy plant suited to harsh, sun-exposed conditions.
