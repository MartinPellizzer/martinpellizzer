Satureja masukuensis

Satureja masukuensis, commonly known as Masuku savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, and its leaves are aromatic, often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Satureja masukuensis is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its antimicrobial properties.
