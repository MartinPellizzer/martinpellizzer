Salix phylicifolia var. laurina

Salix phylicifolia var. laurina, commonly known as the silver willow, is a variant of the willow family native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in riparian zones. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring.

The bark is often reddish-brown and peels in thin layers, contributing to its ornamental value.
