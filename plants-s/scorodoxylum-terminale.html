Scorodoxylum terminale

Scorodoxylum terminale, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing some protection against fire and drought.
