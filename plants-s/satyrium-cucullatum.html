Satyrium cucullatum

Satyrium cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive hooded shape that shelters the reproductive organs.

Satyrium cucullatum is a perennial herb with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.
