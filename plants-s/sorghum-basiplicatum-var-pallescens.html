Sorghum basiplicatum var. pallescens

Sorghum basiplicatum var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale sorghum, is a wild species native to Africa.

It is a tall, robust grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces panicles of small, pale green or yellowish flowers. Its seeds are small and hard, adapted for dispersal by wind.

This species is of interest for its potential use in forage or as a genetic resource for crop improvement.
