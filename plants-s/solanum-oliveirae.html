Solanum oliveirae

Solanum oliveirae, commonly known as the Oliveira's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by berries containing toxic alkaloids. It is often found in open areas and forest edges, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
