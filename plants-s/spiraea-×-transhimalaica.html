Spiraea × transhimalaica

Spiraea × transhimalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features arching branches covered in clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early summer, which are followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
