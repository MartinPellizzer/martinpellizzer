Scrophularia scabiosifolia subsp. stewartii

Scrophularia scabiosifolia subsp.

stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's scrophularia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is valued for its distinctive appearance and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
