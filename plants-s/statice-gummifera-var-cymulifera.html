Statice gummifera var. cymulifera

Statice gummifera var. cymulifera, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, spike-like clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a fine layer of salt-tolerant wax.

This variety is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
