Scaevola beckii var. robusta

Scaevola beckii var. robusta, commonly known as the coastal scabious, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dune environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
