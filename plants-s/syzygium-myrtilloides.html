Syzygium myrtilloides

Syzygium myrtilloides, commonly known as the purple-leaf syzygium, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that turn a striking purple color when mature. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by edible, dark purple berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Syzygium myrtilloides thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
