Salix Pedicellaris Var. Athabascensis

Salix pedicellaris var. athabascensis is a rare and hardy willow species native to the northern regions of North America. It thrives in harsh environments such as tundra and wetlands, showcasing remarkable adaptability to cold climates. This variant is distinguished by its slender stems and small, clustered leaves that help it conserve heat and moisture. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable indicator of ecological health in its habitat. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique plant from environmental threats.
