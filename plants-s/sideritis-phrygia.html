Sideritis phrygia

Sideritis phrygia, commonly known as Phrygian sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Sideritis phrygia is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
