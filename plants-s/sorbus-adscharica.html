Sorbus adscharica

Sorbus adscharica, commonly known as the Caucasian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
