Spergula arvensis var. pomeliana

Spergula arvensis var. pomeliana, commonly known as the field sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and roadside areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers in spikelets. This variant is often found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
