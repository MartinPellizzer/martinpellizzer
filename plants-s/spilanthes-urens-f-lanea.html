Spilanthes urens f. lanea

Spilanthes urens f. lanea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toothache plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant is notable for its pungent, peppery scent and the presence of alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal uses. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its potential health benefits and ornamental value.
