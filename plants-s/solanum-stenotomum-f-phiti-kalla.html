Solanum stenotomum f. phiti-kalla

Solanum stenotomum f. phiti-kalla, commonly known as the Phiti-Kalla tomato, is a botanical variant of the tomato species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, red, and often elongated fruits that resemble cherry tomatoes in appearance. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.

This variety is noted for its unique flavor profile and is sometimes used in traditional cooking.
