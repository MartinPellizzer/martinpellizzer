Senecio libanoticus

Senecio libanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanon groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads.

Senecio libanoticus is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
