Synaptolepis pachyphylla

Synaptolepis pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved synaptolepis, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its woody stems are typically branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

Synaptolepis pachyphylla is often found in dry woodlands and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals.
