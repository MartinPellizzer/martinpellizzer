Sphenocarpus pterocarpus

Sphenocarpus pterocarpus, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The tree produces hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
