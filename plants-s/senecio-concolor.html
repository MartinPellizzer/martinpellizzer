Senecio concolor

Senecio concolor, commonly known as the bluegum lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Senecio concolor is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
