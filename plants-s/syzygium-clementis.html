Syzygium clementis

Syzygium clementis, commonly known as the Clementine apple or clementine tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, sweet, and edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of small, round fruits. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

Syzygium clementis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its contribution to local ecosystems and agriculture.
