Silene italica subsp. coutinhoi

Silene italica subsp.

coutinhoi, commonly known as the Italian catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its delicate pink to red flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the genus Silene.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is considered a rare and endangered plant in some areas.
