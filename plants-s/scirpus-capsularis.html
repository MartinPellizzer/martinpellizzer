Scirpus capsularis

Scirpus capsularis, commonly known as the common bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, three-lobed flowers. Its roots are fibrous and often form dense mats in wetland environments.

Scirpus capsularis is commonly found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.
