Stachys cretica subsp. anatolica

Stachys cretica subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey and surrounding areas. It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers in whorls along the upper stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
