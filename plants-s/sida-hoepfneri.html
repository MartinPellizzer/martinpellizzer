Sida hoepfneri

Sida hoepfneri, commonly known as the silverleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Sida hoepfneri thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often seen along roadsides and in disturbed areas.
