Sloanea usurpatrix

Sloanea usurpatrix, commonly known as the creeping sloanea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth of rainforests.

Due to its adaptability, it can sometimes become invasive in certain regions outside its native habitat.
