Solidago petiolaris var. angusta

Solidago petiolaris var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
