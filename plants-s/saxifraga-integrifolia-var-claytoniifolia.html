Saxifraga integrifolia var. claytoniifolia

Saxifraga integrifolia var. claytoniifolia, commonly known as Clayton's leaf saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

This variant is notable for its distinctive foliage and is often found in alpine and tundra habitats.
