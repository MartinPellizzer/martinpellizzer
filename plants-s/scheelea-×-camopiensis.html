Scheelea × camopiensis

Scheelea × camopiensis, commonly known as the Camopiensis palm, is a hybrid species native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible, coconut-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
