Sorbus herbipolitana

Sorbus herbipolitana, commonly known as the Heribolite rowan, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in woodlands and rocky habitats.

The fruit is an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
