Symphyotrichum novae-angliae

Symphyotrichum novae-angliae, commonly known as New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy purple or pink flower heads that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
