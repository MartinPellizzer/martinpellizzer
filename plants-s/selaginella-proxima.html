Selaginella proxima

Selaginella proxima, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a species of vascular fern that belongs to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around its stems. It reproduces via spores, which are produced in specialized structures called strobila.

Selaginella proxima is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
