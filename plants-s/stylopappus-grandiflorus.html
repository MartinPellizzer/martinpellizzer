Stylopappus grandiflorus

Stylopappus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered stylopappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow-orange ray flowers surrounded by tubular disc flowers, making it visually striking. It typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and hairy leaves adapted to withstand dry conditions.

Stylopappus grandiflorus is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
