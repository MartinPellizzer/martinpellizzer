Syzygium tamilnadense

Syzygium tamilnadense, commonly known as the Tamil Nadu apple or wild apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the southern regions of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, apple-like fruits that are typically harvested when they turn red or yellow. Syzygium tamilnadense thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and potential agricultural purposes.
