Secale cereale var. velutinorufum

Secale cereale var. velutinorufum, commonly known as the red velvet rye, is a variant of rye that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a grass species native to temperate regions and is typically found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant features dense, tufted growth with long, narrow leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
