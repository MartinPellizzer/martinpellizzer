Sophora tonkinensis var. purpurascens

Sophora tonkinensis var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, forming long, pendulous racemes. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a traditional medicinal plant and its ornamental value in horticulture.
