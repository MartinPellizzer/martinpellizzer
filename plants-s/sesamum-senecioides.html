Sesamum senecioides

Sesamum senecioides, commonly known as the African sesame or wild sesame, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Pedaliaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Sesamum senecioides is also known for its aromatic seeds, which have been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
