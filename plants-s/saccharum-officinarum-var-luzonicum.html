Saccharum officinarum var. luzonicum

Saccharum officinarum var. luzonicum, commonly known as Luzon bamboo or bamboo sugar cane, is a variant of the sugarcane plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value rather than for sugar production. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance, it is frequently used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
