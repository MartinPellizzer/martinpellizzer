Sarcocapnos crassifolia var. brevicalcarata

Sarcocapnos crassifolia var. brevicalcarata, commonly known as the short-spined sarcocapnos, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its woody stems are typically branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

This species is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
