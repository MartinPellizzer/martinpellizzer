Salix myrsinites var. pilosa

Salix myrsinites var. pilosa, commonly known as silky willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it blooms in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
