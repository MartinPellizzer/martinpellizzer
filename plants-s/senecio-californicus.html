Senecio californicus

Senecio californicus, commonly known as the California groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer.

Senecio californicus has small, alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.
