Salix erioclada

Salix erioclada, commonly known as the woolly willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, oval leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky white hair. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in the early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
