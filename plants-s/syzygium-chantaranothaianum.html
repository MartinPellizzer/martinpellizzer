Syzygium chantaranothaianum

Syzygium chantaranothaianum, commonly known as the Thai star fruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit, which is typically yellow when ripe. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into the edible fruit after a few months. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and light-colored.

Syzygium chantaranothaianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its flavorful fruit.
