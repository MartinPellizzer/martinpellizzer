Syagrus graminifolia var. nana

Syagrus graminifolia var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf palm or miniature royal palm, is a compact variant of the royal palm, characterized by its short stature and clustered growth habit.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk that is typically covered with a fibrous sheath, and its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, forming a dense crown. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
