Senecio laxifolius

Senecio laxifolius, commonly known as the stiffleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, stiff leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the warmer months. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
