Sabal × brazoriensis

Sabal × brazoriensis, commonly known as the Florida state tree, is a hybrid palm native to the southeastern United States.

It is a sterile hybrid between Sabal palmetto and Sabal mexicana, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has a tall, slender trunk with a crown of stiff, arching leaves that form a fan shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

Due to its ecological importance and aesthetic value, it is a symbol of Florida's natural heritage.
