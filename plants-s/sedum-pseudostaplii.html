Sedum pseudostaplii

Sedum pseudostaplii, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This hardy plant is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
