Senecio amplus

Senecio amplus, commonly known as the broadleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features upright stems with alternate, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in heads, resembling those of dandelions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
