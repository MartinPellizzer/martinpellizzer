Solanum nigrum subsp. nodiflorum

Solanum nigrum subsp.

nodiflorum, commonly known as black nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple or black color.

The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas, demonstrating its adaptability to various environments.

The species is also noted for its chemical composition, which includes alkaloids and other bioactive compounds.
