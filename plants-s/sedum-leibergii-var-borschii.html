Sedum leibergii var. borschii

Sedum leibergii var. borschii, commonly known as Borsch's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it an ideal plant for arid and semi-arid environments.
