Scorzonera hispida

Scorzonera hispida, commonly known as the hispid scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust taproot that is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant has hairy, elongated leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Scorzonera hispida is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
