Sempervivum heuffelii var. bulgaricum

Sempervivum heuffelii var. bulgaricum, commonly known as the Bulgarian houseleek, is a low-growing, mat-forming succulent native to the Balkan region.

It features dense rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with a bluish or reddish tinge, depending on light exposure. The plant produces tall, flowering stalks in late summer, bearing small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
