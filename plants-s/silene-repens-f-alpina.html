Silene repens f. alpina

Silene repens f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine sand catchpole, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in alpine and arctic environments. The plant features slender, prostrate stems covered with fine hairs, and its small, pink or white flowers bloom in clusters. It is adapted to cold climates and can often be found in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
