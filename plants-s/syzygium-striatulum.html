Syzygium striatulum

Syzygium striatulum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves with prominent midribs. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellowish pods containing seeds. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
