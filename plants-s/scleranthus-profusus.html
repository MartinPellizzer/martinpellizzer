Scleranthus profusus

Scleranthus profusus, commonly known as common sickleweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and has a low, sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with five petals. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the stem is often reddish in color.

Scleranthus profusus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
