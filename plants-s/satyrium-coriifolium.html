Satyrium coriifolium

Satyrium coriifolium, commonly known as the woolly orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a distinctive member of the orchid family.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
