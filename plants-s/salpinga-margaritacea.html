Salpinga margaritacea

Salpinga margaritacea, commonly known as the pearl-flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
