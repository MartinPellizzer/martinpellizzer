Solidago rosmarinifolia

Solidago rosmarinifolia, commonly known as coastal goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines.

Solidago rosmarinifolia plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various pollinators.
