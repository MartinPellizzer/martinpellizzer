Senecio pammelii

Senecio pammelii, commonly known as Pammel's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are generally gray-green with a woolly texture.

Senecio pammelii is often found in open areas with well-drained soil and is adapted to arid environments.
