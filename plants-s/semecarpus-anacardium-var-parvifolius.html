Semecarpus anacardium var. parvifolius

Semecarpus anacardium var. parvifolius, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or parvifolius sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, semi-evergreen forests.

The leaves and bark contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
