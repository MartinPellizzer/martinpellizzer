Scindapsus subcordatus

Scindapsus subcordatus, commonly known as the satin pothos, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers moist, well-draining soil. It is known for its ability to climb or trail, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or shelves.

Its air-purifying qualities also make it a favored indoor plant.
