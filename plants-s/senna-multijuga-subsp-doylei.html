Senna multijuga subsp. doylei

Senna multijuga subsp.

doylei, commonly known as the narrowleaf senna, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets, and it is adapted to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
