Stipa × tadzhikistanica

Stipa × tadzhikistanica, commonly known as the Tajikistan needlegrass, is a hybrid grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as steppes and mountain slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and resilience.
