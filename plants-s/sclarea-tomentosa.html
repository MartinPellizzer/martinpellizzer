Sclarea tomentosa

Sclarea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly angelica, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, grayish-green leaves and large, umbrella-shaped umbels of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its aromatic leaves and flowers contain various volatile oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Sclarea tomentosa is also valued in perfumery for its subtle, spicy scent.
