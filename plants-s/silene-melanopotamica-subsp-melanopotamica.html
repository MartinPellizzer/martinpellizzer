Silene melanopotamica subsp. melanopotamica

Silene melanopotamica subsp.

melanopotamica, commonly known as the Namib Desert flax, is a perennial herb native to the Namib Desert in Namibia. It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that store water, adapting to the arid environment.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to extreme drought and high temperatures, making it a unique example of desert flora.
