Stipa appendiculata

Stipa appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects and as a ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
