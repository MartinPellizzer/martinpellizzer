Sykesia

Sykesia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, thick leaves and showy, often violet-colored flowers. Sykesia species typically grow as shrubs or small trees, with some varieties reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences, and the genus is known for its ornamental value.

Sykesia plants are adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
