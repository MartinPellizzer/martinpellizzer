Syzygium brazzavillense

Syzygium brazzavillense, commonly known as the Congo sandpaper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Central Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its rough, sandpaper-like bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by edible, woody fruits.

Syzygium brazzavillense is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
