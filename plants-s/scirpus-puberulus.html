Scirpus puberulus

Scirpus puberulus, commonly known as the soft bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it is often found in shallow water or moist soils. Scirpus puberulus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to the structure of its habitat.
