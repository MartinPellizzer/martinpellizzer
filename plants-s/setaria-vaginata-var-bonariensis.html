Setaria vaginata var. bonariensis

Setaria vaginata var. bonariensis, commonly known as blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
