Sparrmannia ricinocarpa subsp. micrantha

Sparrmannia ricinocarpa subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sparrmannia, is a member of the Ericaceae family. It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
