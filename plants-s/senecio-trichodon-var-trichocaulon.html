Senecio trichodon var. trichocaulon

Senecio trichodon var. trichocaulon, commonly known as the hairy leaf groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact, low-growing habit with succulent-like leaves that store water.

The plant is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
