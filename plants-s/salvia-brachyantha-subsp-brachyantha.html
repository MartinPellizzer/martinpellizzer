Salvia brachyantha subsp. brachyantha

Salvia brachyantha subsp.

brachyantha, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico. It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white, typically in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it both a horticultural and ecological asset.
