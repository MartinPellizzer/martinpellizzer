Selaginella doederleinii

Selaginella doederleinii, commonly known as the Hart's tongue fern, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a terrestrial, evergreen plant characterized by its long, strap-like leaves that resemble a tongue, hence its common name. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

Selaginella doederleinii is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique reproductive structures that produce spores for propagation.
