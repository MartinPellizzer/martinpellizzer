Senecio versicolor

Senecio versicolor, commonly known as the Variable Senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow to orange flower heads that are surrounded by green bracts, creating a striking visual contrast. Senecio versicolor typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
