Senecio carnerensis

Senecio carnerensis, commonly known as the Carner Valley groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
