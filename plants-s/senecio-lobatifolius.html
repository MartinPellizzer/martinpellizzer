Senecio lobatifolius

Senecio lobatifolius, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
