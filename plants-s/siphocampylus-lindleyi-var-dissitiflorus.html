Siphocampylus lindleyi var. dissitiflorus

Siphocampylus lindleyi var. dissitiflorus, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and densely covered with fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are usually pink to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
