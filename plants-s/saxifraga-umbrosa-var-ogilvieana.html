Saxifraga umbrosa var. ogilvieana

Saxifraga umbrosa var. ogilvieana, commonly known as the Highland saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in woodland and heathland habitats. The plant features dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, damp conditions.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to form dense mats, making it a suitable ground cover in shaded areas.
