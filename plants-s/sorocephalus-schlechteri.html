Sorocephalus schlechteri

Sorocephalus schlechteri, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Sorocephalus schlechteri is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
