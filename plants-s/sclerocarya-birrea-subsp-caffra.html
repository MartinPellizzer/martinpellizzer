Sclerocarya birrea subsp. caffra

Sclerocarya birrea subsp.

caffra, commonly known as the marula tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain a single large seed. The fruit is renowned for its high oil content and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.

It also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
