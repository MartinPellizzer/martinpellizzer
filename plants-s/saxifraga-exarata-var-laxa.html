Saxifraga exarata var. laxa

Saxifraga exarata var. laxa, commonly known as the slender saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Saxifraga exarata var. laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
