Sambucus javanica f. chinensis

Sambucus javanica f. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese elderberry, is a variant of the elderberry plant native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters. The plant features compound leaves with 5–7 leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. These flowers give way to dark purple berries in the fall, which are edible but should be consumed with caution.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
