Salsola chenopodiana

Salsola chenopodiana, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit and is native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

Salsola chenopodiana is also known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
