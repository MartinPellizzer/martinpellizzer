Scirpus inanis

Scirpus inanis, commonly known as slender bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and typically grows in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has tall, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can stabilize shorelines.

Scirpus inanis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
