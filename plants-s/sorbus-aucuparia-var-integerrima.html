Sorbus aucuparia var. integerrima

Sorbus aucuparia var. integerrima, commonly known as the white-tailed mountain ash, is a variant of the common rowan tree.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow berries that mature in the fall. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often growing in mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
