Salix pedicellata subsp. antiatlantica

Salix pedicellata subsp.

antiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic willow, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of wind pollination. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
