Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp. brachycalyx

Sideritis hyssopifolia subsp.

brachycalyx, commonly known as narrow-calyx ironwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter calyces compared to other members of the species.
