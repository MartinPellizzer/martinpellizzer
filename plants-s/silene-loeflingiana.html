Silene loeflingiana

Silene loeflingiana, commonly known as the Loefling's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often sprawling or upright depending on environmental conditions.

Silene loeflingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
