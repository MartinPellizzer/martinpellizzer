Solanum volkensii var. himatacanthum

Solanum volkensii var. himatacanthum, commonly known as the red-stemmed nightshade, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by violet or blue berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be toxic if ingested.
