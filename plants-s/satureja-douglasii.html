Satureja douglasii

Satureja douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's savory, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small purple flowers in the summer.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
