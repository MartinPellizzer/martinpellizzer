Smilax semiamplexicaulis

Smilax semiamplexicaulis, commonly known as the climbing smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a unique structure, where the leaflets are partially clasping the stem, giving it its species name. The plant produces yellowish-green, spiky burrs that contain the seeds. Its stems are covered in sharp, thorn-like protrusions, which help it climb and defend itself.

Smilax semiamplexicaulis is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
