Spiraea japonica subvar. bullata

Spiraea japonica subvar. bullata, commonly known as the Japanese weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cascading clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. Its leaves are ovate and dark green, turning yellow in the fall.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
