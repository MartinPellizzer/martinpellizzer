Stachys annua var. lycaonica

Stachys annua var. lycaonica, commonly known as the Lycaonian hemp-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in herb gardens and wildflower habitats.
