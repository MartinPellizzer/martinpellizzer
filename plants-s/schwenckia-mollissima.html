Schwenckia mollissima

Schwenckia mollissima, commonly known as the softleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are soft to the touch, giving it its common name. Its showy purple to pink flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Schwenckia mollissima is often found in moist woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
