Salix × eriocataphylloides

Salix × eriocataphylloides, commonly known as the silver willow, is a hybrid shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
