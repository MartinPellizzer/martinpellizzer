Senecio sarracenicus subsp. jacquinianus

Senecio sarracenicus subsp.

jacquinianus, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, yellowish to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
