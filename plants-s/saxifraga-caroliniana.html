Saxifraga caroliniana

Saxifraga caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in early spring.

Saxifraga caroliniana is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinators.
