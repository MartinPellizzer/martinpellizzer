Solanum malacoxylon f. vulgare

Solanum malacoxylon f. vulgare, commonly known as the roughleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

The plant contains alkaloids and is considered toxic to humans and animals, though it has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
