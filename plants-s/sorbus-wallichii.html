Sorbus wallichii

Sorbus wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually alternate and pinnate, with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
