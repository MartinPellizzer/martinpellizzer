Solanum baturitense

Solanum baturitense, commonly known as the Baturite nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with simple, alternate leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. It produces berries that are toxic to humans but may serve as a food source for certain wildlife.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
