Solanum violifolium f. lilaciniflorum

Solanum violifolium f. lilaciniflorum, commonly known as the lilac-flowered nightshade, is a botanical variant of the species Solanum violifolium.

It is characterized by its striking lilac-colored flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to regions of southern Africa. It typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, edible berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
