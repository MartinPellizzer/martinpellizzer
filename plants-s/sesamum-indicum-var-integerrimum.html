Sesamum indicum var. integerrimum

Sesamum indicum var. integerrimum, commonly known as the African sesame or Ethiopian sesame, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Sesamum.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, oblong seeds. These seeds are rich in oil and are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, dry climates and is cultivated for its nutritional value and aromatic properties.
