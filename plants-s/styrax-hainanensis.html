Styrax hainanensis

Styrax hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan styrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern Chinese province of Hainan.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a sticky, aromatic resin, which is used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments.

Styrax hainanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
