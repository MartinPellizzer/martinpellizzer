Schizonotus ariifolius

Schizonotus ariifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Schizonotus ariifolius is characterized by its robust rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies.
