Sesbania atollensis

Sesbania atollensis, commonly known as the atoll sesbania, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Indian Ocean and Southeast Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Its seeds are often dispersed by water, facilitating its spread across atolls and islands.
