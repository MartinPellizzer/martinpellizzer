Scoparia montevidensis var. flava

Scoparia montevidensis var. flava, commonly known as the yellow scoparia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters, making it visually striking.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
