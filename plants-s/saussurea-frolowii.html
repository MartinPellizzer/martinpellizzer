Saussurea frolowii

Saussurea frolowii, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a woolly texture, adapted to minimize water loss in harsh climates.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
