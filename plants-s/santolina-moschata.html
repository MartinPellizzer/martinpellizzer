Santolina moschata

Santolina moschata, commonly known as sweet santolina, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with a fragrant, herbaceous scent, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Santolina moschata is also used in herbal medicine for its aromatic and antiseptic properties.
