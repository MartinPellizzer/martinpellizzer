Saponaria elegans

Saponaria elegans, commonly known as the "Dove's Foot" or "Saponary," is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a milky sap that is soapy to the touch, giving it its scientific name "saponaria," derived from the Latin word for soap. Saponaria elegans thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
