Scorzonera fischeriana

Scorzonera fischeriana, commonly known as the Fischer's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Scorzonera fischeriana is also valued for its edible tuberous roots, which have been traditionally used in local cuisines.
