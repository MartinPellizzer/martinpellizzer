Salvia galpinii

Salvia galpinii, commonly known as Galpin's sage, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Salvia galpinii is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
