Scirpidium sulcatum

Scirpidium sulcatum, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often ribbed and have a zigzag pattern along their edges. The plant produces small, clustered greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Scirpidium sulcatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
