Syzygium tetrapterum var. pseudotetrapterum

Syzygium tetrapterum var. pseudotetrapterum, commonly known as the red junglefowl, is a variant of the Syzygium genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The fruit is a bright red drupe, which is edible and often used in traditional medicine. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
