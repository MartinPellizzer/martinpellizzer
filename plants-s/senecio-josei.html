Senecio josei

Senecio josei, commonly known as the Joseph's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with narrow, hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Senecio josei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
