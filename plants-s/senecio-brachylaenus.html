Senecio brachylaenus

Senecio brachylaenus, commonly known as the blue ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are densely packed and covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental touch. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking an eye-catching, hardy plant.
