Salix nummularia subsp. pauciflora

Salix nummularia subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow, is a shrub native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and produces catkins that are often clustered in groups of three.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tundra and subarctic ecosystems.
