Salix arctica subvar. oblonga

Salix arctica subvar. oblonga, commonly known as the arctic willow, is a low-growing shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, oblong leaves and small, catkin-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.

Its woody stems and persistent leaves allow it to survive extreme temperature fluctuations and limited sunlight.
