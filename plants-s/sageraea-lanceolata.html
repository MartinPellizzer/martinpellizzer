Sageraea lanceolata

Sageraea lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf sageretia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Sageraea lanceolata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.
