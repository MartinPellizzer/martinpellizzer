Solanum ludovicianum

Solanum ludovicianum, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval, yellow-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits resembling tomatoes. The fruits are edible and contain numerous seeds, making it a valuable species for both culinary and ecological purposes.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
