Sarracenia rubra subsp. wherryi

Sarracenia rubra subsp.

wherryi, commonly known as the Wherry's Pitcher Plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and tubular, reddish-orange pitchers that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant's pitchers secrete a digestive enzyme-laden fluid to trap and digest insects. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires high humidity and bright light for optimal growth.

This subspecies is notable for its striking coloration and is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
