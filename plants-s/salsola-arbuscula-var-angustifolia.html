Salsola arbuscula var. angustifolia

Salsola arbuscula var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf Russian thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with gray-green, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.

Its succulent stems and leaves help it store water, making it a resilient species in arid climates.
