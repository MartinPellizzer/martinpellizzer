Sporobolus trichophorus

Sporobolus trichophorus, commonly known as the sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to sandy and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open habitats. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, a feature that contributes to its widespread distribution.

Sporobolus trichophorus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
