Salix nebrodensis

Salix nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebrodensis willow, is a small, deciduous tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has slender, zigzagging branches and produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue.

Salix nebrodensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal and mountainous terrains.
