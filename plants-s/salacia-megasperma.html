Salacia megasperma

Salacia megasperma, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish bark and opposite leaf arrangement.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.
