Silene lazica

Silene lazica, commonly known as the Caucasian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and clusters of small, pink or reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate to alpine climates. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Silene lazica is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
