Saussurea peduncularis var. corymbosa

Saussurea peduncularis var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that bear numerous small, blue to violet flowers arranged in corymbs. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking floral display.
