Sclerolobium leiocalyx

Sclerolobium leiocalyx, commonly known as the white acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often glandular and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its woody, thorned stems are typical of the Fabaceae family, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
