Syzygium vismioides

Syzygium vismioides, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
