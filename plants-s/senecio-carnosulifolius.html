Senecio carnosulifolius

Senecio carnosulifolius, commonly known as the meaty-leaved senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
