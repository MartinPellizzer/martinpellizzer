Styphelia adscendens

Styphelia adscendens, commonly known as the ascending heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, urn-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in winter and spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its habitat.

Styphelia adscendens is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.
