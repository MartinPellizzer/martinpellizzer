Sophora linearifolia

Sophora linearifolia, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are elongated and linear, giving it its species name "linearifolia.". The plant produces striking clusters of pale blue or purple flowers in the spring, which are followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
