Silaus banaticus

Silaus banaticus, commonly known as Banat's silenus, is a perennial plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinctive, slightly bitter taste.

Silaus banaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
