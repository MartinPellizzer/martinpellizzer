Schizophragma hypoglaucum

Schizophragma hypoglaucum, commonly known as the silver vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in late spring, followed by blue-black berries. It is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb walls or trellises.

Botanically, it belongs to the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae) and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
