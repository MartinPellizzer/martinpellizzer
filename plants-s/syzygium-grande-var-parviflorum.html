Syzygium grande var. parviflorum

Syzygium grande var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered apple cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
