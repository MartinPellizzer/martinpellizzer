Scutellaria albida subsp. pycnotricha

Scutellaria albida subsp.

pycnotricha, commonly known as the white skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers, which are usually white or pale blue, bloom in late summer and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits.
