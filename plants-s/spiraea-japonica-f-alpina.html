Spiraea japonica f. alpina

Spiraea japonica f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine weeping spirea, is a compact, deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and cascading clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant features finely cut, serrated leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Spiraea japonica f. alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscapes for its elegant, flowing form.
