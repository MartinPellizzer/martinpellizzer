Stanleya viridiflora

Stanleya viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and forest edges.

Stanleya viridiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
