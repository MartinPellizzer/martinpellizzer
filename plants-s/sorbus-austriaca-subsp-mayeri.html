Sorbus austriaca subsp. mayeri

Sorbus austriaca subsp.

mayeri, commonly known as the Austrian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and small, oval leaves.

The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
