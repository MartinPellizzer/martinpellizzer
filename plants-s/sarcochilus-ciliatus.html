Sarcochilus ciliatus

Sarcochilus ciliatus, commonly known as the thread-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It features slender, hairy stems and linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with prominent, hairy sepals and petals. It thrives in damp, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Sarcochilus ciliatus is known for its unique morphology and is a subject of interest in botanical studies.
