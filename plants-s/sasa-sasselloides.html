Sasa sasselloides

Sasa sasselloides, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Sasa sasselloides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
