Saxifraga subspathulata var. kumaunensis

Saxifraga subspathulata var. kumaunensis, commonly known as the Kumaun saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows at elevations between 2,500 and 4,500 meters, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant features upright, hairy stems with alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in cymes and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
