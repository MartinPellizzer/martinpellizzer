Solanum tuberosum f. huichinkka

Solanum tuberosum f. huichinkka, commonly known as the Huichinka potato, is a botanical variant of the common potato.

It is characterized by its unique tuber morphology, often featuring a more elongated shape and distinct skin coloration. This variety is native to the Andean region of South America, where it has been cultivated for generations. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and exhibits typical solanum growth habits, including hairy stems and compound leaves.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and potential for agricultural improvement.
