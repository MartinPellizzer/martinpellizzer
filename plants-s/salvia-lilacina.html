Salvia lilacina

Salvia lilacina, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, lavender-blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Salvia lilacina is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
