Selaginella martensii var. divaricata

Selaginella martensii var. divaricata, commonly known as the zigzag spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and distinctive, zigzag-shaped stems. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the spores for reproduction. Its leaves are typically scale-like and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.
