Serapias longibracteata

Serapias longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, flowering stem with several blooms. Its flowers have a unique structure with a spurred lip, typical of many orchid species.

Serapias longibracteata thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
