Sabicea congensis

Sabicea congensis, commonly known as the Congo sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
