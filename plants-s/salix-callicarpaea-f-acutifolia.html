Salix callicarpaea f. acutifolia

Salix callicarpaea f. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved weeping willow, is a unique form of the weeping willow species.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and ponds. Its catkin flowers appear in early spring, contributing to its ecological role in pollination.

The plant's rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
