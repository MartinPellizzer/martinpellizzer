Sinocarum shanii

Sinocarum shanii, commonly known as the Shan mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Sinocarum shanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
