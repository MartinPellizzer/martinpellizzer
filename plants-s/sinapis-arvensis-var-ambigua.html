Sinapis arvensis var. ambigua

Sinapis arvensis var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous field mustard, is a variant of the field mustard species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its invasive potential in certain regions.
