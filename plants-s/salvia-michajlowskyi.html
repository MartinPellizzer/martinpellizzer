Salvia michajlowskyi

Salvia michajlowskyi, commonly known as the Michajlowsky sage, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of southern Peru.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
