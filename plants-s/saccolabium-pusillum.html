Saccolabium pusillum

Saccolabium pusillum, commonly known as the small-flowered saccolabium, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
