Sterculia ovalifolia

Sterculia ovalifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
