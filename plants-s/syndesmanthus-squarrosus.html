Syndesmanthus squarrosus

Syndesmanthus squarrosus, commonly known as the squarrosus hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

Syndesmanthus squarrosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
