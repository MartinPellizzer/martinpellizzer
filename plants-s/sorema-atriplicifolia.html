Sorema atriplicifolia

Sorema atriplicifolia, commonly known as the purple false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged underside. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland or garden settings.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
