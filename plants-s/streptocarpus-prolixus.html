Streptocarpus prolixus

Streptocarpus prolixus, commonly known as the long-flowered streptocarpus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Streptocarpus prolixus is also noted for its ability to produce numerous seed pods, which are distinctive for their twisted, ribbon-like shape.
