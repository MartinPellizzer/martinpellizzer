Stemona filifolia

Stemona filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf stemona, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on elongated pedicels. Its leaves are elongated and linear, with a smooth or slightly rough texture.

Stemona filifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its potential pharmacological properties.
