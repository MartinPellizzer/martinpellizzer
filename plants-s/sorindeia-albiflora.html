Sorindeia albiflora

Sorindeia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered sorindeia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom during the spring and early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a slightly serrated edge.

Sorindeia albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
