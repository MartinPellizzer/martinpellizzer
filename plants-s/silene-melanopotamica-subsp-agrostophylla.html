Silene melanopotamica subsp. agrostophylla

Silene melanopotamica subsp.

agrostophylla, commonly known as the grass-leafed campion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
