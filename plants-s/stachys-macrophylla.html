Stachys macrophylla

Stachys macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Stachys macrophylla is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
