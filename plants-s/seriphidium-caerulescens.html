Seriphidium caerulescens

Seriphidium caerulescens, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.
