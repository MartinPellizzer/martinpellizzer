Senecio tuberculatus

Senecio tuberculatus, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
