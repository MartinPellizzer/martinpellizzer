Sclerolaena blackei

Sclerolaena blackei, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Sclerolaena blackei is drought-tolerant and is used in some regions for ornamental purposes due to its unique visual appeal.
