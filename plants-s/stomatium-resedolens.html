Stomatium resedolens

Stomatium resedolens, commonly known as the red-stemmed aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces showy purple to pink flowers in the fall. Stomatium resedolens typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators, particularly late-season bees and butterflies.
