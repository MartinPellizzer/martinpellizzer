Styrax vilcabambae

Styrax vilcabambae, commonly known as the Vilcabamba styrax, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Styrax vilcabambae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
