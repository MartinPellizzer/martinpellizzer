Sapium marginatum var. spathulatum

Sapium marginatum var. spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive yellowish-green underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common ornamental plant, though it can become invasive in some regions.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
