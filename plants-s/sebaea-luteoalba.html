Sebaea luteoalba

Sebaea luteoalba, commonly known as the yellow moonvine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly waxy texture. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several seeds.

Sebaea luteoalba is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
