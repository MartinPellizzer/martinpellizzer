Sida acuminata var. macrophylla

Sida acuminata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous species characterized by its alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and can be an invasive weed in some regions.
