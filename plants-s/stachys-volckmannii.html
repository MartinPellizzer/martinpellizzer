Stachys volckmannii

Stachys volckmannii, commonly known as Volkmann's hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.

Stachys volckmannii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
