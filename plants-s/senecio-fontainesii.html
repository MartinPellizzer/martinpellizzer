Senecio fontainesii

Senecio fontainesii, commonly known as the Fontaine's groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
