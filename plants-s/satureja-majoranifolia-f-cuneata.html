Satureja majoranifolia f. cuneata

Satureja majoranifolia f. cuneata, commonly known as narrow-leaf savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow elliptic leaves with a distinctive minty fragrance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or herb gardens.

Its foliage is often used in cooking and traditional medicine for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
