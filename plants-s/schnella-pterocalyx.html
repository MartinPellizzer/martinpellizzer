Schnella pterocalyx

Schnella pterocalyx, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Schnella pterocalyx make it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
