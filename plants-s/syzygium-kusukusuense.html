Syzygium kusukusuense

Syzygium kusukusuense, commonly known as the Kusukusu plum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble plums, with a smooth, purple-black skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flavor, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
