Solanum pinnatum var. subintegrifolium

Solanum pinnatum var. subintegrifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that change from green to yellow or red as they mature. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

The species is often found in moist, shaded areas and has a moderate growth habit.
