Senecio papillosus

Senecio papillosus, commonly known as the silky groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow composite flowers arranged in clusters. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio papillosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
