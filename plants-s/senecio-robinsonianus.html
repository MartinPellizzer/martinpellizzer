Senecio robinsonianus

Senecio robinsonianus, commonly known as the Robinson's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
