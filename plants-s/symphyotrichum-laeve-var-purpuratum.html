Symphyotrichum laeve var. purpuratum

Symphyotrichum laeve var. purpuratum, commonly known as the Purplestem ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that are often purplish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
