Salvia sclarea var. calostachya

Salvia sclarea var. calostachya, commonly known as narrow-stemmed clary sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and flowers make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and essential oil production.
