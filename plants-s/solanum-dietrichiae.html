Solanum dietrichiae

Solanum dietrichiae, commonly known as the Dietrich's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
