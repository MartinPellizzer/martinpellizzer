Syzygium rampans

Syzygium rampans, commonly known as the wild apple or mountain apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and elliptical, edible fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy dark green upper surface. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and fruit make it both ecologically and economically significant in its native range.
