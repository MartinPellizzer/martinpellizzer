Sporobolus deserticola

Sporobolus deserticola, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted form and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through features such as deep root systems and drought-resistant foliage. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.

Sporobolus deserticola is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaped gardens.
