Scheelea wesselsboeri

Scheelea wesselsboeri, commonly known as the Wesselsboer's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-green hue.

Scheelea wesselsboeri is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
