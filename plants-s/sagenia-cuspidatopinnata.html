Sagenia cuspidatopinnata

Sagenia cuspidatopinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that are narrow and pinnately divided. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Sagenia cuspidatopinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
