Scrophularia nachitschevanica

Scrophularia nachitschevanica, commonly known as the Armenian figwort, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features erect stems that are often hairy, with opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are tubular and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in some species.
