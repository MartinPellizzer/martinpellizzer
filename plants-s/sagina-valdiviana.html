Sagina valdiviana

Sagina valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian carpetgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
