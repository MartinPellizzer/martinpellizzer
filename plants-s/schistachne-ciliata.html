Schistachne ciliata

Schistachne ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or slender beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that are often covered with delicate cilia, giving it a silky appearance. The plant typically grows in grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate to warm climates. Schistachne ciliata produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological studies and a potential candidate for agricultural or ornamental use.
