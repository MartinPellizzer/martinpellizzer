Scabiosa sylvatica var. terminalis

Scabiosa sylvatica var. terminalis, commonly known as the woodland scabious, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland edges and meadows, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
