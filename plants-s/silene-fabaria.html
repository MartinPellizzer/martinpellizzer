Silene fabaria

Silene fabaria, commonly known as the common catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features erect, branched stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, pink to red flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene fabaria is often found in open habitats and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
