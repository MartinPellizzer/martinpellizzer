Sauropus quadrangularis

Sauropus quadrangularis, commonly known as the Four-angle plant or the Mexican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and large, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom throughout the year in suitable climates. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are also used in culinary applications in some cultures.
