Strophanthus gardeniiflorus

Strophanthus gardeniiflorus, commonly known as the gardenia-flowered strophanthus, is a tropical shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling gardenias. The plant produces bright orange or red berries that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate with a smooth margin.

Strophanthus gardeniiflorus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
