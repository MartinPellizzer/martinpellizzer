Synaedrys nieuwenhuisii

Synaedrys nieuwenhuisii, commonly known as the Nieuwenhuis's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees, primarily in the genus Ficus.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement and are covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines, and thrives in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
