Solanum cardiophyllum subsp. lanceolatum

Solanum cardiophyllum subsp.

lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic alkaloids. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
