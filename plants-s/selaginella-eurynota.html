Selaginella eurynota

Selaginella eurynota, commonly known as the broad-leaved spikemoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its upright stems. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Selaginella eurynota is known for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
