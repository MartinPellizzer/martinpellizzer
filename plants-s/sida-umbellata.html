Sida umbellata

Sida umbellata, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers arranged in an umbel, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Sida umbellata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
