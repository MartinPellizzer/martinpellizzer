Stipa columbiana

Stipa columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous narrow, awned lemmas. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Stipa columbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
