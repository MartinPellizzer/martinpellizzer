Syzygium biniflorum

Syzygium biniflorum, commonly known as the twin-flowered syzygium, is a species of flowering plant native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that often appear in pairs. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Syzygium biniflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
