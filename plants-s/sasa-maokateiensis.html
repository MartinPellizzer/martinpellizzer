Sasa maokateiensis

Sasa maokateiensis, commonly known as the Maokatei bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, clumping growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles, which are often wind-pollinated. Sasa maokateiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
