Sabicea affinis

Sabicea affinis, commonly known as the slender sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Sabicea affinis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
