Setaria gausa

Setaria gausa, commonly known as slender barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as rice fields and marshy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, water, or animals.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
