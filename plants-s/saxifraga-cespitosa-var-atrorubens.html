Saxifraga cespitosa var. atrorubens

Saxifraga cespitosa var. atrorubens, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features dense, compact rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves that are adapted to cold climates. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra or mountainous environments.

Saxifraga cespitosa var. atrorubens is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and its role in alpine ecosystems.
