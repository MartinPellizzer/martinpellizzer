Saxifraga korshinskyi

Saxifraga korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and often have a rosette shape, adapted to conserve moisture in harsh climates.

Saxifraga korshinskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
