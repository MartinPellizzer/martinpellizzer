Saccolabium humile

Saccolabium humile, commonly known as the small-flowered saccolabium, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its seed pods are slender and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Saccolabium humile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
