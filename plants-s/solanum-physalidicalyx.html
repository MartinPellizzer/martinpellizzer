Solanum physalidicalyx

Solanum physalidicalyx, commonly known as the giant eggplant or wild eggplant, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, fleshy, egg-shaped fruits that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy surface, and the flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive purple calyx.

It is often grown for its edible fruits, which have a mild, sweet flavor and are used in local cuisines.
