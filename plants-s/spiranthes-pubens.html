Spiranthes pubens

Spiranthes pubens, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its intricate, spiraling arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers are white to pale pink and bloom from late summer to early fall.

Spiranthes pubens is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
