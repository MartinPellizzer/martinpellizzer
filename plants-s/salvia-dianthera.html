Salvia dianthera

Salvia dianthera, commonly known as the hummingbird sage, is a striking perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. Salvia dianthera thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
