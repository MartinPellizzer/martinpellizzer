Sideritis massoniana f. longifolia

Sideritis massoniana f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered with fine hairs and produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is adapted to rocky and dry soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
