Stalagmitis ovalifolia

Stalagmitis ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf stalagmite plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive, elongated, oval-shaped leaves that resemble small stalagmites.

Native to the humid tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments with high humidity. The plant's leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. Its flowering is rare, with small, pale greenish-white flowers emerging from the base of the leaves.

Stalagmitis ovalifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unusual appearance and air-purifying properties.
