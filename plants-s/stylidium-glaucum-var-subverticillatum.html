Stylidium glaucum var. subverticillatum

Stylidium glaucum var. subverticillatum, commonly known as the silverleaf stylidium, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and silver-blue foliage. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among horticultural enthusiasts.
