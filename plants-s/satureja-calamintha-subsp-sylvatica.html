Satureja calamintha subsp. sylvatica

Satureja calamintha subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as woodland sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or wooded areas and prefers well-drained soils.

The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong mint-like scent, and its small blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
