Solanum tuberosum var. glabriusculum

Solanum tuberosum var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the glabrous potato, is a botanical variant of the potato plant characterized by its smooth, hairless foliage and tubers.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to South America, specifically the Andes region. This variety is notable for its smaller, round tubers that are often used in traditional Andean cuisine. The plant typically grows in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in agricultural research and genetic studies.
