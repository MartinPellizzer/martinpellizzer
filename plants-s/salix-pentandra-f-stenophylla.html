Salix pentandra f. stenophylla

Salix pentandra f. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf willow, is a botanical variant of the common willow.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is typically found in moist, temperate environments. This form exhibits a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. The plant is part of the Salicaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and landscape design.
