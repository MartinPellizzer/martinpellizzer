Scilla villosa var. barba-caprae

Scilla villosa var. barba-caprae, commonly known as the goat beard squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is adapted to Mediterranean climates and thrives in well-drained conditions.

Scilla villosa var. barba-caprae is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and hardy nature.
