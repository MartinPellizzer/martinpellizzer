Sychinium ramosum

Sychinium ramosum, commonly known as the branching sycamore, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically a deep green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its woody stem is often branched and can grow quite tall, making it a striking addition to gardens.

Sychinium ramosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
