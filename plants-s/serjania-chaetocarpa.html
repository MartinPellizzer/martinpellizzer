Serjania chaetocarpa

Serjania chaetocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or Brazilian cherry, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Serjaniae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, simple leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by red, fleshy fruits.

It is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
