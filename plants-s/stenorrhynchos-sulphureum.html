Stenorrhynchos sulphureum

Stenorrhynchos sulphureum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in full sun. Its flowers are small but numerous, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Stenorrhynchos sulphureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking color and unique appearance.
