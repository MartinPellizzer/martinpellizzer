Salvia virginica

Salvia virginica, commonly known as purple meadow sage, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it is often found in moist meadows and prairies. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Salvia virginica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
