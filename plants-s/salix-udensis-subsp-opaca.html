Salix udensis subsp. opaca

Salix udensis subsp.

opaca, commonly known as the Chinese willow, is a shrub or small tree native to China. It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color.

The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverbanks or wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
