Symphyotrichum oolentangiense

Symphyotrichum oolentangiense, commonly known as the Oolentangiense aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, typically purple or pink flower heads that bloom in the fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, open habitats like woodlands and wet meadows, and is often found in the southeastern region of the United States.
