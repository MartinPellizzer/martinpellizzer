Sporobolus scabrifolius

Sporobolus scabrifolius, commonly known as roughleaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, rough-textured leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for landscaping.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
