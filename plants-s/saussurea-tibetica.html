Saussurea tibetica

Saussurea tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan safflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
