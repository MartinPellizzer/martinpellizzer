Solanum minutibaccatum subsp. curtipedunculatum

Solanum minutibaccatum subsp.

curtipedunculatum, commonly known as the small-fruited nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes.

The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it produces tiny, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a bitter taste.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky areas.
