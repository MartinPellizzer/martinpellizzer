Sorbus munda f. tatsienensis

Sorbus munda f. tatsienensis, commonly known as the Tatsienlou rowan, is a rare subspecies of the rowan tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
