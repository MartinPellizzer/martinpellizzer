Solanum trigalense

Solanum trigalense, commonly known as the Trinidad nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, violet-colored berries that are edible but contain toxic compounds. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a rough texture, and it has a strong, often unpleasant odor.

Solanum trigalense belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
