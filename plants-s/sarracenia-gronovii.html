Sarracenia gronovii

Sarracenia gronovii, commonly known as the purple pitcher plant, is a striking carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, tubular leaves that form deep, colorful pitchers to trap and digest insects. The plant's vibrant red and purple hues are enhanced by its long, showy bracts that surround the pitchers. Sarracenia gronovii thrives in moist, acidic soils and requires full sun to flourish.

Its unique adaptation for nutrient acquisition through insect digestion makes it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
