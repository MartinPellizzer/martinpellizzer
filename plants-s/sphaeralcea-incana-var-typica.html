Sphaeralcea incana var. typica

Sphaeralcea incana var. typica, commonly known as the woolly globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and clusters of showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. Its growth habit is typically low and sprawling, with stems that branch profusely.

Sphaeralcea incana var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soils in dry landscapes.
