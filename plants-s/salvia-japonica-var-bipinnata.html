Salvia japonica var. bipinnata

Salvia japonica var. bipinnata, commonly known as Japanese sage or Chinese sage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are square and often branched. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
