Syngonanthus arthrotrichus

Syngonanthus arthrotrichus, commonly known as the threadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of eastern North America.

It typically grows in dense stands, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. Syngonanthus arthrotrichus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies in suitable moist environments.
