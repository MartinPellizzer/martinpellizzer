Stenostephanus purpusii

Stenostephanus purpusii, commonly known as the desert star cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that help protect it from intense sunlight and water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking visual contrast to its desolate habitat. Its adaptations include a thick, fleshy stem for water storage and spines for defense against herbivores.

Despite its tough exterior, it plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
