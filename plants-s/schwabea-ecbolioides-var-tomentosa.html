Schwabea ecbolioides var. tomentosa

Schwabea ecbolioides var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly schwabea, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its unique texture and visual appeal.
