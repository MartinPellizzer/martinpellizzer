Saxifraga aizoon var. linearifolia

Saxifraga aizoon var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stonecrop, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fleshy, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
