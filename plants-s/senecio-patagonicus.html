Senecio patagonicus

Senecio patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
