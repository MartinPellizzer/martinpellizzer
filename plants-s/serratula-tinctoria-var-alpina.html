Serratula tinctoria var. alpina

Serratula tinctoria var. alpina, commonly known as Alpine yellow toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in dye production, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
