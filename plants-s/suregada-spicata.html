Suregada spicata

Suregada spicata, commonly known as the spiky cactus, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically green and covered in small, white flowers during the winter months. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments. Its leaves are reduced to small spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
