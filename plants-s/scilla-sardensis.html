Scilla sardensis

Scilla sardensis, commonly known as the Sardinian squill, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Scilla sardensis has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of flowers in spring.
