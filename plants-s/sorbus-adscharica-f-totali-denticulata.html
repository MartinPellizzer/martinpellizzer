Sorbus adscharica f. totali-denticulata

Sorbus adscharica f. totali-denticulata, commonly known as the fully denticulate whitebeams, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by clusters of small, persistent berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

This form is notable for its unique leaf morphology, which sets it apart from other Sorbus species.
