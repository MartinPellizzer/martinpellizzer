Scabiosa cretica

Scabiosa cretica, commonly known as the crested scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flower heads that resemble small crowns, typically ranging in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. Scabiosa cretica is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is also noted for its potential soothing properties.
