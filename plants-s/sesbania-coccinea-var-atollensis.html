Sesbania coccinea var. atollensis

Sesbania coccinea var. atollensis, commonly known as the red sesbania, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing near coastal areas or in disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
