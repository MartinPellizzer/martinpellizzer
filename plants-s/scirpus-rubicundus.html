Scirpus rubicundus

Scirpus rubicundus, commonly known as the red stemmed bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features upright, cylindrical stems that are typically reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish flowers at the top of its stems, which are pollinated by wind. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks, allowing it to thrive in marshy and pond edges.

Scirpus rubicundus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic wildlife.
