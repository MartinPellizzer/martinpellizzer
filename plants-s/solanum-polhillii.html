Solanum polhillii

Solanum polhillii, commonly known as the Polhill's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution due to the presence of solanine.

Solanum polhillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
