Sebastiania ramosissima

Sebastiania ramosissima, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and clusters of bright red berries that resemble small Christmas ornaments. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically leathery and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant red fruits.

Sebastiania ramosissima is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal color display.
