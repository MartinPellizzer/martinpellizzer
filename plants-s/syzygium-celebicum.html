Syzygium celebicum

Syzygium celebicum, commonly known as the Moluccan apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to edible, red, fleshy berries.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional dishes, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
