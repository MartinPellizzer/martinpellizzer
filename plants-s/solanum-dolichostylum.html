Solanum dolichostylum

Solanum dolichostylum, commonly known as the long-styled nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often with a faint fragrance. Its fruit is a fleshy berry that ripens to a yellowish color, containing several seeds.

The species is notable for its unique floral morphology, particularly the elongated style that distinguishes it from closely related species.
