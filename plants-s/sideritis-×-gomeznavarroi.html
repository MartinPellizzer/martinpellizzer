Sideritis × gomeznavarroi

Sideritis × gomeznavarroi, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
