Sideritis × viciosoi var. aemiliae

Sideritis × viciosoi var. aemiliae, commonly known as the Aemilia's sainfoin, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
