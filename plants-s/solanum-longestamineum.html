Solanum longestamineum

Solanum longestamineum, commonly known as the long-stamen nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its fruits are small berries that contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
