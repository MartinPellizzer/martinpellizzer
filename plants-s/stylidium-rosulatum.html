Stylidium rosulatum

Stylidium rosulatum, commonly known as the rose-leafed triggerplant, is a member of the genus Stylidium in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of red or pink. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rosette-like arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Stylidium rosulatum is native to Australia and is often found in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions.
