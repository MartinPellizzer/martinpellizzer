Solidago littoralis

Solidago littoralis, commonly known as coastal goldenrod, is a herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines, marshes, and dunes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, saline environments. The plant produces showy clusters of golden-yellow flowers in late summer and early fall, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention.

Solidago littoralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
