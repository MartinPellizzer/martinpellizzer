Salix × bottnica

Salix × bottnica, commonly known as the Siberian willow, is a hybrid species native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, cold environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. The plant has slender, often drooping branches and small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are catkins, which appear in early spring, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Salix × bottnica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
