Sericolea ovalifolia var. lamii

Sericolea ovalifolia var. lamii, commonly known as the woolly leaf sericolea, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its ovate, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
