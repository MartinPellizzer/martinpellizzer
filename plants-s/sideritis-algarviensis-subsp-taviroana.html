Sideritis algarviensis subsp. taviroana

Sideritis algarviensis subsp.

taviroana, commonly known as the Algarve sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the southern Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
