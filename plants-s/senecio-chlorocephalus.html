Senecio chlorocephalus

Senecio chlorocephalus, commonly known as the yellow pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
