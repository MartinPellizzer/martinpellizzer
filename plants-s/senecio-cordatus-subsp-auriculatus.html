Senecio cordatus subsp. auriculatus

Senecio cordatus subsp.

auriculatus, commonly known as the auriculate senecio, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features wiry, ascending stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to rocky and sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
