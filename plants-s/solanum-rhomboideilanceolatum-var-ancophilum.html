Solanum rhomboideilanceolatum var. ancophilum

Solanum rhomboideilanceolatum var. ancophilum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical shrub native to Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruits. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent, and its flowers are typically white or pale green. The fruit, which is edible when ripe, has a sweet, juicy pulp surrounded by a hard, woody rind.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
