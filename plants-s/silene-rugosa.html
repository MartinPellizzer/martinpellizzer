Silene rugosa

Silene rugosa, commonly known as the rough hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Silene rugosa is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
