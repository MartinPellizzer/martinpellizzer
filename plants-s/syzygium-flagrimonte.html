Syzygium flagrimonte

Syzygium flagrimonte, commonly known as the Madagascan star fruit, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The flowers are white and clustered, producing a unique, aromatic fragrance that attracts pollinators.
