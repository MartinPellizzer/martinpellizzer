Scutellaria ovalifolia var. hirsuta

Scutellaria ovalifolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy oval-leaved skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

The species is characterized by its hairy texture and distinct floral structure, making it a notable member of the mint family.
