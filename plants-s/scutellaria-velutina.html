Scutellaria velutina

Scutellaria velutina, commonly known as velvet skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Scutellaria velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
