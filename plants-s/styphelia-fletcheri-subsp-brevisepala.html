Styphelia fletcheri subsp. brevisepala

Styphelia fletcheri subsp.

brevisepala, commonly known as the dwarf lepidotes, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a compact, mat-like formation. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters and blooming in late winter to early spring.

This subspecies is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland environments.
