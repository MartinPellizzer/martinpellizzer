Salix × vistulensis

Salix × vistulensis, commonly known as the Vistula willow, is a hybrid species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are usually yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
