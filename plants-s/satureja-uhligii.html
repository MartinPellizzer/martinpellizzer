Satureja uhligii

Satureja uhligii, commonly known as Persian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Satureja uhligii thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.
