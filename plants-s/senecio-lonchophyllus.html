Senecio lonchophyllus

Senecio lonchophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
