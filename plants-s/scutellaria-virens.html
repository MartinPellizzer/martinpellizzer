Scutellaria virens

Scutellaria virens, commonly known as green skullcap, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Scutellaria virens typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
