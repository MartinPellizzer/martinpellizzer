Sophora tomentosa subsp. polyphylla

Sophora tomentosa subsp.

polyphylla, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are covered in a dense layer of silky, white pubescence.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
