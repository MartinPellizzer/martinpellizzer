Solanum semilobatum

Solanum semilobatum, commonly known as the Chinese nightshade, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with lobed margins. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow or orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its alkaloid-rich chemistry, which contributes to its medicinal and toxic properties.
