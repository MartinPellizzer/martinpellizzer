Solanum miyakojimense

Solanum miyakojimense, commonly known as Miyako nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy surface, and the fruits are small, berry-like structures that turn red when mature.

Solanum miyakojimense is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
