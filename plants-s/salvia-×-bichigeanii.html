Salvia × bichigeanii

Salvia × bichigeanii, commonly known as the Mexican hybrid sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Salvia leucantha and Salvia greggii.
