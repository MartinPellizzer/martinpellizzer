Silene ramosa

Silene ramosa, commonly known as the branched catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Silene ramosa is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and ecological importance to pollinators.
