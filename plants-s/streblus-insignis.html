Streblus insignis

Streblus insignis, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, making it a sought-after material in carpentry.

Streblus insignis also plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
