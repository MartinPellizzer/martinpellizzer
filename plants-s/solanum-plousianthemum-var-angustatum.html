Solanum plousianthemum var. angustatum

Solanum plousianthemum var. angustatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
