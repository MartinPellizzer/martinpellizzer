Syzygium taytayense

Syzygium taytayense, commonly known as the Philippine starfruit or taytay fruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers that give way to yellow, star-shaped fruits resembling a starfruit. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and slightly tart flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
