Saxifraga huettii

Saxifraga huettii, commonly known as the Huettii saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Pamir and Tien Shan ranges. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette shape, and are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, providing some protection against cold and arid conditions.
