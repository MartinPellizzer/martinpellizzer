Sideroxylon obtusifolium

Sideroxylon obtusifolium, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, waxy leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits.

This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
