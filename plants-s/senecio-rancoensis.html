Senecio rancoensis

Senecio rancoensis, commonly known as the Ranco Senecio, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
