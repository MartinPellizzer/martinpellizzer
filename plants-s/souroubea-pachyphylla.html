Souroubea pachyphylla

Souroubea pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved souroubea, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, typically in summer. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which reduces water loss.

Souroubea pachyphylla is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
