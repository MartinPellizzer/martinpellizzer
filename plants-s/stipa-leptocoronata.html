Stipa leptocoronata

Stipa leptocoronata, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to golden spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Stipa leptocoronata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
