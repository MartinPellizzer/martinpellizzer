Saccopetalum brahei

Saccopetalum brahei, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, red, berry-like fruits that resemble Christmas ornaments, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Saccopetalum brahei thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or near the sea.
