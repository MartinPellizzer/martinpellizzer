Senecio alatus

Senecio alatus, commonly known as the silverleaf senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy foliage and its ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Senecio alatus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
