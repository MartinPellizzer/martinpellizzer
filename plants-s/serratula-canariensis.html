Serratula canariensis

Serratula canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Serratula canariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
