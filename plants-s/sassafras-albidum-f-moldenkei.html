Sassafras albidum f. moldenkei

Sassafras albidum f. moldenkei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sassafras, is a variant of the sassafras tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by elliptical, orange-red berries that are a key feature of its botanical profile. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, with a distinctive aromatic scent when cut.

This form is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas, showcasing its adaptability to various ecological conditions.
