Sasa owatarii

Sasa owatarii, commonly known as Japanese bamboo or Owatari bamboo, is a clump-forming grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Sasa owatarii is also valued for its use in traditional Japanese crafts, such as basket weaving.
