Sorbus amabilis var. wuyishanensis

Sorbus amabilis var. wuyishanensis, commonly known as the Wuyi mountain whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, persistent fruits that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
