Sabazia pinetorum var. dispar

Sabazia pinetorum var. dispar, commonly known as the Florida pineapple weed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and bract-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils in open areas such as pine flatwoods and disturbed sites. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slight waxy coating.

The inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
