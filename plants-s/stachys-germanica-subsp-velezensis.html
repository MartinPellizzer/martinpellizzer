Stachys germanica subsp. velezensis

Stachys germanica subsp.

velezensis, commonly known as German violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized landscapes.
