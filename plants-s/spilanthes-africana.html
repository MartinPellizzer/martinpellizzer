Spilanthes africana

Spilanthes africana, commonly known as African toothache plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its branched, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for containing compounds such as spilanthol, which are responsible for its pungent and tingling effects. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Spilanthes africana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.
