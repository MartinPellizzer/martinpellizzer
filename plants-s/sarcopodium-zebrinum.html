Sarcopodium zebrinum

Sarcopodium zebrinum, commonly known as the Zebra Cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems that display a striped pattern resembling a zebra. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its succulent foliage. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Sarcopodium zebrinum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
