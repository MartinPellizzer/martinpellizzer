Schisandra propinqua subsp. axillaris

Schisandra propinqua subsp.

axillaris, commonly known as the axillary schisandra, is a member of the Schisandraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and distinctive red berries.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in the leaf axils, giving it its subspecies name. Its berries are rich in lignans and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
