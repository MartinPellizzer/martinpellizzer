Sedum rhodiola var. tachiroei

Sedum rhodiola var. tachiroei, commonly known as the Tachiroe sedum, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
