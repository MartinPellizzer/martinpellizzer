Scutellaria orientalis f. elongata

Scutellaria orientalis f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated skullcap, is a variant of the Scutellaria genus native to eastern Asia.

This perennial herb features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height. Its lance-shaped leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
