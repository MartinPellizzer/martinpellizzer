Senecio pinnatus var. simplicifolius

Senecio pinnatus var. simplicifolius, commonly known as the simple-leaved groundsels, is a variant of the Senecio genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are arranged in a pinnate pattern, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in summer.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
