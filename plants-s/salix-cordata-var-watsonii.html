Salix cordata var. watsonii

Salix cordata var. watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's willow, is a variant of the heartleaf willow, native to parts of China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish-brown underneath. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This variety is also noted for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
