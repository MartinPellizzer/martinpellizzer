Satureja conferta var. elongata

Satureja conferta var. elongata, commonly known as elongated wood sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a fragrant, mint-like aroma.

It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in the summer months.
