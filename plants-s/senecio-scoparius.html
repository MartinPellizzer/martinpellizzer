Senecio scoparius

Senecio scoparius, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features wiry, branching stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine, soft hairs.

Senecio scoparius is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
