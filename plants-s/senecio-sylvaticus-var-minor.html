Senecio sylvaticus var. minor

Senecio sylvaticus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser woodland groundsel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is an important plant for supporting local insect populations, particularly bees and butterflies.
