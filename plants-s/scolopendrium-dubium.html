Scolopendrium dubium

Scolopendrium dubium, commonly known as the lesser tamarack, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and delicate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Scolopendrium dubium is relatively rare and is often found in specific microhabitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
