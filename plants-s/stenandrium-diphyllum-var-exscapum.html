Stenandrium diphyllum var. exscapum

Stenandrium diphyllum var. exscapum, commonly known as the white-flowered stenandrium, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its elegant, white spathes that enclose a cluster of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant features a short, stout rhizome and produces aerial roots that help it climb or anchor itself. Native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural collections.
