Spiraea japonica var. hypoglauca

Spiraea japonica var. hypoglauca, commonly known as Japanese weeping spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching branches that bear clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes bluish underside. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in ornamental landscaping for its graceful appearance and seasonal interest.
