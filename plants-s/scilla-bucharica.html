Scilla bucharica

Scilla bucharica, commonly known as the Caucasian scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging conditions. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine, though its ornamental value is also appreciated in gardens.

Scilla bucharica plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators with its early blooming period.
