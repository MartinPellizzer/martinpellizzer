Sempervivum pruinatum

Sempervivum pruinatum, commonly known as the silver rose or snowflake plant, is a species of succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, silver-gray leaves covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Sempervivum pruinatum is also known for its ability to survive in harsh climates and is often used in horticultural displays for its ornamental value.
