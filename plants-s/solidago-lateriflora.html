Solidago lateriflora

Solidago lateriflora, commonly known as the side-leaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive white or silver underside.

The showy yellow inflorescences bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
