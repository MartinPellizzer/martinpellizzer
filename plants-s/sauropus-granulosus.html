Sauropus granulosus

Sauropus granulosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved sauropus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in small, raised dots, giving them a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
