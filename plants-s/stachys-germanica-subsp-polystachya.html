Stachys germanica subsp. polystachya

Stachys germanica subsp.

polystachya, commonly known as the clustered hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in whorled clusters, which are borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The species is noted for its hairy foliage and its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
