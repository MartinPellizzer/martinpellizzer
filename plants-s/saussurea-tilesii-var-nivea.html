Saussurea tilesii var. nivea

Saussurea tilesii var. nivea, commonly known as the white saussurea, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
