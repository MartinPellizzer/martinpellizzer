Stachys turcomanica

Stachys turcomanica, commonly known as Turkmenian betony, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pale purple or blue in color.

Stachys turcomanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
