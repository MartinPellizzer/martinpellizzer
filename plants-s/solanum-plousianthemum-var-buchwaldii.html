Solanum plousianthemum var. buchwaldii

Solanum plousianthemum var. buchwaldii, commonly known as the Buchwald's nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
