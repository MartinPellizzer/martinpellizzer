Sporoxeia

Sporoxeia, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a type of primitive vascular plant belonging to the order Microsorum.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and the presence of sporangia, which produce spores for reproduction. These plants typically grow in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Sporoxeia has a long evolutionary history, dating back to the Paleozoic era.

Its structure includes a stem with whorled leaves and a root system adapted for anchoring in soil.
