Spiraeanthemum serratum

Spiraeanthemum serratum, commonly known as the serrated spirea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant has slender, arching stems and finely serrated leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming period and compact growth habit.
