Sarcomphalus cinnamomum

Sarcomphalus cinnamomum, commonly known as cinnamon wood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and camphor. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically clustered in inflorescences.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is valued for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
