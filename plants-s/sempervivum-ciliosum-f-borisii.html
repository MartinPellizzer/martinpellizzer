Sempervivum ciliosum f. borisii

Sempervivum ciliosum f. borisii, commonly known as the Borissii's houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or silver marginal hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
