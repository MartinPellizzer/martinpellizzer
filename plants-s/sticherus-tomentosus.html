Sticherus tomentosus

Sticherus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly sticherus, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white, fuzzy hairs.

Sticherus tomentosus is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique texture and ability to provide habitat for small wildlife.
