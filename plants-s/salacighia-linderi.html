Salacighia linderi

Salacighia linderi, commonly known as the Linder's salacine, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with a single, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, tart berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique flavor and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
