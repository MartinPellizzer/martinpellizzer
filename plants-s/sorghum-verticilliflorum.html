Sorghum verticilliflorum

Sorghum verticilliflorum, commonly known as the woolly sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and dense, woolly inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Sorghum verticilliflorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in arid environments.
