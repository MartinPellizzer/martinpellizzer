Santalum

Santalum, commonly known as sandalwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Santalaceae.

These plants are characterized by their parasitic or hemiparasitic growth habits, often relying on other plants for nutrients. Santalum species are native to various regions of Asia, Australia, and the Pacific Islands. They are valued for their aromatic wood, which is used in perfumes, incense, and traditional medicine.

The genus includes several species, with Santalum album being the most commercially significant.
