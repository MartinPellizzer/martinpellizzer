Sorbus helenae var. argutiserrata

Sorbus helenae var. argutiserrata, commonly known as the Helen's rowan, is a variant of the rowan tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Sorbus, with specific adaptations to its alpine habitat.
