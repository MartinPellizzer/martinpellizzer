Stachytarpheta chascanoides

Stachytarpheta chascanoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple bracts that surround small, tubular flowers, which are typically white or pale purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates. Its foliage is often aromatic, with a slight minty scent, and it is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Stachytarpheta chascanoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
