Senecio melanocalyx

Senecio melanocalyx, commonly known as the black-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, almost black leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
