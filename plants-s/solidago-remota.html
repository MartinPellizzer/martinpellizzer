Solidago remota

Solidago remota, commonly known as remote goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, yellow flower heads, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Solidago remota is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.
