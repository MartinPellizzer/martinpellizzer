Sasa takizawana

Sasa takizawana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a species of grass in the genus Sasa.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Sasa takizawana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
