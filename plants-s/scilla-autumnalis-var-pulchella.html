Scilla autumnalis var. pulchella

Scilla autumnalis var. pulchella, commonly known as the autumn squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces clusters of striking blue or violet flowers in the autumn, typically from September to November. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
