Salvia bulgarica

Salvia bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty fragrance.

Salvia bulgarica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.
