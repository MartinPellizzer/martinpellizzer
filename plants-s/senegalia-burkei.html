Senegalia burkei

Senegalia burkei, commonly known as the burke acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically small and arranged in pairs or clusters. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Senegalia burkei is often used for forage by livestock and has traditional uses in some regions.

Its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
