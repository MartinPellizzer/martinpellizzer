Scutachne amphistemon

Scutachne amphistemon, commonly known as the broadleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open areas such as savannas and disturbed soils, where it can thrive in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often hairy and clustered.

Scutachne amphistemon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
