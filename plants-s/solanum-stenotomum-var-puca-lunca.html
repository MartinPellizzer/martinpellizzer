Solanum stenotomum var. puca-lunca

Solanum stenotomum var. puca-lunca, commonly known as the purple moonflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking purple to violet flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruits are small berries that change color from green to red as they mature.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flowering habit, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
