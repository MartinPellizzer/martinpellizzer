Saccolabium semiclausum

Saccolabium semiclausum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, oval-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
