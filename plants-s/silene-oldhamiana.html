Silene oldhamiana

Silene oldhamiana, commonly known as Oldham's campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate and lance-shaped, while the stems are often hairy.

Silene oldhamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
