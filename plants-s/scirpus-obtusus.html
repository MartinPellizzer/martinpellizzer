Scirpus obtusus

Scirpus obtusus, commonly known as broadleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, and its leaves are broad and flat, often with a waxy coating. Its flowering spikes are usually greenish or brownish, and the plant produces small, three-lobed flowers.

Scirpus obtusus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
