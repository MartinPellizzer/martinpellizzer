Sterculia pedunculata

Sterculia pedunculata, commonly known as the African mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with red or purple bracts. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough texture, while its bark is thick and fissured.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
