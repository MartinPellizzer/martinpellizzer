Scilla hispanica subsp. patula

Scilla hispanica subsp.

patula, commonly known as the Spanish squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
