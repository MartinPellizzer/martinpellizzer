Solidago japonica

Solidago japonica, commonly known as Japanese goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, downy hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
