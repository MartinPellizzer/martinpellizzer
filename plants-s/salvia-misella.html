Salvia misella

Salvia misella, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Salvia misella thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warmer climates.
