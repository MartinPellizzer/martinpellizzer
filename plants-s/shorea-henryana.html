Shorea henryana

Shorea henryana, commonly known as Henry's shorea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, heavy seeds and buttressed roots. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

The plant is valued for its durable timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
