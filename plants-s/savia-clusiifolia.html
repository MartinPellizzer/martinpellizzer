Savia clusiifolia

Savia clusiifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Savia clusiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
