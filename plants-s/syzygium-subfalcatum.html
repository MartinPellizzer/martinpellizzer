Syzygium subfalcatum

Syzygium subfalcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Syzygium subfalcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
