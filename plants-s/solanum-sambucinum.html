Solanum sambucinum

Solanum sambucinum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and typically grows as an annual or perennial herb. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant often thrives in disturbed soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
