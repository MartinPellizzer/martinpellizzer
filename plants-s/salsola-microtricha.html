Salsola microtricha

Salsola microtricha, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in deserts and along coastal dunes.

Salsola microtricha plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
