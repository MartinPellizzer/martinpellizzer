Stromanthe amabilis

Stromanthe amabilis, commonly known as the velvet leaf plant, is a tropical perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated, waxy foliage that displays shades of green, white, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture that gives it its common name.

Stromanthe amabilis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
