Spiraea laxissima

Spiraea laxissima, commonly known as the meadow spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Spiraea laxissima is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
