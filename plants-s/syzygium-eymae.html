Syzygium eymae

Syzygium eymae, commonly known as the Eyma apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The fruit is a small, round, red or yellowish drupe, often used in local cuisines for its tart flavor.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
