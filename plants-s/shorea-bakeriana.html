Shorea bakeriana

Shorea bakeriana, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, reaching heights of up to 50 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Shorea bakeriana plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
