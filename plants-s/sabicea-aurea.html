Sabicea aurea

Sabicea aurea, commonly known as the golden sabicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain seeds.

Sabicea aurea is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
