Scheelea martiana

Scheelea martiana, commonly known as the red-flowered grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces striking red or purple inflorescences that are clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Scheelea martiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
