Syzygium conceptionis

Syzygium conceptionis, commonly known as the Conceptionis apple or the Philippine apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Syzygium conceptionis is often found in lowland forests and is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its culinary uses.
