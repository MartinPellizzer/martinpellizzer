Selinum foeniculum

Selinum foeniculum, commonly known as fennel, is a flowering plant in the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and yellow umbels of flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that are typically used in culinary and medicinal applications. Fennel thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in temperate climates.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times for both its flavor and therapeutic properties.
