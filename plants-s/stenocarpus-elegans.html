Stenocarpus elegans

Stenocarpus elegans, commonly known as the silky oak, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests or coastal areas.

Stenocarpus elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Indigenous practices.
