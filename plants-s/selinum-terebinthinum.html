Selinum terebinthinum

Selinum terebinthinum, commonly known as the sacred or holy basil, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic leaves with a strong, spicy scent, and it produces small, white, tubular flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Its leaves are commonly used in cooking and for their purported health benefits.
