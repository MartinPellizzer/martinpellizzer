Syngonanthus retrorsus

Syngonanthus retrorsus, commonly known as the backward-pointing thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
