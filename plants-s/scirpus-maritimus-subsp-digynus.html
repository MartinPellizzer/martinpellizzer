Scirpus maritimus subsp. digynus

Scirpus maritimus subsp.

digynus, commonly known as the salt marsh bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to coastal regions. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of its botanical classification. It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, often found in estuaries and tidal marshes.

Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
