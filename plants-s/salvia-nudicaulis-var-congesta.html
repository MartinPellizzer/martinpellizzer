Salvia nudicaulis var. congesta

Salvia nudicaulis var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are densely arranged along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
