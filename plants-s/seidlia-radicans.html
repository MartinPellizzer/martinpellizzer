Seidlia radicans

Seidlia radicans, commonly known as the creeping root vine, is a perennial plant native to tropical regions.

It features a sprawling growth habit with long, trailing stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across surfaces. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy, glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This plant is often used in horticulture for ground cover due to its ability to suppress weeds and its adaptability to various soil types.
