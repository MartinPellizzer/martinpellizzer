Silene quadridentata f. biloba

Silene quadridentata f. biloba, commonly known as the four-toothed catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a soft, hairy texture.

This fern-like plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
