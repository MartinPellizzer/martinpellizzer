Saxifraga confusa

Saxifraga confusa, commonly known as the confused saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Saxifraga confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
