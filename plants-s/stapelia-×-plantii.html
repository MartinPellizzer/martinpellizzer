Stapelia × plantii

Stapelia × plantii, commonly known as the hybrid African milkwort, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive, unpleasant odor, which attracts pollinators like flies. It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Stapelia hirsuta and Stapelia rhombifolia.

Stapelia × plantii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
