Selaginella helvetica var. vera

Selaginella helvetica var. vera, commonly known as the Swiss clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Selaginellaceae and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Selaginella helvetica var. vera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
