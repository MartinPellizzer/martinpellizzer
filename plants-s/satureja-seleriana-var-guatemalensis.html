Satureja seleriana var. guatemalensis

Satureja seleriana var. guatemalensis, commonly known as Guatemala thyme, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and produces small, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils, making it useful in traditional medicine.

It is also valued as a culinary herb for its distinctive, thyme-like aroma.
