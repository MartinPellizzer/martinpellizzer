Senecio tymphresteus

Senecio tymphresteus, commonly known as the Tymphrestean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in the spring, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
