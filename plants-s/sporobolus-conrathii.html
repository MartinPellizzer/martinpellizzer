Sporobolus conrathii

Sporobolus conrathii, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert and semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
