Sasa hikosanensis

Sasa hikosanensis, commonly known as the Hikosanensis bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Sasa hikosanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
