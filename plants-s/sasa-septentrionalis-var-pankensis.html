Sasa septentrionalis var. pankensis

Sasa septentrionalis var. pankensis, commonly known as the northern sasa grass, is a variant of the sasa plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a clump-forming perennial grass that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant has slender, arching leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which helps in soil stabilization.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in maintaining forest floor biodiversity.
