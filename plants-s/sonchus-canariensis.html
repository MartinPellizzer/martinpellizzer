Sonchus canariensis

Sonchus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island sowthistle, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a composite head. The plant is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a cosmopolitan distribution and is considered an invasive species in some regions.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
