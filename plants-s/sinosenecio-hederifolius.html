Sinosenecio hederifolius

Sinosenecio hederifolius, commonly known as the creeping sineweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, serrated leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
