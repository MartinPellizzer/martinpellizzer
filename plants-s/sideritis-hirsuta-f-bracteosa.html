Sideritis hirsuta f. bracteosa

Sideritis hirsuta f. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate hedge hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features hairy foliage and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and woodland edges.

Sideritis species are known for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
