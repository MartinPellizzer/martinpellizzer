Sanguisorba canadensis

Sanguisorba canadensis, commonly known as Canadian sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant features serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Sanguisorba canadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
