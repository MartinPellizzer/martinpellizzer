Sorbus andreanszkyana

Sorbus andreanszkyana, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rounded crown and smooth, grey bark. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in autumn.

Sorbus andreanszkyana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
