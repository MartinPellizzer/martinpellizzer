Sorbus parviloba

Sorbus parviloba, commonly known as the small-leaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
