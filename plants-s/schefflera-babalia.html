Schefflera babalia

Schefflera babalia, commonly known as the Babalai schefflera, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive crown of large, palmate leaves that radiate from a central stem, giving it a lush, full appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red berries, which are a food source for birds. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded gardens.

Its adaptability and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
