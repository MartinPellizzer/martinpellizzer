Santolina semidentata

Santolina semidentata, commonly known as silver thyme or Spanish santolina, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with finely textured leaves that are covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Santolina semidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
