Sphaerocoma hookeri subsp. hookeri

Sphaerocoma hookeri subsp.

hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's globe thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble small globes, which are a distinctive botanical feature.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually blue to purple in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
