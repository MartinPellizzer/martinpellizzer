Soldanella × handel-mazzettii

Soldanella × handel-mazzettii, commonly known as the hybrid snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

This hybrid species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
