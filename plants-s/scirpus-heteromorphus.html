Scirpus heteromorphus

Scirpus heteromorphus, commonly known as the variable bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, often with a distinctive waxy coating.

Scirpus heteromorphus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
