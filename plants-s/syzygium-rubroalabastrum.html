Syzygium rubroalabastrum

Syzygium rubroalabastrum, commonly known as the red-veined tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with reddish veins and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a pod-like structure containing several seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
