Spiropetalum liberosepalum

Spiropetalum liberosepalum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five spreading lobes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
