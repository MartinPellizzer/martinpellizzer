Sadiria erecta var. longipetiolata

Sadiria erecta var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled upright sadiria, is a distinctive plant characterized by its elongated petioles that support its broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The leaves are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy underside, and they are arranged alternately along the stem. This variety is notable for its upright growth habit, which sets it apart from other varieties of Sadiria erecta. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden cultivation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
