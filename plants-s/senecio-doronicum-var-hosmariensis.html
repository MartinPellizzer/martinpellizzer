Senecio doronicum var. hosmariensis

Senecio doronicum var. hosmariensis, commonly known as the Hosmariensis Groundsel, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically linear to oblanceolate, often covered in fine hairs, and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid environments.
