Stipa kirghisorum

Stipa kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces spikelets that are arranged in dense, compact clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Stipa kirghisorum is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
