Solanum chiapasense

Solanum chiapasense, commonly known as the Chiapas nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the humid lowland regions of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

Solanum chiapasense is often found in tropical rainforests and is adapted to thrive in high humidity and warm climates.
