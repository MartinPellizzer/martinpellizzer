Sedum mirabile

Sedum mirabile, commonly known as the "miraculous stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in late summer, adding visual interest to its habitat. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and low maintenance requirements make it an attractive option for gardeners in drought-prone regions.
