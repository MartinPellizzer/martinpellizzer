Senecio condylus

Senecio condylus, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Senecio condylus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
