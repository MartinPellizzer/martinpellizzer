Solanum rufescens

Solanum rufescens, commonly known as the red-barked nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems that often have a reddish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum rufescens is often found in dry, open habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
