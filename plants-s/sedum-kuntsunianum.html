Sedum kuntsunianum

Sedum kuntsunianum, commonly known as the Mexican snowflake, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
