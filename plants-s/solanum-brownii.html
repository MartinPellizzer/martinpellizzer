Solanum brownii

Solanum brownii, commonly known as the Brown's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant can grow up to 1 meter in height.

Solanum brownii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
