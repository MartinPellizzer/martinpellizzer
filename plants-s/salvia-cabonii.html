Salvia cabonii

Salvia cabonii, commonly known as the Mexican sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Salvia cabonii is often grown in gardens for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
