Salvia barrelieri subsp. bicolor

Salvia barrelieri subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of vibrant purple to pink tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it typically grows in well-drained soils in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice in xeriscaping.

The subspecies bicolor is distinguished by its two-toned flowers, with darker hues at the base and lighter shades at the tips.
