Smelowskia fremontii var. glabella

Smelowskia fremontii var. glabella, commonly known as the smoothleaf fremont's sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a short, stout stem with few branches and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence compared to other varieties of Smelowskia.
