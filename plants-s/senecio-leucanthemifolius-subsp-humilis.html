Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp. humilis

Senecio leucanthemifolius subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
