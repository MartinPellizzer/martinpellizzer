Sycopsis philippinensis

Sycopsis philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy and smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip, and they turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that are important for local wildlife.
