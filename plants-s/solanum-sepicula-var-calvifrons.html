Solanum sepicula var. calvifrons

Solanum sepicula var. calvifrons, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically yellow with five lobes. The plant produces small, round berries that are usually green when unripe and turn red when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect growth habit.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in cultivation and requires caution during handling.
