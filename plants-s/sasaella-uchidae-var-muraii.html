Sasaella uchidae var. muraii

Sasaella uchidae var. muraii, commonly known as the Uchida's bamboo grass, is a variant of the Sasaella genus native to Japan.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, bamboo-like culms and dense, tufted foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
