Sphaerochloa compressa

Sphaerochloa compressa, commonly known as the compact needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often tightly rolled when young. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that mature into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Sphaerochloa compressa is well adapted to drought conditions and is an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
