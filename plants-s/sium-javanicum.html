Sium javanicum

Sium javanicum, commonly known as the Java water-cress, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are often harvested for culinary use, similar to other members of the mustard family.

Sium javanicum thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, making it a common wetland plant.
