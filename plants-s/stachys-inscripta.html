Stachys inscripta

Stachys inscripta, commonly known as the inscribed hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Stachys inscripta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
