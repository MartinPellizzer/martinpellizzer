Sagenia martinicensis

Sagenia martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique sage, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
