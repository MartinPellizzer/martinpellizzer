Selaginella banksii

Selaginella banksii, commonly known as the mountain huckleberry, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores, which are essential for its propagation.

Selaginella banksii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
