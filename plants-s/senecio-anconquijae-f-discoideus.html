Senecio anconquijae f. discoideus

Senecio anconquijae f. discoideus, commonly known as the disc-like senecio, is a unique variant of the Senecio genus native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, disc-shaped inflorescence, which sets it apart from other members of the genus. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Asteraceae family.
