Sparganium × oligocarpon

Sparganium × oligocarpon, commonly known as the fragrant burr rush, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that bear dense, spiky clusters of brownish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by water.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
