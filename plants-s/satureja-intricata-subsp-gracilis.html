Satureja intricata subsp. gracilis

Satureja intricata subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its antiseptic and digestive properties.
