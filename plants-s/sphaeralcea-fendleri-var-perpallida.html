Sphaeralcea fendleri var. perpallida

Sphaeralcea fendleri var. perpallida, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and the stems are often branched and covered in fine pubescence.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
