Silene pernoctans

Silene pernoctans, commonly known as the night-flowering catchfly, is a species of flowering plant in the carnation family.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its flowers have a distinctive scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Silene pernoctans is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
