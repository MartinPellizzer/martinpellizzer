Sclerochiton vogelii subsp. holstii

Sclerochiton vogelii subsp.

holstii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and succulence in its stems.

The species is also noted for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
