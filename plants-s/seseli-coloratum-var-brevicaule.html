Seseli coloratum var. brevicaule

Seseli coloratum var. brevicaule, commonly known as the short-stemmed bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value.
