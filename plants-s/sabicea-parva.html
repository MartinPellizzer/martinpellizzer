Sabicea parva

Sabicea parva, commonly known as the small sabicea, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its flowers are small and often clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Sabicea parva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
