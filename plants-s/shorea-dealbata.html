Shorea dealbata

Shorea dealbata, commonly known as the white-wooded ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and smooth, often pale-colored bark. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
