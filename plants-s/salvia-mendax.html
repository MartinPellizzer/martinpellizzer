Salvia mendax

Salvia mendax, commonly known as the false chia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Salvia mendax is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
