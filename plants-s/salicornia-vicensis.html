Salicornia vicensis

Salicornia vicensis, commonly known as sea beans, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other regions with high salinity. Its ability to tolerate salt makes it an important component of ecosystems in such habitats.

Salicornia vicensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
