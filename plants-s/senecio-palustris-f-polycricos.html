Senecio palustris f. polycricos

Senecio palustris f. polycricos, commonly known as the swamp senecio, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters called cyathia. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and typically have a waxy coating.

This variety is adapted to moist or aquatic environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
