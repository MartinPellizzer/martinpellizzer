Schinus mellisii

Schinus mellisii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries.

The berries contain several seeds and are an important food source for various bird species.
