Solanum sparsespinosum

Solanum sparsespinosum, commonly known as the sparse-spined nightshade, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its sparsely armed stems and small, often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to violet or purple berries. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface.

The species is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants.
