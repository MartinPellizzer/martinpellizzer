Spiraea japonica var. alpina

Spiraea japonica var. alpina, commonly known as alpine spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer, creating a dense, showy display. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has slender, arching branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of silvery-white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
