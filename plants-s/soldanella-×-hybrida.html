Soldanella × hybrida

Soldanella × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Soldanella × hybrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
