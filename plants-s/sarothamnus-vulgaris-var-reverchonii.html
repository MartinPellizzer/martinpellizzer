Sarothamnus vulgaris var. reverchonii

Sarothamnus vulgaris var. reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon's thorny broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
