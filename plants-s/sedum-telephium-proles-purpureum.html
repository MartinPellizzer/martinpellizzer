Sedum telephium proles purpureum

Sedum telephium proles purpureum, commonly known as the Purple Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its vibrant floral display.
