Solanum muricatum f. glaberrimum

Solanum muricatum f. glaberrimum, commonly known as the smooth passionfruit, is a variant of the passionfruit plant characterized by its reduced prickles compared to the typical species.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America. The plant produces large, fragrant fruits with a smooth, glossy skin and a juicy, aromatic pulp. Its flowers are typically white with a distinctive crown of filaments, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

This cultivar is valued for its culinary use in juices, desserts, and beverages.
