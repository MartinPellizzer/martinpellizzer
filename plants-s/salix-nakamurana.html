Salix nakamurana

Salix nakamurana, commonly known as Nakamura's willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, flexible branches and small, elongated leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
