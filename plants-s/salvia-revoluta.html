Salvia revoluta

Salvia revoluta, commonly known as Mexican bush sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Salvia revoluta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
