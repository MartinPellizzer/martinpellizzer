Syzygium leptophlebium

Syzygium leptophlebium, commonly known as the white syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small plums. Syzygium leptophlebium thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it both a horticultural and agricultural interest.
