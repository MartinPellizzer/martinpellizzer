Solanum erythraeum

Solanum erythraeum, commonly known as the red tomato or wild tomato, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to South America and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy berries. These berries contain numerous small, hard seeds and are often used in traditional medicine.

Solanum erythraeum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some culinary uses in certain regions.
