Senecio pilosicristus

Senecio pilosicristus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
