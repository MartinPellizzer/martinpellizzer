Scirpus glaucovirens

Scirpus glaucovirens, commonly known as the silver rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in the Northern Hemisphere.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-brown flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically ranging from 30 to 100 centimeters in length.

Scirpus glaucovirens is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
