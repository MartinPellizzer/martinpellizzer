Salvia trigonocalyx

Salvia trigonocalyx, commonly known as the Chinese sage or purple flower sage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorls along the stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Salvia trigonocalyx is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
