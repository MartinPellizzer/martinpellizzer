Scleria flaccida

Scleria flaccida, commonly known as the soft sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes. The plant features slender, grass-like stems that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes. Its leaves are linear and typically have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

Scleria flaccida is often found in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
