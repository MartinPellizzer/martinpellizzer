Senkenbergia annulata

Senkenbergia annulata, commonly known as the ringed spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a basal rosette of fleshy, yellowish-green leaves with distinct white or pale green markings. Its flowering stems rise above the leaves, bearing yellow flowers arranged in a raceme.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
