Santolina oblongifolia var. ceratophylla

Santolina oblongifolia var. ceratophylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf santolina, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with narrow, oblong leaves that are densely packed, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in the summer, which are borne on short, upright stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
