Senecio ballyi

Senecio ballyi, commonly known as the Bally's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Senecio ballyi is often found in high-altitude environments, where it adapts to cooler and drier climates.
