Saxifraga pensylvanica subsp. eupensylvanica

Saxifraga pensylvanica subsp.

eupensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Its foliage is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
