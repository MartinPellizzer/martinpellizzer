Scolopacium incarnatum

Scolopacium incarnatum, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Scolopacium incarnatum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
