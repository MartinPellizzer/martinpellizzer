Styrax zhejiangensis

Styrax zhejiangensis, commonly known as the Zhejiang styrax, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
