Silene brevipes

Silene brevipes, commonly known as the shortfoot catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and hairy, and it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Silene brevipes is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
