Speculantha amabilis

Speculantha amabilis, commonly known as the sweet-scented bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are known for their subtle, sweet fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to well-drained soils.
