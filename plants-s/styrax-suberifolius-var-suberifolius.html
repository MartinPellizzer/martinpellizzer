Styrax suberifolius var. suberifolius

Styrax suberifolius var. suberifolius, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are typically white or pale pink. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
