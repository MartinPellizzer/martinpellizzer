Spilanthes micralloeophylla

Spilanthes micralloeophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spilanthes, is a perennial herb native to the grasslands of South America.

It belongs to the genus Spilanthes, which is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the family. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Spilanthes micralloeophylla is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.
