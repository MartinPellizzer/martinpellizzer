Syzygium lewisii

Syzygium lewisii, commonly known as the lemon myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that contain high levels of citral, a compound responsible for its distinctive fragrance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, lemon-scented berries. Syzygium lewisii thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

It is often cultivated for its culinary and aromatic properties.
