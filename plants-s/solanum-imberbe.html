Solanum imberbe

Solanum imberbe, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into toxic berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
