Secondatia densiflora var. parvifolia

Secondatia densiflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved secondatia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
