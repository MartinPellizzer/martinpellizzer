Schinus terebinthifolia var. terebinthifolia

Schinus terebinthifolia var. terebinthifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, red, fleshy fruits that are often mistaken for berries. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers, which are followed by the characteristic red fruits. It is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
