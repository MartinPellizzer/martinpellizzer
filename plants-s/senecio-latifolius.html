Senecio latifolius

Senecio latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its broad, waxy leaves are adapted to reduce water loss, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Senecio latifolius is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
