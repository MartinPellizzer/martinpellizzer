Silene parviflora

Silene parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Silene parviflora is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
