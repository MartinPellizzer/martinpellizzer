Sempervivum saxosum

Sempervivum saxosum, commonly known as the alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant is well adapted to harsh, rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

Sempervivum saxosum is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
