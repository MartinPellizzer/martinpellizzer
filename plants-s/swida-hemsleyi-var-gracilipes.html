Swida hemsleyi var. gracilipes

Swida hemsleyi var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender swida, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and fruiting bodies that develop into small, dry capsules.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
