Syzygium szemaoense

Syzygium szemaoense, commonly known as the Sichuan tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically the Sichuan province, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. Syzygium szemaoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
