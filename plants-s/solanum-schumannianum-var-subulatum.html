Solanum schumannianum var. subulatum

Solanum schumannianum var. subulatum, commonly known as the Schumann's nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, and it produces berries that range in color from yellow to red. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
