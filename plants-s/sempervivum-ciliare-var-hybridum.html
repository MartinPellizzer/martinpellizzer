Sempervivum ciliare var. hybridum

Sempervivum ciliare var. hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically green with red or pink hues on the edges. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its compact growth and drought tolerance.
