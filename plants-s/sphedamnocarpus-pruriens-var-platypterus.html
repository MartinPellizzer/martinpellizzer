Sphedamnocarpus pruriens var. platypterus

Sphedamnocarpus pruriens var. platypterus, commonly known as the flat-leaf itchleaf, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is known for its irritant properties, which can cause skin reactions in some individuals.
