Satureja alpina var. parviflora

Satureja alpina var. parviflora, commonly known as alpine savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features small, fragrant leaves with a strong savory aroma, and its flowers are usually pale pink to white. It produces small, four-lobed fruits that contain tiny, oval seeds.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
