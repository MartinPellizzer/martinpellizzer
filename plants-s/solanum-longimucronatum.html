Solanum longimucronatum

Solanum longimucronatum, commonly known as the long-tipped nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Solanum longimucronatum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates.
