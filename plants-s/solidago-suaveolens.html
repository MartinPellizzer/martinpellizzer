Solidago suaveolens

Solidago suaveolens, commonly known as sweet goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Sweet goldenrod plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late season.
