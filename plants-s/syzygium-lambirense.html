Syzygium lambirense

Syzygium lambirense, commonly known as the Lambirens tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a glossy surface. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, spherical berries.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
