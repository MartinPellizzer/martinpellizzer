Sasa makinoana

Sasa makinoana, commonly known as the Kauai bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the island of Kauai in Hawaii.

It is characterized by its dense clumps of slender, hollow stems that grow close to the ground. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Sasa makinoana thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
