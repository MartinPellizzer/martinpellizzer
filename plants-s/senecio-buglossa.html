Senecio buglossa

Senecio buglossa, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural surroundings.

Senecio buglossa is also noted for its hairy leaves, which help reduce water loss and enhance its drought tolerance.
