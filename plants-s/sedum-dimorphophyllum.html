Sedum dimorphophyllum

Sedum dimorphophyllum, commonly known as the "Ghost Plant," is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, leaf-like structures that resemble translucent, waxy leaves, which can change color based on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the growing season. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its fleshy leaves.

Sedum dimorphophyllum is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
