Solanum receptum

Solanum receptum, commonly known as the rare ghost pepper plant, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to the remote regions of northeastern India, where it grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that turn from green to a striking red or orange when mature. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a distinctive central stigma.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, Solanum receptum remains a subject of botanical interest and conservation efforts.
