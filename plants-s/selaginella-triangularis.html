Selaginella triangularis

Selaginella triangularis, commonly known as the tropical clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats or cushions on the forest floor. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are triangular in cross-section, giving it its scientific name. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands.

Selaginella triangularis reproduces via spores and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
