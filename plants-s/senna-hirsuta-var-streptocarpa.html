Senna hirsuta var. streptocarpa

Senna hirsuta var. streptocarpa, commonly known as the scarlet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with small, delicate leaflets.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
