Silene harae

Silene harae, commonly known as the Japanese catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Silene harae has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
