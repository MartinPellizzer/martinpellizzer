Sedum ledebourii

Sedum ledebourii, commonly known as the Desert Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is often found growing on rocky substrates or in sandy soils.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
