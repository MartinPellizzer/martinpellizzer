Smilax muscosa

Smilax muscosa, commonly known as the climbing smilax, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Smilax muscosa is a hardy plant that can spread aggressively through its rhizomes.
