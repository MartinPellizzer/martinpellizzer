Schoenoplectiella × magrathii

Schoenoplectiella × magrathii, commonly known as the hybrid bulrush, is a wetland plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a sterile hybrid between Schoenoplectiella mucronata and Schoenoplectiella polystachya, resulting in unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are slender and often pinkish in color.

This species plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
