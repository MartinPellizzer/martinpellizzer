Selaginella jenmanii

Selaginella jenmanii, commonly known as the Jenman's spikemoss, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with erect, spirally arranged leaves that are often scale-like in appearance. Selaginella jenmanii reproduces via spores, which are produced in specialized structures called sporophylls.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
