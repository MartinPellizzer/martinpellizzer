Syzygium fourcadei

Syzygium fourcadei, commonly known as the fourcade's syzygium, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

Syzygium fourcadei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
