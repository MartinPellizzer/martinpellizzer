Sophronitis coccinea subsp. mantiqueirae

Sophronitis coccinea subsp.

mantiqueirae, commonly known as the Mantiqueira red orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Brazilian state of Minas Gerais. It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers with contrasting yellow markings, which are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
