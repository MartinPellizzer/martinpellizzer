Spathodea uncinata

Spathodea uncinata, commonly known as the purple-edged flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in panicles. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
