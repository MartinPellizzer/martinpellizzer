Saxifraga sochondensis

Saxifraga sochondensis, commonly known as the Sochondenskaya saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Saxifraga sochondensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
