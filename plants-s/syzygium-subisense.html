Syzygium subisense

Syzygium subisense, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant red flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Syzygium subisense thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
