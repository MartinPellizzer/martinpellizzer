Scutellaria parvifolia

Scutellaria parvifolia, commonly known as small-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically blue or purple, arranged in spikes.

Scutellaria parvifolia is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
