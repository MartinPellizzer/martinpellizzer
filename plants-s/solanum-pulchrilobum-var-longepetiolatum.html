Solanum pulchrilobum var. longepetiolatum

Solanum pulchrilobum var. longepetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled beauty berry, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple berries that appear in clusters, often persisting through winter, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with long petioles, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

While primarily cultivated for its aesthetic value, it is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
