Sorbus margaretae

Sorbus margaretae, commonly known as the Margaretae rowan, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically orange-red in color, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
