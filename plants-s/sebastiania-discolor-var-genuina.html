Sebastiania discolor var. genuina

Sebastiania discolor var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, dark purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.
