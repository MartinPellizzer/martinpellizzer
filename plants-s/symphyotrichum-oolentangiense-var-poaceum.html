Symphyotrichum oolentangiense var. poaceum

Symphyotrichum oolentangiense var. poaceum, commonly known as the grassy white coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that give it its common name, and it often grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.
