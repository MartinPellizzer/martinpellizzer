Stemmacantha inermis

Stemmacantha inermis, commonly known as the slender stemmacantha, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
