Scutellaria pseudocoleus

Scutellaria pseudocoleus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Scutellaria pseudocoleus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
