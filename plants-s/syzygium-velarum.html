Syzygium velarum

Syzygium velarum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. The bark is smooth and grayish, often with a slight sheen.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
