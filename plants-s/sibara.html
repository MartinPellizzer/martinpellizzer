Sibara

Sibara, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are white with five petals, and it produces bright red berries that ripen in late summer.

Sibara is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
