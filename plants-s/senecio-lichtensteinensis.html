Senecio lichtensteinensis

Senecio lichtensteinensis, commonly known as the Lichtenstein's groundsel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
