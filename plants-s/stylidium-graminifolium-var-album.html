Stylidium graminifolium var. album

Stylidium graminifolium var. album, commonly known as the white spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and distinctive spurred structure, which is a feature of the Gentianaceae family. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and grasslands. It produces small, tubular flowers that are often visited by insects, facilitating pollination.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
