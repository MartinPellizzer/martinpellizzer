Salsola albida

Salsola albida, commonly known as silverleaf Russian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its pale green to silver-colored leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Salsola albida is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in saline and alkaline soils.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies.
