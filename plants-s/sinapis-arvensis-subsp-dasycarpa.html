Sinapis arvensis subsp. dasycarpa

Sinapis arvensis subsp.

dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. It has small, yellow flowers and is an annual species.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable in the wild.
