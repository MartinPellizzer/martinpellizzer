Saccharum alopecuroides subvar. brasilianum

Saccharum alopecuroides subvar. brasilianum, commonly known as Brazilian sugarcane, is a grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and dense foliage. The plant is notable for its high sugar content in its stems, making it a valuable resource for sugar production. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This subvariety is also used in traditional medicine and for biofuel production due to its adaptability and productivity.
