Sigesbeckia orientalis

Sigesbeckia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental willow herb, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along riverbanks.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in various habitats.
