Syzygium tula

Syzygium tula, commonly known as the tula apple or tula fruit, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are followed by edible, round fruits that resemble small apples.

The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional culinary preparations.
