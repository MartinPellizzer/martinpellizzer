Seidelia triandra f. pumila

Seidelia triandra f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, fleshy, orbicular leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white to grayish hairs. It produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems and the ability to store water in its leaves.
