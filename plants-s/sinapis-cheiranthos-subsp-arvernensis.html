Sinapis cheiranthos subsp. arvernensis

Sinapis cheiranthos subsp.

arvernensis, commonly known as the Alpian mustard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
