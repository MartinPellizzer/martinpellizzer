Scutellaria galericulata subsp. minor

Scutellaria galericulata subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lesser blueweed, is a small herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender stems and small, bilaterally symmetrical blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by aromatic foliage and square stems.
