Scandentia andrei

Scandentia andrei, commonly known as the Andean scandentia, is a climbing plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
