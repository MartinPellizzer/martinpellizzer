Stelis stevensonii

Stelis stevensonii, commonly known as the Stevenson's spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. It produces strikingly intricate flowers with a complex structure, featuring a prominent lip and specialized pollination mechanisms. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful cultivation to mimic its natural habitat.

Stelis stevensonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
