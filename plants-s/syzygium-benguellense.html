Syzygium benguellense

Syzygium benguellense, commonly known as the Benguella apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local use.
