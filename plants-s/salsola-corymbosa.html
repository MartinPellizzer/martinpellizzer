Salsola corymbosa

Salsola corymbosa, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has spiny leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into bristly, spherical fruits. It is well adapted to dry environments and can thrive in disturbed soils.

Salsola corymbosa is often considered a weed but has ecological and historical significance.
