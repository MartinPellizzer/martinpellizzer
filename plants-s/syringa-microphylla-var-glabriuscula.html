Syringa microphylla var. glabriuscula

Syringa microphylla var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or Japanese weeping cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique, drooping form and showy flowers make it a favorite for ornamental landscaping.
