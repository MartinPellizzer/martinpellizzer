Scirpus beccarii

Scirpus beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often growing in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color, which are followed by triangular, boat-shaped fruits. Scirpus beccarii is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to the ecological structure of its habitat.
