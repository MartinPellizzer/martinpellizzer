Sambucus pubens f. dimidiata

Sambucus pubens f. dimidiata, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a flowering shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible when fully ripe but contain toxic compounds when unripe. This species is often found in moist, open woodlands and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
