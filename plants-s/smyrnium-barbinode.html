Smyrnium barbinode

Smyrnium barbinode, commonly known as the hairy cowbane, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed follicles that release seeds when mature.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
