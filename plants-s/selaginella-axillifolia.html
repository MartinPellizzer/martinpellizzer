Selaginella axillifolia

Selaginella axillifolia, commonly known as the common spikemoss, is a species of spikemoss belonging to the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with erect, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces sporangia in terminal clusters, which are responsible for reproduction through spores.

Selaginella axillifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
