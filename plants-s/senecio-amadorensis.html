Senecio amadorensis

Senecio amadorensis, commonly known as the Amadore's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in mountainous regions and has a limited geographic distribution.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
