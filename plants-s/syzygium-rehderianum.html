Syzygium rehderianum

Syzygium rehderianum, commonly known as the mountain lychee, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the highlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a smooth, red or yellowish drupe with a single seed, similar in appearance to lychee but smaller. It thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and requires a warm, sunny environment.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated for its edible fruit.
