Sonchus afromontanus

Sonchus afromontanus, commonly known as the East African sow thistle, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in montane grasslands and forest margins. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while its flowers attract various pollinators.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
