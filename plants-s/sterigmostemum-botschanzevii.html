Sterigmostemum botschanzevii

Sterigmostemum botschanzevii, commonly known as the Botchanzevii stigmaria, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive stigmarian structures, which are specialized, root-like appendages that serve as anchoring organs. The plant exhibits a peculiar morphology with a short, erect stem and a cluster of modified leaves that resemble spines. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a fossil plant, providing insights into ancient ecosystems and plant evolution.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile nature, it is considered a valuable subject for paleobotanical research.
