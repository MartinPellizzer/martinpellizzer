Symplocos crassipes var. penangiana

Symplocos crassipes var. penangiana, commonly known as Penang holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on the upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
