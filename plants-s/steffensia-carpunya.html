Steffensia carpunya

Steffensia carpunya, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Steffensia carpunya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
