Strophopappus glomeratus

Strophopappus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in dense groups at the ends of the stems.

The species is known for its resilience in harsh environments, making it a common sight in dry, rocky habitats.
