Sabina centrasiatica

Sabina centrasiatica, commonly known as the Central Asian juniper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family, which includes cypresses and junipers, and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, berry-like cones. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its aromatic foliage and resilience to harsh climates make it an important component of its native ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local communities.
