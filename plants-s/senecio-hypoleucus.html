Senecio hypoleucus

Senecio hypoleucus, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. Its leaves are generally greyish-green with a woolly texture, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

It is considered a decorative plant but can be invasive in some regions.
