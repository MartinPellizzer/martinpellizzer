Sagenia menyanthidis

Sagenia menyanthidis, commonly known as the yellow water-lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant features floating leaves with a waxy surface and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system consists of rhizomes that anchor it in the substrate and help absorb nutrients.

Sagenia menyanthidis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
