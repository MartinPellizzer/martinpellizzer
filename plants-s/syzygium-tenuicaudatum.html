Syzygium tenuicaudatum

Syzygium tenuicaudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed syzygium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
