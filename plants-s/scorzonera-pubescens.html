Scorzonera pubescens

Scorzonera pubescens, commonly known as the hairy scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, stout stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are tuberous and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Scorzonera pubescens thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
