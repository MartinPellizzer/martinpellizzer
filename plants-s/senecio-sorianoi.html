Senecio sorianoi

Senecio sorianoi, commonly known as the Sorianoi groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water, while its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Senecio sorianoi is a perennial species, often found in open habitats with good drainage.
