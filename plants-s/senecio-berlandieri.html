Senecio berlandieri

Senecio berlandieri, commonly known as the Blueweed or Desert Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio berlandieri is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
