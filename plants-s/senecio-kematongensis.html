Senecio kematongensis

Senecio kematongensis, commonly known as the Kematong groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in many species. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Senecio kematongensis is also noted for its yellowish flower heads, which bloom in the summer months.
