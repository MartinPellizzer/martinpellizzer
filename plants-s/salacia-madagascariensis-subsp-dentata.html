Salacia madagascariensis subsp. dentata

Salacia madagascariensis subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the toothed salacia, is a species native to Madagascar. It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its distinctive toothed leaves.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often yellow or greenish in color, and it produces fleshy, red or orange berries.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
