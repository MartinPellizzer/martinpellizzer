Sempervivum globiferum subsp. lagarinianum

Sempervivum globiferum subsp.

lagarinianum, commonly known as the Alpine houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features rosettes of fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge.

The plant produces compact inflorescences with small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It is well adapted to rocky and sunny environments, thriving in well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cold climates.
