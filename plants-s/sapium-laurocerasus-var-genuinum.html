Sapium laurocerasus var. genuinum

Sapium laurocerasus var. genuinum, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.
