Silene laconica

Silene laconica, commonly known as the Laconic catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Silene laconica is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Its structure and growth habits make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
