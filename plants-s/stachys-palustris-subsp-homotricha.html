Stachys palustris subsp. homotricha

Stachys palustris subsp.

homotricha, commonly known as swamp betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with fine, stiff hairs.

The plant produces spikes of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Stachys palustris subsp.

homotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
