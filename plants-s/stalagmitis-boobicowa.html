Stalagmitis boobicowa

Stalagmitis boobicowa, commonly known as the Crystal Cactus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of the southern hemisphere.

It features a tall, columnar stem covered in dense, waxy spines that help reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant orange flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from deep underground, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The plant's slow growth and striking appearance make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and arid landscapes.
