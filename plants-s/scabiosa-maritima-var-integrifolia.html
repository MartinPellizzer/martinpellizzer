Scabiosa maritima var. integrifolia

Scabiosa maritima var. integrifolia, commonly known as the coastal scabious, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers with a distinctive, button-like appearance. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface and a serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments with salty winds and sandy soils.
