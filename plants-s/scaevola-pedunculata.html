Scaevola pedunculata

Scaevola pedunculata, commonly known as the common fanflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bilaterally symmetrical blue or purple flowers. The plant features opposite leaves that are lanceolate in shape and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Scaevola pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscaping.
