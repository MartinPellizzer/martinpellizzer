Sideritis altiatlantica

Sideritis altiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Sideritis altiatlantica is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
