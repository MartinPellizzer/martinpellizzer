Scirpus tranquebariensis

Scirpus tranquebariensis, commonly known as the Trancebar reed, is a wetland plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically found in marshy or aquatic environments. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Scirpus tranquebariensis is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
