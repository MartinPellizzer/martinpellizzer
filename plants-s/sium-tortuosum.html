Sium tortuosum

Sium tortuosum, commonly known as wild celery or water parsnip, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic properties.

Sium tortuosum thrives in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, sandy soils.
