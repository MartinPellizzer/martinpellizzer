Spiraeanthemum austrocaledonicum

Spiraeanthemum austrocaledonicum, commonly known as the New Caledonia spirea, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are covered in fine, silvery hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and seasonal blooms.
