Sorghum xanthoblepharis

Sorghum xanthoblepharis, commonly known as the yellow-eyed sorghum, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its distinctive yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to yellow flowers, which are often clustered near the top of the stems. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
