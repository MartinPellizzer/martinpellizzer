Solanum hutchisonii

Solanum hutchisonii, commonly known as the Hutchison's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its small, white or pale purple flowers bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
