Spiropetalum erythrosepalum

Spiropetalum erythrosepalum, commonly known as the red-sepal spiraling orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored sepals that range from red to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the base.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
