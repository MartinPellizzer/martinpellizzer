Salvia nanchuanensis f. intermedia

Salvia nanchuanensis f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate nanchuan sage, is a unique horticultural form of the species Salvia nanchuanensis.

It is native to China and is characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral structure. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.

This form is particularly noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil conditions.
