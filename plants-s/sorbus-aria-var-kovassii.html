Sorbus aria var. kovassii

Sorbus aria var. kovassii, commonly known as the Hungarian rowan, is a variant of the rowan tree native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-orange fruits, which are edible when ripe but contain seeds that are toxic if consumed in large quantities. The plant produces small, pinnate leaves and is typically found in mountainous regions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife.
