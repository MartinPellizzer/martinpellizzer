Scrophularia aquatica var. glabra

Scrophularia aquatica var. glabra, commonly known as water figwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, exhibiting a spreading growth habit with erect stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with a fine, smooth texture.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to aquatic conditions.
