Syzygium teysmannii

Syzygium teysmannii, commonly known as the Taysmann's apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Syzygium teysmannii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
