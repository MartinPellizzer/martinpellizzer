Saxifraga decipiens f. hujus

Saxifraga decipiens f. hujus, commonly known as the "False Stonecrop," is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is a succulent perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features fleshy, often blue-green leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pink. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and attractive foliage.
