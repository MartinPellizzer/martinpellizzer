Selliguea coriacea

Selliguea coriacea, commonly known as the rock fern, is a member of the Pterobryaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery fronds.

Selliguea coriacea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitat for other organisms in its ecosystem.
