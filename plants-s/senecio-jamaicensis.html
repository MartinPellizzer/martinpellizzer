Senecio jamaicensis

Senecio jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough texture, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in clusters.

Senecio jamaicensis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
