Solanum caripense var. pilosohirsutum

Solanum caripense var. pilosohirsutum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a yellow or orange color. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
