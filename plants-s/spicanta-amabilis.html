Spicanta amabilis

Spicanta amabilis, commonly known as the fragrant spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces fragrant, pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a smooth texture, turning vibrant shades of red in the fall.

Spicanta amabilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
