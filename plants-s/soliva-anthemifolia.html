Soliva anthemifolia

Soliva anthemifolia, commonly known as common soliva, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Soliva anthemifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
