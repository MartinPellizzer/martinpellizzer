Stachys humilis

Stachys humilis, commonly known as woolly betony, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, white pubescence, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Stachys humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
