Stevia achalensis

Stevia achalensis, commonly known as the Achalensis sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing high concentrations of steviol glycosides, which are natural sweeteners. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its medicinal and commercial potential, it has gained interest in herbal and agricultural research.
