Satyrium marginatum

Satyrium marginatum, commonly known as the narrow-margin orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with distinctive red or purple markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Satyrium marginatum is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
