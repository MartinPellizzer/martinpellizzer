Silene linifolia

Silene linifolia, commonly known as the silky filaments or slender pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, thread-like stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Silene linifolia is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
