Symphyotrichum kentuckiense

Symphyotrichum kentuckiense, commonly known as Kentucky aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Kentucky aster plays an important role in supporting pollinators during the late season.
