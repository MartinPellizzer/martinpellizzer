Satureja × perez-pazii

Satureja × perez-pazii, commonly known as the hybrid savory, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowering stems.

This plant is a naturally occurring hybrid between Satureja hirsuta and Satureja montana, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. It typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The leaves are often used in culinary applications for their pungent, savory aroma, while the flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
