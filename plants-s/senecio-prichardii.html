Senecio prichardii

Senecio prichardii, commonly known as the Chihuahuan Desert groundselt, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy stems and narrow, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of its branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
