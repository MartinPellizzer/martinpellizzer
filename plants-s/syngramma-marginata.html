Syngramma marginata

Syngramma marginata, commonly known as the margined leaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with distinct margins that are often toothed or lobed. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in spring or early summer. Syngramma marginata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
