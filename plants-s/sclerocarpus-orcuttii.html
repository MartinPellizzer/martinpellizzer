Sclerocarpus orcuttii

Sclerocarpus orcuttii, commonly known as the desert hackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native desert ecosystems.
