Salix × teplouchovii

Salix × teplouchovii, commonly known as the Teplouchovii willow, is a hybrid species within the genus Salix, characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It typically exhibits narrow, elongated leaves that are often a vibrant green, with some cultivars displaying variegated patterns. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, commonly found in temperate regions where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscaping projects.
