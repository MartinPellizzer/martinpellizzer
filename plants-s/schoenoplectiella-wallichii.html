Schoenoplectiella wallichii

Schoenoplectiella wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often reddish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Schoenoplectiella wallichii is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its potential use in phytoremediation.
