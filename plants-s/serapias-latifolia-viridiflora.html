Serapias latifolia viridiflora

Serapias latifolia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear basal leaves which are broad and slightly lobed. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive green throat, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in alpine and coastal habitats.
