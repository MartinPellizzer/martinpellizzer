Scrophularia variegata var. libanotica

Scrophularia variegata var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon figwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. It is often found in open habitats such as slopes and gravelly areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
