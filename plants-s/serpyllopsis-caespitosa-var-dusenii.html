Serpyllopsis caespitosa var. dusenii

Serpyllopsis caespitosa var. dusenii, commonly known as the Dusen's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or wetland habitats. The plant forms dense, tufted clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to reddish in color. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in spike-like clusters, and it is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
