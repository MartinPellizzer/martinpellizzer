Senecio picridifolius

Senecio picridifolius, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Senecio picridifolius is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
