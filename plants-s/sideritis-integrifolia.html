Sideritis integrifolia

Sideritis integrifolia, commonly known as the Greek oregano or mountain thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant is often found growing in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antispasmodic and digestive properties.

Sideritis integrifolia is also valued in culinary applications, particularly in Greek cuisine, where it is used to flavor dishes and beverages.
