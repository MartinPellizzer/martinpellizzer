Senacia undulata

Senacia undulata, commonly known as the undulating senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in a cluster. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly wavy edge, giving it its common name.

Senacia undulata is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
