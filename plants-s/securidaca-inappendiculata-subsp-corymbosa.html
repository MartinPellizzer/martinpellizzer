Securidaca inappendiculata subsp. corymbosa

Securidaca inappendiculata subsp.

corymbosa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The bark is smooth and can be grey or brown in color, with a distinctive aromatic scent.
