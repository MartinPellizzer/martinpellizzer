Solanum oxycarpum var. brachycarpum

Solanum oxycarpum var. brachycarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
