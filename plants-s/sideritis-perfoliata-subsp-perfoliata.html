Sideritis perfoliata subsp. perfoliata

Sideritis perfoliata subsp.

perfoliata, commonly known as the leafy sainfoin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in racemes, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas. Sideritis perfoliata subsp.

perfoliata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
