Salacia macrocarpa var. angustifolia

Salacia macrocarpa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved salacia, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that are often red or orange when mature. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.

The species is also notable for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
