Silene saxifraga var. petraea

Silene saxifraga var. petraea, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh conditions of its environment.

Silene saxifraga var. petraea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
