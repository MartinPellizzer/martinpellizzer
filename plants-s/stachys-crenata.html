Stachys crenata

Stachys crenata, commonly known as hairy betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and spikes of purple or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and healing properties.

Stachys crenata is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
