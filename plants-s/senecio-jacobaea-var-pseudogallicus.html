Senecio jacobaea var. pseudogallicus

Senecio jacobaea var. pseudogallicus, commonly known as the Gallic Senecio, is a variant of the common ragwort, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and robust growth habit.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems rise from the center, bearing clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

This variant is notable for its ecological role as a food source for certain insects and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
