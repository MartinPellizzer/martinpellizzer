Sida hermaphrodita

Sida hermaphrodita, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual with alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Sida hermaphrodita is also recognized for its potential use in traditional medicine.
