Saxifraga cardiophylla

Saxifraga cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh, cold environment. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a single umbel. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and tundra habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
