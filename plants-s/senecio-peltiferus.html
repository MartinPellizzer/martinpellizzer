Senecio peltiferus

Senecio peltiferus, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically opposite, narrow, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Senecio peltiferus is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions.
