Smilacina henryi var. szechuanica

Smilacina henryi var. szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan greenbriar, is a climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Smilacaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils that help it climb towards light. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its foliage is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
