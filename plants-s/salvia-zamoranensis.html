Salvia zamoranensis

Salvia zamoranensis, commonly known as Zamora's sage, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Salvia zamoranensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitat.
