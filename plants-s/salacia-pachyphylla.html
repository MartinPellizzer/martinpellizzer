Salacia pachyphylla

Salacia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved salacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its often arid habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
