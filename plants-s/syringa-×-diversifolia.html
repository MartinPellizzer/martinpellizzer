Syringa × diversifolia

Syringa × diversifolia, commonly known as the weeping lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Syringa × diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful, flowing form.
