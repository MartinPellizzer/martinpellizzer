Syringa persica var. alba

Syringa persica var. alba, commonly known as the white Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

The white Persian lilac is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.
