Setaria gracilis f. brevispica

Setaria gracilis f. brevispica, commonly known as the short-awned slender foxtail, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with short awns, which distinguish it from other members of the genus. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its adaptability and spread.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
