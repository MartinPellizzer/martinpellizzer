Sisymbrium hispidulum var. weddellii

Sisymbrium hispidulum var. weddellii, commonly known as Weddell's hoary vetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, perennial herb with hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive drought conditions.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
