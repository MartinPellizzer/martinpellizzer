Solidago virgaurea proles serratifolia

Solidago virgaurea proles serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with fine, hairy hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated at the edges, and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are golden-yellow, occurring in clustered inflorescences at the tips of the stems.

This plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed habitats.
