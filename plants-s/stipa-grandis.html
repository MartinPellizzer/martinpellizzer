Stipa grandis

Stipa grandis, commonly known as the giant feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Stipa grandis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
