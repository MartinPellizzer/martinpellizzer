Stoebe humilis

Stoebe humilis, commonly known as lesser betony, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Stoebe humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
