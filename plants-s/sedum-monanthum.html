Sedum monanthum

Sedum monanthum, commonly known as the one-flowered stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Sedum monanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
