Solanum chlamydogynum

Solanum chlamydogynum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, often spiny fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, with simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or pale purple flowers.

Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, containing solanine and other alkaloids that can cause severe poisoning if ingested.
