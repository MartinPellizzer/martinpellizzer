Sisymbrium suffruticosum

Sisymbrium suffruticosum, commonly known as the creeping whitlowgrass, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks. The plant has hairy stems that often lie close to the ground, and its small white or yellow flowers appear in clusters. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in supporting local insect populations.
