Specklinia cucumeris

Specklinia cucumeris, commonly known as the cucumber fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble the texture of cucumber skin, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small, epiphytic fern, often found on tree trunks or branches in humid environments. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with finely divided segments that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Specklinia cucumeris prefers indirect light and consistently moist conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
