Stemona tuberosa var. minor

Stemona tuberosa var. minor, commonly known as the lesser stemona, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually aromatic. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for containing bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
