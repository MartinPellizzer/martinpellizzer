Silene exscapa subsp. norica

Silene exscapa subsp.

norica, commonly known as the Alpine campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant features showy pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is characterized by its erect stems and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
