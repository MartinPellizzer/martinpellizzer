Senecio macroglossus

Senecio macroglossus, commonly known as the giant lipstick plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that resemble lipsticks, hence its common name. The plant features a thick, woody stem with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
