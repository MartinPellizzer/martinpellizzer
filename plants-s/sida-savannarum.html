Sida savannarum

Sida savannarum, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in savanna and woodland habitats. The plant has small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Sida savannarum is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in areas with seasonal rainfall.
