Salsola kurbanovii

Salsola kurbanovii, commonly known as Kurbanov's saltwort, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers and is often found in desert regions across Central Asia. Its root system helps stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in ecological restoration.

Salsola kurbanovii is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to accumulate heavy metals.
