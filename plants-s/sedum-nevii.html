Sedum nevii

Sedum nevii, commonly known as the alpine stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh, arid environments. The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late summer. It is well adapted to rocky soils and cold climates, making it a hardy choice for alpine gardens.

Sedum nevii is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its resilience and ornamental appeal.
