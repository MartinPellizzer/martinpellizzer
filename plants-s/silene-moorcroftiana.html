Silene moorcroftiana

Silene moorcroftiana, commonly known as the moorland catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Silene moorcroftiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
