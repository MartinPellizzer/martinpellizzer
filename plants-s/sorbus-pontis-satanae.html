Sorbus pontis-satanae

Sorbus pontis-satanae, commonly known as the Pontic mountain ash, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
