Senecio guineensis

Senecio guineensis, commonly known as African ironweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. Senecio guineensis is often found in moist grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
