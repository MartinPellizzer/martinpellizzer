Sterculia albidiflora

Sterculia albidiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered sterculia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
