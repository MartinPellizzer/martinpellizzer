Sinarundinaria petelotii

Sinarundinaria petelotii, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine layer of wax. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
