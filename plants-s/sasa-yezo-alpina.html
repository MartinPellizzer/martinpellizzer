Sasa yezo-alpina

Sasa yezo-alpina, commonly known as the Yezo alpine sasa, is a clump-forming grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine zones. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Sasa yezo-alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
