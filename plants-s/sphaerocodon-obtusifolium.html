Sphaerocodon obtusifolium

Sphaerocodon obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf sphaerocodon, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its growth habit is compact, often forming a rosette structure that helps reduce water loss.

The leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle, further enhancing its drought tolerance.
