Syzygium pearsonianum

Syzygium pearsonianum, commonly known as the Java apple or *Pomelo*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The tree produces large, round fruits with a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its botanical features make it well-suited for humid climates and rich, well-drained soils.

Syzygium pearsonianum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
