Spergularia rupicola var. crassipes

Spergularia rupicola var. crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf sandspurry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune habitats.
