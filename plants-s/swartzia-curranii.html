Swartzia curranii

Swartzia curranii, commonly known as the Curran's Swartzia, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and compound leaves.

Native to tropical regions of South America, this plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and humid environments. It features a straight, slender trunk with smooth, grayish bark and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some areas.
