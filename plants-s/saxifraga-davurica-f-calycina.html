Saxifraga davurica f. calycina

Saxifraga davurica f. calycina, commonly known as the calycine saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a hairy texture that helps it thrive in harsh alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
