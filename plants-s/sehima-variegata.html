Sehima variegata

Sehima variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow hues. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Sehima variegata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
