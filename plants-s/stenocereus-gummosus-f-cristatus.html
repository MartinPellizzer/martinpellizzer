Stenocereus gummosus f. cristatus

Stenocereus gummosus f. cristatus, commonly known as the cristate organ pipe cactus, is a unique variant of the organ pipe cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

This plant is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that form a distinctive crest or "cristate" shape, which is a result of a genetic mutation. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny, arid environment. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
