Scabiosa columbaria var. vera

Scabiosa columbaria var. vera, commonly known as the sweet William or garden scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
