Sabicea velutina subsp. duidensis

Sabicea velutina subsp.

duidensis, commonly known as the Duiden sabicea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
