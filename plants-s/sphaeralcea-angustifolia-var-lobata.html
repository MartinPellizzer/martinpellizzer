Sphaeralcea angustifolia var. lobata

Sphaeralcea angustifolia var. lobata, commonly known as narrowleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, narrow leaves that are often lobed, giving it its varietal name "lobata.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in open plains, grasslands, and rocky areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
