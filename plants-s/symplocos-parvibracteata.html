Symplocos parvibracteata

Symplocos parvibracteata, commonly known as the small-bract evergreen holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Symplocos parvibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
