Sauromatum gaoligongense

Sauromatum gaoligongense, commonly known as the Gaoligong mountain arum, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically greenish-white, with a strong, musky scent that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
