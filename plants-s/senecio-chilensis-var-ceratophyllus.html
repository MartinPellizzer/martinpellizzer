Senecio chilensis var. ceratophyllus

Senecio chilensis var. ceratophyllus, commonly known as the Chilean horse-tail, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The flower heads are usually yellow or orange, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
