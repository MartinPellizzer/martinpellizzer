Silene mollissima var. tomentosa

Silene mollissima var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Silene mollissima var. tomentosa is a perennial species that is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
