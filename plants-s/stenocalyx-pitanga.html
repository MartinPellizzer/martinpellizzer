Stenocalyx pitanga

Stenocalyx pitanga, commonly known as the Brazilian pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
