Salacia undulata

Salacia undulata, commonly known as the wavy salacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Salaciacae and is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive wavy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
