Scutellaria lateriflora var. albiflora

Scutellaria lateriflora var. albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue or white flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the mint family (Lamiaceae).
