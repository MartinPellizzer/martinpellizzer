Scirpus maritimus var. conglobatus

Scirpus maritimus var. conglobatus, commonly known as the clustered bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal and brackish wetlands.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of flowering spikes that rise from rhizomatous stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss. It thrives in saline or brackish environments and plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines.

Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by water and wind.
