Salvia prysmatica

Salvia prysmatica, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Salvia prysmatica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
