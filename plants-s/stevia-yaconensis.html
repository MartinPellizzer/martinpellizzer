Stevia yaconensis

Stevia yaconensis, commonly known as Yacon, is a perennial plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used for consumption. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its roots are rich in carbohydrates and contain natural sweeteners called steviol glycosides, making it a popular alternative to sugar.

Stevia yaconensis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
