Symphoria macrocarpa

Symphoria macrocarpa, commonly known as the Australian ash or white ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in local ecosystems.
