Satureja coccinea

Satureja coccinea, commonly known as scarlet sage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red and purple. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and four-lobed petals, contributing to its ornamental value.
