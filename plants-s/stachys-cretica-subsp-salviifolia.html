Stachys cretica subsp. salviifolia

Stachys cretica subsp.

salviifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces spikes of violet or blue flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
