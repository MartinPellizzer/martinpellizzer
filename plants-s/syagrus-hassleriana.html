Syagrus hassleriana

Syagrus hassleriana, commonly known as the Hassler's palm, is a species of palm native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 5 meters. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Syagrus hassleriana is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
