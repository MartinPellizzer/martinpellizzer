Sarcochilus roseus

Sarcochilus roseus, commonly known as the rose mohave, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a robust, fleshy rhizome and produces erect, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its striking pink to reddish flowers, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Sarcochilus roseus is a hardy species, often used in rock gardens and dry landscapes due to its drought tolerance.
