Saussurea vestitiformis

Saussurea vestitiformis, commonly known as the woolly saffron.

It is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, typically found in rocky and alpine environments. The plant features a robust root system and produces tall, sparsely branched stems covered in dense, woolly hairs. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, with a distinctive saffron-like hue, and are often clustered at the top of the stem.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
