Seris vaginata

Seris vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical annual herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by round, green pods that contain numerous seeds. It is commonly found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields, making it a weed in many regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse conditions contribute to its ecological and agricultural significance.
