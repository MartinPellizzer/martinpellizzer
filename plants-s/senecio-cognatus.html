Senecio cognatus

Senecio cognatus, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to South Africa and features striking yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with hairy, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Senecio cognatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
