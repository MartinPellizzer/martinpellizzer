Stachys tournefortii

Stachys tournefortii, commonly known as the Turkey tail plant, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
