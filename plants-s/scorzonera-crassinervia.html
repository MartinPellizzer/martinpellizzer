Scorzonera crassinervia

Scorzonera crassinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust taproot system, which is often harvested for its edible tuberous roots. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months.

Scorzonera crassinervia is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
