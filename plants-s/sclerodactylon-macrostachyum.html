Sclerodactylon macrostachyum

Sclerodactylon macrostachyum, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and produces heavy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree's bark is deeply fissured and has a rough, scaly texture. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The wood of Sclerodactylon macrostachyum is highly valued for its durability and fine grain.
