Scyphularia sinusora

Scyphularia sinusora, commonly known as the sinuous scyphularia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flower heads that resemble daisies. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
