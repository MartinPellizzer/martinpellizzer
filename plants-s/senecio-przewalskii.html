Senecio przewalskii

Senecio przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Senecio przewalskii is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
