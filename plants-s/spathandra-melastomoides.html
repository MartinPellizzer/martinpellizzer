Spathandra melastomoides

Spathandra melastomoides, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Spathandra melastomoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
