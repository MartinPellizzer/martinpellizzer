Spiraea prunifolia var. prunifolia

Spiraea prunifolia var. prunifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are finely toothed and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early summer, which are borne on the previous year's growth. Its foliage turns a striking red or orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to landscaping.
