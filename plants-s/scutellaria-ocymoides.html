Scutellaria ocymoides

Scutellaria ocymoides, commonly known as narrowleaf skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, blue to violet flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, and it produces distinctive, helmet-like seed capsules.

Scutellaria ocymoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
