Scirpus elegans

Scirpus elegans, commonly known as the graceful bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in spike-like clusters at the top of the stems. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and improve water filtration in wetland ecosystems.

Scirpus elegans is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic life and its aesthetic appeal in wetland restoration projects.
