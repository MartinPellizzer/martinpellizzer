Salacca bakeriana

Salacca bakeriana, commonly known as the sugar palm or betel palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves that form a crown at the top. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling small, hairy apples. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of sugar.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
