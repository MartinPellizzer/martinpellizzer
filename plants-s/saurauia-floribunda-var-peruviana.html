Saurauia floribunda var. peruviana

Saurauia floribunda var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian flame tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. These flowers are followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments within its native habitat.
