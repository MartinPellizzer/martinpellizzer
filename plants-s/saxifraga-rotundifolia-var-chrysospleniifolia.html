Saxifraga rotundifolia var. chrysospleniifolia

Saxifraga rotundifolia var. chrysospleniifolia, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions.

It features compact rosettes of dark green, rounded leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in challenging environments.
