Sphondylium conforme

Sphondylium conforme, commonly known as the upright spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has smooth, green stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens in warm climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
