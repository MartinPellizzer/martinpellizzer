Spiraea ulmifolia var. pilosa

Spiraea ulmifolia var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf spiraea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
