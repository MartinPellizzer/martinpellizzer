Smilax china var. taiheiensis

Smilax china var. taiheiensis, commonly known as the Japanese arrowroot or Chinese smilax, is a perennial climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features a robust, woody stem with sharp, recurved spines along its length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy texture and a prominent midrib.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
