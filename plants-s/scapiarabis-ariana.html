Scapiarabis ariana

Scapiarabis ariana, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers on long, slender stems. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Scapiarabis ariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
