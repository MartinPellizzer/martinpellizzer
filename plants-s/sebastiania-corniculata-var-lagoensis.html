Sebastiania corniculata var. lagoensis

Sebastiania corniculata var. lagoensis, commonly known as the Lagoense beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces showy, violet-purple berries that are a striking feature, often attracting birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, acidic soils.

The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
