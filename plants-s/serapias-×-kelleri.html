Serapias × kelleri

Serapias × kelleri, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Serapias aucubifolia and Serapias microchila.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive hood-like structure. Its leaves are grass-like and basal, forming a rosette, while its stems are erect and bear the floral spikes.

Serapias × kelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, well-drained soils.
