Strophanthus wightianus

Strophanthus wightianus, commonly known as the yellow star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces striking yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from October to December. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine, where various parts of the plant are used for their therapeutic effects.
