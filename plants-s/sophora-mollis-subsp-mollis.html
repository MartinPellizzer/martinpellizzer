Sophora mollis subsp. mollis

Sophora mollis subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the velvet tamarack, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety appearance, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces long, cylindrical pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest edges.

The species is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
