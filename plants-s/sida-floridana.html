Sida floridana

Sida floridana, commonly known as Florida sida, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.

Sida floridana plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
