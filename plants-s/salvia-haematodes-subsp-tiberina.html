Salvia haematodes subsp. tiberina

Salvia haematodes subsp.

tiberina, commonly known as the Tiber sage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and striking violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, though its use is less common compared to other salvias.
