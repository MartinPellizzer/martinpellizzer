Senecio bakeri

Senecio bakeri, commonly known as Baker's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
