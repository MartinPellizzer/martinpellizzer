Salix phanera var. weixiensis

Salix phanera var. weixiensis, commonly known as the Weixian willow, is a rare and ornamental species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, ovate leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation.
