Silene auriculata

Silene auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that have ear-like lobes, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Silene auriculata is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in partial to full sunlight.
