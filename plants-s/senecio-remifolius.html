Senecio remifolius

Senecio remifolius, commonly known as the woollyleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Senecio remifolius is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
