Salix myrioblephara

Salix myrioblephara, commonly known as the many-eyed willow, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in catkins. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
