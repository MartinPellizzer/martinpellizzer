Sonchus sundaicus

Sonchus sundaicus, commonly known as the Sunda sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and disturbed soils. It has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and are surrounded by a series of small, green bracts.
