Spiraea odorata

Spiraea odorata, commonly known as sweet spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features dense, rounded clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems and dark green, serrated leaves that turn red in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Spiraea odorata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
