Solanum lyciifolium

Solanum lyciifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
