Stachys palustris var. nipigonensis

Stachys palustris var. nipigonensis, commonly known as the Nipigon betony, is a variant of the marsh woundwort.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America, typically found in wet or moist habitats such as bogs and marshes. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
