Scirpus lacustris var. occidentalis

Scirpus lacustris var. occidentalis, commonly known as western bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in wetland environments, including marshes, lake shores, and along rivers.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
