Septas capensis

Septas capensis, commonly known as the Cape mountain gum, is a small evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
