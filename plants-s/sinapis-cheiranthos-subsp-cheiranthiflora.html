Sinapis cheiranthos subsp. cheiranthiflora

Sinapis cheiranthos subsp.

cheiranthiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf mustard, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, green, ovate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its fruits are slender siliques that contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.
